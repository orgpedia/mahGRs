Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) has issued a notification under the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Ordinance, 2015, regarding the notification of necessary documents, fees, fixed time limit, designated officers, first and second appellate authorities.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Soil and Water Conservation
Government Resolution No: Parikrama-2018/ PR No.49/ Water-17
Madam Cama Road, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk,
Ministry of Home Affairs, Mumbai-32
Date:- 30/10/2018
Reference:- Maharashtra Ordinance No. 2015 of 2015. 5
Introduction: Good governance is essential for the sustainable development of the economic and social sectors of public life. Transparency, accountability and responsiveness are the three essential elements of good governance. The Maharashtra Right to Public Service Ordinance, 2015, was promulgated to provide for efficient and timely delivery of public service to improve and strengthen the relationship between the people and the administration and to bring accountability and transparency in the administration. It has been implemented in the state on 28/04/2015. In view of the provision mentioned in Section-3 of this Ordinance, it is necessary to complete the service being provided by the department, designated officers, first and second appellate officers and related services by declaring the due date. Following this, the government decided:
Government Decision: A total of 8 services mentioned in the annexure to this Government Decision shall be notified by all the Regional Offices in the State in accordance with Article 3.
(b) The list of public services to be provided by the Soil and Water Conservation Department, the prescribed time limit, the prescribed pattern and fees, the details of the designated officer, the first and second appellate officers should be displayed on the notice board of the office.
(a) The concerned Regional Offices shall not be able to change the 8 services, necessary documents and the due date mentioned in the letter of association with the Government decision.
(d) the procedure for notifying and enforcing the service in accordance with section 3 of the Reference Ordinance. The Chief Engineer, Minor Irrigation (Water Conservation), Pune will be responsible for completing the work by November 10, 2018.
(e) the responsibilities for providing the services notified in accordance with the provisions of the reference Ordinance shall rest with the jurisdictions referred to in the accompanying annex.
(e) A consolidated report of all the Regional Offices regarding the said services shall be submitted to the Government in the prescribed return by the Commissioner (Soil and Water Conservation) Aurangabad without fail on the 1st day of every month.
