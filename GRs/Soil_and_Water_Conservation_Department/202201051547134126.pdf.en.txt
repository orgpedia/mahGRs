Under the Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme, it is proposed to repair the water conservation treatment structures in order to restore the irrigation capacity by preserving and repairing the schemes that require maintenance and repair of the completed water conservation schemes in the state. A total of 41 schemes under Nashik Circle Office will be revised and out of 41 schemes, 7 schemes are in first priority, 33 schemes are in second priority and 1 scheme is included in the revised scheme in third priority.
The total amount for repairs is Rs. 9.3338 crore. After the restoration of these projects, 1384 hectares of irrigated area and 6114 sq.m. of water storage will be restored. The proposal for administrative approval of these works has been submitted to the government by the Additional Commissioner and Chief Engineer, Water Conservation, Aurangabad. Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Nashik has scrutinized/ verified all the estimates at his level and certified the same. Considering the above, administrative approval was under consideration for the amendment proposal of the 41 works mentioned. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
Government Decision: The total budget of 46 renovation projects in Nashik Regional Water Conservation Division is Rs. 9.3338 crore (Rs. These price budgets are being accorded administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions.
