The Proposal:
Water and Land Management Institute (WALMI) Aurangabad, was under the Water Resources Department. However, as per the Government decision dated 31.05.2017, the Valmy Institute has been transferred to the Department of Soil and Water Conservation and brought under the administrative control of the Department of Soil and Water Conservation without affecting the autonomy of the Institute.
Department of Higher and Technical Education, Govt. Narrow 2015/ S.No.481/ 15/ Vs. S. 1, D. As per the decision taken on 12.07.2016, the retirement age of the teachers of Government Technical Colleges/ Diploma Institutes/ Colleges affiliated to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education in the higher education sector in the state has been increased from 58 to 62 years as per the prescribed age and the retirement age of the teachers/ librarians/ deputy librarians/ assistant librarians/ directors, deputy directors and assistant directors, physical education of non-agricultural universities, technical universities and affiliated non-government aided colleges has been increased from 60 to 62 years. Prior to that, the Department of Water Resources, Government Resolution No. Seniwe-2011/ No. 126/11/ Lokshevi (Aastha), Dy. As on 05.09.2011, the age of retirement for teaching posts in Valmiki Institutions was increased from 58 years to 62 years. The earlier decision on the retirement age of the faculty at Valmy Institute was based on the decision taken by the Department of Higher and Technical Education. Therefore, a proposal to increase the retirement age of teachers from 62 to 60 years was under consideration of the government.

The Institute of Water and Land Management (WALMI), Aurangabad is a 100% Government funded Institute established under the Societies Act, 1960.
