The Proposal:
The Central Government has issued and implemented the Common Watershed Development Guidelines, 2011 by amending the Common Guidelines, 2008 with effect from 01.04.2008. Accordingly, the Department of Land Resources, Ministry of Rural Development has announced a Centrally Sponsored Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP). The scheme is 90 per cent funded by the central government and 10 per cent by state governments. Since 2015, the scheme has been included in the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana- Watershed Development Component Plan. Since 2015, the ratio of central and state funding for the scheme has been 60:40. The scheme is being coordinated by the Vasundhara State Level Watershed Development System. As per the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 2008. The State level watershed development mechanism has been constituted under the chairmanship of Principal Secretary (Water Conservation) and registered under the Societies Act, 1860.
The Department of Land Resources, Ministry of Rural Development has sanctioned a total of 1166 watershed projects for the batches 2009-10 to 2014-15. The total area to be treated is 48.74 lakh hectare. 6229 crore. The programme is being implemented in 10630 villages in 272 taluks of 34 districts of the state.
