Government circular:
Guidelines have been issued from time to time under the Circulars in order to ensure adequate funds for development while containing the expenditure on administration. As per this policy, all the departments of the government hire manpower on a contractual basis from contractors under the offices under the department. The Department of Industry, Energy and Labor made the decision. As per the directions of the Cabinet dated 22nd February, 2019, the salary and other allowances as per the provisions of the contract should be paid to the contractual employees working in the government departments by the contractor, including statutory dues.

The same procedure for payment has been applied to all departments. Department of Industry, Energy and Labour. Minimum wages have been fixed as per the notification dated 24th July, 2019.
2. In pursuance of the above, Department of Soil and Water Conservation, Ministry (itself) has initiated outsourcing activities in this regard. As the Authorized Agency for making available such manpower with effect from 01.12.2021," May. The CSC e-Governance Services India Limited initiative is being approved under the following terms and conditions:
1) Ensure that the available manpower is not overstretched. We will be bound to strictly comply with the orders, rules, terms and conditions issued by the Labour Department to ensure that they are paid minimum wages/ remuneration as per the orders issued by the Labour Department from time to time, get all permissible statutory and welfare benefits, comply with the provisions of all statutory deductions such as Provident Fund, Gratuity, ESIS, Business Tax, etc.
(ii) Such contractual manpower should be provided with the administrative basic training required to work in the department.
3) In view of the current COVID-19 pandemic, corona tests and other ancillary health check-ups of such manpower should be conducted from time to time during and after the appointment. It should not be more than the Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS). The cost of this should be deducted from the salary of the workers.
(4) In compliance with all the terms and conditions of the manpower remuneration rate, prescribed service charge rate and other statutory deductions issued by the Labour/ Finance Department of the State, as well as by providing necessary training to the manpower, conducting their health check-up, and no direct" employer-employee" relationship of this manpower will be associated with the Government, only because their services will be received by the Government and the manpower supply system means. CSC e-Governance Services India Limited will be fully responsible for this.
5) The agency working through external mechanism shall be required to pay the salary as per the rate of pay fixed by the Government and the applicable taxes and allowances from time to time. Statutory obligations owed to the manpower provided by the service provider e.g.
Provident Fund, Gratuity, ESIC, Bonus, Labour Welfare Fund, etc. are included in the salary and the responsibility of depositing the legal liability with the concerned authority will be of the concerned service provider and the service provider will submit the documents to the government from time to time.

6) The available manpower will be paid daily allowance/ travel allowance etc. as per the rules for travelling outside the headquarters in accordance with the work assigned to them. Also, insurance cover will be admissible to employees working in essential sectors during the Covid-19 pandemic as per government rules.
7) The work of providing manpower through this external agency is done by M/ s. CSC e-Governance Services India Ltd. The contract will be given to the Central Government Company as well as to the authorized agency or associate partner company of the said company for a period of 5 years from the date of contract.
8) If the work is to be assigned to the company's agency or associate partner company by your organization, you will be obliged to inform the Department of Soil and Water Conservation about it and such organization will be obliged to comply with the terms and conditions made with you by the government.
9) It should be ensured that the government does not have any responsibility for the service of the officers/ employees working on behalf of your organization.
10). CSC e-Governance Services India Limited, a manpower supply organization, will be paid 14% service charge for such supply of manpower as determined by the Labour Department. This service charge should be levied on the total manpower supply cost.
11) The agreement with the service provider shall be entered into at the central level, subject to necessary terms/ conditions as per the guidelines issued by the Finance Department from time to time. Therefore, there is no need to sign a separate contract.
12) The wages (including service charges) are being approved as per the prescribed Minimum Wages rates in the respective cadres as specified by the Labour Department under the Minimum Wages Act for carrying out works through external agencies.
13) The starting salary of the manpower to be provided by the service provider shall be as per the starting salary in the pay scale. However, the head of the office will be allowed to decide the salary at the next stages of the initial salary based on experience and educational qualification.
As per the government policy and the order of the Labour Department, it will be mandatory to provide such manpower to the concerned requisitioning agency within 30 days from the date of requisition. Also, if a person's work is not satisfactory, the organization will be informed about his dismissal 30 days in advance, the organization will be obliged to provide another employee of the same qualification within 30 days.
The payment of the said manpower supply should be submitted to the department within 3rd of every month and the salary of the concerned contractual employees should be paid by 7th of every month.
