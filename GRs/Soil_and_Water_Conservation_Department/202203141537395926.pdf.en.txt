Introduction The Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme is a reference scheme for restoring irrigation potential by preserving and repairing the completed water conservation schemes in the state. It is being implemented. A total of 37 schemes in Aurangabad district will be repaired under the Aurangabad Circle Office. 877.98 lakh. After the restoration of these projects, 1068 hectares of irrigated area and 5290.00 cu.m. of water storage will be restored. The proposal for obtaining administrative approval for the above works is contained in the above Reference Letter No. 5 and 7 have been submitted to the government. Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Aurangabad has scrutinized/ examined all the budgets at his level. Considering the above, 37 schemes have been included in the first priority category out of the 37 mentioned schemes. The following decisions are being taken:
A total of 37 improvement projects in Aurangabad district under the Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme have been approved at an estimated cost of Rs. 877.98 lakh. These price budgets are being accorded administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions.
