The Proposal:
Water scarcity free Maharashtra for all 2019 Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan was launched on 5th December 2014. The deadline for the campaign was March 31, 2019. Thereafter, the Mission will be extended to complete the works in the selected villages during 2018-19. The deadline was extended to March 31, 2020. This campaign is over. The programme has been implemented in mission mode with funds available from various government departments/ NGOs/ Public Partnerships and Private Enterprises (CSR). Under this mission, more than 6 lakh works have been completed in the selected villages from 2015-16 to 2018-19.
The Comptroller and Auditor General of India in its Report No. 3 of the General and Social Sector and Public Enterprises for the year ended 31st March, 2019, has referred to the issues reported by the Accountant General regarding the activities undertaken under the Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan. Cabinet of Ministers Discussion was held in the meeting on 14/10/2020 and an open inquiry was conducted on the works done under Jalyukt Shivar. The Cabinet has taken a decision. Accordingly, a proposal to conduct an open inquiry into the works done under the Jalyukt Shivar scheme was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
As per the decision taken by the Hon'ble Cabinet in its meeting held on 14/10/2020, the Government has decided to conduct an open inquiry into the works done under the Jalyukt Shivar Yojana. The committee is being constituted as follows to find out which works need to be selected for open inquiry only because the number of works is more.
- Member (ii) Mr. Sanjay Belsare, Chief Engineer, Water Resources Department- Member (iv) Acting Director, Soil Conservation and Watershed Management, Pune- Member

(2) The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
(a) The Committee shall recommend to the agencies concerned as to which of the 1128 works examined in the 120 villages in 6 districts mentioned in the Comptroller and Auditor General of India Report are required to be subjected to open enquiry and which are required to be subjected to administrative action/ departmental enquiry only.
(b) As per the letter dated 28th February, 2020 of the Government seeking details of complaints received since 2015 under the Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan, information was received about more than 600 complaints. The complaint should be examined and accordingly, recommendations should be made to the concerned agencies as to which works need to be open inquiries and which works need only administrative action/ departmental inquiries.
(a) Further, the Committee shall recommend to the concerned agencies as to which of the activities undertaken under the Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan and as may be required by the Committee, need to be subjected to open inquiry and which activities require only administrative action/ departmental inquiry.
Open enquiry or administrative action/ departmental enquiry in respect of the works/ works should be initiated immediately by the concerned agencies as recommended by the Committee.
Term of the Committee- The work of the Committee shall be completed within 6 months as per the work assigned by the Committee.
Every month, the committee will submit a report to the government on all the matters recommended by it.
