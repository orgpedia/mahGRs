Preamble: The Cabinet in its meeting held on 24th October, 2017 has decided to implement the State Food Processing Policy, 2017 by making it mandatory for farm based Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) not to obtain water harvesting license for water consumption. But for large industries and mega food parks, legal provisions will be made to take decisions in a stipulated time. " A decision has been taken. Also, as per Section 16 of the Maharashtra Water Resources Authority Act, 2005. The Cabinet Committee has full powers regarding water reservation. Accordingly, the policy has been formulated. For the implementation of the said decision of the Cabinet, the Department of Agriculture and Padum has issued Reference No. The government has taken 2 decisions.
Subject to the above Cabinet decision taken to avoid delay in supplying water to agro-based micro, small and medium food processing industries, the Government was considering issuing necessary instructions to enable proper planning of water storage in the dams under the Department of Soil and Water Conservation.
Government Decision: If the water storage under the soil and water conservation department is to be used as a source (dams, irrigation ponds, storage ponds) for agro-based micro, small and medium food processing industries, then water reservation approval from the soil and water conservation department will not be required. The following steps should be taken to implement the decision:
The applicant/ industrial house/ company/ local body concerned should submit the water application in the prescribed format to the Executive Engineer of the concerned Soil and Water Conservation Department of the project for Micro, Small and Medium Food Processing Schemes based on agricultural inputs. (The prescribed sample is attached to the water application.) The responsibility of ensuring the accuracy of the information in the water application will be of the concerned applicant/ industrial house/ company/ local body. Applicants must submit an application.
