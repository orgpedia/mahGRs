The Adarsh Gaon Sankalp and Project Committee Scheme is based on the concept of village development through public participation.
Various schemes are being implemented by the government for the development of villages. These include a total of 59 schemes of 12 different government departments. Among them, watershed development work has been classified as core area and work of other departments as non-core area. A State-level committee has also been formed to implement the scheme.
In addition to the total 201 villages selected under the scheme, the selection of 100 new villages under the scheme has been approved as per the decision of the Government dated 13th August, 2010. Accordingly, a total of 76 villages have been approved for selection, namely 47 villages under Government Resolution dated 22.12.2014 at Reference 2, 18 villages under Government Resolution dated 14.03.2016 at Reference 4 and 11 more villages under Government Resolution dated 5.09.2018 at Reference 6. For the development of these selected villages, the Water Conservation/ Watershed Development Programme mainly includes works such as tree plantation on bunds, continuous leveling chars, stone bunds, small earthen bunds, small barbed wire bunds, earthen bunds, cement bunds and land levelling (paddy straw). The funds for this have been provided in the budget. Similarly, funds are also provided for the office expenses of the Adarshgaon Sankalp and Project Committee.
