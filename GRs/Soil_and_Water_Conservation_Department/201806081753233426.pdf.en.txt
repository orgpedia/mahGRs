Introduction: The Vidarbha Intensive Irrigation Development Programme (VIDP), under the National Agriculture Development Scheme of the Government of India, was a programme to provide special protected irrigation facilities and increase crop productivity for 11 districts of Vidarbha. The five-year programme was implemented from 2012 to 2017. It expired on March 31, 2017. Under the programme, Rs. 1791.00 crore and for Minor Irrigation (Water Conservation) Rs. 1459.00 crore. A sum of Rs. 3250.00 crore has been earmarked for the project. 874.67 crore has been received and fully spent till the end of March, 2017.
Under the programme of Minor Irrigation (Water Conservation) System, 1870 schemes have been completed and some schemes were in progress. In respect of the works which were in progress under the programme and the works which had been completed but the payments were pending due to lack of funds, the Government decision referred to in (1) was issued to meet the pending liabilities from other schemes due to the termination of the Vidarbha Intensive Irrigation Development Programme.
