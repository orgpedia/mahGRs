Bhartiya Jain Sangh (BJS) has a long experience of working at the village level in watershed development, creating awareness, generating demand and capacity building at the village level. The government has partnered with the Bharatiya Jain Sanghatana (BJS) as a strategic partner to work in more villages in less time by using it in this new scheme. The (Non-Financial) Agreement has been signed on 26.04.2023 for 28 districts (Ahmednagar, Buldhana, Jalgaon, Nashik, Solapur, Akola, Chandrapur, Jalna, Osmanabad, Thane, Amravati, Dhule, Latur, Parbhani, Wardha, Aurangabad, Gadchiroli, Nagpur, Pune, Washim, Beed, Gondia, Nanded, Sangli, Bhandara, Hingoli, Nandurbar, Satara).
BJS will not take any financial compensation from the state government or district administration for this. The obligations of all stakeholders under this agreement are as follows:
Role of State Government: To create awareness in rural areas, to create demand for revival of water bodies, to mobilize people to convert this work into a mass movement, to give appropriate suggestions to District Collectors and District Water Conservation Officers about the role of BJS in building capacities of grass root level stakeholders like Pani Samitis, farmers and Gram Panchayats.
Role of District Collector/ CEO (Zilla Parishad)/ Nodal Officer and District Water Conservation Officer: To assist the BJS in conducting awareness programmes in the areas selected by the District Administration on priority.
The day-to-day activities undertaken by BJS in the district are to mobilize the masses for the mass movement and to collaborate to strengthen the capacities of the grassroots stakeholders like Gram Panchayats, Pani Samitis and farmers.
Instruct all the concerned officers at the district, taluka and village level to cooperate with the BJS for the smooth implementation of the above activities in the villages.
