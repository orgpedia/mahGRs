The Proposal:
Common Guidelines 2021 for the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana - watershed development component 2. 0 have been issued by the Union Department of Land Resources, Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India. Accordingly, the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana - Watershed Development Component 2. 0 was given the go-ahead to be implemented in the state. The approval has been granted under the Government Decision of 27.04.2022. Under the PMKSY WDC 2.0 scheme, watershed development works are undertaken under the Natural Resource Management component. Benefits are also provided to eligible beneficiaries under livelihood activities, production methods.
Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana - Watershed Development Component 2.0 (PMKSY WDC 2.0) is being implemented by the Department of Land Resources under the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India and Department of Soil and Water Conservation in the state of Maharashtra. At present, 140 projects are operational in the state of Maharashtra covering a total of 30 districts with a project treatment area of 52.5 lakh hectares. The amount of funding for the scheme (che: state) is 60:40.
3. "Panjalot Yatra" will be launched by the Central Government in January 2025 to create awareness among the local community about the scheme and to promote the implementation of the scheme through public participation. The planning of this waterfall yatra is under the consideration of the government. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decision.
The government's decision:
In the state, the scheme of Pradhan Mantri Kashi Sinchayee Yojana - Watershed Development Component 2. 0 will be planned in villages of 30 districts in 140 project areas and its outline is as follows:
1) To provide a virtual tour experience to the local citizens of the project area regarding Mobile Theatre Van SI (VR Experience) watershed activities.
2) To take a pledge to conserve and conserve soil and water by taking soil in hand so that a watershed movement is created in every village.

The aforesaid Committees shall implement the Watershed Yatra Programme in accordance with the following instructions and instructions of the Central Government. The District and Taluk Level Committees shall meet on 3rd January 2025 and 5th January 2025 respectively to plan the Yatra.
3. The following actions should be taken to create awareness among the public in the state about the Yatra:
a) Before the start of the Jallok Yatra - 1) To create awareness about the Yatra by holding Gram Sabhas in the villages of the Yatra.
2) Publicity in social media and through the media.
3) Selection of watershed committee members / staff / citizens who have done excellent work in the watershed project as watershed warriors.
4) Conducting watershed operations.
5) Morning walk for pilgrim awareness.
6) To organize painting competition, essay competition in the village of the pilgrimage as well as in the school of the area.
7) To hold a meeting of all the departments under the guidance of the Guardian Minister of the district for organizing the Yatra program.
8) Arrangement of seating arrangements for the crowd present during the event in the pilgrim village, loudspeakers to address the crowd, security arrangements, manpower required for crowd management, provision of snacks and drinking water, medical assistance, power supply, etc., photography of the event, videography, HIST by drones, etc., obtaining necessary permissions, sale of products manufactured under the scheme, branding of the event.
b) Launch of the Panelot Yatra - The launch event of the Panelot Yatra was inaugurated by Shri. Minister of Rural Development, Government of India; State Hon. The Chief Minister of the state, Shri. Deputy Chief Minister; Md. Minister, Soil and Water Conservation; and Shri.
Secretary, Union Department of Land Resources, Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India; Smt. The Secretary, Department of Soil and Water Conservation, Government of Maharashtra and local public representatives are expected to be present. The following activities are expected to participate in the launch event: -
1) Bhoomi Pujan of new project works in each district by the chief guests.
2) The chief guest, dignitaries, local community, watershed committee take the pledge of soil and water conservation and conservation by holding the soil in their hands at the inauguration site. And at the same time, the youth should take a pledge to protect and conserve soil and water by holding soil in their hands through the My Bharat Portal.
3) Horticulture / Tree plantation.
4) Inauguration of Mobile Theatre Van. (Media presence during the groundbreaking ceremony.) )

e) During the waterfall journey (at the stop of the mobile theatre van) - 1) Welcoming the waterfall mobile theatre van.
2) To perform Bhoomi Pujan of new watershed works in each district.
3) Horticulture / Tree plantation (at least 10000 trees per project) 4) Conducting watershed panchayats (Land Water Dialogue) - eminent personalities who have contributed to the catchment area explain to the community the importance of watershed management and guide the citizens on how to participate in the conservation and protection of the catchment area. The discussion should be broadcast through a podcast.
5) To start desilting of ponds and dams in the village area.
6) To select and declare Dharini Tai to create awareness about watershed schemes among the local population.
7) Through the VR Experience Zone, local citizens will be able to experience a virtual tour of the watershed activities. In the van, they will be able to see the plans of the watershed works.
8) Organizing a watershed festival. (Street plays, cultural programmes, bhajans, songs etc.)
9) Donating labour on watershed works. (1000 citizen participation) 10) Shramdaan - Shramdaan should involve digging holes, desilting of ponds, rivulets, construction of small stone check dams, construction of mud dams, farm dam closure works or other NRM related works. Plan so that the waterfront vehicle can pass near the site of the labor, and other events can be held there.
11) Distributing prizes to the underwater warriors.
12) Dedication of completed works.
13) Participation of village youth (registration drive through My Bharat Portal).
14) Encourage local schools to adopt works in the watershed project. Provide a letter of adoption about it. So that school children at an early age know the importance of water scarcity. The cost of maintenance and repair of these works will be met from the watershed development fund through the Gram Panchayat. Local schools should be invited so that school children can participate in Shramdaan, watershed festival, oath taking, water van viewing. There should also be painting competitions, essay writing competitions, folk song competitions on watershed development.
15) To provide benefits of government schemes to the people of the village.
16) Involvement of representatives from the offices of Agriculture, Water Resources, Water Supply and Ground Water Survey in the district in the Yatra.
17) To disseminate and disseminate information about the schemes of the Soil and Water Conservation Department through booklets, pamphlets and posters.
18) Since the event will take place within the project area of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana - watershed development component 2. 0, the DRO, PTO F LRA A NGOs designated in that area should be actively involved in the watershed journey.

19) The programme should also involve participation of village level officers / staff such as Talathis, Gram Sevaks, Agricultural Assistants, Forest Guard, Gram Rozgar Sevaks working under NREGA, APOs, PTO, BFT employees in the villages along the Jallok Yatra route. Water conservation works by the Agriculture Department for groundbreaking of new works in the villages along the yatra route as well as works on watershed development under NREGA and other schemes under convergence should also be included.
20) Works like repair / repair of earlier works in the village in watershed development component 1. 0 of the watershed yatra route should also be done through Shramdaan as well as other schemes so that the earlier constructions are restored.
21) Participation of Nehru Yuva Kendra: - Youth through Nehru Yuva Kendra register on My Bharat Portal and include them in Panelot Yatra like Shramdaan, Cultural Programmes etc.
Every work should be done. Incorporating the registered youth into the Yatra program will create awareness about water conservation among more and more youth. Also, training of youth on the benefits / technical aspects of watershed works by conducting 3 to 4 hours training classes for youth in coordination with Nehru Yuva Kendra and District Water Conservation Officer and conducting a post-training test examination (MCQ) with the signature of the Collector / District Water Conservation Officer. So that in future their participation in watershed development works will increase and watershed development works will be more quality. Programmes for each project should be designed in coordination with the Nehru Yuva Kendra at the district level. Publicity distribution, pasting of posters, preparing citizens for Shramdaan, citizens coming to see the Water Vans before the arrival of the Water Vans and involving these youth to create an atmosphere of total Water Vessel Yatra should be done in the project area.
22) Local social influencers should be included in the programme of the waterfall yatra. They should educate the people about village development, watershed development.
23) To film, photograph and publicise the events during the waterfall yatra in the local social media.
24) Waterfront halts should use posters, banners, and posters to report on the various works that have been done on watershed development.
25) Involvement of FPCs, SHGs, UG's under PMKSY WDC 2.0 in the Jalalota Yatra. To make people aware of the contribution of SHGs designated as Lakhpati Didi under the scheme. Setting up stalls of products of SHGs. Establishing forward leakages by putting up their product displays and connecting SHGs / SHGs with FPCs / FPOs.
26) Local public representatives, MPs, MLAs, Zilla Parishad, Panchayat Samiti members, Gram Panchayat members should participate in this Yatra program.

4. The CEO, Vasundhara Watershed Development Authority, Pune, shall take the following action in connection with the conduct of the Watershed Yatra: -
1) Immediate action should be taken for the promotion and publicity of the Panchalot Yatra as per the instructions of the Central Government. Publicity materials (posters, murals, booklets, etc.) should be designated. It should also be printed and distributed at the district level.
2) Detailed instructions should be issued to the stakeholders regarding active participation of DRO, PTO, LRA and other NGOs, FPCs / FPOs, SHGs, UG under the scheme in the Jalal Yatra.
3) Provide saplings for tree plantation from the Director, Horticulture, Pune.
8) Criteria should be formulated for selection of submarine warriors.
5) Suggestions should be issued regarding the criteria for selection of Dharni Tai and the responsibilities to be assigned to them.
6) A watershed PMP questionnaire should be prepared. Courses and questionnaires should be made available under the My Bharat Portal, a learning AAAS system initiative of the central government. Land water communication, watershed campaigns should be outlined.
7) A waterfront festival programme (street plays, bhajans, songs, cultural programmes) should be chalked out for public awareness along with the yatra. Instructions should be given to the district administration to select the performing teams and provide them with necessary facilities.
8) Administrative and technical approvals for the works to be taken up at the state, district, project level should be completed before the Yatra and necessary preparations should be made for the works to be done through Shramdaan, plantation etc.
9) Allocate work to all District Water Conservation Officers, Project Operational Mechanisms, Reservoir Committees and manpower made available through external agencies for organizing the programmes as per the instructions of the Central Government. Also plan the entire event.
10) Detailed route map of the catchment route should be made available to the concerned.
11) Developing a waterfall van, providing VR experience and other facilities on it, having a waterfall concept model in the waterfall van, and planning how the journey will unfold in the journey route project, etc.
5. Expenditure for the project should be met from the funds made available to the office of Vasundhara Reservoir Development System, Pune under the capacity building component of PMKSY WDC 2.0 scheme. The Chief Executive Officer, Vasundhara Watershed Development System, Pune will be the controlling officer for the expenditure of this fund.
