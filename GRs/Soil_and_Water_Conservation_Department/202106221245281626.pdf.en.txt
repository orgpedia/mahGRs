Under the Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme, it is proposed to repair the water conservation treatment structures in order to restore the irrigation capacity by preserving and repairing the schemes that require maintenance and repair of the completed water conservation schemes in the state. A total of 57 schemes will be revised under Nashik Circle Office, out of which 5 schemes are in the first priority and the remaining 52 schemes are in the second priority. The total amount for repairs is Rs. 8.0291 crore. After the restoration of these projects, 1638.14 hectares of irrigated area and 7276.97 cu.m. of water storage will be restored. The proposal for administrative approval of these works has been submitted to the government by the Additional Commissioner and Chief Engineer, Water Conservation, Aurangabad. Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Nashik has scrutinized/ verified all the estimates at his level and certified the same. Considering the above, administrative approval was under consideration for the amendment proposal of 57 works mentioned. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
Government Decision: The total budget of 57 repair schemes of Nashik Regional Water Conservation Department is Rs. 8.0291 crore (Rs. These price budgets are being accorded administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions.
