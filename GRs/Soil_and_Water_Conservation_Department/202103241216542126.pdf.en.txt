Government Circular: Water Conservation Officer Group-B (Gazetted) under Soil and Water Conservation Department. The final seniority list as on 01.01.2018 is under reference of Govt. Circular dated 12.06.2020 has been issued. Thereafter, after taking into consideration the information received from the concerned Water Conservation Officers and after examining the provisions of the Rules, the Water Conservation Officers of Soil and Water Conservation Department, Group-B (Gazetted) Cadre Officers will be appointed as per the provisions of Forms" A" and" B" respectively. The provisional seniority list as on 01.01.2019 is being published vide this circular.
In case any error/ error is found in the details of Water Conservation Officer, Group-B (Gazetted) included in this seniority list, such as name, date of birth, date of appointment, caste category and place of seniority, etc., then your representations/ objections/ suggestions should be brought to the notice of the Government through registered post/ speed post/ hand delivery along with necessary appropriate proof in writing within 15 days from the date of issue of this circular. If the mistakes/ errors are not brought to the notice of the government in writing within the stipulated time, then we have no objection to it, assuming that the Water Conservation Officer, Group-B (Gazetted), working under the Department of Soil and Water Conservation Department

In the case of objections received under the provisions of 17-09-2018, after completion of the proceedings under the provisions of the last paragraph on page 3 of the said Government Decision, an appropriate decision will be taken regarding the change in the date of seniority of the concerned.
The circular has been sent to all district water conservation officers in their respective jurisdictions.
The Namud Water Conservation Officer, Group-B (Gazetted) should be brought to the notice of the Government immediately, and in the case of such intimation, a copy of the signature taken by him should be preserved in his own records and a copy of the same should be made available to the Government.
