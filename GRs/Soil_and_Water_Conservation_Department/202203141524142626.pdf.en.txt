Introduction The Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme is a reference scheme for restoring irrigation potential by preserving and repairing the completed water conservation schemes in the state. It is being implemented. A total of 32 schemes in Aurangabad district under Aurangabad Circle Office will be repaired. 862.88 lakh. After the restoration of these projects, 846 hectares of irrigated area and 3824.00 c.m. of water storage will be restored. The proposal for obtaining administrative approval for the above works is contained in the above Reference Letter No. 5 has been submitted to the government. Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Aurangabad has scrutinized/ examined all the budgets at his level. Considering the above, out of the 32 schemes, 31 schemes have been included in the first priority and 1 scheme in the second priority in the revised scheme.
A total of 32 repair projects in Aurangabad district of Aurangabad Regional Water Conservation Division under Mukhyamantri Jal Sanvardhan Yojana have been budgeted at Rs. 862.88 lakh (Rs. These price budgets are being accorded administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions.
1) Each of the above schemes is being given separate administrative approval. The order is assembled.
2) The contractor should be made obligated to set conditions in the tender for setting up a water use agency. 10% of the tender amount should be deducted from the contractor's payment until the WUA is established.
