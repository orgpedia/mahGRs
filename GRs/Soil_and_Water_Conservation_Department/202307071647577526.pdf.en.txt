3. Department of Soil and Water Conservation. Meeting held on 19.04.2023 with the representatives of Central Ground Water Board, Government of India and Ground Water Survey and Development Agency.
Reference No. The Central Ground Water Board, Government of India has issued a master plan for Artificial Recharge to Ground Water-2020. A blueprint has been prepared to check the suitability of various artificial groundwater recharge structures by long-term groundwater planning for aquifer recharge in the state according to different geographical conditions. The Central Ground Water Board, Government of India has suggested the constitution of State Level and District Level Committees to ensure the involvement of various administrative departments in undertaking these artificial groundwater recharge structures.
The initiative is planned to be implemented in a phased manner and as per the guidelines issued, State level and District level committees are directed to be constituted to prepare village wise pre-feasibility reports based on the existing completed structures, availability of surplus rainwater and the master plan prepared by the National Aquifer Mapping and Management Programme (NAQUIM). Following this, the government decided:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Soil and Water Conservation Department has been appointed as the State level nodal agency for the proposed artificial recharge programme in the State and is expected to carry out the following activities:

Preparation of feasibility reports of the proposed works and completion of the works subject to availability of funds.
(ii) Setting up of State level and District level Committees for approval and completion of works to be taken up under Artificial Recharge Programme subject to availability of funds.
(iii) To prepare a micro level recharge plan after every five years in consultation with Central Water Board, Government of India.
Establishment of Geographical Information System (GIS) laboratories comprising of experts at district and state level for preparation of Thematic Layers (1:10000) and implementation feasibility reports.
Provision of funds for setting up of Geographical Information System (GIS) laboratories under various schemes and appointment of experts for the proposed works.
State and district-level committees are being formed.
