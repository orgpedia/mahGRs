Introduction The Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme is a reference scheme for restoring irrigation potential by preserving and repairing the completed water conservation schemes in the state. N. No. 4 is being implemented. As many as 201 works in Nashik Regional Area have been identified as reference No. Administrative approval has been granted under Sections 5 to 12. Out of these, 126 schemes are in need of revised administrative approval due to increase in cost of ownership charges and revised tariff of Department of Soil and Water Conservation by more than 10% with effect from 2020-21. The revised price of 97 plans is Rs. 50 lakh and 29 schemes with revised cost of Rs. 50 lakh, all these schemes are subject to revised administrative approvals. Under Form A, 13 and 29 schemes have been received.
Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Nashik has scrutinized/ scrutinized all the budgets at his level. Considering the above, revised administrative approval was under consideration for the 126 works mentioned. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
