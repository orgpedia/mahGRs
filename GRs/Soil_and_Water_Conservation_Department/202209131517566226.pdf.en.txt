Preamble: In the court case of the works carried out under the repair, renovation and restoration programme of the lakes in Beed district under the centrally sponsored Bharat Nirman Yojana, Ma. 1,30,54, 258/- is required to be paid to the Court pursuant to the order dated 16/08/2019 passed by the District Court, Beed and the comments made thereon by the Department of Law& Justice, Ministry of Law& Justice, Mumbai. Accordingly, the existing Centrally Sponsored Scheme with the title-" Repair, Renovation and Restoration of Water Bodies through Local Participation (0 to 100 Ha) (Central Share 90%) (State Share 10%)" is required to be aligned (MAP) on PFMS system.
In order to ensure effective management of funds of Centrally Sponsored Schemes and to bring in greater effectiveness in the process of allocation of funds, the Central Government has taken a number of steps, including: The office memorandum dated March 23, 2021, has directed to adopt the revised procedure. Accordingly, the Finance Department vide No. The Government Circulars 3 and 4 mentioned above provide revised instructions on Public Finance Management System (PFMS) of Centrally Sponsored Schemes. Accordingly, it is necessary to appoint a Single Nodal Agency (SNA) for the implementation of centrally sponsored schemes and open a separate Single Nodal Bank Account in the authorized bank for that scheme.
It has also been directed to appoint at least a Deputy Secretary level officer as the nodal officer for coordination of these Centrally Sponsored Schemes. Accordingly, the matter of appointing a Single Nodal Agency (SNA) and Nodal Officer for the centrally sponsored schemes of the Department of Soil and Water Conservation was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: In the context of Finance Department No. As per the instructions given under the Government Circular and Government Decision dated 03.04.2020, a Single Nodal Agency (SNA) and a Nodal Officer are being appointed for effective implementation and accountable functioning of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Repair, Renovation and Restoration of Water Bodies (Central share 90%) (State share 10%) through local participation (0 to 100 hectares) in the Department of Soil and Water Conservation.
