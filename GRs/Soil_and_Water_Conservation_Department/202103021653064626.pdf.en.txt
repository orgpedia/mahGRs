Introduction The Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme is a reference scheme in connection with restoring irrigation potential by preserving and repairing the schemes that require maintenance and repair from the completed water conservation schemes in the state. It is being implemented. Accordingly, a total of 18 schemes under the Nashik Circle Office will be repaired and the total amount for repair is Rs 5.5975 crore. After the restoration of these projects, 506.60 hectares of irrigated area and 2315.21 cu.m. of water storage will be restored. The proposal for administrative approval of these works has been submitted to the government by the Additional Commissioner and Chief Engineer, Water Conservation, Aurangabad. Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Nashik has scrutinized/ verified all the estimates at his level and certified the same. Considering the above, administrative approval was under consideration for the amendment proposal of the 18 works mentioned. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
