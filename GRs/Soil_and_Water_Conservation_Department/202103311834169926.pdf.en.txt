Memorandum dated March 30, 2019.
Preamble: A sum of Rs. 400.00 crore was budgeted in 2020-21 for conservation and restoration of minor irrigation (water conservation) projects with irrigation potential of 0 to 100 hectares. As per the revised estimate, Rs. 300.00 crore is available.
The following decisions have been taken in this regard:
Decision: Approval is hereby accorded to the Government to disburse a sum of Rs. 10.17 lakhs (Rupees Ten Lakh Seventeen Thousand only) towards payment of maintenance and repair work of the British-era Ozar (Mahadev Mutt) Dam in Niphad Taluka of Nashik District subject to the following conditions and conditions.
a) The funds should be disbursed on payment basis, ensuring completion of repair work, b) No amount in excess of the sanctioned cost should be paid, c) If the funds remain unutilized for any reason, the amount should be immediately returned to the government without distributing it for any other purpose, d) Guidelines in the relevant circular of the Finance Department regarding the disbursement of funds should be strictly followed.
e) The works have been completed 100% and complaints have not been received, and the quality of the work should be ensured by making payment and submitting a compliance report to the government.
The amount so disbursed shall be drawn by the Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Nashik and shall be disbursed expeditiously to the concerned District Water Conservation Officers under him.
