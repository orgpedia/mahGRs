Background: The Department is engaged in construction, maintenance and repair of soil and water conservation structures having limited irrigation potential of 600 hectares using sustainable technologies. The Department is presently undertaking construction of new water conservation and irrigation structures as well as repair and maintenance of existing structures. It is difficult to implement and manage the entire programme with the available manpower as the department does not have sufficient technical manpower required for conducting site inspection, survey and investigation, preparation of Pre-Feasibility Report (PFR), Detailed Project Report (DPR), Structural Design, preparation of tender documents and supervision and monitoring of the projects.
In addition, the lack of a professional project management approach is affecting project scheduling, monitoring, and quality of expected quality and structures.
Therefore, the Department has decided to appoint a professional Project Management Consultant to manage all the construction, repair and maintenance of soil and water conservation projects under the Department. Accordingly, the Department has selected Project Management Consultants to work on the aspects of complete micromanagement and new technology trends and conceptualization solutions, undertaking specific research work, completing legal compliances, profitability ratio analysis, project budgeting and other project management to advise the Department and work directly for quality. In accordance with this Government decision, the Government was considering issuing the following Government Decision to carry out the works in the regional areas through the Project Management Consultants in a proper manner.

The scope and jurisdiction of the panel is limited to the offices under the Department of Soil and Water Conservation. The services of PMC are in the nature of consultancy. The empanelled PMCs are allowed to provide only consultancy services and in no case they will be allowed to supply material and provide construction services. (c) The role of the Sub-Divisional Water Conservation Office:
With respect to professional project management consulting services, the roles and responsibilities of the subdivision office are listed as follows. The Sub-Divisional Office will control the entire field operations and work in coordination with the designated PMC staff with the following aspects.
i.Take the help of PMC as required to independently evaluate the proposed construction, collect information and analyze the feasibility of the project.
(ii) To take the help of PMC for preparation of schedule for repair and maintenance of existing irrigation projects in the area.
Coordinate with project management consultants for all phases during project implementation.
(iv) Assess and validate the quality and disbursement of funds while construction of the project is in progress.
v. Ensure timely availability of materials, equipment and consumables related to the project on filing of proposal/ case.
(vi) To complete the procurement process required for the project in coordination with the Project Management Consultant.
(vii) Submission of running costs and final payments related to the project.
Maintain project-wise control of resources of PMC and contractors.
