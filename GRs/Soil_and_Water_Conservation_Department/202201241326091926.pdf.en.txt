Introduction The Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme is a reference scheme for restoring irrigation potential by preserving and repairing the completed water conservation schemes in the state. It is being implemented. A total of 24 schemes under the Amravati Circle Office will be repaired and a total amount of Rs. 516.19 crore. After the restoration of these projects, 1012 hectares of irrigated area and 3050.64 cu.m. of water storage will be restored. A proposal to get administrative approval for these works has been submitted to the government under the above reference letter. The Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Amaravati has scrutinized/ scrutinized all the budgets at his level. Considering the above, the proposal was under consideration for administrative approval as the 24 works mentioned in the revised plan of the Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme were included in the first priority. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
Government Decision: Under the Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme, the budget of a total of 24 repair schemes in the Amravati Regional Water Conservation Division is Rs. 516.19 lakhs (Rs. These prices are subject to the following terms and conditions
Administrative approval has been granted.
