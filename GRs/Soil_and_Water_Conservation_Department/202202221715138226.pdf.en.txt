Introduction The Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme is a reference scheme for restoring irrigation potential by preserving and repairing the completed water conservation schemes in the state. N. No. 3 is being implemented. A total of 17 schemes in Gevrai taluka of Beed district will be repaired under the Aurangabad Mandal Office. 530.32 crore. With the restoration of these projects, 515 hectares of irrigated area and 3072.54 cu.m. of water storage will be restored. A proposal to get administrative approval for these works has been submitted to the government under the above reference letter. Regional Water Conservation Officer, Soil and Water Conservation Department, Aurangabad has scrutinized/ examined all the budgets at his level. Considering the above, 16 works out of the 17 mentioned are included in the second priority in the revised plan of Mukhyamantri Jal Sanvardhan Yojana and 1 scheme Bhend (Khu) Pt No. 2 is not included in the revised plan. According to the Starred Question No-33341 of the Assembly, it has been answered in the winter session that the schemes will be repaired under the Chief Minister's Water Conservation Scheme. Therefore, the plan needs to be approved by the government. The Regional Water Conservation Officer, Aurangabad has been informed about the inclusion of this scheme in the revised plan. The following decisions are being made.
Government Decision: Under the Mukhyamantri Jal Samvardhan Yojana, the budget of a total of 17 repair schemes in Gevrai taluka of Beed district in Aurangabad Regional Water Conservation Division is Rs. 530.32 lakh (Rs. These price budgets are being accorded administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions.
