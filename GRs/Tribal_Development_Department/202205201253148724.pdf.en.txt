Introduction: Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Vikas Kendra is a scheme implemented by the Central Government. The main objective of the scheme is to collect minor forest produce from the members of the self-help groups in the Scheduled Areas and other areas of Maharashtra mentioned in the Fifth Schedule to the Constitution of India, to sell the collected minor forest produce by adding value to it. As per the guidelines received from the Tribal Cooperative Marketing Federation of India Limited (TRIFED), Government of India for implementation of the scheme of Van Dhan Kendras vide reference no. 3 decisions have been made. As per the provisions of the said Government Resolution, Sabari Finance and Development Corporation Limited, Nashik is functioning as the State Agent Institution (SIP) for implementation of the Scheme. However, for the effective and speedy implementation of the Van Dhan Yojana, the government was considering appointing the Tribal Development Commissionerate, Nashik, as the State Agent Institute (SIP) instead of the Shabari Finance and Development Corporation Limited, Nashik, and forming a" State Level Steering Committee" at the government level to monitor the implementation of the scheme. According to reference no. The revised government decision is being issued as follows by superseding the government decision of 3.
The main objective of this scheme is to improve the quality of life of the tribals by using traditional knowledge, skills and modern information technology to process and sell the minor forest produce produced in the forest.

Notification No. 19/17/2019 of the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Govt. of India The scheme Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Vikas Kendra has been implemented by the Central Government under 26/02/2019. Accordingly, it is being approved to implement the Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Vikas Yojana in the State for value addition and sale of Minor Forest Produce (MFP) and other items, which are found in large quantities in the area, with the help of Self Help Groups (SHGs) in the State, which have at least 70% members of the Scheduled Tribes.
After discussions with the Regional Manager, TRIFED Maharashtra, the Tribal Development Commissionerate (SIP) is being approved as the State Agent Agency to implement the scheme.
The Tribal Cooperative Marketing Federation of India Limited (TRIFED), New Delhi has set a target of setting up 220 Van Dhan Vikas Kendras in Maharashtra with the help of self-help groups of Scheduled Tribes in the scheduled areas to sell value addition on minor forest produce and other items available in their area.
The Tribal Development Department, Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Mumbai will be the nodal department for implementation of Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Vikas Kendra (PMVK) Group of Schemes. The Tribal Development Commissionerate will act as the State Agent Agency.
The guidelines on how to set up a Van Dhan Vikas Kendra Group are available on the website https:// trifed. tribal. gov. in/ pmvdy/ guideline of Tribal Cooperative Marketing Development Federation of India Limited, New Delhi.
Accordingly, to implement the scheme in the State, Van Dhan Vikas Kendras will be formed by setting up Self Help Groups at village level to increase the availability of minor forest produce and sell them. Tribal Cooperative Marketing Federation of India Limited (TRIFED), New Delhi has sanctioned Rs. 5.00 lakh (Rupees Five Lakhs only) and Rs. 10.00 lakh (Rupees Ten Lakh only) will be provided under this scheme. The Central Government has issued guidelines for the utilization of funds received under this scheme. The funds are expected to be spent on the following:

Setting up of Forestry Development Centres:
1 Van Dhan Vikas Kendra Group consisting of 1 Self Help Group (SHG) with a maximum of 20 beneficiaries in the nearest geographical area will be set up.
The self-help group should be at the village level or in a nearby village. Self-help groups will be called Van Dhan Savings Groups.
Self-help groups (SHGs) should consist of a maximum of 20 beneficiaries to form 1 Van Dhan Bachat Group (SHG) in which at least 80% of the beneficiaries should be from Scheduled Tribes.
Women's Economic Development Corporation (MAWIM), PESA or a committee constituted under the Forest Rights Act with the approval of the Gram Sabha or the existing self-help groups of the Gram Sabha itself, tribal cooperative societies, Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission (MSRLM) and NGOs can work as Van Dhan Bachat groups.
The minimum age of a member of Van Dhan Savings Group (SHG) should be 18 years.
The Management Committee of the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra Group shall consist of one representative from each Self Help Group from which the President and Secretary of the Van Dhan Kendra Group shall be elected. The president and secretary must be from the Scheduled Tribes.
Every Van Dhan Bachat group is expected to open a bank account in their nearest bank.
This bank account should be in the name of the Van Dhan Bachat Group, but if the SHG has previously opened a bank account, it can also be subscribed and the transaction is expected to be done with the joint signature of the Chairman and Secretary of the Van Dhan Bachat Group.
