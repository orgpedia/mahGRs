The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules, 2008 and Rules, 2012 have empowered the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers to obtain individual and collective forest rights or both as per Section 3 (1).
These include the right of Scheduled Tribes or other traditional forest dwellers to own and inhabit forest land for cultivating for their livelihood; rights such as the right to dispose of forest land; right to collect, use or dispose of minor forest produce traditionally collected within or outside the boundaries of the village; right to access water, fish and other products, grazing, traditional seasonal resources; right to protect, restore, conserve, manage any social forest resource that has been traditionally protected and conserved for sustainable use.

In order to take note of the individual forest rights holders being eligible for benefits under the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules, 2008 and the amended Rules, 2012, and loans from banks on 7/12 basis and other government schemes, the Department of Revenue and Forest Department vide Reference No. 4 The circular here has directed that in the districts where individual forest rights holders are facing difficulties in getting loans from banks or are not getting the benefits of government schemes, the Collector should give necessary instructions to the concerned bank to remove the problems of the beneficiaries and take measures to get them the benefits of loans and government schemes.
The non-official members in the sub-divisional level and district level committees should take into account the suggestions given during the meeting while taking the final decision.
Individual forest rights holders who have received land pattas should be given priority benefits of land reforms, agri-business and income generation schemes. Priority should be given to individual forest rights holders while selecting beneficiaries for various schemes implemented by the Agriculture and Padum Department, Tribal Development Department, Rural Development Department, etc.
