Preamble: The Government Circular dated 6th April, 2021 in Reference No.1 of the Department of Finance has approved the release of funds in the range of 10-15% of the total annual budgetary allocation for the first 3 months of the financial year 2021-22 (end of June, 2021) for specific committed objectives, taking into account the situation arising out of Corona Virus in the State.
Accordingly, under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, Demand No. T-9 for specific objectives of such a binding nature under Reference No. 2 Funds have been distributed within the limits prescribed under the Act. Now, considering the need for disbursement of funds for Nutrition/ Aahar, it was considered to disburse funds for the first three months of Bharat Ratna Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Amrut Aahar Yojana under 31-Support Grants (Non-Payment).
Government's decision:
Under the District Annual Tribal Utilization Demand No. The Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure to the Controlling Officer of Rakaan No. 6 and the District Collector of the concerned district as per the provision mentioned in Rakaan No. 5 of the accompanying statement to meet the expenditure for the first three months of the financial year 2021-22 for the Bharat Ratna Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana (Sub-heading TX290004) under T-9.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to remain critical for the next 3 to 4 months due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan and spend the funds in the prescribed manner.
