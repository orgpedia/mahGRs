The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Denotified Castes, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes Caste Certificate (Grant and Verification thereof) Act, 2000, the Maharashtra Scheduled Tribes Certificate (Grant and Regulation of Verification thereof) Rules, 2003 and the Maharashtra Scheduled Castes, Denotified Castes, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes (Grant and Regulation of Verification of Caste Certificate) Rules, 2012 have been enacted in the State of Maharashtra. As per the provisions of the said Act and Rules, the competent authority in the State issues caste certificates to the persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Vimukta Jati, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes. As per Section 4 (2) of the Act, 2000, the certificate is valid only after verification by the screening committees. At present, there are Scheduled Caste/ Scheduled Tribe Certificate Scrutiny Committees functioning in the State to examine the validity of the caste certificate as per the provisions of the Act, 2000 and the rules made thereunder. Based on the government's decision, expert members were appointed to the committee. The Committee submitted its report to the Government on 29th May, 2019. The government is expected to take a decision on the recommendations of the report.
Government's decision:
Shri P.V.Hardas, Retired Judge, Hon'ble High Court of Judicature, Government of Tamil Nadu, to study the difficulties being faced by the persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Denotified Castes, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes in obtaining Caste/ Tribe certificates, Validity certificates and to suggest remedies. The committee was constituted by the Bombay High Court. The Supreme Court has made it clear that there is no need to bring any deviation or suggest alternative mechanism in the existing procedure and mechanism as per the guidelines given in Ms. Madhuri Patil vs. Additional Commissioner, Tribal Development, Thane and others case. The existing procedures for issuing validity certificates are appropriate and may serve well in the future as well.
But the committee has made some recommendations with a view to improve the functioning of the existing committees and to strengthen the committees. The following recommendations were made by the committee in its report:
