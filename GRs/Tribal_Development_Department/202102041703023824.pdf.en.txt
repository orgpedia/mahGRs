Government's decision
The circular dated 16.04.2020 of the Finance Department has prescribed the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2020-21, considering the pandemic situation in the State due to Corona virus. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided from time to time on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. Under the Tribal Component Programme, under the Government Decisions, 1 salary for the schemes under the sub-heading of Backward Classes Welfare, 36 Supplementary Grants (Salaries) to the extent of 80% for the purpose, 3 Overtime Allowance, 11 Domestic Travel Expenses to the extent of 30% for the purpose and 6 Telephone, Electricity and Water Charges, 10 Contractual Services, 13 Office Expenses, 14 Leases and Taxes and 24 Petrol, Oil and Lubricants for the purpose. Now, in the month of February, funds have been made available for disbursement through the Department of Finance in the budgetary allocation system for the purpose of 01- Salary, 36- Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) within the limit of 90%, 03- Overtime Allowance, 11- Domestic Travel Expenditure within the limit of 40% and 06- Telephone, Electricity and Water Charges, 10- Contractual Services, 13- Office Expenditure, 14- Leasing and Taxes and 24- Petrol, Oil and Lubricants within the limit of 50%.
Accordingly, the Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of the remaining funds to the concerned Controlling Authority as per column-5 of the accompanying statement, within the limit provided by the Finance Department for the schemes in Demand Number T-5 of the program cost under the Tribal Component Program.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be serious for some time to come due to the outbreak of corona virus, the controlling officer should plan and spend the funds in the prescribed manner. Only regular salary should be paid from the fund allocated for salary.
No pending dues should be paid at present.
