The Proposal:
The circular dated 16.04.2020 of the Finance Department has prescribed the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2020-21, considering the pandemic situation in the State due to Corona virus. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided from time to time on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. 2 Under the Tribal Component Programme, under the Government decisions here, funds have been distributed to the concerned Controlling Authorities for the objectives of 1st- Salary, 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) to the extent of 60% and for the objectives of 6th- Telephone, Electricity and Water Charges, 13th- Office Expenditure, 14th- Lease and Taxes to the extent of 30%. Now, in the month of November, funds have been made available for disbursement through the Finance Department on Budget Fund Distribution System for the purpose of 01-Salary, 36-Assistant Grants (Salary) with a ceiling of 70%. Accordingly, the government was considering to allocate the remaining 10 per cent funds for this purpose.
Government's decision:
Under the Tribal Component Programme, the Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of 10% of the funds as per Pillar-5 to the concerned Controlling Authorities for the schemes in the Supplementary Statement of Demands No. T-2 and T-4 of the mandatory expenditure.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious in the next period due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan and spend the funds in the prescribed manner. This fund should be used for regular payment of salaries. It should not be used to pay any pending liabilities at present.
