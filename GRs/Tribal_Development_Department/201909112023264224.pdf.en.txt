Department of Tribal Development, Govt. 19, dated 05/08/2019 and dt. 31.08.2019 Preamble Grants under Special Central Assistance are provided by the Central Government to the State Governments every year under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Adim Janjatiya Vikas Karyakram. The Tribal Development Department had appealed to the Project Officer, AAV Project Office, Government, Non-Government/ Social/ Private reputed organizations to submit innovative proposals for the grant received under this scheme. A detailed review of the proposals received. The report was submitted to the committee headed by the Chief Secretary. The proposal includes a joint venture. The proposal is mainly to increase the involvement of parents in the decision-making process of school management committees in government ashramshalas and Eklavya residential schools, strengthen school management committees, build capacity through training of members and parents in school management committees.
2. With a view to improve the overall quality of the school if the school management committee at the ashramshala level is strong and decisive, the Tribal Development Department proposed to the Central Government to implement this scheme with the approval of the Executive Committee headed by the Chief Secretary and the Government.
In the Project Appraisal Committee (PAC) meeting held on 12.02.2019 by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, a total amount of Rs. 1000.00 lakh under Letter No. 1 has been approved and this fund is subject to Government Decision No. 3 has been issued to Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik.

At present, 502 government ashram schools and 25 Eklavya residential schools are functioning in Maharashtra.
The Tribal Development Department is responsible for the overall development of the students in these ashram schools.
Therefore, the School Management Committee has been entrusted with the responsibility of improving the overall quality of the school by providing adequate decision-making powers at the local level for school management at the ashram school level.
SHARJAH works to connect parents and school management committees and is working with government schools to build leadership capabilities in school management committees. It builds the capacity of common school management committees to improve children's learning and enable parental and community involvement. Joint institutions are working extensively with Delhi Government and SCERT Delhi. Apart from this, the Joint Institute has also worked to strengthen the School Management Committees in Khunti (Jharkhand) and Hubli (Karnataka). Parental engagement is crucial to ensure holistic development of children and hence the School Management Committee plays a major role as a connecting link between parents and school.
5. SAJHA has implemented a pilot project with Tribal Development Department for strengthening of School Management Committees in Dahanu Project and it has shown good results. In this background, the government was considering a proposal to approve the guidelines for the implementation of the scheme" Parental Involvement through School Management Committees in Government Ashram Schools and EMRS" under special central assistance through an organization with experience in the field.
The government has decided to increase the involvement of parents in the decision-making process of the school management committee, strengthen the school management committee, build the capacity of the parents in the school management committee. 1000.00 lakh is being sanctioned as per Annexure No. 1 annexed to this decision.
2. The Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik shall take immediate action to implement the scheme within the limits of the provisions distributed as per the guidelines of the said scheme and shall take care to submit its utilization certificate along with financial and physical report to the Government.
