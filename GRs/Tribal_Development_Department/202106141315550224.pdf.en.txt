In order to preserve and promote the culture, customs and traditions of the tribals of the scheduled areas and to strengthen the system of self-government of the tribals through the Gram Sabha, the Central Government has issued the Panchayat Area Extension (PESA) Act, 1996 and the State Government has framed the Maharashtra PESA Rules, 2014 for the implementation of this Act. However, the provisions of the PESA Act and the PESA Rules have not yet reached the common man in the PESA sector. 3 here. Governor's office. In pursuance of the letter dated 14.10.2020, a proposal was under consideration of the Government to conduct an evaluation study by the Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune, to suggest measures to the Government for effective implementation of the said matter.
Government Decision: As mentioned in the Preamble, in the present case, following the pattern of sample selection, data is collected from at least 10% of the respondents, namely PESA Coordinators, PESA Mobilizers, Gram Sevaks, Sarpanchs, Gram Kosh Samiti Members and Villagers through questionnaires and an evaluation study is carried out by the Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune and Rs. 17,02,200/- (Rupees Seventeen Lakhs Two Thousand Two Hundred only) is being approved by the Government subject to compliance of the following terms and conditions.
