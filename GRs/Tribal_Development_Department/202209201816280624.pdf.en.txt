The Proposal:
The approach of area development was adopted for the overall progress of the socially and educationally backward people of the hilly and remote areas of the state. the tribal people under this program.
It was decided that the ashramshala should be there as the original focal point for the development of the area. Accordingly, a scheme to run Ashram Schools funded by NGOs was implemented in the state from 1953. The government has issued instructions from time to time about the facilities to be provided in these subsidized ashramshalas.
In order to provide opportunities to the students of Scheduled Tribes to study in English medium, the scheme of providing education to the students in English medium reputed residential schools has been approved by the Government vide Reference No. The scheme has been implemented from 2010-11.
Reference No. 3 The revised procedure for selection of nominated schools has been laid down as per the decision of the Government.
After the implementation of the Right to Education Act in the state, facilities for primary education have been provided through district councils in the wadi hamlets of rural areas. In the changing circumstances, the inclination of the parents of the tribal community is also towards educating their children in the English medium. At present, about 2,40,000 students are studying in a total of 540 aided ashramshalas and about 49,000 students are studying in a total of 148 reputed schools. In addition to the department

There are about 1,90,000 students studying in a total of 497 government ashramshalas. Now English medium government ashramshalas have also been started by the department. Apart from this, 37 Eklavya Model Residential Schools have also provided opportunities to students for education in English medium.
Considering the above facts, it was under the consideration of the Government to assess whether or not adequate facilities are being provided to the ST students in the aided ashramshalas and reputed schools run by the NGOs and how or not they are being provided quality education.
Government's decision:
Now a Monitoring/ Inspection Committee and Supervisory Committee are being constituted to evaluate the aided ashramshalas and reputed schools under the department.

The Supervisory Committee shall examine the health, physical facilities, etc. and recommend to the Supervisory Committee whether the aided ashramshalas and the designated residential schools meet the prescribed criteria.
Video shooting of inspection of nominated schools/ aided ashramshalas should be done by the inspection committee. The Committee shall study the report submitted by the Inspection Committee referred to in (a) above or re-examine the schools, if necessary, and analyze whether or not the aided ashramshalas and designated residential schools should be continued and submit its report to the Government.
The Monitoring/ Inspection Committee constituted as per the aforesaid paragraph 1 (a) shall visit every Aided Ashramshala and Nominated School under this Division within their jurisdiction from 20th September, 2022 to 15th October, 2022 and inspect the concerned Aided Ashramshala and Nominated School as per the check table prescribed by the Department/ Commissionerate. The committee shall submit the inspection report of the aided ashramshalas and reputed schools inspected by it in the third week of October, school-wise but collectively to the member secretary of the monitoring committee. The inspection program of aided ashramshalas and reputed schools of the inspection committee should be prepared by the concerned Additional Commissioner and action should be taken to implement it.

(3) The Member Secretary of the Supervisory Committee referred to in paragraph 1 (b) shall take action in accordance with the convening of the meeting of the Supervisory Committee in the first week of November after receiving the report from the Monitoring/ Scrutiny Committee. The Supervisory Committee shall re-examine some schools in the third and fourth weeks of November, if necessary, after studying the school-wise report received from the Supervisory Committee. The Monitoring Committee shall re-examine and submit its report to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik, in the first week of December.
The Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik should scrutinize the report received from the inspection committee and the supervision committee and submit a school-wise report to the government with its clear opinion in the second week of December. After receiving the report from the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik, the government will decide on the action to be taken against the managements of the aided and reputed schools which have adverse reports.
5. It is incumbent upon the concerned to implement the time bound programme as aforesaid within the prescribed time limit.
6. The said Government Decision is being issued with the concurrence of the Revenue and Forest Departments.
