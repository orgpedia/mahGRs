Preamble: Under the Tribal Development Department, the Government provides maintenance grant, building rent grant and contingency grant to the aided Ashramshalas. Accordingly, under the Tribal Component Programme, during the year 2022-23, Demand No. Subsidiary Grants (Programme Expenditure) (2225F129) for running Ashramshalas and Post-Mulgodiya Ashramshalas to Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) in T-5 (01) (73), Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary) below 31-Rs. 400.00 crore.
Schools have been reopened for the entire academic year 2022-23 due to the reduction in the outbreak of Corona virus. Considering these factors, a decision has been taken to provide grant-in-aid to Ashramshalas under No. 2 this year. Accordingly, the matter of disbursement of funds available for distribution was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Under the Demand No. T-5 of the Tribal Development Department under the head of 2225- Welfare of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Minorities (01) (73), under the scheme of Aided Ashramshalas run by Non-Governmental Organizations (2225 F129), in Chapter No. 31- Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) available for disbursement within the limit of 21% of the budgetary provision below Rs. 8400.00 lakhs (Rupees Eighty Four Crores only) to the Controlling Officer and Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. While spending the distributed fund, the controlling officer should plan and spend the corrective measures in the prescribed manner.
(3) The Controlling Officer shall, in the course of spending the funds disbursed under the said Government Decision, apply the financial rules contained in the Maharashtra Budget Rule Book, the Financial Powers Rule Book.
