Grants under Special Central Assistance from the Central Government, under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme are provided to the State Governments.
At present, there are three primitive tribes (Madia, Kolam, Katkari) in the state. These castes are far away from the basic trends of development and are lagging behind in economic and social aspects in comparison to other castes. These tribes are facing the threat of cultural disintegration and there is a feeling of being left out of the mainstream of development among these tribes, in such a situation it is necessary to create a network of youth and women connected with the government to bring all inclusive development from these groups in a speedy manner. The necessary social capital for development has to be created through this network. Keeping this vision in mind, a proposal worth Rs. 135.60 lakh for the scheme" Building social capital through training and nurturing& o youth leaders (Gram Saathis) 2o each from 3 PVTG communities" was sent to the Central Government for approval.

A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) has been signed between Tribal Development Department and Mumbai University for the implementation of various schemes of the Tribal Development Department. The scheme is proposed to be implemented through the Institute of Economics and Public Policy, University of Mumbai. Therefore, under the programme for participatory development of the primitive tribes (Madia, Kolam, Katkari) groups, the government was considering to approve the guidelines for the implementation of the scheme" Building leadership and building social capital by training 60 PVTG youth for all-inclusive development of the primitive tribal groups in Maharashtra" through the Institute of Economics and Social Policy of the University of Mumbai.
Government Decisions With a view to bring the primitive tribes (Madia, Kolam, Katkari) groups into the stream of development in a participatory manner and to effectively implement the development plans, the scheme titled" Building Leadership and Building Social Capital by Training 60 PVTG Youth for Inclusive Development of the Primitive Tribes Groups in Maharashtra" has been implemented jointly by the Institute of Economics and Public Policy, University of Mumbai and the Department of Tribal Development with a total outlay of Rs. 135.60 lakh is being sanctioned as per Annexure No.1 annexed to this decision.
The Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune should take immediate action to implement the scheme within the limits of the provisions distributed as per the guidelines of the said scheme and take care to submit its utilization certificate along with financial and physical report to the government.
