Letter dated 11.04.2018 from the Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme.
In order to eradicate the serious problems like malnutrition, maternal mortality, infant mortality and low birth weight in the scheduled areas, the government has decided to provide one-time square meal to pregnant women and lactating mothers in the scheduled areas through anganwadis under 105 Integrated Child Development Projects in 16 tribal districts of the state. It was launched on November 18, 2015. The average cost of food per day per beneficiary under this scheme is Rs. 25/- has been approved.
The average cost of food was determined at the time of the launch of the scheme, taking into account the cost of raw materials required to prepare food at that time. Meanwhile, the increase in the price of raw materials and the insufficient amount for food from the regional office have been brought to the notice of the government.
The government has issued a circular to provide additional funds from the self-funding of the district councils to implement the scheme more effectively. Issued on 20.12.2017.
Bharat Ratna Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana is being implemented through Anganwadi workers and Anganwadi helpers. Anganwadi workers and helpers
Under this scheme, the responsibility of cooking and feeding square meals to pregnant women and lactating mothers and providing boiled eggs and bananas to children between the age of 7 months and 6 years has been entrusted to them. This is in addition to the other projects they are working on.
In addition to the regular remuneration to Anganwadi workers and Anganwadi helpers, Rs. 250/- per month.

Government Decision: Bharat Ratna Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam. In pursuance of the Government Decision issued on 18th November, 2015, the State Level Committee for Regulation of 29.01.2018
As per the decision taken in the daily meeting and as per the recommendation of the Commissionerate of Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, the Government is approving the change in the one-time square meal component and the increase in the cost of food and the increase in the remuneration of Anganwadi workers and helpers as follows.
1) The average expenditure per beneficiary per day for one square meal for pregnant women and lactating mothers is Rs. 25/- to Rs. 35/- is allowed.
2) Anganwadi workers and helpers will be given Rs. 250/- each instead of Rs. 500/- per month.
Under Phase 2, eggs/ bananas are being given to children between 7 months and 6 years of age. Its priced at Rs. 5/- instead of Rs. 6/- is accepted.
(4) The one-time square meal shall contain the following components of the diet, namely, Calorie, Protein, Fat, Iron, etc., in accordance with Annexure A and B respectively.
