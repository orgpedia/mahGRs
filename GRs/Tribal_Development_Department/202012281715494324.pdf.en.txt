Procedure to be followed while giving direct benefit to students instead of goods The Commissioner, Tribal Development shall fix the commodity-wise procurement price by determining the specification and number of items to be purchased by the students for each academic year. While fixing the purchase price, the price of the item should be fixed based on the retail selling price (MRP) of the branded item available in the market according to the fixed quantity of the item. Allow students to pay. The amount will be credited to the accounts of students/ parents between June 1 and June 10. Also, the principal will provide a list of the quantity and number of items for which the students have received money class-wise to the students when the students go on summer vacation. Also, it will be ensured through teachers/ principals/ superintendents at the school level that the students have purchased the goods according to the quantity after the school starts.
General Instructions regarding procurement 6.1 Procurement of various materials and goods is carried out by the Department of Industry, Energy and Labor. as per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 1.12.2016. The orders issued from time to time by the Department of Industry, Energy and Labor regarding procurement will remain in force.
6.2 The Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik shall specify the terms/ conditions and specifications in the tender for procurement to be made from the Project Officer level so that the terms/ conditions and specifications remain the same. Also, to ensure uniformity in Ashramshala level procurement, the concerned Project Officers should fix the terms/ conditions and specifications.
6.3 Before finalizing the tender, a pre-bid meeting should be held so that the problems related to the tender of the bidders can be detected in time and resolved quickly.
6.4 The process of inviting tenders should be done as per the schedule specified by the government.
Students will be held responsible if they do not receive the goods/ facilities on time.
6.5 The restrictions imposed by the Department of Finance from time to time in respect of procurement shall continue to apply.

8.1 Supply of eggs, bananas, milk/ nachni/ other fruits One egg, one banana and 250 ml of milk to the students of Government Ashram every day. The following steps should be taken to ensure that the goods are supplied on a daily basis and payments should be made only after confirmation.
8.2 The concerned officers shall ensure that the children are provided with meals, snacks, snacks, eggs, bananas on a daily basis during the regular monthly visit and shall record the same in the register required to be signed by them on the date of visit. The field officers should personally enquire from the children in the ashram regarding the items/ food items to be supplied during the visit. If there is any complaint about the supply or quality, it should be taken care of immediately.
8.3 The concerned Project Officer and the Additional Commissioner shall also make surprise visits on other days in addition to their regular visits to inspect the items/ items being provided to the students.
If any deficiency is found regarding the quality of these items/ substances, necessary action should be taken against the concerned.
8.4 The students of Government Ashramshalas should make an advance demand for all types of textbooks based on the school curriculum, except the textbooks received under the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan scheme, and the textbooks required for other classes by the Project Officer at the nearest depot of the Maharashtra State Textbook Production Board and purchase them within the prescribed time.

9. Supply of vegetables and meat/ chicken/ fish etc.
For vegetables, onions, potatoes, mutton/ chicken/ fish etc. required by the Government Ashramshalas for each semester of the academic year, e-tenders should be invited and purchased as per the prescribed procedure by a committee headed by the Project Officer.
10. Supply of fuel.
10.1 Where gas has not yet been supplied to ashramshalas and hostels in the state, the project officer shall arrange for the supply of gas within one month with the approval of the concerned Additional Commissioner. If the gas supply cannot be regularized, the Superintendent should contact the Forest Department or the Forest Development Corporation office of the Sarpanch in writing and place an order in advance and purchase it on priority. Also, the purchase should be made on the basis of weight rather than size.
At the time of purchase, the superintendent should not participate in the auction. The corporation should request for supply of sarpanch at the hand price fixed by it.
10.2 Contractors and rates for transportation of Sarpanch or Gas Cylinder for the whole year shall be fixed by a committee headed by the Headmaster every year. If the Forest Department and the Forest Development Corporation have informed in writing that the required amount of sarpanch is not available, then in such exceptional circumstances, the committee should make the purchase by inviting tenders.
