Ministry of Law and Justice, New Delhi Government of India Notification No.G.S.R. 876 (E), d. 2nd December, 1985 Preamble: In exercise of the powers conferred by paragraph 6 of Schedule V to the Constitution of India, the President is pleased to appoint Shri. The President, Government of India, on the basis of the 1971 Census, has issued a It has been declared a Scheduled Area of the State of Maharashtra by a notification dated 2nd December, 1985. Between 1981 and 2019, the area of villages and cities in the state of Maharashtra has changed. The area of villages and towns in the original Scheduled Areas declared in the State of Maharashtra has changed between 1981 and 2019.
As per the provisions of Rule 4 of the Maharashtra Gram Panchayat (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Rules, 2014, the Divisional Commissioner has been empowered to declare villages in the Scheduled Areas by notification. In exercise of the said power, the Divisional Commissioner has declared new separate revenue villages out of the revenue villages in the scheduled areas. However, it has come to the notice of the Government that such newly declared villages, despite being in the original declared Geographically Scheduled Areas, are not getting the benefits under the provisions of the Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996 (PESA Act) merely because of the new name. Therefore, the matter of extending the benefits under the Panchayat Related Provisions (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996 (PESA Act) to the villages in the Scheduled Areas which have been converted into the said new independent revenue villages was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
In accordance with the provisions of Rule 4 of the Maharashtra Gram Panchayat (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Rules, 2014, the Divisional Commissioner has issued the following orders: The notification for declaration of new independent revenue villages from the period 1981 to 2019 from the revenue villages in the scheduled areas declared by the notification dated 2nd December, 1985 should be issued on priority.
As per the provisions of Rule 4 of the Maharashtra Gram Panchayat Related Provisions (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Rules, 2014, as aforesaid, the villages which have been converted into separate revenue villages from the revenue villages in the scheduled areas declared by the Divisional Commissioner shall be given the benefits under the Gram Panchayat Related Provisions (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996 (PESA Act).
