Special Central Assistance is provided annually by the Central Government to the State Governments under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme. The State Government will provide grant under the scheme. With the approval of the Executive Committee headed by the Chief Secretary, Maharashtra State, various schemes for the welfare of tribal brothers are proposed to the Central Government. At present, there are three special vulnerable tribes (primitive tribes) in the state- Kolam, Katkari and Madia Gond. There are many problems like malnutrition, disease, child mortality, unemployment. These tribes mainly depend on forest and rain-fed agriculture for their livelihood. At the same time, they have means of livelihood such as poultry and goat rearing. If they do poultry farming in the open space around the house, then the space available near them will not only be used, they will also get the means of income, but their nutritional security will also be ensured. Accordingly, a scheme titled" Establishment of Back Yard Poultry for PVTG Families" was proposed to the Central Government for the year 2016-2017 under the Tribal Development Programme Scheme. In the Project Appraisal (Committee PAC) meeting held by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs on 08/02/2017, a total amount of Rs. 200.00 lakh has been proposed for implementation under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme for the year 2016-17. 1 has been approved and the funds received in this regard have been disbursed to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik, as per the decision of the Government in this regard.

Various activities are also undertaken by tribal women self-help groups to provide financial stability to them. Therefore, in the tribal concentrated areas, the beneficiary families of the primitive land will be provided with means of livelihood like poultry farming through Umeda (MSRLM) and they will be able to generate income. It will also help in their nutritional security, so the government was considering to approve the guidelines for the implementation of the scheme" Establishment of Back Yard Poultry for PVTG Families" through UMED (MSRLM).
Government Decision To provide livelihood to the tribal families in the tribal concentrated areas through Umed (MSRLM) by providing them means of livelihood like poultry farming and to provide them with income along with their nutritional security, the scheme" Establishment of Back Yard Poultry for PVTG Families" is being implemented jointly by the Tribal Development Department and Umed (MSRLM) and accordingly guidelines for implementation of the project with a total cost of Rs. 200.00 lakh are being approved as per Annexure-I attached with this Government Decision.
