Decision No. 29/06/2019 Preamble- Grants under Special Central Assistance from the Central Government, under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme are provided to the State Governments. The Tribal Development Department had appealed to the Project Officer AAV Project Office, Non-Government/ Social/ Private Organizations to submit proposals for the grant received under this scheme. After detailed scrutiny of the proposals received, the selected proposals were submitted to the Executive Committee headed by the Chief Secretary. This proposal includes the proposal of Dharamitra Sanstha. The proposal is mainly aimed at improving the economic condition of the tribal families of Kolam, located in Wardha and Arvi taluks of Wardha district, by providing them means of livelihood like poultry and goat rearing.
2 At present, there are three Special Vulnerable Tribes (Primitive Tribes) in the State, namely Kolam, Katkari and Madia Gond. Kolam community is mainly found in Yavatmal, Wardha, Nagpur, Chandrapur, etc. There are many problems like malnutrition, disease, infant mortality, unemployment etc. in this primitive society. The Kolam community in Wardha district is mainly dependent on forest and monsoon agriculture for livelihood. There are other means of livelihood like poultry and goat rearing, however, lack of proper management, conducive environment, diet, proper market price, etc. is found in poultry rearing or goat rearing. With a view to increasing the productivity of Kolam families by implementing time-bound programmes on these issues in a scientific manner, Dharamitra had submitted a proposal worth Rs. 98.56 lakhs to the Government for implementing" DharaMitra-Developing livelihood opportunities of Kolam community through improved practices of backyard poultry and goat rearing" in Wardha and Arvi taluks of Wardha district.

3 With a view to improve the economic condition of the families of the primitive community of Kolam in Wardha district by providing them means of livelihood like poultry and goat rearing, the Department of Tribal Development has proposed to the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India, a scheme titled" DharaMitra-Developing livelihood opportunities of Kolam community through improved practices of backyard poultry and goat rearing." This proposal was approved in the Project Appraisal (Committee PAC) meeting held by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs on 12.02.2019 with a total outlay of Rs. 98.56 lakh for the year 2019-20 and 2020-21.
As per the letter of reference No. The first tranche of funds has been disbursed. The letter No. 3 has been distributed to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik.
4 Dharamitra is a not-for-profit organization. It has been functioning since 1992 in the States of Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh. It aims at sustainable development of rural people by utilising available resources and using environment friendly technologies. Dharamitra works in the areas of water conservation, agriculture development, health, renewable energy and livelihood. Dharamitra Institute has acted as a training institute for this programme. According to Dharamitra, the Maharashtra government has named it as the mother NGO for watershed management. It acts as a resource organisation for agriculture and livelihood. Therefore, the government was considering a proposal to approve the guidelines for the implementation of the scheme" DharaMitra- Developing livelihood opportunities of Kolam community through improved practices of backyard poultry and goat rearing" approved under the primitive tribe through an organization with work experience in this field.
The government has decided to provide livelihood opportunities to the families of Kolam tribe in Wardha district by improving their economic condition. 98.56 lakh is being sanctioned as per Annexure-I annexed to this decision.
