Grants under Special Central Assistance from the Central Government, under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme are provided to the State Governments.
At present, there are three primitive tribal groups (Madia, Kolam, Katkari) in the state: Kolam, Katkari and Madia. These castes are far away from the basic trends of development and are lagging behind in economic and social aspects in comparison to other castes. These tribes are facing the threat of cultural disintegration and there is a feeling of being left out of the mainstream of development among these tribes, in such a situation it is necessary to create a network of youth and women connected with the government to bring all inclusive development from these groups in a speedy manner. The necessary social capital for development has to be created through this network. Keeping this vision in mind, a proposal worth Rs. 572.82 lakh for the scheme" Training for selected PVTG youth as Gram Saathis" was sent to the Central Government for approval.
2. Ministry of Tribal Affairs. The Project Appraisal Committee (PAC) in its meeting held on 05.05.2020 has proposed a total outlay of Rs. 572.82 lakh for implementation of the scheme" Training for selected PVTG youth as Gram Saathis" for the year 2020-21 and 2021-22.
Letter No. 1 has been approved and Rs. 290 Lakhs Fund Letter No. 2 has been received by the Government. Government Resolution No. Under Section 3, the said fund has been distributed to the Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune.
