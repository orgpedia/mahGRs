The Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik has submitted a proposal to the Government under Reference 8 to provide financial assistance to 75 forest lease holder tribal farmers for setting up shed nets on 0.20 R area per farmer with the objective of enhancing their income through high quality and exportable produce by adopting protected farming practices in the land of tribal farmers who have received land through Forest Rights Act from the said available funds. Under this proposal, 75 tribal farmers holding forest pattas are proposed to be provided 5% beneficiary share of Rs.81,600/- and 9 5% subsidy of Rs. 15,50,400/- for setting up of shed net in 0.20 R area per farmer at a total cost of Rs. 16,32,000/- per beneficiary. Accordingly, the project officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project Office, Nashik, was under the consideration of the government to approve the guidelines for the implementation of the scheme with a fund of Rs. 11,62,80, 000/- for 95% subsidy for 75 beneficiaries in the jurisdiction of Kalwan and Yaval.
Government's decision
Under the Integrated Agriculture Development Programme for IFR, 2016-17, a total amount of Rs. 0.20 crore has been sanctioned to 75 tribal farmers of Nashik, Kalwan and Yaval for the implementation of the scheme. The guidelines for implementation of the scheme worth Rs. 11,62,80, 000/- are being approved as per Annexure-I annexed to this decision.
As per the guidelines, the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik should take immediate action to implement the scheme within the limits of the allocated provision and take care to submit its utility certificate along with financial and physical report to the government.
