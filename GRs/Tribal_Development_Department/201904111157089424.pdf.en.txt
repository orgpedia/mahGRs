The Proposal:
Bharat Ratna Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Amrut Aahar Yojana is a flagship scheme of the Government of India to provide one-time square meal to pregnant women and lactating mothers in the Scheduled Areas. This plan is under consideration. Decision of the Board of Directors of November 18, 2015. It was launched on December 1, 2015. Under this scheme, a woman is given a one-time diet from the time she is determined to be pregnant until delivery and for the first six months of lactating mothers. The decision was taken on August 2, 2016.
Secretary, Tribal Development Department. In the review meeting of" Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Amrut Aahar Yojana" held through video conference on 24.05.2017, efforts should be made to ensure that the Anganwadis which are deprived of this scheme get the benefit of this scheme. The Deputy Chief Executive Officer (Child Welfare) concerned has been directed to submit a proposal to the Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, through the District Collector, to include Mada and Mini Mada areas under the scheme. Accordingly, all the Deputy Chief Executive Officers and Zilla Parishad concerned have been asked to submit a proposal to the government through the respective District Collectors to include Mada and Mini Mada areas under this scheme.

Government Circular No: AVVI-2018/ PR No. 75/ Office- 8
Accordingly, the Deputy Chief Executive Officer (Child Welfare), Zilla Parishad, Nandurbar has issued directions regarding implementation of" Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana" in 31 Anganwadis of 16 villages in the scheduled areas. The proposal dated 25.02.2019 has been submitted to the Government through the Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Belapur, Navi Mumbai. As per the proposal, the government was considering to include these villages under the scheme in order to provide the benefit of this scheme to the villages falling under the scheduled area but which were not covered under the Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Scheme earlier.
Government circular:
As per the proposal submitted by the Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Navi Mumbai to the Government, the villages falling under the Scheduled Area of Nandurbar district but which were not covered under the Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Amrut Aahar Yojana earlier are being included under this scheme and the Anganwadis in the villages are being approved to benefit from this scheme. The scheme should be implemented in the Anganwadi centres included in the list. Under this scheme, a four-member feeding committee should be formed for anganwadis falling under the scheduled area and a joint bank account should be opened. The bank account details should be reported to the Deputy Chief Executive Officer, Nandurbar.
Accordingly, the Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Belapur, Navi Mumbai should provide funds to the concerned Deputy Chief Executive Officer, Child Welfare.
