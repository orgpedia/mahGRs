About 5 lakh students are studying in the ashramshalas and hostels run by the Tribal Development Department. It was decided to implement special projects through the Tribal Development Department with the aim that if a big goal is set before the tribal students, they will work hard to achieve that goal and set an example before other students. As a part of this, in the year 2018, the Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project Chandrapur under the Tribal Development Department and the District Collector Chandrapur had launched an expedition named" Mission Shaurya" to climb Mount Everest. As a follow-up to this mission, Mission Shaurya-2 was launched in 2019. Under this, 203 physically fit and mentally strong students of government and aided tribal ashramshalas in the state were selected. Flagging off 11 students who have been selected to scale Mount Everest under Mission Shaurya. Departure from Mumbai on 5/4/2019. Out of 11 students, 9 were girls. On 23rd May, 2019, we have completed the daring expedition to summit Everest. This proves that tribal students can achieve any major goal in life if they are provided with opportunities. In the successful campaign of" Mission Shaurya-2," the message of self-confidence will reach all sections of the tribal society. The campaign will also be carried out across the state.
A model has been created for tribal students. This will encourage the tribal students of government ashramshalas to take up adventure sports in the future as well. This courageous campaign
After completing the course, the tribal students have added to the lore of Maharashtra. However, in recognition of this achievement, the government was considering giving benefits in the form of prizes to the concerned students and their families as a special matter. The government has taken the following decisions:

Government's decision:
In recognition of the success achieved in" Mission Shaurya-2," the following benefits are being approved under this Government decision for the students and their parents involved in this campaign.
The nine students with serial numbers 1 to 9 in the accompanying prospectus who were successful in the Everest expedition will be given Rs. The award carries a cash prize of Rs. 25.00 lakh. However, two students with serial numbers 10 and 11 in the accompanying prospectus who participated in the campaign were awarded Rs. The award carries a cash prize of Rs. 10.00 lakh.
The responsibility of providing free education to the students and developing their future is being taken up by the Tribal Development Department. The concerned Project Officer and Additional Commissioner will be responsible for this.
3. Since the student belongs to a poor family in a very remote area, the family should be given the benefit of the existing schemes of all the government departments on priority. For this, the concerned District Collector and Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project should prepare a plan in a month and take action.
The expenditure on the cash component of the above award shall be met from the provision under the head 50 Other Expenditure under the head Ashramshala Group (2225 D734) for the financial year 2019-20. The concerned Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project is being authorized as Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
The project officer will deposit 20 per cent of the amount directly into the Aadhaar-linked bank account of the student, while the remaining 80 per cent will be kept as a fixed deposit for five years for the future of the student. After the completion of the period, the amount of the fixed deposit can be withdrawn with the joint signature of the concerned student and the project officer.
