The Proposal:
For the financial year 2021-22. 1 Guidelines have been issued for taking fiscal measures by setting spending priorities as per the decisions of the government. Accordingly, as provided by the Finance Department on the Budget Estimates, Allocation and Control (BEAMS) Distribution System from time to time, funds in the range of 10 to 25% of the budgetary provision for salary matters and other office purposes covered under the mandatory expenditure of the Tribal Development Department, subject to reference no. 2 has been distributed to the concerned Controlling Authorities as per the decision of the Government.
Now, by the end of August, the Department of Finance has decided to implement the mandatory expenditure plans as made available on the Budget Estimates, Allocation and Control (BEAMS) Distribution System under Chapter No.
The Government was considering to disburse 13 per cent of the budgetary provision for salary and 36 per cent of the grant-in-aid (salary), 11 per cent for domestic travel expenses, 10 per cent for overtime allowance, 6 per cent for telephone, electricity and water charges, 13 per cent for office expenses, 14 per cent for lease and taxes and 50 per cent for other expenses, 15 per cent for loans and advances, and 34 per cent for scholarships and scholarships.
Government's decision:
Under the" Compulsory Expenditure" of the Tribal Development Department, Demand No. The Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of funds to the concerned Controlling Authorities as per Rakaana No. 5 for the schemes in Rakaana No. 2 in the accompanying returns under T-2, T-3, T-4 and T-8.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. The amount should be disbursed to the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officer. While spending these funds, the concerned should ensure compliance with the working rules, financial rules, provisions of the budget rule book and all the terms and conditions of the state government.
