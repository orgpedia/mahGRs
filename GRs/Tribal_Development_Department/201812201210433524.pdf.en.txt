Preamble- The Central Government has approved a total of 28 proposals for the year 2017-18 under the Special Central Assistance Scheme with an outlay of Rs. 12810.38 lakh vide its letter dated 30/05/2017.
Under this scheme, the Central Government is providing double income of tribal farmers through agriculture, horticulture and vegetables in collaboration with BAIF. 630.00 lakhs and Rs. 345.00 lakh has been sanctioned. This fund reference No. 2, 3 and 4 have been distributed to the Commissioner Tribal Development, Nashik.
BAIF is a reputed voluntary organization. This institution has been established in the year 1967 by Shri Manibhai Desai, a follower of the Father of the Nation, Mahatma Gandhi. BAIF has been working for the last 50 years and aims to provide sustainable livelihood, prosperous environment, raising living standards and creating self-employment with good human values to tribal families. The BAIF has been implemented in 13 States of Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Rajasthan, Jharkhand, Bihar, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, Maharashtra, Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana and Karnataka. BAIF is committed to creating sustainable livelihoods for the poor in rural areas.
Innovation in rural development and environmental conservation, as well as flexibility in activities, have enabled BAIF to address the specific needs of each family, village, and local area and work accordingly. The participation of the group is the foundation of all BAIF activities. BAIF's work has reached 40 lakh families in 250 districts. BAIF has a long experience in this field.
