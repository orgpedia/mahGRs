the prelude
The Maharashtra State Legislature has passed the Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2022. Accordingly, as per the circular issued by the Finance Department, the budgetary allocation of programme expenditure for the first nine months (ending December, 2022) will be provided by the Finance Department within the limit of 60% of the total annual allocation. Accordingly, for establishment matters within the limits of budgetary provisions under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme (item No. Provision of 35% for meeting the expenditure on 02- Wages, 06- Telephone and Electricity, 10- Contractual Services, 13- Office Expenses and 14- Travel Allowance) and 21% for all other items (excluding salary) has been distributed to the concerned Controlling Authorities on Beams Distribution System.
Now, the Department of Finance has capped the fund allocation on Budget Estimates, Allocation and Control (BEAMS) distribution system at 42% for meeting establishment expenses and 28% for all other items. Accordingly, under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, Demand No. Considering the provision made earlier under T-9, T-10 and T-11, the matter of disbursement of remaining funds (excluding salary) to the concerned Controlling Authorities was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
Under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, the Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of additional 7% funds to the concerned Controlling Officer and Collector as per Pillar-5 in proportion to the budgetary allocation for the districts mentioned in the accompanying prospectus under Demand No. T-9, T-10 and T-11.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. While spending the fund, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner.
