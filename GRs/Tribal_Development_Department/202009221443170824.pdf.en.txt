Government's decision
The circular dated 16.04.2020 of the Finance Department has prescribed the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2020-21, considering the pandemic situation in the State due to Corona virus. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided from time to time on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. Under the Tribal Component Programme, 50% of the budgetary allocation for the objectives of 01-Salary and 36-Assistant Grants (Salary) for the schemes under the District Annual Tribal Appropriation Programme, 20% for the objectives of office expenditure and 10% for other objectives has been distributed to the concerned Controlling Authorities. Now, in the month of October, funds have been made available through the Finance Department on the Budget Fund Distribution System for the purpose of 01- Salary, 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) within a limit of 60% and for the purpose of 06- Telephone, Electricity and Water Charges, 13- Office Expenditure, 14- Leasing and Taxes within a limit of 30%.
Accordingly, for the schemes in the accompanying prospectus in Demand No. T-9 under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, the Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of 10% of the funds as per Pillar-5 to the concerned Controlling Authorities for these objectives.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the State is likely to remain very critical for some time to come due to the lockdown, the Controller General of Expenditure should plan and spend the funds in the prescribed manner.
