The Proposal:
In order to ensure availability of calories and protein from the diet to all pregnant women and lactating mothers in the Anganwadi/ Mini Anganwadi areas under the Scheduled Areas, the scheme of providing one-time square meal to pregnant women and lactating mothers under the Bharat Ratna Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Amrut Aahar Yojana is being implemented under the control of Tribal Development Department and through Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, as per the Government Decisions dated 18th November, 2015 and 2nd August, 2016.
Further, as per the Government decision dated 5th August, 2016, under Phase-II of the scheme, one-time additional feeding of eggs/ bananas is approved for all children in the age group of 7 months to 6 years under Anganwadis in Scheduled Areas and Additional Tribal Sub Plan areas for 4 days a week.
Bharat Ratna Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana is being implemented under 105 Integrated Child Development Projects in 16 tribal districts of the state.
Dr. A.P.J. Bharat Ratna included Anganwadis in villages in Mada and Mini Mada areas to ensure that the benefits of Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana reach the beneficiaries in Anganwadis with more than 50% tribal population in Mada and Mini Mada areas (outside PESA areas) in non-tribal areas other than scheduled areas.

The integrated proposal of the villages was directed to be submitted to the government by the Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Navi Mumbai. The government had taken the decision in this regard.
Government's decision:
Under the Bharat Ratna Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana, 10 Anganwadis of Ahmednagar District and 99 Anganwadis of Chandrapur District, which are connected with more than 50% tribal population in Mada and Mini Mada areas (areas outside PESA area), are being approved under this decision.
The implementation of the scheme and the planning of the funds in the newly added Anganwadi and Mini Anganwadi centers should be done according to the government decisions in reference number 1, 2 and 3.
