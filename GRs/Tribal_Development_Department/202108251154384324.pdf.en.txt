A stipend is provided to cover the expenses of the students. Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a steering committee to recommend to the government to improve the dietary component of the schemes implemented by this department for the complete nutrition of pregnant women, lactating mothers, children and students in ashramshalas in the Scheduled and Additional Tribal Sub Plan areas.
Government Decision: Under Bharat Ratna Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana in 16 districts of Scheduled Areas, supplementary diet is being provided to pregnant women, lactating mothers and child beneficiaries. The Government has decided to constitute a Steering Committee as follows to recommend to the Government regarding improvement in the nutritional and supplementary diet component of the schemes being implemented by this Department for the prevention of malnutrition and child mortality and for the holistic nutrition of the students in the Ashramshalas.

The mandate of the Committee shall be as follows: 1. To review the schemes of nutrition groups being implemented by the Tribal Development Department and suggest suitable changes in the dietary components to enhance nutrition.
2. Suggest measures to the Government for inclusion of food items in the scheme of nutrition groups of Tribal Development Department, including food items containing iron, folic acid and Vitamin B-12, flour and rice, salt with iron and iodine, milk and oil with Vitamin A and D, other calorie, protein, fat, iron, calcium, etc.
3. Consult experts from nutrition related institutions like National Institute of Nutrition, Hyderabad and Food Fortification Resource Centre, Delhi and take appropriate decisions.
(iv) to make recommendations to the Government on the nature, quantity and implementation of dietary nutrition and dietary supplements.
5. To study the norms of supplementary nutrition and dietary component of the Ministry of Women and Child Development, Government of India and suggest measures to the Government.
