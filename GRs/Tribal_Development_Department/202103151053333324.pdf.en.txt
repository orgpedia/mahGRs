GOVERNMENT DECISIONS A circular dated 16th April, 2020 issued by the Department of Finance specifies the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2020-21, taking into account the pandemic situation arising out of Corona virus in the State. Accordingly, the Department of Finance has provided funds from time to time on the Budgetary Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. 2 Under the Tribal Component Program under the Government Decisions here, Demand No. In T-5, under the head 2202-General Education, the provision of 90% of the following budgetary allocations to non-government junior colleges being implemented by the Department of School Education (2202 1948)-36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) has been disbursed to the Department of School Education.
Now the revised estimates for the year 2020-21 have been fixed and in the context of the Finance Department, No. 3 A Government Circular has been issued to provide for the disbursement of funds within the limits of the revised estimates on the Budgetary Fund Distribution System. Taking this into consideration, under the Tribal Component Programme, the remaining amount of the revised estimate limit under the head of Demand No. T-5, 2202-General Education to Non-Government Junior Colleges Subsidiary Grants (2202 1948)-36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) has been increased to Rs. 41.50 lakh to the Additional Chief Secretary, School Education Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai as Head of Administrative Department.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. While spending the fund, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner. This amount should be used for regular payment of salary. It should not be used to pay any pending liabilities at present.
3. The head of the department/ controlling officer should immediately distribute the distributed provisions to the drawing and disbursing officers under him.
