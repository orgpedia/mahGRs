Introduction: As many as 4,638 ST students are enrolled in 110 aided hostels under the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. Under the Tribal Component Programme, grants to the Zila Parishads for educational fees, examination fees, maintenance and scholarships are provided to the Social Justice and Special Assistance Department for the remuneration of the employees of such hostels under this scheme. Accordingly, the matter of disbursement of funds made available by the Finance Department on the Budget Distribution System within the limit of 10 percent of the scheme to pay the pending honorarium of the employees of subsidized hostels under the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Under the Tribal Component Programme, Demand No. 2225 of T-5- Welfare of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Minorities, the Finance Department has provided grants to the Zilla Parishads for payment of pending honorarium to the employees of aided hostels under the Social Justice and Special Assistance Department within the limit of 10% of the budgetary allocation for the scheme. Rs 50.00 lakh
