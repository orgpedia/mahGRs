Preamble: For the Financial Year 2021-22, in the context of Finance Department, No. 1 Guidelines have been issued for taking fiscal measures by setting spending priorities as per the decisions of the government. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as made available on the Budget Estimates, Allocation and Control (BEAMS) Distribution System from time to time, has decided to provide 30% of the budgetary provision for the salary component of the schemes covered under the mandatory expenditure of the Tribal Development Department and 15% for other office purposes subject to reference no. 2 Funds have been disbursed to the concerned Controlling Authorities as per the Government decision.
Now, in the month of August, the Department of Finance, as made available on the Budget Estimates, Allocation and Control (BEAMS) Distribution System, under the Compulsory Expenditure Schemes, vide Chapter No.
The government was considering to disburse 7 per cent for salary, 6 per cent for telephone, electricity and water charges, 13 per cent for office expenses, 14 per cent for rent and taxes, 3 per cent for overtime allowance, 17 per cent for computer expenses, 28 per cent for professional and special services, 10 per cent for loans and advances, 11 per cent for domestic travel expenses and 50 per cent for 45 per cent interest.
Government decision:
Under the" Compulsory Expenditure" of the Tribal Development Department, Demand No. The Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of funds to the concerned Controlling Authorities as per Racket No. 5 for the schemes in Racket No. 2 of the accompanying returns under T-1, T-2, T-3, T-4 and T-8.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. The amount should be disbursed to the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officer. While spending these funds, the concerned should ensure compliance with the working rules, financial rules, provisions of the budget rule book and all the terms and conditions of the state government.
