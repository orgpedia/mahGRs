the prelude
The Maharashtra State Legislature has passed the Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2022. Accordingly, as per the circular issued by the Finance Department, the budgetary allocation of programme expenditure for the first nine months (ending December, 2022) will be provided by the Finance Department within the limit of 60% of the total annual allocation. Accordingly, the Department of Finance has released the Budget Estimates, Allocation and Control (BEAMS) as available on the Distribution System for the month of April under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme vide Demand No. Under T-9 in case No. 01- Salary and Balance No. 34- 10% of the budgetary allocation for the scholarship matter and Demand No. For all other items under T-9, T-10 and T-11, 7% of the budgetary allocation has been earmarked as per the provisions of the Reference No. 2 has been distributed to the concerned District Collector as per the decision of the government here.
Now, to meet the expenditure for the month of May, the Department of Finance has made available funds for disbursement on Budget Estimates, Allocation and Monitoring System (BEAMS) in the range of 25% of the budgetary provision for 01-Wage and 14% for other items. Accordingly, considering the funds released earlier within the available limit, the matter of disbursement of the remaining funds to the concerned controlling authority was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
Under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, the demand numbers T-9, T-10 and T-11 are given below. 01- 15% for Salary, Chapter No. The Government is approving to disburse and spend 4% of the funds for scholarship and 7% for other matters as per Pillar-5 to the concerned Controlling Officer and Collector.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. While spending the fund, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner.
