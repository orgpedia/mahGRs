With a vision to enhance the livelihood of 15,000 tribal families by adopting sustainable farming practices by enhancing the productivity of natural resources, Bharat Gramin Aajeevika Foundation (BRLF) has launched a scheme titled" Improving Livelihood Conditions by Augmenting Land and Water Resources, Sustainable Agriculture Practices, Enhancing Livestock Productivity, Capacity Building of GPs for IFR/ CFR and Convergence of Government Schemes" with an outlay of Rs. A proposal worth Rs. 200.00 lakhs was submitted to the Government for implementation in the tribal areas of Nandurbar, Amravati, Gondia, Gadchiroli, Chandrapur, Yavatmal districts. The proposal will focus on three key areas- soil and water conservation, increasing agricultural productivity, value addition of forest produce and livestock management- to improve the lives of farmers in tribal-dominated taluks of Vidarbha and taluks of Nandurbar district.
The other key components of the project are capacity building of local community/ institutions on rights and entitlements, especially focusing on effective implementation of key government schemes like FRA, CFR and TSP, MGNREGA and NRLM.
Bharat Rural Livelihood Foundation is an autonomous organization registered under the Societies Act, 1860. BRLF was established on 3rd September 2013 by a decision of the Union Cabinet.
