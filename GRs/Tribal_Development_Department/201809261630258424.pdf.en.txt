For the year 2018-19, the Department of Tribal Development had invited proposals from Non-Government/ Social/ Private Organizations for grant under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India, Special Central Assistance and Tribal Development Programme. Detailed presentations and reviews of the proposals received. On 22.03.2018, three panels were constituted for scrutiny of proposals at Sahyadri Guest House. In front of this panel' Grandage Services'. A detailed presentation of the proposal has been made. The proposal was specifically to provide training, certification and employment programmes in the field of aged and old age care for the tribal youth with a view to empowering them through employability.
The objective of the scheme is to provide training and employment to the willing tribal youth to learn the skills required for the care of patients who are elderly or suffering from other ailments. With a view to empowering the tribal youth through employability, a scheme titled" Elderly Care Skill Development with Placement" was proposed to the Central Government for implementing training, certification and employment programmes in the field of care of elderly, old age and sick patients.
2. Ministry of Tribal Affairs. In the Project Appraisal Committee (PAC) meeting held on 23.05.2018, a total amount of Rs. 258.00 lakh in the letter No. 1 vide letter No. The first tranche of funds has been disbursed.
" 3. Grandage Services Ltd. " It is an institution of eminence in the field of education, training and employment in the field of care of the elderly, the aged and the sick. Ministry of Social Empowerment and Justice, Government of India and May Grandage Services Ltd. are the first company to provide training and certification to youth in the field of old age.
The course is equivalent to Level 5 of the National Skill Qualification Framework on Skill Development.
