Circular: As per the Government decision dated 16th December, 2009 of the Tribal Development Department, the responsibility of approving the District Annual Tribal Sub Plan has been entrusted to the concerned District Planning Committee. Accordingly, the responsibility of preparing the district plan lies with the District Collector as the Member Secretary of the District Planning Committee and under his control the concerned Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project. Further, in pursuance of the Government decision dated 27.01.2017 of the Department of Finance, it has been decided to consolidate the under Plan and non-Plan expenditure from the financial year 2017-18 onwards and the budgetary expenditure has been further categorized as" Compulsory Expenditure" and" Expenditure on Programmes" and" Revenue" and" Other (Capital and Credit) Expenditure." As per the decision of the Government, from the year 2017-18, the Tribal Sub-Plan is being called Tribal Component Schemes. Accordingly, the following guidelines are being issued for preparation of District Annual Tribal Sub Plan under Tribal Component Programme for the financial year 2022-23.
The Tribal Component Programme is a part of the Annual Plan of the State and formulation of the Annual Tribal Component Programme is complementary to the Annual State Programme. The draft plans of the District Annual Tribal Component Programme for the year 2022-23 should be prepared by the concerned Project Officer in coordination with the Collector and the district plans should be submitted for approval of the District Planning Committee. After that Ma. It should be submitted for state level meeting to be chaired by the Minister (Tribal Development). 8) Keeping in view the needs of the tribal beneficiaries, it should be decided which schemes are required to be taken up and priority should be given to those works which will benefit a large number of scheduled tribe beneficiaries and then to those schemes which will benefit a smaller population.
