Government's decision:
Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana- Per Drop More Crop under Tribal Component Programme, during 2017-18, a total amount of Rs. A sum of Rs. 1525.30 lakh was disbursed to the Agriculture Department. However, out of the Rs. 1258.70 lakhs has been proposed to be released and approved for expenditure from the budgetary provision for the financial year 2018-19 through note no.
Department of Agriculture& Farmers Welfare No. 4 As per the proposal herein, during the financial year 2018-19, under the Tribal Component Programme, Demand No. T-5, 2401, Crop Protection, 796-Tribal Area Sub Plan, (01) Scheme under Tribal Area Sub Plan, (01) Scheme under State Schemes, (27) Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (Central share) Per Drop More Crop, (33) Financial Assistance Rs. 2384.56 lakh out of the budgeted amount of Rs. 1258.70 lakh (Rupees Twelve Crore Fifty Eight Lakh Seventy Thousand only) is being sanctioned to the Additional Chief Secretary, Agriculture and Padum Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai as Head of Administrative Department for disbursement and expenditure on Budget Distribution System (BEAMS).
The Controlling Officer/ Head of the Department in the case should ensure that the fund is spent in the same year as per the approved plan in accordance with the existing government policy on expenditure. Also, the report of the expenditure made from the provision made, the utilization certificate of the fund, the material objectives achieved in the head/ sub-heading wise, etc. information, the concerned field officer should be instructed to send to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik; Tribal Development Department and Agriculture and Padum Department by the 10th of every month.
The Controlling Officer/ Head of the Department shall ensure that the expenditure incurred on the schemes in the tribal sub-plan area, in the additional tribal sub-plan area and outside the tribal component programme area shall be recorded separately so that the Government can have control over the expenditure on the schemes in the tribal area and outside the area of application.
