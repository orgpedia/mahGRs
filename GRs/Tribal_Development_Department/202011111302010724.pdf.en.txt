Government Circular: In order to increase the efficiency of the Tribal Research and Training Institute, the registration of this institute under the Registration Act 1860 and the Public Trust Act 1950 has been given to this institute. The autonomous status has been conferred by the Government Decision dated 24/12/2013. As per the Memorandum of Association, the Governing Body of the Tribal Research and Training Institute has the power to appoint the Executive Board and its members. The Chairman of the Executive Board and the Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute will have the power to select the Additional Commissioner, Tribal Development, Joint Commissioner, Scheduled Tribes Certificate Scrutiny Committee and Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project.
The term of the non-official members (subject experts) of the Executive Board shall not exceed 2 years.
The same non-official member should not be appointed for more than two terms.
(3) The Chairman and the Commissioner of the Executive Boards and the Tribal Research and Training Institute shall have the power to appoint and select the non-official members and the invited members of the Executive Board.
4. Appointment of members to the Executive Board shall be subject to approval of the Governing Body from time to time.
The Chairman and Commissioners of the Executive Boards and Tribal Research and Training Institutes shall have the power to retire a member on occasion.
5. The Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune shall take action as per the prevailing rules for the daily allowance and travel allowance of non-official members of the Executive Board.
