Government's decision
The Finance Department vide Circular dated 16.04.2020 had approved the release of funds in the range of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary provisions of essential and programme expenditure for the first 3 months of the financial year 2020-21 (ending June, 2020), considering the pandemic situation in the State. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. Under the Tribal Component Programme, a total of 25% of the funds for the mandatory expenditure schemes in Demand No. T-2 and T-4, 01-Salary and 36-Assistant Grants (Salary), have been disbursed to the concerned Controlling Authorities.
At present, funds have been made available for disbursement by the Department of Finance on Budgetary Allocation System (BEAMS) for the purpose of 01-Salary and 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries), subject to a ceiling of 35% of the total budgetary allocation. In view of this, the Government is approving to disburse and spend the remaining 10% of the available allocation within the limit of 35% on the Budgetary Fund Distribution System for the distribution of the objectives of 01-Salary and 36-Support Grants (Salary) for the mandatory expenditure schemes in Demand No. T-2 and T-4 mentioned in the accompanying statement to the concerned Controlling Authorities.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious for some time to come due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds. This fund should be used for regular payment of salaries and not for payment of any pending arrears.
