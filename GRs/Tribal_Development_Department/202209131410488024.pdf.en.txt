Strengthening of Gram Sabhas for sustainable and equitable management of collective forest resources for the benefit of Scheduled Tribes forest dwellers and other traditional forest dwellers.
It is necessary to provide sustainable and sustainable means of livelihood to the rural community whose livelihood depends on forests and adopt effective and efficient management practices for conservation, protection and restoration of forests and natural resources.
As many as 11,425 collective forest rights claims have been received in the state at the end of July 2022. Out of these, 8,433 collective forest rights claims have been finally approved by the District Level Committees. Further, the Gram Sabhas can get collective forest rights. For this, it is necessary to prepare and implement the conservation and management plans of the collective forest rights gram sabhas for taking sustainable measures for the conservation, preservation, protection and conservation of the forests, through MGNREGA/ MLA/ MP funds, district level funds/ NGOs/ CSR public participation by convergence of the funds available through various schemes/ projects from the sanctioned funds of the schemes/ projects under various government departments.

Duties and Responsibilities of the District Committee
To ensure that the beneficiaries of individual and collective forest rights as per the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules 2008 and Amended Rules, 2012 are supported for availing the benefits of various schemes from all Government departments as per the claim recognition.
To ensure that the Gram Sabhas are provided with all possible assistance under Rule 4 of the Forest Rights Rules, 2008, in respect of utilization of training funds, etc., for carrying out their functions, particularly as per the provisions of Section 5 of the Recognition of Forest Rights Act, 2006, for the protection of wildlife, forests, biodiversity, watersheds, water bodies, ecologically sensitive areas, habitats of Scheduled Tribes and other forest dwellers, their cultural and natural heritage and the right of the Gram Sabha to take decisions, to constitute a Collective Forest Rights Management Committee (CFRMC) of the members of the Gram Sabha and to assist the Gram Sabhas in the process of opening of bank accounts of the CFRMC, as may be required.
(b) to assist the Gram Sabhas and the Collective Forest Rights Management Committees (CFRMCs) in the preparation of conservation and management plans in accordance with Rule 4 (1) (f) of the Forest Rights Amendment Rules, 2012 and to provide all assistance to the Gram Sabhas and their Committees in integrating such management plans with the Micro Plan or Current Plan or Management Plan of the Forest Department and with such modifications as may be deemed necessary by the Gram Sabhas and their Committees.
To provide services of various government agencies at the field level to the Gram Sabhas for managing the collective forest rights in collaboration with the Forest Department, Agriculture, Animal Husbandry and Fisheries Department, Rural Development and Water Conservation Department (MGNREGA Component), etc., for the implementation of the collective forest rights and the activities to be taken up as per the Conservation Management Plan prepared by the Gram Sabhas and the Collective Forest Rights Management Committee (CFRMC).
