The proposal-
The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules, 2008 and the Rules, 2012 as amended, provide for the rights of the Scheduled Tribes and other traditional forest dwellers to obtain individual and collective forest rights or both as per Section 3 (1). These include the right of Scheduled Tribes or other traditional forest dwellers to own and inhabit forest land for cultivating for their livelihood; rights such as the right to dispose of any forest resource that has been traditionally collected within or outside the boundaries of the village; rights of ownership to collect, use or dispose of minor forest produce; rights of access to water, fish and other products, grazing, access to traditional seasonal resources; rights of collective forest rights of the Gram Sabhas to protect, restore, conserve, manage any social forest resources that have been traditionally protected and conserved for the continued use of forest lands acquired by them.
Strengthening of Gram Sabhas for sustainable and equitable management of such community forest resources for the benefit of Scheduled Tribes forest dwellers and other traditional forest dwellers and providing sustainable and sustainable livelihood to the rural community whose livelihood depends on forests, effective and efficient management systems for conservation, protection and restoration of forests and natural resources

Under the pilot project of United Nations Development Programme-Ministry of Tribal Affairs (UNDP-MOTA), Khoj had undertaken to prepare conservation and management plans of 50 villages in 5 districts of the state- Nagpur, Gadchiroli, Amravati, Gondia and Yavatmal. In the tenth meeting of the State Level Steering Committee constituted to review the schemes implemented by the NGOs under such pilot projects, Reference No. The government was considering giving administrative approval to the plan to prepare a collective forest rights conservation and management plan for 67 villages in the state with the help of NGOs as determined under Section 10.

The NGOs will be responsible for maintaining a complete record of the implementation of the scheme, submitting information as demanded by the government from time to time, and submitting a quarterly progress report of the scheme to the State Level Steering Committee.
4. An MoU for implementation of the proposed scheme should be signed between the NGOs associated with the villages in Schedule A and the Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune on behalf of the Government.
The Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune will have control over the implementation of this scheme. The Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune shall review the schemes being implemented by the NGO every three months and apprise the State Government of the progress.
The duration of the scheme will be 18 months. Under this scheme, funds will be distributed to the concerned NGOs in a phased manner as per the progress of the work as per the contractual agreement.
The Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune shall disburse the first instalment of funds from the sanctioned grant to the concerned NGOs for the preparation of conservation and management plans for a total of 66 villages in A.No. 1 to 31 and 33 to 67 shown in Annexure-A with the present Government decision. The Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune shall disburse the first installment of the grant from the sanctioned grant to the concerned NGO only after receiving the title of the said village and the resolution of the Gram Sabha.
