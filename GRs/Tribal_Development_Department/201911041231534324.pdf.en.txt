Government circular:
November 26 is celebrated as Constitution Day across the country. It commemorates the invaluable contribution of Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar and other members in the adoption and framing of the Constitution.
The Central Government vide its letter dated 25th October, 2019 has directed to undertake a special campaign from 26th November, 2019 to 14th April, 2020 to create awareness among the citizens about Indian democracy and various provisions of the Constitution as the Constitution completes 70 years of its adoption.
The Central Government has given guidelines for various programmes to be taken during this campaign along with the above letter. Accordingly, all government ashram schools, aided ashram schools, government hostels, various field offices under the Tribal Development Department should organize reading of the Preamble of the Constitution, advocacy competitions, essay competitions, lectures. In the Gram Panchayats, villages, hamlets, etc. covered under tribal applications, the text of the Preamble of the Constitution should be read, its importance explained to the tribal citizens, the basic rights and duties of the citizens should be informed. Seminars, cultural programmes and competitions will be organised at the district, taluk and district levels.
The expenditure for this purpose shall be met out of the funds available under the head' Welfare of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Minorities 2225 4113 Advertisement and Publicity'.
