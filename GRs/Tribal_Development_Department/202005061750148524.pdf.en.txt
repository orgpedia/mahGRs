Introduction: Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Vikas Kendra is a scheme implemented by the Central Government. The main objective of the scheme is to collect minor forest produce from the members of the self-help groups in the Scheduled Areas and other areas of Maharashtra mentioned in the Fifth Schedule to the Constitution of India, to sell the collected minor forest produce by adding value to it. It was under consideration of the State Government to issue separate guidelines for implementation of the scheme of Van Dhan Kendras by the Tribal Cooperative Marketing Federation of India Limited (TRIFED), Government of India, as per the guidelines of Reference No. 1.
Government Decision: Traditional knowledge of the tribals on the minor forest produce produced in the forest,
The main objective of this scheme is to improve the quality of life of the tribals by processing and value addition of minor forest produce using skills as well as modern information technology.
Notification No. 19/17/2019 of the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Govt. of India Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Vikas Kendra (PMVVK) Scheme has been operationalized by Government of India on 26.02.2019. Accordingly, Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Vikas Yojana is being approved to be implemented in the State for value addition and sale of Minor Forest Produce (MFP) and other items received in large quantities with the help of Self Help Groups (SHGs) in the State having at least 70% members belonging to Scheduled Tribes.

Setting up of Forestry Development Centres:
1 Van Dhan Vikas Kendra will be set up with 1 Self Help Group (SHG) with a maximum of 20 beneficiaries in the nearest geographical area.
The self-help group should be at the village level or in a nearby village.
Self-help groups (SHGs) should consist of a maximum of 20 beneficiaries to form 1 SHG in which at least 70% of the beneficiaries should be tribals.
Women's Economic Development Corporation (MAWIM), PESA or a committee constituted under the Forest Rights Act with the approval of the Gram Sabha or the existing self-help groups of the Gram Sabha itself, tribal cooperative societies, Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission (MSRLM) and NGOs can work as Van Dhan Self-Help Groups.
Self-help group (SHG) members must be at least 18 years of age.
The Management Committee of the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra shall consist of one representative from each Self Help Group from which the President and Secretary of the Van Dhan Kendra shall be elected. The president and secretary must be from the Scheduled Tribes.
Every self-help group is expected to open a bank account in their nearest bank.
This bank account should be in the name of the SHG, but if the SHG has opened a bank account earlier, it can also be subscribed and the transaction is expected to be done with the joint signature of the president and secretary of the SHG.
Self-help groups will work as per the directions of the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra.
Existing Women Economic Development Corporation (MAWIM), Maharashtra State Rural Livelihoods Mission (MSRLM), PESA or a committee constituted under the Forest Rights Act with the approval of the Gram Sabha or self-help groups of Gram Sabha, tribal cooperative societies, NGOs/ agencies will also have no objection in setting up Van Dhan Kendras. For this, there is no need to break their previously formed self-help groups. Each centre should have at least 300 beneficiaries.
A member of the managing committee of the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra and the Self Help Group is expected to open an account in the bank and two members appointed by this committee are expected to transact in the bank account with joint signature.

Inspection of Van Dhan Vikas Kendra:
Whether the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra is running smoothly or not, it should be checked through the project committee. Officials appointed by the Shabari Tribal Finance and Development Corporation will also inspect the Van Dhan Kendras. If any irregularity is found in the investigation, the irregular amount will be recovered by freezing the bank account of the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra or seizing the property held by the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra.
Similarly, the Tribal Cooperative Marketing Development Federation of India Limited (TRIFED), New Delhi will inspect and evaluate the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra through a third party agency from time to time. The Van Dhan Vikas Kendra will provide all necessary support to the officials of Tribal Cooperative Marketing Development Federation of India Limited (TRIFED), New Delhi and the third agency appointed by them for the inspection.
