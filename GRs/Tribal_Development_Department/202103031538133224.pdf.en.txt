Preamble: The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules, 2008 and the Rules, 2012 as amended, provide for the rights of individual and collective forest rights or both under Section 3 (1). These include the right of Scheduled Tribes or other traditional forest dwellers to own and inhabit forest land for cultivating it for their livelihood; rights such as the right to dispose of, collect, use or dispose of minor forest produce traditionally collected within or outside the boundaries of the village; right to access water, fish and other products, grazing, traditional seasonal resources; right to protect, restore, conserve, manage any social forest resource that has been traditionally protected and conserved for sustainable use.
