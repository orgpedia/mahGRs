As per the Government decision dated 16.12.2009 of the Tribal Development Department, the responsibility of approving the District Annual Tribal Sub Plan has been entrusted to the concerned District Planning Committee. Accordingly, the responsibility of preparing the district plan will be of the Collector as the Member Secretary of the District Planning Committee and the concerned Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project will be under his control. Further, in pursuance of the Government decision dated 27.01.2017 of the Department of Finance, it has been decided to consolidate the under Plan and non-Plan expenditure from the financial year 2017-18 onwards and the budgetary expenditure has been further categorized as" Compulsory Expenditure,"" Expenditure on Programmes" and" Revenue" and" Other (Capital and Credit) Expenditure." As per the decision of the Government, from the year 2017-18, the Tribal Sub-Plan is being called Tribal Component Schemes. Accordingly, the following guidelines are being issued for preparation of District Annual Tribal Sub Plan under Tribal Component Programme for the financial year 2020-21.
The Tribal Component Programme is a part of the Annual Plan of the State and formulation of the Annual Tribal Component Programme is complementary to the Annual State Programme. The District Collector has been asked to prepare the draft of the annual tribal component programme for 2020-21.

The following guidelines and principles should be strictly followed while preparing the Annual Tribal Sub Plan Plan: 1. As on 25.10.2018, it has been decided to transfer the District Level Schemes of Establishment/ Committed nature under the Special Sector Backward Classes Welfare to the State Level Schemes. Accordingly, a revised formula has been decided to allocate the budget for the State level and district level applications from the total annual budget received from the Planning/ Finance Department under the Tribal Component Programme by deducting the required budget for the establishment (salary and office matters) as well as for the committed expenditure schemes of the Tribal Development Department. As per the revised formula, 5 per cent of the total budget has been allocated for direct subsidy to gram panchayats, 55 per cent for state-level schemes and 40 per cent for district-level schemes.

(7) The District Collector shall himself ensure that while implementing the works/ schemes under the Tribal Component Programme, the area of operation of such schemes is within the tribal area or how.
The concerned District Collector and the scheme implementation officer should ensure that the allocation made for the tribal area cannot be spent in the scheme outside the tribal area.
8) The head of the department concerned in the implementation of the scheme should attach a certificate that the proposed works are in the tribal area to the budget at the time of administrative approval and the collector should not give administrative approval without having such a certificate at the time of administrative approval.

Funds should be used as" Gap Fund" for works which cannot be done from existing schemes through innovative or other forms of funding. The District Planning Committee shall ensure that no separate funds are made available for the same work under any circumstances through two different schemes. However, for some works, if the funds are received through the Centrally Sponsored Schemes, such works should be proposed from it and for the remaining works, the funds available to the district through various sources from the state government should be planned in a convergence manner and a fruitful implementation plan should be prepared.
10) In respect of various schemes, the Project Officers should visit their project areas and hold discussions with the field level implementing officers and accordingly identify the exact requirement in terms of tribal beneficiaries in their area and determine the exact schemes that need to be taken up. While doing so, priority should be given to works which will benefit a large number of Scheduled Tribe beneficiaries in the area where the scheme is to be implemented, and after meeting their needs, priority should be given to schemes which will benefit a smaller population. The District Annual Tribal Component Programme for the year 2020-21 is to be prepared as per the new guidelines issued by the Government. As per the recommendation of the Committee in its Fourteenth Report, the members of the Legislative Assembly should be informed about the amount allocated for the works in their constituencies.
11) The development area/ sub-area/ scheme/ project wise, taluk wise details of the proposed outlay in the District Annual Tribal Component Programme/ District Planning Committee should be made available to the members before the meeting.
12) For the finalization of the 2020-2021 District Annual Plan at the state level, 1 to 4 returns have been prescribed so that the necessary information of each district can be obtained in the prescribed form. The report should be submitted to the government.
