Government's decision
Under the Bharat Ratna Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Amrit Aahar Yojana under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, tribal pregnant women, lactating mothers and children are provided with supplementary nutrition.
Since the scheme is aimed at eradicating malnutrition in tribal areas and keeping in view the need to prioritize health related activities in the wake of COVID-19 outbreak, it has been decided to implement the scheme in the context of No. Under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme 2020-21, funds have been disbursed as per 10% of the budgetary allocation under the 31st Supplementary Grants (Non-salaried) component of the scheme.
The Department of Finance has agreed to allocate funds within the limit of 25% of the budgetary allocation for the financial year 2020-21. Accordingly, under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, Demand No. For the districts mentioned in Pillar No. 2 of the accompanying prospectus in T-9, the Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of 15% of the budgetary provision for the scheme to the concerned Controlling Authorities and Collectors as per Pillar 6.
The funds are being distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to remain critical for some time due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan and spend the funds in the prescribed manner.
