As per the Government decision dated 16.12.2009 of the Tribal Development Department, the responsibility of approving the District Annual Tribal Sub Plan has been entrusted to the concerned District Planning Committee. Accordingly, the responsibility of preparing the district plan will be of the concerned Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project under the control of the Collector as the Member Secretary of the District Planning Committee. Further, in pursuance of the Government decision dated 27.01.2017 of the Department of Finance, it has been decided to consolidate the under Plan and non-Plan expenditure from the financial year 2017-18 onwards and the budgetary expenditure has been further categorized as" Compulsory Expenditure,"" Expenditure on Programmes" and" Revenue" and" Other (Capital and Credit) Expenditure." As per this decision, the Tribal Sub-Plan is being called the Tribal Component Schemes from 2017-18. Accordingly, the following guidelines are being issued for preparation of District Annual Tribal Sub Plan (District Annual Tribal Component Programme) under Tribal Component Programme for the financial year 2019-20.
The Tribal Component Programme is a part of the Annual Plan of the State and preparation of the Annual Tribal Component Programme is supplementary to the Annual Plan Programme. The District Collector has prepared the draft of the annual tribal component programme for 2019-20.

The following guidelines and principles should be strictly followed while preparing the Annual Tribal Sub Plan Plan: 1. As on 25.10.2018, it has been decided to transfer the District Level Schemes of Establishment/ Committed nature under the Special Sector Backward Classes Welfare to the State Level Schemes. Accordingly, a revised formula has been decided to allocate the budget for the State level and district level applications from the total annual budget received from the Planning/ Finance Department under the Tribal Component Programme by deducting the required budget for the establishment (salary and office matters) as well as for the committed expenditure schemes of the Tribal Development Department. As per the revised formula, 5 per cent of the total budget has been allocated for direct subsidy to gram panchayats, 55 per cent for state-level schemes and 40 per cent for district-level schemes.
Since the size of the State Annual Plan for the financial year 2019-2020 is yet to be decided, the budgetary allocation for the Tribal Component Programme is being made as per the above revised formula for district level plans as per Annexure-A, based on the budgetary support received for the current year (2018-19). The Minister (Tribal Development) will chair the State level meeting, where the size of the total resources available for the State Annual Plan for the year 2019-20 and all the District Annual Plans will be decided and the proposed District Annual Tribal Plan 2019-20 will be finalized.

District Annual Tribal Component Programme Plan should be within the limit of maximum outlay as given in Annexure-B and project-wise outlay allocation for districts having more than one Project Office should be as per Annexure-A.

The guidelines in Annexure-D as well as the general suggestions in the Government Circular dated 29th September, 2018 of the Planning Department should be taken into consideration while preparing the District Annual Tribal Component Programme.
4) Under the Tribal Component Programme, from the financial year 2019-20, under the context Since the district level schemes in the Annexure attached to the Government decision dated 25.10.2018 have been transferred to the State level, provision for these schemes will henceforth be made available at the State level, for which no provision should be proposed at the district level.
While determining the sector-wise allocation of funds for development of education, health, agriculture, water conservation, energy, skill development, road development, etc., the funds that the district can receive under various schemes under the Tribal Component Programme and General Area Plan for its fulfillment should be planned after taking into account the earlier expected achievements, results, proposed changes, etc. in this sector. Also, the funds should be used as" gap funds" for works that cannot be done from existing schemes through innovative or other forms of funding. The District Planning Committee shall ensure that no separate funds are made available for the same work under any circumstances through two different schemes. However, for some works, if the funds are received through the Centrally Sponsored Schemes, such works should be proposed from it and for the remaining works, the funds available to the district through various sources from the state government should be planned in a convergence manner and a fruitful implementation plan should be prepared.
The Planning Department has developed a comprehensive scheme information system" MP-SIMS" which is mandatory to be used by all district level offices/ agencies while preparing the District Annual Tribal Plan 2019-20. The district plan to be prepared for small groups, district planning committees and state level meetings should be submitted by the operational agencies in the Plan Information System (MP-SIMS) itself.
7) The District Collector should himself ensure that the area of operation of such schemes for implementation of works/ schemes under the Tribal Component Programme is in the tribal area.
The concerned District Collector and the scheme implementation officer should ensure that the allocation made for the tribal area cannot be spent in the scheme outside the tribal area.

8) The head of the department concerned in the implementation of the scheme should attach a certificate that the proposed works are in the tribal area to the budget at the time of administrative approval and the collector should not give administrative approval without having such a certificate at the time of administrative approval.
9) In respect of various schemes, the Project Officers should visit their project areas and hold discussions with the field level implementing officers and accordingly identify the exact requirement in terms of tribal beneficiaries in their area and determine the exact schemes that need to be taken up. While doing so, priority should be given to works which will benefit a large number of Scheduled Tribe beneficiaries in the area where the scheme is to be implemented, and after meeting their needs, priority should be given to schemes which will benefit a smaller population. The District Annual Tribal Component Programme for the year 2019-20 is to be prepared as per the new guidelines issued by the Government. As per the recommendation of the Committee in its Fourteenth Report, the details of the total outlay proposed for each scheme should be shown separately for the development block/ taluk wise under the project area so as to enable the members of the Legislative Assembly to know the amount of allocation for the works in their constituency.
10) Area/ sub-area/ scheme/ project-wise, taluk-wise details of the budget proposed in the District Annual Tribal Component Programme/ District Planning Committee should be made available to the members before the meeting.
