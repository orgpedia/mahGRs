Introduction: The Government started the Ashramshala Group Scheme from the year 1972-73 with the objective of spreading education among the tribals in the hilly and remote areas of Maharashtra. At present, there are 502 government-run government ashramshalas, of which government primary ashramshalas and secondary government ashramshalas are operational, and some secondary ashramshalas and affiliated junior colleges. From the academic year 2018-19, 21 primary Ashramshalas have been upgraded to Secondary Ashramshala Classes (ECS). 8th to 10th Std.) and increasing the number of batches in 6 Ashramshalas (Std. 8th to 10th). Also, 10 secondary schools have been upgraded to affiliated junior colleges. 11th& 12th), Arts& Science and 5 Higher Secondary Schools.

(a) To that end, the annual result of the students in such ashramshalas should be at least 80%. Also, at least 60% of the students will have to get first class.
(b) Appoint qualified teaching and non-teaching staff trained as per rules in the said Ashramshalas. Teachers who teach English subjects in secondary schools should be given preference in appointment if they have studied in English medium and have secured first class in English subject in their educational qualification.
