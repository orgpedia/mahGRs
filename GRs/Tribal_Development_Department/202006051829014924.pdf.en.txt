Government's decision
The Finance Department vide Circular dated 16.04.2020 had approved the release of funds in the range of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary provisions of essential and programme expenditure for the first 3 months of the financial year 2020-21 (ending June, 2020), considering the pandemic situation in the State. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. 2 As per the decision of the Government, 25% of the funds under the Tribal Component Programme for the sub-development schemes titled" Welfare of Backward Classes" in Demand No. T-5 of the programme expenditure has been disbursed to the concerned Controlling Officer in respect of 01-Salary and 36-Assistant Grants (Salaries).
At present, the Department of Finance has made available funds for disbursement under the Budgetary Allocation Mechanism (BEAMS) for the purpose of 01- Salary and 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) within the limit of 35% of the total budgetary allocation. In view of this, the Government is approving the release and utilization of the remaining 10% of the 35% limit available for disbursement of the objectives of 01-Salary and 36-Assistant Grants (Salaries) for the schemes under the sub-development head" Welfare of Backward Classes" in Demand No. T-5 mentioned in the accompanying statement under the Programme Expenditure to the concerned Controlling Authorities.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious for some time to come due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds. This fund should be used for regular payment of salaries and not for payment of any pending arrears.
