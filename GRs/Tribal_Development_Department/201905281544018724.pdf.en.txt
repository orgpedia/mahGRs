The Central Government provides grants under the Special Central Assistance to the States under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and the Scheduled Tribes Development Programme every year. As per the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006, collective forest rights have been conferred on more than 23 lakh hectares of forest land in the state of Maharashtra. The members of the Gram Sabha must be trained to properly and sustainably enjoy the community forest rights conferred under the Forest Rights Act, 2006. With this vision, Mumbai Institute of Economics and Public Policy, University of Mumbai has taken up the task of creating a diploma course on pilot basis.
2. The course is ready and on a pilot basis at Gram Mendha (Lekha), Taluka. It is being implemented at Dhanora, District Gadchiroli in collaboration with Mumbai Institute of Economics and Public Policy, University of Mumbai and Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune. Necessary corrections have been made in the curriculum through this experiment. Now this course needs to be implemented at the level of entire Maharashtra as a collective forest rights certificate in a relaxed form. As per the MoU between the Department of Tribal Development, State of Maharashtra and the Mumbai Institute of Economics and Public Policy, University of Mumbai, the course will be conducted in collaboration with the Mumbai Institute of Economics and Public Policy and Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune.
A proposal worth Rs. 486.50 lakh for the scheme" Mumbai School of Economics and Public Policy- Extension of Diploma in CFR" for the year 2018-19 was sent to the Central Government for approval.
Ministry of Tribal Affairs. The Project Appraisal Committee (PAC) in its meeting held on 23.05.2018 has approved the proposal of" Mumbai School of Economics and Public Policy- Extension of Diploma in CFR" for the year 2018-19 with a total outlay of Rs. 486.50 lakhs. 02
