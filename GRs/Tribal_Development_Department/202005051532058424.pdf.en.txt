Government's decision
Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan, a Centrally Sponsored Scheme being implemented by the Department of School Education and Sports during the financial year 2020-21 under the Tribal Component Programme, has been approved under Demand No. T-5, 2202, General Education, 01-Primary Education, 796-Tribal Area Sub-Plan, (01) (10) Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan (60% Central Share) Accounts Head 2202 I 648, 31-Supported Grants (Non-salaried) Below Rs. 17500.00 lakhs is budgeted. Further, 40% of the matching State share of the scheme under head 2202 I 657,31-Subsidised Grants (Non-salaried) amounting to Rs. 10000.00 lakhs is budgeted.
In a circular dated 16th April, 2020 issued vide Reference No.1 of the Department of Finance, considering the pandemic situation in the State due to Corona Virus, it has been approved to allocate funds for specific objectives in the range of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary allocation of essential and programme expenditure for the first 3 months of the financial year 2020-21 (ending June, 2020). It has also been made mandatory to obtain the consent of the Finance Department for the distribution of funds in the previous year of the centrally sponsored schemes. Accordingly, as agreed by the Finance Department on the subsidy in respect of School Education Department, (01) (10)-Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan (60% Central Share) Account Head 2202 I 648, 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payment) of Rs. 166.13 lakhs and equivalent 40% State share under head 2202I 657, 31-Support Grants (Non-Payment) Rs. 110.75 lakh for the year 2019-20 to the Additional Chief Secretary, School Education Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai as Head of Administrative Department.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious for the next 3 to 4 months due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds in this distribution.
It should be ensured that this fund is spent in this year as per the approved plan in accordance with the current government policy.
