Introduction: Notifications have been issued by all the concerned departments regarding the implementation of Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) process in the individual benefit schemes of the State Government. As per the notification dated 27.07.2017 of the Tribal Development Department, from the academic year 2017-18, the Government has made it mandatory for the students belonging to the Scheduled Castes category to apply for the benefits under the schemes related to education fees, examination fees, examination fees, maintenance allowance, education loans, etc. on the State level Maha-DBT portal developed by the Directorate of Information and Technology.
In the meantime, as the online process of providing the benefits to be paid by the students after registering their application on the said MahaDBT portal is in progress through the MahaDBT agency, in order to avoid the difficulties of the eligible student in paying the fees in the college, it was decided under the decision of the Government dated 11.12.2017 to deposit 60% of the 50% of the tuition fee and examination fee for the first half of the academic year 2017-18 by taking a bond on the Aadhaar linked bank account of the student. Further, in order to provide the benefits of scholarship schemes on MahaDBT system, as the system is not fully functional till the last phase, it was decided to credit the full benefit of scholarship and tuition fee, examination fee reimbursement scheme to be sanctioned to the students belonging to Scheduled Tribes category for the academic year 2017-18 to the Aadhaar linked bank accounts of eligible students through e-Vikas online computer system developed and operated by the Department earlier. Accordingly, all the concerned departments have taken action to dispose of more than 50 percent of the applications received from eligible students in 2017-18 by the end of March 2018.

However, due to non-submission of scholarship/ free-ship applications of students from some educational institutions to the Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project within the deadline, the respective educational institutions could not receive the amount of tuition fee, examination fee. Also, the applications of the students from some educational institutions were submitted late to the concerned project officer, due to which the educational institutions did not receive the due amount. Also, since the academic year 2017-18 has ended, the amount of tuition fees and examination fees for this year will be deposited in the account of the students and if the students of the last semester do not deposit this amount in the college, then the educational institutions will suffer a loss, some educational institutions had filed petitions in the High Court to pay the amount of tuition fees and examination fees of the students admitted in their colleges directly to the colleges. The petition No. 408/2018. The Bombay Bench of the High Court in the hearing held on 16.4.2018, 23.4.2018 and 26.4.2018 directed to take measures regarding payment of tuition fees, examination fees for the students belonging to Backward Classes and Economically Backward Classes admitted in the colleges in 2017-18. the High Court. Accordingly, The Cabinet in its meeting held on 03.05.2018 has decided to take steps to provide for reimbursement of tuition fees, examination fees of the students belonging to Backward Classes (SC, ST, Vimukt Jati, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes) and Economically Backward Classes admitted in the colleges for the year 2017-18. A government order was being issued in this regard. A decision in this regard will be taken later.
Decision (9) Only those colleges/ educational institutions which have not yet received the amount of tuition fee, examination fee of the ST students admitted in the academic year 2017-18, are being allowed to pay the amount of tuition fee, examination fee payable for the remaining students permissible/ eligible courses to the respective college/ educational institution through online mode on the following terms/ conditions, excluding the eligible ST students admitted in their colleges/ educational institutions who have finally paid the benefit today. :
(i) As prescribed by the Government, the concerned college/ educational institution shall be bound to examine the complete applications of the admitted students from them and submit the same along with the undertaking of the Principal (as per Annexure A) to the concerned Project Officer of the Department. (Such applications need not be re-submitted if they have been submitted earlier along with the Principal's undertaking. (ii) The concerned college shall certify that the Aadhaar of each student has been verified.
(ii) The educational institution/ college shall be required to submit an undertaking to the concerned Project Officer of the Department along with the application that the educational institution/ college has not collected tuition fee, examination fee from the concerned students during the academic year 2017-18.
(iv) The applications of the students submitted by the concerned college/ educational institution in the academic year 2017-18 will be examined by the concerned Project Officer of the Department, who will benefit.

The amount of tuition fee, examination fee of the remaining eligible students, excluding the students who have paid, shall be paid to the concerned college/ educational institution. Payments must be made by June 20, 2018.
(2) The amount of subsistence allowance in the amount of scholarship (tuition fee, examination fee and subsistence allowance) of eligible students for the academic year 2017-18 should be paid online to the Aadhaar linked bank account of the eligible students concerned.
(3) The responsibilities of colleges, educational institutions and universities in respect of scholarships and free-ship schemes of the Tribal Development Department:
(i) The Principal of the college concerned shall be responsible for verifying all the original documents of the students in respect of their eligibility (admission, caste, income, residence, confirmation of passing in the previous year, Aadhaar, back details, etc.).
(ii) It shall be mandatory for the Principal of the college to ensure the recognition of the college, affiliation, recognition of the course, intake capacity and fee structure as agreed by the competent authority.
(ii) to ensure the eligibility of the students and the college as above and to approve the applications of the eligible students immediately. The Principal of the college will be responsible for forwarding/ forwarding the same to the concerned Project Officer by 5th June, 2018. Applications received thereafter will not be considered and the concerned officer will be responsible for the same.
(iv) The Principal of the concerned colleges shall ensure that the amount of tuition fee/ examination fee paid to the colleges in the year 2017-18 is not paid to the students or colleges concerned earlier in the academic year and the same shall not be paid again to such students or colleges for the same period and no double benefit under the scholarship/ free ship scheme shall be provided.
(v) It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Administrative Department to ascertain the eligibility of the colleges concerned and submit the records thereof.
Responsibility of Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project: (1) Before the date of the decision of the Government, the Project Officer shall immediately communicate to the concerned colleges the details of the eligible students of the colleges in their jurisdiction who have been given the benefit for the year 2017-18 and the previous year.
(ii) The applications of the students submitted after verification and verification by the concerned college should be verified by the concerned Project Officer within two weeks after examining the documents such as caste certificate of the concerned student, validity certificate where required, domicile certificate, Aadhaar, previous year's pass certificate/ proof, income certificate, and college guarantee letter (Annexure-A, hard copy) etc.
(ii) Payment of scholarship, tuition fee, examination fee and subsistence allowance should be submitted to the Treasury Office.
