Government's decision
The circular dated 16th April, 2020 issued by the Department of Finance specifies the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2020-21 keeping in view the pandemic situation in the State. Accordingly, the Department of Finance has provided funds from time to time on the Budgetary Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. 2 As per the Government decisions, 45% of the programme expenditure under the Tribal Component Programme has already been released to the concerned Controlling Authorities for the sub-development schemes under the head" Welfare of Backward Classes" in Demand No. T-5 for 01-Salary and 36-Assistant Grants (Salaries).
Now, in the month of September, funds have been made available for disbursement by the Department of Finance on Budgetary Allocation System (BEAMS) for the purpose of 01- Salary and 36- Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) subject to a ceiling of 50% of the total budgetary provisions. Considering the above, for the purpose of sub-development schemes namely 01-Salary and 36-Assistant Grants (Salary) under the head" Welfare of Backward Classes" in Demand No. T-5 mentioned in the accompanying statement under Programme Expenditure, the amount attached in the accompanying statement is Rs. The government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of the remaining 5% of the funds to the concerned controlling authorities.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to remain critical for some time to come due to the lockdown, the Controller should plan and spend the funds in the prescribed manner. This fund should be used for regular payment of salaries. It should not be used to pay any pending liabilities at present.
