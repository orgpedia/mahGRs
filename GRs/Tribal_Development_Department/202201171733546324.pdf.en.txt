Government's decision:
The modalities for disbursement of Government of India Post Matric Scholarship Scheme to the Scheduled Tribes students vide Government Decision No. 1 dated 09.08.2004 are mentioned and the said Government Decision is being superseded. The following revised rules are being prescribed for the disbursement of Post-Matric Scholarship by the Government of India to the students belonging to Scheduled Tribes.
(a) The form of the plan:
The Government of India Post Matric Scholarship Scheme has been transferred from the Central Government to the State Governments since 1959-60. Under this scheme, students belonging to Scheduled Tribes are paid a subsistence allowance according to the group of the course. Also, the fee for the examination, tuition fee, and other fees are reimbursed. Under this scheme, the annual income limit of the parents of the students is subject to the reference No. Rs. 2.50 lakh has been fixed under Section 03. The upper age limit of parents is revised as per the guidelines issued by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India from time to time.
It has been decided to extend the benefits of Government of India Post Matric Scholarship Scheme to the Scheduled Tribes students from the academic year 2018-19 through MahaDBT portal. Students who are eligible for this scheme are required to deposit the scholarship amount in their Aadhaar linked bank account from the MahaDBT portal.

Benefits of Scholarship under the Scheme: The eligible students who are eligible for the benefit of scholarship under the Government of India Scholarship Scheme as per the guidelines dated 01.07.2010 may be sanctioned the fee as per their course.
(i) Living Allowance:- The students residing in hostels and day scholars for classes (I, II, III, IV) as prescribed course-wise as per the guidelines dated 01.07.2010 of the Government of India, should be given Class (I, II, III, IV) wise living allowance.

General Terms and Conditions of Government of India Scholarship Scheme:- The General Terms and Conditions of the Scheme for availing the benefit of scholarship to the students of Scheduled Tribes availing scholarship under the Government of India Post Matric Scholarship Scheme are as follows.
The caste certificate of the student should be from the competent authority. Students studying in professional and technical courses under this scheme do not need a caste verification certificate. (Copy of the original should be scanned and uploaded on the MahaDBT portal. There is no evidence. (ii) Proof of income of the student's parents should be from the competent authority. This income certificate will be valid till the time a new income certificate is required to be submitted. Also, if the father is not alive, the death certificate must be uploaded along with it. Married students are required to attach proof of income of the husband. Income proof of parents will not be accepted. Form 16 will not be accepted. (Copies of the original documents need to be scanned and uploaded on the portal. There is no evidence. (3) If the student is staying in a government hostel, private hostel or college hostel, the certificate of the hostel head will be required to be uploaded along with the application. Accordingly, the permissible subsistence allowance to the concerned students will be paid through the MahaDBT portal. The responsibility of verifying the presence of the student in the hostel will be of the principals of the concerned colleges and the Home Guard/ Superintendent of the hostel.
The attendance of the student should be at least 75% at the time of sanction of scholarship. It will be the responsibility of the college principals to verify this.
The scholarship will be applicable till a student completes a single course (professional/ non-professional). If the student completes the course, he/ she will not be eligible for the scholarship for the next non-professional MA course. However, scholarship will be available for other professional courses such as MBA. This rule will be applicable for professional courses as well.
The scheme will be applicable for any two professional courses.
The student must complete the course for which the scholarship is taken. If he leaves the course halfway and takes admission in another course

Students who have passed or failed in the final examination of Bachelor's or Master's degree in Arts/ Commerce/ Science stream and subsequently admitted to any professional/ technical certificate examination/ diploma/ degree course will be eligible for the scholarship. However, after this, the concerned student will not be able to make any change in the course, otherwise he will be ineligible to get the scholarship for further courses.

The educational institution will be required to submit an online application to the competent authority after verifying all the information of the beneficiary student by entering the Aadhaar number or specific computer number of each beneficiary student and verifying through a computer system that the student has appeared in the examination every year.
