The proposal-
Various schemes are implemented by the Tribal Development Department for the welfare of the people. A part of these schemes is the Tribal Ashram School Group Scheme for the education of tribal students.
At present, there are 502 government ashram schools and 14 Eklavya residential ashram schools in Maharashtra. The Tribal Development Department is responsible for the overall development of the students of this government ashram school. Special programmes are being implemented by the department to ensure their health, nutrition and education in a proper manner.
The Tribal Development Department will provide 24x7 health services to the students of 301 Ashram Schools and 8 Eklavya Residential Ashram Schools in the remote areas through Bharat Vikas Group of India. The date of the decision
The Government is considering a proposal to prepare a computerized health card by collecting information on the health of all the students of 201 Government Ashram Schools and 6 Eklavya Residential Ashram Schools in the remaining remote areas, to establish a command center for the needy and sick students to get 24x7 guidance of specialist doctors, to form a school-level health committee to strengthen health facilities at the school level and to provide health facilities by analyzing the information about the health of the students from time to time.
