Government's decision
In view of the pandemic situation in the State due to Corona virus, the Finance Department vide Circular No.1 dated 16th April, 2020 has approved the release of funds at the department level for the first 3 months of the financial year 2020-21 (end of June, 2020) for the specified objectives of the schemes of mandatory and programme expenditure in the range of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary allocation. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. Under the Tribal Component Programmes, the Government has made full availability of funds to the extent of 35% for the purpose of 01-Salary and 36-Support Grants (Salary), while 10% of the available funds for the purpose of 02-Majri, 06-Telephone, Electricity and Water Charges, 10-Contractual Services, 13 Office Expenses, 14-Lease and Tax, 24-Petrol, Oil and Lubricants, 19-Food Expenses, 45-Interest, have been distributed to the concerned Controlling Authorities.
At present, the Department of Finance is providing 20% of the budgetary allocation for the following purposes: 02- wages, 06- telephone, electricity and water charges, 10- contractual services, 13- office expenses, 14- lease and taxes, 24- petrol, oil and lubricants. Accordingly, under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme, the Government is approving the disbursement and expenditure of 10% of the funds available for distribution as per Pillar-5 to the Controlling Officer of Pillar-6 for the districts mentioned in Pillar-2 of the accompanying prospectus in Demand No. T-9 and T-10 for the said purposes.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. Since the economic condition of the State is likely to remain very critical for some time to come due to the lockdown, the Controller General of Expenditure should plan and spend the funds in the prescribed manner.
