The Proposal:
Considering the situation of the Corona virus epidemic in the state, the Finance Department has given Rs. Circular dated 16.04.2020 has laid down the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2020-21. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided from time to time on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. 2 Under the Government decisions herein, a provision of 70% of the programme cost under the Tribal Component Programme for the purpose of 01-Salary, 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) for the schemes under the sub-heading" Welfare of Backward Classes" has been disbursed to the concerned Controlling Authorities. In this regard, grant-in-aid to NGOs for running Ashramshalas and Post-Mool Udyog Ashramshalas under the Tribal Component Programme has been disbursed to the extent of 70% of the budgetary provision for the financial year 2020-21 under the target of 36-Assistant Grants (Salaries) under Scheme 2225F129. As the funds for salaries under the scheme were insufficient, the government was considering to disburse funds in excess of the 10 per cent limit of the budgetary provision.
Government's decision:
Under the Tribal Component Programme, Sub-Development head" Welfare of Backward Classes" in the programme expenditure, the subsidy subsidy to NGOs for running Ashramshalas and Post-Graduate Ashramshalas in Demand Number T-5 under the head" Welfare of Backward Classes" under the scheme head (2225F129) is Rs. The Government have accorded approval for disbursement and expenditure of Rs. 7800.00 lakhs (Rupees Eighty Seven Crores only) to the Controlling Officer and Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik.
The funds have been distributed on the budgetary fund distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious in the next period due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds in this distribution.
