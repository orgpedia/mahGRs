The Central Government provides grants under the Special Central Assistance to the States under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Adim Janjatiya Vikas Karyakram every year. Proposals for grants under this program. The report was submitted to the committee headed by the Chief Secretary. The proposal has been sent to the central government for approval. The proposal is mainly aimed at addressing the drinking water shortage in government ashram schools and hostels under the Tribal Development Department.
At present, there are 491 government ashram schools, 25 Eklavya residential ashram schools, 11 English medium ashram schools and 490 hostels in Maharashtra. The government ashram schools are mostly located in remote areas in tribal-dominated areas. The Tribal Development Department is responsible for providing basic and necessary facilities to the students of the Ashram School, such as providing abundant food, clean drinking water, and good shelter. Various schemes are being implemented by the department to provide water supply to ashramshalas. In some places, efforts have been made to solve the problem of drinking water by constructing coupons, wells, pipelines, while in some places temporary facilities have been made. In some ashram schools, there is a shortage of water, especially for 2-3 months of summer. They have to depend on other sources for drinking water and sometimes even on tanker water. Therefore, in order to create sustainable facilities in places where there is a shortage of drinking water, the department proposed a scheme titled" Providing drinking water facilities in Government Ashram Schools& Hostels facing drinking water scarcity."
