2. Before declaring a vulnerable wildlife habitat area (Melghat Wildlife Sanctuary), the said committee shall look into the following:
(i) whether or not the provisions of the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006, Rules 2008 and the amended Rules 2012 are being implemented; (ii) whether the scientific parameters are appropriate; (iii) whether co-existence of wildlife and humans in the village concerned is possible; (iv) whether or not they are being compensated fairly as per the Right to Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013 (LARR 2013); or (v) whether or not they are being compensated properly as per the provisions of the Act. 3 To examine whether or how the proceedings are carried out according to the guidelines here and to guide the concerned Gram Sabhas.
(vi) As per the provisions of Section 4 (2) (a) of the said Act, after filing of forest rights claims with the Gram Sabhas as specified in Section 6, it is necessary to verify the same, prepare a map specifying the area of each demand and then submit the said claims to the Sub-Divisional Committee along with the resolution of the Gram Sabha and the final decision on the said claims/ appeals is to be taken by the District Level Committee. Ensure that the process is carried out.
The term of the Committee shall be six months. The committee will submit its report to the government in this regard.
The expenditure for this purpose shall be met from the funds made available by the Commissioner, Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune for implementation of the Forest Rights Act, 2006.
