Decision No. 29.06.2019 and D. 23.07.2019 Preamble: The Central Government provides grants under the Special Central Assistance to the States under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme.
The Tribal Development Department had appealed to the Project Officer, AAV Project Office, Government, Non-Government/ Social/ Private reputed organizations to submit innovative proposals for the grant received under this scheme. A detailed review of the proposals received. The report was submitted to the committee headed by the Chief Secretary. The proposal includes a proposal from Tata Trusts. Under this scheme, the target is to reach the income level of lakhs by involving the tribal families in the villages of Dhadgaon and Akkalkuwa taluka of Nandurbar district involved in the project.
With a view to increase income through education, sanitation, nutrition, etc., the scheme was proposed to the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India, with the approval of the Executive Committee headed by the Chief Secretary.

Tata Trusts is one of the leading social sector organizations in India. Tata Trusts is working in various fields to improve the quality of life of the Indian society. CINL (Collective for Integrated Livelihoods Initiatives) has been set up by Tata Trusts to develop and take initiatives for sustainable programmes that are involved in creating programmes in thematic areas of agricultural productivity stabilization, forest based livelihoods, water resources development, drinking water and sanitation, education, empowerment and empowerment of minority communities. Since 2015, Tata Trusts has been implementing the Lakhpati Kisan and Smart Villages scheme in 18 tribal taluks in four states of Jharkhand, Orissa, Maharashtra and Gujarat.
