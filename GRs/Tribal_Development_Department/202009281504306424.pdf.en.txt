2) Ma. The Governor's Notification No. RB/ TC/ e-13011 (1) (2020)/ Appeal-FRA/ 100, Dt. As per the provisions of the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules, 2008 and Rules, 2012, the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers are entitled to get individual and collective forest rights or both as per Section 3 (1). These include the right of Scheduled Tribes or other traditional forest dwellers to own and inhabit forest land for cultivating it for their livelihood; rights such as the right to dispose of, collect, use or dispose of minor forest produce traditionally collected within or outside the boundaries of the village; right to access water, fish and other products, grazing, traditional seasonal resources; right to protect, restore, conserve, manage any social forest resource that has been traditionally protected and conserved for sustainable use.
These rights are very important in terms of food security and livelihood of the tribal community.
The government has issued guidelines for the implementation of these rules. Section 6 (5) of the said Act provides for the District Level Committee to take a final decision on forest rights records in all cases. However, there is no provision for appeal against the claims rejected by the district level committee. So Ma. The Governor, in exercise of the powers conferred by the Fifth Schedule to the Constitution of India, hereby makes the following amendments in section 6 of the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006, in reference No. 2 Amendments have been made by notification here. Accordingly, the Government was contemplating to constitute a Divisional Forest Rights Committee to take a final decision on the appeals against the decision of the District Forest Rights Committee constituted under the said Act, to those claimants whose individual or community forest rights claims in the Scheduled Areas have been rejected by the District Forest Rights Committee constituted under the said Act.

Government Decision: As per the notification issued by the Governor under sub-paragraph (1) of paragraph 5 of the Fifth Schedule to the Constitution of India for the Scheduled Areas mentioned in clause (1) of Article 244 of the Constitution of India, the Divisional Forest Rights Committee is being constituted under the Chairmanship of the Divisional Commissioner to take final decision on the appeals of forest rights claims in the Scheduled Areas rejected by the District Level Forest Rights Committee under the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules 2008 and the Amended Rules, 2012.

(2) The decision of the said Divisional Forest Rights Committee shall be final and binding on the claims rejected by the District Level Committee under Section 6 (a) of the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and Rules, 2008 and Rules, 2012 as amended.
3. District Level Forest Rights Committee to decide the claims of the claimants. Claimants who have been rejected before 18.05.2020, refer No. 2 is here. within 6 months from the date of notification dated 18.05.2020 and the claimants whose forest rights claims have been cleared by the District Level Forest Rights Committee. The claimants whose claims have been rejected after 18.05.2020, shall inform the District Forest Rights Committee to all concerned about filing an appeal to the Divisional Forest Rights Committee within 90 days from the date of receipt of the order of the District Forest Rights Committee. Also, the concerned Divisional Commissioner should take action on priority to file all the appeals of the claimants who want to file an appeal within the prescribed time.
4. In case the appellants find it difficult to file the appeal within the prescribed time due to the extraordinary situation arising out of the current outbreak of COVID-19, a self-explanatory proposal for extension of the prescribed time should be submitted to the Government by the concerned Divisional Commissioner through the Commissioner Tribal Research and Training Institute, Pune.
5. Filing of appeals in respect of claims rejected by the District Forest Rights Committee within the prescribed time and no eviction proceedings shall be taken until the final decision is taken by the Divisional Forest Rights Committee.
