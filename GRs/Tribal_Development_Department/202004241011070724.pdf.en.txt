Government's decision:
The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2020 (including the amounts specified in column 4 of the Schedule to the Maharashtra Appropriation Act, 2020) for the financial year 2020-21 has been approved by the Legislative Assembly and assented to by the Governor of Maharashtra and is in addition to the provisions of the Maharashtra Act No. 15 Therefore, the Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part-IV, Dt. 18.03.2020 has been published.
Accordingly, vide Circular No.1 of the Department of Finance dated 16.04.2020, considering the pandemic situation arising out of Corona virus in the State, it has been approved to disburse funds in the range of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary provisions of essential and programme expenditure for the financial year 2020-21. However, in the wake of COVID-19, it is necessary to support the beneficiaries under the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme and Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme together, so the funds for these schemes have been made available by the Finance Department for disbursement within the limit of 50 per cent of the total budgetary allocation on the Budget Fund Distribution System at present. Accordingly, under the Tribal Component Programme, Demand No. 2235-Social Security and Welfare in T-5, under the heading of Main Articles, Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, Read No. 2 Approval is being accorded to the Secretary, Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Mumbai as the Head of Administrative Department to disburse funds for the following schemes for the financial year 2020-21 on the Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) as proposed hereinabove.

The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious for the next 3 to 4 months due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds in this distribution.
It should be ensured that this fund is spent in this year as per the approved plan in accordance with the current government policy. Also, the report of the expenditure made from the provision made available, account-wise/ sub-account-wise as well as material objective-wise information, fund utilization certificate, the concerned field officer should be careful to send to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik; the concerned Additional Commissioner, Tribal Development; Tribal Development Department and Social Justice and Special Assistance Department by the 10th of every month.
The Head of the Department/ Controlling Officer shall ensure that the expenditure incurred on the schemes in the tribal sub-plan area, in the additional tribal sub-plan area and outside the tribal component programme area is recorded separately so that the Government can have control over the expenditure on the schemes in the tribal area and outside the sub-plan area. It should be ensured that only the beneficiaries belonging to the Scheduled Tribes are given the benefit of the provision made available under the Tribal Component Programme.
4. The head of the department/ controlling officer should immediately distribute the distributed provisions to the drawing and disbursing officers under him. Also, while spending the funds distributed under this government decision, the head of the department and the controlling officers will have to follow the financial rules in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook, Fiscal Rights Rulebook, as well as in the context of the Finance Department. Strictly follow the instructions given in the Government Circular dated 16.04.2020.
5. The decision of the Government shall be subject to the approval of the Finance Department. The powers conferred on the Administrative Department by the Government Circular dated 16.04.2020 and the Department of Finance. Issued pursuant to the Consent received vide Note dated 23.04.2020.
