Government's decision
The Finance Department vide Circular dated 16.04.2020 had approved the release of funds in the range of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary provisions of essential and programme expenditure for the first 3 months of the financial year 2020-21 (ending June, 2020), considering the pandemic situation in the State. Accordingly, the Department of Finance, as provided on the Budget Fund Distribution System, vide Reference No. As per the decision of the Government here, 25% of the funds have been disbursed to the concerned Controlling Officer for the 1st salary and 36% of the Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) to the concerned districts included in the Demand Number T-9 under the District Annual Tribal Component Programme.
At present, the Department of Finance has made available funds for disbursement under the Budgetary Allocation Mechanism (BEAMS) for the purpose of 01- Salary and 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) within the limit of 35% of the total budgetary allocation. In view of this, the Government is approving the release and expenditure of the remaining 10% of the 35% limit available for disbursement of 01-Salary and 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) to the districts covered under Demand No. T-9 mentioned in the accompanying statement to the concerned Controlling Authorities.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious for some time to come due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should plan the precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds. This fund should be used for regular payment of salaries and not for payment of any pending arrears.
