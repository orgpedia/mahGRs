Grants under Special Central Assistance from the Central Government, under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme are provided to the State Governments. The Tribal Development Department had appealed to the Project Officer, AAV Project Office, Non-Government/ Social/ Private Organizations to submit proposals for the grant received under this scheme. Detailed screening and selection process of the proposal received in this regard was carried out under the chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Tribal Development, through the Innovative Planning and Monitoring Cell, One Forbes, No. It was held at 1, Dr. V.B. Gandhi Marg, Fort, Mumbai.
The proposal has been received from Devrai Art Village. The proposal is mainly to train 50 youth of Madia Gond tribe under the Bhamragad project office in Gadchiroli district in Dhokra craft, wood craft, bamboo craft and jewellery making of tribal people to make them self-reliant. The Madia Gonds, a primitive tribe, are mainly found under the Bhamragad project in Gadchiroli district. Training of these people in trades related to their area will create employment avenues and help them connect with other mainstream sections of the society. With this vision, Devrai Art Village had submitted a proposal worth Rs. 45.16 lakhs to the Government for implementation of" Devrai Art Village- Training at Bhamragad" at Bhamragad in Gadchiroli district.
2 The proposal of Devrai Art Village- Training at Bhamragad submitted by Devrai Art Village for training 50 youth of Madia Gond tribe under Bhamragad Project Office in Gadchiroli district on Dhokra craft, wood craft, bamboo craft and tribal jewellery making to make them self-reliant was approved by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs in the Project Appraisal (Committee PAC) meeting held on 12.02.2019. 1 has been approved. The funds received from the Central Government have been distributed to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik as per the decision of the Government in question.

Devrai Art Village is an organisation that works for tribal art, culture and livelihood. The institute primarily deals with training in handicrafts, marketing of finished goods, and making of craftsmen. At present, they have 20 expert artisans in Dhokra craft, Bamboo craft, Iron craft, Wood craft. He has created and patented Rock Dhokra sculpture and sculpture made of grass and rock. Devrai Art Village currently has students from reputed institutes in the country like Shrishti School of Design, Bangalore, Indian Institute of Craft and Design, Jaipur, National Institute of Design, among others. Therefore, a proposal was under consideration of the government to approve the guidelines for the implementation of the scheme" Devrai Art Village-Training at Bhamragad" under the Adim Janjati Development Program through an organization with long experience in this field.
The government has decided to train 50 youths of the Madia Gond tribe under the Bhamragad Project Office in Gadchiroli district in Dhokra craft, wood craft, bamboo craft and tribal jewellery making and to make them self-reliant," Devrai Art Village-Training at Bhamragad" will be implemented jointly by the Devrai Art Village and the Tribal Development Department. 45.16 lakh is being approved as per Annexure No. 1 annexed to this decision.
