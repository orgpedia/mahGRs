Central- 2019/ S.No.63/ K. 19, dated 29/06/2019 and Dt. 23/07/2019 Preamble Grants under Special Central Assistance are provided by the Central Government to the State Governments every year under Article 275 (1) of the Constitution of India and under the scheme of Tribal Development Programme.
The Tribal Development Department had appealed to the Project Officer, AAV Project Office, Government, Non-Government/ Social/ Private Organizations to submit innovative proposals for the grant received under this scheme. A detailed review of the proposals received. The report was submitted to the committee headed by the Chief Secretary. The proposal was received from the Art India Foundation.
The proposal is mainly to provide drawing and painting training to the students of government ashram schools in the state, guide them in this regard, hold exhibitions of their art, create web pages and organize competitions for the selected students, and prove their creative potential by tapping the latent art qualities in the students and prepare them to face the competitive challenge.
At present, there are 491 government ashram schools, 25 Eklavya Model Residential Schools and 11 English medium schools in the state. The Tribal Development Department is fully responsible for the health and nutrition of the students studying in all these schools. The students studying in the ashramshalas have different art qualities and these students have always been doing proud in different fields. Mission Valiant is an example of this. Apart from enhancing the quality of education, the students of Ashram School can also earn laurels in various fields if they are trained on the necessary aspects in the field of art skills and build their capacity.

4. Art India Foundation- Students of Government Ashram Schools get benefit of a vibrant platform for creative expression. A proposal worth Rs 210.00 lakh was submitted to the government.
3 With a view to imparting drawing and painting training and guidance to the students of Government Ashramshalas, Eklavya Model Residential Schools and English Medium Schools in the State, to hold exhibitions of their art works, to create web pages and organize competitions for the selected students and to prove their creative potential by tapping the latent art qualities in the students and to prepare them to face the competitive challenge, the Tribal Development Department, with the approval of the Executive Committee and the Government, proposed the scheme" Art India Foundation- Students of Government Ashram Schools get benefit of a vibrant platform for creative expression." This proposal has been approved by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs in the Project Appraisal Committee (PAC) meeting held on 12.02.2019 under Letter of Reference (LR) for a total amount of Rs. 210.00 lakh for the implementation of the scheme for the year 2019-20 and 2020-21. The funds have been distributed to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik, as per the decision of the government in question.
Art India Foundation is a public charitable organization. The organisation is primarily concerned with social and educational issues. The company operates in various parts of India. Khula Aasmaan is an initiative by the Art India Foundation. Through the medium of open sky, students are given training in painting, drawing and also to hold an exhibition of their paintings and hold competitions among them.
The open-air activities were aimed at creating a web page for the students and preparing them for the competitive environment by inculcating the art qualities in the students. Students from 500 villages and cities of India have participated through Open Sky. In 2017, Open Skies was awarded as a social innovation project at the National Conference on Social Innovation, organized by the National Innovation Foundation Ahmedabad and other institutions in Pune. The Tribal Development Department has worked on a sponsorship basis with Art India Foundation in 6 Ashram Schools in the area of the work of the Upper Commissioner Thane in the financial year 2018-19. It is proposed to approve the guidelines for implementation of the scheme" Art India Foundation- Students of Government Ashram Schools get benefit of a vibrant platform for creative expression."
The government has decided to provide training to the students in painting, drawing and to hold an exhibition and competition among them. To create a web page for the students and to prepare them for the competitive environment by giving exposure to the art qualities in the students.
