Preamble: - The Government was considering implementing the Maharashtra Resilience Development Programme (MRDP) project with the help of the World Bank to build capacity for future disasters in the Krishna and Bhima river basins of Kolhapur and Sangli districts and to strengthen flood and other risk mitigation schemes and flood control management, institutional capacity building to mitigate the effects of climate change, planning for drought and flood management, and making informed decisions based on science.
The Government's Decisions:
Government approval is being accorded for implementing World Bank assisted projects for capacity building and mitigation of floods and other hazards for future disaster mitigation in Krishna and Bhima river basins of Kolhapur and Sangli districts. The outline of the project is as follows. : -
9) Risk-Aware Water Resource Management.
(a) Integrated dynamic decision support system with forecasting mechanism for reservoir operation.
(b) Strengthening of State Emergency Operations Centres and District Emergency Operations Centres as well as early warning and hydromet systems.
2) Flood control investments.
(a) Measures to be taken in Kolhapur B. Sangli to avoid catastrophic situation due to floods e.g. Drainage and removal of impediments in the flow of water to increase the flow of the river.
(b) Introducing innovative solutions such as Sponge City.
(a) Nature-based multi-purpose grounds / water bodies.
3) Institutional capacity building.
(a) Establishment of Knowledge Lighthouse / Centre for Excellence for Risk-Aware Water Resources Management (b) Strategic Guidelines for Dam Water Resources Management.
4) The analysis.
(a) To study the feasibility of diverting water and utilization of water through flood control with Krishna Bhima Water Transfer Project.
