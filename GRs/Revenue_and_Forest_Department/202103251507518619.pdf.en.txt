Order: Whereas, the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and Central Station House Officer, State of Maharashtra, Nagpur, vide order received from the Deputy Conservator of Forests, Sangli, under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, has decided to take over the management of the Mauje-Dicholi Wildlife Sanctuary, Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary, Dist. Patan, Satara district. A proposal for diversion of 20.00 hectares of reserve forest area for rehabilitation in Sangli was submitted to the State Government vide letter No. 1 above;

- The final approval for the diversion has been given vide letter No. 6 hereunder; and whereas, the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and Central Station Officer, State of Maharashtra, Nagpur vide his letter No. 7 above has informed that the project machinery has fulfilled the conditions laid down in the final approval letter dated 16/07/2020 of the Central Government; therefore, the Government of Maharashtra hereby directs that the Project Authority i.e. Patan, Satara district. A total of 20.00 hectares of forest land which is included in the Schedule below and mentioned in the Project Proposal attached with the letter of Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forest and Central Officer, Maharashtra State, Nagpur for resettlement at Khanapur, District Sangli, should be diverted for this proposal. As stated in the Government of India Order dated 16/07/2020, the demarcated area will not have the status of" Forest." This diversion should be completed subject to fulfilment of the terms and conditions as contained in the letter dated 16/07/2020 of the Government of India and also after fulfilment of the following conditions.
The Project Mechanism shall comply with all the conditions prescribed in the final approval letter of the Government of India mentioned above. Non-compliance with the provisions of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 and the rules and guidelines made thereunder and any of the conditions contained in this Order shall be a violation of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 and legal action shall be taken accordingly.
