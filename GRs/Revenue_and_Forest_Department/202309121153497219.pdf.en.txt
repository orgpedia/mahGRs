Introduction: The rates and criteria for providing assistance to disaster victims affected by natural disasters were fixed for the period from 2015 to 2020 under the Government Decision No. 3. Reference No. 5 here. The Government Decision dated 27.3.2023 has been amended.
The central government has listed natural calamities as cyclone, drought, earthquake, fire, flood, tsunami, hailstorm, landslide, snow avalanche, cloudburst, locust swarm, cold wave and severe cold wave. The state government has declared untimely rain, excessive rain (including damage to crops), accidental fire, sea surge, lightning, incessant rain as calamities and the norms and rates of assistance have been implemented for these disasters. Apart from this, the rates and criteria of increased assistance in the event of natural disasters from time to time are implemented by the state government. In order to provide immediate relief to the victims of natural calamities, the Permanent Cabinet Sub-Committee has been constituted under the Government Decision dated 13th June, 2018 in Reference No. 4 to take a decision on such proposals. The Cabinet Sub-Committee under the Government Resolution No. 4 dated 5th December, 2018 to take immediate measures/ control/ take decisions for the drought period including the drought period of 2018, if drought occurs in the future.
