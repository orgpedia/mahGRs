Preamble: - Mr. Abhay Mhaske, Tehsildar was suspended from government service by order dated 31.1.2020 of the District Collector, Osmanabad for misconduct in office while serving as Tehsildar (SC) G.K. Dharashiv and District Administration Officer, Municipal Administration, Dharashiv. Thereafter, in exercise of the powers conferred by Rule 4 (5) (a) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979, as recommended by the Suspension Review Committee, Mr. Abhay Mhaske, Tehsildar, has been terminated and reinstated in the Government service subject to the decision of the Departmental Inquiry against him, under the relevant Government Order. Presently, Mr. Abhay Mhaske was under consideration of the Government for posting as he was awaiting appointment and the vacancies needed to be filled in line with the forthcoming Assembly elections.
Government Orders: -
In accordance with the provisions prescribed by the Election Commission of India vide letter dated 31.7.2024 as well as the provisions of Section 4 (4) b4 (5) of the Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Employees and Prevention of Delay in discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005, Mr. Abhay Mhaske, Tehsildar is being posted as Tehsildar Mahagaon, G. Yavatmal in the vacant post with the approval of the competent authority in the interest of public service and administrative reasons.
2. The aforesaid order comes into force with immediate effect and the aforesaid Mr. Abhay Mhaske, Tehsildar, should join the post of the incumbent immediately.
The officer concerned shall take care of joining the place of posting within the prescribed period as per the rules of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of assumption of office, Non-Service and Provisions during periods of suspension, dismissal or removal from service) Rules, 1981. Otherwise, the period of their absence is a "non-working day" (8/25) if they do not join the office of the incumbent. 0.) shall be deemed to be so, the concerned shall note.
4. Leave shall not be granted to the officer concerned for any reason except with the prior permission of the Government. Also, as per Rule-30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, no leave applications of officers transferred by the original office shall be entertained. If such applications are received, they should be returned by post to the concerned authorities at the original address.
