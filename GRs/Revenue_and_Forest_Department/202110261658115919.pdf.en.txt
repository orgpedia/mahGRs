Preamble: Proposals were sought from all the Divisional Commissioners seeking funds at the rate of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) for the damage caused to public property/ agriculture crops due to heavy rainfall and flood situation in various districts of the State during August-September, 2021. In the intervening period. As per the decision taken in the Cabinet meeting held on 13.10.2021, orders have been issued to provide assistance to the affected farmers for the damage caused by heavy rains from June to October, 2021 at an increased rate as per the decision of the Government at Reference No. 3. Proposals for funds required to provide assistance to the affected farmers for the loss of agricultural crops due to heavy rains from August to September, 2021 as per the rates prescribed under the Government Decision at Reference No. 1 vide letter No. 4 to 7 from the Divisional Commissioner

Government's decision:
The State Disaster Response Fund and State Government funds have been released to provide assistance to the affected farmers for the loss of their crops due to heavy rains from August to September, 2021. 3 The Government hereby approve to disburse a total amount of Rs. 286084.07 lakh (Rupees Two Thousand Eight Hundred Sixty Four Lakhs Eighty Four Lakh Seven Thousand only) to the districts through the Divisional Commissioner as shown district wise in the annexure attached to this Government decision, which is at present 75% of the required funds as per the enhanced rates fixed by the Government.
(2) As per the annexure attached to this Government Resolution, the Account-wise funds should be distributed immediately by Office-M-11 to the Divisional Commissioner on the BEAMS system.
(3) The instructions and norms contained in all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The enhanced rates of assistance have been approved under the Government decision dated 02.10.2021 at Reference No. 2. Out of the increased rate, the amount disbursed under the head of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be spent for providing assistance at the rate decided by the government at State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) reference number 1 and 3 and the difference between the increased sanctioned rate minus the rate of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be shared with the fund disbursed from the State Government's fund.
The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned.
4. Beneficiaries to be determined after conducting the panchnama. Although the entire amount is being disbursed on the BeAMS system under this government decision, after the beneficiary is identified, the concerned collector should be careful from the point of view of financial discipline to draw the amount from the treasury as per the actual need and then transfer the amount directly to the bank account of the beneficiaries through online mode. This money should not be withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account. Expenditure should be made within the limits of the grant sanctioned under this order. After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
