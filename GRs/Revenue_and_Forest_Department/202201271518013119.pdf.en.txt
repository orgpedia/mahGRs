Maharashtra Rehabilitation of Project Affected Persons Act, 1976.
The Maharashtra Rehabilitation of Project Affected Persons Act, 1999
the prelude
The existing Government decisions, Rehabilitation Act 1976, 1986, 1999 provide for allotment of alternative land to eligible project affected farmers affected by irrigation projects.
As per the provisions of section 10 (1) of the Rehabilitation Act, 1999, if sufficient land is available for rehabilitation of persons affected by an irrigation project, they are rehabilitated on the land or areas of the village benefiting from such project. However, some of the affected farmers have expressed reservations about the allotment of alternative land after a long period of 10 to 30 years. The High Court has issued notices to the affected farmers under Section 16 (2) of the Rehabilitation Act, 1999, in connection with various petitions. As per the order passed by the Hon'ble High Court, Section 16 (2) of the Rehabilitation Act, 1999, is meant to allot alternative land to the affected farmers after they deposit the possession amount with the Government by giving notice. Against the High Court order.
The government has decided to file a special leave petition in the Supreme Court.
According to Against the High Court order. When the special leave petition is pending in the Supreme Court, the affected farmer's transaction of purchase, sale, division, mortgage, transfer, etc. of the alternative land allotted to him will create legal complications and the government will have to pay a fine.

Subject to the final decision of the Special Leave Petition pending in the Supreme Court, the matter of recording of claims for allotment of alternative land was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
In order to assess the eligibility of eligible affected farmers as per the Maharashtra Rehabilitation of Project Affected Persons Act, 1976, 1986 and the prevailing Government Policy/ Decisions in 1999 and earlier, they are required to deposit 65/75 percent of the amount towards possible possession of alternative land with the Government at the time of receiving the compensation amount for the acquired land for allotment of alternative lands. However, some farmers have not deposited the amount of possible possession and are now demanding alternative lands from them. Several petitions were filed in the High Court. The court directed the government to issue a notice to the affected farmers under Section 16 (2) of the Rehabilitation Act, 1999, and deposit the possession amount with the government after receiving the notice.
