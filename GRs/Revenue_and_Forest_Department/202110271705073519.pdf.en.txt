Orders have been issued to provide assistance to the affected farmers at an increased rate for the damage caused by heavy rains under the government decision in reference No. 3. Proposals for the funds required to provide assistance to the affected farmers for the damage caused by excessive rains from August to September, 2021, as per the rates prescribed under the Government Decision at Reference No. 1, as per the report received from the Divisional Commissioners, Konkan, Aurangabad, Nashik and Nagpur. 75% of the funds have been disbursed to Turta under the 4th Government Decision. In the meantime, letters have been received from Divisional Commissioner, Pune, Amravati and Divisional Commissioner, Nashik to the District Collector, Nashik, Jalgaon and Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur to Godagari District vide reference No. 5 to 8.
Accordingly, the government was considering the matter of distributing funds for the distribution of relief to the affected farmers.
Government's decision:
The State Disaster Response Fund and State Government funds have been released to provide assistance to the affected farmers for the loss of their crops due to heavy rains from August to September, 2021. 3 The Government hereby approve to disburse a total amount of Rs. 77415.43 lakhs (Rupees Seven Hundred and Seventy Four Crores Fifteen Lakhs Thirty Three Thousand only) to the districts through the Divisional Commissioner as shown district wise in the annexure attached to this Government decision, which is 75% of the required funds as per the enhanced rates fixed under the Government decision.
(2) As per the annexure attached to this Government Resolution, the Account-wise funds should be distributed immediately by Office-M-11 to the Divisional Commissioner on the BEAMS system.
(3) The instructions and norms contained in all the Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The enhanced rates of assistance have been approved under the Government decision dated 21.10.2021 at Reference No. 2. Out of the increased rate, the amount disbursed under the head of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be spent for providing assistance at the rate decided by the government at State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) reference number 1 and 3 and the difference between the increased sanctioned rate minus the rate of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be shared with the fund disbursed from the State Government's fund. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned.
4. Beneficiaries to be determined after conducting the panchnama. All the district collectors should ensure that there is no need for assistance for crop loss in the same area for the current Kharif season. Under this Government decision, the entire amount is being distributed on the BEAMS system

However, after the beneficiary is identified, the concerned collector should take care of the financial discipline by withdrawing the amount from the treasury as per the actual need and then transferring the amount directly to the bank account of the beneficiaries through online mode. This money should not be withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account. Expenditure should be made within the limits of the grant sanctioned under this order. After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
(5) The accounts of the expenditure made out of the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure made shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. The Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund from the concerned and submitting it to the government as soon as the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund and the State Government Fund are spent.
For the above purpose, the Principal Account is being provided from the funds made available by way of re-appropriation under heading 2245-Finance for Relief of Natural Calamities, 02 Floods, Cyclones, etc. as shown in the accompanying statement.
This Government Resolution is being issued with the concurrence received under the Informal Reference No: 427/ 2021/ Expenditure-9, dated 27.10.2021 of the Department of Finance.
