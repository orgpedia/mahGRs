Orders: - Is the stamp duty required by the Act paid by the government through the e-payment system GRAS for the documents that are issued in connection with the work of the government departments, local bodies and banks in the state? These government departments, local bodies and banks have been entrusted with the responsibility of ensuring this under Section 10D of the Maharashtra Stamp Act. A notification dated 3rd June 2016 has been issued specifying which departments / institutions will have this obligation applicable to which documents.
Accordingly, Reference No. 2 National e-Governance Services Ltd., under a government order, to implement stamp duty under Section 10D of the Maharashtra Stamp Act and to incorporate more and more banks and financial institutions into the system. The Inspector General of Registration and Controller of Stamps, Maharashtra State, Pune was authorised by the Government to sign a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the company (NeSL) for a period of 3 years with effect from December 1, 2020. The duration of the agreement. Expires on 26.10.2024.
National e-Governance Services Ltd. (NeSL) aims to establish a financial system in which data and documents of loans given by the borrower will be available to facilitate loan portfolio, intra-external TST, and centres for down-selling. NeSL has created the Digital Document USAR (DDE) platform under the regulation of IBBI. Working on NeSL's system makes both e-sign and e-stop procedures easier and faster, making the process and transaction less cumbersome. Under Section LoS of the Maharashtra Stamp Act, stamp duty is waived as all banks are available on a single platform through NeSL while charging stamp duty.
