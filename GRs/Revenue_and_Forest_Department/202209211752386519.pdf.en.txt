Order:
In exercise of powers under general approval given by MoEF&CC, Govt. of India
vide Handbook of FCA Guidelines-2019, Chapter 4 (para-4.2), Government of Maharashtra
is pleased to grant 'In-principle' approval to the diversion of 1.803 ha. Forest Land under
section-2 of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 for Trenching and laying of underground
Optical Fiber Cable passing within RoW of existing road NH-130D on Kandoli to Laheri in
Tal. Bhamragad, Dist. Gadchiroli.

2. This 'In-principle' approval is issued subject to the following conditions
1) The legal status of the land shall remain unchanged.
2) User Agency shall submit the Record of Rights of proposed land for diversion along
with Stage-I compliance.
3) The Optical Fiber Cable Line will be laid within maximum size of the trench of 1.65
meters depth & 0.5 meter width with cable line under the OFC pipeline.
4) The land under project will be reclaimed by the User Agency for which the agency
shall submit a bank guarantee at the rate of Rs.50/- per running meter and an
undertaking to that effect shall also be submitted. If the agency fails to reclaim the

land to the satisfaction of concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests, the cost for reclamation will be covered from the bank guarantee.
5) If the cable is going to be laid in the formation width of the road, then additional
charges in form of bank guarantee shall be given at the rate of Rs.35/- per running
meter. Similarly for reclamation of side drain and for tarred portion, the guarantee
will be at the rate of Rs.300/- per meter and Rs.750/- per meter respectively. An undertaking shall be obtained from the User Agency that it has agreed to make good
any loss to Forest/ Environment while laying the cable in the forest area and during maintenance of the same in future.
6) For further maintenance of the project, the agency shall obtain permission from the
concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests as and when required.
7) The forest land shall not be used for any purpose other than specified in the proposal.
8) No felling of trees shall be done during the implementation of project.
9) No new forest area beyond the Right of Way of existing road should be broken for laying Optical Fiber Cable.
10) The User Agency shall pay the cost of Net Present Value, if required in future.
11) Underground Optical Fiber Cable can be laid only outside the confines of the Protected Areas.
12) No labour camp shall be established on the forest land.
13) After completion of the project, User Agency will reclaim the area under RoW
suitably.
14) The layout plan of the proposal shall not be changed without prior approval of the State Government.
15) This sanction is subject to the final clearance by the Department of Information &
Technology for the project and concerned PWD authorities for the project.
16) The User Agency shall compensate the private owners of forest land if any, in
accordance with the prevalent laws, rules & guidelines.
17) The User Agency shall obtain Environmental Clearance as per the provisions of the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, if applicable.
18) All other conditions as may be mandatory under relevant Acts, Rules and Guidelines shall be complied by the User Agency.
19) All the conditions stipulated by the Forest Department shall be binding on User
Agency.
20) The User Agency shall make good of any loss to Forest / Environment.
21) In case, the proposed area falls in the RoW of the road passing through National Parks
and Wildlife Sanctuaries, prior permission of the State Board for Wildlife should be obtained.
22) All necessary approvals required for the project as per the prevailing laws of the land would be obtained by User Agency.
23) Any breach of the conditions by the User Agency will be treated as violation of the
provisions of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980.
