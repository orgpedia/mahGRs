GOVERNMENT DECISION: As the Government has declared drought in Kharif season 2018, Demand No. C-6; Head Accounts Head 2245, Assistance for Relief of Natural Calamities; 01, Avarakshana; 101, Anugraha Sahay; (91) Expenditure as per the norms of State Disaster Response Fund; (91) (03) Expenditure on supply of fodder, water, medicines to animal camps and transportation of fodder to camps (mandatory); 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) (2245 0075) Rs. 51,88,217/- and Demand No. 2245, Assistance for Relief of Natural Calamities; 01, Avarakshana; 101, Anugraha Sahay; (92) Expenditure over and above the State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) norms; (92) (07) Fodder supply (mandatory); 31, Subsidiary Grants (non-salaried) (2245 2149) Rs. 25,36,536/- aggregating to Rs. 77,24,753/- is being disbursed to Divisional Commissioner, Pune through Budget Distribution System (BEAMS).
2.The said fund should be immediately distributed by the Divisional Commissioner, Pune to the District Collector, Satara.
The funds will be disbursed directly to the Cantonment Operators through Electronic Clearing Services without keeping the disbursed funds in their Personal Ledger Account or Bank Account, subject to the condition that the funds are being disbursed, the Collector, Satara should take action accordingly. After receipt of the said funds, the payment of the fodder camp payment should be completed expeditiously, and care should be taken to take action as per the terms, conditions and instructions in the context of the fodder camp.
The funds are being allocated to the Divisional Commissioner, Pune on a budgetary distribution system.
The Divisional Commissioner, Pune should ensure that the funds are being used for the purpose for which they have been transferred. Also, the accounts, records/ records of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the grantee as per the prescribed procedure.
Also, the expenditure incurred should be matched with the expenditure recorded in the Treasury Office and the Accountant General's Office and the utility certificate should be submitted to this department and the Accountant General's Office immediately. Also, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned limit. If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the prescribed time.
5. Controlling authorities to issue a copy of the grant. It should be submitted to the Principal Secretary (Disaster Management, Relief and Rehabilitation) without fail. The Controller may kindly direct the concerned officers to ensure that the computer notation of the headings/ subheadings should be mentioned in the computer note to be attached with the bill to be sent to the Treasury/ Sub-Treasury. Also, the report of the amount drawn from the treasury/ sub-treasury should be submitted to the government.
