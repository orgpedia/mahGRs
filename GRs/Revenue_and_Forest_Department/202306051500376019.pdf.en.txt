The Proposal:
The state has been witnessing heavy rainfall and hailstorms in 2021 and 2022. Accordingly, funds have been disbursed from time to time in accordance with the proposals received from the Divisional Commissioner. However, it was observed that due to the outbreak of COVID-19 as well as some other reasons, some of the proposals seeking funds were not acted upon. Therefore, proposals for assistance to farmers and other losses were directed to be sent to the government. All the Divisional Commissioners, under reference No. The proposals were received through letters 2 to 25. This proposal. The State Executive Committee headed by Chief Secretary. The decision was taken at a meeting held on March 31, 2023. As per the decision taken in the meeting, the government was considering to distribute funds to help the victims.
Government Decision: To disburse assistance to farmers affected by hailstorm and untimely rains during the year 2021 and 2022, for the loss of crops and perennial crops and for the loss of property, a total of Rs. 40170.70

The following precautions shall be taken while allocating assistance for crop and perennial crop loss: (i) As per the extant rules, assistance for crop/ perennial crop loss shall be admissible to those who have suffered loss of 33% or more.
ii) The Tahsildar should immediately fill the information on the computer system in the prescribed format for the distribution of assistance to the affected farmers according to the panchnama completed with the joint signature of the agricultural assistant, talathi and gram sevak as per the prevailing system.
The amount will be directly credited to the bank accounts of the affected farmers.
(iv) No assistance in cash or kind shall be provided to any affected farmer.
(v) The Co-operation Department shall issue appropriate orders so that no recovery is made by any bank from the amount of assistance while crediting the amount directly to the bank account of the account holders.
3. The funds shall be disbursed by the Office of M-11 as indicated in the form attached to this Government Resolution, account-wise and district-wise. All the concerned District Collectors have mentioned above at serial number 27. As notified by 24th January, 2023, the details of all the beneficiaries of the crop loss under this proposal should be prepared in the prescribed format and filled on the computer system. It should be ensured that there is no duplication of information and the norms of the State Disaster Response Fund are followed.
4. While spending the above funds, the instructions and norms of all the relevant government decisions should be strictly followed. Considering the prevailing rates of assistance from the State Disaster Response Fund, the funds disbursed under the head State Disaster Response Fund should be spent for providing assistance at this rate. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned. While providing this assistance, all concerned should ensure that the conditions and conditions prescribed by the Central Government for natural calamities such as cyclones, floods, etc. are met.
5. Similarly, since one-time assistance is permissible for a season, all concerned should ensure that there is no leakage while preparing the information of beneficiaries. It should also be ensured that the assistance is given as per the norms of the State Disaster Response Fund. For this, all the Tahsildars should submit the necessary certificates to the Collector. After this, the information of the beneficiaries should be uploaded on the computer system.
After the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries is completed, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
