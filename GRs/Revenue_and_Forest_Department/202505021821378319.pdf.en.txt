The Proposal: -
The Sand / Sand Export Policy-2025 has been issued vide Government Decision dated 08.04.2025. It details some provisions for the local use of sand and other uses.
Considering the difficulties faced in the supply of sand to the household beneficiaries, the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (GoI), Government of India, has issued guidelines for sand mining from the sand sites within the Gram Panchayat limits. It was under the consideration of the Government to issue a clear order for making available the sand from the sand blocks identified as per the provision of serial number 4 in APPENDIX-IX of the notification dated 28th March, 2020 as well as from the eco-sanctioned baby clusters which have not gone to the auction, for domestic use by the beneficiaries and other local users.
The Government's Decisions:
1) Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India. As per the provision of serial number 4 in APPENDIX-IX, notified on 28th March, 2020, a maximum of 5 brasss should be made available to eligible beneficiaries under various housing schemes of the Government from fixed sand groups as well as from eco-sanctioned baby groups which have not gone to auction. Also, sand seized by the government in cases of illegal quarrying and transportation as well as for any other reason should also be made available to various household beneficiaries without charging any ownership fee. Tehsildar for maintenance up to a maximum of 5 brass without charge of ownership to the beneficiaries after receiving the list of eligible beneficiaries under various housing schemes of the government from the Block Development Officer.
They should provide online passes by mentioning the nearest sand group.
For this, the concerned beneficiary need not apply to the Tehsildar.
The online pass provided by the Tehsildar should be downloaded and delivered to the household beneficiaries within 15 days through the concerned Village Revenue Officer and Gram Sevak / Village Development Officer and a proper record should be maintained. The beneficiaries will be responsible for lifting the sand within 1 month from the date of issuance of the pass by the Tehsildar to the household beneficiaries. This pass will be automatically cancelled if the sand is not picked up during this period. There are some technicalities to making the pass available online.
