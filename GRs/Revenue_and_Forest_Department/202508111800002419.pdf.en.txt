Order :
Whereas, the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests & Nodal Officer,
Maharashtra State, Nagpur, vide his letter under reference No.1 has submitted a proposal
to the State Government, which was received from M/s Reliance Infocomm Private Ltd,
Nagpur for laying of underground Optical Fiber Cable along the existing RoW from
Pathari-Deosarra in Taluka Tumsar Dist. Bhandara
AND whereas, Government of Maharashtra after due consideration, vide its order
under reference No. 2 accorded 'in-principle' approval with certain conditions to above
proposal for diversion of 0.3094 ha. Forest land under section 2 of Van (Sanrakshan Evam
Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980;
AND whereas, the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests & Nodal
Officer, Maharashtra State, Nagpur, vide letters under reference No.3 has submitted
compliance of those conditions to the Government of Maharashtra;
NOW, therefore, in exercise of powers conferred to State Government under general approval given vide MoOEF&CC, Government of India's Handbook of Guidelines
for effective and transparent implementation of the provisions of Van (Sanrakshan Evam
Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980. Government of Maharashtra is pleased to grant Final
Approval for Diversion of 0.3094 ha. Forest Land.
Therefore, the Government of Maharashtra directs that 0.3094 ha. Forest Land
consisting of Zudpi Jungle, Bade Jhar ka Jungel, Reserved Forest, Protected Forest
specifically described in the proposal submitted vide letter under reference No.1 and
mentioned in the Schedule given below, be diverted to the User Agency, 1.e. M/s Reliance
Infocomm Private Ltd, Nagpur for Diversion of 0.3094 ha Forest Land for laying of
Underground optical fibre cable along the existing RoW from Pathari-Deosarra in
Taluka Tumsar Dist. Bhandara.

This approval for diversion of forest land is also subject to compliance of following
conditions by the User Agency-
DCF, Bhandara shall ensure compliance of the condition no. 15 in 'in-principle'
order dated 14.06.2024, before handover actual possession to the User Agency
2. The diversion shall be effected subject to the terms and conditions mentioned in the Order dated 14.06.2024.
3. For ensuring the compliances and for monitoring the duly prescribed activities and also to carry out other statuary duties, user agency shall facilitate unhindered access/entry in the area to the local forest Officer. In case the user agency prohibits the free access or create hindrances, the permission given under this Order for the diversion of forest area for the non-forest use shall be withdrawn.
4. The Chief Conservator of Forests / Deputy Conservator of Forests will reconfirm that compliances under the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 and Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 are not needed for the project.
5. The User Agency shall comply with all the conditions stipulated in the above mentioned 'in-principle' and in this order of Government of Maharashtra. The Chief
Conservator of Forests (Territorial), Nagpur shall ensure the strict compliance of the conditions by the User Agency. CCF will furnish the report in this regard to Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and Nodal Officer, Maharashtra State, Nagpur from time to time.
Non-compliance of any of the conditions mentioned in Government of Maharashtra's order dated 14.06.2024, in this order and rules and guidelines made
/ issued under Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980 shall amount to violation of Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980 and action would be taken accordingly.
7. In case of non-compliance of any of the above conditions, the concerned Chief Conservator of Forests / Deputy Conservator of Forests shall report through the State Government to Deputy Director General of Forests (Central), Regional Office, Ministry of Environment, Forests & Climate Change, Government of India, Nagpur as per procedure laid down in the guidelines under the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980.
