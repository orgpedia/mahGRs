Preamble- The National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project is being implemented in the coastal districts of the State. For this, an agreement has been signed between the Central Government, World Bank and the Government of Maharashtra on 11.08.2015. National Disaster Management Authority, Government of India. As per the order dated 27.11.2020, for the year 2020-21, Rs. 4 crore had been released to the State. The money has been deposited in the state treasury.
As per the guidelines of the Central Government as well as the National Disaster Management Authority and the Financial and Operational Manual of the World Bank, the funds received by the State under Component D of the project from the Central Government are required to be deposited in a separate bank account opened for the National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project. Since the Finance Department had approved the release of funds received from the Central Government and deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the State, the matter was under the consideration of the Government to draw the funds from the treasury and deposit them in the bank account opened for the National Cyclone Risk Reduction Project.
Government Decision- The Government of India is approving the withdrawal of funds to the tune of Rs. 4.00 crore (Rs. Four Crore only) received by the State for the year 2020-2021 for Component D under National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project and deposit the same in the bank account opened for National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project.
Shri Arjun Palav, Accounts Officer and Smt. Rajashree Raut, Financial Advisor and Joint Secretary are being declared as Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Controlling Officer respectively to feed the fund.
