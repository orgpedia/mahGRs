Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests, MS, Nagpur has submitted proposal
for diversion of 1.007 ha. Forest land in favour of M/s. Telesonic Networks Limited, Nagpur for laying of underground OFC duct and Optical Fiber Cable passing within Right of Way of
existing SH-139 & 140 (Satara-Medha-Mahabaleshwar) road.
Order:
In exercise of powers under general approval given by MoEF&CC, Govt. of India vide Handbook of FCA Guidelines-2019, Chapter 4 (para-4.2), Government of Maharashtra is pleased to grant 'In-Principle' approval to the for diversion of 1.007 ha. Forest land in favour of M/s. Telesonic Networks Limited, Nagpur for laying of underground OFC duct and Optical Fiber Cable Line passing within RoW of existing SH-139 & 140 (Satara-Medha Mahabaleshwar) road at Villages- Vatambe, Mukawale, Tal. Jaoli (Medha) & Shindola, Machutar, Tal.Mahabaleshwar, Dist. Satara.

2. This 'in-principle' approval is issued subject to the following conditions
1) The legal status of the land shall remain unchanged.
2) The Optical Fiber Cable Line will be laid within maximum size of the trench of 2.00
meters depth & 1 meter width with cable line under the PVC pipeline.
3) The cost of Net Present Value of Forest land shall be exempted.
4) The User Agency shall defray the cost of Compensatory Afforestation.
5) Compensatory Afforestation shall be taken up by the Forest Department over 2.20 ha.
degraded forest land in Compartment No. 168, Survey No. 1, Gut No. 392/1 of Village Kholawadi, Tal. Wai, Dist. Satara
6) Record of rights of CA land shall be produced with Stage-I compliance report.
7) The land under project will be reclaimed by the User Agency for which the agency shall
submit a bank guarantee at the rate of Rs.50/- per running meter and an undertaking to that effect shall also be submitted. If the agency fails to reclaim the land to the
satisfaction of concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests, the cost for reclamation will be covered from the bank guarantee.
8) If the cable is going to be laid in the formation width of the road, then additional charges
in form of bank guarantee shall be given at the rate of Rs.35/- per running meter.
Similarly for reclamation of side drain and for tarred portion, the guarantee will be at
the rate of Rs.300/- per meter and Rs.750/- per meter respectively. An undertaking shall
be obtained from the User Agency that it has agreed to make good any loss to Forest/
Environment while laying the cable in the forest area and during maintenance of the same in future.
9) For further maintenance of the project, the agency shall obtain permission from the
concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests as and when required.
10) The forest land shall not be used for any purpose other than specified in the proposal.
11) No felling of trees shall be done during the implementation of project.
12) No new forest area beyond the Right of Way of existing road should be broken for laying Optical Fiber Cable.
13) The User Agency shall pay the cost of Net Present Value, if required in future.
14) Underground Optical Fiber Cable can be laid only outside the confines of the Protected Areas.
15) No labour camp shall be established on the forest land.
16) After completion of the project, User Agency will reclaim the area under RoW suitably.
17) The layout plan of the proposal shall not be changed without prior approval of the State Government.
18) This sanction is subject to the final clearance by the Department of Information &
Technology for the project and concerned PWD authorities for the project.
19) The User Agency shall compensate the private owners of forest land if any, in
accordance with the prevalent laws, rules & guidelines.
20) The User Agency shall obtain Environmental Clearance as per the provisions of the
Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, if applicable.

21) All other conditions as may be mandatory under relevant Acts, Rules and Guidelines
shall be complied by the User Agency.
22) All the conditions stipulated by the Forest Department shall be binding on User Agency.
23) The User Agency shall make good of any loss to Forest / Environment.
24) In case, the proposed area falls in the RoW of the road passing through National Parks
and Wildlife Sanctuaries, prior permission of the State Board for Wildlife should be
obtained.
25) The Final approval shall be granted by the State Government after fulfillment of the
above conditions by the User Agency. The actual diversion of forest land shall be
effected thereafter by the Conservator of Forests (T), Nagpur.
26) Any breach of the conditions by the User Agency will be treated as violation of the
provisions of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980.
