The Central Government has notified the Forest (Conservation) Rules, 2022 under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980. Rule 8 (1) of the Rules provides that: The State Government and Union Territory Administration may, by an Order, constitute a Project Screening Committee to examine the completeness of the proposal submitted under clause (i), (ii) or (iii) of section 8 of the Act.
Accordingly, the matter of forming a Project Screening Committee was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: A Project Screening Committee Committee is being constituted to process proposals for diversion of forest land received under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980. The composition of the committee will be as follows:

Committees meet at least twice monthly. The number of quorum for the meeting shall be 3. The meeting will be held through video conferencing.
Allowances to the members of the Committee shall be admissible as per the rules of the Government.

The Member Secretary of the Joint Committee shall convene meetings from time to time in coordination with all the members.
The proposals will be submitted to the State Government on the recommendation/ feedback of the Project Screening Committee.
If the project passes through the regional (wildlife) as well as the regional (FDCM), then the concerned officials of both the regions will have to attend the meeting.
If there is any doubt whether the project is in the wildlife corridor or not, the concerned Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), West/ East, should be called as a special invitee.
