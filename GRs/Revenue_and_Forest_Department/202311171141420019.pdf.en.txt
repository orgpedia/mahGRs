Preamble The Government has decided to take up afforestation by entering into a tripartite agreement with the participation of private and non-governmental organizations for reforestation of degraded forest areas. Approved under FLD 2011/ Q.No. 167/ F-10, dated 23/09/2011. Subsequently, a revised Government Decision No.FLD-2011/ Q.No. 167/ F-10 dated 05/06/2023 has been issued.
In this Government decision, Mr. Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai, an industrial organization, has filed a writ petition before the High Powered Committee. The proposal for afforestation of 25.00 hectares of degraded forest area in Shirpur, District Dhule was approved by the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and Central Station House Officer and Member Secretary, High Powered Committee vide letter No. Cell-17/ Nocell/ 17-4/ P.No. 160/1570/20223-23, Nagpur dated 22/09/2022. In pursuance of the approval given to the proposal by the High Powered Committee vide circular dated 30/10/2023, the Government is issuing the following orders.
GOVERNMENT ORDER After carefully considering the proposal submitted by the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and Central Officer and Member Secretary, High Powered Committee, the Government is now ordering that as per the proposal of the Industrial Society, Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai, submitted by the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and Central Officer, Maharashtra State, Nagpur vide letter dated 22/09/2022, the Government is approving the plantation of trees on a total of 25.00 hectares of degraded forest area in the following places by entering into a tripartite agreement between Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai, an Industrial Society, Mukesh Patel Charitable Trust, Shirpur, a non-governmental organization and Deputy Conservator of Forests, Dhule.

The project is approved subject to the following conditions: i. In the above degraded forest area, the work of planting trees, water and soil conservation, their protection and conservation for 7 years should be done by Mukesh Patel Charitable Trust, Shirpur with the help of Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai.
The expenditure for the project and its management will be borne by Mr. Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai, an Industrial Society.
iii. Tree plantation under the project should be done under the supervision of Forest Department officials. Planting of trees should be completed within 2 years after the agreement.
(iv) Letter of Intent for Project No. 203/ F-10 dated 08/01/2013 to enter into a tripartite agreement between Mukesh Patel Charitable Trust, Shirpur and Deputy Conservator of Forests, Dhule with the financial assistance of Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai. To sign a tripartite agreement on behalf of the Forest Department
Deputy Conservator of Forests Dhule is being authorised.
The Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Head of Forest Force), Maharashtra State, Nagpur or any officer of the Forest Department authorized by him shall have the right to visit the project site and inspect the project and make amendments/ suggestions thereto.
The Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Head of Forest Force), Maharashtra State, Nagpur shall have the power to terminate the tripartite agreement immediately or take any other punitive action in case of breach of any of the conditions of the tripartite agreement by the industrial concern, Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai, or the non-governmental organisation, Mukesh Patel Charitable Trust, Shirpur.
vii. Wherever required, wire fencing should be done at the cost of the industrial unit.
(viii) Provision of irrigation to the plantation should be made as per requirement. The concerned agencies and the Deputy Conservator of Forests should ensure that there is no violation of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 while arranging this irrigation.
ix. Government Resolution No. FLD-2011/ PR No.5/ 2011 issued to Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal, Mumbai, an Industrial Society and Mukesh Patel Charitable Trust, Shirpur, a Non-Governmental Organization. All the terms and conditions of No. 167/ F-10, dated 05/06/2023 shall remain binding.
The concerned agencies and the Deputy Conservator of Forests should ensure that there is no violation of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 and other forest laws while carrying out plantation activities under the tripartite agreement.
