The proposal: A proposal has been received from the Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur seeking funds required to provide assistance to the affected farmers at the rate of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) and enhanced rate for the loss of agricultural crops caused by the cyclone in Mohadi and Tumsar taluks of Bhandara district on October 03, 2021. Accordingly, the government was considering the matter of distributing funds for the distribution of relief to the affected farmers.
Government's decision:
To provide assistance to the affected farmers for the loss of agricultural crops caused by the cyclone that hit Mohadi and Tumsar taluks of Bhandara district on October 3, 2021, from the State Disaster Response Fund and State Government funds, under reference No. 3 The Government hereby approve the release of funds to the tune of Rs. 99.00 lakhs (Rupees Ninety Nine Lakhs only) to the District Collector, Bhandara as shown in the table below.

(2) As per the annexure attached to this Government Resolution, the Account-wise funds should be distributed immediately by Office-M-11 to the Divisional Commissioner on the BEAMS system.
(3) The instructions and norms contained in all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. Subject to the Government Decision dated 02.10.2021 in No. 2
The rates have been increased. Out of the increased rate, the amount disbursed under the head of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be spent for providing assistance at the rate decided by the Government in the context of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) No. 1 and No. 3.
The difference between the approved rate and the rate of the State Disaster Response Fund should be shared from the funds disbursed from the State Government's fund. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned.
4. Beneficiaries to be determined after conducting the panchnama. Although the entire amount is being disbursed on the BEAMS system under this government decision, the financial assistance will be disbursed after the beneficiary is identified.
From the point of view of discipline, the concerned Collector should draw the amount from the treasury as per the actual need and then transfer the amount directly to the bank account of the beneficiaries through online mode.
Take caution. This money should not be withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account. Expenditure should be made within the limits of the grant sanctioned under this order.
After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
5. The accounts of expenditure from the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General of the expenditure made.
Quarterly coordination with the office should be done. Utilization of the above funds from the State Disaster Response Fund as well as from the State Government funds immediately after the expenditure is incurred.
