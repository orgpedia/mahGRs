Preamble: The" Maharashtra Appropriation Act, 2023" has been passed authorizing the Government to withdraw certain amounts from the Consolidated Fund of Maharashtra for the financial year 2023-24 and has been published in the Government of Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part IV dated 29th March, 2023 as Maharashtra Act No. 17 of 2023. Accordingly, the matter of distributing 10 percent funds for urgent matters was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: It is difficult to predict which natural disaster will occur in the state at any time. If any person dies due to a natural disaster in the state, immediate help should be provided to their heirs. Further, in order to provide immediate funds for the purchase of livestock in lieu of house collapse and dead animals, funds are being sanctioned to all the Divisional Commissioners as an urgent matter as mentioned in the accompanying form under the respective head of accounts under the provisions available for the relief of natural calamities.
Guidelines for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2023-24 have been issued vide Government Circular, Department of Finance, dated 12.4.2023. According to the circular, 20 per cent of the annual allocation for the first quarter of this financial year will be disbursed on the BEAMS system. Accordingly, 10% of the funds currently available on the BEAMS system is Rs. 568.29 lakhs (Rupees Five Crores Sixty Two Lakhs Thirty Nine Thousand only), Demand No. C-6, 2245 Assistance for relief of natural calamities, 02 floods, cyclones, etc. (91) Expenditure as per the State Disaster Response Fund norms is being disbursed to all Divisional Commissioners on the Budget Distribution System as mentioned in the accompanying Form A under this heading.

The remaining 10% will be disbursed once the funds are made available by the Finance Department. While disbursing funds to No. 3 The instructions given in the circular should be followed.
The distribution of assistance shall be made in accordance with the decisions taken by the Government at that time and in accordance with the terms and conditions of the Government Decision issued in that decision, the accounts, records/ registers of the distribution of this assistance shall be kept up to date at the level of the office collecting and distributing the grant in accordance with the prescribed procedures. The amount of compensation as per the prescribed procedure and the amount of relief as per the prevailing order, whichever is less, shall be disbursed to the concerned.
In order to have complete control over the expenditure, the Controlling Officers (Divisional Commissioners) should disburse the grants handed over to them to the districts which need the funds, without fully disbursing them to the District Collectors. The grants given to the District Collectors should not be considered for their additional grant demand unless they have spent a substantial amount directly. Also, the grants disbursed by the Divisional Commissioners should be reallocated before seeking additional grants from the government. Also, while demanding more provision, the concerned district collector should get such a certificate that the entire provision demanded can be spent. Explanatory reasons for the additional subsidy should also be mentioned.
Similarly, the monthly expenditure statement for the previous month should be sent to this department by the 5th of every month without fail.

It has been ordered from time to time to ensure that the funds available on the budget distribution system are not unnecessarily drawn into the treasury and lying in the bank for lack of allocation. However, it has been found that the money was withdrawn from the treasury and kept in the bank. Under this government decision, instructions are again being given that the funds should not be withdrawn and kept in the bank. If such a thing is found, it should be noted that disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned considering it as temporary theft.
The Controlling Authorities shall submit a copy of the grant allocation to the Secretary (Relief and Rehabilitation) without fail. The Controller may kindly direct the concerned officers to ensure that the computer note to be attached with the bill to be sent to the Treasury/ Sub-Treasury by the Drawing and Disbursing Officers should mention the notation" Computer Machine" in the subheading/ subheading without fail. Also, the report of the amount drawn from the treasury/ sub-treasury should be submitted to the government.
The details of the expenditure made every month from the fund distributed under this government decision should be filled in the system created by the Central Government www. ndmis, mhagov. in by the concerned district collector.
