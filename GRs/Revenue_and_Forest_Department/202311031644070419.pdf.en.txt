(1) The Inspector General of Registration and the Controller of Stamps, Maharashtra State, Pune and the Chief Controller Revenue Authority, Maharashtra State shall be declared as the competent authority for the issue of stamps which are not saleable but are lying in the Principal Stamp Office, Mumbai and all the District Treasury Offices in the State.
(2) The Inspector General of Registration and the Controller of Stamps, Maharashtra State, Pune and the Chief Controlling Revenue Authority, Maharashtra State shall carry out the entire process of de-listing of the remaining but not saleable stamps under the prevailing provisions of the Bombay Stamp Manual under their control. For this, a strategy should be devised.
3. District Stamp Drawing Committee should be constituted at the district level for the purpose of drawing up the remaining but not saleable stamps as mentioned above.

(6) A detailed list of the remaining stamps and stamp stocks (recording the face value and production value of each stamp) to be desigted in the concerned office shall be prepared in the first instance and signed by all the members of the respective desigting committee mentioned above with a blue ink ball pen with the name, designation and stamp of designation.
(7) The remaining stamps and stamp stocks lying in the respective offices, which are to be disposed of but are not to be sold, shall be disposed of by cutting each of the said stamps into small pieces and burning the said pieces.

9) A detailed report of the remaining but not saleable stamps to be issued in the concerned office and the stamp to be issued in the stamp stock should be submitted along with the certificate of destruction (on the said certificate and report) to the Accountant General Mumbai/ Nagpur and the Director, Directorate of Accounts and Treasuries, Mumbai through the Inspector General of Registration and Controller of Stamps, Maharashtra State, Pune, signed by all the members of the concerned Drafting Committee with the name, designation and stamp of designation in blue ink ball pen.
10) The Chairman, Member and Member Secretary of the concerned Drafting Committee should take care that no rules in the Mumbai Stamp Rule Book are violated under any circumstances.

If such stamp paper or label is found or misused in any way by the Government even after the deletion, the process of proposing criminal action against the Chairman, Member and Member Secretary of the concerned deletion committee along with the provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Service Rules in force should be immediately carried out by the Inspector General of Registration and the Controller of Stamps, Maharashtra State, Pune.
