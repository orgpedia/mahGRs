Orders regarding the appointment of other officers to the posts falling vacant in place of the above officers shall be issued separately.
(3) The Controlling Authorities shall relieve the officers who have been given the said appointment/ posting immediately to attend the place of new appointment. Further, the concerned Controlling Authorities should immediately inform the Government on the e-mail website atul. kode@nic. in about the date on which the concerned officers were relieved from their duties to appear in place of the new appointees and the date on which they appeared in place of the new appointees.
4. The concerned officers should not be granted leave without prior permission of the government. Also, as per Rule 30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, the parent office should not accept any leave application of the transferred officer. If such applications are received, they should be returned to the concerned authorities at the original address by post. If the leave application of the transferred officer is sent to the parent office, the application will not be taken into consideration.
If the concerned officer does not appear at the place of appointment immediately as per the said transfer order or tries to cancel the transfer by exerting any kind of pressure, then disciplinary action will be taken against him considering it as misconduct as the act violates Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979.
