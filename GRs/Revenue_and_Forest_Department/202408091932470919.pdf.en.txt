Introduction: National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA). \\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\ The "Aapda Mitra" scheme is being implemented all over the country under the concept of Aapda Mitra. The "first responder" during a disaster is always the local resident. Therefore, the basic objective of the scheme is to empower the local population with training in disaster management to respond promptly to disasters occurring at the local level. The scheme is going to achieve many things like reducing loss of life, creating awareness about disaster management in the society and creating manpower to respond to disasters at the primary level.
It was decided to expand the scope of the Aapda Mitra scheme under the National Disaster Management Authority's "Aapada Mitra'a Scheme." The scheme covered 20 districts in the state of Maharashtra with a target to train a total of 7,900 Aapda Mitra. The proposed month was completed on April 2023.

The training should include physically and mentally strong general citizens in the age group of 18-45 years, college students, NGOs (400), ASHA workers, Anganwadi workers, government and semi-government employees, etc. However, for doctors, ex-servicemen, construction engineers, the age limit will be up to 55 years but they will not be eligible for accident insurance. The concerned District Disaster Management Authority should register for Aapda Mitra training programmes within the district-wise limits specified in the above table. Interested women should be included in this registration.
