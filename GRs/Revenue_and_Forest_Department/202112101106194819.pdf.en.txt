Preamble: The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) has launched a pilot programme for capacity building in one flood prone district in each State of the country.
The programme was implemented in selected districts of Kolhapur in Maharashtra as per the decision of the government in the context. For Rs. A sum of Rs. 45.40 lakh had been sanctioned for the project. The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) has approved the state's proposal to expand the coverage of the programme to 20 districts of the state. An agreement has been signed between the National Disaster Management Authority and the state government to implement the programme. As stipulated in the agreement, the government was considering opening a separate bank account for the purpose of funding the programme.
The government has approved the opening of a separate bank account in the name of' Maharashtra AapdaMitra' for the implementation of the AapdaMitra program by the National Disaster Management Authority for capacity building in 20 flood-prone districts of the state, including Ahmednagar, Amravati, Bhandara, Chandrapur, Gadchiroli, Jalgaon, Mumbai City, Mumbai Suburbs, Nanded, Nandurbar, Nagpur, Nashik, Parbhani, Pune, Raigad, Ratnagiri, Sangli, Satara, Sindhudurg and Thane.
The expenditure on this will be borne from the bank account opened for the disaster mitra received from the central government. The Secretary, Disaster Management, Relief and Rehabilitation, Director, Disaster Management and Deputy Secretary/ Under Secretary, Disaster Management will be the signatory of the fund.
