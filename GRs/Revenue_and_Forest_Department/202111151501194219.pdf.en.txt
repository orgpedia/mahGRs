Water bodies like Jayakwadi, Nandur, Madhyameshwar, Ujjani etc. are famous for this. Along with this, medium projects, ponds have been built around many cities to provide drinking water to the city as well as water to the industrial areas. A number of species of birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians, amphibians, amphibians, amphibians, amphibians, amphibians, etc., are also found in these water bodies. Government's decision:
Under the Ecotourism State Plan implemented by the Forest Department in the financial year 2021-22, Rs. 50.00 lakh has been budgeted. Of this, Rs. 50 crore had been spent. The Government has sanctioned Rs. 15.00 lakh.
The fund is being handed over to the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Head of Forest Force), State of Maharashtra, Nagpur as Controlling Officer. Care should be taken to implement the work to be taken under this scheme as per the action plan, prescribed procedures and within the stipulated time. Detailed guidelines in this regard should be given to the field officers and the implementation should be reviewed from time to time. The funds will be spent on approved projects. Controlling authorities should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds.
In cases where the Forest Range Officer/ Assistant Conservator of Forests has granted technical/ administrative approval beyond the authority conferred on him, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Nagpur and Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Social Forestry), Pune shall disburse the said funds only after verifying that the revised technical/ administrative approval has been granted by the competent authorities.
The above funds are being disbursed subject to the condition that the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Nagpur will take necessary action against the concerned responsible for the irregularities committed in accordance with the technical and administrative approval and submit a report to the government.
