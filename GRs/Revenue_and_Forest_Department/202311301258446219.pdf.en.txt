(3) The instructions and norms of all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned. While providing this assistance, all concerned should ensure that the terms and conditions prescribed by the Central Government for natural calamities like cyclone, flood, etc. are met. The relief will be admissible if the excess rainfall is more than 65 mm in 24 hours in the mandal as per the criteria for the natural calamity declared by the State Government and the loss caused to the farmers is more than 33 per cent in the villages of the mandal. However, the excess rainfall will not be applicable in areas where there is flooding.
After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The Collector shall issue necessary instructions to all the banks so that the funds given to the farmers in natural calamities through this order as well as the orders issued from time to time in the past should not be diverted by the banks to the loan account or otherwise for recovery. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
(5) The accounts of the expenditure made out of the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure made shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. The Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund from the concerned and submitting it to the government as soon as the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund and the State Government Fund are spent.
