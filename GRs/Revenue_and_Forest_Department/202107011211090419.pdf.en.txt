The project, Mainstreaming Coastal and Marine Biodiversity into Production Sectors in Sindhudurg District, was implemented by the Kandhalvan Cell with financial assistance from the Government of India, UNDP-GEF (United Nations Development Programme Global Environment Facility). Under this project, livelihood programs such as crab farming, canal farming, SRI Paddy cultivation, ornamental fish farming, and onion forest tourism were undertaken through the Onion Forest Cell. The UNDP representative was directed to prepare a proposal to expand the scope of the project at the national level, taking into account its success. The project titled' Enhancing Climate Resilience in Coastal Areas of India" was developed by UNDP and submitted to the Green Climate Fund.
The project is being implemented with financial commitment in the State vide Reference Letter No. The state government has agreed. The Ministry of Finance, Govt. of India, vide Reference No. Approved by 2.
The project titled" Enhancing Climate Resilience of India's Coastal Communities" will be implemented in three states of Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh and Odisha with partial funding support from UNDP-Green Climate Fund and the Green Climate Fund has announced the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India as the implementing agency and Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh and Odisha as the implementing partners. The project has been approved by the Board of the Green Climate Fund in October, 2018 and the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India has signed an agreement with UNDP in September, 2019. Government's decision:
The Government is providing approval for implementation of the UNDP-GCF assisted' Enhancing Climate Resilience of India's Coastal Communities Project in the State with financial commitment.
Objectives of the project: To manage the coast with innovative management approach, cross-sectoral planning.
Investing in ecosystem infrastructure to manage climate shocks and disasters.
Support environmentally resilient livelihoods by creating easy access to financial support and marketing facilities for entrepreneurship development.
Project Outcome: Outcome 1- Enhancing the resilience of coastal and marine ecosystems and their services
Assessing the threats to marine coasts for ecosystem planning and community based adaptation activities.
Group-based conservation and restoration of coastal ecosystems to enhance resilience of ecosystems.
Outcome 2- Environmentally resilient livelihoods to enhance the adaptive capacity of coastal communities
Establishing environmentally resilient livelihoods and industries by strengthening market linkages.
Capacity building of local communities for community based adaptation and environmentally resilient livelihoods.
Outcome 3- Strengthening of coastal and marine management and institutional framework.
Creation of a network of institutions for climate resilience and integrated planning and governance in all coastal States.
Integrating an ecosystem approach into policies, frameworks and budgets of public and private sectors to adapt to climate change.
Information management for coastal resilience.
Project Duration: The duration of the project will be up to 31st December, 2025.

Project cost and share of the state
The total approved cost of the project is USD 130.26 million, of which GCF will contribute USD 43.41 million. The Government of Maharashtra will receive a total grant of US$ 11.43 million from GCF comprising of US$ 2.11 million for project management and US$ 9.32 million for ecosystem restoration and livelihood activities. The details are given in Annexure-I.
Accordingly, the co-financing commitment of the State Government was expected to be US$ 26.85 million, however, as the project was originally planned to receive no grant from GCF, the State Government has limited the co-financing commitment to US$ 19 million (Rs. 140.90 crore).
In view of the change in the US Dollar Conversion Rate over the period of the externally aided project as well as the change in the US Dollar-Rupee Conversion Rate, the Department of Finance has decided to increase the amount of co-financing commitment in Indian currency from Rs. 140.90 crore) will have to be re-approved by the Finance Department.

Availability of funds: The total financial commitment required for the project is Rs. 140.86 crore (Forest Department- Rs. 100.26 crore, Fisheries Department- Rs. 17.20 crore, Agriculture Department- Rs. 18.90 crore and Transport Department- Rs. 4.50 crore). Accordingly, the department concerned will take action to allocate funds as per the annual work plan during the project period.
New Account Heading:- Approval is being given to open a new Accounting Heading namely" UNDP-GCF Project-Enhancing Climate Resilience in Coastal Areas of India" to provide grants for co-financing commitments under the project. However, in the intervening period, the expenditure arising out of the State's co-financing commitment will be met from the Account Head" Onion Forest Conservation and Livelihood Generation Scheme" (2406A229).
Necessary funds for the schemes to be implemented under the project should be made available by the concerned departments from the head of accounts of the existing schemes of that department.
