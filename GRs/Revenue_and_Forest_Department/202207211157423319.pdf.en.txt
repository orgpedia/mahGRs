Preamble: The Government of India has received instructions to spend the funds received under the Centrally Sponsored Schemes during 2021-22 through PFMS system. Accordingly, a Single Nodal Agency (SNA) has been declared for the concerned Centrally Sponsored Scheme. Also, for these SNAs, accounts have been opened in the bank as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the concerned project agencies have been registered on the PFMS system.
The letter dated Reference No. 1 has directed that the funds released by the Central Government under the Centrally Sponsored Schemes in the year 2021-22 under the Central share shall be returned to the Central Government if the State Government has not deposited the same in the account of the concerned SNA at the end of 31.03.2022. However, as per Reference No. 2 letter, the time limit for crediting of funds received under Centrally Sponsored Schemes to the account of SNAs has been extended till 20.07.2022.
The money will then be returned to the central government.
Accordingly, vide Reference No. 3, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur has submitted the information regarding the Central share and corresponding State share received from the Central Government and deposited with the State Government in the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Project Tiger.
Government Resolution: As per the proposal submitted by the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur for Government approval under Reference No. 3, the Project Tiger is being approved to be credited to the account of Single Nodal Agency (SNA) under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme as mentioned in the table below, subject to the following conditions.
