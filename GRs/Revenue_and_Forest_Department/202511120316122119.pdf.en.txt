2. The officers concerned shall be liable to disciplinary action under the provisions of the Representation of the People Act, 1950 and the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct of Business) Rules, 1979, if they are found not to have joined the place of transfer before the expiry of the period of office, in accordance with the said provision prescribed under Rule 15 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of assumption of office, non-acceptance of service and Provisions during periods of suspension, dismissal and removal from service) Rules, 1981.
The officer concerned shall take care of joining the place of posting within the prescribed period as per the rules of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of assumption of office, Non-Service and Provisions during periods of suspension, dismissal or removal from service) Rules, 1981. Those concerned should note that if they do not otherwise join the position of this post, their period of absence will count as a "non-deceasing day."

4. Leave shall not be granted to the officer concerned for any reason except with the prior permission of the Government. Also, as per Rule-30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, no leave applications of officers transferred by the original office shall be entertained. If such applications are received, they should be returned by post to the concerned authorities at the original address.
5. In case of non-acceptance of appointment immediately under the said order or attempt to cancel transfer under any kind of pressure, disciplinary action shall be taken against them on the ground that the act violates Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979 as it is misconduct.
6. Submit a detailed report to the Government immediately through the concerned Divisional Commissioner in case any criminal or other offence of any kind is registered against the aforesaid officers in future.
