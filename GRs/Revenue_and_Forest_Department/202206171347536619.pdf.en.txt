The Proposal:
The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) has approved the implementation of' AapdaMitra' programme for capacity building in 20 flood-prone districts of the state. As per the agreement between the state government and the National Disaster Management Authority, the decision to open a separate bank account for the funds received from the central government was approved under the relevant government decision. Nehru Yuva Kendra, a centrally owned organization, has been authorized to impart training to volunteers in the Aapda Mitra programme. The government was considering releasing funds as the first instalment of funds required for training expenses.
Government's decision:
As all the training of 7,900 volunteers under the Aapda Mitra programme will be done by Nehru Yuva Kendra, the Centre has sought funds for training of 7,900 volunteers.
Accommodation and food will be provided to the trainees during the training period. While spending during this training period, the central government has fixed the rates for the relevant matters. Accordingly, for accommodation, travel, food and training, Rs. 18,000/- has been fixed. As per the guidelines of the Central Government, a total expenditure of Rs. 14,22,00, 000/- (Rupees Fourteen Crores Twenty Two Lakhs only) has been received. Since the training is yet to begin, the Nehru Yuva Kendra will have to bear the first instalment of Rs. 4,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Four Crores only) is being approved by the Government.
