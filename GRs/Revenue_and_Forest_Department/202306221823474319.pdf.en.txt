Preamble: The Central Government has identified 12 natural calamities such as cyclone, drought, earthquake, fire, flood, tsunami, hailstorm, landslide, avalanche, cloudburst, locust attack, cold wave and severe cold. The State Disaster Response Fund will provide assistance to farmers for crop loss and other losses.
The state government has also declared natural calamities such as untimely rain, excessive rainfall (including damage to crops), accidental fire, sea surge, and lightning as natural disasters. As per the guidelines of the Central Government, assistance from the State Response Fund has also been allowed for these natural disasters with a limit of 10%. In the automatic weather station provided in the revenue board, if there is more than 65 mm of rain in 24 hours, the loss of agricultural crops in all the villages of the board is calculated as excess rainfall. If the crop loss is more than 33 per cent, the farmers are given subsidy at the rate prescribed for the area where the loss has occurred. However, even if there is no record of heavy rainfall in the revenue circle, there may be heavy rainfall in the villages of the circle and it may cause damage to the agricultural farmers.
Also, continuous rain in some villages for several days can cause damage to the farmers. In such a situation, farmers need help.

The committee, in its report on determining the criteria for continuous rainfall, had suggested certain criteria for continuous rainfall to provide relief to farmers for losses at prescribed rates. This report. The Cabinet meeting was held in Mumbai on April 5, 2023. A decision to this effect was taken at a Cabinet meeting.
The state government has decided to declare" incessant rain" as a natural calamity and provide compensation to farmers for the losses incurred by them due to incessant rains.
For continuous rainfall, the following criteria are being determined: (1) First Trigger- D. Minimum 10 minutes of play per day for five consecutive days from July 15 to October 15 in the Revenue Circle. If I cause rain; and if the same revenue circle receives 50 percent (1.5 times) or more of the average rainfall of the previous 10 years (excluding drought years) during this period, the first condition of continuous rainfall shall apply.
(2) Second Trigger- In the revenue circle where the first trigger is applied, the Normalized Vegetation Index (NDVI) criterion should be checked by the 15th day from the date of the first trigger.
The second spell of continuous rainfall will be applicable if the Normalized Vegetation Index Variation (NDVI) of kharif crops falls to 0.5 or less during the period from July 15 to October 15.
However, the NDVI of the day on which the continuous rainfall started should be higher than the NDVI of the 15th day.
(3) The survey of the affected area in the revenue circle where the second wave of continuous rain has been affected will be done and if more than 33 percent of the agricultural farmers have suffered losses, then assistance will be provided.

This criterion will also be applicable for the loss of agricultural crops due to all natural calamities other than drought.
