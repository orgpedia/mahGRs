Preamble:
M/s Reliance JIO Infocomm Limited, Pune has submitted a proposal for diversion of 0.831 ha. Forest land under Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 for laying underground Optical Fiber Cable duct and cable within RoW of existing road passing through forest from Village Mhassalvade, Panudre, Pandharepani, Manjre, Mosum and Anuskara Tal Shahuwadi, District Kolhapur to the State Forest Department.
Order:
In exercise of powers under general approval given by MoEF&CC, Govt. of India vide
Handbook of FCA Guidelines-2019, Chapter 4 (para-4.2), Government of Maharashtra is pleased to
grant 'In-Principle' approval to the diversion of 0.831 ha. Forest Land under section-2 of the Forest
(Conservation) Act, 1980 for laying underground Optical Fiber Cable duct and cable Within RoW of
existing road passing through forest land in kolhapur district.

2. This 'in-principle' approval is issued subject to the following conditions
1) The legal status of the land shall remain unchanged.
2) The User Agency shall defray the cost of Compensatory Afforestation over 2.00 Ha degraded
forest land in Gut No 1005, Compt No.1106 of Village Shittur Tarfe Warul, Tal.Shahuwadi, Dist Kolhapur.
3) The Optical Fiber Cable Line will be laid within maximum size of the trench of 2.00 meters
depth & 1.0 meter width with cable line under the PVC pipeline.
4) The land under project will be reclaimed by the User Agency for which the agency shall
submit a bank guarantee at the rate of Rs.50/- per running meter and an undertaking to that
effect shall also be submitted. If the agency fails to reclaim the land to the satisfaction of
concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests, the cost for reclamation will be covered from the bank guarantee.
5) If the cable is going to be laid in the formation width of the road, then additional charges in
form of bank guarantee shall be given at the rate of Rs.35/- per running meter. Similarly for
reclamation of side drain and for tarred portion, the guarantee will be at the rate of Rs.300/ per meter and Rs.750/- per meter respectively. An undertaking shall be obtained from the
User Agency that it has agreed to make good any loss to Forest/ Environment while laying the
cable in the forest area and during maintenance of the same in future.
6) For further maintenance of the project, the agency shall obtain permission from the concerned
Deputy Conservator of Forests as and when required.
7) The forest land shall not be used for any purpose other than specified in the proposal.
8) No felling of trees shall be done during the implementation of project.
9) No new forest area beyond the Right of Way of existing road should be broken for laying Optical Fiber Cable.
10) The User Agency shall pay the cost of Net Present Value and Compensatory Afforestation, if required in future.
11) Underground Optical Fiber Cable can be laid only outside the confines of the Protected Areas.
12) No labour camp shall be established on the forest land.
13) After completion of the project, User Agency will reclaim the area under RoW suitably.
14) The layout plan of the proposal shall not be changed without prior approval of the State Government.
15) This sanction is subject to the final clearance by the Department of Information & Technology for the project and concerned PWD authorities for the project.
16) The User Agency shall compensate the private owners of forest land if any, in accordance
with the prevalent laws, rules & guidelines.
17) The User Agency shall obtain Environmental Clearance as per the provisions of the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, if applicable.

18) All other conditions as may be mandatory under relevant Acts, Rules and Guidelines shall be
complied by the User Agency.
19) All the conditions stipulated by the Forest Department shall be binding on User Agency.
20) The User Agency shall make good of any loss to Forest / Environment.
21) In case, the proposed area falls in the RoW of the road passing through National Parks and
Wildlife Sanctuaries, prior permission of the State Board for Wildlife should be obtained.
22) Any breach of the conditions by the User Agency will be treated as violation of the provisions
of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980.
