The proposal: From June 15th to September 30th, it is celebrated as the Forest Festival. During this period, saplings are supplied by the government at a discounted rate to encourage people to plant trees. Planting and nurturing trees should be a people's programme and the public should be made aware of the importance of afforestation. In this regard, the saplings are supplied as per the revised concessional rates during the Van Mahotsav period.
According to reference no. (2) The continuation of the scheme for the year 2020-21 was approved vide Government Resolution dated 15th June, 2020. The government is planning to launch the scheme in 2021-22.
Government's decision:
Considering the experience of the year 2020-21 and the response of the people, the Government is approving to continue and implement this scheme effectively during the period of Van Mahotsav from 15th June 2021 to 30th September 2021 in the financial year 2021-22.
The programme of" Planting 50 crore trees" was taken up in the State since 2017. Under the programme, a target of planting 33 crore trees was fixed for the year 2019-20. The plantation programme should continue uninterrupted and under this programme, large scale tree plantation programme should be taken up in privately owned fallow areas and on farm bunds, railway embankments, canal embankments and roadsides, community fallow areas and gairan areas. Also, saplings will be supplied at a discounted rate during the Vanamahotsava period to ensure that all common farmers and tree lovers get saplings at a cheaper rate. The supply of saplings during the Van Mahotsav period and during the normal period is being approved for the financial year 2021-22 at the concessional rates mentioned in the table below.
