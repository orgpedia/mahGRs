Preamble: The Departmental Scrutiny Committee has been constituted under the decision of the Government at Reference No. 5 to scrutinize the proposals received from the District Collector for seeking funds from the State Disaster Response Fund for taking various measures to prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus in the State. The Divisional Scrutiny Committee has scrutinized the proposals received from the concerned Collectors for seeking funds from the State Disaster Response Fund and sent their recommendations to the government through a letter at reference number 6 to 9. The meeting of the State Executive Committee was held on 27.04.2021 to decide on the recommendations received from the Divisional Scrutiny Committee. Accordingly, as per the approval given by the State Executive Committee in its meeting held on 27.04.2021, the matter of disbursement of funds for taking various measures in the context of COVID-19 was under consideration of the Government.

The decision was taken by the divisional commissioners of Konkan, Pune, Nashik and Aurangabad to prevent the spread of the Covid-19 virus. Considering the recommendations received from the Divisional Commissioners, Konkan, Pune, Nashik and Aurangabad, and the State Disaster Response Fund, the State Executive Committee has decided to meet the expenditure incurred as per the norms and aspects of the State Disaster Response Fund before 31.03.2021. As per the approval given in the meeting dated 27.04.2021, Rs. 141,64,21, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred and Forty One Crores Sixty Four Lakhs Twenty One Thousand only) is being provided.
The funds being disbursed through this Government Resolution will be utilized for the following matters as approved in the meeting of the Divisional Scrutiny Committee and as per reference No. 4 Expenditure should be made on such items as may be permissible from the State Disaster Response Fund as per the decision of the Government.
1. Measures for quarantine sample collection and screening : (a) Provision for temporary accommodation, food, clothing, medical care etc. for people affected and sheltered in quarantine camps (other than home quarantine) or for cluster containment operations.
(b) Cost of consumables for sample collection.
(c) Support for checking screening and contact tracing.
2. Procurement of essential equipment's/labs for response to COVID-19: (a) Cost of setting up additional testing laboratories within the Government and the cost of consumable and testing kit.
(b) Cost of personal protection equipment for healthcare, municipal, police and fire authorities (c) Cost of Thermal Scanners, Ventilators, Air Purifiers, Oxygen generation and storage plant in hospitals, strengthening ambulance services for transport of patients, setting up containment zones, Covid-19 hospital, Covid-19 care centers and consumables in the Government hospitals.

The funds sanctioned as above should be disbursed on the BEAMS system to the concerned Divisional Commissioner by Office Number 11.
3. Expenditure to be made within the limits prescribed by the Divisional Scrutiny Committee in its meeting.
Expenditure on measures to prevent the spread of COVID-19 should also be met from the National Health Mission. However, the Collector should ensure that while spending the above sanctioned funds, there is no deviation from the expenditure on the same aspects from the schemes of other departments of the state government or the National Health Mission.
The following terms and conditions should be specially followed while spending the fund: 1) It will be mandatory for the concerned District Collector's Office to ensure that these things are not proposed from other schemes to prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus so that there is no duplication.
(2) Wherever necessary, administrative, technical approvals and tender procedures should be followed.
(3) It shall be ensured that all norms for inviting, accepting and procuring tenders, where necessary, shall be in accordance with the procedures and policies of the Government in force at that time.
4) While procuring RT-PCR kits at market price, the prices fixed by Hofkyn Biopharma Limited for RT-PCR kits and VTM kits should be purchased at the minimum price. Also, while purchasing medicines and materials locally, care should be taken to purchase them at minimum rates by comparing the rates of Haffkine and other available rates with the rates received as per the tender.
5) While making purchases, it should be ensured that the rates are uniform for all the districts of the division.

New supply holders/ manufacturers are being made available for Covid Medicine/ Lab Kits- RT-PCR Kit/ Rapid Antigen/ Antibody. So, be aware of this every time you make a purchase. Market prices are falling as new supply holders become available. Therefore, it is not necessary to make a large purchase at once.
7) The district administration should verify the actual requirement and utility as well as the availability of manpower at the time of procurement.
Antigen/ Remdesivir/ Flavipiravir/ N95 should be purchased in small quantities and for a short period. It should be purchased within 15 to 20 days. Although the price is fixed, it should be checked every time.
It will be necessary to include in the purchase order such a condition that the purchasing department gets the benefit of the reduced rate in case of a large order.
9) It is necessary to see the number of patients and daily case load while creating a temporary facility. The preparation should be done by changing the available building of the government institution or government building. New Temporary Centers should be created keeping in mind the number of government beds available in the district, the number of beds and rooms available in private hospitals.
10) The new CCC should be considered to be operational only after the CCC has been filled to at least 75%.
The requirement of Liquid O2 tank will depend on the projected cases and the number of O2 beds available. Be sure to check before you buy. Liquid O2 tank should be purchased for the government/ municipality/ municipal corporation. Dura cylinders/ Jumbo cylinders should be used in private or temporary facilities.
Action on Liquid O2 should be taken only after considering the suggestions of the Public Health Department.
12) Care must be taken when recruiting manpower. There should not be equal allocation of manpower to all institutions. If there are no patients, 100% manpower should not be filled by operationalizing the CCC/ DCHC. The next institutions should be started only after the old CCC/ DCHC is filled up to 75% and the manpower should be filled up accordingly.
13) Purchase of medical equipment and materials. 01.12.2016 and subject to the terms and conditions therein as amended from time to time. 30.1.2019 as well. 28.2.2019 and D. Action should be taken as per the guidelines issued on 29.6.2019 and thereafter.

14) The government. The District level procurement should be carried out with the approval of the Committee headed by the District Collector as the authority has been delegated to procure from the subsidy available at the district level during the COVID-19 period under 24/03/2020. The requirement of procurement should be determined by considering the supply of the items which are procured from the state level. Consideration should be given to the price of the product when purchasing. Be sure to follow these rules when buying. Also, the instructions and guidelines issued from the Commissionerate of Health Services and the Public Health Department from time to time regarding the purchase should be followed.
15) When buying the above items, make sure that they are in accordance with the technical specifications for the same. Procurement should be done within the limit of subsidy received.
Purchase as needed. Ensure that medicines, supplies and materials are not left unused.
The instructions given under the Government Decision at Reference No. 3 and 5 should be followed while spending the above funds. The funds should be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned. The money should not be deposited in any bank account. Expenditure should be made within the limits of the grant sanctioned under this order.
The details of the expenditure made every month from the funds disbursed under this government decision should be filled by the concerned districts in the system www. ndmis. mha. gov. in created by the Central Government. If this information is not entered into this system, further funds will not be distributed.
7. The accounts of expenditure from the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. Also, the Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund immediately after the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund are spent and submitting them to the government.
The expenditure for the above purpose shall be met from the provisions available under the head Demand No. C-6, Principal Accounts Head 2245- Financial Assistance for Relief of Natural Calamities, 80, General, 800, Other Expenditure, (91) Expenditure as per the Standard of State Disaster Response Fund, (91) (02) Expenditure on Prevention of COVID-19 Outbreak (Mandatory), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary) (22454141).
