Introduction: The Finance Department of the State Government has established a Virtual Treasury Office under the control of the Directorate of Accounts and Treasuries, Mumbai, and has provided the facility of depositing government accounts through the Government Receipt Accounting System using electronic means of payment of revenue and non-revenue amounts of various departments of the government. Instructions have been issued under the above-referenced circular of the Finance Department regarding the accounting, reconciliation, refund, defacement of e-challans, etc. of the revenue and non-revenue amounts deposited with the government through the Government Deposit Accounting System.
At present, the maximum revenue in the forest department is collected through e-auction of timber, bamboo and water hyacinth and physical auction at the depots. At present, the total amount received on the e-auction portal of the forest department is deposited in the pooling account. The amount from the pooling account is then transferred to the concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests. Out of this amount, the amount to be deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the Government is deposited in the government accounts by preparing a handwritten invoice and the account generated from the Account Management System is submitted to the Accountant General, Nagpur.

In the first phase, the revenue received from the e-auction of timber, bamboo, firewood should be deposited in the government account through e-challan through the Government Receipt Accounting System. For this, the amount received in the pooling account through the e-auction portal of the department should be transferred to the divisional office and then the amount from the divisional office should be deposited in the government account through the government receipt accounting system. For this, the Maha-e-Auction Portal and the State Bank of India Payment Gateway (SBI, e-payment getaway) should be integrated with the GRAS system. The first phase will start from June 1, 2021.
In the second phase, the revenue received from the physical auction at the depot should also be deposited in the government department through the Government Receipt Accounting System. The second phase will start from July 1, 2021.
In the third phase, the marginal revenue received by the forest department should also be deposited in the government department through e-challan through the Government Deposit Account System. The third phase will start from September 1, 2021.
As mentioned above, the various sub-headings under Heading 0406- Forestry and Wildlife should be included in the GRAS system with eight digit scheme notation in three phases. After each stage, necessary information regarding the change in the procedure for payment of revenue through Gras system and handwritten currency should be submitted to the virtual treasury office.
The accounts of the amount deposited in the government department through the grass system under the heading of forestry should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai through the Virtual Treasury Office and the remaining accounts under the title should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Nagpur from the Divisional Office of the Forest Department.
