Date: October 25, 2023
Preamble: The Government receives a large number of representations from various public representatives and citizens regarding the change in the jurisdiction of the Revenue Offices in the State/ creation of new offices.
However, since the change of jurisdiction/ creation of new offices in the Revenue Offices in the State is a costly and day-to-day affair affecting the common man, it is necessary to take a decision after due deliberation and study. Also, it needs to be examined whether the change in social conditions, increased population, change in the nature of revenue work and other ancillary factors necessitate or how important laws relating to revenue work need to be changed over time. Therefore, the matter of setting up a committee to recommend changes in the jurisdiction of revenue offices/ creation of new offices and changes in the revenue laws as per the requirement was under the consideration of the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: A Committee of retired IAS and other officers is being constituted to recommend changes in the jurisdiction of the Revenue Offices in the State/ creation of new offices and changes in important Revenue Acts, as required. (Retd.)- Chairman 2. Shri Chandrakant Dalvi, Bh. (Retd.) Member 3. Shri Shekhar Gaikwad, Bh. (Retd.)-Member 4. Shri Prahlad Kachre, Retd. Office of the Divisional Commissioner, Pune- Member Secretary
The members of the committee will have the opportunity to invite experts in the subject as invited members if they deem it necessary.

The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
i.Create the office of the Additional Collector as per the need, considering the nature of administrative work in the districts, recommend the creation of necessary posts for it, details of recurring and non-recurring expenditure as well as other ancillary matters.
(ii) to recommend creation/ restructuring of separate Sub-Divisional Offices, creation of necessary posts, details of recurring and non-recurring expenditure as well as other ancillary matters, as may be required, taking into account the nature of administrative work in the Sub-Divisions.
(iii) to recommend the creation of an Additional Tehsil Office, including the creation of necessary posts, details of recurring and non-recurring expenditure as well as other ancillary matters, as may be required, taking into account the nature of administrative work in the Taluks.
(iv) To examine whether the following laws relating to revenue affairs need to be amended from time to time and to advise on the changes, if any.
The Maharashtra Prevention of Fragmentation and Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1947
(b) The Maharashtra Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948 and rules made thereunder; (c) The Maharashtra Agricultural Lands (Ceiling on Land Holdings) Act, 1961; (d) The Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966 and rules made thereunder. The government needs to address the above issues.
The Committee shall submit its report to the Government within a period of 90 days from the date of such decision.
The members of the committee will be allowed to visit any part of the geographical area within the state if they deem necessary. Accordingly, the expenditure on the members of the committee should be borne by the Divisional Commissioner, Pune Division.
Also, the process of providing necessary manpower and meeting arrangements to the committee should be done by the Divisional Commissioner, Pune Division.
