About 13% of Maharashtra's forest cover is bamboo, with Gadchiroli and Chandrapur districts accounting for more than 90% of the state's bamboo production. In order to promote bamboo production and bamboo based entrepreneurship by cultivating and managing bamboo in natural and private sectors and to increase the value added use of bamboo, under the new Bamboo Policy, No. The decision was announced by the government. Accordingly, a Bamboo Research and Training Centre has been established at Chichpally, G. Chandrapur.

Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Research, Education and Training) and ex-officio Director, Chandrapur Forest Administration, Development and Management Academy, Chandrapur, works in the three areas of Research, Education and Training in the Maharashtra Forest Department. Bamboo Research and Training Centres have been established in the state of Maharashtra for the purpose of research and training in the bamboo sector. Therefore, Bamboo Research and Training Centre, Chichpalli, Chandrapur, coming under the control of Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Research, Education and Training) and Designated Director, Chandrapur Forest Administration, Development and Management Academy, Chandrapur, will help in achieving the objectives of research and training in bamboo sector.
