Also, for these SNAs, accounts have been opened in the bank as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the concerned project agencies have been registered on the PFMS system.
The letter dated Reference No. 1 has directed that the funds released by the Central Government under the Centrally Sponsored Schemes in the year 2021-22 under the Central share shall be returned to the Central Government if the State Government has not deposited the same in the account of the concerned SNA at the end of 31.3.2022. However, vide Reference No. 2, the time limit for crediting the funds received under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme to the account of SNA has been extended till 20.7.2022. After that, the money will be returned to the central government.
Accordingly, vide letter No. 3, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), State of Maharashtra, Nagpur has submitted the information regarding the Central share received from the Central Government and deposited with the State Government in the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Modern Forestry and Control Management (Integrated Forest Conservation Scheme) so far and accordingly, the State Government is taking a decision as follows: GOVERNMENT DECISION: As per the proposal submitted by the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), State of Maharashtra, Nagpur for Government approval vide letter No. 3, the funds received under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Modern Forestry and Control Management (Integrated Forest Conservation Scheme) (Tribal Component) shall be spent from the tribal component instead of the Central Sponsored Scheme (General Component)

The total Central share and State share is Rs. 7.34 lakh is being allowed to be deposited in the account of Single Nodal Agency (SNA) subject to the following conditions:
The funds should be deposited in the account of the Single Nodal Agency (SNA) only as per the approval of the government.
The guidelines issued by the Central Government from time to time in accordance with the Centrally Sponsored Schemes being implemented through the PFMS system should be followed.
The Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur shall be fully responsible for ensuring that no irregularity is committed in respect of the said fund.
Ensure that the funds deposited in the Single Nodal Agency (SNA) account are for the tribal relief component.
The Government Decision is being issued pursuant to the Finance Department's informal reference No. 217/22/ Expenditure-10, dated 20.7.2022.
