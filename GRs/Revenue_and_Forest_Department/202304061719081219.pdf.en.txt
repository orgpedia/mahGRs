It has been decided to implement Dr. Syama Prasad Mookerjee Jan Van Vikas Yojana in the State under the Government Resolution No. 1 for the overall development of the villages by improving the quality of forest and wildlife management through sustainable development of the resources of people, water, forest and land in the villages adjacent to the forest, increasing productivity, reducing the dependence of the villagers on the forest, creating complementary linkages to agriculture, providing alternative employment, reducing human-wildlife conflict and thus enhancing the quality of forest and wildlife management through the participation of the villagers. As per the Government decision in Reference No. 3, the scope of the scheme has been expanded to include solar energy fencing.
Accordingly, in accordance with the government's stated criteria of Reference No. 3, all regional and wildlife reports, based on the number of village-wise incidents of forest loss caused by wild animals in the last three years, out of 1173 villages sensitive to agricultural losses, 31005 beneficiaries in 447 villages have been selected for the benefit of solar fencing and in view of the sensitivity of agricultural losses from wildlife, the government has decided to provide solar fencing in the remaining 726 villages as a special case.
