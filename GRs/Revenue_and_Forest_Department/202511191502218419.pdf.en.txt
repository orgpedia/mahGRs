Government Orders: -
As per the provisions of Section 4 (4) and 4 (5) of the Maharashtra Prevention of Delay in discharge of Government Duties and Regulation of Transfer of Government Employees Act, 2005 as demanded by the Housing Department, a vacant officer of the cadre of Deputy Collector is deputed on administrative grounds in front of his name in Column No. The post indicated in section 4 is being posted on deputation with the approval of the competent authority in the interest of public service and for the convenience of the administrator till further orders.
The aforesaid Deputy Collector is being relieved of his present office with effect from the date 19.11.2025 (M.No.) for joining the post of Secretary. The officer concerned should upload the Appointment Report on the portal Wa / CTC https://maharevenuehr.in as to the date on which he / she appeared at the place of new appointment.
3. The officers concerned shall be liable to disciplinary action under the provisions of the Representation of the People Act, 1950 and the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct of Business) Rules, 1979, if they are found not to have joined the place of transfer before the expiry of the period of office, in accordance with the said provisions prescribed under Rule 15 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of assumption of office, non-acceptance of service and Provisions during periods of suspension, dismissal and removal from service) Rules, 1981.
# Page 2
The officer concerned shall take care of joining the place of posting within the prescribed period as per the rules of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of assumption of office, Non-Service and Provisions during periods of suspension, dismissal and removal from service) Rules, 1981. Those concerned should note that if they do not otherwise join the position of this post, their period of absence will count as a "non-deceasing day."
(5) No leave shall be granted to the officer concerned for any reason except with the previous sanction of the Government. Also, as per Rule-30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, no leave applications of officers transferred by the original office shall be entertained. If such applications are received, they should be returned by post to the concerned authorities at the original address.
6. In case of non-acceptance of appointment immediately under the said order or attempt to cancel transfer under any kind of pressure, disciplinary action shall be taken against them on the ground that the act violates Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979 as it is misconduct.
7. In case of any future criminal or other offence against the aforesaid officers, a detailed report shall be submitted to the Government immediately through the concerned Divisional Commissioner.
