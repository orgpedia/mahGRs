Preamble: On 10.06.2021, the State Government has approved the proposal for implementation of the project titled" Enhancing Climate Resilience of India's Coastal Communities" in the State with co-financing support from UNDP-Green Climate Fund. As per the approval of the Cabinet of Ministers, the outline of the project presented in the Government Decision dated 1.07.2021 has been clarified. Further, approval has been accorded vide Government Resolution dated 6.06.2022 for opening of new head of accounts for project expenditure (2406 B272).
2. UNDP-GCF Project titled" Enhancing Climate Resilience of India's Coastal Communities" (2406 B272) has not been allocated for 2022-23. A sum of Rs. 169.67 crore has been allocated for the project.
3. Supplementary demand in the present case sanctioned Rs. 169.67 lakhs, out of which 50% i.e. 84.83 lakh has been released under the Government Resolution dated 31.10.2022 of this Department with the prior approval of the Finance Department. The Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development) vide letter dated 9.02.2023 has requested for release of balance funds of Rs. 84.84 lakh under this head. The Finance Department has been asked to disburse the remaining amount.
Reference No. 73/ Expenditure-10, dated 16.02.2022.

4. In view of the above, the total amount budgeted under Supplementary Demands under this head is Rs. 169.67 Lakhs (02- wages Rs. 58.48 lakhs, 21- Supplies and Materials Rs. 38.98 lakhs and 50 other expenses amounting to Rs. 72.21 lakh) out of the fund of Rs. 84.84 lakh is available for disbursement. The matter is under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: The total budgetary allocation for the year 2022-23 under the UNDP-GCF Project" Enhancing Climate Resilience of India's Coastal Communities" (2406 B272) through Supplementary Demands is Rs. 169.67 lakh out of the total fund of Rs. 84.84 Lakhs (02- wages Rs. 29.24 lakhs, 21 supplies and material Rs. 19.49 lakhs and 50 other expenses amounting to Rs. 36.11 lakhs) is being allowed to be spent subject to the following conditions:
(1) The funds shall be used for the purpose for which they were allocated.
2) Ensure that there are no financial irregularities.
The fiscal limits prescribed by the Department of Finance under the Government Decision dated 11/7/2001 shall continue to apply.
The approval is for the period 2022-23.
(5) It shall be ensured that there is no violation of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980.
6) Utilization certificate should be submitted in accordance with the cost.
2. The expenditure should be shown under Demand No. C-7,2406 Capital expenditure on Forestry and Wildlife under the heading UNDP GCF Project" Enhancing Climate Resilience of India's Coastal Communities" (2406 B272) and should be met from the budgetary grants sanctioned in 2022-23.
While spending the funds, the instructions given by the Finance Department in the monthly fund statement and other matters should be followed.
During the period under consideration for the approval of the title deed for the said project, it was approved under the Government Decision dated 1.06.2021 to bear the cost of co-financing by the Forest Department from the" Conservation of Forest and Livestock Development Scheme (2406 A229)."
However, now the head of accounts for expenditure on the project has been operationalized and funds are being made available under the head of accounts. Therefore, the cost of the project should be calculated from the account head" 2406 B272."
4. The decision of the Government is not binding on the Finance Department.
