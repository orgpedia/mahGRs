3) Government Circular, Finance Department, No. 2022/ No. 43/ Finance-3, D. 4/04/2022 Preamble: To provide immediate relief to the next of kin of farmers who have committed suicide in case of farmer suicides due to three reasons namely failure, indebtedness and difficulty in repayment of loans, the following grants-in-aid have been sanctioned under reference No. 2 As per the procedure laid down in the above Government Decision, in case of suicide of farmers qualified by the District Level Committee, the next of kin of the farmers who committed suicide will be paid Rs. 1.00 lakh has been provided. For this purpose, the provisions made in the financial year 2022-2023 under the head of head accounts 2235 3195 were under the consideration of the government to be distributed to all the divisional commissioners for distribution to all the collectors except Mumbai city and Mumbai suburbs.
Government Decision: In the financial year 2022-2023, a provision of Rs. 17.00 crore is being distributed to all Divisional Commissioners as mentioned in the Statement A accompanying this Government Resolution for crediting to the accounts of all Collectors.
2. In cases of farmer suicides, the prescribed procedures should be completed immediately and assistance should be provided to the heirs of the farmers who have committed suicide.
The expenditure for this purpose should be spent under headings Demand No. C-5, Main Account Head 2235, Social Security and Welfare 02, Social Welfare 104, Welfare of the Aged, Disabled and Destitute (00) (03), Financial Assistance to the heirs of farmers who have committed suicide (Compulsory) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) (2235 3195). The actual expenditure under this head and the expenditure reported by the Accountant General's office should be reconciled. The utilization certificate of the disbursed funds should be submitted to the government immediately.
