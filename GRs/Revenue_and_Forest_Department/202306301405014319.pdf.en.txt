(2) The order shall come into force immediately.
In accordance with the provisions laid down under Rule 15 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Periods of Appointment, Non-Appointment and Removal) Rules, 1981, a period of seven days, including Sundays and Public Holidays, is allotted to the concerned officers for preparation for appointment to the post at the place of posting and the actual period shall be calculated as follows:
(a) one day's journey on transfer to a place within the same district or adjoining district (i.e., a district having common boundary with any place);
(b) on transfer to a place other than the place mentioned in clause (a) above, a period of two days shall be admissible to the officer concerned for travel.

(4) If it is found that the officer concerned has not joined the place of transfer before the expiry of the period of his appointment as per the said provisions laid down under Rule 15 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Appointment, Non-Appointment and Removal of Suspension, Dismissal and Removal from Service) Rules, 1981, he shall be liable to be proceeded against in accordance with the provisions of the Government Order, No. Narrow 2016/ S.No. 186/ 16/ Office 14, Dy. All concerned should note that disciplinary action will be taken as per the provisions of 23.12.2016.
(5) On the passing of the said order, the transferred officer shall ensure that he joins the place of transfer within the period prescribed under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Assignment, Non-Ministerial Service and Provisions in respect of Suspension, Dismissal and Removal) Rules, 1981. All concerned should note that in case of non-appointment of a replacement during this period, the period of his absence shall be treated as" dies non."
6. The concerned officer should not be granted leave without prior permission of the government.
Also, as per Rule-30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, the parent office should not accept any leave applications of the transferred officers. If such applications are received, they should be returned to the concerned authorities at the original address. If the leave applications of the transferred officers are sent to the parent office, these applications will not be taken into consideration.
All concerned should note that if they do not join the post immediately as per the said transfer orders or try to cancel the transfer by exerting any kind of pressure, then disciplinary action will be taken against them considering it as a misconduct as this action is in violation of Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979.
