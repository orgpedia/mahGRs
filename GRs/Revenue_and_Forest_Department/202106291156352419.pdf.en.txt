The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Minor Mineral Exploration (Development and Regulation) Rules, 2013 have been framed to regulate minor mineral exploration in the state. The government issued a notification on this. It has been published in the Gazette dated 18/07/2013. The government has also made a decision. Under 21/05/2015, Sand/ Sand Release Policy has been adopted to facilitate navigation through Coastal Regulation Zone.
The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change has also issued guidelines in this regard. The Supreme Court, the High Court and the The Government has decided on the overall sand/ sand export policy after studying the green tribunal norms and some of the major States. 03/09/2019 has been fixed.
The importance of minor minerals in the development of the state is unparalleled. Minor minerals are mainly used for infrastructure projects such as large, medium and small projects, roads, construction etc. The raising of infrastructure depends on the availability of minor minerals. The Government has taken measures from time to time to curb illegal mining and transportation of minor minerals. However, some incidents of illegal mining and transportation of minor minerals are coming to the notice of the government. The government was considering the formation of a revenue department-wise state-level vigilance team to effectively curb illegal mining and transportation of minor minerals.
Government's decision:
A State Level Vigilance and Monitoring Team comprising of the following officers is being constituted as per the Revenue Department for conducting surprise checks and verifying complaints of illegal mining and transportation of minor minerals. The State level Vigilance and Monitoring Team for each Division will be as follows:

Appointed by the Divisional Commissioner: 1) Deputy Commissioner 1 Team Head 2) Deputy Collector Member 3) District Superintendent Land Records Member 4) District Mining Officer (except the district where the inspection is to be carried out) Member Secretary
The Divisional Commissioner shall issue orders for the appointment of officers for the said team from time to time as may be required.
If during the inspection conducted by this vigilance inspection team, illegal mining of minor minerals, transportation and illegal stock of minor minerals are found, the machinery used for excavation, means of transportation and minor minerals should be seized and handed over to the concerned tehsildar.
The concerned Tahsildar shall take care of the disposal of the stock and machinery of the said minor mineral and
Action should be taken as per rules.
A detailed report of the action taken by the team should be submitted to the concerned Divisional Commissioner and the Divisional Commissioner should instruct the concerned to take action as per the rules.
If the government directs this vigilance team, it can go to another department and take action on the complaint of illegal mining and transportation of minor minerals.
