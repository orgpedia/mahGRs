Introduction: In order to help the farmers in the next season in case of loss due to natural calamities like heavy rains, floods and cyclones, the farmers are given assistance at the prescribed rate from the State Disaster Response Fund at one time in a season in the form of input subsidy. Also, assistance is provided at prescribed rates for other approved items of State Disaster Response Fund. Government Resolution, Revenue& Forest Department No.CLS-2022/ PR No. 349/ M-3, Dy. The Government has approved to provide assistance to the affected farmers for the loss and other damages caused to the agricultural crops in various districts of the State due to natural calamities like heavy rains, floods etc. as on 27.03.2023.
The Divisional Commissioners, Amravati and Aurangabad have issued orders for compensation to the farmers and agricultural land for the loss caused due to heavy rains and floods in the month of June to July, 2023 respectively. 30.08.2023 and D. The proposal for seeking funds has been received by the Government vide letter dated 31.08.2023.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: To provide assistance to the affected farmers and agricultural land for the damage caused by the excessive rainfall and flood situation in the State during the period from June to July, 2023, from the State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF), under reference No. 1 lakh as compensation to the farmers and the State Government. The Government has approved the release of funds amounting to Rs. 107177.01 lakh (Rupees One Thousand One Hundred Seventy Seven Crores Seventy Seven Lakhs One Thousand Only).
The funds shall be disbursed through DBT system by the Office of M-11 as shown in the heading of the account in the form attached to this Government Resolution. The District Collector has mentioned above in serial number 2. The details of all the beneficiaries under this proposal as notified under 24.01.2023 shall be prepared in the prescribed format and fed into the computer system. While furnishing this information, it should be noted that the fund released earlier for the current season to all the departments for the loss of agricultural crops does not include the fund demanded in this proposal.

(3) The instructions and norms contained in all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The funds will be used for the purpose for which they were allocated. While providing this assistance, all concerned should ensure that the conditions and conditions prescribed by the Central Government for natural calamities like cyclone, flood, etc. are fulfilled. The relief will be admissible if the excess rainfall is more than 65 mm in 24 hours in the mandal as per the criteria for the natural calamity declared by the State Government and the loss caused to the farmers is more than 33 per cent in the villages of the mandal. However, the excess rainfall will not be applicable in areas where there is flooding.
After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
(5) The accounts of the expenditure made out of the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure made shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. The Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund from the concerned and submitting it to the government as soon as the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund and the State Government Fund are spent.
For the above purpose, the principal account should be spent from the funds made available by way of supplementary demand under the head of accounts shown in the accompanying statement under heading 2245-Finance for Relief of Natural Calamities, 02 Floods, Cyclones, etc.
