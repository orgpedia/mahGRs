Preamble: The Government has sanctioned additional funds for Aurangabad district for cattle fodder camps in the areas declared drought by the Government during Kharif-2018 season and Rs. 13,06,44, 668/- and 2245 2149 under Rs. 4,68,19, 410/- aggregating to Rs. 17,74,64, 078/- to the Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad vide reference No. 1 here has been distributed as per the government decision. However, due to non-release of BDS due to technical reasons as on 31.3.2022, an amount of Rs. 13,06,44, 668/- and 2245 2149 under the head Rs. 2,32,23, 451/- aggregating to Rs. 15,38,68, 119/- could not be drawn, the Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad Office, vide reference No. 2 has been communicated and requested to provide the said funds. Accordingly, the matter of disbursing the said fund to the Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad through the Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
The funds required for the disbursement of grants to Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad, for the purpose of providing additional funds for cattle fodder camps in the areas declared drought by the Government in Kharif 2018 season for Aurangabad district and to the Collector, Beed, for the purpose of disbursement of grants under audit till the end of August, 2020 are being disbursed through the Budgetary Distribution System (BEAMS) as shown in the table below.

In the current financial year 2022-23, Demand No. C-6, 2245, Natural Disaster Relief Assistance, 01, Avarshana, 101, Anugraha Assistance, (91) Expenditure as per the norms of State Disaster Response Fund (91) (03) Expenditure on supply of fodder, water, medicines to animal camps and transportation of fodder to camps (mandatory), 31 Subsidiary Grants (non-salary) (22450075) and Demand No. C-6, 2245- Assistance for relief of natural calamities, (92) (07) Fodder supply (Compulsory) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) (2245 2149) Out of the funds made available by way of reallocation under the head (including 0.25% administrative expenses), a total amount of Rs. 15,38,68, 119/- (Rupees Fifteen Crores Thirty Eight Lakhs Sixty Two Thousand One Hundred and Nineteen only) is being made available to the Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad on budget system. The funds should be immediately distributed to the District Collectors of Aurangabad and Beed.
3. With the receipt of the said funds, the payment of fodder camp dues should be completed expeditiously, subject to the decision of the Government in respect of fodder camp, d. As on 31.3.2022 Care should be taken to act in accordance with the terms, conditions and instructions in the Government Decision, Government Circular and Government Letter mentioned in Section 3.
The funds will be disbursed directly to the camp operators through Electronic Clearing Services without maintaining a personal ledger account or bank account. The District Collector should take action on it.
The funds are being allocated to the Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad on a budgetary distribution system. The Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad should ensure that the funds are being used for the purpose for which they have been allocated. Also, the accounts, records/ records of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the grantee as per the prescribed procedure. Also, the expenditure incurred should be matched with the expenditure recorded in the Treasury Office and Accountant General's Office and the utility certificate should be submitted to this department immediately.
Also, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned limit. If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the prescribed time.
