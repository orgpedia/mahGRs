(3) The Controlling Officer shall relieve the officer who has been given the said appointment/ posting immediately to appear in place of the new appointee. Also, the concerned Controlling Authorities should immediately inform the Government on the e-mail website sunil. pandhare@nic. in about the date on which the concerned officer was relieved to appear in place of the new appointment and the date on which he appeared in place of the new appointment.
4. The concerned officer shall not be granted leave without prior permission of the Government for any reason. Also, as per Rule 30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, the parent office should not accept any leave application of the transferred officer. If such applications are received, they should be returned to the concerned officer at the original address by post. If the leave application of the transferred officer is sent to the parent office, the application will not be taken into consideration.
If the concerned officer does not appear at the place of appointment immediately as per the said transfer order or tries to cancel the transfer by exerting any kind of pressure, then disciplinary action will be taken against him considering it as misconduct as the act violates Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979.
