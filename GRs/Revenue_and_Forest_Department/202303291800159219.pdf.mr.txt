Order:
Whereas, the Chief Conservator of Forests & Nodal Officer, Maharashtra State, Nagpur, vide letter under reference No.1 has submitted a proposal for diversion of 0.995 ha. Forest land for grant of mining lease for minor minerals in favour of M/s. Chetak
Stone Quarry, Thane in Village- Borivali, Tal. & Dist. Thane to the Government of Maharashtra;
AND whereas, the Government of Maharashtra has submitted above proposal to MoEF, Govt. of India vide letter under reference No.2,
AND whereas, the Regional Office, MoEF&CC, Govt. of India, Bhopal after due consideration, vide letter under reference No.3, accorded 'In-Principle' approval to
divert the 0.995 ha. Forest Land in favour of M/s. Chetak Stone Quarry, Thane for the

AND whereas, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (HoFF), Maharashtra State and Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests & Nodal Officer, Maharashtra State vide letters under reference No.8 have requested State Government to issue diversion order in the matter;;
NOW, therefore, Government of Maharashtra hereby directs that the Forest Area of 0.995 ha. consisting of Reserved Forest land more specifically described in the proposal and as per details given in the Schedule given below, be diverted to the User Agency i.e. M/s. Chetak Stone Quarry, Thane subject to compliance of following conditions:
1. The User Agency shall obtain Environmental Clearance, if required as per prevalent Acts and Rules.
2. The Forest land shall be handed over to the User Agency after the Competent Authorities have granted a Mining Lease and Environmental Clearance as per relevant Acts and Rules in this regard.
3. The period of diversion of the said forest land shall be co-terminus with the period of the mining lease.
4. The forest land must be handed over to the Forest Department by the User Agency on the date of expiry of the mining lease or 20 years from the date of grant of the mining lease, whichever is earlier.
5. Even if the tenure of the mining lease granted under the MMDR or any relevant Act is more than 20 years, the Forest Department shall not be obliged to renew the Forest Clearance after 20 years. The Forest Department will take a decision based on merit at that time.
6. The User Agency shall comply with all the conditions stipulated in the letters dt.16.07.2007 and dt.20.07.2022 of MoEF&CC referred above. The Chief
Conservator of Forests (T), Thane shall ensure the strict compliance of the conditions by the User Agency and shall furnish the report in this regard to
