Preamble: The Departmental Scrutiny Committee has been constituted under the decision of the Government at No. 6 under reference to scrutinize the proposals received from the District Collector for seeking funds from the State Disaster Response Fund for taking various measures to prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus in the State. The Divisional Scrutiny Committee has examined the proposals received from the concerned District Collectors for seeking funds from the State Disaster Response Fund and sent their recommendations to the Government through a letter at reference number 7 to 10.
The meeting of the State Executive Committee was held on 23.08.2021 to decide on the recommendations received from the Divisional Scrutiny Committee. Accordingly, as per the approval given by the State Executive Committee in its meeting held on 23.08.2021, the matter of disbursement of funds for taking various measures in the context of COVID-19 was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Considering the recommendations received from the Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur, Konkan, Aurangabad and Pune, and the State Disaster Response Fund, the State Executive Committee has decided to meet the expenditure of the State Disaster Response Fund in the current financial year to prevent the spread of the COVID-19 virus. As per the approval given in the meeting dated 23.08.2021, Rs. A sum of Rs. 5604.83 lakhs (Rupees Fifty Six Crores Four Lakhs Eighty Three Thousand only) is being provided.

The funds being disbursed through this Government Resolution will be utilized for the following matters as approved in the meeting of the Divisional Scrutiny Committee and as per reference No. 5 Expenditure should be made on such matters as may be permissible from the State Disaster Response Fund as per the decision of the Government herein.
1. Measures for quarantine center, sample collection and screening : (a) Provision for temporary accommodation, food, clothing, medical care etc. for people affected and sheltered in quarantine camps (other than home quarantine) or for cluster containment operations.
(b) Cost of consumables for sample collection.
(c) Support for checking screening and contact tracing.
2. Procurement of essential equipment's/labs for response to COVID-19: (a) Cost of setting up additional testing laboratories within the Government and the cost of consumable and testing kit.
(b) Cost of personal protection equipment for healthcare, municipal, police and fire authorities (c) Cost of Thermal Scanners, Ventilators, Air Purifiers, Oxygen generation and storage plant in hospitals, strengthening ambulance services for transport of patients, setting up containment zones, Covid-19 hospital, Covid-19 care centers and consumables in the Government hospitals.
As per the decision taken by the State Executive Committee, a total of Rs. A sum of Rs. 5604.83 lakhs (Rupees Fifty Six Crores Four Lakhs Eighty Three Thousand only) is being provided.

The funds sanctioned as above should be disbursed on the BEAMS system to the concerned Divisional Commissioner by Office M-11.
3. Expenditure to be made within the limits prescribed by the Divisional Scrutiny Committee in its meeting. Expenditure on measures to prevent the spread of COVID-19 should also be met from the National Health Mission. However, the Collector should ensure that while spending the above sanctioned funds, there is no deviation from the expenditure on the same aspects from the schemes of other departments of the state government or the National Health Mission.
The following terms and conditions shall be specifically followed while spending the fund: 1) The concerned Collectorate shall ensure that these items are not proposed from other schemes to prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus so that there is no duplication.
(2) Wherever necessary, administrative, technical approvals and tender procedures should be followed.
(3) It shall be ensured that all the criteria for inviting, accepting and procuring tenders, where necessary, shall be in accordance with the procedures and policies of the Government in force at that time.
4) While procuring RT-PCR kits at market price, the prices fixed by Hofkyn Biopharma Limited for RT-PCR kits and VTM kits should be purchased at the minimum price. Also, while purchasing medicines and materials locally, care should be taken to purchase them at minimum rates by comparing the rates of Haffkine and other available rates with the rates received as per the tender.
5) While making purchases, it should be ensured that the rates are uniform for all the districts of the division.
New supply holders/ manufacturers are being made available for Covid Medicine/ Lab Kits- RT-PCR Kit/ Rapid Antigen/ Antibody. So, be aware of this every time you make a purchase. Market prices are falling as new supply holders become available. Therefore, it is not necessary to make a large purchase at once.
