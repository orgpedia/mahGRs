Section 3 (1) of the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006, empowers the Scheduled Tribes and other traditional forest dwellers to acquire individual and collective forest rights or both. Under Rule 16 of the Rules, 2008 and the Amended Rules, 2012 of the said Act, the tribals have been granted forest rights in all the schemes of the government in the sanctioned belts, development of agriculture, creation of resources required to raise the living standards of the tribals and increase the production capacity of agriculture.
Eligible forest lease holders need a reliable and permanent source of water for drinking or agricultural purposes. The availability of water sources will make it easier for forest lease holders to carry out legal farming activities. Therefore, for the purpose of creating water sources, the forest lease holders will be allowed to construct wells in their sanctioned forest lease as per the norms of wells in government schemes subject to the ground water policy of the State.
