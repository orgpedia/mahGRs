The Proposal: -
Funds for Centrally Sponsored Schemes will be disbursed through an integrated structure of Public Financial Management System (PFMS), an Integrated Financial Management Information System (State IFMIS) of the State, and e-Kuber System of the Reserve Bank of India to achieve greater efficiency in cash management at both levels of Central Government and State Government.

The Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India has decided to propose an alternative fund-watt mechanism "SNA-SPARSH" (Real Time System of Integrated Quick Transfers) for this purpose vide Reference No. 3 is taken by order here.
Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India Read No. Additional plans for implementation under SNA-SPARSH have been newly incorporated in the office memorandum at 4. Among the newly included schemes is the scheme for the Bann section in the state of Maharashtra. For this, as per the direction of Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India, it is mandatory for the concerned department of the State Government to open a State Linked Scheme-wise separate Drawing Account with the Reserve Bank of India for implementation of Centrally Sponsored Schemes under "SNA-SPARSH." For this, the Finance Department has issued Reference No. 8 The government decision here has directed to make AAAS under the "SNA-SPARSH" system of centrally sponsored schemes being implemented in Maharashtra. Accordingly, a separate withdrawal account is required to be opened at the Reserve Bank of India for each of the state-affiliated schemes and the Finance Department has issued Reference No. The circular at 9 specifies the procedure to be followed for opening this back account.

Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme PECO implemented by the Forest Department as approved above, a separate Drawing Account is required to be opened with the Reserve Bank of India for Forest Fire Prevention & Management (257). A separate withdrawal account is required to be opened with the Reserve Bank of India for the Forest Fire Prevention & Management (257) central scheme run by the forest department. Also, considering the modalities to be followed by the Finance Department for opening this bank account, the Government was considering appointing a Coordinating Officer as well as a single Coordinating Mechanism for each State Affiliated Scheme for the purpose of the "SNA-SPARSH" mechanism.
