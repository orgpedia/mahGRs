४. In the impugned judgment, the Division Bench adverted to the Maharashtra Deputy Collectors
(Recruitment, Fixation of Seniority and Confirmation) Rules, १९७७ (for short, the “Rules”) and
noted that the promotees were given promotion only on temporary basis and such
promotion were made subject to the approval of the Maharashtra Public Service
Commission. The High Court also accounted the quota available for the promotees and
noticed that the promotion was way beyond the earmarked quota for the promotes,
provided under the Rules. Admittedly, the approval of the Public Service Commission was
never obtained for regularising the promotion. The Division Bench also found that promotion
of the promotees were made de hors the procedure prescribed in the Rules and it was made
hurriedly.
# Page 8
५. In the seniority dispute, the High Court clearly noticed the illegal mode of entry for the
promotees. Also, vacancies were not available for their legitimate entry into the cadre.
Moreover, there was no concurrence of the Public Service Commission. The promotions
were in excess of the quota provided for the promotees.
६. The petitioner has raised the contention that inter-se seniority should be determined on the
basis of continuous length of service as provided under Rule १३(५) of the Rules. This might
be acceptable only if induction into the cadre was made in accordance with the Rules. The
temporary promotion as can be seen was ३ hurriedly made just before the direct recruits
were inducted into the cadre. Due process as envisaged by the Rules was not followed for
making the promotion. This was a fortuitous promotion which does not confer seniority right,
only by virtue of service. The promotions were never approved by the MPSC. Moreover, the
order itself reflected that the promotees can have no seniority claim by such fortuitous
promotion.

judgment, the Special Leave Petition is found devoid of merit. The same is accordingly
dismissed. But the State is bound down to their assurance to not to revert back the
concerned promotees despite the irregularities in their promotion de-hors the Rules,
notwithstanding the present order in the SLP.
