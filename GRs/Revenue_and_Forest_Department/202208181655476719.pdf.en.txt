Introduction: The state government receives revenue from various sources every year. For this, the target of revenue collection is set by the Finance Department to all the concerned administrative departments. Accordingly, the revenue collected from the field offices under the control of the Revenue Department every year is credited under the head' 0029-Land Revenue" and' 0853-Deposits from Minor Mineral Exploration Rules." Considering the revenue collection target given by the Finance Department, it was under the consideration of the Government to fix a consolidated revenue collection target for all the Divisional Commissioners under the said head of accounts as well as the Commissioner of Settlement and Director, Land Records, Maharashtra State, Pune for the financial year 2022-2023.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: For the financial year 2022-2023, a total amount of Rs. 6495.63 crore is being fixed at the State level for collection of revenue under the head" 0029-Land Revenue" and" 0853-Recovery from Minor Mineral Exploration Rules." The details of this target are being finalized for all the Divisional Commissioners as well as Jamabandi Commissioner and Director, Land Records, Maharashtra State Pune, as follows.

The district-wise allocation of the above-mentioned targets shall be made by the concerned Divisional Commissioner taking into account the recovery capacity of that district. Regular monthly review of revenue collection should be done as per the target fixed and the report of revenue collection for the completed month should be submitted to the government by the 10th of the following month.
The revenue collection target fixed by the Government as above should be achieved before 31st March, 2023 by following the prescribed procedures. For this, all the controlling officers should be instructed to strictly follow the following instructions by all the Divisional Commissioners as well as the Jamabandi Commissioner and the Director, Land Records, Maharashtra State Pune, from their level.
(a) The records maintained in the department should match the records maintained in the treasury and the audit office. For this, the deposits should be regularly matched with the treasury office and the certificate of matching the deposits with the treasury office should be submitted to the government through the divisional commissioner's office on the e-mail desktrev. mu- mh@gov. in every month without fail.
(b) The amount of revenue to be credited to the Government should be as per the scheme notations titled" 0029-Land Revenue" and" 0853-Deposits from Minor Mineral Exploration Rules" for accurate data of revenue collection. Apart from the scheme indicators, the concerned should ensure that the revenue figures to be assessed by the government as per the other scheme indicators are not shown under the said head of accounts.
c) The amount of revenue collection from the field office should be deposited to the Government through GRAS system only. The concerned should take care that the government does not deposit the said amount in any other way.
