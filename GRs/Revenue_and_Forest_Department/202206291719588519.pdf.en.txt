Introduction: Immediate response to a disaster can reduce the loss of lives and finances. This needs to be addressed with the help of modern technology. The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) has directed the State and District level Emergency Operation Centres to be equipped with all the necessary equipment. Accordingly, the Incidence Response System (IRS) system will be implemented in the state of Maharashtra considering the disaster risk of the state.
The National Disaster Management Authority has issued guidelines regarding the IRS system.
The IRS system is being implemented in the state as per the recommendations of the technical committee of the State Disaster Management Authority. The approval was given by the state executive committee headed by the chief secretary.
The tender process for setting up the system is based on quality and minimum cost. The company has been selected. The work order has been issued on March 14, 2022. Accordingly, the matter of providing the payments received as a result of the progress of this work was under consideration.
Government Decision: Considering the disaster risk of the state, a total of Rs. 31,95,27, 455/- has been allocated for this purpose. The capex expenditure shown by Prutek for the supply of the system is Rs. 18,76,27, 623/- (Rupees Eighteen Crores Seventy Six Lakhs Seventy Seven Thousand Six Hundred and Twenty Three only) amounting to 60% of Rs. 11,25,76, 574/- (Rupees Eleven Crores Twenty Five Lakhs Sixty Six Thousand Five Hundred and Seventy Four only) May. Prutek is being approved to pay.
Arjun Palav, Accounts Officer and Sanjay Dharurkar, Joint Secretary and Financial Advisor have been declared as Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Controlling Officer respectively.
