The Proposal:
As per the revised estimates under the head Nature Conservation and Wildlife Management (Programme) (2406 0775) for the financial year 2022-23, an amount of Rs. 4408.60 lakh has been fixed.
Reference No. 2 of the Finance Department. Guidelines have been issued for the distribution of financial funds for the year 2022-23 in accordance with the Maharashtra Appropriation Act, 2022 through Circulars 1 and 2.
As per Reference No. 3& 4, for the year 2022-23, Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), Maharashtra State, Nagpur and Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur have sanctioned Rs. 2351.23 lakh has been submitted to the Government for approval. The government has taken the decision in this regard.
Government's decision:
In view of the demand for funds made by the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur, vide letter No. 3 and 4 of Reference No. 1 and 2 of the Finance Department, in the revised estimates for the year 2022-23 under the head Nature Conservation and Wildlife Management (2406-0775), an amount of Rs. 299.35 lakhs is being approved for disbursement subject to the following terms and conditions: i. The amount to be spent on this scheme in the year 2022-23 should be met from the funds received through the Budget Distribution System, target-wise, under the head Accounts Heading 2406 Forestry and Wildlife, 110- Conservation of Wildlife (00) (02) Nature Conservation and Wildlife Management (2406 0775).
ii.The Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur shall disburse the funds after verifying that the activities mentioned in the letter of reference No. 3 and 4 are included in the said scheme.
(iii) The funds should be spent on the proposed works under the scheme only after ascertaining that the administrative and technical approval has been granted by the competent authority as required.
The funds disbursed should be spent within the limits prescribed in the scheme as approved by the Government, subject to the prevailing rules, prevailing policy and guidelines prescribed by the Finance Department from time to time, and in accordance with the objectives set out in the proposal.
