The Proposal:
Maharashtra has a 720 km long coastline. Every year during the rainy season, the sandy areas near the beach are eroded and washed away due to the sea water waves or the erosion occurring during the tide. Due to the high tide caused by sand washing, the coastal villages suffer huge economic losses every year due to the erosion of coastal houses, wadis, coconut and poppy plantations as well as the land along the banks. The objectives of the scheme are to reduce coastal erosion by planting marine species, reduce the intensity of waves like tsunami and avoid economic loss of life and property, attract tourists by creating forests of marine species, increase the income of Gram Panchayats and create employment opportunities in rural areas.
