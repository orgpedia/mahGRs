As per the powers conferred on the Government under Sections 38 and 40 of the Act, 1966, the Government was considering to grant lease on regular terms and conditions for a period of 30 years in a free of cost and revenue free manner and to include the provision of renewal of the said lease as per the relevant prescribed policy. In this regard, the government has taken the following decisions after deliberations in the cabinet meeting on July 27, 2023.
3.51 of Part III, S. No. 404 at Washim. The Government hereby approve grant of permission to the Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan, under the Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India, to establish a Kendriya Vidyalaya, subject to the following terms and conditions, to lease the said land on regular terms and conditions for a period of 30 years in a non-occupancy and revenue free manner as per the powers conferred on the Government under Sections 38 and 40 of the Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966.
Conditions: (1) The Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan, under the Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India, shall not transfer, without prior permission of the Government, any Government land or any part thereof (or any interest therein) granted on lease for a period of 30 years to the Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan, Washim District, for the purpose of establishing a Kendriya Vidyalaya at Washim, in a free of cost and revenue-free manner, by way of sale/ donation/ exchange/ mortgage/ lease, private public participation or through external mechanisms or in any other manner. Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan shall not enter into any pecuniary transaction which would create any kind of rights of any other person/ institution/ company etc. on the said land or any part thereof. Also, the land cannot be divided without prior approval of the government's revenue department.
(2) It shall be mandatory to associate the concerned Collector or his representative in the management of Kendriya Vidyalayas, Washim under the Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India, New Delhi.
(3) The government land can be used only for the purpose for which it is granted. If the land or any part of it is to be used temporarily or permanently for any other purpose, prior approval of the Revenue and Forest Department of the Government will be required.
4) Development on the government land will be done with the permission of the concerned planning authority and according to the approved development control rules. Apart from this, it will also be mandatory to take approval/ prior permission/ no-objection from other concerned departments/ government agencies as required.
5) Orders/ instructions issued from time to time by various departments of the State and Central Government in accordance with the proposed purpose and safety shall be binding on the said institution in this case and it shall be obligatory to take necessary precautions/ care accordingly.
Construction must begin within three years of the acquisition of the land. Otherwise, further action will be taken according to the prevailing policy of the government until the planned use begins.
7) Other regular conditions regarding government land clearance will continue to apply in these places.
