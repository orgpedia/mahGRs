The Proposal:
Due to heavy rains in August-September, 2021, the flood situation in various districts of the state has caused damage to the property of the people in some districts of the state. Proposals for the funds required to provide assistance to the affected people at the rates prescribed under the Government Decision No. 1 have been received from the Divisional Commissioner under the letter No. 4 to 7. Accordingly, the government was considering the matter of distributing funds for the distribution of relief to the affected people.
Government's decision:
The Government has decided to disburse a total amount of Rs. 8577.17 lakh (Rupees Eighty Five Crores Seventy Seven Lakhs Seventeen Thousand only) from the State Disaster Response Fund to the districts as per the State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) as shown in the Annexure attached to this Government decision for the distribution of assistance to the affected persons for the flood situation in various districts of the State due to heavy rains in August-September, 2021, assistance to the affected animals, fully destroyed/ partially damaged kutcha/ pucca houses, huts, gunny bags, fish boats and nets, financial assistance for fish seed farming, assistance for the damage of shopkeepers, todressers and poultry sheds and other permissible matters.
(2) As per the annexure attached to this Government Resolution, the Account-wise funds should be distributed immediately by Office-M-11 to the Divisional Commissioner on the BEAMS system.
(3) The instructions and norms contained in all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned. mentioned above. In accordance with the provisions of Annexure A No.5 to the Government Resolution dated 13.5.2015

The assistance is admissible only to farmers holding land up to a limit of 2 hectares and who have not received any assistance or financial assistance/ subsidy under any other government scheme for the period of tilling of agricultural land, removal of soil debris from hilly agricultural land and fish farm repair/ soil layer removal/ restoration. Further, assistance at prescribed rates is admissible to only small and marginal farmers in case of erosion, erosion, erosion and loss of agricultural land due to alteration of river bed/ stream. This condition must be strictly observed.
4. Beneficiaries to be determined after conducting the panchnama. Although the entire amount is being disbursed on the BeAMS system, after the beneficiary is identified, the concerned Collector should draw the amount from the treasury as per the actual requirement from the point of view of financial discipline and only then the amount should be transferred directly to the bank account of the beneficiaries through online mode. This money should not be withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account. Expenditure should be made within the limits of the grant sanctioned under this order. After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts and the amount spent as per the standard of the State Disaster Response Fund should be recorded in the NDMIS system developed by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all the instructions.
(5) The accounts of the expenditure made out of the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure made shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. The Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund from the concerned and submitting it to the government as soon as the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund and the State Government Fund are spent.
For the above purpose, funds are being made available under the head Head 2245- Financial Assistance for Relief of Natural Calamities, 02 Floods, Cyclones, etc.
This Government decision is based on the Informal Reference No. 116/2022/ Expenditure-9 of the Department of Finance. is being issued with the consent received as on 23.03.2022.
