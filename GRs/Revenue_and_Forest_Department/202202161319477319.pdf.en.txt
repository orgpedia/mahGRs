Preamble: Proposals were sought from all the Divisional Commissioners seeking funds at the rate of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) for the damage caused to public property/ agriculture crops due to heavy rainfall and flood situation in various districts of the State during August-September, 2021. In the intervening period. As per the decision taken in the Cabinet meeting held on 13.10.2021, orders have been issued to provide assistance to the affected farmers for the damage caused by heavy rains from June to October, 2021 at an increased rate as per the decision of the Government at Reference No. 3. at the rates prescribed by the Government under reference No. 3

As per the proposal received from the Divisional Commissioner, the affected farmers for the loss suffered by the agricultural farmers due to heavy rains from August to September, 2021, vide reference No. About 75% of the required funds have been disbursed as per the government decisions in 8th and 9th. Now the matter of disbursing the remaining funds to the affected farmers for the loss of agricultural crops due to heavy rains in August-September, 2021 was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
For loss of crop due to heavy rainfall during August-September, 2021, vide Reference No. 3 lakh from the State Disaster Response Fund and the remaining Rs. 103500.14 lakh (Rupees One Thousand Thirty Five Crore Fourteen Thousand only) is being sanctioned by the Government to the concerned districts through the Divisional Commissioner as indicated district-wise in the annexure attached to this Government Resolution.
(2) As per the annexure attached to this Government Resolution, the Account-wise funds should be distributed immediately by Office-M-11 to the Divisional Commissioner on the BEAMS system.
(3) The instructions and norms contained in all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The enhanced rates of assistance have been approved under Government Decision dated 21.10.2021 at Reference No.3. Out of the increased rate, the amount disbursed under the head of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be spent for providing assistance at the rate decided by the government at State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) reference number 1 and 3 and the difference between the increased sanctioned rate minus the rate of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be shared with the fund disbursed from the State Government's fund. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned.
4. Beneficiaries to be determined after conducting the panchnama. Although the entire amount is being disbursed on the BeAMS system under this government decision, after the beneficiary is identified, the concerned collector should be careful from the point of view of financial discipline to draw the amount from the treasury as per the actual need and then transfer the amount directly to the bank account of the beneficiaries through online mode. This money should not be withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
Expenditure should be made within the limits of the grant sanctioned under this order. After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
Accounts of expenditure from the Fund shall be kept at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure shall be reported to the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office on page 2 of page 5.
