Government Resolution:- The State of Maharashtra experienced extreme rainfall during period of May 2025 to
September 2025 which led to floods causing widespread disruption, destruction of public,
private infrastructure, including roads, power distribution systems, water supply schemes, and agricultural lands, residential and commercial properties. The disaster resulted in the
unfortunate loss of 432 human lives and over 173769 livestock. Essential services such as
healthcare, education and electricity were severely affected.
In response to the situation, the Department of Disaster Management, Relief and Rehabilitation, Government of Maharashtra issued letter dated 30 October 2025, requesting
the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) to provide guidance, technical
support and assistance for conducting a Post Disaster Needs Assessment (PDNA), in the
affected areas of Maharashtra. NDMA has responded positively and conveyed its support for
the State-led PDNA, including the nomination of technical experts to assist the State
Government in the PDNA process, as per the reference mentioned above. The PDNA process shall be held w.e.f. 9 February to 27 February, 2026, in close coordination with sectoral
departments and the concerned District Administrations.
In light of the above, sanction is hereby accorded to the Constitution of sector-wise
PDNA teams for the Maharashtra floods, comprising of the following State Government
Officers and NDMAs nominated Sector Exports as per the Terms of Reference (ToR) for the PDNA teams, to undertake a post-Disaster Needs Assessment (PDNA), in the affected areas of
Maharashtra State.

Terms of Reference
Objectives of the PDNA:- The objective of the PDNA 1s to assess the damage caused by the incessant heavy rainfall and flood in Maharashtra and develop a comprehensive recovery strategy, including financial costs and timelines. Specific objectives include: e Estimate the overall socio-economic impact of the disaster on the district and affected communities.
e Assess the effects and impacts to formulate a recovery strategy for early, medium, and long-term recovery and reconstruction needs in a consolidated report.
eIntegrate disaster risk reduction, "build back better" principles, and address gender and environmental concerns in recovery strategies.
e Develop a recovery strategy that reflects the needs and priorities of the affected communities.
Recommend strategies for Disaster Risk Management.
Recommend institutional mechanisms and policy options to support recovery, reconstruction, and long-term disaster resilience.
Conducting Process for the PDNA: This PDNA exercise will be led by the State Government and will be jointly supported by NDMA, NIDM, Miuinistries/ Departments and other agencies. The following process may be followed for PDNA.
1. Setting Up the Team, Preparation, and Coordination; e The Government of Maharashtra will form a multi-stakeholder State-level coordination team, including sector experts, government representatives, and other stakeholders.
e Define coordination mechanisms, roles, responsibilities, and deadlines for sector teams.
e At the district level, the Deputy Commissioners/ District Collectors will lead the team, supported by the District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) and heads of line departments, supported by Executive Engineers and Asst. Executive Engineers from the departments (PWD, PRI Engineering, Minor Irrigation, Water Authority), Rural Development, MEPMA, SC/ST Development Department, Forest Department, Women and Child Department, Social Welfare Department and other relevant departments as planned by the Revenue (DM). The Maharashtra State Disaster Management Authority will coordinate and facilitate the PDNA.
2. Training Program: e NDMA will conduct training for teams, focusing on data collection, analysis, recovery needs assessment, and report writing.
# Page 5
e NIDM will provide templates for data collection, damage assessment, and identifying recovery needs.
3. Sectoral Assessments:
e Sectoral assessments will be done by sectoral teams constituted by the State Government. Assessments will include detailed assessments for each affected sector.
e The district team will support the PDNA sector teams in the provision of baseline information, damage data, GPS locations, photographs, losses, impact, and identifying recovery needs.
4. Drafting of PDNA report: Following the collection of data, the team will prepare the report. The report will present sector-wise damage. The cost estimates of all the damage will be included in the report.
The basis for estimating the cost of damage, including the unit cost, must be included 11 the sector report.
e State Disaster Management Authority will be responsible for drafting the report by compiling the assessment findings, analyses, and proposed strategies into a comprehensive document.
e NDMA will provide comments on each sector, data collected, unit cost estimates, recovery needs, etc., and NDMA would also constitute a Sector Expert Group (SEG) for field assessment, reviewing and finalizing of PDNA.
e State Disaster Management Authority will incorporate comments by the Sector experts and prepare the final report.
5. Sectoral Expert Group (SEG): e Data analysis and assessment: Analyse the damage data collected by the Districts.
e Identification of Recovery Needs: Work collaboratively to identify short-term, medium-term, and long-term recovery needs based on the findings of the data analysis.
e Development of a Recovery Framework: Formulate a comprehensive recovery framework that outlines strategic priorities and a roadmap for reconstruction and rehabilitation efforts in State Government e Formulation of Sectoral Reports: Oversee the development of detailed sectoral reports, providing in-depth insights into the specific recovery requirements of different sectors.
6. Submission of final report: Government of Maharashtra shall submit the final reports to MHA for approval by the competent authority.
