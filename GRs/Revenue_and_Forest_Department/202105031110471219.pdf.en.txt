Whereas, the State Government is convinced that there is a danger of spread of COVID-19 virus in the State of Maharashtra and therefore, it is imperative to take some urgent measures to prevent and combat the spread of the virus, it is found desirable that in exercise of the powers conferred under Section 2 of the Epidemic Diseases Act, 1897, as well as all other enabling provisions of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, the Government may take further measures to break the chain of corona transmission throughout the State of Maharashtra from 8.00 pm on April 22, 2021 to 7.00 am on May 1, 2021.
Office Attendance: Except for emergency services related to management of COVID-19 pandemic, all government offices (State, Central, Local Authorities) will function with only 15% attendance.
In the case of Central Government offices in the Ministry and Mumbai Metropolitan Region, the Head of the Department may decide to maintain higher attendance after obtaining permission for the same from the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Disaster Management Authority.
In case of other government offices, the head of the office may decide to maintain higher attendance after obtaining permission for the same from the local disaster management authority.
In case of all other offices mentioned under Section 5 of the Order, dated 13th April, 2021, they should work with only 15% of their total attendance or with 5 persons, whichever is more.
Clause 2 of the Order dated 13th April, 2021 states that in respect of all office work required for essential services mentioned below, work shall be carried out with the minimum capacity required for the same and in no case, there shall be more than 50% capacity. Attendance of essential service personnel should also be reduced at the field level, but it can be increased to 100% as per requirement.
Marriage Ceremony: The marriage ceremony, as an event, can only be celebrated in a single Mangal office with a maximum total attendance of 25 persons, not exceeding 2 hours. The place where strict adherence to the restrictions is being done, will be closed until the Covid-19 restrictions are implemented as a disaster.
Private passenger transport: Private passenger transport, except bus services, with driver plus 50% seating capacity, can be operated only for urgent or essential services or for valid reasons. It does not require inter-district and inter-city transport and will be limited to the city of residence of the passenger.
Inter-district and inter-city travel will be allowed only if it is essential for essential services or in case of medical emergencies or unavoidable events like funeral of a family member or serious illness. Any person contravening this order shall be punishable with a fine of Rs 10,000.
Private buses can operate at 50% capacity without any passengers on board.
Inter-city or inter-district travel by private buses shall be subject to the following restrictions:
The bus operator shall restrict the number of stops in a city to a maximum of two stops and inform the local disaster management authority about the same and its schedule. The local disaster management authority, if so desired, may ask for modification.
At the stop where the passengers disembark, all the passengers will be stamped with home quarantine for 14 days. This stamp should be put by the bus operator.
Thermal scanners will be used and if anyone has symptoms, they will be taken to corona treatment centers or hospitals.
Local Disaster Management Authority may decide to conduct RAT test at the place where the passengers alight by arranging for one of the laboratories authorised to provide RAT test service. The cost of this test, if so decided, shall be borne by the passenger/ service provider.
