During this assurance, tribal land in Pune, Nashik and Konkan regions was discussed and the matter of appointing a" one-member study committee" to investigate the matter as promised at the time was under the consideration of the government.
""" GOVERNMENT DECISION: In exercise of the powers conferred by section 36A of the Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966 and the rules made thereunder, with the previous approval of the Government and subsequently by order of the Collector, the lands held by the tribal persons in the State have been transferred to non-tribal persons for\"" non-agricultural purposes."""
Thus, a" One-man Study Committee" under the Chairmanship of Divisional Commissioner, Konkan, empowered under the Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966 and Rules thereunder, is being constituted to study the transfers made in the last 10 years in various districts of the State and make recommendations to the Government.
The functions of the Study Committee constituted to study and make recommendations to the Government as assured in the Legislature are as follows:

During the last ten years, the lands held by tribal persons in the State have been transferred to non-tribal persons for" non-agricultural purposes" in various districts of the State, with the authority conferred under Section 36A of the Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966 and rules thereunder, with the prior approval of the Government and subsequently by order of the Collector. To verify and recommend to the Government the non-agricultural reasons for which such lands have been transferred, whether such lands have been used for the relevant non-agricultural purposes or not.
2. Who has been cheated in the sale and purchase of tribal lands or how? Also, to verify and recommend to the government the non-tribal persons or farmers who have purchased the lands of the tribal persons with the prior permission of the competent authority, whether such lands have been developed by the non-tribal persons or farmers, or by anyone else.
The Study Committee shall examine the said issues and submit its study report along with recommendations to the Government within a period of two months.
