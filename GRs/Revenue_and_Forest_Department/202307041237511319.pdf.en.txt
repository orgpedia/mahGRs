Preamble: As per the letter dated 18.12.2015 of the Union Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, New Delhi, 60:40 sharing pattern has been prescribed for non-recurring expenditure activities under Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Project Tiger. As per the revised share pattern, 60% Central share and corresponding 40% State share is expected to be released for non-recurring expenditure activities under Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Project Tiger. Therefore, it is also the responsibility of the State Government to bear 40% of the salary burden (non-recurring) of the officers/ staff working in the Special Tiger Reserve.
In view of the time taken for approval of Annual Enforcement Report (APO) by the Central Government under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme" Project Tiger," the salary requirement of officers/ staff working in Special Tiger Protection Force (STPF) for the year 2023-24 is estimated to be Rs. 1299.112 lakh is proposed to be made available from the State Share 40% (2406-A102) in the head 01-Pay Objective, Principal Chief Conservator of Forest (Wildlife), State of Maharashtra, Nagpur vide their Reference No. 2 letter submitted to the Government for approval. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
Government Decision: In pursuance of the proposal submitted by the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), Maharashtra State, Nagpur for Government approval vide Reference No. 3, the salary of officers/ staff working in Special Tiger Protection Force (STPF) for the year 2023-24 has been increased from Rs. 1299.112 lakh is being approved under the following conditions from the funds budgeted for the year 2023-24 under the head 01-Pay Goal with State Share 40% (2406-A102) of the amount under the letter of credit attached to the fund.
