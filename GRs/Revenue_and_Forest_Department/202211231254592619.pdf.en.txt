The probationary period of Assistant Conservator of Forest Grade Officers appointed in direct service as per Rule 6 of the Entry into Service Rules 1998 for the post of Assistant Conservator of Forest is 3 years which includes 1 year of field training after completion of 2 years of training in the training institute.
Government Decision Finance Department No. No. 7/16/ Financial Reforms- 1D. Restrictions on creation of posts, filling up of posts and revival of posts as on 20th February, 2016 and consequential review-Amendment of the provision for creation of supernumerary posts for technical purposes. The provision is as follows:
Maharashtra Public Service Commission/ Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has decided to create a time limit for the technicality of the examination period/ training period allotted to the selected candidates.
The power to create more number of posts will henceforth remain with the concerned administrative departments. However, it will be mandatory for the department to create the same number of posts as the candidates who appeared for the training and to mention the training period in the order of creation of the maximum number of posts. " There is a provision.
The Department of Finance, Government of India has decided to provide one year field training to Assistant Conservator of Forest Group-A (Junior Grade) as per the provisions of Rule 6 of the Entry into Service Rules, 1998. To create 12 (Twelve) supernumerary posts in the cadre of Assistant Conservator of Forests Group-A (Junior Grade) as mentioned in 20/02/2016 and to appoint the following officers in the Forest Department in the said number of posts
Under this order, approval is being given for one year's field training.
