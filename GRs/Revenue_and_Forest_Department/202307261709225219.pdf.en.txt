Sustainable Bamboo Management in Gadchiroli District.
The Government of Maharashtra
the Revenue and Forest Department
Government Resolution No: LVSU-2023/ P. No. 114/ F-9
Mantralaya, Mumbai 400 032
Date: July 26, 2023
Dr. Devrao Holi, Member of Legislative Assembly, has raised an interesting suggestion under Rule 105 of the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly in the second monsoon session of 2023.
Preamble: In the second monsoon session of 2023, in the context of collective forest rights in the forest department Gadchiroli, Alapalli and Vadsa and harvesting of bamboo through gram sabhas in the compartments under PESA, an interesting suggestion was raised under Rule 105 of the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly.
During the discussion on this important suggestion, a committee will be appointed on the problems related to bamboo harvesting. The minister has given instructions. The government had then decided to form the committee.
Government Decision: The Forest Department is forming the following committee to decide on sustainable bamboo management in Gadchiroli, Alapalli and Vadsa under collective forest rights and PESA.
1. Dr. Devrao Holi, Member of Legislative Assembly, 2. Chief Conservator of Forests (Regional), Gadchiroli Member, 3. Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Gadchiroli Member, 4. Project Officer, Integrated Tribal Development Project Member, 5. Deputy Conservator of Forests, Gadchiroli Member, 6. Divisional Forest Officer Vigilance, Gadchiroli Forest Circle Member Secretary
Expert members may be invited for the work of the Committee, if required.
The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
1. To suggest necessary reforms to provide employment to the local people through bamboo conservation as per the collective forest rights and rights under PESA.
2. To examine and investigate the complaints regarding excessive harvesting of bamboo in the forest areas outside the collective forest rights and to propose suitable action and recommend suitable policy to prevent any such incidents in future.
3. Improvement in Bamboo plantation programme.
(iv) To make recommendations for providing necessary assistance to the Forest Department for preparation and implementation of the Collective Forest Rights Management Plan.
The term of the said Committee shall be three months from the date of this Government Resolution.
