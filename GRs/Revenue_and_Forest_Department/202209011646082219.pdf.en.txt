Rajeev Nohwar vs. Chief Controlling Revenue Authority, State of Maharashtra, Pune and others. 8,44,500 was paid to the bank through e-SBTR on 16/8/2014. However, on 16/7/2016, the applicant had filed an appeal in the office of the Collector of Stamps, Pune Rural, Pune, seeking refund of the stamp duty due to non-completion of the transaction due to judicial reasons between the applicant and the developer (as the applicant had filed an application before the National Consumer Grievances Redressal Commission, New Delhi against the developer) with the Deputy Inspector General of Registration and Deputy Controller of Stamps, Pune Division, Pune and the Chief Controller Revenue Authority, Pune.
As per Section 47 and 48 (3) of the Maharashtra Stamp Act, 1958, in case of an undelivered and unrecognised document, an application for refund of stamp duty is required to be filed within six months from the date of purchase of the stamp. However, the District Collector of Stamps, Pune Rural, Pune and the Deputy Inspector General of Registration and Deputy Controller of Stamps, Pune Division, Pune and the Chief Controller Revenue Authority, Pune had passed an order stating that the stamp duty was not refundable as the applicant had filed the application after the expiry of six months.
Aggrieved by the said order, the applicants have challenged the order of the Stamp Controlling Revenue Authority, State of Maharashtra, Pune. The High Court of Bombay at No. 10088/2018 was filed.
The Hon'ble Court passed an order dated 22/11/2018 dismissing the petition and holding that the Stamp Authority had taken appropriate action under the Stamp Act.

Aggrieved by this order, the petitioner filed an appeal. Special Leave Petition No. 699/2019 filed by the Hon'ble Court vide order dated 24/9/2021 and 18/11/2021 as a special case, the Stamp Duty Refund to the applicant amounting to Rs. 8,44,500/- was issued by the original E-S. The Collector of Stamps, Pune was ordered to pay the interest at the rate of 6% D.C.D. within one month of filing. As per the Supreme Court's order, the concerned applicants have been given a refund of the stamp duty paid by them. Now, Ma. As per the court's decision, the matter of payment of interest to the applicant was under consideration of the government.
