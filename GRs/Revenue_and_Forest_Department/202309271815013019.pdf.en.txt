The said area is covered under the provisions of Rules 5 and 6 of the Maharashtra Land Revenue (Disposal of Government Land) Rules, 1971, as well as the Government Circular, Revenue and Forest Department, No. LND 1071/183736-A II, No. As per the provisions of 09.09.1971, the transfer of Cultural Affairs to the Directorate on a consumption free and revenue free basis is being approved subject to the following terms and conditions.

Terms and Conditions: (1) If the land is to be used for purposes other than the approved purpose, development of the land through BOT/ BOOT/ PPP or other private participation, giving it on lease, etc., it will be necessary to take prior permission of the Revenue and Forest Department. Also, the department will be required to pay such amount of free/ unearned money as may be prescribed for the same.
(ii) The planned utilization of the land finally sanctioned by the Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs is required to be initiated within the next 3 years.
(iii) The said land or any part thereof shall not be mortgaged or transferred in any manner without the prior permission of the Department of Revenue and Forest. Also, no action can be taken that would create a third party interest.
(iv) In future, if the Directorate of Cultural Affairs stops the use of the said land for the approved purpose or violates any of the conditions of the grant of the land and if the matter is brought to the notice of the Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs, but no appropriate action is taken regarding the removal of such violation within the prescribed period, the Revenue and Forest Department of the State Government shall have the right to take back the said land along with the construction thereon.
As Rang Bhavan has been declared as a heritage structure, it will be necessary to seek the opinion/ no-objection of the Mumbai Heritage Conservation Committee before undertaking construction or repair work in the said building.
(vi) The Revenue Department shall have full powers to use the closed theatre and other ancillary facilities being set up by the Directorate of Cultural Affairs free of cost as per requirement.
(vii) The Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs shall be fully responsible for the conservation of the said land.
(viii) The Collector, City of Mumbai shall have the power to prescribe such other terms and conditions as he may deem necessary and expedient.
