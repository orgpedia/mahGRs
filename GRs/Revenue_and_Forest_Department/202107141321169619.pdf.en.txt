On not changing the lien of government lands provided for essential services like petrol pumps, CNG gas, etc.
The Government of Maharashtra
the Revenue and Forest Department

Hatatma Rajguru Chowk, Madam Cama Marg,

Date: July 14, 2021
Government Notification No: Land 2018/ PR No. 90/ J-1, dated 08.03.2019
As per the provisions of Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966 and Maharashtra Land Revenue (Disposal of Government Land) Rules, 1971 as well as various Government Orders of the Department, provisions have been made for leasing out land for essential purposes for 30 years. for essential services (e.g. (e.g. Petrol Pumps/ CNG Gas etc.) Government land provided to companies/ institutions/ individuals, if converted for any other purpose in future, will not serve the public interest and cause inconvenience to the public and loss of revenue to the Government. The Government Notification dated 8.3.2019 provides for making the occupier-1 of the land given on occupancy/ lease for commercial purpose by paying 50% of the cost of annual rate statement upto 3 years from the date of notification and 60% after 3 years from the date of notification. Considering the fact that by using these provisions, such places provided for the purpose of public interest for the essential sector can be converted into Class-1 and the place can be used for personal benefit, it was under the consideration of the government to decide a policy in this regard.
Government Decision: In order to provide public amenities to the citizens as well as essential services to various organizations, central and state government departments, corporations, individuals, the lands/ plots provided by the government for such essential services shall not be converted into Class-II/ Tenant of Government land. Further, this condition should be clearly mentioned in the land supply order while providing land for essential services.
