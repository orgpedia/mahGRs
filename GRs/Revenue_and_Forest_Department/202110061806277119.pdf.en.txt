The Proposal:
The floods in various districts of the state due to heavy rains in July, 2021 have caused damage to the property of the citizens in some districts of the state. As per the decision taken in the Cabinet Meeting held on 03.08.2021 regarding the assistance for the damage caused by this flood, the orders for assistance have been issued under the Government Decision No. 3. As per the rates prescribed under Government decision at Reference No.3 and as per the rates prescribed under Government decision at Reference No.1, the affected persons will be given relief under Reference No.3. 4 crore has been allocated for the assistance. However, the amount was not disbursed to the affected citizens for the losses suffered by the farmers due to heavy rains in July, 2021. As per the directions given in the Cabinet meeting dated 22.09.2021, the matter of disbursement of funds to the farmers for the losses caused by heavy rains in July, 2021 was under consideration of the Government at the rate of State Disaster Response Fund.
Government's decision:
The State Disaster Response Fund for disbursement of assistance to the affected people for the loss of agricultural crops due to floods in various districts of the State caused by heavy rains in July, 2021, as mentioned in the accompanying statement, has released a total of Rs. 36567.00 lakh (Rupees Three Hundred and Fifty Five Crore Sixty Six Lakh only) is being sanctioned by the Government to the districts through the Divisional Commissioner.

(2) As per the annexure attached to this Government Resolution, the Account-wise funds should be distributed immediately by Office-M-11 to the Divisional Commissioner on the BEAMS system.
(3) The instructions and norms contained in all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned.
4. Though the entire amount is being disbursed on BEAMS system under this Government decision, after the beneficiary is identified, the concerned Collector should draw the amount from the treasury as per actual requirement from the point of view of financial discipline and then the amount should be transferred directly to the bank account of the beneficiary through online mode. This money should not be withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account. Expenditure should be made within the limits of the grant sanctioned under this order. After the completion of the distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and the details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts and the amount spent as per the standard of the State Disaster Response Fund should be recorded in the NDMIS system developed by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all the instructions.
(5) The accounts of the expenditure made out of the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure made shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. The Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund from the concerned and submitting it to the government as soon as the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund and the State Government Fund are spent.
For the above purpose, funds are being provided as shown in the accompanying statement under the head Principal Demand No. C-6, Principal Accounts Head 2245- (91) (06) Assistance to farmers for crop loss due to natural calamities (Compulsory), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) (2245 2452).
This Government Resolution is being issued with the concurrence received under the Informal Reference No.: 390/2021/ Expenditure-9 of the Department of Finance, dated 06.10.2021.
