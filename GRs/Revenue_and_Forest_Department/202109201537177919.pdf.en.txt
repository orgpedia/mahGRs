The Proposal:
The Government has accorded approval for continuation of 111 temporary posts created under the Post-Plan Scheme of Nature Conservation and Wildlife Conservation, Tiger Reserve Melghat during the year 2020-2021 from 01.03.2020 to 28.02.2021 as per the Government decision in Reference No.
Now, as per Reference No. 5, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur has submitted a proposal to the Government for approval to continue 111 temporary posts created under the post-plan scheme of Nature Conservation and Wildlife Conservation, Tiger Reserve Melghat for the year 2021-2022 from 01.03.2021 to 28.02.2022.
Provisional posts included in the pattern of the Department and all existing temporary posts created after approval of the review earlier but not included in the pattern, from 01.09.2021 to 28.02.2022.

The power to grant extension has been delegated to the administrative departments under the Government Resolution of Reference No. 7 of the Department of Finance.
Government's decision:
As per the decision of the Government of Finance Department in Reference No. 7, 111 temporary posts created under the Post-Plan Scheme of Nature Conservation and Wildlife Conservation, Tiger Reserve Melghat are being approved for continuation in the year 2021-2022 from 01.09.2021 to 28.02.2022.
Expenditure on establishment of 111 temporary posts created under the post-plan scheme nature conservation and wild animal conservation, tiger reserve Melghat shall be met from the provision available in the financial year 2021-2022 under the head 2406-Forestry and Wildlife (Non-Plan), 02 Ecological Forestry and Wildlife, 110 Wildlife Conservation (00) (00) (01) Nature Conservation and Wild Animal Conservation (2406 0766).
This Government Decision, Government Decision No.1004/ Q.No.12/ (Part-II)/ 2004/ Financial Reforms-1 dated 04.10.2004 of the Finance Department and Government Decision No. No. 8/16/ APC, dated 10.08.2021 is being issued in pursuance of the powers conferred on the Administrative Department.
