The Proposal:
General Administration Department No. 3 and No. Under Section 7, consolidated guidelines have been issued for direct recruitment to the nominated quota posts in Group-B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D cadres under the purview of Ex-Secondary Service Selection Board (outside the purview of Maharashtra Public Service Commission). Further, the decision to fill up all the posts in the cadre of Clerks in Group-C in the State Government Offices through the Maharashtra Public Service Commission has been taken in the context of the General Administration Department No. 6 here. This decision was taken by the Government on 02.11.2022.
Accordingly, the No. 2 It was under the consideration of the Government to constitute district-level committees, regional committees and state-level committees for the recruitment of new posts and to issue revised procedures and guidelines accordingly.
Government's decision:
The following Selection Committees are being constituted for direct recruitment to the posts of Group-B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D cadres in the nomination quota under the purview of the Ex-Secondary Service Selection Board under the Forest Department (outside the purview of Maharashtra Public Service Commission).

Format of the examination: (a) The examination should be conducted in objective multiple choice format through computer based test by the company. Each question will carry a maximum of 2 marks (100).
(b) Marathi, English while selecting candidates for all other posts in Group-B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D for which physical and professional tests are not required. A total of 200 questions will be asked in the exam, each carrying 50 marks. Based on the marks obtained by the candidates in such examination, a merit list shall be prepared from the candidates who have secured at least 45% of the total marks and the eligible candidates in the list shall be recommended as per merit.
For the posts for which a physical test or proficiency test is required to be taken, the exam for questions on Marathi, English, general knowledge and intellectual test should be conducted for a total of 120 marks (total 60 questions) with 30 marks each and a physical test/ professional test of 80 marks. However, only those candidates who secure at least 45% marks in the examination will be allowed to take the Physical Test/ Professional Test. Based on the performance in the written test/ skill test/ interview, the candidates will be shortlisted for the interview.
(d) The selection of candidates for the posts in the cadre requiring qualification less than the passing of the School Examination, where it is necessary to conduct a physical efficiency test, shall be made on the basis of the marks obtained by the candidates in the 60 marks professional test and where necessary 40 marks physical efficiency test. For the posts for which there is no need to take a physical fitness test, a professional test of 100 marks should be taken and based on the marks obtained by the candidates in this, the eligible candidates in the list should be recommended on the basis of merit.
(e) In order to be included in the merit list, the candidate must have secured at least 45% of the total marks.
(f) The candidates selected for the District level posts should have knowledge of local aspects/ features of the concerned district such as geography, social history, climate etc. This should be taken into account when preparing the question paper.
(c) Oral Examination (Interview shall not be conducted) in the selection of candidates for any post in Group-B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D.

10. In case of equal marks to the candidates: While preparing the result of the examination, the priority in the merit list of the eligible candidates who have equal marks in the examination shall be determined as follows: a) Suicide affected farmer's child shall be given first preference.
(b) If there is no child of the farmer who has committed suicide among the candidates with the same marks or if more than one candidate has the same marks as per the above A.No. (a), then the candidate who is older in age should be given preference.
(a) In the case of candidates who are found to be same in both conditions (a) and (b) above, the candidate possessing higher educational qualification (Post Graduate, Graduate, Higher Secondary School Examination, Secondary School Examination, etc.) shall be given preference on the last date of submission of application.
(d) In the case of candidates who are also found to be same in all the three conditions mentioned above in the Annexure (a), (b) and (c), preference shall be given to the candidate having higher level of marks in the minimum educational qualification prescribed in the rules for admission to the service of the said post.
