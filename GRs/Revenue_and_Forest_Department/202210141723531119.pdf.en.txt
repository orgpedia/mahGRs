Introduction: Floods due to heavy rains, landslides or large cracks in the ground, hillslides or landslides, etc. There is no comprehensive policy for rehabilitation of villages/ areas/ hamlets which need to be permanently rehabilitated due to these calamities. The government has issued circular No. 1 regarding rehabilitation of villages affected by natural disasters, home improvement, reconstruction of houses, civic amenities, etc. CTN- 1083/217/ R-12, Dy. Detailed guidelines have been issued under 10/2/1983 wherein instructions have been issued on how to rehabilitate the affected villages by taking policy decisions at the government level from time to time. Earlier, the government had taken a policy decision at the government level to rehabilitate the flood-affected villages in the flood situation caused by heavy rains in 2005. Detailed guidelines have been issued under 9/1/2006. But it has been more than 15 years since the government decided this policy and considering the current economic, physical and social situation as well as the prevailing policies of the government, it is under the consideration of the government to decide a comprehensive policy that will be applicable only to rural areas in order to rehabilitate the villages/ areas/ wadis/ tandas affected/ disaster prone areas due to floods and other natural disasters.
Government Decision: Floods due to heavy rains, landslides or large cracks in the ground, hillslides or cracks, etc. Only those affected in rural areas due to natural calamities and the village/ area/ wadi/ tandas prone to these calamities are being immediately shifted to new places and the following criteria are prescribed for the rehabilitation of eligible beneficiaries in the village.
The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will take a decision regarding permanent rehabilitation of the affected village/ area/ wadi/ tandas in a rural area by shifting them to a new place due to natural calamities such as floods caused by heavy rains, landslides or large cracks in the ground, landslides or cracks.

5. When a village/ area/ wadi/ tandas is decided to be relocated to a new place as above, it should be relocated in the proposed manner following the following:
a) Process of land acquisition: The land for rehabilitation should preferably be government land, but if government land is not available or not suitable for rehabilitation, private land can be acquired. In view of this requirement, the process of land acquisition will be carried out in accordance with the provisions of sections 11 (1), 19 (1), 21 (1), 40 (1) and 108 of the Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013. For this purpose, the District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will make the landholders aware of the benefits under the Act/ Direct Purchase Policy in the case of land acquisition for resettlement in new/ other places and the landholders will have the right to choose one of these options according to the Land Acquisition Act/ Direct Purchase Policy.
Eligible beneficiaries for rehabilitation: 1. The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) shall prepare a list of eligible families for rehabilitation after conducting a survey of the concerned village/ area/ tandas/ habitations and shall take a final decision after scrutinizing/ examining the objections raised by giving due local publicity.
The definition of" family" includes a person in the family, his or her spouse, minor children, minor brothers and minor sisters who are dependent on him. Also, widows, divorcees and women deserted by their families will be treated as separate families.
Explanation (a) An adult person, who is accompanied by or without spouse or children or dependants, shall be deemed to be a separate family under this Act.

e) Action to be taken by the District Disaster Management Authority:
General: The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will issue a preliminary notification on rehabilitation and give it wide local publicity.
The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will finalise the possible rehabilitation plan after discussing with the affected families. While finalizing the plan, the number of families who will accept alternative resettlement, the number of families who will accept self-refoulement and the number of families who will refuse voluntary resettlement will be finalized.
In cases where the number of families to be rehabilitated is 15 or less, they are generally to be rehabilitated by themselves and the benefits of voluntary resettlement shall be admissible to them. Depending on the local situation, the District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) may consider rehabilitation at an alternative site for them as well.
The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will determine the area of the plot to be allotted and the area required for the civic facility.

2. Allotment of land and procedure: 1. District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will publish the government approved rehabilitation plan, alternative land plan, civic amenities etc. in the village.
The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will inform all the rehabilitated families about the plan.
The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will normally allot plots to all the families unanimously. In case of non-compliance, the District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will allot the plot through lottery and the final decision will be taken by the District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA).
(4) Before handing over the title deeds of the plots to be provided to the families concerned, it shall be necessary to make a provision in the title deeds that their original land shall be handed over by those families in the name of the Government and such record shall be taken in the Government Department. Such original land can be used for social forestry or for public purposes as deemed fit by the District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA).
Development of Rehabilitated Land: 1. The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) shall prepare a development plan for the development of the rehabilitated land along with the civic amenity works and submit it to the State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA) for approval.
The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will develop the rehabilitation site as per the guidelines of the Disaster Management, Relief and Rehabilitation Department after the approval of the State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA).
3. Rehabilitation needs to be carried out in a speedy and limited manner, preferably immediately and the affected families should be shifted to safer places. Once the civic amenities are created in place of the alternative, the civic amenities at the original place will be disconnected.
The District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) will declare the rehabilitation as over after the site is fully developed and the plot is allotted. After such declaration, the civic amenities in the plan will be deemed to have been handed over to the Gram Panchayat.
