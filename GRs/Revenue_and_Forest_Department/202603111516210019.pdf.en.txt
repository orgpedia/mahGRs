> This 'In-principle' approval is issued subject to the following conditions:-
The legal status of the land shall remain unchanged.
User agency shall submit revised KML file including the proposed road.
Deputy Conservator of Forest shall ensure that no more than 534 trees shall be
felled.
A perusal of the KML file of the proposed area for compensatory afforestation
shows that the area is fully covered with trees. If afforestation is not possible on
the said area, the Deputy Conservator of Forests will propose other degraded forest land for Compensatory afforestation and accordingly, implement the
compensatory afforestation scheme on the degraded forest land and submit the
record of rights, Maps and KML file along with the Stage-I approval.
User Agency shall submit the Record of Rights of Compensatory Afforestation
land at Beat. Hodri (Compt. No.771), along with Stage-I approval.
6) The demarcation of the proposed forest area shall be carried out as per the direction of the DCF concerned at the cost of User Agency.
7) Compensatory Afforestation:-
a) Compensatory afforestation shall be taken up by the Forest Department over 10.00 ha. Reserved degraded forest land at Compt. No. 771 in village-Block
Forest, Beat- Hodari, Round- Laheri in Gadchiroli District at the cost of the
User Agency;
b) The cost of compensatory afforestation at the prevailing wage rates as per compensatory afforestation scheme and the cost of survey, demarcation and erection of permanent pillars if required on the CA land shall be deposited in
advance with the Forest Department by the project authority;
8) Net Present Value:-
a) The Forest Department shall charge the Net Present Value (NPV) for the
5.00 ha. Forest land to be diverted under this proposal from the User
Agency as per the orders of the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India dated 30/10/2002, 01/08/2003, 28/03/2008, 24/04/2008 and 09/05/2008 in IA
No.566 in WP (C) No.202/1995 and as per the guidelines issued by the Ministry vide letters No.5-1/1998-FC (7.11), dated 18/09/2003 as well as
letter No.5-2/2006-FC, dated 03/10/2006 and 5-3/2007-FC, dated
05/02/2009 & File No.5-3/2011-FC (Vol-I), Dated 06/01/2022 Sha this
regard.
# Page 3
b) Additional amount of the NPV of the diverted forest land, if any, becoming
due after finalization of the same by the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India on
receipt of the report from the Expert Committee, shall be charged from the
User Agency. The User Agency shall furnish an undertaking to this effect.
9) All the funds received from the User Agency under the project shall be
Transferred / deposited to CAMPA _ fund only 'through-e-portal
(https://parivehs.nic.in);
10) The complete compliance of the FRA, 2006 shall be ensured by way of
prescribed certificate from the concerned District Collector.
11) No labour camp shall be established on the forest land and the User Agency
shall provide firewood preferably alternate fuels to the labourers and the staff
working at the site so as to avoid any damage and pressure on the nearby forest
areas;
12) The User Agency shall ensure that because of this project, no damage is caused
to the wildlife available in the area;
13) The user agency shall ensure that any tree felling shall be done only when it is
unavoidable and that too under strict supervision of the State Forest Department;
14) The user agency in consultation with the concerned Deputy Conservator of
forest shall create and maintain alternate habitat/home for the avifauna, whose
nesting trees are to be cleared in this project. Bird's nests artificially made out of
eco-friendly material shall be used in the area, including forest area and human
settlements, adjoining the forest area being diverted for the project, if required.
15) The total forest area utilized for this project shall not exceed 5 ha. and the forest
area diverted shall not be used for any purpose other than those shown in the
diversion proposal. The User Agency shall furnish an undertaking to this effect;
16) The cost of felling of trees shall be deposited by the User Agency with the State
Forest Department;
17) User Agency shall obtain Environment Clearance as per the provisions of the
Environmental (Protection) Act, 1986, if applicable;
18) Sufficient firewood, preferably the alternate fuel, shall be provided by the User
Agency to the labourers after purchasing the same from the State Forest
Department or the Forest development Corporation or any other legal source of
alternate fuel;
19) The User Agency shall ensure compliance to all acts, rules, regulations and
guidelines of the Ministry for the time being in force, as applicable to the subject
project;
20) The layout plan of the proposal shall not be changed without prior approval of
State Government (Forest Department).

21) The period of diversion under this approval shall be co-terminus with the period
of lease to be granted in favour of the user agency or the project life, whichever
is less.
22) The forest land shall not be used for any purpose other than that specified in the
project proposal.
23) The forest land proposed to be diverted shall under no circumstances be
transferred to any other agencies, department or person without prior approval of
the State and Central Government.
24) This approval shall be valid only for the diversion of forest land outside the
confines of the Protected Areas.
25) This sanction is subject to the final clearance by the competent authorities for
the project.
26) All other conditions as may be mandatory under relevant Acts, Rules and
Guidelines shall be complied by the User Agency.
27) Violation of any of these conditions will amount to violation of Van
(Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980 and action would be taken as
per the provision of Adhiniyam and rules / guidelines issued there under;
28) Any other condition that the Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate
Change may stipulate from time to time in the interest of conservation,
protection and development of forests & wildlife.
29) Any breach of the conditions by the User Agency will be treated as violation of
the provisions of the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980.
30) The compliance report shall be uploaded on e-portal (https://parivesh.nic.in/).
