Preamble: The rates and criteria of assistance to be provided to the persons affected by natural calamities on the lines of the Central Government's natural calamity norms. 13.5.2015 has been fixed. The State Government has decided to provide funds at enhanced rates from time to time on various aspects of assistance prescribed under this Government decision. Forest and Revenue Department. In the Government Resolution dated 13.5.2015, the following provisions have been made to provide assistance to farmers for loss of agricultural land up to a limit of 2 hectares as per the norms of the Central Government.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| (a) Siltation on farm land (accumulation of more than 3 inches of sand/ silt/ soil layer is required to be certified by an authorized officer of the State Government) | Rs.12,200/-per hectare for each subject (However, the beneficiaries should not have availed assistance or financial assistance/ subsidy under any other Government scheme). |
| (b) Removal of soil debris from hilly agricultural land.                                                                                                               |
| c) Repairing fish farms/ removing/ restoring the soil layer                                                                                                            |
| d) Agricultural land washed away due to erosion, erosion and change in river course/ flow                                                                              | As per the revenue records, only small and marginal farmers owning agricultural land will be eligible for assistance at the rate of Rs.37,500/-per hectare.                 |
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
As per the norms of the Central Government, the assistance given for the above mentioned loss of agricultural land and other losses is admissible only to small and marginal farmers, therefore, the farmers holding more than 2 hectares of land are not given any kind of assistance even if their agricultural land is damaged due to the above-mentioned reasons. However, there was a demand from the central government to relax this criterion and provide the benefit of this scheme to farmers holding more than 2 hectares of land.
A decision in this regard was pending with the government for some time.

Government Resolution No: CLS-2022/ P.S. No.338/ M-3
Government's decision:
Forest and Revenue Department The Government has decided to allow the farmers holding more than 2 hectares of land, who meet the prescribed terms and conditions, to receive the assistance as per the Government decision dated 13.5.2015.

All the Collectors should submit proposals to the Government through the Divisional Commissioner regarding the loss of agricultural land due to natural calamities in the current season by adopting the prescribed procedures. At the time of submission of the application, it should be ensured that the other conditions prescribed are fulfilled. Also, e-crop inspection or other available records should be taken to ensure the loss of farm land.
The expenditure for the above purpose shall be met from the funds sanctioned and available with the State Government under the head of accounts under head 2245-Finance for relief of natural calamities, 02 floods, cyclones, etc.
