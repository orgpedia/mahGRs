The documents are registered under the provisions of the Registration Act, 1908 and the Maharashtra Registration Rules, 1961. Rule 44 of the Maharashtra Registration Rules, 1961 lists the matters to be examined by the Secondary Registrar before registering a document. Sections 21 and 22 of the Registration Act, 1908 provide that the description of the income in the document must be sufficient to identify it in such a way that it does not create ambiguity.
The office of the Inspector General of Registration has issued a notification regarding the documents being registered on the basis of the Maharashtra Prevention of Fragmentation of Holding Land and its Amalgamation (Amendment) Act, 2015.
The explanatory circular was issued on 12/07/2021. Mr. Govind Ramling Solpure and 2 others protested against this circular. In the High Court, Bombay, Aurangabad Bench, in Writ Petition No. 2111/2022 was filed. In pursuance of the said writ petition. The Bombay High Court, Aurangabad Bench. Decision has been passed on 05/05/2022. In this case, In pursuance of the said judgment of the High Court, Bombay, Aurangabad Bench, the Office of the Inspector General of Registration, Shri. The Bombay High Court, Aurangabad. Review appeal has been filed on 06/06/2022. His decision is still pending.
Section 21 and 22 of the Registration Act mentioned above and Rule 44 of the Maharashtra Registration Rules, 1961 as well as the circular issued by the Office of the Inspector General of Registration. As on 12/07/2021, the Secondary Registrar is facing difficulties in registering the documents in compliance with the said Section, Rules and Circular. Accordingly, it has been decided during the discussion in the Council of Revenue held on 23/2/2023 to constitute a committee under the chairmanship of Inspector General of Registration and Controller of Stamps, Maharashtra State, Pune to discuss/ discuss and propose amendments in the provisions of Section 21 and 22 of the Registration Act with a view to empowering them and making them more effective. Therefore, the Government was contemplating to constitute a Committee to make recommendations to the Government for amending sections 21 and 22 of the Registration Act, 1908.
Government Decision: Section 21 and 22 of the Registration Act and Rule 44 of the Maharashtra Registration Rules, 1961 as well as the circular issued by the Office of the Inspector General of Registration. To remove the difficulty faced by the Secondary Registrar in registering the document by following the said section, rules and circular while registering the document as per 12/07/2021.

The functions of the Committee are: (1) To make recommendations to the Government after making a comprehensive study with a view to empowering sections 21 and 22 of the Registration Act and to amend the provisions therein to make them more effective.
(ii) Sections 21 and 22 of the Registration Act and Rule 44 of the Maharashtra Registration Rules, 1961 and the circular issued by the Office of the Inspector General of Registration. To remove the difficulty faced by the Secondary Registrar in registering the documents by following the said section, rules and circular while registering the documents as per 12/07/2021.
(3) The Chairman of the Committee shall have the right to invite to the meetings of the Committee, in addition to the above-mentioned members, informants or learned officers or experts at the field level for this purpose.
(4) The Chairman of the Committee shall, from time to time, give directions for the meeting, at which time all the above Committee members shall attend the meeting.
The committee will submit a detailed report to the government in a month.
