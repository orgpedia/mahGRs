Preamble: Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Project Tiger, 100% subsidy was received from the Central Government for non-recurring expenditure and 50% for recurring expenditure for development and conservation of Tiger Reserves.
As per the letter dated 18.12.2015 of the Union Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, New Delhi, the share pattern of 60:40 under 100% central share was notified for non-recurring expenditure activities under Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Project Tiger. As per the revised share pattern, 60% Central share and corresponding 40% State share is expected to be released for non-recurring expenditure activities under Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Project Tiger.
As per the revised fund share pattern, the State Government will have to bear 40% of the burden, so it is also the responsibility of the State Government to bear 40% of the burden of salary of the officers/ staff working in the Special Tiger Protection Force.
Government Decision: In view of the time taken by the Central Government to approve the Annual Implementation Report (APO) under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Project Tiger.
