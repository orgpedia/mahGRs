In the Kharif season of 2018, as the government declared drought, cattle fodder camps were set up in Man, Khatav and Phaltan taluks of Satara district. For the financial year 2019-20 and 2020-21, a total of Rs. A sum of Rs. 386,68,83, 273/-was disbursed. For the payment of pending dues for the month of April to August, 2019, out of the grant provided by the Government, Rs. 6,92,74, 677/- has been sanctioned by Pune Division. Dedicated on 31.3.2020.
Thereafter, the Divisional Commissioner, Pune, sanctioned Rs. A sum of Rs. 6,12,53, 481/-has been requested to be provided for payment of fodder camps.
As per the Government Decision, Revenue and Forest Department, dated 4.5.2019, instructions were issued to use Cattle Camp Management System, a mobile based system for keeping records of livestock in fodder camps. This procedure of presence scanning by putting tags in the ears of the cattle in the fodder camps was made mandatory from the date 7.6.2019. It was also instructed to scan the presence of cattle tags and not to register it every day but once a week. As per the decision of the government, the Divisional Commissioner, Pune, was informed about providing information about how the cattle in the fodder camp in Man, Khatav and Phaltan taluka have been registered in the Cattle Camp Management System, and providing information about the pending payment of these fodder camps. Accordingly, the Divisional Commissioner, Pune has decided to extend the period from February 2023 to February 2023. As informed vide letter dated 28.3.2023, the matter of payment of pending dues of cattle feed camps in Man and Khatav taluks was under consideration of the Government.
