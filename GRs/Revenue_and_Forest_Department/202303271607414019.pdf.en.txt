The State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) has been constituted under Section 48 (2) of the Disaster Management Act, 2005 to provide assistance to the citizens affected by various natural calamities occurring in the State. The scheme is 75 per cent funded by the central government and 25 per cent by state governments. For the financial year 2022-23, Rs. 3608.80 crore has been approved for State Disaster Response Fund. It is Rs. A sum of Rs. 1804.40 crore has been earmarked for response and assistance. The State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) can be used only for 12 natural calamities such as cyclone, drought, earthquake, fire, flood, tsunami, hailstorm, landslide, avalanche, cloudburst, locust attack, cold wave and severe cold.
The rates, terms and conditions of various items of assistance and assistance for the distribution of assistance from the State Disaster Response Fund have been fixed by the Central Government. The assistance is admissible from the State Disaster Response Fund at the prescribed rates and for the prescribed matters. Under Section 20 of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, a State Executive Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of the Chief Secretary.
The State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) has been placed under the State Executive Committee headed by the Chief Secretary. Therefore, with the approval of the State Executive Committee, funds are sanctioned for the distribution of assistance to the citizens affected by the above mentioned 12 natural disasters.
