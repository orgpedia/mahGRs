Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests & Nodal Officer, MS, Nagpur has
submitted a proposal for diversion of 1.2729 ha. Forest land for laying of underground
Optical Fiber Cable along the Road in RoW of SH-382, ODR-65, ODR-21 and VR-125 from
Arenda to Pirimili to Yerminar route in Aheri Tehsil of Dist. Gadchiroli in the State of
Maharashtra.
Order:
In exercise of powers under general approval given by MoEF&CC, Govt. of India
vide Handbook of FCA Guidelines-2019, Chapter 4 (para-4.2), Government of Maharashtra is pleased to grant 'In-Principle' approval to the diversion of 1.2729 ha. Forest Land under
section-2 of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 for laying of underground Optical Fiber
Cable along the Road in RoW of SH-382, ODR-65, ODR-21 and VR-125 from Arenda to
Pirimili to Yerminar route in Aheri Tehsil of Dist. Gadchiroli in the State of Maharashtra.

2. This 'In-principle' approval is issued subject to the following conditions:
1) The legal status of the land shall remain unchanged.
2) User Agency shall submit the Record of Rights of proposed land at village- Yerminar
(New Comp. No. 125 & 736), along with Stage-I compliance.
3) The concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests shall ensure legal status of proposed
Forest land, before granting working permission.
4) Concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests shall ensure that the proposed area is not a
part of Wildlife / Tiger Corridor, before granting working permission.
5) User Agency shall defray the cost of plantation (including ten years maintenance) of
100 trees shall be levied from the User Agency towards Compensatory Afforestation.
As far as possible, a mixture of local indigenous species along with 10% RET species
of Gadchiroli District shall be planted and monoculture of any species may be
avoided. Details of land proposed for Plantation (Record of Rights & KML File), shall be submitted before Stage-II/Final approval.
6) The Optical Fiber Cable Line will be laid within maximum size of the trench of 2.00
meters depth & 0.45 meter width with cable line under the OFC pipeline.
7) If User Agency has not submitted bank guarantee or paid security deposit with
concern road authority (NHAI/MSRDC/PWD/ZP), the agency shall submit a bank
guarantee at the rate of Rs.50/- per running meter and an undertaking to that effect
shall also be submitted. If the agency fails to reclaim the land to the satisfaction of
concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests, the cost for reclamation will be covered
from the bank guarantee.
8) If the cable is going to be laid in the formation width of the road, then additional
charges in form of bank guarantee shall be given at the rate of Rs.35/- per running

9) For further maintenance of the project, the agency shall obtain permission from the

10) The forest land shall not be used for any purpose other than specified in the proposal.
11) No felling of trees shall be done during the implementation of project.
12) No new forest area beyond the Right of Way of existing road should be broken for laying Optical Fiber Cable.
13) The User Agency shall pay the cost of additional Net Present Value, if required in future.
14) No labour camp shall be established on the forest land.
15) Underground Optical Fiber Cable can be laid only outside the confines of the Protected Areas.
16) After completion of the project, User Agency will reclaim the area under RoW
suitably.
17) The Proposed work shall be carried out only during day time.
18) The User Agency after implementation of the project shall provide an internet
broadband connection of 100 Mbps speed free of cost for the Allapalli Forest Division Office.
19) The layout plan of the proposal shall not be changed without prior approval of the State Government.
20) This sanction is subject to the final clearance by the Department of Information &
Technology for the project and concerned PWD authorities for the project.
21) The User Agency shall compensate the private owners of forest land if any, in
accordance with the prevalent laws, rules & guidelines.
22) The User Agency shall obtain Environmental Clearance as per the provisions of the
Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, if applicable.
23) All other conditions as may be mandatory under relevant Acts, Rules and Guidelines
shall be complied by the User Agency.
24) All the conditions stipulated by the Forest Department shall be binding on User
Agency.
