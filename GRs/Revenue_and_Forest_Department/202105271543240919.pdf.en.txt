Introduction: The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) has been working for the holistic development of humanity since 1951. In this regard, they are doing substantial work in the areas of environmental management, poverty alleviation, uninterrupted power, disaster management, etc. With the help of United Nations Development Programme, work is being done in many countries of the world in the field of disaster management. In India too, disaster management works are being carried out in Odisha, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Jharkhand etc. by tying up with United Nations Development Programme.
For disaster management, the Government of India was contemplating to enter into an agreement with United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) for providing expert manpower as per requirement and technical inputs for capacity building and preparedness from the State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) as per the recommendations of the 15th Finance Commission.
Government Decision: To enter into an agreement between the Government of Maharashtra and the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) for the implementation of various projects related to disaster management from the funds received by the State Government from the Central Government for disaster management as per the recommendations of the 15th Finance Commission and to provide expert manpower as per the requirement. The Government is approving a budget outlay of Rs. 3,46,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Crores Forty Seven Lakhs only).
The above funds for trained manpower and Project Management Cell will be provided to UNDP over a period of three years from 2021-22 to 2023-24.
Detailed Project Reports (DPRs) in the prescribed format will be prepared by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) as per the guidelines issued for the utilization of funds received from the 15th Finance Commission.
Detailed project reports in the prescribed format will be submitted to the State Executive Committee and, if necessary, to the Central Government by the United Nations Development Programme.
Technical manpower and technical inputs for receipt and utilization of funds for response and relief, reconstruction and recovery, mitigation and capacity building under disaster management.
