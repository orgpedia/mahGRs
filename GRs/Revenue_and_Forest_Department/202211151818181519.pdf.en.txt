Setting up of a Study Group to examine the incidence of snakebites in the State, measures to be taken, compensation and establishment of snakebite testing centres in the State.
The Government of Maharashtra
the Revenue and Forest Department
Government Resolution No. Meeting-1022/ P.S. 342/ F-1
Mantralaya, Mumbai- 400032
Date of release: November 15, 2022
Preamble: The Government was considering the formation of a study group to study the incidents of snakebites in the State and the measures to be taken, compensation and to verify the establishment of snakebite testing centres in the State. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
A Study Group is being set up to study and analyse the incidents of snakebite and deaths in the State, measures to be taken on snakebite incidents, compensation to the person concerned for snakebite incidents and to verify the establishment of snakebite testing centres in the State and collection and handling of snake and snake venom.
Mr. Clement Benn, Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), West, Mumbai- Chairman 2. Smt. Kedar Bhide, Snake Expert:- Member Shri Mallikarjun, Conservator of Forests and Director, Sanjay Gandhi National Park,:- Member Secretary Borivali.
The mandate of the Study Group will be to: (i) Analyse and study the snakebite cases and deaths in the State.
To study the requirement of Anti Snake Venom Serum (ASVS) in the State and make suggestions to ensure its availability.
(iii) Verification of establishment of Snake Poison Check Posts and Snake Poison Repository/ Bank in the State.
The above provisions shall come into force from the date of issue of the Government Order.
The team will submit its report to the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), Maharashtra State, Nagpur within 90 days. The Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), State of Maharashtra, Nagpur will submit the report of the study team to the Government along with their feedback.
