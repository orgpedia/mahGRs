Introduction: In order to help the farmers in the next season in case of loss due to natural calamities like heavy rains, floods and cyclones, the farmers are given assistance in the form of input subsidy at a prescribed rate from the State Disaster Response Fund. Also, assistance is provided at prescribed rates for other approved items of the State Disaster Response Fund. For this, the Government Decision, Revenue and Forest Department No. CLS-2022/ PR No. 349/ M-3, Dy. The State Disaster Criteria and Rates have been prescribed as on 27.03.2023.
The Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur has submitted a proposal to the Government seeking funds to compensate for the damage caused to roads, houses and other properties due to heavy rains and flood situation in Nagpur city in the month of September, 2023
GOVERNMENT DECISION: For the damage caused to roads, houses and other properties due to heavy rains and flood situation in Nagpur city during the month of September, 2023, vide reference No. 1 As per the norms and rates fixed by the Government here, Rs. 838.54 lakh (Rupees Eight Crores Thirty Eight Lakhs Fifty Four Thousand only) is being sanctioned by the Government.

The fund should be disbursed by the Office of M-11 through the Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur to the Collector, Nagpur.
(3) The instructions and norms of all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned. For matters that do not fit into the criteria of the State Disaster Response Fund, assistance should not be disbursed to the departments concerned and the amount of assistance should be deposited by the government. While providing this assistance, all concerned should ensure that the terms and conditions prescribed by the Central Government for natural calamities like cyclone, flood, etc. are met. The assistance will be admissible if the excess rainfall recorded in the mandal is more than 65 mm in 24 hours as per the criteria for excess rainfall for natural calamities declared by the State Government. However, the excess rainfall will not be applicable in areas where there is flooding.
4. The details of the assistance should be published on the website of the districts after the completion of the process of distribution of assistance. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
(5) The accounts of the expenditure made out of the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure made shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. The Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund from the concerned and submitting it to the government as soon as the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund and the State Government Fund are spent.
