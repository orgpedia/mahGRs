GOVERNMENT DECISION: To provide assistance to those affected by the heavy rains and flood situation in the State from March to October, 2023 for the benefit of farmers and agricultural land, property and other damages. 1 and 11 respectively at a total cost of Rs. 3593.16 lakh (Rupees Thirty Five Crores Ninety Three Lakhs Sixteen Thousand only) is being sanctioned by the Government.
2. The funds shall be distributed to all the District Collectors by the Office of the 11th Divisional Commissioner as shown in the heading of the accounts in the form attached to this Government Resolution.
(3) The instructions and norms of all Government decisions in the context should be strictly followed while spending the above funds. The funds will be utilised for the purpose for which they were sanctioned. While providing this assistance, all concerned should ensure that the terms and conditions prescribed by the Central Government for natural calamities like cyclone, flood, etc. are met. The assistance will be admissible if the excess rainfall recorded in the mandal is more than 65 mm in 24 hours as per the criteria for excess rainfall for natural calamities declared by the State Government. However, the excess rainfall will not be applicable in areas where there is flooding.
After the completion of the process of distribution of assistance to the beneficiaries, the list of beneficiaries and details of assistance should be published on the website of the districts. The District Collector will be responsible for the implementation of all these instructions.
(5) The accounts of the expenditure made out of the Fund shall be maintained at the level of the Office drawing up the Fund and the expenditure made shall be reconciled quarterly with the Treasury Offices and the Accountant General's Office. The Divisional Commissioner will be responsible for receiving the utilization certificates of the fund from the concerned and submitting it to the government as soon as the above funds made available from the State Disaster Response Fund and the State Government Fund are spent.
