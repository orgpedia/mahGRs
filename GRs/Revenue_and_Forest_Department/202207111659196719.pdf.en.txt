Introduction: To prevent encroachment on the boundary of Sanjay Gandhi National Park, to protect the forest resources and to reduce the entry of leopards outside the park area, it is necessary to build a protective wall on the forest boundary.
It is also necessary to protect the valuable forest and park areas of the national park to maintain them, to protect the wildlife, to maintain the unbreakable relationship of the environment with human life.
Accordingly, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), State of Maharashtra, Nagpur has requested for grant of administrative approval to the budget for construction of protective wall on forest land of Sanjay Gandhi National Park vide letter No. 2 of reference.
Government Decision: As mentioned in the statement along with the construction of the protective wall on the boundary of Sanjay Gandhi National Park, Rs. The Budget Estimates amounting to Rs. 75.06 lakhs are being accorded administrative approval of the Government subject to the following conditions (excluding financial approval), subject to the technical concurrence given by the Public Works Department:
(i) Proof of ownership of the land should be obtained before commencing the work.
ii.The cost of construction of the said work shall be met from the budget available from the provision sanctioned under the scheme.
(iii) Ensure that there is no violation of the provisions of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 as well as the existing laws/ rules.
iv. Prior to implementation, it should be ensured that the budgetary provisions are for the implementation of the basic features.
