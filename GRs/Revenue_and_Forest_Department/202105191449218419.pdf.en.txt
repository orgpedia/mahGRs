The project is scheduled to be completed in 2017-18 and 2019-20. In the initial phase of the scheme, the Saffron Forest Co-Management Committees have been formed in 103 coastal villages/ wards of the State and the registration is in progress. Under the Livelihood Generation Project, Jitada cage rearing, crab rearing, canal and shell rearing and ornamental fish rearing have been started in the villages where registration has been completed. At present, it is planned to start livelihood projects in most of the villages under this scheme and additional time is required for effective implementation of the scheme, said Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development). 3 has been sent to the government.
