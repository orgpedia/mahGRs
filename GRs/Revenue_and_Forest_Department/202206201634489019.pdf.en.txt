The Proposal:
From June 15 to September 30, the festival is celebrated every year. During this period, saplings are supplied by the government at a discounted rate to encourage people to plant trees. In order to make tree plantation and rearing a people's event, as well as to make the public aware of the importance of afforestation, saplings are supplied at revised discounted rates during the Van Mahotsav period. According to reference no. (2) The continuation of the scheme for the year 2021-22 was approved under the Government Decision dated 4 th June, 2021. The government is planning to launch the scheme in 2022-23.
Government's decision:
Keeping in view the experience of the year 2021-22 and the response of the people, the Government is approving the continuation and effective implementation of this scheme during the period of Van Mahotsav from 15 th June 2022 to 30 th September 2022 in the financial year 2022-23.
The programme of" Planting 50 crore trees" was taken up in the State since 2017. Under this programme, saplings will be supplied at subsidized rates during the Van Mahotsav period to ensure that the plantation programme continues uninterruptedly in the privately owned fallow areas and on farm bunds, railway tracks, canals and roadsides, community fallow areas and gairan areas. The supply of saplings during the Van Mahotsav period and normal period is being approved at the concessional rates mentioned below for the financial year 2022-23.

In order to enable all the government agencies and institutions as well as the semi-government agencies and the people to participate in the tree plantation program on a large scale, saplings are being supplied at a very low and subsidized rate during the monsoon season 2022 from June 15 to September 30 in the state.
If the government agencies, local bodies, etc. who want to plant trees do not have any kind of financial provision for the production of saplings, such agencies will be supplied free of cost to the concerned nursery during this Vanamahotsava period. For this, they should demand the saplings in advance from the concerned Deputy Conservator of Forests (Regional) or Divisional Forest Officer, Social Forestry Department through the accompanying demand letter.
The Deputy Conservator of Forests (Regional)/ Divisional Forest Officer, Social Forestry Department shall issue orders for supply of seedlings to the system wise/ requisitioners from the Regional/ Social Forestry nurseries available in the district and coordinate with other systems.
For private unaided schools/ colleges who want to plant trees, the rate per sapling in 2022-23 will be Rs. 1/- will remain. (The rate is Rs. 1 for the first 100 saplings and Rs. 10/- and per large sapling (20x30 cm) Rs. 40/- per plant (25 x 40 cm) and Rs. 50/-).
If the government machinery or other institutions want to plant the saplings in the government land, if they do not have the funds for it, then the government agency or other institutions and the government landholding organization will provide them with such a certificate that the free supply of saplings to them during this Vanamahotsava period will be reduced to a limit of one thousand (1000) or less (as per demand). Also, if there is a demand from the institutions and if the saplings are available in the government nursery, then the said provision will be applicable during the general period in addition to the Vanamahotsava period.
