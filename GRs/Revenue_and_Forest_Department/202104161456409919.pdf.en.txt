The All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 have been made applicable to the officers of the Indian Forest Service with effect from 2008-09. As per the rules, Indian Forest Service officers who are 40 years of age and above are required to get their medical examination done annually and a summary of their medical examination report is to be attached with their annual performance evaluation report. Accordingly, for the year 2020-21, the Government has decided to conduct medical examinations of Indian Forest Service officers of Maharashtra cadre who are 40 years of age and above in the State as follows: GOVERNMENT DECISION: In accordance with Rule 3 of the All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 and the accompanying Sample 4, the Government has decided to conduct medical examinations of the officers of the Maharashtra cadre who are 40 years of age and above. The Indian Forest Service officers of the State who are 40 years of age or above as on 31.03.2020, should get their prescribed medical examination done from the private hospitals identified revenue division wise as mentioned in Form A attached to this Government Order. At the time of the examination, the following recommendations should be made:
1) Maharashtra. Officers of Indian Forest Service, who are 40 years of age or above as on 31.3.2020, should get all the medical check-ups mentioned in Form B done from the private hospital/ medical institution mentioned in Form A in the Revenue Department where they are working.
2) The Bhavse officers working in the Amravati revenue division should get the medical check-up done from the private hospitals designated for other revenue divisions near them.
3) The medical examination can also be conducted in the government hospitals of the concerned district or government/ municipal hospitals in the revenue division where the facility of conducting all the medical examinations prescribed in Form-B is available.

During the period from the date of this Government decision till 30th June, 2021, the officers of Indian Forest Service shall liaise with the concerned private hospitals to fix the dates of medical examination and conduct the medical examination as prescribed in Form-B.
(5) The officers of the Indian Forest Service shall, after conducting all the medical tests specified in Form B from the hospitals designated for the respective revenue divisions or from the Government or Municipal hospitals, pay the amount required for the medical tests to the said hospital first and obtain reimbursement thereof from their office.
If an Indian Forest Service officer wants to get a medical check-up done from a hospital whose rates are higher than the revenue department-wise minimum rates in Form-A, the expenditure incurred in excess of the revenue department-wise minimum rates shall be borne by the concerned officer at his own cost. It should be noted that the government will not reimburse the excess expenditure over the fixed minimum rate.
(6) The authorities who reimburse the cost of annual medical examination under this Government Decision, Government Decision, Public Health Department, No. MAG-1098/ Process. 220/ Health-3, Dy. The reimbursement of expenses on medical check-ups which were admissible once in every two years as on 31st August, 2006 shall not be payable.
Private Hospitals and Government or Municipal Hospitals identified by the Government for medical examination shall submit a consolidated report of the medical examination of the Indian Forest Service officers in their possession to the Officer, Revenue and Forest Department (Office F-7A), Cell No. For details, call 459 (Extended Building), 4th Floor, Mantralaya, Madame Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Mumbai-400032. After the medical examination, a copy of the said examination report should also be given to the concerned Indian Forest Service officers.
8) All the medical check-ups mentioned in Form-B will be done at one place and on one day. But in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, they should be done on the next day. The date and time of inspection should be mutually decided by the Indian Forest Service officers and the concerned hospitals.
Also, the Indian Forest Service officers should consult the hospital about the preparations/ precautions for the medical check-up.
9) Before going for medical check-up, the officers should give prior intimation to the senior officer concerned that they are going to the headquarters or to a hospital outside the headquarters for medical check-up.

10) 1 day of medical check-up will be considered as duty period.
No leave/ transfer leave will be admissible if the examination is conducted on a public holiday.
Since the period of medical examination is deemed to be the period of duty, it shall be payable (if applicable) at the rate at which travelling allowance/ daily allowance is paid to an officer of the Indian Forest Service while he is otherwise on tour. The rates of such Travel Allowance/ Daily Allowance shall be as per the orders issued by the Finance Department from time to time. This should be considered and provided by the office providing salaries to the concerned officers.
12) The expenditure on medical examination shall be covered under the head of accounts applicable to the salary and allowances at the establishment of the officer concerned and shall be met from the grant sanctioned for that year. However, there will be no financial constraint.
13) If the Indian Forest Service officer wants to get the medical check-up done at a hospital in a revenue division other than the revenue division in which he is working, he is being allowed to get the medical check-up done at the rate permissible in the hospital authorized in the desired revenue division.
2. Medical Examination of Indian Administrative Service Officers to be conducted by General Administration Department. The said Government Decisions are being issued in accordance with the consensus given under 22.1.2015.
