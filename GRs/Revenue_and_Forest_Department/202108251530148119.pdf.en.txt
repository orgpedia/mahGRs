The Sanjay Gandhi National Park and the Tungareshwar Sanctuary are two protected areas under the Sanjay Gandhi National Park division, covering an area of 104.44 sq km and 85.70 sq km respectively. km.
The protected areas are spread across Mumbai, Thane and Palghar districts.
Presently, the entire area is administratively managed by the Conservator of Forests and Director, Sanjay Gandhi National Park, Borivali.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The proposal regarding change in the administrative structure of Sanjay Gandhi National Park has been approved in the meeting of High Level Standing Committee held on 24.03.2021 under the chairmanship of Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Head of Forest Force), Maharashtra State, Nagpur. Accordingly, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), Maharashtra State, Nagpur has submitted the proposal for approval to the Government through a letter of reference and the decision is being taken accordingly.
2. Administrative Reorganisation of Sanjay Gandhi National Park As the regular administrative management of Sanjay Gandhi National Park and Tungareshwar Wildlife Sanctuary requires effective restructuring in terms of wildlife protection and conservation, it is approved to create two separate offices of Deputy Director (South), Borivali and Deputy Director (North), Yeoor, under the Conservator of Forests and Director, Sanjay Gandhi National Park, Borivali, with their administrative structure as per Form A.
Administrative structure of the office of the Deputy Director (South), Borivali.
The Deputy Director (South), Borivali will be primarily responsible for the tourism sector of the National Park, Krishnagiri Uplands, Tulsi Forest Range and Tiger and Lion Safari and will have its headquarters and office at Borivali. All works related to their area of operation and mainly tourism development works, wildlife rescue, encroachment removal, conservation, mini zoo management, etc.

Administrative structure of the office of the Deputy Director (North), Yeoor.
The Deputy Director (North), Yeoor will be primarily responsible for the area of Yeoor Forest Range and Tungareshwar Sanctuary in the National Park and will have his headquarters and office at Yeoor.
At present, there is only one forest range (8570.08 ha) under Tungareshwar Sanctuary. Therefore, it is proposed to divide the Tungareshwar Forest Range into Tungareshwar (North) and Tungareshwar (South) for management purposes. It will also cover all works related to their area of operation, especially in the areas of encroachment, wildlife management and conservation. Headquarters of Assistant Conservator of Forests (North), Yeoor will remain at Yeoor.

3. regarding the required classifications
The Assistant Conservator of Forests (Wildlife-2) will continue to be the Assistant Conservator of Forests (Technical Assistant) in the office of the Conservator of Forests and Director, Sanjay Gandhi National Park. The post of Assistant Conservator of Forests (Conservation-Wildlife) is being transferred to the office of Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), West, Mumbai (Form-B).
The post of Forest Range Officer, Tungareshwar is being subsumed in the new structure as Forest Range Officer, Tungareshwar (North). He is also the Technical Assistant to the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), West, Mumbai.

The post is being made available for the post of Forest Range Officer, Tungareshwar (South) by category (Form-B).

V. Changes in the designations of Field Officers as per the changes in the structure after the administrative reorganization of Sanjay Gandhi National Park will be as per Form-C.
The posts required for this restructuring should be made available from the sanctioned posts in the department and both the offices of the Deputy Director should be accommodated in the existing buildings and facilities in the Sanjay Gandhi National Park.
