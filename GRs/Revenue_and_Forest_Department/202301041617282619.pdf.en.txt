Preamble: A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) has been signed between National Tiger Conservation Authority, New Delhi and the State Government for creation of a Special Tiger Protection Force. Accordingly, a Special Tiger Protection Force has been formed in Pench Tiger Reserve, Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve, Navegaon Nagzira Tiger Reserve and Melghat Tiger Reserve in the state of Maharashtra. Each Project Tiger comprises of 112 officers/ staff including 1 Assistant Conservator of Forest, 3 Forest Range Officers, 81 STPF Forest Guards, 27 Forest Inspectors. A total of 108 posts of Forest Inspectors have been sanctioned in the Special Tiger Protection Force in the state.
2. In order to ease the difficulties of the forest watchers and forest management in the Special Tiger Protection Force, the cadre of the forest watcher may be referred to as Reference No. 4 In order to adjust these posts in the Forest Guard Cadre with equal duties/ responsibilities and pay scales by abolishing the above figure, the Principal Chief Conservator of Forest (Head of Forest Force), M.R.Nagpur has issued Reference No. 5 has been recommended to the Government. Accordingly, the Government was considering adjustment of the posts of' Forest Inspector' cadre in the Special Tiger Protection Force (STPF) to' Forest Guard' cadre with similar duties/ responsibilities and pay scales.
The government has decided to abolish the cadre of' Forest Inspector' in the Special Tiger Protection Force (STPF) of the Forest Department and adjust 108 posts of' Forest Inspector' in the cadre of' Forest Guard' with equal duties/ responsibilities and equal pay scale.
The following terms and conditions should be followed while adjusting the said 108 posts of Forest Inspector Cadre to the" Forest Guard" Cadre. :

(i) All the 108 posts of Forest Inspectors to be adjusted in the Forest Guard cadre and the posts to be increased in the Forest Guard cadre in the Tiger Reserve in which they are located. A total of 36 forest guards, instead of 27 forest guards and 9 forest watchers, will be deployed in one unit of the Special Tiger Protection Force.
(ii) After adjustment from the Forest Inspector (STPF) cadre to the Forest Guard cadre, their service excellence in the Forest Guard cadre is governed by the provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 2021 of the General Administration Department. be determined in accordance with proviso 3. That is, after the adjustment of the forest inspector in the forest guard cadre, his seniority should be taken into account from the day he joined the post.
(iii) After adjustment from the Forest Inspector's cadre to the Forest Guard's cadre, the candidate will have to fulfill the terms and conditions required for promotion to the senior post (i.e., the post of Forest Guard) as per the rules of entry into service from the Forest Guard's cadre.
(iv) Educational Qualification for appointment to the post of Forest Guard in direct service is Higher Secondary School Certificate (HSC) pass, while for appointment to the post of Forest Inspector, the educational qualification is Secondary School Certificate (SSC) pass. As the duties, responsibilities and pay scales of both the posts are same, the condition of educational qualification is being relaxed while appointing these forest inspectors to the post of forest guard through adjustment.
(v) The relevant provisions of the Finance Department for the benefit of previous service, fixation of salary, leave of the Forest Inspector shall continue to be admissible.
(vi) It shall be mandatory for every Inspector of Forests to obtain the consent letter for the recruitment to the post of Forest Guard.
(vii) Every Forest Inspector shall, before induction, furnish a bond to the effect that the terms and conditions prescribed in respect of service and promotion as aforesaid shall remain valid and shall not be appealed in any court/ authority.
