2.The said orders shall come into force with immediate effect. The above-mentioned officers are being unilaterally relieved of their duties at the government level under this order from the post they are holding, with immediate effect, to join the post indicated for transfer. The officers concerned should immediately join the place of posting given by the transfer and the compliance report should be submitted by the concerned to the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati.
3. The officers who have been given 1 year extension under this order in the present working position shall be eligible for transfer at the next general transfer.
4. The said order is an order of discharge and there is no need for fresh order to be issued by the concerned Divisional Commissioner / Collector. The date on which the officer concerned appeared at the place of posting given by the transfer should be immediately communicated to the Government by e-mail / post at deske9-rfd @ mah.gov.in.
5. If it is found that the officer concerned has not joined the place of transfer before the expiry of the term of office, in accordance with the said provision prescribed under Rule 15 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Tenure of Incorporation, Self-Employment and Provisions for Suspension, Dismissal and Removal from Service) Rules, 1981, the Department of General Administration, Government Decision, No. Narrow-2016 / P.S. No. 186/16 K. 14, D. Those concerned should note that disciplinary action will be taken in accordance with the provision in the 23.12.2016.
The officer concerned shall take care of joining the place of posting within the time prescribed under the rules of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of assumption of office, Non-Service and Provisions for suspension, dismissal and removal from service) Rules, 1981. Those concerned should also note that their absence will be treated as a non-deceasing day if they do not otherwise join the position of this post.
7. Leave shall not be granted for any reason except with the previous sanction of the Government if the officer concerned fails to join the place of posting as transferred within the prescribed period. Also, as per Rule 30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, the parent office shall not accept any leave applications of such officers. If such applications are received, they shall be returned by post to the original address of the said officer.
8. Since the above order is being issued with the approval of the competent authority, the SAT officers shall be posted by the Divisional Commissioner in other vacant posts if at the place of posting, the Tehsildar or other officers under probation have been provisionally posted from the level of Divisional Commissioner.

9. All concerned shall note that in case of non-joining of the post transferred under the said order or any attempt to cancel the transfer under any kind of pressure, disciplinary action shall be taken against them as if the act violated Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979 and amounted to misconduct.
The decision is available on the Government of Maharashtra's www.maharashtra.gov.in website.
