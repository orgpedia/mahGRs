Preamble: - Guidelines have been issued under the Government Circular Finance Department dated 29.6.2017 regarding review of sanctioned posts in administrative departments and their subordinate offices and fixation of revised figures. According to the order, a comprehensive review of the sanctioned posts in the Registration and Stamp Department under the Revenue and Forest Department has been undertaken and a proposal has been mooted for increased scope of work in the department and creation of new posts / abolition of existing posts. A high-level committee headed by the Chief Secretary, Shri. The approval has been given in the 7th meeting of 2025 held on 6/8/2025. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to approve the revised structure of total 3952 posts in the Registration and Stamp Department under the Revenue and Forest Department by abolishing 107 posts and creating 965 new posts out of the sanctioned 3094 posts at present.
Government Decision: - Department of Finance, Government Circular, No. As per the guidelines in the 29/6/2017, a comprehensive review of all posts in the Registration and Stamp Department under the Revenue and Forest Department was carried out. Accordingly, the revised structure of the posts in the Registration and Stamp Department has received the approval of the High-Level Committee and the Finance Department.

* * At present, it is mandatory to fill these posts regularly as the recruitment process for these posts has been completed with the approval of the Sub-Committee received for filling up 284 posts of Sepoy Cadre in Group-D. Therefore, out of a total of 705 posts in the sepoy cadre in the proposed structure, 284 posts are currently being declared as dead cadre. These posts in the sepoy cadre shall automatically become vacant by way of promotion / retirement / resignation / death or otherwise and the services of these posts shall thereafter be taken over by external mechanisms as required.
Care should be taken to follow the guidelines in the decision issued by the government from time to time while availing services through external channels.
