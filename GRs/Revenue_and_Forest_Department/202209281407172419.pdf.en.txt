Introduction: In order to help the farmers in the next season in case of loss due to natural calamities like heavy rains, floods and cyclones, the farmers are given assistance in the form of input subsidy at a prescribed rate from the State Disaster Response Fund. Also, assistance is provided for other approved items of the State Disaster Response Fund at prescribed rates. The Chief Minister said that assistance in the form of financial assistance to the affected farmers, who suffered crop loss in various districts due to heavy rains and floods in July, 2022, and assistance for other losses would be provided. Pursuant to the decision taken in the Cabinet Meeting dated 10.08.2022, the Government have decided to issue a notification in the Official Gazette vide Government Resolution, Revenue& Forest Department No.CLS-2022/ P.No.253/ M-3, Dy. The Government has approved to provide assistance to the affected farmers in the form of fixed subsidy at enhanced rates for the loss of agricultural crops in various districts of the State due to natural calamities like heavy rains, floods during the period from June to October, 2022 as under:-

Out of the increased rate, the amount disbursed under the head of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) should be spent for providing assistance at the rate decided by the Government at State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) reference number 1 and 2 and the difference between the increased sanctioned rate and the State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) rate should be shared with the State Government from the amount disbursed from the State Government Fund. While providing this assistance, all concerned should ensure that the conditions and conditions prescribed by the Central Government for natural calamities such as cyclones, floods, etc. are met. The relief will be admissible if the excess rainfall is more than 65 mm in 24 hours in the mandal as per the criteria of excess rainfall for natural calamities declared by the State Government and the resultant loss to the farmers is more than 33 per cent in the villages of the mandal. Similarly, the assistance for removal of silt from agricultural land, mudslides from hilly agricultural land, fish farm repair/ soil layer removal/ restoration and other losses due to erosion, erosion and erosion of agricultural land due to change in river course/ stream is admissible only to the farmers holding land up to a limit of 2 hectares and these beneficiaries should not have availed any assistance or financial assistance/ subsidy under any other Government scheme. 4. Beneficiaries to be determined after conducting the panchnama. Although the entire amount is being disbursed on the BeAMS system under this government decision, after the beneficiary is identified, the concerned collector should be careful from the point of view of financial discipline to draw the amount from the treasury as per the actual need and then transfer the amount directly to the bank account of the beneficiaries through online mode.
