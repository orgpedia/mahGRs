Maharashtra has a 720 km long coastline. Every year during the rainy season, the sandy areas near the beach are eroded and washed away due to the sea water waves or the erosion occurring during the tides. Due to high tides, coastal villages have to bear the brunt of massive erosion of coastal houses, wadis, coconut and poppy plantations as well as coastal land. Keeping all these things in mind, the scheme of" Protection of Coastal Coast through Afforestation" is implemented by the Forest Department to achieve the objectives of planting marine species along the coast to reduce erosion, reduce the intensity of waves like tsunami and avoid economic loss of life and property, attract tourists by creating forests of marine species, increase the income of Gram Panchayats and create employment opportunities in rural areas.
In the financial year 2023-2024, under the scheme" Protection of Marine Coast through Afforestation," construction works will be carried out on 11.00 ha area for the second and third year. The Budget provides for allocation of funds to Budget Estimates, Allocation and Management System (BEMS) within the limit of 70% of the total annual allocation.
Under the Government decision dated 28.07.2023, all the works of afforestation and soil and water conservation in the forest area have been approved to be completed under the Convergence. As per the provisions of the said Government Resolution, all the works to be carried out under the scheme of" Protection of Marine Coast through Afforestation" are also meant to be carried out under the Convergence. However, the convergence scheme is fully operational and funds are being released from the provisions available under the respective schemes for various works completed and in progress under various schemes under the Forest Department. Accordingly, as per the request made vide letter dated 27.10.2023 at Reference No.4, the Government was considering the release of funds for the year 2023-24 under the scheme" Protection of Marine Coast through Afforestation."

The funds being disbursed should be spent only for the works completed and in progress during the current financial year under the scheme" Protection of Marine Coast through Afforestation" (2406 8604). No money will be spent on new projects.
The entire responsibility of this will be of the office of the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Social Forestry), Maharashtra State, Pune. The fund is being handed over to the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Head of Forest Force), Maharashtra State, Nagpur as the Controlling Officer. Controlling authorities should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds. Funds should be disbursed on the condition that the Utilization Certificate (UC) of the works to be done under this scheme should be submitted to the government within 7 days.
Before disbursement of the said funds, the Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Budget, Planning and Development), Maharashtra State, Nagpur, shall ensure that the funds allocated for the year 2023-24 for the said scheme are not diverted to other places other than the budgetary distribution system.
