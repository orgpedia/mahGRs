In urban areas of the state, the scheme is being controlled by the Housing Department and the actual implementation of the scheme is being done by the local civic bodies. Currently, the scheme is being implemented in 382 cities of the state. Under the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana, the state has set a target of 19.40 lakh households. This scheme is being implemented in 4 components in the urban areas of the state and various concessions have been given by the state government under this scheme.
Besides, it is proposed to implement innovative and innovative schemes such as setting up of Maharashtra Housing Development Corporation for effective implementation of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Urban), sanctioning of 2.5 floor area index for projects under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana, approval for implementation of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana in green areas, implementation of Maharashtra Construction Workers Awas Yojana for registered construction workers of Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board, implementation of separate housing policy for Divyangjan, amendment of Slum Rehabilitation Act and Act, formulation of policy for implementation of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana on private land through public-private partnership etc. Inter-departmental approval and consensus is required to implement the scheme. The government was considering the formation of a committee empowered by all the departments concerned in the case to facilitate the process.
Government Decision: As per the details mentioned in the Preamble. The following empowered committees are being constituted under the chairmanship of the Chief Minister.
Empowered Committee of the Cabinet 1. Prime Minister (2). Minister of State (Home) 3. Minister of State (4). Minister for Social Justice and Special Assistance (Member) Minister of Labour (6). Minister of State (Housing) Special Invitee 7. Additional Chief Secretary (Housing)
The Secretary/ Secretary of the concerned department will be present in the meeting.
The Empowered Committee will primarily deal with inter-departmental issues in a time bound manner (usually every 15 days) with all concurrence. It will also facilitate the implementation of the scheme by speeding up the scheme related to housing policy.
