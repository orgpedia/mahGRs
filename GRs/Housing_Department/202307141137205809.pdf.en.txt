Preamble: Approval has been given for inviting fresh tenders along with amendments under Government Resolution dated 28.09.2022 for implementation of Dharavi Redevelopment Project under Dharavi Notified Area. Accordingly, the Chief Executive Officer and Officer on Special Duty (Dhapupra/ Jhopupra) invited bids on 01.10.2022. The last date for submission of bids was 31.10.2022.
It has been extended till 15.11.2022. Accordingly, it was observed that a total of 3 bidders have submitted their proposals as on 15.11.2022.
The e-envelope-1 of all the three bidders was opened on 16.11.2022, their names as per the value received are as follows.
a) Tenderer 1: May Adani Properties Pvt.
b) Tenderer 2: DLF Ltd.
b) Tenderer 3: Shree Naman Developers Pvt.
The highest bidder from the above mentioned bidder was selected as the Lead Partner for the implementation of Dharavi Redevelopment Project on 15.12.2022. The decision was taken at a meeting chaired by the Chief Secretary, Government of Maharashtra. As per the approval given by the Cabinet in its meeting held on 22.12.2022, the Government is taking the following decisions:
Government Decision- (1) As the lead partner for the implementation of Dharavi Redevelopment Project under Dharavi Notified Area, the maximum bidder (Adani Properties Pvt. 5,069 crore) is being approved for appointment.
(2) For the purpose of granting approval in this regard and for the special purpose of the Dharavi Redevelopment Project, the Company Model (Rs. 100 crore (20 per cent equity) and Rs. 400 crore (80 per cent equity). 500 crore).

The Chief Executive Officer and Officer on Special Duty, Dharavi Redevelopment Project/ Slum Rehabilitation Authority are being authorised to take a decision on the implementation of the same immediately.
(3) (a) As specified in the Government Resolution dated 28.09.2022 of the Housing Department, the Special Purpose Company is required to deposit 25% of the redirection amount as the overage of Dhapupra/ Jhopra land as on the last date of submission of tender. As per the Development Control Rules 33 (9) (a) (19) and 33 (10) (a) (1.17), 70% of the excess amount of land received is required to be paid to the entity owning the land. The Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai and other land-owning agencies shall be bound by the said remuneration and conditions and no-objection certificate shall be mandatory for these agencies.
(b) Further, as Dharavi Redevelopment Project is an essential civic project, the Urban Development Department shall take appropriate action and issue a notification/ Government Resolution in the larger public interest under Section 520C of the Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, for expeditiously issuing a No-Objection Certificate to the Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation.
(c) The authority to take action for the development of the project is being given to Dhapupra/ Jhopra even without a no-objection certificate after the deadline given in Section 3C of the Maharashtra Slum (Improvement, Eradication and Redevelopment) (Amendment) Act, 2017.
(4) (a) On the basis of the decision of the State Cabinet dated 21st September, 2022, the In the case of Rental Housing Scheme as mentioned in the Government Resolution dated 28.09.2022, if such land is not available within 10 km radius, Dhapupra/ Jhopra is being authorized to implement the Rental Housing Scheme on suitable land in the Mumbai Metropolitan Region as decided by Dhapupra/ Jhopra Special Purpose Company.
(b) Such lands shall be declared as Dharavi notified area from time to time. (5) In pursuance of the above decision, all the concerned departments should issue orders regarding the Dharavi Redevelopment Project within a week.
