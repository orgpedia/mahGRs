Government Decision In order to bring the required scale and speed to the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana, the construction of houses through joint partnership between private land owners and MHADA with the approval of the government is being approved under the decision of the present government. This policy shall be implemented by incorporating the following principles/ criteria.
Form of Partnership: Private individuals who own land can participate in the scheme as joint partners with MHADA with the approval of the government. The design, construction and marketing of the project will be completed by MHADA.
Selection of partners: The selection of partners will be done on the basis of two criteria namely eligibility criteria and technical selection criteria.
(a) The eligibility criteria/ ground information
1) The land must be in the name of the private partner (firm or person) concerned on the date of the tender,
(2) The land mentioned in the tender should be free and completely free of encumbrance/ encumbrance and should not be subjected to any kind of encroachments, charges, liens, leases, mortgages, mortgages which may affect the implementation of the project. Also, the land should be free from any kind of litigation. Also, the land should not be proposed for acquisition by any authority.
3) The land should be fully owned by the private partner (firm or individual), 4) The land should be in the residential area as per the prevailing Development Plan (DP), 5) The land should be in the notified area of the local civic body, etc.
(a) The application process-
Private participation up to 5 acres Rs. 1 lakh for every acre and Rs. 1 lakh will be required to be deposited with MHADA as pre-project inspection cost. The amount will be used for survey, valuation of property, legal approvals, etc. The amount deposited will not be refundable. Also, a letter of intent to participate in the program must be submitted with the application.

(a) Technical evaluation criteria.

Scope: Projects on Joint Venture basis will be developed in all the areas of implementation of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana in the State.
4) Formula for sharing of cost and income under the project: Under the proposed Joint Partnership Policy, houses will be constructed for the economically weaker section (EWS) and low income group (LIG) beneficiaries. Under the policy, MHADA will complete the housing project on the land received for the housing project by spending on the necessary infrastructure for the construction project, local body fees for project approval, project management costs, marketing costs, administration costs and other fees for construction and marketing. The valuation of the land will be determined on the basis of the annual rate chart during the year of completion of the project. This amount will be considered as the final assessment of the housing project after making a combined assessment of the value of the land and the cost of completion of the project. Thereafter, MHADA will sell the houses in the said housing project to the eligible beneficiaries under the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana as per their sale policy. Under this policy, the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana will be implemented on private land by MHADA and private land owners.

The rest of the amount will be contributed by MHADA. The amount or the area of construction considered in this regard shall be chosen by MHADA and the concerned private land owners by mutual consent, depending on the local conditions. The option chosen must be stated in the contract.
The time period for completion of the project will be decided after discussions between the land owner and MHADA.
5) Distribution of Households: The distribution of Households will be done by MHADA on first-come-first-serve basis or through lottery as per the existing policy of MHADA. The facilities related to the benefits of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana from the Government of India and the Government of Maharashtra will be provided through MHADA.
Disputes: (a) In case of any dispute in respect of land during the execution of the project, the dispute shall be required to be resolved by the private partners at their own cost without any liability to MHADA and without any impact on the execution of the project.
(b) The private partner shall give a bond for payment of compensation to MHADA in respect of the dispute arising out of the transaction before the transfer of land or commencement of the project.
The private partner shall also furnish a bond to MHADA stating that there is no prior liability or dispute before the transfer of the project area.
(a) The terms and conditions of the agreement shall be binding on the partners under the policy; (b) Any dispute between the private partner and MHADA in the project under the policy shall be settled in accordance with the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.
e) MHADA and private partners can make minor changes in the terms and conditions of the joint venture agreement with mutual consent and government approval without affecting the basic tenor of the policy.
Provisions of Development Control Rules (DCR), Development Plan (DP) and Maharashtra Real Estate Regulation Act (MahaRERA) will be applicable while constructing affordable houses on joint partnership basis under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana.
Eligibility of Economically Weaker Sections/ Low Income Groups under the Concession Policy to be admissible for eligible housing projects under the Joint Partnership Policy.
