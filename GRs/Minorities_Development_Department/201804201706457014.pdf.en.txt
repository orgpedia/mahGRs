Introduction The Government has taken an important decision to implement the concept of Green Maharashtra to reduce the intensity and severity of natural disasters such as climate change, rising temperatures, drought and water scarcity. Under this programme, a target of planting 50 crore trees has been set for the period 2017-2019. In addition, in 2016. A unique and ambitious programme of planting 2 crore trees on a single day on 1st July, 2016 has been implemented.
In 2018 and 2019, the target is to plant 13 crore and 33 crore trees respectively. It is very important that the goal of tree plantation is fully achieved and at the same time the trees planted so far are protected, maintained and nurtured well.
Presentation of 4 crore tree plantation programmes during 1st July to 7th July, 2017 In front of the cabinet. It was held on June 13, 2017. As per the discussion, action will be taken. The matter of communicating the suggestions to the administrative departments as agreed by the cabinet was under the consideration of the government. Accordingly, instructions are being issued through this circular.
Government Decision In the field level meetings at Taluka, District and State level by the Minority Development Department, the topics related to tree plantation and rearing should be included as a permanent topic in the agenda at the time of each meeting. In such meetings, review of tree plantation, survival rate of trees planted earlier, planting of new trees in place of dead trees, fixing of sites for tree plantation as per next year's tree plantation targets, availability of saplings, preparation of pits in different time periods as per required technical parameters, availability of funds etc. should be reviewed.
2 The decisions taken in such meetings regarding the tree plantation programme should be recorded in the minutes of the meeting. Further, the implementation of the decisions mentioned in the history and the difficulties faced should be discussed in the next meeting and measures should be taken to solve the problems and difficulties faced by the field officers.
3. The officers at Taluka, District and State level who will visit the field works, a program should be organized to visit the tree plantation sites under the respective departments. During such field visits, the senior officers should inspect whether the trees planted under the concerned administrative department are properly protected, whether new trees are planted in place of dead trees, water arrangements and manpower for protection, etc. Also, you need to take steps to solve the problem.
