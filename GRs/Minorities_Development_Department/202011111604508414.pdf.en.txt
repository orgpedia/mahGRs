In order to provide opportunities to the candidates belonging to the minority community of the state in the government/ semi-government services, for the preparation of competitive examinations like UPSC/ Maharashtra Public Service Commission/ Banking Service Recruitment, etc. as well as for the Common Entrance Examination for admission to professional courses, and for the introduction of special tuition classes for the minority candidates who have failed in class 10th and 12th. 1 has been approved by the government.
Under this scheme, training institutes will be set up in Mumbai, Nashik, Amravati, Nagpur, Thane and Pune. As per the decision of the Government dated 25th June, 2013, the training institutes located at Mumbai, Nashik, Amravati, Nagpur, Thane and Pune have submitted their proposals to the Government seeking grants. It was under consideration of the Government to sanction the first tranche of grants to such institutions from the available provisions during 2020-21.
The government has decided to provide free training to the institutions selected for the training center in Mumbai, Nashik, Amravati, Nagpur, Thane and Pune under the Maulana Azad Free Tuition and Related Scheme. The Government is approving the sanction of the first phase grant as per the Phase Grant Methodology subject to the fulfillment of the terms and conditions laid down on 25th June, 2013.
Accordingly, in the financial year 2020-21, the budget allocated for the said scheme is Rs. 7 crore out of reference no. 3 d. As per the circular dated 16th April, 2020, 10% of the total budget has been provided for. Accordingly, Rs.70 lakh is available for disbursement at the rate of 10%. Out of the provision of Rs.70 lakh available, training institutions as indicated in Annexure-A will be provided Rs. The Government is approving to disburse a grant of Rs. 20,78,000/- (Rupees Twenty Lakh Eighty Eight Thousand only).
