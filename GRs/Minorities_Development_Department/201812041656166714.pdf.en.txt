The proposal-
The United Nations The Declaration on the Rights of National, Ethnic, Religious and Linguistic Minorities was adopted on December 18, 1992. Accordingly, the National Commission for Minorities has suggested that efforts should be made so that the minority citizens can preserve their culture, language, religion, traditions, etc. and express their characteristics effectively. December 18 is observed as" Minority Rights Day" by the Maharashtra State Minorities Commission.
Government Circular- The date 18th December should be celebrated as" Minority Rights Day" at the state level every year. The following events will be held on this day:
Various programs should be organized to make the minority citizens aware of their constitutional and legal rights.
Various programs should be organized for social workers, voluntary groups and students with the help of non-governmental social organizations in schools and colleges in all districts. This should include the following:
a) Poster competition b) Speech competition c) Essay competition d) Prizes to the winners of the above mentioned events e) Lecture, Seminar, Symposium etc.
The schemes implemented by the government for minorities through the Maharashtra State Minorities Commission should be publicized through fairs, seminars etc.
The Maharashtra State Minorities Commission will be responsible for giving instructions to all the districts, deciding the program outline, guiding the organization of the program, etc. to implement this initiative on a large scale in the state. The programme will be implemented on a large scale in the district.
