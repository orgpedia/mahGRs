Government's decision
The government has decided to implement a special program for the rapid development of Marathwada, Vidarbha and North Maharashtra under the program. Self Help Group Scheme for Minority Women to be implemented from 7th August, 2019
The plan should be implemented in a better way. The government has approved a seven-year extension from April 1, 2021, to March 31, 2028.
Under this scheme, the government is approving the formation of approximately 2800 new self-help groups in 15 districts of Aurangabad, Hingoli, Jalna, Osmanabad, Latur, Beed, Chandrapur, Wardha, Godia, Gadchiroli, Amravati, Akola, Buldana, Yavatmal in the revenue division of Aurangabad, Nagpur, Amravati.
Considering that a period of two years is not enough to create self-help groups by motivating women to come together and become financially empowered by keeping them permanently employed, the government is approving the implementation of this scheme in the next seven years (2021-2022 to 2027-28).

Therefore, instead of setting up new centres for the newly formed groups, these groups should be linked to the Community Manage Resource Centres set up by the Women's Economic Development Corporation.
3. Training of Self Help Group women in skill development through Women Economic Development Corporation: Under this programme, training will be conducted in two modes. Classroom training may not be appropriate for all women in self-help groups. Therefore, it would be advisable for some of them to organize training sessions of the required course near their homes, according to their needs. It is planned to provide skill development training to SHG women in the age group of 20-45 years to enhance their latent qualities and provide employment opportunities by combining training with traditional skills as well as local, professional and industrial needs. The training will be imparted by selecting registered expert institutes for imparting training in making various items using waste material.

Skill Development Training to Self Help Group Women through Maharashtra Development Society: On the basis of Skill Gap, women of Self Help Groups or girls of families will be given skill development training through Maharashtra State Skill Development Society. Women will be selected for training after studying their needs, skills and what skills they can learn. This will include women hospitality, accounts and tax assistant, automobile, computer software, hardware, animation and multimedia (assistant cameraman) healthcare, construction, logistics, driver, patient care, old people care, child care, as well as local businesses and local industries.
The training will be imparted by the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society through the centres located in the talukas of the above 15 districts. On-job/ hands-on training will also be provided by visiting expert trainers of Mavim in the villages where Self Help Groups of minority women are located.
5) The social enterprise:
The Social Enterprises Initiative will be launched to provide entrepreneurship development training to women of self-help groups. In this, two social enterprises will be started from each of the 15 districts. A minimum of 20% is required to be invested. The Social Enterprises Initiative will take consultancy support for product labelling, packaging, branding and licensing to make the products of SHGs accessible to a wider market.
Accordingly, an independent marketing expert consultant will be appointed by the Women's Economic Development Corporation.
(a) To undertake innovative activities:
Innovative initiatives on health awareness, social awareness should be launched through the Women Economic Development Corporation to provide entrepreneurship and more opportunities and platforms to the needy women in the field.

7) Collecting funds from the members participating in the savings group:
To meet the cost of operating the groups under this scheme, funds will be collected from the members of the respective groups through the Women Economic Development Corporation by charging a service fee from the financial year 2024-25. Under this scheme, the list of women trainees of self-help groups will be published separately on the website of Maharashtra State Skill Development Society, Women's Economic Development Corporation and the government.
The beneficiaries who have been trained once under this scheme will not be trained again in this scheme and it will be required to be verified by the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and Women's Economic Development Corporation.
The Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and the Women's Economic Development Corporation will be responsible for providing the utility certificate to the government from time to time regarding the allocation of funds made available under the scheme.
The monthly progress report of this scheme will be submitted to the government by the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and Women's Economic Development Corporation.
Under this scheme, it will be the responsibility of the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society to assist the women beneficiaries of the Self-Help Groups of minority women to get self-employment after training in skill development through the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and to follow up the same for the next six months from the date the beneficiaries get self-employment. The Maharashtra State Skill Development Society will have to submit a report to the government and the Women's Economic Development Corporation.
The final decision on making changes in the modalities and nature of the scheme as required shall be taken by the Government.
The expenditure for this scheme will be met from the budgetary allocation under the head of Subsidiary Grants (Programme) 31, Subsidiary Grants-in-Pay (2235C 401) to the Minority Women Self Help Groups (SHGs) under special programme 2018 for Marathwada, Vidarbha and North Maharashtra.
