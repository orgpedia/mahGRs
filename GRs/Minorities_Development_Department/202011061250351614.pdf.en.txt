Introduction: The scholarship scheme for economically weaker and meritorious students of minority communities in the state who are pursuing higher professional education and studying in all courses after Class XII is being implemented as per the government decision dated 14 October 2011.
Under this scheme, the Nodal Officer of the Scheme, Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai; Director, Medical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune have been provided Rs. 49,99,01, 072/- and Rs. 10,41,28, 278/- totalling Rs. 60,40,29, 350/- (Rupees Sixty Crores, Forty Lakhs, Thirty-nine Thousand, Three Hundred and Fifty only) was made available to Minority students for the allotment of scholarships for the financial year 2019-20 from the approved budgetary provision for the financial year 2019-20 and 2020-21 respectively. Now, the Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai vide letter dated 24th August, 2020 has released a sum of Rs. 1,05,97, 399/- (Rupees One Crore, Five Lakhs Ninety Seven Thousand Three Hundred and Ninety Nine only) and the Director, Directorate of Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune vide letter dated 11th August, 2020 has released a sum of Rs. 3,39,430/- (Rupees Three Lakhs, Forty Nine Thousand, Four Hundred and Thirty only) for the financial year 2019-20.
The following decisions are taken by the government:

GOVERNMENT DECISION: Under the head' Subsidiary Grants (2235A 338) for Scholarship Scheme for Minority Students pursuing Higher Vocational Education and all courses after 12th standard," an original budget provision of Rs. 120.00 crore (Rupees One Hundred and Twenty Crore only) has been approved for the financial year 2020-21. Out of which, an amount of Rs. 39.60 crore (Rupees Forty Nine Crore Sixty Lakh only) has been allowed to be spent as 33% of the total expenditure for the entire year. However, at present, only 10% of the amount of Rs 12 crore has been made available. Out of this amount, an amount of Rs. 10,41,28, 278/- has been disbursed as per Government Resolution dated 14.07.2020. The balance amount of Rs. 1,58,71, 722/- is available for disbursement through Budget Distribution System.
As per the letter dated 24.08.2020 dated 24.08.2020 addressed to the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra, State, Mumbai, a further increase of Rs. 1,05,97, 399/- has been sought for the allotment of scholarships for the year 2019-20 to 708 students subject to a ceiling of Rs. Accordingly, the scholarship amount of Rs. 1,05,97, 399/- (Rupees One Crore, Five Lakhs, Ninety Seven Thousand, Three Hundred and Ninety Nine only) to be distributed to Minority students is being disbursed randomly.
Further, vide letter dated 11.08.2020 dated 11.08.2020 addressed to Director Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune, a total amount of Rs. 3,39,430/- is required and requested to be made available for 107 more students of theirs pursuing higher education from minority communities. Accordingly, as demanded by the Director, Directorate of Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune, the Government hereby approves disbursement of scholarship amount of Rs. 3,39,430/- (Rupees Three Lakhs, Forty Nine Thousand, Four Hundred and Thirty only) to the minority students belonging to them, amounting to Rs. 1,09,36, 829/- (Rupees One Crore, Nine Lakhs, Thirty Six Thousand Eight Hundred and Thirty-Nine only).
The amount is being handed over to the concerned Directorate for sanction of scholarship to the students belonging to minority communities who have been eligible for scholarship for the year 2019-20, for which the concerned Coordinating Officer will act as Drawing and Disbursing Officer and its Director as Controlling Officer. Since the disbursement of this scholarship is to be done expeditiously, the question of furnishing the monthly expenditure statement of this fund does not arise.
Both the said Directorates shall comply with the orders and the guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission and the observations of the Comptroller and Auditor General.
Also, if any amount is left out of the funds allocated for the scholarship for the year 2018-19 and if necessary, it should be used for the scholarship for the year 2019-20.
Further, in case any balance is left out of the funds released under the said Government decision, the same is allowed to be utilized later for the purpose of scholarship for the year 2020-21.
5. The details of expenditure and utilization certificate of the aforesaid accounts should be submitted to the Government within the stipulated time. Further, all the instructions/ rules/ regulations mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook and Financial Powers Rulebook as well as issued by the Government from time to time in this regard while spending this grant.
