Preamble: Under the Prime Minister's new 15-point programme for the welfare of minorities, it has been proposed that the Government will make conscious efforts to increase the proportion of minority candidates in Government service. Due to lack of proficiency in Marathi language, candidates from the minority community in the state lag behind in various competitive examinations compared to the general candidates. Therefore, in order to ensure that the minority candidates get full opportunities for success in various competitive examinations, the Marathi Language Foundation has been conducting classes for minority students studying in classes 8, 9 and 10 in Amarathi schools other than English medium in the state since 2006. The scheme No. 1 here. It is being implemented in a revised form as per the Government Decision dated 27th April, 2015. The scheme is under the control of Maharashtra State Minorities Commission vide reference No. 2 d. It is being implemented through the Director, Directorate of Minorities and Adult Education, Pune as per the Government decision dated 8th August, 2016. The plan was to be implemented through the Director, Directorate of Minority and Adult Education, Pune, under the control of the Minority Development Department, instead of the Maharashtra State Minorities Commission.
The government has decided to implement the Marathi Language Foundation Class Scheme for students belonging to minority communities studying in class 8, 9 and 10 in Amarathi schools in the state.
