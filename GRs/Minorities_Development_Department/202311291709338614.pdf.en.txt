The proposal-
The United Nations The Declaration on the Rights of National, Ethnic, Religious and Linguistic Minorities was adopted on 18 December 1992. Accordingly, the National Commission for Minorities has issued instructions to make efforts so that the minority citizens can preserve their culture, language, religion, traditions, etc. and express their characteristics effectively.
Government Circular- The date 18th December should be celebrated as" Minority Rights Day" at the state level every year. In this regard, the following programmes should be organized on this date: 1. Various programmes should be organized to make the minority citizens aware/ aware of their constitutional and legal rights.
Various programs should be organized for social workers, voluntary groups and students with the help of non-governmental social organizations in schools and colleges in all districts. This should include: a) poster competition b) elocution competition c) essay competition d) prizes to the winners of the above mentioned events e) lectures, seminars, symposiums, etc.
3. Maharashtra State Minorities Commission should publicize the schemes implemented by the government for minorities through fairs, seminars, etc.
The Maharashtra State Minorities Commission will be responsible for giving instructions to all the districts, deciding the program outline, guiding the organization of the program, etc. to implement this initiative on a large scale in the state. Also, all district collectors have been entrusted with the responsibility of implementing the programme in a comprehensive manner. Maharashtra State Minorities Commission
