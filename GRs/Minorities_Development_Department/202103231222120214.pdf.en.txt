Preamble: The Grant-in-Aid Scheme for providing infrastructure facilities in Government recognized Private Aided/ Unaided/ Permanent Unaided Schools, Junior Colleges, Industrial Training Institutes, Municipal/ Municipal Council Schools and Disabled Schools, where students of religious minorities are in majority, is being implemented as per Government decision dated 7th October, 2015. Under the said scheme, a total amount of Rs. 4.96 crore has been disbursed to 248 institutions of the concerned district which were received by the Government on or before 18th March, 2020 in the year 2019-20 under the Government Decision No. Now, the Government was considering to disburse grants to the remaining eligible schools from the budgetary provision approved for the financial year 2020-21.
Government Decision- The High Level Selection Committee headed by the concerned District Collector has scrutinized the applications received from them as on 7th October 2015 as per the above Government Decision and after scrutiny, the list of eligible schools, along with recommendations and demand for necessary funds, has been sent to the Government for approval.
Proposals received from all the concerned District Collectors Office are examined at the government level as per the terms and conditions of the Government Resolution dated 7th October 2015 and incomplete or defective proposals are submitted.

The District Collector of the concerned district is being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Controlling Officer for this fund. The Government hereby approve the release of the said funds handed over to the concerned District Collectors subject to the condition of ensuring the fulfillment of the following paragraphs to be disbursed by drawing from the Budget Distribution System (BDS).
3. In the proposal received from the Collectorate to the Government, it has been brought to the notice of the Government that more than one proposal has been received for the same DIES CODE/ INSTITUTE CODE/ License Number and more than one proposal has been received for the same institution's primary, secondary and higher secondary schools in the same building/ premises. However, as per the terms and conditions of the Government decision dated 07.10.2015, subsidy will be provided for only one DIES CODE/ INSTITUTE CODE/ License Number. Also, in case of different schools/ colleges of the same institution in the same building/ premises, which are conducting different classes/ sessions as per separate schedules of the day and have different DIES code, the grant will be sanctioned for only one DIES code for the same building/ premises. The schools are not self-financing. This should be ensured by the concerned District Collector before disbursing the grant. They should also ensure that all the terms and conditions prescribed in the Government Decision dated 7th October 2015 are complied with. Under this scheme, the schools concerned should spend the funds for the specific purpose for which the funds have been sanctioned to the schools in the current year.
After disbursement of the grant to the schools, colleges, industrial training institutes, disabled and municipal/ municipal council schools, the concerned schools, colleges, industrial training institutes, disabled and municipal/ municipal council schools are required to complete the works mentioned in the proposal within a period of three months from the actual receipt of the grant and thereafter submit the utilization certificate in the prescribed format of the grant to the concerned District Collector. The District Collectors and the Accountants General shall submit a report on the utilization certificates received from all the concerned schools, colleges, industrial training institutes, disabled and municipal/ municipal council schools.
