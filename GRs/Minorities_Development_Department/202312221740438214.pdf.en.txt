Under the Prime Minister's New 15-Point Programme, in line with Clause 4, various schemes are implemented by the Minority Development Department for the educational development of minorities in the state and to bring them into the mainstream of the society. There are madrassas
Dr. Zakir Hussain Madrassa Modernization Scheme is being implemented from the year 2013-14 as per the Government decision dated 11.10.2013 to provide benefits like creation of infrastructure for modernization, grant for library, honorarium to teachers etc. Under this scheme, the government was considering to increase the amount of subsidy given to eligible madrassas in the state for infrastructure facilities.
Government's decision
Dr. Zakir Hussain Madrasa Modernization Scheme has increased the amount of grant-in-aid to eligible madrassas in the state for infrastructure facilities to a maximum of Rs. Grant up to a limit of Rs. 10.00 lakh is being approved. Priority will be given to madrassas fulfilling the following conditions while implementing the scheme.
1) The institution running the madrassa should be registered with the Waqf Board of the state.
2) All students studying in madrassas should be enrolled in the nearest school for regular education. Also, the madrasas where mathematics and science subjects are taught by teachers appointed on contract basis will be given priority and the same will have to be certified by the District Education Officer.
3) There should be only one madrasa in a building.
All other terms and conditions of the Government Order dated 11.10.2013 shall continue to be in force.
This Government decision is being issued with the concurrence received under Planning Department Informal Reference No.: 308/1471 dated 18.10.2023 and Finance Department Informal Reference No.: 333/ 23/ Expenditure-1 dated 17.11.2023.
