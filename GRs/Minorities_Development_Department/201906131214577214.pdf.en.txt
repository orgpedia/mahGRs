As per the guidelines of the Ministry of Minority Affairs, Government of India, Rs. Government Decision to Implement Prime Minister's New 15-Point Programme As per the Government Decision dated 5th July, 2016, the Government has decided to implement the Prime Minister's New 15-Point Programme. A state-level committee has been formed under the chairmanship of the Chief Secretary. The committee will review the Prime Minister's New 15-Point Programme and take a decision on the proposal received under the Centrally Sponsored Multi-Sectoral Development Programme (MSDP) and recommend the proposal to the Central Government. However, now the Central Government has decided to restructure the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Multi-Sectoral Development Programme (MSDP) and implement it under the name of Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karykram (PMJVK). Guidelines on Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karyakram (PMJVK) have been received from the Government of India vide letter dated 5th June, 2018.
To decide on the proposal received under this program. A State-level committee, headed by the Chief Secretary, with the Principal Secretary of the concerned department, the District Collector of the concerned district, the head of the lead bank (leadback) in the state, and the people's representatives as determined by the central government, should be formed. As directed by the Central Govt. As per the Government decision dated 5th July, 2016, under the Prime Minister's New 15-Point Programme, Rs. A State-level Minority Welfare Committee has been constituted under the chairmanship of the Chief Secretary. The committee will comprise members of the state-level Minority Welfare Committee as mentioned in the guidelines of the Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karyakram (PMJVK) programme, all other members except the District Collectors of the respective districts and the heads of Lead Backs in the state. Therefore, the government was considering including the district collectors of the concerned districts and the head of the state's Agrin Bank as members in the state-level Minority Welfare Committee and increasing the scope of this committee.
