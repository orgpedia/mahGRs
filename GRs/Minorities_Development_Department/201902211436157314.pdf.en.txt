The grants mentioned in front of the districts mentioned in the joint letter with this government decision are being handed over to the concerned district collectors. The decision of the government allows the withdrawal and disbursement of subsidy from the Budget Distribution System (BDS) within the limit of the funds handed over to them by the concerned collector.
For this, the concerned Collector will act as Controller and Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
The amount approved at the government level should be deposited through ECS in the nationalized bank account of the concerned madrasa as per the letter of association attached by the concerned Collector or the officer in his office authorized by him for this purpose, the remaining amount should be deposited in the government treasury.
Under this scheme, the madrassas concerned should spend the funds for the specific purpose for which the funds have been sanctioned in the current year.
After the distribution of the grant to the madrasas, the madrasas concerned are required to complete the works mentioned in the proposal within a period of three months from the actual receipt of the grant and thereafter submit the utility certificate in the prescribed format of the allocation of the grant to the concerned district collector. The Collector concerned should submit a report of the utilization certificates received from all the madrasas concerned to the government as well as to the Accountant General's office. The District Collector has been appointed as Controller, Government Circular, Finance Department No. AUD-1082/ CR- 90/84/ Cell-5, Dy. The expenditure of the Fund as on 24th September, 1986 should be reconciled and a report submitted to the Government. Also, the appropriation account report of the relevant head of accounts for the financial year 2018-19 must be submitted to the Government by September 30, 2019.
