In order to provide opportunities to the candidates belonging to the minority community of the state in the government/ semi-government services, for the competitive examinations of the Union Public Service Commission/ Maharashtra Public Service Commission/ Banking Service Recruitment, etc. as well as for the preparation of common entrance examinations for admission to professional courses, and for the introduction of special tuition classes for minority candidates who have failed in class 10th and 12th, Reference No. 1 has been approved.
Under this scheme, training centers are selected for training minority candidates in Mumbai, Nashik, Amravati, Nagpur, Thane, Pune and Aurangabad cities of the state. According to reference no. 2. As per the Government decision dated 22.08.2014, a training institute, Adarsh Multipurpose Society, Amravati has been selected for the Amravati Centre for a period of 3 years. The grant has been disbursed to the Institute under the Government Decision of Reference No. 4 to 11 for the year 2014-15, 2015-16 in phases. The institute has now sought disbursal of the grant for 2016-17. Al Ikhlas Educational and Welfare Society, Aurangabad. As per the decision of the Government of Maharashtra, Aurangabad Centre has been selected for a period of 3 years.
The institute has sought disbursal of grants for 2016-17 and 2017-18.
3. After examining the proposals of demand for grants submitted by the above said Adarsh Multipurpose Society, Amravati and Al Ikhlas Educational and Welfare Society, Aurangabad, Reference No. 1, the matter of sanctioning the grant in accordance with the provision mentioned in the Government Decision dated 25.06.2013 and the number of students who have attended 85% of the actual attendance was under the consideration of the Government.

Government Decision: Under the scheme" Maulana Azad Free Tuition and Related Schemes," Government Decision, dated 25.06.2013, grants are being sanctioned to Adarsh Multipurpose Society, Amravati and Al Ikhlas Educational and Welfare Society, Aurangabad as per the number of students who have completed the provision and have 85% attendance.
As per the Budget 2018-19, Rs. 3.00 Crores, 70% of which is Rs. 2.10 crore is available for disbursement. Out of this available fund, as per the decision of the Government vide Reference No.12, Rs. 32,62,000/- has been disbursed. The remaining Rs. 1,77,38, 000/-to Adarsh Multipurpose Society, Amravati and Al Ikhlas Educational and Welfare Society, Aurangabad as mentioned in Annexure-A. Grant of Rs. 24,25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Four Lakh Twenty Five Thousand only) is being approved. In this case, the Under Secretary, Minority Development Department Controller and Drawing and Disbursing Officer will be there to collect this amount.
The Electronic Clearing Service (ECS) of the said amount should be drawn in favour of the Under Secretary (1), Minority Development Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai.
3. The training institutions concerned should maintain year-wise accounting details in this regard and spend only on the items for which grants have been disbursed. The utilization certificate of this amount will have to be submitted to the government by the concerned training institute. It shall be mandatory to submit the utilization certificate of these items to the Government as mentioned in Annexure' B'. Officials of the Minority Development Department will visit the concerned training institute. If it is found that the institution has not complied with the provisions of the Government Decision, dated 25.06.2013, action will be taken against the institution and the subsidy disbursed will be recovered.
This Government decision is in compliance with the conditions contained in the Reference No. 12 of the Department of Finance, Circular No. 6 to 8 of the Circular dated 02.04.2018 and the Annexure thereto. It is being done accordingly.
