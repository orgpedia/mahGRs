Preamble: The Government has decided to set up Self Help Groups of women belonging to minority communities in the State with a view to provide adequate credit by mobilizing them for the economic empowerment of women belonging to the community living in minority dominated areas of the State and to develop entrepreneurial skills among them by providing them necessary information about entrepreneurship. It was approved on February 28, 2012. Under this, in the first phase, approval has been given to set up self-help groups of women belonging to minority communities in Mumbai city, Mumbai suburbs, Nanded, Malegaon, Karanja, Parbhani, Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Bhiwandi, Mumbra Kausa and Miraj. Under this scheme, 2400 Self Help Groups of minority women were to be formed and their 13 Common Resource Centres were to be set up within a period of 8 years.
According to the Women's Economic Development Corporation. As on 31st March 2020, 3200 Self Help Groups and 8 Common Resource Centres have been set up.
As the 8 year period of the scheme ended on 31st March, 2020, the scheme has been referred to Reference No. 3 d. The extension period has been extended by five years (from April 1, 2020 to March 31, 2025) as per the Government decision dated October 1, 2020. 800 new SHGs are proposed to be created in the next 5 years.
The Women Economic Development Corporation (WEDC) will also work towards empowering women from minority communities financially by strengthening existing SHGs, imparting training to SHG members on innovative entrepreneurial skills, and ensuring their business continuity.
