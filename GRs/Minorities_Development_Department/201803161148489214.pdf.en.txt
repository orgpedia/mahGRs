To provide scholarships to students studying in 9th, 10th, 11th and 12th classes and Industrial Training Institutes, which will help in improving the educational quality of students, increasing their employability and improving their economic status, the Government has decided to implement the Dr. Zakir Hussain Madrasa Modernization Scheme for providing infrastructure for modernization of madrasas, providing grants for library and grant for honorarium to teachers. No. 152/10/ Ka-6, Dy. It was approved on October 11, 2013. The expenditure for this scheme is made under the head" Social Security and Welfare, 02 Social Welfare, 200 Other Programmes (01) (31) Schemes under State Schemes, Infrastructure to registered Madrassas in the State and Subsidiary Grants for Salaries of Subject Teachers (2235B227) 31 Subsidiary Grants." An amount of Rs. 08.00 crore has been sanctioned for the year 2017-18 under this head. As per Government Circular, Finance Division No.Finance-2017/ PR.No. 75/ Finance-3, dated 6th February, 2018, the budgetary allocation under the scheme for expenditure up to the end of the financial year is 80% of the total annual allocation of Rs. Rs. 6.40 crore. Under this scheme, the government was considering to allocate grants to madrassas in the financial year 2017-18.

Under this scheme, the madrassas concerned should spend the funds for the specific purpose for which the funds have been sanctioned in the current year.
After the distribution of the grant to the madrasas, the madrasas concerned are required to complete the works mentioned in the proposal within a period of three months from the actual receipt of the grant and thereafter submit the utility certificate in the prescribed format of the allocation of the grant to the concerned district collector. The Collector concerned should submit a report of the utilization certificates received from all the madrasas concerned to the government as well as to the Accountant General's office.
