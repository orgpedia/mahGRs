The proposal-
The Grant-in-Aid Scheme for providing infrastructure facilities in Government recognized Private Aided/ Unaided Schools, Junior Colleges, Industrial Training Institutes, Municipal/ Municipal Council Schools and Disabled Schools having a student population of Religious Minorities is being implemented as per Government Decision dated 7th October, 2015.
Under the said scheme, the eligible schools in the proposal received from the concerned Collectorate to the Government are being approved for sanction of grants for the financial year 2022-23 as follows.
Government Decision: The decision was taken by a high-level selection committee headed by the concerned District Collector. The applications received from them as on 7.10.2015 have been scrutinized and after scrutiny, the proposal along with the list of eligible schools, recommendations and demand for necessary funds have been sent to the Government for approval. All the proposals received from the respective Collectorate will be scrutinized at the government level and the number of schools that qualify in it and they will be approved (maximum limit is Rs. 2.00 lakh) The amount of subsidy is as follows:

A total of 139 schools as shown in the above table and as per the list at Annexure A of the said Government Decision, have been sanctioned Rs. 2,77,95, 974/- (Rupees Two Crores, Seventy Seven Lakhs, Ninety Five Thousand, Nine Hundred and Seventy Four only) is hereby approved by the Government.
The funds will be handed over to the respective district collectors. The District Collector of the concerned district is being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Controlling Officer for this fund. The names of the eligible schools for which grants have been sanctioned along with the details of the nature of the demand for the school, the year in which the grant has been availed earlier, are given in the Annexure to this Government Decision. The Government hereby grants approval for the release of the said funds to the respective District Collectors, subject to the fulfillment of the following paragraphs, to be disbursed by drawing the same from the Budget Distribution System (BDS).
(2) The decision of the Government. Subsidy will be provided for only one DIES CODE/ INSTITUTE CODE/ LICENSE NUMBER as per the terms and conditions as on 07.10.2015. Also, in case of different schools/ colleges of the same institution in the same building/ premises, which are conducting different classes/ sessions as per separate schedules of the day and have different DIES code, the grant will be sanctioned for only one DIES code for the same building/ premises. Also, the government decided before distributing the grant by the concerned collector that the aided schools are not self-financed. The terms and conditions as prescribed in 7.10.2015 shall be adhered to. Under this scheme, the schools concerned should spend the funds for the specific purpose for which the funds have been sanctioned to the schools in the current year.
After disbursement of the grant to the schools, colleges, industrial training institutes, disabled and municipal/ municipal schools, the concerned schools, colleges, industrial training institutes, disabled and municipal/ municipal schools are required to complete the works mentioned in the proposal within a period of three months from the actual receipt of the grant. After this, it is mandatory to submit the utility certificate in the prescribed format of the allocation of the grant to the concerned district collector. Also, the Collector should submit the report of utility certificates to the government and the Accountant General's office in the prescribed format as per the" Annexure-B" accompanying this government decision.
