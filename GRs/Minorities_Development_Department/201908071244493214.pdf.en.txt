Preamble Special Programme of Measures for Rapid Development of Marathwada, Vidarbha and North Maharashtra, 2018. The guidelines have been issued by the Planning Department under the Government Decision No. 1. It includes measures to provide skill development training and employment opportunities to women belonging to minority communities by creating Minority Women Self Help Groups to empower them financially considering their backwardness and weak economic condition. The proposal was under the consideration of the government. Accordingly, considering the opinion of the Planning and Finance Department on the proposal of this Department, the As per the decision of the State Cabinet in its meeting held on 09.07.2019, the Government has decided to implement the above measures as follows:
Government Decision 2. The Government has approved the formation of Self Help Groups for minority women in five cities of the State namely Nanded, Karanja (Washim), Parbhani, Aurangabad and Nagpur and fourteen districts of Aurangabad, Nagpur, Amravati, Buldhana, Yavatmal, Hingoli, Jalna, Osmanabad, Latur, Chandrapur, Beed, Wardha, Bhandara, Gondia and Gadchiroli.
The duration of the scheme will be for two years (2019-20 and 2020-21). For this scheme, Rs. Rs 623.35 crore. The funds will be provided through budgetary provisions in two financial years i.e. 2019-20 and 2020-21.

4. In the fourteen districts of Aurangabad, Nagpur and Amravati revenue divisions namely Akola, Amravati, Buldhana, Yavatmal, Hingoli, Jalna, Osmanabad, Latur, Chandrapur, Beed, Wardha, Bhandara, Gondia and Gadchiroli, 200 new Minority Women Self Help Groups will be formed per district.
The Self Help Groups for minority women will include poor and needy women belonging to minority communities. The SHGs will primarily comprise women belonging to the below poverty line (BPL) category.
For the purpose of establishment of Self Help Groups for minority women, capacity building, mentoring of Bank Linked Self Help Groups and other related processes, Rs. 10,000/- will be made available to Women Economic Development Corporation.
Under this scheme, the Women's Economic Development Corporation will request the government for the necessary funds for the establishment of minority women self-help groups. At present. In addition to the existing Self Help Group Scheme for Minority Women as decided by the Government on 28.02.2012, the Government will make available the funds due to the Women Economic Development Corporation for the said scheme.
The Minority Development Department will be responsible for monitoring the scheme. The Women's Economic Development Corporation will submit a quarterly report on the implementation of the scheme to the government. The Women's Economic Development Corporation will be responsible for providing the utility certificate to the government from time to time regarding the allocation of funds made available for the formation of self-help groups under this scheme.
As per the provisions of the Government Decision issued from time to time by the Department of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship regarding the Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission, skill development training prepared according to the professional demand for skills is being approved to be provided to women belonging to minority women self-help groups through the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society.
The general pattern of skill development training programme for women belonging to Minority Women Self Help Groups will be as follows.
7.1) Nanded, Karanja (Dist. The Women Economic Development Corporation (WEDC) will provide skill development training to women in the recently formed and functioning Self Help Groups for Minority Women under the scheme of Minority Development Department in five cities of Maharashtra viz. Washim, Parbhani, Aurangabad and Nagpur and to about 1500 women in the newly formed Self Help Groups for Minority Women in the above mentioned 14 districts of Aurangabad, Nagpur and Amravati divisions in two years.

7.2) Minority women who have taken skill development training from various other schemes of the government in the last 3 years will not be given skill development training again under this scheme.
7.3 Training shall be provided to trainee women in or near the area in which they are resident.
7.4) For selection of beneficiaries, counseling, pre-training preparation, training materials, trainer honorarium, certificates, equipment kits, placement and other incidental expenses, etc. 21,800/- will be made available to Maharashtra State Skill Development Society. In case of any future change in the training fee by the Skill Development Department, the training fee for the relevant training will be payable as per the decision of the government of that department. However, based on the actual consideration of the duration of the training and the fee to be charged by the society, the required amount will be demanded by the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and accordingly the amount will be paid directly by the Minority Development Department.
7.5 The duration of training shall be at least 3 months.
7.6. Under Pramod Mahajan Skill Development and Entrepreneurship Development Mission, Skill Development Training will be provided to women candidates of Self Help Groups for Minority Women from the training institute appointed by Maharashtra State Skill Development Society as per the demand of vocational skills.
7.7. The list of eligible minority women candidates for skill development training will be made available by the Women's Economic Development Corporation to the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society. Necessary funds for this skill development training will be made available by the government to the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society from time to time.
7.8. Maharashtra State Skill Development Completion Certificate will be issued to each successful beneficiary by the Society on successful completion of skill training. This certification must have acceptability in the market and in the industry concerned.
7.9) All training modules must be industry-based, so that the beneficiaries have access to employment opportunities.
7.10) The concerned training institution will not be entrusted with the responsibility of testing and certifying the skills of the beneficiary. All such institutions will be selected separately through the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society.
7.11) Training classes for minority trainees will be conducted separately, as far as possible. The training institutes where the skill training is going on will be visited during the training and the quality will be verified by the officials of the Minority Development Department during the training.
7.12) In each case, the minority women trainees who have participated in the training, the skill development training given to them and the use of the knowledge gained by the beneficiaries for employment/ business etc. will be monitored by the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and a consolidated report of the concerned district will be submitted by them to the government as well as the Women's Economic Development Corporation.

7.13) The list of trainees will be published separately on the website of Skill Development Society, Women's Economic Development Corporation and Government.
7.14) Beneficiaries who have undergone training under this scheme once will not be trained again in this scheme and it will be required to be verified by Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and Women Economic Development Corporation.
7.15) The Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and the Women's Economic Development Corporation will be responsible for providing the utilization certificate to the government from time to time regarding the allocation of funds made available for training under this scheme.
The monthly report on training will be submitted to the government by the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society.
The Maharashtra State Skill Development Society will be responsible for providing skill development training to women beneficiaries of Self Help Groups for minority women under this scheme and to assist them in getting self-employment and to follow up the same for the next six months from the date of receipt of self-employment by the beneficiaries. The Maharashtra State Skill Development Society will submit the report to the government and MAVIM.
Orders for the distribution of funds under the said scheme shall be issued separately.
