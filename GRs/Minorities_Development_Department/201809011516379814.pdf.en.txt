The Proposal:
In order to provide opportunities to the candidates belonging to minority communities of the State in Government/ Paramilitary Services, for competitive examinations like Central Public Service Commission/ Maharashtra Public Service Commission/ Banking Service Recruitment etc. as well as for the preparation of Common Entrance Examinations for admission to professional courses and for starting special tuition classes for the minority candidates who have failed in class 10th and 12th, Reference No. 1 has been approved. Under this scheme, the training of the above courses is given by selecting the training institutes at Mumbai, Thane, Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Amravati and Nagpur.
Accordingly, training institutes for training of minority candidates in the cities of Mumbai, Nashik, Amravati and Nagpur in the state have been selected. It has been done for 3 years. In pursuance of the above decision of the Government, Diamond Charitable and Educational Trust, Nagpur, a training institute based at Nagpur Training Centre, has submitted a proposal to the Government seeking subsidy for the training provided to the students of Class XI of CET Course for the year 2016-17 who have passed Class XII in the year 2017-18. The training institute Diamond Charitable and Educational Trust has been disbursed the stage grant to 80 students for the Common Entrance Test, as per the Government decision in the above reference number 4,5,6 and 8. The Institute has sought final stage grant for CET 2nd year (Class 12th) for the year 2017-18. The matter of sanctioning grant-in-aid to the above training institute as per the provisions of Government Resolution dated 25.06.2013 was under consideration of the Government.
