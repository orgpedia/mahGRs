Preamble: With the objective of increasing the recruitment of candidates belonging to minority groups in the police service in the state, the Government of Reference No. 1 was issued. The Police Recruitment Pre-Examination Training Scheme is being implemented from 27th July 2009. In order to ensure effective implementation of the scheme, the change in the form and duration of the scheme is mentioned in Reference No. The 2. The said training programme has been revised under the Government Decision dated 17.12.2021. Accordingly, it has been decided to make this training program residential and of 3 months duration. An advertisement was issued at the government level for inviting applications from interested institutions to the District Collector of the concerned district for conducting this training program in the year 2022-23 under the revised Resident Police Pre-Recruitment Training Scheme. Accordingly, after scrutinizing the application received from the desirous institute and according to the site inspection report submitted by the Collector with his clear recommendation, the matter of selecting a qualified training institute for the year 2022-23 was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: The government has taken a decision for the candidates belonging to the minority community in the state. Under the Resident Police Pre-recruitment Training Scheme being implemented from December 17, 2021, the Government is approving the selection of eligible private training institutes in the following 10 districts for the year 2022-23 for 50 students per district as per the site inspection report submitted by the concerned Collector with his clear recommendation after scrutinizing the application received from the concerned Collector.

3. Also the training institute gave the decision of the government. Contract for training as per Appendix A dated 17.12.2021 should be submitted. At the same time, the government has decided to provide training to the police personnel under the Resident Police Pre-recruitment Training Programme. As prescribed on 17 th December, 2021, the grant will be disbursed to the training institute through the Collector in the amount of 50% at the beginning of the training, 25% after 2 months of the training and 25% after the completion of the training after the candidates submit proof of having actually appeared in the physical and written test of the police recruitment.
The selected training institute will provide 3 months residential training to 50 candidates belonging to minority groups. No scholarship will be paid to the trainees under any circumstances.
