The Director, Directorate of Health Services, has sanctioned construction of 50 bedded Sub District Hospital for Minorities at Anjangaon Surji G. (3) Rs. A proposal for construction of hospital at a cost of Rs. 2316.57 lakh has been approved. As per the approval of the Central Government, for the construction of 50-bed Sub-District Hospital at Anjangaon Surji G. Administrative Sanction is being accorded to the Budget Estimate of Rs. 2316.57/-lakhs (Rupees Twenty Three Crores Sixteen Lakhs Fifty Seven Thousand) subject to the following conditions. :
Terms and Conditions 1. The detailed budget for the construction of the hospital shall be prepared by the competent authority of the Public Works Department.
Technical approval is required.
The construction of the hospital should be done as per the technical approval given by the competent authority of the Public Works Department in the detailed budget.
3.The necessary requirements for the construction of the hospital should be completed quickly and for this, the work should be started immediately by conducting a tender process as per the government norms and the same should be informed to the government.
4) Before starting the work, the ownership of the land should be confirmed.
As per the approval of the Central Government, the Central+ State share for construction of 01 District Hospital is 60:40 respectively. Separate Utilization Certificates for allocation of funds in Central and State accounts received for construction should be submitted to the Government in the form of Quarterly Progress Report (QPR) with updated photographs.
(6) No work shall be started on the budget of the hospital construction except the construction of the main building and" other ancillary matters" without the technical approval of the competent authority of the Public Works Department and receipt of actual funds.
While seeking funds for the second phase of the hospital construction, utility certificates in the prescribed format regarding the utilization of all funds received in the first phase, quarterly progress reports (with updated photographs) should be submitted to the government along with the proposal.
If the work is to be done according to the new map, the architectural plan should be approved according to the maps through a private architect appointed by the Public Health Department. Also, the newly appointed architect and district surgeon will be responsible for obtaining the construction permit and occupancy certificate as per the relevant rules of the local rural development agency.
Also, the letter requesting this permission must be signed by the district surgeon.
No separate approval of the maps and architectural plans of the work is required from the Chief/ Deputy Chief Architect of the Public Works Department.
