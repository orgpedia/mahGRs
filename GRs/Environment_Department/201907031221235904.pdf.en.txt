The Maharashtra Pollution Control Board (MPCB) has been asked to provide grants to carry out a massive environmental awareness campaign in the state in line with the ban on plastic and thermocol products.

The Department of Environment
Government Resolution No. Plastic 2019/ S. No.66/ T.C.1
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Madam Cama Marg,
Ministry of Health, Mumbai- 32
Date: 1st of July 2019
Read: 1. Finance Department Government Circular No.Finance-, 2019/ PR No. 44/ Finance-3, D. 01.04.2019 2. Government Notification No. Plastic 2018/ PR No.97/ T.K.1/ D. 23.03.2018 3. Office Order No. Documentary 18/ Department of Environment/ Plastic Bag Ban-Ja.P./ Ka. 7/84 dated 22.11.2018
Preamble: Under the powers conferred under Section 2 and Section 4 of the Maharashtra Biodegradable and Non-biodegradable Waste (Control) Act, 2006, it is necessary to carry out an awareness campaign on plastic and thermocol products throughout the state. Various media should be used to spread awareness. The government was considering providing a grant for the purpose.
Government Decision: The Planning and Implementation Committee has approved a comprehensive media plan to create awareness among the people about the ban on plastic and thermocol through various media.
As per the media plan prepared by the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations, the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations has issued an order dated November 22, 2018 regarding the publication of Rs 25,000/- to the advertiser organization. It has been directed to send the payment of advertisement regarding the broadcast to the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Mumbai. In addition to the above reference No. Work orders have been issued under 3.
In pursuance of the above mandate of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations, sanction for release of funds to the tune of Rs. 25,00,000/-is being accorded. The fund is being distributed to the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board. For this, a cheque of Rs 25,00,000/- should be drawn in the name of Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Mumbai. Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board should take action to pay the payments after verifying that the work of publication has been done as per the above plan in accordance with the payment received.
