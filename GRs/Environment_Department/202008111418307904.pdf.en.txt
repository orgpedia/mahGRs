The Proposal:
The COVID-19 pandemic has led to a significant slowdown in economic and industrial growth globally. Several world-class manufacturing companies from countries in the vicinity of the outbreak of COVID-19 virus are exploring opportunities for industrial investment in other countries with the aim of reducing dependence on one country. Such investee companies are looking for alternate locations in the vicinity which will have minimum disruption in their production chain and will have minimum cost.
In this backdrop, the opportunity to increase industrial investment in the state is also seen in the crisis situation of the Covid-19 outbreak. In such a situation, what measures should be taken to attract foreign investors to the state and increase the industry. A study group appointed under the chairmanship of the Minister (Industries) has suggested effective innovative measures to facilitate world-class multinational and domestic companies to invest in the state of Maharashtra. It was submitted to the Cabinet. According to The Cabinet has approved the same in its meeting dated 25/06/2020. Among these measures, the government was considering reducing the time for providing consent letters related to the Environment Department in providing Mahaparwana/ Fast No-Objection Permits. The government has taken the following decisions:
Industries are required to obtain consent under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and the Air Pollution (Prevention and Control) Act, 1981 and the Hazardous Solid Waste Rules, 2016. The Maharashtra Pollution Control Board (MPCB) is required to give a consent letter within 120 days.
