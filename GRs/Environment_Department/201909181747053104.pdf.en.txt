In order to conserve the lakes, lakes and large water bodies in the state ecologically, the state government has started the State Lake Conservation Plan under the State Plan from the financial year 2006-07. A typical program typically includes the following:
To take measures to prevent pollution by determining the sources of pollution of the lake water.
Removal of harmful and organic sludge accumulated in the lake.
To maintain the quality of the lake, a. to take measures to control the growth of unwanted and infested plants in the lake.
(b) Improving the quality of water in the lake through biological processes in the lake.
Beautification of beaches, development of green belts, fencing, recreation parks, boating, low cost toilets etc.
Programmes for public participation and awareness and measures to be taken according to local conditions to prevent pollution.
Under this scheme, in the financial year 2019-20, the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Sangli received Rs. Kawalpur. The matter of approving the proposal for the conservation of Kavlapur Lake in Miraj G. Sangli was under the consideration of the government.

Out of the total amount sanctioned, 90% will be provided by the State Government and 10% will be provided by Gram Panchayat Kawlapur. The financial structure of the scheme is as follows:

Kawalapur under the State Lake Conservation Scheme. Terms and Conditions for Conservation of Kawlapur Lake in Miraj District Sangli Range
The works mentioned in Form-A should first be taken up after obtaining technical approval. Approved technical plans, budgets, maps, concept drawings etc. of the work to be undertaken in each phase of the scheme shall be mandatorily submitted to the Government before commencement of work. The work will be started after the report is submitted to the government. Photographs should be taken of the present status of the lake in relation to the work to be undertaken (works mentioned in Form-A). A video recording is also required. Photography should be done in this way every two months according to the progress of the work.
(2) The Chief Taxation Officer, Zilla Parishad, shall be appointed as the Project Management Consultant of the Institute/ Government Authority/ Board having expertise and work experience in the field of lake conservation and having adequate technical manpower in respect of the approved expenditure as per Form-A. The Institute shall coordinate the approved expenditure items as per Form-A with the local DSRs from time to time for cost estimation, preparation and evaluation of work plans, maintenance of quality of work, completion of all works within prescribed timelines. The Gram Panchayat should use this institution to prepare technical manpower for the project.
3. The local bodies concerned shall deposit their permissible share in the account in the prescribed proportion of the funds disbursed by the Government. No further instalment will be released without spending the said share on the project as well as without conducting quality audit of the works under the project.
The local body concerned will be fully responsible for completing the work within the stipulated time and maintaining the quality of work. The expenditure of the project should be done under the supervision of the Chief Executive Officer and the District Council.
The Project Implementation Committee should be constituted under the chairmanship of the Chief Executive Officer, District Council with a view to the proper implementation of the project. The committee should include officials, staff, environmental experts, representatives of the above-mentioned project management consultancy organization, as well as representatives of the village panchayat as deemed necessary. The committee will monitor the quality of work and all the activities being carried out under the project. A meeting of the committee will be held every month.
The committee will hold meetings even after the completion of the project as the lake needs to be conserved without the help of the Project Management Consultant. The committee will review the maintenance of the physical aspects, income from the projects, measures and expenditure for the conservation of the projects.
After completion of the work, the local bodies will have to carry out maintenance and repair of the lake at their own cost for at least the next 10 years. The project implementation committee will submit its report to the government every six months.
