The Proposal:
Considering the importance of climate change in the environment, the Department of Environment has been renamed as" Department of Environment and Climate Change." The Department of Environment and Climate Change is working on the five elements related to nature- Earth, Air, Water, Fire, and Sky- to ensure its commitment to nature.
The Department has taken up an innovative initiative" Majhi Vasundhara (My Earth)" to preserve, conserve and protect the environment. Which focuses on all the five elements called" Panchamahabhoota" of nature. They are air, fire, water, sky and land.
This initiative will make the citizens aware of climate change and environmental issues and will help them in their efforts towards environmental improvement. This will help in the sustainable development of the state.

My campaign:
3.1 The" Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan" based on the five elements of nature- Earth, Air, Water, Fire and Sky- is being implemented in the State from 2nd October, 2020. Under this campaign, a competition is held to implement the schemes of the local bodies like Urban Local Bodies and Gram Panchayats in a mission mode.
3.2 Indicator Toolkit is prepared to bring together various Government schemes/ programmes/ measures for protection and conservation of environment based on the five elements of nature and implement them effectively and in a mission mode. In which marks are kept to evaluate the work done. According to the toolkit, various measures are to be implemented in the local bodies over a period of one year from April to March.
3.3 MIS Report on the work done by local bodies from April to March.
I want to fill in the form on the web portal of the Vasundhara Abhiyan. The MIS filled by the local bodies is evaluated through a three-tier system.
3.4 The local bodies that perform well in the desktop assessment are evaluated by visiting the concerned local body through the triage system.
3.5 The local bodies are felicitated on World Environment Day i. e. 5th June by selecting the best performing local bodies as winners on the basis of their performance in Desktop Assessment and Field Assessment.
4. Under the Government decision at Reference No. 6 under the heading of Accounts opened for giving cash awards to the best performing local bodies under Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan.
An amount of Rs. 100.00 crore has been provided by way of reallocation. It was under consideration of the Government to make available the funds to the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board for disbursement to the awardee local bodies.
Government Decision: Considering the matter mentioned in the Preamble, the Government is pleased to approve the release of funds to the tune of Rs. 96,91,00, 000/- (Rupees Ninety-six Crores Ninety-one Lakhs only) to the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Mumbai for providing cash awards to the best performing local bodies under the Majhi Vasundhara Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan. The funds will be utilised by the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Mumbai, in accordance with the procedures prescribed by the government for awarding cash prizes to local bodies under the Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan. Also, the utility certificate regarding the allocation of funds should be submitted to the government.
