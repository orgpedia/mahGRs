GOVERNMENT DECISION:" Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan" based on the five elements related to nature- earth, air, water, fire and sky- was launched in the local bodies of the state on 2 nd October, 2020. " Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan 3.0" was implemented in the local bodies of the State from 1 st April, 2022 to 31 st March, 2023. A total of 16,824 local bodies, including 411 urban local bodies and 16,413 gram panchayats, participated in the campaign.
As per the toolkit issued under" Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan 3.0," for desktop assessment, 7,600 marks were kept for Urban Local Bodies (AMRUT groups), 7,500 marks for Urban Local Bodies (excluding AMRUT groups) and 7,500 marks for Gram Panchayats.
3. In Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan 3.0, Desktop Assessment and Field Assessment of the work done by the local bodies during the campaign period was done through three mechanisms. Appropriation and operation of the prize money: (1) Scheme of measures to be taken out of the prize money: The prize money shall be utilised for undertaking measures for the protection, conservation and preservation of the five elements of nature.
Some of these measures are given below: 1) Measures to enhance green cover of the city/ village (including conservation of biodiversity) e.g. (a) Miyawaki Plantation (b) Amrut Van (c) Memorial Forest (d) Urban Forest (e) Butterfly Garden (e) Public Gardens (ii) Conservation and Maintenance of Old Green Areas (including conservation of biodiversity) 3) Plantation of Plantations 4) Water Conservation Activities 5) Rain Water Harvesting and Percolation 6) Rejuvenation/ Beautification of Rivers, Lakes and Drainages 7) Measures to promote use of Renewable Energy Sources 8) Solar Powered/ LED Powered Lights 9) Promotion of Electric Vehicles Providing Charging Points.
(2) Preparation of Detailed Project Report for distribution of prizes to the winners of various competitions held under Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan: The concerned local body shall prepare a detailed project report for planning measures for the protection, conservation and preservation of the five elements of nature from the total fund of the prize received by them. While preparing this Detailed Project Report, 50% of the total prize money should be used to increase the green cover. The remaining 50% of the amount should be used for other environmental measures schemes. The Government is approving to use the amount up to a maximum of 10% of the 50% amount for other relief schemes to provide prizes to the winners of the competitions to be conducted in My Vasundhara Abhiyan 4.0 (2023-24).
(3) Technical Approval: The works included in the Detailed Project Report shall be taken for technical approval by the Urban Local Bodies through the City Engineer of the nearest Municipal Corporation and by the Gram Panchayats through the Executive Engineer of the Construction Department of the concerned Zilla Parishad. When such a proposal is received by the City Engineer of the concerned Municipal Corporation/ Construction Department of the Zilla Parishad, they should give technical approval to the proposal by following the prescribed procedure.
(4) Administrative approval: Government Decision, Department of Environment and Climate Change dated February 8, 2022. A committee under the chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Environment and Climate Change Department will recommend for administrative approval of the proposals received. After the recommendations of the committee, an order will be issued for granting administrative approval.
Implementation of the project: The works included in the approved Detailed Project Report shall be implemented by the concerned local body.
