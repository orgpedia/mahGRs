Preamble- The State Government has launched the State River Conservation Plan dated 01/03/2014 to conserve the rivers of the State ecologically. A typical program typically includes the following:
The measures for collection, interception, diversion and transportation of sewage from the point source to the treatment plant etc. are not covered under the city's underground drainage scheme.
Establishment of appropriate technology based sewage treatment system and its ancillary components etc. for treatment of wastewater keeping in view the quantity, quality and local geographical conditions of wastewater.
Establishment of necessary systems/ facilities/ arrangements etc. to enable recycling and reuse of treated wastewater in the project area or nearby agriculture, industrial units, water bodies etc., so that the revenue generated can be used to meet the cost of maintenance of the project etc.
Construction of low cost toilets in the vicinity of the river, linking the effluent to the sewage treatment system or similar measures for treatment etc.
River ghat development, measures to prevent river bank erosion, conservation of river resources etc.
Provisional appointment of Consultants/ Specialist Engineers etc. as per requirement for capacity building of technicians, training, research as well as for the success of the scheme as per prescribed standards.
Other important activities to reduce pollution as per the local conditions of the scheme, such as management and disposal of urban solid waste in a scientific manner, generation and utilization of non-conventional energy, public participation, etc.
Under this scheme, the government was considering to approve the proposal of Chudamani River submitted by the Chief Officer, Warud Municipal Council, G. Amaravati for the financial year 2021-22 and to release the corresponding funds. The government has taken the following decisions:

Government's decision:
Under the State River Conservation Plan, the proposal for ecologically conserving the Chudamani River submitted by the Chief Officer, Warud Municipal Council, G. Amaravathi, is being approved in principle with a limit of Rs.15 crore. Of the Rs. 7,27,35, 444/- (Rupees Seven Crores Twenty Seven Lakhs Thirty Five Thousand Four Hundred Forty Four)
Only) Such funds are being sanctioned for distribution to the Principal, Warud Municipal Council, G. Amaravati.
In order to conserve the river's environment, the work of sewer interception and diversion, installation of phytoride technology, installation of STP, silt trap, aeration, low-cost sanitation, removal of water hyacinth, etc. should be limited to core work- 80% and non-core work- 20% as per the guidelines issued by the Central Environment and Forest Department under the National River Conservation Plan. It should be noted that the proposed fund should be based on the District Schedule Rate (DSR) issued by the Water Resources Department and similar authorities.
After the revised Detailed Project Report in this case is received by the department, the administrative and final approval will be taken at the government level.
The Chief Officer, Warud Municipal Council, G. Amaravathi should ensure that the funds distributed under this government decision are spent only after receiving administrative and final approval.
