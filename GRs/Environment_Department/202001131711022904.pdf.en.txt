Environment Service Scheme (ESS) was started in the year 2011 in the secondary schools of the state of Maharashtra by the Department of Environment with the aim of creating an environmentally sensitive and aware future generation by inculcating the importance of environmental conservation and protection in the students at the school level. In the first phase, 50 schools in 12 districts of the state will be covered. The project is being implemented by the Centre for Environment Education (CEE), Pune. In this regard, to implement the above programme in the financial year 2019-20, the Centre for Environmental Education, Pune has issued reference No. 5 and No. Under Section 7, the Department of Environment has received a total budget of Rs. 1,28,89, 435/-. In pursuance of the said scheme, a review meeting was held under the chairmanship of Principal Secretary (Environment) on 19th August, 2019 and as per the decision taken in the said meeting, the Centre for Environment Education (CEE), Pune, has decided to implement the scheme in a phased manner.
