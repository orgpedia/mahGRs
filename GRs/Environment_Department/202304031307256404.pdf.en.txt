In order to conserve the lakes, lakes and large water bodies in the state from the environmental point of view, the state government has started the State Lake Conservation Plan from the financial year 2006-07 under the State Plan. A typical program typically includes the following:
1. Identify sources of pollution of lake water and take measures to prevent pollution.
2. Removal of hazardous and organic sludge from the lake.
To maintain the quality of the lake, a. to take measures to control the growth of unwanted and nuisance plants in the lake.
(b) Improving the quality of water in the lake through biological processes in the lake.
Beautification of beaches, development of green belts, fencing, recreation parks, boating, low cost toilets etc.
Programmes for public participation and awareness and measures to be taken according to local conditions to prevent pollution.
Under this scheme, the proposal of Mukteshwar Lake submitted by the Chief Officer, Ausa Municipal Council, G. Latur, in the financial year 2022-23 was under the consideration of the government for in-principle approval and release of corresponding funds.
Government's decision
Under the State Lake Conservation Scheme, the proposal for the environmental conservation of Mukteshwar Lake presented by the Chief Officer, Ausa Municipal Council, was approved by the Steering Committee. Discussion was held on 27.02.2023. Subject to the decision in the minutes of the meeting, in-principle approval is being accorded in the limit of Rs. 4,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Four Crore only). In line with the work being proposed to conserve and protect the lake environmentally, the RCC Compound Wall/ Gabion Wall should be included with chain-link fencing at the required places and at the required height, instead of the complete RCC Compound Wall, Gabion Wall or similar RCC structures. The works to be taken up include tree fences, Sewage Treatment Plant (STP), Drainage Management System, Desilting, Dewatering, Plantation/ Afforestation, Aerators cum fountains, Solar Lighting, Immersion Ghats, Nirmalya Kalash and Awareness generation etc.
As per the National Lake Conservation Plan (NLCP) guidelines, only centage charge/ contingency charge will be allowed.
A chart of basic information about the lake should be submitted.
A resolution regarding the share of the local body to be lifted, a resolution of the local body about who will maintain the lake after its conservation is completed, and information about if the work of the said lake is going on under other schemes should be submitted.
Current status of water quality report (PH, TSS, BOD, COD, DO, Nitrogen, Phosphorous, E-coli)- Water quality report prepared by collecting water samples from different places (at least 5 to 6) in the lake area to measure the pollution level of the lake.
The ownership of the said lake should be informed (copy of seven-twelve documents) The fund required for works related to lake beautification like non-core work (construction of ghats, gardens, pathways, installation of benches) etc. should not be more than 25%. The proposed fund should be based on the District Schedule Rate (DSR) issued by the Water Resources Department and similar authorities. (Details Cost Estimate).
As per the above, the detailed project report related to the lake should be submitted to the department, following which the administrative and final approval will be taken at the government level.
