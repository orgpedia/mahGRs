Preamble In order to conserve the rivers of the State ecologically, the State Government has launched the State River Conservation Plan dated 01/03/2014. A typical program typically includes the following:
(i) Measures for collection, interception, diversion and transportation of sewage from the point where it is discharged into the river from the city/ village are not included in the city's underground sewerage scheme.
2) Installation of appropriate technology based sewage treatment system and its ancillary components, etc. for treatment of wastewater, taking into account the quantity, quality and local geographical conditions of wastewater.
(iii) Establishing the necessary systems/ facilities/ arrangements for recycling and reuse of treated wastewater in the project area or nearby agriculture, industrial units, water bodies, etc., so that the revenue generated can be used for meeting the project maintenance expenses, etc.
4) Construction of low cost toilets in the vicinity of the river, connecting the effluents to the sewage treatment system or taking similar measures for treatment, etc.
5) River valley development, measures to prevent erosion of river banks, conservation of river resources, etc.
Provisional appointment of Consultants/ Specialist Engineers etc. as required for the success of the classes established under the scheme, capacity building of technicians, training, research and as per the standards prescribed in the scheme.
7) Other important activities to reduce pollution as per the local conditions of the scheme, such as urban solid waste management and disposal through scientific methods, non-conventional energy generation and utilization, public participation, etc.

Separate orders will be issued by the government regarding the distribution of funds.
