Preamble The matter of issuing a Government Resolution regarding sanctioning and disbursement of Subsidiary Grants to Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority was under consideration of the Government
GOVERNMENT DECISION 2. In pursuance of the above proposal, the Government have decided to sanction and disburse grant-in-aid to the Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority as under:
(1) The Ministry of Environment and Forests, Government of India has constituted the Maharashtra Coastal Management Authority (MCZMA) under the Environment Protection Act, 1986 to protect and conserve the coastal areas of the State ecologically.
The work of determining the ecologically sensitive areas in the coastal areas of the state, preparing its Marine Zone Management Plan (CZMP), protecting the mangroves and coordinating the environmental management of the entire coastal area is done through the Maharashtra Coastal Management Authority (MCZMA).
(3)" Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority Subsidiary Grants Under this programme, a total amount of Rs. 5 crore has been budgeted under the schemes for the year 2022-23 for the implementation of the programme mentioned in the reference dated 12th August 2009. Of these, Rs. A sum of Rs. 2.45 crore has been disbursed through BEAMS system.

(4) The above funds should be deposited in the account opened in State Bank of India in Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Area.
(5) The amount paid for the specific work sanctioned under the schemes sanctioned for the purpose of such grant shall be spent within the limits of the expenditure approved by the Government within the prescribed time limit and in such proportion as may be prescribed by the Government. This money cannot be used for any other purpose.
(6) The Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority shall submit to the Department of Environment by 15th of April after the completion of each economic utility certificate.
(7) The accounts of the amount of expenditure to be made from the grant-in-aid should be kept in accordance with the prevailing accounting code and audited and their report should be sent to the Department of Environment.
(8) The Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority shall be bound to comply with the terms and conditions laid down by the State Government from time to time.
(9) The amount shall be withdrawn from the treasury through the Admiralty and Accounts Officer and paid to the Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority. Shilpa Shirke, Cell Officer has been declared to have contributed Rs. 2 crore 45 lakh.
