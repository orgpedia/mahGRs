In order to protect and conserve the coastal area of the State ecologically, the Ministry of Environment and Forests, Government of India, vide notification dated 31/12/2008, has constituted the Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority in the State. Under this, the coastal areas of the state are environmentally sensitive.
The authority is responsible for determining the area and preparing its management plan, protecting the mangrove forest and coordinating the overall coastal environmental management. the maritime area
An agreement in this regard has been signed with the centrally-funded agency that prepares the management plan and they are paid based on the progress of the work. Marine Area Management Plan prepared
Around Rs 12 crore will be spent on this. The Maharashtra Coastal Zone Management Authority (3435 1294) has been directed to submit the Marine Zone Management Plan to the Union Environment Ministry by the end of April.
Government's decision:
The Revised Estimates for the financial year 2017-18 have been finalised and an amount of Rs.1.60 crore has been sanctioned under the State River Conservation Plan (3435 1329) under the head Accounts of this Department. The funds have been disbursed through the BIMS system. The funds are being made available through reallocation under the head Maharashtra Coastal Zone Authority (3435 1294).
