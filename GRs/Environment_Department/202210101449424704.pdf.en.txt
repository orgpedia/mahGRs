Preamble: As per the provisions of the Biomedical Waste (Management and Handling) Rules, 1998, guidelines were laid down under the Government Decision dated 10/4/2003 regarding the revised fee to be charged by all medical establishments while applying for Biomedical Waste Management Authorisation.
The Biomedical Waste (Management) Rules, 2016 issued by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change on 28th March, 2016 are applicable to all types of medical establishments generating biomedical waste. Also, the Central Pollution Control Board has categorized medical establishments under the category of industries under the orders issued from time to time since 2012. All medical establishments are required to obtain a consent letter from the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981.
3. All medical establishments are required to obtain" Combined Consent and Biomedical Waste Management Authorization" under Biomedical Waste Management Rules, 2016 and Government Order dated 25/8/2011, instead of issuing Consent separately under Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. Medical establishments are charged separately for obtaining Biomedical Waste Management Authorization and Consent Letter. Out of this fee, the medical establishments will have to pay for the biomedical waste management authority as per the government decision dated 10/4/2003.

The proposal to abolish the authorisation fee was under the consideration of the government. As a result, the following decisions are made:
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 1. Issue of fees for Biomedical Waste Management Authority (BMWM Authorization) under the provisions of Biomedical Waste Management Act, 1998. The Government Decision dated 10/4/2003 is hereby rescinded with immediate effect.
Date and time for obtaining Common Consent Letter and BMWM Authorization from Maharashtra Pollution Control Board to all medical establishments in the State as contained in the Biomedical Waste Management Rules, 2016. The Maharashtra Pollution Control Board will charge only the requisite consent letter fee as per the Government decision mentioned in 25/8/2011 or as amended from time to time.
