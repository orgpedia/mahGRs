In order to conserve the lakes, lakes and large water bodies in the state ecologically, the state government has started the State Lake Conservation Plan under the State Plan from the financial year 2006-07.
Under this scheme, in the financial year 2021-22, the Executive Principal, Municipal Council Parli-Vaijanath, Dist.
To approve the proposal of traditional pond submitted by Beed and to release funds related to it. The Government decision was issued on 31.03.2022. However, due to technical glitches in the bill portal, the payments for disbursement of funds could not be prepared and submitted to the accounts and accounts office.
The funds have not been released for 2021-22. It is now Rs. 1,75,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Seventy Five Lakh only) was under consideration of the Government for disbursement during the financial year 2022-23.
Government's decision
Under the State Lake Conservation Scheme, the proposal to conserve the traditional lake environmentally, presented by the Chief Officer, Municipal Council Parli Vaijnath, G. Beed, was approved by the Steering Committee. It was discussed in the meeting dated 17.03.2022 and subject to the decision in the minutes of the meeting of the said Committee, a proposal for environmentally sound conservation of the traditional pond was approved. The in-principle approval has been accorded under the Government Resolution dated 31st March, 2022 for an amount of Rs. 3,50,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Crore Fifty Lakh only). Now, out of this, a sum of Rs. 1,75,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Seventy Five Lakh only) is being sanctioned to be distributed/ released to the Principal, Municipal Council, Parli-Vaijanath, G. Beed subject to the following terms and conditions.
(a) In order to conserve the ecological aspect of the lake, works such as sewer interception and diversion, installation of Phytoride Technology, installation of STP, silt trap, aeration, low cost sanitation, removal of water hyacinth, etc. should be proposed in accordance with the priority. These works are being carried out as per the guidelines issued by the Central Environment and Forest Department under the National Lake Conservation Plan.
