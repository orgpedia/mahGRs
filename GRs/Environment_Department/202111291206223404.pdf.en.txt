The Proposal:
In order to ensure effective management of funds of Centrally Sponsored Schemes and to bring in more efficiency in the process of allocation of funds, the Central Government has issued the following guidelines: It has been directed to adopt the revised procedures under the memorandum dated March 23, 2021. Accordingly, the Finance Department. As per the Government Circular dated 1 st July, 2021, revised instructions have been issued regarding Fund Distribution and Regulation Management (PFMS) of Centrally Sponsored Schemes. Accordingly, a single nodal agency (SNA) is required to be appointed for empanelment of Centrally Sponsored Schemes and a separate savings bank account is required to be opened in the bank authorised for the scheme. It has also been directed to appoint at least a Deputy Secretary level officer as the nodal officer for coordination of these centrally sponsored schemes. Accordingly, the Government was considering the appointment of a Single Nodal Agency (SNA) and Nodal Officer for the management of funds disbursement and utilization of two centrally sponsored schemes namely National Aquatic Ecosystem Conservation Programme and National River Conservation Plan (Mula-Mutha River, Pune-Pollution Mitigation Project) being implemented by the Department of Environment and Climate Change.
