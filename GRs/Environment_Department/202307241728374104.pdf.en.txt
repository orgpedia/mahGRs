The proposal-
Celebration of festivals, especially at the public level, strengthens the human community's bond with nature. But the recent celebration of festivals shows that inadvertent pollution of water and air, which are the main components of nature, is harmful to all human society. Environmental laws and their implementation are in the larger public interest and must involve the participation of all.
In the meantime, Ma. Suo Motu Public Interest Litigation filed in the High Court, Nagpur Bench No.
03/2021. As per the directions of the court, a committee was constituted to draft a policy for celebrating eco-friendly festivals in line with the guidelines of Central Pollution Control Board dated 12/05/2023.
The committee has submitted its report to the government. A technical committee was also formed to submit a technical report to reduce pollution and celebrate the festival in an environment-friendly manner. The committee sent its report to the government.

Functions of the committee:
1) To examine the possibility of reducing the polluting elements in Plaster of Paris idols and submit a preliminary report within the next three months.
To review the draft policy submitted by the Committee constituted in pursuance of the guidelines issued by the Central Pollution Control Board dated 12/05/2020 on the celebration of eco-friendly festivals and submit recommendations to the Government for implementation of the said policy.
