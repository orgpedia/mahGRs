In order to conserve the lakes, lakes and large water bodies in the state ecologically, the state government has started the State Lake Conservation Plan under the State Plan from the financial year 2006-07. A typical program typically includes the following:
1.Take measures to prevent pollution by identifying sources of water pollution.
2. Remove the harmful and organic sludge that has accumulated in the soil.
3.To maintain the quality of the lake, a.To take measures to control the growth of unwanted and infested plants in the lake.
(b) Improving the quality of water in the lake through biological processes in the lake.
Beautification of beaches, development of green belts, fencing, recreation parks, boating, low cost toilets etc.
4.Programmes for public awareness and measures to be taken according to local conditions to prevent pollution.
Under this scheme, the government was considering to give in-principle approval to the proposal of Dhawaleswar Lake submitted by the Collector, District Sangli, in the financial year 2023-24 and to release the corresponding funds.

It has been observed that jet aerators, gardens, lawns and utilities with high maintenance requirements have been proposed. Since the main objective of the State Lake Conservation Plan is to rejuvenate the lake, reduce pollution and conserve the lake, instead of excluding the works of jet aerator, garden, lawn and utilities, the main works of conservation should be included along with the planting/ afforestation of trees, solar street lights, benches, Nirmalya Kalash.
As it has been observed that the proposed works include various items such as centage charge/ contingency charge, labour insurance, architect's fees and consultancy fee, only GST will be admissible from now on.
As per the above, the detailed project report related to the lake should be submitted to the department, following which the administrative and final approval will be taken at the government level.
