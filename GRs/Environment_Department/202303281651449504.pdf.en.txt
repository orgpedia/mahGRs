In order to conserve the lakes, lakes and large water bodies in the state ecologically, the state government has started the State Lake Conservation Plan under the State Plan from the financial year 2006-07.
Under this scheme, in the financial year 2021-22, the proposal of Subhrakamal Lake submitted by the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri, was approved and an amount of Rs. 74,97,000/- to be released. The Government decision was issued on 31.03.2022. However, due to technical glitches in the bill portal, the payments for disbursement of funds could not be prepared and submitted to the accounts and accounts office. The funds have not been released for 2021-22. It is now Rs. 74,97,000/- (Rupees Seventy Four Lakhs Ninety Seven Thousand only) was under consideration of the Government for disbursement during the financial year 2022-23.
Government's decision
The proposal for the environmental conservation of Subhrakamal Lake submitted by the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri, under the State Lake Conservation Plan was approved by the Steering Committee. It was discussed in the meeting dated 17.03.2022 and subject to the decision in the minutes of the meeting of the said Committee, a proposal for ecologically conservation of Shubharkamal Lake was approved. The in-principle approval has been accorded under the Government Resolution dated 31st March, 2022 for an amount of Rs. 1,50,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Fifty Lakh only). It is now Rs. This Government resolution approves the release of funds amounting to Rs. 74,97,000/- (Rupees Seventy Four Lakhs Ninety Seven Thousand only) to the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri subject to the following terms and conditions.
In order to conserve the environmental aspect of the lake, works such as sewer interception and diversion, installation of Phytoride Technology, installation of STP, silt trap, aeration, low cost sanitation, removal of water hyacinth should be proposed. These works are being carried out as per the guidelines issued by the Central Environment and Forest Department under the National Lake Conservation Plan.
