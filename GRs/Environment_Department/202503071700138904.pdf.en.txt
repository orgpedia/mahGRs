Read: - 1) Air Pollution Control (Approval, Rejection or Cancellation of Consent) Guidelines, 2025 issued under GSR 84 (E), dated 29th January, 2025 of the Union Ministry of Environment and Forests.
Preamble: - The Union Ministry of Environment and Forests has issued the Air Pollution Control (Approval, Rejection or Cancellation of Consent) Guidelines, 2025 under GSR 84 (E), dated 29th January, 2025. The said guidelines relate to the air pollution control consent process for industrial projects under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981.
The said guidelines explain the procedures, fees, rules and responsibilities required for industrial pollution control. In the said guidelines, Paragraph No. 8 provides for the time limit for grant of consent under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. If the consent letter application is not disposed of by the State Pollution Control Board within the said time limit, the matter will be dealt with in the said instructions in paragraph no. Instructions have been given to submit to the State Level Control Committee constituted under section 15. Paragraph No. 1 of the said guidelines provides for the constitution of a State Level Control Committee to monitor and regulate the implementation of the said Guidelines for Settlement of Consent Petitions as well as the process of Settlement of Consent Petitions. Suggestions are given in 15.
The implementation of the guidelines for disposal of consent forms as provided in the Air Pollution Control (Approval, Rejection or Cancellation of Consent) Guidelines, 2025 issued by the Union Ministry of Environment and Forests and constitution of a State Level Control Committee for monitoring and regulating the process of disposal of consent forms was under the consideration of the Government of India and the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board as per the powers conferred under Section 18 of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and Section 18 of the Water (Prevention, Control and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. In this regard, the government has taken the following decisions:
Government Decisions: - 1. Powers conferred under Section 18 of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and the Air Pollution Control (Approval, Rejection or Rejection of Consent) Guidelines, 2025 issued under GSR 84 (E), dated 29th January, 2025 of the Union Ministry of Forest and Environment, vide Paragraph No. 15 Guidelines for Disposal of Consent Petitions pursuant to the rights obtained below.

(b) Functions of the Committee. - 1) The Committee shall monitor the implementation of the Air Pollution Control (Approval, Rejection or Revocation of Consent) Guidelines, 2025 and regulate the implementation of these guidelines.
If the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board does not take a decision within the specified period to dispose of the application for consent, the matter shall be referred to the State Level Control Committee and the Committee shall dispose of the application within thirty days from the date of receipt thereof.
3) The Member Secretary of the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board will submit these cases to the State Level Control Committee.
4) The Committee shall also dispose of cases which have come before the State Level Controlling Committee.
5) In deciding on such an application, the Control Committee at the State level shall consider the reasons for the delay in granting or refusing consent and recommend appropriate disciplinary action if the reasons for the delay are not found to be appropriate and it shall be incumbent upon the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board to comply with such decisions.
6) The Control Committee at the State level may, if it finds a violation of the law in a case, recommend to the Committee to present the case before the Adjudicating Officer concerned appointed under Section 239A of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981.
7) At least one meeting of the State Level Regulatory Committee shall be held every month.
2. The Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, as per the power conferred under section 18 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, is directed to: -
1) In case of non-decision in the specified time period for disposal of applications for consent letters issued under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, such cases shall be referred to the Control Committee at the State level.
The said Committee shall examine the cases where no decision has been taken within a specified period of time in accordance with the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and shall recommend to the Government for direction to the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board regarding such application. on it
