In order to conserve the lakes, lakes and large water bodies in the state ecologically, the state government has started the State Lake Conservation Plan under the State Plan from the financial year 2006-07. A typical program typically includes the following:
1. Identify sources of pollution of lake water and take measures to prevent pollution.
2. Remove the harmful and organic sludge that has accumulated in the soil.
To maintain the quality of the lake, a. to take measures to control the growth of unwanted and nuisance plants in the lake.
(b) Improving the quality of water in the lake through biological processes in the lake.
Beautification of beaches, development of green belts, fencing, recreation parks, boating, low cost toilets etc.
4.Programmes for public awareness and measures to be taken according to local conditions to prevent pollution.
Under this scheme, the government was considering to give in-principle approval to the proposal of Dargoba lake submitted by the Collector, District Sangli, in the financial year 2023-24 and to release the corresponding funds.
Government Decision: Under the State Sarovar Conservation Scheme, the proposal to conserve Pare (Dargoba) lake ecologically presented by the Collector Sangli was approved by the Steering Committee. At the meeting held on 04.07.2023 and subject to the decision in the minutes of the said meeting, a sum of Rs. 3,71,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Lakhs) was paid.
