Introduction: Plastic and thermocol products have been banned throughout the state under the powers conferred under Section 2 and Section 4 of the Maharashtra Biodegradable and Non-Biodegradable Waste (Control) Act, 2006. A massive awareness campaign is needed to make the plastic ban effective. Various media should be used to spread awareness. The government was considering providing a grant for the purpose.
Government Decision: The Directorate General of Information and Public Relations has prepared a comprehensive media plan for the public awareness of plastic and thermocol ban through various media, with a limit of Rs 10 crore and it has been approved by the Media Planning and Enforcement Committee.
As per the media plan prepared by the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations, the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations has issued a publicity work order to the advertiser organization for an amount of Rs. 3,28,24, 049/- as on March 28, 2018. It has been directed to send the payment of advertisement regarding the broadcast to the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Mumbai.
In pursuance of the aforesaid mandate of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations, sanction is being accorded for release of funds to the tune of Rs. 3,28,24, 049/-. The fund is being distributed to the Member Secretary of Maharashtra Pollution Control Board. 3,28,24, 049/- in favour of Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Mumbai.
