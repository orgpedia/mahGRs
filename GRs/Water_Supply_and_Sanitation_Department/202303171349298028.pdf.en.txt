Preamble: Under Jal Jeevan Mission, Reference No. 05 by the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Chandrapur, vide letter dated 05.07.2020, to Shivapur Port, Chandrapur District, T. Proposals have been submitted to the Government for grant of ex-post facto approval for acceptance of tenders for additional rates for Chimur and other 3 village tap water supply schemes and accordingly revised administrative approval for the scheme design and budget. The plan and budget of the said schemes in Chandrapur district is being prepared at the level of Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Chandrapur vide Reference No. The administrative approval has been granted vide order dated 03-04 and considering the increase in the rates of these schemes due to acceptance of tenders, the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Chandrapur has accorded revised technical approval to these schemes.
Accordingly, the matter of giving revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the 4 existing tap water supply schemes in Chandrapur district was under the consideration of the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Revised administrative approval is being accorded to the budget and design of the following 4 Taps Water Supply Schemes in Chandrapur District, as mentioned in the table below, subject to the following conditions.

Conditions
1. All remaining works should be completed within the stipulated time at the revised cost as per Annexure-A.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad concerned shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zila Parishad, Chandrapur that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
