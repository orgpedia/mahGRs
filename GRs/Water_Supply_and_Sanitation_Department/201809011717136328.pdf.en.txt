Government's decision:
Enjoying (t. Dapoli, Ratnagiri), Rs. The revised budget of Rs. 23,56,028/- (Rupees Twenty Three Lakhs Fifty Six Thousand Eighty Eight only) is being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions.
Conditions
(1) All the remaining works should be completed in 3 months at the revised cost mentioned above.
(2) The terms and conditions mentioned in the original administrative approval and the revised technical approval granted to the scheme shall be strictly complied with.
(3) On completion of the scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials and contractors should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
(4) The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned shall not receive any grant from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred annually for the implementation, maintenance, repair (subject to special amendments) of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
(5) On completion of the scheme, it shall be mandatory for the local body (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme and the accounts of the scheme shall be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of the water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
(7) Action should be taken in accordance with the prevailing policy on dowry.
(8) It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the said scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
2. The revised cost of the scheme is Rs. 3,04,134/- (Rupees Three Lakh Four Thousand One Hundred and Thirty Four only) from the State share under National Rural Drinking Water Programme/ District Planning Board/ Funds available under Minimum Needs Programme.
