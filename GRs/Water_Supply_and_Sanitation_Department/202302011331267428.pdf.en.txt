The Proposal:
The Government has, vide Government Resolution dated 23.3.2017, sanctioned a sum of Rs. 219.5620 crore for the financial year 2022-23 under the head 22158052 as grant-in-aid towards payment of salary, allowances and pension of retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Now, the matter of further disbursement of funds under the above head is under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Demand No.22215, Water Supply and Sanitation 199, Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations (01) Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations (01) (01) Assistance to sanction pension benefits to Government employees transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Authority (MJA) for the financial year 2022-23

Grants (Compulsory) (2215/052) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) under the head' Grants-in-Aid' amounting to Rs. 28,82,47, 000/- (Rupees Twenty Eight Crores Eighty Two Lakhs Forty Seven Thousand only) are being released to the Authority subject to the following conditions.
(i) Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the grant disbursed under this Government decision is for the pensionary benefits of retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for the year 2022-23 and the amount from this grant is spent for the same works. These funds should not be used for retirement benefits.
(ii) Expenditure on other items of the Fund shall be deemed to be financial irregularities and the officers/ employees concerned shall be liable to disciplinary action for such action.
(iii) Separate accounts should be maintained of the amount of free subsidy.
(iv) The Authority shall inform the Government from time to time about the allocations made under the free grants.
(v) The accounts of the Authority as per Rule No. 3 of Annexure 2 to the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959 shall be submitted by the Authority for inspection along with relevant documents.
(vi) The Authority shall submit the utilization certificate of the free subsidy to the Government. The Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall certify the amount of grant-in-aid and expenditure as approved and released as indicated in the Utilization Certificate. The utility certificates should be signed by the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Only such utility certificates will be acceptable to the government.
vii) The expenditure of the released subsidy should be reconciled with the expenditure reported to the Accountant General's Office and the prescribed report should be submitted to the Accounts Officer, Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department on the prescribed date.
(viii) The Authority shall ensure that the retirement benefits of any retired employee do not remain pending for a long period. Also, old cases should be settled on priority while paying retirement benefits to retired employees. A chronological list of retired officers/ employees should be prepared and action taken to pay pensionary benefits on a priority basis. The certificate that such action is being taken should be submitted to the government when requesting further funds.
