The original administrative approval has been granted vide order dated 07.08.1996. The plan of Rs. The revised budget was prepared by the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Gondia, at a staggering cost of Rs 104.95 lakh. Revised Technical Approval has been granted on 08.07.2021. Accordingly, the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Nagpur has proposed to give revised administrative approval in respect of payment of final dues of the present tap water supply scheme vide letter no. 4 submitted to the Government. Accordingly, the matter of granting revised administrative approval to the revised plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
The Government has decided to release Rs. Revised administrative approval is being granted for the revised budget of Rs. 104.95 lakh/- (Rupees One Crore Four Lakh Ninety Five Thousand) subject to the following conditions:
Conditions:
1) All the works of the scheme should be completed at the revised cost mentioned above.
2) The terms and conditions mentioned in the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.

(3) The Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad concerned shall not receive any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery in the scheme.
4) After the completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad concerned shall be responsible to take over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zila Parishad, Buldana that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to transfer and run the scheme. After that, the account will be closed.
6) Necessary planning for 100% tap connections should be done by the Gram Panchayat.
7) It is necessary to check the record of tap connections given so far under Jal Jeevan Mission and timely register the information on IMIS system about the tap connections to be given in future.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21 per cent of the water supply schemes in the state were closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
9) Action should be taken according to the prevailing policy on dowry.
10) It shall be the responsibility of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran that the said scheme shall not be revised under any circumstances.
The revised cost of the scheme is Rs. 30.37 lakhs (Rupees Thirty Lakhs Thirty Seven Thousand only). Grants under Y-02, Zilla Parishad Schemes (02) (15) Grants for tap water supply scheme Jal Jeevan Mission (State share 50%) (Programme) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) to be shared from the grants sanctioned under the head Accounts (2215 2071) and to be finally spent under the same.
