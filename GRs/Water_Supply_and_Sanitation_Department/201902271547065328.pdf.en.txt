The expenditure on water scarcity measures in rural and urban areas of the State is met from the grants available under the head" Assistance for Natural Disaster Mitigation" (2245). For the measures taken/ to be taken during the scarcity period of 2017-18 and 2018-19, the concerned Divisional Commissioner has issued Reference No. The Department has received a proposal for seeking funds under letter No. 3 to 10. For the financial year 2018-19, the Department of Revenue and Forest (Relief and Rehabilitation) has transferred the funds to this Department as per the Government Resolution No. 11. The funds were to be distributed to the divisional commissioners of Konkan, Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Amravati and Nagpur.
Government's decision:
A sum of Rs. 14700.00 lakhs (Rupees One Hundred and Forty Seven Crores only) is being sanctioned and released to the Divisional Commissioners, Konkan, Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Amravati and Nagpur, to meet the expenditure on the measures to be taken/ implemented for drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas as per the norms laid down by the Government, subject to the terms and conditions laid down in the extant Standing Government Resolution and subject to the following conditions:
The Divisional Commissioner, Konkan, Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Amravati and Nagpur shall disburse the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad and Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council for schemes in urban areas.

The funds shall be released for the implementation of the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the period 2017-18 and 2018-19 and for the payment of pending dues during this period.
For rural and urban schemes which have been approved from the government level under scarcity, 50 percent of the funds at the beginning of the scheme and 30 percent after that as mentioned in the decision of the government should be disbursed after reviewing the progress of the scheme at the level of the Divisional Commissioner, the remaining 20 percent should be given only after the scheme is implemented.
4) It should be ensured that the funds are not kept in the personal ledger account or bank account and are distributed directly to the contractors through electronic clearing services.
The District Collectors who have not received the remedial certificates of the funds distributed in the financial year 2016-17 and 2017-18 for the drinking water shortage in rural/ urban areas, should disburse the funds for rural remedial measures to the concerned CEOs, ZPs and the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Councils for the urban areas only after verifying that they have received the remedial certificates of the entire expenditure (100%) of the funds distributed in the financial year 2016-17 and 2017-18. Also, the funds should not be spent unless the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Adhikaran, Mumbai submits the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed under the scarcity measures in 2017-18 to the Government. Further, all Divisional Commissioners and Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Mumbai shall submit the utilization certificate of the funds received from the Government in the financial year 2018-19 before the said Government decision to the Government before 10th March, 2019. Funds are being distributed on this basis.
6) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed for the related measures earlier.
7) Further, while seeking funds for the measures to be taken for the relief of scarcity, a case-wise demand as to the period for which the proposed measures to be taken for the relief of scarcity, the amount of funds required for it, as well as information about the funds disbursed in the current financial year, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc., and the utilization certificate therefor should be submitted. Besides, it should be noted that the funds will be approved in the future.
The Divisional Commissioner shall submit to the Government every month, along with the monthly expenditure report, information on the physical progress of the district for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed.
The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. Page 3 of 6 every 3 months
