The Jalswarajya-2 programme will be funded by the World Bank and the proceeds will be available under the Programme for Results. The total cost of the projects is$ 235 million, of which 70% will be debt ($ 165 million) and 30% ($ 70 million) will be state government. The financing under this instrument is linked to the Disbursement Linked Indicators (DLIs). The funding will be received after ensuring the achievement of the target through an independent screening mechanism.
