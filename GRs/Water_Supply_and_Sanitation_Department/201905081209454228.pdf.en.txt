The Maharashtra Acquisition of Drinking Water Supply Act, 1983 was promulgated on 15th April, 1983. As per the provisions of Section 3 of the Act, the Collector or the officer authorised by the District Collector has been empowered to acquire water reserves of private owners by written order for providing drinking water from wells or other water bodies to provide drinking water to the villagers in the rural areas of the State during the period of water scarcity. Under Section 8 (a) and 8 (b), the compensation payable to the owners of water bodies after acquisition of the private owners has been fixed.
Section 8 of the Maharashtra Acquisition of Drinking Water Supply Act, 1983 has been amended to make provisions for payment of compensation under Sections 8 (a) and 8 (b) of the original Act, 1983 for payment of water to well owners who have acquired drinking water wells.
The revised rates of remuneration for wells acquired during the period of water scarcity have been approved as per the Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Government Resolution, dated 30th November, 2018. However, the matter was under consideration of the Government to revise the remuneration to be paid to the private well/ borewell owners based on the quantum of water drawn from the well. This proposal. The approval was given at the meeting of the Cabinet Sub-Committee held on 02.05.2019 under the Chairmanship of the Minister for Relief and Rehabilitation. The government was contemplating issuing a notification in this regard.
Government Decision: The rates of compensation for wells acquired for drinking water scarcity are being revised. Accordingly, Rs. 12/- per litre. Further, the amount at this rate or the revised rate as on 30.11.2018 is Rs. 450/- (excluding water lifting equipment)/ Rs. Maximum amount of Rs.600/- (including equipment for lifting water) is allowed to be paid to private bore well/ simple well holder.
