The project report has been approved. According to Palam Taluk (Dist. 65 villages of Parbhani Grid Water Supply Scheme Rs. 5664.09 lakh under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
Government Decision: In view of the facts stated in the Preamble, the Palam Taluk (Dist. Parbhani) for 65 villages with a capacity of 40 litres per day and Rs. 4522/- per annum. The budget and plan at a nominal cost of Rs. 5664.09 lakhs (Rupees Fifty Six Crore Sixty Four Lakh Nine Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
The scheme should be implemented by the Maharashtra Life Authority. The contractor will be required to operate the project for at least three years after the completion of the project. The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as the maintenance of the scheme is carried out for the repair of reference No. 7 here. Follow the guidelines issued by the government on October 17, 2014.
The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Bids with higher prices will not be accepted. It should be ensured that the work of the scheme is completed within the administrative approved cost. If the cost of the scheme increases, the state government will not be responsible for it and such schemes will be eligible for cancellation at any stage. Disciplinary action will also be taken against the concerned at the government level.
(iii) No work order shall be issued for the execution of the work of the scheme unless the necessary land acquisition and all other permissions for the scheme have been obtained. If the order is issued without fulfilling this condition, the administrative approval given under this government decision will be considered canceled.

Work orders should not be issued without obtaining an undertaking from at least 80% of the beneficiaries/ tap connection holders involved in the scheme to take up meter connection at their own cost and to fill the water table required for maintenance and repair of the scheme.
(v) If a village refuses to take water even after the Gram Sabha resolution/ assurance letter is given, at least 75% of the water supply should be recovered from it. The villagers should be informed in advance about the tender process.
(vi) Existing schemes should not be operated in the villages covered under the scheme so that the scheme is not used seasonally. Also, the pumping machinery and electricity connection of the existing schemes in the included villages should be removed.
(viii) The works of the scheme should be completed in a time bound and planned manner in a phased manner at the approved cost.
(viii) No other work should be started until the scheme is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
IX. To meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the tariff of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it shall be increased from time to time.
No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
The scheme should cover 100% household tap connections.
XII. Reference No. 5 The policy of openness should be implemented in accordance with the provisions of the Government Decision of January 22, 2014.
XIII. No additional subsidy shall be provided by the Government in case of increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason.
XIV. The conditions contained in the order of technical approval of the said scheme shall be complied with. Also referred to as No. 12 here. The provisions of the Government Order dated 7th May, 2016 should be strictly followed.
