Introduction: Sonai Karjagaon and 16 other villages (Tt. Nevasa, G. Ahmednagar) The budget of Regional Water Supply Scheme has been accorded the original administrative approval as per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 1. As per Reference No. 2, Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Mumbai has accorded technical approval to the original budget. However, the cost of the project has increased due to the receipt of tenders for the work at higher rates. The tender for the work of this scheme has been approved post facto under reference number 3. However, the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Regional Department, Nashik has given revised technical approval to the budget of the scheme presented under reference number 4. Accordingly, the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Regional Department, Nashik had submitted a proposal to the Government under Reference Letter No.5 to give revised administrative approval to the draft and budget of the proposed Regional Water Supply Scheme. Accordingly, the matter of granting revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: GRAPAPU-2018/ PR No.280/ PAPU-17 GOVERNMENT DECISION: Sonai Karjagaon and 16 other villages (Tt. Newasa, G. Ahmednagar) Regional Water Supply Scheme at a revised cost of Rs. 7941.75 lakhs (Dhobal) (Rs.
Conditions
All the remaining works should be completed before 31st March, 2020 at the revised cost mentioned above.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
As soon as the scheme is actually completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, the officers of Maharashtra Life Authority/ Zilla Parishad/ concerned Gram Panchayat should jointly conduct an inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The concerned Zilla Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Chief Engineer/ Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority that the scheme has been actually completed.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
8. Proceedings to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
It shall be the responsibility of the Maharashtra Life Authority to ensure that the said scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
