Preamble: Under Jal Jeevan Mission, Reference No. As per the letter dated 05.07.2020, the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Washim has submitted proposals to the Government for grant of ex-post facto approval for acceptance of tenders for the additional rates of the 2 existing tap water supply schemes in Washim district and accordingly revised administrative approval for the design and budget of the schemes. The plan and budget of these schemes in Washim district have been prepared at the level of Chief Executive Officer, Zila Parishad, Washim vide Reference No. The administrative approval has been accorded vide order dated 03-04 and considering the increase in the rates of these schemes due to acceptance of tenders, the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Washim has accorded revised technical approval to these schemes.
Accordingly, the matter of giving revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the 2 existing tap water supply schemes in Washim district was under the consideration of the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: Revised administrative approval is being accorded to the revised budget and plan of the following two tap water supply schemes in Washim district.

Revised administrative approval is being accorded to the revised budget and design of the above mentioned 2 tap water supply schemes in Washim district subject to the following conditions.
Conditions
1. All remaining works should be completed within the stipulated time at the revised cost as per Annexure-A.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Washim that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. So the plan will be financially viable.
