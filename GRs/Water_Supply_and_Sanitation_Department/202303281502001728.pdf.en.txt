The Proposal:
The Government has taken over the responsibility of payment of salary, allowances and pension to the employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority vide Government Resolution dated 23.3.2017 and the grant is being disbursed to the Authority under the head 2215A052 as grant to meet the expenditure on pension of retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority. Further, as per the Government decision dated 23.09.2021, revised retirement pay as per 7th Pay Commission has been sanctioned to the retired employees of the Authority with effect from 1.04.2017. Demand for the amount of admissible arrears as per actual consideration after taking into account the revised pension of all retired employees in respect of the allowable arrears for the period from 1.04.2017 to 30.09.2021

The Authority has issued instructions to the Government under the Government Resolution dated 23.09.2021. Accordingly, the Authority has considered the dues and submitted a report to the Government vide letter dated 29.07.2022 and sought funds. A total budget provision of Rs. 248.38 crore has been made for the financial year 2022-23 under the head 22158052. Since this provision is not sufficient to meet the regular pension of retired employees of the Authority as well as the pending expenditure of the Seventh Pay Commission, additional funds have been made available through Supplementary Demands under the aforesaid heads in the Winter Session of December, 2022. Out of this, Rs. 193.10 crore has been released to the Authority as per the Government Resolution dated 01.02.2023 and the remaining funds provided by the Finance Department are under consideration of the Government. The following decision has been taken:
Government's decision:
Employees who have retired from Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran will get a total of Rs. 193,11,00, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred and Ninety Three Crores Eleven Lakhs only) is being released as grant to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran.

(i) Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the amount disbursed under this Government decision is spent for the same purposes as the grant is sanctioned for the regular pensionary benefits of retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for the year 2022-23 as well as for the allowable arrears of the Seventh Pay Commission. This amount should not be used for any other retirement benefit.
(ii) Expenditure on other items of the Fund shall be deemed to be financial irregularities and the officers/ employees concerned shall be liable to disciplinary action for such action.
(iii) The Authority shall maintain separate accounts of the amount spent towards the arrears of the Seventh Pay Commission.
iv) The Authority shall inform the Government from time to time about the allocation of funds from this grant.
(v) The accounts of the Authority as per Rule No. 3 of Annexure 2 to the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959 shall be submitted by the Authority for inspection along with relevant documents.
(vi) The Authority shall submit the utilization certificate of this grant to the Government. The Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall certify the amount of grant-in-aid sanctioned and released as well as the actual expenditure shown in the Utilization Certificate. The utility certificates should be signed by the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Only such utility certificates will be acceptable to the government.
vii) The expenditure of the released subsidy should be reconciled with the expenditure reported to the Accountant General's Office and the prescribed report should be submitted to the Accounts Officer, Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department on the prescribed date.
(vii) The amount received against the 7th Pay Commission arrears should be paid by the Authority to the concerned retired employee without any delay. In case of delay in payment of dues to any retired employee, the liability for delay shall be fixed.
ix) The Director (Finance) shall ensure that the amounts of allowable dues payable to the employee concerned are accurate.
