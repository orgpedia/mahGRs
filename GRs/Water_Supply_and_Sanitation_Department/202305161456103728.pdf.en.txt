The above-mentioned Junior Geologist is being relieved unilaterally from the office mentioned in Rakana No. 3 from the date of this order i.e. 18/05/2023 (M. No. The order is a discharge order and no fresh order is required to be issued by the concerned office.

The head of the office and the establishment officer concerned will be held responsible if there is a case of non-payment of salary and allowances due to non-recruitment of an officer without fixing a mandatory waiting period.
(3) The officers being posted under the said order shall be required to join immediately at the place of their posting. If any officer fails to appear at the place of posting or tries to directly or indirectly pressurize the transfer order, it should be recorded in the confidential report of the concerned officer. Such officers will be liable for disciplinary action as the act violates Rule 3 (3) and Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979.
Since these transfers are required to be effected effectively, the salary of all the transferred officers as on 18/05/2023 shall be drawn by the office in which they are presently working. The salary from the date 19/05/2023 should not be withdrawn from the pre-transfer office under any circumstances. After the concerned officer arrives at the transfer office, the post-transfer office should deduct the salary and submit a report to the government. The salary from 19/05/2023 onwards is liable to be paid by the Drawing and Disbursing Officer of the office where he was working before the transfer.
Instructions are being given to all the heads of departments and offices of the Ground Water Survey and Development System.
These orders will come into force with immediate effect. The Commissioner, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency is being informed to immediately submit to the government the information whether the transferred officers have joined the transferred office or how.
