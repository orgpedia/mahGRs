1) The Zilla Parishad should spend this grant only for the implementation of new tap water supply schemes approved under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2019-20. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
3) The implementation agency will be responsible for conducting a third-party technical examination of the works of the tap water supply schemes as per the guidelines given under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme and to fulfill the observations made.
(iv) The expenditure on water supply schemes for which tenders have been accepted at higher rates shall not be more than the original administrative sanction cost of the scheme unless revised administrative sanction is obtained.
5) The funds disbursed to the Zila Parishads should not be kept in the Personal Provident Fund (PLA) or any other such account.
6) When the funds are sought under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program, the funds will not be sanctioned in the future without submitting information about the funds distributed in the current financial year, the expenses incurred, the balance funds, etc.
7) The physical progress of the works for which funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai every month along with the monthly expenditure report. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
(8) The District Councils shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made out of this grant. In order to ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme and the terms and conditions of this Government Decision dated 7th May, 2016 are being fulfilled by the Zilla Parishad, the Accountant General, Maharashtra 1/2 Mumbai/ Nagpur and the officers authorized in this Department are empowered to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned implementing agencies for audit.
