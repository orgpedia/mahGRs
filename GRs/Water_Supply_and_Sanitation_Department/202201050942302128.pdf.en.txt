1. the implementation of the plan
i.With the World Bank funding available under the Jalswarajya-2 programme being based on specific target achievement indicators, it is mandatory to provide tap connections with 100% water measurement and the water supply from these tap connections should be continuous and satisfactory.
ii. the hive. The terms and conditions mentioned in the revised technical approval order of the semi-urban tap water supply scheme of Purandar, G. Pune should be strictly followed.

iv. As the scheme proposes to connect water meters with taps, action should be taken as per the policy of water meters approved by the Government.
2. maintenance and repair.
After the completion of the physical work of the scheme, the Gram Panchayat will be responsible for the annual maintenance and repair budget of the scheme, to charge the necessary water table with the approval of the Gram Sabha, and to carry out maintenance and repair for a period of 5 years from the contractor appointed by the parent/ Gram Panchayat or through a separate process of the Gram Sabha.
Provision for maintenance and repairs for the initial 1 year is mentioned in the Detailed Project Report. The Gram Panchayat concerned will be responsible for paying the cost of maintenance and repair for the subsequent 4 years to the contractor for the salary of the workers, electricity payments and chemicals required for water purification. This expenditure should be borne by the Gram Panchayat from the amount of water table collection.
iii.No subsidy will be provided by the Government for maintenance and repair of the scheme.
3. technical test. Before the inspection by the third-party verification mechanism, the technical inspection of the quality of the work/ material of the scheme should be done by the quality inspection team of the Maharashtra Life Authority and a report should be submitted to the government.
