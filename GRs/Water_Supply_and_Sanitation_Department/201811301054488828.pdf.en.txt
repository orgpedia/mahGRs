The Maharashtra Acquisition of Drinking Water Supply Act, 1983 was promulgated on 15th April, 1983. As per the provisions of Section 3 of the Act, the Collector or the officer authorised by the District Collector has been empowered to acquire water reserves of private owners by written order for providing drinking water from wells or other water bodies to provide drinking water to the villagers in the rural areas of the State during the period of water scarcity. Under Section 8 (a) and 8 (b) of the Act, the compensation payable to the owners of water bodies after acquisition of the private owners has been fixed.
Section 8 of the Maharashtra Acquisition of Drinking Water Supply Act, 1983 has been amended to replace the provision of payment of compensation under section 8 (a) and 8 (b) of the original Act of 1983 for payment of water compensation to the owners of the wells from which the drinking water well has been acquired.

Provision has been made to enter into an agreement regarding remuneration by the authorized officer and the ceiling on the rate of remuneration is to be decided by the State Government from time to time.
At present, as per the Government decision dated 30th April, 2012 in Reference No. 3, after acquiring a private well, the owner of the well has not provided the equipment including electric/ diesel pump and electrical connection to draw water. 300/- per day and if the owner of the well has made available the equipment including electric pump/ diesel pump and electricity connection to draw water, Rs. 400/- is being paid. Since there has been a lot of price rise at all levels during the period, the matter of increasing the well acquisition cost was under the consideration of the government.
