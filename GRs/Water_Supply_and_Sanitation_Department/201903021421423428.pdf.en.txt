The Government has decided to provide Rs. The revised budget of Rs. 172.254/- (Rupees One Crore Seventy Two Lakhs Twenty Five Thousand Four Hundred only) is being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions.
1) Technical approval should be given to revise the price of the scheme.
2) The scheme will be implemented through the District Council.
3) The terms and conditions of the original administrative approval of the scheme, the government decision and the order of technical approval should be strictly followed.
As per the CAG report of 2013, 21 per cent of the water supply schemes in the state have been shut down due to non-payment of electricity bills due to lack of maintenance. Therefore, the water tariff should be increased from time to time as per the requirement to keep the scheme financially viable.
5) Once the project is completed, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, G. P. Nanded has actually completed the scheme. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad will be responsible for taking over the scheme within 1 month from the date of receipt of the notification in this regard. As soon as the project is completed, the concerned executive engineer, rural water supply department, GP Nanded, concerned gram panchayat officer and contractor should conduct a joint inspection of the project. If any defect is found in the inspection, it should be rectified before handing over the plan.
6) The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad will not get any assistance/ grant from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation of the schemes, their maintenance, repairs and special repairs, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
7) Action should be taken according to the prevailing policy on open defecation.
8) It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the scheme is not revised.
The remaining work of the project should be completed within 6 months.
