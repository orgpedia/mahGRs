The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2023 has been approved by the Legislature for the amount specified in Pillar-4 of the Schedule. It has been assented to by the Governor and is published as Maharashtra Act No. 17 of 2023 in the Government of Maharashtra, Gazette Extraordinary Part-IV, dated 29th March, 2023.
The total amount of Rs. 55.8963 crore has been budgeted for the financial year 2023-24 under the head Demand No.Y-2" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (2215 1843)" for the implementation of Water Supply Programme. Among them, the government is considering to distribute the funds made available by the Finance Department on the budget control system to the respective district councils as per the need.

Government Resolution No: Budget-1423/ No. 95/ Papu-23
Government's decision:
For the financial year 2023-24, a total grant of Rs. 5,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Five Crores only) is being sanctioned to the Chief Accounts and Finance Officer of the concerned Zilla Parishad as shown in the' Statement A' accompanying this Government decision to meet the salary expenditure of Class I and Class II officers on the establishment of Zilla Parishad Rural Water Supply Department under the head" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (01) Divisional/ Sub Divisional Establishment (Mandatory) for implementation of Water Supply Programme (22151843)."
(2) The grants sanctioned under this Government Resolution shall be subject to such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Government from time to time. All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts shown in the' Return A" accompanying this Government decision. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
(3) Care should be taken to demand further necessary funds only after ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said Government decision is submitted to the Government. The concerned District Councils should note that the funds will not be disbursed until the next demand letter is received. Also, the expenditure incurred should be reconciled with the expenditure recorded by the Accountant General's office from time to time. No further funds will be released if the works are delayed. The subsidy will be credited to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
4) Before drawing the funds from the treasury and while spending, the working rules, financial rules, provisions of the budget rule book of the state government and all the terms and conditions should be followed.
