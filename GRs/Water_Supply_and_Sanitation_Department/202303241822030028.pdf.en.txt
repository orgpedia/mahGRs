Terms and Conditions
The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati and Nagpur should distribute the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, for taking measures under the scarcity in rural areas.
The funds should be released for the implementation of the measures taken to address the drinking water shortage in rural areas for the scarcity period 2020-2021 and 2021-22 and for the payment of pending dues during this period. This money cannot be used for other projects.
3) The Divisional Commissioner should allocate funds on priority to measures such as acquisition of wells, supply of water by tankers and acquisition of new bore wells.
4) It should be ensured that the funds are disbursed directly to the contractors through Electronic Clearing Services/ RTGS, without keeping the personal ledger account or bank account.
5) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed to the concerned measures earlier.
6) Further, while seeking funds for the measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity, the subject-wise/ project-wise demand as to what are the proposed measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity for what period, how much funds are required for it, information about the funds disbursed, expenditure incurred, balance funds etc. in the current financial year, as well as the utilization certificate thereof, should be submitted. Besides, it should be noted that the funds will be approved in the future.
7) The funds for the rural measures being implemented by the Maharashtra Life Authority are made available to the Maharashtra Life Authority on demand, as per the decision of the government. Therefore, the funds should not be distributed to the Maharashtra Life Authority through the Zilla Parishad or the Collector.
8) GPS on private tankers while making payments out of the funds released for the measures taken for supply of water through tankers/ drinking water as per the decision of the Government.

Such trips will not be admissible if the tanker transport holder claims that the tanker has been diverted due to non-installation of the system or the GPS system is switched off. as well as GPS.

9) Since the funds being disbursed under this Government decision are being disbursed for the deficit period 2018-19, 2020-21 and 2022-22 and are actually for completed projects, the funds should be spent within 10 days. Hence, the funds should be immediately disbursed to the concerned offices/ Zilla Parishads/ Collectors within two days. If the money is not spent within 10 days, it will be considered a financial irregularity. The utilization certificate of these funds should be submitted to the government before April 30, 2023.
The District Collector/ Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad/ Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the various schemes/ water supply related works taken up in the previous year have been completed with the provision made for the relief of natural disasters.
11) The concerned Divisional Commissioner/ CEO, Zilla Parishad/ Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Authority should ensure that water is an essential/ essential item, there is no malpractice under it, the same scheme is not repeated every year and unnecessary expenditure is not incurred on it.
The Divisional Commissioner shall submit to the Government, every month, along with the monthly expenditure report, information on the physical progress of the district for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the certificate with quarterly verification should be submitted to this department immediately.
Also, the District Collector/ Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad as well as Member Secretary, Maharashtra Life Authority should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances. If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time. Ask for the necessary funds. No more funds should be required.
While the funds are being released as per the demand received from the field offices, the same amount of funds required for the scarcity period 2020-21 and 2021-22 should be drawn through the Budget Distribution System. Proposals should be submitted to the competent authority for disciplinary action against the concerned if additional funds are drawn and remain unutilized.
The Water Supply and Sanitation Department should be informed about this.
14) Provide action plan-wise, scarcity period-wise information regarding the allocation of the funds.
