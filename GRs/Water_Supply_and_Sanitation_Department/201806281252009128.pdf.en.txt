The Government has taken an important decision to implement the concept of" Green Maharashtra" to reduce the intensity and severity of natural disasters such as climate change, rising temperatures, drought and water scarcity. Under the programme, a target of planting 50 crore trees has been set for the period 2017-2019. In addition, an ambitious programme of planting 2 crore trees on a single day on 1st July, 2016 was also implemented.
The target is to plant 13 crore and 33 crore trees in 2018 and 2019 respectively. It is very important that the goal of tree plantation is fully achieved and at the same time the trees planted so far are protected, maintained and nurtured well. Dated 1st July 2017 to 7th July,

Accordingly, the following circular is being issued.
Circular In the field level meetings at Taluka, District and State level under the Water Supply and Sanitation Department, the topics related to tree plantation and plantation should be included as a permanent topic in the agenda at the time of each meeting. In such meetings, review of tree plantation, survival rate of trees planted earlier, planting of new trees in place of dead trees, fixing of site for plantation as per the target of tree plantation in the next year, availability of saplings, preparation of pits within the stipulated time as per the required technical parameters, availability of funds, etc. should be reviewed.
The decisions taken in the meetings regarding the tree plantation programme should be recorded in the minutes of the meeting. Also, the implementation of the decisions mentioned in the chronicle and the difficulties encountered should be discussed in the next meeting and measures should be taken to solve the problems and difficulties faced by the field officers.
3. The officers at the Taluka, District and State level who will visit the field works, a program should be organized to visit the tree plantation sites under those departments.
During such field visits, senior officers should inspect whether the trees planted under the concerned administrative department are properly protected, whether new trees have been planted in place of dead trees, water arrangements and manpower for protection, etc. Also, you need to take steps to solve the problem.
4. Examine the sites selected for the proposed tree plantation, prepare pits of the required size within the stipulated time and provide necessary guidance to the field officers regarding the water and protection arrangements for the trees to be planted at such sites and the arrangement of funds for the tree plantation.
