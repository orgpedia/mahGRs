The Proposal:
A budget provision of Rs. 50.8148 crore has been made for the financial year 2021-22 under the head Demand Number Y-2" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (01) Divisional/ Sub Divisional Establishment for Implementation of Water Supply Programme (2215 1843)" to meet the salary expenditure of Class I and Class II officers on the establishment of Rural Water Supply Department in Zila Parishad.
Out of this provision, Rs. 12.29 crore has been released to the Zila Parishads as grant in aid for payment of salaries as per the Government decision vide Notes 2, 3, 4 and 5 of the Vachha. However, the Pune Zilla Parishad has informed that the funds disbursed so far are not enough to pay salaries till May. Accordingly, the matter of disbursement of additional salary grant to Pune Zilla Parishad is under consideration of the Government.

Further, the Department of Finance has also provided for domestic travel expenses under head 11, domestic travel expenses, within the limit of 5% of the budgetary provision of Rs. 4.00 lakh has been made available on the BIMS system. It is under consideration of the Government to disburse funds for travel expenses in the current year to the District Councils which have incurred physical travel expenses during the financial year 2020-21.
Government's decision:
For the financial year 2021-22, under the head" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (01) Divisional/ Sub Divisional Establishment (Compulsory) for implementation of Water Supply Programme (2215 1843)," a total grant of Rs. 30,00,000/- (Rupees Thirty Lakh only) is being sanctioned to the Chief Accounts and Finance Officer of the concerned Zilla Parishad as shown in the" Statement A" accompanying this Government decision.
(2) The grants sanctioned under this Government Resolution shall be subject to such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Government from time to time. All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts shown in the' Return A" accompanying this Government decision. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
(3) Care should be taken to demand further necessary funds only after ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said Government decision is submitted to the Government. The concerned District Councils should note that the funds will not be disbursed until the next demand letter is received. The subsidy will be credited to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
