The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2021 has been approved by the Legislature and assented to by the Governor of Maharashtra and is published as Maharashtra Act No. 5 of 2021 in the Government of Maharashtra, Gazette Extraordinary Part-IV, dated 15th March, 2021. The Department of Finance has released 10% of the total annual budgetary provision as a temporary measure for mandatory expenditure for the financial year 2021-22 through Budget Control System.
Demand No.Y-2" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 196, Assistance to Zilla Parishads (01) Assistance to Zilla Parishads/ Panchayat Institutions for meeting the salary expenses of Class-3 and Class-4 officers and employees on the establishment of Rural Water Supply Department in Zilla Parishads.

Under the head Establishment Grants to Zilla Parishads under Section 183 of the Maharashtra Zilla Parishads and Panchayat Samitis Act, 1961 (01) (05) Regular Establishment (Compulsory) (22151881), a grant of Rs. 26.1081 crore for the financial year 2021-22 has been made available by the Department of Finance in the Budget System vide Government Circular dated 6.4.2021. The Government is considering to disburse grants to the Zila Parishads considering the total salary expenditure of the respective Zila Parishads for the financial year 2020-21.
Government's decision:
For the financial year 2021-22, a total grant of Rs. 26,10,81, 000/- (Rupees Twenty Six Crores Ten Lakhs Eighty One Thousand only) is being sanctioned to the Chief Accounts and Finance Officer of the concerned Zilla Parishad as shown in the Statement A accompanying this Government Decision for the Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) under the head" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 196, Assistance to Zilla Parishads (01), Assistance to Zilla Parishads/ Panchayat Institutions, Establishment Grants to Zilla Parishads under Section 183 of the Maharashtra Zilla Parishad and Panchayat Samitis Act, 1961, 36 (01) (05) Regular Establishment (Mandatory) (22151881)."
(2) The grants sanctioned under this Government Resolution shall be subject to such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Government from time to time. All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts shown in the' Return A" accompanying this Government decision. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
(3) Care should be taken to demand further necessary funds only after ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said Government decision is submitted to the Government. The concerned District Councils should note that the funds will not be disbursed until the next demand letter is received. The subsidy will be credited to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
