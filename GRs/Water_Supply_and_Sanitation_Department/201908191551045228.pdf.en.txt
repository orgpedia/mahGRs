The Government has taken the responsibility of payment of salary, allowances and pension to the employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority under the Government Decision dated 23.3.2017 and since the Government is likely to take a long time to pay the pension expenses of the retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority through the Treasury from the regular Principal Accounts Title 2071, the new Account Title created temporarily for the purpose of providing subsidy to the Authority for the financial year 2018-19 on the retirement salary of the retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority has been extended for the year 2019-20 under the Government Decision dated 10.1.2019 with the consent of the Principal Accountant General's Office and the Finance Department.

The Department of Finance, vide circular dated 8th July, 2019, has made available the budgetary provisions of mandatory expenditure required at the end of the month of December for the year 2019-20 on the budgetary system within the limit of 60% of the total annual provision. Out of the said provision, a total amount of Rs. 75,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Seventy Five Crores only) has been released to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for the four months April, 2019 to July, 2019, as per the Government Resolution No.5 of the WADA. Now, considering the actual expenditure incurred by the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran during the four months from April to July, 2019, the Government is considering to disburse the pension grant to the retired officers/ employees of the Authority for the five months from August, 2019 to December, 2019.
Government's decision:
In the financial year 2019-20, a grant of Rs. 77,05,80, 000/- (Rupees Seventy Seven Crores Five Lakh Eighty Thousand only) under the head of Demand No.Y-2, 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 199, Assistance to Other Non-Government Organizations (01) Assistance to Other Non-Government Organizations (01) (01) Assistance to Government Employees (Subsidiary Grants (Compulsory) (2215 A052) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary) for sanction of pension benefits to Government Employees transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran (MJA) was released for the month of August, 2019 to December, 2019 subject to the following conditions:
(a) The Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the amount of the grant is spent for the same purposes as the grant is sanctioned for the pension benefits of retired officers/ employees of the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran.
(b) This fund should not be used for any outstanding dues as on 23.3.2017. Expenditure on other items of this fund will be treated as financial irregularity and the officers/ employees concerned will be liable for disciplinary action for such action.
e) Separate accounts of the amount of free subsidy should be maintained.
