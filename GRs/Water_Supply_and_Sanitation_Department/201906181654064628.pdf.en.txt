The plan and plan at a nominal cost of Rs. 124.68/- (Rupees One Crore Twenty Four Lakh Sixty Two Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
1) The scheme should be implemented by the District Council, Raigad. It will be mandatory for the contractor to run the scheme for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as the maintenance of the scheme is carried out for the repair of the reference No. 7 The guidelines contained in the Government Resolution dated 17th October, 2014 are to be followed.
2) Even if the scheme will be run by the contractor for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
3) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
4) No work order should be issued to the scheme unless the necessary land acquisition permissions are obtained for the scheme.
If the order is issued without fulfilling these conditions, the administrative approval given under this government decision will be considered canceled.
5) 100% household tap connections and meters should be included in the scheme. Also, at least 80% of the tap connection holders should take an undertaking for getting the meter connection at their own cost and a copy of it should be laminated and given to the tap connection holder and a copy should be kept with the Gram Panchayat. Thereafter, the orders were issued.
6) If a villager refuses to take water even after the gram sabha resolution/ assurance letter, at least 50 per cent of the water supply should be recovered from him. In this regard, the villagers should be given an idea before the tender process and the Gram Sabha should take a resolution in this regard.
7) The scheme should be included on the central government's IMIS system.
8) The project should be completed in a time-bound manner.
No other work should be started until the plan is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
9) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time.
