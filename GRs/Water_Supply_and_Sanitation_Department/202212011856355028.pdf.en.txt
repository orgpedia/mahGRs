1) The implementation of this scheme should be done through the District Council, Thane. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned Gram Panchayat will be responsible to take over the scheme within 1 month from the date of receipt of the notification from the concerned Executive Engineer that the scheme has been actually completed. After the scheme is actually completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and the concerned Gram Panchayat (2). The officers thereof shall conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they shall be rectified before handing over the scheme.
3) It will be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the Jal Jeevan Mission program. Every house should have a tap connection. In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
4) The contractor will be obliged to run the scheme for at least 1 year. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed.
5) Even if the scheme is to be run for at least one year through the contractor after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 4 th September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 5/10 th of the cost of the scheme shall be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayats for the maintenance of the water supply scheme.
The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
7) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
8) Land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme should be obtained within the prescribed time.
9) To ensure that the scheme is not used seasonally, the Gram Panchayat should ensure that the existing scheme in the village included in the scheme is not operated.
10) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time. Also, to determine the required water table for the scheme to be self-sufficient and increase it as per the need.
