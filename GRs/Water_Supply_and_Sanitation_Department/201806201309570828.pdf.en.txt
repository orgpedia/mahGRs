Hence, the cost of living has increased. Srivardhan, G. Administrative sanction for the water supply scheme in Raigad was under consideration of the government.

2. Reference No. By the decision of the government in the 3rd, the public charge for rural water supply schemes has been completely abolished. Therefore, the funds allocated by the government for the implementation of this scheme are subject to Article No. 3 shall be as set forth in the governing decision.
The scheme is granted administrative approval subject to the following conditions:
The scheme should be implemented by the District Council, Raigad. After completion of the scheme, the District Council shall ensure that it has been successfully completed and after completion of the scheme and testing, the maintenance of the scheme shall be carried out by the District Council/ Shikhar/ Joint Committee as mentioned in the Government Resolution No. 4.
2. To meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall provide at least Rs. 1100/- per family per year should be fixed and increased with time. The Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Raigad should himself ensure that the villagers are ready to fill the water table. The government will not provide any subsidy for the maintenance and operation of the project.
3. The sources of the scheme should be strengthened. 100% household tap connections should be included in the scheme.
(4) If the expenditure of the scheme increases for any reason, no government subsidy will be provided for the increased expenditure.
5. Action should be taken as per the prevailing policy to make the village open defecation free.
The work should be carried out in a timely and planned manner.
7. The work in the scheme should be done in stages. No other work should be done until the development of the phenomenon. (Other work in the scheme should be done only after ensuring that sufficient water is available in the source.) Prior permission of the Water Supply and Sanitation Department should be obtained before accepting the higher price tender. The work should not be started without the approval of the department concerned.
Existing resources should be strengthened.
10. To make the scheme self-reliant, it will be mandatory to install and increase the required water table, the officials of the Zilla Parishad should give detailed information to the villagers in the Gram Sabha, and only then the work of the scheme should be started.
The conditions of the technical approval of the scheme, the guidelines of the National Rural Drinking Water Programme as well as the Government Decision in paragraph 2 shall be complied with.
The expenditure on this scheme should be met from the central and state funds under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme received by the Zilla Parishad.
