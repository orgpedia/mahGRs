The proposal-
The proposed construction of the Ground Water Information and Technology Centre under the World Bank funded National Hydrology Project includes construction of an extension building, furniture for the extension building, strengthening of the existing building, furniture for the existing building, internal roads, motor garage, water supply, facade glass replacement, electrification of the existing building, etc.
GOVERNMENT DECISION 1. Administrative approval is being accorded for the construction of the Ground Water Information and Technology Centre and strengthening of the existing building under the World Bank funded National Hydrology Project subject to the following conditions.
1. The said budgets have been revised in accordance with the Government Circular dated 25.09.2019 of the Public Works Department to Rs. The Chief Engineer concerned has given approval after the competent authority, which grants technical approval to the budgets of less than Rs 15 crore, provides technical concurrence to the budget.
The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV-2013/ Q.No.177/ T.C.1, D. Action should be taken as per the guidelines mentioned in 10.01.2014.

The Superintending Engineer, Public Works Department concerned should visit the building at least three times during the construction of the building and ensure the quality of work and submit such reports to the Director, Ground Water Survey and Development System in writing from time to time.
8. Inclusion of facilities other than building construction in the building construction budget should be included as per the requirement after verifying at the level of the Director, Ground Water Survey and Development Mechanism, taking into account the various suggestions given by the government.
9. The Director, Ground Water Survey and Development Authority shall personally visit the building after completion of construction and before taking possession of the building and ensure that the work done is of high quality. If there are any lapses, it should be rectified by the Public Works Department and its site inspection report should be preserved in the records.
10. The Director, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency shall ensure that the utility certificate is submitted to the Government as soon as the construction work is completed.
