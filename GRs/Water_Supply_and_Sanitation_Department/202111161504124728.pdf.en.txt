Preamble As per the working guidelines of the Central Government regarding Jal Jeevan Mission, for implementation of Centrally Sponsored Jal Jeevan Mission in the State, the Department has issued Reference No. 2 d. The guidelines have been issued as per the Government decision dated 04.09.2020. Under the Jal Jeevan Mission, a target has been set to provide sustainable and pure drinking water through individual tap connections to all rural households in the State by 2024. The funding pattern of Jal Jeevan Mission is 1:1 between the Centre and the States.
Paragraph No. 2 of the Working Guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission. In 7.14, the Flexi fund is mentioned as follows. -
Flexi fund under JJM can be used to achieve the following objectives 1. To undertake mitigation/restoration activities in case of natural calamities, or to satisfy local requirements in areas affected by internal security disturbance 2. to pilot innovation to improve efficiency States & UTs are advised to set aside 5% of the annual allocation under JJM to take care of unforeseen challenges/ issues arising out of natural calamities and internal disturbance which may be used by the state at the fag end of the financial year, if remain unutilized.
Further, regarding the implementation of the Centrally Sponsored Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) Phase-II, Reference No. 3 d. The guidelines have been issued under the Government decision dated 11.10.2021

Under Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) Phase 2, the target has been set to declare entire rural Maharashtra Open Defecation Free (ODF Plus) by 2024-2025. The Central Government has issued guidelines in this regard in the month of July 2020. The guidelines for flexi funds are as follows:
The States Can use Flexi funds as per Ministry of Finance Guidelines issued in this regard from time to time for innovations/ technology optionsat the State level to meet the local needs and requirements within the overall objective of the scheme.
Central Government Reference No. 1 d. Guidelines on Flexi Funds have been issued vide order dated 06.09.2016. The committee to be constituted for the Flexi Fund is as follows:
The State Who want to avail of the flexi fund facility, Should constitute a State level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC) on the lines of RKVY to sanction projects or activities under the flexi fund component.
Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a state-level committee for the sanction of flexi funds under the above two schemes under the department.
The government has taken a decision to sanction flexi funds under Jal Jeevan Mission and Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) Phase-II. A State-level committee under the chairmanship of the Chief Secretary has been constituted.
