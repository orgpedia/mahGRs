Under the programme, administrative approval is being granted subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
1) The scheme should be implemented through the concerned District Council. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned Gram Panchayat will be responsible for taking over the scheme within 1 month from the date of receipt of information from the concerned Executive Engineer that the scheme has been completed. On the actual completion of the scheme, a joint inspection of the scheme should be conducted by the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran/ Zilla Parishad and the concerned Gram Panchayat officer and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
2) It will be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the Jal Jeevan Mission program. Every house should have a tap connection.
In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
3) One-time expenditure on household tap connections (FHTC) under Jal Jeevan Mission should be reduced. Under this program, the responsibility of ensuring that there is no duplication in spending on household tap connections (FHTC) will be the responsibility of the concerned executive engineer.
4) The contractor will be obliged to run the scheme for at least 1 year. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as provide for the maintenance and repair of the scheme. Follow the guidelines issued by the government on October 17, 2014.
5) Even if the scheme is to be run for at least one year through the contractor after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
6) Reference No. As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 1st September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 10% of the cost of the scheme will be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayat for maintenance of the water supply scheme. The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
7) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
8) Land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme should be obtained within the prescribed time.
9) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time. Also, it will be mandatory for the Gram Panchayat to determine the required water table and increase it as per the need to make the scheme self-sufficient.
