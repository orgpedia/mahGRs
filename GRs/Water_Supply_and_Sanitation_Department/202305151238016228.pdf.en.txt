The Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Palghar has accorded the original administrative approval to the budget of Tap Water Supply Scheme at Vikramgad, Palghar District vide order No. 7. However, the cost of the scheme has increased due to higher rate of tenders received for the work of the scheme and increased Goods and Services Tax. The Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Palghar has given the revised technical approval to the budget of the said scheme under reference no.8. Accordingly, the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Palghar, had submitted a proposal to the government under reference letter No. 9 to give revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the present tap water supply scheme.
Accordingly, the matter of granting revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.

Vikramgad, Palghar (Rs. The revised budget of Rs. 67.08 lakhs (Rupees Six hundred and eight thousand only) is being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions.
Conditions
1.All the works of the scheme should be completed within the prescribed time frame at the revised cost as mentioned above.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Palghar that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
7. Action to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
