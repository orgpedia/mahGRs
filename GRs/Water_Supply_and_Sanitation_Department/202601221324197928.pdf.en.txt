4) The Gram Panchayat / Zilla Parishad concerned shall not receive any assistance from the Government for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred annually for implementation of the scheme, its maintenance, repairs (including special modifications), depreciation in the scheme or for renovation of machinery.
5) On completion of the scheme, it will be mandatory for the local body (concerned Gram Panchayat / Zila Parishad) to implement the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
6) A CAG report in 2013 shows that 21 per cent of water supply schemes in the state have been shut down due to poor maintenance / non-payment of electricity bills. 9) Since no administrative expenditure is admissible under the Jal Jeevan Mission, it should be excluded if 4% of the administrative expenditure has been taken up in the revised budget of the scheme.
10) It should be ensured at the Zilla Parishad level that the tendering process of the scheme is carried out properly.
