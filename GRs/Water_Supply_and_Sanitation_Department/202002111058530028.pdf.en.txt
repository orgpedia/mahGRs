For Rs. A sum of Rs. 1369.88 lakhs (Rupees Thirteen Crores Seventy Nine Lakhs Eighty Eight Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released to the Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran through Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions.
The fund should be spent only for the implementation of regional tap water supply schemes approved to be revived under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2019-20. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
Funds disbursed to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should not be kept in personal provident fund (PLA) or any other such account.
(iv) No funds will be sanctioned in future under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme unless information is furnished regarding the funds disbursed during the current financial year, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc.
v. The physical progress report for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai along with the monthly expenditure report every month. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure incurred from this grant. In this regard, vide No. The Accountant General, Maharashtra-1/ 2, Mumbai/ Nagpur, as well as the authorized officers of this department, will have the right to see the books and accounts of the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority for audit, so that the government can ensure that the guidelines set by the government under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program are being fulfilled by the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority.
Also, the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran will provide full support and facilities to the Accountant General to carry out his work properly.
