Government's decision:
As per the norms laid down by the Government for the alleviation of drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas, the Divisional Commissioner, Pune is being sanctioned and released funds to the tune of Rs. 600.00 lakhs (Rupees Six Crores only) under head Accounts 22450093 for the measures taken/ to be taken for the alleviation of drinking water scarcity in his department through the Budget Distribution System subject to the following conditions and terms and conditions as laid down in the Standing Government Resolution in force.
Terms and Conditions
(1) The Divisional Commissioner, Pune shall disburse the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad for taking measures under scarcity in rural areas.
2) Urban measures which are being implemented through Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils.
For this, the Divisional Commissioner should distribute it to the concerned District Collector. The Collector should disburse the funds to the concerned Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council.
The funds should be released for the implementation of the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the scarcity period 2018-19 and for the payment of pending dues during this period. Care should be taken to ensure that the funds are not used for other short-term measures.
4) The Divisional Commissioner should allocate funds on priority to the measures like well acquisition, tanker water supply and even if the proposal submitted by the Divisional Commissioner does not ask for the measures approved by the government, these measures should also be allocated on priority in accordance with the order of the concerned administrative approval.
5) The amount of utilization certificates that have been submitted out of the funds disbursed in the financial year 2018-19. The process of disbursing the same percentage of funds to those districts should be done from the level of Divisional Commissioner.
6) Make sure that the funds are not kept in a personal ledger account or bank account and are distributed directly to the contractors through electronic clearing services.
7) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed for the related measures earlier.

8) The funds for urban and rural measures being implemented by the Maharashtra Life Authority should not be distributed to the Zilla Parishad. The funds should be immediately disbursed by the concerned District Collector to the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, who is implementing the scheme. Also, for the measures being implemented through the Municipal Corporation/ Municipal Council, the District Collector should distribute the funds to the concerned Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council.
(9) Further, while seeking funds for the measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity, the matter-wise demand as to the period for which the proposed measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity, the amount of funds required for it, as well as the information regarding the funds disbursed, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc. in the current financial year, as well as the utilization certificate therefor, should be submitted. Besides, it should be noted that the funds will be approved in the future.
10) GPS on private tankers while making payments out of the funds released for the measures taken for supply of water through tankers/ drinking water as per the decision of the Government.
The system will be installed and only those trips of tankers whose fare is recorded on the GPS system will be allowed for payment. Such trips will not be admissible if the tanker transport holder claims that the tanker has been diverted due to non-installation of the system or the GPS system is switched off. as well as GPS.
Payments should not be authenticated as the system does not register and funds should not be disbursed for payments authenticated in this manner.
The Divisional Commissioner shall submit to the Government, every month, along with the monthly expenditure report, information on the physical progress of the district for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the relevant certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2019-20. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
