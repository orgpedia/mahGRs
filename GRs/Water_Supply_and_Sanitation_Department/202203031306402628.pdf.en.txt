Preamble: Expenditure on measures taken to alleviate water scarcity in rural areas of the State is met from the grants made available by this Department under the head (2215 A 195).
The Department has received the proposal of Pune Division seeking funds for the measures taken/ to be taken during the period of scarcity in 2012-13 and 2019-20. During the financial year 2021-22, the funds budgeted under the objective of Account Head (2215A 195) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Plan), were under consideration of the Government to disburse funds for the measures demanded and actually completed by the Divisional Commissioner, Pune.

Government's decision:
In accordance with the criteria laid down by the Government for the alleviation of drinking water scarcity in rural areas, a sum of Rs. 113.19 lakhs (Rs.1 crore thirteen lakh ninety nine thousand only) is being sanctioned and released to the Divisional Commissioner, Pune, to meet the actual cost of the remedial measures taken for the alleviation of drinking water scarcity in his department (Head of Accounts 2215 A 195) through the Budget Distribution System subject to the terms and conditions laid down in the extant Standing Government Resolution and the following conditions.

Terms and Conditions
The Divisional Commissioner, Pune shall disburse the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad for taking measures under the scarcity in rural areas.
The funds should be released for the implementation of the measures taken to address the drinking water shortage in rural areas for the period 2012-13 (Satara) and 2019-20 (Sangli) and for the payment of pending dues during this period. This money cannot be used for other projects.
3) The Divisional Commissioner should allocate funds on priority to the measures like well acquisition, tanker water supply and even if the proposal submitted by the Divisional Commissioner does not ask for the measures approved by the government, these measures should also be allocated on priority as per the order of the concerned administrative approval.
4) It should be ensured that the funds are disbursed directly to the contractors through Electronic Clearing Services/ RTGS, without keeping the personal ledger account or bank account.
5) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed to the concerned measures earlier.
