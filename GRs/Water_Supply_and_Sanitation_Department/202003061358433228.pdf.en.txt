The grant is to be spent by the implementing agencies on priority for completion of works of schemes in the villages/ hamlets covered under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (Jal Jeevan Mission) under the Annual Action Plan for the year 2019-2020 under the Tribal Appropriation Programme and the Centrally Sponsored Schemes in which Government participation is mandatory.
2) Please be advised that this fund is required to be spent in the financial year 2019-20 only. Costs will be the responsibility of the parties involved.
(3) The implementing agency shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made out of this grant.
The Accountant General, Maharashtra- 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur, as well as the officers authorised by this Department, shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of the implementing agencies for the purpose of audit, so that the Government may ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme are being implemented by the agencies. Also, the Accountant General will be provided with full support and facilities to enable him to carry out his work properly.
The Zilla Parishad Offices shall reconcile the expenditure of the said grant with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office and submit the report of the reconciliation to the Office of Papu-10/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai on the prescribed date.
Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-10 Office.
As per the guidelines of the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (Jal Jeevan Mission), the expenditure for schemes undertaken under tribal deployment should be 50% from the Central fund and 50% from the State fund.
