The expenditure on water scarcity measures in rural and urban areas of the State is met from the grants available under the head" Assistance for Natural Disaster Mitigation" (2245). Reference No. 18 for measures taken/ to be taken during the period of shortage in 2017-18 and 2018-19 Administrative approval was given to the measures under the shortage under the government decision in 2. For the financial year 2018-19, the Department of Revenue and Forest (Relief and Rehabilitation) has transferred the funds to this Department as per the Government Resolution of Reference No. 3. The matter of disbursement of this fund to the Divisional Commissioner, Nashik was under the consideration of the government.

Government's decision:
An amount of Rs. 362.38 lakhs (Rupees Three Crores Sixty Two Lakhs Thirty Eight Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released to the Divisional Commissioner, Nashik through the Budget Distribution System to meet the expenditure on the measures to be taken/ implemented for drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas as per the norms laid down by the Government, subject to the following conditions and terms and conditions as stipulated in the extant Standing Government Resolution.
1) The Divisional Commissioner Nashik shall disburse the funds to the District Collector Jalgaon for taking measures under the scarcity in urban areas. The Collector Jalgaon should immediately distribute this fund to the Chief Officer Erandol Municipal Council.
(2) For the measures being taken through the Municipal Corporation/ Municipal Council, the concerned Collector shall disburse the said funds to the respective Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council.
The funds should be released for the implementation of the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the scarcity period 2018-19 and for the payment of pending dues during this period.
For rural and urban schemes which have been approved at the government level under scarcity, 50 percent of the funds are being provided under this government decision at the beginning of the scheme as mentioned in the government decision.
The remaining 30 per cent of the funds of the scheme should be disbursed by the Divisional Commissioner, Nashik after reviewing the progress of the scheme at his level and the remaining 20 per cent should be released only after the scheme is implemented.
6) Make sure that the funds are not kept in a personal ledger account or bank account and are distributed directly to the contractors through electronic clearing services.
7) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed for the related measures earlier.
8) Further, while seeking funds for the measures to be taken for the relief of scarcity, a case-wise demand as to the period for which the proposed measures to be taken for the relief of scarcity, the amount of funds required for it, as well as information about the funds disbursed in the current financial year, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc., and the utilization certificate therefor should be submitted. It should be noted that the funds will not be approved in the future.

The Divisional Commissioner shall submit to the Government every month, along with the monthly expenditure report, information on the physical progress of the district for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the relevant certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time.
The funds will be used in the 2018-19 financial year. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
11) The expenditure in this regard will be borne by the Government as mentioned in the Statement of Disbursement of Funds in this Government Resolution" Demand No. C-6, 2245- Assistance for relief of natural calamities, 01- Avarakshana, 102- Drinking water supply, (91)- Expenditure as per the State Disaster Response Fund norms, (91) (01) Emergency supply of drinking water (2245 0093) (Dattamat) (Post Pay) 31, Subsidiary Grants.
12) The Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall not fail to mention the computer code of the subordinate/ sub-heading in the computer slip to be sent to the Treasury/ Deputy Treasury Officer and attached with the payment. Also, a report of the amount withdrawn from the treasury/ sub-treasury should be submitted to the government.
The details of the disbursement of funds under this Government decision are as follows: The Divisional Commissioner, Nashik should immediately disburse the funds to the District Collector, Jalgaon. The Collector Jalgaon should immediately distribute this fund to the Chief Officer Erandol Municipal Council.
