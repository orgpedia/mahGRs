The Proposal:
Mouje Nagaon (Ta. The Chief Executive Officer, Raigad Zilla Parishad, Alibag has submitted a proposal for the approval of the government under reference number 8 as the per capita cost of the tap water supply scheme is higher than the prescribed norm. The proposal for the said tap water supply scheme has been accorded technical approval under letter of reference No. 7 of the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Raigad Zilla Parishad, Alibag. The approval was given by the Technical Committee headed by Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, Regional Division, Thane in its meeting held on January 11, 2019. Therefore, Nag. Mahad, Raigad (Rs. 62.72 lakh was under the consideration of the Government for grant of administrative approval under Jal Jeevan Mission Programme.
Government's decision:
Mouje Nagaon (Ta. 55 per litre), Rs. 6,892/- per annum. 62.72/- (Rupees Sixty Two Lakhs Seventy Two Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval under the Jal Jeevan Mission Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
1) The scheme should be implemented by the Chief Executive Officer, Raigad Zilla Parishad, Alibaug. The contractor is required to run the project for at least 1 year after the completion of the project

The Chief Executive Officer, Raigad Zilla Parishad, Alibag shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed and the maintenance of the scheme is carried out by the concerned No. 2 The guidelines contained in the Government Resolution dated 17th October, 2014 should be followed.
2) The provisions of the Government Decision dated 11th February, 2019 in Reference No. 5 and 10th March, 2021 in Reference No. 9 should be adopted.
3) Even if the scheme will be run by the contractor for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 04.09.2020 in Reference No.6, 10% of the cost of the scheme shall be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayat as public contribution for maintenance of the water supply scheme as per the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India. The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
5) Village Action Plan needs to be prepared before starting the work as per the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission of the said village.
6) Every household should be provided with a tap connection under this scheme. In this regard, it is necessary to register information on IMIS about the tap connection given so far and the tap connection to be given next. The Gram Panchayat will be responsible for planning the new connection.
7) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
8) Land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme should be obtained within the stipulated time.
9) 100% household tap connections and meters should be included in the scheme. Also, at least 80% of the tap connection holders should take an undertaking for getting the meter connection at their own cost and a copy of it should be laminated and given to the tap connection holder and a copy should be kept with the Gram Panchayat. Thereafter, the orders were issued.
10) The existing schemes in the villages covered under the scheme should not be run so that the scheme is not used seasonally.
11) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat should fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time.
12) 20% of the recovered water table should be kept with the implementing agency for major maintenance repairs. Maintenance repair of pipes and other pipes should be done every 5 years and every 3 years.
No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred annually for running, maintaining and repairing the scheme, for the purpose of maintenance of the scheme, for the repair of the machinery or for the reduction of the scheme.
14) It should be ensured that the village under this scheme is open defecation free but it will always remain open defecation free.
15) If the cost of the scheme increases for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
16) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled.
