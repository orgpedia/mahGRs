3) Even if the scheme will be run for at least 1 year through the contractor after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
4) Reference No. As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 4 th September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 10% of the cost of the scheme will be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayats for the maintenance of the water supply scheme. The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
5) Every household should be provided with a tap connection under this scheme. In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
7) Necessary procedures and all other permissions regarding land acquisition for the scheme should be obtained within the stipulated time.
8) To ensure that the scheme is not used seasonally, the Gram Panchayat should ensure that the existing scheme in the village included in the scheme is not operated.
9) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time. Also, it will be mandatory for the gram panchayat to determine the required water table and increase it as per the need to make the scheme self-reliant.
