Introduction: The original administrative approval has been granted by the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Palghar under reference no. 1 to the budget of the tap water supply scheme in Dahanu, G. Palghar. As per Reference No. 2, the Superintending Engineer, Bharat Nirman Cell and Coordinator, Navi Mumbai have given the original technical approval to the budget. However, the cost of the project increased due to the receipt of tenders for the work at an inflated rate. The tender for the project has been approved under Reference No. 3. The Superintending Engineer, Bharat Nirman Cell and Coordinator, Konkan Division, Thane have given revised technical approval to the budget of the scheme under reference number 4. Accordingly, the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad Palghar, has submitted a proposal to the government under reference letter No. 5 to give revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the present tap water supply scheme. Accordingly, the matter of giving revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Mausam Bahare (Ta. Dahanu, Palghar (Rs. The revised administrative sanction of Rs. 284.56 lakhs (Rupees Two Crores Eighty Four Lakhs Fifty Six Thousand only) is being granted subject to the following conditions.

Government Resolution No: GRAPAPU-1520/ PR No. 08/ PAPU-06 Conditions
(1) All the remaining works should be completed in 17 months (except rainy season) at the revised cost mentioned above.
(2) The terms and conditions mentioned in the original administrative approval and the revised technical approval granted to the scheme shall be strictly complied with.
(3) On completion of the scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Palghar that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
(4) The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned shall not receive any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (subject to special amendments) of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
(5) On completion of the scheme, it shall be mandatory for the local self-government institution (the Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of the water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
(7) Action should be taken in accordance with the prevailing policy on dowry.
(8) It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the said scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
2. The revised cost of the scheme is Rs. 21.08 lakh (Rupees Twenty One Lakh Eight Thousand only) from the State share under National Rural Drinking Water Programme/ District Planning Boards/ funds available under Minimum Needs Programme.
3. Revised administrative approval for the scheme is to be accorded by the Finance Department. No.of 17/04/2015
