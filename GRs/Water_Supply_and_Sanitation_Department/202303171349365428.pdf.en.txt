The decision was taken by the state government under the Jal Jeevan Mission. Devasthanam, Malegaon, G. The price of Washim tap water supply scheme has been increased by Rs. Revised cost estimates amounting to Rs.1,12,73,417/- (Rupees One Crore Twelve Lakh Seventy Three Thousand Four Hundred Seventeen only) (excluding 4% administrative expenses) are being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions.
Conditions: 1) All the works of the scheme should be completed within the prescribed time frame at the revised cost mentioned above.
2) The terms and conditions mentioned in the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
(3) The Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad concerned shall not receive any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery in the scheme.
4) After the completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad concerned shall be responsible to take over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zila Parishad that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to transfer and run the scheme. After that, the account will be closed.
6) Necessary planning for 100% tap connections should be done by the Gram Panchayat.
7) In view of Jal Jeevan Mission, it is necessary to check the record of tap connections given so far and to report the information on IMIS system about the tap connections to be given in future in a timely manner.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21 per cent of the water supply schemes in the state were closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
