3) On completion of the scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. 4) The Gram Panchayat / Zilla Parishad concerned shall not receive any assistance from the Government for the implementation of the scheme, maintenance, repairs (subject to special modifications), depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year.
5) On completion of the scheme, it will be mandatory for the local body (concerned Gram Panchayat / Zila Parishad) to implement the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
6) According to a CAG report in 2013, 21 per cent of water supply schemes in the state have been shut down due to poor maintenance / non-payment of electricity of bills due to lack of these maintenance repairs. 7) Action should be taken in accordance with the prevailing policy on extortion.
8) It shall be the responsibility of the Zilla Parishad that the present scheme shall not be revised under any circumstances.
9) Since no administrative expenditure is admissible under the Jal Jeevan Mission, it should be excluded if 4% administrative expenditure has been taken out of the revised budget of the scheme.
