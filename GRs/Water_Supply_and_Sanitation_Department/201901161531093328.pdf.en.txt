Administrative approval is being granted for the project and the cost of the project (Rs. Nineteen thousand four hundred only) subject to the following conditions.
Necessary action should be taken for in-principle approval of the scheme before the Regional Technical Scrutiny Committee headed by the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority Board, Aurangabad.
2) The scheme should be implemented by the District Council, Beed. The contractor will be responsible for the maintenance and repair of the plan for at least 1 year after the implementation of the plan.
3) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in a specified manner 4) No work order should be issued to the scheme without obtaining the necessary land acquisition, technical approval and all other permissions for the scheme 5) The work of the scheme should be completed in a time-bound and planned manner in a phased manner at the cost of labour.
6) No other work should be started until the origin of the plan is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
7) Correct the details of expenditure shown in the Detailed Project Report for operation and maintenance of the scheme and keep it to a minimum. Also, to meet the expenditure and to make the scheme financially self-sustaining, the concerned Gram Panchayat should fix the minimum water tariff for the household tap holder and increase it from time to time. It will be the responsibility of the enforcement officer to give all these things to the Gram Panchayat.
No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for running, testing and maintenance of the scheme or for renovation of machinery.
9) Strengthening of existing resources.
10) If there is an increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason, the government will not provide subsidy.
11) Action should be taken regarding open defecation as per the existing provisions of the government.
12) The conditions for the technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled.
13) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
14) If any irregularity, misappropriation and malpractices are found in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity will be subject to disciplinary action.
