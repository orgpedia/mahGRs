Read No. In pursuance of the above letter, the Central Government has revised the guidelines for declaration and verification of villages as Open Defecation Free Plus (ODF Plus) under Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) Phase-II. Government Decision: Instructions are being issued to declare and verify the villages as Open Defecation Free Plus (ODF Plus) under Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) Phase-II.
As per the Government decision dated 27th October, 2015 in Reference No. 3, all the Gram Panchayats which have been declared as Open Defecation Free (ODF) in the rural areas (which did not exist as villages or habitations in the rural areas of the concerned district earlier) and which have not been declared and verified earlier, should be declared and verified as Open Defecation Free (ODF) in the present situation.
As the main resource agencies at the state and district level are not in existence at present, for this verification, the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad should verify the village by forming a committee or through inter-Taluk verification and accordingly the records of the villages should be made on the SBM-MIS of the Central Government as per the directions of the Government decision dated 27th October 2015.
After the first phase of verification as per Government Decision, dated 27th October, 2015, to ensure the continuity of sanitation in the Gram Panchayats which have been declared Open Defecation Free as per the instructions in Government Decision, dated 18th July, 2019, at Reference No.4, the second phase of verification of Open Defecation Free Gram Panchayats should be carried out and the records of the Gram Panchayats should be made on the SBM-MIS of the Central Government.

By year-wise planning and meeting the following criteria, the district should progressively declare all villages and the entire district as ODF Plus by 2024-25.
Definition of ODF Plus:" ODF Plus means construction/ restoration of toilets, community toilet complexes, solid and waste water management and other ancillary works in villages rated as open defecation free for rural areas. " A) The criteria for making villages/ districts ODF+ are as follows: (i) ODF sustainability There should be toilet facilities in schools, anganwadis and gram panchayat offices in the village.
The village should have public toilet facilities for the mobile/ migrant population as required.
At least 5 messages of Information, Education and Communication (IEC) on sanitation-free sustainability, proper use of toilets, solid waste and wastewater management, hygiene habits and sustainable sanitation etc. should be displayed in the village.
(ii) Solid Waste Management All public places (primary schools, anganwadi centres, gram panchayat offices) and at least 80% of households should have effective solid waste management.
Under Solid Waste Management, there should be an adequate system of classifying and collecting individual and public level compost peat, plastic waste for animal and farm waste etc.
The following aspects under solid waste management should be considered while declaring villages as open defecation free.
All institutional buildings in the village such as schools/ anganwadis/ gram panchayat offices have facilities under solid waste management.
2. The village has collective compost pits for processing of non-degradable waste.
3. The village has a garbage transport vehicle facility for collection and transportation of waste.
4. Shed for collection and segregation of waste is available in the village.
If the above facilities are not available in the village and solid waste management is done in another way, then the following certificate should be uploaded on SBM-MIS from PM 05.

(iv) Visible Sanitation of the Village The visible sanitation of the village should be ensured by ensuring minimum generation of waste and minimum stagnant water and no generation of plastic waste dumps in all public places and 80% of the households in the village.
Geo-tagging of individual and public level solid waste and sewage management, completed public toilets, school and anganwadi sanitation/ toilets etc. should be done through the mobile application of SBM-Phase|| provided by the Central Government.

(ii) The Gram Sabha/ Monthly Meeting of the concerned Gram Panchayat shall be required to take a resolution in this regard for making a self-declaration that the village fulfilling the criteria mentioned in" A" above is eligible for ODF Plus rating under the above three stages. After self-declaration by Gram Panchayat as Open Defecation Free Plus (ODF Plus), the mobile user should upload the accompanying" Form B" on SBM Phase|| Mobile App.
(c) Verification Process for ODF Plus Declared Villages: After the ODF Plus declaration of a village as excellent/ model is made on the mobile application of SBM-Phase II, the entire verification of the village as excellent/ model should be done by the tri-state agency identified at the district and taluka level within 90 days after the declaration of the village as excellent/ model. However, if at the time of verification, a third party does not exist, the entire verification of the village should be done by the committee mentioned at" D" below. The verification committee should complete the verification of a village in a maximum of 2 days.
If the above committee finds any error during the verification of the villages, the committee should give such feedback. These errors should be rectified by the concerned village within 1 month. After the error is rectified by the concerned village, if the village has fulfilled the criteria of ODF Plus by verifying the errors again by the committee mentioned in the following" D," the information about the villages being ODF Plus, the Block Development Officer, Panchayat Samiti should inform the Deputy CEO (Water and Sanitation). As per the letter, the Central Government has provided a module to declare more ODF Plus villages by registering them on IMIS.
The Deputy CEO (Water and Sanitation) shall register the villages as per the said module.
Constitution of ODF Plus Verification Committee: (i) The Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad shall constitute a committee of district/ taluk/ village level officers and employees or non-official members. A quorum of the Committee shall consist of four members. The members of the committee will include district, taluk and village level officers, employees, sarpanches, gram panchayat members, swachhagrahis, anganwadi workers, health workers, women self-help groups, non-government members and other members notified by the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad. The powers to induct members shall remain with the Chief Executive Officer, District Council.
