Government Resolution, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, No: Majipra-2014/ PR No.33 (A)/ Papu 23, dated 23rd March, 2017 2) Government Resolution, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, No: Majipra-1117/ PR. 235/ PAPU 23, dated 7th September, 2018 Preamble: The Government has taken over the responsibility of salaries, allowances and pensions of the officers/ employees of the Maharashtra Life Authority under the Government Resolution No. 1 and as per the provisions of the Government Resolution, the monthly income received from the Maharashtra Life Authority towards ETP and technical service charges is deposited with the Government. As per the provisions of the same government decision, it has been decided to create a separate head of account at the department level to meet the expenses on regular salaries, allowances and pensions of the officers/ employees of the Maharashtra Life Authority and make provision for funds as required.
In order to make financial provision for providing subsidy to the Authority for giving retirement benefits to retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, a new accounting year for the financial year 2018-19 has been created in the context of the Government Resolution No. 2 in consultation with the Principal Accountant General's Office and the Finance Department.
Reimbursement of expenditure incurred so far by the Authority towards retirement salary of retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran and the remaining funds to the Authority.

The proposal for supplementary demand was submitted to the Finance Department in the winter session of the legislature in 2018 for making it available. The Finance Department has agreed to disburse Rs. 200.00 crore of the requisitioned funds to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran by sanctioning the same through Supplementary Demands. Accordingly, the matter of release of grant-in-aid of Rs. 200,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Two Hundred Crores only) towards retirement benefits of retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
A grant of Rs. 200,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Two Hundred Crores only) to the Authority towards retirement benefits of retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran is being released under the head" Demand No. Y-2, 2215-Water Supply and Sanitation, 199-Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations, 01-Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations, (01) (04) Government Grants for sanction of pension benefits to Government employees transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran (2215 052) (Compulsory/ Datta Mat), 31-Assistant Grants (Non-Pay)" subject to the following conditions:
Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the amount of grant is spent for the same purpose as the grant is sanctioned only for the pension benefits of retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran.
(2) The expenditure of this fund on other matters shall be deemed to be financial irregularities and the officer/ employee concerned shall be liable to disciplinary action for such action.
3) Separate accounts should be maintained of the amount of free subsidy.
The amount of grant-in-aid should be spent only for the specified purpose for which it has been sanctioned and released till 31st March, 2019.
The Maharashtra Jeevan Authority should inform the government from time to time about the allocation from the free subsidy.
As per Rule No. 3 of Annexure 2 to the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959, the accounts of the Authority shall be submitted to the Maharashtra Life Authority for inspection along with relevant documents.

Patil, Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran as Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Mr. Chetan Nikam, Deputy Secretary in charge, Water Supply and Sanitation Department as Controlling Officer.
The pension subsidy given by the government will have to be provided through ECS in the bank account of the beneficiary. Under no circumstances should the pension grant be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
The expenditure incurred in the present case shall be met from the approved financial provisions of 2018-19 by deducting expenditure under the head" Demand No.Y-2, 2215-Water Supply and Sanitation, 199-Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations, 01-Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations, (01) (04) Government Grants for sanction of pension benefits to Government employees transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran (2215A052) (Compulsory/ Datta), 31-Assistant Grants (Non-Salary)."
