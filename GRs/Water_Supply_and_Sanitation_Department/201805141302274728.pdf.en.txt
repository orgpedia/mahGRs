The proposal was considered for approval by the Secretary Level Standing Committee in its meeting held on 03.05.2018.

Government's decision:
Considering the facts mentioned in the Preamble, the Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad has sanctioned Rs. The administrative approval is being granted to the proposal of Rs. 54.35 lakhs (Rupees Fifty Four Lakhs Thirty Five Thousand only) subject to the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Aurangabad should first declare that there is a shortage of drinking water in Sillod city.
(2) The plan shall be implemented by the City Council, Sillod.
3) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Ensure that the work is completed within the approved cost.
The tender of the scheme is being allowed to be taken out for a short period (7 days).
The tender process must be completed by May 19, 2018. If the tender process is not completed by this date, the approval of the scheme will be considered canceled.
6) The plan. The water supply is to be completed and commissioned by 25th May, 2018.
The government will provide a subsidy of 90% i.e. Rs. 48.92 lakh out of the total cost of Rs. 54.35 lakh. The remaining amount i.e. 10% of the scheme (being Sillod' B' Municipal Council) is to be borne by the Municipal Council, Sillod as a contribution of Rs. 5.43 lakh.
The work will begin after the project's funds are allocated.
8) All the work mentioned in the proposal will have to be done by the Municipal Council, Sillod.
9) It should be ensured that the works of the scheme are completed immediately at the approved/ approved cost. The government will not be responsible if the cost of the scheme increases.
10) The Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad should provide 50% of the funds for this scheme at the beginning of the scheme and the remaining 50% should be given only after the scheme is implemented.
11) If there is an increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
Since the work of the scheme must be completed on time, the progress report should be sent to the government every Monday of the week from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
