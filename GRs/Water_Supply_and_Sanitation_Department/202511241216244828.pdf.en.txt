Preamble: - Guidelines have been issued in accordance with the circular in reference to settlement of pending promises by constitution of permanent "Committees on Assurance" under the chairmanship of Secretary in each Department from time to time for fulfillment of pending promises in the Department of Ministries. According to the circular, during the session of the Maharashtra legislature, promises are made by the minister as well as the minister of state during the proceedings of both the houses. It is the statutory responsibility of the concerned Ministries / Departments to deliver on those promises expeditiously. Accordingly, it was under the consideration of the Government to constitute a permanent "Committees on Assurance" headed by the Coordinating Officer of the Department and other Joint Secretaries / Deputy Secretaries and other responsible officers of the Department under the Chairmanship of the Secretary for the time-bound fulfillment of the promises and expeditious disposal of long pending promises and other parliamentary arms in the Department.
Government Circular: - The following permanent "Committees on Assurances" are being constituted to meet the pending commitments and other parliamentary arms in the Department.

2. Functions of the Committee. - 1. The Committee shall meet every fifteen days to regularly review the pending promises and other parliamentary instruments of the Department.

2. Set a time limit for the concerned executive and field officers to settle the pending promises and review it at the next meeting.
3. To direct the concerned Executive to act first by fixing a time limit for settlement of promises pending for more than two years.
4. Invite the field officers for meetings with the information / information of the promises with a view to expeditiously getting the information of the promises and disposing of the promises expeditiously.
5. To decide on the request to be made to the Committee Chairman, Legislative Assurances Committee, regarding the omission of promises which cannot be fulfilled.
6. To direct the Board concerned to return the promises received from the other department to the department concerned within fifteen days by taking a clear decision as to whether or not the transfer should be accepted.
7. To take a decision on matters referred for transfer to other departments requiring follow-up at the level of Secretary and to bring the matter before the General Administration Department (GAD) for adjudication if such cases are pending with other departments for more than fifteen days.
