The Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, a rural water supply programme of the State Government, is being implemented across the State over a period of four years from 2016-17 to 2019-20. A sum of Rs. 227.13 crore has been provided for the financial year 2017-18 under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme (Account Head No.22159942). Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, the government was considering sanctioning and releasing grants to the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for the implementation of tap water supply schemes.
Decision: Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, administrative approval has been given to the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for the implementation of new tap water supply schemes worth Rs. 9088.95 lakhs (Rupees Ninety Crores Eighty Eight Lakhs Ninety Five Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released on Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions:
Conditions: 1) The Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Adhikaran, Mumbai shall ensure that the grant is spent only for the implementation of the new water supply schemes to be taken up under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.

The funds will be used in 2017-18. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
3) Funds disbursed to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should not be kept in personal provident account (PLA) or any other such account.
4) When the funds are sought under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program, the funds will not be sanctioned in the future without submitting information about the funds distributed in the current financial year, the expenses incurred, the balance funds, etc.
5) The physical progress of the works for which funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai every month along with the monthly expenditure report. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
6) The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made from this grant. The Accountant General, Maharashtra-1/ 2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the authorized officers of this Department shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for audit to ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the scheme of NRDWP and the terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated 7th May, 2016 are being fulfilled by the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Also, the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran will provide full support and facilities to the Accountant General to carry out his work properly.
7) The expenditure of the grant should be reconciled with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the report of the reconciliation should be submitted to the Office Papu-19/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai by the Maharashtra Life Authority on the prescribed dates.
Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-19 Office.
8) The provisions of the Finance Department, Government Circular No. Finance-2017/ P.S. No. 75/ Finance-3, dated 18.04.2017 should be strictly followed. Also, the necessary certificates should be submitted to this department.
