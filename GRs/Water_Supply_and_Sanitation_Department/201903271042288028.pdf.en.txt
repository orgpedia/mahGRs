Preamble: With a view to improve the rural water supply sector in the State and to provide clean and adequate drinking water to the rural masses, vide Reference No. The Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme is being implemented in the state for a period of four years from 2016-17 to 2019-20. Under the programme, Rs. Administrative approval has been given for revival of 28 Regional Water Supply Schemes at a cost of Rs. 9985.95 lakhs. The Finance Department had earmarked Rs. 1130.82 lakh has been provided by way of reallocation. The fund of Rs. 1074.82 lakh was under the consideration of the Government for sanction and release of funds to the Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for revival of defunct Regional Water Supply Schemes.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: MUPAYO-2018/ PR No.157/ PAPU-19
Government's decision:
Considering the facts mentioned in the Preamble, the administrative sanction under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme for revival of defunct regional tap water supply schemes amounting to Rs. A sum of Rs. 1074.82 lakhs (Rupees Ten Crores Seventy Four Lakhs Eighty Two Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released to the Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran through Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions.
This fund should be spent only for the implementation of regional tap water supply schemes approved to be revived under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2018-19. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
Funds disbursed to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should not be kept in personal provident fund (PLA) or other such accounts.
(iv) No funds will be sanctioned in future under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme unless information is furnished regarding the funds disbursed during the current financial year, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc.
v. The physical progress report for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai along with the monthly expenditure report every month. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure incurred from this grant. In this regard, vide No. 4 The Accountant General, Maharashtra- 1/2, to ensure that the guidelines set by the Government under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme are being fulfilled by the Maharashtra Life Authority.
