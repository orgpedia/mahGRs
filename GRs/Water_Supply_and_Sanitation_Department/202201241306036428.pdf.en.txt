Introduction: Mauje Borgaon and 2 villages (Tt. Administrative approval has been granted to the budget of Tap Water Supply Scheme at Gevrai, G.Beed) under Reference No. 1. As per Reference No. 2, the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Beed has given revised technical approval to the budget of the said scheme. Accordingly, the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Beed had submitted a proposal to the Government under Reference Letter No. 3 for administrative approval to the revised design and budget of the present tap water supply scheme. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the revised plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Mauje Borgaon and 2 villages (Ta. 140.93 lakhs (Dhobal) for tap water supply scheme at Gevrai, G.Beed (Rs. The revised budget of Rs.1 crore forty lakh ninety three thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval subject to the following conditions.
Conditions
All the remaining work should be completed in 3 months at the revised price mentioned above.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. Scheme by concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Beed

The Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad concerned shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation that the scheme has been actually completed.
As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
7. Action to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the present scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
The enhanced cost of Rs. 15.26 lakh (Rupees Fifteen Lakhs Twenty-Six Thousand only) in the revised cost of the scheme should be met from the sanctioned grant under the head Demand No.Y-02, 215 Water Supply and Sanitation, 102 Rural Water Supply Programme, National Rural Drinking Water Mission (Jal Jeevan Mission) (State Share) (2215 2071) and the final expenditure thereunder.
