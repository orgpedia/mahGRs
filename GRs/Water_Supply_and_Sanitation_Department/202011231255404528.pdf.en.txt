Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation, Government of India vide letter No. 11011/58/2020-JJM D. 01.10.2012 Preamble Jal Jeevan Mission has been launched from the year 2019. For implementation of Jal Jeevan Mission in the State, Reference No. Guidelines have been issued under letter No. 1. Under the Jal Jeevan Mission, the Government is committed to provide potable water through individual tap connections to all households, schools, anganwadis and other government institutions in rural areas by 2024. However, to provide tap water supply in all schools and anganwadi centres in the country, the Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation, Govt. The Prime Minister A 100-day special campaign has been announced from 02.10.2020.
Out of 93,675 Anganwadi Centres functioning under 449 Rural and Tribal Integrated Child Development Projects in the State, 52269 Anganwadi Centres are not getting tap water supply at present.
For safe health of children, pregnant women, lactating mothers, adolescent girls in Anganwadi centres, 24 hours quality drinking water should be available. Accordingly, the government is considering providing quality drinking water to all rural Anganwadi centres in the state through a special campaign launched under the Jal Jeevan Mission.

Government Decision Under Jal Jeevan Mission Reference No. The government has decided to provide quality drinking water through taps to the families in rural areas of the state. On the same lines, the following modalities are being elaborated to provide piped quality drinking water to every Anganwadi Centre in rural areas.
1. The Anganwadi Supervisor and Junior Engineer, Samagra Shiksha concerned shall personally visit the Anganwadi Centres which do not have piped water supply and prepare a detailed written requisition and budget for providing water supply.
(2) The Anganwadi Supervisor and the Junior Engineer, Samagra Shiksha concerned shall submit the proposal for administrative approval to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad as per the following instructions, after technical approval as mentioned in the accompanying table.
Proposals regarding borewells should be submitted to the Ground Water Survey and Development of the concerned district. They should provide the certificate of origin immediately. Technical approval should be given as mentioned in the accompanying chart.
(4) The proceedings pending the receipt of the aforesaid administrative approval shall be completed within 30 days from the date of issue of this Government Order.
In order to achieve the target of the 100 days campaign, time bound action should be taken as follows, taking into account the local conditions.
