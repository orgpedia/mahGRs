Preamble: Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme. According to the order of 1. Manikandan (Retd. Pathardi, G. Ahmednagar) Rs. Administrative approval has been granted for a budget of Rs. 81.92 lakh. However, due to a technical change, the price of the plan has increased by 14.99 percent. Therefore, the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Ahmednagar, issued the order vide No. 2 for Rs. A revised budget of Rs 94.20 lakh has been submitted to the government for administrative approval. The revised budget has been given technical approval by the executive engineer, rural water supply department, zilla parishad, Ahmednagar. According to m. Manikandan (Retd. Pathardi, G. Ahmednagar), the matter of giving administrative approval to the revised budget of the tap water supply scheme was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: In view of the facts mentioned in the foregoing, the Hon. Manikandan (Retd. Pathardi, G.
Ahmednagar), Rs. The revised administrative approval is being granted under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to the following conditions:- The budget is Rs. 94.20 lakh (Rupees Four-Nine Lakh Twenty Thousand only).

1) The scheme will be implemented through the District Council.
2) The terms and conditions of the original administrative approval order and the technical approval order of the scheme should be strictly followed.
3) Guidelines issued from time to time under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme should be strictly followed.
The CAG report in 2013 showed that 21% of the water supply schemes in the state were closed due to non-payment of electricity dues due to lack of maintenance. Therefore, the water tariff should be increased from time to time as per the requirement to keep the scheme financially viable.
5) After testing the scheme, the accounts should be closed by transferring the scheme to the concerned Gram Panchayat.
6) The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad will not get any assistance/ grant from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation of the scheme, its maintenance, repairs (subject to special amendments), depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
7) Action should be taken according to the prevailing policy on open defecation.
8) It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the scheme is not revised.
9) The remaining work of the project should be completed within the stipulated time.
2. The revised cost of the scheme is Rs. 12.28 lakh (Rupees Twelve Lakhs Eighty Eight Thousand only) from the State share under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
Revised Administrative Sanction to the said Scheme has been issued vide Finance Department Regulations, No. 30/ 2013/ 2013, Part-II, D. In the Government Resolution dated 17th April, 2015, in Part I, in sub-section (6) of sub-section (3), para. 27 (a) is being issued in accordance with the authority conferred on this Department.
