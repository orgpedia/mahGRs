For details: MoU signed on 18th January, 2018 with Israel Government Company (Mekorot) for carrying out survey and other administrative work for preparation of feasibility report of Water Grid Scheme to overcome the persistent water shortage in Marathwada region. The agreement was signed on February 21, 2018. As per the agreement, the companies will submit six reports for the grid in Marathwada and a preliminary concept report for each district.
Accordingly, as per the decision taken in the cabinet meeting held on February 24, 2019, the government has decided to first construct a grid for drinking water for Marathwada division and to plan, implement, operate and maintain the same through Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Permission was granted on 2.
Accordingly, Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran has issued a letter No. Under Section 4, the preliminary concept report prepared by the engineers of Maharashtra Life Authority for Latur and Osmanabad districts has been submitted for approval. Accordingly, Rs. 1712.91 crore and Rs. 1409.57 crore. The Cabinet in its meeting held on 09.09.2019 has approved the administrative approval and release of RFQ for an amount of Rs. 3122.18 crore. The proposal was under consideration of the government.

GOVERNMENT DECISION: In view of the facts stated in the proposal, the preliminary concept report prepared for Latur and Osmanabad districts under Marathwada Grid has been prepared at a cost of Rs. 1712.91 crore and Rs. 1409.57 crore. The administrative approval for the estimated amount of Rs. 3122.18 crore (Rupees Three Thousand One Hundred and Twenty Two Crore Eighteen Lakhs only) is being accorded subject to the following conditions.
1) The first qualifying application" RFQ" should be invited for the tender based on the Hybrid Annuity Model (HAM) for this work.
3) The draft should be approved by the government by including all the items to be covered under the" RFQ" (including financial burden and schedule of year-wise budgetary provision).
4) Final approval of the RFP and the final DPR. The approval of the High Powered Committee (HPC) headed by the Chief Secretary should be taken.
The final decision should be taken in consultation with the Finance Department after a complete financial analysis of the Hybrid Annuity Model (HAM) as well as other options.
6) Separate proposals on the total financial implication including the total burden of funds, external assistance loans and other sources of funds, maintenance repairs and electricity costs will be prepared in consultation with the Department of Finance. It will be presented before the Cabinet.
7) Separate comments should be submitted regarding the financial burden on the Tertiary Network.
