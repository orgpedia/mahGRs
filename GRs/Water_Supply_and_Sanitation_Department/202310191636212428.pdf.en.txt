Preamble: Under Jal Jeevan Mission, Reference No. 11 The Chief Executive Officer,
The Zilla Parishad, Chandrapur has decided to give ex-post facto approval to the acceptance of tenders for the 14 existing piped water supply schemes in Chandrapur district and accordingly to the design and implementation of the schemes.
The revised administrative sanction proposals have been submitted to the Government. The plan and budget of the said schemes in Chandrapur district is being prepared at the level of Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Chandrapur vide Reference No. The administrative approval has been granted vide order No. 03-10 and in view of the increase due to acceptance of tender of excess rates of the said schemes, the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Chandrapur has accorded revised technical approval to the said schemes.
Accordingly, it was under consideration of the government to give revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the existing 14 tap water supply schemes in Chandrapur district.
Government's decision:
Revised administrative approval is being accorded to the budget and design of the 14 existing tap water supply schemes mentioned in the table below in Chandrapur district subject to the following conditions.

Government Resolution E No: WSSD- 1304/1/2023/ WSD (WS13)// 659736 The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad is responsible to take over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation that the scheme has been actually completed.
As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
7. Action to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the present scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval to the scheme should be strictly followed.
10. It should be ensured at the Zilla Parishad level that the tender process has been carried out properly.
11.As administrative expenses are not admissible under Jal Jeevan Mission, if 4% of administrative expenses have been taken in the revised budget of the scheme, then it should be excluded.
