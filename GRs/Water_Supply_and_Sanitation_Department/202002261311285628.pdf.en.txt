Government's decision:
Considering the facts mentioned in the Preamble, 156 villages (t. 55 per litre), Rs. 704/- per month
Water supply scheme worth Rs. 376.91 lakhs (Rupees Three Crores Seventy Six Lakhs Ninety One Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
The scheme will be implemented by the Maharashtra Life Authority. It will be mandatory for the contractor to run the scheme for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme. The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as the maintenance of the scheme is carried out for the repair of reference No. 7 here. Follow the guidelines issued by the government on October 17, 2014.
After the implementation of the scheme, the scheme will be run for at least 1 year through the contractor, but the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
(iii) The works of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
The scheme should cover 100% household tap connections and meters. Also, at least 80% of the tap connection holders should take an undertaking for getting the meter connection at their own cost and a copy of it should be laminated and given to the tap connection holder and a copy should be kept with the Gram Panchayat. Thereafter, the orders were issued.
(v) If a villager refuses to take water even after the Gram Sabha resolution/ assurance letter is given, at least 50% of the water supply should be recovered from him. In this regard, the villagers should be given an idea before the tender process and the Gram Sabha should take a resolution in this regard.
The scheme should be included on the central government's IMIS system.
VII. In order to meet the expenditure incurred for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall charge the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it shall be increased from time to time.

Government Resolution No: Ragrape-2019/ P.S. 132/ Papu-13 VIII. 20% of the recovered water table should be kept for major maintenance repairs. The maintenance repair of the pipes of the scheme should be done every 5 years and maintenance repair of other pipes should be done every 3 years.
No assistance/ grant will be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
X. All villages under the scheme shall be open defecation free but shall ensure that they remain open defecation free at all times.
XI.If there is an increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
XII. The conditions in the order of technical approval of the said scheme should be fulfilled. Also referred to as No. 2 The provisions of the government decision here should be strictly followed.
XIII. Referred No. 10 here. As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 15th June, 2015, it will be mandatory to conduct a third stage technical examination of the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat shall be bound to determine the water table required for the scheme to be self-sustaining and to increase it as necessary. In this regard, the officials of the zilla parishad should give detailed information to the villagers in the gram sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
XV. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency shall be obliged to submit the certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
XVI. In case of any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice noticed in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity shall be liable for disciplinary action.
XVII. It shall be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme.
XVIII. As the scheme progresses, the Water and Sanitation Support Organisation (WASO), Belapur will be responsible for obtaining utilisation certificates of the expenditure and proof of completion of the scheme from the concerned implementing agencies and submitting the same to the Government.
