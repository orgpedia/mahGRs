Government's decision:
As per the norms laid down by the Government for the alleviation of drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas, the Divisional Commissioner, Konkan is being sanctioned and released funds to the tune of Rs. 32.14 lakhs (Rupees Thirty Two Lakhs Fourteen Thousand only) under the head of Accounts 22450093 for the measures taken/ to be taken for the alleviation of drinking water scarcity in his/ her area through the Budget Distribution System subject to the terms and conditions laid down in the prevailing Standing Government Resolution and the following conditions.

Terms and Conditions
1) The Divisional Commissioner, Konkan, shall disburse the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad for taking measures under the scarcity in rural areas.
2) Urban measures which are being implemented through Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils.
For this, the Divisional Commissioner should distribute it to the concerned District Collector. The Collector should disburse the funds to the concerned Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council.
The funds should be released for the measures taken to mitigate drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the period 2014-15 and for payment of pending dues during the period. This money cannot be used for other projects.
4) The Divisional Commissioner should distribute the funds on priority basis to the measures like well acquisition, tanker water supply and even if the proposal submitted by the Divisional Commissioner does not ask for the measures approved by the government, these measures should also be distributed on priority basis as per the order of the concerned administrative approval.

The Divisional Commissioner shall submit to the Government, every month, along with the monthly expenditure report, information on the physical progress of the district for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the certificate with quarterly verification should be submitted to this department immediately.
Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances. If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2020-21. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
14) If the funds being distributed under the said Government Decision are not expendable, the Government shall be reimbursed through the Budget Distribution System. Return to November 2020.
Though the funds are being released as per the demand received from the field offices, the same amount of funds required for the period of shortage, 2014-15, should be drawn through the Budget Distribution System. Proposals should be submitted to the competent authority for disciplinary action against the concerned if additional funds are drawn and remain unutilized. The Water Supply and Sanitation Department should be informed about this.
15) Provide action plan-wise, scarcity period-wise information regarding the allocation of the funds.
16) The expenditure in this regard is as mentioned in the statement regarding the distribution of funds in this Government Decision" Demand No. C-6, 2245- Assistance for relief of natural calamities, 01- Avarakshana, 102 Drinking water supply, (91)- Expenditure as per the norms of State Disaster Response Fund, (91) (01) Emergency supply of drinking water (2245 0093) (Dattamat) (Post Pay) 31, Subsidiary Grants.
17) The Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall not fail to mention the computer code of the subordinate/ sub-heading in the computer slip to be sent to the Treasury/ Deputy Treasury Officer and attached with the payment. Also, a report of the amount withdrawn from the treasury/ sub-treasury should be submitted to the government.
