The Proposal:
The project is being implemented by the Central Government with the financial assistance of the World Bank and under this project, various development works have been taken up in the state through the Ground Water Survey and Development Mechanism in the case of groundwater. The project has been expanded to the national level and is now being implemented as the National Hydrology Project. The funds received under the National Hydrology Project are 100% grant in aid and the Central Government had sanctioned component wise funds to the tune of Rs. 36.00 crore for the State of Maharashtra.
The Central Government vide Reference No. 4 has prepared a Project Action Plan for the entire country under the National Hydrology Project with an outlay of Rs. 3640 crore to Rs. Rs 2000 crore limit

The revised administrative approval granted for the above matters is subject to the following terms and conditions.
(i) The revised administrative approval to the National Hydrology Project is being granted within the limit of funds available from the Central Government. Also, work orders under the project should be issued only after the funds are available.
2) The National Hydrology Project should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the directions given from time to time.
(3) The work of the National Hydrology Project should be completed within the stipulated time and within the limits of the provisions of the revised administrative approval.
4) New elements that create financial liabilities should not be included in the project without government approval.
The Director, Ground Water Survey and Development System, Maharashtra State, Pune will be responsible for ensuring quality and excellence in the implementation of the project works as per the revised administrative approval.
6) No additional expenditure beyond the limit of revised administrative approval granted for any purpose for the said scheme.
7) Requirement/ demand of material under the project should be ascertained/ verified by the Directorate and only then the necessary procedure for procurement of these items should be carried out.
8) It should be ensured that the specifications of these materials are as per requirement and up to date.
9) The technical specifications of the items to be purchased should be in accordance with the specifications agreed by the experts. Also, it should be ensured that the technical specifications are not in such a way that it will benefit any particular company and only then should the purchase be made.

10) It should be ensured that the items to be purchased are purchased only with proper and correct specifications by conducting pre/ post-dispatch random sample inspection before and after supply.
After the supply of the items to be purchased, the necessary agreement will be made with the supplier manufacturers regarding storage and distribution, supply chain management and repair and maintenance of the items, and it should be ensured that these items do not remain unused and are used within the stipulated time by proper management.
The expenditure on procurement and other aspects under the project should be met from the grants sanctioned under the National Hydrology Project.
13) It should be ensured that there is no duplication of procurement of material under the project.
The guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) should be strictly followed while carrying out the entire procurement process of the items under the project.
15) Necessary maintenance repair agreement should be made with the supplier for maintenance repair of the purchased material.
Utilization certificate for the utilization of funds provided under this project should be submitted to the Government by the Director, Ground Water Survey and Development System, Maharashtra State, Pune.
17) The certificate of completion of the project should be provided by the Director, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency, Pune to the Central Government as well as the State Government immediately.
This Government decision is being issued in accordance with the consensus arrived at under Informal Reference No. 67/ 1443 of the Planning Department dated 25/09/2020 and Informal Reference No. 233/ Expenditure-3 of the Finance Department dated 19/10/2020.
