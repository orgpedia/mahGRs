Administrative approval is being accorded to the project under Jal Jeevan Mission Programme subject to fulfilment of following terms and conditions.
1) The implementation of this scheme should be done through the concerned Maharashtra Life Authority, Regional Department. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Summit Committee/ District Council to take over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, that the scheme has been actually completed. Once the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran/ Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The contractor will be obliged to run the scheme for at least 1 year. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, and shall follow the guidelines in the Government Decision on the maintenance of the scheme.
3) Even if the scheme will be run for at least one year through the contractor after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 4 th September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 10% of the cost of the scheme will be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayat as public contribution for the maintenance of the water supply scheme. The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
5) Every household should be provided with a tap connection under this scheme. In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
7) Land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme should be obtained within the stipulated time.
8) To ensure that the scheme is not used seasonally, the Gram Panchayat should ensure that the existing scheme in the village included in the scheme is not operated.

9) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time. Also, it will be mandatory for the gram panchayat to determine the required water table and increase it as per the need to make the scheme self-reliant.
In this regard, the officials of the Maharashtra Life Authority should give detailed information to the villagers in the gram sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
10) 20% of the recovered water table should be kept with the implementing agency for major maintenance repairs. Maintenance repair of pipes and other pipes should be done every 5 years and every 3 years.
No assistance/ grant will be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
12) It should be ensured that the villages under this scheme are open defecation free but they will always remain open defecation free.
13) If the cost of the scheme increases for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
14) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled. Also referred to as No. The provisions of the Government Decision 1 to 8 should be strictly followed.
15) The third technical examination of the scheme will be mandatory as per the government decision issued from time to time regarding the third technical examination of the scheme.
16) It will be mandatory for the Gram Panchayat to determine the necessary water table and increase it as per the need for the scheme to be self-sufficient. In this regard, the officials of the Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Life Authority should clearly give detailed information to the villagers in the Gram Sabha.
17) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
18) In case of any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity shall be liable for disciplinary action.
