The Proposal:
To prevent sexual harassment of women in the workplace. The Supreme Court has made the guidelines in the Vishakha judgment binding on the government. As per the guidelines in the Vishakha Judgment, instructions for constitution of the revised Women Grievance Redressal Committees have been issued vide Government Order dated 19.06.2014 in No.3 of the aforesaid chapter of the Department of Women and Child Development.
In pursuance of the Government decision dated 18th May, 2018 of this Department, the Women Grievance Redressal Committee for Water Supply and Sanitation Department (itself) has been reconstituted. However, since some of the officers in the committee have retired from government service and some have been transferred to other departments, the matter of reconstitution of the committee was under the consideration of the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In accordance with the guidelines issued by the Supreme Court to prevent sexual harassment of women employees in the government service and as per the provisions of the Government Decision dated 19th June, 2014 in the above mentioned No. 3 of the Department of Women and Child Development, the Committee constituted under the Government Decision dated 18th May, 2018 at Reference No. 4 to redress the grievances of women employees of the Department is being reconstituted as follows.

Women employees working in this department can submit their complaints to the chairman or member secretary of the above committee. Women employees of the department are informed through this government decision that they should immediately submit any complaint regarding sexual harassment to the Women's Grievance Redressal Committee.
The committee will have the following functions:
(i) To take necessary measures to prevent sexual harassment of women and to provide a safe working environment, as per the guidelines of the Supreme Court.
2) Complaining about sexual harassment.
3) If the complaint is found to be true, it should be investigated and disciplinary action should be taken against the concerned.
4) Review of disciplinary action.
5) Submit a report on the action taken in this regard to the State Women's Grievance Redressal Committee.
