As per the letter dated 30.01.2023 from the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri, in the Preamble Reference No. 05, 4 water supply schemes in Ratnagiri district have been notified for grant of fresh administrative approval by cancelling the administrative approval granted earlier due to technical changes in the budget. These schemes were earlier given administrative approval at the level of Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri. However, the administrative approval of the said 04 schemes has been cancelled by the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri vide letter No. 06 under reference.
As per the proposal submitted under Reference No. 06, the proposal for sanction of separate tap water supply schemes is received for approval of the Government as the per capita cost of the said 04 water supply schemes in Ratnagiri district is higher than the prescribed norms. These standalone water supply schemes are included in the Annual Action Plan 2021-22 of Jal Jeevan Mission. Also, the District Water and Sanitation Mission has directed to submit these schemes for the prior approval of the government as the per capita cost of the scheme is higher than the government norms. These schemes have been approved by the Technical Scrutiny Committee at the specified level and technical approval has been provided by the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri. Since the per capita cost of these schemes is more than the norm, the Minister has approved the proposal of all the 04 separate water supply schemes mentioned in Annexure-I. This has been approved by the High Powered Committee (HPC) under the Chairmanship of Secretary (Water Supply and Sanitation) in its meeting dated 09.02.2023. According to the Chief Executive

The administrative approval of 4 separate water supply schemes in Ratnagiri district, which were cancelled at the level of officials, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri, was under the consideration of the government to give administrative approval under the Jal Jeevan Mission program.
Government's decision
In view of the facts stated in the Preamble, as per the approval of the High Powered Committee, administrative approval is being accorded to the budgetary cost and per capita expenditure of 04 separate tap water supply schemes mentioned in Annexure-I in Ratnagiri district under Jal Jeevan Mission programme subject to fulfilment of following terms and conditions.
1) The scheme should be implemented through the District Council Ratnagiri. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned Gram Panchayat will be responsible to take over the scheme within 1 month from the date of receipt of the notification from the concerned Executive Engineer that the scheme has been actually completed. On the actual completion of the scheme, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials shall conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
2) It will be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the Jal Jeevan Mission program. Every house should have a tap connection. In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
3) The contractor will be obliged to run the scheme for at least 1 year. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed.
4) After the implementation of the scheme, the scheme will be run for at least 1 year through the contractor, but the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 4 th September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 5/10 th of the cost of the scheme shall be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayat for maintenance of the water supply scheme.
The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
7) Land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme should be obtained within the stipulated time.
8) To ensure that the scheme is not used seasonally, the Gram Panchayat should ensure that the existing scheme in the village included in the scheme is not operated.
9) Domestic, non-domestic and institutional as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme by the concerned Gram Panchayat to meet the expenses incurred for running and maintenance repair of the scheme.

The rate of water tap should be fixed for the tap connection holder and it should be increased from time to time. Also, it will be mandatory for the gram panchayat to determine the required water table and increase it as per the need to make the scheme self-reliant. In this regard, the Zilla Parishad officials should clearly inform the villagers in the Gram Sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
10) 20% of the recovered water table should be kept with the implementing agency for major maintenance repairs. Maintenance repair of pipes and other pipes should be done every 5 years and every 3 years.
No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
12) It should be ensured that the villages under this scheme are open defecation free but they will always remain open defecation free.
13) If the cost of the scheme increases for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
14) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled.
15) The third technical examination of the scheme will be mandatory as per the government decision issued from time to time regarding the third technical examination of the scheme.
16) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
17) If any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice is found in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity will be liable for disciplinary action.
18) As per the progress of the scheme, the State Water and Sanitation Mission, Belapur will be responsible for obtaining utilization certificates and proof of completion of the scheme from the concerned implementing agencies and submitting them to the government.
19) Government Circular No. 17 of the Department regarding the Functionality of Household Tap Connections under Jal Jeevan Mission. JJM 2020/ Q.No.115/ Papu 10, Dy. Appropriate action should be taken as on 20.10.2021.
20) The responsibility of ensuring that the issues raised by the Technical Scrutiny Sub-Committee are redressed will be of the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad.
21) The working guidelines of the Central Government of the Jal Jeevan Mission while implementing the water supply scheme, the Government of India. The Executive Engineer concerned will be responsible for ensuring that the guidelines of the Government Decision dated 04.09.2022 as well as the Government Decisions/ Circulars/ Instructions issued by the Government from time to time in pursuance of the said programme are followed.
22) The Executive Engineer concerned will be responsible for obtaining the resolution of the Gram Panchayat regarding the necessary space for the implementation of the scheme, the transfer of the public distribution scheme and the Gram Panchayat resolution regarding the maintenance and repair of the scheme, as well as for completing the scheme within the mission period.
