On the proposal received from the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati, which includes a temporary water supply scheme for the tap water supply scheme of the Chikhli Municipal Council to supply water to Chikhli town, nearby Gokulnagar, the DC said that the proposal has been submitted to the Municipal Commissioner. In the Standing Committee meeting held on 09.05.2019, after detailed discussion, the Standing Committee approved Rs. The proposal was approved at a cost of Rs 13.51 lakh. Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.
The government has decided to provide an emergency water supply scheme for the Gokulnagar settlement near Chikhli town, district Buldana, at a total cost of Rs. 13.51 lakh (Rs. It was approved in the Standing Committee meeting held on 9th May, 2019.
Accordingly, the proposal is being accorded administrative approval subject to the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Buldana should first declare that there is a shortage of drinking water in Chikhli town.
(2) The scheme shall be implemented through the Municipal Council, Chikhli.
3) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
Bids at higher prices will not be accepted.
4) Ensure that the project is completed within the approved cost.
The government will not be responsible if the cost of the scheme increases.
The tender process must be completed by May 29, 2019. Approval of the scheme will be cancelled if the work order is not issued by this date.
6) The water supply from the scheme should be started by completing the work of the scheme within the period of June 15, 2019. However, if the water supply is not resumed by this date, the approval of the scheme will be considered canceled.
The government will provide a subsidy of Rs. 12.16 lakh i.e. 90% of the total cost of Rs. 13.51 lakh. The remaining amount i.e. 10% of the amount of the scheme is to be paid by the Municipal Corporation, Chikhli as a contribution of Rs. 1.35 lakh.
