In his letter dated 02.01.2020, the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has submitted a proposal to the Government for administrative approval for a special repair of tap water supply scheme at a cost of Rs. 163.49 lakh under the Regional Rural Water Supply Scheme for 40 villages of Malpathar in Pusad Taluka of Yavatmal district under the scarcity measures for the period 2019-20.
A Standing Committee under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department has been constituted to give administrative approval to temporary water supply schemes/ measures outside the prescribed financial and physical parameters in urban and rural areas to mitigate drinking water scarcity.

The Committee in its meeting held on 26.02.2020 discussed the proposal in detail and approved the proposal included in the budget for replacement of old and outdated pumping machinery. Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.
In view of the facts stated in the preamble of the Government Resolution, the proposal of Rs. 163.49 lakh (Rs.1 crore sixty three lakh fifty nine thousand only) involving the works proposed under the proposal of special amendment of the tap water supply scheme under the Regional Rural Water Supply Scheme for 40 villages of Malpathar in Pusad Taluka of Yavatmal district is being given administrative approval under scarcity subject to the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Yavatmal should first declare that there is a shortage of water in the respective villages of Pusad taluka.
2) All special repair works mentioned in the proposal should be carried out by the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, Yavatmal.
3) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Ensure that the work is completed within the approved cost.
The tender process for the scheme should be completed by the end of March 2020.
It is necessary to start water supply from the scheme by completing the works of the scheme by 30th April, 2020.
6) The month. If the scheme is not completed by April 30, 2020, the administrative approval of the scheme will be deemed to be cancelled.
7) Steps should be taken to provide individual tap connections to 100% households under the said scheme. The scheme is being approved subject to fulfilment of the above conditions. The gram panchayat, zilla parishad and panchayat samitis should take action in this regard.
8) As soon as the above works are completed, the proposal for the special amendment of the present tap scheme under the transfer of the scheme to the District Council is being approved.
9) After the tender process for this scheme, 50% of the fund should be given at the beginning of the scheme. The remaining funds will be released after the completion of the project. The request for funds should be submitted to the government through the Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Belapur CBD, Navi Mumbai.
