Preamble: The Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, a rural water supply programme of the State Government, is being implemented across the State over a period of four years from 2016-17 to 2019-20. A sum of Rs. 227.13 crore has been provided for the financial year 2017-18 under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme (Account Head No.22159942). Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, the government was considering sanctioning and releasing grants to the respective district councils for the implementation of tap water supply schemes.
Government Decision: Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, for the implementation of the tap water supply schemes that have been given administrative approval, the concerned District Councils will be given Rs. A sum of Rs. 705.72 lakhs (Rupees Seven Crore Five Lakh Seventy Two Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released on Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions:

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. MUPPEYO-2017/ PR. No.13/ PAPU-19 (SIKHANA) 1) The District Councils should spend this grant only for the implementation of the tap water supply schemes approved under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
The funds will be used in 2017-18. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
3) The funds disbursed to the Zila Parishads should not be kept in Personal Accounts (PLA) or such other accounts.
4) When the funds are sought under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program, the funds will not be sanctioned in the future without submitting information about the funds distributed in the current financial year, the expenses incurred, the balance funds, etc.
5) The physical progress of the works for which funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai every month along with the monthly expenditure report. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
(6) The District Councils shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made out of this grant. The Accountant General, Maharashtra 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the officers authorized in this Department shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned implementing agencies for audit to ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme and the terms and conditions of this Government Decision dated 7th May, 2016 are fulfilled by the Zilla Parishads. Also, the concerned enforcement agencies will provide full support and facilitation to the Accountants General to enable them to perform their functions properly.
7) The expenditure of the grant should be reconciled with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the reconciliation report should be submitted to the Office of the concerned implementing agencies Papu 19/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai on the prescribed dates.
Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-19 Office.
8) The provisions of the Finance Department, Government Circular No. Finance-2017/ P.S. No. 75/ Finance-3, dated 18.04.2017 should be strictly followed. Also, the necessary certificates should be submitted to this department.
