Preamble: With a view to expedite the work of solving the drinking water problem in the State, a separate Department of" Water Supply and Sanitation" has been set up. As per the Government Decision of Reference No. 4 and 5, this Department has approved to take necessary measures to be taken in case of scarcity of drinking water in rural and urban areas in the State due to insufficient rainfall or other reasons. In addition to the National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF)/ State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) norms, the budget of the Department of Water Supply and Sanitation is required to make provisions for meeting the expenditure incurred for the purpose of mitigation of drinking water scarcity arising out of the water crisis in accordance with the opinion of the Department of Revenue and Forest (Relief and Rehabilitation) and Finance. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the government to give administrative approval to the measures to be taken to alleviate the shortage according to the potential water shortage action plan.
As per Section 25 of Section 4 of the Maharashtra Ground Water (Development and Management) Act, 2009, the district administration has the power to declare any area where there is a possibility of drinking water scarcity as a water scarcity zone at any time of the year (even during the monsoon).
In view of the above provisions of the Maharashtra Ground Water (Development and Management) Act, 2009, the District Collector of the place where the water scarcity area is declared shall first take preventive action in accordance with the provisions of the said Act with a view to preventing possible adverse effects on the water of public origin.
The District Collectors are empowered to take the following measures to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in the villages/ hamlets where the availability of potable water per day is less than 20 litres per capita and in urban areas where the per capita water supply is less than 50 per cent or 50 litres per capita per day, whichever is less.
1) Drain the water/ drain the water.
2) Acquisition of private wells.
3) Supply of water through tankers/ bullock carts.
4) To complete the works of water supply schemes in progress immediately.
5) Special repairs of the plumbing schemes.
6) Special inspection of the drilling wells.
