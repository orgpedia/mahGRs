In his letter dated 24.04.2018, the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has submitted a proposal to the Government for obtaining administrative approval for the special renovation of the tap water supply scheme of the Chikhaldara Municipal Council at a cost of Rs. 62.21 lakh under the scarcity measures for the scarcity period of 2017-18.
The Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department to give administrative approval to the urgent water supply schemes/ measures beyond the prescribed financial and physical parameters in urban and rural areas to overcome the drinking water shortage, in its meeting held on 03.05.2018, discussed the proposal in detail and approved the proposal for construction of 150 m.m. of water supply schemes in the budget.
The proposal includes laying of diameter DIK-9, 2700 m long pipeline from Bir Lake to Kalapani Lake, supply and installation of 1 submarine pump of 25 horsepower with 159 m head and electrical panel box and other works accordingly.

Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.
Considering the facts stated in the Preamble of the Government Resolution, the proposal for special repair of the tap schemes of the Chikhaldara Water Supply Scheme for the period of scarcity in 2017-18 at a cost of Rs. 62.21 lakh (Rupees Sixty Two Lakhs, Twenty One Thousand only) under the scarcity measures scheme is being accorded administrative approval under this Order subject to the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Amravati should first declare that there is a shortage of drinking water in Chikhaldara city.
The government will provide a subsidy of Rs. 59.10 lakh i.e. 95% of the total cost of Rs. 62.21 lakh.
The remaining amount i. e. 5% of the scheme amounting to Rs. 3.11 lakh is to be paid by the Municipal Council, Chikhaldara as public contribution.
(3) No work order shall be issued in the scheme unless 5% of the population of the said scheme is paid by the Municipal Council to the Maharashtra Life Authority.
4) The implementation of this scheme should be done by the Maharashtra Life Authority.
5) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner and it should be ensured that the work of the scheme is completed within the approved price.
The tender of the scheme is being allowed to be taken out for a short period (7 days).
The tender process must be completed by May 20, 2018. If the tender process is not completed by this date, the approval of the scheme will be considered canceled.
8) The plan. The water supply from the scheme is required to be completed by June 6, 2018.
9) Divisional Commissioner, Amravati should provide funds to the extent of 50% at the beginning of the scheme to the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority Board, Amravati. The remaining 50% of the funds will be released after the implementation of the scheme.
10) Since the work of the scheme must be completed on time, a progress report should be sent to the government every Monday of the week from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
