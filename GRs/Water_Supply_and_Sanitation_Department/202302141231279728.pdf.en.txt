Preamble As the per capita cost of water supply schemes in Ratnagiri district is higher than the prescribed norms, the proposal for sanction of separate tap water supply schemes has been submitted by the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri under reference number 5 and 6 for the approval of the Government. These standalone water supply schemes are included in the Annual Action Plan 2021-22 of Jal Jeevan Mission. Also, the District Water and Sanitation Mission has directed to submit these schemes for the prior approval of the government as the per capita cost of the scheme is higher than the government norms. These schemes have been approved by the Technical Scrutiny Committee at the specified level and technical approval has been provided by the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Ratnagiri. Since the per capita cost of these schemes is more than the norm, the proposal for all the 02 separate water supply schemes mentioned in Annexure-I of the said schemes has been approved. This has been approved by the High Powered Committee chaired by the Minister (Water Supply and Sanitation) in its meeting dated 28.12.2022 and 18.01.2023. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the budget of these 2 separate water supply schemes in Ratnagiri district under the Jal Jeevan Mission program was under consideration of the government.

The expenditure is being administratively approved under the Jal Jeevan Mission programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
1) The scheme should be implemented through the District Council Ratnagiri. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned Gram Panchayat will be responsible to take over the scheme within 1 month from the date of receipt of the notification from the concerned Executive Engineer that the scheme has been actually completed. On the actual completion of the scheme, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials shall conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
2) It will be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the Jal Jeevan Mission program. Every house should have a tap connection. In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
3) The contractor will be obliged to run the scheme for at least 1 year. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed.
4) After the implementation of the scheme, the scheme will be run for at least 1 year through the contractor, but the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 4 th September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 5/10 th of the cost of the scheme shall be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayat for maintenance of the water supply scheme. The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
7) It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Executive Engineer to ensure that the schemes become financially self-sustaining by making necessary increase in the water tariff of the schemes.
8) Land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme should be obtained within the prescribed time.
9) To ensure that the scheme is not used seasonally, the Gram Panchayat should ensure that the existing scheme in the village included in the scheme is not operated.
10) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat should fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time. Also, it will be mandatory for the gram panchayat to determine the required water table and increase it as per the need to make the scheme self-reliant. In this regard, the Zilla Parishad officials should clearly inform the villagers in the Gram Sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
11) 20% of the recovered water table should be kept with the implementing agency for major maintenance repairs. Maintenance repair of pipes and other pipes should be done every 5 years and every 3 years.

No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred annually for operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
13) It should be ensured that the villages under this scheme are open defecation free but they will always remain open defecation free.
14) If the cost of the scheme increases for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
15) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled.
16) It will be mandatory to conduct the third technical examination of the scheme as per the government decision issued from time to time regarding the third technical examination of the scheme.
17) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
18) In case of any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity shall be liable for disciplinary action.
As per the progress of the scheme, the responsibility of obtaining utilization certificates of expenditure and proof of completion of the scheme from the concerned implementing agencies and submitting them to the government will be with the State Water and Sanitation Mission, Belapur.
