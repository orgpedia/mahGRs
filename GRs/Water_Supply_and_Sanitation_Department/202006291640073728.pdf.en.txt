The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Civil Services Rules have been made applicable to the officers/ employees of Maharashtra Life Authority vide Government letter, Urban Development Department, dated 21/01/1984.
Further, as per the Government Decision, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, dated 23/03/2017, the Maharashtra Civil Service Rules and Government Decisions issued by the Government from time to time have been made applicable to the officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran.
All Saturdays and Sundays have been declared as holidays for State Government offices on the lines of Central Government by implementing a five-day week as per the Government Order dated 24/02/2020.
The Maharashtra Life Authority vide No. 2 vide letter dated 27/02/2020, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran has requested to implement a five-day week for all offices of the Authority, except for regular establishments, converted permanent/ temporary establishments and employees of daily wage establishments.

Government Circular No: Majipra-1120/ PR No.66/ Papu-23
Government circular:
Considering the facts stated in the Preamble, in terms of Maharashtra Life Authority No. 2 In response to the request in the letter, the Government hereby order as follows:
The five-day week is being implemented for all offices other than the offices/ employees mentioned in Article 5 of the Maharashtra Life Authority from July 4, 2020. Hence, all Saturdays and Sundays will be holidays from July 4, 2020 onwards.
The working hours of all offices of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran will be increased by 45 minutes from 09.45 am to 06.15 pm from Monday to Friday.
The working hours for the constables in the office of Maharashtra Life Authority will be from 09.30 am to 06.30 pm from Monday to Friday.
4) The above office hours shall include a maximum of half an hour lunch break between 01.00 pm and 02.00 pm.
The five-day week order will not be applicable to regular establishments, converted permanent/ temporary establishments, working establishments, daily wage establishments, safai karamcharis, etc. at water supply stations in Maharashtra Life Authority, whose services are considered essential.
The circular will come into effect from July 4, 2020.
