The expenditure on water scarcity measures in rural and urban areas of the State is met from the grants available under the head" Assistance for Natural Disaster Mitigation" (2245). Reference No. 18 for measures taken/ to be taken during the period of shortage in 2017-18 and 2018-19 The administrative approval was given to the measures under the shortage under the government decision 3-7. For the financial year 2018-19, the Department of Revenue and Forest (Relief and Rehabilitation) has transferred the funds to this Department as per the Government Resolution No. 9. The funds were to be distributed to the Divisional Commissioners of Nashik, Aurangabad and Amravati and the Member Secretary of Maharashtra Life Authority, Mumbai.
Government's decision:
An amount of Rs. 1378.89 lakhs (Rupees Thirteen Crores Eighty Eight Lakhs Ninety Nine Thousand only) under the head of accounts 22450093 is being sanctioned and released to the Divisional Commissioners, Nashik, Aurangabad and Amravati and Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Mumbai through the Budget Distribution System subject to the terms and conditions as provided in the extant Standing Government Resolution and the following conditions:
The Divisional Commissioner, Nashik, Aurangabad and Amravati shall disburse the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad and to the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council for the schemes in urban areas.
(2) The Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Adhikaran shall disburse the funds to the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Adhikaran for the measures to be taken by the Maharashtra Jeevan Adhikaran under the drinking water scarcity.

The funds will be released for the implementation of the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the period 2017-18 and 2018-19 and for the payment of pending dues during this period.
For rural and urban schemes which have been approved from the government level under scarcity, 50 percent of the funds at the beginning of the scheme and 30 percent after that as mentioned in the decision of the government should be distributed after reviewing the progress of the scheme at the level of the Divisional Commissioner, while the remaining 20 percent should be given only after the scheme is implemented.
5) Make sure that the funds are not kept in a personal ledger account or bank account and are distributed directly to the contractors through electronic clearing services.
The District Collectors who have not received the remedial certificates from the District Collectors for the year 2016-17 and 2017-18 for the purpose of providing drinking water to rural/ urban areas, shall disburse the funds for rural remedial measures to the concerned CEOs, ZPs and the Commissioners, Municipal Corporations/ Chief Executive Officers, Municipal Councils for the urban areas only after verifying that they have received the remedial certificates for the entire expenditure (100%) of the funds for the years 2016-17 and 2017-18. Further, the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Mumbai has not submitted the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed under the scarcity measures in 2017-18 to the Government.
This money should not be wasted. Further, all Divisional Commissioners and Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Mumbai shall submit the utilization certificate of the funds received from the Government in the financial year 2018-19 before the said Government decision to the Government before 10th March, 2019.
7) Government Circular, Department of Finance. The districts which have fulfilled the conditions as on 18.04.2017 and 30.04.2017. The funds should be distributed to the districts.
8) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed for the related measures earlier.
9) When the funds are sought for the measures to be taken to overcome the shortage, what are the proposed measures to be taken for the shortage, for what period, how much will be the funds?

In this regard, subject-wise demand, information about the funds disbursed in the current financial year, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc., as well as the utility certificate should be submitted. Besides, it should be noted that the funds will be approved in the future.
10) The Divisional Commissioner shall submit to the Government, every month, along with the monthly expenditure report, information on the physical progress of the district for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed.
The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the relevant certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances. If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time.
The funds are to be spent in 2018-19. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
12) The expenditure in this regard will be borne by the Government as mentioned in the Statement of Disbursement of Funds in this Government Resolution. C-6, 2245- Assistance for relief of natural calamities, 01- Avarakshana, 102- Drinking water supply, (91)- Expenditure as per the State Disaster Response Fund norms, (91) (01) Emergency supply of drinking water (2245 0093) (Dattamat) (Post Pay) 31, Subsidiary Grants.
13) The Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall not fail to mention the computer code of the subordinate/ sub-heading in the computer slip to be sent to the Treasury/ Deputy Treasury Officer and attached with the payment. Also, a report of the amount withdrawn from the treasury/ sub-treasury should be submitted to the government.
