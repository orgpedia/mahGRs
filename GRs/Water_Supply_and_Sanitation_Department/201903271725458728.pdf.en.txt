The proposal-
In view of the drought situation in the State, it has been decided to disburse the ongoing electricity dues of rural as well as urban areas for the period of 8 months from November, 2018 to June, 2019 in the areas declared as drought areas from the funds received under scarcity. Accordingly, the Revenue and Forest departments have released funds to the department to meet the expenditure on these measures. The government was planning to distribute the funds to the districts.
GOVERNMENT DECISION-To meet the pending electricity bills of tap water supply schemes in the areas declared drought by the Government and the ongoing electricity bills of rural and urban areas for the period from November, 2018 to February, 2019, a sum of Rs. 11748.94 lakh (Rupees One Hundred Seventeen Crore Forty Eight Lakh Ninety Four Thousand Only) has been provided as per the attached form.

It is being released to the concerned Divisional Commissioners for distribution subject to the following conditions.
All CEOs should spend this fund immediately for payment of ongoing electricity dues for the period November, 2018 to February, 2019 for all rural and urban water supply schemes in the areas declared drought.
2) Despite the fact that orders have been issued from time to time to ensure that the funds available on the budget distribution system are not unnecessarily drawn from the treasury and lying in the bank in the absence of allocation, strict instructions are again being given under this government decision not to withdraw the amount from the treasury and keep it in the bank. If such a thing is found, it should be noted that it will be considered a temporary violation and disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned. All the Zila Parishads are being instructed to ensure that the funds are required to be spent in the current financial year itself and do not lapse or are not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
(3) The Divisional Commissioner shall, at the time of appropriation of the funds entrusted to him, ensure that the funds for the purpose for which they have been transferred are being utilised for the same purpose and that no funds have been released earlier for the purpose for which the funds were allocated. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office which draws and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the relevant certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time.
4) Expenditure in respect of" Demand No. C-6, 2245- Assistance for relief of natural calamities, 01 depreciation, 102- Supply of drinking water, (91)- Expenditure as per the standard of State Disaster Response Fund, (92) (01) Emergency supply of drinking water (2245, 2185) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried).
