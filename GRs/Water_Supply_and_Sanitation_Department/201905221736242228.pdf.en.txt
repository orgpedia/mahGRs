The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has submitted a proposal to the Government for administrative approval for temporary water supply scheme from the water treatment plant at Mauje Shemba Bu to the wells of the 10 village tap schemes located in Kandari Dam in Nandura Taluka for the water shortage period of 2018-19. The proposal is for grant of administrative approval to temporary water supply schemes/ measures beyond prescribed financial and physical parameters in urban and rural areas to mitigate drinking water scarcity.

2) The supplementary water supply scheme mentioned in the proposal should be implemented by the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Buldana.

4) The Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Buldana should check the capacity of the proposed 10 villages to be provided water from the WTP of Mauje Shemba Gram Panchayat, as well as the overhead lines and pumps etc.
5) The District Council must take responsibility for running the scheme of the present 10 villages during the scarcity period and after that. A decision in this regard will be taken by the district magistrate.
(6) A resolution of the Gram Panchayat concerned shall be obtained to recover the additional expenditure for the operation of the scheme from the Gram Panchayats of the villages covered under the scheme.
7) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
8) Ensure that the work is completed within the approved cost.
The tender process must be completed by June 6, 2019.
10) If the tender process is not completed by June 6, the approval of the scheme will be considered canceled.
11) No-objection resolution from the Shemba Gram Panchayat to lift water from their water purification center, the capacity of the Shemba Gram Panchayat's scheme to supply water to these 10 villages should be examined. Also, the resolution of every Gram Panchayat and Zila Parishad regarding the readiness to run the scheme should be obtained. After all these things are fulfilled, the consent of the government should be taken to give the work order of the scheme.
The water supply from the scheme should be started by completing the works of the scheme by June 30, 2019. However, if the water supply from the scheme does not start by this date, the approval of the scheme will be cancelled.
13) After the work mentioned in the proposal of the scheme is completed through the Zila Parishad, Buldana, the maintenance of the scheme and the management of the scheme will be required to be done by the concerned Zila Parishad.
14) The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati should give 50% of the funds at the beginning of the scheme as soon as the work order is issued after the tender process for the said scheme. After that 30% of the funds should be distributed at the level of the Divisional Commissioner taking into account the progress of the scheme.
The remaining 20% will be paid after the project is completed.
Since the work of the scheme needs to be completed in time, the progress report should be sent to the government every Monday of the week from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
