Government Resolution No: Papuyo-2018/ No.50/ Papu18 Rs. 63,19,900/- (Rupees Five Hundred only) has been accorded revised Technical Approval.
Accordingly, the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Aurangabad has proposed to give revised administrative approval to the draft and budget of the proposed tap water supply scheme vide letter No. 6 submitted to the Government. Accordingly, the matter of granting revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Rs. The revised budget of Rs. 63,19,900/ (Rupees Sixty Three Lakhs Nineteen Thousand Nine Hundred only) is being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions.
Conditions
All the remaining work should be completed in 3 months at the revised price mentioned above.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Aurangabad that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials and contractors should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
