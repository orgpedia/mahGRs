Terms and Conditions
1) The Divisional Commissioner, Konkan, Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Amravati and Nagpur should distribute the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad for taking measures under the scarcity in rural areas.
2) Urban measures which are being implemented through Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils.
For this, the Divisional Commissioner should distribute it to the concerned District Collector. The Collector should disburse the funds to the concerned Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council.

The funds will be released for the implementation of measures taken to mitigate drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the period 2018-19 (October 2018 to September 2019) and for payment of pending dues during this period. This money cannot be used for other projects.
4) The Divisional Commissioner should distribute the funds on priority basis to the measures like well acquisition, tanker water supply and even if the proposal submitted by the Divisional Commissioner does not ask for the measures approved by the government, these measures should also be distributed on priority basis as per the order of the concerned administrative approval.
5) Ensure that the funds are disbursed directly to the contractors through Electronic Clearing Services/ RTGS, instead of keeping it in a personal ledger account or bank account.
6) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed for the related measures earlier.
7) Further, while seeking funds for the measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity, the subject-wise/ project-wise demand as to what are the proposed measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity for what period, how much funds are required for it, information about the funds disbursed in the current financial year, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc., and the utilization certificate therefor should be submitted. Besides, it should be noted that the funds will be approved in the future.
8) GPS on private tankers while making payments out of the funds released for the measures taken for supply of water through tankers/ drinking water as per the decision of the Government.
The system will be installed and only those trips of tankers whose fare is recorded on the GPS system will be allowed for payment. Such trips will not be admissible if the tanker transport holder claims that the tanker has been diverted due to non-installation of the system or the GPS system is switched off. as well as GPS.
As the system is not recorded, the payments should not be authenticated and funds should not be disbursed for such authenticated payments. Hence, the funds should be immediately disbursed to the concerned offices/ Zilla Parishads/ Collectors within four days. The utilization certificate of these funds should be submitted to the government before November 23, 2020.

The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the certificate with quarterly verification should be submitted to this department immediately.
Also, the Collector/ Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances. If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2020-21. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
If the funds being distributed under this Government Decision are not expendable, the Government should return the funds to the Government through the Budget Distribution System by November, 2020.
Though the funds are being released as per the demand received from the field offices, the same amount of funds required for the shortage period 2018-19 should be released through the Budget Distribution System.
