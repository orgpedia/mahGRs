The Proposal:
The Government has, vide Government Resolution dated 23.3.2017, taken over the responsibility of payment of salary, allowances and pension to the employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority and has released a sum of Rs.62,72,39,250/-to the Authority for the financial year 2020-21 under the head 22158052 as grant to meet the expenditure on pension of retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority. Now the matter of disbursement of further funds made available by the Finance Department under the above head of accounts is under consideration of the Government.

Government's decision:
For the financial year 2020-21, a grant of Rs. 62,72,39, 250/- (Rupees Sixty Two Crore Seventy Two Lakhs Seventy Nine Thousand Two Hundred and Fifty only) is being released to the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority under the head" Demand No. Y-2, 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 199, Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations (01) Assistance to other Non-Government Organizations (01) (01) Assistance to Government Employees Transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Authority (Subsidy Compulsory) (2215 A052) 31, Assistance Grants (Non-Salary)" subject to the following conditions:
(a) The Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the amount of the grant is spent for the same purposes as the grant is sanctioned for the pension benefits of retired officers/ employees of the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran.
(b) The funds shall not be used for repayment of any previous dues. Expenditure on other items of this fund will be treated as financial irregularity and the officers/ employees concerned will be liable for disciplinary action for such action.
e) Separate accounts of the amount of free subsidy should be maintained.
(e) The Authority shall inform the Government from time to time about the allocations made under the free grants.
(a) The accounts of the Authority as per Rule No. 3 of Annexure 2 to the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959 shall be submitted by the Authority for inspection along with relevant documents.
f) The Authority shall submit the utilization certificate of the free subsidy to the Government.

The amounts of grants and freebies shown as well as the amounts of expenditure should be certified. The utility certificates should be signed by the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Only such utility certificates will be acceptable to the government.

Patil has been posted as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Mr. R. M. Gengaje, Deputy Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, has been posted as the Controlling Officer.
The pension subsidy given by the government will have to be provided through ECS in the bank account of the beneficiaries. Under no circumstances should pension grants be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
The expenditure in the said case should be included in the financial statement for the year 2020-21 under the head" Demand No.Y-2, 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 199, Assistance to other Non-Governmental Organizations, (01) Assistance to Government Employees transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran Grants (Compulsory) (2215 A052) 31, Assistance Grants (Non-Payment)."
