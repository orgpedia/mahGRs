Now the remaining Rs. A sum of Rs. 1474.18 lakhs was under consideration of the Government for disbursement to the Divisional Commissioners, Pune and Aurangabad.
Government's decision:
A sum of Rs. 1474.18 lakhs (Rupees Fourteen Crores, Seventy Four Lakhs, Eighteen Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released to the Divisional Commissioner, Pune and Aurangabad under the head of accounts 22450093 to meet the expenditure on the measures taken/ to be taken for drinking water scarcity in Pune and Aurangabad divisions as per the norms laid down by the Government for mitigation of drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas, subject to the following conditions and terms and conditions laid down in the extant Standing Government Resolution.
(1) The Divisional Commissioner, Pune and Aurangabad shall ensure that the said funds are distributed to the respective District Collectors in accordance with Form-A attached with the said funds.
The funds will be released for the implementation of the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the period 2017-18 and for the payment of pending dues during this period. Care should be taken to ensure that the funds are not used for other short-term measures.
(3) For rural and urban schemes which have been approved at the government level under scarcity, 50% of the funds are to be disbursed at the beginning of the scheme and 50% after the completion of the scheme as mentioned in the decision of the government. Accordingly, the Collector in respect of urban schemes and the Chief Executive Officer in respect of rural schemes and the Zilla Parishad should not disburse the entire remaining funds to the concerned without ensuring 100% completion of the scheme.
