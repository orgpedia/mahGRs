Preamble- In rural areas of the State, some amount of saline water is available in drinking water sources. It is necessary to install water purification plants under National Rural Drinking Water Programme on temporary basis, develop safe sources in quality-affected villages till permanent water purification measures are taken to provide pure drinking water to the villages by dissolving this saline water. In view of the fact that the level of alkali in drinking water in some taluks of Chandrapur and Jalna districts is more than the limit, it is proposed to install water purification plants to provide pure drinking water to the villages by dissolving the alkali mixed water.

The main objective of installing water purification plants in selected water quality affected habitations is to provide clean and chemical free water to the habitations affected by chemical agents under the 5% Earmark Water Quality of National Rural Drinking Water Programme as per the guidelines of the Central Government. Installation of water purification plants in Chandrapur and Jalna districts is being done for a limited period till the second sustainable and pure water supply scheme.
Under the 5% Earmark Water Quality of National Rural Drinking Water Programme, the tender process for the purchase of water purification plant to provide chemical free and pure water to the chemical affected habitations in Chandrapur and Jalna districts will be done by the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai.

(a) Preference shall be given to those habitations where there is no alternative available and where revival of traditional sources is not possible and where there is no alternative source of potable water.
(b) Water purification plants shall be set up where all sources of public drinking water in the habitations are contaminated by chemical agents and no other measures (such as tap water supply schemes/ regional tap water supply schemes) have been taken or are being taken.
The Director, Water and Sanitation Support Organisation (WSSO) inviting tenders for the above work shall primarily implement the following instructions.
The expenditure for installation of water purification system as a temporary measure to provide safe drinking water to water quality affected villages will be met from the supplementary demand received from the State share of the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP) for the financial year 2017-18.
In the first phase, water purification plants will be set up in approximately 80 wards in Chandrapur and Jalna districts respectively.
(c) It may be mentioned while inviting tender that the cost of maintenance repair for 10 years is to be borne by the agency/ system supplying the water purifier.
(d) To provide potable water to the local population at the lowest possible cost, suitable water purification plants should be selected taking into account the natural, geographical conditions and population of the area. The capacity of water treatment plants should be between 500 and 1000 LPH.
