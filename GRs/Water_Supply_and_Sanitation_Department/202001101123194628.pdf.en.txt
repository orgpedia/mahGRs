the prelude
Satara District Collector, Satara, has submitted a proposal to provide water from the Tembhu Upasana Irrigation Scheme on the Krishna River for drinking and livestock to Mayani Lake and surrounding villages and 16 villages in the eastern part of Man taluka. Minister for Relief and Rehabilitation. It has been approved as a special matter with the consent of the Chief Minister. The administrative approval has been given under the Government Decision dated 27th March, 2018 of the Department.
Accordingly, the fund for payment of work dues on the said scheme has been released to the Divisional Commissioner, Pune vide Government Resolution No. 04 for an amount of Rs. 665.99 lakhs (Rupees Six Crores Sixty Five Lakhs Nine Thousand only).
The matter of giving specific instructions regarding the appropriation of this fund was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
Divisional Commissioner, Pune and Collector, Satara are being declared as the Controlling Officer and Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the payment of the dues for the work done in the scheme to provide water from the Tembhu Upasana Irrigation Scheme on the Krishna River for drinking and livestock to the Mayani Lake and surrounding villages and 16 villages in the eastern part of Man taluka.
In this case, the amount of Rs. 665.99 lakhs (Rupees Six Crores Sixty Five Lakhs Nine Thousand only) distributed on the budget distribution system should be drawn by the Collector, Satara and distributed to the Superintending Engineer, Sangli Irrigation Board, Sangli by cheque.
