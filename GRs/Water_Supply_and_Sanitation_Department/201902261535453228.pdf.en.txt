Introduction: Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, Md. Alegaon-Navegaon 14 villages (Tt. Patur, G. Akola) The proposal for revival of Regional Tap Water Supply Scheme is under reference No. 1 has been sent to the Government for approval. The plan of Rs. 629.12 lakh has been prepared by the Superintending Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority Board, Akola under reference No. 2 Technical approval has been granted under the order. A technical committee under the chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department was also constituted. The detailed project report for revival of the scheme has been approved in the meeting held on 31st October, 2018. The reference No. 3 As per the provisions of the Government Decision here, the Maharashtra Life Authority is responsible for the revival, further maintenance, operation and control of regional water supply schemes under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program. However, the general body meeting of the district council has decided to transfer the scheme for day-to-day maintenance. It has also been decided to revive and run this scheme through the District Council, Akola, so that the implementation of this scheme is done in time.

Government Resolution No: Mupeyo-2019/ No.15/ Papu-19 Accordingly, Rs. The matter of administrative approval of the budget of Rs 631.07 lakh was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: In view of the facts stated in the Preamble, Md. Alegaon-Navegaon 14 villages (Tt. Patur, G. Akola) Rs. 631.07 lakh (Rs. Six crore thirty one lakh seven thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following conditions.
The responsibility of revival, further maintenance, repair, operation and maintenance of these schemes will be of the District Council, Akola.
The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Bids with higher prices will not be accepted. It should be ensured that the work of the scheme is completed within the administrative approved cost.
(iii) No other work should be started until the scheme is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
In this scheme, only budgetary provision has been made to replace all the pipelines. Therefore, action should be taken to replace the existing pipeline as per the need only after a quick test of the scheme.
V. An agreement should be made with the villages included in the scheme to take water. If the villagers refuse to take water after the agreement is signed, 75 percent of the water should be recovered from them. The villagers should be informed in advance about the tender process.
VI. Reference No. 5 As per the instructions in the government circular here, all the house owners involved in the scheme should take an undertaking in the prescribed format about getting the meter connection and filling the water table.
VII. Separate arrangements should be made for each village for water supply so that there is no problem if the water supply of a village has to be stopped due to non-payment of water dues.
VIII. To meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it shall be increased from time to time.
No assistance/ grant will be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
