The Proposal:
A budget provision of Rs. 47.4905 crore has been made for the financial year 2020-21 under the head Demand No.Y-2" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (01) Divisional/ Sub-Divisional Establishment for Implementation of Water Supply Programme (2215 1843)" to meet the salary and non-salary expenses of Class I and Class II officers on the establishment of Rural Water Supply Department in Zilla Parishad. However, the Department of Finance had made available only Rs. 4.7490 crore out of the budgetary provision for the mandatory expenditure for the financial year 2020-21 on the budgetary system under the Government Circular dated 16.4.2020 in the wake of the pandemic situation. This grant has been distributed to the District Councils as per the Government Decision dated 30.4.2020.

However, as the salary fund is insufficient, the matter of disbursement of subsidy to the Zila Parishads for the next salary expenditure is under consideration of the Government, taking into account the total salary expenditure on the treasury of the respective Zila Parishads for the year 2020-21.
Government's decision:
For the financial year 2020-21, the following are the details of the District Councils Rural Water Supply under the head 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (01) Divisional/ Sub Divisional Establishment (Compulsory) for implementation of Water Supply Programme (22151843)
A total grant of Rs. 5,50,00, 000/- (Rupees Five Crore Fifty Lakhs only) as shown in the" Return A" accompanying this Government Resolution is being sanctioned to the concerned Chief Accounts and Finance Officer to meet the salary expenditure of Class I and Class II officers in the establishment of the Supply Department.
(2) The grants sanctioned under this Government Resolution shall be subject to such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Government from time to time. Accordingly, action should be taken for payment of salaries as per the procedure mentioned in the Government Circular No. 2020/ No. 62/ Koshasra-5, dated 1st April, 2020 of the Finance Department for the period mentioned therein. Considering the overall economic situation of the State in view of the health and economic disaster caused by COVID-19, it is necessary to curtail expenditure and defer some expenditure.
3. All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers shall ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts shown in the' Return A" accompanying this Government decision. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
4. Care should be taken to demand further necessary funds only after ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said decision is submitted to the government. The concerned district shall not disburse the funds until further requisition is received.
