The Proposal:
With the aim of improving the rural water supply sector in the state and providing pure and adequate drinking water to the rural people, the Department under the reference of No. 1 here. As per the Government decision dated 7th May, 2016, the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme is being implemented in the State from 2016-17 to 2019-20. Regarding the maintenance repair incentive subsidy to be distributed to the regional water supply schemes under this programme, vide Reference No. 2 Guidelines have been issued as per the decision of the government here. However, in the said Government decision, the cost of maintenance repair/ catchment recovery and levies for the year 2017-18 has been taken into account in the Maintenance Repair Incentive Scheme. Therefore, the Regional Offices are being asked to consider the maintenance repair cost/ catchment charges for the year 2018-19 and 2019-20 while submitting the proposals. Accordingly, the following recommendations are made:
Government circular:
In view of the facts stated in the Preamble, the maintenance repair cost/ catchment charges of the previous financial year should be taken into consideration while submitting the proposals for maintenance repair incentive grant for each financial year under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
