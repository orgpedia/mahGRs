Government's decision
Considering the facts stated in the Preamble, a sum of Rs. 13.17 lakh (Rs.13 lakh seventeen thousand only) is being released to the Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur to meet the expenditure incurred on measures taken under drinking water scarcity in the summer of 2014 in the Municipal Council Hinganghat area, subject to the following conditions:
The government will provide a subsidy of Rs. 13.17 lakh i.e. 90% of the total cost of Rs. 14.63 lakh. The remaining amount i. e. 10% of the scheme amounting to Rs. 1.46 lakh is to be paid by the Municipal Council Hinganghat as public contribution.
(2) The funds shall be distributed by the Divisional Commissioner to the Municipal Council Hinganghat through the Collector, Wardha, only after the Municipal Council Hinganghat has spent 10 per cent of the population of the scheme in the first instance.
(3) The Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur shall disburse the due grant of the scheme to the Collector, Wardha.
4) The Collector, Wardha should submit the utility certificate in this regard.
Expenditure on Demand No. 2. Y-2, (02) (11) For taking temporary measures to overcome the acute drinking water shortage, the Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils should be provided with the provision of Auxiliary Grants (Compulsory), 31 Auxiliary Grants (Non-salaried), (2215A201) from the head and the final expenditure thereunder and the same should be met from the grant sanctioned for the financial year 2021-22.
This Government decision is being issued in concurrence with the Finance Department's Informal Reference No. 259/ Expenditure-3, dated 29.07.2021.
