Government's decision:
In view of the facts stated in the Preamble, for the implementation of the Regional Water Supply Schemes approved to be revived under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme (Head of Accounts No. 22159951 under) Rs. A sum of Rs. 1.90 crore (Rupees One Crore Ninety Lakh only) is being sanctioned and released to the Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran through Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions.
This fund should be spent only for the implementation of regional tap water supply schemes approved to be revived under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2020-21. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
Funds disbursed to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should not be kept in personal provident fund (PLA) or other such accounts.
(iv) No funds will be sanctioned in future under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme unless information is furnished regarding the funds disbursed during the current financial year, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc.
V. The material progress for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed should be submitted along with the monthly expenditure report every month. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure incurred from this grant. In this regard, vide No. 4 The Accountant General, Maharashtra-1/ 2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the authorized officers of this Department may ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Government under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme are being fulfilled by the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority.
