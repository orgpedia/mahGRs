In his letter dated 20.02.2019, the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has submitted a proposal to the Government for administrative sanction of Rs. 99.32 lakhs for special improvement of Regional Water Supply Scheme Khambora 64 Khedi, District Akola under scarcity measures for the period 2018-19.
The Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department to give administrative approval to temporary water supply schemes/ measures outside the prescribed financial and physical parameters in urban and rural areas to overcome the drinking water shortage, in its meeting held on 9th May, 2019, discussed the proposal in detail and approved the proposal for the construction of a Rs. Works worth Rs. 74.31 lakh have been sanctioned. Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: Scarcity 2019/ PR No. 37/ Papu 14
Considering the facts mentioned in the Preamble of the Government Resolution, the proposal of Khambora 64 Khedi, District Akola involving works proposed under Special Amendment of Regional Water Supply Scheme at a cost of Rs. 74.31 lakh (Rupees Seventy Four Lakh Thirty One Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval under scarcity subject to the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Akola shall first declare water scarcity in the area of Regional Water Supply Scheme Khambora 64 Khedi, District Akola.
All special repair works mentioned in the proposal should be carried out by the executive engineer, rural water supply department, zilla parishad, Akola.
3) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Ensure that the work is completed within the approved cost.
The tender process must be completed by May 29, 2019.
Approval of the project will be cancelled if the project is not completed by that date.
It is necessary to start water supply from the scheme by completing the works of the scheme by June 15, 2019.
6) After the completion of the works of the said scheme mentioned in the proposal through the Zilla Parishad, Akola, the maintenance of the scheme and the management of the scheme will be required to be done through the concerned Gram Panchayat Maintenance Repair Mechanism.
7) The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati should provide 50% of the funds at the beginning of the scheme after the tender process for the said scheme. The remaining 30% of the funds should be disbursed after considering the progress of the scheme at the level of Divisional Commissioner. The remaining 20% will be paid after the project is completed.
8) Since the work of the scheme must be completed in time, the progress report of the scheme should be sent to the government every Monday from the tender process.
