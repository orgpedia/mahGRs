The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has submitted a proposal to the Government for administrative approval of works worth Rs. 423.90 lakh under drinking water scarcity in the area of Akola Municipal Corporation for the scarcity period of 2017-18. This proposal was made by the Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department to give administrative approval to the urgent water supply schemes/ measures beyond the prescribed financial and physical criteria to address the drinking water shortage in urban and rural areas.

The standing committee had sought clarification from the municipal corporation on some issues.
This disclosure was received in his letter dated 26.04.2018. In pursuance of this disclosure, the Chairman of the Standing Committee approved a total of 120 new borewells with hand pumps and 75 new borewells with sub-pumps proposed in the expanded area of the city. The proposal for a cost of Rs. 128.25 lakh was accorded administrative approval vide Government Resolution dated 07.05.2018. As the remaining measures proposed are likely to start soon, it was decided to hold a meeting with the Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department again and take further decisions.
As per the discussion held in the meeting of the Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department on May 19, 2018, it is necessary to take measures under the shortage proposed by the Akola Municipal Corporation, so the government is considering to give administrative approval to 60 hand pumps including the new borewell and 40 wells including the new borewell submersible pump.
In view of the facts mentioned in the Preamble to the Government Resolution, the proposal for the measures to be taken under the drinking water scarcity for the Akola City for the scarcity period 2017-18 at a cost of Rs. 66.00 lakh (Rupees Sixty Six Lakh only) as mentioned in the proposal, is being accorded administrative approval under this Order subject to the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Akola shall first declare that there is a shortage of drinking water in Akola city.
The Government will provide a subsidy of Rs. 59.40 lakhs i.e. 90% of the total cost of Rs. 66.00 lakhs of the cost of the scheme.
The remaining amount i. e. 10% of the scheme amounting to Rs. 6.60 lakh is to be paid by Akola Municipal Corporation, Akola as public contribution.

(3) No work order shall be issued under the scheme unless 10% of the population of the scheme is paid by the Municipal Corporation.
4) This scheme should be implemented through Akola Municipal Corporation.
As per the approval of the standing committee, hand pumps along with the new borewell proposed in the area of Akola Municipal Corporation and submersible pumps along with the new borewell are to be taken. Their location should be determined through groundwater survey and development system. Also, new borewells should be taken up at places certified by the Ground Water Survey and Development Agency.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner and it should be ensured that the work of the scheme is completed within the approved price.
The tender of the scheme is being allowed to be taken out for a short period (7 days).
8) The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati shall make available to the Commissioner, Akola Municipal Corporation, Akola, funds equivalent to 50% of the subsidy payable at the beginning of the scheme for the said scheme. The remaining 50% of the funds will be released after the implementation of the scheme.
Since the work of the scheme needs to be completed in time, the progress report should be sent to the government every Monday of the week from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
