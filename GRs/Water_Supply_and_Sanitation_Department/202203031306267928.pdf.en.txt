Preamble: Expenditure on measures taken to alleviate water scarcity in rural areas of the State is met from the grants made available by this Department under the head (2215 A 195).
The Department has received the proposal of Nashik Division seeking funds for the measures taken/ to be taken during the scarcity period of 2018-19 and 2020-21. During the financial year 2021-22, the funds budgeted under the objective of Account Head (2215A 195) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Plan), were under consideration of the Government to disburse the funds for the measures demanded and actually completed by the Divisional Commissioner, Nashik.
Government's decision:
As per the norms laid down by the Government for addressing the drinking water scarcity in rural areas, the Divisional Commissioner, Nashik has been provided with a sum of Rs.2215/- (Rs.2215/-A195) to meet the cost of the completed action plan on the drinking water scarcity measures in his/ her area.

Terms and Conditions
The Divisional Commissioner, Nashik shall disburse the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad for taking measures under the scarcity in rural areas.
The funds should be released for the implementation of the measures taken to address the drinking water shortage in rural areas for the period 2018-19 and from October, 2020 to June, 2021, as well as for the payment of pending dues during this period. This money cannot be used for other projects.
3) The Divisional Commissioner should allocate funds on priority to the measures like well acquisition, tanker water supply and even if the proposal submitted by the Divisional Commissioner does not ask for the measures approved by the government, these measures should also be allocated on priority as per the order of the concerned administrative approval.
4) It should be ensured that the funds are disbursed directly to the contractors through Electronic Clearing Services/ RTGS, without keeping the personal ledger account or bank account.
5) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed to the concerned measures earlier.
(6) While seeking funds for the measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity, the matter-wise/ project-wise demand as to what measures are proposed to be taken for mitigation of scarcity for what period and how much funds are required for the same is also being processed.
