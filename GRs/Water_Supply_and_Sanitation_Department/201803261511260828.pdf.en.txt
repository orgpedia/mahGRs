Preamble In the financial year 2016-17, in respect of Central share of grants received under the Scheduled Tribes Area Sub-Plan (Tribal Sub-Plan) from the Central Government under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Rural Drinking Water Programme, a demand No. T-5, Accounts Head 2215 Water Supply and Sanitation, 01 Water Supply, 796, Tribal Area Sub Plan (01) Schemes under Tribal Area Utilization (01) (09), Schemes under State Schemes, National Rural Drinking Water Programme (Central Share, 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried)) (2215 9853).

Under Section 3, the Tribal Development Department has handed over the budget to this department through the distribution system. The proposal to sanction these funds to the implementing agencies for rural water supply schemes was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision In the financial year 2017-18, for the above mentioned components for tap water supply schemes, under the head 2215 9853, Rs. 1,09,67, 000/- crore (Rupees One Crore Nine Lakh Sixty Five Thousand only) is being disbursed to the Director, Water& Sanitation Support Agency, Belapur, Navi Mumbai for distribution of funds under Tribal Applications (Chemical Contamination) to the Drawing and Disbursing Officer (Cash Branch), Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai on Budget Distribution System (BEAMS).
Conditions: (1) The grant shall be spent on priority basis by the concerned implementing agencies for completion of works in the villages/ hamlets where physical completion of the schemes under National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP) for chemical contamination has been made mandatory as per the priorities included in the prescribed Annual Action Plan for Tribal Areas and Central Assistance Schemes in which Government participation is mandatory. Also, this subsidy should be spent on priority for completion of schemes in physically complete but financially incomplete villages/ wards/ Fluoride/ Arsenic, Sansad Adarsh Gram, Open Defecation Free villages/ wards.
(ii) The respective implementing agencies shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure incurred from these grants. The Accountant General, Maharashtra- 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the authorised officers of this Department shall be empowered to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned implementing agencies for audit to ensure that the conditions and guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme are being complied with by the concerned implementing agencies. as well

(viii) The funds provided under the Tribal Appropriation Schemes for the year 2012-13, 2013-14, 2014-15, 2015-16, 2016-17 should be spent only for the village schemes included in the Action Plan for Tribal Appropriation. Also, the allocation made for areas outside the tribal areas should be spent for the tribal people of that area. The allocation made under Tribal Appropriation should not be spent outside the tribal area or under the general scheme. The utility certificate should be submitted to the government in time.
