Preamble: The proposal for a tap water supply scheme, namely Mauje Jaigad Regional Water Supply Scheme, T.G. Ratnagiri, has been accorded technical approval vide letter No. 2 of reference from the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, Thane. The Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Thane has submitted a proposal for the approval of the government to approve the tap water supply scheme as the per capita cost of the present tap water supply scheme is higher than the prescribed norms.
Therefore, the matter of administrative approval for the increased per capita expenditure of the Mauje Jaigarh Regional Water Supply Scheme, T.G. Ratnagiri, was under consideration of the Government.

The plan and the budget amounting to Rs. 32.22 lakhs, 65,500 only) is being accorded administrative approval under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
1) The scheme should be implemented by the Maharashtra Life Authority. It will be mandatory for the contractor to run the scheme for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme.
The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as the maintenance of the scheme is carried out for the repair of reference No. 6 Follow the guidelines in the Government Resolution dated 17th October, 2014.
2) Even if the scheme will be run by the contractor for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
(3) The budget of the scheme should be given fresh technical approval.
4) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
A committee should be formed under the chairmanship of the Collector to acquire land and obtain all other permissions required for the scheme. The committee will review the progress of railway/ road/ forest land crossings and land acquisition every month.
6) 100% household tap connections and meters should be included in the scheme. Also, at least 80% of the tap connection holders should take an undertaking for getting the meter connection at their own cost and a copy of it should be laminated and given to the tap connection holder and a copy should be kept with the Gram Panchayat. Thereafter, the orders were issued.
7) If a village refuses to take water even after the gram sabha resolution/ assurance letter, at least 50 per cent of the water supply should be recovered from it. In this regard, the villagers should be given an idea before the tender process and the Gram Sabha should take a resolution in this regard.
8) The existing schemes in the villages covered under the scheme should not be run so that the scheme is not used seasonally.
9) The village included in the scheme should be included on the central government's IMIS system. The works should be completed in a time-bound and planned manner in a phased manner. No other work should be started until the plan is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
10) An agreement should be made on stamp paper with each gram panchayat to take water from the scheme in bulk.
11) To meet the expenses incurred for the operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall implement the scheme as mentioned in the proposal for domestic, non-domestic

Water tariff for domestic and institutional tap connections should be fixed and increased periodically.
12) 20% of the recovered water table should be kept with the implementing agency for major maintenance repairs. Maintenance repair of pipes and other pipes should be done every 5 years and every 3 years.
No assistance/ grant will be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
14) All villages under the scheme should be open defecation free but it should be ensured that they are always open defecation free.
15) If the cost of the scheme increases for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
16) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled. Also referred to as No. 2 The provisions of the government decision here should be strictly followed.
17) under reference No. As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 15th June, 2015, it will be mandatory to conduct a third stage technical examination of the scheme.
18) It will be mandatory for the Gram Panchayat to determine the necessary water table for the scheme to be self-reliant and increase it as per the need. In this regard, the officials of the Maharashtra Life Authority should give detailed information to the villagers in the gram sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
19) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
20) In case of any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity shall be liable for disciplinary action.
21) It will be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme.
