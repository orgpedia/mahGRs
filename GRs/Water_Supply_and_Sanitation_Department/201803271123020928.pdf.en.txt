The proposal, which includes the release of water through a long PSC pipeline into a drain at Kapsevadi, was approved by the Cabinet. Minister for Relief and Rehabilitation. The Chief Minister has given his approval as a special case. The Forest and Revenue Department has issued a notification in this regard. A sum of Rs. 905.21 lakh has already been released to the Water Resources Department under the scarcity fund as per the Government decision dated 17th January, 2018.
As the scheme is related to drinking water and water for livestock in the villages around Mayani Lake and 16 villages in the eastern part of Man taluka, the Water Supply and Sanitation Department should give administrative approval to the proposal. The meeting was held on 12th March, 2018 under the chairmanship of the Minister for Relief and Rehabilitation. Accordingly, the Department of Revenue and Forest has issued a Government Sanction Letter dated 16th March, 2018. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the proposal was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
Considering the facts mentioned in the preamble, Rs. 472.89 lakh has been provided for filling up of Mayani Lake and surrounding villages and 16 villages in the eastern part of Man Taluka by providing water from Krishna River through Tembhu Upasa Irrigation Scheme for drinking water and livestock and Rs. 432.32 lakhs (Rupees Nine Crores Five Lakhs Twenty One Thousand only) are being administratively approved as a special case under scarcity subject to the following conditions: 1. The Collector, Satara shall first declare that there is scarcity of drinking water in the villages of Mayani Talav area and 16 villages of Man taluka.
2) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Bids with higher prices will not be accepted.
3) The work of the scheme must be completed within the administrative approved cost. The plan cannot be changed.
4) The government will not be responsible for the increase in the cost of the scheme and such a scheme will be eligible for cancellation at any stage.
5) The work of this scheme should be completed immediately so that this scheme can be used during the water shortage period of 2018.

6) The scheme should be implemented by the Water Resources Department and after the implementation of the scheme, its maintenance should be done by the Water Resources Department. No separate fund will be provided for this.
7) Since the present scheme is designed to provide water for drinking and livestock under scarcity, the water of this scheme must be used for the same purpose. Water cannot be used for irrigation or other purposes.
(8) The budget of the scheme should be subject to technical approval of the competent authority.
If the work of the scheme is started before the technical approval is taken, the administrative approval given through this will be automatically cancelled.
