Preamble: The Divisional Commissioner, Pune vide his letter dated 03.03.2018, has approved the proposal for implementation of Regional Piped Water Supply Scheme (RPWS) for Gandhinagar and 13 villages for the period of scarcity in 2017-18. A proposal for special amendment of the tap water supply scheme, which includes the pressure tube and gravity line and other works in the Karveer, G. Kolhapur scheme, has been submitted to the government for administrative approval.
The Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department in its meeting held on 14.03.2018 to give administrative approval to temporary water supply schemes/ measures outside the prescribed financial and physical parameters in urban and rural areas to address the drinking water shortage.

The government pointed out that such a large-scale amendment to the scheme was not possible. It was submitted to the Chief Minister for approval.
The Hon'ble Chief Minister has approved the proposal as a special case under the Shortage Management Scheme. As approved by the Hon'ble Chief Minister, the matter of granting administrative approval to the scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Considering the fact mentioned in the Preamble of the Government decision and by allowing 4% ETP charges instead of 9%, Gandhinagar and 13 villages have been included in the Regional Tap Water Supply Scheme. The proposal of Rs. 1052.52 (Rupees Ten Crores, Fifty Two Lakhs, Fifty Two Thousand only) under the Special Repair of Tap Water Supply Scheme, Karveer, G. Kolhapur, which includes works listed in the Annexure, is being given administrative approval subject to the following conditions:
(1) All special repair works mentioned in the said Preamble shall be carried out by the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Kolhapur.
2) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Bids with higher prices will not be accepted.
3) Once the tender process is completed, the project must be completed within the time stipulated in the terms and conditions of the contract. No extension will be granted under any circumstances.
4) Ensure that the work is completed within the administrative approved cost.
The government will not provide more funds. If the cost of the scheme increases, it will be taken seriously by the government and disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned.
Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority Board, Pune To comply with the conditions of the Technical Sanction Order dated 03.02.2018.
(6) If any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice is found in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for it will be liable for disciplinary action.
7) The Divisional Commissioner, Pune should provide 50% of the funds at the beginning of the scheme after the tender process for the said scheme. The remaining funds will be released after the completion of the project.
8) The project must be completed on time.
