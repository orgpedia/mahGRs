Preface: Mouje Kumbhargaon (Ta. The Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Satara has given the basic administrative approval to the budget of the tap water supply scheme in Patan, G. Satara, as per the order of Reference No. 1. As per Reference No. 2, the Superintending Engineer, Bharat Nirman Coordination and Control Room, Pune has accorded the original technical approval to the Budget. However, the cost of the project has increased due to the high cost of the project. Post facto approval has been granted under Reference No. 3 of the tender for the work of this scheme.
The Superintending Engineer, Divisional Water Supply Cell, Pune has given revised technical approval to the budget of the scheme presented under reference number 4. Accordingly, the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Zilla Parishad, Satara, had submitted a proposal to the government under Reference Letter No. 5 to give revised administrative approval to the design and budget of the present tap water supply scheme. Accordingly, the matter of granting revised administrative approval to the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.

Government Decision: Patan, Rs. The revised budget of Rs. 2,42,50, 158/- (Rupees Two Crores Forty Two Lakhs Fifty Thousand One Hundred and Fifty Eight only) is being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions.
Conditions
All the remaining work should be completed in 3 months at the revised price mentioned above.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned is responsible to take over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation that the scheme has been actually completed by the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Satara.
As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
