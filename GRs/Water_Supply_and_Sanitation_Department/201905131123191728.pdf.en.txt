GOVERNMENT DECISION The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has issued a proposal regarding the administrative approval of temporary supplementary tap water supply scheme in Mauje Kinagavaraja, Taluka Sindkhedaraja, District Buldana. 3 submitted to the government. In this proposal, the Standing Committee has approved the proposal of Rs. 60.13 lakh (Rupees Sixty Lakh Thirteen Thousand only) for the temporary supplementary tap water supply scheme in Namud Kinagavaraja Taluk Sindkhedaraja District Buldana in its meeting held on 09.05.2019. Accordingly, under this order, administrative approval is being given to the proposal of temporary supplementary tap water supply scheme in Kinagavaraja Taluka Sindkhedaraja District Buldana subject to the following conditions: 1. The Collector, Buldana shall first declare that there is a shortage of drinking water in the villages of Mauje Kinagavaraja, Taluka Sindkhedaraja.
2) The supplementary water supply scheme mentioned in the proposal should be implemented by the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Buldana.
3) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
4) Ensure that the project is completed within the approved cost.
The tender process must be completed by May 29, 2019.
If the process is not completed by 29th May, 2019, the approval of the scheme will be deemed to be cancelled.
6) The water supply from the scheme must be started by completing the works of the scheme by June 15, 2019. However, if the water supply from the scheme does not start by this date, the approval of the scheme will be cancelled.
7) After the work mentioned in the proposal of the scheme is completed through the Zilla Parishad, Buldana, the maintenance of the scheme and the management of the scheme will be required to be done through the concerned Gram Panchayat.
8) The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati should provide 50% of the funds at the beginning of the scheme after the tender process for the said scheme. After that 30% of the funds should be distributed at the level of the Divisional Commissioner taking into account the progress of the scheme. The remaining 20% will be paid after the project is completed.
