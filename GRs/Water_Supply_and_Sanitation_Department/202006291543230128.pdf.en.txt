Government order:
As a result, Shri Ravindra Ramchandra Padse, retired Deputy Engineer (Mechanical), Ground Water Survey and Development System, Mechanical Sub-Division, Zilla Parishad, Sindhudurg, was suspended from Government service under Government Order dated 12.06.2012 for dereliction of duty by remaining absent despite instructions to attend the Video Conference of National Rural Drinking Water Programme chaired by Principal Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department and not fulfilling the targets of 100 schemes in the district under National Rural Drinking Water Programme during 2011-12. Thereafter, vide order dated 26.07.2013, Shri Padse was reinstated in Government service.

Accordingly, in the said case, a departmental enquiry was ordered against Mr. R.R. Padse, retired Deputy Engineer (Mechanical) under Rule 10 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979 vide Government Memorandum No. 3 dated 25th July, 2013.
On examination of the charge sheet against Mr. Padse, Retired Deputy Engineer (Mechanical) and the representation submitted by him and on verification of the documents available, it has been observed that the charge sheet against Mr. R.R. Padse, Retired Deputy Engineer (Mechanical), Ground Water Survey and Development System, No. 1 and 2 are not valid. Therefore, under the order dated 22/06/2020 dated 22/06.2020, it has been decided to close the departmental inquiry in the matter of Mr. Padse by declaring him innocent with the approval of the disciplinary authority.
Accordingly, the suspension of Mr. R.R. Padse, Retd. Deputy Engineer (Mechanical), vide order dated 26.07.2013 at Reference No.2, was revoked and he was reinstated in Government service. However, he has since been given posting under the Government Order dated 18th November, 2013. Therefore, Shri Padse has remained without posting from 26th July, 2013 till his actual joining on 25th November, 2013.
Padse, Retired Deputy Engineer (Mechanical) has submitted an application to the Government requesting to regularize his suspension period as duty period and it has been concluded that the suspension of Mr. Padse was not justified.
Accordingly, in the above background, the regularization of the period of suspension of Shri R.R. Padse, Retd. Deputy Engineer (Mechanical) is being decided by invoking the provisions of Rule 72 (a) and (b) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Assumption of Office, Non-Acceptance of Service and Remuneration during Suspension, Dismissal and Removal from Service) Rules, 1981, as under:
(1) In exercise of the provisions of Rule 72 (a) and (b) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Incumbency, Post-retirement Service and Provisions in respect of Suspension, Dismissal and Removal) Rules, 1981, Shri R.R. Padse, Retd.
