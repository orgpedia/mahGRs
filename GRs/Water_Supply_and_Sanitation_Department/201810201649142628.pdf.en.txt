Preamble: National Rural Drinking Water Programme. Manjaram. Proposal for Administrative Approval for the Scheme and Budget for Increased Per Capita Cost of Tap Water Supply Scheme at Naigaon (Khai), Dist. 1 has been sent to the Government for approval. The technical approval for the project has been accorded by the Superintending Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Nanded at a cost of Rs 594.62 lakh. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
The government has taken a decision. Manjaram. Naigaon (Khai), G. Nanded, Rs. 3619 with an increased per capita expenditure of Rs. Administrative Sanction is being accorded to the project with a budget of Rs. 6,17,28, 286/- (Rupees Six Crores Seventeen Lakhs Eighty Eight Thousand Two Hundred and Eighty Six only) subject to the following conditions.

The scheme will be implemented by Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Nanded. The contractor will be responsible for the maintenance and repair of the plan for at least 1 year after the implementation of the plan.
2) Technical approval should be given to the remaining cost of the scheme.
3) As the citation tube of the scheme passes through the forest department, it will be the responsibility of the Maharashtra Life Authority to get the permission of the forest department.
5) No work order should be issued to the scheme without obtaining necessary land acquisition and all other permissions for the scheme.
6) The project should be completed in a time-bound manner.
7) No other work should be started until the origin of the plan is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
8) The cost of operation and maintenance of the project should be kept to a minimum by correcting the details of the cost shown in the Detailed Project Report. Also, to meet the expenditure and to make the scheme financially self-sufficient, the concerned Gram Panchayat should fix the minimum water tariff for the household tap holder and it should be increased from time to time. All these matters will be handed over to the concerned officials.
No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred annually for running, maintaining and repairing the scheme.
10) Existing hymns should be strengthened.
11) If the cost of the scheme increases for any reason, the government will not provide subsidy.
12) Action should be taken regarding the release of open defecation as per the existing provisions of the government.
13) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled. Also, it will be mandatory to conduct a third technical examination of the scheme according to the prevailing policy of the government.
14) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
If any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice is found in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity will be liable for disciplinary action.
The Water and Sanitation Support Organization (WASO) will be responsible for obtaining utilization certificates of the expenditure and completion of the scheme from the concerned implementing agencies and submitting them to the government as the scheme progresses.
17) It should be ensured that the scheme is implemented in accordance with the prevailing government policy.
