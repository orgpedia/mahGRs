Government's decision
Considering the facts stated in the preamble. Chirgaon Ta. Mhasala, G. Raigad Water Supply Scheme with a per capita cost of Rs. Administrative approval is being accorded under Jal Jeevan Mission Programme subject to fulfilment of following terms and conditions for the project with a total estimated cost of Rs. 75,85,397/- (Rupees Seventy Five Lakhs Eighty Five Thousand Three Hundred and Ninety Seven only).
1) The scheme should be implemented through the District Council, Raigad. After the completion of the implementation of the scheme, it is handed over to the concerned executive engineer within a month from the date of receipt of the notification of the scheme. The concerned Gram Panchayat shall be responsible for conducting a joint inspection of the scheme by the engineer, Zilla Parishad and the concerned Gram Panchayat officer and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
2) It will be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the Jal Jeevan Mission program. Every house should have a tap connection. In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
3) The contractor will be obliged to run the scheme for at least 1 year. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed.
4) After the implementation of the scheme, the scheme will be run for at least 1 year through the contractor, but the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 4 th September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 5/10 th of the cost of the scheme shall be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayat for maintenance of the water supply scheme.
The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
7) Land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme should be obtained within the stipulated time.
8) To ensure that the scheme is not used seasonally, the Gram Panchayat should ensure that the existing scheme in the village included in the scheme is not operated.

9) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and increase it from time to time. Also, it will be mandatory for the gram panchayat to determine the required water table and increase it as per the need to make the scheme self-reliant. In this regard, the Zilla Parishad officials should clearly inform the villagers in the Gram Sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
10) No assistance/ grant will be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
11) It should be ensured that the villages under this scheme are open defecation free but they will always remain open defecation free.
12) If the cost of the scheme increases for any reason, no additional subsidy will be provided by the government.
13) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled.
14) The third technical examination of the scheme will be mandatory as per the government decision issued from time to time regarding the third technical examination of the scheme.
15) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
16) If any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice is found in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity will be liable for disciplinary action.
As per the progress of the scheme, the responsibility of obtaining utilization certificates of the expenditure and proof of completion of the scheme from the concerned implementing agencies and submitting them to the government will be with the State Water and Sanitation Mission, Belapur.
