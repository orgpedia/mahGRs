The original administrative approval has been granted under Reference No. 1 to the budget of the tap water supply scheme at Vasmat (G. Hingoli). As per Reference No. 2, the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Hingoli has accorded technical approval to the original budget. Accordingly, the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Hingoli, has submitted a proposal to the Government under Reference Letter No. 3 for administrative approval to the revised design and budget of the present tap water supply scheme, along with ex-post facto approval for the acceptance of the tender at an additional rate. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the revised plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Rs. 97.78 lakh (Rs. The revised budget of Rs. Ninety Nine Lakhs Seventy Eight Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval subject to the following conditions.

Conditions
1.The remaining work should be completed within the stipulated time.
The terms and conditions mentioned in the original administrative approval as well as the technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zila Parishad that the scheme has been actually completed.
As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
7. Action to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the present scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
