Preamble: The Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad had submitted a revised proposal of supplementary water supply scheme for villages under Lasur Station and 13 Villages Regional Water Supply Scheme for the scarcity period of 2017-18 at a cost of Rs. 2906.44 lakh and later at Rs. 2064.94 lakh. This proposal was considered for approval in the meetings held on 20.03.2018 and 03.04.2018 respectively of the Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department for administrative approval of temporary water supply schemes/ measures outside the prescribed financial and physical parameters in urban and rural areas for mitigation of drinking water scarcity.

The proposal was discussed in detail in the Standing Committee meeting and the revised proposal of the scheme, which includes the Approach Channel, Jack Well and Pump House, Approach Bridge, was rejected as it could not be taken up under scarcity and the temporary water supply scheme under scarcity was permissible for the scarcity period. However, the regional water supply scheme in the present proposal is closed due to lack of water in the source. Therefore, considering the fact that the villages included in the Regional Water Supply Scheme have to be supplied water through tankers, and in order to permanently solve the problem of water scarcity in the area, it is necessary to take a supplementary water supply scheme in this area, this proposal has been made as a special matter, according to the opinion of the Revenue and Forest Department (Relief and Rehabilitation). Minister for Relief and Rehabilitation. It was presented to the Chief Minister. The Chief Minister has given his approval as a special case and the matter of administrative approval to the scheme was under consideration of the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION In view of the above mentioned facts and in addition to the works in the Annexure, a proposal for an amount of Rs. 2063.02 lakhs (Rupees Twenty Crores, Sixty Three Lakhs, Two Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval subject to the following conditions: 1. The Collector, Aurangabad shall first declare that there is scarcity of drinking water in the concerned villages covered under Lasur Station and 13 Villages Regional Water Supply Scheme.
The works of Lasur station and temporary supplementary tap water supply scheme under the 13 villages regional water supply scheme mentioned in the proposal should be done by the executive engineer Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Aurangabad.
3) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Bids with higher prices will not be accepted.
4) Once the tender process is completed, the project must be completed within the time stipulated in the terms and conditions of the contract. No extension will be granted under any circumstances.
5) It should be ensured that the work of the scheme is completed within the administrative approved cost.
The government will not provide more funds. If the cost of the scheme increases, it will be taken seriously by the government and disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned.
