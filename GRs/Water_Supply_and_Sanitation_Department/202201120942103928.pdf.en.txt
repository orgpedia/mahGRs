Such a modest budget and design is being accorded administrative approval under the Jal Jeevan Mission programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
1) The implementation of this scheme should be done through the Maharashtra Life Authority, Regional Department, Thane. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Summit Committee/ District Council to take over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, that the scheme has been actually completed. Once the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran/ Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The contractor will be obliged to run the scheme for at least 1 year. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as the maintenance of the scheme is carried out for the repair of the reference No. 2 here. Guidelines in the Government Resolution dated 17th October, 2014 should be kept pending.
3) Even if the scheme will be run for at least one year through the contractor after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
4) Reference No. As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 4 th September, 2020 and the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, Government of India, 10% of the cost of the scheme will be required to be kept with the Gram Panchayats for maintenance of the water supply scheme. The Gram Panchayat and the Zilla Parishad will be responsible for collecting this amount.
5) Every household should be provided with a tap connection under this scheme. In this regard, the information about the tap connections and the connection to be provided so far needs to be recorded on the IMIS system. The Gram Panchayats concerned will be responsible for making necessary planning regarding the new connection.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
7) All other permissions required for the scheme and necessary procedures regarding land acquisition should be obtained within the stipulated time.
8) To ensure that the scheme is not used seasonally, the Gram Panchayat should ensure that the existing scheme in the village included in the scheme is not operated.
9) In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat shall fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time. Also, it will be mandatory for the gram panchayat to determine the required water table and increase it as per the need to make the scheme self-reliant. In this regard, the officials of the Maharashtra Life Authority should give detailed information to the villagers in the gram sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
