The expenditure on water scarcity measures in rural and urban areas of the State is met from the grants available under the head" Assistance for Natural Disaster Mitigation" (2245). The Standing Committee under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department has recommended measures to be taken during the scarcity period of 2017-18. The decision was approved by the 5-8. Accordingly, 50% of the subsidy due from the government for these schemes will be disbursed and the Departmental Commissioner, Nashik will issue a reference No. The Department has received a proposal for seeking funds under Section 9. For the financial year 2017-18, the Department of Revenue and Forest (Relief and Rehabilitation), vide its Government Resolution dated 16.10.2017, was considering the issue of release of funds to Divisional Commissioners, Amravati, Nashik and Aurangabad from the funds available with the Department.
Government's decision:
A sum of Rs. 1455.39 lakhs (Rupees Fourteen Crores, Fifty Five Lakhs, Nineteen Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released to the Divisional Commissioners, Amravati, Nashik and Aurangabad through the Budget Distribution System subject to the terms and conditions laid down in the prevailing Standing Government Resolution and the following conditions, to meet the expenditure incurred/ to be incurred on the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water shortage in Konkan and Pune divisions as per the norms laid down by the Government.
Terms and Conditions
Divisional Commissioners of Amravati, Nashik and Aurangabad should ensure that the funds are disbursed to the respective District Collectors as per the scheme.
