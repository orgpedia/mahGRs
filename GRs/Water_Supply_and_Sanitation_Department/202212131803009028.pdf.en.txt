GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: SUPRAMA-2022/ PR. No. 1852/ PAPU13 2. The original administrative approval accorded to the scheme as well as the terms and conditions mentioned while granting the revised technical approval should be strictly adhered to.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad concerned shall be responsible to take over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zila Parishad, Wardha that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (subject to special amendment) of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
7. Action to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the present scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval to the scheme should be strictly followed.
10. It should be ensured at the Zilla Parishad level that the tender process has been carried out properly.
The cost escalation for the 23 schemes mentioned in Annexure-A should be met from the grants sanctioned under the Jal Jeevan Mission Programme as per the guidelines issued from time to time and the final expenditure should be borne by the State Government.
