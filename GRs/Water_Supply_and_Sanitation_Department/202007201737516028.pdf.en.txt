Government order:
Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonpe, Senior Geologist, Ground Water Survey and Development System, District Senior Geologist, Ground Water Survey and Development System, Sangli was suspended from Government service under Government Order dated 21/06/2013 for dereliction of duty in connection with non-implementation of programmes of Ground Water Recharge Schemes in Sangli District, non-compliance of National Rural Drinking Water Programme and other irregularities. Thereafter, Mr. Tonpe was reinstated in Government service vide Government Order No.2 dated 28/03/2014.

Accordingly, in the said case, a departmental enquiry was ordered under Rule 8 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979, against Shri Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonpe, Senior Geologist vide Government Memorandum No. 3 dated 12/03/2015.
On examination of the allegations against Mr. Tonpe, Senior Geologist and the representations made by him and considering the findings of the enquiry report submitted by the Inquiry Officer vide letter dated 29/07/2016, it is observed that all the three allegations against Mr. B.R. Tonpe, Senior Geologist, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency are proved. Accordingly, vide order dated 06/01/2018 dated 06/04, it has been decided to withhold further increment of Mr. Tonape for a period of two years in respect of his departmental enquiry, without effecting any further increment, with the approval of the Disciplinary Authority.
Accordingly, the suspension of Mr. Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonpe, Senior Geologist, was revoked vide order dated 28/03/2014 dated 02.09.2014 and he was reinstated in Government service. It is concluded that Mr. Tonape's suspension was justified as the senior geologist was found guilty and sentenced.
Therefore, Mr. B.R. Tonpe, Senior Geologist has submitted an application to the Government to regularize his suspension period and compulsory waiting period.
Accordingly, the suspension of Mr. Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonpe, Senior Geologist, was revoked vide order dated 28/03/2014 dated 02.09.2014 and he was reinstated in Government service. However, he has since been given posting vide Government Order dated 20/05/2014. Therefore, Mr. Tonpe has remained without posting from 29/03/2014 till the date of actual joining of the post i.e. 21/05/2014.
Accordingly, in the above background, the Government have decided to regularize the period of suspension of Shri Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonpe, Senior Geologist, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency, by invoking the provisions of Rule 72 (a) and (b) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Assumption of Office, Non-Acceptance of Service and Removal from Service) Rules, 1981 and by imposing the mandatory period of suspension.

The period of waiting and the period of holding office is being decided by invoking the provisions of Rule No. 2 (a), Note No. 1 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (General Duty of Service) (Amendment) Rules, 2009 as follows:
(1) In exercise of the provisions of Rule 72 (a) and (b) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Appointment, Post-retirement Service and Provisions during Suspension, Expulsion and Removal) Rules, 1981, the period of suspension of Shri Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonape, Senior Geologist, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency, dated 02/07/2013 to 28/03/2014 is hereby declared as" Suspension Period." However, this suspension period is being considered for retirement benefits.
(2) After the suspension of Mr. Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonpe, Senior Geologist, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency is revoked, the entire period for which he was to be posted in the Government service (including the period of holding office) i.e. from 29/03/2014 to 21/05/2014 is the entire period without appointment, shall be governed by the provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Services (General Duty of Service) (Amendment) Rules, 2009, Rule No. 2 (a) is being regularized as" Compulsory Waiting Period" as per the provisions of Note No. Mr. Tonape shall be paid in full the salary and allowances to which he would have been entitled if he had been on duty. Also this period is being considered as duty period for all purposes.
Shri Bhalchandra Ramchandra Tonpe, Senior Geologist is presently working at District Senior Geologist Office, Ground Water Survey and Development System, Satara. Therefore, considering the amount due to Mr. Tonpe, Senior Geologist, the payment should be made from the Senior Geologist's Office, Ground Water Survey and Development System, Satara. This order is being issued in accordance with the provisions of Rule 72 (3) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Incumbency, Post-retirement Service and Provisions in respect of Suspension, Dismissal and Removal) Rules, 1981 and Rule 2 (a) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (General Duty of Service) (Amendment) Rules, 2009.
