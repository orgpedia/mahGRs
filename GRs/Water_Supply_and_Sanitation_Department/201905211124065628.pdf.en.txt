Preamble: The Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, a rural water supply programme of the State Government, is being implemented across the State over a period of four years from 2016-17 to 2019-20. Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme (No. 2215 9942) In the financial year 2019-20, a budgetary provision of Rs. 300.00 crore has been made. 100.00 crore has been provided. Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, the government was considering sanctioning and releasing grants to the respective district councils for the implementation of tap water supply schemes.
Government Decision: Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, the Osmanabad Zilla Parishad has been given administrative approval for the implementation of tap water supply schemes. A sum of Rs. 412.26 lakhs (Rupees Four Crore Twelve Lakh Twenty Six Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released on Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions:
1) Zilla Parishads should spend this grant only for the implementation of tap water supply schemes approved under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2019-20. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.

No.20/ PAPU-193) As per the guidelines given under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, the responsibility of conducting the third technical inspection of the works done before the implementation of the second and third phase of the tap water supply schemes and fulfilling the observations made will be the responsibility of the entire implementing agency.
4) Funds disbursed to the District Councils should not be kept in Personal Accounts (PLA) or such other accounts.
5) When the funds are sought under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program, the funds will not be sanctioned in the future without submitting information about the funds distributed in the current financial year, the expenses incurred, the balance funds, etc.
6) The physical progress of the works for which the funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai every month along with the monthly expenditure report. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
(7) The District Councils shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made out of this grant. The Accountant General, Maharashtra 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the officers authorized in this Department shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned implementing agencies for audit to ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme and the terms and conditions of this Government Decision dated 7th May, 2016 are fulfilled by the Zilla Parishads. Also, the concerned enforcement agencies will provide full support and facilitation to the Accountants General to enable them to perform their functions properly.
8) The expenditure of the grant should be reconciled with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office and the reconciliation report should be submitted to the Office of the concerned implementing agencies Papu 19/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai on the prescribed dates.
Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-19 Office.
9) The provisions of the Finance Department, Government Circular No. 44/ Finance-3, dated 01.04.2019 should be strictly followed. Also, the necessary certificates should be submitted to this department.
