(i) The Collector, Akola shall distribute the said grant to the respective Urban Local Bodies mentioned in the return as above by way of re-allocation.
(ii) Separate accounts shall be maintained of the amount of grants-in-aid sanctioned and released under the scheme.
(ii) The District Collector concerned shall re-allocate the grants sanctioned and released by the Government to the respective Municipal Corporations and Municipalities in the district and report the same to the Government forthwith.
iv) The above sanctioned and released amount to the concerned Municipal Corporations and Municipalities should be spent for the specific purpose for which it has been sanctioned and released, by 31st March, 2018.
(v) The Collector shall keep records of the year-wise details of the funds disbursed to the respective Urban Local Bodies in his district, the works completed, the incomplete works, the amount spent and the amount not spent, etc., and shall make such information available to the Government forthwith, as may be required.
(vi) The Auditor General of Maharashtra, Mumbai/ Nagpur or the officer of this department with special powers is being empowered to examine the accounts of expenditure made for these schemes by the concerned Municipal Corporations and Municipalities and the Collector from the grants sanctioned and released under this scheme. If the concerned documents are demanded by the Accountant General or the officer of the department given special powers, they should be immediately made available by the concerned municipal corporation and municipality and the collector.
(vii) As per Rule No. 3 of Annexure 2 to the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959, the accounts of the concerned Urban Local Bodies shall be submitted to the Accountant General by the concerned Municipal Corporation, Municipality and Collector for inspection along with relevant documents as per the demand of the Accountant General.

(vii) The concerned Municipal Corporation and the Principal Municipal Corporation and the District Collector shall submit to the Government the utilization certificate and the certificate of completion of the works of the grants received from the Government under this scheme. The chief municipal officer shall certify and sign the program-wise amounts of grants and expenditures, approved and released, indicated in the Utilization Certificate and Completion Certificate (COMPLETION CERTIFICATE). Utilization Certificate and Completion Certificate should be signed by the Collector. Such certificates will be acceptable to the government. Similarly, the certificate of completion of the work should also be sent to the Accountant General.
ix) The Collector shall ensure that the grants sanctioned and released to the concerned Urban Local Bodies under this scheme are utilized in the relevant financial year itself as per the guidelines of the scheme by following the prescribed procedure.
x) 100% expenditure on future maintenance and repair of works taken up under this scheme shall be borne by the respective Urban Local Bodies from their own funds. The government will not provide any subsidy for the maintenance and repair of this scheme.
(xi) The expenditure of the grants-in-aid sanctioned and released shall be reconciled with the expenditure reported to the Accountant General's Office and the said report shall be submitted to the Government on the prescribed date.
The necessary action should be taken in accordance with the instructions in the Government Decision issued under No. Nadpi-2004/ PR No. 28/ Papu-22, dated August 25, 2006, regarding the urban Dalit Vasti Water Supply Schemes.
Expenditure in the said case, vide Demand No.Y.22215, Water Supply and Sanitation, 01- Water Supply, 192- Assistance to Municipalities/ Municipal Councils (02) State Plan- (02) (10) Subsidiary Grants (Special Component Plan) for Urban Dalit Habitat Water Supply Schemes of Municipalities and Municipal Councils (2215 2062) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried), shall be covered under this head and shall be met from the current financial sanction.
