Preamble: Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has sanctioned an emergency water supply scheme for the water scarcity period of 2018-19 for Goregaon, Konati, Jaulka, Pimperkhed and Garkhed taluks. A proposal of Rs. 17,89,760/- has been submitted to the Government for a solution to fill tankers from a public well in Sindkhedraja, District Buldana. This proposal was considered for approval in the meeting held on 21st June, 2019 of the Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department to give administrative approval to the measures beyond the prescribed financial and physical parameters in urban and rural areas for addressing the drinking water shortage.

Given the nature of the proposal, it is not only proposed to build hydrants but also include a pipeline, pump etc. as a temporary water supply scheme. Therefore, the proposal should have been approved at the level of the Collector by June 15, 2019, as prescribed under the Government Decision, dated May 16, 2019. The standing committee considered the proposal in view of the delay in the matter. The Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department in its meeting held on 21st June, 2019 discussed the proposal in detail and approved the proposal. Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.
The decision was taken by the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati.
The proposal for grant of administrative approval for the scheme of filling tankers from the public well at Sindkhedraja, District Buldana at a cost of Rs. 17,89,760/- (Rupees Seventeen Lakhs Ninety-Nine Thousand Seven Hundred Sixty only) is at Reference No. 3 submitted to the government. The proposal for this price has been approved by the Standing Committee in its meeting dated 21st June, 2019. Accordingly, Goregaon, Konati, Julka, Pimparkhed, Garkhed, etc. Administrative approval is being accorded to the proposal for a measure to fill tankers from the public well at Sindkhedraja, District Buldana subject to the following conditions: 1) The supplementary water supply scheme mentioned in the proposal should be implemented by the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Buldana.
2) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
3) Ensure that the work is completed within the approved cost.
4) After the work mentioned in the proposal of the scheme is completed through the Zilla Parishad, Buldana, the maintenance of the scheme and the management of the scheme will be required to be done by the concerned Zilla Parishad.
5) The wells from which the water is currently being filled in the tankers, the wells should be inspected to increase the inflow of water by taking measures such as horizontal bore, deepening, etc.
6) Also, it should be verified whether the existing scheme pumping sets can be used at the public well at the proposed site. If it is possible to use the pump of the working scheme, then the pump appendages in the proposed work should be excluded.

7) The material required for this temporary scheme should be used if it is available in the heavy goods collection with the Zila Parishad/ Panchayat Samiti.
8) While verifying, the tender process should be continued, however, before giving work orders, priority should be given to complete the process and take less expensive measures, and accordingly work orders should be issued if necessary and if other options are not available. While giving work orders, it should be checked that if it rains and the shortage is over, then these shortage relief works should not be done.
9) The pipes, pumps, etc. used in the temporary plan to be approved should be removed for use in the same or another place next year after this shortage period and placed in the custody of the Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad, Buldana.
10) The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati should provide 50% of the funds at the beginning of the scheme after the tender process for the said scheme. After that 30% of the funds should be distributed at the level of the Divisional Commissioner taking into account the progress of the scheme. The remaining 20% will be paid after the project is completed.
11) Since the work of the scheme must be completed on time, regular progress reports should be sent to the government from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
After the completion of the scheme, the detailed report of the submitted scheme (including compliance of the above conditions) should be submitted to the government before July 31, 2019.
