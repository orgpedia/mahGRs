Preamble: Under the Central Government's ambitious Jal Jeevan Mission, the Government aims to provide safe and pure drinking water to the rural population as per the prescribed standards in water quality control and survey programmes. For this purpose, inspection and survey of all public drinking water sources in the State, including setting up of laboratories, strengthening of laboratories, NABL accreditation of laboratories, increasing the participation of women in water quality related work (mainly water quality survey), carrying out sanitation survey of public resources, etc.

In order to build confidence and create awareness about the quality of water being supplied to the rural population of the state, it is necessary to provide water testing facilities at affordable prices to encourage private sampling in addition to public water testing campaigns. The testing rates have been fixed as per the Government Decision dated 28th March, 2018 for testing of private water samples in laboratory. As per the guidelines of Jal Jeevan Mission, to bring uniformity in the testing rates of samples received from private individuals/ institutions in the country, the rates have been fixed under the above Reference No. (05) dated 19.11.2020. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the government to revise the fee to be charged for testing private water samples in the laboratory under the Ground Water Survey and Development System, as well as to determine the policy for preliminary testing by the private samples FTK received in the laboratory.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Government decision dated 28th March, 2018 regarding the fee to be charged for testing of private water samples is being revised.
1) Fee and prescribed component for testing of private water samples in laboratory. As per the instructions in the letter dated November 19, 2020, the charges for testing of private water samples in the laboratory under Ground Water Survey and Development System for testing are being revised and the revised rates are being determined as per Annexure A attached.
2) Fee and prescribed components for testing of private water samples by FTKs: Rs. The facility is being made available in all the divisional, district and sub-divisional laboratories of the state at a nominal rate of Rs 5/-.

The above rates for FTKs shall be applicable for Government/ Cooperative/ Private Institutions/ Educational Institutions/ Schools/ Colleges, Research Scholars and persons residing in rural areas.
3) Field Test Kits Material and Procurement Required for Laboratories: To provide testing to the people of the state with the help of private sample field test kits, 300 nos of biological and 04 nos of chemical field test kits are required for each laboratory considering the period of one year (25 private water samples per month). Accordingly, the Director, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency will be responsible for the distribution of field test kits to each laboratory.
The field test kits received by the laboratories under the Ground Water Survey and Development System should be recorded in the register. As per the requirement, the monthly report using field test kits should be submitted in the prescribed format to the Deputy Director and Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad with the signature of a senior geologist and the Deputy Director should submit the consolidated monthly report of the department to the Director, Ground Water Survey and Development System, Pune.
Procedure for testing of water samples on private basis: 1. The laboratory should take the information of the samples received from the interested persons, government/ private institutions, colleges, research students in the prescribed format in the form of application (Form-B) from the concerned.
2. Access to private water samples should be provided by charging the required fee.
The amount received within a week should be handed over to the accounting branch in the senior geologist's office.
4. The amount received for testing of private samples from the laboratory should be deposited in the account of the government as prescribed every month by the Accounts Branch in the Senior Geologist's Office.
The report of the water samples examined by the laboratory should be prepared in form (a), signed by the concerned senior geologist every week and sent/ given to the concerned person, government/ private organization, college, research student.

5) Note on the website: It will be the responsibility of the concerned laboratories to take the information of the number of field test kits received and the private samples examined by the kits on the website of the Central Government.
Senior geologists at the district level, regional deputy director at the divisional level and deputy director (hydrology project) at the state level will control the inspection.
6) Information, Education and Communication Campaign: It is very important to spread awareness about water sample testing on a private basis to reach out to all rural population. Awareness should be created on this subject in coordination with the Water Quality Cell and Information, Education and Communication Consultant in the Zilla Parishad Office. Below are the issues that should be addressed.
The information about the water quality laboratory should be displayed in the form of a banner/ flex at important places in the district, taluka and village. The information should primarily include the location of the laboratory, contact number, facilities available in the laboratory and the rate charged for testing water samples on a private basis.
The rates and laboratories for testing water samples on private basis should be publicized through All India Radio/ other media.
The cost of water quality awareness (IEC) should be met from the provision made under the Jal Jeevan Mission Assistance Fund.
The Director, Ground Water Survey and Development Agency should send a quarterly report to the Government on the increase in testing of water samples on a private basis as a result of public awareness (IEC) on the subject.
7) The groundwater survey and development system should submit a comprehensive review of the response received from the citizens for testing private water samples through FTKs and the actual implementation process after one year to the government.
8) Funding& Distribution: Funds required for procurement of Field Test Kits for testing of water samples on private basis should be spent from 2% funds of Water Quality Monitoring& Surveying programme under Jal Jeevan Mission.
