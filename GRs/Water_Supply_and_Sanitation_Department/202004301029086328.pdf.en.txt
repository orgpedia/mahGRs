The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2020 has been approved by the Legislative Assembly and assented to by the Governor of Maharashtra and is published as Maharashtra Act No. 15 in the Government of Maharashtra, Gazette Extraordinary Part-IV, dated 18th March, 2020. However, the Department of Finance has released the Budget Estimates for the first three months (April, 2020 to June, 2020) of the Budget Estimates for the Financial Year 2020-21 through the Budget Estimates, Distribution and Control System (BEMS) in the range of 15-25% of the total annual outlay.
The Department of Finance has sanctioned an amount of Rs. 4.7490 crore for the financial year 2020-21 under the head Demand No.Y-2" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (2215 1843) for the implementation of Water Supply Programme.

Considering the total expenditure on the treasury of the respective Zilla Parishads in 2019-20, the Government is considering to disburse the said grant-in-aid to the Zilla Parishads for three months from April, 2020 to June, 2020.
Government's decision:
For the financial year 2020-21, a total amount of Rs.4, 74,90,000/- (Rupees Four Crore Seventy Four Lakh Ninety Nine Thousand only) is being sanctioned to the concerned Chief Accounts and Finance Officer for meeting the salary expenditure of Class I and Class II officers on the establishment of Zilla Parishad Rural Water Supply Department under the head" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 01, Water Supply 001, Director and Administration (01) (01) Divisional/ Sub Divisional Establishment (Mandatory) for implementation of Water Supply Programme (2215 1843)."
(2) The grants sanctioned under this Government Resolution shall be subject to such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Government from time to time. Accordingly, action should be taken for payment of salaries as per the procedure mentioned in the Government Circular No. 2020/ No. 62/ Koshasra-5, dated 1st April, 2020 of the Finance Department for the period mentioned therein. Considering the overall economic situation of the State in view of the health and economic disaster caused by COVID-19, it is necessary to curtail expenditure and defer some expenditure.
3. All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers shall ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts shown in the' Return A" accompanying this Government decision. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
(4) To ensure that the necessary funds are sought further by ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said Government decision is submitted to the Government.
