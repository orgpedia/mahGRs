Considering the fact mentioned in the preamble of the government decision, 55 litres per capita per day in 15 villages (G. Chandrapur) of Pombhurna taluka is Rs. 5911/- per annum for water supply scheme. 29,94,67, 197/- (Rupees Thirty-nine Crore Four-nine Lakhs Sixty-six Thousand One Hundred and Ninety-seven only) is being accorded administrative approval under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions. The scheme will be implemented by the Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation.
The contractor will be required to operate the project for at least one year after the completion of the project. The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Chandrapur shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, and the maintenance of the scheme is carried out by the concerned No. 6 Follow the guidelines in the Government Resolution dated 17th October, 2014.
Although the scheme will be run for at least one year through the contractor after the implementation of the scheme, the water strip of even this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water should be given to the agency running the strip scheme.
(iii) The works of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Bids with higher prices will not be accepted.
(iv) The administrative approval granted under this Government Order shall be deemed to be cancelled if the scheme is given a work order without obtaining the necessary land acquisition and all other permissions for the scheme.
V. 100% household tap connections and meters should be included in the scheme. Also, at least 80 per cent of the tap connection holders should take an undertaking to get the meter connection at their own cost and a letter of the same should be laminated and given to the tap connection holder and a copy should be kept with the Gram Panchayat. Thereafter, the orders were issued.
(vi) If a villager refuses to take water even after the Gram Sabha resolution/ assurance letter is given, at least 50% of the water table should be recovered from him. In this regard, the villagers should be given an idea before the tender process and the Gram Sabha should take a resolution in this regard.
The scheme should be included in the central government's IMIS system.
(viii) The works of the scheme should be completed in a phased manner in a time bound and planned manner at the approved cost. No other work should be started until the plan is developed.
Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.

IX. To meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the Gram Panchayat concerned shall fix the minimum rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and increase it from time to time. Consent of the villagers is required for the water tariff. If the villagers do not accept the water tariff, tenders should not be floated.
X. 20% of the recoverable water table should be kept for major maintenance repairs. The maintenance of the pipes of the scheme should be done every 5 years and the repair of the other pipes should be done every 3 years.
No assistance/ grant will be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
XII. All the villages covered under the scheme shall be ensured that they are free from open defecation.
XIII. No subsidy shall be provided by the Government in case of increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason.
XIV. The conditions contained in the order of technical approval of the said scheme shall be complied with. Also referred to as No. 2 The provisions of the government decision here should be strictly followed.
XV. It shall be mandatory to carry out the third technical examination of the scheme as per the provisions of the Government Decision dated 15th June, 2015 of this Department.
XVI. It shall be obligatory on the part of the Gram Panchayat to identify the water table required for the scheme to be self-sufficient and to increase it as per the need, the officers of the Maharashtra Life Authority shall clearly inform the villagers in the Gram Sabha about this and only then the work of the scheme should be started.
XVII. On completion of the scheme, the concerned implementing agency shall be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
XVIII. If any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice of any kind is noticed in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity shall be liable for disciplinary action.
It shall be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme.
