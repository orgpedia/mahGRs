The plan is being accorded administrative approval under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
(1) The scheme shall be implemented by the District Council. It will be mandatory for the contractor to run the scheme for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as the maintenance of the scheme is carried out for the repair of the reference No. 5 here. Follow the guidelines issued by the government on October 17, 2014.
The CAG report of 20013 shows that 21% of the water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. The presented scheme is not self-sufficient in terms of maintenance repair. Therefore, the water tariff should be increased as per requirement to make the scheme financially viable. The responsibility of this will be of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad.
3) Even if the scheme will be run by the contractor for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme, the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
4) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
A committee should be formed under the chairmanship of the Collector to obtain land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme. The committee will review the status of railway/ road/ forest land crossings and land acquisition every month.
6) 100% household tap connections and meters should be included in the scheme. Also, at least 80% of the tap connection holders should take an undertaking for getting the meter connection at their own cost and a copy of it should be laminated and given to the tap connection holder and a copy should be kept with the Gram Panchayat. After that, the order should be executed.
7) If a village refuses to take water even after the gram sabha resolution/ assurance letter, at least 50 per cent of the water supply should be recovered from it. In this regard, the villagers should be given an idea before the tender process and the Gram Sabha should take a resolution in this regard.
8) The existing schemes in the villages covered under the scheme should not be run so that the scheme is not used seasonally.
9) The village under the scheme should be included on the central government's IMIS system. The project work should be completed in a time-bound and planned manner in a phased manner.
No other work should be started until the plan is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
10) An agreement should be made on stamp paper with each gram panchayat to take water from the scheme in bulk.
