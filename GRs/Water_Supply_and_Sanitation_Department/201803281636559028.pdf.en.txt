Preamble: The Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, a rural water supply programme of the State Government, is being implemented across the State over a period of four years from 2016-17 to 2019-20. A sum of Rs. 227.13 crore has been provided for the financial year 2017-18 under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme (Account Head No.22159942). Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, the government was considering sanctioning and releasing grants to the respective district councils for the implementation of tap water supply schemes.
Government Decision: Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, for the implementation of the tap water supply schemes that have been given administrative approval, the concerned District Councils will be given Rs. 5311.10 lakhs (Rupees Fifty Three Crores Eleven Lakhs Ten Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released on Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions:
1) Zilla Parishads should spend this grant only for the implementation of tap water supply schemes approved under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.

Government Resolution No: MUPAYO-2017/ PR No.13/ PAPU-19 (SIKHANA) 2) The fund is to be spent in the financial year 2017-18. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
3) The funds disbursed to the Zila Parishads should not be kept in the personal provident fund (PLA) or any other such account.
4) When the funds are sought under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program, the funds will not be sanctioned in the future without submitting information about the funds distributed in the current financial year, the expenses incurred, the balance funds, etc.
5) The physical progress of the works for which funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai every month along with the monthly expenditure report. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
(6) The District Councils shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made out of this grant. The Accountant General, Maharashtra 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the authorized officers of this Department shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned implementing agencies for audit to ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme and the terms and conditions of this Government Decision dated 7th May, 2016 are fulfilled by the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Also, the concerned enforcement agencies will provide full support and facilitation to the Accountants General to enable them to perform their functions properly.
7) The report of the expenditure of the subsidy should be reconciled with the expenditure registered in the Accountant General's Office and the report of the reconciliation should be submitted to the Office Papu-19/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai by the concerned implementing agencies on the prescribed dates. Utilization certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu 19 office.
8) Department of Finance, Government Circular No.Finance-2017/ PR No. 75/ Finance-3, dated 18.04. Strictly follow the rules of 2017. Also, the necessary certificates should be submitted to this department.
