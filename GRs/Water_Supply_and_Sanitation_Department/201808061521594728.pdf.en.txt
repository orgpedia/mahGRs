Preamble: With a view to provide pure and adequate drinking water to the rural population of the State. 2 The Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme is being implemented in the state from 2016-17 to 2019-20. A budget provision of Rs. 325.00 crore has been made for the financial year 2018-19 for implementation of new water supply schemes to be taken up under the programme (Head of Accounts No.22159942). 227.50 crore has been provided.
Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, the government was considering sanctioning and releasing grants for the implementation of water supply schemes in Gadchiroli district.
Government Decision: Rs. A sum of Rs. 1014.65 lakhs (Rupees Ten Crore Fourteen Lakh Sixty Five Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released on Budget Distribution System (BDS) subject to the following conditions:
1) The Zilla Parishad, Gadchiroli should spend this grant only for the implementation of tap water supply schemes approved under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.

Government Resolution No: MUPAYO-2018/ PR No.125/ PAPU-192) The funds are to be spent during the financial year 2018-19. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
(3) The funds disbursed to the Zila Parishad shall not be kept in the Personal Provident Fund (PLA) or any other such account.
4) When the funds are sought under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program, the funds will not be sanctioned in the future without submitting information about the funds distributed in the current financial year, the expenses incurred, the balance funds, etc.
5) The physical progress of the works for which funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai every month along with the monthly expenditure report. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office that collects and disburses the grants in accordance with the procedures set out in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
(6) The District Councils shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made out of this grant. The Accountant General, Maharashtra 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the authorized officers of this Department shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned implementing agencies for audit to ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme and the terms and conditions of this Government Decision dated 7th May, 2016 are fulfilled by the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Also, the concerned enforcement agencies will provide full support and facilitation to the Accountants General to enable them to perform their functions properly.
7) The expenditure of the grant should be reconciled with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office and the reconciliation report should be submitted to the Office of the concerned implementing agencies Papu-19/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai on the prescribed dates.
Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-19 Office.
8) The provisions of the Finance Department, Government Circular No. Finance-2018/ P.S. No. 69/ Finance-3, dated 02.04.2018 should be strictly followed. Also, the necessary certificates should be submitted to this department.
