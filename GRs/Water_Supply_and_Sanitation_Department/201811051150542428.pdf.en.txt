The National Rural Drinking Water Programme is a centrally sponsored programme in the state. In FY 2018-19, Demand No. Y-02, Head of Accounts 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation, 01, Water Supply, 102, Rural Water Supply Programme, (02) Grants under Schemes to Zilla Parishads, (02) (15) Grants for Piped Water Supply Scheme-National Rural Drinking Water Programme (State), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payment) (2215 2071).
The State Government has decided to allocate Rs. A grant of Rs. 10.00 crore (Rupees Ten Crores only) is being sanctioned and released to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Mumbai through Budgetary System for Coverage (BEAMS) subject to the following conditions.
The grant is to be spent by the implementing agencies on priority for completion of works in the villages/ hamlets covered under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDP) as per the Annual Action Plan (AAP) and the progress of the Centrally Sponsored Schemes in which Government participation is mandatory.

The funds are to be spent in the financial year 2018-19. Please ensure that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
The Government may ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme are fulfilled by the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority, so that the Accountant General, Maharashtra- 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur, as well as the authorized officers of this Department, shall have the right to see the books and accounts of the implementing agencies for audit. Also, the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran will provide full support and facilities to the Accountant General to carry out his work properly.
The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall reconcile the expenditure of the said grant with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office and submit the report of the reconciliation to the Office of the Commissioner, Papu-10/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai on the prescribed date. Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-10 Office.
The expenditure for the schemes taken up under the coverage as per the guidelines of National Rural Drinking Water Programme shall be 50% from Central Fund and 50% from State Fund.
7. The records of expenditure on Central and State accounts should be updated periodically by taking them on the IMIS system without fail. A Nodal Officer should be appointed to record the expenditure incurred under the programme on the IMIS system. If the information is not updated on the IMIS system, the responsibility should be fixed on the concerned NODEL OFFICER.
The funds should be spent only for the schemes related to the progress of the villages included in the action plan. Also, plan your time and money accordingly to make the most of your available funds.
The terms and conditions of the Government Circular, Department of Finance dated 02.04.2018 in respect of the aforesaid funds should be strictly adhered to. Also, the necessary certificates should be submitted to this department.
