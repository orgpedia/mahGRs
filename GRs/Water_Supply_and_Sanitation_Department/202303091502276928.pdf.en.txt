Introduction: The state of Maharashtra has a high rate of groundwater depletion. Groundwater is mostly used for irrigation of crops.
As a result, the ground water level in some parts of the state is decreasing day by day. So the catchment areas in such areas fall under the category of over-exploited, exploited, partially exploited. The extraction of more groundwater through bore wells than recharge is affecting the availability and quality of groundwater.
Atal Bhujal Yojana is being implemented in 1442 villages of 13 districts of the state with financial assistance from the Central Government and the World Bank under the relevant government decision to strengthen participatory groundwater management, through effective implementation of demand and supply management system to stop the decline in groundwater level and deteriorating quality due to uncontrolled depletion of groundwater.
Ground water management through public participation is the core of Atal Bhujal Yojana, for which, preparation of village level water records, greater convergence of existing state and centrally sponsored schemes to meet the deficit in ground water availability

The objective of the scheme is to achieve sustainability in ground water availability at village level.
In order to ensure that more and more people of the villages involved in the implementation of the Atal Bhujal Yojana participate in the implementation of the Atal Bhujal Yojana and everyone should participate actively to maintain the groundwater system in the village, in order to create a strong competition among the villages and to achieve the main breath of the Atal Bhujal Yojana" Ground Water Management through People's Participation," the government was considering to start a competition under the name of" Ground Water Prosperous Village" under the Atal Bhujal Yojana by giving incentive funds to the villages doing excellent work at the district level and at the state level and to the officers, employees and the District Participating Organizations (DIPs) appointed in the Atal Bhujal Yojana by awarding them with a certificate and a mark of appreciation. Accordingly, considering the prosperity of the villages through groundwater management, the government is taking the following decisions to organize the Groundwater Rich Village Competition.
Government Decision- The Government is approving the launch of Groundwater Enriched Village Competition for creating a strong competitive environment for greater participation of people in the implementation of the scheme in the villages covered under Atal Bhujal Yojana, preparation of water security plans and implementation of works included in it, convergence of State and Central sponsored schemes and awarding of Incentive Funds to the villages doing excellent work at the district level and state level and awarding of certificates and honorarium to the officers, employees and District Participating Institutions (DIPs) appointed in Atal Bhujal Yojana.
(i) The scope and duration of the Ground Water Enriched Village Competition will be applicable only to the villages covered under the Atal Ground Water Scheme. The Ground Water Enriched Village Competition will be conducted for two financial years 2022-23 and 2023-24 and every year, the eligible villages that come first, second and third at the state and district level and the officials, employees and District Participating Institutions (DIPs) appointed in the Atal Ground Water Scheme will be given awards or certificates and honorarium to be given in accordance with this competition.

(ii) Availability and utilization of funds: Funds required for State level and District level prizes and for necessary publicity, publicity and programmes under Ground Water Rich Village Competition are being sanctioned from the incentive funds received by the State under Atal Ground Water Scheme. The amount will be disbursed to the respective Gram Panchayats through the Project Director, Atal Bhujal Yojana, Maharashtra and Commissioner, Ground Water Survey and Development System.
The Gram Panchayats concerned may, with the approval of the Gram Sabha, spend the funds received from the Ground Water Rich Village Competition on any water conservation, groundwater recharge or water saving works for which funds have not been received or received from other schemes or funds are not sufficient for the works approved in the Water Security Plan. The award money cannot be spent on any work that is not approved in the water security plan.
3) Eligibility and evaluation method for the competition: a) All villages included in the Atal Bhujal Yojana can participate in this competition. The villages participating in the competition should apply to the District Senior Geologist within one month of the current year/ by September 15, 2023 of the next year with a copy of the decision by the Water Supply and Sanitation Committee.
(b) Taluk wise list of villages desirous of participating in the competition should be finalized by the Senior Geologist within 15 days from the date of acceptance of application. The list of the villages should be submitted to the sub-divisional level committee.
(c) Each participating village will be assessed on the basis of the progress of works done by the Sub-Divisional Level Committee till the end of May every year as per the score given in Annexure-I. According to the first year
