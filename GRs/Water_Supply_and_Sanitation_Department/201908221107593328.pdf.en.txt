Reference No. for measures taken/ to be taken during the period of scarcity 2018-19 The Department has received a proposal for seeking funds under letter No. 4 to 6. For the financial year 2019-20, the Government was considering to disburse the funds demanded by the Divisional Commissioner, Konkan and Pune from the funds disbursed by the Revenue and Forest (Relief and Rehabilitation) Department to this department through its Reference No. 7 Government Resolution.
Government's decision:
As per the norms laid down by the Government for the solution of drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas, a sum of Rs. 1864.00 lakhs (Rupees Eighteen Crores Sixty Four Lakhs only) is being sanctioned and released to the Divisional Commissioner, Konkan and Pune through the Budgetary Distribution System subject to the following conditions and terms and conditions as provided in the Standing Government Resolution issued to the Divisional Commissioners, Konkan and Pune under the head' Expenditure on measures taken/ to be taken for the solution of drinking water scarcity in their respective areas'.
Terms and Conditions
1) The Divisional Commissioner, Konkan and Pune should directly distribute the funds to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad for taking measures under the shortage in rural areas.
2) Urban measures which are being implemented through Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils.
For this, the Divisional Commissioner should distribute it to the concerned District Collector. The Collector should disburse the funds to the concerned Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council.
The funds should be released for the implementation of the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in rural/ urban areas for the scarcity period 2018-19 and for the payment of pending dues during this period. Care should be taken to ensure that the funds are not used for other short-term measures.
4) The Divisional Commissioner should allocate funds on priority to the measures like well acquisition, tanker water supply and even if the proposal submitted by the Divisional Commissioner does not ask for the measures approved by the government, these measures should also be allocated on priority in accordance with the order of the concerned administrative approval.
