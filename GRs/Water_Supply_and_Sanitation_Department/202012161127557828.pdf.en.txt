The Proposal:
A budget provision of Rs. 10.1305 crore has been made for the financial year 2020-21 under the head 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) to meet the non-salaried expenditure on establishment of Rural Water Supply Department in Zila Parishads vide Demand No.Y-2" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 196, Assistance to Zila Parishads (01) Assistance to Zila Parishads/ Panchayat Institutions, Establishment Grants to Zila Parishads under Section 183 of the Maharashtra Zila Parishads and Panchayat Samitis Act, 1961, (01) (05) Regular Establishment (Compulsory) (2215 1881).

Government's decision:
For the financial year 2020-2021, a total grant of Rs. 1,01,30, 500/- (Rupees One Crore One Lakh Thirty Five Thousand Five Hundred only) is being sanctioned to the concerned Chief Accounts and Finance Officer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad as shown in the statement' A' accompanying this Government decision for the Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payment) under the head" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 196, Assistance to Zilla Parishads (01), Assistance to Zilla Parishads/ Panchayat Institutions, Establishment Grants to Zilla Parishads under Section 183 of the Maharashtra Zilla Parishad and Panchayat Samiti Act, 1961 (01) (05) Regular Establishment (Mandatory) (22151881)."
2. The said fund is being released subject to the deposit of unutilized funds of 2019-20 under the aforesaid heads to the Government and the funds shall not be drawn from the treasury unless the unutilized funds are deposited with the Government. The non-salaried fund should be utilised by the respective Zila Parishads for meeting travel expenses, office expenses, lease, motor vehicles, petrol/ oil/ lubricants etc.
The grants sanctioned under this Government decision shall be subject to the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government from time to time. Considering the overall economic situation of the State in view of the health and economic disaster caused by COVID-19, it is necessary to curtail expenditure and defer some expenditure.
4. All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers shall ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts shown in the statement A accompanying this Government decision. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
(5) Care should be taken to demand further necessary funds only after ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said Government decision is submitted to the Government. The concerned District Councils should note that the funds will not be disbursed until the next demand letter is received. The subsidy will be credited to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
