The proposal-
In view of the drought situation in the State, it has been decided to clear the pending electricity dues of rural and urban water supply schemes which have been closed due to non-payment of electricity dues, by paying 5% of the principal amount, to start the supply of electricity and tap water supply and to pay the ongoing electricity dues of rural and urban areas for the period of 8 months from November, 2018 to June, 2019 in the areas declared as drought. Accordingly, the Revenue and Forest departments have released funds to the department to meet the expenditure on these measures. The government was planning to distribute the funds to the districts.
Government Decision- Power dues of tap water supply schemes in areas declared drought by the Government and rural and urban areas for a period of 8 months from November, 2018 to June, 2019

A sum of Rs. 2450.00 lakhs (Rupees Twenty Four Crores Fifty Lakhs only) is being released to the concerned Divisional Commissioners to meet the cost of current electricity bills, subject to the following conditions.
1) The drinking water schemes in rural and urban areas in areas where the government has declared drought in the state are closed due to power supply disruption due to non-payment of outstanding electricity dues. Also, the Divisional Commissioner should immediately make available to the concerned Chief Executive Officer of the Zilla Parishad an amount equal to 5 percent of the outstanding electricity dues of the water supply schemes for such schemes where drinking water can be supplied to the people after the scheme is launched.
The funds should be spent on payment of ongoing electricity dues for a period of 8 months from November, 2018 to June, 2019 for all rural and urban water supply schemes in the areas declared as drought.
(3) The funds shall be utilised for meeting the current dues during the period of shortage only after the balance amount is utilised for meeting 5% of the outstanding dues of the schemes for which water supply is to be started by paying the electricity dues.
4) After paying the outstanding electricity dues, the scheme-wise report should be submitted to the government on every Monday of the week.
5) While orders have been issued from time to time to ensure that the funds available on the budget distribution system are not unnecessarily drawn from the treasury and lying in the bank in the absence of allocation, strict instructions are again being given under this government decision not to withdraw the amount from the treasury and keep it in the bank. If such a thing is found, it should be noted that it will be considered a temporary violation and disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned. All the Zila Parishads are being instructed to ensure that the funds are required to be spent in the current financial year itself and do not lapse or are not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
