Preamble Mauje Fardapur under National Rural Drinking Water Programme, Ta. Proposal for Administrative Approval for Budget and Plan for Increased Per Capita Cost of Tap Water Supply Scheme at Soygaon, G. Aurangabad Subject to the approval of No. 1 has been sent to the Government for approval. The Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Aurangabad has accorded technical approval to the project at an estimated cost of Rs. 927.32 lakh (approx.). Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
Mauje Fardapur Rs. 4060 with an additional cost of Rs. Administrative Sanction is being accorded to the project and the cost of Rs. 9,27,32, 100/- (Rupees Nine Crore Twenty Seven Lakh Thirty Two Thousand Hundred only) subject to the following conditions:

The scheme will be implemented by Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Aurangabad. The contractor will be responsible for the maintenance and repair of the plan for at least 1 year after the implementation of the plan.
2) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
(3) No work order shall be issued for the scheme unless the land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme have been obtained. If the order is issued without fulfilling these conditions, the administrative approval given under this government decision will be considered canceled.
(iv) The works of the scheme should be completed in a time bound and planned manner in a phased manner at the approved cost.
5) No other work should be started until the emergence of the plan. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.
6) The details of expenditure shown in the Detailed Project Report for operation and maintenance of the scheme should be corrected and kept to a minimum. Also, to meet the expenditure and to make the scheme financially self-sustaining, the concerned Gram Panchayat should fix the minimum water tariff for the household tap holder and increase it from time to time.
No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for running, testing and maintenance of the scheme.
8) Strengthening of existing resources.
9) If there is an increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason, the government will not provide subsidy.
10) Refer No. 4 Openness policy should be implemented in accordance with the provisions of the Government Decision of January 22, 2014.
11) The conditions of the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled. Also, it will be mandatory to conduct a third technical examination of the scheme as per the provisions of the existing government decision.
12) It will be mandatory for the Gram Panchayat to determine the necessary water table for the scheme to be self-sufficient and increase it as per the need. In this regard, the officials of the zilla parishad should give detailed information to the villagers in the gram sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.

13) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency will be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
