The Proposal:
Budget provision of Rs. 244.00 crore has been made for the financial year 2020-21 under the head 36, Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) to meet the non-salary expenditure on establishment of Rural Water Supply Department in Zila Parishads vide Demand No.Y-2" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 196, Assistance to Zila Parishads (01) Assistance to Zila Parishads/ Panchayat Institutions, Establishment Grants to Zila Parishads under Section 183 of the Maharashtra Zila Parishads and Panchayat Samitis Act, 1961 (01) (05) Regular Establishment (Mandatory) (22151881). Out of this, a total grant of Rs. 46.80 crore has been released to the Zila Parishads for payment of salaries.

Decisions of this Department have been issued under the Decisions dated 30.4.2020 and 11.6.2020.
However, since the salary subsidy to the Zila Parishads is insufficient, the matter of disbursement of subsidy to the Zila Parishads for the next salary expenditure is under consideration of the Government, taking into account the total salary expenditure on the treasury of the respective Zila Parishads for the year 2020-21.
Government's decision:
For the financial year 2020-21, a total grant of Rs. 14,20,00, 000/- (Rupees Fourteen Crores Twenty Lakhs only) as shown in the Statement A accompanying this Government Decision, under the head" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 196, Assistance to Zilla Parishads (01) Assistance to Zilla Parishads/ Panchayat Institutions, Establishment Grants to Zilla Parishads under Section 183 of the Maharashtra Zilla Parishad and Panchayat Samiti Act, 1961, 36, Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) under the head (01) (05) Regular Establishment (Mandatory) (22151881)" is being sanctioned to the concerned Chief Accounts and Finance Officer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad.
(2) The grants sanctioned under this Government Resolution shall be subject to such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Government from time to time. Considering the overall economic situation of the State in view of the health and economic disaster caused by COVID-19, it is necessary to curtail expenditure and defer some expenditure.
(3) All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers shall ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts shown in the statement A accompanying this Government decision. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
4. Care should be taken to demand further necessary funds only after ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said decision is submitted to the government. The concerned District Councils should note that the funds will not be disbursed until the next demand letter is received. The subsidy is transferred to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through ECS.
