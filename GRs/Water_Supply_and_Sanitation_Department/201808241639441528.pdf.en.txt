Central Government letter No. W- 11020/197/2015-Water- 1/150, dated 15.03.2018 Preamble In the financial year 2018-19, in respect of Central share of grant-in-aid received from the Central Government under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDP), a demand No. Y-02, Accounts Head 2215 Water Supply and Sanitation, 789, Special Component Plan for Scheduled Castes, 01, Schemes under Scheduled Castes Appropriation, (01) (01) National Rural Drinking Water Programme (Scheduled Castes Sub-Plan) (Central Share), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) (2215 9844) The Department of Finance is of the opinion that the funds received from the Central Government under the above mentioned sub-scheme during the financial year 2017-18 have been credited to the Consolidated Fund of the State as at the end of the financial year on 16.03.2018. The proposal was under consideration of the Government to sanction the funds for rural water supply schemes to the implementing agencies during the financial year 2018-19.
Government Decision: In the financial year 2018-19, a subsidy coverage of Rs. 25.4898 crore (Rs. Twenty five crore forty eight lakh ninety eight thousand only) under the head 2215 9844 for the above mentioned components for tap water supply schemes is being sanctioned and released to the concerned implementing agencies through the Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) as per the Statement-A attached with this Government Decision for this component on the following terms.

Conditions: (1) This fund shall be spent in proportion to the population under the Scheduled Castes Appropriation.
(11) The concerned implementing agencies are to spend this grant in the financial year 2018-19 only under the Scheduled Castes Appropriation as per the instructions of the Central Government for coverage of tap water supply schemes in rural habitations having majority of/ dominated by SC population as per the instructions of the Central Government and the State Government from time to time.
(iii) The respective implementing agencies shall maintain separate accounts of expenditure incurred from these grants.
(iv) The Accountant General, Maharashtra- 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur as well as the officer authorised in this section shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned enforcement agencies for audit so as to ensure that the above conditions are complied with by the concerned enforcement agencies.
The concerned enforcement agencies shall provide full support and facilitation to the Accountant General to enable him to discharge his functions in a proper manner.
Government Circular, Rural Development and Water Conservation Department, No. As per the instructions in RCN-189/ CR 3027/28, dated 05/09/90, the expenditure of the said grant should be reconciled with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office and the reconciliation report should be submitted to the Divisional Commissioner (Development Branch), by the concerned implementing agencies on the prescribed dates and to the Office of the Commissioner, Papu-10/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai. Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-10 Office.
(vi) As per the guidelines of the National Rural Drinking Water Programme, the expenditure for the schemes undertaken under the coverage component is required to be 50% from the Central Fund and 50% from the State Fund.
(vii) The expenditure incurred on account of Central and State share should be recorded on the IMIS system without fail as under and the same should be updated from time to time as under:

(IX) First of all, all the expenditure made for rural water supply schemes from the funds made available earlier to all the district councils of the state should be first updated on the IMIS system. Also, a time-bound programme should be chalked out to utilise the funds made available and expenditure plans should be made and utilisation certificates should be submitted to the government in time.
