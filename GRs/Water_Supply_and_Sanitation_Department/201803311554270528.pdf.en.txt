Administrative approval has been given to revive 18 regional water supply schemes that were closed under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme. Out of these 18 schemes, 17 schemes are being implemented by Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme, Rs. A sum of Rs. 86.82 lakh has been provided in the Revised Estimates. Accordingly, the matter of providing funds for the revival of 17 regional schemes of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran was under consideration of the government.
The government has decided to implement 17 schemes under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme. 86,82,000/- (Rupees Eighty Seven Lakhs Eighty Two Thousand only) is being sanctioned and released by the Director Finance, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Mumbai through Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) subject to the following conditions.
Conditions: 1) The fund is to be spent in the financial year 2017-18. It should be ensured that it does not lapse or is not withdrawn from the treasury and deposited in the bank account.
2) Funds disbursed to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should not be kept in personal provident account (PLA) or any other such account.

(3) No funds will be sanctioned in the future without submitting the information about the funds distributed, the expenditure incurred, the balance funds, etc. in the current financial year while seeking funds under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Program.
4) The physical progress of the works for which funds have been disbursed should be submitted to the Director, Water and Sanitation Support Society, Belapur, Navi Mumbai every month along with the monthly expenditure report. The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser, in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the prescribed certificate should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the controlling officer should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances.
5) The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran should maintain separate accounts of the expenditure made from this grant. The Accountant General, Maharashtra 1/2, Mumbai/ Nagpur and the authorized officers of this Department shall have the right to inspect the books and accounts of the concerned implementing agencies for audit to ensure that the guidelines laid down by the Central Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme and the terms and conditions of this Government Decision dated 7th May, 2016 are fulfilled by the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Also, the concerned enforcement agencies will provide full support and facilitation to the Accountants General to enable them to perform their functions properly.
6) The expenditure of the grant should be reconciled with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office and the reconciliation report should be submitted to the Office of the concerned implementing agencies Papu 19/ Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai on the prescribed dates.
Similarly, the utility certificate should be submitted to the Accountant General's Office, Mumbai/ Nagpur and Papu-19 Office.
7) The provisions of the Finance Department, Government Circular No. Finance-2017/ P.S. No. 75/ Finance-3, dated 18.04.2017 should be strictly followed. Also, the necessary certificates should be submitted to this department.
