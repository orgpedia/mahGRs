The Proposal:
The Government has, vide Government Resolution dated 23.3.2017, taken over the responsibility of payment of salary, allowances and pension to the employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority and has released a total amount of Rs. 91.90 crore to the Authority for the financial year 2021-22 under the head 2215A052 as grant to meet the expenditure on pension of retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority. Now the matter of disbursing funds for further expenditure under the above head of accounts is under consideration of the Government.

Government Resolution No: Budget 1421/ PR No.71/ Papu-23
Government's decision:
In the financial year 2021-22, a grant of Rs. 101,93,00, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred and One Crore and Ninety Three Lakh only) is being released to the Authority under the head" Demand No. Y-2, 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 199, Assistance to Other Non-Government Organizations (01) Assistance to Other Non-Government Organizations (01) (01) Assistance to Government Employees Transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Authority (Subsidiary Grants (Compulsory) (2215 A052) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary)" subject to the following conditions:
(i) Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the amount of the subsidy disbursed under this Government decision is spent for the same activities as the grant is sanctioned for the pension benefits of retired officers/ employees of the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran after March, 2017.
This amount should not be used for retirement benefits due before March 2017.
(ii) Expenditure on other items of the Fund shall be deemed to be financial irregularities and the officers/ employees concerned shall be liable to disciplinary action for such action.
(iii) Separate accounts should be maintained of the amount of free subsidy.
(iv) The Authority shall inform the Government from time to time about the allocations made under the free grants.
(v) The accounts of the Authority as per Rule No. 3 of Annexure 2 to the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959 shall be submitted by the Authority for inspection along with relevant documents.
(vi) The Authority shall submit the utilization certificate of the free subsidy to the Government. The Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall certify the amount of grant-in-aid and expenditure as approved and released as indicated in the Utilization Certificate. The utility certificates should be signed by the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran.
Only such utility certificates will be acceptable to the government.

vii) The expenditure of the released subsidy should be reconciled with the expenditure reported to the Accountant General's Office and the prescribed report should be submitted to the Accounts Officer, Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department on the prescribed date.
(vii) The Authority shall ensure that the retirement benefits of any retired employee do not remain pending for a long period. Also, old cases should be settled on priority while paying retirement benefits to retired employees. A chronological list of retired officers/ employees should be prepared and action taken to pay pensionary benefits on a priority basis.
The certificate that the said proceedings are being carried out should be submitted by the Director (Finance) while making further demand for funds.
Rajendra Madke has been appointed as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Mr. Ram Gangje, Deputy Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department, has been appointed as the Controlling Officer.
The pension subsidy given by the government will have to be provided through ECS in the bank account of the beneficiaries. Under no circumstances should pension grants be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
