The Centrally Sponsored Jal Jeevan Mission is being implemented in the State as per the Government decision dated 04.09.2021. Under Jal Jeevan Mission, the financial ratio between Centre and State is 1:1.
As per the Jal Jeevan Mission guidelines, funds received under the Central and State accounts were being made available to the State Water and Sanitation Missions on the Single Nodal Account of the PFMS system. The central government. In the letter dated 06.02.2021, it was informed that the Single Nodal Accounts of the State Water and Sanitation Mission will be made operational as Escrow Accounts. Accordingly, it has been decided to work with the Central Government to implement it as a single nodal account (Escrow Account). The MoU was signed on 23.08.2021. Since the launch of Escrow Account in the financial year 2021-2022, the central share of funds from the central government is directly deposited in the Single Nodal Accounts (Escrow Account) instead of being deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the State for the component under Jal Jeevan Mission. The Central Government vide No. The first part of the first installment for the financial year 2023-2024 under the order of 2 Rs. 1861.065 crore has been made available in the account of JAL JEEVAN MISSION, MAHARASHTRA ESCROW. It was under consideration of the Government to approve the recording and spending of the said funds under the respective heads of accounts to the State Water and Sanitation Mission.
