The Proposal:
To meet the non-salaried expenses of the daily wage workers on the regular establishment of the Rural Water Supply Department in the Zilla Parishad.

Some expenses need to be covered. Accordingly, as per the Government Circular dated 16th April, 2020 of the Finance Department, the expenditure under other objectives i.e. 31st Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary) should not be incurred by the departments without the approval of the Finance Department. Accordingly, in its submission to the Department of Finance, the Department of Finance has made available only 20% of the total allocation for the month of April-June and the month of July-September under the target of 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payment).
Accordingly, the issue of disbursement of ex-gratia is under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
To meet the non-salaried expenses of the daily wage workers on the regular establishment of the Rural Water Supply Department in the Zilla Parishad.
For the financial year 2020-21, a sum of Rs. 1,68,000/- (Rupees One Lakh Sixty Six Thousand only) is being disbursed to the concerned Zilla Parishads under the head 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payable) under the head" 2215, Water Supply and Sanitation 196, Assistance to Zilla Parishads (01) Assistance to Zilla Parishads/ Panchayat Institutions, Establishment Grants to Zilla Parishads under Section 183 of the Maharashtra Zilla Parishad and Panchayat Samiti Act, 1961 (01) (07) Daily Wage Labourers on Regular Establishment (Mandatory) (22151914)."

The said fund is being released subject to the deposit of unutilized funds of 2019-20 under the aforesaid heads to the Government and the funds shall not be drawn from the treasury unless the unutilized funds are deposited with the Government. The non-salary fund should be utilised by the respective Zila Parishads for office expenses, travel expenses etc. as per requirement.
The grants sanctioned under this Government decision shall be subject to the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government from time to time. Considering the overall economic situation of the State in view of the health and economic disaster caused by COVID-19, it is necessary to curtail expenditure and defer some expenditure.
4. All the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers shall ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the amounts indicated in this Government Resolution. Also, ensure that the funds are used for the intended purpose.
(5) Care should be taken to demand further necessary funds only after ensuring that the utilization certificate of the funds disbursed earlier as well as under the said Government decision is submitted to the Government. The concerned District Councils should note that the funds will not be disbursed until the next demand letter is received. The subsidy will be credited to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
