Preamble: Under the above Read No. 1, the Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation, Government of India, has issued general instructions regarding the effective implementation of the Gobardhan programme. In order to ensure effective implementation of the Gobardhan programme, a State Technical Advisory Committee (STAC) has been constituted under the Role of State in the guidelines. Accordingly, for the successful implementation of Gobardhan programme in the State of Maharashtra, a State-level Technical Advisory Committee (STAC) has been constituted under Read No. 2 to select the technical agency to support the proposals to be implemented by the Gram Panchayat, to approve the proposals prepared and submitted by the technical agency, and other matters. The role of the District Water and Sanitation Cell in the last paragraph of this Government Decision has been mentioned here that the District Water and Sanitation Cell (DWSC) headed by the Chief Executive Officer for the implementation of the Gobardhan program should act as the nodal agency for the coordination committee to approve and control the proposals of the respective districts. Instead, the government was considering the formation of a separate district-level committee under the Gobardhan programme.
Government Decision: In order to successfully implement the Gobardhan program in the state of Maharashtra, the following district-level committees are being formed:

The above District Level Committee headed by the Chief Executive Officer for the implementation of the Gobardhan programme is to act as the nodal agency for approving and controlling the proposals of the respective districts.
While approving the proposals of the districts, the following points should be examined. (1) A resolution of the Gram Sabha is required for approval of the proposal. 2) The Gram Panchayat is required to give consent letter regarding the availability of space for the implementation of Gobardhan programs. 3) The proposal should be submitted in the prescribed format as per the guidelines of the Central Government. 4) The consent letter of the Gram Panchayat is required to bear the expenditure of the concerned Gram Panchayat in excess of the maximum amount permissible as per the norms of the Central Government. 5) Gramsevaks and Block Development Officers of the concerned Gram Panchayat should be invited for meetings as per the requirement while examining the proposal.
Term of office: The term of office of the Committee shall be two years. The expenditure in this regard should be met from the grant sanctioned under the Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin).
