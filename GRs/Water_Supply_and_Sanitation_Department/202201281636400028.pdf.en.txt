Introduction: Mouze Majgaon (Ta. Basic Administrative Sanction has been accorded under Reference No. 1 to the budget of Tap Water Supply Scheme at Radhanagari, Dist. As per Reference No. 2, the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Kolhapur has granted technical approval to the original budget. As per Reference No. 3, the Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Kolhapur has given revised technical approval to the budget of the said scheme. Accordingly, the Superintending Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority Board, Sangli, had submitted a proposal to the government under reference letter No. 4 to give administrative approval to the revised plan and budget of the present tap water supply scheme. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the revised plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.

Government Resolution No: GRAPPU-2021/ PR No. 307/ PAPU18 Conditions
1) The closure of the accounts of the above-named scheme should be done immediately.
(2) The process of fixing the responsibility of expenditure above the approved price on the then concerned officer should be carried out by the Maharashtra Life Authority.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Kolhapur that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
7. Action to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the present scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
The additional cost of Rs. 7.32 lakh (Rs.7.32 lakh only) in the revised cost of the scheme should be met from the grant sanctioned under the head Demand No.Y-02, 2215 Water Supply and Sanitation, 102 Rural Water Supply Programme, Jal Jeevan Mission (State Share) (2215 2071) and finally be spent under the same.
