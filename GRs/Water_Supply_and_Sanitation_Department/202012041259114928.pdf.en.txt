Introduction: Under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme, Sonai Karjagaon and 16 other villages (Dist. Nevasa, G. Ahmednagar) The budget of Regional Water Supply Scheme has been accorded the original administrative approval as per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 1. The revised budget has been accorded administrative approval under the Government Resolution at Reference No.22. With the completion of the project works, the cost of the project has been saved to the tune of Rs. 2.00 crore. Accordingly, the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, Regional Division, Nashik had submitted a proposal to the government under Reference Letter No. 4 to approve the cost of the increased distribution system from the cost savings of the present Regional Water Supply Scheme. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the revised budget of the scheme by approving the cost of increased distribution system from the savings of the present scheme was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: Under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme, Sonai Karjagaon and 16 other villages (Dist. Newasa, G. Ahmednagar) Regional Water Supply Scheme at a revised cost of Rs. 7778.91 lakhs (Dhobal) (in letters seventy seven crore seventy eight lakh ninety one thousand only) is being given revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions.

The concerned Zilla Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Chief Engineer/ Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority that the scheme has been actually completed.
The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (subject to special amendments), depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of the water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
