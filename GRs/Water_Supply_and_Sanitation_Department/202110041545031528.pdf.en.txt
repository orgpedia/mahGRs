Preamble: Expenditure on measures taken to alleviate water scarcity in rural areas of the State is met from the grants made available by this Department under the head (2215 A 195).
The Department has received the proposal for seeking funds from Nashik, Amravati and Nagpur divisions for the period October, 2020 to March, 2021 for the measures taken/ to be taken during the shortage period of 2020-21. In the financial year 2021-22, the funds budgeted under the target of Account Head (2215A 195) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Plan) were demanded by the Divisional Commissioners of Nashik, Amravati and Nagpur

Government's decision:
As per the norms laid down by the Government for addressing the drinking water shortage in rural areas, the Divisional Commissioners, Nashik, Amravati and Nagpur are being sanctioned and released funds to the tune of Rs. 1043.21 lakh (Rupees Ten Crore Thirty Three Lakhs Twenty One Thousand only) under the Expenditure on Measures to be taken/ implemented for addressing the drinking water shortage in their respective divisions through the Budget Distribution System subject to the terms and conditions laid down in the extant Standing Government Resolution and the following conditions:

Terms and Conditions
The Divisional Commissioner, Nashik, Amravati and Nagpur shall disburse the funds directly to the concerned Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad for taking measures under the scarcity in rural areas.
The funds should be released for the implementation of the measures taken to mitigate the drinking water scarcity in rural areas for the period 2019-20 (i.e. October, 2020 to March, 2021) as well as for the payment of pending dues during this period. This money cannot be used for other projects.
3) The Divisional Commissioner should allocate funds on priority to the measures like well acquisition, tanker water supply and even if the proposal submitted by the Divisional Commissioner does not ask for the measures approved by the government, these measures should also be allocated on priority as per the order of the concerned administrative approval.

4) It should be ensured that the funds are disbursed directly to the contractors through Electronic Clearing Services/ RTGS, without keeping the personal ledger account or bank account.
5) While disbursing the funds, it should be ensured that the funds have not been disbursed to the concerned measures earlier.
6) Further, while seeking funds for the measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity, the subject-wise/ project-wise demand as to what are the proposed measures to be taken for mitigation of scarcity for what period, how much funds are required for it, information about the funds disbursed, expenditure incurred, balance funds etc. in the current financial year, as well as the utilization certificate thereof, should be submitted. Besides, it should be noted that the funds will be approved in the future.
7) The funds for the urban and rural measures being implemented by the Maharashtra Life Authority have been made available to the Maharashtra Life Authority as per the demand. Therefore, the funds should not be distributed to the Maharashtra Life Authority through the Zilla Parishad or the Collector.
8) GPS on private tankers while making payments out of the funds released for the measures taken for supply of water through tankers/ drinking water as per the decision of the Government.
The system will be installed and only those trips of tankers whose fare is recorded on the GPS system will be allowed for payment. Such trips will not be admissible if the tanker transport holder claims that the tanker has been diverted due to non-installation of the system or the GPS system is switched off. Also, since the GPS system does not register, payments should not be authenticated and funds should not be disbursed for such authenticated payments. Hence, the funds should be immediately disbursed to the concerned offices/ Zilla Parishads/ Collectors within four days. The utilization certificate of this fund should be submitted to the government before November 14, 2021.
The District Collector/ Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad/ Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the various schemes/ water supply related works taken up in the previous year have been completed with the provision made for the relief of natural disasters.

11) The concerned Divisional Commissioner/ CEO, Zila Parishad should ensure that since water is a vital/ essential item, there is no malpractice under it, the same scheme is not repeated every year and unnecessary expenditure is not incurred on it.
The Divisional Commissioner shall submit to the Government, every month, along with the monthly expenditure report, information on the physical progress of the district for the purpose for which the funds have been disbursed.
The accounts of the allocation of funds should be kept up to date at the level of the office of the recipient and disburser of the grant in accordance with the procedures laid down in the records. Similarly, the information about the actual expenditure to be incurred every month should be submitted to the government by the 5th of the next month. The expenditure incurred every three months should be verified with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's office and the certificate with quarterly verification should be submitted to this department immediately. Also, the District Collector/ Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad as well as Member Secretary, Maharashtra Life Authority should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned funds under any circumstances. If any amount is not spent from the subsidy disbursed, the amount should be returned to the government within the stipulated time. Ask for the necessary funds. Don't ask for more money.
Though the funds are being released as per the demand received from the field offices, the same amount of funds required for the scarcity period 2020-21 (from October, 2020 to March, 2021) should be drawn through the Budget Distribution System. Proposals should be submitted to the competent authority for disciplinary action against the concerned if additional funds are drawn and remain unutilized. The Water Supply and Sanitation Department should be informed about this.
