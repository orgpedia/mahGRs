In his letter dated 02.05.2018, the Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad has submitted a proposal worth Rs. 24.97 lakhs for the special repair of the tap water supply scheme in Ner, Taluka and District Jalna under the scarcity measures for the period 2017-18 to the Government for administrative approval.
Approval for Proposal involving works of 2000 Meter HDPE pipeline of Pressure and other works in line with it

was given. Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.
Considering the facts mentioned in the preamble of the Government decision, the administrative approval is being given to the proposal worth Rs. 24.97 lakh (Rs. 24 lakh, ninety-nine thousand only), which includes the works proposed under the special amendment of the tap water supply scheme in Ner, Taluka and District Jalna, under the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Jalna should first declare that there is a shortage of water in Ner, Taluka and District Jalna.
2) All special repair works mentioned in the proposal should be carried out by the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Jalna.
3) The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner. Ensure that the work is completed within the approved cost.
The tender of the scheme is being allowed to be taken out for a short period (7 days).
The tender process must be completed by May 20, 2018. Approval of the project will be cancelled if the project is not completed by that date.
It is necessary to start water supply from the scheme by completing the works of the scheme by June 6, 2018.
7) After the completion of the works of the said scheme mentioned in the proposal through the Zilla Parishad, Jalna, the maintenance of the scheme and the management of the scheme will be required to be done through the concerned Gram Panchayat.
8) The Divisional Commissioner, Aurangabad should provide 50% of the funds at the beginning of the scheme after the tender process for the said scheme. The remaining funds will be released after the completion of the project.
Since the work of the scheme needs to be completed on time, the progress report should be sent to the government every Monday of the week from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
