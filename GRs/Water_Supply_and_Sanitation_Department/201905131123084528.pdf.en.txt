Preamble: The Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur has submitted a proposal to the Government for grant of administrative sanction for works amounting to Rs. 41.59 lakh under drinking water scarcity in Municipal Council Wadi area for the scarcity period 2018-19.
This proposal was considered for approval in the meeting held on 09.05.2019 of the Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department for giving administrative approval to the urgent water supply schemes/ measures beyond the prescribed financial and physical parameters for mitigation of drinking water scarcity in urban and rural areas.
As the municipal council needs to take measures under the shortage proposed by Wadi, the government is considering to give administrative approval to some of the proposed works of a total of 30 borewells worth Rs. 41.59 lakh, excluding computer charges, at a total cost of Rs. 40.55 lakh.

Government's decision
In view of the facts stated in the Preamble, the proposal for the measures to be taken under drinking water scarcity in the Municipal Council Wadi area for the scarcity period 2018-19 at a cost of Rs. 40.55 lakh (Rs. Forty Lakh Fifty Five Thousand only) as mentioned in the proposal, is being accorded administrative approval under this Order subject to the following conditions:
1) The Collector, Nagpur should first declare that there is a shortage of drinking water in the Municipal Council Wadi area.
The government will provide a subsidy of Rs. 36.49 lakh i.e. 90% of the total cost of Rs. 40.55 lakh. The remaining amount i.e. 10% of the scheme Rs. 4.06 lakh is to be paid by the Municipal Council Wadi as public contribution.
3) The remaining funds should be distributed by the Divisional Commissioner to the Municipal Council Wadi through the Collector Nagpur only after the Municipal Council Wadi has spent the first 10 percent of the population of the scheme.
4) The scheme should be implemented through the Municipal Council.
5) The location of the proposed bore wells in the municipal council wadi area should be determined by the Ground Water Survey and Development System, as approved by the Standing Committee. Also, new borewells should be taken up at places certified by the Ground Water Survey and Development Agency.
The work of this scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner and it should be ensured that the work of the scheme is completed within the approved price.
7) The Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur shall make available to the District Collector, Nagpur, funds equivalent to 50% of the subsidy payable at the beginning of the scheme for the said scheme.
The remaining 30% of the funds should be disbursed after considering the progress of the scheme at the level of Divisional Commissioner. The remaining 20% will be paid after the project is completed.
Since the work of the scheme must be completed on time, the progress report should be sent to the government every Monday of the week from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
