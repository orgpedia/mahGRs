Preamble: Annual Action Plan for 2018-19 of National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDP). Bhartek (Ta. Motala, G. Buldana) tap water supply scheme has been included. The plan No. 1 has been submitted to the Government for administrative approval. The technical approval for the project has been given by the Executive Engineer, Rural Water Supply Department, Zilla Parishad, Buldana. Further, the Technical Committee under the Chairmanship of Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Regional Department in its meeting dated 23.10.2018 approved the Detailed Project Report of the scheme.

Government Resolution No.: Rgraphe-2018/ P.S. 288/ Papu-13 is given. So m. Bhartek (Ta. Motala, G. Buldana) Rs. 36.91 lakh under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme.
Government's decision:
Considering the facts stated in the preamble, Md. Bhartek (Ta. 40 per litre), Rs. 4597/- per month. 36.91 lakhs (Rupees Thirty Six Lakhs Ninety One Thousand only) is being accorded administrative approval under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme subject to fulfilment of the following terms and conditions.
The scheme should be implemented by the District Council, Buldana. It will be mandatory for the contractor to run the scheme for at least 1 year after the implementation of the scheme. The District Council shall ensure that the scheme is successfully completed, as well as the maintenance of the scheme is carried out for the repair of the reference No. 7 here. Follow the guidelines issued by the government on October 17, 2014.
After the implementation of the scheme, the scheme will be run for at least 1 year through the contractor, but the water table of this one year should be recovered from the villagers. The recovered water table should be handed over to the agency running the scheme.
(iii) The works of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
(iv) No work order shall be issued for the scheme unless the necessary land acquisition permissions have been obtained for the scheme. If the order is issued without fulfilling these conditions, the administrative approval given under this government decision will be considered canceled.
V. 100% household tap connections and meters should be included in the scheme. Also, at least 80% of the tap connection holders should take an undertaking for getting the meter connection at their own cost and a copy of it should be laminated and given to the tap connection holder and a copy should be kept with the Gram Panchayat. Thereafter, the orders were issued.
(vi) If a villager refuses to take water even after the gram sabha resolution/ assurance letter is given, at least 50% of the water supply should be recovered from him. In this regard, the villagers should be given an idea before the tender process and the Gram Sabha should take a resolution in this regard.
The scheme should be included on the central government's IMIS system.
(viii) The works of the scheme should be completed in a time bound and planned manner in a phased manner at the approved cost. No other work should be started until the plan is developed. Other works in the scheme should be carried out only after making sure that there is enough water available in the source.

Government Resolution No: Ragrapay-2018/ P.No.288/ Papu-13 IX. In order to meet the expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme, the concerned Gram Panchayat should fix the rate of water tap for domestic, non-domestic and institutional tap connection holders as mentioned in the proposal for implementation of the scheme and it should be increased from time to time.
X. 20% of the recovered water table should be retained for major maintenance repairs. Maintenance repair of pipes and other pipes should be done every 5 years and every 3 years.
No assistance/ grant will be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the operation, maintenance and repair of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
XII. All the villages under the scheme shall be open defecation free but it shall be ensured that they remain open defecation free at all times.
XIII. No additional subsidy shall be provided by the Government in case of increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason.
XIV. The conditions contained in the order of technical approval of the said scheme shall be complied with. Also referred to as No. 2 The provisions of the government decision here should be strictly followed.
XV. Reference No. 10 here. As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 15th June, 2015, it will be mandatory to conduct a third stage technical examination of the scheme.
The Gram Panchayat shall be bound to determine the water table required for the scheme to be self-sustaining and to increase it as necessary. In this regard, the officials of the zilla parishad should give detailed information to the villagers in the gram sabha and only then start the work of the scheme.
XVII. After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency shall be required to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
XVIII. If any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice of any kind is noticed in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity shall be liable for disciplinary action.
It shall be mandatory to meet the standards prescribed for rural water supply schemes under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme.
