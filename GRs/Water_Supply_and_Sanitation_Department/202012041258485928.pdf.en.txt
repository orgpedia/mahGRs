GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: GRAPAPU-2019/ PR No. 245/ PAPU-17 GOVERNMENT DECISION: Under National Rural Drinking Water Programme, Mauje Shahupuri (Nimsahari) (Dist. Revised administrative approval is being accorded to the revised cost estimate of Rs. 4298.48 lakh (Dhobal) for the tap water supply scheme in G. Satara (A.P.) subject to the following conditions.
Conditions
1.The remaining three months should be completed at the revised price mentioned above.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Life Authority shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, Satara that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran and the concerned Gram Panchayat officer should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Life Authority will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenses incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (including special repairs), depreciation in the scheme or for the renovation of machinery.
The necessary planning regarding 100% tap connection should be done by the Gram Panchayat.
6. In view of Jal Jeevan Mission, it is necessary to check the record of tap connections given so far and timely report the information on IMIS about the tap connections given so far.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Life Authority) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. This program is financially viable.
