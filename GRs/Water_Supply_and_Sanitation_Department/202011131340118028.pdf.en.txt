Separate accounts should be maintained for the amount of grants sanctioned and released under the scheme.
The Collector concerned should reallocate the grant sanctioned and released by the government to the concerned Municipal Corporation/ Municipality in the district and inform the same to the government immediately.
The concerned Municipal Corporations and Municipalities should spend the above sanctioned and released amount only for the specific purpose for which it has been sanctioned and released till 31st March, 2021.
The Collector shall keep a record of the funds disbursed to the respective Urban Local Bodies in his district, the year-wise details of the works taken up from the received funds, the completed works, the incomplete works, the amount spent and the amount not spent, etc. and shall make this information available to the Government immediately as required.
The powers to examine the accounts of expenditure made for these schemes by the respective Municipal Corporations and Municipalities and the Collector from the grants sanctioned and released under this scheme are being delegated to the Accountant General, Maharashtra, Mumbai/ Nagpur or the officer of this department with special powers. If the concerned documents are demanded by the Accountant General or the officer of the department given special powers, they should be immediately made available by the concerned municipal corporation and municipality and the collector.
As per Rule No. 3 of Annexure-II of the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959, the accounts of the concerned Urban Local Bodies should be submitted to the Accountant General by the concerned Municipal Corporation, Municipality and Collector for inspection along with the relevant documents as per the demand of the Accountant General.
Under this scheme, the concerned municipal corporation, chief officer, municipality and district collector have to give the government the grant, utility certificate and completion certificate. The chief municipal officer shall certify and sign the utility certificate and the completion certificate (COMPLETION CERTIFICATE), the program-wise amounts of grants and expenditures approved and released as indicated in this.
Utilization certificate and completion of work, the collector should sign on it, such certificates will be acceptable to the government. Similarly, the certificate of completion of the work should also be sent to the Accountant General.
The Collector should ensure that the grants sanctioned and released to the respective Urban Local Bodies under this scheme are utilized in the respective financial year itself following the prescribed procedure and as per the guidelines of the scheme.
The 100% expenditure on future maintenance and repair of the works taken up under the scheme shall be borne by the respective Urban Local Bodies from their own funds. The government will not provide any subsidy for the maintenance and repair of this scheme.
The expenditure of grants-in-aid sanctioned and released should be reconciled with the expenditure reported to the Accountant General's Office and the prescribed report should be submitted to the Government on the prescribed date.
