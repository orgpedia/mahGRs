The Proposal:
The Government has, vide Government Resolution dated 23.3.2017, taken over the responsibility of payment of salaries, allowances and pension to the employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority and has released a sum of Rs. 193.8330 crore to the Authority for the financial year 2021-22 under the head 2215A052 as grant to meet the expenditure on pension of retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Authority. Now the matter of disbursement of funds made available by the Finance Department under the above head of accounts is under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Financial Year 2021-22 for payment of pension benefits to retired employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for the period January, 2022 to March, 2022

During the year, a sum of Rs. 54,55,36, 210/- (Rupees Fifty Four Crores Fifty Five Lakhs Sixty Six Thousand Two Hundred and Ten only) under the head" Subsidiary Grants (Compulsory) (2215 A052) 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) for sanction of pensionary benefits to Government employees transferred to Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran" is being released to the Authority subject to the following conditions.
(i) Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall ensure that the grant disbursed under this Government decision is for the pensionary benefits of retired officers/ employees of Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for the month of January, 2022 to March, 2022 and the amount from this grant will be spent for the same works. These funds should not be used for retirement benefits.
(ii) Expenditure on other items of the Fund shall be deemed to be financial irregularities and the officers/ employees concerned shall be liable to disciplinary action for such action.
(iii) Separate accounts should be maintained of the amount of free subsidy.
(iv) The Authority shall inform the Government from time to time about the allocations made under the free grants.
(v) The accounts of the Authority as per Rule No. 3 of Annexure 2 to the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959 shall be submitted by the Authority for inspection along with relevant documents.
(vi) The Authority shall submit the utilization certificate of the free subsidy to the Government. The Director (Finance), Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran shall certify the amount of grant-in-aid and expenditure as approved and released as indicated in the Utilization Certificate. The utility certificates should be signed by the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. Only such utility certificates will be acceptable to the government.
vii) The expenditure of the released subsidy should be reconciled with the expenditure reported to the Accountant General's Office and the prescribed report should be submitted to the Accounts Officer, Papu-20, Water Supply and Sanitation Department on the prescribed date.
(vii) The Authority shall ensure that the retirement benefits of any retired employee do not remain pending for a long period. as well as retired employees.
