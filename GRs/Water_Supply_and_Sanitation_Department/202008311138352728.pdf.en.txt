Preamble: The Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme is being implemented in the State from 2016-17 to 2019-20 as per the decision of the Government dated 7th May, 2016 at No.1 under the purview of the Department with the objective of providing pure and adequate drinking water to the rural population of the State.
Duration of the programme It expires on March 31, 2020. The present scheme needs to be extended to complete the works of the ongoing/ incomplete water supply schemes sanctioned under the said programme. Accordingly, the Government was considering to extend the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme for two years to complete the works of the water supply schemes sanctioned under this programme.
In view of the above, the Cabinet in its meeting held on 08.07.2020 has approved the extension of the existing scheme for completion of works of ongoing/ incomplete water supply schemes under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme for a period of two years (upto 2021-22), with an outlay of Rs. A sum of Rs. 430.00 crore has been sanctioned.

Government's decision:
In view of the above mentioned facts, the present scheme is being extended for two years (upto 2021-22) to complete the works of the ongoing/ incomplete water supply schemes sanctioned under the Chief Minister's Rural Drinking Water Programme.
No new water supply schemes shall be approved during this period. Also, the guidelines issued by the government from time to time under the Chief Minister Rural Drinking Water Programme will remain in force.
