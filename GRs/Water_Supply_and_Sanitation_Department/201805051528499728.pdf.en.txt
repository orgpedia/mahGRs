The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati has submitted a proposal to the Government for administrative approval of works worth Rs. 423.90 lakh submitted for approval of measures under drinking water scarcity in the area of Akola Municipal Corporation for the scarcity period of 2017-18. This proposal has been approved by the Standing Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Water Supply and Sanitation Department on 03.04.2018 for administrative approval of urgent water supply schemes/ measures beyond the prescribed financial and physical parameters to address the drinking water shortage in urban and rural areas. The same was considered for approval in the meeting held on 23.04.2018.

The proposal received from the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati, includes 1) repair of existing hand pumps, 2) repair of existing closed electric pumps, 3) hand pumps with new bore wells, 4) sub pumps with new bore wells and 5) plan repairs in the extension areas. The proposal was discussed in detail in the Standing Committee meetings dated 03.04.2018 and 23.04.2018 and the Standing Committee had sought clarification from the Municipal Corporation on certain issues. This disclosure has been received through his letter dated 26.04.2018. In pursuance of this disclosure, the Chairman of the Standing Committee approved a total of 120 new borewells proposed in the city's extended area along with hand pumps at a cost of Rs. 0.60 lakh. 72.00 lakh and 75 new borewells with sub pumps at a cost of Rs. 0.75 lakh to Rs. 56.25 lakh. The proposal was approved at a cost of Rs 128.25 lakh. Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.
In view of the facts mentioned in the Preamble of the Government Resolution, the proposal for the measures to be taken under drinking water scarcity for the Akola city for the period 2017-18 at a cost of Rs. 128.25 lakh/- (Rupees One Crore, Twenty Eight Lakhs, Twenty Five Thousand only) as mentioned in the proposal, is being accorded administrative approval under this Order subject to the following conditions:
1) The District Collector, Akola shall first declare that there is a shortage of drinking water in Akola city.
The government will provide a subsidy of 90% of the total cost of Rs. 128.25 lakh i.e. Rs. 115.43 lakh.
The remaining amount i. e. 10% of the scheme amounting to Rs. 12.82 lakhs is to be paid by Akola Municipal Corporation, Akola as public contribution.
(3) No work order shall be issued under the scheme unless 10% of the population of the scheme is paid by the Municipal Corporation.
4) This scheme should be implemented through Akala Municipal Corporation.
4) As approved by the Standing Committee, the proposed new borewell in the extension area of Akola Municipal Corporation along with hand pumps and new borewell along with sub-mersible pumps are to be taken. Their location should be determined through groundwater survey and development system. as well

New borewells should be taken up in places certified by the Ground Water Survey and Development Agency.
6) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner and it should be ensured that the work of the scheme is completed within the approved price.
The tender of the scheme is being allowed to be taken out for a short period (7 days).
8) The Divisional Commissioner, Amravati shall make available to the Commissioner, Akola Municipal Corporation, Akola, funds equivalent to 50% of the subsidy payable at the beginning of the scheme for the said scheme. The remaining 50% of the funds will be released after the implementation of the scheme.
9) Since the work of the scheme must be completed on time, a progress report should be sent to the government every Monday of the week from the tender process of the scheme to the start of water supply from the scheme.
