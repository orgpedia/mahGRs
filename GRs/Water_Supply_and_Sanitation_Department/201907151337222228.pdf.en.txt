Preamble: National Rural Drinking Water Programme Pertur, G. Proposal for Administrative Approval of Jalna Tap Water Supply Scheme Budget under Reference No. 1 has been sent to the Government for approval. The plan of Rs. 368.57 lakh has been approved by the Superintending Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority Board, Aurangabad vide letter No. Technical approval has been granted. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the plan and budget of the present scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Jalna, Rs. Administrative Sanction is being accorded to the Budget and Plan at a cost of Rs. 404.14 lakhs (Rupees Four Hundred Four Lakh Fourteen Thousand) subject to the following conditions.
(1) The scheme will be implemented/ implemented by the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran. The implementation, operation and maintenance of this scheme will be done through the Maharashtra Life Authority. There is a need to plan

(2) The work of the scheme should be done by inviting e-tenders in the prescribed manner.
(3) No work order shall be issued for the scheme unless the land acquisition and all other permissions required for the scheme have been obtained.
(4) The works of the scheme shall be completed in a phased manner in a time bound and planned manner at the approved cost.
(5) The expenditure for operation and maintenance of the scheme shall be kept to a minimum by correcting the details of expenditure shown in the Detailed Project Report. Also, to meet the expenditure and to make the scheme financially self-sustaining, the Gram Panchayat concerned should fix the minimum water tariff for the household tap holder and increase it from time to time.
(6) No assistance/ grant shall be provided by the Government to the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad for the maintenance of the machinery or depreciation of the scheme to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for operation, inspection and maintenance of the scheme.
(7) Strengthening of existing resources.
(8) No subsidy shall be provided by the Government in case of increase in the cost of the scheme for any reason.
(9) The conditions in the order of technical approval of the scheme should be fulfilled.
Also, it will be mandatory to conduct a third-party technical examination of the scheme as per the provisions of the existing government decision.
(10) The Gram Panchayat shall be bound to determine the water table required for the scheme to be self-sufficient and to increase it as necessary. In this regard, the officials of the Zilla Parishad/ Maharashtra Life Authority should clearly inform the villagers in the Gram Sabha and only then the work of the scheme should be started.
(11) At least 80% of the citizens will be required to take a guarantee letter to take water through the meter by taking a water connection from the proposed scheme.
(12) After the implementation of the scheme is completed, the concerned implementing agency shall be obliged to submit a certificate of completion of the scheme to the competent authority.
(13) If any irregularity, misappropriation or malpractice of any nature is noticed in the implementation of the scheme, the concerned officers/ employees responsible for the irregularity shall be liable for disciplinary action.

(14) The Water and Sanitation Support Organisation (WASO) shall be responsible for obtaining the utilisation certificate of the expenditure and the proof of completion of the scheme from the concerned implementing agency and submitting it to the Government as per the progress of the scheme.
(15) It should be ensured that the scheme is implemented in accordance with the prevailing government policy.
(16) The arrears due to the Gram Panchayat shall be required to be paid before the commencement of the scheme. No work order should be issued without paying the outstanding electricity bill.
(17) It shall be for the Executive Engineer concerned to examine whether the accounts of the previous scheme have been completed in financial terms or how. Account needs to be completed.
(18) Under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme, 176 villages have been covered under the grid scheme. Daily water charges will be charged. Also, a guarantee will have to be taken from the Gram Panchayat that they will not be left out of this scheme.
The expenditure on this scheme should be met from the central and state funds of the National Rural Drinking Water Programme received by the Zilla Parishad.
