Preface: Purak Murbad, Ta. Murbad, G.Thane, the budget of the tap water supply scheme is referred to as Reference No. The administrative approval has been given in accordance with the government decision of 1. However, the cost of the scheme has increased due to difference in tariff rates between 2010-11 and 2011-12 and higher rate of acceptance of bids as per prevailing tariff rates for 2011-12. The Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Thane has given revised technical approval to the budget of the scheme presented under reference number 4. Accordingly, the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority, Thane has sanctioned Rs. Proposal for Revised Administrative Sanction to the Plan and Budget of the Tap Water Supply Scheme at a nominal cost of Rs. 2166.94 lakhs (Rupees Twenty One Crore Sixty Six Lakh Ninety Four Thousand only). 5 submitted to the Government.
The proposal had been approved by the government.
Government's decision:
Purab murabda tha. Murbad, Thane Rs. Revised administrative approval is being accorded to the plan and budget of the tap water supply scheme at a nominal cost of Rs. 2166.94 lakh (Rupees Twenty One Crore Sixty Six Lakh Ninety Four Thousand only) subject to the following conditions.

Conditions
(1) All the remaining works should be completed in 6 months at the revised cost mentioned above.
(2) The terms and conditions mentioned in the original administrative approval and the revised technical approval granted to the scheme shall be strictly complied with.
(3) On completion of the scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the concerned Executive Engineer, Maharashtra Life Authority Division, Thane that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned executive engineer, officers of Maharashtra Life Authority Department/ Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
(4) The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned shall not receive any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (subject to special amendments) of the scheme, depreciation in the scheme or renovation of machinery.
(5) On completion of the scheme, it shall be mandatory for the local self-government institution (the Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of the water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
(7) Action should be taken in accordance with the prevailing policy on dowry.
(8) It shall be the responsibility of the Maharashtra Life Authority Department to ensure that the said scheme is not revised under any circumstances. Also, the Maharashtra Life Authority should not demand any additional amount.
(9) The terms and conditions mentioned in the revised technical approval order of the scheme shall be strictly adhered to.
2. The revised cost of the scheme is Rs. 296.94 lakh (Rupees Two Hundred and Ninety Five Crores and Ninety Four Lakh only) from the State share under National Rural Drinking Water Programme/ District Planning Board/ Zilla Parishad Thane under Minimum Needs Programme.
3. This Government decision is based on the informal reference No. 115/1443, dated 06/04/2015 and vide Finance Department's Informal Reference No.447/ Expenditure-3, Dt. Issued with approval received under 14/10/2015.
