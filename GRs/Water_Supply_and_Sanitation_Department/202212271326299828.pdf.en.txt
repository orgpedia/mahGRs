As per the Government decision dated 20.10.2022, instructions have been issued regarding Goods and Services Tax in contracts of rural water supply schemes. According to the government services. The GST rate of 18% has been notified with effect from 18.07.2022. Accordingly, the Chief Executive Officer, Raigad Zilla Parishad, Alibaug has issued a notification regarding the levy of Goods and Services Tax at the rate of 18% for the schemes which have been given administrative approval at the rate of 12% at the government level under Jal Jeevan Mission in Raigad district. 2 has been requested. Accordingly, the matter of giving administrative approval to such schemes was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision
Under Jal Jeevan Mission, 26 schemes listed in the Annexure have been given administrative approval at the rate of 12% at the government level in Raigad district. The revised administrative approval is being granted subject to the following conditions, namely, the face value as per 7% GST at 18%.
It shall be the responsibility of the Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad, Raigad to ensure that all the remaining works are completed within the stipulated time frame of the Jal Jeevan Mission programme at the revised cost mentioned above.
The terms and conditions mentioned while granting the original administrative approval as well as the revised technical approval granted to the scheme should be strictly followed.
The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned will not get any assistance from the Government to meet the recurring expenditure incurred every year for the implementation, maintenance, repair (subject to special amendments), depreciation or renovation of machinery.

After completion of this scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. The Gram Panchayat/ Zilla Parishad concerned shall be responsible for taking over the scheme within one month from the date of receipt of intimation from the Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad, Raigad that the scheme has been actually completed. As soon as the scheme is completed, the concerned Executive Engineer, Zila Parishad and the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad officer should conduct a joint inspection of the scheme and if any defects are found in such inspection, they should be rectified before handing over the scheme.
After the completion of this scheme, it will be mandatory for the local self-government institution (concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad) to hand over the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
According to a CAG report in 2013, 21% of water supply schemes in the state have been closed due to non-payment of electricity bills. Therefore, in order to make the scheme financially viable, it will be the responsibility of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Zila Parishad to increase the water tariff as per the need.
7. Action to be taken as per the prevailing policy on trespass.
It shall be the responsibility of the District Council to ensure that the present scheme is not revised under any circumstances.
The expenditure on this scheme should be met from the sanctioned grant as per the guidelines issued from time to time under the Jal Jeevan Mission programme and the final expenditure should be made under it.
3. Revised Administrative Sanction to the Scheme is to be issued by the Finance Department vide Government Resolution No. V.P. 2013/ P.S. No. 30/2013/ Regulations, Part-II, Sec. 17.04.2015 in Part I, Sub-Division-3, A.No.6, Paragraph No. 27 (a) as per the authority conferred on the department.
