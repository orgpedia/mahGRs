Proposal: Decision of the Government. The State Government has approved the Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy 2023-28 on 02.06.2023. Accordingly, the Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy and all its related schemes will come into force from the date of issuance of this Government Resolution. It will be valid till 31.03.2028. The policy, under Section No. 11 (3)" Captive Market- The Department of Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles shall formulate a scheme in due course with detailed guidelines to ensure that every family below the poverty line in the State gets one saree free of cost every year. " It said. Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Affairs The families belonging to Below Poverty Line (Yellow Ration Card holders) who fulfill the criteria prescribed under the Government decision dated 17.07.2013 have been included in the Antyodaya Anna Yojana family group. Therefore, the government was considering to start a captive market scheme to provide one saree free of cost to Antyodaya ration card holder families in the state every year through the Department of Textiles.
Government Decision: As per the Government Decision dated 02.06.2023, the" Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy 2023-28" of the State has been announced. As per the policy, the government is implementing a scheme to distribute one saree per family free of cost to Antyodaya ration card holder families.
2.1 Duration of the Scheme: The duration of the Scheme is proposed to be for a period of five years from 2023 to 2028.

2.2 Beneficiaries: (a) Antyodaya ration card holder families in the state will be able to take advantage of this scheme.
(b) According to the data provided by the Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection. As on 31st December 2022, the number of Antyodaya ration card holder families in the State is 24,58,747.
(c) The number of Antyodaya ration card holder families is likely to increase/ decrease every year. The number of beneficiaries of this scheme will increase/ decrease according to their number.
2.3 Nodal Agency: Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation Limited. Navi Mumbai is being designated as the nodal agency. The Corporation shall carry out the production programme of sarees taking into account the technical parameters prescribed by the Mechanized Cooperative Societies/ Units registered under Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (M.S.M.E.) in the State registered with it.
2.4 Financial Burden: (a) As per the price agreement entered into by the Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation through e-tendering for the scheme for the year 2023-24, the price per saree is Rs. 355/- (plus GST 5%). Also, under this policy, the price of the saree will be fixed according to the rate agreement made every year for the policy period. If the rate for the year in which the saree is to be distributed has not been fixed for any reason, the price should be charged according to the rate of the previous year.
(b) The expenditure for the production, transport, advertisement, publicity, storage, pawning of sarees for this scheme shall be borne by the State Government. After that, the corporation will be responsible for making payments to the concerned.
The financial burden will be determined on the basis of the increase/ decrease in the number of Antyodaya ration card holder families every year as well as the rate contract made by the Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation every year.
2.5 DISTRIBUTION ARRANGEMENT: The sarees will be distributed under the Public Distribution System of the State Government. Suits will be distributed to the beneficiaries from fair price shops.

3. The production programme, quality of saree, colour scheme, price, quality of saree, publicity of the scheme and other expenses, etc. will be decided with the approval of the government on which festival the saree should be distributed every year.
The modus operandi of the scheme will be as follows: 1) The list of supplier agencies and rates of sarees have been fixed through e-tendering following the procedure prescribed by the Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation regarding the supply of sarees. The Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation will supply sarees to Antyodaya ration card holder families in the state under the free saree supply scheme from the empanelled entities of the registered suppliers who have participated in the e-tender. The institutions in this list are national cooperatives in the state registered with the corporation as well as the state itself.

The production of sarees should be done by the Primary Machinery Cooperative Societies registered under the Maharashtra State Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, and Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises in the state.
(2) Under no circumstances shall sarees be procured and supplied from private traders other than those listed in the above mentioned item (panel).
(3) The Corporation shall recover 1% of the amount due from the machinery cooperatives financed by the Government under the National Cooperative Development Corporation Scheme mentioned in point number 1 above after the production of sarees and deposit the same in the Government accounts by diverting the amount as recoverable amount of the cooperative societies towards repayment of the loan.
4) The corporation will be responsible for ensuring that the production capacity of the institutions mentioned in the above point number 1 (panel) is checked and the institutions are not unfairly treated and the corporation will be responsible for producing the same amount of production.
5) Sarees should not be manufactured and supplied from other hymns using the names of the institutions in point number 1 above.
6) The Corporation should ensure that the quality of sarees is good as prescribed by the Government and sarees are produced properly by the institutions.
7) Packing as prescribed by the government, coding by the organization which has produced the saree before distributing it to the beneficiary family. The corporation will be responsible for the Temporary Ink Mark printed on the saree, etc. regarding the scheme in which the saree is distributed under the scheme.
8) It shall be the responsibility of the Corporation to get the sarees tested by a government approved laboratory for the availability of sarees as per the prescribed norms before supplying to the beneficiaries.
9) The Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection should provide a list of Antyodaya ration card holder families to the corporation according to the fair price shop in every village in every taluka of the state. According to the list available, bundles of sarees will be prepared and delivered to the taluk-level warehouse by the corporation according to the name of the fair price shop.
10) Since the distribution of sarees for the Antyodaya ration card holder family is proposed to be done within the time fixed by the government, all the sarees will be distributed before the prescribed time.

The Machinery Corporation will be fully responsible for the delivery of the Public Distribution System (PDS) to the system and it will be mandatory for the Machinery Corporation to submit regular reports on the action taken in this regard to the Government as well as the Commissioner, Textiles.
11) In exceptional circumstances, if damaged or torn sarees are found while transporting to the block level godown of the Public Distribution System (PDS) system, the corporation will replace the sarees.
However, the Corporation will decide on the replacement of the sarees if they are damaged while delivering the sarees from the godown to the beneficiaries.
The Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department will be responsible for the distribution and monitoring of sarees from the Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation to the Public Distribution System (PDS) from where the sarees will be distributed to the Antyodaya ration card holder family and the Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department should submit a report on the action taken in this regard to the Textile Department within 15 days.
13) Funds will be made available to the Corporation to advertise the information of the scheme to the beneficiaries. After the draft/ text of the advertisement is approved by the government, the corporation will be responsible for publishing the advertisement through the Directorate of Information and Public Relations.
5. The said Government Decision is being issued in concurrence with the Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection.
