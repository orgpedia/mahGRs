The Proposal:
The Director (Sericulture) has proposed to set up infrastructure facilities such as silk cell market and post-harvest guidance and training centre on 1.00-acre land at Agricultural Produce Market Committee, Baramati, District Pune, to provide market access to the mulberry silk cells produced by the farmers of the state and to promote post-harvest processing. Submitted on February 26, 2021. Under this project, training will be provided in reeling, twisting, dyeing, silk fabric manufacturing, waste collection and value addition industry from pupa to silk. A budget of Rs. 956.62 lakh has been received from the Public Works Department, Pune for construction of building and allied facilities for the project. Further, the estimated cost of machinery and material for the project is Rs. 180.50 lakh as per the unit cost of Central Silk Board and prevailing market rates. GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: KEPUYO-2021/ P.S. NO. 30/ Reshim/ Cell
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In order to provide market access to the silk products produced by the farmers of the State and to promote the post-harvest processing industry, administrative approval is hereby accorded in principle for setting up of a silk cell market and post-harvest processing facilities, including a guide and training centre, on 1.00-acre land in the main premises of the Agricultural Produce Market Committee at Baramati, Pune at a cost of Rs. 956.62 lakh (Rs.9 crore, 49 lakh, sixty-two thousand only) as per the Recapitulation Sheet in Annexure-A attached to the Budget, subject to the following terms and conditions: 1) The land in the Agricultural Produce Market Committee shall be taken on lease for the first 29 years as per the prevailing Government policy before commencing the physical work.
2) Before starting the work, the sample map, layout map and detailed map should be approved by the architect.
3) While working on the provisions made in the rough form, the work should be taken up only after preparing a detailed budget.
4) In the course of actual work, action should be taken according to the guidelines mentioned in the Government Decision, Department of Environment, No. ENV-2013/ Q. No. 177/ T.K.1, dated January 10, 2014.
5) Necessary statutory permissions should be obtained from the local body before starting construction at the project site.
6) The tender notice for the work should be published after receiving the certificate that the planned place is in the possession of the consumer department.
The construction of the project should be carried out in accordance with the instructions of the Government Decision, Public Works Department, No. BDG-2017/ No. 60/ Buildings-2, dated July 7, 2017.
8) The electrification and other ancillary works should be properly planned and completed during the construction period itself.
9) Action should be taken in accordance with the instructions given by the government from time to time regarding the special facilities to be provided for the disabled in the building.
10) Special care should be taken so that the subject work does not require revised administrative approval.
11) Tender proceedings should be carried out only after the approval of the Director (Silk) on behalf of the Consumer Department regarding the matter of furniture and layout.
The Director (Sericulture) will be responsible for the management and provision of the expenditure incurred after the establishment of the Silk Fund Buying and Selling Market and Post-Fund Process Guide and Training Centre and ancillary facilities under the appropriate scheme.
