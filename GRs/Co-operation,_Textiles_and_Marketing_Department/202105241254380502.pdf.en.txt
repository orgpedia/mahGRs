Section 24-A of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960 provides that" every institution shall organise co-operative education and training for its members, officers and employees through the State Federal Institutions or State Apex Training Institutions as may be specified by the State Government by notification in the Official Gazette." As per the Economic Survey 2020-21 of Maharashtra, as on 31st March, 2020, there are about 2.12 lakh cooperative societies in the State with a total membership of about 5.5 crore. The number of directors of these institutions is more than 25 lakh and the number of employees is about 3.5 lakh. As per Section 24A of the Co-operative Act, it is mandatory for a co-operative society to provide co-operative education and training to its members, directors and officers/ employees.
