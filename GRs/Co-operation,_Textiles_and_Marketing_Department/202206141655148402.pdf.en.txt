Maratha Co-operative Bank Ltd. The Board of Directors of the Bank, Mumbai, having expired on 30.03.2020, had submitted the preliminary list as per the order of the Sub-Registrar Cooperative Societies, Mumbai. However, the elections could not be held due to Covid-19 restrictions.
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has imposed restrictions under Section 35 of the Act on 31.08.2016 on the Maratha Co-operative Bank and the Board of Directors has taken prompt steps to rescue the Bank. However, due to demonetisation and Covid-19 restrictions, the bank could not come out of the restrictions imposed by the RBI as the assets of the outstanding borrowers in the bank's possession were not sold. The Reserve Bank of India has extended the moratorium period till 28.02.2022. Deposits up to Rs 5 lakh have been allowed under Section 18A of the DIC. In the meantime, the Maratha Co-operative Bank and the Cosmos Bank Ltd. The proposal has been sent to the Reserve Bank of India, the Pune Municipal Corporation and the District Collector.
Accordingly, the provisions of Section 73AA of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960 and the powers of the Government to exempt the provisions of the Act under Section 157 are not applicable to the Maratha Co-operative Bank Ltd. In view of the ongoing process of amalgamation of the Bank and the proposal submitted to the Reserve Bank for securing deposits up to Rs. 5.00 lakhs, the Board of Directors of the Bank has decided to merge the Bank with Maratha Sahakari Bank Ltd. by relaxing the provisions of Section 73AA of the Act in accordance with the provisions of Section 157 of the Act. The stage at which the elections to the Mumbai Bank will be held is being postponed for a period of three months from the date of this order.
