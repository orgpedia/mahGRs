The Proposal:
The government has approved the implementation of mulberry plantation programme in 25 districts of the state under the Mahatma Gandhi Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme.
The Government has developed an online computer system to regulate the field activities of the silk industry at all levels. The field offices have been instructed to fill the information of mulberry cultivation clusters identified in this system. Accordingly, the field offices have filled up information about 119 groups and their villages. According to this information, the government decision dated March 27, 2017 has been approved to implement the mulberry planting program in 119 clusters in the state. However, in addition to the above-mentioned groups, the Regional Office has been instructed to submit its own proposal if the demand for a new group for mulberry cultivation is received. For the expansion and development of sericulture in an effective manner and for the integrated development of the mulberry cultivation to post processing industry in the state, mulberry cultivation in a group manner is necessary. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to approve the cluster at Umri, District-Nanded proposed by the Director (Silk) vide his letter dated 15.03.2018.
Government's decision
For the expansion and development of sericulture in an effective manner and for the integrated development of the mulberry cultivation to post processing industry in the state, mulberry cultivation in a group manner is necessary. For this" Silk Online Control Computer System" is working. In addition to the 119 clusters approved under the Government Resolution dated 27th March, 2017, a new cluster at Umri, District Nanded is now being approved. With the addition of this cluster, the total number of clusters in the State is now 120.
The guidelines on mulberry cultivation and clusters, as mentioned in the Government decision dated 27th March, 2017, shall continue to be applicable.
