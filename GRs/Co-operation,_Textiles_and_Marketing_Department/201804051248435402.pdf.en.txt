The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2018, as specified in Column-4 of the Schedule to the Constitution, has been approved by the Legislature and assented to by the Governor of Maharashtra and is in operation from the date of enactment of the Maharashtra Act, 2018. 22 Therefore, it is informed that the Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary, Part IV dated 27th March, 2018 has been published. This has been notified vide Government Circular dated 02.04.2018.
Under this Act, the Department of Administrative Reforms has been provided with mandatory and programmatic budgetary allocation for the first nine months of 2018-19 (ending December, 2018) to meet the fixed costs of the 2018-19 budget and administrative expenses of the ongoing schemes, subject to a ceiling of 70% of the total annual allocation. The provisions mentioned in column 9 and 10 have been approved. 4 are being handed over to the Executing/ Drawing and Disbursing Officers. The Budgetary allocation for Compulsory and Programme expenditure for 2018-19 is being approved in the prescribed manner subject to a ceiling of 70% of the total annual allocation and 100% of the provision for loan waiver (Cooperation Department). However, the offices implementing the schemes concerned have to submit their reports to the Finance Department. The approval of the Planning/ Social Justice/ Tribal Development and Expenditure Branch and Budget Branch of the Finance Department should be obtained where necessary after considering the checklist for the mandatory/ programmatic items mentioned in the Annexure to the Circular dated 2.4.2018 and the government decision of financial approval should be issued after mentioning the informal references.
The facility of drawing forward/ backward funds as per monthly statement for the period April to December has been provided in the Budget Estimates, Distribution and Control System.
Administrative Departments are not required to obtain separate approval of the Finance Department in respect of matters covered under the powers conferred on them under the Financial Powers Rule Book Part-I, Finance Department Government Decision No. VYPR-2013/ P.S. No. 30/2013/ Regulations-Part-II, dated 17.4.2015 or under the Financial Powers Rule Book Part-II conferred on those Departments from time to time.

The concurrence of the Finance Department will be required in respect of matters for which financial powers have not been specifically delegated to the departments, other than those mentioned in the aforesaid Government Order.
The administrative departments should follow the rules mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook and Financial Powers Rulebook regarding funds. Be sure to follow the instructions below as well.
Provisions for MLA Local Development Fund, Loan Waiver (Cooperative Department) will be available for 100% disbursement. The Department should obtain utilization certificates of previous grants from various Government/ Administrative Institutions as well as grant-in-aid institutions before sanctioning the grants-in-aid. No further subsidy should be released until 75% of the subsidy given 3 months ago is utilized.
Before disbursing the grants to the local bodies, the amount coming to the state government from these institutions should be reviewed and the remaining grants should be disbursed only after recovering it. If the funds made available for expenditure on a programme are unutilized, the funds cannot be released for the same purpose in the current year without the approval of the Finance Department.
While drawing the scheme payments of individual beneficiaries, the grants of individual beneficiaries should not be drawn unless the list of beneficiaries is fixed and linked with the payment with proper details. The programmes of individual beneficiaries should be linked with Aadhaar number and the subsidy should be provided through Electronic Clearance System (ECS) only.
Provisions for Centrally Sponsored Schemes and corresponding State share will be available for 100% disbursement. In respect of Centrally Sponsored Schemes, the concerned department should ensure that the funds are received from the Finance Branch of the Finance Department on the funds and only then if the central funds are received, the funds as well as the state government's equal share should be distributed by the administrative department at their level. However, the consent of the Finance Department is required to spend the previous year's Central Government funds in the current year. The funds allocated to the Centre and the State should not be diverted to other Central/ Centrally Sponsored Schemes.
The payments towards scholarships, reimbursement of tuition fees, individual beneficiaries and grants-in-aid should be made through the Electronic Clearance System (ECS). All such details should be linked to Aadhaar.
The Department shall not incur expenditure in respect of the construction proposal without obtaining administrative approval/ technical approval as well as approval of the Committee of Secretaries, where necessary. Similarly, this fund should not be provided to the Public Works Department in the form of deposit construction.

Purchase payments should be drawn from the Treasury unless the supplier is determined by completing the process of purchasing materials in accordance with the guidelines accompanying the Government Decision dated December 1, 2016 of the Department of Industry, Energy and Labor, and following the conditions set out in paragraph 4.7 of this Guideline. Cheques for purchase should be issued in the name of the supplier concerned and preferably through Electronic Clearance System (ECS).
The expenditure in respect of computerization to be incurred by the department should be as per the government tariff. Wherever necessary, the approval of the Information and Technology Department should be obtained.
Many times funds are spent through short payments, but not submitting the detailed payment of the same may lead to objections and misappropriation. Funds under the head of account should not be drawn without the consent of the Finance Department unless the old detailed payments under that head are submitted.
In the Finance Department. All the terms and conditions mentioned in the circular dated 2.4.2018 (with the accompanying annexure), including the terms and conditions, as well as the rules of business of the State Government, financial rules, should be followed while spending the funds.
