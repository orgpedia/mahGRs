The Proposal: -
The General Financial Rules, 2017 of the Government of India prescribe procedures for disbursing funds to state governments and regulating the allocation of funds through the Public Financial Management System (PFMS). Accordingly, the Central Government has decided to implement SNA-SPARSH (Real Time System of Integrated Quick Transfer), an alternative fund distribution mechanism, in a phased manner to disburse funds of Centrally Sponsored Schemes through an integrated framework of Public Financial Management System (PFMS), State Integrated Financial Management System (State IFMIS) and Reserve Bank of India's e-Kuber System (e-Kuber) to achieve greater efficiency in cash management at both Central and State levels. Accordingly, the Central Government vide Reference No. MIATA in 3 has issued guidelines regarding the operation of the system.

The Central Government vide Reference No. 37 schemes have been included in the memorandum of 7. Among them, the scheme of computerization of centrally sponsored primary agricultural credit supply cooperatives in the cooperative sector has been included in the above system.
Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to appoint a Single Nodal Agency (SNA), a single coordinating mechanism for the scheme of computerization of Centrally Sponsored Primary Agricultural Credit Supply Cooperative Societies for opening of Drawing Accounts in the Reserve Bank of India on State-wise basis under Centrally Sponsored Schemes for implementing the mechanism of Alternative Fund Distribution System (SNA-SPARSH) for disbursal of funds for Centrally Sponsored Schemes.
Government Decision: - The Government is approving the appointment of a Single Nodal Agency for the scheme of Computerization of Centrally Sponsored Primary Agricultural Credit Supply Cooperative Societies under the Department of Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles as follows.
