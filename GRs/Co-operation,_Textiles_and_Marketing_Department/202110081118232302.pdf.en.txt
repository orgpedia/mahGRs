4. Letter from the Commissioner of Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies, Maharashtra State, Pune vide No. Krishipat-8/ Nidhimagani/ 2021-22/21 dated 18/06/2021.
Preamble: The Government have decided to provide incentive concession to the farmers of the State in connection with the recovery of interest rates by the Cooperative Agricultural Credit Societies vide Government Resolution dated 24/11/1988. Accordingly, as per the Government decision dated 02/11/1991, the Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Interest Subvention Scheme has been made applicable to the farmers who have taken crop loans from 01/04/1990. Under this scheme, interest subvention is being provided to farmers who take crop loans from cooperative agricultural credit institutions and repay it in full by June 30 every year.
The scheme is also applicable to farmers taking loans from nationalised banks, rural banks and private banks. However, this scheme is not applicable to overdue loans, as well as medium-term and long-term loans. Under this scheme, farmers are given interest subvention linked to the interest rate on crop loans availed by cooperative agricultural credit societies and banks. As per the Government decision dated 03/12/2012, interest subvention at the rate of 3% per annum on crop loans up to Rs. 1.00 lakh and 1% per annum on crop loans beyond Rs. 3.00 lakh was introduced. Further, the Government vide its decision dated 11/06/2021, provided interest subvention of 3% to the farmers availing short term crop loan upto Rs. 3.00 lakh and repaying the same on time, the loan will be available to the farmers at 0% interest rate.
As per the policy of the State Government, Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh has sanctioned Rs. 10400.00 lakh has been approved for the financial year 2021-22.
60% of the budget for the scheme is allocated to the Planning and Finance Department.
