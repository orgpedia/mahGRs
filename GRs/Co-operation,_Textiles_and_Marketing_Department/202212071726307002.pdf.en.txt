Introduction: Section 88 of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960 mandates the Registrar to appoint an authorised officer and conduct an inquiry against the erring promoters etc. As per the existing provisions, such inquiry is required to be completed within 2 years. However, the registrar is allowed to extend the inquiry for a maximum period of 6 months, citing reasons. It is also provided that the Government may, on the report of the Registrar or on the basis of reasons in writing in person, extend the said period from time to time as may be necessary for completing the inquiry proceedings under sub-section (1) of section 88.
The office of the Divisional Joint Director, Nagpur is being re-examined under the order dated 16.06.2014 to determine the responsibility of the bank officials/ officers for the financial losses incurred by the bank during the period 2000-01 in accordance with the audit and the special report thereon. From the passing of the order dated 16.06.2014 to the final extension granted by the Divisional Joint Director, Cooperative Societies, Nagpur vide his order dated 05.11.2019, a total period of 5 years and 3 months has elapsed. During this period, Excluding the total period of 2 years and 9 months of stay granted by the High Court, the actual period of inquiry has been 2 years and 6 months. Since the period of 2 years and 6 months under the control of the Registrar for the conduct of Section 88 Inquiry has expired on 05.11.2019, the Government has decided to extend the period of 2 years and 6 months under the control of the Registrar for the purpose of investigation. In pursuance of the order dated 06.11.2019, the investigation under Section 88 of the Nagpur GMS Bank has been extended by one year i.e. upto 05.11.2020. Thereafter, vide Government Order dated 21.05.2021, the period of enquiry under Section 88 of GMS Bank, Nagpur was extended till 05.11.2021.

the order
An extension of six months i.e. from 6th November, 2022 to 5th May, 2023 is being granted to complete the ongoing enquiry proceedings under Section 88 of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960 of the Nagpur District Central Cooperative Bank. The authorized officer should complete the inquiry within this period.
The Registrar of the Bank and the Divisional Joint Director, Cooperative Societies, Nagpur shall periodically review the work of the enquiry and ensure that the enquiry is completed on time and shall render appropriate assistance to the authorised officers for the completion of the enquiry.
