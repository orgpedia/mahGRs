The Proposal:
In order to fix the rates of the collateral cost for the procurement to be made under the Minimum Support Price Scheme (MSP) under the Government Order, dated 3/9/2019, the Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary (Food) in its meeting held on Friday, 4.9.2020, has recommended an amount of Rs. 40/- per quintal will continue to be applicable and it was decided that NAFED should submit a proposal to the government after discussing with the concerned to fix the transportation rate. Accordingly, the Government Order had indicated that the transportation cost for procurement at the guaranteed price would be the same as the rate fixed under the Public Distribution System for the concerned district.
However, the Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection, under the new transport system as decided by the Government on 4th April, 2017 and as on 15th January, 2021, has fixed the base rates for direct transport for Municipal Corporation areas and for all Municipal Corporations, Municipalities/ Councils and Rural areas at the district level in two phases.
Under the Minimum Support Price (MSP) scheme, the farm produce is transported from the procurement centre to the warehouse of the Warehousing Corporation. Therefore, the public distribution system

It is not advisable to fix transport charges for transportation of foodgrains and other essential commodities at the rates fixed by the Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection for Phase-I and Phase-II transportation. For this, on the same lines, it is necessary to fix the rates of transportation of agricultural produce purchased under the Minimum Support Price scheme by the Marketing Department.
As per the preliminary discussions held in the meeting held on 5.2.2021 and 4.3.2021 under the chairmanship of Managing Director, Maharashtra State Cooperative Marketing Federation, Mumbai regarding the determination of transportation cost and submission of joint proposal to the Government, the rates available per kilometer under the Public Distribution System from the season 2016-17 onwards or the rates available per kilometer in the nearest year and in the nearest district where both the rates are not available and the transportation rates approved by NAFED and the average index of transportation and communication in the Consumer Price Index obtained from the Reserve Bank of India for each year from 2016 to 2019, the district-wise rates per kilometer for the season 2019-20 for the total 22 districts have been decided by the Federation. This transportation rate has also been approved by NAFED, Mumbai.
The government was considering to approve the proposal of transport tariff jointly submitted by NAFED, Mumbai and Panan Federation, Mumbai.
Government's decision:
