Introduction: Section 102 of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960 lays down the procedure for the assessment of co-operative societies. As per the provisions of the said section, the Registrar may initiate winding up proceedings after making an inquiry under section 83 or after making an inspection under section 84 or after receiving the report of the auditors who have audited the accounts of the institution or if the members pass a resolution at the special general meeting of the institution. as well as institutions. The winding up is done in the case of an institution which has not commenced the work or which has, ii) ceased to function or iii) has ceased to comply with any of the conditions of registration and management contained in this Act or the rules or bye-laws. The matter of determining the criteria in respect of assessment/ demerger of cooperative yarns which have not made satisfactory progress in accordance with the said provisions of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, was under consideration of the Government, accordingly, the Government is taking the following decisions:- GOVERNMENT DECISION: In accordance with the procedure for the assessment of cooperative yarns in accordance with the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, the Government is taking a decision regarding the process of assessment/ demerger of cooperative yarns if they do not meet the following criteria.
The criteria for undertaking the process of valuation/ demerger of cooperative spinning mills are as follows: 1) In case of cooperative spinning mills which have not demanded the final instalment of Government share/ short amount and the mill has not come under production despite payment of Government share earlier by the Government, the Government amount will be recovered by selling the land held by the spinning mill after conducting demerger proceedings.

2) If the government has not completed 33% of the total project report even after 1 year by giving 15% of the total stake, then such spinning will be taken in abeyance.
3) If the government has not completed 66% of the total project report even after 2 years by giving 30% of the total stake, then such spinning will be taken in abeyance.
4) Co-operative yarn mills which have been given full or 99% government shareholding and have not started production even after 4 years will be taken into deprecation.
(5) The above No. Under Sections 1 to 4, the cooperative weavers are being given a period of 6 months from the date of the said Government decision to take further necessary action. During the said period of 6 months, the co-operative spinning mills are required to progress in their operations in proportion to the Government shareholding.
6) The yarn mills which have come under production using only government shareholding should raise loans (as per project report) within the next 3 months and if the yarn mills do not raise loans, action will be taken against them.
7) Co-operative yarns which have not been audited should be audited immediately within 3 months and those who have serious flaws in the audit will also be penalized.
The Director Textiles, Nagpur will be responsible for strictly adhering to the above criteria. The Director Textiles, Nagpur shall ensure that the provisions of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960 are not violated while proceeding in accordance with the said criteria.
