The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2019 (including the amounts specified in column 4 of the Schedule to the Maharashtra Appropriation (Vote on Account) Act, 2019) has been approved by the Legislature and assented to by the Governor of Maharashtra and is published in the Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary as Maharashtra Act No. 08 of 2019, Part IVD. This was published on July 4, 2019. This has been notified vide Government Circular dated 08.07.2019.
Under this Act, the Department of Administrative Affairs is required to provide for the expenditure of the first nine months of the year 2019-20 (ending December, 2019) for meeting the expenditure of the Standing Budget 2019-20 and the administrative expenses of the ongoing schemes within the limit of 60% of the total annual provision (excluding the June, 2019 Supplementary Demand) in the Annexure A attached to this Government Decision. The provisions mentioned in column 9 and 10 have been approved. 4 are being handed over to the Executing/ Drawing and Disbursing Officers. However, the offices implementing the schemes concerned have to submit their reports to the Finance Department. The approval of the Planning/ Social Justice/ Tribal Development and Expenditure Branch and Budget Branch of the Finance Department should be obtained where necessary after considering the checklist for the mandatory/ programmatic items mentioned in the Annexure to the Circular dated 08.07.2019 and the Government decision of financial approval should be issued after mentioning the informal references. The facility of drawing forward/ backward funds as per monthly statement for the period April-19 to December-19 has been provided in the Budget Estimates, Distribution and Control System.
Financial Powers to Administrative Departments Rule Book Part-I, Department of Finance Government Resolution No.VYPR-2013/ P.No. 30/2013/ Regulations-Part-II, D. No separate approval of the Finance Department is required in respect of matters covered under the powers conferred under 17.4.2015 or under the financial powers conferred under Part II of the Financial Powers Rules, 2015 from time to time.

The concurrence of the Finance Department will be required in respect of matters for which financial powers have not been specifically delegated to the departments, other than those mentioned in the aforesaid Government Order.
The administrative departments should follow the rules mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook and Financial Powers Rulebook regarding funds. Be sure to follow the instructions below as well.
The Department should obtain utilization certificates of previous grants from various Government/ Administrative Institutions as well as grant-in-aid institutions before sanctioning the grants-in-aid. No further grant should be released until the utilization of the earlier grant is completed.
Before disbursing the grants to the local bodies, the amount coming to the state government from these institutions should be reviewed and the remaining grants should be disbursed only after recovering it. If the funds made available for expenditure on a programme are unutilized, the funds cannot be released for the same purpose in the current year without the approval of the Finance Department.
While drawing the scheme payments of individual beneficiaries, the grants of individual beneficiaries should not be drawn unless the list of beneficiaries is fixed and linked with the payment with proper details. The programmes of individual beneficiaries should be linked with Aadhaar number and the subsidy should be provided through Electronic Clearance System (ECS) only.
In respect of Centrally Sponsored Schemes, the concerned department should ensure that the funds are received from the Finance Branch of the Finance Department on the funds and only then if the central funds are received, the funds as well as the state government's equal share should be distributed by the administrative department at their level. However, the consent of the Finance Department is required to spend the previous year's Central Government funds in the current year.
The payments towards scholarships, reimbursement of tuition fees, individual beneficiaries and grants-in-aid should be made through the Electronic Clearance System (ECS).
The Department shall not incur expenditure in respect of the construction proposal without obtaining administrative approval/ technical approval as well as approval of the Committee of Secretaries, where necessary. Similarly, this fund should not be provided to the Public Works Department in the form of deposit construction.
The Department of Industry, Energy and Labour has issued its No. Purchase payments should be drawn from the Treasury, unless the supplier is determined by completing the guidelines accompanying the Government Decision dated 1st December, 2016, Bhankas-2014/ Q.No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4 and following the conditions in paragraph 4.7 of this Guideline. Cheques for purchase should be issued in the name of the supplier concerned and preferably through Electronic Clearance System (ECS).
