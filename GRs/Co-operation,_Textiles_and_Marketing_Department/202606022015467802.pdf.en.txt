Introduction Farmers in Maharashtra are an important part of the state's rural economy. However, natural calamities such as erratic rainfall, hailstorms, droughts, heavy rains and floods often cause crop damage. Alternately, farmers incur huge economic losses. As a result, the affected farmers are unable to repay their farm loans on time. Such farmers are not able to get fresh crop loans from banks as they are in arrears.
To reduce the debt burden on farmers, the government has in the past provided substantial support to farmers from time to time by announcing the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Shetkari Samman Yojana, 2017, Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Relief Scheme, 2019 and the Incentive Benefit Scheme under Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Relief Scheme, 2022.
The farmers of the state are seen to be stuck in a vicious cycle of repeated defaults even after loan waivers. There is, therefore, a need for short-term and long-term measures to bring about a paradigm shift in the agriculture sector and in the lives of farmers to permanently address the debt burden of farmers. To submit study recommendations to the Government in this regard, Mr. WATT Pardeshi, Md. The meeting was chaired by Principal Additional Adviser to the Chief Minister and CEO, Mitra. The High Powered Committee was constituted by the Government Decision of 30.10.2025. The committee The report has been submitted to the government on 30.04.2026.

On 30.09.2025, eligible farmers who have defaulted on crop loans will get Rs. The "Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Holkar Farmers Debt Relief Scheme" has been announced to provide debt relief up to ₹2.00 lakh.
