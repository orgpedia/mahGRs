Read: - Provisions in section 73A of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960.
The election process for the cooperative societies in the state is carried out by the State Cooperative Election Authority under the provisions of Section 73A of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960.
Presently, by-elections have been announced in Rahuri, District Ahilyanagar and Baramati, District Pune assembly constituencies in the state. This by-election is likely to create difficulties in terms of manpower, police deployment, availability of polling stations required for the cooperative elections as the respective administrative and police agencies will be busy.
According to the provisions of Section 73A of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, the government has the power to postpone the elections to the cooperative societies in cases where, in the opinion of the state government, it is not in the public interest to hold the elections to the cooperative societies, as any election programme of the state legislative assembly or legislative council or of the Lok Sabha or local authority falls during the elections to the class of any institution. Considering this provision, the government is issuing the following orders.
