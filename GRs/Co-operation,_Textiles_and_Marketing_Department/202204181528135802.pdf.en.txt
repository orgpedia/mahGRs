The total number of cooperative sugar mills in Maharashtra is 107 and the number of multi-state cooperative sugar mills is 11. The responsibility of the executive directors of the cooperative sugar mills is important. Earlier, a panel of 66 executive directors was formed in 2005 and 100 in 2015 to work as executive directors on cooperative sugar mills in the state. Out of these, a total of 69 Executive Directors are presently working in various cooperative sugar mills.
Sugar industry is changing and ethanol, biogas, pharmacare are being developed on a large scale. Therefore, it is proposed to have a new panel of executive directors of cooperative sugar mills in the state with some changes to meet the new challenges.
Considering the requirement of the cooperative sugar mills in the state for the next 5 years, a proposal to form a panel of additional 50 executive directors is under consideration of the government

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: SASAKA-2021/ PR. NO. 251/25-C, GOVERNMENT DECISION:
The government has decided to form a committee as follows for the selection of eligible candidates for the post of executive director of cooperative sugar mills in the state.
Sugar Commissioner, State of Maharashtra, Pune: Chairman 2) Director General, Vasantdada Sugar Institute, Pune: Member 3) Managing Director, Maharashtra State Cooperative: Member Sugar Factory Association, Mumbai 4) Joint Director (Administration), Sugar Commissioner's Office, Pune 5) Expert persons in this field (maximum two) Special invitees. The Executive Directors selected by the Committee shall be appointed in the first instance in the junior pay scale.
The Government has decided to include 66 Executive Directors from the 2005 panel and 100 Executive Directors from the 2015 panel and 50 Executive Directors to be selected as per this Government decision in the new panel of Executive Directors announced earlier by the Office of the Sugar Commissioner. It will be mandatory for all cooperative sugar mills in the state to appoint an executive director from this panel.
According to this government decision, if there is any problem or ambiguity while selecting a new executive director, it should be resolved by the sugar commissioner at his level.
The examination for selection to the post of Executive Director of Cooperative Sugar Mills will be conducted by an external agency and the educational qualification, age limit, nature of examination and procedure of appointment etc. required for the post will be as indicated in the annexure attached to the said Government Resolution.
