Government Decision, Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles Department No. Policy-2023/ PR No. 81/ TEX-5, dated 02.06.2023. (Paragraph No. 7.4) Preamble: The Government Decision dated 02.06.2023 has announced an Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy 2023-28 to promote the growth of the textile sector in the State. One of the objectives of this policy is to provide additional incentives to handloom weavers with a view to ensure livelihood security and economic and social development of handloom textile weavers. This policy
Section No. 7.4.It is stated that free electricity up to 200 units per month will be provided to the families of handloom weavers. The government was considering to provide free electricity up to 200 units per month to the families of handloom weavers.
Government Decision: As per the Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy 2023-28, to provide additional incentives to handloom weavers with a view to ensuring livelihood security and economic and social development of handloom textile weavers, the Government has decided to provide free electricity up to 200 units per month to handloom weaver families.
2. Eligibility of beneficiary family handloom weavers under the scheme: (a) The beneficiary handloom weaver should be the latest handloom registered weaver of the Central Government and should have such an identity card.
(b) If there are more beneficiaries in the same family, only one beneficiary can apply for the scheme.
(c) The weaver should have been in business for six months before the date of application.
As a proof of the last 6 months of weaving, the bill for the purchase of raw materials/ the bill for the sale of pakka goods/ the certificate of the concerned organization regarding the payment of weaving labor to the weaver if he is a member of the corporation, federation or organization.

(d) The applicant must have proof of residence in Maharashtra.
(e) Power connection should be in the name of the beneficiary handloom weaver family.
(e) The beneficiary will have to pay the bill amount for the consumption of more than 200 units of free electricity from 1 to 200 units (200 units) per month to the families of handloom weavers.
(e) The weaver family will be required to pay the electricity bill regularly.
No late fee will be charged by the government.
(f) The beneficiary handloom weavers under the scheme will be regularly reviewed by the Government machinery. If the beneficiary is found to have left the handloom business in this review, such beneficiary will not be eligible for the electricity subsidy rebate.
Implementation of the plan:
(a) The person possessing the above qualification shall submit an application to the Regional Deputy Commissioner concerned in Annexure A attached.
(b) The applications received every month shall be examined by the Technical Officer/ Staff of the office of the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner Textiles and Weavers Service Centre. The concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner (Textiles) will scrutinize and scrutinize the applications received and submit the list of handloom weavers along with their recommendations to the Commissioner (Textiles) Textiles Commissionerate, Nagpur for approval.
(c) The applications received from the office of the Regional Deputy Commissioner, Textiles, shall be placed before the following Joint Committee.
(a) Commissioner (Textiles), Textiles Commissionerate, Nagpur Chairman (b) Joint Commissioner Technical, Textiles Commissionerate, Nagpur Member Secretary (c) Chief Engineer, MSEDCL, Nagpur or his representative Member (d) Regional Deputy Commissioner Textiles (concerned)- Member (e) Deputy Director, Weavers Service Centre (concerned)- Member (f) Maharashtra State Handloom Corporation Limited.
