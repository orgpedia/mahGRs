Government's decision:
In compliance with the Government Decision Read No. 1 regarding supply of textiles by Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation, the following procedures are prescribed for supply of textiles by Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation to Government/ Semi-Government Offices:
(1) The machinery cooperatives which have been financed by the State Government under the Rasavini Scheme registered with the Maharashtra State Machinery Corporation shall supply the fabric produced by such institutions and other machinery cooperatives as well as by the textile units registered under the Small and Micro Industries.
(2) Under no circumstances should cloth be supplied by purchasing cloth from private traders other than the institutions mentioned in No. 1) above.
(3) If the institutions mentioned in No. 1 above are not able to supply the cloth produced by them, the Machinery Corporation shall take action in accordance with the Government Decision of the Department of Industry, Energy and Labor (No. 2) read above while purchasing cloth from other sources.
(4) After the supply of textiles from the machinery cooperatives which have been financed by the Government under the NSS scheme mentioned in No. 1 above, an amount of 1% of the amount payable by the machinery corporation to the concerned institutions or the amount recoverable from the concerned institution, whichever is less, shall be paid by the machinery cooperative societies. The Corporation should repay the loan and deposit the amount in the government account.

5. In view of the productivity of all the above No. 1 institutions, they should be given orders for supply in almost equal proportion to the production capacity. Also, all institutions should adopt a cyclical approach while placing orders for supply of fabrics to ensure that orders for supply of fabrics are received.
(6) In order to prevent the supply of cloth from other sources using the name of the above mentioned No. 1), the transport and other documents should be sought by the Machinery Corporation from the concerned institutions and those documents should be examined at the corporation level in some supply cases.
(7) The Corporation shall ensure that the quality of fabric shall be as prescribed by the Government offices/ undertakings and as per the specifications laid down by the Government and the fabric shall be supplied within the prescribed time limit.
8) The textile demands received from the Machinery Corporation should be dealt with at the corporation level in accordance with the existing rules and the corporation should submit a quarterly report to the government on the action taken in accordance with such demands. Also, the textile supply demands that will be received by the government should be reported to the machinery corporation from the government level for further action.
The above procedures are effective with immediate effect and the Machinery Corporation shall strictly follow the above procedures while supplying fabrics to Government/ Semi-Government offices and undertakings as per their demand.
