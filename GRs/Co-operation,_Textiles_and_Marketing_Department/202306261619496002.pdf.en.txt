The Maharashtra Agricultural Produce Marketing (Development and Regulation) Act, 1963, and the following Rules, 1967, are in force in the State. Before the coming of this rule/ law i.e. before the formation of the state of Maharashtra and before the re-organization of the state, employees were working in the then Agricultural Produce Market Committees in various parts of the state including the Mumbai Bilingual Association. The existing rules for these employees were the same as for government employees. Service rules were applicable to some Marathwada employees and some Vidarbha employees.
In this case, the employees who retired after 25/05/1967 from the Agricultural Produce Market Committee in the state have demanded the benefit of retirement pay from the government level like the government employees. Vinod Kalyanrao Shiradkar-Deshmukh, who retired from the Nanded Agricultural Produce Market Committee, has sought information from the government under the Right to Information Act, 2005, from time to time.
