The Proposal:
Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of" Silk Samagra-ISDSI (Integrated Scheme for Deveoplement of Silk Industry) Scheme 2018-19 to 2019-20," various schemes for expansion and development of silk industry were approved vide Government Resolution dated 24.05.2019. The scheme had expired on March 31, 2020. However, since the Central Silk Board has approved the continuation of the Silk Samagra Yojana during the financial year 2020-21 vide letter dated 12th May, 2020, the administrative approval for continuation of the scheme in the State during the financial year 2020-21 has been accorded vide Government Resolution dated 14th January, 2021.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: KENPUYO-2020/ PR No.6/ Silk Cell
As requested by the Director (Silk), the Central Silk Board has intimated vide its letter dated 05.06.2020, regarding immediate expenditure on priority basis of the unutilized central share of Rs. 14.906 lakh for the year 2018-19. Accordingly, the Department of Finance has approved the proposal for sanctioning and utilization of funds to the tune of Rs. 20.639 lakh out of the total budgetary provision of Rs. 14.906 lakh as Central share and Rs. 5.733 lakh as State share, which remained unutilized out of the funds received for Scheduled Tribes Beneficiaries (TSPs) during 2018-19 under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of" Silk Samagra" in 2020-21. Therefore, the matter of making available the unutilized funds under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Silk Samagra (ISDSI) to the Director (Silk) on Budget Distribution System was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The Government hereby approve the release and utilization of unspent Central share of Rs. 14.906 lakh and corresponding State share of Rs. 5.733 lakh out of the funds received for the Scheduled Tribes Beneficiaries (TSPs) during 2018-19 under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Silk Samagra (ISDSI) for the following items: (Rs. in Lakhs)

The funds shall be spent by the Director (Silk) as per the administrative approval given to the revised standards of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of" Silk Samagra" (ISDSI) and the guidelines in this regard. Also, it should be ensured that the funds are spent on the schemes and things for which the central government has provided funds.
