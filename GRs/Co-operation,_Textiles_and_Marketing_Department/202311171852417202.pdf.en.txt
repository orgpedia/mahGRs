Introduction: The context. As per the Government decision dated 11.01.2017, the Co-operative Yarn Millers have received financial assistance from Financial Institutions/ National Cooperative Development Corporation to the tune of Rs. 3,000/- per month for a period of 5 years and interest up to 12% is being paid by the Government. Co-workers who. The scheme covers yarns whose loans are sanctioned up to 31.03.2019. This is an interest subvention scheme. It is valid till 31.03.2024.
Since the scheme is for 5 years, the cooperative weavers under production and under partial production have been requested to extend the duration of the scheme. Accordingly, a proposal was under consideration to extend the scheme for payment of interest on loans taken by Co-operative Yarn Millers from Financial Institutions/ N.S.V.M. for a further period of 5 years by amending the scheme. In pursuance of the decision taken in the Cabinet meeting held on Thursday, 19.10.2023, the Government is taking the following decisions:
Government's decision:
In order to ensure the smooth functioning of the Co-operative Yarn Mills in the State and to overcome the financial constraints, the Co-operative Yarn Mills, on the lines of the scheme implemented under the Government decision dated 11.01.2017, with the approval of the Cabinet, have sanctioned a sum of Rs. 5000/- for 5 years.

The scheme for providing interest upto a maximum of 12% on medium term loans (including moratorium period of two years) is being approved under this Government decision with the following terms and conditions.
After the issuance of the government decision of the proposed scheme, the co-operative yarn mills which will take a new loan of 5 years medium term till the next 1 calendar year, will remain eligible for the proposed scheme.
To take advantage of this scheme, the co-operative weavers should take a medium term loan of 5 years from the financial institution/ NSS. The benefit of this scheme will be applicable only to the co-operative weavers who have taken loans for the next 1 calendar year after this decision.
The loan should be taken for a period of 5 years only as per the Reducing Balance Method of the term loan type. Other types of loans such as cash credit, goods mortgage cash credit, bullet repayment type of loan (repayment of the loan amount in a lump sum after the loan period), restructuring loan, etc. as well as loans based on Monthly Compounding Basis will not be eligible for this scheme.
4). In order to avail the benefit of this scheme, the 29 co-operative yarn mills which are presently availing the benefit of this scheme under the Government decision dated 11.01.2017, are required to first submit a certificate from the concerned bank that they have repaid the entire loan taken under the scheme announced under the Government decision dated 11.01.2017 with the entire principal amount with interest. Only after this, if the yarn mills take fresh term loans from financial institutions/ NSS within the prescribed period of 5 years, they will be eligible for this scheme.
5) Co-operative yarn mills which will take advantage of the proposed scheme will be required to repay the principal in 5 equal installments every year after a moratorium period of two years as per the Reducing Balance Method. Also, if the yarn mills do not make the repayment of the principal amount within the stipulated time, these yarn mills will automatically be ineligible to take advantage of the scheme.
6) The benefit of this scheme will be stopped after two years to the co-spinning mills who do not bring the spinning plant to full production in the next two years.
