The Asian Development Bank (ADB)-funded Maharashtra Agribusiness Network (MAGNET) project has been approved to be implemented in all districts of the state for a period of six years (from 2020 to 2026). The Maharashtra Agribusiness Network (MAGNET) has been set up under the Societies Registration Act, 1860. The total cost of the project is US$ 142.9 million (approximately Rs. 1000.00 crore), 70% (US$ 100 million) of the amount has been approved as a low-interest loan from Asian Development Bank and 30% (US$ 42.9 million) of the amount has been approved to be invested from State Government's own funds. For the year 2021-22, a total outlay of Rs. 11000.00 lakh has been earmarked for the Magnet project, out of which Rs. 3300.00 lakh is for State share and Rs. 7700.00 lakh is for external share. Out of which, in the Monsoon Session of July, 2021, a supplementary demand for Rs. 2640.40 lakh for State share and Rs. 6183.40 lakh for external share for the Magnet project has been approved for a total amount of Rs. 8823.80 lakh. Out of the amount sanctioned through Supplementary Demand, the Government was under consideration to disburse the first instalment of Rs. 1259.00 lakh to the State Account and Rs. 2835.00 lakh to the External Account for the financial year 2021-22, totalling Rs. 4094.00 lakh, as approved by the Finance Department for implementation of the Magnet Project.
