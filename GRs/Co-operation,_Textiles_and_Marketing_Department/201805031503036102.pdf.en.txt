Regarding the formation of a committee to determine the amount of subsidy for running cooperative spinning and textile projects on non-conventional energy.

Introduction- Textile sector plays a very important role in the economic structure of the state. After agriculture, textile industry is one of the most important industries in the state. After agriculture, this industry has the potential to create maximum employment. The state government has announced the Maharashtra Textile Policy 2018-23. A committee of the Director General Maharashtra Energy Development Agency (MEDA) and the Director Textile Industries will be formed to decide the amount of subsidy to run cooperative weavers on solar energy in the state's textile policy for three years. That was decided. Accordingly, a committee was constituted by the Government to suggest necessary measures in this regard.
Government Decision: As per the State Textile Policy 2018-23, the Government is approving the appointment of a Committee comprising of the following members under the Chairmanship of Director, Textiles, to suggest and recommend measures to determine the quantum of subsidy for running the cooperative weavers and other textile industries in the State on non-conventional energy.
The members of the Committee shall be as follows: 1. Director (Textiles), Directorate of Textiles, Nagpur Chairman 2. Director General, Maharashtra Energy Development Agency (MEDA), Member 3. Director (Commerce), MSEDCL, Member 4. Joint Director (Textiles), Directorate of Textiles, Nagpur Member Secretary 3. The Committee shall study the following aspects and submit measures and recommendations to the Government.
The functions of the Committee will be as follows: (i) To determine the quantum of subsidy for running cooperative societies on non-conventional energy, to determine the eligibility criteria for subsidy, to frame the framework for implementation and to suggest measures required for ease of governance.
(ii) To determine the quantum of subsidy for running textile projects other than cooperative societies on non-conventional energy, determine the eligibility criteria for subsidy, outline the implementation and suggest measures required for administrative simplification.

The committee will submit its report to the government within two months.
