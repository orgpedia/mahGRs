Preamble: In pursuance of the decision of the Government in this regard, the" Incentive Benefit Scheme under Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Waiver Scheme 2019" has been announced and the scheme is under implementation in the State.
The eligible farmers will be given incentives. Fund management, transfer of benefit amount directly to the beneficiary's loan account, technical assistance in computer portal and pool account, customer support, report generation, delivery and synchronization of fund transfer, continuous follow up with various banks and primary agricultural credit institutions, SMS/ email intimation, monitoring of funds, etc.
Government Circular:" Incentive Benefit Scheme under Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Waiver Scheme 2019" The selection of State Bank of India Bank for the scheme, as well as fund management, transfer of benefit amount from the scheme directly to the beneficiary's loan account, technical assistance in computer portal and pool account, customer support, report generation, delivery and reconciliation of fund transfer, continuous follow up with various banks and primary agricultural credit institutions, intimation through SMS/ email, monitoring of funds etc.
The State Bank of India is required to complete the payment obligations within the time limit of Transaction+ 1 day and refunds due to default in the payment process must also be completed within the time period of Transaction+ 1 day.
Monthly/ quarterly reconciliation of transactions in the said bank account and audit will be required.
In case of closure of the said bank account, the balance of the balance should be reconciled and the remaining amount should be deposited immediately by the Commissioner of Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies, Maharashtra State, Pune under the appropriate government account head.
State Bank of India will not charge any fee for all services/ transactions in the said account.

If the amount of unpaid/ rejected cases in State Bank of India Bank remains more than one day (excluding bank holidays), then the bank will have to pay interest at the rate of 10% per annum on such inoperative funds.
The work order will be issued separately to State Bank of India Bank as above.
Accordingly, the Commissioner of Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies, State of Maharashtra, Pune is being authorized to enter into a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with State Bank of India Bank. The Cooperation Commissioner should immediately initiate the process of opening a zero balance savings account in State Bank of India. The Commissioner for Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies, State of Maharashtra, Pune should review the implementation of the MoU from time to time.
