To provide relief to the farmers having outstanding and unpaid dues up to Rs. 2.00 lakh, the Government has decided to launch the" Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Waiver Scheme 2019." The Cabinet meeting was held on 24.12.2019. According to the A Government Resolution for the scheme was issued on 27.12.2019 and the scheme is under implementation. So far, about 19 lakh account holders have been given the benefit of the scheme and the process of giving the benefit to the rest is underway.
The objective of the scheme is to provide crop loans to farmers in Kharif 2020 season by opening their loan accounts with the benefit of loan waiver. Once the list of eligible account holders is published in the scheme, it is meant to be authenticated by the account holders in the list.
After such authentication, the amount of loan waiver on the loan account of the account holders who have authenticated is given by the government to the concerned banks. While the scheme was implemented in March, 2020, it is currently being implemented in the State since 24.03.2020 under the Disaster Management Act, 2005 due to COVID-19 (Corona Virus) across the world. It is closed until May 31, 2020. Not only this, the source of income of the state has also been affected due to this epidemic. Also, the funds available have been diverted for the treatment of the disease, the Finance Department said. In the absence of funds, it will not be possible to provide benefits to the remaining eligible beneficiaries of the" Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Waiver Scheme 2019" immediately. The beneficiaries will not be able to get new crop loans in the ongoing Kharif 2020 season if their accounts are not verified.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: CRICMA 1219/ PR. NO. 157/2-C
The Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Waiver Scheme 2019 aims to provide loan waiver benefits to farmers in arrears, by opening their loan accounts and providing them with crop loans in Kharif 2020.
It is to get a loan. In view of the above facts, the Government was considering to direct the banks to ensure that the accounts of eligible beneficiaries under the scheme are cleared at the earliest and they get fresh crop loans in Kharif 2020 season.
Government's decision:
Under the Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Loan Waiver Scheme 2019, the government is approving the following actions for the farmers whose loan accounts have not yet been credited with the benefit of loan waiver.
a) Accounts in District Central Co-operative Bank: 1) Under the Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Loan Waiver Scheme 2019, the government should provide crop loans for Kharif 2020 to the beneficiaries whose loan accounts have not been given benefits under the scheme.
2) The District Central Cooperative Banks should show the outstanding amount from the list issued under the scheme on the loan account of the above-mentioned defaulting farmers under the loan waiver scheme. The District Central Cooperative Banks should inform the various executive cooperative societies concerned in this regard. The various executive cooperative societies concerned should show the amount standing on the loan account of such farmers as" coming from the government" and they should provide crop loans to such farmers for Kharif 2020.
(3) The interest on the amount received from the Government shall be charged by the District Central Co-operative Banks from 01.04.2020 till the date of receipt of such amount by them as mentioned above. Such funds will be given by the government to the concerned district central cooperative bank with interest. However, if the crop loan for Kharif 2020 is made available by the respective District Central Banks to the farmers whose loan account has not been credited to the loan account in the list published on the portal, the government will pay interest to the respective District Central Cooperative Banks on the amount due from the government under the loan waiver scheme of such account holders.
(b) Accounts in Commercial Banks and Gramin Banks: 1) Under the Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Loan Waiver Scheme 2019, the commercial banks and Gramin Banks should show the amount of loan waiver of the beneficiaries in the list published on the scheme portal by the Government as coming from the Government.
