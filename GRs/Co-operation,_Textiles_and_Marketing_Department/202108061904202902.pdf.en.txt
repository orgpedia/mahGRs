Department of Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles, Government Resolution No: 2020/ S.No. 07/22 dated 02.03.2020.
In exercise of the powers conferred by clause (1) of section 154 (a) of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, the appointments of the non-official members of the Maharashtra State Co-operative Council to the post of Chairman and Members were cancelled by the decision of the Government in question. Hence, the Council had to be reconstituted and a new Chairman/ Member appointed. Accordingly, a proposal was under consideration to appoint Mr. Vidyadhar Vamanrao Anaskar, Pune, as the President of Maharashtra State Cooperative Council for the purpose of reconstituting the Council. The government has taken the following decisions:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In exercise of the powers conferred by the provisions of section 154 (a) (1) of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, the Maharashtra State Cooperative Council is being reconstituted by this Order. Further, as per the provisions of the said Article 154 (a) (4), the tenure of the Council shall be for a period of three years from the date of August 6, 2021, or until further orders, whichever is less.
In exercise of the powers conferred by clause (1) of section 154 (a) of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, the Government hereby appoint the Maharashtra State Co-operative Council (MSCSC) as the Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Company. Council) Mr. Vidyadhar Vamanrao Anaskar, Ra.
Riturang Co-operative Housing Society Ltd. Phase-II, Opp City Light Bakery, Aranyeshwar, Tavare Colony, Pune is being appointed as Chairman with effect from 06.08.2021.

The Maharashtra State Cooperative Societies Act, 1960
The provisions of section 154A (3) shall be as follows:
(1) To advise the State Government on all such matters relating to the cooperative movement; (2) To review the cooperative movement and suggest ways and means of coordinating the functioning of the cooperative societies in the State; (3) To suggest ways and means for removing difficulties faced by the cooperative societies; (4) To report to the State Government on all such matters as the State Government may refer to the Council; (5) To recommend schemes and policies for the development of the cooperative movement in the State; (6) To develop the existing projects for the development of the cooperative movement, particularly for the development of the backward classes and economically weaker sections of the society.
To evaluate and suggest new projects; 7) To advise the State Government in implementing special projects for economic development through cooperative mode; and 8) To undertake studies for any of the above purposes through the Department or a specially constituted Board.
