The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2023 (Maharashtra Appropriation Act, 2023) has been passed by the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly. The Governor has given his assent to the same and the same has been published in the Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part IV dated 29th March, 2023 as Maharashtra Act No. 17 of 2023. 12.04.2023 is mentioned in the circular.
2. Demand No. 2023-24 for the financial year as per Government Circular in respect of Finance Department. Under V-2, V-6, the funds will be disbursed under the targets authorized to the administrative departments based on the Budget Estimate 2023-24 (White Book) as approved by the Finance Department.
The Budgetary Allocation and Control System (BEMS) will be allocated the budgetary allocation of Budget Estimates for the first nine months of 2023-24 (December 2023), subject to the requirement of 70% of the total annual outlay, after the monthly fund statements, budget estimates, allocation and reporting by the administrative departments on the Control System (BEMS).
4. For schemes under mandatory expenditure as per the circular under reference of the Finance Department, in the Annexure A attached to this Government Resolution, Column No. The approved provision in Pillar No.8. The controllers referred to in section 6 shall be handed over to the authorities in a phased manner. Funding will be disbursed at 20% for the first quarter April-June 2023, 20% for the quarter July-September 2023, and 30% for the quarter October-December 2023. They should ensure that the budgetary provision for the new year 2023-24 is spent in the prescribed manner within the limit of 70% after fulfilling the conditions mentioned in the circular under the Finance Department as well as the prevailing terms, conditions and conditions.
The amount of expenditure of the funds disbursed for the year 2023-24 should be properly planned by the Controlling Authorities. This should be reviewed every three months to ensure that there is no excessive spending in the last month of the year.
All the Controllers and Disbursing Officers under the Department of Co-operative Marketing and Textiles have to ensure compliance of the provisions of the Rules of Business of the State Government, Financial Rules, Budget Rule Book and Rules of Procedure of the Department of Finance before drawing and spending the funds made available on the Budget Estimate Allocation and Control System subject to the conditions mentioned in the circular. The guidelines in paragraphs 4 to 18 of the circular dated 12.04.2023 and the guidelines in the accompanying Annexure shall be strictly followed.
