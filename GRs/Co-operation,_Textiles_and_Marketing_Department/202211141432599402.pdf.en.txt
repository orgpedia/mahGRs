Introduction: Eggs are supplied to the silk farmers of the state through the government egg production center in Gadhinglaj G. Kolhapur. A seed is needed to produce eggs. For this, seed eggs are brought from Mysore (Karnataka) state and supplied to the local seed bank farmers. The seeds produced by them are procured from the centre at Gadhinglaj in Kolhapur district and the eggs are produced using the seeds. At present, the Directorate of Sericulture is under reference from the farmers. Mulberry Silk Seed Funds are procured from the farmers at the rate of Rs.600 to Rs.650/- per kg as per the rates prescribed vide letter dated 17th September, 2013. National Silkworm Seed Organisation (NSSO), Central Silk Board, Bangalore. As per the circular dated December 29, 2021, the rates of seed kosha have been revised from₹ 900 to₹ 1000/- per kg depending on the pupation percentage. At present, farmers are not ready to take the seed fund as the rate of the local farmers' seed fund is less than the Central Silk Board's rate. This affects the production of eggs. Farmers in the State have been demanding a hike in the price of sugarcane. Therefore, as per the said circular of Central Silk Board, the Director (Silk) has issued a reference regarding the revision of procurement price of mulberry silk seed funds as per the pupation percentage. The proposal has been submitted vide letter dated March 17, 2022. Accordingly, the matter of revising the purchase price of mulberry silk seed treasury was under the consideration of the government.
