Preamble: In order to resolve various issues arising during the online recruitment process in the District Central Co-operative Bank, Reference No. 5, dated 15.06.2018, the Commissioner for Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies vide Reference No. The proposal has been submitted vide letter dated 10.12.2021. Accordingly, reference No. The government was considering amending the Government Order No. 1 and No. 2.
Government Resolution: As per Section 79A (1) of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, the reference number issued for the recruitment process of all District Central Cooperative Banks in the state under the authority of the Government. The Government is pleased to amend the Government Order No. 1 and No. 2 as follows:
The recruitment process will be conducted online only.
In the investigation of various complaints received so far, the offline examination system adopted for the recruitment of servants should be discontinued in view of the external interference found in the answer sheets, marksheets, etc. in the post-exam period. Henceforth, the examination for recruitment to various posts in all district central cooperative banks in the state should be conducted only through computerized online mode.
The online recruitment will exclude Class IV employees such as security guards, drivers (excluding the post of peon), posts in senior management, middle management and junior management as mentioned in Chapter V of the State Level Task Force Report of NABARD and special/ technical posts. The report will include 25 posts for Category A banks, 21 posts for Category B banks, 18 posts for Category C banks and 15 posts for Category D banks.

ii.The organization should have conducted online recruitment process at least three times in the past for commercial banks (nationalized banks/ private banks/ regional rural banks), district central cooperative banks, urban cooperative banks and various departments of the government. While selecting the institute, the institution with the capacity to conduct the examination as per the requirement and the availability of manpower required for the same should be considered on priority.
The institution should not have been blacklisted or found guilty of examination malpractices, corruption, criminal interference, etc. in the investigation of complaints received against it in connection with any recruitment.
The selected institution must have the facility to carry out the recruitment process efficiently and efficiently through online mode. If it is not possible to conduct the examination in a single session considering the number of examinees in a district, the online examination should be conducted in the nearest larger district location or in more than one session keeping in view the availability of infrastructure as per the number of candidates.
It will be the responsibility of the institution conducting the online examination to ensure that the difficulty level of the question paper remains the same while conducting the examination in more than one session.
