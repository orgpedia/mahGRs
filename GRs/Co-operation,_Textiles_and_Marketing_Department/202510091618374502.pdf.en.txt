Preamble: - The Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India has decided to propose SNA-SPARSH (Timely System Accelerated Transfer), a fund flow mechanism for disbursal of funds of Centrally Sponsored Schemes through an integrated structure of Public Financial Management System, Integrated Financial Management Information System of the State and E-Kuber System of the Reserve Bank of India, to achieve greater efficiency in cash management at both Central and State levels, vide order No. 2.
Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India, Reference No. An additional 22 schemes and 10 states have been newly included for implementation under "SNA-SPARSH" under the Office Memorandum at 3. The newly added Yo state also includes the state of Maharashtra. As per the direction of the Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India, it is mandatory for the concerned department of the State Government to open an affiliate scheme-wise separate withdrawal account with the Reserve Bank of India for implementation of Centrally Sponsored Schemes under "SNA-SPARSH." For this, the Finance Department has issued Reference No. 4 Under 26 Centrally Sponsored Schemes being implemented in Maharashtra as per the Government decision here.
