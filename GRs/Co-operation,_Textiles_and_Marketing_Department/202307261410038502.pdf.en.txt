Preamble: The Government have decided to provide incentive concession to the farmers of the State in connection with the recovery of interest rate by the Cooperative Agricultural Credit Societies vide Government Resolution dated 24/11/1988. Accordingly, as per the decision of the Government dated 02/11/1991, the Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Interest Subvention Scheme has been implemented from 01/04/1990 to the farmers who have taken crop loans. Under this scheme, interest subvention is being provided to farmers who take crop loans from cooperative agricultural credit institutions and repay it in full by June 30 every year. The scheme is also applicable to farmers who take loans from nationalised banks, rural banks and private banks. However, this scheme is not applicable to overdue loans, as well as medium-term and long-term loans. Under this scheme, interest subvention is being provided to farmers linked to the interest rate on crop loans availed by cooperative agricultural credit societies and banks. As per Government decision dated 03/12/2012, interest subvention@ 3% per annum has been provided on crop loans upto Rs. 1.00 lakh and@ 1% per annum on crop loans upto Rs. 3.00 lakh. Further, the Government vide its decision dated 11/06/2021 has decided to provide interest subvention of 3% to the farmers availing short term crop loan upto Rs. 3.00 lakh and repaying the same on time, at zero per cent (0%) to the farmers.
