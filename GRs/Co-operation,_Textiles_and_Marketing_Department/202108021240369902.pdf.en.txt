Official Memorandum of the Government of India, Ministry of Finance, No.F.No.1 (13)/ PFMS/ FCD/ 2020, d. 23rd March, 2021 (2) Circular to the Government, Finance Department, No. 2021/ No. 35/ Exchequer Proceedings-4, D. July 1, 2021.
Preamble: In order to ensure effective management of funds of Centrally Sponsored Schemes and to bring more efficiency in the process of allocation of funds, the Government of India vide its Order dated 23.03.2021 has been directed to adopt a revised methodology.
Accordingly, it is necessary to appoint a single nodal agency (SNA) for the implementation of centrally sponsored schemes and open a separate single bank account in the authorized bank for that scheme. Further, it has been directed to appoint at least a Deputy Secretary level officer as Nodal Officer for coordination of the said Centrally Sponsored Schemes. Accordingly, the matter of appointing a single nodal agency (SNA) and a nodal officer for the centrally sponsored schemes for the development of the silk industry in the state was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
Finance Department Reference No. As per the instructions issued vide Circular No.2, the Director (Silk), Directorate of Silk, Maharashtra State, Nagpur is being appointed as the Single Nodal Agency (SNA) for implementation of Centrally Sponsored Schemes for development of Silk Industry in the State under Department of Textiles. Further, Deputy Secretary (Silk), Department of Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles is being appointed as Nodal Officer for coordination of these Centrally Sponsored Schemes.
Funds for Centrally Sponsored Schemes will be received by the Central Government as per the revised methodology from 01.07.2021. Therefore, the Single Nodal Agency (SNA) appointed as aforesaid, has been entrusted with the task of managing the Fund Distribution and Utilization (PFMS) of Centrally Sponsored Schemes under the Department of Finance vide No. 2 Necessary action should be taken immediately in accordance with the instructions given in the circular.
