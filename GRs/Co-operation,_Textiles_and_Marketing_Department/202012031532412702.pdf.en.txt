It was under consideration of the government to relax the classification of institutions which have expired till they get a temporary classification of two years.
Government Decisions: The recommendations made by the committee of the Divisional Joint Director, Cooperative Societies, Mumbai, regarding the labor cooperative societies, the opinion of the Cooperative Commissioner on it and Ma. Minister (Cooperation) As per the directions given in the meeting with the Speaker, Maharashtra Legislative Assembly, the Tahsildar could not be satisfied due to lack of documents and other documents, for the existing labor cooperatives whose classification has been ended, in the context of this section, No. 1 decision of the government. 21.01.2017 vide Para No. Notice No. 2 The government is allowing relaxation to these institutions for two years till they get temporary classification.
In the absence of Tahsildar's certificate and other documents, the classification of labor cooperatives should be given a temporary classification of two years as per the prevailing system. The government will decide the next three years on the basis of the work that will be received in these two years. They should be given classification as per the condition of 21.01.2017 and they should be given two years of work. In case of non-fulfillment of the conditions in the Government Decision dated 21.01.2017, the institutions should not be given further classification and appropriate action should be taken against them as per rules. The exemption from classification will be applicable to such institutions only for the next two years from the date of issue of this Government Order.
