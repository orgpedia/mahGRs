To sanction non-interest bearing loan to Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Marketing Federation to pay the amount of cotton procured at MSP to the farmers in the season 2019-20.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Co-operation, Marketing and Textiles
GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: KAATUS-2019/ PR No. 53/10-C
Ministry Extension, Mumbai-400032.
The March 20, 2020.
Reference:- Department of Finance Government Resolution No.Finance-2019/ P.No. 92/ Finance-3, dated 8th July 2019 Preamble:
As per the order dated 01.10.2019 of the Ministry of Textiles, the Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Marketing Federation has been appointed as the Deputy Manager of Cotton Corporation of India Limited (CCI), the Chief Agent of the Central Government, for procurement of cotton in the State at MSP.
However, no Line of Credit, Letter of Comfort or Advance will be issued to the Cotton Marketing Federation by the Central Government/ CCI for this purpose. Therefore, in order to ensure timely payment of cotton dues to the farmers after procurement of cotton at MSP in 2019-20 season, it was under consideration of the Government to sanction margin money to the Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Marketing Federation for taking loans from banks/ financial institutions.
Government's decision:
A budgetary provision of Rs. 160.00 crore has been made for the year 2019-2020 under the head" Marginal Amount (Programme) for Implementation of Cotton Procurement Scheme." Finance Department, Govt. As per the powers conferred vide Order dated 8th July, 2019, the Cotton Producers Marketing Federation of India (CAPF), under the Cotton Purchase Scheme for 2019-20 season, has sanctioned an amount of Rs. 96.00 crore (Rupees Ninety Five Crores only) out of the budgeted amount of Rs. 160.00 crore for timely payment of dues of cotton purchased/ to be procured from farmers, to the Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Producers Marketing Federation (MACPF) in the form of non-interest bearing loan from bank/ financial institution subject to the following conditions:
1) The margin money will be used by the federation as a margin for payment of arrears of cotton purchased at the minimum support price to the cotton farmers. The entire responsibility of this will be of the Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Federation.
2) The margin money should not be invested in long-term deposits in other banks.

3) The margin money will have to be repaid to the government immediately after the end of the season. If this is not possible, the reasons for this will have to be reported to the government.
4) It will be mandatory to submit the utilization certificate to the government within 3 months after utilization for the purpose for which the Margin Money has been distributed.
5) The Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Federation should enter into a written agreement with the Cotton Corporation of India in this regard.

The Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers' Federation has not yet returned the margin money of Rs 62 crore out of Rs 200 paid to the government in 2015-16, 2016-17, 2017-18 and 2018-19. This amount should be returned to the government by the federation before March 31, 2020.
