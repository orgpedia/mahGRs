In Maharashtra, orange production is concentrated in Amravati, Nagpur, Akola and Buldhana districts of Vidarbha. The post-harvest loss of orange fruit is approximately 25 to 30 percent. The rate of producing value-added products by processing oranges in the state is very low. Also, the amount of processing of by-products is negligible. Therefore, if the orange growers of the state are provided financial assistance by the government for setting up post-harvest processing plants, the post-harvest losses of oranges will be reduced and the orange growers will get the right price and will be able to send good quality oranges to the domestic and foreign markets. Also, the value addition of oranges will be benefitted by preparing by-products by processing the orange.
A sum of Rs 20 crore will be provided for setting up modern orange processing units in Nagpur, Morshi, G. Amravati and Buldhana districts. The approval for the scheme for setting up of modern orange processing units at Amravati and Sangrampur in Buldhana district was under the consideration of the Government.
Under this Government decision, approval is being given to the following schemes for setting up of modern orange processing units in Amravati and Sangrampur, District Buldhana.
Setting up of modern orange processing units at Amravati and Sangrampur, Distt. Buldhana. "
(2) The objective of the scheme is to set up orange processing facilities in the orange growing districts of Nagpur, Amravati and Buldhana.
(3) The beneficiaries of the scheme shall be as follows:
1. Cooperative Processing Societies 2. Farmer Producer Companies 3. Farmers Groups 4. Agricultural Produce Market Committees 5. Private entrepreneurs.

(c) By-product Processing Projects: The project will include modern projects for processing of by-products of oranges such as extraction of oil from orange peel through cold press method. The estimated cost of the project is Rs. 30.00 lakhs.

(6) Form of financial assistance for the scheme 1) The financial assistance to be provided by the State Government for the projects to be constructed under the scheme shall be in the form of grant.
(2) Firstly, the beneficiaries will be required to spend at least 15% of the estimated cost of the selected projects mentioned in (4) above. The beneficiaries should get the remaining 85 per cent of the financial assistance sanctioned as loan from the bank as per their requirement.
After completion of the project and submission of completion certificate to the government through the Marketing Board, the government will provide subsidy up to 50 percent of the estimated cost of the project. The amount will be credited to the beneficiary's bank account.
(7) Criteria for selection of beneficiaries: The following criteria should be fulfilled by the beneficiaries for the said scheme.
1) Beneficiaries must have their own space for the project.

(8) Implementation of the Scheme: The Scheme shall be implemented as follows:
The Executive Director, Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board, Pune will be the nodal agency for the scheme.
2) The Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board should advertise the scheme for setting up modern orange processing centers in all major newspapers of the state, the official websites of the Directorate of Marketing and the Agricultural Marketing Board.
3) The Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board should select a consultant on the basis of the following criteria to prepare a model project report by determining the capacity of the components of the scheme.
a) The person should be an expert in the field of post-harvest management and processing of agricultural produce.
(b) The consultant should have at least 5 years of experience in the field of preparation of project reports, selection of machinery, preparation of tenders and construction of projects.
c) The tender process should be carried out to select the consultants with the lowest rates.
4) In case of receipt of more applications than the number of projects identified under the proposed scheme, the applications fulfilling the criteria shall be selected on" first-come-first-serve" basis.
The proposal of the project selected by the beneficiaries out of the three types of projects proposed under the scheme should be scrutinized with the help of the consultant appointed by the Agricultural Marketing Board and after examining whether the proposal received is feasible or not, the complete proposal should be submitted for the approval of the government.
6) After the proposal is approved by the government, a letter of in-principle approval will be given to the beneficiaries by the Board of Trustees.
Thereafter, the beneficiaries should initiate the project work by conducting the tender process and appointing a contractor as per the requirement.
8) After the completion of the project, the Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board should inspect the work physically and issue a certificate of completion.
After submitting the completion certificate of the project to the government, the government will disburse the subsidy up to a maximum of 50 percent of the estimated cost of the project to the Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board, Pune, and then the subsidy amount will be directly credited to the beneficiary's account through the Marketing Board.
10) It will be mandatory for the Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board to submit the utilization certificate of the government subsidy.
As the Executive Director, Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board will be the nodal agency for the scheme, an amount equivalent to 1% of the financial grant from the Government for the scheme will be admissible to them for administrative expenses (such as appointment of consultant, payment of consultancy fee and other ancillary matters, etc.).
12) It will be mandatory to implement the project only by the beneficiaries who have undertaken the project.
