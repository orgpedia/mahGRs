Section 24A of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960 provides that every institution shall conduct co-operative education and training for its directors, members, officers and employees through the State Federal Institutions or State Apex Training Institutions as may be specified by the State Government by notification in the Official Gazette.
As on March 31, 2021, there are about 2.17 lakh cooperative societies in the state with a total membership of about 5.75 crore. The number of directors of these institutions is more than 25 lakh and the number of employees is about 20 lakh. As per Section 24A of the Co-operative Act, it is mandatory for a co-operative society to impart co-operative education and training to its directors, members and officers/ employees.
In view of the above, Reference No. 1 The circular here lays down the criteria for recognition of cooperative training institutions as State Apex Training Institutions. As per Section 24-A of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, certain institutions in the state have been notified as State Apex Training Institutes. However, in view of the large number of members of cooperative societies in the state and the mandatory requirement of training to these members at least once in 5 years, it is still necessary to notify some institutions as training institutions. The National Institute of Post-Sugar Technology, Talegaon. A proposal to notify Maval, G. Pune as State Apex Training Institute under Section 24-A of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960 was under consideration of the Government.
