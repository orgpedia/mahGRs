The Proposal: -
There are currently 39 district-level cooperative banks functioning in the state. The main objective of these banks is to provide credit to primary agricultural credit co-operative societies and to provide credit to other co-operative societies in the district. District Central Co-operative Banks have an important role to play in providing farm / crop loans to farmers at the district level. Also, the existence of village-level development agencies depends on the financial position of these banks. District Central Co-operative Banks are the parent institutions of Primary Co-operative Societies under the concept of Co-operative.
The jurisdiction of district central cooperative banks is limited to each district. Also, all the members of these banks are from that district. Hence, if local candidates are preferred in the recruitment of District Central Co-operative Bank employees, such employees will be able to serve the customers / members / depositors of the bank better. Therefore, it was under the consideration of the government to formulate a policy regarding the preference of local candidates in the recruitment of district central cooperative banks in the state.
The Government's Decision-
In order to give preference to local candidates in recruitment to District Mediation Co-operative Banks in the state, the banks should follow the following procedures:
70% of the seats should be reserved for the candidates having Domicile Certificate / Domicile Certificate in the district under the jurisdiction of the concerned District Central Co-operative Bank.
The remaining 30% of the seats will be open to candidates from outside the district. However, if candidates from outside the district are not available in sufficient numbers, these seats can be filled by local candidates.
The said Government Decision shall also apply to the District Central Co-operative Banks which have published the recruitment advertisement before the date of this Government Decision.
