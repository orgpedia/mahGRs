Introduction: Restructuring of Maharashtra State Cooperative Council. This was decided by the Government on 5.4.2018. A proposal to include other members in the Maharashtra State Cooperative Council was also under consideration. The following decision has been taken:
In exercise of the powers conferred by clause (a) of section 154 of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, the Government hereby reconstitute the Maharashtra State Co-operative Council. This was decided by the Government on 5.4.2018. The term of the Council will be for a period of three years from 21.02.2018 or until further orders, whichever is less. The members of this committee are:
1) Shri Dattatraya Kashinath Kulkarni, Osmanabad.
2) Mr. Dilip Gopalrao Rajurkar, Amravati.
3) Mr. Sitaram Baji Rane, Thane.
4) Shri Ramdas Trimbak Deore, Nashik.
5) Mr. Shivaji Hindurao Patil. Sangli.
Sanjay Bhende, Nagpur.
The functions of the Maharashtra State Co-operative Council shall be as per the provisions of Section 154 (a) (3) of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, as follows: 1) To advise the State Government on all such matters relating to the co-operative movement.
(ii) To review the cooperative movement and suggest ways to coordinate the functioning of cooperative societies in the State.
3) Suggest ways and means to overcome the difficulties faced by the cooperative societies.
(4) To report to the State Government on all matters which the State Government may refer to the Council.
5) To recommend schemes and policies for the development of the cooperative movement in the state.
To evaluate the existing projects and suggest new projects for the development of the cooperative movement, especially for the development of backward classes and economically weaker sections of the society.
