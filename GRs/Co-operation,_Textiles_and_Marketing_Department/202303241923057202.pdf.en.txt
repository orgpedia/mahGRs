Preamble: The Director (Sericulture) has submitted a proposal under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of" Silk Samagra" (ISDSI) for the year 2021-22 for providing Central Share of Rs. 5.85 lakh and Supplementary State Share of Rs. 2.25 lakh to the beneficiaries belonging to SC category and Central Share of Rs. 17.4615 lakh and Supplementary State Share of Rs. 6.716 lakh to the beneficiaries belonging to ST category for a total amount of Rs. 32.2775 lakh. The Central Silk Board has approved the utilization of the said Central share of funds during the financial year 2022-23 vide letter dated 14.06.2022, as per the terms of reference. Accordingly, as per the request of the Director (Reshim), the matter of disbursement of the said funds was under consideration of the Government.

(2) The said fund is approved to be disbursed and spent subject to the following conditions:
The fund should be spent by the Director (Reshim) as per the administrative approval given to the revised standards of the ISDSI scheme and the guidelines in this regard. Also, it should be ensured that the funds are spent on the schemes and things for which the central government has provided funds.
(2) The Director (Silk) shall take action to submit the utilization certificate of the expenditure incurred from the funds disbursed to the Central Silk Board and the Government within the stipulated time.

3) The Director (Silk) shall take action to disburse the subsidy to the eligible beneficiaries only after ensuring that the benefits under the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme, Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Yojana Project and other government schemes are not given while giving the subsidy under this scheme.
4) Since the Central Government is receiving category-wise funds for this scheme, the funds should be budgeted for the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes categories by taking separate headings from the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance and the Department of Tribal Development respectively.
3. An amount of Rs. 23.3115 lakhs (Rupees Twenty Three Lakhs Thirty One Thousand One Hundred and Fifty only) is being released for Central share under the said scheme. Grants under V-2, 2851, Village Industries and Small Scale Industries, 110, Mixed Village Industries and Small Scale Industries and Co-operative Societies, (02) Assistance to various Co-operative Societies, (02) (49) Development of Silk Industry (Centrally Sponsored) (Central Share) (Programme) (28511269), 31, Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payment)" for the financial year 2022-23.
Further, an amount of Rs. 8.966 lakhs (Rupees Eight Lakhs Ninety Six Thousand Six Hundred only) is being released to the State exchequer and the said expenditure is being met from the" Demand No. V-2, 2851, Village Industries and Small Scale Industries, 110, Mixed Village Industries and Small Scale Industries and Co-operative Societies, (02) Assistance to various Co-operative Societies, (02) (45) Sericulture, Khadi Village Industries and Village Industries Development (Programme) (28511204), 31, Subsidies (Non-Payment) to be met out of the grants available under the" Subsidy for the Financial Year 2022-23."
The Director, Directorate of Silk, Nagpur is being authorized as Controlling Officer (V003) to draw and disburse the said funds from the Treasury.
The said fund is being handed over to the Director, Directorate of Silk, Nagpur on Budget Distribution System.
5. This Government decision has been taken with the concurrence of the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, Tribal Development Department and Planning Department. 125/2023/ Expenditure-2, Dy. is being issued as per the approval given on 20.03.2023.
