Letter from the Commissioner of Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies, Pune No. O./ S. P./ 2021, dated 11.05.2021.
Preamble: For effective recovery of unpaid loans of urban cooperative banks in the state, the government has earlier approved the implementation of a one-time loan repayment scheme as recommended to the government by the Commissioner and Registrar of Cooperative Societies, Maharashtra State, Pune.
Periodic extension of the one-time loan repayment scheme has so far helped in reducing the mounting NPAs of urban cooperative banks. Urban cooperative banks will be included in this scheme by closing the outstanding loan accounts.
Urban Cooperative Banks for improving the balance sheet and for effective implementation of the scheme.
A proposal to extend the one-time loan repayment scheme was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
In exercise of the powers conferred by section 157 of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, for effective recovery of unproductive loans of urban co-operative banks, the Government hereby exempts the urban co-operative banks from the provisions of Rule 49 of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Rules, 1961, for implementing the said scheme subject to the guidelines of the Reserve Bank of India.

Approved vide Circular No. 15.11.2017 and G.O.S.R.S. Circular No. 26.02.2018
The one-time loan repayment scheme is being extended up to 31.03.2022.
The scheme will be applicable to all loan accounts which are included in the category of doubtful or loss making loans as on 31.03.2019.
The scheme will also be applicable to the loan accounts which have been included in the sub-standard category of non-performing loans at the end of 31.03.2019 and subsequently have been classified as doubtful and bad.
The other provisions of the Reference Day dated 15.11.2017 and the Corrigendum dated 26.02.2018 shall continue. The applications received under this scheme after 28.02.2022 should be decided by 31.03.2022.
