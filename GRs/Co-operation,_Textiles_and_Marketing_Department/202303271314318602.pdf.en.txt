Preamble: In view of the fall in the market price of onion in the state in the beginning of February 2023 this year and the demand for subsidy from various farmers' organizations and farmers, a committee constituted under the Government Decision, dated 28/2/2023, under the chairmanship of Dr. Sunil Pawar, former Director of Marketing, visited the market committees of the state and met the farmers, traders, traders, representatives of various organizations, exporters, experts, etc.
After meeting with the scientists and taking information from various agencies, the report has been prepared and submitted to the government on 9/3/2023. In the report, the committee has proposed short-term and long-term measures. Among the short-term (immediate) measures, the government was considering to provide subsidy to the farmers who sold their late kharif onion between February 1, 2023 and March 31, 2023 in the respective Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC), Private Market Committee (PMC), directly to the marketing licensee or to the procurement centers opened for the purchase of late kharif onion by NAFED.
Government Decision: Rs 350 per quintal to farmers who have sold red onion in the late Kharif season from 1st February 2023 to 31st March 2023 to the Agricultural Produce Market Committee, Private Market Committees, Direct Marketing Licensee or NAFED in the state

The government has decided to provide a subsidy of 200 quintals per farmer.
The terms and conditions prescribed for implementation of the Scheme are as follows: i. Farmers will be given a subsidy of Rs 350 per quintal and a maximum of 200 quintals per farmer.
The scheme will be applicable to the farmers who sell late Kharif red onion from 1st February, 2023 to 31st March, 2023 in the respective Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC), Private Market Committee (PMC), directly to the Marketing Licensee or in the procurement centres opened for procurement of late Kharif onion by NAFED.
The scheme should be implemented for all the market committees in the state except Mumbai Agricultural Produce Market Committee, Mumbai.
The scheme will not be applicable for onions imported from foreign countries and traders.
The amount will be directly transferred to the bank accounts of the farmers through direct bank transfer.
The subsidy will be paid through ICICI Bank.
Onion farmers should apply for subsidy under this scheme on plain paper along with Onion Sale Slip/ Sale Receipt, Extract of 7/12, their Bank Savings Account Number etc. to the Market Committee to which the onion is sold.
(viii) Proposals for providing subsidy to farmers should be prepared by the concerned Market Committee. The market committee will be responsible for preparing the proposal. The proposals should be examined by the Taluk Assistant Registrar and submitted to the District Sub-Registrar, Cooperative Societies. Once the District Deputy Registrar, Cooperative Societies approves the suitable proposals, the list should be submitted to the Director of Marketing, Maharashtra State, Pune for approval. The funds will be transferred directly to the bank accounts of the farmers.
For the proper implementation of this scheme, the Secretary of the concerned Agricultural Produce Market Committee, Taluka Assistant/ Sub-Registrar and District Sub-Registrar will act as controllers to finalize the beneficiaries of this scheme and will be responsible for it.
X. In a case where the sale deed in respect of 7/12 is in the name of the father and in the name of the son and other family members and the crop inspection is recorded in respect of 7/12, the father and son or other family members may, with the consent of the father and son or other family members, proceed as mentioned in vii.
