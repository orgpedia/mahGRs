Under the cooperative department, the administrative and audit activities of various cooperative institutions in the state such as district central bank, urban cooperative banks, cooperative credit societies, various executive bodies, labor cooperative societies, water supply institutions, employer cooperative credit societies, housing cooperative societies, moneylender licenses, cooperative sugar factories, agricultural produce market committees, purchase and sale associations, etc. are controlled. Orders are passed under the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act 1960 and Rules 1961, Maharashtra Agricultural Marketing (Regulation) Act 1963, Maharashtra Savadari (Regulation) Act 2014 etc. Effective implementation of various schemes of the government at the field level is done by controlling/ guiding the functioning of cooperative societies.
In view of the scope of these activities, it is necessary to provide in-depth technical training to the field officers and staff in judicial and legal matters to enable them to perform their duties with quality. The training will help in improving the quality of work of officers and staff.
