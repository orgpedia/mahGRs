Section 24-A of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960 provides that" every institution shall organise cooperative education and training for its members, officers and employees through the State Federal Institutions or State Apex Training Institutions as may be specified by the State Government by notification in the Official Gazette." As per the Economic Survey 2020-21 of Maharashtra, as on 31st March, 2020, there are about 2.12 lakh cooperative societies in the State with a total membership of about 5.5 crore. The number of directors of these institutions is more than 25 lakh and the number of employees is about 3.5 lakh. As per Section 24A of the Co-operative Act, it is mandatory for a co-operative society to provide co-operative education and training to its members, directors and officers/ employees.
In the above circumstances, for recognition of Cooperative Training Institutions as State Apex Training Institutions, Reference No. 1 The criteria are set out in the circular. As per Section 24-A of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, some institutions in the state have been notified as State Apex Training Institutes. However, in view of the large number of members of cooperative societies in the state and the obligation to provide training to these members at least once in 5 years, it is still necessary to notify some institutions as training institutions. The proposal to notify it as a training institute was under the consideration of the government. In this regard, the Government has taken the following decision in accordance with the recommendation of the Committee headed by the Commissioner and Registrar of Cooperative Societies, Maharashtra State, Pune.
In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of section 24-A of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, the Government hereby appoint the Maharashtra State Non-Agricultural Co-operative Credit Societies Federation Ltd. Mumbai is recognized as a State Apex Training Institute. A notification in this regard is being published. Approval is subject to the following terms and conditions.
Maharashtra State Non-Agriculture Co-operative Credit Societies Federation Ltd. The institute should have a registered lease agreement of at least five years for the building and other facilities of the training centre. Thereafter, after every five years, the registered lease agreement for the next five years should be made and its report should be submitted to the Commissioner of Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies, Pune.
(2) A quarterly report on the training provided by the above institution should be submitted to the Commissioner of Cooperation and Registrar, Cooperative Societies, Pune and a copy of the report should be sent to the Government.
