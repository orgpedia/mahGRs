GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: SAPAS 2020/ P.C. 71/24 C
A subsidy of Rs.200 per quintal and a maximum of Rs.200 per quintal per farmer has been sanctioned to eligible farmers who have sold onion from 1st November, 2018 to 15th December, 2018. The period of Onion Subsidy under the Scheme has been extended from time to time and the revised due date has been fixed from 1st November, 2018 to 28th February, 2019.
An amount of Rs. 114.80 crore was made available from the Contingency Fund for disbursement of first phase of onion subsidy vide Government Resolution, dated 16/2/2019. Thereafter, Rs.387.3031 crore has been provided for the second phase of subsidy through Supplementary Demands for Grants issued vide Government Resolution, dated 7/8/2019. Considering the delay in disbursement of first phase onion subsidy, the second phase onion subsidy through ICICI Bank has been approved under the Government decision dated 19/8/2019 and the distribution of onion subsidy is in final stages. So far, a total of Rs. 503.55 crore has been disbursed to 5,53,204 farmers.
The terms and conditions mentioned by the Finance Department while approving the distribution of onion subsidy through ICICI Bank under the Government Decision, dated 19/8/2019. It is as follows:
The number of " The interest received from this savings account should be used for the implementation of this scheme. "
49.48 lakh has been credited to the account of the Directorate General of Fince at the end of June, 2021 from the funds deposited in ICICI Bank.
Directorate of Panan vide letter dated 22/7/2021 has sought release of funds to the tune of Rs. 6,39,200/- for 20 eligible beneficiaries in Washim district and Rs. 22,25,280/- for 136 eligible beneficiaries in Sangli district vide letter dated 17/8/2021 aggregating to Rs. 28,64,480/-.
Considering the situation created by Corona in the state, it will take a lot of time to make funds available by submitting a new supplementary demand for onion subsidy.
The Finance Department has given permission to use the interest amount for the implementation of the scheme.
Accordingly, it was under the consideration of the government to approve the disbursement of the interest amount in the ICICI Bank account of the Directorate of Marketing to the eligible beneficiaries of Washim and Sangli districts for onion subsidy.

Government's decision:
Since the interest received from ICICI Bank is required to be utilized for onion subsidy and considering the situation arising out of COVID-19 in the State, it is being approved to disburse the pending onion subsidy to the eligible beneficiaries of Washim and Sangli districts from the interest received from ICICI Bank.
The subsidy will be paid through ICICI Bank.
