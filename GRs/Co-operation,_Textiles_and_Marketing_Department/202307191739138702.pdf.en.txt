The Proposal:
The non-performing assets (NPAs) of urban cooperative credit institutions in the state are increasing as they have to make provision for unpaid loans. As a result, credit institutions have to make huge provisions. This affects the credit score of the institutions. This creates a sense of insecurity among investors. As a result, some credit institutions are facing difficulties due to the decrease in the deposits of urban cooperative credit institutions and the withdrawal of large deposits in some places.

In view of the recommendations of the Commissioner of Cooperation and Registrar of Cooperative Societies, Maharashtra State, Pune, for the purpose of cleaning, the State Government has decided to implement the Samachar Repayment Scheme for all Urban Cooperative Credit Societies in the State. Approval of the Government by the Government Decision of 27/9/2007
had been given. In accordance with the recovery made through this scheme, under the scheme of reimbursement of treatment No. The extension was granted under Sections 2,3,4, 5,6,7, 8,9,10, 12, 13, 14 and 15.
Considering the substantial performance in the recovery of credit institutions through this scheme, the scheme has been revised with changes. The proposal to extend the deadline to 31/3/2024 was given by the Cooperation Commissioner. The letter dated 22.6.2023 has been received by the Government. Considering the recommendation of the Cooperation Commissioner to extend the said scheme, the State Government is taking the following decisions.
Government's decision:
The Government has approved the" Revised Compensation Repayment Scheme" for Urban/ Rural Non-Farm Cooperative Credit Societies as mentioned in Annexure A to this decision. The period is being extended up to 31.3.2024. The scheme is being implemented in accordance with the provisions of Section 157 of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960 and Section 49 of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960.
