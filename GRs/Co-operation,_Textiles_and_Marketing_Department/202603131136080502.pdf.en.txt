The Asian Development Bank (ADB) is organizing the Asia and the Pacific Food
Systems (AFS) Forum 2026 with the theme "Feeding the Future, Sustaining the Planet," scheduled to be held from 16 "to 19TM March 2026 at ADB Headquarters, Manila, Philippines.
The forum will bring together policymakers, development partners, private sector representatives, researchers, and international experts to discuss strategies for transforming food systems across Asia and the Pacific region.
The forum will focus on key themes such as climate-resilient agriculture, sustainable
food production systems, food and nutrition security, digital innovations in agriculture, and strengthening rural economies. It will also showcase regional initiatives and innovative
approaches aimed at improving productivity, enhancing resilience to climate change, and
promoting sustainable agricultural development.
ADB has invited representatives from the Maharashtra Agribusiness Network
(MAGNET) Project to participate in the forum and share perspectives from Maharashtra on
strengthening agricultural value chains, improving post-harvest management, and promoting agribusiness investments. Participation in the forum will provide an opportunity to exchange
knowledge, engage with global experts, and explore potential collaborations and partnerships for strengthening food systems in the state.
Considering the above potential benefits, the Government of Maharashtra, through the
Maharashtra Agribusiness Network Project (MAGNET), has planned a visit to Manila, Philippines from 15TM to 20 March 2026 (inclusive of travel time)
