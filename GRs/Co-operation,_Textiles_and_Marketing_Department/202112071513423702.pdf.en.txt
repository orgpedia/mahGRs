Preamble: During the financial year 2019-20, under the Promotion and Outreach Programme of the Silk Industry, a sum of Rs. 192.83 lakh was sanctioned as per the Government Resolution dated 22nd August, 2019. However, the payments for the financial year 2019-20 were kept pending as the Directorate of Sericulture did not receive the payments in time from the concerned due to the outbreak of COVID-19 in the State. For the year 2020-21
The Finance Department had approved the release of funds to the tune of 35 per cent of the total budgetary allocation. Accordingly, a sum of Rs. 19,96,290/-is payable under the Government Decision dated 8th December, 2020 and under the Government Decision dated 24th February, 2021.

A total amount of Rs. 35.00 lakhs was sanctioned for disbursement and expenditure amounting to Rs. 15,03,710/-. Accordingly, the Director (Reshim) has taken action to clear the pending dues. Pursuant to the letter dated 18.06.2021 addressed to the Director (Sericulture), the Finance Department was requested to make available funds to the tune of Rs. 8,56,920/-for payment of pending dues in the year 2019-20 under the Promotion and Publicity Programme from the budgetary provision of Rs. 150.00 lakh for the financial year 2021-22 for promotion and publicity of sericulture. Accordingly, the Finance Department has approved the release of funds to the tune of₹ 15 lakh i.e. 10 per cent of the budgetary provision. Accordingly, under the Promotion and Publicity Programme of the Silk Industry, a Government Resolution dated 4 th August, 2021 has approved the release and expenditure of Rs. 8,56,920/- towards payment of pending dues for the financial year 2019-20.
Accordingly, the process of clearing the pending payments is underway at the Directorate General of Silk.
Now, the Director (Sericulture) vide letter dated 28.10.2021 has requested for release of funds to the tune of Rs. 1,70,000/- towards payment of pending dues for the financial year 2019-20 under the Promotion and Publicity Programme.
Therefore, out of the 10% (₹ 15 lakh) of the budgetary provision of 2021-22 approved earlier by the Finance Department, a sum of₹ 6,43,080/- is available for disbursement, minus the amount of₹ 8,56,920/- disbursed. Therefore, under the promotion and publicity program of the silk industry, the government was considering to disburse funds to meet the expenses of pending payments for the financial year 2019-20.
Government's decision:
It is hereby approved to disburse and spend a sum of Rs. 1,70,000/- (Rupees One Lakh Seventy Thousand only) towards payment of pending dues for the financial year 2019-20 under the Promotion and Publicity Programme of the Silk Industry.
2. While spending this fund, the terms and conditions laid down by the Finance Department from time to time should be followed. It should be ensured that the utilization certificate of this fund is submitted to the Accountant General and the Government within the prescribed time.
The Accountant, Directorate of Silk, Nagpur is being authorized as Drawing and Disbursing Officer and the Director, Directorate of Silk, Nagpur as Controlling Officer (V003) for the purpose of drawing and disbursing the said funds from the Treasury.
The said fund is being handed over to the Director (Silk), Directorate of Silk, Nagpur on Budget Distribution System.
