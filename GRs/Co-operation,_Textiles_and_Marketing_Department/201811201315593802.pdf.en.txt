Preamble The Textile Policy 2018-23 of the State has been notified vide Government Resolution dated 15.02.2018. In this policy, the government has decided to promote silk farming and the ancillary industry based on it. Several schemes are being implemented by the central and state governments for the development of the rural areas. Considering the scope of employment generation in the silk industry, the scheme of creation of infrastructure and strengthening of existing facilities for the growth of sericulture and sericulture in the state has been implemented under the Government Decision dated May 3, 2018, to bring the sericulture industry to the center of the state's economy. Sasmira, a Centrally Sponsored Institute, has proposed to set up a Gas Chromatography Mass Spectroscopy facility in partnership with the State Government for fabric quality certification for determining the quality of agro-based textile/ agrotextile products. Submitted on January 31, 2018. Accordingly, the government was considering to allocate funds to Sasmira Institute to study agrotextile products in Maharashtra.
SASMIRA (Synthetic and Art Silk Mills Research Association) is an organization that researches on synthetic and processed products related to the textile industry. It provides scientific and technical advice to the entrepreneurs working in the textile industry.

SASMIRA had submitted a proposal to create a facility for certification of textile quality for determining the quality of agro-based textile/ agrotextile products with the participation of the State Government. Accordingly, the government is giving approval to set up a gas chromatography mass spectroscopy facility in the state through SASMIRA, which will be useful for testing and certifying the quality of textile products. Approval is hereby accorded for release of funds to the tune of Rs.70,00,000/- (Rupees Seventy Lakh only) to SASMIRA through Director (Sericulture) for setting up of Gas Chromatography Mass Spectroscopy testing facility.
