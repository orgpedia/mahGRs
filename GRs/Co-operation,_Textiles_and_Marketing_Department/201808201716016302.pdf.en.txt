The new textile policy of Maharashtra is going to give a new impetus to the textile sector in the state.
It aims to create 10 lakh jobs in the next five years and double the income of farmers by 2022. Accordingly, the government was considering a scheme to provide special incentives for the production and use of non-traditional fibres.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: The Government has decided to take up a scheme to provide special incentives for the production and use of non-traditional fibres to all sections of the people in the State. The plan includes the following:
Special incentives for production and use of non-traditional fibres- Research will be focused on the production and use of non-traditional fibres (bamboo, banana, maize, flax, cowpea, coir, etc.). Production and use of non-traditional fibres will be encouraged. These crops will be included in the MNREGA scheme for the production of fibre crops. Irrigation facilities will be provided where required.
