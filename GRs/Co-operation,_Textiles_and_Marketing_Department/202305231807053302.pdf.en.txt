the order
Nashik District Central Co-operative Bank. The financial position of the bank has deteriorated as the management of the bank has remained with the board for a longer period due to the stay order of the High Court, the bank has not been able to maintain the 9% Capital Adequacy Ratio (CRAR) prescribed by the Reserve Bank from 2017 to March, 2021. Therefore, the bank is in contravention of the provisions of Section 11 of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949. Similarly, the banking license is at risk as the bank cannot meet the required eligibility criteria for the bank license.
The board of administrators appointed on the bank must stay for some more time to improve the financial position of the bank and bring the bank to the previous position. Since the Board of Governors will require some time to recover the outstanding loans, improve capital adequacy, increase banking business and bring professional management in the functioning of the Bank during this period, the stage at which the election of the said Bank under the order dated August 27, 2021, will be held till March 31, 2022, read No. 1 was postponed.
Accordingly, in order to restore the financial position of the Nashik District Central Co-operative Bank, the term of the Administrator of Nashik District Central Co-operative Bank has been extended from 31.03.2022 to 30.06.2023 vide letter dated 16.02.2023 by relaxing the provisions of Section 110O (1) (iii) of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960.
Accordingly, the Board of Administrators appointed at the said Bank will be given adequate time to improve the financial position of the Nashik District Central Co-operative Bank, recover the unpaid loans, increase the capital adequacy of the Bank, take effective measures to reduce the outstanding balance and restore the Bank to financial health. In view of this fact, the election process of the Nashik District Central Cooperative Bank has been completed by exempting the provision of section 73AA from the power of the government under section 157 of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960.
