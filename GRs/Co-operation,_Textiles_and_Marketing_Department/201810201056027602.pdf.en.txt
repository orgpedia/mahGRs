The Asian Development Bank He visited the state from November 21-24, 2017. The Asian Development Bank (ADB) has said that though Maharashtra offers good opportunities for value chain creation, it would be advisable for the state to introduce new projects without seeking an extension of the Agricultural Trade Infrastructure Investment Programme (AIDIP) project in order to have enough time for implementation.
A meeting of the State Level High Powered Committee constituted for implementation of Agriculture Trade Infrastructure Investment Programme (AIDIP) project under the Chairmanship of Hon'ble Chief Secretary was held on 14.03.2018. The state-level high-power committee has been directed to prepare a new roadmap for the AIDIP project and to obtain financial assistance from the Asian Development Bank. In this backdrop, considering the experience of implementing Asian Development Bank assisted projects in the state, the implementation of such a project in the state on the lines of the scheme of the Union Ministry of Food Processing Industries but within the framework of the agreement with the Asian Development Bank can get a good response from the investors. Therefore, it was under consideration of the Government to give in-principle approval to the proposed design submitted to the Government by the Project Director of Asian Development Bank assisted Maharashtra Agribusiness Network (MAGNET) Project, AIDIP, dated 05.01.2018.
