Introduction: Under the Government Resolution No. 1, the State Government has decided to provide short-term crop loans at 6% interest to the farmers of the State. As per the policy of the central government, banks should provide loans to farmers at 6% interest rate instead of 7% where banks are going to provide loans to farmers at 7% interest rate. For this purpose, the financial burden of the 1% interest differential amount is on the government. The benefit of this decision is being given to the private banks which are distributing short term crop loans up to Rs. 3.00 lakh to the farmers in Kharif and Rabi season from the year 2006-07 to the District Central Cooperative Banks, Nationalized Banks and Regional Rural Banks of the state from the year 2013-14.
The government was considering sanctioning funds under the scheme at the rate of 1 per cent for providing short-term crop loans to farmers for the current financial year 2019-20.
