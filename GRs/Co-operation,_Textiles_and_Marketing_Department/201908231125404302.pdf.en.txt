Preamble: Farmers producing Tur and Gram who had registered on NeML portal for procurement under Minimum Support Price (MSP) Scheme during 2017-2018 season. Subsidy of Rs.1000/- per quintal has been sanctioned to the farmers who could not procure Tur and Gram from them vide Government Decisions dated 19th June, 2018 and 1st November, 2018 respectively. The limit for the subsidy is 10 quintals per hectare and 2 hectares per farmer. Initially, Rs 70 crore has been made available to Panan Mahasangh from the Contingency Fund for disbursement of this subsidy to the farmers, while the remaining grant of Rs 480 crore has been nationalized through Government Guarantee approved by the Finance Department.

A loan must be taken from the bank. Panan Mahasangh has taken a total loan of Rs 340 crore from Bank of Baroda at an interest rate of 8.50 per cent and so far, Rs 399.50 crore has been disbursed as subsidy to 3,13,987 beneficiaries under the scheme. As per the Government Decision dated 30th March, 2019, Rs.20 crore has been provided to the Federation to repay the loan taken by the Federation from the Bank. The government has to pay the interest on the loan and the interest on it. In order to reduce the financial burden on the government due to higher interest rates on bank loans provided by the Pannon Federation for providing subsidy to tur and gram farmers, a sum of Rs. 320.07 crore has been sanctioned as per the demand of the Pannon Federation in the second (monsoon) session of 2019 through supplementary demand.
The circular dated 9th July, 2019 of the Department of Finance has approved the disbursement of budgetary provisions (including supplementary demands) for the financial year 2019-2020. Accordingly, the Government was contemplating to provide 90% of the sanctioned supplementary demand i. e. Rs. 288.00 crore under the head' Financial Assistance (2425 2542) for payment of the amount of collateral expenses incurred for the purchase of agricultural produce by the Maharashtra State Cooperative Marketing Federation.
Government Decision: For the subsidy scheme implemented to provide Rs.1000 per quintal subsidy to the tur and gram farmers in the state in the 2019-20 season, the government is providing an amount of Rs. 288.00 crore (Rupees Two Hundred and Eighty Eight Crores only) to reimburse the loans made available by the Panan Mahasangha from the nationalized banks under the government guarantee of Rs.480 crore sanctioned by the Finance Department.
