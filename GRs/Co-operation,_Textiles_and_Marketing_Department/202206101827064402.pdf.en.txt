Introduction: All cooperative institutions in the state are regulated by the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960. The provisions of the said Act regulate matters relating to the management of co-operative societies and the protection of the interests of its members. As per Section 168 of the Act, the State Government has the power to publish in the Gazette such provisions as it may deem necessary to remove any difficulty arising in the implementation of the provisions of the said Act.
In order to ensure the smooth functioning of the cooperative societies, as well as to amend the provisions of the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act 1960, Rules 1961 and the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies (Election to Committees) Rules 2014, as demanded by some public representatives and citizens of the state, proposals are sent to the government through the Cooperative Commissionerate after informal discussions with senior officials of the department. Also, in certain court cases in pursuance of various provisions of the Co-operative Act, Hon. Some judgments of the court are given in accordance with the provisions of the law. Some of these judgments have far-reaching implications for the cooperative movement.
It is very necessary to amend the Cooperative Act from time to time to avoid the said far-reaching consequences and to ensure smooth functioning of the cooperative institutions. While proposing such amendments to the Government, it was under the consideration of the Government to constitute a Permanent Standing Committee of Expert Members to prepare proposals for amending the Cooperative Act/ Rules, taking into consideration the need to enable detailed discussion and deliberation, to amend the Act/ Rules without undue delay, and to present the Government's case strongly in the Court as well. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
