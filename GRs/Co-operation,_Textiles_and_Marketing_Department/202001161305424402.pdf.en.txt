There are 153 lakh farmers in the state. These farmers take loans from commercial banks and district central cooperative banks for farming and agriculture-related activities. For four consecutive years, from 2015-16 to 2018-19, a huge amount of less than 50 paise was released due to drought-like situation in various parts of the state. Meanwhile, farmers in several parts of the state have been affected by the unseasonal rains.
Due to the loss of farmers in the wake of this natural disaster, agricultural loans could not be repaid on time in the last few years. As a result, farmers are facing a lot of difficulties in getting loans for agricultural activities. As a result, the crop loan allocation in 2019-20 is highly unsatisfactory. In the winter session of 2019. The Chief Minister announced the Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Waiver Scheme. Consider the above facts. """"""" Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers Debt Waiver Scheme 2019"""" has been launched to provide relief to farmers who have taken short term crop loans for the period from 1.4.2015 to 31.3.2019 and also to those who have restructured/ restructured their short term crop loans during this period.""" This has been approved in the Cabinet meeting held on 24.12.2019. Under the scheme, farmers will be given a loan waiver under the Mahatma Jotirao Phule Farmers' Debt Waiver Scheme 2019. This has been notified vide Government Resolution dated 27.12.2019.
