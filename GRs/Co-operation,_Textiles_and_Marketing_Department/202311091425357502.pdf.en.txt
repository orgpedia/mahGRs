Government Resolution, Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles Department No. Policy-2023/ PR No. 81/ TEX-5, dated 02.06.2023. (Para 7.8)
In order to promote the growth of the state's textile industry and to provide necessary support to the textile industry, the Government of Maharashtra has announced an Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy 2023-28, as per the relevant government decisions. Accordingly, the Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy and all its related schemes shall come into force from the date of issue of this Government Resolution and shall be implemented by the Government. It will be valid till 31.03.2028.
Paragraph 7.8 of the Policy provides for festival allowance to certified and registered weavers in five traditional textile sectors on the occasion of Ganesh Chaturthi.
The textile industry of Maharashtra has a long history. These fabrics are made from a variety of materials, including cotton, silk and wool. The idea is to provide additional incentives to traditional textile weavers to discourage them from migrating to other jobs by ensuring livelihood security. The following five textile industries in Maharashtra will be considered as traditional textile industries.
1) Paithani Saree- Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar 2) Himru- Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar 3) Karwat Kathi- Vidarbha 4) Ghongdi- Western Maharashtra 5) Khan Fabric- In order to promote the traditional textile weavers of the state of South Maharashtra, a proposal was under consideration of the government to prepare a festival allowance scheme for certified and registered weavers of the above five traditional textile sectors on the occasion of Ganesh Chaturthi.
The government has decided to provide Rs. 5 lakh per male to certified and registered weavers in five traditional textile areas of the state- (1) Paithani Saree-Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, (2) Himru Shawl-Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, (3) Karwat Kathi Saree-Vidarbha, (4) Ghongdi-West Maharashtra and (5) Khan Fabric-South Maharashtra. 10,000/- for women and Rs. The scheme of payment of festival allowance of Rs.15,000/- is being approved under this Government decision. As it is the first year of the scheme, the festival allowance will be sanctioned on the occasion of Makar Sankranti instead of Ganesh Chaturthi in the year 2023-24. Thereafter, from 2024-25, the festival allowance will be sanctioned on the occasion of Ganesh Chaturthi.

Criteria for eligibility for payment of festival allowance to traditional textile weavers under the scheme: (a) Certified and registered handloom weavers under district/ regional divisions of five traditional textile zones in Maharashtra, namely (i) Paithani Saree-Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, (ii) Himroo Shawl-Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, (iii) Karwat Kathi Saree-Vidarbha, (iv) Ghongdi-West Maharashtra and (v) Khan Fabric-South Maharashtra.
b) Must be a resident of Maharashtra.
(c) The name must be included in the list of handloom census conducted by the Central Government in the recent past.
d) Proof of being a traditional textile weaver is required.
e) Beneficiaries must have a bank account in their name.
Procedure of the scheme: (1) The above 5 categories of traditional textile weavers included in the list of handloom census conducted by the Central Government in the recent past should submit a complete proposal to the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner (Textiles) along with the necessary documents to avail the benefit of festival allowance (the proposal will be accepted offline until the online system is developed).
(2) On receipt of the application from the applicant in the list of the recent Handloom Census conducted by the Central Government, the Regional Deputy Commissioner (Textiles) concerned shall verify the identity of the applicant weaver as a weaver in the above mentioned traditional area by taking evidence from the applicant weaver and after scrutinizing and examining the proposal received, submit the list of traditional handloom weavers along with his recommendations to the Commissioner (Textiles) Textiles Commissionerate, Nagpur for approval.
(3) The Commissionerate of Textiles, after scrutinizing and examining the said list, shall ensure that per male weaver allotted to certified and registered traditional textile weavers under the said scheme, a sum of Rs. 10,000 for women and Rs. 15,000 festival allowance to be credited to the bank accounts of traditional textile weavers through DBT system. The Commissioner of Textiles should take appropriate action in this regard.
The scheme will be operational from the date of announcement of Integrated and Sustainable Textile Policy 2023-28 till March 31, 2028.
