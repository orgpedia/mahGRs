The Proposal:
As per the Government Resolution No. 1, the State Government has decided to provide short-term crop loans at 6% interest to the farmers of the State. As per the policy of the central government, banks should provide loans to farmers at 6% interest rate instead of 7% where banks are going to provide loans to farmers at 7% interest rate. For this purpose, the financial burden of the 1% interest differential amount is on the government. The benefit of this decision is being given to the District Central Co-operative Banks, Nationalized Banks and Regional Rural Banks in the State during Kharif and Rabi seasons since 2006-07 and to the private banks who have disbursed short term crop loans up to Rs. 3.00 lakh to the farmers since 2013-14.
In accordance with the policy of the State Government, 50% of the 33% of the budgetary provision under No. 3 and No. 4 under the financing scheme at the rate of 1% for the supply of short-term crop loans to the farmers has been approved by the Planning and Finance Department. No.5 has been approved for distribution.
The government was contemplating to release the remaining funds.
