The Supplementary Appropriation Bill, to be included in the Appropriation for the financial year ending 31st March, 2023. The governor has agreed. And it has been published as Maharashtra (Second Supplementary) Appropriation Act, 2022 (Maharashtra Act No. 40 of 2022) in the Maharashtra Government Gazette Extraordinary, Part-IV dated 26th August, 2022.

Supplementary demand as shown in the accompanying statement under the Supplementary Appropriation Act aggregating to Rs. 5146, 10,82,000- (Rupees Five Thousand One Hundred and Forty Six Crores Ten Lakh Eighty Two Thousand only) (Under Programme) (Mandatory) (Datta Mat). After the approval of the Department of Planning and Finance, the financial sanction orders for the related schemes regarding the expenditure of Supplementary Demand will be handed over to the concerned Implementing/ Controlling Officer in column 5 of the statement accompanying the provision, after being separately issued by the concerned Plan Implementation Offices.
3. The approval of the Planning and Finance Department should be obtained on the basis of the supplementary demands as per the delegation of financial powers, where necessary.
