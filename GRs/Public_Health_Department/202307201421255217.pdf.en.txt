The Proposal:
30 bedded Rural Hospital, Vadakki G. The proposal for administrative approval of the budget and plan amounting to Rs. 1699.52 lakhs was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The budget for construction of building for 30 bedded Rural Hospital, Vadaki G. Yavatmal is based on the tariff schedule of 2022-23. The budget includes the construction of the building (ground floor is 3319.20 sq.m. The budget has made provisions for rainwater harvesting, furniture, electrification, water supply and sewerage, fire prevention, parking, internal roads, protection walls and gates, geo-development, air-conditioning system, CCTVs, etc. 1699.52 lakhs are being accorded administrative approval under this Government Order subject to the following terms and conditions:
(1) Before the construction of the building, the technical approval of the Chief Engineer (Vidyut) for the Vidyutikaran work and the Chief Engineer (Vastu) for the construction work should be obtained.

2) The technical approval of the Chief Engineer, Public Works Department will be required before inviting tenders for the work.
3) Administrative approval is being given subject to the condition that the work is funded from the available budget for construction.
4) When e-tendering the work, the work should be tendered together without breaking it into pieces.
5) The head of the health institution concerned should ensure the availability, suitability and adequacy of space for work.
6) The cost of the proposed work should not be unnecessarily increased according to the situation in the field and the requirements of the health institutions.
As the said budget is a rough model budget, the detailed budgets of these works shall be technically approved by the competent authorities of the Public Works Department in their respective areas.
8) The Public Works Department will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the relevant rules of the local rural development agency.
9) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV 2013/ Q.No.177/ T.C.1D. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014.
10) The maps of the hospital should be revised after examining the maps of the hospital, considering the oxygen pipeline required for the hospital, the place for air conditioners and the arrangement of their duct, the facilities to be provided to the disabled, the pathways to be done in the hospital for those persons, etc. and the functionality of the hospital.
Also, the maps of the hospital should be revised accordingly in consultation with the experts in hospital designing and the doctors and staff associated with the hospital.
11) The building should be constructed within the limit of administrative approval price by incorporating amendments in the budget as per the guidelines of IPHS 2022 2022 issued by the Central Government.
12) It should be ensured that the area of the proposed building is in accordance with the permissible standards of the staff.
13) The concerned Deputy Director of Health Services and District Surgeon should review the construction at the field level from time to time.
14) Public Works Department, Government Decision No. BDG2017/ No. 60/ Ima-2, d. Action to be taken as on 07.07.2017.

15) The tender for the work should be published only after receiving a certificate that the planned place is in the possession of the consumer department.
The entire responsibility of fulfilling the conditions mentioned in 1 to 15 above will be of the concerned Executive Engineer, Public Works Department and District Surgeon.
The expenditure on the said construction will be covered under Demand No. H-8, Head of Accounts 4210 Capital expenditure on medical care and public health, 02 Rural Health Care, 04 Public Health, 104 Community Health Centre, (00) (01) Major Construction (42100391), 53 Major Construction.
The Commissioner, Health Services, Mumbai should immediately submit a proposal to the Government to allocate funds in the budget for the construction of a 30-bed rural hospital, Vadaki G. Yavatmal.
