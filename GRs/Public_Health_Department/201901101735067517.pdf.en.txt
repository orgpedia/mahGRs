In the first phase, the Rajiv Gandhi Jeevan Arogya Yojana was implemented in eight districts (Mumbai City, Mumbai Suburban, Raigad, Dhule, Nanded, Amravati, Solapur, Gadchiroli) with effect from 2nd July, 2012. The scheme is being implemented in collaboration with the National Insurance Corporation (NIC). Reference No. As per the Government decision dated 13th April, 2017, Rajiv Gandhi Jeevan Adhikar Arogya Yojana has been approved to be continued as Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana (MJPJAY) from 1st April, 2017.
As per the decision taken in the Cabinet meeting held on 11th September, 2018 regarding the increase in the number of current patients receiving treatment in the institutions under the control of the Department, to ensure that more and more patients take advantage of the infrastructure and specialist doctors in the institutions under the control of the Department, and accordingly, the institutions of the Department should use the amount received through insurance as per the guidelines of the scheme for the strengthening of the respective institutions and for some important recurring expenses, to encourage them by paying some of the funds received under the scheme as incentives, to select the company through the e-tender process for preparing the claims of the scheme through external sources, to dedicate some of the funds received under the scheme to the State Government, etc.

3) The external source company selected through competitive bidding is being allowed to pay up to 03% of the amount received under the scheme in respect of the cases where claims are made to the concerned insurance company in respect of the treatment provided under the scheme in the proposed hospital, along with the required documents.
The remaining 52% of the funds received by the hospitals under the Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana in the respective financial years in the form of insurance, after spending for the purposes of the above mentioned items, are being approved to be spent on the matters mentioned in Annexure A attached with the respective recognized hospital, as per the procedure laid down by the Committee to be set up under the Chairmanship of the District Surgeon/ Medical Superintendent of the concerned institution and with the approval of the Deputy Director, Health Services Board and within the financial limits to be fixed by the Government from time to time.
