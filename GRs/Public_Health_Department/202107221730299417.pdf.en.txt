Letter from Director, State Blood Transfusion Council, Mumbai No. 22nd June, 2021 Preamble: With a view to ensure that the relatives of the patient do not have to rush for the blood required by the patient in the government/ private hospitals of the state, as well as to provide safe blood supply at the processing fee fixed by the government, the State Government through the State Blood Transfusion Council has decided to implement the innovative scheme/ initiative" Jeevan Amrit Yojana (Blood on Call)" in the districts of the state. The decision has been taken 1. In the 38th meeting of the Governing Body of the State Blood Transfusion Council, a technical committee was formed to review the scheme. 5 District Blood Banks in the respective districts of the State

26/ A-5 Blood Banks run by Charitable Institutions, Blood Banks run by Indian Red Cross Society, etc. MoUs with Non-Government Blood Banks were approved under the scheme on a pilot basis till 30th June, 2021. However, the restrictions imposed due to the outbreak of the second wave of Covid-19 and the limitation of office attendance have been found to be limiting the implementation of this scheme. Hence, the government is planning to extend the deadline. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government Decision: As per the Government Decision dated 15.03.2021, Government District Blood Banks have been approved to enter into Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Non-Government Blood Banks in the district under the innovative scheme/ initiative Jeevan Amrit Seva- Blood on Call. The period of the scheme implemented under the said Government decision is 30.06.2021 and the limit will now be extended to 31.12.2021.
The project is on an experimental basis. This will be applicable till 31.12.2021.
The State Blood Transfusion Council should conduct a comparative study after signing MoU with District Blood Banks and Charitable Institutions, Blood Banks/ Indian Red Cross Society, etc. by 31.12.2021 and submit a report to the Government by 10th of every month. After the expiry of the period of the scheme, the review report of the scheme should be submitted immediately by the Director, State Blood Transfusion Council, Mumbai. Based on the response, a decision will be taken on the future course of action.
