The Proposal:
Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, New Delhi. As per the National Health Policy 2017 vide letter dated 27.07.2017, it has been prescribed that the nurse in the nursing cadre should be a graduate (B.Sc Nursing). As per the earlier norms, the minimum educational qualification was a General Nursing Midwifery (GNM) diploma and a three-year course after 12th Science. The Indian Nursing Council (INC) has decided to discontinue all GNM courses from 2022. However, due to the Covid pandemic, the grading of GNM course to BSc Nursing has been allowed in a phased manner.
In the first phase, the Central Government has sanctioned Rs.6 crore each for upgradation of Nursing Training Centres in Sindhudurg, Nashik and Satara districts and out of this fund, Rs. 3,20,50, 000/- (Rupees Three Crore Twenty Lakh Fifty Thousand only) has been transferred to the bank accounts of each institution for augmentation or modification of existing structures. Accordingly, the Deputy Director Health Services (Nursing), Mumbai, has issued the technical approval of the Executive Engineer, Public Works Department, Sindhudurg, for the construction plans and budget. The proposal has been submitted to the government in accordance with the letter mentioned in 01. Accordingly, the government was considering granting administrative approval for the cost of alterations required for upgrading the existing building of Nursing Training Centre Sindhudurg. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:

Government's decision:
The construction plan and budget proposed by the Executive Engineer, Public Works Department for the necessary changes/ construction and conversion of the existing Nursing Training Centre building for upgrading the District Hospital, Sindhudurg Affiliated Nursing Training Centre into a B.Sc Nursing College is being approved by the Administration subject to the following terms and conditions.
The said administrative approval is in respect of the grant of Rs. 3,20,50, 000/- (Rupees Three Crores Twenty Lakhs Fifty Thousand only) from the Central Government for the construction or alteration (including electrification) of the building of Nursing Training Centre, Sindhudurg and the existing building of the hostel. However, a proposal has been submitted through the Deputy Director (Nursing) Health Services for an amount of Rs. 3,20,00000/- (Rupees Three Crore Twenty Lakh only) for the construction of additional structure or alterations (including electrification) required in the building of Nursing Training Centre, Sindhudurg and the existing building of the hostel. The cost of additional work (minor expenses like AC, electrification etc.) will be admissible in the maximum limit of Rs. 3,20,50, 000/- (Rupees Three Crore Twenty Lakh Fifty Thousand only).
2. Prepare a detailed budget for the proposed changes and construction and electrification and get it attested by the concerned Chief Engineer, Electrical Engineer. Also, while working on the provisions made in a staggered manner, detailed budgets/ tariff plans should be approved.
3. All the works mentioned should be tendered together without separate tendering of each work.
Government Decision on the actual construction of the Environment Department, No: ENV 2013/ p. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in No. 177/ T.K.1, dated 10.01.2014.
The District Surgeon, District General Hospital, Sindhudurg will be responsible for ensuring that the above conditions are fulfilled and the construction is carried out as per the rules.
