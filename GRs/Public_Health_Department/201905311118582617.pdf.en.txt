The Proposal:
Some Medical Officers of the cadre of Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Scale S-20: 56100-177500) were unauthorisedly absent for a long time due to various reasons. Also, the medical officer was unauthorisedly absent for a long period of time without obtaining prior permission from the government.
As public health service is an essential service and there is a shortage of medical officers under the department, the government was considering to reinstate these unauthorized absentee medical officers in the government service after their unauthorized absence subject to specified terms and conditions.
Government's decision:
Maharashtra Medical and Health Services Group-A (Pay Band Rs. 15600-39100+ Grade Pay Rs. 5400), the Medical Officer mentioned in the table below in this cadre was unauthorisedly absent for a long time.
The Medical Officers mentioned in the Table below are being recruited subject to the terms and conditions mentioned in the Table under this paragraph and are being given such posting as may be indicated against their names in the Table below for joining the post of Medical Officer Group-A.

Terms and Conditions:
(1) Since the concerned Medical Officer, Group-A is an unauthorised absence, the period from the date of his unauthorised absence to the date of actual joining shall be deemed to be a period of service as per Rule 47 (1) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982 and the said unauthorised absence shall be deemed to be the period of engagement of the Medical Officer. Also, the entire period of his absence should be treated as a break in service and the same should be clearly mentioned in his joining order.
(2) The Medical Officer concerned shall be medically examined by the Medical Board before being appointed and shall be certified by the Medical Board as physically/ mentally competent to perform the service.
(3) The character and conduct report of the concerned medical officer (police investigation report) should be taken. (A police report should be taken that no complaint of criminal nature or under the Prevention of Corruption Act is pending.) )
(4) Any regular/ temporary service prior to unauthorised absence as mentioned in No. 1 shall not be added to the service after joining after unauthorised absence and the service shall not be availed continuously.
(5) No salary and other incidental service benefits shall be admissible for the period from the date of unauthorised absence till actual joining, no leave shall be granted during the period of such unauthorised absence, the period shall not be regularised for any purpose or any representation thereon shall be entertained. Also, a sanction letter of Rs.
100/- should be obtained from the concerned Medical Officer by the concerned Deputy Director, Health Services.
(6) An affidavit shall be submitted to the Government through the Deputy Director, Health Services concerned for Government information that the concerned medical officer has not done any private business/ job or foreign tour during the period of unauthorised absence.
(7) Even if the concerned medical officers are recruited after unauthorised absence, the old pension scheme prior to 2005 will not be applicable to the concerned medical officers as the period of absence as well as the service before that will not be taken into account.

(8) All the concerned unauthorised absentee medical officers to be recruited. The contributory pension scheme introduced as per the Government decision dated 31/10/2005, which is presently subsumed in the NPS scheme, shall continue to be applicable.
(9) All the medical officers concerned shall not be entitled to any benefit under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982.
(10) Also, no provident fund benefits will be available.
Unauthorized absentee medical officers posted under this order shall join their place of posting within 15 days from the date of receipt of the said order of posting.
Otherwise, the order will be automatically cancelled after 15 days. The head of the office should not appoint a medical officer who will join after 15 days.
