Preamble: Due to the vacancies in 3 hospitals, namely Rural Hospital, Mahabaleshwar, G. Satara and Primary Health Centre, Taldev and Primary Health Centre, Tapola, the citizens are not getting the best health services/ facilities. Keeping in view the interest of the citizens of the area of this hospital, considering the proposal submitted by the Director, Health Services in this regard and considering the work and experience of the institutions providing health services in this area, the proposal to let the above 3 hospitals run on a pilot basis by a voluntary organization was under consideration of the Government. A decision in this regard is being taken.
The Government is granting approval to operate two Primary Health Centres at Satara and Primary Health Centre, Taldeo and Primary Health Centre, Tapola, Indian Red Cross Society, Panchgani (Mahabaleshwar), Bel-Air Hospital at G.S.Satara on pilot basis for a period of one year subject to the following conditions:
(1) The Rural Hospital, Mahabaleshwar and Primary Health Centre, Taldev and Primary Health Centre, Tapola, be transferred to Bel-Air Hospital, Panchgani (Mahabaleshwar), G. Satara, under the aegis of Indian Red Cross Society, for a period of one year.
(2) It shall be obligatory on the institution to implement only medical services and state-sponsored health-related programs in the said hospital.

(3) The Institute shall be bound to work in accordance with the guidelines framed by the Government (Directorate General of Health Services) for the implementation of the National Programme.
The Central Government/ State Government has also given some annual targets for the success of the National Health Program at various levels, which will be mandatory for the Bel-Air Hospital in Panchgani (Mahabaleshwar), G. Satara, under the Indian Red Cross Society.
5) Indian Red Cross Society Sanchalit Panchgani (Mahabaleshwar), Bel-Air Hospital in G. Satara will get administrative and technical guidance from the government machinery.
Therefore, the concerned government officials/ officials will visit and guide the institution whenever required. For this, it will be mandatory for the organization to cooperate with the state government officials and officials.
6) The Directorate General of Health Services has prepared certain forms for regular review of the health programme. It will be mandatory for the institution to send information regularly as per the guidelines made from time to time in that form, as well as to attend meetings at the block level, district level and state level as required. Also, the information on how the subsidy was spent will have to be provided by the government in the manner prescribed from time to time and it will have to be made available to the government officials for inspection at any time. If the expenditure on the hospital is not in accordance with the rules, then the grant given to the institution will have to be returned to the government if this expenditure is not approved by the government.
(7) The standards of the officers and employees to be appointed by the Institute shall be in accordance with the general government policy and the profile of the employees and the qualifications prescribed for those posts.
The grant to the Institute shall be equal to the expenditure on medicines, equipment, machinery, salaries, etc. incurred by the said 3 hospitals at the time of issuing this order or the amount of expenditure incurred by the Institute directly, whichever is less, or as per the rules framed by the Government for grant of Government grants.
8) It will be mandatory for the institution to conduct training of officers and staff of the institution and other technical staff in government institutions. Apart from this, if the need arises, the institute can get this training done from other NGOs, but the expenditure on it will not be admissible for the grant.

(10) Since the grant to the institution as aforesaid is in the nature of reimbursement, the institution shall be required to bear the expenditure on payment of salary to the employees of the institution and other admissible matters in the first instance. The cost will be given to the institution as a year-end grant on the basis of their annual audit. For some reason, the grant given in excess of one year will be adjusted in the next year's grant or if the government decides to recover that one amount, the institution will be obliged to return that one amount.
(11) The State Government shall in no case be responsible for the health facilities provided by the Institute to the public.
12) It will be mandatory for the institution to follow the instructions given by different levels of government.
If the institution is not able to run these health institutions for some reason, the institution shall hand over the buildings and all other properties, equipment and financial details to the government by giving at least 3 months' notice to the government. However, in such a situation, the government will not take responsibility of any officer or staff appointed by the organization. The Government will not sanction any grant for any capital expenditure required to run the institution or any expenditure for its repair and maintenance.
The organization should decide in consultation with the local community if it is willing to provide services beyond the basic services such as outpatient services in the morning and afternoon at the primary health center, services related to immunization and mother child care in remote areas of the area, services related to various national health programs, essential services, 24-hour delivery services, special attention to the treatment and management of children under 0 to 6 years of age under the Integrated Child Development Program, free services to patients under the poverty line, free hospital services to patients under various national health programs.
15) The organization should either retain the existing health department staff in the primary health center to be run according to the figure of posts fixed by the government for the primary health center or appoint the staff of the organization on a contractual basis.

Take a decision. However, such employees should be in accordance with the figure and should be paid the same salary as the government employees.
These three health institutions are being given to run for a period of one year initially and after one year, their activities should be submitted to the government for examination. After this examination, a decision will be taken to extend the time to run these 3 health institutions.
17) The rural hospital and primary health centre will be supervised and supervised by the district surgeon and district health officer of the concerned district. The other programme officers under the Public Health Department will supervise the functioning of the Primary Health Centre, it will be mandatory to submit reports from time to time at the senior level to the voluntary organization, it will be mandatory to attend the meetings organized by the Health Department, Zilla Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and other departments at the district/ taluka level from time to time.
The performance of these 3 hospitals will be regularly evaluated. Accordingly, the use of various health services should be improved than before. Also, the growth of various services provided under the National Health Programme should be around 20-25 per cent per annum. If sufficient growth is seen, the NGO will be provided with an additional 25% of the incentive amount next year.
(2) In addition to these terms and conditions, if any other conditions are required to be prescribed in the existing Act/ Rules in the interest of the Government, a joint agreement should be entered into with the institution.
The Deputy Director of Health Services, Kolhapur, shall submit a report to the Government every three months on the rural hospital in Mahabaleshwar Taluka and two primary health centres in Taldeo and Tapola within a period of one year. As per the report, the Indian Red Cross Society will sanction grant-in-aid to Bel-Air Hospital, Panchgani (Mahabaleshwar), G. Satara, as per the prescribed expenditure.
