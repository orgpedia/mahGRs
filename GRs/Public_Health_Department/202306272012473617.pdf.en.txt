The Proposal:
National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM).
Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The funds for the Social Component (SCSP) for National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) will be provided to the State Health Societies as Central and State share from the allocation made in the approved budget for 2023-24.
