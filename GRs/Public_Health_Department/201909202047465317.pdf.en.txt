Preamble: The Government has decided to select the service provider by inviting open tender through the National Health Mission to provide the service of ambulances equipped with medical equipment with state-of-the-art communication system to provide immediate medical assistance to the disaster victims in the state. A Tender Committee was constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Health) vide order dated 28.10.2010. The tender committee has qualified the tender submitted by M/ s BVG India and awarded the contract for the operation of Emergency Response Service (EMS) of a total of 937 ambulances, 233 Advance Life Support (ALS) and 704 Basic Life Support (BLS), across the state in the next 5 years. The supplier was given the commissioning order as per the government decision dated March 28, 2013. The scheme has been operationalized w.e.f. 26.01.2014. The scheme was receiving funds from the Central Government through National Health Mission in 2014-15 and 2015-16 to some extent and the remaining funds were being disbursed through the State Plan. As per the decision of the Government in the aforesaid Act No. 2, the scheme was transferred from National Health Mission to Director, Health Services with effect from 2016-17. The scheme is being implemented from 2016-17. As per the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed with the service provider in this regard, the contract expired on 31.01.2019, however, as a matter of urgency, the service provider was given extension to pay the February 2019 dues at the rate of January 2019, as per the Government decision dated 31.03.2019, so that the ambulance service can continue without any interruption in patient care. Thereafter, as per the decision taken by the Committee headed by A.M.S. (Finance) constituted vide Government Resolution dated 3rd January, 2019, it was decided to extend the period for continuation of ambulance services without interruption from 1st March, 2019 to 30th June, 2019 at the rates as per Government Resolution dated 8th May, 2019 and from 1st July, 2019 to 31st October, 2019 vide Government Resolution dated 29th July, 2019. A decision in this regard is being taken.
Government Decision: The total recurring expenditure for the month of July, 2019 for the year 2019-20 on the equipped ambulance of Toll Free Number 108 under the Maharashtra Emergency Medical Services Scheme is Rs. 23,41,51, 004/- 70% of the amount of Rs. 16,39,05, 703/ (Rs. Sixteen crore forty-nine lakh five thousand seven hundred and three) payments (at 2018-19 rates) are being approved.
The details of the services provided by the above ambulances should be submitted to the government every month. According to the agreement, 70 per cent of the monthly payment should be recovered from the advance payment and the remaining 30 per cent should be recovered from the payment if a penalty is imposed on the payment. The payment for the month of July, 2019 should be at the rate of 2018-19.
