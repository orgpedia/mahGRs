The Proposal:
The health system in India needs to be better equipped to meet the needs at primary, secondary and tertiary health levels in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic. The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the inadequacy of essential public health facilities such as laboratory capacity, intensive care units, isolation beds, oxygen supply and ventilators. Keeping this in mind, the Central Government has approved the Pradhan Mantri Ayushman Bharat Health Infrastructure Mission in September, 2021 and started its implementation from October, 2021. The duration of the scheme is 5 years and will be implemented from 2021-22 to 2025-26. The scheme is 60 per cent funded by the central government and 40 per cent by state governments. A total of Rs. The objectives of the Pradhan Mantri Ayushman Bharat Health Infrastructure Mission are as follows: 1. To empower public health institutions at the grass root level to provide universal health coverage.
To elaborate and build a disease surveillance system enabled by information technology.
To support research work on COVID-19 and other infectious diseases.
