The Proposal:
Under the National Health Mission, a total of 60 Mobile Medical Units (40 old and 20 new) have been operationalized through service providers under the National Health Mission to provide health services to the needy people in the remote and inaccessible areas of the state. Free medical services are provided to the needy and poor people by visiting the mobile medical unit in these selected places. The mobile medical unit has a dispensary vehicle with a total of 6 staff members including female medical officer, staff nurse, pharmacist, lab technician, two drivers. Since the service provider of the old 40 Mobile Medical Units and 8 Mobile Medical Units had expired on 31.03.2021, the matter of approving the tender process for selecting the new service provider was under consideration of the Government.
Decision of the Government: As proposed by the Office of the Commissioner of Health Services, the proposal to implement the tender process for the next 3 years for the mobile medical unit to be operated by an external service provider at 08 places in Parbhani, Nanded, Satara, Raigad, Palghar, Nandurbar-Dhadgaon, Nandurbar Akkalkuwa, Nandurbar-Taloda under the National Health Mission in 2021-22 is being given administrative approval subject to the following conditions:

Terms and Conditions:
1) The service of the mobile medical unit is proposed by the external service provider. In which the vehicles, machinery, medicines and staff required for the medical unit, fuel expenses and other expenses are to be borne by the external system itself and the amount of OPEX bill per month after the start of work should be paid to the external system.
2) The need/ demand for this service should be verified. After that, the tender process will begin.
3) Make sure that the specifications are as per requirement and up to date. The technical specifications of the matter should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. The responsibility of sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the items approved in the PIP will be of the concerned programme head.
The tender process should be carried out only on the items included in the proposal.
4) Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labor Department, No. The tender process should be carried out as per the provisions of the Revised Manual of Procedures for Office Purchases to be made by Government Departments published under Section 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated 1st December, 2016.
5) The rates of the proposed items are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. Hence, the price should be fixed after the tender process. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the supplier has supplied the item to other places.
6) Pre/ post dispatch random sample inspection should be done to ensure proper and correct specification regarding this service.
7) The expenditure for the said component is Rs. Rs. 1144.80 lakh is to be met from this subsidy. It should be ensured that the tender process is completed within the stipulated time of administrative approval.
8) Also, it should be ensured that there is no duplication of these things.
9) The guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) should be strictly followed while implementing the entire tender process.
