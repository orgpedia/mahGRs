Presently, there are 53 dialysis centres in the state. Keeping in view the need for dialysis services in other government institutions in the state in addition to this center, Reference No. Under the project, it is proposed to start dialysis services through external system on PPP basis in 5 sub-district hospitals with 100 beds, 13 sub-district hospitals with 50 beds and one rural hospital with 30 beds. The proposal had been approved by the government.
Government's decision:
Since the Commissionerate of Health Services is required to provide dialysis services to the patients in the various hospitals mentioned in Annexure-I attached with the functions under it as proposed, it is being approved to grant administrative approval for the same subject to the following conditions for conducting the prescribed tendering process for selection of service provider for making the said services available from external system on PPP basis. :

The administrative approval is granted subject to the following conditions: :
1) The need/ demand for this dialysis service should be ascertained.
Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
2) It should be ensured that the specifications of the dialysis service are as per requirement and up to date.
The technical specification of the requirements for laboratory services should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (technical committee/ officials).
Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company.
The responsibility of sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the items approved in the PIP will be of the concerned programme head. Only the items included in the proposal should be purchased.
As proposed in the project, at present, there are 19 hospitals including 5 sub-district hospitals with 100 beds, 13 sub-district hospitals with 50 beds and 30 bedded rural hospital, Mahad.
The tender process should be carried out as per the provisions of the revised rule book of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by the government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labour Department, No. Bhankhas-2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated December 1, 2016.
Similarly, it should be ensured that the provision of the Finance Department, Government Decision dated 02.02.2013, dated 02.12.2013, dated 02.08.2016 and dated 27.04.2022, and the Government Decision and Guidelines issued by the Government from time to time, are fulfilled in order to take these services from the external system.
5) Further action on the tender should be taken only after finalizing the terms and conditions of service and the scope of work to be included in the tender document while selecting the external service provider for dialysis services through the existing tender document scrutiny committee in the Commissionerate.
6) Patients other than those who qualify in Category A, B and C as per the criteria of MJPJAY, the Government has decided to provide Rs. For one dialysis session as per SAMRIDH-2013/ PR No.462/ PART-2/ Arogya-3, Rs. 200/- will be charged and deposited with the Patient Welfare Committee, from this fee, the service provider will be paid an equal amount per beneficiary for these beneficiaries.
