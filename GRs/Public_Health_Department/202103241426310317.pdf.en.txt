The Proposal:
In order to provide accessible, affordable, efficient, responsive and reliable health services to the rural population, the Government of India has launched the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) from April, 2005 across the country. Under this scheme, health services like Reproductive and Child Health Programme, regular immunization, Mission Flexipool, strengthening of infrastructure and pulse polio vaccination etc. are provided. Accordingly, a proposal was under consideration of the Government to provide 60% Central share of grant to State Health Societies for Socially Special Component (SCSP) for National Rural Health Programme under National Health Mission. The proposal does not include any proposal for purchase of goods or capital goods. There is no short payment pending for one year under the respective head of accounts of these programmes. As per the policy of the Government, the funds on this proposal are being spent by depositing them in a separate bank account of the State Health Society, and also to submit necessary certificates in this regard in compliance with all the relevant provisions/ terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated April 16, 2020 of the Finance Department or to certify the necessary matters separately to the State Health Society.
The head of the programme will be responsible for the mechanism through which the funds will be spent.
The grant is conditional and will be disbursed only after submission of Utilization Certificate (UC) of the aforesaid fund as per Government Circular No. 2013/ PR No. 85/2013/ Budget-3, dated 25th April, 2013 of the Finance Department.
