Terms and Conditions:
1) The requirement/ demand for the said material/ equipment etc. shall be verified/ verified. Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
2) The specifications of the said materials/ equipment etc. shall be ensured to be as per requirement and up to date. The technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. The responsibility of sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the items approved in the PIP will be of the concerned programme head. Only the items included in the proposal should be purchased.
(3) The prescribed procurement procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the revised rule book of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labour Department, No. 2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry 4, dated December 1, 2016.
(4) The rates of the proposed purchases are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. Therefore, the minimum rates available on GeM portal, tariff agreements of other states, National Medical Devices Authority should be taken into account. Also, the minimum rates received in the tender if the item is being purchased or has been purchased in the Haffkine institution should be recorded as the estimated rate at the time of placing the tender and the rate should be determined at the time of procurement. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the supplier has supplied the item to other places.
(5) It should be ensured that the items to be purchased are purchased only after pre/ post dispatch random sample inspection.
(6) Storage and distribution of the items to be purchased after supply, supply chain management and inventory management should be done in a proper manner to ensure that these items do not remain unused and are used in a timely manner.
