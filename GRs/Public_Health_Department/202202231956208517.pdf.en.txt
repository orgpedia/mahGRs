Terms and Conditions:
(1) The requirement/ demand for the purchase of such vehicles shall be ascertained.
After this, the tender process for the purchase of these items should be carried out on the GM Portal.
(2) It shall be ensured that the specifications for the purchase of such vehicles are as per requirement and up-to-date. Also, the technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed upon by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials).
(3) To ensure that the procurement process is not carried out in such a manner that the technical specifications of the items to be procured benefit the particular company.

Also, the responsibility of sending the requisition letter as per the technical specifications of the matters approved from the balance reserve budget will be of the respective head of the function. As the case may be, only the items included in the proposal should be purchased.
(4) The prescribed procurement procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the revised rule book of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labour Department, No: Bhankhas-2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated 1st December, 2016.
(5) While purchasing vehicles, it should be ensured that the price ceiling policy of government vehicles under the Government Decision, Finance Department, Rank: Vehicle-2017/ Q. No. 41/17/ Regulations, dated 28.07.2020 is met.
(6) The rates of the proposed purchases are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. Therefore, the price should be determined during the purchase process. Also, it should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the supplier has supplied the items to other places.
(7) It should be ensured that the vehicles to be purchased are purchased only with proper and proper specifications by conducting pre/ post dispatch random sample inspection.
(8) Storage and distribution of the vehicles to be purchased, as well as supply chain management and inventory management should be done in a proper manner to ensure that the vehicles do not remain unused and are used within the stipulated time.
(9) The expenditure for the said procurement shall be met as per the guidance and approval of the Central Government from the remaining grants as committed budget in the year 2021-22 under" FMR Code" 2.1.3.1" and" FMR Code" B 11.2.4" for Blood Collection& Transport Vans in NHM Administrative Approval 2019-20 and 2017-18 respectively.
(10) It should be ensured that there is no duplication of the purchase of such vehicles. Also, the guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) should be strictly followed while carrying out the entire procurement process.
(11) Supply orders shall be issued only after the approval of the Procurement Committee after the completion of the procurement process. Further, action should be taken in accordance with the provisions of the Government Resolution, Public Health Department, No: Purchase-2016/ PR No. 240/ Health-8, dated 26th July, 2017.
(12) The vehicles to be purchased for blood collection and blood supply shall be under the control of the hospital head and the head of the blood bank of the hospital.
