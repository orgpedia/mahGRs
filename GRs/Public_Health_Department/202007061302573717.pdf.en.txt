The Proposal:
The World Health Organisation (WHO) has declared the novel coronavirus outbreak a global health emergency. The World Health Organization (WHO) and the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India have been issuing advisories on a daily basis for prevention and control of the virus.
In view of the increasing number of corona patients in the state of Maharashtra, urgent and emergency measures have been initiated for the prevention and control of the disease. This includes patient tracing, isolation and overall prevention and control of the virus. The Epidemic Diseases Act, 1897 has also been invoked. In this situation, the number of corona infected patients in the state is being reviewed daily to control the outbreak and take preventive measures. However, in view of the increasing number of cases and the emerging situation, it has become imperative to take care of patients from private medical professionals and private hospitals as well. According to
Orders have been issued from time to time at the level of Chief Secretary.
In this background. The Chief Secretary held a meeting with the President and representatives, Indian Medical Association (Maharashtra State). During the meeting, various orders, guidelines, notifications, etc. issued by the Central Government and the State Government regarding COVID-19 treatment were discussed. During the meeting, representatives of the Indian Medical Association (IMA) highlighted the difficulties being faced by private medical practitioners in the availability of PPE kits for COVID-19 treatment, and the challenges being faced at the community level in the aftermath of COVID-19 treatment. The aims and objectives of the Committee are as follows:
To make available to all concerned the various government decisions issued regarding the prevention and control measures of the corona virus infection in the state as well as the protocol regarding medication.
To provide necessary Government support in providing Personal Protective Equipment (PPE Kits, Gloves, Masks) to private medical practitioners and their hospital staff.
To take necessary measures to ensure social security and amicable treatment to private medical professionals and health workers providing COVID-19 treatment.
To coordinate with the Government and private medical practitioners on the implementation of various measures being disseminated by WHO, Ministry of Health& Family Welfare, Government of India at the State level/ District level.
