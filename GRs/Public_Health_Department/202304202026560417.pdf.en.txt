The Proposal:
30 bedded Rural Hospital, Nardana. The proposal for administrative approval of the budget and plan amounting to Rs. 1377.60 lakh was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The budget for construction of main building of 30 bedded Rural Hospital, Nardana G. Dhule is based on the schedule of 2020-21. According to the attached recapitulation sheet, Rs. 1377.60 lakhs, the administrative approval is being accorded under this Government Resolution subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) Before inviting tenders for the work, the technical approval of the Chief Engineer, Public Works Department will be required.
2) Administrative approval is being given subject to the condition that the work is funded from the available budget for construction.
3) When e-tendering the work, the work should be tendered together without breaking it into pieces.

4) The head of the health institution concerned should ensure the availability, suitability and adequacy of space for work.
5) The price of the proposed work should not be increased unnecessarily according to the situation of the area and the needs of the health institutions.
As the said budget is a rough model budget, the detailed budgets of these works shall be technically approved by the competent authorities of the Public Works Department in their respective areas.
7) The Public Works Department will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the relevant rules of the local rural development agency.
8) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV 2013/ Q.No.177/ T.C.1D. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014.
9) After checking the hospital maps, instructions are being given to revise the hospital maps by considering the oxygen pipeline required for the hospital, the place for air conditioners and the arrangement of their duct, the facilities to be provided to the disabled, the pathways to be done in the hospital for those persons, etc. and its functionality. The maps of the hospital should be revised in consultation with the experts in hospital designing and the doctors and staff associated with the hospital.
10) The building should be constructed within the limit of administrative approval price by incorporating amendments in the budget as per the 2022 guidelines of IPHS issued by the Central Government.
11) It should be ensured that the area of the proposed building is in accordance with the permissible standards of the staff.
12) The concerned Deputy Director of Health Services and District Surgeon should review the construction at the field level from time to time.
13) While constructing the hospital building, it should be ensured that the planning of the hospital building according to the fire safety norm is done in accordance with Part 4 Fire and Safety of the National Building Code vol-1.
14) The estimates and maps should be checked by the concerned Chief Architect and Chief Engineer (Architecture/ Vidyut).

The entire responsibility of fulfilling the conditions mentioned in 1 to 14 above will be of the concerned Executive Engineer, Public Works Department and District Surgeon.
The expenditure on this work will be covered under Demand No. H-8, Head of Accounts 4210- Capital Expenditure on Medical Care and Public Health, 02- Rural Health Care, 04- Public Health, 104- Community Health Centre, (00) (01) Large Constructions (42100391), 53- Large Constructions.
The Commissioner, Health Services, Mumbai should immediately submit a proposal to the Government to allocate funds in the budget for the construction of the main building of the 30-bed rural hospital, Nardana G. Dhule.
