Preamble: National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health care to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM). Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The Government was contemplating to provide Central share and State share to the State Health Societies from the provision in the approved budget for the year 2020-20 for the pending grants under Tribal Appropriation Scheme 2019-20 for the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Health Mission. A decision in this regard will be taken later.
Government Decision: Under the Tribal Appropriation (TSP) for the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) implemented under the National Health Mission (NHM), the Department of Tribal Development has issued Reference No. Out of the approved budgetary allocation for 2020-21, the Central share out of the head 2210F738 has been increased to Rs.
4533.00 lakhs and 2210E455 out of State share of Rs. 3220.00 lakh is being sanctioned to the State Health Societies.
