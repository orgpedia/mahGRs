The Proposal:
The National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural poor, the marginalized and the needy. Factors affecting health (e.g. Food, environment, sanitation, safe water supply, women and child development etc. From the year 2013-14, the Government of India has decided to take up the above health related programmes in urban areas under the name of" National Urban Health Mission." The Centre's share and the state's share is 60:40. The proposal for providing the pending Central and State share for 2019-20 under the general schemes of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Urban Health Mission to the State Health Societies from the budgetary provision of 2020-21 was under consideration.
