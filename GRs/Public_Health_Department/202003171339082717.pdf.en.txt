The Proposal:
The state budget for 2020-21 has been presented. In the budget speech of the Finance Minister, it was announced to convert the sub-district hospital in Sakoli G. Bhandara into a 100-bed hospital. Upazila Hospital, Sakoli G. Upazila Hospital in Bhandara will be upgraded to 100 bedded sub-district hospital. It was approved by the Government on 17 January 2013. The proposal to give administrative approval to the budget and plan for the construction of the building of the 100-bed sub-district hospital was under the consideration of the government. The following decision has been taken:
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 2. The budget for construction of building for 100 bedded Sub District Hospital at Sub District Hospital, Sakoli G. Bhandara has been prepared by the Executive Engineer, Public Works Department, Bhandara. The rates are based on the rates of 2016-17. According to the Recapitulation Sheet attached with Rs. 6123.52 Lakhs. Administrative Sanction is being accorded under this Government Resolution subject to the following terms and conditions:

1) The detailed budget and plan of the work should be approved by the concerned Chief Engineer (Vidyut) and Chief Engineer (Architecture) of the Public Works Department.
2) The detailed budget and plan of the work should be approved by the concerned Chief Engineer Public Works Department.
3) While preparing the detailed budget, the Goods and Services Tax (GST) should be levied at the rate applicable.
(4) The work shall be subject to the technical approval of the competent authority of the Public Works Department before inviting tenders for the work.
5) The provision of furniture and electrical works should be shown in detail and the rate separation should be submitted to the government.
6) Before starting the construction, the map should be approved by the architect, S.B. Department Nashik.
(7) At the time of tendering, a single tender should be issued for all the works mentioned without separate tenders for each work.
8) As it is possible to increase the cost of the proposed work according to the situation of the area and the requirements of the health institutions, the necessary proposals should be made by the area officers of the Public Works Department.
9) As the said budget is a rough model budget, the detailed budgets of these works will be technically approved by the competent authorities of the respective areas of the Public Works Department. While granting such technical approval, the provisions should be finalised with the concurrence of the Deputy Directors of the respective Health Service Boards of the Health Department.
10) If the work is to be done according to the new model maps, the architectural plan should be approved by the private architect appointed by the Public Health Department. The newly appointed architect and district surgeon will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the rules of the local rural development agency. Also, the letter requesting this permission must be signed by the district surgeon.
The maps and architectural plans of these works do not require separate approval from the Chief/ Deputy Chief Architect of the Public Works Department.
12) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV-2013/ No. 177/ T.C. 1D. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014.
13) Ensure that the above tasks are completed within this amount.
