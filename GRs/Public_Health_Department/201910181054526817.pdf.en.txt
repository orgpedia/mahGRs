Preamble: The Government has decided to select the service provider by inviting open tender through the National Health Mission to provide the service of ambulances equipped with medical equipment with state-of-the-art communication system to provide immediate medical assistance to the disaster victims in the state. A Tender Committee was constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Health) vide order dated 28.10.2010. The tender committee has qualified the tender submitted by MeBVG India and a total of 937 ambulances, 233 Advance Life Support (ALS) and 704 Basic Life Support (BLS), have been sold across the state.

The Government of India vide its Order dated 28.03.2013, has issued the Operational Order to M/ s BVG India for implementation of Emergency Response Services (EMS) over the next 5 years. The scheme has been operationalized w.e.f. 26.01.2014. The scheme was receiving funds from the Central Government through National Health Mission in 2014-15 and 2015-16 to some extent and the remaining funds were being disbursed through the State Plan. As per the decision of the Government in the aforesaid Act No. 2, the scheme was transferred from National Health Mission to Director, Health Services with effect from 2016-17. The scheme is being implemented from 2016-17. As per the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed with the service provider in this regard, the contract expired on 31.01.2019, however, as a matter of urgency, the service provider was given extension to pay the February 2019 dues at the rate of January 2019, as per the Government decision dated 31.03.2019, so that the ambulance service can continue without any interruption in patient care. Thereafter, as per the decision taken by the Committee headed by A.M.S. (Finance) constituted vide Government Resolution dated 3rd January, 2019, it has been decided to extend the period for continuation of ambulance services without interruption from 1st March, 2019 to 30th June, 2019 as per Government Resolution dated 8th May, 2019 and from 1st July, 2019 to 31st October, 2019 vide Government Resolution dated 29th July, 2019. A decision in this regard is being taken.
Government Decision: The recurring expenditure on the equipped ambulance of the toll free number 108 under the Maharashtra Emergency Medical Services Scheme for the year 2019-20 is estimated to be Rs. 23,08,56, 512/- 70% of the amount of Rs. 16, 15,99,558/- (Rupees Sixteen Crores Fifteen Lakhs Nine Thousand Five Hundred and Fifty Eight only) is being allowed to be paid (at 2018-19 rates).
The details of the services provided by the above ambulances should be submitted to the government every month. As per the agreement, 70 per cent of the monthly payment is to be paid in advance and the remaining 30 per cent is to be paid as penalty.
