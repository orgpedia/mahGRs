In order to make health facilities more effective, the Central Government has launched the Health Technology Assessment Programme, through which health services in the country will be examined and evaluated. Accordingly, the matter of allowing a survey to assess the services provided by health institutions in the state was under consideration of the government.
Government Circular- Under the above letter of the Department of Health and Family Welfare of the Central Government, the Central Government has taken up the Health Technology Assessment Program, stating that the Central Government is committed to providing health-related facilities to the entire country. Under this programme, health services in the country will be assessed. For this, a coordinator has been appointed to inspect and evaluate the health services provided by the health institutions in the state.
The Medical College and KEM Hospital, Mumbai, has been appointed as the coordinator and the agency will conduct a survey of the health services provided by the health institutions in Thane, Pune and Beed districts and submit a report to the central government.
In this regard, it is informed that Deputy Directors of Health Services of Thane, Pune and Aurangabad have been appointed to conduct a survey of health related services under the Health Technology Assessment program.
