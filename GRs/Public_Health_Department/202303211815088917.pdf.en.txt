The Proposal:
At present, there are 53 such centres in the state. Keeping in view the need for dialysis services in 63 other government hospitals in the state, in addition to this centre, reference no. Under the project, it is proposed to start dialysis services through an external system on PPP basis in hospitals with 50 beds and above at present, including other hospitals as per requirement and newly operationalized health institutions from time to time. The proposal had been approved by the government.
Government Decision: To start dialysis centers in 63 hospitals with 50 beds and above in the state on a PPP basis, the necessary amount of approximately Rs. 2776.93 Lakhs out of which the approved Supplementary Action Plan for the year 2022-23 has provided Rs. 1114.59 lakh has been approved in principle. Accordingly, the government has decided to start dialysis centres in 63 hospitals of 50 beds and above mentioned in the statement in Annexure-A with this decision on PPP basis.

Administrative approval is being accorded for implementing the prescribed tender process for selection of suppliers through a competitive bidding process on a segment-wise basis, subject to the following conditions. : 1) The need/ demand for this dialysis service should be ascertained. Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
2) It should be ensured that the specifications of the dialysis service are as per requirement and up to date. The technical specification of the requirements for laboratory services should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (technical committee/ officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. The responsibility of sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the items approved in the PIP will be of the concerned programme head.
Only the items included in the proposal should be purchased.
The project will have the scope of setting up dialysis centres in 63 existing hospitals of 50 beds and above on PPP basis as proposed in the proposal, which will include other hospitals as per requirement and newly functional health institutions from time to time.
The tender process should be carried out as per the provisions of the revised rule book of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by the government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labour Department, No. Bhankhas-2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated December 1, 2016.
Similarly, it should be ensured that the provision of the Finance Department, Government Decision dated 02.02.2013, dated 02.12.2013, dated 02.08.2016 and dated 27.04.2022, and the Government Decision and Guidelines issued by the Government from time to time, are fulfilled in order to take these services from the external system.
5) Further action on the tender should be taken only after finalizing the terms and conditions of service and the scope of work to be included in the tender document while selecting the external service provider for dialysis services.
6) Also, it should be ensured that there is no duplication in the purchase of these items.
The Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) guidelines should be strictly followed while implementing the entire procurement process.
