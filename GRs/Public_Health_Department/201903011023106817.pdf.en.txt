Preamble: In the approved Action Plan for 2018-19 under the National Health Mission, the matter of obtaining administrative approval for the tender process for the purchase of 20 mobile medical units and ancillary vehicles was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
As per the PIP approved by the Government of India in 2018-19, 20 Mobile Medical Units have been sanctioned and proposed at Yavatmal, Sindhudurg, Palghar, Nashik, Solapur, Aurangabad, Kolhapur, Nanded, Buldhana, Chandrapur, Ahmednagar, Raigad, Beed, Amravati, Akola, Hingoli, Jalgaon, Dhule, Jalna and Thane. As per the PIP approved by the Government of India in 2018-19, under the Grant-in-Aid entitled FMR Code 2.1.1.1 (Capex) (Amount Approved However, State to Operationalize MMUs on Turnkey basis), the proposal of the Commissioner (Health Services) and Mission Director, National Health Mission, Mumbai, regarding the procurement of 20 Mobile Medical Units is being administratively approved for tendering process.

The above administrative approval is being granted subject to the following terms and conditions.
1) The requirement/ demand of the material should be ascertained. Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
2) The specifications of the material should be verified as per requirement and as up-to-date. The technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials).
Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company.
After that, you have to buy them.
(3) The prescribed procurement procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the revised rule book of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labor Department, No. 2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated December 1, 2016.
4) The rates of the proposed procurement are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. Therefore, the price should be determined at the end of the purchase process. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the supplier has supplied the item to other places.
5) Pre/ post dispatch random sample inspection of the items to be purchased should be done to ensure that only the items with proper and correct specifications are purchased.
6) Storage and distribution of the items to be procured after supply, as well as supply chain management
