A trust named" Chief Minister's Assistance Fund, Mumbai" has been constituted under the Public Trust Societies Act under the chairmanship of Hon'ble Chief Minister. This trust provides financial assistance to patients suffering from serious disorders like heart surgery, kidney transplant, cancer etc. Apart from this financial assistance, it is mandatory to provide treatment to the poor and needy patients at affordable rates through a hospital registered under the Public Trust Institutions Act. However, there was no special arrangement for it. Therefore, a new cell under the Chief Minister's Relief Fund Trust, the Chief Minister's Medical Assistance Cell, has been set up to control the assistance given under the Chief Minister's Relief Fund and the assistance given by the hospitals established under the Public Trust Act. It was established on August 13, 2015.
With regard to strengthening of the aforesaid cell and to scrutinize/ examine the applications seeking financial assistance for medical treatment, Reference No. The committee was formed as per the decision of the government. The government was contemplating to constitute a new committee in the wake of the retirement or transfer of the present members of the committee.

Committee's mandate: 1) The aforesaid Committee will assist the employees of CM's Relief Fund in their day-to-day work for providing technical assistance, scrutinizing applications and providing clarifications, if required.
2) To check whether the patient is a beneficiary of Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana/ Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram/ charitable hospitals.
(3) To recommend for treatment in such hospital as the Committee may recommend, if some of the surgeries/ diseases in the application received are not serious in nature and the treatment of the said diseases is to be done after some time.
4) After scrutiny, on applications that could not get the benefit of government schemes, the committee will recommend the amount of financial assistance for the treatment of common diseases in the Chief Minister's Assistance Fund, taking into account the amount under the Mahatma Jyotiba Fuje Jan Arogya Yojana (packages).
(5) If the amount of treatment from the Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana for certain diseases has been exhausted completely and additional funds are required for the said disease, in such cases, the committee shall recommend appropriate financial assistance.
6) To check whether the patient is eligible for financial assistance from the Chief Minister's Relief Fund by looking at the condition of the patient.
7) To guide patients to get more financial support from other charitable organization.
