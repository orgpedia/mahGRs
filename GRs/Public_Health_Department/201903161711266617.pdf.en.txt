Preamble: The matter of obtaining administrative approval for payment of enhanced honorarium to Nursing School cooks, security guards and sweepers from April, 2018 onwards from the grants available during the financial year 2018-19 under the National Health Mission was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Under the National Health Mission, the honorarium of the staff in the post of cook, security guard and cleaner in Nirsingh School has been approved in the PIP for 2018-19 as per the increased honorarium. In PIP 2018-19, under FMR Code 9.2.2, Rs. A grant of Rs. 135.56 lakh has been sanctioned. Accordingly, administrative approval is being accorded to the proposal of the Additional Mission Director, National Urban Health Mission, Mumbai for payment of honorarium to the cooks, security guards and cleaners working in the respective nursing schools from April, 2018 as per the proposed enhanced honorarium.

The above administrative approval is being granted subject to the following terms and conditions: 1) The requirement/ demand for the said matter should be ascertained/ verified.
Action should be taken in this regard.
If the remuneration of the employee working in the post of cook, security guard and sweeper is less than the proposed remuneration in the 2018-19 PIP, their remuneration should be implemented from April, 2018 as per the approved remuneration in 2018-19. The annual increment will be applicable on the revised remuneration as per their work assessment report.
3) If the remuneration of the employee working in the post of cook, security guard and sweeper is more than the proposed remuneration in the year 2018-19 PIP, the existing remuneration should be paid. Also, the annual increment will be applicable to the existing salary as per their assessment report.
4) Vacant posts of cooks, security guards and cleaners should also be recruited at revised pay.
In FY 2018-19, under the head HR for Nursing in FMR Code 9.2.2, Rs. A grant of Rs. 486.38 lakh has been sanctioned. The support staff will get Rs. A grant of Rs. 135.56 lakh has been sanctioned to meet the expenses of the cooks, security guards and cleaners in the nursing schools.
6) It should be ensured that there is no duplication in the allocation of grants for these items.
The Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) guidelines should be strictly followed in the entire process.
The proceedings shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, in Annexure to the Government Resolution, Finance Division, No. 2013/ No. 30/ Part- 2/2013/ Regulations, dated 17th April, 2015.
The Commissioner, Health Services and Missions is fully responsible for fulfilling the conditions mentioned in the above sequence 1 to 8 and ensuring that the procurement is as per the rules and requirements.
