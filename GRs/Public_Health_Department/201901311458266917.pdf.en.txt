Preamble: The proposal of the Directorate General of Health Services to approve the cost of repairing the vehicle number MH 12 AH 6763 Qualis in the office of the Joint Director, Health Services, (HIWZRO), Pune-1 was under consideration of the Government. A decision in this regard is being taken.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: For the repair of vehicle MH 12 AH 6763 Qualis, which is in use in the office of Joint Director, Health Services, (HIWZRO), Pune-1, Rs. Administrative Sanction is being granted for expenditure of Rs.63,991/- subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) The vehicle must be used only within the authority and jurisdiction certified by the Directorate General of Health Services or the competent authorities of the State Health Transport Organization.
2) The vehicle must be used by the area officer to whom the vehicle is provided. Also, it should not be used anywhere other than the area to which it is given.
(3) In the case of institutions providing vehicles under the control of the District Councils or institutions providing vehicles subsidized by the District Councils, the responsibility of paying the risk and liability of legal claims made by a third party in the case of accident, theft, etc. of the vehicle shall lie with the institution to which the vehicle is provided and the head office/ department of the institution.
The Health Department on behalf of the Government of Maharashtra will not pay any liability in this regard.
4) The vehicle owner will always be responsible for protecting the capital invested by the health department from fire, flood, earthquake, accident, theft, arson, social riots, natural or human disaster, etc. In case of damage to the vehicle due to this reason, the vehicle holder (Zilla Parishad/ Zilla Parishad Aided Institutions and their respective Head Offices/ Departments) shall be liable to pay to the Health Department the amount of depreciation deducted from the value of the vehicle at that time.

In exceptional circumstances, for example. 7) In the event of a vehicle accident, the drivers are required to report it to the police inspector of the concerned police station and make a panchnama about it.
8) The details of the repair of the vehicle and the expenses incurred on it, as well as the details of the tyres, tubes, batteries, etc. installed in the vehicle should be written in the vehicle history book from time to time.
9) The above instructions should be strictly followed by all concerned, otherwise disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned by fixing responsibility on them.
10) The cost should be appropriately covered.
