The Proposal:
National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM).
Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The Government was considering to release the pending Central and State share of National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) funds to the State Health Societies for the years 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21 from the approved budgetary allocation of 2020-21.
Government's decision:
