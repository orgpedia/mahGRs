As per Rule 10 (4)& 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, it is the policy of the Government to retire officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity from Government service in public interest by reviewing their service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 01 here. The Government Decision dated 10.06.2019 prescribes the procedure and general instructions regarding service review. Accordingly, for the review of services of officers in the cadre of Medical Officer in Group-A (Pay Level S-20: 56100-177500 in the Pay Matrix), Maharashtra Medical and Health Services under the Commissionerate of Health Services, Reference No. 02 here. As per the Government decision dated 02.11.2021, a Departmental Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Public Health Department.
Reference No. As per the proposal mentioned at 3-5, Medical Officer in Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-20: 56100-177500 in the Pay Matrix) under the Commissionerate of Health Services

The proposal to review the services of the officers of this cadre at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, was submitted to the Departmental Review Committee headed by Principal Secretary, Public Health Department in a circular manner. As per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, which evaluated the service of the said officers, it was under the consideration of the Government to approve the officers who have been found eligible to remain in service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: As per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, a total of 25 officers in the cadre of Medical Officer in Group-A (Pay Level S-20: 56100-177500 in the Pay Matrix) of Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, under the Commissionerate of Health Services, are being approved to continue their service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
The service book of the officers mentioned in the attached form should be taken on record that they have been approved to continue the service after their 50/55 years of age or after completing 30 years of qualifying service.
