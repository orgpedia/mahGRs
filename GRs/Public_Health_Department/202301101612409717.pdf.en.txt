Preamble: The National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Heart Disease and Stroke under the National Programme for Control of Non-Communicable Diseases is being implemented in 34 districts of the State. Rise in non-communicable diseases
63% of deaths are due to non-communicable diseases, of which cancer accounts for 9%. The state government has taken a policy decision to procure medicines and medical equipment required for health institutions in the state from Haffkine Corporation. Therefore, it is mandatory for the Haffkine Corporation to conduct a tender process to procure all types of medicines required by patients.
There is a delay in making available the required chemotherapy drugs for cancer patients through the tender process. Timely chemotherapy for cancer patients
If chemotherapy is given on time, the patient can get some relief from their pain. However, if these drugs are not available in time, cancer patients have to endure pain. The number of cancer patients is increasing in the state.

To provide timely access to chemotherapy drugs to patients suffering from rare diseases like cancer, the project implementation plan for 2022-23 to 2023-24 has approved Rs. As an exceptional case of providing relief to cancer patients under a grant of Rs 300 lakh, the matter was under consideration of the government to issue administrative approval for procurement from the supplier at the price fixed by Tata Memorial Hospital for the period of receipt of the drug.
Government's decision:
Considering the increasing number of cancer patients in the state, to ensure timely availability of chemotherapy drugs to patients suffering from rare diseases like cancer, out of the grant sanctioned in the project implementation plan for 2022-23 to 2023-24, Rs. In order to provide relief to the cancer patients from the subsidy of Rs.300 lakhs, as an exceptional special case, the tender process has been published and administrative approval is being granted to procure the chemotherapy drugs mentioned in Annexure-A from the supplier at the rate fixed by Tata Memorial Hospital for the period till the receipt of chemotherapy drugs, subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) The need/ demand for the said chemotherapy drugs should be ascertained. Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
2) It will take some time to get the chemotherapy drugs through the tender process. The drugs required during the intervening period shall be procured as per requirement from the supplier mentioned in Annexure-A at the price fixed by Tata Memorial Hospital for the period for which the chemotherapy drugs are received by publishing the tender process in this regard as an exceptional case.
3) It should be ensured that the specifications of the said medicines and materials are as per the requirement and as per the requirement. The technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. After that, you need to buy the necessary drugs.
4) The rates of the proposed procurement are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. The final price should be determined after the purchase process. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and the supplier concerned does not exceed the price at which these items are supplied to other places.

5) Pre/ post-dispatch random sample inspection of the items to be purchased should be done to ensure that only the items with proper and correct specifications are purchased.
6) Storage and distribution of the items to be procured after supply, supply chain management and inventory management should be done in a proper manner to ensure that these items do not remain unused and are used within the prescribed time. Also, care should be taken to ensure that the drug is not misused.
The expenditure for the said procurement should be met from the approved provision in S. No.109 in the approved Project Implementation Plan for 2022-23 and 2023-24.
8) Also, it should be ensured that there is no duplication in the purchase of these items.
9) After the completion of the procurement process, the supply order should be issued only after the approval of the procurement committee.
10) The tender process should be carried out as per the provisions of the revised rulebook of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labor Department, No. Bhankhas-2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated December 1, 2016.
Similarly, it should be ensured that the provision of the Finance Department, Government Decision dated 02.02.2013, dated 02.12.2013, dated 02.08.2016 and dated 27.04.2022, and the Government Decision and Guidelines issued by the Government from time to time, are fulfilled in order to take these services from the external system.
The guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) should be strictly followed while carrying out the entire procurement process.
12) Supply orders should be issued only after the approval of the Purchasing Committee after the completion of the procurement process. In this regard, the Public Health Department, vide Government Resolution No. Procurement-2016/ Sec. 240/ Health-8, dated 26th July, 2017 should be dealt with as per the provisions of the Government Resolution.
The Commissioner, Director of Health Services and Mission, National Health Mission, Mumbai and the respective Programme Heads will be responsible for ensuring that the conditions mentioned in the above Table 1 to 12 are fulfilled and the procurement is as per the rules and requirements.
