Introduction: At present, along with newspapers and television channels, social media is being used more and more. Therefore, information about the activities, policies, etc. of the National Health Mission should reach the citizens and the youth of the state should use social media.
In view of the growing trend, social media platforms such as Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Whatsapp, Telegram, blogs and websites etc. can be effectively used to increase their participation.
The proposal to appoint an agent was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: In order to disseminate information about the activities, policies and schemes of the National Urban Health Mission, information and service facilities at the level of Municipal Corporation and Municipal Council to the citizens and to increase their participation in the social media in view of the increasing trend of the youth of the state, it is estimated that the available platforms in social media such as Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, whatos app, Telegram, blogs and websites on the Internet will be effectively used to appoint" Social Media Agents" for a period of one year. Administrative approval is being accorded for an expenditure of Rs. 64.00 lakhs.

This agent will plan and set up a cell in all the eight circles in coordination with the Information Education and Communication Cell at the state level and the National Urban Health Mission.

Awareness will be created through social media agents verified by the Public Health Department.

The agent will review the work done every month and report how many users have reached the relevant message and awareness has been created using social media.
