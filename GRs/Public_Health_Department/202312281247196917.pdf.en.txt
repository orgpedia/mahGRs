Introduction: Many pregnant women in India, both below the poverty line and above the poverty line, are forced to work for wages despite not being physically able to do so till the last stage of pregnancy. As a result, maternal and infant mortality rates are high and the health of pregnant women and children is adversely affected. The Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana was launched on 1st January, 2017. This is done through Section 1.

Ministry of Women and Child Development. As per the" Mission Shakti" guidelines dated 14 th July, 2022, a total of fourteen schemes have been integrated in two parts under Mission Shakti. Out of these, a total of 6 schemes are in the" SHAKTI" category and Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana has been included in these schemes. The revised guidelines for the implementation of the scheme and the benefit to the beneficiaries under the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana are being approved to be implemented in the state from 2023-24 as follows. The scheme will be implemented with 60 per cent funding from the central government and 40 per cent from the state government.
Benefits and their distribution under the scheme will be as follows:
Under the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, after the eligible beneficiary fulfills the conditions and documents, she will get Rs. 5000/- (Rs. 5000 only) in two installments and in case of second girl child, in a single phase after the birth of the girl child, Rs. 6000/- (Rs. 6,000 only) will be credited to the Aadhaar Enabled Bank Account or Post Office Account (DBT).
Earlier, the benefit of this scheme was being given to the beneficiaries through the escrow account of the Commissioner, Health Services, Mumbai. However, as per the guidelines of the Central Government, After 31st March, 2023, the benefit is to be disbursed to the beneficiaries through SNA account having signature authority of Additional Director, Joint Director/ Deputy Director and Financial Advisor, Family Welfare, Pune, instead of closing the escrow account and payment of benefit through DBT is being approved.

The objectives of the scheme are:
A healthy diet for pregnant and lactating mothers should be encouraged to improve maternal and child health.
The health of newborns should be improved and maternal and infant mortality rates should be reduced and controlled.
This benefit will be beneficial in improving sex ratio at birth, preventing female foeticide and welcoming female births.
To increase the rate of institutional delivery by increasing the rate at which beneficiaries avail the facilities of health institutions.
Along with the birth of the newborn, the rate of birth registration should be increased.
In order to avail the benefit of Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, the beneficiary must belong to at least one of the following groups (copy of proof of at least one identity card is required to be attached):
The average annual family income of women is Rs. less than 8 lakhs.
Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes women.
Women with 40% and above disability.
(iv) Women holding BPL ration cards.
Women beneficiaries under Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PMJAY) under Ayushman Bharat.
e-SHRAM card holder women.
Women farmers to be beneficiaries under Kisan Samman Nidhi.
Women holding MNREGA job cards.
Pregnant and lactating Anganwadi Workers (AWWs)/ Anganwadi Helpers (AWHs)/ ASHAs.
Women beneficiaries of ration cards under the Food Security Act, 2013.
At least one of the following documents must be submitted along with the application:
(i) A copy of the beneficiary's Aadhaar card or Aadhaar Enrolment (EID) document along with the prescribed documents.
2) Fully filled Mother and Child Protection Card which should contain the date of last menstrual period, date of registration of pregnancy, records of ante-natal check-up.
