The Proposal:
National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM).
Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. It was under consideration of the Government to provide the pending State share of the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) for the year 2020-21 to the State Health Societies from the sanctioned budgetary allocation of 2021-22 for the Social Component (SCSP). The details of grants disbursed so far in this case are as follows.
