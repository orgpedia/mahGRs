Provided that in all cases where the parents of a Government servant, (a) are drawing pension or (ii) are drawing income from other sources even though they are not drawing pension or (iii) are drawing income from other sources including pension, as the case may be, at the time of availing treatment, their total income from all sources at the time of availing treatment is within the limit of the financial benefit (i.e., Rs. 3,500/- plus dearness allowance admissible on the date of commencement of treatment) available to a retired State Government servant drawing basic pension of Rs.3,500/- per month, they are deemed to be dependent on the Government servant for the purpose of medical reimbursement and their ailments. This provision is also applicable in determining the dependency of an unmarried sister and a divorced sister of a government employee and in case a woman government employee chooses her parents over her in-laws. After the revision of the pension of the retired government employee as per the recommendation of the commission, the government was considering to revise the above basic pension or the maximum income limit per month.
Government Decision In this regard, the Government has decided as follows: (a) In all cases where the parents of a Government servant are (i) drawing pension or (ii) earning income by other means, including non-pension, or (iii) earning income by other means, including pension, the total income at the time of availment of treatment shall be deemed to be dependent on the Government servant for the purpose of medical reimbursement, if the total income at the time of availment of treatment is within the limit of the financial benefit (i.e. Rs.9,000/- plus dearness allowance admissible on the date of commencement of treatment) available to a retired State Government servant drawing basic pension of Rs.9,000/- per month.
(b) The above provision shall apply in determining the dependency of unmarried sister and divorced sister of a Government servant and in case a woman Government servant chooses her mother-in-law in place of her parents, in such case the mother-in-law in-law in-law shall also be required to determine her dependency.
(2) This order shall take effect from the date of publication.
This Order is being issued in concurrence with the Department of Finance vide its informal reference No. 26/ Service-5 dated 23rd April, 2019.
