Preamble: As per the recommendations of the 13th Finance Commission, the grant of honorarium to ASHAs for follow-up of all children in 17 tribal blocks and low birth weight children in the remaining 61 blocks of tribal prone areas has been approved vide letters dated 13.03.2015 and 16.03.2017. Under this, 90 visits are given to newborns and children of 78 taluks of tribal-prone areas by ASHA volunteers in the first 6 months at the rate of one day and 12 visits are given at the rate of 1 in every 15 days in the next 6 months. The programme has been running regularly in the tribal districts since 2015-16 till date. For this programme, grants up to the end of March 2022 were also provided to the tribal districts. However, after March 2022, there will be no Intensified Homebase Neonatal Care (I-HBNC) subsidy under the 13th Finance Commission. Additional Director, Health Services, Pune has proposed that this programme should be continued. Therefore, to continue Intensified Homebase Neonatal Care (I-HBNC) programme under the 13th Finance Commission, an amount of Rs. 647.19 lakh. As per the action plan approved earlier under the 13th Finance Commission, some new components were proposed and an increase in the number of units/ institutions in some of the components. Some of the proposed components have already been purchased.
