Administrative approval was sought from the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Submitted by the Directorate General of Health Services vide letter dated 14/9/2020
EMD Head of ESWL Machine at Divisional Reference Service Hospital, Amravati
In the financial year 2020-2021, to repair the ESWL machine by purchasing the wave part
Account No. 4210 1128 CHAPTER-52- MACHINE AND EQUIPMENT Out of the target of Rs. 28,56,000/-
(Rupees Twenty Eight Lakhs Fifty Five Thousand only) to be spent within the limit of subsidy disbursed on BDS system.
Administrative approval is granted subject to the following conditions:
Conditions: 1) The requirement/ demand of EMD head wave part of the said ESWL machine should be ascertained. Only after this, the program head should take care to mention in the requisition form the specifications of the material to be purchased for the repair of the said items as approved by the technical committee.

(2) The technical specifications of the said items should be as per the specifications approved by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officers). It should also be ensured that the technical specification is not tailored to benefit any particular company.
(3) The prescribed procurement procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions contained in the Revised Manual of Procedures for Office Purchases to be made by Government Departments published under the Government Decision, Industries, Energy and Labour Department, No. 2014/ No. 82/ Part-|/ Industry-4, dated 1st December, 2016.
4) The rates of the proposed procurement are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. Therefore, it should be ensured that the price fixed during the procurement process is less than the market price and does not exceed the rate at which the supplier has supplied the item to other places.
5) Pre/ post dispatch random sample inspection of the items to be repaired should be done to ensure that only items with proper and correct specifications are purchased.
6) Storage and distribution of the items to be purchased after purchase, supply chain management and inventory management should be done in a proper manner to ensure that these items do not remain unused and are used in a timely manner.
7) The expenditure should be met from the subsidy disbursed in the budget for the financial year 2020-21 from the targets of head No. 4210 1128, 52 Machinery and Equipment.
8) It should be ensured that there is no duplication of the purchase of these items.
9) The guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) should be followed in the entire procurement process.
10) Government Resolution, Finance Department, No.VYPR-2013/ P.No. 30/2013/ Regulations, Part-2, D. The conditions contained in Annexure 17.04.2015 to the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-Division-II, No. 4, Rule No. 7, Column No. 6 are to be satisfied.
The concerned Programme Head as well as the Directorate General of Health Services shall be fully responsible for fulfilling the conditions mentioned in the above Table 1 to 10.
