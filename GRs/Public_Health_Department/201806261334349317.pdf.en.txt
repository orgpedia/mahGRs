Introduction It is very important to protect, maintain and nurture the climate as well as the trees that have been planted so far. In front of the cabinet. It was held on June 13, 2017. As per the discussion held at that time, the government has taken an important decision to implement the concept of" Green Maharashtra" in order to take action in this regard and to reduce the inflammation and severity of natural disasters such as climate change, rising temperature, drought and water scarcity. Under the programme, a target of planting 50 crore trees has been set for the period 2017-2019. In addition, in 2016. A unique and ambitious programme of planting 2 crore trees on a single day on 1st July, 2016 has been implemented. The target is to plant 13 crore and 33 crore trees in 2018 and 2019 respectively. The goal of tree plantation should be fully achieved. As agreed by the Council of Ministers, the matter of issuing necessary instructions to the regional offices was under the consideration of the government. Accordingly, the following instructions have been issued:
Decision of the Government- 1. In the field level meetings at the Taluk, District and State level under the Public Health Department, the topics related to tree plantation and rearing should be included as a permanent topic in the agenda at the time of each meeting. In such meetings, tree plantation will be reviewed, the trees planted earlier will be kept alive.

Review should be done regarding the pre-preparations in terms of quantity, planting of new trees in place of dead trees, fixing of site for plantation as per the target for tree plantation in the coming years, availability of saplings, preparation of pits within the prescribed time as per the required technical parameters, availability of funds etc.
The decisions taken in such meetings regarding the tree plantation programme should be recorded in the minutes of the meeting. Further, the implementation of the decisions mentioned in the history and the difficulties encountered should be discussed in the next meeting and measures should be taken to solve the problems and difficulties faced by the field officers.
3. The officers at Taluka, District and State level who will visit the field works, a program should be organized to visit the tree plantation sites under those departments. During such field visits, senior officers should inspect whether the trees planted under the concerned administrative department are properly protected, whether new trees have been planted in place of dead trees, water arrangements and manpower for protection, etc. Also, you need to take steps to solve the problem.
4. Also inspect the sites selected for the proposed tree plantation, prepare pits of the required quantity in the prescribed time and arrange water and protection for the trees to be planted at such places, arrange funds for the tree plantation, and give necessary guidance to the field officers.
