The Proposal:
National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM). Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Rural Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The Government was contemplating to provide Central and State share to the State Health Societies from the pending funds of the year 2019-20 and the current financial year's funds allocated in the approved budget of 2020-21 for Tribal Appropriation (TSP) for the National Urban Health Mission.
