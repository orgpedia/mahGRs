Preamble: Under the Government Decision dated 28.10.2010, a tender committee was constituted under the chairmanship of the then Additional Chief Secretary to select the service provider for providing medical assistance to the victims of any emergency in the first golden hour and to provide the service of such ambulances equipped with the latest medical equipment and facilities to save their lives. The Scheme was implemented in the State from 2014 onwards as the tender committee had qualified the sole tender submitted by M/ s BVG India and issued the commissioning order under the Government decision dated 28.03.2013 for the operation of 937 ambulances of 233 Advance Life Supporting and 704 Basic Life Supporting across the State for the next five years. Thereafter, the five-year tenure of the appointed service provider has expired in January, 2019. The Union Cabinet in its meeting dated 11.12.2018 constituted a Committee under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Finance) to implement the scheme in the State. As per the final report dated 31.08.2019, the Committee has recommended extension of the service provider as per the original terms and conditions of the said agreement for a further period of 5 years from the date of termination of the said agreement. As President's rule was imposed at the time of taking the decision, Dharki was asked to approve the scheme.
