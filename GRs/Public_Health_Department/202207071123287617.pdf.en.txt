The Proposal:
As per the National Strategic Plan 2020-2025, India aims to be TB-free by 2025. Important steps need to be taken. For that it is important to detect all TB patients in the community, diagnose TB early and provide proper treatment. LTBI is a very important part of treatment to prevent TB in susceptible people. Latent TB Infection Management (LTBI) initiative is being implemented in the state of Maharashtra as per the guidelines of the Central Tuberculosis Cell. Accordingly, as per the guidelines received from the Central Government, the Government was considering the formation of a State Advisory Committee at the State level to implement the PMTPT activities under the National Tuberculosis Elimination Program.
Also, the State Technical Support Unit has been received in the State of Maharashtra through the Central Tuberculosis Cell under the World Bank TB Project to make India TB-free. As per the directions of the Central Government, the matter of forming a governing committee at the state level for the effective implementation of the State Technical Support Unit (State Technical Support Unit) was under the consideration of the Government.

The government has decided to set up a state-level advisory committee.
Guidelines for Programmatic Management of TB Preventive Treatment in India 2021 have been received from the Central Government. As per the guidelines, the Government is approving the constitution of State Advisory Committee and Governing Committee at the State level under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Public Health Department for implementation of PMTPT activities under National TB Elimination Programme.

the Working of the State Advisory Committee
To hold meetings initially every month and thereafter every quarter after the launch of Akshaya Plus activities in all the selected districts of the State.
To make necessary planning for effective and speedy implementation of Central Government guidelines on Prevention of Tuberculosis (PMTPT).
To prepare an action plan for the qualitative implementation of PMTPT in the state and to expand it to all health promotion centers, talukas and districts.
The action plan includes training of manpower, testing, treatment centres, digital devices, TB Prevention India Op, Drug Supply Chain Management, community support, etc.
Think about things.
Seeking grants in the project implementation framework for TB preventive treatment activities as per NHM/ NTEP guidelines.

Naming of health institutions to take care of beneficiaries eligible for PMTPT and conducting training on all services at district/ taluka level.
Determination of high prevalence of TB in the State. Prioritize eligible people from at-risk groups such as health care workers, prisons, mines, slums, tribals, migrants, etc.
To review the status of implementation of Prevention of Tuberculosis (PMTPT) and to address the related difficulties in implementation to ensure that the PMTPT policies and guidelines are being followed in the State.
It should be ensured that there is timely supply of testing, drugs and equipment related to the PMTPT initiative.
The Chairman of the Committee may invite specific members associated with the programme to the meeting as per the need for improvement of the programme.
The cost of the program meeting will be covered under the revised National Tuberculosis Control Program titled FRM Code 16.1.2.2.14 Supervision and Monitoring.
Travel allowance will be admissible to non-official committee members. The TA/ DA expenditure will be covered under the revised National Tuberculosis Control Programme as per NHM guidelines under the heading FRM Code 16.1.2.2.14 Supervision and Monitoring.
