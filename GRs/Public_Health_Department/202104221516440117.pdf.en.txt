The Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products (Prohibition of Trade and Commerce, Production, Supply and Distribution) Act, 2003 (COTPA Act, 2003) has been enacted to prohibit the use of tobacco. COTPA-2003 is applicable to all tobacco products and is applicable to the entire country. The National Tobacco Control Programme is being implemented in all the districts of the State since 2016-17 under the National Health Mission. Awareness is being spread through various media about the ill-effects of tobacco and tobacco consumption.
2. d. The Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products (Packaging and Labelling) Rules, notified vide GSR 182 (E) dated 15th March, 2008, have been amended from time to time to display specific health warnings on all tobacco product packs. It has been made mandatory to display 85% health warning warning on both sides of the packaging of tobacco and tobacco products. GSR 331 (E) dated 3rd April, 2018 has been amended in Aadhaar Rules, April, 2018.

Earlier, as per the Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products (Packaging and Labelling) Regulations, the health warnings on tobacco product packages were changed every 24 months. Now, the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare has made the third amendment to the Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products (Packaging and Labeling) Act.
There is a need to create awareness about the health effects of tobacco and tobacco products, especially among young children and uneducated people about the warning of specific health warnings.
The Central Government has notified new specified health warnings as per GSR 458 (E) of the Gazette Notification dated 21st July, 2020. Mandatory display of tobacco and tobacco products on the packaging of tobacco products covering at least 85% of the main display area. The new rules will come into effect from December 1, 2020.
