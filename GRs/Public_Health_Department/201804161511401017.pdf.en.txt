Introduction: Plague has not been reported in Maharashtra since 1953. The plague survey team was closed down by a government decision of November 26, 1987. However, the suspected plague was detected in the state in August/ September, 1994, in a village in Beed district. A total of 634 patients were found infected with the plague in all the districts of the state. Therefore, the Plague Control Committee was re-established on 3 October 1994. Since then, not a single case of coronavirus has been reported in the state. However, as a precautionary measure, the plague team is regularly checking rats and fleas in Beed, Latur, Osmanabad, Pune, Satara and Solapur districts.
