Preamble: National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April, 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM).
Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The proposal for providing Central and State share to the State Health Societies from the provision in the approved budget for 2020-21 was under consideration of the Government for the pending funds for 2019-20 for the Social Component (SCSP) for National Urban Health Mission.
