Preamble: The proposal of the Directorate General of Health Services to approve the estimated expenditure for the repair of the vehicle used by the Joint Secretary, Public Health Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai was under consideration of the Government. A decision in this regard is being taken.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The vehicle used by Joint Secretary, Public Health Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai No. MH 01 SA 7799 (Honda City) Administrative Sanction is being accorded for expenditure to the tune of Rs. 1,13,557/- subject to the following conditions:
1) The vehicle must be used only within the authority and jurisdiction certified by the Directorate General of Health Services or the competent authorities of the State Health Transport Organization.
2) The vehicle must be used by the same officer to whom the vehicle is provided. Also, it should not be used anywhere other than the area to which it is given.
(3) The department or institution to which the vehicle is supplied and the head office/ department of the institution shall be responsible for paying the risk and liability of legal claims made by the third party in case of accident, theft, etc. of the vehicle in the case of the institutions to which the vehicle is supplied. The Health Department on behalf of the Government of Maharashtra will not pay any liability in this regard.

4) The vehicle owner will always be responsible for protecting the capital invested by the health department from fire, flood, earthquake, accident, theft, arson, social riots, natural or human disaster, etc. In case of damage to the vehicle due to this reason, the vehicle holder (aided institutions and their respective head offices/ departments) shall be liable to pay to the Health Department the value of the vehicle at that time minus the depreciation value.
In case of damage to the vehicle due to the reasons mentioned in item No. 4 above, it will be obligatory for those organizations to pay compensation in the amount to be determined by the Directorate General of Health Services/ State Health Transport Organization.
The vehicle holder should not use his/ her vehicle for the implementation of any programme other than health services without prior written permission of the Directorate of Health Services/ State Health Transport Organizations.
In exceptional circumstances, for example. There is no problem in allowing the vehicle to be used for other purposes as per the orders of the Collector during elections, emergency situations, etc. However, in such cases, the permission of the Directorate General of Health Services or the State Health Transport Association is required.
7) In the event of a vehicle accident, the drivers are required to report it to the police inspector of the concerned police station and make a panchnama about it.
8) The details of the repair of the vehicle and the expenses incurred on it, as well as the details of the tyres, tubes, batteries, etc. installed in the vehicle should be written in the vehicle history book from time to time.
9) The above instructions should be strictly followed by all concerned, otherwise disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned by fixing responsibility on them.
10) The cost should be appropriately covered.
The expenditure will be covered under the head Demand No.R-1, 2210-Medical Services and Public Health, (01) Mofussil Hospitals and Dispensaries, (01) (01)-Non-Teaching Hospitals in Mofussil Areas (Compulsory), 51-Motor Vehicles (2210 0146) for the financial year 2018-19.
