Government Resolution No. 1: General Administration Department No. LPL-2017/ No. 21/ K.P. 10.06.2019 2) Government Decision No: Public Health Department No.Committee-2020/ No.27/ Service-2, Dy. 20.02.2020 3) Letter from the Director, Health Services, Mumbai No. 16.07.2020 Preamble: As per Rule 10 (4)& 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, in public interest, it is the policy of the Government to review the services of officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, and retire them prematurely from Government service. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 01 here. The Government Decision dated 10.06.2019 prescribes the procedure and general instructions regarding service review. Accordingly, for the review of services of District Surgeons, District Health Officers, Specialists and above cadre officers in Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-23: 67700-208700 in the Pay Matrix) under the Commissionerate of Health Services, Reference No. 02 here. As per the Government decision dated 20.02.2020, a Departmental Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary, Public Health Department.
As per the proposal, the Government was considering to allow the officers of the cadre of Deputy Director in the Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-25: 78800-209200 in the Pay Matrix) to continue their service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, subject to review of their services at the prescribed age.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The officers nominated in the attached form in the cadre of Deputy Director, Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-25: 78800-209200 in the Pay Matrix) under the Commissionerate of Health Services are being approved to continue their service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
The service book of the officers mentioned in the attached form should be taken on record that they have been approved to continue the service after their 50/55 years of age or after completing 30 years of qualifying service.
