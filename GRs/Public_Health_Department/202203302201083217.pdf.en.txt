The Proposal:
National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM).
Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. Under the National Health Mission (NHM), the Government of India has released Rs. 4452.88 lacs to the corresponding State Account under the head 2210G609. 2968.59 lakh is required to be made available to the State Health Societies. Hence, the Rs. 2968.59 lakh was under consideration of the Government to be provided to the State Health Societies by disbursing from the budgetary provision in 2021-22. The details of grants disbursed so far in this case are as follows.
