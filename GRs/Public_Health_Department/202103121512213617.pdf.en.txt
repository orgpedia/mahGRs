Government Resolution No. 1: General Administration Department No. LPL-2017/ No. 21/ K.P. 10.06.2019 2) Government Decision No: Public Health Department No.Committee-2020/ No.27/ Service-2, Dy. 20.02.2020 3) of Director, Health Services, Mumbai vide letter No. Vacancies/ Cell-1/ TE-2/ (PR)/ S.P.P./ Deputy Director Promotion Proposal/ 3213/2020, Dy. 04.12.2020 Preamble: As per Rule 10 (4)& 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, in public interest, it is the policy of the Government to review the services of officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, and retire them from Government service prematurely. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 01 here. The Government Decision dated 10.06.2019 prescribes the procedure and general instructions regarding service review. Accordingly, for reviewing the services of the officers of the cadre of District Surgeon, District Health Officer and Specialist in the Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-23: 67700-208700 in the Pay Matrix) within the Commissionerate of Health Services, Reference No. 02 here. As per the Government decision dated 20.02.2020, a Departmental Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Public Health Department.
The Departmental Review Committee under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Public Health Department, has recommended for review of the services of District Surgeons, District Health Officers and Specialists in the cadre of Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-23: 67700-208700 in the Pay Matrix) at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. Presented in the meeting dated 11.01.2021. As per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, which evaluated the service of the said officers, it was under the consideration of the Government to approve the officers who have been found eligible to remain in service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
