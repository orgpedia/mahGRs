The mandate of the Committee: (1) The Committee shall study the matter in detail in the light of the complaint made by Smt. Shumbhagi Bhostekar.
(2) To study the Regulation of Surrogacy Bill 2016 introduced by the Central Government in Parliament and make recommendations on the procedure to be followed in the State till the Bill is finalised and instructions are received from the Central Government regarding its implementation.
3) To study the guidelines on Assisted Reproductive Technology issued by the Indian Council of Medical Research and to determine the guidelines for its implementation in the State.
4) To make guidelines for registration/ licensing of Assisted Reproductive Technology Centres in the State and for care and maintenance of surrogate mothers and children.
(vi) Formulation of procedure for registration of surrogate mothers and their spouses, seed and sperm donors in Assisted Reproductive Technology Centres/ Dispensaries.
6) Creation of Grievance Cell at Divisional level.
The committee will submit its report to the state government in three working days.
Travel allowance and daily allowance will be admissible to the members of the committee as per the government rules.
The reimbursement of the expenditure will be made at the level of Additional Director Health Services (Family Welfare) from the funds available at the state level of registration fee under the PCPNDT Act.
