The Proposal:
Accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable healthcare for rural population
In order to provide services, the Central Government has launched the National Rural Health Mission from April, 2005 across the country. Under this scheme, health services like Reproductive and Child Health Programme, regular immunization, Mission Flexipool, strengthening of infrastructure and pulse polio vaccination etc. are provided. Accordingly, under the National Health Mission, a proposal was under consideration of the Government to provide 60% Central share and 40% State share grant to the State Health Societies for Socially Specialized Component (SCSP) for National Rural Health Programme.
Government's decision:
Provision made in the Budget Estimates 2020-21 for Socially Special Component (SCSP) beneficiaries under National Rural Health Mission (NRHM)

The Programme Head/ Director (Finance& Accounts), National Health Mission, Mumbai has informed the Government that the conditions from 1 to 10 have been fulfilled and has submitted a certificate regarding the same. Therefore, in this case, in the Annexure to the Government Circular dated 16th April, 2020 of the Finance Department, Issue No. Conditions 1 to 10 are being certified as fulfilled. There are no serious objections raised in the audit of the State Health Society in respect of the funds disbursed earlier. The money will be directly transferred to the bank account of the beneficiary. The proposal does not include any proposal for purchase of goods or capital goods. There is no short payment pending for one year under the respective head of accounts of these programmes. As per the policy of the Government, the funds on this proposal are being spent by depositing them in a separate bank account of the State Health Society, and also to submit necessary certificates in this regard in compliance with all the relevant provisions/ terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated April 16, 2020 of the Finance Department or to certify the necessary matters separately to the State Health Society.
The head of the programme will be responsible for the mechanism through which the funds will be spent.
The grant is conditional and will be disbursed only after submission of Utilization Certificate (UC) of the aforesaid fund as per Government Circular No. 2013/ PR No. 85/2013/ Budget-3, dated 25th April, 2013 of the Finance Department.
Administrative approval should be obtained for the purchase to be made where necessary in accordance with the provisions of the Government Decision, Department of Industry, Energy and Labour, dated December 1, 2016.
