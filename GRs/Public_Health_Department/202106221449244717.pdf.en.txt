Terms and Conditions:
1) The detailed budget and plans of the work should be approved by the concerned Chief Engineer, who is the highest technical officer at the field level.
2) Elevation should be improved in terms of ventilation by using natural resources in the building.
3) While preparing the detailed budget, the Goods and Services Tax (GST) should be levied at the rate applicable.
(4) The work shall be subject to the technical approval of the competent authority of the Public Works Department before inviting tenders for the work.
5) The provisions of the works should be shown in detail and the rates for the same should be submitted to the government by submitting the break-up.
(6) A single tender shall be issued for all the works mentioned in the tender without separate tenders for each work.
7) As it is possible to increase the cost of the proposed work according to the situation of the area and the requirements of the health institutions, the necessary proposals for this should be made by the area officers of the Public Works Department.
8) As the said Budget is a broad model Budget, the detailed estimates of these works shall be technically approved by the competent authorities of the Public Works Department in their respective areas. While granting such technical approval, the provisions should be finalised with the concurrence of the Deputy Directors of the respective Health Service Boards of the Health Department.
9) If the work is to be done according to the new model maps, the architectural plan should be approved by the private architect appointed by the Public Health Department. The newly appointed architect and district surgeon will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the rules of the local rural development agency. Also, the letter requesting this permission must be signed by the district surgeon.
The maps and architectural plans of these works do not require separate approval from the Chief/ Deputy Chief Architect of the Public Works Department.
11) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV-2013/ No. 177/ T.C.1d. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014. Be sure to complete the above tasks.
