Preamble: As per the recommendations of the 13th Finance Commission, the Central Government has allocated Rs. 367.00 crore has been received by the State Government. To reduce the infant mortality rate, work related to equipment, machines, repair of delivery rooms and water supply to health institutions will be done. A high-level committee, headed by the Chief Secretary, has approved the action plan. Based on the data on infant mortality, the geographical focus has been fixed considering the places in the state where the infant mortality rate is high and the process of procuring the necessary equipment and equipment is underway to reduce the infant mortality rate rapidly. Accordingly, under the Government decision dated 28.12.2021, an amount of Rs. Administrative approval was accorded to the tender process for an amount of Rs. 2703.67 lakhs. Equipment and materials have been purchased. However, it was not possible to purchase the Eicher truck (32 cubic metres) as the rate was higher than the administrative approval rate (10 per cent). Department of Industry, Energy and Labor Government Decision No. Bhankhas 2014/ No.82/ Part- 3.1.2.1 dated 1st December, 2016 provides for obtaining approval of a higher authority by the concerned procuring department in case of a deviation of 10% from the rate of administrative approval. In pursuance of this provision, the matter of granting revised administrative approval to the tender process for the purchase of Eicher Truck (27.83 CuM) MS Close Container submitted by the Director (2), Health Services, Commissionerate of Health Services, Pune was under consideration of the Government.

The revised administrative approval is being granted subject to the following conditions: (1) The need/ demand for the said medicinal and material materials should be ascertained. Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
2) It should be ensured that the specifications of the said medicines and materials are as per the requirement and up to date. The technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. The responsibility of sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the items approved in the PIP will be of the concerned programme head. Only the items included in the proposal should be purchased.

4) The rates of the proposed procurement are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. Therefore, the minimum prices available on the GeM Portal, other state tariffs, National Pharmaceutical Pricing Authority (NPPA), National Medical Devices Authority (NDMA) should be considered.
