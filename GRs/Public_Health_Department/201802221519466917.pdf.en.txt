Introduction: Many pregnant women in India, both below poverty line and above poverty line, are forced to work for wages till the last stage of pregnancy and even after delivery, they are forced to work for wages even when they do not have the physical capacity. This affects the health of the newborn and the mother. This has led to a rise in maternal and infant mortality rates. Therefore, in order to improve the health of mothers and children by encouraging pregnant women and lactating mothers to take nutritious food, to improve their health and to improve the health of newborns and to reduce and control the maternal and child mortality rate, the Ministry of Women and Child Development, Government of India has approved the implementation of a new Centrally Sponsored Scheme Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana (PMMV) in the entire State with effect from 01.01.2017.
