The Proposal:
The work of upgradation of 100 bedded Sub District Hospital at Achalpur G. Amaravathi to 200 bedded General Hospital has been sanctioned at a cost of Rs. A proposal to give administrative approval to the budget and maps of the work amounting to Rs. 55.49 crore was under consideration of the Government.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 2. The budget for upgradation of 100 bedded sub-district hospital at Achalpur G. Amravati to 200 bedded general hospital is based on the 2018-19 tariff list. The said budget has been prepared by the Executive Engineer, Public Works Sub-Division, Achalpur. The Budget has made provision for 4 per cent of contingency expenditure, 5 per cent of green building and 12 per cent of Goods and Services Tax. Administrative approval is being accorded under this Government Resolution to the projects and schemes with a budget of Rs. 55.49 crore subject to the following conditions:
Terms and Conditions: 1) Technical approval of the competent authority of the Public Works Department will be required before inviting tenders for the work.

2) Before starting the construction, the map should be approved by the architect, S.B. Department Nashik.
(3) A single tender shall be issued for all the works mentioned in the tender without separate tenders for each work.
4) As the cost of the proposed work may increase according to the situation of the area and the requirements of the health institutions, the necessary proposals should be made by the area officers of the Public Works Department.
(5) As the said budget is a rough model budget, the detailed budgets of these works shall be technically approved by the competent authorities of the Public Works Department in their respective areas. While giving such technical approval, the provisions should be finalised with the concurrence of the Deputy Director of Health Services Board in the Department of Health.
6) If the work is to be done according to the new model maps, the architectural plan should be approved by the private architect appointed by the Public Health Department. The newly appointed architect and district surgeon will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the rules of the local rural development agency. Also, the letter requesting this permission must be signed by the district surgeon.
The maps and architectural plans of these works do not require separate approval from the Chief/ Deputy Chief Architect of the Public Works Department.
8) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV 2013/ Q.No.177/ T.C.1D. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014.
9) Ensure that the above tasks are completed within this amount.
3. Expenditure on upgradation of 100 bedded sub-district hospital at Achalpur G. Amravati to 200 bedded general hospital should be covered under Demand No. H-8, Head of Accounts 4210- Capital expenditure on medical services and public health services, 01- Civil Health Services (Medical Assistance)- Scheme Buildings under Five Year Plans, 110- Hospitals and Dispensaries, (1) (a) Scheme Buildings under Five Year Plans, (00) (01) Major Constructions (42100014), 53- Major Constructions.
4. The Director, Health Services, Mumbai should immediately submit a proposal to the Government to allocate funds in the budget for the upgradation of the 100-bed sub-district hospital at Achalpur G. Amravati to a 200-bed general hospital.
