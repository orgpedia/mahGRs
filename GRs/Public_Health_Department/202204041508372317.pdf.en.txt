Introduction: The above mentioned reference No. As per the Government decision dated 05.08.2020, it was approved to provide CT Scan Services (32 Hospitals) and MRI Services (23 Hospitals) in selected health institutions in the State through an external service provider. The tendering process is underway. Now, as per the proposal of the Commissionerate dated 16.02.2022, the hospital which currently has MRI Scan service and CT Scan service owned by the department has also been included in it. As per the new proposal, there are 22 new places for MRI services and 32 new places for CT scan services and 31 places for the old CT scan machines owned by the government. The decision was taken in the meeting of the Tender Drafting Committee in 3 and it has been proposed to give revised administrative approval for the same.

Accordingly, the matter of obtaining administrative approval for the tender process to provide CT Scan services and MRI services under Free Radiological Services in the health institutions of the state from the grant received under the National Health Mission was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
As proposed by the Commissionerate of Health Services, the Mission Director, National Health, Mumbai and the concerned Programme Officer of the Mission will be responsible for the availability of funds from the Central Government by proposing adequate provision in the action plan for the tender process for selecting service providers through external mechanism for CT Scan Services at 31 old and 32 new government owned health institutions and MRI Services at 22 new places in the state. Step 2: The procedure is as follows:
2.1 MRI Scan Services
. The bidders while paying the rate for MRI Scan Services will also be required to pay the rate for the CT Scan Machines currently owned by the Department. The MRI CT ratio should be 1:2.
(ii) Private patients should be charged at CGHS rates for MRI services.
(iii) The tenderer shall not be allowed to take private patients for examination at the place where the CT scan machine owned by the department is kept.
(iv) As per the Government Decision No. 1220/ PRASHAMA-10/ HEALTH, dated 05.08.2020, the successful service provider will be expected to enter into an agreement as per the terms and conditions of the tender document for the next 5+ 2 i.e. 7 years. After that, if the third party evaluation report is satisfactory, it will be further extended with the approval of the government for 3 years.
2.2 CT scan services.
Where the CT scan machines are owned by the Department, the per patient rate permissible to the tenderer shall be 50% of the minimum rate of 1.
(ii) Private patients should be charged at CGHS rate for CT scan services.
(iii) The tenderer shall not be allowed to take private patients for examination at the place where the CT scan machine owned by the department is kept.
