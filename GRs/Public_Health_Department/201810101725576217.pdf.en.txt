(2) The supervisory officers shall strictly comply with the provisions of the Government Order for the appearance of the transferred medical officers at the place of their transfer. In case of non-compliance, the concerned Deputy Director, District Surgeon, District Health Officer, Medical Superintendent will be held personally responsible.
3) In the said transfer order, the medical officers will be replaced by the medical officers who have been absent for a long time to fill the vacancies of medical officers and who have resigned.
change is displayed. The medical officers should be immediately posted at the place of posting and necessary proposal regarding the medical officers who have resigned or are in unauthorised absence for a long time should be submitted in a prescribed manner through the Director, Health Services.
4) No leave of any kind shall be granted to the transferred medical officers, otherwise the responsibility will be fixed on the authority granting leave.
The transfer order is likely to result in double posting of some medical officers.
Regarding that. The concerned Deputy Director shall take action as per the directions in the Government Letter No. 2017/ PR 538 (Part-II)/ Service-3 dated 12.03.2018.
6) The transferred medical officers should join the new place of posting after being relieved. All concerned are being made aware that the absence of the Medical Officers who do not attend the place of posting and remain mutually absent will be treated as" unauthorised absence" and the possibility of interruption in their service cannot be ruled out.
7) The Directorate of Health Services should initiate action against the medical officers who do not appear at the place of their posting. So that the medical officers will reach the new place of posting on time. The concerned Supervisory Officer and Deputy Director Health Services (Circle) will be responsible for relieving the concerned officers within one week from the date of issue of this order.
8) Medical officers who have been posted in place of medical officers who have been unauthorisedly absent for a long time should be made to join and for a long time unauthorised.
