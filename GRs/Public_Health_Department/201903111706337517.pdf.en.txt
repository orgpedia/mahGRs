Regarding the formation of a committee to change the formula of the auxiliary grant being given to Nowrosjee Wadia Maternity Hospital, Parel, Mumbai.
The Government of Maharashtra
the Department of Public Health

Gokuldas Tejpal Hospital, 10th Floor
Ministry, Mumbai- 400001.
The date: March 8, 2019.
Preamble: The Trust Deed Agreement dated 27.07.1926 has been signed between the then Government of Bombay, the Municipal Corporation of Bombay and Mr. Wadia. The massive expansion of the Nauroji Wadia Hospital has led to a huge increase in the cost of the hospital, which is burdening the Mumbai Municipal Corporation and the government.
Therefore, it was under consideration of the government to change the formula of grant-in-aid to be given to Nowrosjee Wadia Maternity Hospital and the agreement with Nowrosjee Wadia Hospital.
In the light of the above facts, a committee is being constituted to review the agreement signed between the then Government of Mumbai, the Mumbai Municipal Corporation and Mr. Wadia in 1926 and to change the formula of grant-in-aid to Wadia Maternity Hospital.
Commissioner, Mumbai Municipal Corporation Committee Chairman 2) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary, Finance Department: Member 3) Principal Secretary, Public Health Department: Member 4) Joint Secretary/ Deputy Secretary, Law and Justice Department: Member 5) Joint Director (Finance and Administration), Health Services, Mumbai: Member 6) Dean, KEM Hospital, Parel, Mumbai: Member 7) Deputy Director, Health Services, Mumbai Circle, Thane: Member Secretary

The functions of the Committee shall be as follows: 1) To examine the trust deed dated 1926 between the then Government of Bombay, the Municipal Corporation of Bombay and Mr. Wadia and to determine the responsibilities of the Government and the Municipal Corporation by changing the formula of grant-in-aid to the hospital and to prepare a draft of a new agreement by negotiating accordingly.
(ii) In doing so, the minimum/ maximum limit of funds to be provided to Wadia Hospital shall be determined by taking into account the number of beds in the Government Hospital, the available staff, the facilities provided to the patients and the funds provided by the Government for the same.
The Committee will submit its final report within a period of two months.
