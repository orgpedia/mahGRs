The Proposal:
The National Viral Hepatitis Control Programme (NVHCP) under the National Health Mission has been launched in 35 districts of the state. Under this programme, 9 Model Treatment Centres (MTCs) and 27 Treatment Centres (TCs) have been set up in the state. One of the objectives of the programme is to prevent the outbreak of Hepatitis by screening and treating all patients with Hepatitis B and Hepatitis C in all the treatment centres.
In the approved Project Implementation Plan for 2020-21, under NVHCP PIP FMR Code 6.2.23.2, Rs. 349.03 lakh (HBsAg and Anti HCV tests will be procured centrally and supplied to the state). Accordingly, vide Government Decision dated 02.02.2021, the tender process for procurement of Rapid Kits for testing of Hepatitis B and Hepatitis C was accorded administrative approval. Accordingly, Rapid Kits for Hepatitis C have been purchased. But the rate of Hepatitis B rapid kits is higher than the administrative approval rate (Rs. 6/- each) 10% (Rs. 7.75 per kg) could not be purchased. 3.1.2.1 dated 1st December, 2016 of the Department of Industry, Energy and Labour provides for obtaining approval of a higher authority by the concerned procuring department in case of a deviation of 10% from the rate of administrative approval. In pursuance of this provision, the matter of granting revised administrative approval to the tender process for the purchase of Rapid Kits of Hepatitis B submitted by the Joint Director (Hospitals), Commissionerate of Health Services, Mumbai was under consideration of the Government.
