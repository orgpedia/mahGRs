Preamble: The Central Government has converted Primary Health Centres (PHCs) and Sub-Centres into Arogyavardhini Kendras under Ayushman Bharat Scheme to address the issues in health services in the State. The first point of contact is expected to provide a variety of preventive, promotive, curative, rehabilitative and palliative care health services to the people in the jurisdiction of such Arogyavardhini Kendra. The Arogyavardhini Kendra is expected to play an important role in public health delivery and focus on collective community action for social and environmental factors affecting health, supporting the process of social responsibility and collective response.

The Welfare Committee is a registered body and follows the principles of decentralization and devolution of administrative and financial powers for the management of health services. The patient welfare committee comprises representatives of panchayat raj institutions, voluntary organisations, eminent persons and officials from the government sector, including health. The patient welfare committee has the right to generate and utilize funds on facilities. Also, under the National Health Mission, funds are provided to the Patient Welfare Committees at various levels.
Under the Ayushman Bharat scheme, the government was considering changing the name of the Patient Welfare Committee at the Primary Health Center (PHC) level to Jan Arogya Samiti (JAS) by converting the Primary Health Center (PHC) and Sub-Center into Arogyavardhini Center, and improving the structure and functioning of the said committee and creating a Jan Arogya Samiti (JAS) instead of the Sub-Center Level Strengthening Committee.
Government's decision:
As per the guidelines of the Central Government, Primary Health Centres (PHCs) and Sub-Centres have been converted into Arogyavardhini Kendras under the Ayushman Bharat Yojana to address the problems in health services in the state. Accordingly, the Government hereby approves the constitution and functioning of the Patient Welfare Committee functioning in the Primary Health Centres and renaming it as Jan Arogya Samiti by changing the name of Patient Welfare Committee and creation of Jan Arogya Samiti in place of the existing Strengthening Committee functioning at the Sub-Centre level.
Further, the composition and functioning of the Public Health Committees at the Primary Health Centre and Sub-Centre level are approved as follows: Objectives of the Public Health Committees: (1) To create an institutional platform for administrative management and responsibilities of health care facilities, social responsibility and community participation.
(2) To work with the Village Health, Nutrition, Water Supply and Sanitation Committee (VHNSC) on various national health related programmes, health promotion, social environmental determinants and to provide appropriate support to the Arogya Vardhini team.
(3) To ensure proper utilization and management of the Untied Grants available and to act as an umbrella to coordinate with the health system.
(4) To promote and facilitate follow up of patients of different age groups at the community level for treatment and laboratory tests.
(5) To ensure the participation of organized volunteers (NSS, NCC, RED CROSS, SCOUTS, YUVA Group) in the field for various treatment, support, survey, etc. at the community level.

General Instructions for the above Primary Health Centre and Sub-Centre level Committees: The term of office of all general members shall be for a minimum period of three years. All members of the Committee shall be represented on the Committee.
Other eminent members include Village Health, Nutrition, Water Supply and Sanitation Committee (VHNSC) Chairpersons/ Members but if their term has expired, such members cannot claim the post of member in the Public Health Committee.
A member of the public health committee should be elected in the gram panchayat meeting and the proposal should be submitted for the approval of the district health society.
In the selection of the members of the Public Health Committee, a total of 1/3 rd representatives should be elected from all the habitations/ PADA (SC/ ST) groups in the sub-centre area, in which at least 50% representation should be women.
Formation/ composition/ role of Jan Arogya Samiti (JAHC) Publicizing all the information about the phone numbers of the members etc. in the front of the Anganwadi/ School/ Government offices in the sub-centre area.
Role and Responsibilities of the Jan Arogya Samiti: 1) To assist, assist and support the Ayushman Bharat Arogya Vardhini Kendra in ensuring the provision and accountability of quality health services for all.
2) To ensure that the list of health services provided at Ayushman Bharat Arogya Vardhini Kendras are as per the Citizen's Charter.
3) To identify preventive and promotive health care services being provided in Arogyavardhini Primary Health Centres and Sub Centres under Ayushman Bharat Scheme. Services such as non-communicable diseases, critical illnesses, vision, ear, nose, etc. will continue to be available. Also to ensure that camps are organized for pregnant women, lactating women, young children, adolescents; and yoga wellness.
4) Ensure that Arogya Vardhini Kendras have safe drinking water, quality nutritious food, garbage free surroundings, clean toilets, clean linen, clean waiting rooms, proper security arrangements, proper disposal and maintenance of bio-medical waste and regular waste.
5) Ensure availability of essential medicines and diagnostic kits at the Arogya Vardhini Kendra level.
6) Arogyavardhini Kendras will have a friendly atmosphere and cultural encouragement. The corrective mechanism should be responsive through training/ direction/ sensitization.
7) To ensure that no fee is charged for any health services provided at the Arogyavardhini Kendra.
8) To ensure that the poor and vulnerable sections of the society do not face any difficulty in accessing health services at the Arogyavardhini Kendra level and are not denied services at the time of their visit.

To promote redressal of grievances received from the public in accordance with social responsibility.
Meetings of the Public Health Committee:
The Public Health Committee shall hold at least 12 meetings per year on a fixed date.
The Member Secretary of the Public Health Committee shall convene the monthly meeting. The date, time, etc. of the day of the meeting shall be communicated to all the distinguished members at least seven days in advance along with the draft agenda. Ensure that clear information about the meeting is communicated to each member.
The quorum required for the meeting shall be 50% of the members of the Committee present.
If there is not enough quorum to start a meeting of the Public Health Committee, the meeting shall be adjourned and the meeting shall be held on the same day by taking out a notice sheet according to the time convenient to the other members to fill the quorum.
