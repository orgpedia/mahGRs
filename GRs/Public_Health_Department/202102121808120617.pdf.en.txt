The Proposal:
National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM). Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Rural Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The Government was considering to provide Central and State share to State Health Societies from the provision in the approved budget for 2020-21 for Tribal Appropriation (TSP) for National Rural Health Mission.
Government's decision:
The Central Share Account for the financial year 2020-21 has been increased from Rs. 2210F738 to Rs. 2210F738 for disbursement of Central and State share from Tribal Subsidy (TSP) for National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) under NHM. 17239.32 Lakhs and State share 2210E455 Ors.
