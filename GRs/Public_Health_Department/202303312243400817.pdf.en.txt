Proposal- In the state. As per the Government decision dated 26.02.2019, it has been decided to integrate the revised Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana with Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana. The Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana, which is being implemented by the state government, is applicable to families in Maharashtra. However, residents of 865 Marathi-speaking villages in the Maharashtra-Karnataka border areas claimed by the Maharashtra government are demanding implementation of the Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana. List of 865 Villages in the State of Karnataka, which have been given the rights in the Maharashtra-Karnataka border area by the Government of Maharashtra It is dated July 10, 2008. Considering all these aspects, it was proposed to partially amend the scheme approved by the Cabinet earlier. Accordingly, the government was considering to include the residents of Marathi-speaking villages in the 865 villages claimed by the Maharashtra government in the Maharashtra-Karnataka border area in the existing Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana.
The Government has decided to include the beneficiaries of the Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana in 865 villages of 12 tehsils of Belgaum, Karwar, Kalaburagi and Bidar districts, claimed by the Government of Maharashtra in the Maharashtra-Karnataka border areas, under 1) Antyodaya Anna Yojana, 2) priority group families and 3) Annapurna ration card holders.
2. The benefit of the scheme is being given to the above families on a guaranteed basis.

In case of any change in the existing Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana in future and any other change in the criteria of the beneficiaries, the changes will be applicable to the beneficiary families of 865 villages claimed by the Government of Maharashtra in the Maharashtra-Karnataka border area.
The ration cards issued by the competent authority in the State of Karnataka will be examined to check the eligibility of beneficiary families in 865 villages claimed by the Government of Maharashtra in the Maharashtra-Karnataka border area to be given the benefit of Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana. The identity of the person will be ascertained through Aadhaar card and in case of non-Aadhaar card, 14 identity cards approved by the original scheme. Accommodation will be ensured through ration card and Aadhaar card. Also, the self-declaration form of the family member (as attached) will be mandatory as per the draft prescribed by the State Health Guarantee Society.

The families of 865 villages in the said border area are being allowed to avail the benefits of Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana from any government/ private empanelled hospitals in the state of Maharashtra (including KLE Hospital in Belgaum and Government Medical College Hospital in Panaji).
Similarly, in addition to the existing 1000 hospitals empanelled under Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana, it is being approved to empanel an additional 140 hospitals in 8 districts of Maharashtra adjoining Karnataka and 10 Marathi-speaking hospitals in Marathi-speaking districts of Belgaum, Karwar, Kalaburagi and Bidar bordering Maharashtra-Karnataka.
The modalities for empanelment of additional 150 hospitals will be as follows:
Additional insurance premium will be decided in consultation with the concerned insurance company for appointment of additional 150 hospitals.
The claims of the eligible beneficiary families of Maharashtra who are coming to the additional 150 hospitals will be paid through the insurance company and the claims of the eligible beneficiary families of Karnataka will be paid through the State Health Guarantee Society on a guaranteed basis.
The claims of Ayushman card beneficiary families in Maharashtra who come to the additional 150 hospitals will be paid through the insurance company and the State Health Guarantee Society on a guaranteed basis as per the prevailing practice. All claims of Ayushman card beneficiary families in Karnataka will be paid through the State Health Guarantee Society on a guaranteed basis.
The amount of expenditure for this will be made available by the State Government under the general head of the existing Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana.
