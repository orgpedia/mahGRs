Preamble: Accessible, Affordable, Efficient, Accountable and Reliable Health Care to Rural Population
The Government of India has launched the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) from April, 2005 across the country. Under this scheme, health services like Reproductive and Child Health Programme, regular immunization, Mission Flexipool, strengthening of infrastructure and pulse polio vaccination etc. are provided. Accordingly, under the National Health Mission, the Government was considering to provide 60% Central share of grants to the State Health Societies from the General category for National Rural Health, National Urban Health and other Health Programmes.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Under the National Health Mission (NHM), for disbursement of 60% Central share under General Category (G) for National Rural Health, National Urban Health and other Health Programmes in 2022-23, an amount of Rs. 222173.69 lakh has been budgeted. Out of this provision, the pending amount for the year 2021-22 has been increased to Rs. 2373.00 lakh has been disbursed to State Health Societies. Now the remaining Rs. An amount of Rs. 50159.00 lakh is hereby approved for disbursement to State Health Societies.
