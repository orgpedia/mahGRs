As per Rule 10 (4) and 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, it is the policy of the Government to review the services of officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity in the public interest at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, and retire them from Government service prematurely. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 01 here. The Government Decision dated 10.06.2019 prescribes the procedure and general instructions regarding service review. Accordingly, for re-evaluation of the services of officers in the cadre of Medical Officer in Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-20: 56100-177500 in the Pay Matrix) within the Commissionerate of Health Services, Reference No. 02 here. As per the Government decision dated 02.11.2021, a Departmental Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Public Health Department.
The proposal was submitted to the Departmental Review Committee under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary, Public Health Department in a circular format for reviewing the services of Medical Officers in the cadre of Maharashtra Medical and Health Services, Group-A (Pay Level S-20: 56100-177500 in the Pay Matrix) at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. Officers who are in the age group of 50/55 years or 30 years as per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee after evaluating the service of the said officers

GOVERNMENT DECISION: In accordance with the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, the Medical Officers in the cadre of Group-A (Pay Level S-20: 56100-177500 in the Pay Matrix) of the Maharashtra Medical and Health Services under the Commissionerate of Health Services are being approved to continue their service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
The service book of the officers mentioned in the attached form should be taken on record that they have been approved to continue the service after their 50/55 years of age or after completing 30 years of qualifying service.
