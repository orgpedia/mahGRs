The Proposal:
Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, New Delhi. As per the National Health Policy 2017 vide letter dated 27.07.2017, it has been prescribed that the nurse in the nursing cadre should be a graduate (B.Sc Nursing). As per the earlier norms, the minimum educational qualification was a General Nursing Midwifery (GNM) diploma and a three-year course after 12th Science. The Indian Nursing Council (INC) has decided to discontinue all GNM courses from 2022.
However, due to the Covid pandemic, the grading of GNM course to BSc Nursing has been allowed in a phased manner. In the first phase, the Central Government has sanctioned Rs.6 crore each for upgradation of Nursing Training Centres in Sindhudurg, Nashik and Satara districts and out of this fund, Rs. 3,20,50, 000/- (Rupees Three Crore Twenty Lakh Fifty Thousand only) has been transferred to the bank accounts of each institution for augmentation or modification of existing structures. Accordingly, the Deputy Director Health Services (Nursing), Mumbai, has issued the technical approval of the Executive Engineer, Public Works Department, Satara. The proposal has been submitted to the government in accordance with the letter mentioned in 01. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the cost of change required for upgrading the existing building of Nursing Training Center Satara was under the consideration of the government. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
The Executive Engineer, Public Works Department, has been entrusted with the task of carrying out necessary alterations/ construction and conversion of the existing building of the Nursing Training Centre for upgrading the attached Nursing Training Centre of the District Hospital, Satara into a B.Sc Nursing College.
