Reference No.- As per the Government decision mentioned in para 1, a committee was constituted to fix the rates of RTPCR tests for COVID-19 by the Indian Institute of Medical Sciences and NABL accredited private laboratories. The committee had submitted a report to the government regarding the rates to be charged for RTPCR testing by private laboratories in consultation with private laboratories, considering the limited resource availability in the then lockdown situation. According to reference no. The revised maximum rates of RT-PCR tests were fixed in accordance with the Government Decision dated 24.10.2020 superseding the rates fixed under Government Decisions dated 13.06.2020, 04.07.2020, 07.09.2020 and 24.10.2020.

Under Mission Begin Again, industrial/ pharmaceutical institutions have been started in the state.
Transport has also started. Reagents, VTM kits and PPE kits, RNA extraction kits required for testing are available at very affordable prices on GeM portal.
The maximum price of N95 masks is Rs. 14/- to Rs. 49/- has been fixed.
With ICMR approving various manufacturing companies, the availability has increased. Also, the cost of transportation has come down. As a result, the cost of testing for private laboratories has come down. The number of labs accredited by ICMR and NABL has also increased significantly in the state. Keeping all these factors in mind, it was necessary to reduce the rate of RTPCR testing. In view of reference no. The committee constituted in accordance with the government decision mentioned in Section 1 had directed to revise the rates. The committee's report has been accepted by the government. Accordingly, the matter of fixing the revised rates of RTPCR tests was under the consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government Decision: Rates of RTPCR tests for COVID-19 by Private Laboratories vide Reference No. The revised rates of RTPCR test including all taxes are being fixed as under by superseding the rates fixed as per the Government decision mentioned in Section 5. These rates are being fixed as per the directions of the competent authority under the Epidemic Diseases Act, 1897.
No private laboratory in the state can charge more than this limit.
