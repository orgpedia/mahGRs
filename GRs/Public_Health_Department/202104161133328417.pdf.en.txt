The Proposal:
For installation of new 320 KVA Generator at Divisional Reference Service Hospital, Nashik, Rs. The proposal for administrative approval of the preliminary budget and plan amounting to Rs. 31,14,294/- was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The cost of installation of new 320 KVA Generator at Divisional Reference Service Hospital, Nashik is based on the Budget Estimate 2018-19. This budget was presented by Deputy Engineer, Public Works (Vidyut), Sub-Division-1, Nashik. The certification has been done by the Divisional Office, Pune; Medical Superintendent, Divisional Referral Hospital, Nashik and Deputy Director Health Services, Nashik Circle. Accordingly, a total of Rs. 31,14,294/- is being accorded administrative approval under this Government Resolution subject to the following terms and conditions:

Terms and Conditions:
1) The detailed budget and plans of the work should be approved (Vidyut) by the concerned Chief Engineer, who is the highest technical officer at the field level.
2) While preparing the detailed budget, the Goods and Services Tax (GST) should be levied at the rate applicable.
(3) The work shall be subject to the technical approval of the competent authorities of the Public Works Department before inviting tenders for the work.
4) The provisions of the works should be shown in detail and the rates for the same should be submitted to the government by submitting the break-up.
(5) At the time of tendering, a single tender should be issued for all the works mentioned without separate tenders for each work.
(6) As the cost of the proposed work may increase according to the situation of the area and the requirements of the health institutions, the necessary proposals should be made by the area officers of the Public Works Department.
As the said budget is a rough model budget, the detailed budgets of these works will be technically approved by the competent authorities of the respective areas of the Public Works Department. While granting such technical approval, the provisions should be finalised with the concurrence of the Deputy Directors of the respective Health Service Boards of the Health Department.
8) If the work is to be done according to the new model maps, the architectural plan should be approved by the private architect appointed by the Public Health Department. The newly appointed architect and district surgeon will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the rules of the local rural development agency. Also, the letter requesting this permission must be signed by the district surgeon.
The maps and architectural plans of these works do not require separate approval from the Chief/ Deputy Chief Architect of the Public Works Department.
10) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV-2013/ No. 177/ T.C.1d. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014. Be sure to complete the above tasks.
The entire responsibility of fulfilling the conditions mentioned herein 1 to 10 above will be of the respective Head of Programmes as well as the Commissioner Health Services, Mumbai.
