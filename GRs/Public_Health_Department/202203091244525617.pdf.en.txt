The Proposal:
Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, New Delhi. As per the National Health Policy 2017 vide letter dated 27.07.2017, the nursing staff for nursing cadre is required to be a graduate (B.Sc Nursing). The minimum educational qualification for the post of midwifery nurse is GNM (General Nursing Midwifery). The Indian Nursing Council (INC) has decided to discontinue all GNM courses from 2022.
However, due to the Covid pandemic, the grading of GNM course to BSc Nursing has been allowed in a phased manner. In the first phase, the Central Government has sanctioned Rs.6 crore each for upgradation of Nursing Training Centres in Sindhudurg, Nashik and Satara districts and out of this fund, Rs. 3,20,50, 000/- (Rupees Three Crore Twenty Lakh Fifty Thousand only) has been transferred to the bank accounts of each institution for augmentation or modification of existing structures. Accordingly, the Deputy Director Health Services (Nursing), Mumbai, has issued the technical approval of the Executive Engineer, Public Works Department, Nashik, for the construction plans and budget. The proposal has been submitted to the government in accordance with the letter mentioned in 01. Accordingly, the matter of administrative approval of the cost of alterations required for upgrading the existing building of Nursing Training Centre, Nashik was under consideration of the Government. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
The Executive Engineer, Public Works Department has proposed to upgrade the existing building of District Hospital Nashik Affiliated Nursing Training Centre into B.Sc Nursing College by making necessary changes/ construction and conversion in the building of Nursing Training Centre.
