The objectives of the scheme are as follows:
A healthy diet for pregnant and lactating mothers should be encouraged to improve maternal and child health.
The health of newborns should be improved and maternal and infant mortality rates should be reduced and controlled.
This benefit will be beneficial in improving sex ratio at birth, preventing female foeticide and welcoming female births.
To increase the rate of institutional delivery by increasing the rate at which beneficiaries avail the facilities of health institutions.
The birth rate should increase with the birth of a newborn.
To avail the benefit of Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, the beneficiary must belong to at least one of the following groups (copy of proof of at least one identity card is required to be attached).
The average annual family income of women is Rs. less than 8 lakhs.

Women with 40% and above disability.

The features and implementation of the scheme will be as follows.
All new beneficiaries of PMMVY 2.0 whose LMP is after the date of publication of Mission Shakti guidelines.
Benefits will be provided as per the guidelines.
If a woman has already received the first installment of maternity benefit under PMMVY 1.0 and is eligible for cash incentive as per the norms approved under PMMVY 2.0. If she has received the first and second instalment under PMMVY 1.0, she can avail the remaining benefits as per the new PMMVY 2.0 guidelines.
The time limit for payment of benefits has been reduced from 730 days before the date of last menstrual period (LMP) to 510 days for the first child, and up to 210 days from the date of birth of the second child, if it is a girl child. The beneficiaries are required to apply for the benefit within the prescribed time period and after the expiry of the period, the beneficiaries will not get the benefit under this scheme. Also, if the beneficiaries have submitted the handwritten form, but the form is not being accepted online for any reason through the new computer system of the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, then the benefit will not be payable to such beneficiaries.
The age of the beneficiary should be minimum 18 years and maximum 55 years to avail the benefit of the scheme. (Beneficiaries can register on the portal only if they are between the age of minimum 18 years and maximum 55 years.) )

The expected actions to be taken by various departments for implementation of PMMVY will be as follows: 1) Health Department: 1) Ensuring that MCP cards are available and used.
2) ANC services to pregnant women and registration of childbirth, ensuring timely vaccination of the child, immediate registration of services provided to the scheme beneficiaries on the RCH portal and updating the information of the services.
3) Promoting the scheme for screening under Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA).
4) Exclusive promotion of institutional delivery, early initiation of breastfeeding, colostrum feeding and exclusive breastfeeding during the first six months.
5) The beneficiaries coming to the rural hospital, sub-district hospital, women's hospital, district hospital should be informed about the scheme. There, the responsible officer of the hospital should be appointed in writing as the scheme coordinator for the scheme. Action should be taken as per the directions of the Taluk Matru Vandana Yojana Cell.
6) Sensitization and training programs should be organized for all the concerned employees under PMMVY.
2) Women and Child Development Department 1) The scheme should be widely publicized through Anganwadi workers.
2) Wherever the health department feels the need to register the beneficiary and get the documents, it should provide appropriate support.
3) There should be active participation of Anganwadi workers in the work according to AAA so that the beneficiary of this scheme is not deprived.
3. Panchayati Raj Institutions: (i) To organize community awareness programmes.
2. Panchayati Raj Institutions to provide additional incentives to mothers from their own funds.
3) Conducting social audit/ redressal of grievances.
4) To distribute the birth certificate of the child required to take advantage of the scheme to the beneficiaries without delay.
4) UIDAI Department: Provide Aadhaar number to the beneficiaries who do not have Aadhaar and those who have it should link the mobile number to the Aadhaar card. Since the beneficiaries of this scheme are pregnant, special camps should be organized for them.
