Preamble: The Government has decided to select the service provider by inviting open tender through the National Health Mission to provide the service of ambulances equipped with medical equipment with state-of-the-art communication system to provide immediate medical assistance to the disaster victims in the state. A Tender Committee was constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Health) vide order dated 28.10.2010. The tender committee decided to qualify the tender submitted by M/ s BVG India and award the contract for the operation of Emergency Response Service (EMS) of a total of 937 ambulances, 233 Advance Life Support (ALS) and 704 Basic Life Support (BLS), across the state in the next 5 years. BVG India, the service provider, has been issued a Government Order dated 28th March, 2013. Sadar

The scheme was launched on January 26, 2014. The scheme was receiving funds from the Central Government through National Health Mission in 2014-15 and 2015-16 to some extent and the remaining funds were being disbursed through the State Plan. As per the decision of the Government in the aforesaid Act No. 2, the scheme was transferred from National Health Mission to Director, Health Services with effect from 2016-17. The scheme is being implemented from 2016-17. As per the MoU signed with the service provider in this regard, the contract expired on 31.01.2019, however, as a matter of urgency, the service provider was given extension to pay the February 2019 dues at the rate of January 2019, as per the Government decision dated 31.03.2019, so that the ambulance service can continue without any interruption in patient care. Thereafter, as per the decision taken by the Committee headed by A.M.S. (Finance) constituted vide Government Resolution dated 3rd January, 2019, it was decided to extend the period of service from 1st March, 2019 to 30th June, 2019 for continuation of ambulance services without interruption as per Government Resolution dated 8th May, 2019 for the year 2018-19, as per which the Government was considering to approve recurring expenditure of 70% of the payment for the month of May, 2019 and remaining 30% of the payment for the month of February, 2019 to April, 2019 on the said scheme. A decision in this regard is being taken.
Government Decision: The total recurring expenditure for the month of May, 2019 for the year 2019-20 on the equipped ambulance of Toll Free Number 108 under the Maharashtra Emergency Medical Services Scheme is Rs. 23,46,68, 288/- 70% of the amount of Rs. 16,42,67, 760/ (Rs. 16 crore forty two lakhs sixty seven thousand sixty six only) and 30% of the payment for the month of February, 2019 to April, 2019 aggregating to Rs. 21,11,15, 388/- aggregating to Rs. 37,53,83, 148/ (Rs. Thirty seven crore thirty five lakh eighty three thousand one hundred and forty eight) are being sanctioned at 2018-19 rates.
The details of the services provided by the above ambulances should be submitted to the government every month. If a penalty is imposed at the time of payment, the amount of the penalty shall be recovered from the payment. Accordingly, Rs. 83,31,210/- should be deducted as penalty.
