Preamble: Under the Deputy Director, Health Services, Nagpur Circle, Nagpur, 13 Junior Scribes were appointed from 1989 to 1993 by the Social Welfare/ Employment Department after conducting interviews after considering the eligibility, age and other conditions prescribed by the Selection Committee. The appointment was initially for a period of three to six months. However, due to large number of vacancies in the tribal areas, the services of 13 junior scribes were continued without interruption. The employees were being paid regular wages. These employees have also passed the post-service entrance examination. These employees have complied with all the terms and conditions mentioned in the General Administration Department No. SRV-1099/ P.S. 6/99/12 dated 8th March 1999. In the instant case, as per the decision of the Government, the appointing authorities were provided with the authority to grant temporary appointments for three and six months respectively to the posts under the jurisdiction of the Service Selection Board/ Regional Secondary Service Board in view of the administrative exigencies and such appointments (Secondary Service Board/ District Selection Committees/ Service Offices) were regularized as many of them continue for administrative reasons without interruption. At that time, the services of the 13 employees mentioned in Annexure A of this Government Decision were not regularized in the instant situation as the department did not receive the proposals of these 13 employees from the Regional Office within the stipulated time for regulation. As the services of these employees were not regularized and they did not get the benefits of service like assured promotion, promotion etc., they had filed complaints in the Industrial Court No. 277, 278, 279, 280 and 283 respectively. The contempt petition in this case is dated 1/2011. Writ Petition No. 2040/2011 of the High Court, Nagpur and Writ, Ma. In the High Court, considering the judgments given in the petition No. 5239/2011 and the writ petition No. 1293/2012, all the cases have been decided in favor of the employees. The government was considering regularising the services of the 13 employees. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:

Governance Decision No: Narrow 2016/ QS 1/ SEWA 5
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of this Government Decision, it is hereby approved to regularize the services of the thirteen employees mentioned in Annexure A of the said Government Decision with effect from 08.03.1999. Further, the Government has also decided to allow the employees to avail all the benefits of the Assured Progress Scheme (including retirement) for their uninterrupted service from the date of their original appointment.
