The Proposal:
Director (Finance& Accounts), National Health Mission, Mumbai had requested for release of State share amounting to Rs. 39.38 lakhs (40%) under the head" 2210 G583" out of the financial year 2021-22 provision for Socially Special Component under National AYUSH Mission vide Reference Letter No. Further, the Commissioner Health Services and Mission Director, National Health Mission, Mumbai vide letter No. 2 to the Commissioner, NHM, Mumbai has informed that the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission, launched in September, 2014, has been designed to provide AYUSH health services and education to the general public, enhance the quality of Ayurvedic, Homoeopathic and Unani medicines and supply of quality medicinal plants in a sustained manner. All necessary funds should be allocated for this.

Meanwhile, under the National AYUSH Mission, a grant of Rs. 18.85 lakh for the financial year 2014-15 was sanctioned for the Socially Specialized Units at the rate of 75% of the Central share and Rs. 6.28 lakh at the rate of 25% of the State share. Out of which, Central share has been received under the Government decision under reference number (3) and since State share is pending, the matter of disbursement of the said pending subsidy was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Under the National AYUSH Mission, a grant of Rs. 18.85 lakh was sanctioned for the year 2014-15 for the Socially Specialized Units at the rate of 75% of Central share and Rs. 6.28 lakh at the rate of 25% of State share. Further, for the financial year 2019-20, a grant of Rs. 655.26 lakh was sanctioned against 60% Central share and Rs. 436.84 lakh against 40% State share. Out of this, it was decided at the government level to disburse Rs. 655.26 lakh to the Centre and Rs. 436.84 lakh to the State through the original budgetary allocation and supplementary demand.
However, due to the cap of 50% of the Finance Department, the original budget provision of Rs. 1281.29 lakh was made in the year 2020-21 as the subsidy received at the end of March was Rs. 327.63 lakh on Central share and Rs. 218.42 lakh on State share. Further, an amount of Rs. 185.32 lakh has been revised to Rs. 39.38 lakh as against the original and supplementary demand of Rs. 224.70 lakh. Out of which, Rs. 327.63 lakh has been released towards previous pending Central share and Rs. 185.32 lakh towards State share at the end of March 2020.
