Preamble: National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April, 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM). Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included.
The proposal for providing the pending Central and State share to the State Health Societies from the provision in the approved budget for 2018-19 under the general scheme for the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Health Mission was under consideration of the Government. A decision in this regard is being taken.
Decision of the Government: Approval is being given to disburse the pending Central and State share for 2016-17 and 2017-18 from the approved budgetary allocation for 2018-19 to the general beneficiaries for various programmes being implemented under National Health Mission.
