The Proposal:
The National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural poor, the marginalized and the needy. Factors affecting health (e.g. Food, environment, sanitation, safe water supply, women and child development etc. From the year 2013-14, the Government of India has decided to take up the above health related programmes in urban areas under the name of" National Urban Health Mission." The Centre's share and the state's share is 60:40. The Government was considering a proposal to provide funds to State Health Societies under the general component of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Urban Health Mission (NUHSM) from the budgetary provision of 2021-22. The details of grants disbursed so far in this case are as follows.
