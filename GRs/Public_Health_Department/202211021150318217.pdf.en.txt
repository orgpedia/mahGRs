The Proposal:
Upgradation of 50 bedded Sub District Hospital to 100 bedded Sub District Hospital RVG Wardha at a cost of Rs. 3545.95 lakh was under the consideration of the Government for administrative approval of the budget and maps. The following decision has been taken:
Government's decision:
2. Upgradation of 50 bedded Sub District Hospital to 100 bedded Sub District Hospital, Arvi G. 3545.95 lakh has been received and the same is based on the Rate Schedule for 2022-23. The Executive Engineer is prepared by the Public Works Department-2, Arvi. He has also been certified by the Superintending Engineer, Public Works Board, Wardha; Chief Engineer, Regional Department of Public Works, Nagpur and District Surgeon, Wardha. According to the Recapitulation Sheet attached with Rs. 3545.95 lakhs are being accorded administrative approval under this Government Resolution subject to the following terms and conditions:

Terms and Conditions:
1) Before inviting tenders for the work, the technical approval of the Chief Engineer, Public Works Department will be required.
2) Administrative approval is being given subject to the condition that the work is funded from the available budget for construction.
(3) The work shall be tendered together without separate tenders.
4) The head of the health institution concerned should ensure the availability, suitability and adequacy of space for work.
5) Construction of new hospital building by demolishing the existing hospital building.
The proposed hospital building is Building G+ 2, with an area of 7602.89 sq.m.
Also, the rate of Rs. 27,000/- per sq.m.
(7) As the cost of the proposed work is likely to increase according to the situation of the area and the requirements of the health institutions, the necessary proposal should be approved by the zonal officers of the Public Works Department.
8) As the said Budget is a broad model Budget, the detailed estimates of these works shall be technically approved by the competent authorities of the Public Works Department in the respective areas.
9) The Public Works Department will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the relevant rules of the local rural urban development agency.
10) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV 2013/ Q.No.177/ T.C.1D. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014.
