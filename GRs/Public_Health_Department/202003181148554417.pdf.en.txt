As per the Government Decision dated 28.03.2013, the Operation Order was issued to MeBVG India for the operation of Emergency Response Services (EMS) of a total of 937 Ambulances (ALS) and 704 Basic Life Support (BLS) in the next 5 years. The scheme has been operationalized w.e.f. 26.01.2014. The scheme was receiving funds from the Central Government through National Health Mission in 2014-15 and 2015-16 to some extent and the remaining funds were being disbursed through the State Plan. As per the decision of the Government in the aforesaid Act No. 2, the scheme was transferred from National Health Mission to Director, Health Services with effect from 2016-17. The scheme is being implemented from 2016-17. As per the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed with the service provider in this regard, the contract expired on 31.01.2019, however, as a matter of urgency, the service provider was given extension to pay the February 2019 dues at the rate of January 2019, as per the Government decision dated 31.03.2019, so that the ambulance service can continue without any interruption in patient care. Thereafter, as per the decision taken by the Committee headed by A.M.S. (Finance) constituted vide Government Resolution dated 3rd January, 2019, the rates for the year 2018-19 for the continuation of the ambulance service without interruption shall be revised from 1st March, 2019 to 30th June, 2019 as per the Government Resolution dated 8th May, 2019 and from 1st July, 2019 to 31st October, 2019 as per the Government Resolution dated 29th July, 2019. from 1st November 2019 to 31st January 2020. As per the Government letter dated 04.01.2020, it has been decided to extend the time period for sanctioning recurring expenditure on the scheme for the remaining 30% of the payment for the month of August, 2019 to October, 2019.
