The Proposal:
The government has decided to invite open tenders through the National Health Mission to provide the services of ambulances equipped with medical equipment with state-of-the-art communication systems to provide immediate medical assistance to disaster victims in the state. As on 28.10.2010, the Additional Chief Secretary (Health)

A committee had been formed for the tender. The tender committee has decided to qualify the tender submitted by M/ s BVG India for the implementation of Emergency Response Service (EMS) of 937 ambulances across the state in the next 5 years. BVG India, a service provider, was issued a work order under Government Resolution 2013, dated March 28, 2013. Since the scheme was launched on 01.02.2014, the service provider has been given a deadline of 01.02.2014. It ended on January 31, 2019. A committee has been formed under the chairmanship of the Additional Secretary (Finance) to recommend to the government to extend the contract with the service provider or appoint a new service provider in connection with the Maharashtra Emergency Medical Services Project. Since this service is an essential service and it will take some time for further decision, considering the recommendation made by the committee to continue this ambulance service without interruption, the government has decided to continue the service. from March 1, 2019. The service provider was granted an extension till June 30, 2019, as an urgent matter in accordance with the January 2019 rates. The deadline The deadline ended on June 30, 2019, and since the service provider has not yet been decided, the matter of extending the service provider was under the consideration of the government. A decision in this regard is being taken.
Government Decision: To continue the service of well-equipped ambulances under the Maharashtra Emergency Medical Services Scheme without interruption. Approval is being given to extend the period from 01.07.2019 to 31.10.2019 or till such date as may be decided by the Committee for appointment of Service Providers, whichever is earlier, as an urgent matter at the rate of 2018-19.
The terms and conditions of the earlier agreement for payment of recurring expenditure on the well-equipped ambulances operating under the Maharashtra Emergency Medical Services Scheme for the said period shall continue to apply.
