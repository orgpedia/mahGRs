Government circular:
As per the Government decision dated 26.02.2019, Ayushman Bharat-Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana is being implemented in the State from 01.04.2020 in convergence with Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana. This scheme is completely cashless and the beneficiaries will be provided free treatment in the empanelled hospitals. Under the Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana, each family will get Rs. Rs 1.5 lakh. Treatment is permissible up to Rs. 2.5 lakh. Further, under the Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana, each family will get Rs. Benefit of treatment up to Rs 5 lakh is permissible.
Under this scheme, both government and private hospitals have been approved. The government is receiving complaints from citizens and public representatives that the government is not informing about the implementation of the revised Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana and Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana in the approved private hospitals, denying treatment in the health insurance scheme by the approved private hospital, providing low quality services, demanding more money from the patient while the service is free under this scheme. The following recommendations are made for the effective implementation of the scheme:

The beneficiaries of the combined Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana and Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana include Yellow, Keshari, Antyodaya and Annapurna ration card holders, white ration card holders, farmers and construction workers and their families in 14 drought-affected districts, children in orphanages in government ashram schools, senior citizens in government old age homes, journalists and their families with disabilities. A valid ration card/ department issued identity card and photo is required to avail the benefit of the scheme.
The empanelled hospital is required to put up banners/ boards on the facade clearly displaying the name of the scheme, complete details of the scheme.
2) Arogya Mitra has been appointed as a guide to the patients in the empanelled hospital of Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana. Arogyamitra is responsible for registering patients with the help of their ration card. Regular visits to the hospital and the patient
It is the responsibility of the health minister to ensure that all the benefits of the scheme are availed.
Also, the Arogya Mitra must fully cooperate with the patient and the patient's relatives in providing follow-up services from the time the patient is admitted to the hospital to the time he/ she goes home. However, it has been observed from the complaints received by the government that no action is being taken in this regard from some employees/ health workers. In case of any negligence or negligence on the part of the health worker in attending to the prescribed work/ attending to the patient and the patient's relatives or any such other matter, the Chief Executive Officer, State Health Guarantee Society, Mumbai shall take necessary immediate action as per rules.
3) The District Coordinators are required to visit all the affiliated hospitals in all the districts once a week and review the activities of the Arogya Mitras. Also, the district coordinator and the divisional manager should make efforts to reach the information of the scheme to the general public.
As per Para 2 of the Government Resolution dated 26.02.2019, Annexure-I specifies the composition and functions of the Committees for Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana and Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana. In the said Government decision, Annexure- 1 No. 5 District Control and Grievance Redressal Committee has been constituted. The District Collector is the chairman of the committee and the District Surgeon is the member secretary. To conduct a comprehensive review of the functioning of the scheme in the district, to suggest to the insurance company/ implementing support agency for the smooth implementation of the scheme, to approve the hospital in the district jurisdiction, to cancel the temporary suspension or recognition of the functioning hospital, to recommend to the State Health Guarantee Society, to resolve the complaints received at the district level.

As per Para 5 of the Government Decision dated 21.09.2018, in case of irregularities in denial of treatment or sub-standard services in the health insurance scheme, it has been approved to impose a penalty of five times the package amount on the hospitals empanelled under Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana and Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana and to reimburse the amount spent by the beneficiary/ the amount taken from the beneficiaries (one part) and transfer the remaining four parts to the administrative account of the society. It has also been instructed to take legal action in case of non-recovery from the concerned authorized hospital, non-provision of claims of the hospital, suspension of the hospital, cancellation of the hospital's affiliation. Accordingly, action should be taken by the State Health Guarantee Society against the approved hospital in the scheme.
7) Dated 29.04.2021 Ma. The Minister of Public Health and Family Welfare reviewed the details of Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana and Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana being implemented in 225 hospitals in the state through video conference. There were many shortcomings in the programme. Non-opening of Jan Arogya Yojana accounts, non-appointment of medical coordinators, non-installation of scanner-printer-internet etc. facilities, non-submission of claims to the insurance company etc. All district surgeons have been asked to take immediate action in this regard.
8) Under this scheme, the funds collected through the subordinate bodies of the Commissionerate of Health Services under the control of the Public Health Department are to be utilized. The Government Decision dated 02.01.2019 has been issued. As per the decision, 20 per cent of the amount received through insurance is paid to the officers and employees of the institution providing patient care as incentive allowance. The hospital is allowed to spend 52 per cent of the fund on the matters mentioned in the Schedule A of the said Government Resolution. In view of this, the Commissionerate of Health Services should effectively implement the scheme in the empanelled government hospitals.
