The proposal of the Directorate General of Health Services to approve the estimated expenditure for repair of the vehicle which met with an accident on 12.02.2018 was under consideration of the Government. A decision in this regard is being taken.
Government Decision: Pune Zilla Parishad, Health Department Headquarters No. HH 12 RR 1020 for Rs. Administrative Sanction is being granted for expenditure to the tune of Rs. 1,32,000/- subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) The vehicle must be used only within the authority and jurisdiction certified by the Directorate General of Health Services or the competent authorities of the State Health Transport Organization.
2) The vehicle must be used by the area officer to whom the vehicle is provided. Also, it should not be used anywhere other than the area to which it is given.
(iii) the institution to which the vehicle is provided is liable to pay the risk and liability of legal claims made by a third party in case of accident, theft, etc. of the vehicle in respect of the institutions supplying the vehicle under the control of the District Councils or the institutions supplying the vehicle under the subsidy of the District Councils; and

It will be the head office/ department of the institution. The Health Department on behalf of the Government of Maharashtra will not pay any liability in this regard.
4) The vehicle owner will always be responsible for protecting the capital invested by the health department from fire, flood, earthquake, accident, theft, arson, social riots, natural or human disaster, etc. In case of damage to the vehicle due to this reason, the vehicle holder (Zilla Parishad/ Zilla Parishad Aided Institutions and their respective Head Offices/ Departments) shall be liable to pay to the Health Department the amount of depreciation deducted from the value of the vehicle at that time.
In case of damage to the vehicle due to the reasons mentioned in item No. 4 above, it will be obligatory for those organizations to pay compensation in the amount to be determined by the Directorate General of Health Services/ State Health Transport Organization.
The vehicle holder should not use his/ her vehicle for the implementation of any programme other than health services without prior written permission of the Directorate of Health Services/ State Health Transport Organizations. In exceptional circumstances, for example. There is no problem in allowing the vehicle to be used for other purposes as per the orders of the Collector during elections, emergency situations, etc. However, in such cases, the permission of the Directorate General of Health Services or the State Health Transport Association is required.
7) In the event of a vehicle accident, the drivers are required to report it to the police inspector of the concerned police station and make a panchnama about it.
8) The details of the repair of the vehicle and the expenses incurred on it, as well as the details of the tyres, tubes, batteries, etc. installed in the vehicle should be written in the vehicle history book from time to time.
9) The above instructions should be strictly followed by all concerned, otherwise disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned by fixing responsibility on them.
10) The cost should be appropriately covered.
