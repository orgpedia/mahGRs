The Proposal:
To extend the coverage of the scheme to all citizens for a limited period so that COVID-19 patients do not have to bear the financial burden of getting treatment in an authorized private hospital and to provide health assurance and financial relief to all citizens during the COVID-19 pandemic.
Benefit of treatment under Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana should be available to the beneficiary patients as well as other patients and the treatment methods reserved for government hospitals should be available.
The decision has been taken with a view to ensure availability of necessary resources in the context of prevention of COVID-19 pandemic to the doctors, other staff and ancillary staff of the hospitals empanelled under the scheme, with effect from 23rd May, 2020. The deadline was extended from April 1, 2021 to June 30, 2021. Since the scheme had expired on June 30, 2021, the Government was considering extension of the period from July 01, 2021 to September 30, 2021.
Government's decision:
In view of the current number of COVID-19 patients in the state of Maharashtra and the benefits being provided to the citizens of the state under the Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana, in order to provide health assurance and financial relief to all the citizens during the current pandemic crisis, the scheme, which came into effect under the Government decision dated May 23, 2020, is being extended for a period of three months from July 1, 2021, to September 30, 2021, excluding 120 treatments opened to private affiliated hospitals.
Out of 134 treatments reserved for Government recognized hospitals, 120 treatments were opened for private recognized hospitals under the Government decision dated 23rd May, 2020.
The treatment will be provided at the government hospital.
