As per the Maharashtra State Service (Medical Care) Rules, 1961 and rules prescribed thereunder from time to time, this Government has decided to allow the private hospital" Jeevan Jyoti Cancer Hospital, Jalgaon" to reimburse the medical expenses incurred for the treatment and surgery of serious diseases like cancer and its aftermath for the State Government employees and their families. The following conditions must be met:
Under Section 41A of the Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950, 10% of the patients are treated free of cost and 10% of the patients are treated at a discounted rate.
To provide trained medical officers and staff as per the norms prescribed under the Bombay Nursing Homes Act, 1949.

3) The availability of equipment in the hospital for researching the disease for which treatment has been approved, as well as for performing surgery according to the diagnosis made after the tests.
4) The fee should be charged at the rate at which it is charged in government hospitals.
5) Active participation in the campaign of national programs implemented by the central government, state government is necessary.
The hospital will be required to renew the registration as per the Mumbai Nursing Home Act from time to time.
The reimbursement of medical expenses incurred by the hospital for treatment and surgery related to the above-mentioned diseases shall be admissible as per the prescribed rules. The government employee will be required to take prior consent of the authorized officer for any medication other than the approved treatment in the hospital, except in case of emergency.
