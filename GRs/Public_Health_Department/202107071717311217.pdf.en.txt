The Proposal:
For the prevention of visual impairment in children aged 6 to 18 years in all government and aided schools in the state, if those children are diagnosed with visual impairment on time and provided with appropriate glasses free of cost, their school activities will not be affected. In view of this, it has been decided to provide free spectacles to the students of 81,556 government and semi-government schools in the state as per the requirement after conducting eye check-up and certifying them scientifically. The decision has been taken as per the decision of the Government of India and it has been decided to implement this programme as a new scheme of the State Government.
Also read the above. 2 As per the decision of the government, it has been approved to open a new account head for this scheme. Now the matter of declaring Drawing and Disbursing Officer as well as Controller Officer for this head of account was under the consideration of the government.

Government's decision:
The Chief Administrative Officer (Family Welfare, Parental Care and School Health), Pune is being declared as" Drawing and Disbursing Officer" under Rule 156 of the Maharashtra Treasury Rules, 1968, for implementing the scheme of providing free spectacles under the Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram for the prevention of visual impairment among children in the age group of 6 to 18 years in all government and aided schools in the state. Additional Director, Health Services (Family Welfare, Parental Care and School Health), Pune is being declared as" Controlling Officer."
This Government Decision is based on the Informal Reference No. 117/21/ CoPR-5 of the Department of Finance, No. Consent is being issued as of June 22, 2021.
