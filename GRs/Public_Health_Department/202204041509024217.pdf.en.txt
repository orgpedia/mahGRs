Preamble: In order to provide accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population, the Government of India has launched the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) from April, 2005 across the country. Under this scheme, health services like Reproductive and Child Health Programme, regular immunization, Mission Flexipool, strengthening of infrastructure and pulse polio vaccination etc. are provided. Accordingly, under the National Health Mission (NHM), 40% State share of COVID-19 grant for National Rural Health Programme (NRHP) in 2021-22 under General Category has been increased from Rs. 123556.10 lakh has been disbursed to the State Health Societies and the remaining Rs. 0.67 lakh was under the consideration of the Government from the budgetary provision of 2022-23.
Government's decision:
The Department of Finance has approved the release of 40% of pending State share of 2021-22 for COVID-19 from General Category for National Rural Health Programme under National Health Mission. For the financial year 2022-23, for the head of accounts 22105246, Rs. 81200.00 Lakhs
