The Proposal:
30 bedded Rural Hospital, Chalisgaon. The proposal for administrative approval of the budget and plan amounting to Rs. 1322.39 lakh was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The budget for construction of residential building for 30 bedded Rural Hospital, Chalisgaon, Jalgaon is based on the schedule of 2022-23. According to the attached recapitulation sheet, Rs. 1322.391 lakhs are being accorded administrative approval under this Government Resolution subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) Before inviting tenders for the work, the technical approval of the Chief Engineer for the construction work, the Chief Engineer for the construction and Vidyut works, the Public Works Department will be required.
2) Administrative approval is being given subject to the condition that the work is funded from the available budget for construction.

3) When e-tendering the work, the work should be tendered together without breaking it into pieces.
4) The head of the health institution concerned should ensure the availability, suitability and adequacy of space for work.
5) The price of the proposed work should not be increased unnecessarily according to the situation of the area and the needs of the health institutions.
As the said budget is a rough model budget, the detailed budgets of these works shall be technically approved by the competent authorities of the Public Works Department in their respective areas.
7) The Public Works Department will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the relevant rules of the local rural development agency.
8) When working directly with the Department of Environmental Protection, Government Decision No. ENV 2013/ Q.No.177/ T.C.1D. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014.
9) It should be ensured that the area of the proposed building is in accordance with the permissible standards of the staff.
10) The concerned Deputy Director of Health Services and District Surgeon should review the construction at the field level from time to time.
11) While constructing the hospital building, it should be ensured that the planning of the hospital building is done according to Part 4 Fire and Safety in the National Building Code vol-1 according to the fire safety norm.
12) The estimates and maps are to be examined by the concerned Chief Architect and Chief Engineer (Architecture/ Architecture). Also, ensure that the structure is in accordance with the IPHS guidelines.
The entire responsibility of fulfilling the conditions mentioned in 1 to 12 above will be of the concerned Executive Engineer, Public Works Department and District Surgeon.
The expenditure on this work will be covered under Demand No. H-9, Head of Accounts 4210- Capital Expenditure on Medical Services and Public Health, 02- Rural Health Services, 04- Public Health, 200- Other Programmes Scheme under Five Year Plans, (00) (01) Major Constructions (4210 0141), 53 Major Constructions.
