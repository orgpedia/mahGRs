The Proposal:
Maharashtra Medical and Health Services Group-A (Pay Band Rs. 15600-39100+ Grade Pay Rs. 5400), some of the Medical Officers of this cadre were unauthorisedly absent for a long period of time for various reasons.
Also, some medical officers were absent for postgraduate courses without prior permission from the government.
As the public health service is an essential service and there is a shortage of medical officers under the department, as per the request made by him regarding the submission of these unauthorized absentee medical officers and in accordance with the proposal submitted by the Director of Health Services, Mumbai to the government regarding the submission of the concerned subject to the prescribed terms and conditions, the matter of reinstating the concerned medical officers in the government service after unauthorized absence was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
Maharashtra Medical and Health Services Group-A (Pay Band Rs. 15600-39100+ Grade Pay Rs. 5400), some of the Medical Officers of this cadre were unauthorisedly absent for a long period of time for various reasons.
However, as per the request made by them and the proposal submitted by the Director Health Services, Mumbai to the concerned medical officers for deputation subject to specified terms and conditions, the concerned medical officers are being engaged subject to the terms and conditions mentioned under the table in this paragraph and they are being given such posting for joining the post of Medical Officer Group A as shown against their names in the table below.

Terms and Conditions:
(1) Since the concerned Medical Officer, Group-A is an unauthorised absence, the period from the date of his unauthorised absence to the date of actual joining shall be deemed to be a period of service as per Rule 47 (1) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982 and the said unauthorised absence shall be deemed to be the period of engagement of the Medical Officer. Also, the entire period of his absence should be treated as a break in service and the same should be clearly mentioned in his joining order.
(2) The Medical Officer concerned shall be medically examined by the Medical Board before being appointed and shall be certified by the Medical Board as physically/ mentally competent to perform the service.
(3) The character and conduct report of the concerned medical officer (police investigation report) should be taken. (A police report should be taken that no complaint of criminal nature or under the Prevention of Corruption Act is pending.) )
(4) No regular/ temporary service before unauthorised absence as mentioned in No. 1 shall be added to the service after joining after unauthorised absence and the service shall not be availed continuously. Further, as per Rule 47 (1) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, the period of unauthorised absence of the concerned medical officer due to a break in service shall not be called for in the future.
(5) No salary and other incidental service benefits shall be admissible for the period from the date of unauthorised absence till actual joining, no leave shall be granted during the period of such unauthorised absence, the period shall not be regularised for any purpose or any representation thereon shall be entertained. Also, a sanction letter of Rs. 100/- should be obtained from the concerned Medical Officer by the concerned Deputy Director, Health Services.
(6) An affidavit shall be submitted to the Government through the Deputy Director, Health Services concerned for Government information that the concerned medical officer has not done any private business/ job or foreign tour during the period of unauthorised absence.
(7) Even if the concerned medical officer is engaged after unauthorised absence, the period of absence as well as the service preceding it shall not be taken into account.

The old pension scheme before 2005 will not be applicable to the concerned medical officers.
(8) All the concerned unauthorised absentee medical officers to be recruited. The contributory pension scheme introduced as per the Government decision dated 31/10/2005, which is presently subsumed in the NPS scheme, shall continue to be applicable.
(9) No benefit under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982 shall be admissible to all the medical officers concerned.
(10) Also, no provident fund benefits will be available.
Unauthorized absentee medical officers posted under this order shall join their place of posting within 15 days from the date of receipt of the said order of posting.
Otherwise, the order will be automatically cancelled after 15 days. The head of the office should not appoint a medical officer who will join after 15 days.
