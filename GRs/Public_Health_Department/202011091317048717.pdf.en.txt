Preamble: National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health care to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM). Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The Government was contemplating to make available the State share of the pending grants for the year 2019-20 under Tribal Appropriation to the State Health Societies under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Health Mission (NHM) from the provision made in the approved budget for the year 2020-21. A decision in this regard is being taken.
