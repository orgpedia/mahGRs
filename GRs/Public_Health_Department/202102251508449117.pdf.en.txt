The Proposal:
The existing 189 bedded Women's Hospital has been upgraded to 400 beds vide Government Resolution dated 28.08.2009. The 200-bed hospital will be constructed at a cost of Rs. 4561.58 lakh has been accorded administrative sanction vide Government Resolution dated 12.04.2013. This work is in progress. The remaining works such as central medical gas pipeline system, solar water heater, rooftop plot, CCTV, etc. The proposal for administrative approval of budget and maps amounting to Rs. 6,45,69, 000/- was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
2. Rates for the works of Medical Gas Pipeline System, Solar Water Heater, Roof Top Plot, CCTV etc. at District Women's Hospital, Amravati are based on the Budget Estimates 2019-20.
The budget was presented by Deputy Engineer, Public Works Department-1, Amravati. Ba.
It is certified by the Divisional Office, Amravati. Accordingly, a total of Rs. Administrative approval is being accorded under this Government Resolution for the projects and schemes amounting to Rs. 6,45,69, 000/- subject to the following terms and conditions.

Terms and Conditions:
Terms and Conditions:
1) While preparing the detailed budget, the Goods and Services Tax (GST) should be levied at the rate applicable.
(2) The work shall require the technical approval of the competent authority of the Public Works Department before inviting tenders for the work.
3) The provisions of the works should be shown in detail and the rates for the same should be submitted to the government by submitting the break-up.
(4) At the time of tendering, a single tender should be issued for all the works mentioned without separate tenders for each work.
5) As the cost of the proposed work can increase according to the situation of the area and the need of the health institutions, the necessary proposals for this should be made by the area officers of the Public Works Department.
As the said budget is a rough model budget, the detailed budgets of these works will be technically approved by the competent authorities of the Public Works Department in the respective areas. While granting such technical approval, the provisions should be finalised with the concurrence of the Deputy Directors of the respective Health Service Boards of the Health Department.
7) If the work is to be done according to the new model maps, the architectural plan should be approved by the private architect appointed by the Public Health Department. The newly appointed architect and district surgeon will be responsible for obtaining construction permits and occupancy certificates as per the rules of the local rural development agency. Also, the letter requesting this permission must be signed by the district surgeon.
The maps and architectural plans of these works do not require separate approval from the Chief/ Deputy Chief Architect of the Public Works Department.
9) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV-2013/ No. 177/ T.C.1d. Action should be taken in accordance with the guidelines mentioned in January 10, 2014. Be sure to complete the above tasks.
