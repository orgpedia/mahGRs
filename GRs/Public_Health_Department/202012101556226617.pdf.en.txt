Financial provision
The expenditure on this aspect is in line with the commitment made for Procurement of Equipment under FMR Code 6.1.1.18 a of Project Implementation Plan 2019-20. 673.67 lakh will be met from the committed unspent balance.
However, the single source procurement proposal is being accorded administrative approval as per Para 3.2.6 of the State Procurement Policy subject to the following conditions:
The requirement/ demand of the materials and machinery should be verified by the Mission Director, National Health Mission, Mumbai.
(2) The prescribed procurement procedure shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions contained in the Revised Manual of Procedures for Office Procurement to be carried out by Government Departments published under Government Decision, Industries, Energy and Labour Department, No.Bhanks-2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-3/ Industries-4, dated 01.12.2016.
3. It should be ensured that the specifications of such items are as prescribed and updated by the Central TB Division. The technical specifications should be as per the specifications approved by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials) and the technical specifications are of the original equipment manufacturer (OEM) and a single source certificate has been submitted. The concerned programme head will be responsible for sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the approved items in the project implementation plan. The procurement process should be carried out only on the items included in the proposal.
4. The rates of the proposed items are estimated at the time of administrative approval and hence the rates are to be determined during the procurement process. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the price at which the supplier has supplied the items to other places.
The expenditure for the said aspects is in addition to the amount committed for Procurement of Equipment under FMR Code 6.1.1.18 a of the Project Implementation Plan 2019-20. 673.67 lakh should be met from the committed unspent balance.
It should be ensured that the procurement process is completed within the prescribed time frame of administrative approval.
6) Ensure that there is no duplication.
