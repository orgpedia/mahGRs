The Proposal:
National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Government of India in April 2005 with the objective of providing accessible, affordable, efficient, accountable and reliable health services to the rural population. In the year 2014-15, the Government of India merged all Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Health with the National Health Mission (NHM).
Under this National Health Mission, now centrally sponsored schemes like National Health Mission, National Urban Health Mission and National Disease Control Programme have been included. The Government was considering to provide the Central and State share of funds for the Social Component (SCSP) of the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) to the State Health Societies from the allocation made in the approved budget for 2023-24.
