Preamble: In the Supplementary Project Implementation Plan (PIP) for the year 2019-20, the Government was considering to grant administrative approval to conduct the tender process for the commissioning of Boat Ambulance cum Mobile Medical Unit on a pilot basis on the sea route from Mandwa to Gateway of India from the committed grant.
Decision of the Government: Administrative approval is being given to conduct the tender process for the commissioning of Boat Ambulance cum Mobile Medical Unit on a pilot basis on the sea route from Mandwa to Gateway of India from the committed grant in the Supplementary Project Implementation Plan (PIP) for the year 2019-20.
The said administrative approval is being granted on a pilot basis for a period of one year subject to the following conditions: :

1) The service of Boat Ambulance cum Mobile Medical Unit is proposed by an external system. In this, the boat, machinery, medicines, staff, fuel and other expenses for the medical unit should be done by the external system itself and the monthly operating expenses (Opex) should be paid to the external system after the work starts.
2) The need/ demand of the matter should be ascertained.
Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
3. External Agency for Boat Ambulance cum Mobile Medical Unit The Project Director (EMS), National Health Mission, Mumbai and concerned external agencies, as the case may be, shall be responsible for compliance of the Maharashtra Maritime Board's Water Transport Rules, Vessel Licenses and all the relevant Acts, Rules and Regulations of the appropriate authorities for the project to be implemented through the tender process.
4) Ensure that the specifications are as per requirement and up to date.
The technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. The responsibility of sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the items approved in the PIP will be of the concerned programme head. Only the items included in the proposal should be tendered.
(5) The prescribed procurement procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the revised rulebook of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labor Department, No. 2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated December 1, 2016.
6) The rate for availing this service through external sources should be fixed at the end of the tender process. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the price at which the supplier has supplied the items to other places.

9) Also, it should be ensured that there is no duplication in the purchase of these items.
