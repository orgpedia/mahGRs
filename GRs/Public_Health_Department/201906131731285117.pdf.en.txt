Government Decision The intake capacity of the Government Medical College, Baramati has been fixed at 100 and in this regard, approval is being given to temporarily transfer the Government Medical College, Baramati to meet the criteria of the Indian Council of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals for starting the Women's Hospital, Baramati and Rural Hospital, (Rui) Baramati and Silver Jubilee Sub-District Hospital, Baramati under the Health Department to meet the criteria of the Indian Council of Medical Sciences.
Terms and Conditions: 1. Women's Hospital, Baramati, Rural Hospital, (Rui) Ta. The three hospitals, Baramati and Silver Jubilee Sub-District Hospital Baramati, are to be temporarily transferred for a period of 3 years. Even if these hospitals are transferred, the building and the land will remain in the name of the District Surgeon, Pune. There won't be any change.
The District Surgeon, Pune shall enter into an agreement with the newly appointed Dean thereof for a period of 3 years only. After that, it will automatically stop working. The Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals shall complete the process of setting up a Medical College Hospital as per the standards of the Indian Council of Medical Sciences within 3 years and hand over the movable and immovable property to the Public Health Department on the day of conclusion of the contract.
After this, the deputation of machinery and staff on deputation in the current situation will automatically be terminated and they will be transferred to the Public Health Department.
The residential premises, offices, hospital buildings and all premises within the premises of these three hospitals, except the AYUSH building, shall be under the administrative control of the Dean, Medical College, till such time as the agreement is in force.
The Dean, Medical College and District Surgeon, Pune, will have to take prior permission from the Public Health Department (Administrative Department) if they want to make any changes in the basic structure of the building. If the building of these hospitals needs to be modified as per their standards for the approval of the Indian Council of Medical Sciences as per the requirement, the proposal should be submitted to the government by the District Surgeon, Pune, following the procedure prescribed by the government. The changes will be made after the approval of the government.

The MBBS qualified Medical Officers of Group-A of these three hospitals will work on deputation to the Medical College for a period of 3 years and will work as Accident Department, Medical Officer, Resident Medical Officer as per requirement.
The services of Group A cadre specialists presently working in all the three hospitals from the date of signing of the MoU shall be on deputation for a period of 3 years or till the appointment of the specialist by the Department of Medical Education and Drugs and shall be automatically transferred to the Department of Public Health after the expiry of their deputation period. There is no need for the government to take any decision on it. The Department of Medical Education and Drugs should make arrangements to make available the services of specialists required for the proposed medical college from the specialists available to them in other medical colleges.
Group C and Group D staff, under the Dean, Medical College will be made available on deputation for a period of 3 years. The establishment of Group-C and Group-D employees (during the period of deputation) will be under the direct control of the Administrative Officer/ Chief Administrative Officer of the concerned hospital and indirectly under the control of the Dean.
8. Salaries and allowances of officers and staff during the period of their re-appointment shall be paid by the Directorate of Medical Education and Research. Accordingly, the staff on deputation will be taken back by the Public Health Department.
After the transfer process is completed, the Department of Medical Education and Medicines shall appoint an independent Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the proposed Government Medical College. The Drawing and Disbursing Officer appointed by the Department of Medical Education and Medicines will act as Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the salary of the staff on deputation.

12. To make the new medical college functional, the Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals should start a new head of account for the medical college and make available the necessary funds separately under it.

13. The Board of Examination for Persons with Disabilities under the Silver Jubilee Sub-District Hospital, Baramati will henceforth be under the Dean, Medical College and General Hospital, Baramati. At present, the Divyang Mandal will remain under the control of the Medical Superintendent until the new login ID and password of the self-reliance system is received.
After signing of MoU between Public Health Department and Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals Department, the services of safety, hygiene, washing clothes, food, etc. should be started again through the medical college. The dean will take a decision on the continuation of non-medical services currently available in these three hospitals. If non-medical services are to be continued, the control and payment of the same shall be the responsibility of the Dean.
15. Women's Hospital, Baramati, Rural Hospital, (Rui) Tt. The machinery and heavy equipment available in the three hospitals, Baramati and Silver Jubilee Sub-District Hospital, Baramati, will be transferred to the Medical College and Hospital. Henceforth, the cost of repair and maintenance should be borne by the Dean, Government Medical College and Hospital, Baramati, from their own funds. The public health department cannot be asked for this fund. All these equipment should be handed over to the public health department in the current state after the contract ends. Also, the buildings of the hospital should be handed over to the Public Health Department in good condition after the expiry of the contract.
Services under National Health Mission of all three hospitals (e.g. SNCU). AYUSH, JSSK etc.) and its staff will be under the control of Medical Superintendent at the hospital level and District Surgeon at the district level. However, the technical control over these services will be of the Dean, Medical College and the General Hospital.
The Patient Welfare Committees constituted under the National Health Mission of all the three hospitals will be suspended for a period of 3 years or until the hospitals are handed over to the Medical Education Department.
Other buildings such as hospitals. Residences, offices, AYUSH building etc. will remain with the District Surgeons as before. However, if some of these buildings are required to get the rating of the Indian Council of Medical Sciences, they can be taken in consultation with the district surgeon.
17. On the day of transfer, Women's Hospital Baramati, Rural Hospital Rui Ta. Transfer of stock of medicines available in three hospitals namely Baramati and Silver Jubilee Sub District Hospital Baramati to Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals.
