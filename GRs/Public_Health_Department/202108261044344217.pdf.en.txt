Government circular:
In view of the current COVID-19 situation, the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities, Government of India, vide Reference No. 2 The Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India, New Delhi has been advised to issue guidelines to all the centres regarding prioritization of testing, treatment and vaccination of persons with disabilities for COVID-19. Accordingly, the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Mumbai vide Reference No. Under the letter, instructions were issued to the concerned regarding giving priority to the disabled persons in the Covid testing and treatment centers and hospitals in the state.
As per Section 25 (1) (a) of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, it is necessary to take care of the health of persons with disabilities by giving priority to their illness and treatment. At present, the Covid-19 situation in the state is such that people with disabilities have to stand in long queues for testing, treatment and vaccination. People with disabilities are at a higher risk of contracting Covid-19 as their immunity is not as good as that of the general population. Also, standing for long periods of time in the queue and travelling becomes a challenge. Keeping in mind the physical capabilities of the disabled persons, a separate queue should be made for them so that they do not have to stand in the queue for long and they do not get inconvenienced.
