The Proposal:
Under the National Health Mission (NHM), the Government was considering granting administrative approval for the purchase of Cardboard Boxes as per the guidelines of the Government of India for providing necessary medicines to TB patients under the Revised National Tuberculosis Control Programme (RNTCP) during the financial year 2018-19.
Government's decision:
For the revised National Tuberculosis Control Programme (NTCP) under National Health Mission (NHM) for the financial year 2018-19, a total amount of Rs. 324.86 lakh has been provided. Out of this grant, the amount proposed by the Joint Director, Health Services (Leprosy and Tuberculosis), Pune for the purchase of cardboard box for providing necessary medicines to TB patients is Rs. Administrative approval is being granted by executing the tender process for an amount of Rs. 141.00 lakhs. The details of the Cardboard Box purchase and subsidy are as follows:

The administrative approval is being granted subject to the following conditions: 1) The requirement/ demand for cardboard boxes for providing necessary medicines to the TB patients should be verified. Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated.
2) It should be ensured that the specifications of the cardboard box are as per the requirement and up-to-date to provide the required medicines to the TB patients. The technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. After that, you have to buy them.
(3) The prescribed procurement procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the revised rule book of procedures for office procurement to be carried out by government departments published under the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labor Department, No. 2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry 4, dated December 1, 2016.
4) The rates of the proposed procurement are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. The final price must be determined at the end of the purchase process. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the concerned supplier has supplied these items to other places and a certificate should be obtained from the concerned.
5) Items to be procured should be pre/ post dispatch random sample inspection to ensure that the goods are purchased with proper and proper specifications.
6) Storage and distribution of the items to be purchased after supply, as well as supply chain management and inventory management should be done in a proper manner to ensure that these items do not remain unused and are used in a timely manner.
7) The expenditure for the purchase of the drug should be met from the subsidy sanctioned under the National Health Mission (NHM) under the head' Procurement of Sleeves& Drug Boxes' (FMR Code 6.5.2) of the programme.
