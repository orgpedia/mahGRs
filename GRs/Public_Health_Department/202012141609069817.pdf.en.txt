The Proposal:
The Rajiv Gandhi Jeevanadayi Arogya Yojana (RGGJAY) has been implemented in eight districts of Mumbai city, Mumbai suburbs, Raigad, Dhule, Nanded, Amravati, Solapur and Gadchiroli in the first phase from 2nd July 2012. The scheme has been implemented across the state from 21st November, 2013.
Department of Public Health, Government of India vide its decision dated 13th April, 2017 has approved continuation of Rajiv Gandhi Jeevanadayi Arogya Yojana with effect from 1st April, 2017 under the new name Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana.
It has been decided to implement both the schemes of Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana and Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana jointly on insurance and assurance mode as per the Government decision dated 21st September, 2018. Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana along with Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana has been launched in the State from 23rd September, 2018.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. MFUYO-2020/ Q.No.153/ HEALTH-6 dated 14.12.2020
Thereafter, as per the Government decision dated 26.02.2019, Ayushman Bharat-Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana is being implemented in the State jointly on Insurance and Assurance mode by merging the two schemes with Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana. This scheme has been implemented with effect from 01.04.2020 in association with United India Insurance Company.
The Government was considering to rename Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana as" Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana."
Government's decision:
The scheme" Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana" should henceforth be known as" Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana." The provisions of the existing scheme will continue.
