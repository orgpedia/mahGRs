Government's decision:
In order to ensure smooth functioning of the Laboratory Services in the Health Institutions in the State, the tender process for providing services by a new service provider through tender process for a period of next 5 years is being accorded administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions. :
1) The requirement/ demand for the said laboratory services should be ascertained. Thereafter, e-tendering process will be initiated. This e-tendering process should be carried out through the procurement room of the Commissionerate.
2) Laboratory service specifications should be verified as per requirement and up to date.
The technical specifications of the items required for laboratory services should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (technical committee/ officials).
Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. The responsibility of sending the requisition as per the technical specifications of the items approved in the PIP will be of the concerned programme head. Only the items included in the proposal should be purchased.
The project will cover Primary Health Centres-1839, Urban Primary Health Centres-651, Rural Hospitals-362, Sub-District Hospitals (50 and 100 beds)-92, District Hospitals-23, General Hospitals-8, Women Hospitals-15, Psychiatric Hospitals-4, Tuberculosis Hospitals-5, Leprosy Hospitals-4, Referral Hospitals-2, Ortho Hospitals-1, and new functional health institutions from time to time.
As per the guidelines of the Central Government's Free Diagnostic Scheme, 134 tests at the district hospital level, 97 at the rural hospital level (SDH 50+ RH) and 63 at the primary health center level and tests suggested by experts as per local/ state requirements should be included under this project.
5) The estimated cost of the project is Rs. The estimated cost of the project is Rs. 180.00 crore per annum with an annual increment of 3% per annum.
For this project, phlebotomists are to be appointed to take samples (blood/ urine/ stool/ CSF etc.) of patients in the health institution within the time prescribed by the service provider.
Also, the service provider has to set up laboratories at various places in the state.
