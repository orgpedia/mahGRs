The Proposal:
To extend the coverage of the scheme to all citizens for a limited period so that COVID-19 patients do not have to bear the financial burden of getting treatment in an authorized private hospital and to provide health assurance and financial relief to all citizens during the COVID-19 pandemic.
With a view to ensure that the benefits of treatment under Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana reach the beneficiary patients as well as other patients, and the treatment methods reserved for government hospitals are available and the doctors, other staff and ancillary staff of the hospitals empanelled under the scheme are provided with necessary equipment in connection with the prevention of COVID-19 pandemic, a decision has been taken on 23rd May, 2020. Government's decision:
In view of the current number of COVID-19 patients in the state of Maharashtra and the benefits being provided to the citizens of the state under the Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana, in order to provide health assurance and financial relief to all the citizens during the current pandemic crisis, the scheme, which came into effect on May 23, 2020, is being extended for a period of three months from April 1, 2021, to June 30, 2021, excluding 120 treatments opened to private affiliated hospitals.
