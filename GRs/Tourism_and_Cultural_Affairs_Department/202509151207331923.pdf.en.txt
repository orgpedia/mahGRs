Introduction: - The famous ancient temple of Shri Tuljabhavani Maa Ambabai, one of the three and a half Shakti Peethas of Maharashtra in Tuljapur, is famous all over the country. Legend has it that the Bhavani sword was given to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj by his mother Tuljabhavani Devi.
About 50 lakh devotees from different parts of the country including Maharashtra, Karnataka and Telangana state visit this place on these yo days of Shardiya Navratri festival. The city of Tuljapur fades with religious, cultural, and social colours during this period. The festival focuses on organizing a variety of cultural programmes such as songs, bhajans, dances, classical and folk music on Tuljabhavani Maa, evoking devotion to the deity Sri Tuljabhavani Maa Ambabai. It is attended by renowned artists from Maharashtra as well as at the national level. Accordingly, various activities are carried out for 10 days during Navratri by encouraging local artists.
Sri Kshetra Tuljabhavani Devi Temple, Naldurg Fort, Sant Goroba Kaka Ancient Temple at Ter, ASKART Temple at Yermala, Ramalinga Sanctuary at Yedshi, Paranda Bhuikot Fort, etc., are some of the religious and tourist places worth visiting from a tourist point of view. Yedeshwari Devi of Yermala is referred to as the younger sister of Mother Tuljabhavani. Therefore, a devotee who comes to Tuljapur must have a darshan of the goddess ASLAN at Yermala.
