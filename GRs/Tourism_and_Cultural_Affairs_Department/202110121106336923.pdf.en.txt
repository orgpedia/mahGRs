Guidelines for reopening of theatres under" Break the Chain" in the wake of COVID-19.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs
Government Resolution No. 8220/ P. No. 211 (Part-I)/ Sec. 4 Madam Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk,
Ministry (Extension), Mumbai- 400 032.
Date is October 11, 2021
Read: 1) Government Decision Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs, No. Narrow (8220/ P.No.211/ S. 4, dated November 5, 2020 2) Government Order Public Health Department, No. Corona 2021/ Q. No.366/ A-5, dated 11th August, 2021, dated 16th August, 2021 and dated 24th September, 2021
The proposal: The Chief Minister has announced on September 25, 2021, that theatres and cinema halls in the state will be allowed to open after October 22, 2021, in compliance with health rules. In pursuance of this declaration, Reference No. 2 It was under the consideration of the Government to lay down guidelines for opening of theatres in a controlled manner subject to compliance of the preventive measures prescribed for prevention of the spread of COVID-19 virus in the orders issued here. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In the wake of COVID-19, the Government is permitting the opening of theatres in the State in a controlled manner with effect from 22nd October, 2021 subject to the guidelines in Annexure-A and Annexure-I annexed to this Government Decision.
2. Keeping in view the noble intention of the Government behind granting this permission, it is necessary for all concerned to operate the theatres in a manner that they do not violate any of the restrictions of the Central Government and the State Government regarding COVID-19. Action will be taken against those found violating these guidelines.
3. The mandatory instructions in the order issued by the Government from time to time to prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus will continue to apply in this case.
