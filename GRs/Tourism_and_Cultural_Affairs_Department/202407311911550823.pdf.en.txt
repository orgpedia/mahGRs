Terms and Conditions: - 1) The above works should be of quality nature, for which actual works should be done through reputed contractors by appointing architectural experts (1116112 3856 102000) and proper control and quality control should be done of those works.
2) The procedure prescribed under the financial rules and various regulations should be followed while spending from the funds disbursed.

3) The tender process for the works approved by the administrator should be carried out immediately and the work should be ordered to start immediately. It will also be mandatory to complete these works within a period of three years.
4) Care should be taken to ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned subsidy under any circumstances. Expenditure in excess of the sanctioned grant shall be the responsibility of the District Collector concerned and no additional grant shall be made available against the excess cost.
5) The expenditure from the sanctioned provision should be made as per the prevailing orders of the Finance Department and the sanctioned works should be completed immediately.
6) Plaques stating that the works were done from the funds provided by the Department of Tourism under the Regional Tourism Development Scheme should be placed at the worksites.
7) Every scheme in respect of which funds are being made available shall require the approval of the competent administrator and the financial authority in accordance with the return of the financial authority.
8) Every such scheme shall be subject to such procedures as may be required by financial regulations with respect to construction or purchase or service supply contracts to be made. In this regard, the Government Circular, Finance Department, No. The relevant enforcement agencies shall ensure that the instructions of 6th February, 2002 are strictly followed. 9) Technical approval should be taken at the prescribed level.
10) Compliance with the regulations of the Public Works Department will be mandatory in cases of proposed works of cheap cost.
11) Ensure that the said provision is not used for non-plan work (maintenance / repair etc.).
12) It shall be mandatory to obtain a no-objection certificate from the said Devasthan / Institution in respect of the works which are proposed for the development of the pilgrim centres and are to be carried out at the site of the Devasthan / Institute. Further, the Director, Directorate of Tourism and the District Collector concerned shall be responsible for the maintenance / repair of such work after its completion by the concerned agency.
It will be mandatory to enter into an agreement with the Devasthanam / Sanstha. Such a condition should be included in the agreement to note that no further funding will be made available by the government for such maintenance repairs.
