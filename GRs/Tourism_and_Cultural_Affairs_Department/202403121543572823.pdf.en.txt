Preamble: - Ramtek is the city consecrated by the feet of Lord Shri Ram in Nagpur district.
The hilltop Sri Ramaswamy Temple Complex in this Ramtek town is a centre of attraction for devotees and tourists. Recognizing the historical importance of the site, the monuments (temples and gates) in the Sri Ramaswamy Temple complex at Gadamandir Ramtek have been declared as state protected monuments. Among the monuments declared as State Protected Monuments and at the extreme end of the temple complex are the hilltop Ram Mandir, Lakshman Mandir and Ram Kund ancient Yadav Kalin memorials, the memorial being renovated during the Bhosale period. Varah Darwaza, Shirpur Darwaza, Bhairav Darwaza, and Gokul Darwaza are respectively designed to reach the monument. The Varaha Darwaza at the very beginning and the Gokula Darwaza at the end demarcate the Ram and Lakshmana temple complex. Kewal Shirpur Darwaza, a state protected monument at Gadmandir Ramtek in Nagpur division, has been taken up for conservation and repair work at a cost of Rs. 4,30,97, 989 / - to the architect May.
The Detailed Budget prepared by Indrajit and Roopa Nageshkar, Kolhapur is presented by the Director of Detailed Budgeting, Directorate of Archaeology and Museums. Accordingly, a proposal to grant administrative approval to the budget for the conservation and repair work of this state protected monument was under consideration of the government.
