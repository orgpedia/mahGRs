PREAMBLE :-
The Government of Maharashtra regularly participates in various international trade
fairs and exhibitions. Such events provide valuable exposure and offer excellent opportunities for the State to showcase its tourism potential, engage with global stakeholders, and attract investments. In this context, the Director, Directorate of Tourism, has proposed participation
in the International Tourism Exhibition ‘IMEX Frankfurt 2025’ to be held in Germany from 19" to 237" May, 2025. This international event will help promote Maharashtra Tourism by
showcasing its diverse destinations and tourism products, sourcing new services, and
strengthening its global outreach through trade shows, fairs, and public events. Accordingly,
the Tourism Department, Government of Maharashtra, has decided to participate in IMEX Frankfurt 2025, scheduled from 19" to 23™ May, 2025.
RESOLUTION :-
The permission is hereby granted to the concerned delegation to participate in the International Tourism Exhibition ‘IMEX Frankfurt 2025 in Germany from 19" to 23११ May,
2025 (Including Travel Time). The members of the delegation led by Shri. Indranil Naik,
Hon’ble Minister of State for Tourism will be as follows :-
1) Shri. Atul Patne, Principal Secretary, Tourism, Government of Maharashtra.
2) Dr. Bhagwantrao Patil, Director, Directorate of Tourism, Government of Maharashtra.
3) Shri. Santosh Rokade, Deputy Secretary, Tourism, Government of Maharashtra.
2. The Terms and Conditions for participation of delegates are as follows :-
a. The entire period of the visit shall be treated as a period of duty.
b. The members will be entitled to draw full pay and allowances as they would have drawn had they remained on duty in India.
c. The members shall be entitled to T.A./D.A. for the entire duration of the visit to the
said event in Frankfurt, Germany, as per Government rules.
d. They shall also be entitled to claim incidental expenditure to cover local costs such
as passport fees, medical certificates, and travel expenses from their place of duty to the port of embarkation in India, and from the port of disembarkation back to their
place of duty, including applicable charges by the Airports Authority.
