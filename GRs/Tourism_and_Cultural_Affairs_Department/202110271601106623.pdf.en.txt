For the year 2021-22, a budget provision of Rs. 150.00 crore has been made for' Prasiddhi (3452 1931)'. However, for this purpose, the Department of Finance has approved the release of funds amounting to Rs. 45.00 crore i. e. 30% of the budgeted Rs. 150.00 crore for the financial year 2021-22.
Under the Government decision dated 7.1.2019 in Reference No.2, a policy has been formulated to provide financial assistance through sponsorship by the Department of Tourism to events organized by private organizers at national/ international and state levels.
Accordingly, the relevant items in the Annual Publicity Action Plan 2021-22 should be implemented in accordance with the policy set out in Reference No. 2 to the Government Decision dated 7.1.2019.
Terms and Conditions:
1) The Directorate of Tourism shall take prior approval and final approval of the Government for the implementation and expenditure of each of the items approved in the Media Plan. The funds have to be utilised with the approval of the government.
(2) The administrative and financial approval of the competent authority shall be obtained in accordance with the reversal of financial powers in respect of every matter in respect of which funds are being made available.
(3) The procedure prescribed under the Financial Rules and various Regulations should be followed while making expenditure out of the funds disbursed.
4) The Director, Directorate of Tourism will be responsible for ensuring that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned grant in any case. It should not be spent just because the provision is available.
5) The General Administration Department has issued orders by amending the Government Advertisement Distribution Policy and from time to time under the Government Decision in their context. While implementing this plan, action should be taken according to the government decision in the context of the General Administration Department. However, as an exception, the selection of Advertising Agents, Social Media Agencies for carrying out publicity campaigns and organizing various festivals/ events at the Directorate of Tourism level has been approved vide letter dated 8/9/2020 and action should be taken accordingly.
6) Before proposing any commitment, the potential for tourism benefits from the programme/ activity/ activity should be carefully examined first.
7) The expenditure of this amount should be reconciled with the Accountant General's office and a report should be submitted to the government. Expenditure incurred for the purpose is as under: Demand No.ZD 4," 3452-Tourism, 01 Infrastructure for Tourism, 101-Tourism Centres (02) Schemes under Five Year Plans, Schemes under State Plans (02) (04) Publicity (3452 1931) 50 Other Expenditure (Non-Payment)"
