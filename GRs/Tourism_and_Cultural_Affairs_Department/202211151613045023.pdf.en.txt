The Proposal:
In order to make the administration of the state more dynamic and people-oriented, the use of information technology in the administrative work of the state to provide services to the citizens at affordable prices, quickly and transparently. The State e-Governance Policy has been formulated under the Government Resolution No. 1 and a comprehensive action plan has been prepared in this regard. Paragraph 12.3 of the said Government Decision directs the Administrative Departments to constitute Project Implementation Committees to monitor the e-Governance projects.
Reference No.2 for implementation of computerization and e-governance system in Tourism Sub-Division and Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation and all other zonal offices, mandals etc. under this Sub-Division. A Project Implementation Committee has been formed.
Similarly, a Project Implementation Committee was also under consideration to implement the computerization and e-governance projects of the offices under the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs itself as well as the Cultural Affairs Sub-Division. As Tourism Sub-Division and Cultural Affairs Sub-Division are Sub-Divisions of the same Department, for proper coordination, a Project Implementation Committee for Tourism Sub-Division has been constituted vide Reference No. The government was considering the formation of a single project implementation committee for the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs itself and the department offices/ corporations under the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs.

Government's decision:
The Government is approving the constitution of Project Implementation Committee for implementing the project of computerization and e-governance of the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs and the departments/ offices/ corporations under this Department as follows: Designation of officers.
Member Co/ Deputy Secretary (Tourism), Tourism& Cultural Affairs Member Department 8. Co/ Deputy Secretary (Establishment), Tourism& Cultural Affairs Member Department 9. Co/ Deputy Secretary (Registration Branch), Tourism& Culture Member Secretary Department of Works
The Committee may, if necessary, invite officers of the Department/ Subordinate Offices of the Department/ other Departments, experts in specific fields, in addition to the above members, for the meetings of the Project Implementation Committee.
The functions and powers of the Committee shall be as follows: 3.1. To formulate a policy consistent with the e-Governance Policy of the Directorate of Information Technology.
3.2. Determine the scope of all e-governance projects to be implemented by the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs, all zonal offices and corporations and guide the tendering process by finalizing the RFP (Request for Proposal) to start the e-governance project for the department.
3.3. To take a final decision regarding the online functioning of this Department and all the Regional Offices and Corporations under this Department.
3.4. To take decisions for the purchase of necessary hardware/ software etc. as well as development of computer systems required for implementing e-governance policy.

3.5. To select consultants from reputed organizations as per the requirement for various computerization projects to be implemented by this Department and to approve the project proposal in the prescribed manner. 3.6. The Project Implementation Committee shall be empowered as per the decision taken by the Directorate of Information Technology from time to time and as per the authority conferred.
3.7. To advise, coordinate and control and review the e-Governance project.
3.8. To approve proposals for computerization/ IT projects/ procurement of computer materials above Rs. To recommend to the High Powered Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of the Chief Secretary.
Power to grant administrative approval to a project approved by the Project Implementation Committee is vested in the Finance Department, vide Government Resolution No.1013/ P.S. 30/2013/ Regulations, Part II, D. Part I, Sub-Division-III, No.4, Paragraph No.4 of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Annexure 17 of April, 2015. As per section 27 (2) (a), the Tourism and Cultural Affairs Department will continue to function.
The projects sanctioned in this manner within the limit of Rs.5 crore shall not require fresh approval of Finance Department/ Planning Department/ Information Technology Department. However, if there is no consensus on the implementation of any project, such a project will be presented before the High Powered Committee for approval.
