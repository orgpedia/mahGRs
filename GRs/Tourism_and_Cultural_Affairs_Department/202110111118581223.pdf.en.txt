The government is taking preventive measures to control the spread of Covid-19 in the state. The decision to create awareness in the society by the artists in the field of experimental art in the state about the precautions to be taken to prevent this infection and the vaccination to be done accordingly was taken in the above mentioned No. The above decision was taken by the government. Under sub-sections 2, 3 and 4, the directorate has proposed to change various aspects of the said government decision. Accordingly, the No. The matter of overruling the government decision on 1 was under consideration of the government. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
Government Resolution No. By superseding the Government Resolution dated 1st May, 2021, the Government is taking the following decisions:
2. Rs. 3,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Crores only) is being approved through this Government Resolution.

3. Awareness programmes will be conducted by folk artists of different folk art forms, performing artists.
4. Topics to be selected for awareness: Corona Awareness In the wake of Corona, marriage ceremonies in the presence of a minimum number of people, religious matters in the presence of a minimum number of people, festivals, customs, events, festivals, observing the corona rules of the government, use of masks, frequent washing of hands with soap, care of the elderly and young children. The artists have to create awareness through their art on topics such as the government's corona regulations, vaccination, etc. as well as on topics suggested by the collector or the officials authorized by him according to the local situation and need.
Participation and selection of artists by the Collector's office: The selection of artists from each district of the state for this awareness program will be done by the committees headed by the Collector with the help of the regional office under the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations. Corona awareness programs will be controlled by a selection committee headed by the concerned district collector. The committee will monitor the programme. The committee will comprise of the respective district information officers of the Information and Public Relations Department. The District Information Officer is expected to act as a coordinator in this campaign.
6. Payment of work orders and artist's remuneration: The work orders shall be issued to the said artists by the Collector or by the officers authorised by him. After the completion of the program, the concerned collector should take action regarding the payment of funds to the concerned artists in their bank accounts. For this purpose, Rs. 8,30,000/- will be transferred. The entire amount will be deposited in the bank accounts of the beneficiaries.
The program must be completed within 8 days. Also, the utility certificate in this regard should be sent to the Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs, Mumbai within one month.
Certifying Authority: After the performance of the program at the Gram Panchayat level in the village, the Talathi/ Gramsevak of the village will be required to give a certificate to the artist regarding the completion of the program as per the pattern given in Schedule C.

For the demand of remuneration, the artists will have to attach the said certificate with the application in the prescribed format in Annexure A and B, as the case may be. Artists will not have to go to the place of the district to submit applications for honorarium demand, so such applications will be accepted in the concerned Tahsildar office. The Tahsildar of the respective taluk will be responsible for sending the demand letter to the Collector's office within a maximum of five days.
In urban areas, after the performance of the program in group or solo form, the Commissioner or the officer authorized by him in the concerned Municipal Corporation area/ the Chief Officer or the officer authorized by him in the municipal area will be required to give a certificate to the artist according to the form provided in Schedule C regarding the completion of the program.
Artiste honorarium: For awareness programme on COVID-19 infection prevention and vaccination, the honorarium will be paid as follows: 1 Vasudeva, Bahuroopi, etc. The soloist is expected to perform at least 3 performances in a day. A single artist can perform for a maximum of 10 days. That is, a single artist can get a total honorarium of up to five thousand rupees.
2 Groups of two or three artists will be required to perform at least two performances per day, each artist in the group will be paid a remuneration of Rs. A maximum of 10 groups can be entered.
The maximum limit of artists participating in awareness programs in each district will be 166. It will be mandatory for the artists concerned to follow the conditions and rules set by the government from time to time while presenting thematic awareness awareness programs. The responsibility of ensuring that no one is criticized while promoting and spreading this awareness will be on the presenters and artists.
The total expenditure limit for each district for this purpose will be Rs. 8,30,000/-.
5 The artist participating in the promotion and dissemination of awareness should give an undertaking that he is participating on his own responsibility.
