The Proposal: -
Protection of cultural heritage of the state under Article 49 of the Constitution of India.
It is the duty of the state to preserve and conserve. The process of declaring a state protected monument under the provisions of the Maharashtra Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act, 1960 is carried out by the Directorate of Archaeology and Museums under the state government. The Government intends to formulate a plan with the objective of bringing the State Protected Monuments, especially the forts, in a better state of conservation and development of tourism facilities thereon to make these places people-oriented and world-class tourist destinations, as well as promote local employment in the field of tourism and cultural heritage conservation, for conservation of forts and promotion of tourism.
Accordingly, a nomination proposal has been submitted by the Directorate of Archaeology and Museums for a UNESCO World Heritage Nomination for the Maratha lustre architecture in the state of Maharashtra. The proposals are submitted to UNESCO by the Directorate of Archaeology and Museums, Archaeological Survey of India, New Delhi. But before the proposal was submitted to UNESCO, Ma. A proposal to constitute a UNESCO World Heritage Sites Control Committee under the chairmanship of the Chief Secretary has been submitted by the Director, Directorate of Archaeology and Museums, in a letter to the government under reference. Accordingly, the formation of the committee was under the consideration of the government.
The Government's Decisions:
