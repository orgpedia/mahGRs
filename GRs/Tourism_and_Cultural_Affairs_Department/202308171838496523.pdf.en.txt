Read: Letter from the Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs No. 3685/ S.C.C.
Preamble: The administrative and financial sanction for the" Dhrupad" cultural programme and its expenses was under consideration in the name of Padma Shri Shankar Bapu Apegaonkar, who has dedicated his entire life to the service of music by connecting people across India by performing his art in both the Warkari sect and classical Dhrupad through his Pakhawaj.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Directorate of Cultural Affairs organized" Dhrupad" Music Festival in the name of Padmashri Shankar Bapu Apegaonkar from 15th to 17th April, 2023 at Ambajogai, Beed. The total expenditure incurred on organizing the award ceremony is Rs. 9,94,569/- which is being given administrative and financial sanction under this Government decision.
The expenditure is to be met from the financial provision approved for the year 2023-24 under the head of Accounting Demand No.Z-2, 2205, Art and Culture, 102, Art and Culture Operations, (02) State Dance, Drama, Tamasha and Music Festival, (02) (02) State Art, Culture Preservation, Conservation, Various Festivals and Other Programmes (Programmes) (2205 1507).
For this, the Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs is being declared as the Controlling Officer and the Accountant, Directorate of Cultural Affairs as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
4. The said expenditure is covered by the Government Decision No. VIPR-2013/ EXCHANGE, dated the 17th April, 2015, Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-section-3, S. No. 4, Para No. 27 (2) (a) is being sanctioned as per the powers conferred on the administrative department.
In this case, the Government Decision No. 5 of the Finance Department. The Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs, Mumbai will be responsible for the proceedings under all the paragraphs of the Finance Act, 2023/ S.No.40/ E.No.3 dated 12th April, 2023 and the conditions contained in the Annexure.
