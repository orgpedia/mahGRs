02. An operational mechanism is being appointed for the above mentioned works as per the proposal submitted by the Collector, Jalna. Accordingly, the following terms and conditions must be complied with.
Terms and Conditions: - 1) The above works should be of quality, for which the actual works should be done through reputed contractors by appointing architectural experts (1112116 3956 1011601) and proper control and quality control should be done.
2) The procedure prescribed under the financial rules and various regulations should be followed while spending from the funds disbursed.
3) The tender process for the works approved by the administrator should be carried out immediately and the work should be ordered to start immediately. It will also be mandatory to complete these works within a period of three years.

4) Care should be taken to ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned subsidy under any circumstances. In case of excess of the sanctioned grant, the responsibility of the same shall lie with the concerned Collector and no additional grant shall be made available against the excess grant.
5) The work to be done from the sanctioned provision should be done as per the prevailing orders of the Finance Department and the sanctioned works should be completed immediately.
6) Plaques stating that the works were done from the funds provided by the Department of Tourism under the Regional Tourism Development Scheme should be placed at the worksites.
7) Every scheme in respect of which funds are being made available shall require the approval of the competent administrator and the financial authority in accordance with the return of the financial authority.
8) Every such scheme shall be subject to such procedures as may be required by financial regulations with respect to construction or purchase or service supply contracts to be made. In this regard, the Government Circular, Finance Department, No. The concerned enforcement agencies should ensure that the instructions of 6th February, 2002 are strictly followed. Also, the measures in the Government Decision Finance Department No. BGT- 10.00/рдк No. 56/2000 Financial Reforms, dated 10.9.2001 should be implemented precisely.
9) Technical approval should be taken at the prescribed level.
10) Compliance with the regulations of the Public Works Department will be mandatory in cases of proposed works of cheap cost.
11) Ensure that the said provision is not used for non-plan work (maintenance / repair etc.).
12) It shall be mandatory to obtain a no-objection certificate from the said Devasthan / Institution in respect of the other works proposed for development of the pilgrimage sites and to be carried out at the site of the Devasthan / Institute. Further, the Director, Directorate of Tourism and the District Collector concerned shall be responsible for the maintenance / repair of such work after its completion by the concerned agency.
It will be mandatory to enter into an agreement with the Devasthanam / Sanstha. The agreement should include a condition that it should be noted that no further funding will be made available by the government for such maintenance repairs.
The agreement should also include a condition that all facilities and tourist spots created by the work will be open to all citizens.

13) Monthly statements of the physical progress of the works to be carried out from this fund as well as the expenditure to be incurred from the fund should be submitted to the Directorate of Tourism by the 10th of every month without fail. Also, the completion certificate and utilization certificate regarding the funds should be submitted to the government through the Directorate of Tourism without fail after the completion of the work.
14) The Collector, Jalna, shall disburse the funds only after ensuring that the work is not completed from any other scheme / fund. If the sanctioned work has been completed from any other scheme / fund, the funds disbursed to the concerned work should be returned to the government and appropriate action should be taken against the concerned for any irregularity.
15) While demanding the remaining funds, the utility certificate B should be physically inspected by the quality control system of the work and a certified report (with photographs) on the quality of the work and the current status of the work should be submitted to the government.
16) After the completion of the work, the management of the work and other ancillary works shall be the responsibility of the implementing agency and the concerned agencies.
17) Plan in advance while the work is in progress so that the tourist facility can be used immediately after the work is completed.
Expenditure on works in the above table shall be distributed from the approved provision under the head "Demand No. ZD-4, 3452 - Tourism, Infrastructure for Tourism, 1019 Tourism Centres (02) Schemes under Five Year Plans - Schemes under State Schemes, (02) (198) Grants-in-aid for basic amenities for various places for tourism development (3452 1958) 31 Supplementary Grants." Orders regarding the distribution of funds for these works will also be issued separately.
The Assistant District Planning Officer / District Planning Officer, Jalna is being declared as the "Drawing and Disbursing Officer" and the Collector, Jalna as the "Controlling Officer" for the purpose of drawing and disbursing the amount of grant-in-aid sanctioned in this case (under the Regional Tourism Development Scheme) to the concerned. The amount of grant sanctioned as per the said Government Decision shall be disbursed by them to the concerned by submitting the payments at the District Treasury Office, Jalna. A compliance report on this action should be submitted to the government by the Directorate of Tourism within a month.
