Preface: - Trimbakeshwar is one of the 12 Jyotirlinga Jagat Devasthanas in the country and one of the most sacred places in South India. A number of religious events such as Simhastha Kumbh Mela every twelve years, Ganga / Godavari festival in the month of Magha, Mahashivaratri festival in the month of Magha, Yatra of Sant Shrestha Parnishtinath Maharaj, Shravan month circumambulation attract a large number of devotees / pilgrims and tourists for pilgrimage tourism. The event has a specific religious and tourist significance as the Simhastha Kumbh Mela is scheduled to be held at Trimbakeshwar from 2026 to 2028. Special temples have been built on the site of this tourist spot and have a special religious and spiritual significance.
2. Considering the tourist significance of the place, the reference No. Trimbakeshwar, Nashik has been given the status of a "Category B" tourist destination by the Government of India. Reference No. The Government decision at 03 sets out the criteria for selecting tourist destinations in the state and declaring them as "A" category tourist destinations. Accordingly, the District Collector, Nashik has issued a circular to the Tribak Municipal Council, Trimbakeshwar District Nashik regarding the status of "A * Category Tourist Site." The proposal was submitted under letter 4 here.
3. Accordingly, the issue of granting the status of "A" tourist destination to Tribak Municipal Council, Trimbakeshwar Nashik was under consideration of the Government as it met the criteria required for "A" category tourist destination status.
The Government's Decisions:
Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs, Government Decision, No. TDS - 2015/12 Q. No.1065 / Tourism, Govt. February 22, 2016 and the clean-up. As on 08.03.2016, Tribak Municipal Council, Trimbakeshwar Nashik is being accorded the status of A Category Tourist Site as per the criteria and HAA prescribed for declaring it as "A" Category Tourist Site in the State.
