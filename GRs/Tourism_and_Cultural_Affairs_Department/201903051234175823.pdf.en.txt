The approval granted under this Government Decision is subject to the following terms and conditions:
Terms and Conditions: 1) The expenditure on the project should be incurred only after the budgetary provision for the project is approved and the receipt is made available.
2) Separate funds for maintenance and repair of the property will not be provided by the Department of Tourism. Care should be taken that this provision is not used for non-plan works (maintenance/ repair etc.).
(3) The procedure prescribed under the Financial Rules and various Regulations shall be followed while making expenditure out of the funds disbursed.

(9) Every such scheme shall be governed by the financial rules relating to construction or procurement or contract for supply of services. 11) In this case, it will be mandatory to obtain a no-objection certificate from the appropriate officer or institution.
12) The Project Consultant shall be appointed by the Collector, Mumbai City, as per rules.
Monthly reports on the physical progress of the project works and the expenditure incurred from the funds received should be submitted to the government without fail by the 10th of every month. Also, after the completion of the work, the certificate of completion and the utility certificate regarding the funds should be submitted to the Tourism Department through the Collector, Mumbai City without fail.
After the completion of this work, the responsibility of maintenance of this work and other ancillary works will be of the Collector, Mumbai City.
The Collector, Mumbai City will be responsible for submitting utilization certificates of the funds received to the Government and the Accountant General's Office, as well as for issuing completion certificates of the work.

17) It will be mandatory to obtain a no-objection certificate from the concerned temple/ institution in respect of the works proposed to be done on the site of the temple/ institution for the development of the pilgrimage sites. Also, it will be mandatory for the District Collector to enter into an agreement with the concerned Devasthan/ institution for the maintenance/ repair to be carried out by the concerned institution after the completion of such work.
It should be noted that the government will no longer provide any funds for such maintenance repairs and such a condition should be included in the contract. The agreement should also include the condition that all facilities and tourist places created by this work will be open to all citizens.
After the completion of this work, the responsibility of maintenance of this work and other ancillary works will be of the Public Works Department and related agencies along with the Collector, Mumbai City.
The expenditure mentioned above shall be met from the budgetary provision for the year 2017-18 under the head' Demand No.ZD-4,3452-Tourism, Infrastructure for Tourism, 101 Tourist Centres (02) Schemes under Five Year Plans-Schemes under State Schemes, (02) (18) Grants for basic amenities for various places for tourism development (3452 1958) 31 Subsidiary Grants'. The amount of grant sanctioned as per this decision should be disbursed to the concerned by submitting the payments to the concerned district treasury office.
