Reference:- 1. Letter dated 11/05/2022 from the Director, Central Board of Secondary Education.
2.M. Letter from the Union Minister of Tourism, Ministry of Tourism, New Delhi. 29/05/2023.
Preamble: Ministry of Tourism, Government of India is establishing Youth Tourism Clubs as part of Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav. Youth Tourism Boards are to be set up to create young ambassadors of Indian Tourism who will be globally known as the torchbearers of tourism, rich heritage and culture of the country. This will help in inculcating the spirit of team spirit, managerial leadership and service oriented attitude among students and youth and creating awareness about sustainable and responsible tourism.
The formation of Youth Tourism Clubs for students from class 7 onwards in all recognized schools/ junior colleges and schools in the state was under the consideration of the government.
Government circular:
As part of Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav, as per the directions of Ministry of Tourism, Government of India, Youth Tourism Clubs are to be set up for students from class 7 onwards in all recognized schools/ junior colleges and schools in the State.
The School Education and Sports Department and the Higher and Technical Education Department should take necessary action in this regard.
2. A copy of the Handbook brought out by the Central Government for this purpose is attached. In addition to this, various initiatives for promotion and dissemination of tourism through the Youth Tourism Board will be required to implement the guidelines/ policies issued in this regard from time to time on the website of the Ministry of Tourism, Government of India.
In the financial year 2023-24, as an incentive grant for the formation of Youth Tourism Boards, Rs. 10,000/- and Rs. A total of Rs 25,000. 25.00 crore to the Directorate of Tourism on a first-come-first-serve basis.
