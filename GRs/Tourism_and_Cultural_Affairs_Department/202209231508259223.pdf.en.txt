Every year, September 17 is celebrated as Marathwada Liberation Day. On this day, the Nizam's rule in Marathwada ended and the region became a part of India. Thirteen months after Indian independence, on 17 September 1948, when the Indian Army invaded the Hyderabad State, on the same day, the army chief of Hyderabad surrendered on 17 September 1948, and the Nizam himself surrendered. At the same time, the Hyderabad Liberation War was successful and the national flag was hoisted in the state of Hyderabad.
The matter of approving the program to be held on September 17, 2022 on the occasion of Marathwada Liberation Day and its expenditure was under the consideration of the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Government have accorded approval for the" Amrit Mahotsav Marathwada Cultural Programme" to be held at Aurangabad on 17th September, 2022 on the occasion of Marathwada Liberation Day and the expenditure of Rs. 10,00,000/- (Rupees Ten Lakhs only) as mentioned in the table above.
