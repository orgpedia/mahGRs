Read:- Government Decision, Tourism and Cultural Affairs Department No. NAASA 4815/ P.No.117/ S. 4th of October, 2015
Preamble: Under the above Government decision, a Drama Screening Committee was constituted under the Grant-in-Aid Scheme for new theatre productions to theatre production societies. Since the term of the said Drama Screening Committee had expired, the matter of reconstitution of the new Drama Screening Committee was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Under the Grant-in-Aid Scheme for Professional and Experimental Dramas, the following Dramatic Screening Committees (Professional, Musical and Experimental) are being constituted.
The examining committee to be formed to examine the professional and musical as well as experimental dramas is as follows.
Dinu Pednekar, Mumbai Member 9. Shri Arvind Aundhe, Mumbai Member 10.Sh. Sunil Deolekar, Mumbai Member 13. Shri Digambar Prabhu, Mumbai Member 14.Smt.Shital Kshirsagar, Mumbai Member

In this committee, the Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs is being appointed as the Member Secretary of the committee for organizing the meetings of the committee. However, since the examination of the plays must be done by experts, the director, cultural affairs, who is an administrative officer, will not have the right to examine the play.
The term of office of these Committees shall be for a period of three years from the date of issue of this Government Resolution or until such period as the Committee may be reconstituted.
The members of the Drama Screening Committee will be paid an honorarium of Rs.500/-per play and their travel expenses will be sanctioned as per the AC two-tier travel rate of the Railways.
Arrangements for their stay and food will be made by the Directorate of Cultural Affairs.
If the members of the Drama Screening Committee are associated with the writing/ direction/ acting/ technical/ music or other aspects of the play applied for the grant, then the said member will not be able to examine the relevant play.
At least 11 out of 22 members must be present to judge the play.
7. Meeting allowance to non-official members of the Committee shall be admissible in accordance with the prevailing Government decision of the Finance Department.
For this, the Director, Directorate of Cultural Affairs is being declared as the Controlling Officer and the Accountant, Directorate of Cultural Affairs as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
