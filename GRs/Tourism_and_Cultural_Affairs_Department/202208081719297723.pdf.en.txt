The Proposal:
The Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum, Pune is a joint venture between the Government of Maharashtra and Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum, Pune. 19.3.1962 and thereafter. This is in accordance with the agreement signed on 12.04.1975. According to the agreement, the museum is owned by the government and the museum is managed by the state. The Board of Management of the Museum is headed by the Chief Secretary. The institute is being provided grant-in-aid by the government towards rent, taxes and management expenses of the museum space in Pune. As the institution was not registered at present, the matter of registering the museum under the existing law was under the consideration of the government.
The State Museum is envisaged in the Budget Speech of 2021. Accordingly, the Tourism and Cultural Affairs Department has decided to construct a State Museum. The State Museum Advisory Committee was constituted under the Government decision dated 28th April, 2021. The committee has submitted its report. The matter of deciding the place for the planned State Museum is under consideration of the Government. Survey No., provided by Government in the name of Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum, Pune. Since the 6 acre site at 23, Mauje Bawdhan Budruk, Taluka Mulshi, District Pune is spacious and has not been constructed yet, it is technically necessary to study whether the State Museum can be built on this site.
