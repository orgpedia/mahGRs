Introduction: There is a lot of scope for tourism in the state and tourists need a good guide to get the best and correct information about the diverse tourist places and make their visit enjoyable and unforgettable. At the same time, it is possible to properly implement the local rules and safety measures through the guides. In this regard, the Director, Directorate of Tourism vide letter dated 24.08.2021 in Reference No.2 has requested to approve the implementation of Tourist Facilitator (Guide) Training Programme at selected tourist places in the State and to provide necessary funds for the said training programme. Accordingly, the matter of approving the implementation of the tourist facilitator (guide) training program in the selected tourist places and providing the necessary funds to the Director, Directorate of Tourism for the same was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: To implement the Tourist Facilitator (Guide) Training Program in the selected tourist places of the state and the training program required for this, as mentioned in the following table, a total of Rs. 56.00 lakhs (including taxes) is being sanctioned to the Director, Directorate of Tourism subject to the following terms and conditions:

(2) Terms and conditions:
(i) The procedure prescribed under the Financial Rules and various Orders/ Regulations shall be followed while making expenditure out of the funds disbursed.
2) It should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned grant under any circumstances. If the expenditure exceeds the sanctioned grant, the responsibility will be of the Director, Directorate of Tourism, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and no additional grant will be provided for the excess expenditure.
3) The sanctioned funds should be spent in the financial year 2021-22 and the process of implementing the training program should be done immediately.
4) Competent administrative and financial approval is required to be obtained in accordance with the reversal of financial powers in respect of the scheme for which funds are being made available.
5) Necessary permissions and technical approvals should be taken at the prescribed level.
6) The Director, Directorate of Tourism, Maharashtra State, Mumbai will be responsible for submitting the utilization certificates of the funds distributed to the government and the Accountant General's office, as well as the certificate of completion of the works.
The expenditure mentioned above shall be met from the budgetary provision for the year 2021-22 under the head Demand No.ZD-4,3452-Tourism, Infrastructure for Tourism, 101 Tourist Centres (02) Schemes under Five Year Plans-Schemes under State Schemes, (02) (18) Grants for basic amenities for various places for tourism development (3452 1958) 31, Subsidiary Grants. The Chief Accounts Officer, Directorate of Tourism, Maharashtra State, Mumbai is being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Joint Director, Directorate of Tourism, Maharashtra State, Mumbai as the Controlling Officer for drawing and disbursing the amount of Subsidiary Grants sanctioned. The amount of grant sanctioned as per this decision should be disbursed to the concerned by submitting the payments to the Admiralty and Accounts Officer, Mumbai and the compliance report on the proceedings should be submitted to the government within one month.
