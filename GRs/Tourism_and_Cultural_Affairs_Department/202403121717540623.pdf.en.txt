Preamble: - Ramtek, a town consecrated by the feet of Lord Shri Ram in Nagpur district, the hilltop Sri Ramaswamy Temple complex in Ramtek town is a centre of attraction for devotees and tourists. Recognizing the historical importance of the site, it has been declared as a state protected monument by the state government. At the extreme end of the temple complex are the hilltop Ram Mandir, Lakshman Mandir, and Ram Kund, ancient Yadava Kalin monuments, all of which appear to have been renovated during the Bhonsle period. The Varah Darwaza, Shirpur Darwaza, Bhairav Darwaza and Gokul Darwaza are respectively designed to reach the monument, with the Varah Darwaza at the very beginning and the Ram and Lakshman temple precincts bounded by the last Gokul Darwaza. In the same order, the Gokul Darwaza is preceded by the wider Bhairav Darwaza, which has strong bastions on either side and is connected to the Darwaza by a rampart. Kewal Bhairav Darwaza, Gadamandir Ramtek, G. Nagpur, a protected monument, was taken up for preservation and repair work in May. Made by Indrajit and Rupa Nageshkar, Kolhapur, Rs.
