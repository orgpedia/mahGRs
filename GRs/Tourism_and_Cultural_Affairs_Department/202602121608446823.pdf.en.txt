02. Research on cutlery in Konkan, discovery of new sites and art of cutlery.
A World Heritage Desk is being set up for documentation. The year of the desk is 2026.
It will be operational for the period 2029. Under the World Heritage Desk 1) Mumbai and 2) Ratnagiri.
There will be two branches. The roles and responsibilities of these branches are as follows:
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| Branch    | Roles and Responsibilities                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Mumbai    | To undertake an in-depth review of the literature on cutlery in India and internationally, to study historical maps, satellite images and archival tools, and to create a database of cutlery. To develop methods and standards in accordance with UNESCO guidelines for the inspection, registration, classification and conservation assessment of cutlery. To prepare research articles, conference presentations and policy reports. In addition, to work on World Heritage Sites as prescribed by the Director, Directorate of Archaeology and Museums.                                                                                  |
| Ratnagiri | The work of the Research Desk at Ratnagiri is mainly based on field inspection, site verification and local participation. 2. Systematic field inspection of cuttings, GPS based mapping, photography and drone documentation, as well as study of geographical and cultural context of the sites. 3. Collection of local traditions, folklore and place-maps, assessment of the status of the sites and threats (erosion, human intervention, etc.), making preliminary recommendations for conservation and protection. 4. Creating awareness by interacting with local communities and developing local participation for site inspection. |
