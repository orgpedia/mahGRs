2. Alibaug has performed the task of protecting the sea borders of the state during the Swarajya period of Maharashtra with great bravery. Alibaug is a beautiful beach town on the western coast of Maharashtra. Alibag is home to historical tourist sites like the historic Colaba Fort of the Shiv period on the sea, the Samadhi of Sarkhel Kanhoji Raje Angre, the 150-year-old British-era observatory, ancient temples of the Shiv period such as Shrikankeshwar, Shri Siddheshwar, Shri Rameshwar, Shri Balaji Temple, Shri Kashi Vishweshwar Temple, Jewish Synagogue, Hirakot Fort and Lake, Khanderi and Undheri Forts, etc.
The sea shore and the sea-side Shiva-era Colaba Fort, Hirakot Lake, Khanderi Andheri Fort, Sarkhela Kanhoji Raje Angre's Samadhi, Shriknakeshwar, Shri Siddheshwar, ShriRameshwar, as well as the wide beaches, Shri Balaji Temples and Devasthanas of Alibaug are said to be about 350 years old. The British Museum is about 150 years old. Many Shiva temples and ancient shrines can still be seen in Alibaug city and Panchkroshi.

Government Resolution No: TDS- 2020/07/ PR No.400/ Tourism, 4. As per letter dated 14/12/2020, it is recommended to accord' B' category tourist destination status to the tourist destination" Alibaug, G. Raigad." Accordingly, the government was considering to give' B' category status to Alibaug, G. Raigad.
Government Decision: Approximately 8 to 10 lakh tourists visit the tourist spot" Alibag, G. Raigad" every year. Considering the state level importance of this tourist place, the number of tourists visiting this tourist place, historical and geographical importance etc., the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs, Government vide Decision No. TDS- 2016/11/ P.S. 862/ Tourism, DTHS, Govt. The Government hereby approves declaration of Alibaug, G. Raigad as a Category' B' tourist destination as per the criteria and procedure prescribed for declaration of' B' tourist destinations in the State under 25/4/2017.
