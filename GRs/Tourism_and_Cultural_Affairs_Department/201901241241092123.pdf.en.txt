Government Decision: To promote the development and promotion of tourist destinations in the State of Maharashtra at national and international level and to celebrate various tourism festivals, an outlay of Rs. 78.00 crore has been budgeted for the Department of Tourism for the financial year 2018-2019 under the heading" Publicity." As per the Government Circular dated 2.4.2018 of the Department of Finance, 70% of the budget outlay i.e. Rs. 54.60 crore is available for disbursement as per the Monthly Fund Distribution System.
Gram Panchayat Arnala, Tt. during 2nd and 3rd February, 2019. The Government hereby approve the release of funds to the tune of Rs. 5.00 lakhs (Rupees Five Lakhs only) to the District Collector, Palghar for the Tourism Festival to be held at Vasai, District Palghar during the financial year 2018-19, subject to the following terms and conditions.
Terms and Conditions: 1. The Collector shall inspect the festival/ programme events and ensure that the funds given are spent for the purpose of organising the same festival.
Competent administrative and financial approval is required to be obtained in respect of each scheme in respect of which funds are being made available, subject to the reversal of financial powers.
(3) The procedure prescribed under the Financial Rules and various Regulations shall be followed while making expenditure out of the funds disbursed.
4. The Collector shall be responsible for ensuring that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned grant in any case and the excess expenditure shall not be reimbursed by the Government.
(5) Every such scheme shall be governed by the financial rules relating to construction or procurement or contract for supply of services. the government in this regard
The concerned enforcement agencies should ensure that the instructions of the circular, Finance Department, No. 1/ No. 15/ Treasurer, dated 6th February, 2002 are strictly followed. Further, the measures contained in the Government Resolution, Department of Finance, No. BGT- 10.00/ P.S. No. 56/2000/ Financial Reforms, dated 10.9.2001 should be implemented in letter and spirit.
