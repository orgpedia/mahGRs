Government Decision: An amount of Rs. 65.00 crore has been budgeted for the financial year 2017-2018 to promote the development of tourism destinations in the State of Maharashtra at national and international level and to celebrate various tourism festivals. Out of this, 70% i.e. Rs. 45.50 crore was available for disbursement at the end of December, 2017 as per the Monthly Fund Distribution System. Out of this, under the Government decision dated 20.1.2018, a sum of Rs. 12.25 crore has been sanctioned by approving the part of Maharashtra Tourism Publicity Media Plan 2017-18.
The Finance Department has approved the expenditure of the balance funds allocated to the Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation for the publicity of 2016-17 from the available funds for the publicity of 2017-18. However, the Finance Department had informed that the same amount would be reduced from the available funds for this year's publication. The balance amount for 2016-17 was Rs. 8.49 crore.
The Finance Department had initially provided for the disbursement of 70% of the budgeted funds.
Accordingly, 70% of the total budgeted amount of Rs. 65.00 crore i.e. Rs. 45.50 crore was available for fund disbursement. But now, as per the circular dated 6.2.2018 of the Finance Department at Reference No.6, 80% of the total outlay of Rs. 65.00 crore i.e. Rs. 52.00 crore has been made available for disbursement. Of this, Rs. 12.25 crore has been spent.

The Government hereby approves payment of Rs. 31.26 crore (Rupees Thirty One Crore Twenty Six Lakh only) to Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation subject to the following terms and conditions:
Terms and Conditions: (1) The provision made by the Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation in accordance with the above Media Plan shall be made with the approval of the Chairman, Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation after following the prescribed procedure. The funds not earmarked for specific purposes will be used with the approval of the government.
2) The funds sanctioned for the publicity should be spent by the Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation as per the plan mentioned above.
(3) Competent administrative and financial approval is required to be obtained in respect of each scheme in respect of which funds are being made available, subject to the reversion of financial powers.
