The land of Maharashtra is endowed with a very ancient and rich cultural heritage, which includes caves like Ajanta-Verul, which are well known as world heritage, forts like Raigad and Sindhudurg, which stood during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, temples like Shri Markadeya and Trimbakeshwar, which stood during the Yadava and Maratha period and were adorned with beautiful sculptures, medieval dargahs and tombs, as well as colonial architecture. Among the historical and ancient monuments, 288 monuments have been preserved as national importance by the Archaeological Survey of India. Also, 386 monuments have been declared as protected by the Cultural Affairs Department of Maharashtra State through the Directorate of Archaeology and Museums. These include the caves of Ghatotkach and Dharashiv, forts like Rajgad, Sinhagad, Manikgad, and forts like Garh Jejuri, Niransinghpur, Shri.
Temples like Tulja Bhavani, birth places of great men like Lokmanya Tilak, Swatantryaveer Savarkar and monuments like Gateway of India. As the funds available for conservation of all protected monuments in the State level schemes are very limited, the Government has approved under the Contextual Government Decision to allocate 3% funds for the next three years from the financial year 2023-2024 under the District Annual Plan (General) for conservation of State Protected Monuments. Accordingly, the matter of issuing detailed guidelines regarding the allocation of this fund was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: Guidelines are being finalized for the allocation of 3% funds available for the next three years from the financial year 2023-24 under the District Annual Plan (Sarva Sadhana) for the conservation of forts, temples and important protected monuments etc.in the State

(1) The conservation and preservation of the State protected monuments in the State shall be undertaken from this fund.
The Assistant Director, Archaeology Department shall prepare a detailed district-wise proposal of the districts under his command for undertaking works under the District Annual Plan (General).
3. While preparing the proposal, the following factors should be considered in the selection of works. Historical, Archaeological and Architectural significance of the monument. Period of monuments, iii. Present status of the memorial, iv. The number of tourists/ devotees visiting, the demand of V.M. representatives/ local citizens/ researchers,
The following activities can be taken up from this fund. Conservation and Protection, ii. Protection, iii. Maintenance and repair, iv. Facilities for tourists/ devotees (toilets, drinking water etc.) Decoration, vi. Chemical preservation work, vii. information boards, direction boards, etc.
The Director, Directorate of Archaeology and Museums will provide technical approval to the works proposed by the Assistant Director, Department of Archaeology.
The District Collector and Member Secretary, District Planning Committee shall accord administrative approval to the proposals given technical approval by the Director, Directorate of Archaeology and Museums.
The Assistant Director, Archaeological Department will be responsible for the implementation of the works under this scheme.
After administrative approval under the said scheme, the necessary funds shall be disbursed by the concerned District Collector to the concerned Assistant Director, Archaeological Department as indicated in the following table, according to the progress of the work-
