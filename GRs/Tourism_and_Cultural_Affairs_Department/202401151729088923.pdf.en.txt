The Proposal: -
British naval officer Captain James Inchward describes the Golden Fort as being built on an irregular island about one-fourth of a mile from the mainland. The fortification is bisected in solid rock, complemented by large stone slabs about four feet square in size. It's so hard that many bullets hit him.
Stuck adjacent, he also describes the Goa fort as having 44 cannons.
Goa Fort was described as being in good condition in 1862. The fort, which had been under Peshwa control since the Battle of Suvarnadurg in 1775, was taken over by the British in 1817. There is also a large tomb of a Roman Catholic church and cemetery on the fort. At present, the fort's retaining wall has collapsed due to the impact of strong sea waves. Two towers have also collapsed. Trees have grown. Considering these aspects, the Director, Directorate of Archaeology and Museums has sanctioned a sum of Rs. 6,71,92, 381 / -
