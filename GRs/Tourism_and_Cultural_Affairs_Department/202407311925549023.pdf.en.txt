02. An operational mechanism is being appointed for the above mentioned works as per the proposal submitted by the Collector, Thane. Accordingly, the following terms and conditions must be complied with.
Terms and Conditions: - 1) The above works should be of quality, for which the actual works should be done through reputed contractors by appointing architectural experts (1112116 3956 1111601) and proper control and quality control should be done.
2) The procedure prescribed under the financial rules and various regulations should be followed while spending from the funds disbursed.
3) The tender process for the works approved by the administrator should be carried out immediately and the work should be ordered to start immediately. It will also be mandatory to complete these works within a period of three years.

4) Care should be taken to ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned subsidy under any circumstances. In case of excess of the sanctioned grant, it shall be the responsibility of the District Collector concerned. No additional grant shall be made available to meet the excess cost.
5) The work to be done from the sanctioned provision should be done as per the prevailing orders of the Finance Department and the sanctioned works should be completed immediately.
6) Plaques stating that the works were done from the funds provided by the Department of Tourism under the Regional Tourism Development Scheme should be placed at the worksites.
7) Every scheme in respect of which funds are being made available shall require the approval of the competent administrator and the financial authority in accordance with the return of the financial authority.
8) Every such plan shall be subject to such procedures as may be required by financial regulations with respect to construction or purchase or service supply contracts. In this regard, the Government Circular, Finance Department, No. The concerned enforcement agencies should ensure that the instructions of 6th February, 2002 are strictly followed. Also, the measures in the Government Decision Finance Department No. BGT- 10.00/рдк No. 56/2000 Financial Reforms, dated 10.9.2001 should be implemented precisely.
9) Technical approval should be taken at the prescribed level.
10) Compliance with the regulations of the Public Works Department will be mandatory in cases of proposed works of cheap cost.
11) Ensure that the said provision is not used for non-plan work (maintenance / repair etc.).
12) It shall be mandatory to obtain a no-objection certificate from the said Devasthan / Institution in respect of the other works proposed for development of the pilgrimage sites and to be carried out at the site of the Devasthan / Institute. Also, on completion of such work, it will be mandatory for the Director, Directorate of Tourism and the concerned Collector to enter into an agreement with the concerned Devasthan / Institution regarding the maintenance / repair to be carried out by the concerned organization. The agreement should include a condition that it should be noted that no further funding will be made available by the government for such maintenance repairs.
The agreement should also include a condition that all facilities and tourist spots created by the work will be open to all citizens.

13) Monthly statements of the physical progress of the works to be carried out from this fund as well as the expenditure to be incurred from the fund should be submitted to the Directorate of Tourism by the 10th of every month without fail. 17) Plan in advance while the work is in progress so that the tourist facility can be used immediately after the work is completed.
