Introduction: Shivneri Fort, the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, as well as historical forts, caves, famous temples, mausoleums, Devraya, ghats, waterfalls, famous rivers, dams, wells, creeks, historical structures, bells ringing stones, World Heritage Site at Khodad, Vikram Communication Satellite at Arvi, plateaus, ravines, Kukdi river at Manikdoh, Kukdi river at Ranjana Khachkharg, Borigaon Earthquake Ash, famous factories, agro-tourism centre, Tamasha Pandhari Narayangaon, Asia's first winery at Chato Indage, Leopard Remediation Centre at Manikdoh, Food Culture, Natural Bridge, Belhe Village
Government Resolution, Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs, No.TDS 2015/11/ PR No. 1021/ Tourism, No. 2 of the Government Decision, Tourism Development of Special Tourist Districts/ Regions in Table-1, Table-4, Paragraph-4, 4.1, dated 4.5.2016 provides that other Special Tourist Regions/ Estates may be notified by the State as may be required. As per this provision, the request for declaring Junnar taluka of Pune district as a special tourism zone was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: From the tourism point of view, Junnar taluka of Pune district has the following characteristics: 1. Junnar is the only taluka with the highest number of 350 caves, seven forts with natural beauty, including Shivneri Fort, the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.
