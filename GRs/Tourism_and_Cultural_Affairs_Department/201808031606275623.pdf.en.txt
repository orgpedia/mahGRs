The Central Government has expressed displeasure over the closure of visitor centres at Ajanta and Verul in Aurangabad district and has directed to make these centres operational immediately. The International Buddhist Conclave 2018, August Conclave 2018 is being organized by the Government of India from 25th to 22nd August, 2018. 23rd day of the conference. Maharashtra has been included in this. 8th day of 2018.
The Government was considering providing funds for the immediate operationalisation of the two Visitor Centres at Ajanta and Verul, to be held at Aurangabad.
Government Decision: An outlay of Rs. 175.00 crore has been budgeted for 2018-19 under Regional Tourism Schemes for providing infrastructure facilities at tourist destinations. As per the Government Circular dated 2.4.2018 of the Department of Finance, 70% of the budget outlay i.e. Rs. 122.50 crore is available for disbursement at the end of the month of December, 2018 as per the Monthly Fund Distribution System.
2. It is hereby approved to disburse a sum of Rs. 5.00 crore (Rupees Five Crores only) to Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation (MTDC) for operationalization of Visitor Centres at Ajanta and Verul in Aurangabad District at a cost of Rs. 2.50 crore each for AVC (Application Visibility and Control) and EVC (Electronic Visibility and Control).

The approval granted under this Government Resolution is subject to the following terms and conditions: 1) The expenditure out of the funds disbursed shall be as per the procedures prescribed under the Financial Rules and various Regulations.
2) It should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the sanctioned grant under any circumstances.
If the expenditure is more than the sanctioned grant, it will be the responsibility of the Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation and no additional grant will be provided for the excess expenditure.
Also, the operational mechanism will remain the same as mentioned in the earlier order.
The expenditure to be incurred from the sanctioned allocation should be made by 31st March, 2019 and the sanctioned works should be completed immediately.
4) Information boards of the Tourism Department should be installed at the workplaces of the Tourism Department.
v) Competent administrative and financial approvals are required to be obtained in accordance with the delegation of financial powers in respect of each scheme in respect of which funds are being made available.
(6) Every such scheme shall be governed by the procedures required under the financial rules relating to construction or procurement or contract for supply of services. In this regard, the concerned enforcement agencies should ensure strict compliance of the instructions of the Government Circular, Finance Department, No.
Further, the measures contained in the Government Decision Finance Division No. BGT- 10.00/ P.S. No. 56/2000/ Fiscal Reforms, dated 10.9.2001 should be implemented precisely.
7) Technical approval should be taken at the prescribed level.
8) It will be mandatory to follow the rules of the Public Works Department in the cases of proposed works of roads.
(9) It shall be ensured that the said provision is not used for non-plan works (maintenance repairs etc.).
10) No-objection certificate from the concerned Devasthan/ Institution in respect of the works proposed to be carried out on the site of the Devasthan/ Institution for the development of the pilgrimage sites.
