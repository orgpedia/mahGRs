The Proposal:
Shri Bhawani Mata Temple, located at Pardi, Punapur Road in Nagpur district, is a place of great religious, cultural and tourist importance. The temple enjoys immense faith from local devotees as well as devotees from all over the Vidarbha region. Bhavani Mata is known as the village deity of Pardi village. Goddess Bhavani is considered to be the embodiment of Shakti, worshipped and vatsal for the welfare of the people.
Also, according to the information given by the Shri Bhavani Mata Seva Samiti, the idol of the goddess has appeared spontaneously at the place. The legend of Lord Shri Parashuram is behind the establishment of the present temple. Temple-like rice temples at Pardi are found only in Mahur, Ambejogai, and Amravati in Maharashtra. The temple was located in a 10,710 feet plot in a field in Punarur from Pardi village. Regarding the temple concerned, the village elders also state that the temple existed from before their birth.

The authority to select and submit proposals to the Government for category 'B' tourist spots in the district has been delegated to the Committee headed by the Collector in the Government Decision at Reference No. 2, Government Decision at Reference No. 9. 3. Considering the religious, cultural and tourist significance of the place as well as the large presence of devotees and tourists, it was under the consideration of the Government to accord the status of SP Class Tourist Site to Shri Bhawani Mata Tourist Site Pardi, Punapur Road TJ Nagpur.
