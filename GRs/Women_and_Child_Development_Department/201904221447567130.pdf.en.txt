The Proposal:
Facilities in government schools Public Interest Litigation No. 28/2014 filed in the High Court, Bench, Nagpur regarding the filling up of vacancies of Superintendents in Children's Homes. The High Court issued orders. Accordingly, these posts will be filled up from the candidates recommended for appointment directly by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission and to some extent through the nearest general transfer. It was brought to the notice of the High Court that the process of general transfers should be done within 4 weeks. The High Court has passed the order dated 6.3.2019. Accordingly, the matter of general transfers of Group-B officers in the Department of Women and Child Development to the posts of Child Development Project Officer/ Superintendent/ District Women and Child Development Officer and similar posts was under consideration.
The Government Order:
In the case of officers proposed to be transferred with the approval of the authorities who are liable to be transferred in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Regulation of Transfers of Government Employees and Prevention of Delay in Discharging Government Duties Act, 2005, the following officers of the department shall be appointed in their name in accordance with the provisions of section 4 (1), 4 (2) and 4 (3) of the said Act, as per the following rules: 4 The places shown here are being transferred in the interest of public service and for administrative reasons.

Amravati, Shri.
Babu Hauserao Nagargoje, Superintendent, Government Children's Senior Children's Home, G. Aurangabad, has been given one year extension in the existing post.
Commissioner, Women and Child Development Commissionerate, Pune and Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Navi Mumbai are hereby informed that orders for posting of their subordinates to the above-designated officers shall be issued forthwith. The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune and Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Navi Mumbai should relieve the transferred officers from their present charge and ask them to join immediately in place of their appointment. The concerned should ensure that their next salary and allowances will be withdrawn on the post they hold. Offices whose heads are transferred should submit a proposal to the government for disciplinary action against those officers who do not relieve the transferred officers within the stipulated time.
However, the officers whose services have been requisitioned for the Lok Sabha Elections-2019 shall be required to perform the duties prescribed for the conduct of elections in the first instance and shall be relieved forthwith.

4.The concerned officers should report at the place of new appointment and inform the government of the date of taking charge. Disciplinary action should be proposed to the government against the officers who do not join the transferred post within the stipulated time after appointment.
(5) The transfer benefits shall be admissible to the officers who are being posted as above as their posting is being done on administrative grounds.
6. Confidential reports, service books and final salary certificates of the transferred officers along with the posting order as per the above should be sent to the new office immediately.
No leave shall be granted to the officers posted as above without prior approval or any leave applications of the officers posted as per Rule 30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, shall be accepted by the parent office.
8. The office to which the officer is being posted as above shall verify whether or not he was on leave/ absence while being posted to the post to which he is being posted. If the officer is absent on medical grounds, he should be sent before the medical board before being produced. If others are found to be on leave, they should fill the leave application without fail and then submit it. Also, the directions in the government decisions/ circulars issued by the government from time to time regarding the leave should be followed.
