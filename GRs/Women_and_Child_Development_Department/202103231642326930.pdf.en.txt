The Proposal:
The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015. The Juvenile Justice Committee under the High Court of Bombay had suggested to the Government to develop a comprehensive computer system for effective implementation of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015. Accordingly, a computerized system has been created to make all the activities online, collect information, control information in line with the effective implementation of the Juvenile Justice Act. Two separate modules have been developed for the Juvenile Justice Board and the Child Welfare Committee, respectively, to work out the mechanism for children in conflict with law and children in need of care and protection. Out of the above two modules, the module on children in conflict with law has been prepared and the matter of implementing this system was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision
The Government has decided to implement the Module on Conflicted Child Rights in the Juvenile Justice Information System (JJIS) developed by the Department of Women and Child Development for effective implementation of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015. The system is available at www. jis. maharashtra. gov. in.

1) The police system for children in conflict with law, the Juvenile Justice Board, the officers and staff of the Observation Home should provide this system. It will be applicable from April 1, 2021.
2) The system should be used to its full capacity and the work related to the juvenile justice system should be done through computer system and the present manual system will be abolished.
The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune will be responsible for the control of this scheme at the state level. The District Women and Child Development Officer will be in charge of the district-level monitoring.
Necessary technical/ guidelines for the smooth functioning of the system should be issued by the Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune.
5) MahaIT will be responsible for timely resolution of technical difficulties encountered during the implementation of the system.
District Women and Child Development Officer of the concerned district will be responsible to ensure that all Juvenile Justice Boards have good speed and quality internet facility and the services of data entry operators.
7) Instructions regarding the regular use of this system by the police system should be given through the Special Inspector General of Police, Women and Child Crime Prevention Department, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the necessary facilities for this should be made available to all police stations.
8) The State Level Senior Controlling Officers should ensure that the problems/ shortcomings, if any, in the implementation of the system are immediately removed and all the stakeholders are trained from time to time.
