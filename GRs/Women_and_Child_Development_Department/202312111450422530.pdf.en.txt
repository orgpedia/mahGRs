Pravin Darekar, Shri. During the discussion held on 27/07/2023, the issue of setting up a research team for orphans above the age of eighteen has been raised. On this
The Minister of Women and Child Development has assured to form such a research team-research committee after discussing with NGOs, various units of the department and senior officials who have experience in this field. Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a research committee to understand the problems of orphans over the age of 18 and recommend necessary measures.
Government's decision:
In view of the above background, to understand the problems of orphans above 18 years of age in the state and to recommend necessary measures, Read No. 1 The decision of the government to constitute a research committee is being approved by this government decision. The composition and functions of the Committee are as follows: The Committee is composed of: (i) Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Commissionerate of Women and Child Development, Pune- Chairman; (ii) Deputy Commissioner, Child Development, Commissionerate of Women and Child Development, Pune- Member Secretary; (iii) Deputy Commissioner, Women Development, Commissionerate of Women and Child Development, Pune- Member Secretary; (iv) Shri. Dr. Asha Mukundan, Member, Juvenile Justice Committee, Bombay High Court, Mumbai- Member 8) Shri. Alpa Vora, UNICEF Representative, Mumbai- Member 9) Shri. Aniruddha Patil, Expert in the field of Child Development- Member 12) Shri Atul Desai, Expert in the field of Child Development- Member 13) Shri. Shreya Bhartiya, Mumbai- Member

The functions of the committee are: 1) To understand the problems of orphans above the age of 18 years in the state 2) To find out the reasons for these problems 3) To find viable solutions to these problems 4) To review and submit a list of schemes of the government's personal benefit schemes in which the beneficiaries include orphans above the age of 18 years.
The term of the Research Committee will be six months to suggest measures for the education, vocational training and skill development of orphans. The committee will submit its recommendations to the government in this regard. At the end of the term, the committee will be dissolved.
