As per Rule 10 (4) and 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, it is the policy of the Government to review the services of officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity in the public interest at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, and retire them from Government service prematurely. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 1 here. The Government Decision dated 10/6/2019 prescribes the procedure and general instructions regarding service review.
Accordingly, District Women and Child Development Officer and similar posts at the field level under the Department of Women and Child Development, Group-A (Gazetted) Pay Level S- 20:56100-177500 for the review of the services of the officers of the cadre, Reference No. 2 here. As per the Government decision dated 13/2/2020, a Departmental Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of A.M.S./ Principal Secretary/ Secretary (Mawbawivi).
District Women and Child Development Officers and similar posts at the zonal level under the Department of Women and Child Development have been recommended for re-evaluation of services of Group-A (Gazetted) officers at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. It was introduced on 6.11.2020 in a circular manner. As per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, which evaluated the service of the said officers, it was under the consideration of the Government to approve the officers who have been found eligible to continue in service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.

Government Decision: In pursuance of the recommendation made vide Circular dated 6.11.2020, the District Women and Child Development Officer and similar posts at the field level under the Department of Women and Child Development are being allowed to continue the services of the officers mentioned in the attached form in the cadre of Group-A (Gazetted) at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
