The proposal: A writ petition 961/2013 has been filed in the Aurangabad High Court. The High Court has directed the School Education Department and the Women and Child Development Department to jointly formulate an ECCE Policy. Accordingly, the Department of School Education and Department of Women and Child Development have prepared a draft ECCE Policy. The ECCE Policy has been prepared jointly by the Department of School Education and the Department of Women and Child Development. The Secretary (MoS& E) will present the draft policy to the Executive Committee for approval. The meeting was held on 14th January, 2019 at 12.15 pm in their new Administration Building, 3rd Floor. After discussing the policy in detail in the meeting, the draft policy has been amended as suggested by the committee.
3. General Council for the Implementation of the Policy on Care and Education under Early Childhood. Minister (M.& B.) ), presided over. The ECCE Policy has been prepared jointly by the Department of School Education and the Department of Women and Child Development. The draft policy has been sent to the members of the Council vide letter dated 17th January, 2019 for their comments on the amendments. No comments/ suggestions have been received. The government was considering the implementation of the policy on early childhood care and education in the state.
The Government of Maharashtra has approved the implementation of the Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) Policy in the State of Maharashtra with effect from 1st March, 2019.
The Policies framed to implement the policy on Early Childhood Care and Education include the following salient features: (The English version of the policy is attached. )

The policy will come into effect from March 1, 2019.
The state-run institutions imparting education to children in the age group of 0 to 6 years will have to enter the number of children, information of teachers providing education, along with Aadhaar card on the portal. The portal for registering
It will be implemented through CSR by the Department of Women and Child Development.
All private/ government kindergartens/ anganwadis (by whatever name called) in the State providing education to children in the age group of 0 to 6 years shall be supervised by the local authority (Zilla Parishad/ Municipal Corporation/ Municipality).
Separate instructions will be issued by the Department of Women and Child Development on what should be the mechanism to check the quality of education provided by all private/ government kindergartens/ anganwadis (by whatever name called) in the state providing education to children in the age group of 0 to 6 years.
5) Teachers/ Anganwadi workers/ helpers in all private/ government kindergartens/ anganwadis (by whatever name called) in the state providing education to children in the age group of 0 to 6 years will be trained through e-learning and a certificate will be made available through the portal.
All private institutions (by whatever name called) in the State imparting education to children in the age group of 0 to 6 years shall be at liberty to fix the fee to be charged for providing education to such children.
The policy will be reviewed and amended every six months by the Executive Committee/ General Council constituted by the Government in the decision dated 3rd June 2016 of the Department of Women and Child Development.
