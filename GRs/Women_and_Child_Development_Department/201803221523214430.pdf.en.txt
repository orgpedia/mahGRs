The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 came into force with effect from January 2016. Section 54 of the Act provides for the constitution of' Inspection Committees' at the state and district level for inspection of institutions registered and recognized under the Act.
The Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2018 have been framed by the State Government under Section 110 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015. notified vide Notification dated 13.3.2018. Considering the provisions of the said Rule 43 of the State Government, the matter of constituting a State Level Scrutiny Committee and a District Level Scrutiny Committee was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: In accordance with the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, the definition of children in need of care and protection as defined in Section 2 (21) for' children in conflict with law' and' children in need of care and protection' requires periodic inspection of government and non-government institutions providing residential services to children. Now, as per the decision of the Government, the following State Level Inspection Committees and District Level Inspection Committees are being constituted for inspection of these institutions:

The District Screening Committee constituted as aforesaid shall function as follows: 1. To inspect all Child Care Institutions in the district in Sample 41 of the Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2018.
Inspection of the facilities in the children's residential institutions in the district at least once in three months.
To submit the findings of the District Screening Committee to the State Government through the District Child Protection Cell and the Maharashtra State Child Protection Society as required and to make suggestions for the improvement and development of child care institutions in accordance with the provisions of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 and rules made thereunder.
4. Interact with children and seek their feedback by visiting child welfare institutions.
The District Child Protection Cell shall take necessary follow-up action on the report of the District Screening Committee and submit the action taken report to the State Government through the Maharashtra State Child Protection Society as required.
The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune and the Chairperson, State Screening Committee shall take the following action for the appointment of non-official persons in the State Level Screening Committee:
The Commissioner Women and Child Development shall publish an advertisement in the newspaper for receiving nominations of non-government persons.
(2) On receipt of nominations of interested persons, the Chairman shall appoint suitable persons as members of the Screening Committee.
At the time of appointment of a non-official member, the person should be at least a graduate with at least 10 years of experience in the field of child rights, care, protection and welfare. Age should not be less than 35 and not more than 65.
The term of non-official members shall be three years from the date of appointment.
3. District Women and Child Development Officer shall take the following action as per the orders of the Chairperson District Magistrate for appointment of non-government persons in the screening committee at the district level: 1. District Women and Child Development Officer shall publish an advertisement in the local newspaper for receiving nominations of non-government persons.
(2) On receipt of nominations of interested persons, the same shall be scrutinized and suitable persons shall be appointed.
