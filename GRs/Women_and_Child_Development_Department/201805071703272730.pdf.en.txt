(1) Department of Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles, Government Decision No. SAPAS 2017/ PROCESS 198/24 C, dated 23rd October, 2017, dated 16.4.2018 and dated 20.4.2018 Preamble: The Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Navi Mumbai, under the Department of Women and Child Development, is implementing the Centrally Sponsored Supplementary Nutrition Scheme for malnourished children in rural, tribal and urban areas of the State. Under this scheme, various types of nutritious and nutritious food are provided to malnourished children at the Anganwadi level in the state. Accordingly, the diet includes khichdi with a mixture of rice and turmeric. At present, khichdi consisting of 20 grams of tur dal per beneficiary is distributed to the beneficiaries on 8 days of every month.
The Maharashtra State Cooperative Marketing Federation Limited (MSSMF) will procure khichdi from farmers in the state under the state government's support price scheme. It was under the consideration of the Government to approve the purchase and determine the procedure of appropriation from the turdali made available for sale by Bombay. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decision:
Government Decision: Procurement of Tur dal for khichdi to be distributed to the beneficiaries of the state under the Centrally Sponsored Supplementary Nutrition Scheme is limited to the Maharashtra State Cooperative Marketing Federation from the Tur dal purchased by the state government from the farmers of the state under the Basic Price Scheme. Per kg, Rs. 55/- is being approved for purchase.

4. Maharashtra State Co-operative Marketing Federation Limited. The payment of the tur dal supplied by the Maharashtra State Co-operative Marketing Federation Ltd., Mumbai should be made to the District Office of the Maharashtra State Co-operative Marketing Federation by deducting/ deducting the payment from the respective Women Self Help Groups/ Mahila Mandals.
The Women and Child Development Officer (ZP) and Child Development Project Officer (Civil) should register the demand for tur dal required for the first two months of supplementary nutrition for the beneficiaries under them with the office of the concerned District Livestock Federation.
The Women and Child Development Officer (ZP) and Child Development Project Officer (Civil) will be responsible for sending the certified data and utility certificate regarding the allocation of tur dal supplied under this scheme to the Commissioner, Integrated Child Development Services Scheme, Navi Mumbai.
