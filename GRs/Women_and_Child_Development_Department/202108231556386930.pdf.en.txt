Introduction: Due to the socio-economic progressive change of the country, many women are leaving their homes in search of jobs in urban areas. At present, the scheme of" Hostels for Working Women" is operational for providing safe and comfortable accommodation to single women, unmarried, widowed, divorced, separated, out of wedlock women in the workplace. The scheme is being implemented on 75:25 sharing basis between the Centre and the state governments. Central government. As per the guidelines in the decision dated 22.11.2017, the Government was considering to partially modify the existing scheme and make available the hostel building for employed women on rent and implement the revised scheme in the ratio of 60:15:25 between Centre: State: Non-Governmental Organizations respectively.
Decision of the Central Government. As per the guidelines in the decision dated 22.11.2017, the scheme is being approved to be implemented in the State in the ratio of 60:15:25 between Centre: State: NGOs as follows.
The revised scheme of Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Hostels for Working Women will have the following features: (i) Keeping in view the population and demand for hostels for working women in the State, the revised scheme of Hostels for Working Women will be implemented in the State by making available 50 hostels on building rent for Mumbai Suburban District-6, Mumbai City District-4, Thane District-4, Pune District-4 and one each for the remaining 32 districts of the State.
The entry capacity of these hostels shall be limited to a maximum of 100 entries per hostel.

5) The intake capacity of the institution should be at least 25 at the time of applying under this scheme.
6) Under the revised scheme of" Working Women's Hostel," the total monthly salary of those working in the metropolitan city for admission to the hostel is Rs. 50,000/- and the total consolidated gross salary in other cities is Rs. Women with income up to Rs 35,000 are eligible for the scheme.
7) Women working in these hostels as well as women undergoing job training will also be admitted. The number of women receiving job training shall not exceed 30%.
8) Single occupancy in a room shall not exceed 15% of the total monthly salary for single occupancy and double occupancy shall not exceed 10% of the total monthly salary for double occupancy in a room and 7.5% for combined dormitory occupancy.
9) Rs. 3,000 each and Rs. The maximum fare is Rs. 2,000.
10) The amount of revenue received from the rent charge shall be allowed to be used by the institution operator for all kinds of facilities provided to the admitted women.
A woman entering the hostel can stay with her daughter up to the age of 18 and son up to the age of 5.
12) Day Care Centre will be provided for the children of the women living in the hostel. The fee for children in foster homes will be limited to a maximum of 5% of the child's mother's salary or the amount of the child's actual expenses, whichever is less.
If the income of the woman staying in the hostel increases, such women will have to leave the hostel within six months.
Under this scheme, a woman can stay in the hostel for a maximum period of 3 years. However, in exceptional cases, the period may be extended up to six months at a time with the approval of the District Management Committee.
However, the total period of stay of a woman in a hostel shall not exceed 5 years.

The new guidelines issued by the central government will not be applicable to the working women's hostels currently operating in the state.
Beneficiaries of the scheme:- The following beneficiaries can apply for admission to the working women's hostels 1) Any employed woman (single, widow, divorced, married etc.).
However, the woman's husband or close family members may not be residing in the city.
2) Seats will be reserved for differently abled candidates as per the prevailing reservation system.
3) Women who are trained for employment will also be admitted. However, the trainee women should be admitted only after providing space to these working women. The number of women trainees shall not be more than 30% of the total strength.
4) If the beneficiary woman has a daughter up to the age of 18 years or a son up to the age of 5 years, her accommodation should be arranged with her mother. Also, day care centres should be made available to such women.
Entities eligible to apply and their priority:- The following institutions/ agencies will be eligible for the scheme: 1) State Government Agencies including Women Development Corporations, Women Finance Corporation etc.
2) Urban Municipal Corporation and Cuttack Mandal.
(a) Panchayati Raj Institutions.

Experience of implementing schemes/ Acts of the State Government for women or related organization for at least 3 years. The government should also submit a report on their performance.
• There should be a separate constitution of the concerned institution. It should clearly define the powers, duties and responsibilities of the board of directors.
To be eligible for the grant under the scheme, the concerned institution should have three years of experience in the field of women welfare/ social sector/ women's education before the date of application for grant.

9. Mode of disbursement of funds for hostels functioning on lease basis:
The approval given by the State Level Project Approval Committee shall be initially for a period of two years. The period will be extended for another two years after the completion of the required documents.
The first year's fund will be disbursed immediately after the approval of the institute's proposal. However, the proposal will have to be accompanied by a new lease deed, two photographs of the hostel mentioning that the hostel is being funded by the Ministry of Women and Child Development.
The second year's fund will be disbursed after submitting the utilization certificate of the first year's fund expenditure and the inspection report of the District Management Committee and other documents.
