Reference: 1) Letter dated 27th June, 2016 from the Ministry of Women and Child Development, Government of India, No. SW- 57/6/2015-Swadhar.
Preamble: As per the letter dated 27.06.2016 issued by the Central Government, the two Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Swadhar Yojana 2002 and Short Stay Home have been merged to launch a new" Swadhar Griha Yojana 2015" for the destitute, destitute, women affected by natural calamities/ domestic violence in the State. Under this scheme, women in distress, women in family conflict and their children are provided food, clothing, shelter and medical assistance, legal counseling and guidance through helpline.
The scheme was earlier being implemented as a 100% centrally sponsored out-of-budget scheme and the grants were being given to the institutions mutually on the recommendation of the state. However, as per the guidelines of the new" Swadhar Greh Scheme 2015" of the Centre, the funding pattern for the scheme will be 60% Central share and 40% State share. Accordingly, the matter of implementing the new Centrally Sponsored" Swadhar Greh Scheme 2015" in the State was under consideration of the Government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In view of the above, the Government hereby approve the implementation of" New Self-Help Home Scheme 2015" in the State on 60:40 Centre-State ratio from the year 2015 for the rehabilitation of destitute, destitute, women who have been rendered homeless in natural calamities/ domestic violence, as awarded by the Central Government vide its letter dated 27.06.2016.
The objective of the scheme is to support the destitute, destitute and destitute women of the State socially and economically by providing them residential arrangements, care, training and rehabilitation facilities as per the guidelines of the Centre in respect of the new Centrally Sponsored Scheme" Swadhar Griha Yojana 2015."

(b) The benefits under the scheme:
According to the new Swadhar scheme guidelines of the central government, homeless women who are victims of domestic violence between the ages of 18 and 60 will be given admission for a maximum of one year. Widowed homeless women of other categories will be given admission in self-help homes for a maximum of 3 years. However, if there are no other options available for such a woman, the duration of her stay will be extended.
Women above 55 years of age can stay in self-help homes for a maximum of 5 years. After that, they are given benefits under the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Yojana implemented by the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. Hence, they will not be able to avail the benefits under the new scheme.
Under this scheme, daughters up to the age of 18 years and sons up to the age of 8 years of the admitted victim can take shelter with their mother in the self-help home. For this, as per the guidelines of the Center, a subsidy of Rs. 1300/- per month is given for food, clothing etc. for the women admitted in the shelter home. Further, Rs. 175/- per month and Rs. 100/- will be paid. Further, Rs. 1800/- per entry will be admissible. Under this scheme, 2/3 rd of the amount payable to the woman concerned will be admissible as per the guidelines of the Central Government.

Procedure for submission of proposals for new Swadhar Schemes: (i) The institutions functioning under the new Swadhar Schemes should submit the proposal to the Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune along with the inspection report of the concerned District Women and Child Development Officer.
(2) The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune shall ensure that the proposal received is complete as per the terms and conditions of the guidelines of the Central Government and submit the proposal to the Government with a self-explanatory recommendation.
The proposals received by the Government should be examined by the concerned Cell Officer/ Deputy Secretary and the proposal should be submitted to the State Project Sanctioning Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Secretary, Women and Child Development for approval.
The proposals approved by the State Project Sanctioning Committee should be recommended to the Central Government for grant. The proposals received from the Central Government should be considered for grant approval.
5) The names of the NGOs that have been sanctioned grants under this scheme will be made available on the government website through the Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune.
The amount of subsidy sanctioned under this scheme will be directly credited to the bank account of the concerned NGO through DBT.

Accordingly, if there is a discrepancy between the inspection report submitted by the District Women and Child Development Officer under the new Swadhar scheme and the report received from the tertiary institution, then the responsibility will be fixed on the concerned officers after investigation as per the recommendation of the State Project Approved Committee.
