Introduction: Ujjwala scheme is being implemented by the central government to prevent unethical trade. Under the scheme, women and girls rescued by the police from sexual exploitation and prostitution are rehabilitated through NGOs by providing them care, education and training. The scheme has five components- prevention, rescue, rehabilitation, reintegration and resettlement. Earlier, the central government used to provide grants to the NGOs implementing the scheme on the recommendation of the state government. Hence, the funds were being diverted. However, as directed by the Ministry of Finance, Government of India, Ujjwala Yojana is being implemented as a sub-scheme of the new scheme" Women Safety and Empowerment."
Earlier, the share of the central government and NGOs was 90: 10 under this scheme. However, in the new scheme, the Central Government, State Government and NGOs will have a share of 60:30:10 respectively.
2 The revised scheme as per the changed norms of the Central Government. Cabinet approval has been taken and accordingly, Government Resolution dated 2018.03.27 on Ujjwala Yojana has been issued.
The scheme will be implemented in the ratio of 10:30:60 between the Centre, States and Institutions from 2016-17.
