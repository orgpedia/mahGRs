The Proposal:
As per the circular issued by the Finance Department, the Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2018 has been passed by the state legislature. As per the circular, the budgetary allocation of mandatory and programme expenditure for the first nine months of 2018-19 (ending December, 2018) will be distributed through the system (BEAMS) within the limit of 70% of the total annual allocation.
For the financial year 2018-19, a total of₹ 2,46,18, 000/- (Rupees Two Crores Forty Six Lakh Eight Thousand only) for 36 Auxiliary Anusandhan (Salaries) and₹ 5,66,50, 000/- (Rupees Five Crores Sixty Six Lakh Fifty Thousand only) for 31 Auxiliary Anusandhan (Non-Salaries) has been sanctioned under head 2235-2161. As per the circular issued by the Finance Department under the head 2235-2161, a proposal was under the consideration of the government to disburse the funds available for salary and non-salary matters to the Maharashtra State Women's Commission within a limit of 70%.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As per the circular under reference of the Department of Finance, for the financial year 2018-19, the budgetary provision available for 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) under head 2235-2161 is 50% i.e., Rs. 86,16,300/- (Rupees Eighty Six Lakh Sixteen Thousand Three Hundred only) and 31 Subsidiary Grants (Postsalary) subject to a limit of 70% of the budgetary provision of Rs. 2,46,18, 000/- (Rupees Two Crore Forty Six Lakh Eighteen Thousand only) and 50% of the budgetary provision of Rs. 1,72,32, 600/- (Rupees One Crore Seventy Two Lakh Thirty Two Thousand Six hundred only).
District Women and Child Development Officer, Mumbai Suburban is being declared as Drawing and Disbursing Officer for disbursement of this fund to Maharashtra State Women's Commission.
The Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall disburse this fund to the Maharashtra State Women's Commission.
However, the said funds are not drawn from the treasury and are being spent by the Maharashtra State Women's Commission.
