The Proposal:
Various government and non-government organisations are working in the state to take care of children under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 under the Women and Child Development Department. Many NGOs, philanthropists are providing funds and essential items to the organizations working for the care of children in the state during the emergency due to the Corona (COVID-19) virus. However, the virus has not yet been contained, and the government was considering providing necessary funds to each district as a matter of urgency from the Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice Fund for sanitizers, soaps, masks, medical treatment, and other urgent needs arising out of COVID-19, in the best interest of the children.
Government's decision:
In accordance with the decision taken by the Maharashtra State Child Fund Management Board and as proposed by the Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune under the letter of reference, a total ofâ‚¹ 16,75,000/- (Rupees Sixteen Lakhs Seventy Five Thousand only) is being made available from the State Juvenile Justice Fund for the urgent needs of children in the institution working for the care of children in the emergency situation caused by the Corona (COVID-19) virus.
The following steps should be taken to ensure the availability of funds: :
1,00,000/- each for districts having more than 500 children in child care institutions, 50,000/- each for districts having 101-499 children in institutions and 25,000/- each for districts having 1-100 children in institutions.
The funds will be used for COVID-19 related emergency needs such as food grains, special nutrition for sick children, ambulance services, medicines, essential medical tests, sanitizers, soaps, masks and other emergency needs.
