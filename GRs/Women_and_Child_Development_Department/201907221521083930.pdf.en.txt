Preamble: The Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2019 has been published in the Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part IV, dated 4th July, 2019, as Maharashtra Act No. 08. Accordingly, vide circular dated 8.7.2019 of the Finance Department, it has been approved to disburse the funds including mandatory and programme expenditure within the limit of 60% of the total annual allocation to meet the expenditure for nine months upto December, 2019-20.
Accordingly, instructions have been given under the letter dated 11/7/2019 to issue orders separately by the concerned office to approve the disbursement of funds/ expenditure in respect of the respective schemes subject to the terms and conditions mentioned in the said circular of the Finance Department and in compliance with the guidelines issued by the Planning and Finance Department, prevailing norms, delegation of financial powers etc. Accordingly, under the Women and Child Development Department, the matter of disbursement of funds admissible under the head of the main account- 7610- loans to government employees, etc., was under consideration.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: As per the provisions of the Circular dated 8.7.2019 in Reference No.1 of the Department of Finance, for the financial year 2019-20, to meet the expenditure for the 9 months ending December 2019, the demand No.X-4 for the main account head 7610-Government Employees, etc. The Government is approving the release of funds to the tune of Rs.18667/- (Rupees One Crore Eighty Six Lakh Sixty Six Thousand only) through the Budget Distribution System.
The Commissioner, Women and Child Development Commissionerate, Pune will be the Controlling Officer for this fund. Accordingly, the Commissioner, Women and Child Development Commissionerate, Pune should strictly follow the checklist items mentioned in the circular dated 8.7.2019 in Reference No-1 of the Finance Department, the guidelines of the Central Government as well as the guidelines issued by the Planning Department and the Finance Department in this regard, the prevailing norms, the delegation of financial powers.
