Preamble: The provisions of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 provide for adoption of such children to willing parents for rehabilitation of orphaned, abandoned, abandoned children and the Central Adoption Resource Authority (CARA) is responsible for regulating such adoption cases. The State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA) is responsible for taking necessary action in respect of adoption cases at the state level and it is necessary to establish a State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA) in each state.
2. the government. The State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA) has been constituted under the Juvenile Justice Act, 2000 vide Corrigendum dated 15.05.2012 and is deemed to have been established under the Act, 2015 vide Section 67 (2) of the Juvenile Justice Act, 2015. Therefore, a State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA) is established in the State and as per the provisions of the Adoption Regulation, 2017, a Governing Body of the State Adoption Source Agency (SARA) is required to be established.
The government's decision:
Now, as per the said Regulation, the Governing Board of State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA) is being constituted and the composition of the Governing Board shall be as follows:

(ii) The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, Sections 62 (2) and 65 (2) and the Adoption Regulation, 2017, Regulation No. Article 33 (2) specifies the functions and responsibilities of the State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA).
Regulation No.3 of the Adoption Regulations, 2017. 33 (1-D) The Member Secretary, State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA), Regulatory Board and Commissioner, Women and Child Development Commissionerate, Pune shall be responsible for convening meetings of the Governing Body of the State Adoption Resource Agency (SARA) as per requirement and at least once in three months to review the adoption activities and to resolve the bottlenecks in the adoption process and mechanism.
The term of office of the non-official members of the Governing Body shall be three years from the date of such decision of the Government.
