Government's decision
As per the National Child Policy, family is a fundamental right of every child. As per the guidelines of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, admission of children to the institution is expected to be chosen as the final option.
The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, seeks to rehabilitate and rehabilitate" children in need of care and protection" and" children in conflict with law."
The Act provides for" Institutional Services" and" Institutional Services."
Non-institutional services include family-based foster care, sponsorship, and aftercare for children between the ages of 18 and 21.
3. Under the said Government decision, the guidelines on" Foster Care" prepared by the Expert Committee constituted at the level of Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune on the basis of the guidelines of the International and National Child Policy on Children, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 and Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2018 on the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of" Child Protection Services" (ICPS) are being accepted as per" Annexure-A," Guidelines on Sponsorship as per" Annexure-B" and Guidelines on" After Care" as per" Annexure-A."
Necessary action should be taken to provide the benefits of non-institutional services such as" Foster Care,"" Sponsorship" and" After Care" to eligible children based on the criteria and methods set out in these guidelines. The Sponsorship and Foster Care Advisory Committee (SFCAC) at the district level should be constituted by the concerned District Women and Child Development Officer. The cost of providing such services to the children concerned should be met from the funds available under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of' Child Protection Services'.
