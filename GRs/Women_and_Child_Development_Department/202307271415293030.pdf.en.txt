Objectives of the scheme: To create an environment for realizing the full potential of women by converging the schemes and programmes for women at the central/ state/ district level.
To guide women at the district/ taluka/ gram panchayat level in the country through the Women Empowerment Centre for their planned empowerment and development at various institutional levels in the areas of healthcare, high quality education, professional careers, vocational counselling, financial planning, entrepreneurship, backward and forward linkages, health and safety of the working class, social security and digital literacy.

9) Human Resource: As per the guidelines of the Central Government, no permanent posts will be created for working under the component scheme" Women Empowerment Centre" under the application of" Shakti" in the umbrella scheme" Mission Shakti." The contractual manpower required for the scheme and their eligibility will be as per the guidelines of the umbrella scheme" Mission Shakti." However, their appointment will be subject to the annual evaluation of the work being satisfactory. The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune is hereby authorized to select all the staff required for this scheme from the Commissionerate level in a proper manner. The appointment of these employees will be made as per the prevailing government decision.
Verification of character and antecedents by police officers and medical fitness certificate from a government hospital/ institution will be mandatory for all contractual employees before appointment. All the contractual employees working under this scheme will be entitled to salary, leave concession and other ancillary matters as per the guidelines of the umbrella scheme" Mission Shakti" of the Central Government.
Method of distribution of funds:
After the proposal of the state government is approved by the Union Ministry of Women and Child Development, the funds will be transferred to the bank account of the Single Nodal Agency (SNA), which has been opened separately for the component scheme" One Stop Centre." Recurring and non-recurring grants will be transferred to the respective districts from the state level.
The funds will be deposited in the separate accounts opened at the district level for the Sambal applications. The PF account must be linked to the bank account. All payments, including salaries, wages and services, will be made only through the Advance Expenditure and Transfer (EAT) module in the PFMS account. All central grants previously disbursed to States and districts should be transferred to the SNA accounts opened for Mission Shakti within the period specified by the Ministry of Women and Child Development.
