Introduction: Due to the socio-economic progressive changes of the country, many women are leaving their homes for jobs in urban areas. The State Government has launched the' Working Women's Hostel" scheme to provide safe and comfortable accommodation to single women, unmarried, widowed, divorced, separated, out of wedlock women in their place of employment. Central government. 14.7.2022 As per the guidelines, two sub-schemes namely" Sambal" and" SHAKTI" are being implemented under" Mission Shakti." The scheme includes a component scheme" Sakhi Nivas" for women employed in the application of" SHAKTI."
Central government. The Government has taken a decision in accordance with the guidelines dated 22.11.2017. As there was no response from the NGOs for the 50 hostels approved under the ratio of 60:15:25 under Centre: State: NGOs respectively as on 23.08.2021, it was under consideration of the Government to adjust these 50 hostels under" Mission Shakti" in the scheme of" Sakhi Nivas" and approve them for administrative expenses, building rent, maintenance management in the ratio of 60: 40 between Centre: State respectively.

1. the central government. The Government has taken a decision as per the guidelines dated 22.11.2017.
Under Mission Shakti, 50 hostels have been adjusted in the scheme of" Sakhi Niwas" in the ratio of 60:15:25 in the ratio of Centre: State: Non-Governmental Organizations, Mumbai Suburban District-6, Mumbai City District-4, Thane District-4, Pune District-4 and one each for the remaining 32 districts of the State and they are being adjusted in the ratio of 60: 40 in the ratio of Centre: State for administrative expenses, building rent, crib management respectively.
The Central share for the scheme will be 60:40.
3. No woman (single, widow, divorced, unmarried, married, trainee) admitted in" Sakhi Niwas" Hostel shall have her husband or immediate family members residing in the city.
Priority will be given to women from disadvantaged sections of society. In case of shortage of accommodation, special preference will be given to the disabled beneficiaries.
Under the scheme of" Sakhi Niwas," a total of Rs. 50,000/- and Rs. Women with a monthly income of up to Rs 35,000 will be eligible for the scheme.

Government Decision No. Sakhini 2022/ PR No.298/ WORK-2 6. Any employed woman already residing in a hostel whose income is more than the prescribed limit shall be required to leave the hostel within six months of exceeding the income limit.
7. Women working in Sakhi Niwas Hostel and women undergoing training for employment will be admitted. The number of women trainees shall not be more than 30% of the hostel capacity. The income limit may be relaxed by the District Magistrate or any other authorized officer as per local requirement and subject to availability of accommodation.
8. Entrants working in" Sakhi Niwas" will not be charged more than 15% of the total monthly salary for single bedroom, 10% for double bedroom and 7.5% for dormitory.
The rent for women undergoing on-the-job training shall not be more than the lowest rent charged to any employed women residing in the facility. The fare for such trainees may be charged by the institution/ institution sponsoring the training and if it is not possible, it will be charged from the trainee woman.
The hostel rent does not include the use of mess and other facilities like washing machine. A fee will be charged for entry.
Women with small children will be accommodated in single/ double bedrooms instead of dormitories, as far as possible.
12. If space is available in the day-care/ crèche in" Sakhi Niwas," children of other working women living in the vicinity will also be admitted. The fee charged for children in day care centres should not exceed 5% of their mother's remuneration or actual expenditure whichever is less.
If the beneficiary woman has a daughter up to the age of 18 years or a son up to the age of 12 years, her accommodation will be arranged with her mother. Also, day care centres should be set up for such women.
Working women are not allowed to stay in the hostel for more than three years. In exceptional circumstances, the district administration may allow working women to stay in the hostel after a period of three years with a written reason, subject to the condition that the period of extension shall not be more than one year at a time. However, the total period of stay of the woman in the' Sakhi Niwas" shall not exceed five years.

The following types of institutions/ agencies will be eligible for the scheme: 1) State Government Agencies including Women Development Corporations, Women Finance Authority etc., 2) Municipal Corporations and Cuttack Mandal, 3) Panchayat Raj Institutions, 4) Self Help Groups, 5) Recognized Colleges/ Universities, 6) Institutions working in the field of Women Welfare/ Social Welfare/ Women's Education, which are registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 or State Acts in force at that time, Public Charitable Trusts Societies Act, Cooperative Societies Act and such institutions shall fulfil the following criteria:
(a) Should have experience of implementing schemes/ Acts of the State Government related to women or the concerned organization for at least 3 years. Also, the government should report that their work is satisfactory.
b) The institution concerned should have a separate constitution. It should clearly define the powers, duties and responsibilities of the board of directors.
(c) To be eligible for grant under the said scheme, the concerned institution should have three years of experience in the field of women welfare/ social sector/ women education before the date of application for grant.
d) The financial position of the organization should be good and the organization should have facilities, resources and experienced staff to manage the project.
CCTV cameras should be installed at the main entrance, inner entrance and in the hostel premises. Also, the image should be preserved.
f) The institution should have a" crèche" for children living with the women in the hostel.
(g) The formal rules and regulations of admission, admission to day-care centres for children, details of hostel management, admission procedure, rent and other surcharges should be clearly defined and displayed on the facade.
It will also be mandatory for the corporate bodies like Confederation of Indian Industry (CII), Indian Chamber of Commerce and Industry (ASSOCHAM), Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI) etc. to admit working women other than its female employees in the hostel under this scheme.

Method of distribution of funds:
Under Mission Shakti, the Centre and the state will share 60:40 equity in the scheme. The funds will be transferred to the bank account of the Single Nodal Agency, which has been opened separately for the component scheme" Sakhi Niwas." The grants will be transferred to the respective districts from the state level.
The amount will be deposited in a separate account opened at the district level for this scheme.
The PF account must be linked to the bank account. All other payments, including salary, wages and services, will be made only through the advance expenditure and transfer (EAT) model in the PFMS account.
All central grants previously disbursed to States and districts should be transferred to the SNA accounts opened for Mission Shakti within the period specified by the Ministry of Women and Child Development.
11. Central Government. As per guidelines dated 14.7.2022, all the facilities/ amenities mentioned under the component scheme" Sakhi Niwas" are required to be provided.
The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune shall monitor and submit quarterly reports to the Government from time to time on how the funds distributed at the district level are being properly utilized. Also, the Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune will submit a proposal to the State Government regarding the demand for grants to the Central Government under the said component scheme, submission of utility certificates, statement of expenditure, physical/ financial reports, etc.
The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune is being declared as the State Level Controlling Officer of this component scheme. They should review the implementation of the scheme at least twice a year.
Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune will be responsible for the implementation of the component scheme" Sakhi Niwas" under the application of" Shakti" in the umbrella scheme" Mission Shakti" in the state as per the guidelines of the Central Government.
