The Proposal:
The Supreme Court in Petition No. 116/1998 in the case. The provisions of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 are required to be complied with in accordance with the order dated 7.5.2019 of the Supreme Court. The Women and Child Development Department has announced the Women Policy 2014 and the Children Policy 2014. This policy is for the disabled women and children.
Provisions have been made to provide equal opportunities while bringing them into the mainstream. The implementation of these provisions pertains to various administrative departments as well as their subordinate sectoral establishments. In order to ensure compliance of the provisions of Section 4 and 5 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the provisions of the above mentioned Women Policy and Child Policy need to be strictly implemented. Accordingly, the government was considering issuing instructions at the department and district level.
Government circular:
The Women and Child Development Department has released the Women Policy 2014. Case No. 19 is related to women with disabilities. Necessary provisions have also been made under this women policy to bring women with disabilities into the mainstream of society by giving them equal treatment. This includes care, care, rehabilitation, vocational education, awareness among women with disabilities. The departments concerned are expected to take action in this regard.
Similarly, the Department of Women and Child Development has announced the Child Policy 2014. The case No. Section 5 deals with the health, education and development of children with physical and mental disabilities. This includes developing the potential of such children and ensuring that there is no discrimination among them, including inclusion of these children in mainstream programmes and organising special programmes for their development. Further, under Rule 7.6 of the Policy, necessary provisions have been made for the development and provision of facilities to all children, especially children with special needs. This has to be implemented by the concerned departments and special care has to be taken to bring these children into the mainstream and protect their rights.
