The Proposal:
Section 41 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 makes it mandatory for residential institutions taking care of children to have a registration certificate issued by the state government. Accordingly, some institutions which had not obtained registration certificates earlier, have applied for the same. The High Court, Bombay, Bench at Aurangabad in Writ Petition No. 7821/2021 and other petitions were filed in pursuance of these petitions. The court gave. The result has been declared on 08/09/2022 as follows.

A total of 74 proposals were submitted to the government. In pursuance of these proposals, the Government under the Chairmanship of Secretary, Women and Child Development has taken a decision to grant registration certificate to the institutions dealing with children in need of care and protection under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015. Meeting of the Secretary Level Committee constituted on 13/11/2018. It was held on 30/06/2023. As per the decision taken in the meeting, the matter of approving the registration certificates of the institutions was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
In pursuance of the orders of Hon'ble High Court, Bombay Bench Aurangabad, applications for registration certificate by NGOs desirous of taking care of children under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 and the report received on the basis of physical inspection of the concerned institutions are being approved under Section 41 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 as follows.
(1) A voluntary organisation referred to in Annexure A is being approved for grant of registration certificate on grant-in-aid basis for a period of five years from the date of issue of the said Government Order.
2. The increase in intake capacity of one of the NGOs mentioned in Annexure-B is being approved on grant-in-aid basis.
(3) The proposals of the NGOs referred to in Annexure-A are being approved on grant-in-aid basis for a period of three months subject to rectification of defects from the date of issue of the said Government decision and in case the defect is not rectified by the institution within this period of three months, the certificate of registration of the said institution shall be cancelled.
(4) The proposals of the NGOs mentioned in Annexure-D are being approved on a non-subsidised basis for a period of three months subject to rectification of defects from the date of issue of the said Government Decision and in case of failure to rectify the defects within this period, the certificate of registration of the said institution shall be cancelled.
(2) It shall be mandatory to comply with the provisions of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, the Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2018 and the rules made by the Central and State Governments from time to time.
The Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune shall issue the certificate of registration to the institutions receiving the certificate of registration as per the approval and the Commissionerate level shall inform the institutions in Schedule C and Schedule D about the defects in their proposal and submit the proposal to the Government in accordance with the defects made by the institutions within the prescribed time.
