Government Decision: To change the orphan reservation policy, read No. 5 Read the Government Resolution No. 6 This government decision is being issued by authorizing the government's clean sheet here.
Orphans are being allowed to apply reservation of up to 1 percent of the available posts in education and government (semi-government and government-aided institutions) recruitment on the lines of the disabled. The eligibility criteria, nature of reservation, terms and conditions, guidelines for implementation of reservation, procedure for issue of certificate for orphans and pattern of orphan certificate are being prescribed.
Orphan Reservation Eligibility Criteria 1. The category of' Institutional' shall include children whose parents have died before attaining the age of 18 years and who have been brought up in institutions recognized by the Government (whether or not the information of their kin or caste is available).
(In the institutions dealing with care and protection of children functioning under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, under the Department of Women and Child Development,
This will include orphans who have been brought up in orphanages or similar institutions approved by departments other than the department. )
The category of" out of institution" shall include children whose parents have died before attaining the age of 18 years and who are brought up outside/ in the care of a relative in a government recognized institution.
Nature of reservation 1) Orphan reservation will be implemented on the lines of Divyang reservation.
2) The reservation will be applicable for admission to hostels and professional courses in educational institutions and for recruitment to government posts.
The posts reserved for orphans shall be equivalent to 1% of the total number of posts available for recruitment as well as the total number of seats available for admission.
(4) The posts reserved for orphans shall be divided into institutional and non-institutional categories as follows:- If the number of seats available for the orphan category in education and employment is equal, the seats shall be divided equally between institutional and non-institutional categories.
If there are odd number of seats available for the orphan reservation category, the seats should be divided equally first and the remaining plus 1 post should be made available for the institutional category in the first recruitment. And if there are odd seats available in the next recruitment also, then the remaining 1 post after equal distribution of seats as above will be filled up in that recruitment.

If two posts are available in the next recruitment process, one post will be available for both categories.
In the next recruitment process, if three posts are available again, one post will be available to institutional and two posts will be available to non-institutional candidates.
Terms and Conditions 1) To avail the benefit of reservation, the candidate must have an orphan certificate issued by the Department of Women and Child Development.
2) The candidate applying for the orphan reservation will be required to submit a certificate that he is a domicile of Maharashtra state.
(3) The candidate applying for the orphan reservation in the post must have obtained the minimum educational and other qualifications required for the post as per the provisions of the Service Admission Rules of the concerned post.
4) A candidate who joins the government service after taking advantage of the orphan reservation will be given temporary appointment subject to the verification of the orphan certificate. The concerned appointing authority/ Head of the Department will be responsible for verifying the orphan certificate from the Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune within 6 months after the appointment.
5) The authority concerned will be responsible for strictly following the provisions of the government decision while implementing the orphan reservation.
6) The Department of Women and Child Development will be able to give a decision in accordance with the orphan reservation.
7) As the Orphan Reservation is a parallel reservation, the criteria and conditions set by the General Administration Department from time to time in connection with the reservation will continue to apply to the Orphan Reservation.
Guidelines for implementation of reservation 1) While calculating the reserved posts for orphans, there is a ratio of 1 post out of 100 posts, but if the number falls in the fraction after calculating 1% of the available posts, then the fraction less than 0.50 should be ignored while 0.50 or more should be considered as 1.
(2) The posts of orphan reservation for recruitment or educational admission shall be shown separately in proportion to the number of posts available. These posts should not be shown in the open or social reservation category.
(3) However, on selection on merit to the post reserved for orphans, the orphan candidate concerned shall be included in the category of the social class to which he belongs. And it will be calculated on the point of its social class.

Procedure for issue of Orphan Certificate for Orphans in the category of" Institutional" 1) An orphan who is residing in an institution dealing with children under the Department of Women and Child Development or who has left the institution after attaining the age of 18 years and fulfills the criteria for Orphan Certificate shall apply for Orphan Certificate to the Superintendent of the concerned institution.
(2) The Superintendent of the institution concerned shall, on the application made by the applicant, make himself available to the Superintendent of the institution concerned, as required by the institution in which the applicant resides.
Also, the parents of the applicant should be traced and it should be ensured that none of them is alive.
(3) The Superintendent of the institution shall submit a proposal to the District Women and Child Development Officer stating that the parents of the applicant have been traced by the concerned agency and it has been confirmed that none of them is alive and that the applicant is an orphan as per the criteria based on the information and available documents.
The District Women and Child Development Officer shall scrutinize the proposal received from the Superintendent of the institution after considering the following documents/ evidences- the information submitted by the Superintendent of the institution, the birth-death register, the death certificate issued by the Gram Sevak/ Headmaster/ Ward Officer or other competent officer, the proof of age obtained from the Government District Hospital as required, the school leaving certificate, the copy of the admission exit register, the inquiry house report, the previous orders regarding the Child Welfare Committee/ Child Justice Board, etc.
5) After this scrutiny, the District Women and Child Development Officer shall submit the proposal for declaring the concerned applicant as an orphan to the Child Welfare Committee of the concerned district with self-explanatory feedback.
