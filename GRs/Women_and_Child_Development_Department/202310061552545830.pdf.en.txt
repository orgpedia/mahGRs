The proposal-
Empowerment of women is essential for the overall development of the country.
Various schemes are being run by the central and state governments for the development of women. Many schemes are being implemented for the empowerment of women, but for their social and economic upliftment
A joint effort is needed. Also, the process of" women empowerment" needs to be made people-oriented and involve public participation. Accordingly, the" Chief Minister's Women Empowerment Campaign" will be implemented in the state with the objective of organizing women, training them, making them self-reliant, accelerating the implementation of government schemes related to women by making them people-oriented, representatives of all government agencies implementing schemes related to women will be gathered under one roof to give the benefits of various schemes to the beneficiaries and to organize a felicitation ceremony for those beneficiaries. The approval was granted in accordance with the Government Resolution dated 20.9.2023. The decision was taken by the government. The Government decision dated 21.9.2023 was stayed till further orders. Now, the Chief Minister is implementing the' Women Empowerment Campaign." The Government Decision dated 20.9.2023 is being superseded by the following revised Government Decision.
Government's decision
The government has decided to implement the Chief Minister's Women Empowerment Campaign to accelerate the implementation of government schemes related to women in the state. Under this campaign, representatives of all the government agencies implementing schemes related to women will come together under one roof and provide the benefits of various schemes to the concerned women beneficiaries.
The instructions for this are:
1) All districts of the state. from October 2, 2023. " Mukhyamantri Mahila Sashaktikaran Abhiyan" should be implemented by October 1, 2024.

The main coordination of this campaign will be done through the Chief Minister's Public Welfare Cell in the Chief Minister's Secretariat. A committee will be formed under the chairmanship of the Chief Minister, which will include representatives of various training institutes, secretaries of the Industries Department, bank officials, representatives of NGOs, experts, online marketplaces and chambers of commerce. Committee meetings are held on the first Monday of each month. Control and Monthly Review. The Minister for Women and Child Development will take up the matter and submit a report. The Prime Minister and It will be presented to the Chief Minister. An IPS officer from the Chief Minister's Office will act as the nodal officer for coordination in the state. All relevant administrative departments need to be converged. The campaign will be led by the Chief Minister. The campaign will be coordinated by the Chief Minister's Public Welfare Cell, Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune, Chief Executive Officer (UMED) and Managing Director, Women's Economic Development Corporation (Mavim). The District Collector will be the head of the district for this campaign.
The Chief Executive Officer of the Zilla Parishad for rural areas and the District Joint Commissioner (Municipal, Administration Branch) for urban areas will be the nodal officers for this mission.
There will be control. The Commissioner of the concerned Municipal Corporation will be the nodal officer for the municipal area. The details of this campaign will be sent to the Chief Minister's Public Welfare Cell every Monday.
For effective implementation of this mission, the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation will set up a Public Welfare Cell comprising of the Chief Officer for Urban Areas, National Urban Livelihood Mission (NULM) for Municipal Areas and Maharashtra Rural Livelihood Mission (MSRLM) Group Development Officer for Rural Areas. The district level committee will also look into the matter.
Under this campaign, the nodal officers concerned should take action to inform the Shakti Groups/ Women Self-Help Groups of all the districts of the state about the schemes of various departments of the government, check their eligibility for various schemes, prepare a list of proposed beneficiary women, fill up applications from the concerned to take advantage of the schemes, etc. with the help of field officers.
The Block Development Officers and District Programme Officers for urban areas and District Coordination Officers for rural areas, Women Economic Development Corporation should take action to achieve the target of providing direct benefit to at least 1 lakh women in each district. Also, a 2-day program should be organized at the district level/ taluka level to benefit women beneficiaries in a representative manner. All applications must be received prior to the event. The event will be organised in coordination with the Commissioner, Women and Child Development, Pune.
