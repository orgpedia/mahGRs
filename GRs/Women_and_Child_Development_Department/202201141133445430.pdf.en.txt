Introduction: Ujjwala scheme is being implemented by the central government to prevent unethical trade.
Under the scheme, women and girls rescued by the police from sexual exploitation and prostitution are rehabilitated through NGOs by providing them care, education and training. The scheme has five components- prevention, rescue, rehabilitation, reintegration and repatriation. Earlier, the central government used to provide grants to the NGOs implementing the scheme on the recommendation of the state government. Hence, the funds were being diverted. However, as directed by the Ministry of Finance, Government of India, Ujjwala Yojana is being implemented as a sub-scheme of the new scheme" Women Safety and Empowerment." Under the new scheme, the central government, state governments and NGOs will have a 60:30:10 share.
2. Central Government Read No. The funds due under Ujjwala Yojana under 03 have been made available for disbursement in the current financial year. Accordingly, within the limits of the funds released by the Finance Department, the funds due for the year 2019-20 of the institutions functioning under the Ujjwala Yojana have been made available for disbursement in the current financial year. It was under consideration of the Government to disburse the funds due under Ujjwala Yojana to 03 institutions for the year 2019-20 within the limit of available funds of Central (60%) and State (30%) share.
Government Decision: For the year 2019-20, the Central share of Rs. 24,32,260/-and State share of Rs. 12,16,129/- aggregating to Rs. 36,48,389/- is being sanctioned to Commissioner Women and Child Development, Pune.
These funds are to be spent from the funds budgeted in the financial year 2021-22 under the heads of Social Security and Welfare, 02 Social Welfare, 103 Women Welfare, 02 Assistance to NGOs (02) (07) Ujjwala Yojana (2235B971) Central share and (02) (08) Ujjwala Yojana State share (2235B989) respectively.
