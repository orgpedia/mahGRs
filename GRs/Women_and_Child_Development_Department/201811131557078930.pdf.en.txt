Preamble: The Department of Women and Child Development takes action in accordance with the provisions of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015. Section 41 of the Act makes it mandatory to register child care institutions providing residential services to children in conflict with law and children in need of care and protection. The validity of the certificate of registration will be five years.
The Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2018 have been notified vide notification dated 13.3.2018. As per the provisions of the Juvenile Justice Act, 2015, the certificate of registration under the Juvenile Justice Act, 2000 is deemed to be admissible under the Juvenile Justice Act, 2015.
Rule 22 (b) of the State Government Rules, 2018 provides that the validity of the certificate of registration under the Juvenile Justice Act, 2000/2006 shall be for a period of one year from the date of notification of this rule, after which the institutions are required to apply for the certificate of registration under the Act, 2015. In view of this provision, the government had notified the interested NGOs taking care of children through newspaper advertisements and on the department's website to apply for the registration certificate. Accordingly, the Commissioner, Commissionerate of Women and Child Development, Pune, was to submit a report to the government in respect of the institutions that had completed the necessary documents and the matter of forming a secretary-level committee to take further action in accordance with the report was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: A three-member Committee of Secretaries is being constituted under Section 41 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 to process the applications received for registration certificate of Government and Non-Governmental Organisations as under:
Secretary, Department of Women and Child Development. Chairman (2) Principal Secretary, Tribal Development Department. Member (3) Secretary, Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. the members
