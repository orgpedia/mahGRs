Preamble: The Legislature has passed the" Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2018" as per the circular of the Finance Department referred above. As per the circular, the budgetary allocation of mandatory and programme expenditure for the first nine months of 2018-19 (ending December, 2018) will be distributed through the system (BEAMS) within the limit of 70% of the total annual allocation.
For the financial year 2018-19, a total of₹ 46,46,000/- (Rupees Forty Six Lakhs Forty Six Thousand only) for 36 Auxiliary Allowance (Salary) items and₹ 5,50,53, 000/- (Rupees Five Crore Fifty Lakhs Fifty Three Thousand only) for 31 Auxiliary Allowance (Non-Salary) items has been sanctioned under head 2235B 942. As per the circular issued by the Finance Department under the head 2235B942, the proposal to disburse funds available for salary and non-salary matters to the Maharashtra State Social Welfare Board within a limit of 70% was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
As per the circular of the Finance Department, for the financial year 2018-19, the budgetary provision available for 36 Subsidiary Allowances (Salaries) under head 2235B942 is Rs. 32,52,200/- (Rupees Thirty Two Lakhs Fifty Two Thousand Two Hundred Only) and 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary) subject to a limit of 70% of the budgetary provision of Rs. 5,50,53, 000/- (Rupees Five Crore Fifty Lakh Fifty Three Thousand Only) subject to a limit of Rs. 46,46,000/- (Rupees Forty Six Lakh Forty Six Thousand Only).

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: SAKBO-2018/ PR No. 50/ K-10 2. District Women and Child Development Officer, Mumbai Suburban is being declared as Drawing and Disbursing Officer for disbursement of the said fund to Maharashtra State Social Welfare Board.
The Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall disburse this fund to the Maharashtra State Social Welfare Board. However, without drawing the said funds from the treasury immediately, the District Women and Child Development Officer, Mumbai Suburban (Drawing and Disbursing Officer) shall examine the expenditure/ fund requirement of the Maharashtra State Social Welfare Board and submit the payment of expenditure to the treasury. Care should be taken so that the disinvestment funds do not remain with the Social Welfare Board. Also, the Secretary, Maharashtra State Social Welfare Board and Commissioner, Women and Child Development Commissionerate, Pune should take care of submitting the utility certificate to the government.
