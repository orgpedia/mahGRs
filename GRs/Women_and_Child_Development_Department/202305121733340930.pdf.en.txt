A total of 42" One Stop Centres," one each for the five districts of Mumbai Suburban, Aurangabad, Nashik, Nagpur and Thane, which have been approved vide letter dated 16.12.2022, are being approved to be started under" One Stop Centre" component of" Sambal."
1) Objectives of the scheme: The component scheme" One Stop Centre" will be the mainstay of" Sambal" deployment as well as all district level activities. The One Stop Centre is a component scheme aimed at providing immediate assistance to women in distress, both in private and public spaces under one roof and to provide immediate access to medical, legal facilities, temporary shelter, police assistance, psychological and psychological support to combat any form of violence in emergency as well as non-emergency situations. It has been in effect since April 1, 2015.
The" One Stop Centres" will be expanded to provide support through institutional/ group counselling, psychosocial/ legal counseling to help women in distress due to mental harassment or any other distress. These" One Stop Centres" for development and empowerment of women will work to connect women with government schemes/ initiatives through state/ district level centres as per their (economic and social) needs. Similarly, these" One Stop Centres" will keep a record of the statutory authorities coming under various Acts of the State/ District level/ Ministry and will contact them as and when required. " One Stop Centres" will also act as a link to" Shakti Sadan" by providing legal aid and psychosocial counselling to housewives, businesswomen.
The" One Stop Centres" will be the main district level centres of the Ministry for coordination and integration with other activities under the Nirbhaya Fund such as Women Helplines, Anti-Human Trafficking Units (AHTUS), Women Help Desks (WHDs) and Fast Track Special Courts (FTSCS), District Legal Services Authorities (DLSA), etc.
2) The function of the" One Stop Center" center:
To provide psychological and counseling, medical, police assistance, temporary shelter, legal advice, etc. services to the distressed women in need of care and protection under one roof through these centers.
Nature of the scheme: 1. The oppressed and distressed women of the state will get the benefit of this" One Stop Center" in emergency and non-emergency situations.

9) Mode of Fund Distribution: The Central Government will have 100% shareholding in the scheme" One Stop Centre" component of" Sambal" deployment under" Mission Shakti." The funds will be transferred to the bank account of Single Nodal Agency, which has been opened separately for the component scheme" One Stop Centre." The grants will be transferred to the respective districts from the state level.
The amount will be deposited in a separate account opened at the district level for this scheme.
The PF account must be linked to the bank account. All other payments, including salary, wages and services, will be made only through the advance expenditure and transfer (EAT) model in the PFMS account.
All central grants previously disbursed to States and districts should be transferred to the SNA accounts opened for Mission Shakti within the period specified by the Ministry of Women and Child Development.
