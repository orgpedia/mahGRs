INTEGRATIVE CHILD PROTECTION SCHEME: The scheme of Integrated Child Protection Scheme (ICPS) is being implemented by Maharashtra State Child Protection Society since 2012-13 in the State as per the guidelines of Government of India with an objective to enhance and strengthen the quality of operational machinery related to Centrally Sponsored Child Protection. The scheme was renamed as Child Protection Scheme (CPS) from 2017-18.
Now, from 2022-23, this scheme has been included under the" Mission Vatsalya" scheme.
Under this scheme, the employees of government and non-government organizations are paid salary. The Centre and state share in the scheme is 75:25. Read No. 2 Government Circular at the Mission
The guidelines regarding the Vatsalya scheme have been issued and as per the guidelines of the Central Government (Read No. It has been suggested that the Centre-State share of Mission Vatsalya be 60:40.
Under Mission Vatsalya, various government and child services related schemes have been brought under one umbrella, thereby providing enforcement mechanisms for child protection services regulated under the Juvenile Justice Act. Under this scheme, Maharashtra Child Protection Society (Head Office), State Adoption Society, District Child Protection Cell, Government Children Homes/ Observation Homes, Voluntary Children Homes/ Observation Homes, Special Adoption Agencies, Open Homes, Child Welfare Committee and Juvenile Justice Board are required to provide staff, administrative expenses and other necessary services for various components.
