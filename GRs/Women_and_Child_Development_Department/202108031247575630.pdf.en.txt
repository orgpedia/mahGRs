The Proposal:
Section 41 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 makes it mandatory for residential institutions taking care of children to have a registration certificate issued by the state government. The validity period of this certificate has been extended to 5 years. Also, the certificate of registration issued under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 and the Amended Act, 2006 are deemed to be valid under the Act, 2015.

As per the provisions of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 and the Amended Act, 2006, residential institutions for children in the state were issued registration certificates by the Commissioner, Women and Child Development Commissionerate, Pune under Section 34 (3) of the Act. However, since the validity period is not mentioned in most of the registration certificates issued under the Act, 2006, as per Rule 22 (b) of the Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2018, the validity period of registration certificates obtained under the Act, 2006 shall be extended. Government's decision:
Pursuant to the application for registration certificate made by the NGOs interested in taking care of children under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 and the report received on the basis of the physical inspection of the concerned institutions, it is decided to grant registration certificate to 5 NGOs mentioned in Annexure-A under Section 41 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 for a period of five years from the date of issue of this Government decision and approval is being given for capacity building of 2 NGOs. Direct institution-wise registration certificates will be issued at the level of Commissioner, Women and Child Development Commissionerate, Pune.

All the concerned institutions mentioned in Annexure-A are being drawn to the provisions of Rule 28 and particularly Rule 28 (6) of the Maharashtra State Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2018 and the concerned institution should ensure compliance of the said rule.
