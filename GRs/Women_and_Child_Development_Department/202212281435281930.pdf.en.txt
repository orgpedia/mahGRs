Date of release: December 28, 2022
Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS), a total of 553 child development projects are being implemented in the State, of which 364 projects are in rural areas, 85 projects in tribal areas and 104 projects in urban areas. Out of these, a total of 110486 Anganwadi Centres are functional.
Under the Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) scheme, 6 services are provided to the beneficiaries which include Pre-school education to children in the age group of 3 to 6 years through Anganwadi workers/ Mini Anganwadi workers at Anganwadi Centres. The Department of School Education, in collaboration with UNICEF, preschool education experts and institutions, has developed the Aakar Bal Shiksha, which has been implemented in every Anganwadi centre since 2016.
As per the Monthly Progress Report for the month of June 2022, the number of beneficiaries in the age group of 3 to 6 years who are pursuing pre-school education in Anganwadi Centres is 29, 71,292. The government was considering issuing a decision to take measures jointly with the Department of Women and Child Development and the Department of School Education to ensure that all the children who are taking pre-school education in the Anganwadi Center and who have completed 6 years of age on December 31, are admitted to the school in the first class and all these children are admitted to the first class.
Government's decision:
In order to bring children in the age group of 3 to 6 years under the Integrated Child Development Services Scheme in the state after pre-primary education, the government is approving the following measures in association with the School Education Department.
1) The children who are studying in the Anganwadi centre. All children who have completed the age of 6 years on 31 December should prepare a list with the full name and residential address of the child who has completed the age of 6 years in the next academic year in the month of January every year and before the date of 15 January, the Anganwadi centers in rural and tribal areas should be prepared by the nearest Zilla Parishad School and the Anganwadi centers in the urban projects by the nearest Municipal Corporation/ Municipality.

3) The headmaster should register the children admitted in class I on the Saral portal as per the list received from the Anganwadi worker and report the record to the concerned Anganwadi worker in accordance with Annexure' B'.
4) After receiving information from the headmaster about the children admitted in class I as per Annexure' B," the Anganwadi worker should submit this information to the concerned supervisor/ headmistress. The headmistress/ supervisor after receiving the information should report the data in the monthly progress report of the Integrated Child Development Services Scheme.
