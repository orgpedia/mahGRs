Read:- (1) Government Decision, School Education and Sports Department, No. MASHA-2012/ (86/12)/ MASHI-1, dated 19th January, 2013 (2) Government Resolution, School Education and Sports Department, No. SSN-2015/ No. 12/ TNT-2, dated 11th December, 2020 (3) Government Circular, Finance Department, No. Preamble to the Finance Bill, 2023/ No.40/ 3 dated 12th April, 2023:
Reference No. (1) As per the Government decision dated 19th January, 2013, the Government has frozen the salary subsidy due at the stage at which the salary subsidy was admissible as per the Fifth Pay Commission in the year 2008 to the teachers/ non-teaching staff of private primary/ secondary and higher secondary schools in the State under the School Education Department who were on salary subsidy as on 1st April, 2008, and increased their post-pay subsidy (4% post-pay subsidy and 1% building rent/ maintenance subsidy) by 5% to about Rs. 266.82 crore has been approved to be made available from 1st April, 2013.
Reference No. (2) Instead of applying the revised figure for the Class IV employees of the recognized private partially/ fully aided middle and high schools in the State as per the Government decision, dated 11th December, 2020, the Per-shala peon allowance has been applied for the post which will lapse after the Class IV employees retire as per the prescribed age.
Reference No. (3) In accordance with the Government Circular herein, for the financial year 2023-24, a proposal is under consideration of the Government to sanction non-salary grant to the private aided primary/ secondary and higher secondary schools in the State to meet their non-salary expenditure and to pay peon's allowance to the private partially/ fully aided secondary and higher secondary schools in proportion to the posts vacant as per the prescribed age of the class IV employees.

Government Resolution No: Wages-2023/ S.No. 133/ SM-4,
Government's decision:
In accordance with the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 19th January, 2013, the recognized primary, secondary and higher secondary schools in the State will be provided Rs. 49,17,78, 507/- (Rupees Eleven Fifty Crore Seventeen Lakh Seventy Eight Thousand Five Hundred and Seven only)
The government has approved the release of this amount as a grant.
(1) The wage subsidy as per the 5th Pay Commission payable as on 01.04.2008 shall be frozen and 5% of the wage subsidy shall be paid accordingly, as per the salary of teachers/ non-teaching staff drawing salary in 100% non-plan.
(2) The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009. As per the directions of the Supreme Court, the schools which have completed the physical facilities and other requirements by 30th March, 2013, should be given priority to the schools having the highest number of teachers and which are directly functioning, as per the merit.
(3) Also, Refer No. (2) In accordance with the decision of the Government herein, the private partially/ fully aided secondary and higher secondary schools should be disbursed non-salary grant to pay peon's allowance in proportion to the posts vacant as per the prescribed age of the class IV employees.
(4) The said non-salary grant is being handed over under the Commissioner (Education), Directorate of Education, Maharashtra State, Pune and the non-salary grant shall be distributed subject to the terms and conditions of the order issued by the Government from time to time. Also, the Commissioner (Education), Directorate of Education, Maharashtra State, Pune should submit the details of the non-salary grant allocated to the schools to the government.
