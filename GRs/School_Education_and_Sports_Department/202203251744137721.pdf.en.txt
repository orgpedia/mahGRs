Preamble: In pursuance of the Central Government's Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, the State Government has passed the Maharashtra Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Rules, 2011 and in accordance with sub-section (3) of section (4) of this rule, where a school is not available within a walkable distance of one kilometre from the habitation for children studying in classes I to V as per clause (a) of sub-section (1) of Annexure-I, section 4 of this rule and within a walkable distance of three kilometres from the habitation for children studying in classes VI to VIII as per clause (b) of sub-section (1) of section 4 of this rule, the State Government shall provide free and compulsory education to all children studying in classes VI to VIII within a distance of one kilometre from the habitation Transportation allowance/ facilities shall be provided for the purpose of providing elementary education in school for children belonging to such small habitations as may be prescribed by the State Government or the local authority. As per the Appraisal Report of the Annual Work Plan and Budget for 2021-2022 under the Transport Facility initiative of the Central Government, transport facilities are also to be provided to schools for a distance of more than 5 km.

The Maharashtra Primary Education Council, after analysing the data received from all the districts/ municipal corporations regarding the distance between the schools and the settlements, has identified 3,493 habitations in rural and urban areas and a total number of 17,450 students to provide the benefit of transport facility to the students.
Having regard to the above provisions, the Government have decided as follows: GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the Central Government's Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, the State Government has passed the Maharashtra Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Rules, 2011, in terms of sub-section (3) of section (4) of the said Rules, where, in accordance with clause (a) of sub-section (1) of section 4 of the said Rules, the children studying in classes I to V in the habitations within a walkable distance of one kilometer from the nearest habitation and in accordance with clause (b) of sub-section (1) of section 4, the children studying in classes VI to VIII in the habitations are required to be accompanied by a school teacher. Also, a total of 3,493 habitations in rural and urban areas of the state with a total student strength of 17,450 have been made eligible as mentioned in Annexure-A, Annexure-B, Annexure-C and Annexure D for providing transport allowance for the purpose of providing primary education in school for children in small habitations as determined by the state government or local authority where school is not available for students studying in secondary classes within a distance of 5 km.
1 km from the residence of the student of 1st to 5th class. 3 km from the residential area of students from 6th to 8th grade. 9th and 10th grade students. Since there are no schools in the area, the residents are being asked to pay transport allowance so that the students can go to school.
No school will be closed as a result of this decision.
