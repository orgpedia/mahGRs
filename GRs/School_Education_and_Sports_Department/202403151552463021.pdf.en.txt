Preamble: - Paragraphs from the New National Southern Policy 2020. 8 deals in detail with standardization and accreditation for school education.
New National Education Policy. 8.9 is as follows.
The goal of the school education regulatory system should be to continually improve educational outcomes; the system should not impose additional restrictions on schools, prevent innovation, or undermine the morale of teachers, principals, and students. All things considered, the objective before the regulation should be to empower schools and teachers in a credible manner to strive for excellence and do their best, while implementing complete transparency and maintaining the integrity of the system through public disclosure of all excesses, procedures and academic outcomes.
The new National Education Policy. 8. 5 (') is as follows.
To ensure compliance with the required quality standards, an effective quality self-regulation or accreditation system will be established for all levels of education, including pre-primary schools, and for private, government, and endowment-based schools. To ensure that all schools comply with certain minimum professional and quality standards, States / UTs shall set up an independent, state-wide body, the State School Standards Authority (5555), to establish a set of minimum standards (i.e., safety, security, basic infrastructure, number of teachers for subjects and classes, economic reliability and best practices of governance) based on 5555 basic criteria, which shall be followed by all.
