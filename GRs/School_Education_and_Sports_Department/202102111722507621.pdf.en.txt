The Proposal:
Six states (Himachal Pradesh, Maharashtra, Odisha, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh and Kerala) have been selected on the basis of their performance in the Performance Grading Index for the World Bank-funded project Strengthening Teaching Learning and Results for States (STARS).
Under the Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan programme, the STARS project aims to bring about fundamental reforms in the education sector at all levels, including providing quality education from pre-primary to class 12 and enhancing learning outcomes, improving teacher training, improving assessment and assessment. Strengthening and upgradation of vocational education from class 6 onwards has been included in this scheme. The project will be implemented across the state. The Cabinet has approved the proposal and the government is considering it.
The government has approved the implementation of the World Bank-funded project" Strengthening Teaching-Learning and Results for States" (STARS).

The objectives of the STARS project are: a) To provide quality and enjoyable education to pre-primary students and to focus on improving learning outcomes through regular and dynamic efforts.
(b) To lay emphasis on improving the quality of teaching and training of teachers and to formulate a comprehensive policy to make the educational administrative system transparent.
e) To improve the assessment system of students in the state.
(e) To bring out of school children, children with special needs, girls and students from disadvantaged communities into the mainstream of education.
1.1. The STAR project has been decided to focus on the following aspects as per the Performance Grading Index:
Strengthening of Early Childhood Education Bringing Anganwadis within the premises of primary schools as far as possible, ensuring quality of curriculum, study material, training to enable Anganwadi workers and teachers to impart joyful education. To start some model schools in pre-primary education on a pilot basis and expand it to a large extent.
(ii) Improvement in assessment/ tests.
(a) Reforming the examination system, revising the questions, training teachers in terms of PISA (Programme for International Student Assessment), pre-study preparation, conducting State Result Survey (SAS) based on National Result Survey (NAS), etc.
b) Reforms in Curriculum Creation of a separate cell for conducting examinations, use of assessment data of examinations for planning training of teachers, challenges in teaching more than one class of students, reforms in examination system by various schools/ examination boards, raising the standard of teachers training, besides setting up of an" Assessment Centre" to study the achievements and assessment methods in the field of education at national and international levels.
To develop teachers through online and blended training and to develop a separate Teacher Training Platform for effective control over training.
The overall development of teachers:
Improvement in teaching methods using information and technology, training of teachers and checking their knowledge after regular intervals through SCERT, DIET/ BRC, certification of teachers on the basis of training.

B. Improving the teaching-learning system in the school classroom: To take measures to improve the teaching-learning system. Determine the needs of each child according to his educational level and bring him to a certain educational level accordingly and control it.
School leadership: Providing the leadership training needed to lead a school.
Facilitating the transition from school to work/ school to higher education: Vocational training required for children to acquire skills to complete their education and start a business, training of teachers in the required areas.
Career Counselling for children to become professionals in coordination with Skill Development Department. To provide them with an internship in different industrial clusters after training.
V. Effective administration and decentralized management: Computerization of all aspects of teacher attendance, transparent selection, appointment and transfer systems, regular supervision and inspection of schools and teachers, use of technology for management of teacher's service books and teacher training.
Implementation of the project: The STARS project will be implemented at the state level by the Integrated State Implementation Society (SIS) of the Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan. The District Education Officer will be the head of the District Education Committee and the District Collector/ Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad will be the head of the District Education Committee. The office will prepare the annual project action plan and budget of the district and monitor the physical and financial progress of the project.

State Specific Grants will be provided by the Centre to enable the States to perform better after achieving the set targets and the State Government can use these grants to undertake innovative initiatives to improve the quality of education. The performance of the state in Shagun, PMS and UDISE will be used to determine the indicators for payment of subsidy.
Funding will be provided for the following:
(a) To provide infrastructure for empowerment of pre-primary teachers, to provide study material, to enhance technical capacity.
(b) To improve the evaluation system, the performance of the State will be evaluated at the national level. Funds will be provided for teacher training and capacity to use data for continuous assessment and assessment through various activities in the classroom.
The funds will be used for ICT pedagogy for classroom teaching empowerment, teacher and school leadership development, impact of work in the education system, performance of SCERTs, DIETs, etc.
d) Evaluation of students for transitioning from school to higher education through work, vocational education as well as counseling, e) Decentralization of training and management to provide improved services, administrative and educational information management.
Financial Management: The administrative and financial management of the Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan will be used for the STAR project. For this, it is mandatory for the participating states to use the method of PFMS to provide funds at the school level.
Procurement under the schemes will be done as per the World Bank procurement norms. The guidelines on procurement will also be issued separately by the central government.
Duration of the project: 7. A new head of account (externally aided project) will be opened for the scheme for which separate Government decision will be issued.
