Against this order, the In Writ Petition No. 563/2015 filed in the High Court, Aurangabad Bench. In view of the directions of the High Court, a revised order was issued vide Government Resolution No.6 dated 7th April, 2016 to declare these 37 batches eligible for grant in phases.

As per the decision of the Cabinet in its meeting held on 14th June, 2016, orders have been issued vide Reference No.7 dated 19th September, 2016 for sanction of total 20% subsidy with effect from 1st September, 2016 to the Primary/ Secondary School batches declared eligible for subsidy before 14th June, 2016. Accordingly, 20 per cent subsidy was admissible to 56 teachers of these 37 batches with effect from September 1, 2016.
56 teachers of these 37 batches have been paid salary for the period from 1st June, 2012 to 28th February, 2015 without prior approval of the Government. Paragraph 4.2 of the Government Decision dated 19th September, 2016 specifies that grants should not be given to schools which have been found to have given salary grants in the past at the level of the Regional Officers. As a result, 56 teachers of these 37 batches have not been sanctioned the grant since September 1, 2016. The organisation concerned. In Writ Petition No. 563/2015 filed in the High Court, Aurangabad Bench, Hon. The High Court has passed the following orders dated 25/04/2018.
Time granted. The learned AGP shall also instruct the concerned officials that if there is no legal impediment, the salary shall be disbursed.
Considering the order of the Hon'ble High Court, the matter of sanctioning salary subsidy to 56 teachers of these 37 batches was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The Government hereby approve the grant of 20% subsidy admissible as per the Government Order dated 19th September, 2016 in respect of 18 posts of D.Ed. teachers and 38 posts of Graduate Teachers in 37 additional batches of Sripatrao Bhosale High School, Osmanabad run by Adarsh Shikshan Prasarak Mandal, Osmanabad with effect from 1st December, 2018. The grant is granted subject to the following conditions:
(1) All the terms and conditions of the Government Resolution, dated 19th September, 2016 must be fulfilled.
(2) Ma. In Writ Petition No. 563/2015 and Civil Application No. 11709/2018 filed in the High Court. The grant is being sanctioned subject to further orders passed by the High Court and the final decision thereon.
(3) Subsidies to the said teachers are being sanctioned by relaxing the order in paragraph 4.2 of the Government Resolution No. 7, dated 19th September, 2016.
(4) The arrears of salary grant to the said teachers for the period from 1st September, 2016 to 30th November, 2018 at the rate of 20% shall be paid as per the prescribed procedure.
