Introduction: The Sports Policy 2012 has been formulated to implement the welfare schemes for the players in the state by keeping in mind that the state of Maharashtra should be a leader in the field of sports, the interest of exercise should be nurtured among the citizens, sports-related technical training for the players of the state to perform well at the national and international level, improvement in the quality of sports, glory to the players, quality sports facilities. The policy for its implementation is dated 22.05.2014. As per the recommendations of the committee, a District Sports Training Centre will be set up in every district to provide training facilities to the sportspersons of the state. The decision was approved by the government. For the year 2022-23, Rs. 450.00 lakh is available as per the provisions of Department of Finance, Government Circular dated 04.04.2022 to the extent of 60% of the provision. However, vide letter dated 05.04.2022 from the Additional Chief Secretary (Finance), it has been approved to make available funds@ 7% of the budgetary allocation per month for the purpose of 31st Auxiliary Grants (Non-salaried) for disbursement as per monthly statement. Accordingly, for the month of April to September, 2022, 42% i.e. Rs. 189.00 lakh to 24 District Sports Training Centres. 1,73,17, 500/- was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Resolution: No. E-3, Principal Accounts Head 2204, Sports and Youth Services, 104, Sports and Games, (10) Establishment of Sports Training Centres, (10) (03), Establishment of District Sports Training Centre (2204 5682), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-Monorary). 450.00 lakh to 24 District Sports Training Centres as shown in Annexure-A below. 1,73,17, 500/- (Rupees One Crore, Seventy Three Lakhs, Seventeen Thousand, Five Hundred only) and placed under the Commissioner, Sports and Youth Services.
