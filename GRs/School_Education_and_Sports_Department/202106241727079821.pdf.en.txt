Due to the cancellation of the 10th class examination, the procedure for admission to 11th class for the year 2021-22 has been laid down under the decision of the government. Accordingly, it has been decided to conduct a Common Entrance Test (CET) for admission to Class XI across the state. The Common Entrance Test (CET) for admission to Class 11 will be conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education (MSBSHSE). B.S.E., C. I.S.C.E., all international boards, etc.) will be held for students.
The Common Entrance Test for admission to Class 11 will be completely optional for the students.
The Common Entrance Test will be based on the Class 10 syllabus of the state board.
The question paper will have 25 questions each from English, Mathematics, Science and Social Science. The exam will be conducted online. The format of the question paper will be Multiple Choice Objective type questions. The exam will be OMR based.
There will be a single question paper/ paper of 100 marks for this Common Entrance Test and the duration of the examination will be two hours.
The Common Entrance Test will be conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education/ Maharashtra State Council of Examination under the supervision of the Commissioner of Education. The list of examination centres will be announced by the State Board/ Council of Examination.
Since the Common Entrance Test for admission to Class 11 is completely optional for students; after the result of Class 10, the option to enter the examination for students will be made available on the portal by the State Board/ Examination Council.
Students who have appeared for the Class 10 board exams will not have to pay any fee for the Common Entrance Test as they have already paid the examination fee for the Class 10 exams.
However, the C.B.S.E. Students of other Boards like I.S.C.E., all International Boards, etc. will have to pay the fee as prescribed by the State Board/ Council of Examination for the Common Entrance Test.

The Class 10 results are expected to be declared by July 15.
The Common Entrance Test will be conducted within 2 weeks (approximately end of July or first week of August) after the result of Class 10.
Students who want to appear for the entrance exam should start preparing for the exam.
Students who have appeared for the Common Entrance Test while conducting the admission process for Class 11 will be given preference in all junior colleges on the basis of merit on the basis of the marks obtained in the Common Entrance Test. That is, in the first phase of the admission process of class 11, students who have taken the common entrance test will be given priority admission according to merit. After the admission of the students who have taken the common entrance test, the remaining seats left vacant in the junior colleges will be open for the students who have not taken the common entrance test and the students who have not taken the common entrance test on those seats will be given admission according to the merit based on the marks obtained as per the assessment method of class 10.
