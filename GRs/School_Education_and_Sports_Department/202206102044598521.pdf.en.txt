Government Circular: Reference No. According to the government circular of 1, instructions have been issued regarding the opening of schools in the upcoming academic year 2022-23 to bring uniformity and consistency in the duration of schools across the state.
2nd Monday of the academic year 2022-23. The schools will be opened on June 13, 2022, and considering the temperature of Vidarbha in the month of June, the schools there will be opened on the fourth Monday of June. It will begin on June 27, 2022. Following are the recommendations:
1). From 13th June 2022 to 14th June 2022, all the teachers and non-teaching staff of the school should attend and organize cleaning of the school, beautification of the school, addressing the teachers and non-teaching staff regarding the COVID-19 pandemic and health issues. From June 27, 2022 to June 28, 2022, all the teachers and non-teaching staff of the school should attend, clean the school, beautify the school, address the teachers and non-teaching staff regarding the impact of COVID-19 and health issues.
2) School teachers and other staff should take necessary action at the department level to take both doses of COVID-19 preventive vaccination and the subsequent booster dose.
Emphasis should be laid on increasing the coverage of COVID-19 preventive vaccination of students aged 12 years and above.
4) The school administration should ensure that Covid appropriate behaviour is followed in the school and school premises.
5) The school administration should instruct the concerned parents not to send such children to school if they have fever or other COVID-like symptoms and to isolate such students at the school level immediately if any of the students present in the school have fever or other COVID-like symptoms.

The school administration should make arrangements, students with Covid-like symptoms should be immediately subjected to Rapid Antigen Test or RTPCR test.
6) The school administration should take action to conduct Rapid Antigen Test or RTPCR test of the students who are in close contact with the students who have tested positive for COVID-19.
7) It should be ensured that the instructions/ instructions issued from time to time by the School Education Department, Health Department and Local Disaster Cell in the Covid situation are strictly followed.
8) In the pre-school preparation, the local administration should pay close attention to how the health and safety of the students can be better maintained.
9) Children/ parents coming to school should be enlightened/ preached in accordance with the spirit of COVID-19.
These orders are being issued with the consent of the Public Health Department.
