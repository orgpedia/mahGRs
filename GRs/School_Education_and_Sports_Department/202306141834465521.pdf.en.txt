The benefits of Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, Maharashtra Civil Services (Depreciation of Pension) Rules, 1984 and General Provident Fund are not admissible to the employees who are covered under Defined Contribution Pension Scheme/ National Pension System.
Subsequently, the Central Government Reference No. 11 The Central Civil Services (Implementation of National Pension System) Rules, 2021 dated 30.03.2021 have made family pension admissible to the family of a Central Government employee under the National Pension System in case of death in service or sickness pension admissible to a retired employee. The Central Government Reference No. The Central Civil Services (Payment of Gratuity under National Pension System) Rules, 2021, dated 23.09.2021, have made death gratuity admissible to the family of a National Pension System (NPS) employee in Central Government service in case of death while in service, retirement gratuity to an employee who has retired on account of illness and retirement gratuity admissible to employees retiring from Government service.
In line with the above decision of the Central Government, the family pension and death gratuity to the family of an employee in service of the State Government in case of death during the service period and sickness pension and service gratuity to an employee who has retired and the procedure for sanctioning service gratuity to the employees retiring from service shall be as per the provisions of the provisions of the State Government Service Definition Pension Scheme/ National Pension System. 13 has been laid down under the Government Decision of the Finance Department here. This provision will be applicable to the employees working in the 100% aided posts of teachers and non-teaching posts in the Zilla Parishads of the state as well as in the recognized private 100% aided primary, secondary/ higher secondary and teacher schools. However, separate orders in this regard should be issued by the administrative departments of the concerned ministries and the action should be taken at their level. In pursuance of this instruction, the Government was considering to issue separate orders in respect of teachers and non-teaching staff in 100% aided posts in Zilla Parishads, recognized private 100% aided primary, secondary/ higher secondary and teachers' schools in the State.

The families of the teachers/ non-teaching staff who died between 01.11.2005 and the date of this decision will have to submit the option in Form-3 along with the said Government decision to the Head of Office to get the death gratuity, family pension arrears and regular family pension. Accordingly, the benefit of family pension will be admissible to the family pensioner on the condition that the amount of ex-gratia grant received by him and the amount of government contribution under the defined pension scheme/ National Pension System is adjusted with interest/ dividend. After this amount is adjusted, the family pension will be admissible to the family pensioner on a regular basis.
In the event of death or sickness of a teacher/ non-teaching staff in service, who is presently working under the National Pension System (NPS) and is to be appointed in future, the family pension or sickness pension as per the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, or the accumulated amount deposited in its Permanent Pension Account Number (PRAN) under the National Pension System (NPS), shall be submitted to the Head of Office in Form-2 along with the family or its receipt, respectively, as per the orders issued by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) from time to time.
