The" Olympic Museum" will be developed at the Shiv Chhatrapati Sports Complex in consultation/ collaboration with Heritage, Lausanne, Switzerland and Indian Olympic Association, as per the guidelines/ guidelines of these bodies. The Olympic Museum has a special significance in the world of sports and will help spread the Olympic movement. Also, glorious events in the field of sports and the achievements of outstanding athletes will be remembered and new players will be encouraged. The Olympic Museum will not only boost sports but also cultural, educational and tourism sectors. This will increase the name of the state at the national and international level.
Reference No. In view of the No Objection (NOC) issued by the Indian Olympic Association, New Delhi and the Olympic Foundation for Culture and Heritage, Lausanne, Switzerland, respectively, vide letters No. 7 and No. 8 respectively, and in terms of Reference No. As proposed by the Commissioner, Sports and Youth Services, an agreement has been signed with Dream to Play Welfare Foundation, Mumbai to manage/ operate the Olympic Museum, to constitute an Olympic Museum Steering Committee in this regard, and to provide financial assistance to the tune of Rs. 8.84 crore was under consideration of the Government: The Government has decided to develop the Olympic Museum in the Olympic Building to be constructed at Shivchhatrapati Sports Complex, Mhalunge-Balewadi, Pune as per the rules/ guidelines of the Indian Olympic Association, New Delhi and the Olympic Foundation for Culture and Heritage, Lausanne, Switzerland. Administrative Sanction is being accorded for an amount of Rs. 8.84 crore.
An" Olympic Museum Steering Committee" is being constituted for the management and operation of the Olympic Museum as follows:

The functions and powers of the aforesaid Olympic Museum Steering Committee shall be as follows: 1) To develop and operate the Olympic Museum.
2) Selection of works of art, sculpture, objects, etc. in the Olympic Museum.
3) Decide on the internal structure of the Olympic Museum after taking into consideration the rules/ guidelines and standards given by the Indian Olympic Association and the Olympic Foundation for Culture and Heritage.
To implement sports development activities, short courses and courses under the Olympic Value Education Programme.
(v) To appoint architect and consultant for the Olympic Museum with the approval of the Committee, to decide on the expenditure to be incurred on the necessary aspects for operation/ management of the project from the Government grants, museum tickets and proceeds from the activities, to generate new sources of income.
6) Follow the guidelines.
The Indian Olympic Association, New Delhi and the Olympic Foundation for Culture and Heritage, Lausanne, Switzerland, are the official bodies. On the basis of the No Objection (NOC) given under section 7 and 8, the Olympic Museum Implementation Committee will enter into an agreement with the Dream to Play Welfare Foundation Mumbai for the management and operation of the Olympic Museum in the planned Olympic building. The agreement will set out the responsibilities relating to ticket revenues, social responsibility funds, income from sports development activities and courses, and administrative expenses and all ancillary matters.
