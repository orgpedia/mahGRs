In order to amend the various conditions in the proposal for grant of next increment to the schools/ batches already sanctioned vide Reference No. The Ministerial Errors Committee was set up under the Government Resolution No. 6. This error is dated 13th September, 2020. A meeting chaired by the Minister (Revenue) had submitted the report of the recommendations made by the committee to the government.
The report was discussed in the Cabinet meeting held on 14th October, 2020 and it was decided in the Cabinet meeting to examine the proposals submitted by the Commissioner (Education) to the Government through the School Education Department instead of the Finance Department before giving direct grants to the Primary, Secondary and Higher Secondary Schools which were declared eligible for grants under the Government decision dated 13th September, 2019. It was also decided in the cabinet meeting to examine the schools which were earlier given 20 percent grant before sanctioning the next increased 20 percent phase grant. Also the. Partial modification of the Government Decision dated 13th September, 2019. It was decided in the Cabinet meeting to approve the extension from November 1, 2020. Accordingly, an inspection team was appointed by the School Education Department. The inspection team visited the secondary schools/ batches in the state at the field level.
The inspection team inspected the primary/ middle/ high schools and submitted the report of the secondary schools/ batches to the government and according to the report, the matter of declaring the eligible secondary schools/ batches eligible for grants was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: As per Form-A of the Government Resolution No. 6, a total of 1746 teachers and 411 non-teaching posts have been sanctioned in 128 secondary schools comprising 642 teachers and 414 non-teaching posts and in 268 secondary schools comprising 798 class units comprising 1104 teachers as per Form B. The 20% subsidy was approved from April 1, 2019. The government is taking decisions.
The proposals received from the Commissioner of Education have been examined by the School Education Department and a total of 1070 teachers and 206 non-teaching posts of 308 teachers and 206 non-teaching posts in 61 secondary schools and 762 teachers posts in 543 classes/ batches of 181 secondary schools in Form A and Form B along with the schools that have qualified in this examination are being sanctioned 20 percent subsidy from November 1, 2020. Also, the proposals for filling up the deficiencies of the schools/ batches which are shown in Form A-1 and Form A-2 and are disqualified due to lack of certain documents should be submitted to the government within 30 days.
The schools have been found eligible for grant-in-aid on the basis of information received at the field level that they meet the criteria and conditions of the Government Decision dated 15th November, 2011 and 16th July, 2013 regarding the eligible secondary schools/ batches. The posts of teachers/ non-teaching staff as approved in this Government decision should be considered on the basis of the last accreditation (2018-19).
The schools in Form A and B declared eligible for grant under this Government decision need not be re-examined while sanctioning 20 per cent grant. A school is not eligible for a grant.
