Maharashtra State Olympic Games to be funded from District Annual Plan
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of School Education and Sports
Government Resolution No.- Narikas- 1022/ PR No. 47/ CRUSE- 2,
Ministry Extension, Mumbai- 400 032.
Date: 28th of December 2022.
Reference:- Government Decision Synopsis, d. 27.5.2022
The Proposal:
The Government of Maharashtra in association with the Maharashtra Olympic Association has approved the conduct of Maharashtra State Olympic Games in the State as per the Government Decision dated 27th May 20122. 19,07,94, 600/- has been disbursed.
The government has also made a decision. Regarding the organization of the Maharashtra Olympic Games in the state as of 01.06.2022. The State Level Organizing Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Minister Sports and Youth Services. As on 27.5.2022, the expenditure budget for the competition does not include the expenditure on Pancha Mandhan Accreditation Card, food arrangements for the officers/ staff/ volunteers responsible for the organization, security, cleanliness, T-shirts for the volunteers, temporary overlays for the organization of the competition etc. 328.68 lakh and Rs. 422.76 crore. 751.44 lakh and the cost of the 7 new sports to be included is Rs. 400.00 Lakhs to a total of Rs. 1151.00 lakh has been decided to be provided from the Annual Plan for Pune, Jalgaon, Nashik, Mumbai City, Nagpur, Amravati and Aurangabad districts. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to make available this fund for the Maharashtra State Olympic Games" a special case" by reallocating it from the savings of other schemes in the financial year 2022-23 under the scheme" Stadium Development Scheme of the District Annual Plan."
Government Decision: Under the scheme" Stadium Development Scheme" under the District Annual Plan General 2022-23 for the Maharashtra State Olympic Games, the Government is approving the following funds to be made available to the concerned District Sports Officers as a" special case" for the districts of Pune, Jalgaon, Nashik, Nagpur, Mumbai City, Amravati and Aurangabad in the financial year 2022-23 through reallocation from the savings of other schemes.
