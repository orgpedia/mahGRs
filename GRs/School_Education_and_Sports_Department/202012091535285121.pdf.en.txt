Background:
To make school students understand the importance of environment in day-to-day life, students have included the subject" Environment" in their school curriculum. Also, various activities are carried out to protect the environment in their village for actionable learning. Children are taught the concept of" Reduce, Re-use, Re-cycle" for environmental conservation. As part of this concept, the government was planning to implement a pilot project for recycling of textbooks.
Under the Samagra Shiksha scheme, free textbooks are distributed to students of classes 1 to 8 every year by the government. Approximately Rs. It will cost over Rs 200 crore. Some of the textbooks distributed to school students are properly preserved and maintained by the students, such textbooks can be collected and recycled next year. Recycling the book will save a certain amount of paper. The government has decided to implement a pilot project to recycle textbooks on a pilot basis in the academic year 2021.
Circular:
Under the Samagra Shiksha, children and parents of schools where the free textbook scheme is implemented by the government are urged to deposit old (in the year 2019-20 or 2020-21) used textbooks in their school. This will be a voluntary programme. This will help the students to keep the books in good condition.
