Samagra Shiksha, a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, has been launched from 2018-19, integrating all the three schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan, Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan and Teacher Education. Samagra Shiksha Scheme is run by the Maharashtra Primary Education Council, Mumbai. The objectives of the scheme are to provide quality education to all students, enhance learning outcomes, ensure equity and inclusive education at all levels.
An implementation plan has been provided by the Government of India to meet the objectives of the scheme. The scheme is 60 per cent funded by the central government and 40 per cent by state governments. The grants under the scheme are distributed from the state level to the district (DPO/ URC) to the taluka (BRC/ CRC) school (SMC/ SDMC) level.
As per the Central Government Order dated 23rd March, 2021 in Reference No.3 of the Central Government, the funds received under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme are to be managed through single nodal agency using PFMS system. Further, the funds received under School Nutrition Scheme are to be managed through single nodal agency using PFMS system as per the order of the Central Government dated 31st May, 2021 in the aforesaid Reference No.5.

Government Resolution No. Samagra-2021/ PR No. 112/ SD-1 As per the above order of the Central Government, in order to implement the Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Samagra Shiksha Yojana and School Nutrition Scheme in an effective and speedy manner, a proposal was submitted to the Finance Department to allow funds to be deposited in the account of private banks for using computer technology based systems/ services with the help of private banks and the same was approved by the Finance Department. Accordingly, the government was considering to allow the funds of these centrally sponsored schemes like Samagra Shiksha Yojana and School Nutrition Scheme to be deposited in private bank accounts. The government's decision is as follows:
Government's decision
The opening of accounts of Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha and School Nutrition Scheme in private banks of ICICI Bank is allowed subject to the following conditions: The State Project Director, Samagra Shiksha, Maharashtra Prathmik Shiksha Parishad, Mumbai will act as the single nodal agency for the Samagra Shiksha scheme and the Director of Education (Primary) Maharashtra State, Pune will act as the single nodal agency for the school nutrition scheme.
No additional charges will be levied by the private bank 2) Funds will not be lying in the bank for a period exceeding T+ 1.
3) The bank should submit information about the transactions made during the month to the School Education and Finance Department every month.
4) The State Project Director, Samagra Shiksha, Maharashtra Primary Education Council, Mumbai and the Director of Education (Primary), Directorate of Primary Education, Maharashtra State, Pune should sign an agreement with the concerned bank.
5) The bank accounts of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha and School Nutrition Scheme should be audited through the chartered account of the department and the balance should be deposited in the new bank account immediately.
This Government Resolution is being issued pursuant to the approval given by the Department of Finance vide its Informal Reference No. 106/2021/ Treasurer P.S. 5 dated 07/06/2021.
