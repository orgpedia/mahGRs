The proposal received under the reference letter to transfer the Marathi medium unaided secondary school Aurangabad to N-4 Cidco, a multi-purpose organization serving human development, was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: The Government has decided to approve the transfer of the said school to the subject institution on the following terms and conditions:
(1) The condition of minimum marks shall not be relaxed for any reason in case of change of management of the school.
(2) The Divisional Deputy Director of Education, Aurangabad shall be required to ensure that there is no change in the executive of the All Maharashtra Rural Education Development Board, Aurangabad, which has consented to the transfer. However, if there is a change in the executive, the consent of all the members of the new executive will be required for the transfer.
(3) The school shall be provided with a pucca building, all physical facilities and educational facilities as per the orders issued by the Government from time to time. Notarized affidavit on stamp paper of Rs.100/- will be required to be submitted.
(4) Qualified teaching/ non-teaching staff who have consented to the transfer shall be accommodated and their conditions of service shall not change. Notarized affidavit on stamp paper of Rs.100/- will be required to be submitted.
(5) The school shall continue to function after transfer on the same basis as the grant-in-aid.

(6) In accordance with the revised criteria of accreditation in the Government Decision, dated 28/08/2015, as well as in the orders issued from time to time by the Government regarding schools, the number of teachers/ non-teaching staff as per the permissible posts shall remain in operation and not more than that number of teachers/ non-teaching staff shall be employed.
It will also be mandatory to take action as per the government policy regarding filling up of vacancies from the permissible posts by additional staff.
(7) Care should be taken that in the case of a school where there is a change of management, there is no change in any of the terms and conditions of government permission/ approval.
The new institution shall be bound to comply with the Acts, Rules, Orders, etc., as may be prescribed in this behalf from time to time.
(9) The change of management, government decision, Department of School Education and Sports, No: Mashah 2011/ (210/11)
MASHI-3 will come into force with effect from 17th February, 2012 as per the rules and procedures prescribed thereunder.
(10) If any fresh complaints are received from the Government or the field officers of the Education Department regarding the change of management or the terms and conditions regarding the change of management are violated.
The Government shall have the power to cancel the transfer if it is found that the conditions of service of the teaching/ non-teaching staff working in the institution prior to the change of management have been breached by the new institution.
