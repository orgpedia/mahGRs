The Proposal: -
The National Education Policy-2020 is the first education policy of the 21st century. The Kothari Commission in the post-independence era 1964-1966 and the National Education Policy in 1986 have led to many reforms in education in the state and the country. Now after almost 34 years, the National Education Policy-2020 has been approved by the Union Cabinet in its meetings held on 29th July 2020. This new National Education Policy has a structural change from the earlier structure of 105-2 − 3 to 5 − 3 − 3 − 4. It covers education from the foundational level to the undergraduate level. The implementation is going on in a phased manner. The policy is based on constitutional values, built on five fundamental pillars: access to education for all, equity, quality, affordable education, and accountability. The policy is also aligned with Karabaya's Sustainable Development Goals achieved by 2030.
In line with the work set out for the implementation of the National Education Policy-2020, Reference No. The Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Commissioner (Education), Maharashtra State, Pune vide Government Decision dated 24.06.2022. Also, for effective implementation of this education policy, Reference No. 4 vide Government Decision dated 16.01.2023, an Inter-Departmental Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of A.M.S. / Principal Secretary / Secretary, Department of School Education & Training, Mantralaya, Mumbai.
According to the National Education Policy-2020, the National Education Policy 2020 is designed to ensure quality and effective implementation of the National Education Policy in the state by reviewing the infrastructure level based on Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) as well as the components of school education, teacher education and adult education in Figure 5-3-3-4. Chaired by the Minister (School Education), Reference No. The State Level Steering Committee has been constituted vide Government Decision dated 24.05.2023.
The Committee includes Reference No. The new members have been inducted under the Government Decision 6B7.
The books prepared by National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT), New Delhi based on National Curriculum Framework (Framework Level) 2022 and National Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2023 are being used by 23 states of the country. These textbooks have been produced by renowned, experienced experts from various fields across the country. Various competitive examinations at the national level are prepared based on these textbooks. These textbooks are used in schools of different boards across the country. The State Level Steering Committee, headed by Hon'ble Minister (School Education), State of Maharashtra, after due consideration by eminent and experienced experts in the field of education, has decided to use the Class-wise textbooks of the subject prepared by the National Council of Educational Research and Training in the State of Maharashtra also in a phased manner with necessary modification and contextualization.
The State Curriculum Framework (Framework Level) 2024 based on the National Curriculum Framework (Framework Level) 2022 and the Basic Education Curriculum 2024 have been prepared by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune. Also, the State Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2024 has been prepared based on the National Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2023. The School Education Curriculum 2025 is under preparation.
The State Level Steering Committee has approved the State Curriculum Framework (Basic Level) 2024, Infrastructure Curriculum 2024 and State Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2024. In view of this, the implementation of this new curriculum for all sub-levels of basic level B school education was under the consideration of the government.

Textbooks and Textbooks: - As per the provision of the 7th State Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2024, textbooks prepared by the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT), New Delhi in line with the National Education Policy 2020, shall be adapted with all necessary modifications for the state of Maharashtra. The curriculum must include elements such as foundational literacy and numeracy, learning to be holistic, integrated, enjoyable, and interesting, reducing curriculum content to enhance essential learning and logical thinking, experiential learning, etc., as outlined under the National Education Policy 2020.
Necessary reforms in the overall aspects of assessment, teaching methodology, schedule, monitoring and control, teacher training, etc. should also be issued by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune.
7 Textbook preparation for the preparation of Bal Bharati textbooks should involve the respective heads of departments and subject experts of the State Council of Educational Research and Training from time to time.
