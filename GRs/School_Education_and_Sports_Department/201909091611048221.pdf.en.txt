Department of School Education and Sports, Government Resolution No.MASHHA 2011/ (210/11) MASHI-3 dated 17th February, 2012 Reference: Departmental Deputy Director of Education, Amravati Division, Amravati vide letter No.Prath/ C/ 2600/2018 dated 30th June, 2018.
The proposal received under the reference letter to transfer the permanent English medium unaided primary school, Amravati, to the Igniting Maids Multipurpose Educational Society, G. Amravati, was under consideration of the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Government have decided to approve the transfer of the said school to Igniting Maids Multipurpose Educational Society, G. Amaravathi on the following terms and conditions: (1) The condition of minimum marks of the school where the management has changed will not be relaxed on any ground.
(2) The school shall continue to function after the transfer on the same basis as the grant-in-aid.
(3) As the school is a permanent English medium unaided school, the entire responsibility of the teachers/ non-teaching staff working in the school shall be on the institution.
(4) Care should be taken to ensure that there is no change in any of the terms and conditions of the Government permission/ approval in case of a school where there is a change of management.
The new institution shall be bound to comply with the Acts, Rules, Orders, etc., as may be prescribed in this behalf from time to time.
(6) The said change of management shall be effected in accordance with the rules and procedures prescribed in the Government Decision, School Education and Sports Department, No: Mashah 2011/ (210/11) Mashi-3, dated 17th February, 2012.
(7) If any fresh complaints are received with the Government or the field officers of the Education Department regarding the change of management or if any breach of the terms and conditions regarding the change of management is noticed or if the service conditions of the teaching/ non-teaching staff working in the institution before the change of management are violated by the new institution, the Government shall have the power to cancel the transfer.
