The four districts are Nandurbar, Nashik, Dhule and Jalgaon. Nashik is the third largest district in the state in terms of area and the Divisional Sports Complex, Nashik is benefiting the citizens, sportspersons and sports lovers of the large area of Nashik and nearby districts. However, the citizens and players of the district, located in the remote geographical area of Jalgaon, Dhule, far away from Nashik city, do not get the expected benefit of the Divisional Sports Complex, Nashik. For this purpose, Dhule and Jalgaon districts of Nashik revenue division have been allocated Rs. Mehrun, TG Jalgaon Group No. 343 available for Rs. 14.65.
As a special case on the area, the matter of approving the construction of a new and additional zonal sports complex was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: The District Collector, Jalgaon vide reference No. 6 here. vide order dated 08.06.2023. Mehrun, TG Jalgaon Group No. Out of 343, 14.65 ha (36.20 acres) has been made available to the Department of Sports for construction of Divisional Sports Complex. Considering the distance from the existing Nashik Divisional Sports Complex, geographical diversity, decentralization of the availability of sports facilities of international standards as well as availability of suitable and adequate land and other factors, the MOU for Jalgaon and Dhule districts under Nashik Revenue Division has been approved. The establishment of an additional and separate Zonal Sports Complex at Mehrun, T.G. Jalgaon is being accorded in-principle approval of the Government as a special case and subject to fulfillment of the following terms/ conditions.
(1) The Government has taken a decision under the Maharashtra State Sports Policy-2001. The State Sports Development Committee has been constituted on 26.03.2003. The committee has the technical, administrative and financial powers to set up sports complexes (Taluka/ District/ Divisional). As per this provision, the approval of the State Sports Development Committee regarding the establishment of the above zonal sports complex should be taken in the next meeting.
The basic facilities (such as roads, water, electricity etc.) required for the land acquired for the proposed Zonal Sports Complex should be provided through the existing schemes at the district level as well as the District Annual Plan.
