Circular: Department of School Education and Sports, Government Circular, Dy. 8th to 12th grade. 8th to 12th grade. As of September 24, 2021, schools in rural areas of the state from class V to class XII and in urban areas from class VIII to XII will be closed for the academic year 2021-2022. The guidelines have been issued to start from October 4, 2021. As the corona infection is decreasing day by day in the state, classes 1 to 4 in rural areas and classes 1 to 7 in municipal areas have been closed. Approval is being given to start from December 1, 2021.
In the circular dated July 07, 2021, a committee has been constituted under the chairmanship of the Sarpanch to start schools in rural areas. So the. As per the circular dated August 10, 2021, committees have been formed under the chairmanship of the Commissioner for Municipal Corporation areas and the Collector for the opening of schools in municipalities/ nagar panchayats/ rural areas.
2.1 The following matters should be discussed by the Committee before starting the school.
(i) The prevalence of COVID in the respective town/ village should have been reduced before the opening of the school.

Government Circular No: 2021/ P. No. 178/ SD-6 (ii) All teachers and non-teaching staff must be vaccinated. The district administration should make a plan in this regard. The Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad/ Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Principal, Municipal Council and Education Officer should follow up with the District Collector and make efforts to ensure 100% vaccination.
(ii) Parents of students should not be allowed to enter the school premises to avoid crowding.
(iv) Strict adherence to all Covid related norms e.g. in schools with more students, in two sessions, one student on one bench, 6 feet distance between two benches, maximum 15-20 students in a class, frequent hand washing with soap, use of mask, sending students to medical officer or nearest health centre if any symptoms.
(v) If the student is found to be infected with Covid, the principal should take action to close the school immediately and disinfect the school, isolate the students and start medical treatment on the advice of the medical officer.
Swap classes in the morning and afternoon on the day of transfer, priority for certain core subjects, etc.
Follow the instructions provided with the SOP.
2.3) The teachers of the school concerned should preferably stay in the same town/ village or they should be careful not to use public transport as much as possible.
2.4) All the above matters should be reviewed by the Commissioner of Municipal Corporation in urban areas and the Collector in other areas along with the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad/ Principal, Municipal Council, Education Officer, Health Officer and where necessary, the concerned.
The guidelines issued vide Government Circular dated 7th July, 2021, dated 10th August, 2021 along with the additional guidelines issued vide Circular dated 24th September, 2021 are annexed herewith as Annexure-A and Annexure-B for opening of schools as above. Be sure to follow these instructions carefully.
4. d. Students of classes 1 to 4 in rural areas and classes 1 to 7 in municipal areas should be welcomed by the students and teachers of senior classes in the school from 1 December 2021.
5. d. For schools starting from December 1, 2021, the education officer, group education officer, extension officer should visit the regular schools, review and guide where necessary and submit the report of the visit to the divisional deputy director of education. The departmental deputy director should submit a consolidated report to the director of education.
In addition to the above guidelines, the instructions given by the government from time to time regarding COVID-19 should also be followed.
