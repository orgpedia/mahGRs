Introduction: India is known as a country of youth, about 40 percent of the total population of the country is youth and the youth are in majority in the state. Youth play an important role in human resource development.
Youth are an integral part of society and are an essential part of the development process.
Given the number of youth in the society, there is a need to think independently for them.
In the era of globalization, there is a need to empower the youth to face new challenges.
The role and contribution of youth in various fields at the international and national level has led to the identification of youth as a unique group in the society. Youth have also been recognized as the main source of human resource development.
Youth empowerment is the process of incorporating the abilities, rights, and decision-making of young people into the lives of other young adults. The Empowerment of Youth

This can be done from homes, through schools and colleges, through youth circles, through government policies and through organizing various activities through the society. Social decision-making, organizational planning and educational reform are key components of youth empowerment.
The State Youth Policy-2012 has been formulated in this regard. To empower the youth, to participate in development, to make the youth lead a vibrant, stress-free and responsible life with the help of modern technology, schemes such as 1) Organizing Youth Festival, 2) Establishment of Youth Training Center, 3) Organizing Leadership Personality Development Program, 4) Youth Welfare Extension in Rural Areas, 5) Implementing various programs for community participation, 6) Establishment of State Youth Development Fund and 7) SwayamSiddha Women Self-Defense Training Program are implemented at Taluka, District and State level.
Out of these programmes, it is proposed to organize some' Yuva Sansad' programmes to talk about Yuva Jagar Maharashtra on a pilot basis this year in view of the youth awakening under the leadership personality development programme. The objective of the scheme is to enhance the constructive role of youth power in nation building and to provide opportunities to develop leadership qualities in youth by encouraging them to take up social work in national and state building by understanding their thoughts and aspirations. So far, the Youth Parliament program has been organized at the central level, on the same lines, youth awareness in the state is on the rise in Maharashtra
The matter of organizing the' Youth Parliament' program is under the consideration of the government.
Government Resolution No. 8, dated 24th July, 2019, is being superseded and considering the background as detailed in the Preamble, the Government is giving administrative approval to implement the programme scheme of' Yuva Sansad' to develop youth power and leadership qualities by partially modifying the leadership personality development programme and to engage youth power constructively in the social work of nation and state building. The detailed programme/ agenda for implementation is as under:
There is a need to create awareness among the youth about social issues and to know their innovative ideas and expectations. To provide an open platform for exchange of ideas, suggestions for improvement of government schemes, to take their views into account in the formulation of programmes, to develop leadership qualities in the youth and to involve the youth in the national and state development process.

Day-to-day programme outline: The Speaker of the Parliament, the Council of Youth Ministers should be selected from among the members participating in the Youth Parliament. There will also be the Leader of the Opposition and other members of Parliament.
A secretariat of 8 to 12 learned youth should be selected to keep all the proceedings, proceedings and records of the Youth Parliament.
The discussion in the Youth Parliament should be in line with the government schemes decided.
The members of the Youth Parliament can suggest new topics to be discussed in the Youth Parliament.
Session 1 Youth Parliament should be of more than 6 to 7 hours duration with first session and second session.
In the Youth Parliament, the issues of development at the local level should be discussed.
At the beginning of the session, the President of the Youth Parliament will guide all the members and the House on the working of the Youth Parliament. Members should be administered oath collectively and each member of Parliament should be given at least 3 minutes to speak. Also, the concerned ministers should be given time to respond to the discussion.
Session 2- With the permission of the Speaker of Parliament, the questions should be fixed and placed before the Speaker in order.
