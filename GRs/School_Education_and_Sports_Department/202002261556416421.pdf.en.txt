The provision of School Tribunal has been made under Section 8 of the Maharashtra Private Schools Employees (Conditions of Service) Regulations Act 1977. At present, there is no formal mechanism in place to deal with complaints that do not fall under the jurisdiction of the school tribunal.
The government observed that the number of court cases was increasing as there was no mechanism to act on the complaints of teachers and non-teaching staff of private aided/ partially aided schools. The petitions in this regard. As per the directions of the Court, the Department of Higher and Technical Education has constituted a Grievance Redressal Committee to deal with the grievances of the teaching and non-teaching staff and officers under the Government Order dated 18/12/2018. On the same lines, a Grievance Redressal Committee has been constituted to deal with the grievances of the teachers and non-teaching staff of the private aided/ unaided/ partially aided schools recognized under the Government decision of reference no. 1 and corrigendum no. 2. The Commissioner of Education, No. Ancillary instructions have been issued under Circular 3.
The Grievance Redressal Committee is working on the complaints of teachers and non-teaching staff.
However, considering the fact that the number of complaints from students/ parents and schools/ institutions is increasing day by day, the government was considering the formation of a Grievance Redressal Committee in all schools in the state to redress the grievances of students/ parents, schools/ institutions.
Government Decision: Grievance Redressal Committees are being constituted in all the schools of the state to redress the grievances of the students/ parents, schools/ institutions as follows:
