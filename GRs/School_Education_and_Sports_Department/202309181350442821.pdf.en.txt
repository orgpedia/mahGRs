Introduction: The State Government is striving to provide quality education to all students in rural and urban areas of the State. Various schemes and programmes are being implemented by the Government of India. However, in a high population state like Maharashtra, if all the activities have to be implemented, then it is up to the last person.
There can be some limitations. Vidyanjali is an initiative of the Ministry of Education and Literacy, Government of India to mobilize public and private sector institutions and public participation to promote school education.
The National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 has highlighted the need for public participation as well as active participation of the private sector in achieving its goals. In line with this role of the central government, the government was considering a new scheme, the School Adoption Scheme, to ensure the availability of infrastructure and necessary resources in urban and rural areas of the state in collaboration with philanthropists, NGOs, corporate offices and to spread quality and quality education through it.
Accordingly, the following decisions were taken:
Scope of the scheme: The' Adoption School Scheme' will be applicable only for all medium schools under the government and local bodies in the state.
Objectives of the Adoption School Scheme: i) To develop a system for repair, maintenance and painting of school buildings.
(ii) To help in improving the quality and standard of education system in Maharashtra.
(ii) To increase the enrolment of students and thereby to spread education far and wide.
(iv) To mobilize resources for the spread of quality education.

3.2 Functions of the Coordination Committee: 3.2.1 The Coordination Committee shall meet at least once in three months. In case of State Level Coordination Committee, the responsibility will be of Commissioner (Education) and in case of Regional Coordination Committee, the responsibility will be of Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Collector/ CEO, Zilla Parishad.
3.2.2 Accept or reject proposals after thorough scrutiny of the proposals received.
3.3.3 Define the general criteria for acceptance of proposals.
3.3.4 To determine the terms and conditions in the agreement to be made in accordance with the proposals accepted, taking into account the general standards.
3.3.5 The Coordination Committee shall have the power to take decisions on the administrative and technical aspects of the scheme.
3.3.6 To authorize the appropriate authority to sign the agreement pursuant to the proposal that has been accepted.
3.3.7 Evaluation of adopted schools through DIET.
3.3.8 Take necessary steps to give wide publicity to the School Adoption Scheme so that more and more donors can participate in the scheme. The head of the Regional Coordination Committee, the responsible officials and the school administration should give adequate publicity to the scheme by adopting the route of advertisements in local newspapers, posters, effective use of social media, etc.
4.1 Donors from the community/ Public and Private Sector Undertakings/ Non-Governmental Organizations/ Corporate Offices etc. (hereinafter referred to as Donors) may adopt a School under any one or more Government/ Local Government Institutions in the State for a period of 5 years or 10 years.

4.2 Donors shall assume guardianship of the adopted school and shall supply goods and services as per the requirements of that school for a specified period.
4.3 Donors will not be allowed to make cash donations under this scheme. Only goods and services can be donated. A representative list of goods and services required by the schools as per their requirements is being attached to this Government decision as Annexure' A'. The list is representative in nature and the donor can also supply other innovative and time-befitting goods and services that are not included in it and are required for the overall development of the school.
4.4.1 Public and Private Sector Undertakings/ Corporate Offices will be required to have CSR Certificate if they want to participate in the scheme as a donor through Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR). Such a donor will have to be registered with SEBI as a registered entity in the stock exchange. Similarly, they should have complied with the provisions made from time to time in the Companies Act, 2013 in respect of CSR.
4.4.2 Non-Governmental Organisations/ Voluntary and Charitable Organisations are required to be registered with the Endowments Commissioner to participate in the scheme.
__ 4.4.3 All donors, including private donors, will be required to comply with all applicable statutory provisions regarding income tax, audit, etc.
4.4.4 Anti-social elements with serious criminal antecedents will not be allowed to participate as donors.

5.4 If a decision is taken to accept the proposal, the Committee shall enter into a Memorandum of Understanding with the concerned donor. The committee may authorize any officer to enter into this agreement.
5.5 Both Parties shall be bound to comply with the terms and conditions of the Agreement. Any breach of this provision can be challenged in a court of law.
5.6 A donor who has accepted the General Guardianship can cancel the MOU with a prior notice of 6 months. However, the contract cannot be cancelled under any circumstances during the period the academic session is in progress. The donor who has accepted the nomenclature based exclusive guardianship, however, cannot cancel the contract before the prescribed period.
Terms and Conditions of the Scheme: 6.1 The donor shall not interfere in any manner with the management, administration, control and functioning of the school adopted by him.
6.2 Donor shall not claim any ownership rights in respect of the goods and services supplied by them after their supply.
6.3 The supply of goods and services by the donor shall not create any liability on the school administration.
6.4 Donors should self-assess the work done. The evaluation will be done by the Regional Coordination Committee.
6.5 Participating donors shall be audited annually by a Chartered Accountant and report to the Regional Coordination Committee.
6.6 If the cost of goods and services supplied by the donor is of recurring nature, such cost shall be borne by the donor for the duration of the contract.
6.7 The donor has to ensure that the quality of goods and services is good.
The Donor will be responsible for the AMC/ CMC with the manufacturer for maintenance and repair. The cost of transportation and installation related to goods and services will also have to be borne by the donor.
6.8 If the donors demand exemption under the Income Tax Act, the responsibility of the concerned school administration will be to make every effort to get the certificate.
6.9 At present, the funds received under various schemes/ heads of accounts will be admissible to these schools. However, the donor may provide additional funds as may be required after taking into account the budget of various works under the Adoptive School Scheme.
