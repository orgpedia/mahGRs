Preamble: - Provided that one of the two teachers to be appointed in schools having a strength of less than 10 years in the State shall be a retired teacher.
This does not mean that all schools with low enrolments will have retired teachers. Also, it would not be advisable to appoint retired teachers when the state has a large number of eligible unemployed candidates with a D.Ed. or B.Ed. qualification. Therefore, if such a place is given, the students will not suffer educational losses without the teacher's post remaining vacant. Also, eligible unemployed candidates with D.Ed. and B.Ed. qualification will get an opportunity to work in a teaching position. Therefore, it was under the consideration of the government to appoint qualified candidates with D.Ed. / B.Ed. qualification in a school having less than 10 marks.
Government Decision: - Regarding appointment of one of the two sanctioned teachers in the schools having 20 and less than 20 marks in the local bodies from among retired teachers or unemployed teachers with D.Ed., B.Ed. qualification, read the aforesaid No. 4 of the government's decision. 05.09.2024 is being superseded by this decision.
