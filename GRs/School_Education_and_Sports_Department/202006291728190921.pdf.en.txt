Introduction: 1.1 In order to ensure that maximum number of students of the state enter the National Defence Academy (NDA) competitive examination and join the army and serve the country, as well as to provide leadership-oriented soldier education at the secondary level by developing a disciplined, self-confident team spirit, competent and voluntary organizations have been approved to start 30 such military schools in every district of the state and to start 100% subsidized military schools from 1996-97.

The decisions have been prescribed vide dated 15th November, 2011 and dated 16th July, 2013. The Government was considering declaring 5 additional batches of Matyodari School, Partha Sainik School, Jalna as eligible for grant and sanctioning grant for 10 posts of teachers in these 5 batches.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 2.1 In the meeting held on 05.03.2020, the Cabinet decided to sanction grant subsidy at the rate of 20% to a total of 10 teaching posts of 5 additional batches of 1 each of classes 6th to 10th in Partha Sainik School run by Matsyodari Shiksha Sanstha.
2.2 In accordance with the above decision, a total of 20% subsidy is being sanctioned for 10 posts of 5 additional batches and 2 teachers per batch in Matsyodari Education Society Sanchalit Partha Sainik School from the academic year 2020-21 onwards, subject to the following terms and conditions, until further orders of the Government.
Terms and Conditions: (1) In the case of the first sanctioned batch having 45 numbers, the maximum strength of the additional batch shall be 30 as per the prescribed norms.
(2) The recruitment of teachers must be made in accordance with the reservation policy prescribed by the Government and the prescribed procedure for recruitment.
(3) Appointment of teachers must be subject to personal approval.
(4) The grant shall be admissible only to the posts of teachers for which the said grant is sanctioned.
(5) The grant shall be admissible to the military school where the attendance of students, teachers and non-teaching staff of the school is recorded through biometric system.
(6) The number of students in the school will be calculated on the basis of the number on the District Educational System (U-DIS) and the Aadhaar card number of the students studying in the school.
(7) The grant will not be admissible unless the Aadhaar card number of all the teachers in the school and the personal recognition order are filled in a simple system.
(8) It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Education Officer to verify the authenticity of all the information as per the" Saral" system before disbursing the actual grant.
(9) It shall be obligatory to recruit the next teachers in the military schools in accordance with the prevailing rules and procedures regarding entry into service.
Autonomy of Government:- It is the discretion of the Government to evaluate the batch sanctioned on permanent non-subsidy basis, to declare the assessed batch eligible for subsidy, to sanction subsidy to the batch and the Government subsidy is applicable to the eligible batch as per the availability of funds.
