The proposal: As per the decision of the Government dated 05.07.2002, General Knowledge has been permitted to be introduced in the Military Schools from the academic year 2002-03.
Since then, the subject of General Knowledge was taught only in military schools.
The, however. The revised subject plan and assessment scheme for Class 11 and for Class 12 from 2020-2021 has been finalised. The subject of' General Knowledge', which is only for military schools, has been excluded in this scheme.
However, there is a demand to continue the subject of General Knowledge in military schools as it is useful in providing up-to-date knowledge, day-to-day activities in subjects such as history, geography, defence science, general ability and general knowledge.
The main objectives of teaching General Knowledge in military schools are as follows.
1) To help students develop self-learning habits.
To develop the ability of the students for NDA and other competitive examinations.
3) To make the students aware of the developments at national and international level.
8) To develop interest among students to join the armed forces.

In the revised subject plan and evaluation scheme of 11th and 12th, the subject of General Knowledge was under the consideration of the government to continue as before only for military schools.
Government's decision:
In the revised subject plan and assessment scheme for the 11th and 12th classes, it is approved to continue as before on the condition that no new independent teacher will be appointed for the subject of General Knowledge for military schools only.
