Introduction: Refer No. 1 of the Government's decision. A Central Government defined contribution pension scheme has been implemented in the State for employees who are appointed on or after 1st November, 2005. Reference No. The government has decided that the state government will join the Central Government's National Pension Scheme. The defined contribution pension scheme has been renamed as National Pension System (NPS).
The National Pension System (NPS) is a pension scheme implemented by the state governments on the lines of the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA). The State Government has signed an agreement in this regard with the National Pension Scheme Trustee Board (NPS TRUST) set up by the Central Government as well as with National Securities Depositories Limited (NSDL) e-Governance Infrastructure Limited (NSDL) as the Central Record Keeping Agency (CRA) on 10/10/2014.
Reference No. The decision of the Board of 1. Defined Contribution Pension Scheme has been implemented for the officers/ employees of Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education appointed on or after 1st November, 2005.
As the State Board is not able to join the National Pension Scheme on time, the Board has to pay the interest on the contribution under the defined contribution scheme and has to bear unnecessary financial burden. Reference No. 2 It has been decided that the State Government will join the Central Government's National Pension Scheme as per the order issued here.

Government's decision:
Defined Contribution Pension Scheme:- (a) The Defined Contribution Pension Scheme shall be applicable to all the Mandal Officers/ Employees who have been/ will be appointed on regular basis in the service of Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education, Pune and the nine Subordinate Divisional Boards in the State on or after 1st November, 2005.
(b) in a manner other than the regular/ approved manner of the employee concerned (e.g.
The defined contribution pension scheme shall not be applicable to an employee who is appointed on contract/ contract basis, for a particular committee period, for a project or in any other irregular manner. It shall be the responsibility of the Head of the Office concerned to ensure that every employee appointed to the service of the Board is appointed in the prescribed manner, with the approval of the competent authority, to a regular post in the regular pay scale.
(e) The Board shall, from the date of appointment of each employee concerned, credit the amount of the employee's contribution and the employer's contribution and the interest thereon as per rules. The amount to be deposited under this scheme should be deposited by the board by opening a separate bank account.
The National Pension Scheme:
The National Pension Scheme (NPS) will be applicable to the officers/ employees appointed in the Board on or after November 01, 2005 and applying the defined contribution pension scheme.
The following steps should be taken to implement the plan:
a) Central Records Maintenance Agency: NSDL e-Governance Infrastructure Ltd. The National Securities Depositories Limited (NSDL) will act as the central record-keeping agency for e-governance infrastructure. Provision of Permanent Pension Account Numbers to all officers and employees under the National Pension Scheme, maintenance of records of accruals, and all other functions assigned by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) in this regard.

Trust Bank: The Bank which shall be determined by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) from time to time shall be the Trust Bank for the Scheme.
Fund Manager: The fund manager will be appointed by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA).
Registration with Central Record Keeping Agency:- The Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education should submit the Letter of Consent and Master Creation Form to participate in the National Pension Scheme to the Central Record Keeping Agency.

10) Procedure to be followed in respect of deductible excess/ less contribution:
The Drawing and Disbursing Officer will be responsible for collecting the prescribed 10% (Basic Pay+ Dearness Allowance thereon) contribution from the salary dues of the employees every month and depositing the prescribed contribution of the employer every month. The Drawing and Disbursing Officer will be responsible for adjusting the excess amount in the employee's account for the next month and for depositing the required amount in the next month in case of less amount.
It shall be the responsibility of the employee to ensure that the contributions being credited to the National Pension Scheme account of the employee are correct every month and to make use of the facilities provided by the Central Maintenance Agency.
If any error is found in it, the employee must give a written statement to the Drawing and Disbursing Officer. The employee who does not take action in this way will not have the right to make any complaint (legal) in the future.
The Annual Return of Permanent Pension Account Number (PRAN) of the employee will be sent to the concerned employee by the Central Record Keeping Agency every year. Also, the Central Record Keeping Agency shall make available to the registered employee the details of the amount deposited in the account of the concerned employee at any time on its website.
After the National Pension System (NPS) is operationalized, the partial refund and retirement of the employees of the Board, voluntary retirement, death or termination of membership of the scheme and the consequential benefits of the scheme can be processed by the Controlling Officer to the Central Record Keeping Agency as per the norms prescribed by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) and the benefits will be payable to the employees and their heirs as per the instructions from time to time.
