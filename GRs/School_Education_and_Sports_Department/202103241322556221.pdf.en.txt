Preamble: In pursuance of the Central Government's Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, the State Government has passed the Maharashtra Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Rules, 2011 and in accordance with sub-section (3) of section (4) of the said rule, where a school is not available within a walkable distance of one kilometre from the habitation for children studying in classes I to V as per clause (a) of sub-section (1) of Annexure-I, section 4 of the said rule, and within a walkable distance of three kilometres from the habitation for children studying in classes VI to VIII as per clause (b) of sub-section (1) of section 4 of the said rule, the State Government shall provide free and compulsory education to the children studying in the said habitation.
The Maharashtra Primary Education Council, in order to issue a government decision on declaring habitations, has sought information from all chief executive officers, zilla parishads regarding the distance between schools and habitations.

After the analysis, 3073 habitations in rural and urban areas and a total of 16,334 students have been identified to provide the benefit of transport facility to the students.
Based on the above, the government has taken the following decisions:
The Maharashtra Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Rules, 2011, framed by the State Government in pursuance of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, inter alia, provide for transport allowance/ transportation facilities for the purpose of providing primary education in school for children belonging to small habitations, as may be prescribed by the State Government or the local authority, where there is no school within the area or limits of the nearest school as specified under sub-section (3) of section 4 of the said rule, in the areas specified under sub-section (1) (a) and (b) of Annexure 1, section 4 of the said rule.
