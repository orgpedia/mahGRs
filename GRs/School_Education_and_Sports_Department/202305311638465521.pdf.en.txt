Date of release: May 31, 2023
Introduction: Various countries in the European Union are experiencing a shortage of skilled manpower.
Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) in Maharashtra provide training in skill development to students. The students of classes 9 to 12 are being taught skill-based subjects. The state has a large number of skilled manpower. This skilled manpower can be provided with job opportunities in European Union countries. However, for this it is necessary to provide additional training to these students for some advanced skills, the German language, etiquette to be observed abroad, etc. A task force of ministers and secretaries of all the departments concerned was under the consideration of the government to discuss the matter.
Government Decision: To suggest measures to be taken to provide skilled manpower to European countries as per their demand. The Government is approving the setting up of the following Task Force under the Chairmanship of the Minister (School Education).

The working group will be as follows:
(i) To study the modalities of training to be provided to the European Union for providing skilled manpower.

(ii) Knowledge of foreign language is required to be imparted to the students from school life.
Accordingly, discussions will be held on imparting knowledge of German language in government/ local body schools.
(iii) Plumber, Electrician, Nursing, Hotel Management, Medical Equipment Technician, Various Technicians etc.
Create a training plan for this.
(iv) To work out the modalities for setting up of at least 1 to 8 Centres of Excellence in each academic discipline. To create temporary training centres by creating state-of-the-art facilities in the existing Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) till the Centre of Excellence starts functioning.
(v) Measures to provide at least 80% to 90% of the skills required by the EU for skilled/ technical manpower from these training centres/ centres of excellence.
(vi) Draft Memorandum of Understanding to be signed with Germany in this regard.
The committee will submit its report to the government in a month.
