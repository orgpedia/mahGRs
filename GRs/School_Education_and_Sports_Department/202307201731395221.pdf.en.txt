Pr/ RTE- 520/2023/4009, Dy. 25.05.2023 Preamble: As per Section 12 (1) (c) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, at least 25% of the total number of seats in the respective classes at the entry level in private unaided schools shall be reserved for children belonging to disadvantaged and weaker sections. Further, under Section 12 (2) of the said Act, provision has been made for reimbursement of educational fee of the student admitted under RTE 25% by the State Government. Accordingly, the Joint Director of Education (Primary), Maharashtra State, Pune has requested the Government to provide funds as per the aforesaid Reference 2. For the academic year 2023-2024, Rs. A sum of Rs. 200.00 crore has been allocated for this. Accordingly, as per the request made by the Joint Director of Education (Primary), Maharashtra State, Pune, RTE has decided to provide 25% reimbursement of educational fees of the admitted student up to Rs. 40.00 crore is under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the provisions made under Section 12 (1) (c) and Section 12 (2) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, RTE has decided to provide Rs. Approval is being given to hand over a sum of Rs. 40.00 crore (Rupees Forty Crores only) to the Director of Education (Primary), State of Maharashtra, Pune.
The Director of Education (Primary), Maharashtra State, Pune shall disburse the funds in proportion to the reasonable demand made by the districts. The funds released should be used only for reimbursement of academic fees for the years 2022-23 and 2023-24. A separate fund will be provided for reimbursement of the already pending education fees.

3. Government circular for reimbursement of educational fees. As on 21.8.2019, the following should be verified.
The details of the fees to be charged for the students of class 1 to 8 should be declared on the Saral portal or the RTE portal. The school has its own website. Such schools should also declare the details of fees on the website.
The school should attach a certificate of recognition of RTE under the Right to Education Act.
3) Students who have already been admitted to the school through the online lottery system should ensure that they are studying in the concerned school through the Saral and RTE portal.
All students should have their Aadhaar card registered on the Saral portal. Only the number of students enrolled should be considered. Also, only 25 per cent of the total number of students will be considered for reimbursement.
(5) No school shall be entitled to reimbursement of tuition fee on the ground of acquiring any land, building, equipment or other facilities free of cost or at a concessional rate as provided in the proviso to section 12 (2) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, to the extent of the number of children to whom the condition of free education is imposed.
4. The grant shall be spent for the purpose for which it has been sanctioned.
All the conditions of the Government Circular dated 12.4.2023 of the Finance Department are being fulfilled.
