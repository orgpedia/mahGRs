The Proposal:
Section 12 (1) (c) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 provides for reservation of 25% seats at the entry level in private unaided schools for children belonging to disadvantaged and weaker sections. With reference to No. The Director of Education (Primary), Maharashtra State, Pune has submitted a request for funds to the government through a letter. A sum of Rs 150 crore has been allocated for this purpose in the Budget 2019-20. Of this, 60 per cent of the funds are available for distribution. The matter was under consideration of the government. The government's decision is as follows:
Government's decision:
As per Section 12 (1) (c) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, a sum of Rs. 90 crore (Rs. Approval is being given to handover to the State of Maharashtra, Pune.

Government Resolution No: RTE-2019/ PR No.150/ SD-1 2. Director of Education (Primary) to disburse funds as per demand of districts.
The funds will be used for reimbursement of 50% of the tuition fees for the academic year 2018-19.
The reimbursement of the academic fee for the academic year 2018-19 should be done after verifying the following aspects as per the Government Circular dated August 21, 2019.
The schools, which are providing 25 per cent admissions under the Right to Education (RTE) Act, have released the details of the fee to be charged for students of classes 1 to 8 on the Saral portal or the RTE portal. Schools that have their own website should also declare the details of the fee on it.
The school should attach a certificate of recognition of RTE under the Right to Education Act.
Students who have been admitted through online admission should ensure that they are studying in the same school through the Saral and RTE portal.
All students should be enrolled on Aadhaar card as well as on Saral system.
It should be ensured that schools do not take advantage of government land or government land on lease at concessional rates.
The schools should submit their report in the accompanying Form-A. The report should be compiled at the district level and the Director of Education (Primary), Maharashtra State, Pune should submit a consolidated report of all the districts to the government.
