In order to provide quality sports facilities, technical training, improvement in the standard of sports, to enable the players of the state to perform at national and international level, under the State Sports Policy-2012, educational institutions run by the private/ government and approved by the concerned departments of the state government, working for the development of various sports and registered under the Public Trust Act 1950 or the Societies Registration Act 1860, sports boards, sports associations, etc. 1 has been approved by the government. As per the said decision, the matter of inclusion of registered cooperative housing societies in the list of eligible institutions for disbursement of sports facility subsidy was under consideration.
Government Decision- As per the State Sports Policy-2012, conditions have been laid down for the creation of sports facilities in educational institutions, private clubs, sports boards, authorities, local bodies in the state and to provide sports subsidy for the physical fitness of sportspersons and general public in rural and tribal areas in order to increase the performance of sportspersons and to produce international and national players.
