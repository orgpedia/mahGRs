Attendance of teachers and non-teaching staff associations

Department of School Education and Sports
GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: SRIKH- 2020/ PR No. 136/ TNT-1
4th Floor, Extension Building, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk
Madame Cama Marg, Mantralaya, Mumbai- 400 032
Date: 18th of March 2020
Government Resolution No. 1: Narikari-2014/ No. 165/ TNT-1 dated 15th September, 2014
Preamble: There are about 6 lakh teachers and 74,000 non-teaching staff working in the aided and unaided primary, upper primary, secondary, higher secondary/ junior colleges, teachers' schools of local self-government institutions and private managements in the State. The teachers and non-teaching staff have formed various organisations to press for their demands. These organizations organize two to three days sessions for various purposes. The period of time required to attend the said session as well as to travel to and from the place of the session shall be deemed to be the period of duty or special casual leave is sought to be granted for this period. This demand is accepted by the administrative department of the local bodies. Accordingly, the government is taking the following decisions regarding the attendance of teachers and non-teaching staff in the sessions organized by these organizations.
Government's decision: The Government Decision dated 15th September, 2014 is hereby superseded and approval is granted to the teaching and non-teaching staff to attend the session of their Association subject to the following terms and conditions: 1. The Association should be recognized. The session should be held only during the non-scheduled (during long holidays) period.
2) Prior permission of the concerned Director of Education (Directorate of Primary Education/ Directorate of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education) should be obtained for holding the convention.
3) Allowing the session while the academic work is going on, if the teacher is present in large numbers in the session, if the school is closed during this period, the students will suffer academic loss. Therefore, the said session should not be allowed to be held during a period other than a long vacation. Such proposals should not be sent to the government for approval. Such proposals will not be considered at the government level.

7) The cost of travel to and from the conference should be borne by the teaching and non-teaching staff themselves. Travel allowance, daily allowance will not be admissible to them for the session.
The teachers and non-teaching staff availing the benefit of special leave to attend the convention shall not be allowed to attend the convention/ seminar/ conference of other organizations for a period of one year from the date of grant of such leave.
9) Special leave for the said period should be granted after verification of attendance at the session. The attendance certificate of the teachers during the session is binding on the organization, besides, the attendance of the teachers in the session will not be considered.
