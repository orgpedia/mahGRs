The aforesaid funds/ grants made available by the Department of Finance on the BEAMS system during the financial year 2020-2021 are being released subject to the following terms and conditions: 1. No expenditure shall be incurred for purposes other than those for which the funds/ grants are sanctioned. Also, you can't switch to other titles.
2) The progress report of the said expenditure should be submitted to the government by the 10th of every month by the controlling authorities as per the monthly fund statement.
3) The rules mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget rule book should be followed while disbursing the subsidy. Further, the Finance Department shall pay the subsidy after obtaining the approval of the competent authority in accordance with the terms and conditions of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-Division-I as provided under Government Decision No. V.P.-2013/ PR. 30/2013/ Regulations, Part-II, dated 17th April, 2015.
(4) It shall be ensured that action is taken in accordance with the suggestions given by the High-Level Committee of Secretaries/ Cabinet as well as the Finance Department, where necessary, with regard to the allocation of funds on salary in accordance with the creation of posts.
(5) When providing grants to private NGOs, the grant should be paid with the approval of the competent authority in accordance with the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-1, Sub-Division I, serial number 27, rule 149.

Government Decision No. Anuvyee 4919/ (32/19)/ Budget 6) In the case of the distribution of provisions for which an administrative sanction order is required, the grant shall be disbursed after the approval of the competent authority as provided under Part-I, Sub-Division-III, Schedule-IV, Rule 27 (2).
7) The post-salary grants to the Zila Parishads should not be disbursed without their grants being determined.
8) The approval of the Finance Department is required in respect of the disbursement of funds for the schemes provided under the new head in accordance with the new expenditure.
9) Before sanctioning the grants-in-aid to various Government/ Administrative Institutions and Aided Institutions, the Department should obtain utilization certificates of the expenditure of the grants-in-aid disbursed earlier. Further grants should not be disbursed unless the utilization of the earlier grant is completed. The treasury officers should not pass the payment unless a copy of the bank account statement for the previous month is attached with the payment submitted for drawing funds from the subsidiary grant.
10) The salary subsidy given by the government will have to be transferred to the bank account of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the salary subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
11) Before disbursing the grants to the local self-government institutions and corporations, the amount coming to the state government from these institutions should be reviewed and the remaining grants should be disbursed only after recovering those amounts. Also, detailed information should be collected about the utilization of the grants distributed by the government to these institutions and where the unutilized amount is kept. If the unaccounted money is kept in the bank, then its bank account statement should be submitted to the government. If the funds made available for expenditure on a programme are unutilized, the funds should not be released for the same purpose in the current year without the approval of the Finance Department.
12. While drawing the payments of beneficiaries under the individual beneficiary programme, the grants of individual beneficiaries shall not be drawn unless the list of beneficiaries is fixed and linked with the payment with proper details. The programmes of individual beneficiaries should be linked with Aadhaar number and the subsidy should be disbursed through Electronic Clearance System (ECS).
Payment of scholarship/ tuition fee reimbursement, individual beneficiaries as well as subsidy etc. should be made through Electronic Clearance System (ECS) only. All such details should be linked to Aadhaar.
Administrative approval/ technical approval in respect of the construction proposal as well as the approval of the Committee of Secretaries, where necessary, shall be obtained and the work-wise funds shall be budgeted. Once the availability of funds for new works is ensured, the work should be started by issuing tender notices and commissioning orders, and the funds should be distributed as needed. The old works should be reviewed and completed on priority and the funds should not be provided to the Public Works Department in the form of deposit construction.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. 4919/ (32/19)/ BUDGET 15) In respect of Centrally Sponsored Schemes, the receipt of funds from the Central Government should be confirmed by the concerned Department through the Finance Branch of the Finance Department on receipt (for which no reference to the Expenditure/ Budget Branch is required) and thereafter, if the funds have been received, the matching share of the State Government should be disbursed by the Administrative Department at their level. There is no need to refer the fee separately to the Finance Department for concurrence. However, if the funds of the Central Government for the previous year are to be spent in the current year, it will be mandatory to obtain the consent of the Planning, Tribal Development Department and Social Justice and Special Assistance Department as well as the Finance Department, as the case may be.

This has been notified vide Government Resolution dated 1.12.2016. As per the government's decision and guidelines. August 24, 2017 and d. Purchase payments should be drawn from the treasury without the completion of the procurement process as per the provisions of the Government Decision dated 8th December, 2017 and without the determination of the supplier and in compliance with the conditions of this Guideline. Cheques for purchase should be issued in the name of the supplier concerned and preferably through Electronic Clearance System (ECS).
17) Funds are often spent by departments through short payments. However, non-submission of its detailed payment may lead to accounting objections and possible default. However, henceforth, the departments should not draw funds under the head of accounts without the consent of the Finance Department unless they submit the head under which the old detailed payments are pending.
18) The departments should note that it is a kind of irregularity that the funds left unspent for various reasons are technically shown as spent by the departments by transferring them to their subordinate corporation. Provisions shall not be drawn or disbursed unless the previous unutilized funds under them (kept outside the purview of their own provident fund account/ corporation account or otherwise showing expenditure) have been fully spent. It is also pointed out that under the self-registration accounts, the departments are found to be having huge funds lying unutilized. If the funds are not spent within the stipulated time, they should be returned to the government. If the unaccounted money is kept in the bank, its bank account statement should be submitted to the government.
19) In the case of expenditure on payment of outstanding dues, the funds shall not be withdrawn unless it is satisfied that the material purchased has been physically received and its accounts maintained properly.
