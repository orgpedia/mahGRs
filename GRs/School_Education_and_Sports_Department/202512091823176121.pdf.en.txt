Necessary precautions should be taken regarding the safety and security of the students during the organized trip.
6) KshetraVeta (National Education Policy-based experiential learning): The National Education Policy requires that students be given experiential learning.
Accordingly, teachers should plan field visits for students to post offices, banks, police stations, gram panchayats, municipalities, historical sites, local industries, etc.
It should be seen that such field visits will develop an understanding of various procedures, responsibilities, public service, documentation, AGEN skills among the students by observing the various processes firsthand.
7) Conduct of elocution competition:
Each school should organize oratory / storytelling / debate or poetry reading competitions on at least 3 subjects during the year.
Topics to be chosen should include patriotism, environment, science, social values, democracy, technology, health, sanitation, etc.
Emphasis should be laid on developing students' original speech, correctness of Marathi language, confident presentation, time planning, and expressive skills.
Winners should be encouraged with certificates.
8) Small Enterprises Festival (Business Education Initiative):
The activity should be designed to help students understand business skills, money management, bargaining, sales skills, and customer behavior.
Students themselves will set up small shops of vegetables, handicrafts, home-cooked food, exchange of books, recyclables, etc.
Students should be given hands-on practical experience in buying and selling, profit and loss, and accounting.
If possible, this activity and the parents' gathering should be held on the same day.
The above activities will develop students' self-confidence, communication skills, social awareness, physical-psychological development, financial literacy, and practical life skills.
3. Every school should prepare an annual implementation plan of all these activities and submit the action taken report to the District Education Officer (Primary / Secondary).
The Commissioner (Education) should also provide a link on the department's website and schools should fill in the information about these activities on the leak.
