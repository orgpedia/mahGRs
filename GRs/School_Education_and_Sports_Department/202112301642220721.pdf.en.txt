The Election Commission of India was established on 25th January 1950. The foundation day of the Election Commission of India is celebrated as National Voters' Day across the country since 2011. The main objective of celebrating National Voters Day is to encourage and facilitate voters, especially the new voters, to register their names in the electoral rolls.
This day is dedicated to the voters of the country and is used to increase the participation of voters in the electoral process. On the occasion of National Voters" Day, voters are given voter ID cards.
In this backdrop, National Voters' Day should be compulsorily celebrated every year on 25th January in all medium schools (Class IX and X) and all junior colleges of all the mandals of Maharashtra to make them good and responsible citizens. The following programmes will be held on the day:
Essay, elocution, rangoli, painting, poster, reel (one minute video), meme, song, satire, poetry, slogan etc. )
