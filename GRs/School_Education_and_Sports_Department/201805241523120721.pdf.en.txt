The aforesaid funds/ grants disbursed during the financial year 2018-2019 may be released immediately to the Drawing and Disbursing Authorities subject to the following terms and conditions: 1) No expenditure shall be incurred for purposes other than the purpose for which the funds/ grants are sanctioned. Also, you can't switch to other titles.
2) The progress report of the said expenditure should be submitted to the government by the 10th of every month by the controlling authorities as per the monthly fund statement.
3) While disbursing the post-salary subsidy, the Finance Department issued Government Decision No. 2013/ No.
The subsidy should be paid with the approval of the competent authority as per Rule 40, Roll No. 6 of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-Division I, dated 17th April, 2015.
4) Payment of the arrears of wage subsidy under Rule 4, Rule 39 (b), Note 4, Rule 5, Rule 39, (b), Note 5 and Rule 6, Rule 40 shall be made with the approval of the competent authority.
5) The non-salaried grants given to the Municipal Corporation and Municipalities cannot be spent without the permission of the Finance Department.
(6) When providing grants to private non-governmental organizations, the grant should be paid with the approval of the competent authority in accordance with the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-1, Sub-Division I, serial number 27, rule 149.
(7) In the case of distribution of provisions for which an administrative sanction order is required, the grant shall be disbursed after the order is issued with the approval of the competent authority as provided under Part-I, Sub-Division-III, Roll No. IV, Rule 27 (2).
(8) Subsidies for the Zilla Parishads shall not be disbursed without their grants being determined.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: ANUV 4918/ (42/18)/ BUDGET 9) The approval of the Finance Department is required for the disbursement of funds for the schemes provided under the new head in accordance with the new expenditure.
10) Before sanctioning the grant-in-aid to the various Government/ Administrative Institutions as well as the aided institutions, the Department should obtain the utilization certificates of the expenditure of the grant-in-aid disbursed earlier. Further, no further subsidy shall be released until 75% of the subsidy given 3 months ago is utilized. The treasury officers should not pass the payment unless a copy of the bank account statement for the previous month is attached with the payment submitted for drawing funds from the subsidiary grant.
The salary subsidy provided by the government will have to be transferred to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the salary subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
12) Before disbursing the grants to the local self-government institutions and corporations, the amount coming to the state government from these institutions should be reviewed and the remaining grants should be disbursed only after recovering those amounts. Also, detailed information should be collected about the utilization of the grants distributed by the government to these institutions and where the unutilized amount is kept. If the unaccounted money is kept in the bank, then its bank account statement should be submitted to the government. If the funds made available for expenditure on a programme are unutilized, the funds should not be released for the same purpose in the current year without the approval of the Finance Department.
13. While drawing payments to beneficiaries under the individual beneficiary programme, grants to individual beneficiaries shall not be drawn unless the list of beneficiaries is fixed and linked to the payment with proper details. The programmes of individual beneficiaries should be linked with Aadhaar number and the disbursement of subsidy should be done through Electronic Clearance System (ECS).
Payment of scholarship/ tuition fee reimbursement, individual beneficiaries as well as subsidized grants, etc. should be made through Electronic Clearance System (ECS) only. All such details should be linked to Aadhaar.
15) Work-wise funds are required to be budgeted after obtaining administrative approval/ technical approval in respect of the construction proposal as well as approval of the Committee of Secretaries, where necessary. Once the availability of funds for new works is ensured, the work should be started by issuing a tender notice for the work and the work order should be issued and the funds should be disbursed as per the requirement. The old works should be reviewed and completed on priority and the funds should not be provided to the Public Works Department in the form of deposit construction.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. 4918/ (42/18)/ BUDGET 16) In respect of Centrally Sponsored Schemes, the receipt of funds from the Central Government should be confirmed by the Finance Division of the Department concerned on the expenditure (for which no reference to expenditure/ budgetary branch is required) and thereafter, if the funds have been received, the State Government's matching share should be disbursed by the Administrative Division at their level. There is no need to refer the fee separately to the Finance Department for concurrence. However, if the funds of the central government from the previous year are to be spent in the current year, it will be mandatory to get the approval of the Finance Department. Funds from the Central and State share budgeted under Centrally Sponsored/ Centrally Sponsored Programmes should not be diverted.
17)" Manual of Procedure for Purchase of Office to be made by the Government Department," Department of Industries, Energy and Labour vide its No. This has been notified vide Government Resolution dated 1.12.2016. Purchase payments should be drawn from the treasury without the completion of the procurement process as per the decision of the Government and the accompanying guidelines and without the determination of the supplier and in compliance with the conditions of this guideline.
Cheques for purchase should be issued in the name of the supplier concerned and preferably through Electronic Clearance System (ECS).
18) Funds are often spent by departments through short payments. However, non-submission of its detailed payment may lead to accounting objections and possible default. However, henceforth, the departments should not draw funds under the head of accounts without the consent of the Finance Department unless they submit the head under which the old detailed payments are pending.
19) The departments should note that it is a kind of irregularity that the funds that have remained unutilized for various reasons are technically shown as spent by the departments by transferring them to their subordinate corporation. Provisions shall not be drawn or disbursed unless the previous unutilized funds under them (kept outside the purview of their own provident fund account/ corporation account or otherwise showing expenditure) have been fully spent. It is also pointed out that under the self-registration accounts, the departments themselves are found to be having huge fund outgo. If the funds are not spent within the stipulated time, they should be returned to the government. If the unaccounted money is kept in the bank, its bank account statement should be submitted to the government.
20) The expenditure in respect of computerization to be incurred by the departments should be as per the provisions of the Government Decision No. 1716/ PR No. 286/39 dated 9th August, 2016 of the Department of Information and Technology.
21) In the case of expenditure for making outstanding payments, the funds shall not be withdrawn unless it is satisfied that the material purchased has been physically received and its accounts maintained properly.
