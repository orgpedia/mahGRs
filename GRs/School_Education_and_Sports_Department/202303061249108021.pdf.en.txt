As per Section 12 (1) (c) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009, 25 per cent seats in private unaided schools at the entry level are to be reserved for children belonging to disadvantaged and weaker sections. As per Section 12 (2) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, the fee reimbursement for students admitted under RTE 25% is made to the respective schools at the rates fixed by the Government annually and the fees fixed by the schools in that year, whichever is lower. The reimbursement rate for the academic year 2019-2020 is Rs. 17670/- per student and later on during the lockdown due to the outbreak of COVID-19, the schools in the State were closed. 8000/- has been reduced to Rs. As the impact of COVID-19 in the country continued in 2020-2021 and 2021-2022, the fee reimbursement rates were not revised. Since the schools have started regularly for the academic year 2022-2023, the fee reimbursement rate for the academic year 2019-2020 is Rs. 17670/- per student was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
Section 12 (1) (c) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 provides for free education up to upper primary level in private unaided self-financing schools for children belonging to disadvantaged and weaker sections. For this purpose, the schools concerned are reimbursed the educational fees for the academic year 2022-2023 as per the rates fixed by the Government under Section 12 (2) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009.
The rate of reimbursement of educational fee for the year 2019-2020 is Rs. 17670/- per student was fixed. However, for the academic year 2023-2024, the fee reimbursement rate per student for the academic year 2020-2021 was Rs.

While reimbursing the educational fee, the following should be verified as per the Government Circular dated 21.08.2019:
Schools which have admitted children belonging to disadvantaged and weaker sections in at least 25% of the seats under the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, should declare the details of the educational fee to be charged for students of classes 1 to 8 on the Saral portal or on the RTE portal. Schools that have their own website should also declare the fee details on the website.
The school should attach a certificate of recognition of RTE under the Right to Education Act.
3.Students who have been admitted to the school earlier through the online lottery system should ensure that they are studying in the concerned school through the Saral and RTE portal.
All students should have their Aadhaar card registered on the Saral portal. Only the number of students enrolled on Aadhaar shall be considered and only 25% of the actual number of students shall be considered for reimbursement
(5) No school shall be entitled to reimbursement of tuition fees to the extent of a certain number of children who have been conditioned to receive free education on the ground that they have received any land, building, equipment or other facilities free of cost or at a concessional rate as provided in the proviso to section 12 (2) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009.
