Introduction: - Various activities are undertaken by the National Council of Educational Research and Training, New Delhi to promote creativity, inventiveness and innovation in science and mathematics among students.
(a) The "National Children's Scientific Exhibition" is a part of this initiative. At the state level, selected exhibits are selected in order of merit for the national level exhibition to be held next year.
(b) Science exhibition begins at the Taluk level. The winning students at the taluk level won at the district level pedagogical exhibition and at the district level pedagogical exhibition.
Students participate in state-level pedagogical exhibitions.
(a) Every year, a district of Maharashtra is entrusted with the responsibility of organizing a state-level science exhibition.
The State Institute of Science (Regional Education Authority), Nagpur acts as the coordinating mechanism / institution.
(S) Science exhibitions are held every year at the Taluk level, District level and State level in the State. Students who fail in these competitions are given prizes. The government was considering introducing special initiatives to increase student participation and interest in science and technology among students, as well as to make science exhibitions more comprehensive and inspiring in Maharashtra.
The National Education Policy, 2020 emphasizes on transforming the quality of research as well as developing a holistic approach to create a developed Maharashtra. Accordingly, the state-level science exhibition will have upper primary level (classes 6B to 8B).

The Government's Decisions:
Science education has been given utmost importance in the National Education Policy, 2020. The emphasis is on developing a scientific temperament, rational thinking, and problem-solving ability in students. Students are being made easier to understand science through experiential and experiential learning. Digital labs, artificial intelligence (51), and computer thinking have been incorporated using technology to educate students. It also aims to empower students for the future by promoting research and innovation. For this, approval is being given to launch the "Mukhyamantri Vidyarthi Vigyan Vari" initiative and organize the following visits under this initiative.
(1) A total of 2148 students (358 X 6 = 2148), each of whom will be the first 6 to come to the block level science exhibition in the state, along with 20 accompanying supervisors / science teachers, should be organised to visit Raman Science Centre, Nagpur / Pimpri-Chinchwad Science Centre, Pune or Nehru Planetarium, Mumbai, whichever science centre is nearby.
(2) A total of 180 students and 08 supervisors / teachers selected from Primary and Secondary (General Group), Primary and Secondary (Disabled Group) and Primary and Secondary (Tribal Group) at the district level in the state should be organized to visit the ISRO Centre at Bengaluru.
(iii) A total of 575 participants, namely 51 outstanding students, 01 teacher, 01 teacher and 02 administrative officers / coordinators, who participated in the State Level Science Exhibition, are being recognized for organizing and incurring expenses for the visit to the NASA Center in the USA.
(4) Students selected at the Taluk level and District level in the State are being allowed to spend the funds by making provisions in the District Plan (DPDC) at the respective District level.
