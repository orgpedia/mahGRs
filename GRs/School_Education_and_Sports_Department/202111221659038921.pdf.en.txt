the prelude
Constitution is the foundation of any nation. The Constitution of India is a unifying thread that unites people of all faiths, religions, languages, castes and creeds. The Constitution of India provides for equality of opportunity, liberty of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship for all citizens. It was dedicated to the nation on November 26, 1949.
The Fundamental Principles, Fundamental Rights and Duties enshrined in the Constitution of India by Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar have been cherished by the citizens of independent India.
In order to inculcate the fundamentals of the Constitution in the minds of school children and to make them aware citizens, it is necessary to spread and propagate the knowledge of the Constitution, the fundamentals of the Constitution. With the objective of making the students fully familiar with the Constitution from school life and becoming aware of the fundamentals of the Constitution to become responsible citizens, the government was considering to organize various programs for the students and teachers of primary to high schools of all management mediums in the state on the occasion of Constitution Day.
All students should be fully familiar with the Constitution of India so that all students understand the scope and inclusiveness of the provisions of the Constitution and the fundamentals of the Constitution are engraved on the minds of the students. Constitution Day of India

In order to inculcate due respect for the Constitution by embracing it in one's life to become a citizen, the Government has decided to implement the" My Constitution, My Pride" initiative from 23rd November to 26th November, 2021.
Various educational programs should be organized for students and teachers of primary to high schools of all mediums of management in the state. school during this period.
Various activities such as essay writing, poetry writing, painting competition, elocution competition, slogans, poster making etc. should be organized in online/ offline mode. Students, parents, teachers and media persons attended the programme. During this period, the Preamble of the Indian Constitution should be read simultaneously in schools and junior colleges at 10 am on Constitution Day. Students, teachers and parents.
Seminars, lectures by experts on the Constitution, constitutional values and their inclusion in the school curriculum should be organised.
Under the" My Constitution, My Pride" initiative, the following educational programs should be organized.
