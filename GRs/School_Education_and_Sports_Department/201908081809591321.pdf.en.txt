Ministry (Extension), Mumbai-32
Date: August 8, 2019.
Reference 1. Department of School Education and Sports, Government Resolution No. 2118/ No. 243/ S.D. 4, D. July 9, 2019.
The government has decided to reconsider the subject plan and assessment scheme for classes 9 to 12. The committee was constituted under the chairmanship of the Commissioner of Education on July 9, 2019. Under this decision, the government was considering a revised subject plan and a revised assessment plan for the 11th and 12th grades.
The revised subject plan and revised assessment scheme for Class 11 and for Class 12 from 2020-21 onwards is being finalised as mentioned below.
The final assessment for Class 11 and for Class 12 from the academic year 2020-21 will be 600 marks instead of 650.
2) The marks obtained will be converted into grades instead of the existing 50 marks given to the subject of Environmental Science and the subject of Water Safety will be included in the subject of Environmental Science.
3) The written assessment is accompanied by an internal assessment to measure the different abilities of the students as well as a continuous assessment of the progress of the students.
4) The internal assessment will be allowed to be carried out throughout the year and not at the end of the academic year.
The 12th annual examination will be based on the entire syllabus of Class 12.
The format of the question paper and the component-wise weightage of the question paper for the 11th and 12th sessions as well as the annual examination will be decided by the State Board with the help of the Study Committee/ Study Group of the respective subjects.
To pass the 11th and 12th class, the student will have to score at least 35 percent marks in written and internal assessment in each subject.
