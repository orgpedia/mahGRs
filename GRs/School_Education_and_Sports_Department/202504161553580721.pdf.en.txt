Introduction: - The National Education Policy-2020 is the first education policy of the 21st century. The Kothari Commission in the post-independence era 1964-1966 and the National Education Policy in 1986 have led to many reforms in education in the state and the country. Now after almost 34 years, the National Education Policy-2020 has been approved by the Union Cabinet in its meetings held on 29th July 2020. This new National Education Policy has a structural change from the earlier structure of 10-2 − 3 to 5 − 3 − 3 − 4. It covers education from the foundational level to the undergraduate level. The National Education Policy is being implemented in the state in a phased manner. The policy is based on constitutional values, built on five fundamental pillars: access to education for all, equity, quality, affordable education, and accountability. The policy is also aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals to be achieved by 2030.

The books prepared by National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT), New Delhi based on National Curriculum Framework (Framework Level) 2022 and National Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2023 are being used by 23 states of the country. These textbooks have been produced by renowned, experienced experts from various fields across the country. Various competitive examinations at the national level are prepared based on these textbooks. These textbooks are used in schools of different boards across the country. The State Level Steering Committee, headed by Hon'ble Minister (School Education), State of Maharashtra, after due consideration by eminent and experienced experts in the field of education, has decided to use the Class-wise textbooks of the subject prepared by the National Council of Educational Research and Training in the State of Maharashtra also in a phased manner with necessary modification and contextualization.
The State Curriculum Framework (Framework Level) 2024 and the Infrastructure Curriculum 2024 based on the National Curriculum Framework (Framework Level) 2022 have been prepared by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune. Also, the State Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2024 has been prepared based on the National Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2023. The School Education Curriculum 2025 is under preparation.
The State Level Steering Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Hon'ble Minister, School Education, State of Maharashtra has approved the State Curriculum Framework (Infrastructure Level) 2024, Infrastructure Curriculum-2024 and State Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2024. In this view, the implementation of this new curriculum for all sub-levels of the foundational level and school education level was under the consideration of the government.

Language Policy: At present, only two languages are being taught in all management schools of Marathi and English medium from class 1 to 4. In the rest of the medium schools, Marathi and English are compulsory as per the three-language formula and three languages are being studied - Medium Language, Marathi and English.
According to the State Curriculum Framework School Education 2024, Hindi will henceforth be compulsory as a third language in Marathi and English medium schools for classes 1 to 5B. In other medium schools, three languages - medium language, Marathi and English - will be taught for classes I to V. The language policy for classes 6A to 90B will be similar to the State Curriculum Framework - School Education.

Textbooks and Textbooks: - As per the provision in the State Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2024, textbooks prepared by the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT), New Delhi in line with the National Education Policy 2020, shall be adapted with all necessary modifications for the State of Maharashtra. The curriculum must include elements such as foundational literacy and numeracy, learning to be holistic, integrated, enjoyable, and interesting, reducing curriculum content to enhance essential learning and logical thinking, experiential learning, etc., as outlined under the National Education Policy 2020.
Necessary reforms in the overall aspects of assessment, teaching methodology, schedule, monitoring and control, teacher training, etc. should also be issued by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune.
Textbook preparation for preparation of Bal Bharati textbooks should involve the participation of the respective heads of departments and subject experts of the State Council of Educational Research and Training from time to time. The State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune, should verify whether or not these textbooks are suitable for the implementation of the prescribed learning decisions. These final textbooks will then have to be approved by the State Level Coordination Committee headed by the Commissioner (Education). Textbooks can be implemented only after the approval of the State Level Coordination Committee.
As per the implementation schedule, the Maharashtra State Textbook Production and Curriculum Research Board, Balabharati, Pune, will be responsible for making the class-wise textbooks available in all the required mediums.
Textbook supplements, handbooks, etc. should be produced by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune.
Curriculum, textbooks, content, etc., of Children's Kitchens 19, 2, 3 should be prepared by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune and Maharashtra State Textbook Production and Curriculum Research Board, Balabharati, Pune office and handed over to the Department of Women and Child Development for printing and distribution.
Subject Plan: The subject plan as suggested in the State Curriculum Framework (Framework Level) 2024 and State Curriculum Framework (School Education) 2024 prepared by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune should be implemented as per the implementation stages. For further clarification, detailed instructions may be issued by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune, as required.
Evaluation: - Guidelines on assessment for school students as per the provisions in the National Education Policy and State Curriculum Frameworks should be issued separately by the State Council of Educational Research and Training, Maharashtra, Pune. For this, the Holistic Progress Card (HPC) should be used.
