The Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Servants and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005 came into force with effect from 1st July, 2006.
As per Section 6 of the Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Employees and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005, the competent authority for transfer has been declared. Provided further that the competent authority transferring under proviso 2 to this section may, by general or special order, delegate its powers under this section to any of its subordinate authorities. Accordingly, the decision to transfer the powers of various cadre officers in Group-A and Group-B of Maharashtra Education Service has been taken by the Government Decision dated 30th May, 2015.
As per the provisions of the said Act, a proposal for transfer of officers who have completed three years of service as on 31st May, 2019 was submitted to the Civil Services Board. urban
The officers mentioned in the table below are being transferred and posted to the post indicated in column No. 3 against their names with the prior approval of the competent authority as per the recommendation of the Service Board.

The said officers shall assume charge of their new post forthwith and shall not proceed on unauthorised leave. Also, the concerned should note that if any form of application is submitted to cancel the transfer, they will be eligible for disciplinary action.
Commissioner (Education), Pune, as per this order, shall immediately inform the concerned Controlling Officer about the immediate release of the said officers from their present posts and shall ensure that the transferred officers join the given place of posting within the prescribed period and submit a report to the Government.
In order to ensure effective implementation of this order, the salary of the transferred officers should not be drawn by the office of the transferor under any circumstances with effect from 1st July, 2019.
