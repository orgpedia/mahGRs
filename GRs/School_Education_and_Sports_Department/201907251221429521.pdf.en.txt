The Proposal:
The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 has been implemented for primary and upper primary schools. Since there is a need to frame a definite policy on night schools established as a social need for the convenience of the economically weaker sections of the society, the Government decision in Reference-1 has been passed with regard to the service matters of the teachers and non-teaching staff of the night school, the sustainability of the night school etc. However, the said Government decision dated 17.5.2017 is not being implemented properly. The members of the Legislative Council had submitted to the government. Accordingly, pursuant to the Government Decision dated 17.5.2017 and the Maharashtra Private Schools Employees (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1981, the matter of setting up a study group to resolve the issues related to the night shift was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
A Study Group is being appointed to suggest a solution to the problems faced by the night schools and to implement the Government decision dated 17.5.2017 in a proper manner as follows: 1. Director (Secondary and Higher Secondary), Maharashtra State, Pune-Chairman 2. Deputy Director of Divisional Education, Mumbai Member Secretary 3. Masoom (Non-Governmental Organization in Mumbai Division)- Non-Governmental Member

The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
(1) The Government Decision No. To review the problems/ reasons for non-implementation of the provisions of SAMARIKA-2016/ PR No.58/ TNT-1, dated 17.5.2017 and suggest remedial measures.
(2) To recommend to the Government to revise the workload/ tenure of the night school for the teaching and non-teaching staff of the night school as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Private School Employees (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1981 and to calculate the financial burden arising therefor.
The committee will submit its report to the government within four weeks.
