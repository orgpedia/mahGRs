Circular:
COVID-19 is spreading in the state. This virus spreads through the air. Spitting in public places by a person infected with this virus can also affect other people and increase the spread. Similarly, tuberculosis and other diseases can also be caused by spitting elsewhere.
In such a situation, it is the responsibility of the government to protect the health of the students, protect their health and provide them with a healthy environment for education. Therefore, the government was considering issuing anti-spitting guidelines in all educational institutions to prevent the health infection caused by spitting in the school premises and to prevent the students from getting infected with Covid and other such diseases. The following guidelines should be followed by all schools.
Guidelines: Spitting in the premises of all educational institutions (as educational institutions are in public places) is prohibited.
2. To inculcate the values of cleanliness among school children, teachers should make them aware of the importance of public hygiene, personal hygiene, which are included in the school curriculum as a common priority.
Students are learning through online education during the lockdown period due to the outbreak of COVID-19. Before the start of classes on this online platform, information should be given about the diseases caused by spitting and it should be encouraged to adopt the values of cleanliness.
4. Guide school children about the health hazards of spitting in public places with the help of local health workers and to discourage them from doing so, to guide them to protect their own health.
Teachers are role models for students. Hence, the teachers should strictly adhere to the guidelines. A fine of Rs 200 will be imposed on the violators. The maximum amount is Rs 1200. Penalty will be imposed.

It is the responsibility of the parents as well as the general public to protect the health of the students. A fine of Rs 200 will be levied on those who violate the rule. The maximum amount is Rs 1200. Penalty will be imposed.
The authority to collect the fine in this case will be the head of the concerned educational institution i.e. the principal/ principal.
8. Regular monitoring of these guidelines shall be done by the head of the educational institution concerned or by the teachers authorised by the head of the educational institution.
The Commissioner (Education), Maharashtra State, Pune, Director of Education (Secondary and Higher Secondary), Maharashtra State, Pune, Director of Education (Primary), Maharashtra State, Pune have been directed to issue necessary instructions to the principals regarding the issuance of" anti-spitting" guidelines in all educational institutions from the academic year 2021-22.
