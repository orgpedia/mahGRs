The Proposal:
1.1 In order to ensure that maximum number of students of the State enter the competitive examination of National Defence Academy (NDA) and join the army and serve the country, as well as to develop a disciplined, self-confident team spirit and to provide leadership-oriented soldier education at the secondary level, the competent and voluntary organizations have been approved to start thirty schools imparting military education in every district of the State and to start a batch of 40 students at the class 5 level from 1996-97 at 100% subsidy.
1.2 The criteria for evaluation of Government recognized private primary and secondary schools in the State, which have been recognized on permanent basis on non-grant basis and excluding the permanent term, have been prescribed vide order dated 15th November, 2011 and dated 16th July, 2013. The 6 additional batches of teachers from class 5th to 10th meeting the criteria are 36 teachers from a total of 18 batches from the 3 Sainik Schools mentioned below

Terms and Conditions:
(9) After the first sanctioned batch is 45 times, the maximum number of students in this additional batch should be 45 as per the prescribed norms.
(2) The recruitment of teachers must be made in accordance with the reservation policy prescribed by the Government and the prescribed procedure for recruitment.
(3) Appointment of teachers must be subject to personal approval.
(4) The grant shall be admissible only to the posts of teachers for which the said grant is sanctioned.
(5) The grant shall be admissible to the military school where the attendance of students, teachers and non-teaching staff of the school is recorded through biometric system.
(6) The number of students in the school will be calculated on the basis of the number on the District Educational System (U-DIS) and the Aadhaar card number of the students studying in the school.
(7) The grant will not be admissible unless the Aadhaar card number of all the teachers in the school and the personal recognition order are filled in a simple system.
(8) It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Education Officer to verify the authenticity of all the information as per the" Saral" system before disbursing the actual grant.
(9) It shall be obligatory to recruit the next teacher in the military schools in accordance with the prevailing rules and prescribed procedures regarding the entry into service.
3. Autonomy of the Government:- It is the discretion of the Government to evaluate the batch sanctioned on permanent non-subsidy basis, declare the assessed batch eligible for grant, sanction the grant to the batch and the Government will apply the grant to the eligible batch as per the availability of funds. This subsidy will not be applied retrospectively. Also, even if the batch is sanctioned on a permanent non-grant basis in any academic year, the Government will modify the grant formula keeping in view the financial position of the State, financial resources available from time to time, needs of the State and their priorities and the grant formula will be applicable as and when the grant is to be introduced.
4. Under the said Government decision, grants are being sanctioned to the posts of teachers in the units of these Military Schools and the orders regarding the distribution of direct grants will be issued separately.
This Government Resolution is being issued in accordance with the Informal Reference 1141/ Expenditure 5 of the Department of Finance, dated 7.12.2017.
