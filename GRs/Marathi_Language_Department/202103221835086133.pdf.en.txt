At present, the translation of English documents such as" Legislative Business, Budgetary Publications, Government Bills, Central and State Acts and Notifications," which are required to be submitted in Marathi language under Section 4 and 5 of the Maharashtra Official Language Act, 1964, is done through the Translation Branch of the Directorate of Languages. This includes confidential work as well as legislative and legislative work. It is mandatory for the language directorate to do this work. However, recently, many departments, government and semi-government offices, autonomous bodies, in addition to the above tasks, receive English documents for translation at the Directorate of Languages, e.g., department policy, plans, expert committee reports, etc. Therefore, in addition to the above mandatory tasks, the government was considering the formation of a private translator's registry to translate the documents received in English and Marathi into Marathi and English respectively.
Government Resolution: Meeting at 1, 2013/ No. 162/ Language 2, Dy. 19th April, 2014 and No. 2 here. NAMIKA 2017/ Q. No.83/ Language-2, D. The following Rules are being framed by superseding both the Government Decisions dated 25th January, 2018.

The government is also approving the setting up of a panel of private translators, as proposed earlier, for translating English and Marathi language content from documents in government and semi-government offices, autonomous bodies, etc. into Marathi and English respectively. The period of such nomination shall be three years or until further orders of the Government.
