Preamble: The Reference Government Resolution, dated 24th August, 2016 lays down a comprehensive procedure for the operation of the online system of" Aapka Sarkar." A computerized system" Aapka Sarkar" has been launched in the state to redress the grievances of the people in a time-bound manner. Complaints received on this system are to be handled by the Secretaries/ Principal Secretaries/ Additional Chief Secretaries of the concerned administrative departments at the level of the Ministry and the activities are to be reviewed at that stage. Similarly, the Chief Secretary and the Chief Minister's Secretariat will review the grievance redressal activities filed in this system from time to time. Accordingly, the issue of providing comprehensive information on grievance redressal on the" Aapka Sarkar" portal was under consideration.
GOVERNMENT Circular: In view of the above, the following instructions are being issued for effective implementation of Grievance Redressal on" Aapka Sarkar" Portal:
Complaints received on the system are to be handled by the Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Additional Chief Secretary of the concerned administrative departments at the level of the Ministry. Accordingly, complaints received on the system should be reviewed every month, grievances should be redressed within the stipulated time and the complainant should be informed on the portal.
(b) to direct the concerned officers responsible for redressal of grievances which have not been redressed within the stipulated time to take immediate remedial action. Each Secretary shall review the same in the following statement by the 5th day of every month.

(c) The Heads of Departments of all the Regional Offices under the State Government shall review the complaints received from their offices as well as their subordinate offices on the" Aapka Sarkar" portal and submit a report to their Administrative Department of the Ministry by 5th of every month in the above mentioned return.
(d) In case of complaints which are not redressed within the stipulated time at the field office level, they should submit a proposal to the senior office for guidance/ feedback/ approval. Receive feedback, resolve the complaint and inform the complainant on the portal accordingly.
e) In cases where it is not possible to resolve the complaint within the stipulated time as per the provisions of the rules, government decisions, circulars available, and if there are circumstances in which the resolution may take some additional time, then the complainant should be informed on the portal by stating appropriate reasons.
(e) if the Secretary of the Department concerned is unable to redress the grievances received in relation to the administrative departments of the Ministry within the prescribed time limit or the grievances are not redressed, the Secretary in charge of the Department shall, after giving necessary reasons therefor, direct the Secretary in charge of the Department to resolve the grievances within the prescribed time. The applicant should be informed that it is not possible to resolve the complaint with the approval of the Minister and should be registered on the portal.
