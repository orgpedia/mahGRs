The government has decided to implement the policy. As on 1st May, 2001, the Government has approved the distribution of classified advertisements of all Corporations/ Boards, Authorities, Commissions, State Government Companies at Government approved rates at their level. The government has also decided to make the process of preparing, distributing, planning and implementing the campaign through the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations. It was established on July 21, 2017. As per the request of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations. Cancellation of procedures for implementation of Government Advertisement Schemes prescribed as on 1st June, 2018. The Government is considering minor amendments in the procedure for implementation of the Government Advertisement Distribution Policy prescribed under the Government Decision dated 21st July, 2017. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government Decisions: The following procedures should be adopted in relation to the preparation and implementation of media plans.
Government departments/ semi-government organizations will send proposals to the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations for the preparation of a media plan, which should specify the following:
(a) Details of the schemes/ schemes/ programmes to be publicised (in 10 sentences) (b) The expected message to be conveyed through the campaign (c) The general schedule of the media plan (d) The budgetary allocations available for the implementation of the media plan (e) The total amount for the following media of the media plan (Newspaper, Doordarshan, Television, All India Radio, Private Apps and Channels, Social Media, External Media, etc.

6. Media Plan The Planning and Implementation Committee shall approve the media plan.
Thereafter, after the approval of the Minister of the department concerned, the department concerned will issue a formal approval order to the Directorate General of the department for the implementation of the media plan.
After the approval, the Media Plan Planning and Implementation Committee will take further action on the media plan regarding the publication. The department concerned shall provide details of the office through which the payment of the expenditure on publicity will be made (name, address, contact number and GST number of the office).
8. Selection of advertising agency for preparation of advertisement (creative) will be done by the committee.
The committee will guide the advertising agencies in creating creative content for various media. Organizations on the shortlist will be given a period of 8 to 10 days to prepare a draft creative of the advertisement.
The Directorate General shall submit to the Creative Committee its draft/ draft of the budget of the expenses paid to the institutions or within the limits of the value determined earlier. The Committee will select one or more institutions as per requirement on the basis of Quality and Rates (QCBS) after considering the draft creative submitted by the institution and the cost to be charged by them.
The selected institutions will be advised to start work through the Directorate General. For this, the concerned agencies will be given at least 15 days to prepare their advertisement. After that, the Directorate General will publish the advertisement according to the media plan.
The rates fixed by the Directorate General/ DAVP will be applicable for the media plan. Media for which standard rates do not apply (e.g. Out of home media, social media, etc.) or any other requirement, the scope of work will be included.
