Government circular
The 74th anniversary of India's Independence Day will be celebrated on August 15, 2021. The government under the" Break-the-Chain" to break the chain of corona virus. Orders are due June 4, 2021. Like last year, the following instructions are being given to organize the Independence Day program in the state: 1. The Independence Day should be celebrated by hoisting the flag at all the divisional/ district/ sub-divisional/ taluka headquarters as well as at the Gram Panchayat headquarters in the state.
Divisional Commissioners of Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Nashik, Amravati and Konkan should make appropriate arrangements for holding flag hoisting ceremonies in their respective divisions as well as at the district headquarters.
The main government program of the state. The Chief Minister will unfurl the National Flag at Mantralaya, Mumbai at 9.05 am. In Pune. The flag will be hoisted by the Governor.
4. District and sectoral headquarters. The Prime Minister will hoist the flag.
The main official ceremony of the flag hoisting should be held simultaneously across the state at 9.05 am. On this day from 8.35 am to 9.35 am. No flag hoisting or any other official or semi-official function should be held during this period. If an office or institution wants to have its own flag hoisting ceremony, they should do it at 8.35 am. before 9.35 a.m. to be organized later.
6) The national anthem should be played while saluting the national flag. The band prepared at the time of opening should be played before and after opening.
The theme of the speech should be one that highlights the significance of Independence Day and inspires the audience to work for the unity and integrity of the country.
8. the flag. In case the Minister/ Minister of State is unable to reach the venue of the function on time due to unavoidable reasons, the Divisional Commissioner at the Divisional Headquarters and the District Collector at the District Headquarters shall ensure that the flag hoisting ceremony is conducted smoothly. The respective district and tehsil level officers will hoist the national flag at the sub-divisional headquarters and taluk headquarters respectively, while the sarpanch or village head will hoist the national flag at the gram panchayat headquarters.
9) Local public representatives, freedom fighters, wives and parents of the martyred soldiers, as well as Corona warriors such as doctors, sanitation workers, health workers, and some citizens who have overcome this disease.

Considering the background of the Corona virus, the organizers will be fully responsible for ensuring that the entire program of Independence Day will be held by following all the rules regarding social distancing, the organizers should take action to invite the dignitaries accordingly. All participants must wear the national dress.
11. Mask is mandatory for all. Strictly follow all the rules of safe work and broadcast live through the website so that more and more people can watch the ceremony at home.
12. Spread the slogan of" Atmanirbhar Bharat" through this program and social media.
During the day, various programs such as tree plantation, debate competition at the inter-school/ inter-college level, quiz/ patriotic essay and poetry competitions should be organized through online mode. Important schemes should be launched, patriotic songs/ speeches of selected students should be organized through social media. Government buildings should be illuminated, webinars should be organized on any topic, patriotic online campaigns should be run by NSS and NYKS, songs related to patriotism or national integration should be promoted/ given messages through social media and digital media, citizens should take necessary action to celebrate Independence Day through innovative initiatives like waving the national flag from the balcony/ terrace of the house.
All public and government buildings in the state (even in villages) as well as forts of historical importance (e.g. Raigad, Sinhagad, Shivneri, Purandar, Vasai, Pratapgarh, Daulatabad and Sitabardi should be made national flags.
The instructions issued under the Government Circular No. FLG- 1091/30 dated 20th March, 1991 and No. FLG-1091 (2)/ 30 dated 5th December, 1991 and Circular No. FLG 1098/343/ 30 dated 11th March, 1998 regarding the proper manner of hoisting the National Flag shall be strictly followed. It should also be ensured that the training of flag-hoisting is done in color.
It should also be ensured that the national flag is in good condition and is lowered at sunset.
The Divisional Commissioners of Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Nashik and Amravati will make arrangements for the flag hoisting ceremony at their headquarters, so there is no need for the district collectors to hold separate programmes. Divisional Commissioner, Konkan will make arrangements for the ceremony at Konkan Bhavan.
There is no need for the District Collector, Mumbai City to take action for flag hoisting.
2. Notwithstanding the above general directions, the concerned Divisional Commissioners and District Collectors are being allowed to take necessary appropriate action in their respective jurisdictions with the concurrence of the Guardian Minister, in view of the COVID-19 Positivity Rate, to celebrate the Independence Day in a befitting manner.
While celebrating this event, care should be taken to strictly follow the instructions given by the Ministry of Home Affairs and Ministry of Health and Family Welfare in the wake of Corona virus.
