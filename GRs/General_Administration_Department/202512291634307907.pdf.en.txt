Government circular:
The government had earlier issued a notification in Reference No. 1, regarding when and how to inform the applicant about the charges to be levied while furnishing information under the Right to Information Act, Look and Rules thereunder. 1 here. The circular has been issued on 17th November 2017. However, the State Information Commission, State of Maharashtra has issued its Reference No. 2 The letter herein points out that the provisions of the said circular are not in consonance with the Act and requests the Government to fix a revised procedure in accordance with the provision of the Act.
Section 7 (9) of the Right to Information Act, 2005 requires the public information officer to provide the information to the applicant as soon as practicable after receipt of the information application and in any case within 30 days from the date of the request. Also, section 7 (3) (a) provides for the exclusion of the period between the sending of the said notice and the delivery of the notice after the notification of the additional notice is sent, for the purpose of computing the period of 30 days. According to this provision, there is no provision for intimation of information charges within a certain period of time after receipt of the application and the primary responsibility of the Public Information Officer is to furnish the information within the time limit prescribed in the Act.
3. In pursuance of the above provisions, the Government directs that, in an application received under the Right to Information Act, 2005, if a fee is to be charged for furnishing the information, the Public Information Officer shall, in accordance with the provisions of the Act and taking into account the circumstances of each case, inform the applicant by post or e-mail as soon as practicable the amount of the fee for furnishing the information, including the page number b of the information and the cost of the postage. The Public Information Officer should also ensure that the entire process of providing the information is completed within the time limit prescribed in the Act.
