The Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Servants and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: In view of the fact that the outbreak of COVID-19 in the State has not yet been contained and considering the possibility of a third wave of this epidemic, instructions were given to make limited changes in the current financial year 2021-22 till August 14, 2021. The following changes are proposed to be made in the current financial year 2021-22.
(1) In view of the fact that Maharashtra is presently a Corona State and the possibility of a third wave, as well as the adverse impact on the State's economy due to the COVID-19 pandemic and the consequent restrictions, in order to limit the expenditure on transfer allowance, general transfers within the limit of 25% of the total number of working posts shall be made with the approval of the competent authority as mentioned in Section 6 of the Act.
(2) While effecting all transfers within the limit of 25%, all eligible officers/ employees who have completed the prescribed period in the concerned post, including those who have completed more than the prescribed period in the concerned post, should be transferred on priority.
3) First of all, the general changes. It will be completed by August 9, 2021.
4) Only after the completion of the process of general transfers, the posts which remain vacant will be transferred for special reasons. from August 10, 2021. It will be available until August 30, 2021. Therefore, the officers/ employees working in the post which is not vacant cannot be transferred to another post on special grounds.
In cases where the competent authority is satisfied that the transfer is necessary for special reasons under Section 4 (4) of the Transfer Act, the transfers to be made on such special reasons shall be made only after stating the reasons in writing.

(6) Also, transfers to be made on special grounds shall be made in accordance with Section 4 (5) of the Act with the approval of the senior authority nearest to the competent authority mentioned in Section 6.
7) Transfers for special reasons should be limited to 10 per cent of the total number of working posts.
(8) In making general transfers and transfers on special grounds, care shall be taken to obtain the recommendations of the Civil Services Board and to comply with all the provisions of the Transfer Act.
9) Departments in which the computer system (online) has been fully or partially developed to process the transfers should use this system. Also, the departments which have not developed this system should develop this system in future.
(10) After issuing orders for general transfers and transfers on special grounds, the transferred officers/ employees shall ensure that they join the place of transfer within the period of assumption of office as provided under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Term of Office, Non-Ministerial Service and Provisions for Suspension, Dismissal and Removal) Rules, 1981. If he does not join the post of a replacement during this period, the period of absence shall be deemed to be non-dies.
11) The action to be taken in accordance with the above-mentioned instructions should be completed by August 30, 2021.
