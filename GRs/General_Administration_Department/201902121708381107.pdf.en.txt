GOVERNMENT Circular: 1.1 The State Government has issued instructions to all public authorities from time to time regarding effective implementation of Section 4 of the Right to Information Act, 2005.
1.2 The Government Circular, dated 9.5.2014 has instructed all public authorities to publish information on 17 items under the provisions of Section 4 on the website, direct the Assistant Public Information Officer, Public Information Officer and the Appellate Authority, update the information on the website.
1.3. In pursuance of the order issued by the State Information Commissioner, vide Government Circular, dated 28.1.2016, all the Departments of the Government and the field offices under them have been instructed to update the information on the website at least twice a year on 1st January and 1st July every year to make the information available to the applicants under the Right to Information Act, 2005.
1.4 As per the Government Circular dated 13.4.2018, the following matters have been brought to the notice of all public authorities.
Voluntary disclosure of information by public authorities on a large number of matters for which information is sought under the Right to Information Act will reduce the number of RTI applications. The First Appellate Authority should review the compliance of its order if it seeks to satisfy the applicant so as to help reduce the number of second appeals to the Commission.
However, the Government is still receiving repeated complaints at various levels regarding non-adherence to the provisions of Section 4 of the Right to Information Act, 2005 by many public authorities and non-updation of information on the website.

3. Ma. State Information Commissioner, Brihanmumbai In pursuance of the orders dated 25.1.2018 and 18.1.2019, respectively, 1 and 2, all public authorities shall take the following actions:
In order to reduce the number of RTI applications, all public authorities are required to make suo motu disclosures under Section 4 of the Right to Information Act, 2005.
As per the provision of section 4 (1) (a) of the Right to Information Act, 2005, all records shall be properly indexed and indexed and computerized. All such records should be computerized within a reasonable period and as per the availability of resources and linked through a network to various systems across the country to facilitate access to such records.
Public authorities shall publish information on 17 items mentioned in Section 4 (1) (b) of the Right to Information Act, 2005 and update the publications every year.
The information on the website should be updated at least twice a year on January 1 and July 1.
The above matters should be brought to the notice of the Heads of Departments under them, the public authorities and all concerned by the Heads of Departments of all Ministries and Departments and they should be advised to take necessary action.
