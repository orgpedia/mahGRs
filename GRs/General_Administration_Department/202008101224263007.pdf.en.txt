Government circular
The 73rd anniversary of Indian Independence Day will be celebrated on Saturday, August 15, 2020. Considering the background of the corona virus infection, there will be limitations in holding this event like every year. In view of these facts and precautionary measures, the following directions are being issued for the conduct of Independence Day functions in the State this year: 1) The anniversary of Independence Day will be celebrated by hoisting the flag at all the divisional/ district/ sub-divisional/ taluka headquarters in the State.
Accordingly, Divisional Commissioners Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Nashik, Amravati, Konkan should make appropriate arrangements for holding the flag hoisting ceremony at the divisional and district headquarters of their respective divisions.
3) The main government program of the state. The Chief Minister will unfurl the National Flag at Mantralaya, Mumbai at 9.05 am. In Pune. The flag will be hoisted by the Governor.
4) The rest of the departmental and district headquarters. The Prime Minister will hoist the flag.
5) The main official ceremony of the flag hoisting should be held simultaneously across the state at 9.05 am on that day from 8.35 am to 9.35 am. No flag hoisting or any other official or semi-official function should be held during this period. If an office or institution wants to have its own flag hoisting ceremony, they should do it at 8.35 am. before 9.35 a.m. to be organized later.
6) The national anthem should be played while saluting the national flag. The band prepared at the time of opening should be played before and after the opening.
7) The theme of the speeches to be delivered on this occasion should be one that elaborates the significance of 15th August Independence Day and inspires the attendees to work for the unity and integrity of the country.
8) The flag. In case the Minister/ Minister of State is unable to reach the venue of the function on time due to unavoidable reasons, the Divisional Commissioner at the Divisional Headquarters and the District Collector at the District Headquarters shall ensure that the flag hoisting ceremony takes place smoothly. The respective district magistrates and deputy commissioners will hoist the national flag at the respective district headquarters.
9) Encourage local public representatives, freedom fighters, wives and parents of martyred soldiers, as well as corona warriors such as doctors, sanitation workers, health workers and some citizens who have overcome this disease.

10) Considering the background of the Corona virus, it will be the full responsibility of the organizers to ensure that the entire program of Independence Day is completed by following all the rules regarding social distancing.
The organizers should take action accordingly to invite the dignitaries. All participants must wear the national dress.
Masks are mandatory for everyone. Strictly follow all the rules of safe travel and broadcast live through the website so that more and more people can watch the ceremony at home, 12) The slogan of" Atmanirbhar Bharat" should be spread through this program and social media.
During the day, various programs such as tree plantation, debate competition at the inter-school/ inter-college level, quiz/ patriotic essay and poetry competition should be organized through online mode. Initiate important schemes, organize patriotic songs/ speeches of selected students/ students through social media, light up a government building, organize a webinar on a topic, conduct a patriotic online campaign through NSS and NYKS, promote patriotic or national integration songs/ messages through social media and digital media, citizens should take necessary action to celebrate Independence Day through innovative activities like waving the national flag from the balcony/ terrace of the house.
14) All public and government buildings in the state (even in villages) as well as forts of historical importance (eg. Raigad, Sinhagad, Shivneri, Purandar, Vasai, Pratapgarh, Daulatabad and Sitabardi should be made national flags.
15) The instructions given by the Government under Circular No. FLG- 1091/30 dated 20th March, 1991 and No. FLG-1091/ (2)/ 30 dated 5th December, 1991 and Circular No. FLG 1098/343/30 dated 11th March, 1998 regarding the proper manner of hoisting the National Flag should be strictly followed. It should also be ensured that the training of flag-hoisting is done in color. It should also be ensured that the national flag is in good condition and is lowered at sunset.
The District Collectors of Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Nashik and Amravati will not have to hold separate functions as the Divisional Commissioners will arrange the flag hoisting ceremony at their headquarters. Similarly, the Divisional Commissioner, Konkan Division and Collector, Mumbai City need not take action for the event in Mumbai. In Mumbai Suburban District, the District Collector will make all preparations for the flag hoisting. Divisional Commissioner, Konkan will make arrangements for the ceremony at Konkan Bhawan.
2. Notwithstanding the general directions as above, the concerned Divisional Commissioners and Collectors are being empowered to take necessary appropriate action with the concurrence of the Guardian Minister regarding the celebration of Independence Day in their respective jurisdictions considering the extent of spread of Corona Virus infection.
