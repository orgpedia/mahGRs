Preamble:- The rules regarding the use of helicopters/ aircraft in the Government fleet have been prescribed by the Government decision dated 13/2/2015. This decision of the government needed to be restructured as some aspects of this decision had become outdated. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
Governing Decision: The decision of the Governing Council dated 13/2/2015 is hereby superseded.
The rules regarding the use of aircraft/ helicopters in the government fleet are as follows.
Government aircraft/ helicopters will be used only for official purposes.
2) Ma. The Governor, Ma. The Prime Minister and The Deputy Chief Minister will have priority for the use of aircraft/ helicopters in the fleet of the State Government.
3) Ma. The Governor, Ma. The Prime Minister and The Deputy Chief Minister should be provided with a government aircraft/ helicopter by the Director Aviation, Directorate General of Civil Aviation, Mumbai, on demand. The coordination in this regard will be done by the General Administration Department (Aviation).
4) Ma. With the approval of the Chief Minister, State Guest or other distinguished person as well as Union Ministers, State Ministers, senior officials of the Central and State Governments will be provided with government aircraft/ helicopters for air travel to places where regular air service is not available.
5) As shown in the table below. The Governor, Ma. The Prime Minister and The staff accompanying the Deputy Chief Minister when he/ she uses the government aircraft/ helicopter shall be provided with a copy of his/ her report.
Those who have to go as part of duty, as well as engineers and technical staff involved in maintenance, can travel by government aircraft/ helicopter. However, such personnel will be allowed to travel by air/ helicopter only if space is available in the aircraft/ helicopter.

6) Ma. No non-government person will be allowed to travel in government aircraft/ helicopter without prior permission of the Chief Minister. Non-government persons will travel by government aircraft/ helicopter on their personal responsibility. However, the state government will not be responsible for any loss of life or property due to the unfortunate incident during the journey.
7) The Director of Aviation is responsible for the conduct of test flights and training and checks of pilots. The programme will be presided over by the Chief Minister.
