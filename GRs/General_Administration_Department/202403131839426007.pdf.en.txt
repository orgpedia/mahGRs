4. Now, the committee wants to examine in detail the old Nizam period records pertaining to Marathwada at Hyderabad (Telangana State). Accordingly, one more visit is to be made to Hyderabad (Telangana State) for transfer of old Nizam period records / documents related to Marathwada from Telangana State to Maharashtra State. In this regard, necessary correspondence is to be made with the State Government of Telangana and the relevant records / documents are to be transferred to the State of Maharashtra. Also, records of the Kunbi caste are found in the archives of the State Archives and all records of the State Archives are required to be examined and reviewed in order to find out the records of the Kunbi caste.
5. The committee is also to visit the Marathwada division to examine the records of more and help increase the number of Kunbi, Maratha-Kunbi, Kunbi-Maratha caste records.
Records found in the state so far are yet to be scanned, authenticated and published on the website and a detailed review of the process of scanning and publishing records of records on the website is required to complete this work at the earliest. The Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance has been asked to simplify the process of proving the genealogy in the context of Kunbi, Maratha-Kunbi, Kunbi-Maratha caste in view of the difficulties being faced in proving the genealogy to obtain the caste certificate. Under the government decision of 25.01.2024, a committee has been formed under the chairmanship of Tehsildar. Accordingly, the committee is required to review the work done on genealogy. Also, records of Kunbi, Maratha-Kunbi, Kunbi-Maratha caste records are being translated / transliterated into Marathi script from Modi, Udu, and Barsi scripts, and many more are yet to be translated / transcribed. The committee is expected to take approximately 2 months to complete the above-mentioned proceedings.
Considering all the above, the government has taken the following decision.
