In the second phase of the Corona disaster, most of the districts of the state have been affected by the Corona virus on a large scale and an unprecedented situation has arisen. The officers and employees of the Public Health Department, Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals Department, Revenue Department, Home Department and other related departments of the state government are taking effective measures to deal with the Corona disaster. Help is pouring in from all quarters. In the face of this disaster, various officers and employees associations/ federations of the state have dutifully submitted a memorandum to the government to deduct one or two days' salary for the relief work to help the corona victims in the state for treatment and corona preventive measures.
Circular- In the second phase of the Corona disaster, a serious situation has arisen due to the large-scale outbreak of the Corona virus in most of the districts of the state. The State Government/ Semi-Government Group-A and Group-B Gazetted Officers are requested to contribute two days of their salary each for the month of May, 2021 and Group B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D employees of the State to the Chief Minister's Relief Fund. It is also being appealed to the retired gazetted officers to donate two days' pension from their retirement salary to the Chief Minister's Relief Fund.

The following procedure should be adopted for deduction of one/ two days' salary (Mahi May 2021) from the salary of government officers/ employees and depositing the amount in the Chief Minister's Relief Fund and submitting its account:
(i) Payments for the month of May, 2021 should be made as per the instructions of the Government.
However, after regular deduction of salary and deduction of one/ two days salary, the balance amount of salary shall be paid to the concerned officer/ employee by cheque/ cash/ in the prescribed manner.
At present, the salary of the officers/ employees whose salary is credited to the account according to the details of their bank account made available to the government, should be informed before depositing the remaining amount of salary after regular deduction of salary to the concerned bank.
(ii) In case of deduction of wages for the said one/ two days, the same shall be calculated on the basis of the total amount of basic pay+ dearness allowance.
(iii) A separate register shall be maintained for recording the amount collected at the time of disbursement of salary as above (for the month of May, 2021).
(iv) The Head of the Department/ Head of the Office and the Collector/ CEO/ Chief Officer, Municipal Council/ Municipal Commissioner, Municipal Corporation should print a copy of the receipt generated on the website of the Chief Minister's Relief Fund www.cmrf.maharashtra.gov.in or deposit the same in the Chief Minister's Relief Fund Bank Account as per the details mentioned below and send the receipt of the same along with the list of donors of the amount collected to the Chief Minister's Relief Fund Cell, Chief Minister's Secretariat, 6th Floor, Main Building, Mantralaya, Mumbai-400 032 within 7 days from the date of receipt of payment.
