The central government has decided to admit girls to the National Defence Academy in Pune. Accordingly, at the state level, it is being considered to start a pre-service military education institute for girls on the lines of the military pre-service education institute for boys in Aurangabad.
The building of the Department of Higher and Technical Education in Nashik is ready with hostel facilities.
A Committee is being constituted to examine whether the building is suitable for the Girls Pre-Service Education Institute and how a Girls Pre-Service Education Institute can be started in the Girls Hostel of Ex-Servicemen at Nashik and to inspect the suitable Government site at Nashik for the permanent building of the Institute.
1) Divisional Commissioner, Nashik Division Chairman.
District Collector, Nashik Member.
Joint Secretary (Office-Secretary), Member of General Administration Department.
Ministry of Health, Mumbai
Director, Sainik Welfare Department, Pune Member.
5) Joint Director, Department of Higher and Technical Education, Nashik Member.
Director, Military Pre-Service Education Institute, Aurangabad Member Secretary.
The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
The committee will inspect the building of the Higher and Technical Education Department in Nashik and ensure that the necessary facilities for the Girls Pre-Service Education Institute, such as classrooms, hostels, offices, dining rooms, housekeeping, recreation rooms, library, computer room, auditorium, playgrounds, swimming pool, etc. are available or possible to be built at this place. To ensure that the facilities which are not available in this building and place can be availed in other nearby government, semi-government, private institutions or how.

2) To examine the matter whether the building of the Institute of Higher and Technical Education will be permanently transferred to the Institute of Military Pre-Service Education for Girls. Consult the Department of Higher and Technical Education.
3) The educational institutions available from the building to the 11th and 12th grades, it is possible to coordinate with other institutions for training or how to achieve the desired education and training students.
The committee will inspect the building of the Department of Higher and Technical Education and the hostel for the daughters of ex-servicemen as above. If it can not be arranged in this building, it will be examined whether it will be possible to provide military pre-service education for girls, at least temporarily. Also, the cost of maintenance, staffing, and maintenance should be considered.
5. In addition to examining the above, suitable place should be proposed for setting up a separate educational institution for girls by examining the availability of government land in the Nashik area.
The committee has to submit its report to the government within 15 days.
