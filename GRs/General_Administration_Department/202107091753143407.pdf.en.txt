Reference: 1. The Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Servants and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005. Notification dated 25.5.2006 2. Government Resolution dated 10th May, 2021 of the General Administration Department
Preamble: As per the provisions of the Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Employees and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005, general transfers of state government officers and employees are carried out in the month of April and May every year.
However, due to the outbreak of COVID-19 pandemic, in the current financial year 2021-22, Rs. As on 30th June, 2021, no transfers (including general transfers and those due to some exceptional circumstances or special reasons) shall be made and further orders may be issued after periodically reviewing the situation arising out of outbreak of COVID-19. The decision was taken by the 2. Accordingly, the Government was contemplating to issue instructions regarding transfers of State Government officers/ employees during the current financial year 2021-22.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In view of the fact that the outbreak of COVID-19 is not yet under control in the State and considering the possibility of a third wave of this epidemic, the following limited changes are being approved in the current financial year 2021-22 till 14th August, 2021.
(1) In view of the fact that Maharashtra is presently a Corona affected State and the possibility of a third wave, as well as in view of the adverse impact on the State's economy due to the COVID-19 pandemic and the consequent restrictions, in order to limit the expenditure on transfer allowance, general transfers within the limit of 15% of the total number of working posts shall be made with the approval of the competent authority as mentioned in Section 6 of the Act.
(2) While effecting all transfers within the limit of 15%, all eligible officers/ employees who have completed the prescribed period in the concerned post, including those who have completed more than the prescribed period in the concerned post, should be transferred on priority.
3) First of all, the general changes. To be completed by July 31, 2021.

4) Only after the completion of the process of general transfers, the posts which remain vacant will be transferred for special reasons. from 1st August 2021. It will be available until August 14, 2021. Therefore, the officers/ employees working in the post which is not vacant cannot be transferred to another post on special grounds.
(5) The transfers to be made on special grounds shall be made with the approval of the senior authority nearest to the competent authority referred to in section 6 of the Act.
6) Transfers for special reasons should be limited to 10 per cent of the total working posts.
(7) In making general transfers and transfers for special reasons, care shall be taken to obtain the recommendations of the Civil Services Board and to comply with all the provisions of the Transfer Act.
8) Departments in which the computer system (online) has been fully or partially developed to process the transfers should use this system.
(9) After issuing orders for general transfers and transfers on special grounds, the transferred officers/ employees shall ensure that they join the place of transfer within the period of assumption of office as provided under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Incumbency, Non-Ministerial Service and Provisions for Suspension, Dismissal and Removal) Rules, 1982. If he does not join the post of a replacement during this period, the period of absence shall be deemed to be non-dies.
10) The action to be taken in accordance with the above-mentioned instructions shall be ensured to be completed by August 14, 2021.
