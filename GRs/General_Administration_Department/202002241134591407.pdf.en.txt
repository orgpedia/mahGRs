The Proposal:
As per the Government circular dated 13th June, 1985, a five-day week was implemented in Government offices in the State. Further, clarificatory instructions have been issued vide circular dated 12th February, 1987 as to the offices to which these orders are applicable.
In pursuance of the Government Order dated 31st August, 1988, vide Circular dated 13th June, 1985, all the administrative offices of the Government shall, with effect from 1st September, 1988, remain closed on the second and fourth Saturdays of every month and shall remain open on all other Saturdays for full time.
However, since the five-day week has already been implemented for Central Government offices, the Government was considering to implement the five-day week for Government offices in the State, considering the demand from various officers/ employees associations in this regard. A decision in this regard was taken in the Cabinet meeting held on 12th February, 2020 and the following Government Decision is being issued.

Government's decision:
After due consideration, the Government, in accordance with the decision taken by the Government, by superseding the Government Decision dated 31st August, 1988, hereby order as follows:
(1) With effect from 29th February, 2020, a five-day week is being implemented for Government offices and all Saturdays and Sundays shall be holidays.
The working hours of all government offices are being increased by 45 minutes and will be from 9.45 am to 6.15 pm from Monday to Friday.
(3) The working hours for constables in all government offices will be from 9.30 am to 6.30 pm from Monday to Friday.
(4) The above office hours shall include a maximum of half-an-hour lunch break between 1.00 pm to 2.00 pm as per the Government Circular dated 4th June, 2019.
(5) The order regarding the 5-day week shall not apply to government offices to which the Factories Act is applicable or the Industrial Disputes Act is applicable or whose services are considered essential, and to government colleges, technical institutions, schools, police forces, fire brigade, sanitation workers, etc.
The list of such offices is annexed at Annexure' A' and is illustrative and the five-day week shall not apply to other offices falling within the categories indicated in this list.
The decision will come into effect from February 29, 2020.
