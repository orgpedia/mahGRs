Introduction: Directorate General of Information and Public Relations. Chief Minister, Shri. The Chief Minister also. Government programmes/ meetings of the Minister are filmed at various locations. For this, the existing video and still cameras of the Directorate General have become outdated. As a result, it is not possible to capture the meeting on camera. Similarly, most of the cameras used in the Divisional and District Information Offices under the Directorate General of Police are also in disrepair and their warranty has expired as they were purchased 7 to 8 years ago. Therefore, there are problems such as faulty filming, recording being stopped, etc. while shooting with such cameras. Modern cameras are a matter of daily use and very important for the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations. In such a situation, the government was considering administrative approval for the purchase of state-of-the-art video and still cameras and accompanying literature in the financial year 2023-24 within the limit of the funds received for the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations and the estimated amount of expenditure for this.
Government Decision: A total of 10 still cameras and their accessories for the Directorate General of Information and Public Relation and the Divisional and District Information Offices under the Directorate General, and a total of 10 video cameras and their accessories for the purchase and payment of approximately Rs. 2,15,72, 248/- (Rupees Two Crores Fifteen Lakhs Seventy Two Thousand Two Hundred and Forty Eight only) is being given administrative and financial sanction under this Government Resolution.
