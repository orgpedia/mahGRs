The recommendations for the Padma Awards (Padma Shri, Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan) to be announced on Republic Day, 26th January, 2021 are to be forwarded to the Central Government. The Padma Awards are one of the highest civilian awards of India. These awards are given for outstanding service in various fields. The rules and regulations in this regard are available on the website of Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India www. padmaawards. gov. in.
The Government of India has prescribed the following criteria for the award of Padma Awards:
1) Excellence Plus should be the criterion while recommending. Community service should be a part of this.
2) Padma Awards being the second highest civilian award in India, while recommending the awardees, it should be considered whether the recommended awardee has received any previous National Award/ State Award in his/ her field of work.
(3) While recommending names for the award, meritorious persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes, women, disabled and weaker sections of the society should also be considered.
4) Posthumous award should not be recommended. However, in exceptional cases of the highest quality, persons who have passed away after January, 2019 may be recommended. People who have died before should not be recommended for Padma awards.
Recommendations for Padma Bhushan can be made after 5 years of receiving the award. The recommendation for Padma Vibhushan can be made after 5 years of receiving the Padma Bhushan. However, this limit may be relaxed in exceptional cases.
Except for doctors and scientists in government service and public enterprises, other employees/ officers are not eligible for Padma Awards.
Proposals for suitable recommendations on the basis of the above criteria should be sent by the District Collector/ Divisional Commissioner/ Ministerial Administrative Department to this Department latest by 31st July, 2020. All the field offices should send their proposals through the concerned ministry department.
The following suggestions should be taken into consideration when making a proposal for an award.
a) Names should not be sought by advertising in newspapers or any other media while searching for eligible persons. However, systematic efforts should be made to find out the persons whose work is highly commendable and who are really deserving of this award, but who are not in the limelight.
