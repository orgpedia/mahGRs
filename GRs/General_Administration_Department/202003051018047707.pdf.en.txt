Maharashtra Legislative Assembly, Committee of Privileges for the year 2016-17, 2017-18
Report submitted to the House on March 22, 2018.
Circular:
The comprehensive guidelines regarding the treatment of Members of the Legislature/ Parliament with respect and courtesy, prompt action on their letters/ applications/ representations etc. have been issued vide Circular dated 27th July, 2015.
The Privileges Committee of the Legislative Assembly has submitted its report for the year 2016-17, 2017-18 to the House regarding the breach of privilege and contempt of the honorable members due to the absence of the concerned Tahsildar in the meeting held with the members of the Legislative Assembly of Baglan Taluka of the district to review the damage caused to public property and agriculture due to heavy rains in the month of July and August, 2016 in Nashik district. The report has expressed deep resentment over the lack of respect and courtesy given to women members of the legislature by government officials. Accordingly, in pursuance of the recommendation made in paragraph 6 of the said Report, the following suggestions are being incorporated in the guidelines issued vide Circular dated 27th July, 2015:

Government Circular No: WIMS 2018/ PR No.200/ Office No.18 (Rs. And why )
Suggestions for respect and courtesy to the members of the Legislature/ Parliament especially women members:
(1) The Ministries/ Departments are hereby informed that it shall be brought to the notice of the officers/ employees of all Government offices, semi-Government offices/ institutions, Government controlled and similar institutions under their control that the Members of the Legislature/ Parliament, especially the women members, should be treated with respect and courtesy while visiting the Government offices for work.
The government officers/ employees should listen to them carefully and help the public representatives in their work as much as possible as per the government rules.
(ii) The guidelines dated 27th July, 2015 shall be followed by all. Disciplinary action will be taken against the concerned officers/ employees in accordance with Section 10 (2) and (3) of the Regulation of Transfer of Government Employees and Prevention of Delay in Performing Government Duties Act, 2005 as well as the Maharashtra Civil Services Rules.
