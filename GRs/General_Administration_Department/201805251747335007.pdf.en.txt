2. The following instructions shall be strictly followed by all the concerned departments so that the transfer orders are implemented promptly and efficiently.
(1) The concerned department of the transferred officers shall relieve the officers of that department immediately without waiting for the appearance of the substitute officer. The period of superannuation of the transferred Under Secretaries will be upto 31st May, 2018 (M.No.).
(2) The transferred officers shall be reinstated without delay on their arrival in the department to join after being relieved from other departments as per the order of transfer. In case of non-recruitment of an officer, the establishment officer of the concerned department will be held responsible for sanctioning the compulsory waiting period.
(3) If any officer directly or indirectly pressurises any officer to modify the transfer order, such officer shall be liable to disciplinary action.

(4) Since the said transfers are required to be effected effectively, all the officers transferred shall be eligible for the benefit of the Government of India upto the date of their retirement and not later than the maximum period of two years. The salary from 01.06.2018 should not be withdrawn from the department before the transfer under any circumstances, after the concerned officer appears in the department of transfer, the post-transfer department should draw the salary and submit the report to the General Administration Department.
Instructions are being issued to all the administrative departments that if the drawing and disbursement officer of the department who was working before the transfer of salary after 01.06.2018 is paid, he will be held personally responsible. Also, the number of officers transferred. Salaries up to 31st May, 2018 are to be paid by the existing departments. The Pay and Accounts Officer, Mumbai is also being informed that the salary from June 1, 2018 is to be paid through the transferred department.
