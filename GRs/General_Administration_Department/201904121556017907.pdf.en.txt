The Proposal:
The State Election Commission is a constitutional body independent of the Government and as per Article 243K (3) of the Constitution, it is obligatory on the part of the Government to provide all manpower as determined by the State Election Commission.
243K. (3) The Governor of a State shall, when so requested by the State Election Commission, make available to the State Election Commission such staff as may be necessary for the discharge of the functions conferred on the State Election Commission by clause (1)
The State Election Commission had proposed to merge the existing cadre of equal pay scale, and computer operators and librarians were the lone posts, leaving them with no scope for promotion. For this, the inclusion of them in the cadre of scribe-typist, the development of 3 posts of sepoys that have been vacant for a long time was under consideration at the government level.
High-level committee of secretaries. The contours of the posts of the State Election Commission have been approved in the meeting held on March 17, 2018. The revised prospectus along with the prospectus has been issued vide letter No.Padhani-2018/ Q.No. 22/18/ VISU-1 dated 20th December, 2018.
Government's decision:
As per Reference 3, 79 posts in the office of the State Election Commission have been sanctioned as per the prospectus attached with the Annexure.
Out of the 31 cadres in the State Election Commission, 6 cadres of 22 cadres have been formed on the basis of equal pay scale and the remaining 9 cadres have been formed without any change in the total 15 cadres.
