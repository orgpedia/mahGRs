In order to take immediate action regarding the filling of vacancies in the Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals Department and Public Health Department, a separate cell will be created in the office of the Maharashtra Public Service Commission and 4 new posts will be sanctioned for a period of 3 years.

Read:- Letter No. 0121/ PR No. 51/2023/ I of Maharashtra Public Service Commission Proposal for June 7, 2023: The High Court, Aurangabad Bench has dismissed the Public Interest Litigation No. 47/2021. In the hearing dated 20.04.2023, the court directed to fill the posts of teachers in government medical colleges and affiliated hospitals immediately. Accordingly, the Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals has requested to create a separate cell in the Maharashtra Public Service Commission in connection with the recruitment of direct service quota posts of academic and non-academic cadre doctors on the establishments of the Government Medical, Dental Ayurveda and Nursing Colleges affiliated to the colleges under them and the Maharashtra Institute of Mental Health. Vacancies in the public health department also need to be filled.
Accordingly, the Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) has submitted a proposal for an increased post for the separate cell considering the existing sanctioned posts and scope of work of the Commission. Accordingly, the High-Level Committee of Secretaries has approved the creation of a separate cell in the Maharashtra Public Service Commission for 3 years by creating 4 posts of Under Secretary/ Cell Officer 1 post, Assistant Cell Officer 2 posts and Clerk-Typist 1 post. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
Creation of a separate cell in the office of Maharashtra Public Service Commission for immediate action regarding filling up of vacancies in Medical Education Department and Public Health Department.
