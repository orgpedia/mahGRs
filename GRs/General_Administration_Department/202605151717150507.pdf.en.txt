Subject: - Right to Information Online (Version 2.0) regarding inclusion of all zonal offices in the state on this website.
Sir,
In the state of Maharashtra, the website "Right to Information Online" has been made operational for citizens to access information under the Right to Information Act, 2005.
At present, the website includes a total of 215 offices, including ministerial departments and district-level head offices under their jurisdiction, and a list of these is available on the portal.
Earlier, due to the limited server capacity of the portal, there were technical limitations in the inclusion of the remaining field offices in the state on the portal. However, the portal has now been updated to operationalize the revamped portal "Right to Information Online - Version 2.0."
Also, a Project Implementation Cell has been set up for the management and coordination of the portal. Therefore, it is planned to include Survey Regional Offices / Corporations / Subordinate Offices in the State on the portal in a phased manner.
An officer of the concerned public authority is registered as the "Nodal Officer" by the National Informatics Centre (510) while incorporating any public authority on the online Right to Information portal. The Public Information Officer under their jurisdiction is then added to the portal by the nodal officer concerned. Accordingly, the Nodal Officer concerned should ensure that all the Public Information Officers of the concerned public authority are included on the portal. Also, it should be ensured that the changes required due to posting / transfers / retirement etc. of PIOs are updated on the portal from time to time.

If a request is received from the departments, action will be taken to appoint a separate nodal officer for each subdivision.
In cases where there are separate offices at the village level in the state and for which a limited number of Public Information Officers have been appointed, the senior office concerned shall be designated as the "Nodal Office" and one officer from that office shall be designated as the "Nodal Officer."
For example, the Village Revenue Officer (Talathi) will be designated as the Public Information Officer, while the Tehsil Office of the concerned Taluka will act as the nodal office. In such a situation, an officer from the tehsil office will be registered on the portal by the NIC as the nodal officer.
Thereafter, other Public Information Officers under their jurisdiction as well as village level Talathis will be included on the portal by the respective Nodal Officers. Similarly, the Village Development Officer (Gram Sevak) will be designated as the Public Information Officer, while the office of the Group Development Officer in the concerned Panchayat Samiti office will act as the nodal office. In such a situation, the administrative departments will only be required to provide the information of the taluk level offices and the nodal officers of those offices to the General Administration Department. There is no need to provide information to the public information officer.
7. As aforesaid, if only one Public Information Officer is appointed in a Taluk level office, the concerned District level office shall act as the nodal office for such office. In such a situation, the administrative departments will only be required to provide the information of the district level offices and their nodal officers to the General Administration Department.
8. Since all the offices across the state will come on the online RTI portal, mapping of the area offices under the respective department is required so that the office to which the user wants to apply for the RTI can be found. For this, the department itself should present the structure information in the form of a tri-diagram.
9. In view of the above procedure B instructions, all the field offices under the jurisdiction of your administrative department should be submitted to the General Administration Department by filling in the prescribed form A * and B along with the information of your department as well as the concerned field offices for inclusion on the "Right to Information" portal. Also, a soft copy of this information should be sent to the email address rtipmu.gad@mah.gov.in. The field offices should submit the information through their administrative department only, without submitting it directly to the department.
