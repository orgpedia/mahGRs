The All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 have been made applicable to the officers of the Indian Administrative Service with effect from 2007-08. As per these rules, the IPS officers of the age of 40 years and above are required to undergo the medical examination prescribed under the above rules every year and the summary of the report of their medical examination is required to be attached with the annual performance evaluation report. In the year 2020-21, the Government has decided to conduct the following medical examinations of the IPS officers of the State who are 40 years of age and above as prescribed under the All India Services (Performance Evaluation Report) Rules, 2007:
Government's decision:
As per Rule 3 of the All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 and the accompanying Sample 4, the Indian Administrative Service officers of the State who are 40 years of age or above as on 31.3.2020, shall get their prescribed medical examination done in the current year from a private hospital identified revenue division-wise as mentioned in Form-A attached to this Government decision. During the examination, the following recommendations should be made:
State of Maharashtra for the year 2020-21. The I.P.S. Officers who are 40 years of age or above as on 31.03.2020 should get all the medical check-ups mentioned in Form-B done from the private hospitals/ medical institutions mentioned in Form-A for the Revenue Department in which they are working.
2. The BPS officers working in the Amravati Revenue Division should get the medical check-up done from the private hospitals designated for other revenue divisions near them.
3. Medical examination may also be conducted in Government Hospitals in the concerned district or Government/ Municipal Hospitals in the revenue division where the facility of conducting all the medical examinations prescribed in Form-B is available.
The I.P.S. officers shall, after conducting all the medical tests specified in Form B from the hospitals designated for the respective revenue divisions or from the Government or Municipal hospitals, first pay the amount required for the medical tests to the said hospital and get the reimbursement from their office. If a medical examination is to be conducted from hospitals having rates higher than the revenue department-wise minimum rates, the expenditure incurred in excess of the revenue department-wise minimum rates shall be borne by the concerned officer at his own cost. It should be noted that the government will not reimburse the excess expenditure over the fixed minimum rate.
The reimbursement of expenses on medical check-ups admissible once in every two years under the Government Decision, Public Health Department, No. MAG-1098/ PR No. 220/ Health-3, dated 31st August, 2006 shall not be paid to the officers who reimburse the expenses of annual medical check-ups under this Government Decision.
During the period up to 30.06.2021, from the date of this Government decision, the officers of the Indian Administrative Service shall, in consultation with the concerned private hospitals, fix the dates of medical examination and conduct the medical examination as prescribed in Form-B.
Private hospitals and government or municipal hospitals designated by the government for medical examination should send the consolidated report of the medical examination of their BPSC officers to the Cell Officer, General Administration Department (Office 9), 558, 5th Floor, Mantralaya (Extension), Mumbai-32. Also, after the medical examination, a copy of the said examination report should also be given to the concerned BPS officers.
All the medical check-ups mentioned in Form-B will be done at one place and on one day. But in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, they should be done on the next day. The date and time of examination should be mutually decided by the medical officer and the concerned hospital. Also, the medical officer should take the preparation/ precautions for the medical check-up in consultation with the hospital.
9. 1 day of medical check-up shall be treated as duty period. No leave/ transfer leave will be admissible if the examination is conducted on a public holiday.
Since the period of medical examination is deemed to be the period of duty, it shall be payable at the rate at which the travelling allowance/ daily allowance is paid to the officers of the Indian Administrative Service, when they are otherwise on tour. The rates of such Travel Allowance/ Daily Allowance shall be as per the orders issued by the Finance Department from time to time. This should be considered and provided to the concerned officers by the pay-paying office.
Before going for medical check-up, the officers should give prior intimation to their senior officer concerned that they are going to the headquarters or to a hospital outside the headquarters for medical check-up.
12. Expenditure on medical examination to be defrayed under the head of accounts applicable to the salary and allowances at the establishment of the officer concerned and to be met from the grant sanctioned for the year. However, there will be no financial constraint.
13. If I.P.S. officers want to conduct medical examinations in a hospital of a revenue division other than the revenue division in which they are working, they should do so in the desired revenue division.
