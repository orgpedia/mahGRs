Read:- 1) General Administration Department, Government Resolution No: MAVAJ-2018/ PR No. 235/ KA34, dated 20.12.2019 2) Letter No. Documentary 2020/ Folk Art/ Ka- 7/526 dated 31.12.2020
Introduction: Although traditional media like newspapers and electronic media are widely used to convey information about various schemes of the government to the public, it has been proved many times that folk art and street plays are effective means of communicating the public welfare schemes of the government to the public. Therefore, the list of institutions to use folk art and street theatre to inform the public about the various schemes of the government by using folk art in rural areas and street theatre in urban areas to reach the people with the experiences of the beneficiaries of government schemes and schemes has been approved under the government decision dated December 20, 2018.
2. Due to the impact of Covid infection, the process of preparing the list of institutions presenting folk art was delayed due to the control of office attendance, so we are considering to extend the list.
Government's decision:
The list of institutions allotted for the use of folk art and street theatre under the government decision dated December 20, 2018, is being extended till March 31, 2021.
2. Simplification of folk art and street theatre in the wake of COVID-19 Maintain social distancing in open grounds and during performances and take appropriate precautions as per the guidelines prescribed by the State Government.
3.The process of preparing a new list of the organization should be completed immediately by the Information and Public Relations Office.
4. Form and conditions of performance and remuneration of troupes of institutions performing folk art and street theatre. As per the Government decision dated 20.12.2019.
The list shall remain in force till March 31, 2021, from the date of issue of the Government Order.
