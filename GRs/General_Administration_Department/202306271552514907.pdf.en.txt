The Proposal:
A policy decision to start a two-year integrated probationary training programme for officers recruited to the government service through the Maharashtra Public Service Commission through the State Service Examination on the lines of the All India Service Officers was taken in the above mentioned Reference No. The government has taken a decision. Accordingly, the Combined Probationary Training Programme for Group A and Group B (Gazetted) Cadre Officers is being implemented by the Training Institutes at Yashoda, Pune and Vanamati, Nagpur respectively. Paragraph No. 9 of the said Government Decision provided as follows:" The candidate selected for interview by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission shall be medically examined before the interview at the Government Medical College situated at the place of the concerned interview centre. Instructions will be given to the Deans of the concerned medical colleges through the Department of Medical Education and Medicines to provide the necessary facilities and systems in time.
As per the above provision, after the declaration of the result of the State Service Main Examination, the candidates who were recommended for interview were subjected to medical examination at the interview stage. However, in pursuance of the decision of the Government at Reference No. 3, it has been decided to modify the said procedure and conduct medical examination of the candidates recommended by the final result of the said examination instead of the candidates selected for interview through State Service Examination.
If a candidate is found ineligible for the post recommended in the medical examination at the time of appointment after the recommendation due to the above mentioned procedure of medical examination, the candidate will not be selected for any other post and the candidate will be left out of the selection process despite obtaining qualifying marks for other posts. Accordingly, all the candidates selected for interview through the State Service Examination have been medically examined and obtained

A change in the prescribed procedure for medical examination of candidates was under the consideration of the government to enable the final recommendation to be made on the basis of the medical report and the priority of the posts given by the candidates. The government has taken the following decisions:
Decision of the Government: 1. Medical examination of the candidates who have qualified for the interview after the preliminary examination and main examination of the State Service Examination of the Maharashtra Public Service Commission will be conducted by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission at the interview stage.
All the candidates who have qualified the interview shall be called for medical examination by establishing adequate Divisional Medical Boards (DMBs) at the places specified by the Commission for medical examination.
The medical examination letter along with the necessary instructions from the Commission will be provided to the candidates online.
The number of candidates to be sent daily for examination before the Medical Board shall be determined by the Commission at that time in consultation with the Medical Board taking into account the number of candidates who will be eligible for interview.
The Commission will provide an external link to the Divisional Medical Board on the Commission's website for uploading the medical reports of the examined candidates in the prescribed manner and recording the necessary entries for the result process.
The medical examination report of the concerned candidate submitted by the Medical Board will be made available in the candidate's account on the Commission's online application system for the information of the candidate.
If the candidate wants to appeal on the medical examination report, a period of 7 days will be provided by the Commission. Also, the candidate will be given the facility to submit the application form online in the account of the candidate.
All the appealed candidates will be called for medical examination only by forming an Appellate Medical Board (AMB) in the Government Medical College, Mumbai.
A medical board will be set up by the government.
(8) The Commission shall allow the candidate to submit the order of preference for the cadre for which he/ she is medically as well as physically and educationally qualified, after considering the medical examination report submitted by the Divisional Medical Board and the Appellate Medical Board.
On the basis of the said medical examination, action will be taken by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission in order to prepare a list of recommended candidates after considering the eligibility/ ineligibility of the candidates for the respective cadre.

If the period of time from the medical examination of the candidates before the interview by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission to the appointment of the candidate to the recommended post is more than one year, then the recommended candidates will be required to undergo a medical examination before and after the appointment.
This Government Decision is being issued in accordance with the consensus reached under the informal references dated 15th February, 2023 and 30th May, 2023 respectively of the Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals and the Department of Public Health. The decision will come into effect from the State Services (Main) Examination, 2022.
