Preamble: The rules framed for the sanction of donations/ financial assistance to institutions from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund and the rules regarding the administration of the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund accordingly. 27/8/1969, dt. 8/10/1970, d. Government Decision of 4/3/2017 and Government Preamble. The norms and provisions have been laid down under 27/9/2017.
The Government has decided to sanction grant/ financial assistance to the institutions from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund. In 4.3.2017, the matter was under consideration of the Government. Accordingly, the government has decided on the rules regarding the administration of the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund. 27/8/1969, dt. 8/10/1970, d. Government Decision of 4/3/2017 and the Government Preamble. 27/9/2017, it is proposed to issue the following Government Decision regarding the rules, criteria and provisions regarding the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund.
The Government Decision regarding the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund is being amended by this Government Decision to allocate financial assistance from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund by amending the Government Decision and Preamble from Reference No. 1 to 4 as follows:
1. This fund may be called the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund.
2. Applications seeking financial assistance from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund received at the Chief Minister's Office. Written orders/ recommendations of the Chief Minister have been received, such applications will be forwarded to the department for further appropriate action. The applications will be submitted to the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund Committee after seeking detailed proposals from the concerned institutions as per the government decision.
3. (a) Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund Committee: Ma. The Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund Committee has been constituted under the chairmanship of the Chief Secretary. The complete proposals received from the above as well as from the department will be presented before this committee for sanctioning donations/ financial assistance from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund. The Committee, with the recommendation of a fully qualified proposal in accordance with the criteria set out in the Government Decision. It will be submitted to the Chief Minister for approval.

Deputy Secretary/ Joint Secretary, General Administration Department (Office- 25) Member Secretary (b) Management of Funds: 1. As per the Chief Minister's discretion, the amount from this fund can be used to provide financial assistance to social, educational, cultural, charitable or similar institutions or semi-public institutions or organizations in the state of Maharashtra.
(2) the overall control, management and administration of the Fund; The Chief Minister will remain present.
3) The Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Additional Chief Secretary and Chief Administrative Officer of the General Administration Department of the Government will be the" Controlling Officer" of the Fund for the purpose of administration of the Fund. All the action to be taken in respect of the Fund shall be deemed to have been taken by the Chief Minister as the sole administrator of the Fund and shall be taken either by the Chief Minister or by the Controlling Officer after obtaining the approval of the Chief Minister.
The financial transactions of the Fund are in the name of the Controlling Authority through the Personal Accounts Department of the Reserve Bank of India. The Controlling Officer shall have the right to transact financial transactions in relation to this account.
5) The transactions in the fund will be recorded in the return forms' A' and' B' attached with the decision of the government. Also, these records will be authenticated by the" Controlling Officer" from time to time.
(c) Criteria/ Conditions/ Conditions of Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund: For seeking financial assistance from the" Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund," the charitable institutions will be required to fulfill the following criteria/ conditions/ conditions. The institution is required to submit further documents/ certificate (as proof) for financial assistance.
1) The applicant charitable institution must be registered under the" Societies Registration Act 1860" or" Maharashtra Public Trusts Arrangement Act, 1950." A certificate of registration must be submitted with the request for financing.
The applicant charitable institution should have completed five years of registration under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 or the Maharashtra Public Trusts Arrangement Act, 1950 and should be functioning as it is.
3) The selection of the members of the management committee of the charity should be done in a democratic manner. In this regard, there should be specific and appropriate provisions in the constitution and/ or establishment of the institution/ establishment rules/ regulations/ by-laws of the institution.
4) The proposal should be accompanied by an accurate list of the names, addresses and telephone/ mobile numbers of the working office bearers of the organization.
(5) If the activities sought for by the institution are receiving grants from the Government, the activities of such institution shall not be allowed to be funded from the" Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund."

6) 50% of the total cost of the activity/ target demanded as per the complete proposal of the charitable institutions or after considering the recommendation of the Charity Fund Committee. The financial assistance will be sanctioned as per the decision of the Chief Minister.
7) Copies of the audited accounts of the institution for the last 5 years, along with a certificate from the Chartered Accountant certifying the accuracy of the accounts of the institution, must be submitted by the institution.
8) Copies of Income Tax Returns for the last five years of the organization are required to be submitted.
9) The institution must submit a certificate from the concerned office that the audit report and return of the institution for the last five years have been submitted regularly to the concerned Public Trust Register Office/ Charity Commissioner's Office.
10) No complaint/ claim/ appeal is pending against the registered institution under the" Societies Registration Act, 1860" or" Maharashtra Public Trusts Arrangement Act, 1950" in respect of which the institution is required to submit a certificate from the concerned competent authorities in the concerned Public Trusts Registration Office/ Charity Commissioner's Office.
11) The institution should submit a certificate that the institution has not received any financial assistance from the" Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund" in the last five years.
If any financial assistance has been received in the past from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund or any other trust system, the utilization certificate certified by a Chartered Accountant should be submitted along with the proposal. In case of seeking financial assistance for construction of a new building or for extension or renovation of a building, documents showing ownership of the land on which the proposed building/ project is to be constructed, Blue Print of the project certified by a registered architect/ architectural engineer, Project Cost Budget, permission to use the land for non-agricultural purposes by the competent authority, certificate of commencement of construction by the planning authority permitting construction etc. are required to be submitted along with the proposal. A copy of the updated annual inspection report and school set approval must be submitted with the proposal. Priority will be given in sanctioning financial assistance to the proposed project to be implemented by the institute with the objective of enhancing the quality of education and continuous holistic development of the students.
15) The institution should submit a certificate specifying the period of income tax exemption and the period of exemption, if any, under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. (Bank statement attached)

17) If the institution proposes to provide facilities to orphanages/ old age homes, etc., the inspection report given by the competent authority regarding the quality and performance of the institution concerned shall be attached with the proposal.
18) The institution must submit an updated certified copy of the record of the immovable and movable properties of the institution in the register (Annexure-I) maintained in the office of the Charity Commissioner.
19) The institution is required to submit a budget/ quotation etc. as to the total expenditure for the activity for which funding is sought from the institution. How much will the organization spend on this project? How much financial assistance is expected from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund? The institution is required to submit the detailed details of how the institution is going to provide for the remaining expenditure.
20) If the institution to be funded is a hospital, then the hospital will have to provide free medical facilities to poor patients as per Section 41A of the" Maharashtra Public Trust Arrangement Act, 1950." The institution must submit a certificate of such competent authority.
The institutions which are given financial assistance are required to submit a detailed statement of expenditure, completion report along with utilization certificate certified by a Chartered Accountant to the Government within one year.
22) For seeking donations/ assistance from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund, it is necessary to submit information along with complete documents as per the provisions of the government decision as well as the attached statement" C" and" D."
23) The amount to be sanctioned from the Chief Minister's Charitable Endowment Fund to the institution for the purpose for which it will be sanctioned. It will be used for the same purpose. In case of misappropriation of financial assistance or violation of any condition, the amount of financial assistance shall be recovered from the office bearers of the institution or institution along with penal interest at the rate of 12 per cent as arrears of land revenue and any expenditure incurred for recovery of financial assistance.
The financial assistance given to the institution should be utilized for the proposed project/ work within one year, in case of default in utilization of the amount disbursed, a notice of two months shall be given to the institution concerned. In case of non-submission of Utilization Certificate by the institution despite two months notice, the amount of donation from such institutions shall be recovered as arrears of land revenue by the concerned Collector with penal interest of 12% and all the provisions of the above sub-clause (twenty three) shall apply to such cases with appropriate modifications.
