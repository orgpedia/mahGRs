The proposal: The Supreme Court Decision of 6 July 2017. High Court, Nagpur. Instructions were given to implement the order of 28.9.2018 in the state. Accordingly, the officers/ employees of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations and subordinate offices who are appointed from the Scheduled Tribes category but have not been able to submit the caste validity certificate have been issued a Government Resolution No. 210/34-A, No. 4.2.2020 and Government Decision No. Mavz-2020/ P.S. 31/34-A, Dt. 25.8.2020 and Government Resolution, No. Mavaj- 2021/62/ P. No.
7/34-A, D. 3.3.2021, the appointments to the above posts were made for a period of 11 months or till the date on which they would have retired, whichever is earlier.
Appointments to the higher posts in the intervening period in this regard shall be governed by the Government Decision No. 308/16-B, D.C. 2018/ 2018. All the Administrative Departments have been directed to issue appropriate orders to all the field offices/ establishments under them regarding the extension of time as on 28.10.2021 and to implement the same immediately. Accordingly, the matter of extension was under consideration of the Government as the extension of the number of posts in the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations and subordinate offices was expiring on 04.12.2021.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As the 11 months of the post of the majority of the officers/ employees of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations, who were transferred to the superannuation due to non-submission of caste certificate of the Scheduled Tribes as per the provisions of the Government Decisions dated December 21, 2019 and 28.10.2021 of the General Administration Department, is expiring on December 4, 2021, the following officers/ employees are being temporarily extended for 11 months under this Government Decision or until the recommendations of the group in this regard are received by the Government and the Government takes a final decision, whichever is earlier.
