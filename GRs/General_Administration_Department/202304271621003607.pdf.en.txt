Various development programmes/ schemes are implemented by various administrative departments of the Government of Maharashtra and their subordinate field offices. Evaluation exercise evaluating the implementation of these development schemes/ programmes as well as the office functioning, decision making process, brings out the achievements and shortcomings in the implementation of the schemes and the functioning/ decision making process. To improve the decision-making process and to make the schemes/ programmes reach the common man in a better and effective manner by removing these shortcomings.
The government was considering conducting an evaluation study through the Quality Council of India (QCI), a tertiary agency, to suggest measures and disburse funds for the same.
As mentioned in the Government Resolution Preamble, the Quality Council of the State Government by compiling information through questionnaires regarding the decision-making process in the implementation of various development programs/ schemes by various ministerial administrative departments and their subordinate field offices.
The Government of India (QCI) has sanctioned Rs. 18,90,000/-+ GST+ Travel Expenses, etc. are being approved by the Government. The distribution of funds in the proposed case will be carried out as follows.

The Cell Officer (Office), General Administration Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai-32 has been declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the purpose of drawing and disbursing the said funds from the Treasury.
4. Nodal officers to be appointed at the Ministerial level and district level by all the Ministries/ Departments to provide all necessary support to the Quality Council of India (QCI) and to perform the role of coordinator.
5. Guidelines for conducting assessment studies are given below.
(a) The finalisation of questionnaire by the Quality Council of India (QCI) should be done by conducting an evaluation study in accordance with the provisions regarding the office procedures and decision-making processes in the various ministerial administrative departments of the State Government and their subordinate field offices, taking into account the purpose, requirements and objectives of the evaluation report.
This evaluation exercise should be completed without adversely affecting the day-to-day office functioning of the Ministry Departments and their subordinate offices.
(c) The Quality Council of India (QCI) shall be mandated to implement a time bound programme of evaluation studies within the prescribed time frame.
(d) All the work of the evaluation exercise by the tertiary institution shall be done by the Secretary/ Principal/ Assistant Professor (P.P. S. R.& K. will be monitored and controlled.
(e) It shall be the responsibility of the concerned department to provide the information required for carrying out the assessment study to the Quality Council of India (QCI), a tertiary institution.
The Quality Council of India (QCI) will be the third agency responsible for submitting the evaluation report to the government based on objective evidence.
After completion of the assessment study, a self-explanatory report should be submitted to the government by the Quality Council of India (QCI) along with the measures and recommendations to be taken.
