Corona virus infection is on the rise across the country and many districts in the state are under lockdown. The officers and employees of the Public Health Department, Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals Department, Revenue Department, Home Department and other related departments of the state government are taking effective measures to deal with the Corona disaster.
Help is pouring in from all quarters. A lot of financial support will be needed to deal with this crisis. The Government has received representations from various officers and staff associations of the State to dutifully deduct one/ two days salary from the salary of May, 2020 mutually to support the relief work in the event of disaster.
Circular-
In view of the serious situation due to the large-scale outbreak of Corona virus in most of the districts of the state, all the officers and employees of the state have been asked to contribute to the relief and rehabilitation work to deal with this disaster. P. S., Bh. The officers/ employees of Group-A and Group-B (Gazetted) of the State Government should not have any objection in contributing two days salary each of their salary for the month of May, 2020 and Group-B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D employees of the State Government should contribute one day salary each to the Chief Minister's Relief Fund.
(2) The heads of departments/ offices of all the Ministries/ Departments of the State Government and all the Government/ Semi-Government offices under them, Zilla Parishads, Panchayat Samitis, Municipal Corporations, Municipalities, Municipal Councils, Public Undertakings, Corporations, Boards and all the autonomous bodies should bring this circular to the notice of all the officers/ employees of their department/ office and explain to them the seriousness of the issue. Also, if they have any objection to the deduction of one/ two days' salary, they should inform the head of your department/ office in writing.

The following procedure should be adopted for deduction of one/ two days' salary (Mahi May 2020) from the salary of government officers/ employees and depositing the amount in the Chief Minister's Relief Fund and submitting its account:
(i) Payments for the month of May, 2020 should be made as per the instructions of the Government.
However, after regular deduction of salary and deduction of one/ two days salary, the balance amount of salary shall be paid to the concerned officer/ employee by cheque/ cash/ in the prescribed manner.
At present, the salary of the officers/ employees whose salary is being credited to the account as per the details of their bank account provided by them to the government, should be informed before depositing the remaining amount of salary after regular deduction of salary to the concerned bank.
(ii) In case of deduction of wages for the said one/ two days, the same shall be calculated on the basis of the total amount of basic pay+ dearness allowance.
(3) A separate register shall be maintained for recording the amount collected at the time of disbursement of salary as above (for the month of May, 2020).
(iv) The Head of the Department/ Head of the Office and the Collector/ CEO/ Chief Officer, Municipal Council/ Municipal Commissioner, Municipal Corporation should print a copy of the receipt generated on the website of the Chief Minister's Relief Fund www.cmrf.maharashtra.gov.in or deposit the same in the Chief Minister's Relief Fund Bank Account as per the details mentioned below and send the receipt along with the list of donors of the amount collected to the two designated Mutual Chief Minister's Relief Fund Cells, Chief Minister's Secretariat, 6th Floor, Main Building, Mantralaya, Mumbai- 400 032 within 7 days from the date of receipt of payment.
