The Proposal: -
As per Section 15 (3) of the Right to Information Act, 2005, the State Chief Information Commissioner and the State Information Commissioner are appointed on the recommendation of a selection committee headed by the Hon'ble Chief Minister. Also, Section 15 (5) requires that the person in question for such appointment be of eminence in public life and have extensive knowledge and experience in law, administration, journalism, social service, science and technology, management or similar fields.
The Hon'ble Supreme Court in Anjali Bhardwaj v. Government of India & Ors (Writ Petition-Civil No. 436/2018, the command. 15.02.2019) as well as subsequent orders directing that the eligibility criteria, the method of scrutiny and the framework of the selection process must be made public to ensure transparency, objectivity and quality-based selection in the process of appointment of Information Commissioners.
3. For the effective implementation of the said court directions, the Government may, by order of No. The Government hereby constituted a Search and Scrutiny Committee under the Chairmanship of a retired Judge of the High Court with the Chief Secretary and the Additional Chief Secretary, General Administration Department as its members vide its Judgment dated 7th January, 2025.
The Committee has been empowered to determine the eligibility criteria, terms and conditions of advertisement to be published for the vacancies, to ensure a selection process consistent with the Act and the court directions, to lay down criteria for objective screening of applicants and to make necessary recommendations.
The aforesaid Committee was held on 24th April, 2026 to fix the terms and conditions for the advertisement to be published for the post of State Chief Information Commissioner and State Information Commissioner. In the meetings of the said Committee, the terms and conditions to be incorporated in the advertisement for the selection of the State Chief Information Commissioner and State Information Commissioner have been fixed and the Committee has decided to publish the same by Government Decision.

The Government's Decisions:
Reference No. 2 of the Search Committee constituted vide Government Decision dated 7th January, 2025. As per the decision taken by the Committee in its meetings held on 24.04.2026, the following terms and conditions are being prescribed for the selection of State Chief Information Commissioner B State Information Commissioner: -
Basic Qualification: - The applicant should be of public SSAA standing under Section 15 (5) of the Right to Information Act, 2005. It will require knowledge in one or more of the areas of law, administration, journalism, social services, science and technology, and management.
Minimum Experience: The applicant should have at least 20 years of experience for the posts of State Chief Information Commissioner and at least 15 years for the posts of State Information Commissioner in one or more of the above mentioned fields. Experience in senior positions in government, quasi-government, autonomous bodies, public sector, reputed private sector and social / charitable organisations will be preferred.
3.वबय Limit: - Minimum age should be 45 years and maximum age should be 63 years as on the last date of submission of application.
Education: - The applicant should be at least a graduate in any discipline.
BMA: - The applicant should have knowledge of the Right to Information Act and related laws. Must have knowledge of quasi-judicial procedure, commitment to the values of transparency, accountability and good governance, administrative leadership and decision-making ability.
Conditions relating to submission of application: - The application will be required to be submitted in the prescribed format, it will be mandatory to submit detailed resume, experience statement, proof of work.
Application Fee: Rs. A fee of Rs.1000 / - will be payable. It will be mandatory to attach the receipt to the application by paying the fee in any of the modes of payment of information charges under the Right to Information Act.
8.शपथपत R: - The applicant will be required to submit an affidavit along with the application stating that no offence has been registered against him, no criminal case is warranted, and no disciplinary proceedings are pending or proposed if he is in the service of a government or a semi-government.
