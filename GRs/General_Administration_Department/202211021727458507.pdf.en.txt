Introduction: The Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015 has come into force in the state. As per the guidelines of Ease of Doing Business (BRAP), 15 days have been given to resolve the grievances of the applicants. In order to ensure effective implementation of the Maharashtra Right to Service Act, 2015 and to resolve the grievances of the citizens in a time-bound manner, the Maharashtra State Right to Service Commission has recommended to the government that the administrative departments should resolve all types of grievances of applicants, excluding appeal cases, under the Maharashtra Right to Service Act, 2015 within a time frame of 15 days.
Circular: In addition to the above recommendation made by the Maharashtra State Right to Service Commission, in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015, hereinafter referred to as the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015, except for the applications received in respect of the provision of the service notified under the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015 and the appeal applications received in accordance with the provisions of the said Act.
