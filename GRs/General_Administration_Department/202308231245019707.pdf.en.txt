DV vehicles have been kept under control. Just as there is a committee in the Public Health Department to check the payment of medical expenses when proposals for the repair of vehicles are submitted to the government through the collector offices, there must be a mechanism at the ministerial level to ensure that the payments are reasonable.
The matter of forming a committee to examine the payments regarding the repair of DV vehicles beyond the prescribed limit regarding the repair of vehicles was under the consideration of the government, according to which the government decision is being issued as follows.
Government's decision:
DV vehicles have been placed under all the District Collector's Offices for the transportation of state guests/ dignitaries coming to the state. Proposal for Revision of Vehicles As per the provisions of Part-I, Sub-Division-2, No.19-B of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, a sum of Rs. 50,000/- and Rs. Payments of more than Rs. 60,000/- are received by the Government for approval. However, just as there is a committee in the Public Health Department to examine the payment of medical expenses, there must be a mechanism at the ministerial level to ensure that the payments are reasonable.
D.V. Vehicle Repair Charges Rs. 50,000/- and Rs. If the amount exceeds Rs. 60,000/-, the concerned payments shall be submitted by the Collector's Office to the Scrutiny Committee, with the approval of the Scrutiny Committee through the Collector's Office and only after the Scrutiny Committee certifies that the expenditure is fair and reasonable, the payments shall be sent to the Government level for approval.

V. Repair charges of vehicles Rs. 50,000/- and Rs. The Committee shall examine the payments made and certify whether the expenditure is just and reasonable before remitting the payments exceeding Rs.60,000/-to the Government for approval.
Action to be taken by the District Collector:
(1) The Collector's office shall submit the payments to the committee by the 5th of the following month.
(2) The Committee shall, within one month from the receipt of the payment, examine and certify whether the expenditure is reasonable and reasonable.
