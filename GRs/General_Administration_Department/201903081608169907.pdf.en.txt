Introduction: The State Government has set up 206 Martyrs Memorials in the State in memory of those who sacrificed their lives for the country by participating in the Indian Freedom Struggle, Hyderabad Liberation Struggle and Goa Liberation Movement. Since 34-35 years have passed since the construction of these monuments, they need to be repaired/ renovated. The Minister for Finance, Planning and Forests, in his budget speech for 2015-16, allocated Rs. An amount of Rs. 25 lakh had been provided for. Accordingly, funds for repair/ renovation of the Martyr Memorial were handed over to all the concerned District Collectors in the financial year 2016-17 and 2017-18. The concerned district collector has demanded that the work of some martyr memorials is still pending. During the year 2018-19, Rs. 12,00,00, 000/- (Rs. Twelve crore) has been sanctioned and accordingly, the proposal to hand over the remaining grant for repair and renovation of the Martyr's Memorial to the concerned District Collector was under consideration of the Government. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
Government Decision:- Considering the matter mentioned in the Preamble, a fund of Rs. 12,00,00, 000/- (Rs. 12 crore) available for the year 2018-19 for the repair/ renovation of the Martyr Memorials in the State has been sanctioned in the Winter Session of 2018-19 for the incomplete Martyr Memorials out of 206 Martyr Memorials in the State.
2. As per the attached prospectus, the Collector Yavatmal has issued a notification to the effect.
The funds should be used only for the martyr memorials at Babhulgaon and for the 10 works mentioned in the statement. The District Collectors should ensure that they do not spend more than the grant allocated to them and that the amount allocated should be spent only for the 10 items approved by the government.

3) At present, Rs. 27,92,200/- (Rs. Twenty-seven lakh ninety-two thousand two hundred only) is being distributed to the Collector Yavatmal as per the attached statement.
4. The Collector Yavatmal should immediately complete the administrative approval, tender process and other necessary administrative matters for the works demanded by the Collector Office and indicated in the accompanying statement. Also, the Collector should ensure that the repair works are carried out as per the prevailing standards and tariff sheets of the Public Works Department. Collector Yavatmal should inspect the present condition of the memorial before releasing funds for the memorial. If the monument is in good condition, no funds should be released for it. The Collector may, if necessary, divert such funds for other memorials in the district itself or dedicate them to the Government before the end of the current financial year.
The amount of grants spent from the funds distributed to the District Collectors should be reconciled with the Accountant General-1, Mumbai/ Accountant General-2, Nagpur within the prescribed time and submit a monthly report of the expenditure to the government on a regular basis. In this regard, the Government Order No. Action to be taken as per Expenditure Compensation 2011/ S.No. 139/2011/ SWASAC-1, dated 16/8/2011.
The expenditure for the current financial year shall be met from the approved budgetary provision for the financial year 2018-19 under the head (a) No.A-42059-Public Works, (80) General, 053-Preservation and Repair (00) (01) Subsidiary Grants for Preservation and Maintenance of Martyrs Memorials (20590015) and shall be covered under the same head.
The Collector of Yavatmal should submit the utilization certificate of the distributed fund to the government within three months.
