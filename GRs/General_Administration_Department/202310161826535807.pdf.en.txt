It has been decided to organize Vigilance Awareness Week in the State every year by Government Order dated 22nd October, 2001. This year's. The Central Vigilance Commission has announced that Vigilance Awareness Week will be observed from 30th October to 05th November, 2023. This year's theme is" Say No to Corruption; Commit to the Nation."
Accordingly, the Vigilance Awareness Week is also to be organized in the state from October 30 to November 5, 2023. In this regard, the following recommendations have been made:
Vigilance Awareness Week should be organized during the above period by all the departments of the State Government and all the heads of departments/ offices under their control, recognized enterprises of the State Government, cooperative societies, autonomous institutions.
b) The event will begin on 30th October, 2023 at 11.00 am with a pledge to eradicate corruption. All officers/ staff shall take pledge in the presence of Head of Department/ Head of Office or senior most officer. (A sample is attached.) )
(a) After the promise. Governor and M. The message given by the Chief Minister on the occasion of this week will be read out to the attendees. (Copies of the message are attached.) )
d) This week. Governor and M. The Chief Minister's message should be disseminated to the people of the state.
On the occasion of this week, the following programs should be organized: a) Posters/ Cloth boards/ signboards should be put up on the facade of offices and public places (such as railway stations, bus stands, airports, etc.) to spread awareness about corruption eradication. Use of electronic social media such as WhatsApp, e-mails, SMS etc. for creating awareness.
(b) Public awareness messages on corruption eradication should be disseminated through variable message system boards at strategic locations in big cities like Mumbai and Pune.
Organize debate competitions/ lectures/ essay competitions/ workshops/ seminars/ interviews at the level of offices/ schools/ colleges to highlight the ill-effects of corruption.

d) Publication and distribution of special booklets on corruption eradication and awareness.
(e) Call upon the non-governmental organizations, voluntary organizations and general public of the State to participate in the vigilance awareness campaign in a big way/ enthusiastically. For this purpose, innovative and attractive schemes/ programmes like street plays, poetry readings, etc. should be planned.
All the departments, heads of departments and heads of offices of the state government should organize various competitions/ lectures/ essay competitions etc. activities in their subordinate offices on the subject of corruption eradication.
Since no separate funds will be provided for this week's event, each office will have to spend from their available funds.
5. The Director General, Anti-Corruption Bureau has been entrusted with the responsibility of organizing Vigilance Awareness Week. They should submit a report to the government in this regard.
All the Ministries/ Departments are requested to bring these suggestions to the notice of all the Heads of Departments and Heads of Offices under them.
