Circular:
The members of Parliament/ Members of Legislative Assemblies, etc., who have been given the status of Ministers by the Government, may represent the interests of the people and public works.
In order to avoid delay in disposal, a comprehensive revised Circular dated 11th March, 2016 has been issued superseding all Circulars on how and whether Government officials should attend meetings held for disposal of pending works. Similarly, it has been clarified vide circular dated 29th August, 2018 that the meetings convened by the Chairpersons of the Government Committees whose Chairpersons are Non-Governmental Persons/ Members of Parliament/ Members of Legislative Assemblies shall be attended by the concerned officers in the jurisdiction of the Committee concerned with the subject matter of the meeting. However, other Members of Parliament/ Members of Legislative Assemblies, who are required to convene a meeting in connection with the works in their constituencies, shall be required to convene the meeting at the office of the Collector in accordance with the circular dated 11th March, 2016.

The Ministers of the Government have the power to supervise the administrative work concerned and to give orders to the officers concerned by convening a meeting or calling them for a meeting and holding discussions with them. However, such powers are not available to non-official members such as the Leader of Opposition in the Legislative Assembly and the Legislative Council, who are accorded the status of Ministers. Therefore, Government officials are not expected to attend the meetings or tours organized by such non-official members/ Members of Parliament/ Members of Legislative Assembly. However, in order to ensure that issues of public interest as well as public works are not hampered, the concerned Collector should seek a list of pending works from the members of the district who are willing to hold a meeting for the immediate disposal of pending works in the constituencies of Members of Parliament and Members of the Legislative Assembly. Meetings with the concerned officials should be held on a regular basis every month. Regarding the meeting. Members of Parliament/ MLAs should also be invited for discussion.
3. All the Ministries/ Departments are hereby informed to bring these instructions to the notice of all the Heads of Departments/ Offices under their administrative control and the instructions contained in the said circulars are to be strictly followed.
