Preamble: In order to regulate the telecast of private television channels and cable networks, the Central Government is required to constitute State and District Level Controlling Committees under the provisions of the Cable Television Networks (Regulation) Act, 1995. Also Ma. The Supreme Court in its judgment dated 12.01.2017 in WP (C) 387 of 2000 Common Cause Vs. Union of India& Ors., has directed that the Committee shall also regulate the broadcasting of Private FM Radio Stations, Community Radios. Accordingly, the government was considering reconstitution of the State Controlling Committee.
Government Decision: The Government is approving the establishment of a State Level Controlling Committee as per the Cable Television Networks (Regulation) Act, 1995 to regulate the programmes broadcast by private television channels and FM radio stations and community radio stations in the State.
The state-level committee is being constituted.

(b) The term of office of the members of the Committee shall be for a period of five years. They will not be eligible for re-nomination. A new member can be nominated to fill the vacancy. The committee will meet twice in the year.
(c) The powers and functions of the State Level Controlling Committee shall be as follows:
To see whether district level committees have been formed or not.
To see whether their meetings are held as per the rules.
To see whether the authorized officers are performing their duties.
To see how many cases have been dealt with and what decisions they have come to.
Provide advice and guidance to the district level committees.
To take decisions on the matters specified by the committee at the district or local level.
