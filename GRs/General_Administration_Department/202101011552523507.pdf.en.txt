The Ministry Restaurant provides catering services through credit sales for meetings held by officers of the rank of Chief Secretary, Secretary and Joint/ Deputy Secretary. The Ministry, after reviewing the outstanding credit sale dues of restaurants, has observed that most of the departments/ offices have been defaulting on credit sale dues due to non-payment of food dues on time. Also, the Accountant General's Office under the audit of the restaurant has raised strong objections to the outstanding amount of credit sales.
2. In this background, it has been decided that the concession on sale of foodgrains by restaurants to the administrative departments of the Ministry will be given only for one week.
(3) The administrative departments concerned shall have the power to determine the manner in which/ at the level of any officer, the demand for the sale of food items by the restaurant shall be recorded by the department. However, the joint secretary/ deputy secretary establishment of the concerned administrative department will be responsible for receiving the payment from the restaurant and paying it within the next week. If the payment for the previous week is not made in the following week, the concerned administrative department/ office will not be able to give the concession of sale of loan.
