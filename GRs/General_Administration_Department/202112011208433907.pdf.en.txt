The Proposal:
A person with vast and long administrative experience, he was instrumental in putting the state's financial and administrative machinery back in action quickly and effectively in the wake of Covid-19. Since there was an urgent need in the office of the Chief Minister, the matter of appointing an experienced person as the" Principal Advisor" in this office was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
Shri Sitaram Kunte, Shri. (1985) as Chief Secretary. Having retired on 30.11.2021 and having vast experience in various important positions in the administration, he is being appointed as" Principal Advisor" in the Chief Minister's Secretariat.
Shri Kunte will discharge the responsibilities assigned to him from time to time to restore the economic and administrative machinery of the State in the wake of COVID-19 in a speedy and effective manner. He was also given the responsibility to take special measures to promote new industrial investment in the newly created challenging situation in the state. Other important tasks and responsibilities assigned by the Chief Minister will be fulfilled.
The terms and conditions of the appointment shall be as follows:
The amount received by Shri Sitaram Kunte after deducting his pension (including Dearness Allowance and Dearness Allowance) from the total salary (including Basic Pay and Dearness Allowance) before retirement is being fixed as monthly wages.
(2) Mr. Sitaram Kunte shall be entitled to such other allowances and perquisites as are admissible to the Chief Secretary (excluding the accommodation reserved for the post).
