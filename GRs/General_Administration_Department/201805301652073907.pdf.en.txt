Preamble: Under Rule 36A of the Maharashtra General Provident Fund Rules, 1998, the procedure for adjustment of missing records of deposits or expenditure in the Vanini Account has been prescribed.
Accordingly, the aforesaid Reference No. 4) It has been reported that there are records of Missing Credits on the account of Maharashtra General Provident Fund Subscribers and information about the names, account numbers and month of Missing Credits of such Subscribers has already been sent to the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers by the Accountant General's Office along with the Annual Account Statement. However, every year, the subscriber is found to have missing credits in the annual statement of accounts.
All the Drawing and Disbursing Officers have also been informed that the information regarding the missing credits and unposted items of the subscribers has also been made available on the website of the Accountant General's Office http:// www. agmaha. cag. gov. in for the service of the State Government. However, the Accountant General has regretted that the number of missing credits and unposted items has not been reduced so far and has suggested that instructions should be issued to all government offices (all the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers) through the administrative departments of the Ministry to resolve the record of missing credits and unposted items in the accounts of the depositors and to take necessary action to reduce the number of such items completely.

Government Circular No: Bhanini 1118/ (P. No. 06/18)/ 13-A
Regarding reducing the number of missing credits in the account of Provident Fund subscribers, the above reference No. 1 has been notified by the government. Similarly, the procedure for resolving the records of missing credits has also been prescribed in the Potnium (A) of Rule 36A of the Maharashtra General Provident Fund Rules, 1998. However, every year, the Accountant General's Office has come to the notice of the Maharashtra General Provident Fund Subscribers that there are a large number of missing credits on the subscriber's account in the annual accounting statement. At present, 46755 subscribers have Missing Credits and 31380 subscribers have Unposted Items.
Government Circular: All concerned offices need to take immediate action regarding the above situation.
All the Drawing and Disbursing Officers of all the Ministries/ Administrative Divisions as well as the Subordinate Regional Offices of the Department shall make available the list of Missing Credits and Unposted Items available on the website of the Accountant General or the State Government under the link important links and accordingly, all the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers shall verify the records of their office for complete redressal of Missing Credits and Unposted Items and take appropriate immediate action for their redressal as per the prescribed procedure.
All Provident Fund subscribers should also check whether or not the missing credits are shown against their name and inform the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers accordingly.
The procedures prescribed in Rule 36A of the Maharashtra General Provident Fund Rules, 1998 and the provisions thereunder shall be scrupulously followed by the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officers. The head of the concerned office should take necessary action immediately so that the missing amounts not properly recorded in the provident fund are immediately recorded in the accounts of the subscribers. In cases where there is undue delay even after receipt of information from the Accountant General about the missing amounts, the interest payable on account of such delay shall be recovered from the employee and/ or head of the office causing the delay. All the heads of the offices and the drawing and disbursing officers will be responsible for the action to be taken in this regard.
The Accountant General (Accounts and Accounts)-1, Maharashtra, Mumbai vide the aforesaid Reference No. The letter dated 3, 4, and 5 is available on the website of the General Administration Department www. gad. maharashtra. gov. in and all concerned should take action as per the instructions given by the Accountant General.
