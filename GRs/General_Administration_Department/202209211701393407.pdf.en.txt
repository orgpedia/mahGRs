The Maharashtra Appointment of Selected Candidates Act, 2022 (Maharashtra Act No. 45).
Introduction: The Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC), through a competitive selection process, directly recruited candidates belonging to the Educationally and Socially Backward Classes (ESBC) and Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC), including Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Vimukta Jati (A), Nomadic Tribes (B), Nomadic Tribes (C), Nomadic Tribes (D), Special Backward Classes, Other Backward Classes and Economically Weaker Sections, in the public services and posts. Many candidates in the said category were given appointments on the basis of their merit in the competitive examinations. After the judgment of 27th June, 2019. The Supreme Court The appointments made in the public services to the candidates belonging to socially and educationally backward classes till the stay order issued on 9.9.2020 are exempted. Protection was granted by court order. However, following due process, the concerned Selection Authorities have decided to hold the examination. Before 9th September, 2020, some of the candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Vimukt Jati (A), Nomadic Tribes-B, Nomadic Tribes-C, Nomadic Tribes-D, Special Backward Classes, Other Backward Classes, Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) and Economically Weaker Section (EWS) were selected. However, the selected candidates could not be given appointment letters due to the COVID-19 pandemic and lockdown in the state.
After the judgment of the Hon'ble Supreme Court, the original selection lists were revised by the respective selection authorities. Due to revision of the original selection lists, the candidates belonging to the said categories which were at the bottom of the original selection lists could not make it to the revised selection lists and hence they have been deprived of appointments in Government services. such

The cabinet decided to introduce a bill in the legislature to create a maximum number of posts for the candidates who were deprived of appointment.
Accordingly, the Maharashtra Legislature has passed the" Maharashtra Creation of Overwhelming Number of Posts and Appointment of Selected Candidates Act, 2022." Section 3 of the said Act provides for the creation of supernumerary posts for the above candidates, and for the appointment to the department-wise and cadre-wise posts mentioned in the Annexure to the Act, by the competent authority concerned. The candidates who who were selected prior to 9.9.2020. The candidates who could not be given appointments in government services before 9.9.2020 are in the same position as the candidates who have been given appointments before that date and they are victims of circumstances, therefore, the selection process of the candidates who have not been given appointments in government services before that date will be held as per schedule. 9.9.2020 and recommended by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission, District Selection Committees and other Selection Authorities after due process of selection. However, it was under consideration of the Government to issue detailed instructions under Section 4 (4) of the said Act for giving appointments to the candidates who could not be given appointment letters due to the COVID-19 pandemic and lockdown in the State. The government has issued orders towards this.
In compliance with the Maharashtra Appointment of Superintendents of Posts and Selection of Selected Candidates Act, 2022, the Government under Section 4 (4) of the Act, while giving appointment to the candidates who have been deprived of appointment as per the provisions of this Act, the concerned administrative departments and the zonal offices, authorities and corporations under them shall take the following action.
(1) The Ministerial Administrative Department and the Regional Offices, Authorities, Corporations and Local Bodies under it shall create a maximum number of posts equivalent to the number of posts in the Schedule to the Maharashtra Appointment of Selected Candidates Act, 2022.
(2) In accordance with Section 4 (3) of the said Act, the appointing authority shall forthwith intimate the candidates concerned to join the service by written letter/ electronic means.
The candidates who are being appointed to the post of Superintendents are required to attend the concerned office with necessary documents within 30 days after they have been informed by the appointing authority in writing/ through electronic means.
(4) The appointing authority shall ensure that the selection of the candidates for appointment to the above posts is made/ recommended by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission, the District Selection Committee, the competent authority for selecting the candidates for the Government, Government Departments and their Subordinate Offices or Authorities.
(v) In the case of superannuation, the candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Denotified Castes-A, Nomadic Tribes-B, Nomadic Tribes-C, Nomadic Tribes-D, Economically Backward Classes, Educationally and Socially Backward Classes (ESBC) and Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) are eligible to apply.
