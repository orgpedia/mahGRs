Introduction - Examination centres have been set up in all the districts of Maharashtra for various competitive examinations conducted by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission. The authority to conduct the examinations on behalf of the Commission has been delegated to the District Collectors of the respective districts and they have been declared as Drawing and Distributing Officers for the conduct of the examinations. However, examinations held in Mumbai city, Mumbai suburbs, and Navi Mumbai are conducted by the office of the Maharashtra Public Service Commission.
Group-A and Group-B (Gazetted), Group-B (Gazetted) and Group-C in the service of the State Government.
In line with the policy decision taken by the government with a view to implementing the recruitment of cadres through the commission, the functioning of the commission is going to increase to a great extent. Hence, limited officers / staff of the Commission are available at all district headquarters as well as at Mumbai / Navi Mumbai.
It is going to be very difficult to conduct the exams at the exam center. Hence, it was under the consideration of the Government to entrust the work of conducting the examinations in Mumbai city, Mumbai suburbs and Navi Mumbai to the Collector Mumbai city, the Collector Mumbai suburbs and Divisional Commissioner, Konkan Division, Navi Mumbai and to declare the Collector Mumbai city, the Collector Mumbai suburbs and the Divisional Commissioner, Konkan Division, Navi Mumbai as the "Drawing and Disbursing Officer" to meet the expenditure for conducting the examinations.
The Government's Decision-
The examinations conducted by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission in Mumbai city, Mumbai suburbs and Navi Mumbai are being entrusted to the Collector Mumbai City, Collector Mumbai Suburbs and Divisional Commissioner, Konkan Division, Navi Mumbai. 02. Expenditure incurred by the District Collector Mumbai City, District Collector Mumbai Suburban and Divisional Commissioner, Konkan Division, Navi Mumbai for conducting the examination as aforesaid shall be apportioned from the grant-in-aid sanctioned by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission to the aforesaid officers in the respective financial year under the head Demand No. A-3, General Administration Department, 2051 Public Service Commission, (102) State Public Service Commission, (00) (01) Maharashtra Public Service Commission (weighted).
The concerned officer shall withdraw the amount required from the concerned district treasury on a short payment and shall be responsible to submit the details of the expenditure to the Secretary, Maharashtra Public Service Commission every month. Once the payment has been verified and signed, the Secretary, Maharashtra Public Service Commission shall forward the same to the Accountant General of Payments, Mumbai / Nagpur, as provided in the Maharashtra Contingency Expenditure Rules, 1965.
