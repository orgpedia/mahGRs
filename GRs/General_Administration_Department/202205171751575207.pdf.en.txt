Read:-: Government Circular, General Administration Department, No: PARIKH-2020/ Q.No.62/ 18 (Rs. And why Dated the 13th October 2020.
Preamble: In view of the outbreak of COVID-19 virus, the demand that the hearing of the first appeals under the Right to Information Act, 2005, as well as the hearings of the semi-judicial nature being conducted by the various competent authorities under various Acts, should be conducted through video conferencing, has been made. In pursuance of the PIL (L) 3144/ 2020 filed in the Bombay High Court, Shri Shailesh Gandhi and others Vs. Government of Maharashtra, instructions have been issued vide Circular dated 13th October, 2020 to conduct the said hearing through video conferencing in the circumstances of outbreak of COVID-19 virus.
In the said petition, the petitioner vide his representation dated October 26, 2020, has sought to make this mode of conducting hearings through video conferencing permanent and not just for the COVID period. Against this backdrop, the government was considering issuing revised instructions.
Government circular:
The Circular further approves the option of Video Conferencing (Online Hearing) for holding Administrative Hearings of quasi-judicial nature in all Government Offices subject to the following conditions:
(a) Even if the option of attending the office for physical hearing is available to the applicants/ parties as the COVID restrictions have been lifted under the present COVID order, the hearing authority should provide the option of conducting the hearing through video conferencing (online) and the hearing authority should take an appropriate decision as to the mode by which the hearing should be conducted.

(b) The Hearing Authority shall strictly follow the preventive instructions regarding COVID 19 virus issued by the Government or the local authority from time to time, if the applicant/ party is called in person (in person) to the office for a hearing of quasi-judicial nature.
(c) If the applicant/ party seeks a hearing through video conferencing (online) for a quasi-judicial hearing, the option of such online hearing shall be made available to him by the Hearing Authority. Adequate facilities should be made available in the office.
(3) The provisions of this Order shall remain in force until further orders.
This circular is being issued in accordance with the consensus arrived at under the Finance Department's Informal Reference No.667/ 2021/ Expenditure-4 dated 10/12/2021.
