The Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology (RGSCST) has been established under the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 with the objective of promoting, promoting and promoting the adoption of science and technology for the benefit of society in various social issues of the State. As per Rule 14 of the said Act, the Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology (RGSCST) is funded by the Consolidated Fund of the Government in the form of Grants-in-Aid every financial year.
Demand No.A-5 for the Commission on Science and Technology for the year 2023-24, Head of AccountsSecretariat-Social Services090Secretariat (00) (03) Coordination and Research in Science and Technology (Programme) (2251 0042) 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payment) Under the heading Detailed Accounts Rs. 92.00 crore (Rupees Twenty Two Crores only) has been approved.
As per the Government Circular dated 12.04.2023 of the Department of Finance regarding disbursement of grants, the Department is getting 70% of the total funds sanctioned for schemes under the programme through Budget Estimates, Allocation and Control System (BEAMS), out of which 20% funds will be available for disbursement for the quarter April to June, 2023.
