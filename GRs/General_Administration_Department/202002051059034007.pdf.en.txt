Creation of 10 supernumerary posts for officers/ employees of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations who have been appointed from the Scheduled Tribes category but have not been able to produce caste validity certificate or have submitted other caste validity certificate.
The Government of Maharashtra
the General Administration Department
GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. MAWAJ 2019/ PR No. 210/34-A
Madam Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk,
Ministry, Mumbai- 400 032.
Date is February 4, 2020.
Reference: Government Resolution, General Administration Department No. BCC-2018/ P. No. 308/16-B, D. 21.12.2019
Preamble: Instructions have been given to create more number of posts for the officers/ employees who have been appointed from the Scheduled Tribe category under the Government decision dated 21.12.2019 under the reference of the General Administration Department, but have not been able to submit the caste certificate or other caste validity certificate, and to send a requisition to the Maharashtra Public Service Commission to fill the vacancies of the officers/ employees appointed to the post of the said Scheduled Tribe. Accordingly, the government was considering the creation of a total of 10 additional posts of senior assistant director-1, clerk typist-3, driver-2, peon-1, messenger-1, helper-1 and dungeon assistant-1 of the officers/ employees of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations under the General Administration Department. The government will take a decision on this.
Government's decision:
A person who has submitted a caste validity certificate of a special backward class or any other backward class after the certificate of Scheduled Tribe has been invalidated, against the decision of the caste verification committee.
However, a petition was filed in the court. The court has not stayed and the caste validity certificate is invalid.
As per the order, 10 supernumerary posts are being created on the establishment of the concerned office for the reinstatement of the officers/ employees who have been terminated from service.
Statement No. 1:- A total of 7 officers/ employees who could not produce the caste validity certificate, submitted the Other Backward Classes caste validity certificate and pending court cases regarding the caste validity certificate.
Return No.2:- 3 employees whose services have been terminated due to invalidation of caste validity certificate.
A total of 10 supernumerary posts as mentioned above are being created at the establishment of the concerned office. The process of obtaining approval of the Finance Department has been separately carried out as per the procedure laid down in the Government Resolution No. ASANK-1001/ PR No. 29/2001/ Financial Reforms, dated 10.09.2001.

8. The expenditure on salary of superannuation should be covered under the head of salary of the department in which the concerned officer/ employee is presently working and should be met from the sanctioned grant of the respective financial year.
