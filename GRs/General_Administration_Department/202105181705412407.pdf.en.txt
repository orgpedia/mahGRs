Read:- 1. Government Circular No.Japuti2020/ Q. No. 95/29 of the General Administration Department Ministry of Home Affairs letter dated 19/9/2021 Public, dated 13th May, 2021
Circular:
As per the Government Circular dated 14.01.2021, it has been decided to hold the Anti-Terrorism and Anti-Violence Oath Ceremony on 21.05.2021.
Further, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India vide letter No.2 dated 13th May, 2021, has notified the observance of Anti-Terrorism and Anti-Violence Day on 21st May, 2021 by taking a pledge against terrorism and violence.
3. Ministry of Home Affairs is, however, requested to take necessary action in the context of the instructions issued by the Government of India vide its letter dated 13th May, 2021 and to observe the Anti-Terrorism and Anti-Violence Day on Friday, the 21st May, 2021 and accordingly to take the Anti-Terrorism and Anti-Violence Pledge at the pre-lunch hour.
4. In order to take necessary precautions to prevent the spread of corona virus in the state, the program should be organized in the presence of minimum officers and staff by keeping proper social distance, wearing a face mask and following the instructions issued from time to time.
