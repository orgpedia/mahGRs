The Proposal:
The 103rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 2019 has been passed by the Parliament. It amends Articles 15 and 16 of the Constitution of India. Clause (6) has been inserted in Article 15 of the Constitution. According to the amendment, any special provision may be made for the advancement of the economically weaker sections of the citizens other than the categories mentioned in clauses (4) and (5) of Article 15 and for the economically weaker sections other than the categories mentioned in clauses (4) and (5) of Article 30, in other educational institutions other than minority educational institutions referred to in clause (1) of Article 30 as well as in private educational institutions, whether subsidized by the State Government or not. The State shall not be debarred from making any special provision as aforesaid by virtue of any of the provisions of article 15 or sub-clause (g) of clause (1) of article 19 or clause (2) of article 29. This reservation will be in addition to the existing reservation for the backward classes. (Explanation: For the purposes of Articles 15 and 16, economically weaker sections shall be such as the State Government may from time to time notify on the basis of economic income and other economic infirmities).
Further, by amending Article 16 of the Constitution by inserting a clause (6) therein, it is provided that the State shall not be prevented from reserving up to 10% of the seats for economically weaker sections in addition to the reservation provided for in clause (4) of Article 16 in public services.
The Constitution Amendment Act passed by the Parliament has come into force with effect from the Gazette dated 14th January, 2019.
This should be implemented in the state. Considering this, the state government had decided to reserve 10% seats in government, private, aided and unaided institutions, excluding minority educational institutions, for the economically weaker sections in the open category. Government's decision:
Apart from the categories provided for under Article 15 (4) and (5) and Article 16 (4) of the Constitution of India, 10% reservation is prescribed in the total number of seats in Government Educational Institutions/ Aided Schools, Colleges, All Higher Educational Institutions, Unaided Schools, Colleges and Educational Institutions for the economically weaker sections (EWS) of the State. This reservation shall not apply to the minority institutions referred to in clause (1) of Article 30 of the Constitution.
Also, 10% reservation is being prescribed in direct service posts for the economically weaker sections of the state in government establishments, semi-government establishments, boards/ corporations/ urban local bodies/ rural local bodies, authorities.
The benefit of reservation for economically weaker sections shall be admissible subject to the following conditions:
(a) Government Educational Institutions/ Aided Schools, Colleges, Educational Institutions, Unaided Classes for the economically weaker sections of the State who are not included in the categories of persons covered by the Maharashtra State Service Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Scheduled Tribes, Nomadic Tribes, Special Backward Classes and Other Backward Classes Act, 2001 (Maharashtra Act No. 8 of 2004) and the Maharashtra State Reservation of Seats in Educational Institutions in the State and of Appointments or Posts in the Public Service under the control of the State (Maharashtra Act No. 62 of 2018)
10% seats are reserved for the students of schools, colleges and universities. This reservation shall not apply to minority educational institutions referred to in clause (1) of Article 30 of the State Constitution.
Also, 10% of the total number of posts in all cadres of direct services in Government establishments, semi-government establishments, Boards/ Corporations/ Urban Local Bodies/ Rural Local Bodies/ Authorities shall be reserved for economically weaker sections.
(b) 10% of the total reservation in the State for the Backward Classes and Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBCs) in the State of Maharashtra, as prescribed under the Maharashtra State Service Reservation Act, 2001 (Maharashtra Act No. 8 of 2004) for the Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Scheduled Tribes, Scheduled Tribes, Scheduled Tribes, Special Backward Classes and Other Backward Classes, or

(a) Eligibility for the benefit of reservation for Economically Weaker Sections: (1) The family of the applicant/ candidate whose combined annual income is less than Rs. Applicants whose income is less than Rs 8 lakh will be considered as economically weaker and will be eligible for the benefit of this reservation.
(2) The family for the benefit of this reservation shall include the parents and siblings of the applicant/ candidate below 18 years of age and the children and spouse of the applicant/ candidate below 18 years of age.
The total income of the family shall include the income of the members of the family from all sources i.e. wages, agricultural income, business, profession and all other sources, the annual income of the previous financial year on the date of filing of the application shall be Rs. It should be less than 8 lakhs.
(3) In order to avail the benefit of reservation for the economically weaker sections, it shall be mandatory to submit the certificate of eligibility of the competent authority in the form prescribed herewith (Annexure-A).
The format of the application form (Annexure-B), documents/ evidence to be attached with the application form (Annexure-C) and declaration (Annexure-D) should be as per the format attached.
(4) To avail the benefit of this reservation, the person or his family shall be registered in the State of Maharashtra. Must be a resident on or before October 13, 1967.
(5) The age, examination fee and other allowable concessions to the candidates belonging to this category shall be as per the rules applicable to the Other Backward Classes by the State Government from time to time.
(d) the procedure relating to the certificate of eligibility-
(1) The Tahsildar is being declared as the competent authority to issue the eligibility certificate for the benefit of reservation for the economically weaker sections and the Collector shall have the powers to declare more than one Tahsildar as the competent authority, if required.
(2) It shall be mandatory to issue the eligibility certificate to the beneficiary within one month (30 days) from the date of receipt of application along with necessary documents from the beneficiary for obtaining the necessary eligibility certificate for the economically weaker section.
