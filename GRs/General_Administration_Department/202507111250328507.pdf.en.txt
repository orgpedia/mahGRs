In the case of appointment of Controlling Officers against Group-I and Group-II Government Officers and Group-III B Non-Gazetted Employees of Group-IV in the State with a view to expeditiously completing the pending departmental inquiries, vide Notification No. Detailed instructions have been given by the Government Circulars 1 to 3. Now with a view to expeditiously disposing of the cases of departmental inquiry by removing the difficulties faced by the investigating officers, the Reference No. The following new orders are being issued by amending the Government Circulars 1 to 3.

1) Subsidiary / Co-ordinator dealing with the matters of Divisional Vigilance for the functioning of the currently pending and future commencing Divisional Vigilance in the Administrative Department of the Ministry.
The Secretary will continue to be the designated Nodal Officer.
2) A senior officer with regard to departmental inquiry in respect of departmental vigilance cases in the field office shall be appointed as the nodal officer.
3) In case of departmental inquiry, the appointment orders of the investigating officer and the presenting officer should mention the name, designation, office address, contact number and email ID of the nodal officer without fail. And copies of those orders should be forwarded to the nodal officer.

The duties and responsibilities of the Nodal Officer shall be as follows: - (1) The Nodal Officer shall, subject to the provisions of Rule 8 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979, examine the various stages in the functioning of the departmental inquiry, in particular the investigating officer and
Be up-to-date on the Haast and responsibilities of the presenting officers.
(2) The nodal officer shall follow up on the receipt of the appointment order by the vigilance officer so that the proceedings of the first hearing shall commence within the prescribed period.
(3) Provide full assistance and expedite the matter to the investigating officer as well as the presenting officer in resolving any difficulties arising out of the documents or other difficulties relating to the investigation.
(4) A monthly review of the progress of pending cases with the vigilance officer shall be compulsorily taken in writing by the presenting officer concerned. If the presenting officer is not responding or is found to be in a hurry in discharging his duties, he should be given an understanding at the nodal officer level at the first instance. A copy of the same should be forwarded to the concerned disciplinary authority. If there is still no improvement, a recommendation should be made immediately to the relevant disciplinary authority for action against the presenting officer.
(5) All correspondence made by the investigating officer and the presenting officer to the disciplinary authority at different stages during the investigation process for the purpose of meeting or clarifying certain matters shall also be forwarded to the nodal officer. The disciplinary authority should also forward the correspondence with the investigating officer and the presenting officer to the nodal officer.
However, it is not necessary to send the documents of evidence in Choksi and the inquiry report to the nodal officer. The nodal officer should take note of this correspondence and follow up to ensure that the matters mentioned in it are met.
(6) The Nodal Officer shall submit a monthly report of the progress of the Choksi case to the disciplinary authority.
The nodal officer will be liable for disciplinary action if he defaults in his duties.
