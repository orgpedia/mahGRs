The proposal-
Several people have lost their lives due to floods in Kerala. Many people have also been left homeless. This is a national disaster and we need help from all quarters. Huge financial support will be required to compensate for the loss of life and property caused by natural disasters. The state government has also offered assistance. A team of doctors led by the Minister of Water Resources and Medical Education, along with various materials, immediately rushed to the state of Kerala to help. In response to this call for humanitarian relief, the Maharashtra State Gazetted Officers Federation, State Government Employees Central Association, Maharashtra and Zilla Parishad Employees Union have dutifully proposed to the government to deduct one day's salary from the salary of the gazetted officers of the state for August 2018 for this relief work. Retired officers/ employees have also been asked to contribute one day's salary from their pension for the Kerala State Disaster Response Fund. In view of the severe nature of the disaster in the state of Kerala, the state government has decided to provide more money from the Chief Minister's Relief Fund for the ongoing relief work. For this purpose, they are requested to contribute one day's salary each from their salary for the month of September 2018 to the Chief Minister's Relief Fund.
All the Ministries/ Departments of the State Government and all the Government/ Semi-Government offices under them, Zila Parishads, Panchayat Samitis, Municipal Corporations, Municipalities/ Municipalities, Public Undertakings, Corporations, Boards and Head of Departments/ Offices of all the autonomous bodies should bring this circular to the notice of all the officers/ employees of their department/ office and explain to them the seriousness of the issue and deduct one day's salary from the salary of September, 2018.
The officer/ employee who has an objection to the collection of the amount from the monthly salary should give a personal letter to the establishment officer of the concerned office. The salary of such officers/ employees should not be deducted.
The Association of Retired Officers/ Employees has also expressed their willingness to contribute one day's pension from their pension for the Kerala flood victims and such retired officers/ employees should inform the concerned Treasury Office about the voluntary deduction of their one day's salary.
The following procedure should be followed for deduction of one day's salary (month of September 2018) and depositing the amount in the Chief Minister's Relief Fund and submitting its account:
(i) The entire amount of salary payments for the month of September 2018 shall be withdrawn.
However, after regular deduction of salary and deduction of one day's salary, the balance amount of salary shall be paid to the concerned officer by cheque/ cash/ in the prescribed manner.
At present, the salary of the officers/ employees whose salary is being credited to their bank account as per their bank account details provided by them to the government, should be informed before depositing the remaining amount of salary after regular deduction of salary to the concerned bank.
(ii) In the case of deduction of 1 day's salary, it shall be calculated on the basis of the total amount of basic pay+ grade pay+ dearness allowance.
(iii) A separate register shall be maintained to record the amount collected at the time of disbursement of salary (for the month of September, 2018).
