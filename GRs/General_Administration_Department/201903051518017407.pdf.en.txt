2) Government Resolution No.BUD1417/ PR No. 172/ SWASAC-1, D. 14/02/2018 Preamble: The Government has set up 206 Martyrs Memorials in the State in memory of those who laid down their lives for the country by participating in the Indian Freedom Struggle, Hyderabad Liberation Struggle and Goa Liberation Movement. Since 34-35 years have passed since the construction of these monuments, they need to be repaired/ renovated. The Minister for Finance, Planning and Forests, in his budget speech for 2015-16, allocated Rs. An amount of Rs. 25 lakh had been provided for. Accordingly, funds for repair/ renovation of the Martyr Memorial were handed over to all the concerned District Collectors in the financial year 2016-17 and 2017-18. The concerned district collector has demanded that the work of some martyr memorials is still pending. A sum of Rs. 12,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Twelve Crores) has been sanctioned in the Winter Session 2018-19 and accordingly, a proposal was under consideration of the Government to hand over the balance amount for repair and renovation of the Martyr Memorial to the concerned District Collector. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
Government Decision:- Considering the matter mentioned in the Preamble, a fund of Rs. 12,00,00, 000/- (Rs. 12 crore) available for the year 2018-19 for the repair/ renovation of the Martyr Memorials in the State has been sanctioned in the winter session of 2018-19 for the incomplete Martyr Memorials out of 206 Martyr Memorials in the State.
The funds allocated for the first and second phase of works in 2016-17 and 2017-18 have been dedicated to the government by the collector offices of Parbhani, Osmanabad, Latur, Beed, Gadchiroli, Nanded, Jalgaon, Wardha, Satara and Hingoli in 2016-17 and the collector offices of Parbhani, Latur, Beed, Nanded, Satara, Hingoli, Jalna, Ahmednagar, Amravati, Solapur and Aurangabad in 2017-18. In 2016-17, the funds from the collector offices of Sangli, Nagpur and Nashik and in 2017-18, the funds from the collector offices of Nashik and Palghar have lapsed.

3. As per the prospectus now attached, the concerned District Collector shall use the funds only for the martyr memorials shown in front of their districts and for the works shown in the prospectus. All the District Collectors should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the grants released to them and that the amount disbursed should be spent only for government approved purposes.
At present, Rs. 7,65,09, 031/- (Rupees Seven Crores Sixty Five Lakhs Nine Thousand and Thirty One only) is being distributed to the District Collectors of Kolhapur, Nashik, Palghar, Ahmednagar, Beed, Wardha, Osmanabad, Latur, Parbhani, Solapur, Jalgaon, Bhandara and Satara as per the attached statement.
(5) All the concerned Collectors should immediately complete the administrative approval, tender process and other necessary administrative matters for the works demanded by the Collector's Office and indicated in the accompanying statement. Also, the Collector should ensure that the repair works are carried out as per the prevailing standards and tariff sheets of the Public Works Department. All the concerned Collectors should inspect the present condition of the memorial before releasing funds for the memorial. Funds should not be released for a monument if it is in good condition. Such funds may be diverted by the concerned Collector for other memorials in the district itself, if required, or dedicated to the Government before the end of the current financial year.
The amount of grants spent from the funds distributed to the District Collectors should be reconciled with the Accountant General-1, Mumbai/ Accountant General-2, Nagpur within the prescribed time and submit a monthly report of the expenditure to the Government on a regular basis. In this regard, the Government Order No. Action to be taken as per Expenditure Compensation 2011/ S.No. 139/2011/ SWASAC-1, dated 16/8/2011.
The expenditure for the current financial year shall be met from the approved budgetary provision for the financial year 2018-19 under the head (a) No.A-42059-Public Works, (80) General, 053-Preservation and Repair (00) (01) Subsidiary Grants for Preservation and Maintenance of Martyrs Memorials (2059 0015) and shall be covered under the same head.
The concerned District Collector should submit the utilization certificate of the distributed fund to the government within three months.
