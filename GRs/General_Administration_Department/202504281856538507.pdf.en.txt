Introduction: - The share of Maharashtra Global Capacity Centres (GCCs) for India's IT services exports is currently estimated at US $60 billion and is expected to grow to over US $190 billion by 2020. The number of Global Capacity Centres (GCCs) is expected to increase from the current 1,900 to 2,500 and is expected to create 2 million new jobs. To take advantage of this opportunity for industry growth, it is necessary to formulate an integrated policy for Maharashtra to emerge as a preferred investor.
For this, the Department of Maharashtra World Capacity Centre Task Force has been constituted under the aforesaid reference. The Task Force has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Director, Directorate of Information Technology, Mumbai vide Government Decision dated 97.01.2025. The task force was required to submit recommendations to the government within 3 months. However, the government was considering extending the deadline as it would take some time for the committee to submit its recommendations.
The Government's Decisions:
To formulate an integrated policy to enable Maharashtra to emerge as a preferred investor, the Minister of State for Finance and Corporate Affairs, Smt. The Maharashtra Global Capacity Centres Task Force has been constituted under the chairmanship of Director, Directorate of Information Technology, Mumbai vide Government Decision dated 17th January, 2025. The task force was required to submit recommendations to the government within 3 months. However, since the committee will need some more time to submit its recommendations, the task force has been given a one-month extension to submit its recommendations to the government. The deadline is being extended until May 17, 2025.
