The All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 have been made applicable to the officers of the Indian Administrative Service with effect from 2007-08. As per these rules, the IPS officers of the age of 40 years and above are required to undergo the medical examination prescribed under the above rules every year and the summary of the report of their medical examination is required to be attached with the annual performance evaluation report. The Government has decided to conduct the following medical examinations for the officers of the State Government who are of the age of 40 years and above as prescribed under the All India Services (Performance Evaluation Report) Rules, 2007 for the year 2019-20:
Government's decision:
As per Rule 3 of the All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 and the accompanying Sample 4, the Indian Administrative Service officers of the State who are of the age of 40 years or more as on 31.3.2019, shall get their prescribed medical examination done in the current year from a private hospital identified revenue division wise as mentioned in Form-A attached with this Government decision. During the examination, the following recommendations should be made:
1.Maharashtra State. The I.P.S. Officers who are 40 years of age or more as on 31.03.2019 should get all the medical check-ups mentioned in Form-B done in the current year (2019-20) from the private hospitals/ medical institutions mentioned in Form-A for the Revenue Department in which they are working.
2. The BPS officers working in the Amravati Revenue Division should get the medical check-up done from the private hospitals designated for other revenue divisions near them.
The medical examination can also be done in the government hospitals of the concerned district or government/ municipal hospitals in the revenue division where the facility of conducting all the medical examinations prescribed in Form-B is available.
After the I.P.S. officers have conducted all the medical tests indicated in Form B from the hospitals designated for the respective revenue divisions or from government or municipal hospitals, the hospital is required to conduct medical tests.

The amount should be paid first and the reimbursement should be done from your office. The reimbursement of expenses on medical check-up, which is admissible once in every two years as per the Government Decision No. MAG-1098/ PR No. 220/ Health-3, dated 31st August, 2006, will not be paid to the officials who reimburse the cost of annual medical check-up under this Government Decision.
During the period from the date of this Government decision till 31st March, 2020, the officers of the Indian Administrative Service shall liaise with the concerned private hospitals to fix the dates of medical examination and conduct the medical examination as prescribed in Form-B.
Private hospitals and government or municipal hospitals designated by the government for medical examination should send the consolidated report of the medical examination of their BPSC officers to the Room Officer, General Administration Department (Office 9), 558, 5th Floor, Mantralaya (Extension), Mumbai-32. Also, after the medical examination, a copy of the said examination report should also be given to the concerned BPS officers.
All the medical check-ups mentioned in Form B will be done at one place and on one day. But in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, they should be done on the next day. The date and time of examination should be mutually decided by the medical officer and the concerned hospital. Also, the medical officer should take the preparation/ precautions for the medical check-up in consultation with the hospital.
1 day of medical check-up will be considered as duty period.
No leave/ transfer leave will be admissible if the examination is conducted on a public holiday.
Since the period of medical examination is deemed to be the period of duty, it shall be payable (if applicable) at the rate at which travelling allowance/ daily allowance is paid to the officers of the Indian Administrative Service while they are otherwise on tour. The rates of such Travel Allowance/ Daily Allowance shall be as per the orders issued by the Finance Department from time to time. This should be considered and provided to the concerned officers by the pay-paying office.
10. Before going for medical check-up, the officers shall give prior intimation to their senior officer concerned that they are going for medical check-up to the headquarters or to a hospital outside the headquarters.
The expenditure on medical examination shall be defrayed under the head of accounts applicable to the salary and allowances at the establishment of the officer concerned and shall be met from the grant sanctioned for that year. However, there will be no financial constraint.
