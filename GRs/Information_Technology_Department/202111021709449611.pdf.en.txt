The Proposal:
The State Government has constituted the Rajiv Gandhi Commission on Science and Technology (RGSCST) as per the Act No. 1 to promote and promote the application of science and technology in various issues and developmental activities faced by the society. As per Rule 4 of the said Act, the said Commission has been reconstituted vide Government Notification No. 4. Earlier, vide Notification No.3, the Government had appointed Shri Anil Shrikrishna Manekar, Director General (Retd), National Council for Science Museums, Calcutta as the Executive Head of the Commission in the capacity of Member Secretary on contract basis for a period of three years. Shri Manekar, Member Secretary had requested the Government to relieve him from the said post at the earliest, even before the expiry of the said period, on account of personal reasons. The matter of appointment to the post of Member Secretary was under consideration of the Government in accordance with the recommendation made by the Search Committee constituted to appoint a suitable person to the post falling vacant after accepting his request in principle.
Government's decision:
The Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology Act Rule 4 (Maharashtra Act of 2004, Rule No. In accordance with the procedure laid down in clause (b) of section 4 (1) (b) of the Act, Shri Narendra Shah, retired Professor, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Bombay is being appointed as Member Secretary, Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology.
