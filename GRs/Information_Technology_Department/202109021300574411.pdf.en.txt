Government Resolution: The Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission has been constituted under the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 with the objective of adopting, promoting and promoting science and technology for the benefit of society in various social issues of the state. As per Rule 14 of the said Act, the Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology (RGSCST) is funded by the Consolidated Fund of the Government in the form of Grants-in-Aid every financial year.
The approved budgetary allocation for the Commission for Science and Technology for the year 2021-22 is Rs. 49.46 crore (Rs. However, as per the Government decision dated 24.06.2021 of the Department of Finance, the Department will receive only 60% of the total funds sanctioned for the schemes under the programme, subject to the formula that the first instalment of 30% of the total funds sanctioned to the Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission in the financial year 2021-22 under the detailed accounting head 31-Support Grants (Non-Salary) will be Rs. 14,10,00, 000/- (Rupees Fourteen Crores Ten Lakhs only) has been approved under Government Circular No.Finance-2021/ P.No. 48/ Finance-3 dated 24th June, 2021 of the Department of Finance.
As per the above approval, the Under Secretary, General Administration Department (GAD) has sanctioned Rs. 14,10,00, 000/- (Rupees Fourteen Crores Ten Lakhs only) should be submitted and approved. The cheque should be drawn in the name of Member Secretary, Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission and handed over to the Commission.
The amount of this grant should be used only for the purpose mentioned in the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 and the guidelines of the Finance Department should be strictly followed while spending from it. The utilization of this grant should be examined and a detailed report of the utility certificate and programs should be submitted to the government.
The above expenditure should be put under the head Demand No. A-5,2251 Secretariat, Social Services, 090 Secretariat, Schemes under Five Year Plans, State Schemes (00) (03) Coordination and Research in Science and Technology (2251 0042), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-salaried) and it should be shared from the approved grants for the financial year 2021-22.
