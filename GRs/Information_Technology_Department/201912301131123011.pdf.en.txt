Government's decision:
The Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission has been established under the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 with the objective of adopting, disseminating and promoting science and technology for the benefit of society in various social issues of the state. As per Rule 14 of the said Act, the Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology (RGSCST) is allocated grants-in-aid in each financial year from the Consolidated Fund of the Government.
Regular officers/ staff have been appointed in the Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission with effect from May, 2018 and are being paid emoluments and allowances. For this purpose, the Department of Science and Technology has re-instituted 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) under the heading 2251 Secretariat Social Services as the main head of accounts for the salaries and allowances of the officers/ employees of the Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission in the Monsoon Session of July, 2019.
Budgetary allocation for Science and Technology Cell for the year 2019-20 is Rs. 35.00 crores) only. Of these, Rs. 2.50 crore (Rupees Two Crore Fifty Lakh only) has been diverted under the head 36-Assistant Grants (Salaries) through redistribution.
Of this, 60 per cent will be paid. An amount of Rs. 1,50,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Fifty Lakh only) is being sanctioned towards salary and allowances of officers/ staff of Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission.
4. Under Secretary, General Administration Department (V.T.C.) Rs. 1,50,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Fifty Lakh only). The cheque for the amount should be drawn in the name of" Member Secretary, Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission" and handed over to the Commission.
The amount of this grant should be used only for the purpose mentioned in the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 and the guidelines of the Finance Department should be strictly followed while spending from it. The utilization of this grant should be examined and a detailed report of the utility certificate and programs should be submitted to the government.
