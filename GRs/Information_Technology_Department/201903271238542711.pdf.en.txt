Government Resolution: The Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission has been constituted under the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 with the objective of adopting, promoting and promoting science and technology for the benefit of society in various social issues of the state. As per Rule 14 of the said Act, the Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology (RGSCST) is funded by the Consolidated Fund of the Government in the form of Grants-in-Aid every financial year. The budgetary allocation for the Science and Technology Commission for the year 2018-19 is Rs. 30,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Thirty Crores only). 70% of which is Rs. 21,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Twenty One Crores only) has been released to Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission.
Now, as per the Finance Department Government Circular No. 2018/ No. 69/ Finance-3, dated 25.01.2019, the limit of expenditure of the Fund in the Budget has been allocated to 80% of the programme expenditure. Accordingly, in the revised estimates for the financial year 2018-19, the balance subsidy out of the sanctioned limit of 80% subsidy is Rs. 3,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Crores only) to be submitted and approved by the Under Secretary, General Administration Department (GAD). The cheque for the amount should be drawn in the name of" Member Secretary, Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission" and handed over to the Commission.
The amount of this grant should be used only for the purpose mentioned in the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 and the guidelines of the Finance Department should be strictly followed while spending from it. The utilization of this grant should be examined and a detailed report of the utility certificate and programs should be submitted to the government.
