Government Resolution: The Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission has been constituted under the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 with the objective of adopting, promoting and promoting science and technology for the benefit of society in various social issues of the state. As per Rule 14 of the said Act, the Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology (RGSCST) is funded by the Consolidated Fund of the Government in the form of Grants-in-Aid every financial year.
The approved budgetary allocation for the Science and Technology Commission for the year 2020-21 is Rs. 37.20 crore (Rupees Thirty Seven Crores Twenty Lakhs Only). However, as per the decision of the Government dated 4th May, 2020 of the Department of Finance, the Department would be getting only 33% of the total funds sanctioned for the schemes under the programme, subject to the formula that the Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission would be getting the first instalment of 10% of the total funds sanctioned for the financial year 2020-21 under the detailed accounting head 31-Support Grants (Non-Payment) amounting to Rs. 3,72,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Crores Seventy Two Lakhs only) has been sanctioned.
