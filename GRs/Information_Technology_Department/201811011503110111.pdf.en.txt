Government Resolution: The Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission has been constituted under the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 with the objective of adopting, promoting and promoting science and technology for the benefit of society in various social issues of the state.
As per Rule 14 of the said Act, the Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology (RGSCST) is funded by the Consolidated Fund of the Government in the form of grants-in-aid in each financial year. Budgetary allocation for Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission for 2018-19 is Rs. 30.00 crore (Rupees Thirty Crores only). 70% of which is Rs. An amount of Rs. 21,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Twenty One Crore only) is being sanctioned for Rajiv Gandhi Commission for Science and Technology.
The Under Secretary, General Administration Department (GAD) has sanctioned Rs. 21,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Twenty Crores only). The cheque should be drawn in the name of" Member Secretary, Rajiv Gandhi Science and Technology Commission" and handed over to the Commission.
The amount of this grant should be used only for the purpose mentioned in the Maharashtra Act No. 15 of 2004 and the guidelines of the Finance Department should be strictly followed while spending from it. The utilization of this grant should be examined and a detailed report of the utility certificate and programs should be submitted to the government.
The above expenditure should be put under the head Demand No. A-5,2251 Secretariat, Social Services, 090 Secretariat, Schemes under Five Year Plans, State Schemes (00) (03) Coordination and Research in Science and Technology (2251 0042), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-salary) and should be met from the sanctioned grants for the financial year 2018-19.
