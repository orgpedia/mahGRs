Introduction: Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) is conducting Combined Competitive Training Programme (CPTP) for the candidates recommended on the basis of the final result of the State Service Examination through the training institutes of Yashoda, Pune and Vanamati, Nagpur. The General Administration Department is implementing semester wise Post Graduate Degree Courses affiliated to the University of Mumbai for the candidates who will be trained under the Combined Peripheral Training Programme (CPTP) as per the provisions prescribed under Government Decision No. TRN-0517/ PR No.36/ CPTP, dated 21.07.2017. The Computer Course is part of the Practical Component of the Master's Degree Course in Session-1 and the trainees are given hands-on training in this subject. The proposal to exempt officers who have passed post-graduation course from computer aptitude tests was received from the training institute Yashoda, Pune.
Government approved MS-CIT is a computer course designed by MKCL.
The computer courses in the post-graduate courses have also been designed by the training institutes of Yashoda, Pune and Vanamati, Nagpur with the help of MKCL.
3. Computer courses in the Master's degree programme under the Integrated Peripheral Training Programme are equivalent to the course of MS-CIT. Therefore, the government has decided to exempt the officers who have passed this course from passing the Computer Eligibility Test (MS-CIT or similar examination).

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: MATSAM 1719/ PR. NO. 18/ SE. 5/39
Government's decision:
It is mandatory for the officers/ employees of Group A, B and C of the state government to hold the computer qualification certificate within the prescribed time. However, the Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) will be conducting the examination for the recommended candidates on the basis of the final result of the state service examination.
The officers who have passed the post-graduate degree course under the Combined Competitive Training Programme (CPTP) will be deemed to have passed the computer aptitude test and will not be required to submit the computer aptitude test certificate again.
