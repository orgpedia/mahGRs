The Proposal:
Under the National Skill Development Mission (NSDM), Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE), Government of India has launched the Skill Acquisition and Knowledge Awareness for Livelihood Project (SANKALP). The amount of Rs. 5.54 crore has been released to the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society, Mumbai vide Government Resolution No.5. In this decision, the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society, Mumbai has been declared as the" Control, Drawing and Disbursing Officer."
However, since the CEO Maharashtra State Skill Development Society, Mumbai has been declared as the" Nodal Agency for implementing the activities related to skill development as per the vision and policy of the Government," the Commissioner, Commissionerate of Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship, Navi Mumbai is required to be declared as the" Controlling Authority" of the scheme. Therefore, the Government Decision dated 19/10/2020 is being superseded and the revised Government Decision in this regard is being issued as follows.

The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India, under the National Skill Development Mission (NSDM), is implementing the" Skill Acquisition and Knowledge Awareness for Livelihood Project (SANKALP)" to strengthen the institutional mechanism at the country and state level, create a pool of quality trainers, centralize all skill training programs at the state level, create an effective mechanism for monitoring and evaluation of skill development training programs, provide opportunities for skill training for socially backward classes, create skilled manpower required for the manufacturing sector and supplement the Make in India initiative. The duration of the project is 6 years and the Central and State Governments share in the project cost is 60: 40.
The project has been accorded administrative approval for implementation in the State vide Government Resolution dated 30/08/2019.
