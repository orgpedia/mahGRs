Preamble: Department of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Govt. As per the Government decision dated 07.02.2018, the standard procedure for obtaining LoI through online mode for opening of new Private Industrial Training Institutes on permanent non-grant basis and transfer of new trades, additional batches, trades to existing Private Industrial Training Institutes has been approved. The above decision was taken by the government. As per the Government Order dated 23rd May, 2019, the procedure has been revised and Private Industrial Training Institutes have been allowed to issue State Government Intent to Private Industrial Training Institutes (PITIs) through computer system on the basis of documents and undertaking submitted by the institutions through online mode, excluding physical verification for Intent to Entitlement.
The Government has approved a separate programme for obtaining Letter of Intent (LOI) from the Government for setting up of new units and new business/ additional batch/ change of business in the existing private units. Accordingly, under Reference 4 to 7 and 13, the Director, Directorate of Vocational Education and Training has recommended 62 new and 26 existing Private Industrial Training Institutes meeting the required standards for the purpose of Intent to Start a New Business. Also, 118 institutions that do not meet the required standards have not been recommended. It shall be the responsibility of the Directorate to notify the institutions which have rejected such recommendation and the institutions may rectify the errors within 01 year by paying the requisite fee as per the Government Decision dated 07.02.2018.
However, the issue of grant of Intent letters to new units/ additional units in the new units and existing units recommended by the Directorate on permanent basis without subsidy subject to the following terms and conditions was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: From the year 2021-22, LoI is being issued to 62 new Private Industrial Training Institutes mentioned in Annexure A for starting new Industrial Training Institutes on permanent non-subsidized basis and to start new trades/ additional batches in 26 existing Private Industrial Training Institutes mentioned in Annexure B.
Terms and Conditions:
Business courses will be run on a permanent basis on a non-subsidised basis.
For all the Business Courses for which LOI is being issued by the State Government, it will be mandatory for the Institute to ensure 100% compliance of the required equipment, space, power supply, qualified staff and other aspects as per the standards of DGT, New Delhi.
