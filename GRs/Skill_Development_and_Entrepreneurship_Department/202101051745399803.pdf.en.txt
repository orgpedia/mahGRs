Preamble: The Commissioner, Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship, Maharashtra State has sanctioned the first/ second benefit of pay structure of the post of promotion under the Assured Progress Scheme under the revised service as per the provisions of the Government Order dated 01.04.2010 of the Finance Department, vide Department's Government Order dated 28th June, 2018. In the meantime, the Department of Finance, Government of India, vide its decision dated 02.03.2019, has implemented the Revised Assured Progress Scheme with three benefits with effect from 01.01.2016, after 10, 20 and 30 years of regular service during the tenure of the Seventh Pay Commission. Accordingly, a proposal was under consideration for grant of 1st/ 2nd benefit, as the case may be, to the then Assistant Director, 7 officers of Group-A cadre included in the Government Order dated 28th June, 2018. Accordingly, the order dated 28th June, 2018 in respect of the following 7 officers of the cadre of Assistant Director, Group-A is being partially cancelled and revised orders are being issued in respect of them.
Government order:
As per the Government decision dated 02.03.2019 of the Department of Finance, the Seventh Pay Commission has provided for grant of first/ second benefit of pay structure of the post of promotion under the Assured Progress Scheme under the revised service of three benefits. Accordingly, the following officers who have completed 10 years of regular service in the post of Assistant Commissioner, Group-A (Pay Level S18: 49,100-1, 55,800) in the establishment of Commissioner, Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship are being sanctioned the pay scale of the post of Deputy Commissioner (Pay Level S23: 67700-208700).

From 01.01.2016 to the date of issue of the order regarding the entitlement of the benefit of the revised in-service progression scheme as per the Seventh Pay Commission, those who have been granted the first and second benefits of the assured in-service progression scheme as per the Government Decision dated 01.04.2010, are being granted this benefit in the seventh pay commission pay scale of the post of promotion as per the pay matrix of the Seventh Pay Commission as per the scheme of three benefits. Therefore, it has been mentioned in the Government Resolution at Reference No. 3 that the amount of undue benefit should be adjusted with the amount of revised benefit and the difference amount should be made admissible. Accordingly, the Commissioner, Commissionerate of Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship and the concerned Divisional Deputy Commissioner should implement the above order and submit a compliance report to the Government.
