Introduction: National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme. The scheme is implemented in the state as per the Central Government Apprenticeship Act, 1961. The scheme is being implemented through a notification issued by the central government from time to time.
Under this scheme, candidates who have completed their training in Industrial Training Institute are trained as apprentices in Industrial Establishments. Accordingly, the Central Government pays the stipend per candidate to the industrial establishments. In order to ensure proper utilization of funds available under the scheme, the Central Government vide Reference No. Under letters 2 and 3, instructions have been given to open SNA account by linking National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme with PFMS system. Accordingly, the matter of approving new accounting and computer statistics for the scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Reference No. Administrative approval has been granted. The new accounting and computer specifications required to budget the funds for this Centrally Sponsored Scheme and to meet the expenditure on the educational stipend of the trainees are being approved as under.
