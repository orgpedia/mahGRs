The Proposal:
The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India has approved the implementation of the second phase of the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana 2.0 (100% Centrally Sponsored, State Managed-PMKVY 2.0-CSSM) in the State vide Government Resolution No.8 dated 01.04.2016 for the period 2016-18 to 2019-20. However, the scheme ended in 2020. The approval for continuation and implementation of the scheme in the financial year 2020-21 was accorded vide letter No.
Now the Central Government has approved the implementation of the third phase of the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana 3.0 (100% Centrally Sponsored, State Managed- PMKVY 3.0 CSSM) under the letter No. 13. 6,01,50, 000/- has been released to the State Government. The matter of sanctioning the said fund to the Commissionerate of Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship, Navi Mumbai for disbursement and expenditure through Public Financial Management System (PFMS) and Budget Estimates, Distribution and Control System was under consideration.
Government's decision:

The Commissioner, Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship shall draw the funds allocated for this scheme by the State Government from the treasury and make it available for expenditure to the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society, Mumbai. For this, Commissioner, Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship, Navi Mumbai is being declared as" Controlling Officer" and Assistant Director (Accounts) Konkan Bhawan is being declared as" Drawing and Disbursing Officer."
4. Maharashtra State Skill Development Agency, the" nodal agency" for implementing skill development programmes in the State, will be the implementing agency for the projects covered under the scheme.
The activities under the scheme shall be implemented in accordance with the extant guidelines of the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India or as amended by the Central Government from time to time and within the prescribed time frame. No new infrastructure should be created for implementation of activities, projects should be implemented by utilising available infrastructure/ training facilities.
The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India, has issued a Common Guidance Note through a contextual notification for implementing skill development programmes at the country level. It will be mandatory for the implementing agency to spend as per the provisions of the Common Guidance Note or as amended by the Central Government from time to time and for the activities/ projects for which funds have been received from the Central Government and the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society will be responsible for sending the utilization certificate of the expenditure to the Central Government within the prescribed time limit.
The Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society should take care of sending the utilization certificate and other details of the expenditure to the concerned ministry of the central government as well as to the government. These accounts should also be made available to the Auditor General at the time of audit.
8. The guidelines/ rules/ terms/ conditions issued by the Finance and Planning Department should be followed while spending the fund.
The said Government Decision is being issued with the concurrence received under Finance Department Informal Reference No. 304/ Expenditure-6, dated 23rd December, 2021.
