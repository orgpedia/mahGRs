Government Resolution: The 75th Independence Day was celebrated on 15th August, 2021, the date of India's Independence. On the occasion of the 75th year of Independence, the Central Government has organized various activities in the country and the state to mark the Amrit Mahotsav of" Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav" and the decision is being taken to increase the coverage of these activities by 15th August, 2023.
The programmes and activities of Amrit Mahotsav are based on the following five important themes:
India's Freedom Struggle: In July 1945, the Indian Civil Service was established to rehabilitate the service personnel who had been demobilized due to the Second World War. After the partition of India in 1947, the rehabilitation of the people displaced due to partition was done by the Seva Yojana Office. of the World Trade Organization in 1948. Article 2 of C-88 provides for the establishment of a system of employment throughout the world. In 1956, as per the report submitted by B. Shivrao Committee to the Central Government, the offices of the Service Scheme started doing the work of business guidance and counseling and the service scheme system was transferred to the State Government. The Directorate of Technical Education was established in 1948. Technical education schools train youth for employment and self-employment by providing them with various vocational training such as craftsmen, tannery trained artisans, technical skilled artisans.
