Introduction: In 1986, the Maharashtra State Board of Vocational Training (presently known as the Maharashtra State Board of Vocational Training), under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training, was established to cater to the needs of vocational education for the students who have dropped out of secondary education due to the rise in industrialization, changing technology, social and economic changes and to provide short-term vocational courses for students pursuing special education (add-on courses) to meet the requirement of vocational education to promote employment and self-employment.

Maharashtra State Skill Development Board was established. Business oriented courses are run by this board to achieve the goal of employment and self-employment. Due to the increase in industrialization, changing technology, social and economic changes, etc., the courses of the Board are being improved and increased in accordance with the time and the Board is conducting such courses in the form of part-time and full-time courses of 6 months, 1 year, and 2 years duration.
In order to expand the scope of Maharashtra State Skill Development Board, it was approved to implement Tailor Made courses with various reputed institutions vide letter No. 4. The courses offered by the registered institutes/ managements are approved by the Boards to offer innovative, industry-relevant, skill-compliant, business-oriented tailor-made courses. Terms and Conditions:
The institutions implementing the courses of Maharashtra State Skill Development Board will have to comply with the order issued from time to time by the Government, Directorate of Vocational Education and Training, Maharashtra State Skill Development Board and the concerned offices under the Directorate.
The institutions which have been granted provisional approval to run the courses of the Board are required to complete all the infrastructure facilities required for the courses such as weapons, machinery, equipment and furniture, teachers of prescribed educational qualifications, appointment of non-teaching staff, placements etc. within one month.
3. The institutions which are fully equipped with basic facilities and other aspects shall be required to submit the application in the prescribed format to the District Vocational Education and Training Officer of the concerned district within a period of 1 month from the date of issue of the order of recognition.
4. The District Vocational Education and Training Officer shall inspect/ examine the institutions whose applications for completion of infrastructure are received within the prescribed time limit by the District Level Committee.
Institutions which have been approved to run courses of Maharashtra State Skill Development Board. Students will be admitted in the same place and the same place will be considered for approval, if there is a change in the place, the approval will be cancelled.

6. Approval to the institutions for running the courses is based on the total area in the site document submitted by the institution and the available space in the map. However, during the course infrastructure inspection, the space required for that course should be at least 200 sq ft for theory. The students will not be allowed to take admission in courses in 1 class room and 1 or maximum of 2 rooms for breakfast.
Only those institutions which fully meet the prescribed infrastructure and other requirements will be granted approval for admission of students by the Maharashtra State Skill Development Board.
No future grants or financial assistance will be provided even if the commencement of new/ more batches of approved courses is approved on a self-financing basis. Also, the students admitted for this course will not be allowed the benefit of Government of India Post-Matric Scholarship, as well as tuition fees/ examination fees.
9. The institutions which have been granted recognition by the Board for running the courses shall not admit students for the course unless the approval for admission is obtained from the Board. If a student enters the institution without the approval of the institution to admit him, or if he enters more than the approval, then such admission will not be accepted. The Maharashtra Prohibition of Unauthorised Establishment of Unauthorised Institutions and Courses of Unauthorised Study in Agriculture, Animal and Fishery Sciences, Health Sciences, Higher Technical and Vocational Education in the State of Maharashtra (Prohibition) Act, 2013 shall apply to the Institute.
Since the courses have been approved on a self-financing basis, the responsibility for the service/ salary and related matters of all the staff appointed by the Institute for this course shall be the sole responsibility of the Institute. The government will not be responsible for this.
Provisional approval has been granted as per the records in the application/ proposal received from the concerned institutions, in case any discrepancy/ illegality is found in it in future, the recognition of the course in the concerned institution will be cancelled immediately and legal action will be taken against such erring institutions.
If the Government decides to discontinue or make any changes in these courses in the future, it shall be required to be implemented by the Institute without any delay.
