The Proposal:
Presentation of State Budget 2019-20 on 18th June, 2019 The Finance Minister said," Class X and XII examinations are a turning point in the lives of young people and if young people fail at this stage, then there are obstacles in the way of the future. The Government intends to provide employability to such youth by providing them employable skill development training to build their confidence and ability to stand on their own feet. " He announced.
Skill Setu is a new scheme proposed by the Department of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship to provide skill development training to build confidence among the youth who fail in class 10th and 12th. As per the decision taken in the meeting dated 26/09/2019 of the Empowered Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary, State of Maharashtra, the matter of approval to implement the said scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: The Class 10 and Class 12 examinations are a major turning point in the lives of young people, and if young people fail at this stage, they create obstacles in the path of the future. Such youth should have confidence and stand on their own feet

Under this decision, the government is approving the implementation of the" Skill Setu Programme Scheme for Students who have not passed Class X and XII" to make these students employable by providing them with employable skill development training to build employability.
Objective of the scheme
As per the decision of the Department of School Education and Sports, dated 27th September, 2018, students who could not pass in three or more subjects in Class 10 examination have been declared" eligible for skill development" without fail on the mark sheet. Hence, students should be given the benefit of this skill training programme.
On the same lines, while presenting the State Budget for the year 2019-20 on 18th June, 2019, Hon. In pursuance of the announcement made by the Finance Minister, a separate proposal is being submitted to the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education to declare students who could not pass in three or more subjects in Class 12 as well as in Class 10 as" eligible for skill development" without giving a negative marking on the mark sheet. This will also provide the benefit of skill education program to such students of class 12.
To impart skill training to unskilled manpower and upskilling of existing skilled manpower for career progression.
To provide skill development training to build confidence among the youth who fail in class 10th and 12th to make them capable of standing on their own feet.
To provide opportunities for employment/ self-employment by providing employable skill development training to the students who fail in class 10th and 12th.
Implementation and control of the program
The changing national and international scenario in terms of employment has led the state government to work on the lines of the central government to further promote skill development and entrepreneurship. Separate Skill Development and Entrepreneurship Department has been established on 15/01/2015. Maharashtra State Skill Development Society has been registered on 15th February, 2011 under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 under the Skill Development and Entrepreneurship Department.
Various skill development schemes of the central and state governments are implemented by various departments of the state government. The result should be uniformity and the control, planning and effectiveness of national and state-level policies on skill development, employment and entrepreneurship.

In this regard, it has been decided to implement all the schemes related to skill development in the state through the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society. The institute is functioning as the" nodal agency" for the skill development scheme. The State Skill and Entrepreneurship Department has launched the" Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission (PMKUVA)" under the Government decision dated 02.09.2015 to implement skill development programmes in the State.
The scheme to provide employable skill development training to students who fail in Class 10 and 12 will be implemented through the Maharashtra State Skill Development Society and the Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training (MSCVT).
Beneficiaries of the scheme
Under this scheme, students who have not passed in three or more subjects in the Class 10 and 12 examinations conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education from the academic year 2018-19 will be the beneficiaries of this scheme.
Eligibility and Qualification of Candidates
The eligibility and eligibility criteria for the candidates will be as follows:
Educational Qualification: The candidate must have passed Class 10 or Class 12.
The candidate must be a resident of Maharashtra.
Proposed Districts: All districts of Maharashtra Skill Training (Area& Curriculum)
The areas of skill training and courses will be as follows.
For class 10th, it will be necessary to complete the NSQF affiliated courses Level-1 and Level-2.
For class 12th, it will be necessary to complete the NSQF affiliated courses Level-3 and Level-4.
Selection of training institutes
The selection of training institutes for this scheme will be done in the following manner.
Must be registered with Maharashtra State Skill Development Society. Also, the training institute which is not listed under Maharashtra State Skill Development Society will have to provide VTI Id or S.M.A.R.T (Skill Management and Accreditation).

Course structure and training.
The structure of the training and the methodology of training under this scheme will be as follows.
The training will be imparted through Maharashtra State Skill Development Society (MSSDS) and Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training (MSCVT) accredited institutes.
ii) After the training institute uploads the list of trainees for a batch for training, the unique Training Batch Number for that batch will be given by Maharashtra State Skill Development Society through" Mahasvayam" portal.
ii) After receiving the Training Batch Number through the" Mahasvayam" portal, it will be mandatory to notify the schedule of the training program to the concerned training institution within seven days.
iv) Every training institution shall be required to make available the daily biometric attendance report of the beneficiaries and trainers undergoing training to the stakeholders through the" Mahasvayam" portal.
(v) The training institute shall be required to complete the batch of training within the prescribed period.
vi) The officers of the Skill Development and Entrepreneurship Department will make surprise visits to the training institutes during the training period to verify the biometrics and physical attendance of the trainees and other related reports.

Evaluation and Certification
After the completion of the training, candidates will be eligible for evaluation if they have at least 75% attendance in the training class. After the successful completion of the course, candidates will be awarded a certificate from SSC/ MSCVT as per the course chosen by them.
The objective of the scheme is to reimburse the training cost and to instill confidence and self-reliance in the youth. As such, the failed students who have completed the skill training will have a big contribution in the future.
