The standard operating procedure in Form A of the said Government Decision, under Article No. 10 (10) of the" System of Quality and Improvement of the Institution," provides as follows: 10.1 In order to maintain the quality and standard of the business courses approved by the Government, the training quality of the students admitted regularly from the Directorate level will be monitored physically every year. If the result of an institution is less than 75 percent in 3 consecutive years, necessary action will be taken to cancel the recognition of the institution.
10.2.Any institution whose admission to a course approved by the Government is less than 50% of the sanctioned intake for 3 consecutive years will be automatically de-recognized.
The number of students taking admission for two-way business courses in the non-government aided institutions teaching business courses in the state fell below 50 percent of the intake capacity due to the outbreak of Corona in the academic year 2021. Government's decision:
The online standard operating procedure for granting permission to start business courses on permanent basis on non-subsidized basis to new institutions and more batches of Pre-Vocational Courses,+ 2 level Vocational and Higher Secondary Business Courses has been notified vide Notification No.
(1) It is hereby approved by the Government. In the said Government Decision, it is being approved to relax the provisions of Article No. 10.2 of the Standard Operating Procedure in Form A for the institutions offering professional courses for a period of one year from 2021-2022. Considering the low intake of the student during the Covid period, the process of de-recognition should not be done in the year 2021-22 and if done, it should be cancelled.
Institutions with less than 50% of the intake capacity in 2022-23 will be considered for admission under reference no. 1 Action should be taken according to the conditions given in the government decision here. In such a case, the institution should be taken into account if the admission in the academic year 2018-19 and 2019-20 is less than 50 percent.
The institute will be obliged to implement the courses as per the National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF) in the subject of business courses if the government makes changes in the courses.
