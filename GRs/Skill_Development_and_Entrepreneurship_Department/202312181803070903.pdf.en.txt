Pravin Darekar, Member of Legislative Council, Shri Prasad Lad, in pursuance of rule 101 of the Maharashtra Legislative Council, issued a notice vide No. 376 dated 14.12.2023 on the suggestion that" some tribal persons in the State have converted to Christianity/ Islam by renouncing their religion, despite having converted to other religions, these tribal persons are taking all the benefits and reservations through the Tribal Development Department, some days ago, a march of the tribal community was held in Mumbai Metropolitan City on the occasion of Constitution Day, in which it was demanded that persons and communities converting to other religions should be removed from the list of Scheduled Tribes and the benefits of reservation, many such bogus tribal students were found to have taken admission in ITIs of the Skill Department, the action to be taken by the Government against such bogus tribal students and the response of the Government." The Minister (Skill, Employment, Entrepreneurship and Innovation) presented the statement. During the discussion in the Legislative Assembly. The Minister (K.R.O. He announced the formation of a committee under the chairmanship of the former Vice-Chancellor to determine the scope of the committee. The committee will be headed by former VC Dr Muralidhar Chandekar.
Mrs. Pratibha Dharam Singh Masram, Taj Nagar, Manewada Road, Nagpur and Advocate Kiran Bhagwan Gabhale, Saipark Dighi, Pune have been appointed as non-official members in the committee. The Director, Directorate of Vocational Education and Training will be the Member Secretary of the Committee. Orders will be issued separately regarding the inclusion of other members in this committee.
The functions of the Committee are as follows:
1) To verify if the tribal trainees who are original cadre tribals but converted thereafter have taken admission in the seats reserved for tribal students in the Industrial Training Institutes of the State. Also, how many cases are there in the state?
2) What measures should be taken to ensure that non-tribals or those who have converted will not get admission in the seats reserved for tribal students in the Industrial Training Institutes of the state? In this regard, the Constitution, Fundamental Rights and various notifications of the Central Government. The court has to take all these decisions into consideration and suggest measures to the government.
3) To suggest measures to the Government on how to propagate tribal culture, customs, traditions, customs, tribal dress etc. in tribal areas and industrial training institutes in tribal areas, even if the tribal trainees have converted to other religions, so that tribal culture can be protected and preserved.
