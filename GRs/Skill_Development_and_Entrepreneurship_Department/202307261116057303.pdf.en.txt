Introduction: Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya Job Fairs are organized to provide employment/ self-employment to the educated unemployed in order to effectively implement the government's skill, employment, entrepreneurship and innovation policy. Under reference As per the Government decision dated 7 th November, 2022, the expenditure for conducting these melas has been revised and the limit of expenditure for conducting offline job melas at Revenue Department and District level has been fixed at Rs. 5.00 lakh per mela. Out of this, an amount of Rs. 1.00 lakh has been mentioned in the said Government Decision to be spent for the promotion and publicity of the Mela. However, it has been observed that the subject-wise details of the remaining amount of Rs. 4.00 lakh have not been mentioned in the said Government decision, leading to confusion in all the field offices.
The Minister of Skill, Employment, Entrepreneurship and Innovation has directed to sign at least 1000 agreements with nominated industries, industry associations, placement agencies, etc. to provide employment in all revenue divisions of the state. Accordingly, Industry Meetings are proposed to be organized in all the departments. Funds need to be allocated for this. Funds available for Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Job Fairs as per requirement. It was under consideration of the Government to allow the State's industries to be used for fairs within the limits prescribed under the Government Decision dated 7 th November, 2022 and also to fix a subject-wise ceiling of Rs. 5.00 lakh per fair as fixed under the said Government Decision.

1. Funds available for Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya Job Fairs as per requirement. It is hereby permitted to be used for Industry Meets in the State within the limits prescribed under the Government Decision dated 07.11.2022.

3. The item-wise provision of expenditure in the aforesaid statement is indicative and the cost-saving mechanism should be adopted as per the policy of the Government.
In exceptional cases, the expenditure limit on the items mentioned in the above statement may be revised by the Deputy Commissioner at the level of Revenue Division and the Assistant Commissioner at the district level with the approval of the Commissioner, Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship. However, care should be taken that the total expenditure does not exceed₹ 5.00 lakh per mela.
The students mentioned in the Roll Number (8) of the above prospectus will be required to do the following:
1) Notification of vacancies 15 days before the meeting. Communicating with employers, factories, traders as required. Also promoting and publicizing the event.
2) To manage the fair on the day of the fair and to assist the employees of the concerned offices.
3) To help the staff of the concerned offices to update the information of the candidates engaged in the job fair on the portal for the next few days after the fair.
The above-mentioned works are of a minor nature and the offices at the district/ divisional level can make changes in them as per the need and assign additional works. However,
