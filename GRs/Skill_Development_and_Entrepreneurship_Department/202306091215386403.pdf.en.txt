Preamble: The Department of Skill, Employment, Entrepreneurship and Innovation has been implementing various skill training and innovation schemes since 2015 to achieve the goal of" Skill Maharashtra, RozgarYukta Maharashtra." For this purpose, the department organizes professional training required for the industrial sector through Industrial Training Institutes in the state, as well as short and long-term skill development training programs, guidance on employment and self-employment, employment fairs, startup yatras, Startup Week, Grand Challenge, etc. Experts for the implementation of the program
There is a need to strengthen human resources and modern infrastructure. In view of this fact, the then Hon'ble Chief Minister on 03.12.2021 had directed to provide financial assistance for the implementation of vocational education and skill development schemes and strengthening of necessary infrastructure in the State. After detailed discussions between the World Bank and the concerned senior government officials, the World Bank and the Government of Maharashtra decided to jointly implement the Development of Applied Knowledge and Skill for Human Development (DAKSH) project.
The preliminary project report of the project has been submitted to the Department of Economic Affairs (DEA), Government of India through the Finance Department. It was submitted for approval on January 10, 2023. Accordingly, the Scrutiny Committee of the Department of Economic Affairs (DEA), Government of India, in its meeting held on 27.02.2023, has given its approval.
Accordingly, the matter of granting in-principle approval for Development of Applied Knowledge and Skill for Human Development (DAKSH) and setting up of" Project Monitoring Committee,"" Project Implementation Agency" for planning, implementation, coordination, control and monitoring of the project was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: In accordance with the approval given by the Department of Finance, Department of Economic Affairs, Government of India," in-principle approval" is being given to implement the World Bank-funded Development of Applied Knowledge and Skill for Human Development (DAKSH) in the state.
