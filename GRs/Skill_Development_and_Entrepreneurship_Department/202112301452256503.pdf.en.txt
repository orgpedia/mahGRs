The Proposal:
The online standard operating procedure for allowing new institutions and more batches of Pre-Vocational Courses,+ 2 level Vocational Courses and Higher Secondary Vocational Courses to start Business Courses on permanent non-subsidized basis has been notified vide Department Reference No. The decision was taken on May 10, 2018.
The procedure for recognition of Pre-Vocational Courses and Vocational Courses at+ 2 level has been notified vide Reference No. The approval was granted vide letter dated 12th June, 2020.
The institutions which had applied for recognition on the government's web portal vti.dvet.gov.in and completed all the facilities required for business during the stipulated time for granting recognition on a permanent non-grant basis to new institutions and more batches of Pre-Vocational Courses,+ 2 Level Vocational Courses for the year 2021-22.

The monitoring programme of the requested institutions has been completed by the committees appointed by the Government Industrial Training Institutes. The Oversight Committees have uploaded their inspection reports on the web portal vti. dvet. gov. in. The reports of the selected Vocational Courses Institutions, which have been examined and recommended for approval by the Monitoring Committees, are available on the Directorate's login. Institutions which have not been recommended by the Oversight Committee can apply for re-evaluation for the next session.
Also the. As per the letter dated 11th August, 2021, for the academic year 2021-2022, new batches of Pre-Vocational Courses,+ 2 level Vocational Courses and more will be allowed to start Business Courses on permanent basis on non-subsidized basis. The deadline has been extended to August 31, 2021. Accordingly, the application received is being scrutinized at the Directorate level and a separate government decision will be issued after receiving a recommendation for approval from the Directorate.
The application received for the optional business course component at+ 2 level will be scrutinized at the Directorate level and the proposal along with the appropriate recommendation from the Directorate will be sent to Reference No. 3, 4, 5, 6 and 7. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to grant approval to start business courses on permanent non-subsidized basis to new institutions and more batches of vocational courses at+ 2 level from the year 2021-22.
Government's decision:
After scrutinizing the documents received through e-mail from the educational institutions, the Directorate has recommended that the institutions fulfilling the required standards should be approved to implement the+ 2 level vocational courses, the details of which have been attached in the accompanying Form A. These institutions are being approved to start new/ additional batches of vocational courses at+ 2 level in existing as well as new institutions from the year 2021-22 on permanent non-subsidized basis subject to the following terms and conditions:
Terms and Conditions:
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill all the terms and conditions prescribed in the Secondary School Code 1981. After obtaining the permission, the institution will be required to maintain all the accounts required as per the Secondary School Certificate and MEPS Rules 1981 at the institution level. If the institution does not comply with the rules of secondary school education, or

The recognition of the institute will be cancelled immediately if it does not comply with the order and circular issued by the government.
(ii) No grant or financial assistance shall be provided in future even if approval is granted on permanent non-grant basis to start new/ additional batches of approved courses.
(3) The institutions implementing the approved courses shall be bound to comply with the orders issued from time to time by the concerned offices under the Government/ Directorate/ Directorate.
4) It will be mandatory for the institution to charge the tuition fee from the students as determined by the government from time to time. Students cannot be charged more than the prescribed fee.
5) It will be mandatory for the institutions to put up a signboard on the facade saying" Bilingual Business Courses at+ 2 level approved on permanent non-grant basis." The number of sanctioned batches of courses and the sanctioned intake capacity should be mentioned on the notice board.
(6) Since the said courses have been approved on permanent non-grant basis, the respective institution will be fully responsible for the service/ salary and related matters of all the staff employed by the institution for this course. Government is not responsible for this.
The appointment of teaching staff required for the approved course, their minimum educational qualification and experience should be done immediately by the institute in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Private Schools Employees (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1981, as well as orders issued by the Government from time to time. The action taken report should be submitted to the Directorate through the Regional Office.
8) If the government takes a policy decision to discontinue or make any changes in the course in the future, it will be necessary to implement it by the institution without any delay.
9) For admission to the approved batches of courses, the institution should strictly follow the social/ parallel reservation and other rules issued by the government from time to time and admissions should be made in order of merit.
10) The institution should immediately inform the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education that the course has been started in the institution after the approval of the courses and take the corresponding action.
The intake capacity for new/ additional batches of the approved course is 50 per batch, if the intake is less than 50% of the approved intake capacity.

Permission of the concerned District Vocational Education and Training Officer will be required to continue the batches.
The recognition of a recognized course shall automatically lapse if the institution does not start the course in the academic year in which it is approved or if the intake of the batch of the course is less than 50% of the sanctioned intake for 3 consecutive years. After this, the institution will have to renew the approval process to start the course.
13) In order to maintain the quality and standard of the business courses approved by the government, the training quality of the students admitted regularly from the directorate level will be directly monitored by the institution with less than 75% results every year. If the result of an institution is less than 75% in 3 consecutive years, necessary action will be taken to cancel the recognition of the institution.
The Government shall have the right to modify the above terms and conditions or to impose new terms and conditions to improve the quality of education.
15) The institution concerned should admit the students only after ensuring that the teachers in the subject of language/ biology, etc. are not overstretched due to the sanctioned batch of bilingual course.
16) Unauthorized admission shall not be made in excess of the sanctioned intake as per the sanctioned batch of the approved business course under the Business Course.
