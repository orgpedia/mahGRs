The central government has issued revised guidelines to increase the participation of the state government in this scheme. Accordingly, special projects of the states and centrally sponsored state managed component activities are to be implemented by the state governments.

State specific projects under the scheme Under this initiative, the criteria for formulating innovative, creative and practical projects for imparting skill training to the underserved sections of the society with a view to mainstreaming the vulnerable, disadvantaged and marginalized sections of the society into the economy of the country have been set by the Central Government.
A target of 4,89,152 candidates has been set for fresh training under the Centrally Sponsored State Managed Component (CSMM) initiative. Out of 4,89,152 targets set for fresh training, 25% i. e. 1,67,127 candidates have been targeted for skill training in Maharashtra.
In the guidelines of the Central Government, provision has been made to provide training in 1) job role courses at the central level in addition to the 1222 job roles prescribed by the Sector Skill Council (SSC), 2) job role courses at the state level in addition to the 1222 job roles prescribed by the SSC, 3) job roles and courses related to traditional professions, 4) 221 job role courses at the central level in addition to the job roles prescribed by the Sector Skill Council (SSC).

It will be mandatory to implement the activities under this scheme as per the existing guidelines of the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship of the Central Government or as amended by the Central Government from time to time and within the prescribed time. No new infrastructure should be created for implementation of activities, projects should be implemented by utilising available infrastructure/ training facilities.
The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India has issued a Common Guidance Note through a contextual notification for implementing skill development programmes at the country level. The Implementing Mechanism shall be required to spend the same on the activities/ projects for which funds have been received from the Central Government as per the provisions of the Common Guidelines or as amended by the Central Government from time to time.
