Government Corrigendum: Seats available under PPP scheme in Government Industrial Training Institutes as well as private
The Government has decided to introduce Vocational Training Fee Reimbursement Scheme to provide the benefit of training fee reimbursement to the students belonging to economically weaker sections belonging to socially and educationally backward and open category who take admission through Central Online System for courses under Craftsmen Training Scheme from Industrial Training Institutes from the admission session 2019-20. It was approved on May 29, 2019.
The Supreme Court in Civil Appeal No. 3123/ 2020 (SLP (C) No. 15737/2019 dated 05.05.2021, the final order invalidating the Socially and Educationally Backward (SEBC) reservation does not apply to the candidates belonging to the Socially and Educationally Backward (SEBC) category. Hence, these candidates are eligible for the post. Therefore, if the candidates fulfill the eligibility criteria for the benefit of reservation of Economically Weaker Section in the open category as per the Government Decision dated February 12, 2019 and Government Decision dated May 31, 2021, then the benefit of 10% reservation for Economically Weaker Section is admissible to them. Following this, the government took decision. The amendment was considered by the government on May 29, 2019.

The training fee reimbursement will be provided to the Socially and Educationally Backward (SEBC) students admitted in the academic year 2019-20 and 2020-21.
The Socially and Educationally Backward (SEBC) students admitted in the year 2021-22 will be given the benefits of the Economically Weaker Section (EWS) category as mentioned in the General Administration Department Government Decision dated February 12, 2019 and the Government Decision dated May 31, 2021. For this, it will be mandatory to submit a certificate in the prescribed form given by the competent authority in case of falling into the economically weaker section.
All open category students belonging to Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) who are taking admission from 2021-2022 will be required to submit a certificate in the prescribed format issued by the competent authority regarding their belonging to the Economically Weaker Section.
