The Proposal:
As per the decision of the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance dated 09.01.2006, approval has been accorded for setting up of six Zonal Level Higher Industrial Training Institutes for the children of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhists. Of these, four Industrial Training Institutes of Nagpur, Amravati, Nashik and Aurangabad have been completed and given administrative approval and necessary designations have been made for them.
A High Level Industrial Training Institute for the children of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhists will be set up in Chembur, Mumbai Suburban District, in collaboration with the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. There is a small number of government higher level industrial training institutes in the Mumbai division for the children of scheduled castes and neo-Buddhists. The institute will help the candidates in getting job and business opportunities.
To provide skilled manpower required for industrialization, it was under consideration of the government to start an Industrial Training Institute for Scheduled Castes in Chembur, a suburb of Mumbai.
Government's decision:
Approval is being given to start a High Level Industrial Training Institute for the children of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhists at Chembur, Mumbai Suburban with the help of Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. The expenditure incurred on the construction of building for the Industrial Training Institute, salaries of the staff, stipend for the trainees of the Institute, office expenses for administrative work and recurring and non-recurring expenditure for the machinery and equipment is covered under the scheme of Social Justice and Empowerment.
