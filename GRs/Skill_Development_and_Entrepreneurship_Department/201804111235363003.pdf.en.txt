Read:- Government Circular No: Finance-2018/ No.69/ Finance-3 dated 02.04.2018.
The Proposal:
The Department of Finance vide the said Circular, published as" Maharashtra Act No. 22" in the Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part IV, dated 27th March, 2018, has directed that the budgetary allocation of mandatory and programme expenditure required for the first nine months of 2018-19 (ending December, 2018) shall be released subject to the terms and conditions of the circular on Controlling Expenditure (BEAMS) within the limit of 70% of the annual provision. Also, the MLAs will disburse 100% of the provisions for local development programmes, agriculture-related programmes and state share under centrally sponsored programmes. Action is required to be taken in accordance with No. 7 (6). Accordingly, for the year 2018-19, it was under the consideration of the Government to allocate the budgetary funds for the first nine months (April, 2018 to end December, 2018) to the concerned Controlling Authorities under the Department of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship on the Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) to meet the expenditure on A-Revenue Account and expenditure under C-Debt Head.
Government's decision:
For the first nine months (April, 2018 to April, 2018), the total annual budget allocation for the financial year 2018-19 is limited to 70% of the total budget outlay as per the directions in the Circular to the Government under reference of the Department of Finance.

As per the instructions in the Government Circular in the context of the Department of Finance, the funds to be allocated on the Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) for the first nine months of the financial year 2018-19 within the limit of 70% of the budgetary allocation are being disbursed subject to the following terms and conditions:
(1) No expenditure shall be incurred for purposes other than those for which the fund/ grant is sanctioned.
Also, it cannot be used under other headings.
(2) The progress report of the said expenditure shall be submitted to the Government by the Controlling Officer by 10th of every month as per the monthly fund statement.
(3) When the MLA distributes 100% of the provisions for local development programmes, agricultural programmes and the State share under the Centrally Sponsored Programme, the circular under reference to the Department of Finance shall be superseded by a circular.
Action will be required to be taken in accordance with No 7 (6). Accordingly, the funds will be disbursed on the Budget Distribution System (BEAMS).
(4) While disbursing the non-salary grant, the Finance Department, Government Decision No.
The grant should be made with the approval of the competent authority as per the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-Division I, No. 6, Rule 40, provided under Regulation 30/2013/ Part-II, dated 17th April, 2015.

(7) When making provisions for the distribution of which an administrative sanction order is required, the grant shall be distributed after the approval of the competent authority as provided under Part-I, Sub-Division-III, A. No. 4, Rule 27 (2).
(8) The Department/ Commissioner's Office/ Directorate should obtain Utilization Certificates of the previous grants from various Government/ Administrative Institutions as well as the grantee institutions before sanctioning the grants-in-aid. No further grant should be released until the utilization of the earlier grant is completed.
(9) Subsidies of individual beneficiaries shall not be withdrawn unless the list of beneficiaries is determined and attached to the payment with proper particulars while drawing the scheme dues of individual beneficiaries.
(10) Scholarships, reimbursement of tuition fees, payments to individual beneficiaries should be made through Electronic Clearance System (ECS) only.
(11) No expenditure shall be incurred in respect of a construction proposal without obtaining administrative approval/ technical approval as well as approval of the Committee of Secretaries, where necessary.
