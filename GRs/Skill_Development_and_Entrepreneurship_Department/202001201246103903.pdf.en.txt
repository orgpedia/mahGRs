The Proposal:
Under Skill Development and Entrepreneurship Department, 417 Government Industrial Training Institutes are functioning under Craftsman Training Scheme, 8 institutions under Advanced Vocational Training Scheme, 1 institution under Hi-Tech Training Scheme, 2 institutions under Mechanization Team. Under the Craftsman Training Scheme, 417 Government Industrial Training Institutes train approximately more than 2 lakh apprentices in 102 different vocational courses. Under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training, 10,133 employees are working under the schemes of Craftsmen Training Scheme, Apprenticeship Scheme, Advanced Training Scheme, Hi-tech Training Scheme, Mechanization Team.
In the government industrial training institutes, there are employees like principal, vice-principal, inspector, manager, assistant trainee consultant. It is a continuous process of training the staff and officers in new methods and procedures. Keeping this in mind, the government was considering to take up a programme to train the officers and staff to increase the quality of training and to create momentum in administrative work by improving the efficiency and productivity of the available manpower.
Government Decision: In view of the above, the training programme for officers and staff in Industrial Training Institutes is being approved subject to the following terms and conditions:
Under this scheme, priority should be given to the staff and teaching staff of the Government Industrial Training Institute.
These training programmes should be conducted at the district/ divisional level and at all the facilities available.
The training programme should include up-to-date knowledge of relevant business curriculum, use of modern audio-visual media, instructional materials, new teaching methods/ technologies, psychological aspects as well as methods of handling trainees, etc.
The training to be imparted to the officers should cover aspects such as leadership development, administrative and financial matters, student welfare, good governance and managerial skills.
Reduce the number of accidents by providing safety training to officers/ staff.
The officers/ staff should be trained to handle the latest machinery and equipment efficiently.
The officers/ employees should be covered under Government Rules, Right to Information Act, 2005, Court cases, Financial, Warehousing Management, Establishment matters, Administrative matters, as well as identification of advanced technologies and training of skills. It should also include counseling, positive attitude, evaluation process, interaction with private industrial establishments, general skill entrepreneurship, apprenticeship training, employment and self-employment. Induction and service training should be included. The duration of the training program should be determined in accordance with the requirements.
