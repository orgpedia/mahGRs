The Proposal:
The" Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission" is implemented under the Government Decision dated 02/09/2015 of the Department of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship to empower the youth in the working age group of 15 to 45 years in the state through skill development, up-skilling and re-skilling and provide them employment/ self-employment opportunities in industries, services and similar sectors where there is more demand. The campaign is multi-pronged and comprehensive. Inclusion of vocational education in school education, giving priority to skill training to harassed/ abused women/ girls, amendments/ changes in accordance with the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 and special projects were under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The infrastructure/ training facilities available under the new projects in the state will be used for skill and entrepreneurship development training programs, through which more and more youth of the state will be benefited.

Government Decision No. KOUVIU-2018/ No.1/ Mission-1 approves implementation of the following 03" Special Projects" under Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission for skilling and providing maximum opportunities for employment/ self-employment.
Financial assistance will be provided to Industrial Associations/ Corporate Industries who are willing to undertake large scale skill development and entrepreneurship training programmes using the infrastructure/ training facilities available with them under this special project.
(i) The Industrial Association/ Corporate Industry must have been in operation for a period of at least 3 years.
ii) The industrial association should be at the divisional or state level and the number of active units should be at least 50.
The training programme shall be for a minimum period of 5 years and the minimum target for skill training under the said training programme shall be 10,000 candidates.
iv) The project cost shall be admissible within the limit of 70% of the training cost per candidate permissible under the Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission for the training programme.
The total cost of the project shall not be less than Rs. 50.00 crore.
vi) Financial assistance to the extent of 30% of the project cost will be provided for the purchase of the required machinery and equipment as per the standard for the training programme. It will be mandatory to start physical training within a period of 6 months after the assistance.
(vii) The balance amount of the project cost will be paid to the concerned Industrial Association/ Corporate Industries as follows:

In order to effectively implement the Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission in the State, the following amendments/ changes are being made in the working of the Mission.
Priority to skill and entrepreneurship development training programmes for oppressed/ abused women/ girls- It is necessary to provide opportunities for skill training to all sections of the society. For this, the women and girls who have filed a complaint with the Women's Commission against the atrocities committed on them or the Women's Commission has taken cognizance of it on its own, will be given priority in the skill and entrepreneurship development training program under the Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Mission.
Implementation of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016- In accordance with the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, 5% of the funds available for the Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission will be used for skill and entrepreneurship development training of persons with disabilities. Out of this 5% fund, 1% will be used for imparting skill training to the differently abled persons to make their daily life easier such as through Bell script to the blind persons and through sign language to the deaf persons.
3) Inclusion of Business Education in School Education In order to motivate more and more students at school level to take up skill subjects, Paragraph No. 9.5 of the Government Decision dated 02/09/2015 of the Department regarding Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Development Mission has mentioned the inclusion of Business Education in School Education. Around 1500 secondary schools in the state teach V1, V2, V3, and V4 business subjects to students of classes 9 and 10. From the academic year 2018-19, the written examination of 30 marks of these subjects will be conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education, Pune and the skill test of 70 marks will be conducted by the Maharashtra State Council of Vocational Training (MSCVT). The remaining skill test fee will be paid from the Pramod Mahajan Skill and Entrepreneurship Mission after deducting the skill test fee received from the Department of School Education from the fee fixed by the Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training.
