Preamble: The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India has decided to implement the second phase of Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (100% Centrally Sponsored, State Managed-PMKVY 2.0-CSSM) from 2016-18 to 2019-20 in the State.

The decision has been approved by the state government. Under this scheme, a total of 1 crore youth have been targeted for training from 2016-17 to 2019-20. The Central Government has issued revised guidelines to increase the participation of the State Government in this scheme.
The second phase of the scheme was scheduled from 2016-18 to 2019-20. However, the scheme ended in 2020. 9 Under this letter, the scheme has been approved to be continued and implemented in the financial year 2020-21.
Under the" Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (100% Centrally Sponsored Scheme)" programme, the State Government has received Rs. 85,77,62, 615/-for the year 2017-18 from the Centre through Public Financial Management System (PFMS) for implementation of Special Projects and Centrally Sponsored State Managed Component activities.

The Commissioner, Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship shall draw the funds allocated for the scheme by the State Government from the treasury and make the same available for expenditure to the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society. The Maharashtra State Skill Development Agency, the nodal agency for implementing the skill development programme in the state, will be the implementing agency for the projects under the scheme. For this, Commissioner, Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship, Navi Mumbai is being declared as the" Controlling Officer" and Assistant Director (Accounts), Konkan Bhawan is being declared as the" Drawing and Disbursing Officer."

It will be mandatory to implement the activities under this scheme as per the existing guidelines of the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship of the Central Government or as amended by the Central Government from time to time and within the prescribed time. No new infrastructure should be created for implementation of activities, projects should be implemented by utilising available infrastructure/ training facilities.
The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India has issued a Common Guidance Note through a contextual notification for implementing skill development programmes at the country level.
It will be mandatory for the implementing agency to spend as per the provisions of the Common Guidance Note or as amended by the Central Government from time to time and for the activities/ projects for which funds have been received from the Central Government and the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society will be responsible for sending the utilization certificate of the expenditure to the Central Government within the prescribed time limit.
The Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra State Skill Development Society should take care of sending the utilization certificate and other details of the expenditure to the concerned ministry of the central government as well as to the government.
