The Proposal:
As per the New Education Policy of 1986, emphasis was laid on teaching work experience subjects in secondary level education so that students could be prepared for the world of work in future life. The state government has provided useful vocational courses based on skills in the curriculum as an alternative to the work experience subject at the secondary school level for many years.
At present, 169 Government Technical Secondary Schools/ Centres offer pre-exam technical/ vocational courses in secondary schools.
While the trainees are undergoing training from Government Technical Secondary Schools/ Centres, the trainees are given job oriented training to enable the trainees to get employment. It makes it easy for the trainees to self-discovery about their own achievements, to generate activity and for the trainees to gain knowledge about social knowledge and the mode of production. Developing confidence and a sense of responsibility to work collaboratively, either on their own or with the help of others. Under the scheme, the trainees will get hands-on training in the trades of their choice such as weapons and equipment, books as per requirement.
At present, under the Scheduled Castes Component Programme under the Technical Education Development Sector, funds are being made available regularly to provide free weapons and serial books to the trainees of the Scheduled Castes through the District Planning and Development Committee and the expenditure was being incurred accordingly. However, some of the District Collectors may provide administrative approval for the implementation of the scheme to the concerned District Business and Training Officers.

The offices have been notified. In view of the request made by the Directorate, the Government was considering to approve the implementation of the free book bank scheme under the Scheduled Component Programme under the Scheme for Enhancing the Facility of Pre-Examination Technical/ Vocational Education in Secondary Schools under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training.
Government's decision:
The Government has accorded administrative approval for the implementation of the Free Book Bank Scheme under the Scheduled Component Programme under the Scheme for Enhancing the Facility of Pre-Examination Technical/ Vocational Education in Secondary Schools under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training under the Department of Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship.
Guidelines of the scheme: 1. To enhance the facilities of pre-examination technical/ professional education in the secondary schools run by the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training under the Department of Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship.
(2) As the scheme is a district level scheme, the demand for expenditure under head 2203 under the demand programme of the concerned district should be made to the District Planning Committee of the concerned district. Also, the District Vocational Education and Training Officer should ensure that the funds are budgeted and spent as per the prescribed rules every year. The budget for the year 2021-22 has already been sanctioned to all the districts under the Scheduled Castes Programme for District Annual Plans. This year's expenditure should be met from the approved budget.
As the scheme is a district level scheme, the concerned District Vocational Education and Training Officer should make a request to the District Planning Committee as per the guidelines issued by the Planning Department from time to time.
The Joint Director, Vocational Education and Training, Regional Office is being authorized to give technical approval to the books, references and related matters to be drawn from the funds available under this scheme.
The benefit of this scheme should be paid through the direct benefit transfer process as per the existing policy of the government regarding individual benefit schemes.

2) Eligibility Criteria:
1.The beneficiary of this scheme is required to attend his training full time as per the rules.
2. The benefit of this scheme will be given to the youth of the Scheduled Castes only.
The benefit of this scheme will be given to the trainees admitted to the training.
The head of the institution will be responsible for providing direct benefits under this scheme. The District Vocational Education and Training Officer will be responsible for monitoring the scheme.
The benefit of this scheme should be paid through the direct benefit transfer process as per the existing policy of the government regarding individual benefit schemes.
