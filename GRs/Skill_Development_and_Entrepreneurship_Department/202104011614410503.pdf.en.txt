The Proposal:
Craftsmen Training Scheme was launched by the Government of India in 1950 and under this scheme, National Council for Vocational Training was established in 1956 at the national level to maintain uniformity in standards of training across the country. The National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT) formulates the policy on Government Industrial Training Institutes/ Private Industrial Training Centres, their regulation, formulation of various vocational courses, standards for construction, electrical supply, machinery, training of directors, national level testing, issuance of national vocational certificates, etc. The Council is headed by the Hon'ble Minister of Labour, Government of India and comprises representatives of the Central and State Governments, representatives of employers and employees associations and experts on various subjects. The actual implementation of the Craftsmen Training Scheme was initially being done by the Central Government. Since 1969, the state government has been implementing the scheme and spending on the government's industrial training institutes.
Under the Scheduled Castes Component Programme under the development sector of Labour and Workers Welfare (Industrial Training Institute), funds are regularly provided for providing free serial books to the trainees of Scheduled Castes through the District Planning and Development Committee and accordingly expenditure is being incurred on the scheme. However, some of the District Collectors have informed the offices of the District Vocational Education and Training Officers concerned to provide administrative approval for the implementation of the scheme. In this background, the matter of issuing a government decision of administrative approval regarding the Craftsmen Training Scheme was under the consideration of the government in accordance with the request made by the Directorate.
Government's decision:
The budgetary provisions and annual receipts under the Labour and Employment (Industrial Training Institutes) Development Sector under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training under the Department of Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship are required to be spent in the respective financial years.

Administrative approval is being given under this Government decision to implement the scheme of free book bank under the Scheduled Castes Component Programme for the students belonging to Scheduled Castes (including neo-Buddhists) undergoing training under the Craftsmen Training Scheme.
(a) The purpose of the scheme:
The main objectives of the Craftsmen Training Scheme are as follows: 1. To provide skilled manpower required for various activities of the factory 2. To empower the youth to get employment in the factory by providing job oriented training 3. To empower the youth to start self-employment in manufacturing and other service sectors by providing skill training.
To enhance the quality, efficiency and productivity of industrial establishments by providing systematic training to workers.
Guidelines of the scheme: 1. Training of craftsmen being implemented in the State by the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training under the Department of Skill Development, Employment and Entrepreneurship.
The District Vocational Education and Training Officer will be responsible for providing necessary training facilities to the trainees undergoing training in the Government Industrial Training Institute through the scheme.
(2) As the scheme is a district level scheme, the demand for expenditure under head 2225 under the demand programme of the concerned district should be made to the District Planning Committee of the concerned district. Also, the District Vocational Education and Training Officer should ensure that the funds are budgeted every year and spent as per the prescribed rules.
As the scheme is a district level scheme, the concerned District Vocational Education and Training Officer should request for funds from the District Planning Committee as per the guidelines issued by the Planning Department from time to time.
The Joint Director, Vocational Education and Training, Regional Office is being authorized to give technical approval to the books, reference materials and other ancillary items to be purchased from the funds available through this scheme.
3) Eligibility Criteria:
1. The beneficiary trainee who is eligible for this scheme is required to attend his training full time as per the rules.
(2) The benefit of this scheme should be given to the youth of the Scheduled Castes.
The benefit of this scheme will be given to the trainees admitted to the training.
4. The responsibility of providing direct benefit to the eligible beneficiaries under the said scheme shall be that of the concerned industrial concern.
The head of the training institution will be the head.
