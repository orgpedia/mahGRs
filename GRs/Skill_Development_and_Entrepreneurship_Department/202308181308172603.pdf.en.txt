The Proposal:
The quality of vocational education and training in the country needs to be improved. The creation of skilled manpower is essential for industrial value addition and aims to improve the efficiency of imparting skill training through Industrial Training Institutes and Vocational Training. The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship has decided to implement the Skill Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement (STRIVE) project in the country in collaboration with the World Bank. These projects will involve participation of small and medium enterprises, trade associations, Industry Clusters/ Industry Associations (IC/ IA), with their support, in improving the quality of training in Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) and expanding the scope of apprenticeship.
The project is funded by the World Bank under the Scheme for Strengthening Skills for Industrial Value Enhancement (STRIVE). Accordingly, the Central Government has issued the Operations Manual for the implementation of the STRIVE project. Accordingly, the proposal to implement STRIVE project in the State has been approved by the Cabinet in its meeting held on 13.08.2019.
The World Bank assisted project" Skills Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement (STRIVE)" has been approved for implementation in the State in the following four Result Areas as per the decision of the Government in the context (2):
Improving the efficiency of the Industrial Training Institute. (Improved Performance of ITIs), 2. To enhance the capacity of the State for Industrial Training Institutes and Apprenticeship Training.
(Increase Capacities of State Government to support ITIs and Apprenticeship Training), 3. Increase capacity for training and acquisition of knowledge under Industrial Training Institutes and Apprenticeships. (Improved Teaching and Learning Capabilities), 4. To enhance the quality and scope of apprenticeship training scheme. (Improved and Broadened Apprenticeship Training)
Under component number 1, a total of 400 government and 100 private institutions will be covered at the country level in two phases through the central government.
Phase 1: 160 government institutions and 40 private institutions
Phase II: 240 government institutions and 60 private institutions

Component No. In the first phase, a total of 38 government and 4 private offices will be opened in the state.
Administrative approval was granted under Reference No. 03 for implementation of the said project in the institutions. The State Steering Committee has decided to exclude 4 Government OPDs namely Panvel, Guhagar, Khed (Rajgurunagar) and Latur (Girls) from the project.

In the second phase of the project, the Institute Strategic Plan (ISP) of the prescribed format from the Government Industrial Training Institutes was invited online and their evaluation was done at the directorate level. The National Steering Committee in its meeting held on 08.01.2021 approved 38 Government Industrial Training Institutes in the second phase on competitive basis out of the recommended institutes.
