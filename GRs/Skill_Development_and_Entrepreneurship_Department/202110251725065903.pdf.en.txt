Introduction: The Apprenticeship Training Scheme under the Apprenticeship Act, 1961 is being implemented in the State under the control of Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India to impart necessary training to skilled artisans by utilising the infrastructure available in the industrial establishments. The Maharashtra Apprenticeship Act, 2018 has been enacted to amend the Apprenticeship Act, 1961 for the State of Maharashtra to implement the scheme. As per the amendment made in the Act, it is mandatory for the state to locate at least 2.5% to a maximum of 25% apprenticeship seats of the total manpower in an establishment with 30 and above manpower (including contractual employees). The duration of apprenticeship is 6 to 36 months. Trained and fresher candidates are trained under the Apprenticeship Training Scheme.

The scope of the scheme is being expanded. Therefore, the recruitment of apprentice candidates in government, semi-government and private institutions under the Apprentice Training Scheme
In order to incentivize the growth of apprenticeship, the Government has approved the introduction of" Maharashtra Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme" to supplement the Apprenticeship Training Scheme. The scheme aims to train 1 lakh candidates annually.
The issue of providing new accounting head and computer statistics for budgeting the funds for the scheme was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
Maharashtra Apprenticeship Training Scheme (MAATS) is being implemented to promote apprenticeship in government, semi-government and private institutions.
