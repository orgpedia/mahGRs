Introduction: The Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training (MSCVT) under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training, Government of Maharashtra was established vide Reference No. 1 here. It was established on September 25, 1984. The Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training is responsible for conducting short-term vocational courses as well as approving the start of courses in Industrial Training Institutes, evaluating and certifying students after their training is completed, in order to expand the scope of vocational training in the state.
Reference No. 3 here. The Council has been reconstituted vide Government Resolution dated 05.03.2011. Also, the Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training is registered as a Society under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 vide Reference No. 7 here. The decision was made on January 18, 2017.
In 1986, the Directorate of Technical Education was bifurcated into the Directorate of Technical Education (DTE) and the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training (DVET). Maharashtra State Board of Vocational Education (MSBVEE) has been constituted under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training. 2 here. It was established on January 23, 1986.
The main objective of the Board is to start short and long term business courses for the students who have dropped out in the secondary and higher secondary school streams and after the students complete the courses, they are evaluated and given certificates. Also, the main responsibilities of the board are to grant affiliation to private institutions who want to run short and long-term courses, evaluate students and provide certificates.
Reference No. 4 is here. The Board has been reconstituted vide Government Resolution dated 04.10.2011. Reference No. Here are 11. The Board has been renamed as Maharashtra State Skill Development Board vide Government Resolution dated 11.02.2021.
The two institutes under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training have a largely similar functioning pattern. Both the institutes share the same responsibilities of recognition of short term skill courses, skill training, assessment and certification by the institute.

Maharashtra State Council of Vocational Training In the 9th meeting held on 31st May, 2021, a resolution has been passed to merge the said Council with Maharashtra State Skill Development Board.
The Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training Act, 2021 It was released on January 20, 2022. Notification to come into effect from 26th January, 2022. It was released on January 25, 2022.
A proposal to dissolve the Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training by merging the two institutes working for the same objectives under the Directorate was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
At the Cabinet meeting held on December 8, 2021, The Cabinet has approved the merger of Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Training (MSVTC) with the Maharashtra State Council for Vocational Education and Training (MSVTET).
The Director, Directorate of Vocational Education and Training, Maharashtra State, Mumbai in coordination with the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training shall take the following action regarding the merger of Maharashtra State Council of Vocational Training with the Board.
2.1 All the courses under the purview of the Council, Vocational Training Institute (VTI), Skill Assessment Agency (SAA) and Inspecting Agency (IA) shall be brought under the purview of the Board.
2.2.Reference of Section No. 8 here. The prescribed functions of the Council shall be carried out by the Board in accordance with the Government Resolution dated 26th December, 2017.
2.3 All movable and immovable property in the possession of the Council shall be transferred to the Board.
2.4. Transfer the registration fee, inspection fee, reserve amount, etc. in respect of Industrial Training Institutes, Pre-Vocational Course Institutes,+ 2 level Higher Secondary Vocational Course Institutes and Bilateral Vocational Course Institutes to the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training in the Bank Account of the Council and transfer the remaining amount to the Board's account subject to certain conditions.
