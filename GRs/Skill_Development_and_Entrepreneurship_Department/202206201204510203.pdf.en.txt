Introduction: Industrial Training Institutes play a major role in creating skilled manpower in the State.
Industrial Training Institutes play a major role in creating world-class and business-oriented, skilled manpower in accordance with the demands of the establishment. Industrialization is increasing rapidly in the state due to economic liberalization. With the changing process of industrial technology production, the need for skilled manpower is increasing in the industries. It is also necessary to include new courses as per the demand of industrial establishments based on new technology.
Government Industrial Training Institute, Ambad G. Jalna has a Taluk level Industrial Training Institute and as on date, 16 batches of 08 trades are functioning in the Institute with an average intake capacity of 212 per annum. A total of 316 trainees are undergoing training at the institute.
At present, the industrial establishments based on solar energy and related works in Marathwada have a large number of manpower needs, a total of 3 new batches of business courses have been selected for the said UP institute, which have local needs and demand of industrial establishments, as well as opportunities for employment and self-employment.
Accordingly, the Government was considering to approve the proposal to start a batch of new business courses with high demand at Government Industrial Training Institute, Ambad G. Jalna to develop the skills of the needy students of Jalna district and create employment.
Government Decision: Government Industrial Training Institute, Ambad G. Jalna has a Taluk level Industrial Training Institute and as of now 16 batches of 08 trades are functioning in the institute. Approval is being given to start new batches of 03 business courses from 2022-23. Courses offered are Electronics, Mechanical and Electrical.
Details of the new units are given in Annexure A. Approval to start this course is subject to the following conditions.
Approval of DGT, New Delhi should be obtained for starting business courses from the institutions.
