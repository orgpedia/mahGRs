The Proposal:
The online standardized procedure for allowing new institutions and more batches of Pre-Vocational Courses,+ 2 Level Vocational Courses and Higher Secondary Vocational Courses to start Business Courses on a permanent non-subsidized basis has been approved by the Department vide its aforesaid Reference No. The decision was taken on May 10, 2018.
Approval for recognition of Pre-Vocational Courses and Vocational Courses at+ 2 level has been accorded vide letter dated 12.06.2020 in Reference No. Thereafter, due to lockdown due to COVID-19 situation, the revised time-bound schedule for the academic year 2020-21 has been notified vide Reference No.2. Approval was granted on July 28, 2020.
New institutions and more batches of Pre-Vocational Courses,+ 2 level Vocational Courses to be approved on permanent non-grant basis for the year 2020-21. As on 04.08.2020), the inspection programme of the institutions which had applied for approval on the Government web portal vti.dvet.gov.in and requested for inspection after completing all the facilities required for business, has been completed by the committees appointed by the Government Industrial Training Institutes. The Oversight Committees have uploaded their inspection reports on the web portal vti. dvet. gov. in. Accordingly, from the year 2020-21, the Government was considering to grant approval to start business courses on a permanent basis on a non-subsidized basis to new institutions and more batches of Pre-Vocational Courses and+ 2 Level Vocational Courses.
Government's decision:
Approval is being accorded for starting of new/ more batches of Pre-Vocational Courses and+ 2 level Vocational Courses in existing as well as new institutions on permanent non-subsidized basis from the year 2020-21 subject to the following terms and conditions:
The details of the 77 institutions which have been recommended by the Directorate for recognition of vocational courses have been attached in the accompanying Form A after scrutinizing the documents received through e-mail from the educational institutions and accordingly meeting the required standards. Further, the details of 02 institutions recommended by the Directorate for Pre-Vocational Courses are attached in Form B.
Terms and Conditions:
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill all the terms and conditions prescribed in the Secondary School Code 1981. After obtaining the permission, the institution will be required to maintain all the accounts required as per the Secondary School Certificate and MEPS Rules 1981 at the institution level. The recognition of the institution will be cancelled immediately if the institution does not follow the rules of secondary school education or does not follow the order, circular issued by the government and the directorate.
(2) No grant or financial assistance shall be provided in future even if approval is granted on permanent non-grant basis for starting new/ additional batches of approved courses.
3. The institutions implementing the approved courses shall be bound to comply with the orders issued from time to time by the concerned offices under the Government/ Directorate/ Directorate.

4. It shall be mandatory for the institution to charge the tuition fee from the students as fixed by the Government from time to time. Students cannot be charged more than the prescribed fee.

It shall be mandatory for institutions to display signboards on their facades indicating that they have been approved on permanent non-grant basis for Pre-Vocational Courses/ Bilingual Business Courses at+ 2 level. The number of sanctioned batches of courses and the sanctioned intake capacity should be mentioned on the notice board.
(6) Since the said courses have been approved on permanent non-grant basis, the respective Institute shall be fully responsible for the service/ salary and related matters of all the staff employed by the Institute for the said courses. Government is not responsible for this.
The appointment of teaching staff required for the approved course, their minimum educational qualification and experience should be done immediately by the institute in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Private Schools Employees (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1981, as well as orders issued by the Government from time to time. The report of the action taken should be submitted to the Directorate through the Regional Office.
(8) If the Government takes any policy decision regarding discontinuation or modification of the said courses in future, it shall be required to be implemented by the Institute without any further delay.
The institution should strictly follow the social/ parallel reservation and other rules issued by the government from time to time for admission to the approved batches of courses and admissions should be made in order of merit.
10.After the approval of the above courses, the institute shall inform the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education about the commencement of the said course in the institute and take immediate action accordingly.
11.If the intake capacity of the new batches is 50 per batch and less than 50% of the sanctioned intake, permission of the concerned District Vocational Education and Training Officer will be required to continue such batches.
12.If a recognized course is not started by the institution in the approved academic year or if the intake of the batch of the said course is less than 50% of the sanctioned intake for 3 consecutive years, the recognition of the institution will automatically be terminated. After this, the institution will have to renew the approval process to start the course.
