Introduction: The Budget for the year 2019-20 was presented to the Legislative Assembly on 27th February, 2019. As the budget is interim, the Maharashtra Appropriation (Vote on Account) Bill, 2019 has been passed by the Legislative Assembly to provide for the expenditure for the four months from April, 2019 to July, 2019 for the period up to the passing of the Appropriation Bill by way of an additional budget. The Bill has now been converted into an Act and published as Maharashtra Act No. 2 by the Government of Maharashtra, Gazette Extraordinary, Part IV, dated 8th March, 2019.
Under this Act, the Administrative Department has been authorized to incur expenditure from the proposed accounting for the year 2019-20 from 1st April, 2019 to 31st July, 2019. As per the circular issued by the Finance Department, it was proposed to disburse the arrears mentioned in the accompanying statement regarding the mandatory items on the basis of the Budget Estimates 2019-20 (White Book) book as approved by the Finance Department. Further, it was proposed to issue guidelines in the distribution of grants in accounts in accordance with the relevant Government Circular in respect of the provisions under the programme. It was under consideration of the Government to disburse the Head of Accounts on BEAMS system to the concerned Controlling Authorities under the Department of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship in the proportion of 1/3 rd of the total budgetary allocation. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:

GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As per the Government Circular in the context of the Finance Department, out of the budgetary provisions for the financial year 2019-20 as shown in column 6 and 7 of Annexure-A attached to this Government Decision, it is hereby approved to transfer the amount of contribution allocated on BEAMS system to the Controlling Authority as per the directions in the Government Circular in the context of the Finance Department for meeting the expenditure for four months from April, 2019 to July, 2019.
The funds allocated on the BEAMS system should be immediately disbursed by the Controlling Authority to the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Authority subject to the following terms and conditions: The funds/ grants may not be spent for purposes other than those for which they are sanctioned. Also, it cannot be used under other headings.
2) The progress report of the expenditure should be submitted to the government by the 10th of every month by the controlling officer according to the monthly fund statement.
The grant should be made with the approval of the competent authority as per the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-Division I, No. 6, Rule 40, provided under Regulation 30/2013/ Part-II, dated 17th April, 2015.
5, Rule 39, (b) Note-5 and in accordance with Rule 40 of A. No. 6, the grant shall be made with the approval of the competent authority.
(5) When providing grants to private NGOs, the grant should be paid in accordance with the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-1, Sub-Division 1, No. 27, Rule 149, with the approval of the competent authority.
(6) In the case of the distribution of provisions for which an administrative sanction order is required, the grant shall be distributed after the approval of the competent authority as provided under Part-I, Sub-Division-III, A. No. 4, Rule 27 (2).
Utilization Certificates of previous grants should be obtained from various Government/ Administrative Institutions as well as Aided Institutions by the Department/ Commissioner's Office/ Directorate before sanctioning the Aided Grants. No further grant should be released until the utilization of the earlier grant is completed.
8) Subsidies of individual beneficiaries should not be withdrawn without ascertaining the list of beneficiaries and linking it with the payment with proper details.
Scholarships, reimbursement of tuition fees, payments to individual beneficiaries should be made through the Electronic Clearance System (ECS).

10) No expenditure shall be incurred in respect of a construction proposal without obtaining administrative approval/ technical approval, as well as approval of the Committee of Secretaries, where necessary. Similarly, this fund should not be provided to the Public Works Department in the form of deposit construction.
11) Purchase payments should not be drawn from the treasuries until the supplier is determined after completing the process of purchasing the material. The purchase cheque should be paid in the name of the supplier concerned. No advance payment shall be made to the supplier unless there is a specific provision in the tender/ agreement.
12) The expenditure on computerization should be made as per the government tariff and approval of the Department of Information and Technology should be obtained where necessary.
(13) In the case of expenditure on the payment of dues, the funds shall not be withdrawn unless it is satisfied that the material purchased has been physically received and its accounts maintained properly.
The actual disbursement of funds will be made only after the orders for sanction of plan-wise administrative and financial expenditure in respect of the provisions under the programme have been issued separately by the concerned authority, as per the relevant Government circular.
The funds disbursed on BEAMS distribution system should not be drawn from the treasury and kept in a separate bank account. Also, the Drawing and Disbursing Officer should draw the funds from the treasury on the budget distribution system only through the D.C. Bill. C. No funds should be withdrawn by submitting bills.
The said Government Resolution is being issued as per the approval of Rule No. 149 of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978 and as per the directions issued under the Government Circular No: Finance- 2019/ PR No. 44/ Finance- 3, dated: 1st April, 2019, as the case may be.
