Introduction: The Industrial Training Institute aims to make the trainees employable by providing vocational training. For this purpose, the training of business courses relevant to industrial establishments is imparted to the trainees and they are made employable by providing training according to the work machinery available in the industrial establishment.
For this, there is a need to have more and more industry friendly Industrial Training Institutes.
Also, the students need to be well trained and have a good environment. Industrial Training Institute should have courses in line with industrial establishment based on state-of-the-art machinery and new technology. For this purpose, Institute Management Committees (IMCs) have been constituted to upgrade the status of Industrial Training Institutes under the Public Private Partnership (PPP) and STRIVE (Skill Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement) schemes. Out of 417 Government Industrial Training Institutes (GITIs) in Maharashtra, Management Committees have been constituted in 286 institutions.
3. Decision of the Government of the Department of Higher and Technical Education in the Industrial Training Institute. The Management Committee of the Institute was constituted on 12th July 2002. The structure, duties and responsibilities of the Institute Management Committee have been given in it. MC has been established. The Centrally Sponsored Centre of Excellence Scheme was implemented in 12 O.P. Institutions in the State since 2005. The project was implemented through the IMC.
The Government of India vide its Order dated 30.06.2007 has approved the constitution of an independent Management Committee in 87 O.P. Institutions to implement the World Bank assisted Vocational Training Improvement Project. The Management Committee of these institutions was given limited financial powers to spend the funds available from the Central Government.

However, the income of all government industrial training institutes in the state is deposited in the institute's personal provident fund (PLA) account and remains unutilised. The funds should be transferred to the IMC's separate back account and used to upgrade the status of the institution. Besides, the government was considering the formation of an Institute Management Committee in the remaining 131 institutions, without differentiating between the IMC in only those institutions covered under the Public Private Partnership (PPP) World Bank and STRIVE (Skill Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement) scheme and not in other industrial training institutions. In this regard, the government has taken the following decision: Government Decision: It is being decided to form Institute Management Committees in all Government Industrial Training Institutes in the state. Duties and Responsibilities of the Institute Management Committee (IMC): 1) To prepare the Institute Development Plan (IDP)/ Institute Strategic Plan (ISP) of the Institute and submit it to the District Skill Development Committee for approval.
(ii) Define the long-term objectives/ goals of the organization, analyze the problems and challenges faced by the organization and determine the strategy to deal with them. To decide the conceptual procurement policy of the organization as per the guidelines of the Central Government.
3. Setting up Key Performance Indicators for improvement in the organization, prepare year wise details of funds required as per the needs of the organization.
4) To determine the need of trainers, machines and equipment and other services for the development of the O.P. organization and to carry out corrective works accordingly.
(v) To enter into Performance Based Grant Agreements on behalf of the O.P. Agency in respect of implementation of Central/ State projects.
6) Timely implementation of various works to be done as per ISP under Central/ State projects.
Monitoring the progress of implementation of Central/ State projects.
8) To follow the procurement procedures and audit procedures prescribed by the Central Government for Central/ State projects.
Various schemes of the Central/ State Government e.g. PMKVY/ PMKUVA/ Sankalp to be launched in Industrial Training Institutes.
To start various skill training courses under MSBVE on self-financed basis.
11) Determining the admission process at the institute level for this course.
The Institute Management Committee (IMC) will have to open a separate bank account for the courses run on a self-financing basis.
The Institute Management Committee (IMC) shall have the power to appoint staff/ training institute on contract basis only for units run on self-financing basis. No approval is required for this.
The services of the employees who are taken with the approval of the Institute Management Committee (IMC) will be on the basis of honorarium and with the approval of the IMC. No new posts will be created by the government or the sectoral machinery for this.
15) To decide on the fee of the course, the appointment of the directors of PMKVY/ PMKUVA/ MSBVE and their remuneration.
16) The services of Data Entry Operator, Clerk-Typist, Accountant can be taken on the basis of honorarium for the work of the organization. Part-time employees/ persons registered with employment exchanges etc. and those who are not employed should be considered on priority.
17) Security guards for the security of the institution should be appointed on the basis of honorarium by the prevailing rules of the government/ security agency.

(iii) To see that the affairs of the Society are carried on efficiently and in accordance with the provisions of the Memorandum of Association, Rules and Regulation and bye laws of the Society (or equivalent).
4) To take necessary decisions on the matters which in the opinion of the Chairman are important and require immediate action and for which there is not enough time available to hold the meeting of the IMC.

6) To prepare the minutes of each meeting and submit it to the Director, Vocational Education and Training, Directorate of Vocational Education and Training and the Government.
7) To organize seminars, workshops and seminars to increase employment and self-employment.
