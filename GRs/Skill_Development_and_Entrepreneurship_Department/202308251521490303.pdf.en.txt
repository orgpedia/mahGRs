The Proposal:
The Maharashtra State Board of Vocational Training (now Maharashtra State Board of Vocational Education and Training) was established in 1986 under the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training to cater to the need of vocational education for the students who have dropped out of secondary education due to the rise in industrialization, changing technology, social and economic changes and to provide short-term vocational courses for the students who are pursuing special education (add-on courses) to promote employment and self-employment.

Due to the increase in industrialization, changing technology, social and economic changes, etc., the curriculum of the Board has been improved and increased over time. At present, the Board is conducting courses in various groups of 6 months, 1 year and 2 years in part-time and full-time formats. Applications have been invited from eligible private institutions for the programme.
Accordingly, applications received from private institutions interested in starting courses of the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training have been examined and monitored by the District Vocational Education and Training Officer of the concerned district and the eligible institution has been recommended to the Board. The proposals received from the District Vocational Education and Training Officers will be scrutinized by the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training and the list of eligible institutions will be sent to the Government for approval. 6 are presented here. Accordingly, from the year 2023-24, the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training (MSVTET) was under consideration of the government to grant approval to start vocational courses on a self-financing basis to new institutions/ batches of paramedical certificate courses.
Government's decision:
The Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training (MSBSTET) has recommended that the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training (MSBSTET) should consider the proposals submitted by private educational institutions willing to implement the courses of the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training (MSBSB). The details of those institutions are annexed in the accompanying Form A and the said institutions are being approved to start the Board's Certificate Course in Paramedical Group from the year 2023-24 in the new institution on a self-financing basis subject to the following terms and conditions.
Terms and Conditions:
The institutions approved to implement the courses of the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training will have to comply with the orders issued from time to time by the government, the Directorate of Vocational Education and Training, the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training and the concerned offices under the Directorate.
Students should be admitted to the place where the institutions have been approved to run the courses of the Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training. Also, the approval of the same place will be considered, if there is a change in the place, the approval will be cancelled.

The Maharashtra State Skill, Vocational Education and Training Board will be obliged to implement the curriculum as per the National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF) if the government makes changes in the curriculum being implemented by it.
The institutions approved for conducting courses in the paramedical group will have to obtain the approval of the concerned Council/ Board in the field of medicine, wherever necessary.
Candidates who have completed training in Diploma and Certificate level courses of Paramedical will be eligible to work as Health Assistant.
Diploma and certificate level courses in paramedical are not medical courses and candidates will not be eligible to prescribe any drugs to any patient.
Candidates who have completed diploma and certificate level courses of paramedical will be prohibited from using the title" Doctor."
