The Proposal:
As per the New Education Policy of 1986 of the Central Government, with a view to encourage more and more youth to take up employment/ self-employment by teaching business courses at the+ 2 level, minimum skill-based business courses comprising 70% business education and 30% general education were introduced in the State from 1988-89. The objective of the scheme is to enable students to acquire minimum business-related skills to start their own business or get employed in an industrial establishment and reduce the burden on them towards higher education. The name of the minimum skill-based curriculum of this course has been decided by the Department of Higher and Technical Education. 2/3/2009 and it has been changed to Higher Secondary Vocational Courses (H.S.C. Vocational).
In view of the growing demand for this vocational course, applications/ proposals received from interested institutions for starting new/ additional batches of Higher Secondary Vocational Courses at+ 2 level in the academic year 2017-18 on permanent non-subsidized basis were scrutinized at the level of Director (Vocational Education& Training). Accordingly, the Director of Vocational Education and Training has recommended approval for starting new/ more batches of Vocational Courses at+ 2 level from the academic year 2017-18 on permanent non-subsidized basis in the non-government institutions meeting the criteria/ standards prescribed in the proposal recommended by the Director of Vocational Education and Training under Reference No. The letter was written by 1. However, since the academic year 2017-18 is over, the matter of granting recognition to the institution from the academic year 2018-19 was under consideration of the Government.

Terms and Conditions
1) The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill all the terms and conditions mentioned in the Secondary School Code 1981.
(ii) No grant or financial assistance shall be provided in future, even if approval is granted on permanent non-grant basis for starting new/ additional batches of approved courses.
(3) The institutions implementing the approved courses shall be bound to comply with the orders issued from time to time by the concerned offices under the Government/ Directorate/ Directorate.

4) It will be mandatory for the institution to charge the tuition fee from the students as determined by the government from time to time.
5) It will be mandatory for the institutions to display a signboard on the facade saying" Recognized+ 2 Level Higher Secondary Vocational Courses on Permanent Non-Grant Basis." The number of sanctioned batches of courses and the sanctioned intake capacity should be mentioned on the notice board.
After the approval order is issued, it will be mandatory for the concerned institution to provide all the infrastructure required for the approved course as per the standard within a period of one month.
(7) Since the said courses have been approved on permanent non-grant basis, the Government shall not be responsible for the service/ salary of all the staff appointed by the Institute for the said course and the related matters.
8) The institution should take action in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Private Schools Employees (Conditions of Service) Rules 1981 as well as the orders issued by the government from time to time regarding the appointment of the necessary staff for the approved courses, its minimum educational qualification and experience.
9) If the government decides to discontinue or make any changes in these courses in the future, it will be required to be implemented by the institution without any delay.
10) The institutions should strictly follow the social/ parallel reservation and other rules issued by the government from time to time for admission to the approved batches of courses and should be given admission in order of merit.
After the approval of the courses, the institute should immediately inform the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Examination about the start of the course to the institute, and take corresponding action.
The intake capacity of new batches/ courses beyond the sanctioned strength is 50 per batch and if the intake is less than 50% of the sanctioned strength after the academic year 2018-19, permission of the concerned District Vocational Education and Training Officer will be mandatory for continuation of such batches. Similarly, in case the intake is less than 50% of the sanctioned intake for 3 consecutive academic years, the course will be discontinued.
The Government shall have the right to modify the above terms and conditions or to insert new terms and conditions to improve the quality of education.

14) Before admitting students to the approved batch of Higher Secondary Business Course at the+ 2 level, the Director (Personnel and Trainees) shall appoint a committee at the local level and admit them with the permission of the Directorate under the supervision of the committee.
