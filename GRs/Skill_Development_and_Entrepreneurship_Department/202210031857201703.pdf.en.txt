Preamble: Government Industrial Training Institutes (GITIs) are presently functioning in 419 locations in the State and the skilled manpower generated through Vocational Training as per the times is in short supply. Therefore, it is necessary to increase the intake capacity of vocational training courses in industrial training institutes. It is necessary to provide the facilities of business courses to the trainees by making optimum use of the available machinery of the existing Industrial Training Institutes in the state. The National Skill Development Council (NSDC), set up under the chairmanship of the Prime Minister, has set a target of creating 500 million skilled manpower in the country by 2022. Accordingly, it is imperative to make available more and more skilled manpower by substantially increasing the accessibility in the Industrial Training Institutes.
The decision was taken by the Higher and Technical Education Department. As per 21.09.2006, approval has been given to start new Government Industrial Training Institutes in the newly created 32 taluks of the State from the year 2006-07.
