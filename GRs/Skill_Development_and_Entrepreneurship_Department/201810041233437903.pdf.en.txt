The Proposal:
The Director, Vocational Education and Training had requested the Government through a letter of reference to grant approval to the Government Industrial Training Institute, Nagothane, as Basic Training Provider (BTP) under the National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) for imparting Basic Training in the third batch of three courses namely Attendant Operator (Chemical Plant), Instrument Mechanic (Chemical Plant) and Maintenance Mechanic (Chemical Plant) in the chemical sector. Accordingly, the Government was considering the matter of recognizing the Government Industrial Training Institute, Nagothane as a Basic Training Provider (BTP).
Government's decision:
The Government Industrial Training Institute, Nagothane has been selected as Basic Training Provider (BTP) under the National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) in one batch of three courses namely Attendant Operator (Chemical Plant), Instrument Mechanic (Chemical Plant) and Maintenance Mechanic (Chemical Plant) in the chemical sector in three sessions in October, January and April every academic year.

The commencement is being approved in principle subject to the following terms and conditions under this Government Decision.
The guidelines issued by the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, New Delhi, Government of India for National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) Basic Training Provider should be followed.
As a Basic Training Provider (BTP), the manpower required to provide basic training should be made available on a permanent hourly basis. The honorarium of this manpower is given to the Director of Crafts in the Government Industrial Training Institute.
The remuneration permissible per hour of demonstration shall be so much.
National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) Basic Training Provider (BTP) will be paid Rs 7,500/- (500 hours/ 3 months) per student. No additional funds will be provided by the government for this.
Basic training should be provided by utilizing the available building (space) and equipment of the courses of Attendant Operator (Chemical Plant), Instrument Mechanic (Chemical Plant), Maintenance Mechanic (Chemical Plant) in the institute. However, the Principal, Industrial Training Institute should ensure that there is no impact on the training of vocational courses under the Craftsmen Training Scheme.
