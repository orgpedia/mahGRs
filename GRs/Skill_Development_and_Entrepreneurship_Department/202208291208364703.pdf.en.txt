There is a need for training to enhance the changes taking place in the industrial establishment as well as the latest technical knowledge in the state. In an industrial set up, technology is changing rapidly and new techniques need to be learnt to use the latest technology. Thus, it is a continuous process to impart up-to-date training to employees and officers in new methods and processes.
Keeping this in mind, the government was considering to undertake a programme to train officers and staff and approve the expenditure for it in order to increase the quality of training and speed up administrative work by improving the efficiency and productivity of the available manpower.
Government Decision: In view of the above, the training programme of Group-C technical/ non-technical cadre employees, officers, technicians, instructors in the Industrial Training Institute under the command of the Director, Directorate of Vocational Education and Training, Regional Office of Vocational Education and Training, Nashik Division is being approved subject to the following terms and conditions.
Under this scheme, priority should be given to the staff and teaching staff of the Government Industrial Training Institute.
These training programmes should be conducted at the district/ divisional level and at all the facilities available.
The training programme should include up-to-date knowledge of the relevant business curriculum, use of modern audio-visual media, instructional materials, new teaching methods/ technologies, psychological aspects as well as methods of handling trainees, etc.
4. The training to be imparted to the officers should cover aspects such as leadership development, administrative and financial matters, student welfare, good governance and managerial skills.
Reduce the number of accidents by providing safety training to officers/ staff.
The officers/ staff should be trained to handle the latest machinery and equipment efficiently.
The officers/ employees should be covered under the Government Rules, Right to Information Act, 2005, Court Cases, Financial, Warehousing Management, Establishment matters, handling of Administrative matters, as well as identification of advanced technologies and training of skills etc. It should also include counselling, positive attitude, evaluation process, interaction with private industrial establishments, general skills, entrepreneurship, apprenticeship training, employment and self-employment.
