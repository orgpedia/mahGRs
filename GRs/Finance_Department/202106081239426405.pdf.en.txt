The proposal: The Supreme Court and the The Commissioner of Sales Tax has been given the financial powers by the Government Order dated 29.10.2003 to appoint special/ senior counsels as may be required by the Commissioner of Sales Tax from the list of counsels consented by the Department of Law and Justice to effectively represent and conduct the affairs of the Commissioner of Sales Tax in the matter after the cases relating to Sales Tax are filed in the High Court.
The Goods and Services Tax (GST) Act, 2017 came into effect from July 1, 2017.
The State Tax Department, after representing the Government in the Court in the court cases where the appointment of Special Counsel has been approved by the Finance Department and the Department of Law and Justice, has requested for the approval of the Special Commissioner of State Tax and the Additional Commissioner of State Tax for the State of Maharashtra and for the concerned jurisdiction respectively, the financial powers of the Commissioner of State Tax to provide appropriate fees to the concerned Special Counsel. Accordingly, the matter of restoration of financial powers to the Commissioner of State Taxes was under consideration.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: In view of the large number of clerks in the office of the Commissioner of State Taxes and the busyness of the Commissioner of State Taxes in the administrative work, the Government have decided to restore the financial powers conferred on the Commissioner of State Taxes/ Commissioner of Sales Tax under the Government Decision dated 29th October, 2003, to the Special Commissioner of State Taxes for the State of Maharashtra and to the Additional Commissioner of State Taxes for their jurisdiction in the following cases.
(a) The appointment of a Counsel in a court case in the list of Counsel approved by the Department of Law and Justice at a rate to be determined by the Department of Law and Justice.
