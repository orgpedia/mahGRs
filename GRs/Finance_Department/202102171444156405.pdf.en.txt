Preamble: The Government of India has appointed Cotton Corporation of India as the Principal Agent for procurement of cotton at MSP for the year 2020-21. The Cotton Corporation of India has appointed Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Marketing Federation Limited, Mumbai as Deputy Manager in the State for implementation of the scheme. The Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers' Federation has received a loan from Bank of Maharashtra, Mumbai at an interest rate of 6.35 per cent for timely payment of dues to farmers for cotton purchased under this scheme. The proposal of the Department of Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles to provide a guarantee to the Government for raising a loan of Rs.1500 crore (Rs.1,500 crore) and to waive the guarantee fee on the Government guarantee was approved by the Cabinet.
The Cabinet gave. It has been approved in the meeting dated 20.1.2021. Accordingly, the matter of issuing a government guarantee was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
The Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Marketing Federation, Mumbai (NIDCO) has received a total loan of Rs. 6.35 lakh from Bank of Maharashtra, Mumbai (DHANCO) for timely payment of dues of cotton procured by Cotton Corporation of India during 2020-21 season under Minimum Support Price Scheme. Government guarantee is being sanctioned for raising loans up to Rs.1500 crore (Rs.1,500 crore).
The period of repayment of the loan against the said Government shall be valid for a period of 6 months from the date of issue of the Government Resolution.

The following terms and conditions should be included in the agreement with Dhanco Bank regarding the loan taken on government guarantee.
3.1) Government Guarantee Total Rs. The loan amount will be limited to Rs 1500 crore.
The validity of this order shall be for a period of 6 months from the date of issue of this order.
3.3. Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers' Federation Ltd. is a subsidiary of DANCO Bank, which provides loans to farmers for loans from banks, loans from Cotton Corporation of India, and loans for disbursement to farmers and repayment to banks. A separate Escrow A/ C will be opened in the name of Mumbai and the loan taken from this account will be repaid.
3.4 The Government Guarantee will not be applicable for penal interest and any other dues for delay in repayment of loan from Dhanco Banks.
3.5) In case of default in repayment of loan by Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers' Federation, the loan will be sanctioned by Dhanco Bank only after obtaining sufficient security for recovery of the loan. This will be checked by the concerned bank from time to time and at least once in every six months.
3.6) In case of default in repayment of loan by Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Marketing Federation, the process of recovery of loan should be completed by selling/ auctioning the movable/ immovable property of the Federation. Also, banks cannot use government-guaranteed securities for collateral without making all efforts to recover the loans.
3.7 The banks will be required to inform the government about the delay in repayment of loans or payment of interest by the Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers' Federation within 90 days.
3.8) Dhanko Banks shall not, without prior approval of the State Government, grant loans to Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers' Marketing Federation in excess of the limits prescribed in the order. Also, the borrower will not take any loan from any other financial institution for this purpose.
3.9) If a decision is taken to recover the sale of movable/ immovable property of Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Growers Marketing Federation from Dhanco Bank, a committee should be formed for this purpose and one representative each from the Department of Cooperation, Marketing and Textiles and the Department of Finance should be included in that committee.
The valuation of movable/ immovable property at the time of sale/ auction will be required to be done by the tertiary institution as per the rules of the government. Also, the finalization of the auction process with the report of both the representatives of the government in the said committee will require the approval of the government.
A breach of any of the conditions set out in clauses (3.1) to (3.9) above shall render the Government Guarantee invalid.
