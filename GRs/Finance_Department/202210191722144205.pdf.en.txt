Preamble: Proposals for revised Administrative Sanctions. Deputy Chief Minister (Finance) The meeting of the Expenditure Priority Committee was held on 19 th September, 2022. During the meeting, while examining various proposals for revised administrative approval, it was observed by the Expenditure Priority Committee that the Government Decisions of the Finance Department were not in accordance with the provisions of the Act. As per the provision dated 29th December, 1976, cases where the cost of revised administrative sanction is more than the cost of original administrative sanction by Rs.5 lakh or 15% of the cost of original administrative sanction, whichever is higher, are placed before the Expenditure Priority Committee.
During the discussion on the revised administrative sanction proposals, the Divisional Secretaries present requested to increase the limit of Rs. 1 crore from the original administrative sanction price of Rs. 5 lakh for submitting the revised administrative sanction proposals to the Expenditure Priority Committee. After the discussion, the committee decided to spend Rs. 5
Instead of Rs. 1 crore has been proposed. Accordingly, the government was considering the issue.
Government Decision: The Government Decision of the Finance Department on the recommendation of the Expenditure Priority Committee. As per the provision dated 29th December, 1976, the provision regarding submission to the Expenditure Priority Committee for grant of revised administrative approval in cases where the cost of revised administrative approval is Rs. 5 lakh or 15% of the cost of original administrative approval, whichever is higher, is being amended from Rs. 5 lakh to Rs. 1 crore.
