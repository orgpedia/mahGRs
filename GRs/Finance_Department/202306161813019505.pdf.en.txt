Circular: Supplementary Statement of Expenditure for the current financial year under Article 205 of the Constitution of India will be presented in the July, 2023 session of the Legislature as usual. In view of the provisions of the Constitution as well as the Maharashtra Budget Rule Book, the Supplementary Statement of Demands/ Expenditure is submitted to the Legislature in respect of the following matters: (a) Supplementary Demands or Appropriations have not yet been submitted to the Legislature in respect of amounts sanctioned or to be made out of the Contingency Fund.
(b) only such matters as may be prescribed by order of the Court and the expenditure in the form of loads and the expenditure on new matters which may be planned to be incurred after obtaining approval from the Legislature by way of a supplementary demand.
(a) Typical demands relating to bringing the expenditure on new works to the notice of the Legislature.
2. In view of the limited resources of the State, in order to ensure proper planning of budgetary expenditure and control of budgetary expenditure, it is necessary to consider proposals for urgent and urgent expenditure on bonded labour, court cases and supplementary demands for expenditure in respect of Centrally Sponsored Schemes. Accordingly, supplementary demand proposals should be submitted by all the Ministries/ Departments for the following purposes only.
Supplementary demands/ appropriations not yet submitted to the Legislature in respect of amounts sanctioned or to be made out of the Contingency Fund.
(2) the expenses incurred and the new matters on which the expenditure is planned to be incurred after obtaining the approval of the Legislature by way of a supplementary demand.
(iii) For the Central and State share of Central/ Externally Aided/ Centrally Sponsored Schemes for which no provision has been made in the Budget or where more than 50% of the provision has been made and funds are required,

For matters where more than 70% of the budgetary provisions have been spent and for which more provision is required.
In view of the aforesaid facts, all the Ministries/ Departments are required to forward all supplementary demands for mandatory and programme expenditure in respect of the above mentioned items to the Department of Finance for registration in the first instance along with the proposal and the information in Annexure-B, C and D depending on the instructions and procedures in Annexure-A attached.
In this regard, the schedule for forwarding the proposals regarding the supplementary demand for programme and mandatory expenditure to be included in the session of July, 2023 to the Planning Department (Development Area Office)/ Social Justice and Special Assistance Department/ Tribal Development Department and Finance Department is given separately as follows. Since the registration of supplementary demand proposals will be done online on the BEAMS system, it is imperative that all the concerned departments take action as per the prescribed schedule.
