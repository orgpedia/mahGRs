The functions of the Committee shall be as follows: (a) To implement a time bound programme for prevention of sexual harassment at workplace.
(b) to publish the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the amendments/ suggestions made in this regard by the Government in the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979 (i.e. suggestions regarding sexual harassment).
(e) To conduct periodic meetings of women officers/ staff of the State Lotteries Directorate to take cognizance of complaints of sexual harassment received by them and to conduct inquiries.
(e) to investigate into such complaints and recommend action against the erring employees, if any, for disciplinary action.
a) Review of such action/ disciplinary proceedings.
(b) To submit to the Government an annual report of the work done in this regard.
Women officers/ employees of the State Lotteries Directorate can submit their grievances to the Chairman/ Member Secretary of the Committee. An allowance of Rs.200/- (Two Hundred) per working day for an appointed member of an internal committee of a Non-Governmental Organization or a Union and such member shall be paid as much as the cost of travelling by train in three-tier air-conditioned class or air-conditioned taxi/ bus or auto-rickshaw.
Accordingly, the payments submitted by the non-official members to the Chairman/ Member Secretary of the Committee in attendance for each working day of the Internal Committee shall be paid through the Accountant (Cash), Directorate of State Lotteries, Mumbai, following the prevailing procedures and rules.
Women officers/ employees can lodge complaints of sexual harassment with the" Internal Complaint Redressal Committee" constituted in the office of the Directorate of State Lotteries. The Chairperson or, as the case may be, the member of the Committee may be relieved of his office by the appointing authority if he is found to be in contravention of the provisions of section 16 of the Act or has been convicted of an offence or is pending investigation for an offence under any law for the time being in force or is found to be guilty of any disciplinary action or is pending disciplinary action against him or has abused his office by continuing in office in a manner prejudicial to the public interest, and the office so vacated or any casual vacancy caused may be filled by fresh nomination in accordance with the provisions of this section.
