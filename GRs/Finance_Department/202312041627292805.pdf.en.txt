The Central Government has introduced a defined contribution pension scheme for employees appointed on or after 1st November, 2005. As per the Government decision of Reference No. 2, the State Government has decided to incorporate National Pension System on the lines of Central Government. The National Pension System (NPS) was implemented from April 1, 2015. Also Reference No. Under the Government Resolution No. 3, the modalities have been prescribed for implementation of Level-1 of the National Pension System within the State.
It has been more than eight years since the Government of Maharashtra adopted the National Pension System and the implementation of the National Pension System at every stage (e.g. Delay in registration/ timely deposit of monthly contributions/ partial withdrawal/ exit from National Pension System etc. This has been brought out in the Auditor General's Audit Report 13, 2020 as well as in the functional review meetings held with Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) on 26.08.2022, 15.02.2023 and 15.05.2023 to address the gaps in the implementation of the National Pension System.
At present, various new modalities have been adopted at the State level for implementation of National Pension System (NPS) as per the mandate of Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA).
It has also been noticed that there is a delay in the implementation of PRAN generation, monthly contribution, partial withdrawal, exit from the National Pension System, etc. in respect of officers/ employees on deputation.
