Government circular:
The Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) has issued a detailed procedure for partial withdrawal of funds to the members of the National Pension System (NPS) during their service period. As per the Act No. 1. 3 is prescribed. Accordingly, the following provisions are being brought to the notice of the departments:
Employees under the National Pension Scheme (NPS) can withdraw only 25% of their own accumulated contribution amount for the following reasons, as per the circular issued by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) from time to time:
(c) for higher education of self or adopted son/ daughter.
b) For the marriage of self or adopted son/ daughter.
c) For purchase of house in the name of self or spouse of the employee. (However, if the employee has a house in his own name or in the name of his spouse, such employee cannot withdraw the amount for this purpose.) )
As per the above notification of Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority dated 10/08/2017 and Circular dated 09/04/2020, the withdrawal of amount is admissible to the members of National Pension Scheme, legal spouses/ children/ adopted children/ dependent parents of the members for the following serious ailments (on the basis of medical certificate):
1) Cancer 2) Kidney failure- End stage renal failure 3) Primary pulmonary arterial hypertension 4) Multiple sclerosis

Under the National Pension Scheme, employees can withdraw only the accumulated amount of the employee's accumulated contribution after the completion of a period of 3 years from the date of their appointment.
4) The employee can withdraw the partial amount a maximum of 3 times during their entire service period.
5) Government employees should submit written or online applications to their Drawing and Disbursing Officers for withdrawal of partial amount. Attach the required documents along with the application form.
6) The Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall scrutinize the application of the employee within 3 days of receiving the application for withdrawal of the amount. If the demand is in accordance with the rules, such an application should be submitted online with the necessary documents to the Subordinate and Accounts Office/ Treasury Office.
7) The Appropriation and Accounts Office/ Treasury Office should complete the scrutiny of the application within 3 days and submit it online to the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (NSDL), if the demand form is as per the rules. Otherwise, the case should be withdrawn.
a) For the employees on deputation:
The deputed staff shall submit their application to the Head of Department/ Head of Service Controlling the Service Cadre in the office of the Head of Office under whose jurisdiction the service is performed. The head of the office shall take appropriate action in accordance with the above procedure.
