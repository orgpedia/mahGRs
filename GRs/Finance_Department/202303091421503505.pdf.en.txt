The Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995. The Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016.
Introduction: The Rights of Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 has been amended to provide for the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016. Also, a proposal to relax the age limit for disabled children on the lines of the central government was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
Under Section 2 (zc) of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the following categories of disability are included as mentioned in the Annexure. The Government women employees with disabled children and Government male employees without children and wife belonging to that category will also be entitled to Child Care Leave as prescribed under the Government Order dated 21st September, 2016: 1. Loco Motor Disability (Orthopaedics) 2. Leprosy Cured Person (Leprosy cured/ discharged) 3. Cerebral Palsy (Cerebral Palsy) 4. Dwarfism (Physical Deterioration) 5. Muscular Dystrophy (Muscular Dystrophy) 6. Acid Attack Victims (Acid attack victims) 7. Blindness (totally blind) 8. Low Vision (partially blind) 9. Hearing Impairment
10.Speech and Language Disability

The above mentioned disability shall be as per the definition of disability in the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016.
The conditions of this leave entitlement are prescribed in accordance with the Government Decision dated 21st September 2016. Out of these conditions, the condition mentioned in Annexure (ii) that the said leave can be availed up to the age of 22 years of the disabled child is excluded. Also, the condition that this leave cannot be taken for less than five days at a time is being added.
(3) This order shall come into force on the date of issue.
