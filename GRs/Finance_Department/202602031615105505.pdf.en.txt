Government circular:
Departments are expected to plan and spend the funds available to them keeping in mind the cash flow while introducing the budgetary distribution system. However, it has come to the notice of the government that some departments are incurring huge expenditure in the last three months of the financial year.
2. With a view to control and regulate expenditure on non-essential or non-priority matters, the following decisions are being taken: -
1) In the financial year 2025-2026. All administrative divisions and their subordinate offices may not approve any procurement proposals on or after February 15, 2026. Also, proposals relating to furniture repairs, Xerox machines, computers, equipment or spare parts repairs, casual workshops, seminars, office rentals, etc. should not be approved and submitted to the Finance Department.
2) Exceptions will be made in the case of purchase of medicines within the limits of available funds. Also, the essays will not be applicable to proposals for procurement under Centrally Sponsored Schemes and State Share under the same as well as Buy Aided Projects.
3) Proposals for procurement from the District Annual Plan as well as the Local Development Fund of the people's representatives may be submitted to the Finance Department. The Finance Department will have the power to decide on the matter.
