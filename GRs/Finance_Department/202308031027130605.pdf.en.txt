Government circular
Rule 232 (v) of the Government of India General Financial Rules, 2017 provides for the regulation of the devolution of funds to the State Governments and the appropriation of funds through the Public Financial Management System (PFMS). Accordingly, the Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India has issued guidelines for effective control of availability and utilization of funds in respect of funds released to the State Governments for Centrally Sponsored Schemes and to reduce the float. The" SNA Model" has been operationalized from 1 st July, 2021.
Guidelines for implementation of" SNA Model" have been issued by Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance, Government of India vide Office Memorandum No.F.No.1 (13)/ PFMS/ FCD/ 2020 dated 23.03.2021. These guidelines have been brought to the notice of all concerned through the circular mentioned at serial number 2 in the above mentioned" Vachana" of this Department. Thereafter, all concerned have also been apprised of the suggestions given by the Government of India from time to time regarding the" SNA Model."
The principle of" just in time release" as provided under Rule 230 (7) of the General Financial Rules, 2017 of the Government of India is meant to be applied for disbursement of all grants to the extent possible. Public Finances to achieve greater effectiveness in Cash Management at both Central Government and State Government levels.

Government Circular No: Parichay-2023/ PR No.47/ Koshaprasad-4
It has been decided by the Government of India to propose an alternative fund flow mechanism, SNA-SPARSH (Real Time System of Integrated Quick Transfers) in a phased manner to disburse funds to Centrally Sponsored Schemes through an Integrated Framework of Fund Management System (PFMS), State Integrated Financial Management Information System (State IFMIS) and e-Kuber System of Reserve Bank of India. The schemes and States to be included under this proposed Alternative Fund Flow Mechanism will be notified by the Central Government from time to time.

A copy of this office memorandum is being made available to all the concerned administrative departments through this circular.
All the concerned administrative departments implementing the Centrally Sponsored Schemes should take a serious note of the aforesaid guidelines of the Government of India in the Office Memorandum dated 13th July, 2023. All stakeholders should make provisions for taking appropriate action as per the instructions received from the Government of India in the near future for implementation of the SNA-SPARSH Model.
