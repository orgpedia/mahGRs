In respect of Centrally Sponsored Schemes, necessary instructions have been issued vide Government Circular No.2 dated 5th December, 2023 regarding transfer of Central share of funds and matching State share of funds received from the Central Government to a single Nodal Account within the prescribed time frame.
Accordingly, the Finance Division will now send certified information about the funds deposited by the Central Government in the Consolidated Fund of the State to the Secretaries of all the Ministries and Departments on every working day. Hence, there is no need to refer the departments to the Finance Department.
In this background, the basic budgetary allocation made for Centrally Sponsored Schemes and the budgetary allocation made through Supplementary Demands for Grants, Central share and matching State share are being distributed 100% to all the Ministries/ Departments on BEAMS system.
In view of the instructions in the Government Circular dated 5th December, 2023 at Reference No. 2. The revised instructions are being issued by superseding the instructions on the disbursement of funds under Centrally Sponsored Schemes at Annexure No. 13 and Annexure No. 4 as under:
(1) The Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary of the concerned Ministries/ Departments shall receive the order of disbursement of the funds from the Central Government and confirm that the funds have been deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the State at their level on the basis of the e-mail sent by the Finance Branch to the Department on every working day. In the case of schemes where the funds released by the Central Government are received in the Consolidated Fund of the State, the funds of the said Central Account shall be matched by the State

The funds received from the Central Government shall be immediately disbursed for transfer to the single nodal account along with the balance State share within 30 calendar days from the date of deposit of the said funds in the Consolidated Fund of the State as prescribed by the Central Government vide letter dated 16.02.2023.
(2) Orders for disbursement of funds shall be issued within a period of 10 days from the date of receipt of funds from the Central Government and 30 calendars in any case after making payment to the Treasury.
It should be ensured that such funds are deposited in the single nodal account of the concerned scheme before the day. No need to send money to the finance department.
The Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary of the concerned Ministry/ Department will be responsible for taking action accordingly and to ensure that the funds are not disbursed unless the Central Government funds are received in the Consolidated Fund of the State.
4) In cases where reallocation is required as the budgetary provision is not sufficient, the necessary proposal should be submitted to the Finance Department in the prescribed manner.
Further, if the Central Government funds for the previous year are to be spent in the current year, it will be mandatory to obtain the consent of the Planning Department, Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and Tribal Development Department as well as the Finance Department, as the case may be.
6) If interest is to be paid as mentioned in the letter dated 16th February, 2023 of the Central Government, the entire responsibility of the same shall be of the concerned administrative department of the Ministry.
