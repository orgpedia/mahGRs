Proposals for disbursement of funds for the following objectives 05- Prizes, 12- Foreign Travel Expenses, 16- Publications, 17- Computer Expenses, 20- Other Administrative Expenses, 26- Advertisement and Publicity, 30- Other Contractual Services, 31- Subsidiary Grants (Non-Payment), 32- Contributions, 33- Financial Assistance, 35- Grants for Construction of Capital Trust, 50- Other Expenditure, 51- Motor Vehicles, 52- Machinery and Equipment, 53 Major Constructions and 54 Investments to the Finance Department through the Planning Department/ Social Justice and Special Assistance Department/ Tribal Development Department as per requirement with concrete support and after ensuring that the available funds are spent, will be forwarded to the Department of Finance on 14th February, 2023. All departments should note that proposals received after February 14, 2023 will not be accepted. Further, the sanction of Planning Department/ Social Justice and Special Assistance Department/ Tribal Development Department and Finance Department, as per requirement, should be obtained from the Supplementary Demand Fund before release of funds for the supply of August, 2022 and December, 2022.
Before disbursement of funds as above, the funds should be disbursed keeping in view the instructions in the Annexure along with the Government Circular dated 4 th April, 2022.
Since the savings in the budgetary provision will be useful in achieving the annual targets set under the Maharashtra Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Rules, the powers of the administrative departments to reallocate the budgetary provisions are being suspended and the administrative departments should not reallocate the funds without obtaining the approval of the Finance Department for mandatory expenditure in respect of reallocation and the Planning/ Social Justice and Special Assistance Department/ Tribal Development Department and Finance Department as required for programme expenditure. Also, the unspent amount should be deposited in government accounts if the funds were previously drawn and kept in bank/ self-financing accounts.
(5) It shall be ensured that the funds made available to the administrative departments within the aforesaid limits are spent in the distribution to the field offices or administrative departments under them. These funds shall not be kept lying unutilized in the bank accounts of their subordinate officers/ boards/ corporations, in their own provident fund accounts or outside the government accounts.
The administrative department shall strictly follow the guidelines and prevailing norms issued earlier by the Planning and Finance Department, the delegation of financial powers and the instructions given by the Finance Department from time to time, as mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook and the Financial Powers Rulebook, while distributing and spending the funds as above.
Further, the instructions issued and received from the Finance Department from time to time regarding the proceedings regarding the proposals to be purchased by the end of this financial year should be strictly followed.
