Circular: Supplementary Statement of Expenditure for the current financial year under Article 205 of the Constitution of India will be presented as usual in the December, 2023 session of the Legislature. In view of the provisions of the Constitution as well as the Maharashtra Budget Rule Book, the Supplementary Statement of Demands/ Expenditure is submitted to the Legislature in respect of the following matters: (a) Supplementary Demands or Appropriations not yet submitted to the Legislature in respect of amounts sanctioned or to be made out of the Contingency Fund.
(b) only such matters as may be prescribed by order of the Court and the expenditure in the form of loads and the expenditure on new matters which may be planned to be incurred after the approval of the Legislature by way of a supplementary demand.
(a) Typical demands relating to bringing the expenditure on new works to the notice of the Legislature.
2. In view of the limited resources of the State, in order to ensure proper planning of budgetary expenditure and control of budgetary expenditure, it is necessary to consider the proposal of urgent and urgent expenditure on bonded labour and court cases and supplementary demands for expenditure in connection with the Centrally Sponsored Scheme. Accordingly, supplementary demand proposals should be submitted by the departments only for the following purposes.
Supplementary demands/ appropriations not yet submitted to the Legislature in respect of amounts sanctioned or to be made out of the Contingency Fund.
(iii) For Central/ Externally Aided/ Centrally Sponsored Schemes, the Central and State share is not provided in the Budget or more than 50% of the provision is spent and funds are required for such matters.
(iv) expenditure items for which no provision has been made in the budget or new items announced in the budget speech.
For matters where more than 70% of the budgetary provisions have been spent and for which more provision is required.

In view of the above, all the Ministries/ Departments are required to forward all supplementary demands for mandatory and programme expenditure in respect of the above mentioned items to the Finance Department for registration in the first instance along with the information in Annexure-B, C and D depending upon the instructions and procedures in Annexure-A attached with the proposal.
In this regard, the schedule for forwarding the proposals regarding the supplementary demand for programme and mandatory expenditure to be included in the December, 2023 session to the Planning Department (Development Area Office)/ Social Justice and Special Assistance Department/ Tribal Development Department and Finance Department is given separately as follows.
