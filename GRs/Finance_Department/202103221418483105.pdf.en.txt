The National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) has sanctioned 411 projects (44 roads and 367 bridges) worth Rs. 814.2666 crore to the Government of Maharashtra under Rural Infrastructure Development Fund Phase XXVI (RIDF-XXVI). 651.4124 crore (Rupees Six hundred and fifty one crore forty one lakh twenty four thousand only). A brief outline of the program is as follows:
The loan is available for completion of 411 projects of Public Works Department upto 31/03/2023.
(2) The interest on the loan is payable at the rate of 2.75 per cent after every three months period of the calendar year, until further directions from NABARD.
(3) The loan amount to be sanctioned by NABARD will be based on the actual expenditure incurred on the projects for which the loan has been sanctioned since 01/04/2020. The amount of loan (excluding last instalment) to be withdrawn at each time will be in multiples of Rs.1 lakh and each demand will be Rs. Not less than 10 lakhs.
(4) The amount of the loan taken out at each time shall be deemed to be a separate loan for repayment. The loan is to be repaid in five equal annual installments within seven years. This seven-year period includes an initial grace period of two years.
(5) The total financial provision for the completion of the project as per the sanction order of NABARD will be made by the Government first. For this, the Public Works Department should ensure that the necessary budgetary provisions are made in the prescribed time and in the prescribed manner. After the actual expenditure, the loan amount from NABARD will be taken as reimbursement.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: NABARD-2020/ PR No.71/ FINANCE GOVERNMENT DECISION:
The Government have taken the following decisions in this regard:
(1) NABARD has sanctioned Rs. 411 projects (44 roads and 367 bridges) of Public Works Department under RIDF-26 for completion. 651.4124 crore (Rupees Six hundred and fifty one crore one hundred and forty four lakh twenty four thousand only) has been approved by the Government.
(2) The terms and conditions prescribed by NABARD in their letter dated 12/03/2021 have been accepted by the Government and the Government has taken note of the suggestions made by NABARD. The Finance Department will act as the coordinating department for this loan scheme.
(3) The Government shall pay to NABARD interest at the rate of 2.75 per cent per annum on the existing loan. The Government will pay the interest to NABARD as per the directions received from NABARD from time to time. The interest rate will be paid on the total loan balance for the quarter period. Interest will be paid on the first day of the month following each quarter of the calendar year. If the interest remains outstanding, the interest will be paid from the date of arrears at the rate of interest applicable at that time.
(4) The projects for which loans have been sanctioned during the year 2020-21. Demand for reimbursement of expenditure incurred directly from 01/04/2020 till date will be submitted to NABARD.
NABARD has relaxed the grace period to be considered as initial two years from the date of withdrawal of loan. NABARD has allowed the government to not repay the loan during this period. The government has decided to provide this facility. Therefore, the amount withdrawn at each time will be a separate loan and the loan will be repaid in equal installments over five years after the grace period of two years.
(6) The Chief Engineer, Regional Department of Public Works, Pune is being appointed as the Coordinating Officer for the above loan from NABARD. All the responsibilities related to the implementation of the program at the field level will be of the Coordinating Officer, Chief Engineer, Public Works Regional Department, Pune.
