Proposal: Department of Finance, Government of India. As on 20.8.2014, an advance limit has been fixed for the purchase of motor cycles, scooters, mopeds, bicycles for Government employees and three wheelers, either self-propelled or man-powered, for differently-abled State Government employees. Now, the Finance Department has issued a notification. As per the recommendations of the 7th Pay Commission, the pay scale of the State Government employees as on 30th January, 2019 has been revised to Rs. have been revised from 1.1.2016. As per the revised pay scale, the government was considering to increase the price limit and advance limit for the purchase of various types of vehicles.
Government's decision:
For the purchase of new Motor Cycles/ Scooters/ Mopeds/ Cycles and three wheeled Automatic Cycles or Man Powered Three Wheeled Cycles for Divyang State Government Employees, the advance will be admissible to the Government Officers/ Employees as per the revised wage limit under Rule 136, 137 and 139 of the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959 and subject to the conditions mentioned below.

(2) A copy of the permanent license obtained by one of the family members from the competent authority to drive a motor cycle/ scooter/ moped vehicle shall be submitted.
(3) The authority competent to grant the advance shall be satisfied that the officer/ employee applying for the advance before the advance is approved has the capacity to repay the entire advance before his/ her retirement.
(4) The competent authority may, after satisfying the requirements of the applicant, grant the vehicle in advance in accordance with the financial powers conferred by the Financial Powers Rules, 1978.
(4) The advance holder shall purchase the vehicle within one month from the date of advance approval of the vehicle. Also, the year of manufacture, date of purchase and registration documents of the purchased vehicle should be submitted to the government. In case of non-payment, the entire amount of the advance shall be recovered from the advance holder along with penal interest.
(6) The vehicle shall remain mortgaged with the Government until the full repayment of the vehicle advance with interest.
For this, the concerned employee will be required to fill the mortgage in the prescribed form and procedure. In case of non-payment, the entire amount of the advance will be recovered from the advance holder along with penal interest.

(7) Recovery of advance should be started from the next month in which the advance is disbursed.
(8) The applicant will be responsible for regular recovery of the vehicle advance as well as if the recovery is more than the sanctioned advance.
(9) Even if the advance holder remains on unauthorised leave or abstains from his duties for any other reason, the advance holder concerned shall be responsible for regular repayment of the vehicle advance and other advances from the Government.
(10) The rates of interest shall be as prescribed by the Finance Department from time to time.
(11) Also, no advance will be granted for the purchase of old motor cycles/ scooters/ mopeds/ bicycles and old three-wheelers for disabled state government employees.
(12) It shall be the responsibility of the concerned advance holder to insure motor cycles, scooters, mopeds, bicycles and automatic three-wheelers for disabled government employees, scooters with adoption and mopeds to the Government Insurance Fund and it shall be necessary to ensure that it continues.
(13) Advance will be admissible only once during the entire service period for bicycles/ two-wheelers/ three-wheelers. However, this condition is being relaxed for the purchase of three-wheeled bicycles for disabled state government employees.
(14). Applicants who have more than two children on or after 1st May, 2001 (except for second conjoined children) will not be eligible for the benefit of this advance.
The total deduction from the salary of a government officer/ employee should not be more than 50% of his monthly financial benefit.
