As per the Government Notification dated 30th January, 2019, it has been decided to credit the arrears of 7th Pay Commission to the Employees' Provident Fund Scheme account in 5 equal installments over the next 5 years from the year 2019-20 and pay the same in cash to the retired employees. The procedure in this regard has been prescribed by the Government Circular Finance Department, dated February 20, 2019. Further, instructions have been issued vide Government Circular dated 30th May, 2019 regarding payment of arrears in cash in 5 equal installments over 5 years to the employees who are covered under National Pension Scheme or Defined Contributory Pension Scheme.
Also, the payment of pension arrears in cash in five years, in five equal instalments, has been ordered as per the Government Decision dated 24th January, 2019 and 1st March, 2019.

In view of the situation arising out of the COVID-19 pandemic in the State and its adverse impact on the revenue collection of the State, the Government has decided to defer the payment of 2nd instalment of 7th Pay Commission arrears due as on 01.07.2020 to the State Government and other eligible employees and pensioners by one year, as per the decision of the Department of Finance, dated 23.06.2020.
Now the third installment of 7th Pay Commission arrears due to State Government and other eligible employees and pensioners as on 1st July, 2021.
However, the Covid-19 pandemic is not over yet.
Also, there were restrictions on daily transactions in the state for the last few months. As a result, the revenue collection has not improved.
In the above background, the question of payment of second and third instalments of arrears was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
The Government now directs that the amount of 2nd installment of 7th Pay Commission arrears of pay and pension respectively, payable to State Government and other eligible employees and retired employees as on 01.07.2020, shall be credited to the account of Provident Fund Scheme or paid in cash, as the case may be, as mentioned below:
(a) The amount of the second installment of pension arrears to the pensioners should be paid in cash along with the pension for the month of July, 2021.
The second installment of the arrears of the state government employees should be paid along with the salary for the month of August 2021.
(c) The second installment of arrears of eligible employees in all Zilla Parishads, Government aided schools and all other Government aided institutions should be paid along with the salary for the month of September, 2021.
In the case of employees in (b) and (c) above
(i) The dues of the employees to whom the provident fund scheme is applicable shall be credited to their provident fund account and the dues of the employees to whom the National Pension Scheme or the Defined Contributory Pension Scheme is applicable shall be paid in cash.

The employees (including employees covered under the Provident Fund Scheme) who have retired or died between 1st June, 2020 and the date of this Government Order, shall be paid the second instalment of arrears of salary in cash.
Interest on the amount of the second instalment of arrears to be credited to the Employees' Provident Fund Scheme account will be admissible from July 1, 2020.
The amount of the second installment deposited in the account of Provident Fund Scheme will not be withdrawn for 2 years from July 1, 2020, till June 30, 2022.
(4) The other provisions of the Government Orders No. 1 to 5 read above in respect of payment of dues shall be complied with.
The payment of 3rd installment of 7th Pay Commission arrears due as on 01.07.2021 to State Government and other eligible employees and retired employees is being kept in abeyance. Separate orders will be issued for payment of third instalment of arrears.
