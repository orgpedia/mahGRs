circular
In view of the expected shortfall in the State's tax and non-tax revenue in the financial year 2021-22 due to the outbreak of COVID-19 pandemic and its impact on the State's financial system, the Government has decided to take fiscal measures. As on 14.10.2021, for the first nine months of the financial year 2021-22, budget estimates, allocations and control, to the extent of 75% of the total annual allocation and 60% of the programme expenditure, excluding mandatory expenditure for 21-supply and material, 26 advertisement and publicity, 31-support grants (non-salary), 32-assistance and 33-finance, and 100% for MLA Local Area Development Programme and District Annual Plan
It is agreed to be distributed on the system (beams). Further, the process of disbursement of funds under 01-Salary, 04-Pension and 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salaries) as per requirement is being done at the level of Finance Department.
To give impetus to the economy of the state, 100 percent funds are now being made available to the administrative departments for the capital account programs and mandatory expenditure matters.
All the concerned administrative departments should plan the expenditure of the fund with respect to capital expenditure by March 31, 2022 and submit the proposals for distribution of funds to the Finance and Planning Department as per the requirement before February 15, 2022. All the departments should note that the Finance Department will not accept proposals for disbursement of funds received after 15 th February, 2022.
(4) The administrative departments shall ensure that the funds so made available to them are spent properly while disbursing the same to the field offices or administrative departments under them. These funds cannot be kept unutilized in the bank accounts of their subordinate officers/ boards/ corporations, in their personal provident fund accounts or outside the government accounts.
The administrative department shall strictly follow the guidelines and prevailing norms issued earlier by the Planning and Finance Department, the delegation of financial powers and the instructions given by the Finance Department from time to time, as mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook and the Financial Officers Rulebook, while distributing and spending the funds as above.
