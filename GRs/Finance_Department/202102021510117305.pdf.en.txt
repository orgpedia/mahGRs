Price limit of the house for advance approval of construction:
Finance Department No.Ghabhab-1015/ P.No.19/ Service-5, Dy. In the X and Y category of cities, the ceiling price of ready-to-build new house/ old house (excluding cost of land) is being prescribed at a maximum of Rs. 2.00 crore for new construction/ purchase and Rs. 1.00 crore for other places, as per the Government decision dated 16.12.2016.
The competent authority for advance approval of construction of houses shall have full powers to relax the ceiling limit up to 25% on case to case basis, based on merit. The amount should be submitted to the Finance Department.
Recovery of house construction advance:
(a) The recovery of the advance with interest shall be made in such manner as to be completed before the retirement of the advance holder. For the purposes of A, E and F above, interest shall be charged in the first 192 monthly instalments of the principal advance and then 48 monthly instalments, for a maximum period of 20 years. The amount of interest shall be recovered in a maximum of 96 monthly installments, first 72 monthly installments of the original advance and then 24 monthly installments. The advance holder can increase the amount of monthly instalment to pay the advance and interest amount in a shorter period as per his wish.
In cases where the service period of the advance holder is less than the above mentioned time period, the period of recovery of principal and interest should be fixed in such a way that the recovery of principal and interest is completed before retirement, taking into account the remaining service period.
If the house building advance is sanctioned in lump sum, the recovery of the advance should be started from the next month immediately after disbursement of the advance.
If the amount of the house building advance is sanctioned in more than one instalment, the recovery of the advance should be started from the corresponding month after the disbursement of the first instalment of the advance.

The following conditions shall apply for the advance approval:
(1) The officer/ employee who is to be sanctioned the house construction in advance should have been appointed in the service of the Government as per the rules of recruitment to the post concerned and should have served at least 5 years after such appointment.
Similarly, at the time of sanctioning the advance, it is mandatory for the officer/ employee to have a minimum of 5 years of service left.
(2) For all the above purposes, advance shall be admissible only once in the entire Government service.
However, the amount equal to the difference between the first advance sanctioned and the house construction advance permissible under this Government decision for the repair of the house constructed/ purchased by taking advance from the Government arising out of natural calamity can be allowed for the second time as house construction advance. The amount of this advance shall be equal to the difference mentioned above or the cost of repairing the house or₹ 21 lakh, whichever is less, shall be admissible as advance under this purpose for repairing the house.
(3) A registered mortgage shall be required to be taken in advance for any purpose within the meaning of the Housing Rules. Also, a personal bond/ surety, etc. will be required.
