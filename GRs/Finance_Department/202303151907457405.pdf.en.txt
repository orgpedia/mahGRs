Government Circular Every year in the budget session in the month of March, funds are made available to various departments through supplementary demand and revised budget. Available funds. Since it is mandatory to submit the payments to the Treasury/ Sub-Treasury as well as to the Accounts and Accounts Office by the end of March 31, the number of payments submitted to the Accounts and Accounts Office and the Treasury/ Sub-Treasury Office in the month of March increases considerably. As a result, systems such as treasury nets, beams, and servicing are stressed, causing breakdowns and slow down. Also, there is not enough time to make payments. Also, payments made late at night at the sub-treasury level cannot be passed due to bank working hours. Thus, to overcome the difficulties that often arise at the end of March, all administrative departments, all offices under them, Drawing and Disbursing Officers as well as the Pay and Accounts Office/ all treasuries/ sub-treasuries should take care of the following actions.
1). Continuation of late payment counters in the Treasury/ Sub-Treasury Offices and in the Offices of Accounts and Accounts on 31st March, 2023: In order to avoid wastage of Government funds on 31st March, 2023, the Offices of Accounts and Accounts, District Treasury Offices and Offices of Sub-Treasuries will remain open till late night on 31st March, 2023. This will enable the acceptance of payments and the completion of ECS/ NEFT/ CMP/ e-Kuber payments.
2). Approval for making Government payments by cheque on 31st March, 2023: Dy. On March 31, 2023, the branches of the Reserve Bank of India, State Bank of India and other banks handling the financial affairs of the government in Mumbai and Nagpur will remain open till late at night, but after 12.00 pm, due to changes in the date in the computer system of the banks, the transaction is recorded on the next date. Therefore, the Reserve Bank of India has been directed to ensure accurate accounting of government transactions. 5) Non-submission of payments required to be submitted at the District Treasury level at the Sub-Treasury level: The payments of large amounts of personal benefits from the grants received through the Revised Budget and Supplementary Demands are submitted to the Sub-Treasury Office at the end of the year by the Revenue Department, Agriculture Department, Tribal Development Department, Public Health Department, etc.
Since there is only one scribe and one assistant accountant/ sub-accountant working at the sub-treasury level, there is a limitation in scrutinizing such payments and there is a possibility of irregularities. Therefore, such payments should be submitted to the District Treasury Office only, not to the Sub-Treasury Office.
All the Drawing and Disbursing Officers at the sub-treasury level should be given written instructions in this regard.

7. Controlling Authorities to make available the grants received through Supplementary Demand on BEAMS system expeditiously:
After sanctioning of grants to various departments by the government as per the eighth revised budget and supplementary demands, the administrative departments and heads of departments provide grants to the drawing and disbursing officers on the BEAMS system without any delay, after which there is a delay in the preparation of payments, submission to the sub-treasury etc. Due to the increased load. On 31st March, there are instances of breakdown of BEAMS system as well as Treasury Net system due to additional stress. For this, the revised budgetary/ supplementary demand based subsidy should be made available on BEAMS system by all the Departments/ Controlling Authorities immediately.
Action to be taken by the Divisional Joint Director in accordance with the activities of the Department for the month of March: A. The Divisional Joint Director offices should control the implementation of the instructions, circulars, government decisions received from the Government and the Directorate level in accordance with the activities of the Department for the month of March at the Treasury/ Sub Treasury level.
B. Vacancies in the Treasury offices under the Divisional Joint Director's Office should be reviewed and staff at the level of Joint Director should be made available by planning wherever possible in the Treasury where manpower is insufficient to carry out the work.
A review meeting of the officers of the Treasury/ Sub-Treasury offices should be held to settle the work for the month of March and accordingly, guidance and measures should be taken in accordance with the problems raised by the Treasury offices.
The working of the treasury to be done by the end of March should be controlled by the offices of the Joint Director and its progress report should be submitted to the Directorate, Accounts and Treasury Office from time to time.
