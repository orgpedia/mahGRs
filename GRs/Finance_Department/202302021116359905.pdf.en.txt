Circular:
As usual, the Supplementary Statement of Expenditure for the current financial year under Article 205 of the Constitution of India will be presented in the March, 2023 Session of the Legislature. In view of the provisions of the Constitution as well as the Maharashtra Budget Rule Book, the Supplementary Statement of Demands/ Expenditure in respect of the matter mentioned below is submitted to the Legislature.
(a) matters in respect of which no supplementary demands or appropriations have so far been submitted to the Legislature in respect of the amounts sanctioned or to be made out of the Contingency Fund.
(b) only such matters as may be prescribed by order of the Court and the expenditure in the form of loads and the expenditure on new matters which may be planned to be incurred after obtaining approval from the Legislature by way of a supplementary demand.
(a) Typical demands relating to bringing the expenditure on new works to the notice of the Legislature.
2. In view of the limited resources of the State, in order to ensure proper planning of budgetary expenditure and control of budgetary expenditure, it is necessary to consider proposals for urgent and urgent expenditure on bonded labour, court cases and supplementary demands for expenditure in respect of Centrally Sponsored Schemes.
Accordingly, supplementary demand proposals should be submitted by the departments only for the following purposes.
(1) Requests for supplies not yet submitted to the Legislature in respect of amounts sanctioned or to be made out of the Contingency Fund.
(2) for the provision to be made in a court case,
(3) For cases where more than 90% of the budgetary provision has been spent and for which funds are not available for the current financial year by way of reallocation/ reallocation and more provision is required, (4) For cases where provision for Central/ Externally Aided/ Centrally Sponsored Schemes has not been made in the budget or more than 80% of the provision has been made and more funds are required,

In view of the aforesaid facts, all the Ministries/ Departments are required to send all the Supplementary Demands for Compulsory and Programme Expenditure in respect of the aforesaid items along with the proposal in Annexure-A and the information in Annexure-B, C and D, depending upon the procedure, to the Department of Finance for registration in the first instance.
In this regard, the schedule for forwarding the proposals regarding the supplementary demand for programme and mandatory expenditure to be included in the March, 2023 session to the Planning Department (Development Area Office)/ Social Justice and Special Assistance Department/ Tribal Development Department and Finance Department is given separately as follows. Since the registration of supplementary demand proposals will be done online on the BEAMS system, it is imperative that all the concerned departments take action as per the prescribed schedule.

Note:- Since the registration of Supplementary Demand Proposals is being done through online mode, if the status of all Supplementary Demands (including the information in Annexure B, C and D) and the report on BEAMS system is not received by the Finance Department's Budget Branch within the prescribed time after checking it from the concerned Budget Office, then the proposals of such Supplementary Demands will not be accepted due to the online process and the entire responsibility will be of the concerned administrative department. Therefore, it is mandatory for the departments to register all the proposals with the Finance Department in the first instance.
