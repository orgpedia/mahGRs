Guidelines on Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jayanti 2021.
This year is Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jayanti. It will be celebrated on 11th April 2021. In view of the current situation of the second wave of COVID-19 and the increasing number of Corona patients, the Department of Revenue and Forest, Ministry of Disaster Management, Relief and Rehabilitation, Mumbai, vide its order dated 4th April, 2021, vide its order No. It is necessary to celebrate Mahatma Jotiba Phule Jayanti this year in a simple manner by following lines 1, 2 and 13. Accordingly, the following guidelines have been issued:
Every year on April 11, the birth anniversary of Mahatma Jyotiba Phule is celebrated in the state.
This year, Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jayanti is expected to be celebrated from 7.00 am to 8.00 pm in a very simple manner to avoid the growing outbreak of COVID-19.
2) This year, in view of the increasing outbreak of COVID-19 virus, the number of followers at the place of paying floral tributes to the statue or statue of Mahatma Jyotiba Phule should not be more than 5 at a time.
There should also be a celebration of Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jayanti by strictly following all the rules of social distancing and hygiene (masks, sanitizers, etc.).
Every year on the occasion of Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jayanti, various programs are organized across Maharashtra. But this year, in view of the increasing outbreak of the Covid-19 virus, the performance of songs, lectures, street plays, etc. in public places or any other cultural programs should not be organized on the occasion of the birth anniversary.
4) Health related activities/ camps on Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jayanti (e.g. Blood donation) can be organized with prior permission of the local administration and through this awareness can be created about diseases like Corona, Malaria, Dengue, etc. and their preventive measures as well as cleanliness. However, while organizing health related activities, special attention should be paid to the observance of social distancing as well as hygiene rules (masks, sanitizers, etc.) at the venue.
5) To prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus in the state, the rules prescribed by the Government Relief and Rehabilitation, Health, Environment, Medical Education Department as well as the concerned Municipal Corporation, Police Administration, Local Administration should be strictly followed. Also, if any other guidelines are issued by the government after this circular and in the period between the start of the actual birth anniversary program, they should also be followed.
