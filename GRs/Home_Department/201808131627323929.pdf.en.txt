Circular:
Tehseen Poonawalla filed a writ petition in the Supreme Court regarding mob lynching. 754/2016 has been filed. Accordingly, on 17.07.2018, the Hon. The Supreme Court has given the verdict. Paragraph 40 of the said Order lays down the detailed directions as to the action to be taken by the Central Government and the concerned State Governments and others. The State Government should take the following steps in this regard:
Preventive measures: 1. As per the Supreme Court's order, all superintendents of police in Maharashtra are being appointed as nodal officers in their districts. To assist the nodal officers, one Deputy Superintendent of Police rank officer in their district is being deputed to take preventive measures against mob violence and mob lynching. The Deputy Commissioner of Police of the concerned Circle is being appointed as the Nodal Officer in the office of the Commissioner of Police and an Assistant Commissioner of Police of the concerned Circle is being appointed as his assistant for his assistance. Detailed appointment orders in this regard should be issued by the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the Commissioner of Police, Brihanmumbai.
(2) Nodal officers shall constitute a special task force to receive intelligence/ reports on such incidents and persons likely to indulge in such violent activities or to spread hatred, false news, rumours, inflammatory statements etc.
(1) The Nodal Officer shall instruct the officers-in-charge of the concerned police station in his jurisdiction to take more precautions in the areas where such incidents are likely to occur.
(2) The Nodal Officer shall hold regular meetings with the local intelligence unit of the district at least once in a month. Police officers in their respective areas should be called for these meetings. Identification of miscreants, mob violence or those who take law into their hands, stop dissemination of inflammatory propaganda material.

(3) If any caste or tribe is subjected to such violence, the Nodal Officer shall take appropriate decision and take action to destroy such atmosphere.
(4) The Nodal Officer shall take the help of the Police Patil in his area of jurisdiction to prevent such incidents from occurring.
(4) If a police officer is of the opinion that a group or group has a violent tendency or a tendency to take the law into their own hands and commit violence, it shall be the responsibility of the officer to disperse such group/ group in accordance with the provisions of Section 129 of the Indian Penal Code.
5) The Director General of Police should issue a circular to all the Commissioners of Police and all the Superintendents of Police regarding the police patrolling in sensitive areas in view of the past incidents and the intelligence report received by the police. There should be seriousness in patrolling in such a way that anti-social elements involved in the above mentioned crimes remain within the framework of law and are not afraid to take law into their own hands.
6) The district level media should give instructions to the effect that the consequences of mob violence or taking the law into their hands will be very serious under the law. Nodal officers should also hold meetings with police personnel in their areas and instruct them to pass on the information to their villages.
7) The nodal officer should take action to prevent those who display inflammatory and irresponsible messages and videos on various social websites or instigate incidents of mob violence and taking the law into their own hands.
8) The police shall register a First Information Report (FIR) under Section 153 (A) of the Indian Penal Code or other relevant sections against the persons responsible for the incidents of mob violence or taking the law into their own hands, and against the persons responsible for circulating irresponsible messages or videos on social media.
(a) The measures:
(1) If, despite taking preventive action, the police come to know of any incident of mob violence, the police station under whose jurisdiction the incident took place shall immediately file a First Information Report (FIR) under the Indian Penal Code or any other law.
(2) The Chief Police Officer of the police station in which such a first information report (complaint) has been filed shall inform the district nodal officer concerned of the incident.
