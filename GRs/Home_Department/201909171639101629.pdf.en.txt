There are 60 jails in the state, including Central Jail-9, District Jail 1-19, District Jail 2-23 and District Jail 3-9. The number of undertrial prisoners in jails is 26,559 i.e. 75%.
The State Government has entered into a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Azim Premji Philanthropics Initiative Private Limited (APPI) for providing legal aid/ advice to the inmates for the purpose of preparing a model manual for providing legal aid/ assistance to the under-trial prisoners in the jails as well as for reducing the number of under-trial prisoners in the jails. A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) has been signed on 28th June, 2018 and the proceedings are in progress. However, the Government was considering setting up of a Committee for more effective implementation of the MoU.
Government Decision: A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was signed between the State Government and Azim Premji Philanthropic Initiatives (APPI) for taking action on the matters related to reducing the number of undertrial prisoners in the jails in the State, providing legal assistance to the undertrial prisoners in coordination with the District Legal Services Authority, increasing the capacity of the lawyers assisting the authority in legal matters, increasing the strength of the District Legal Services Authorities. It was held on June 28, 2018. As per the MoU, the Government is approving the constitution of Project Implementation and Monitoring Committee as follows:

The functions and principles of the Committee will be as follows: To take necessary measures to reduce the number of undertrial prisoners in the jails of the State, to provide legal assistance to the undertrial prisoners in coordination with the District Legal Services Authority, to increase the capacity of the legal aid lawyers of the authority, to increase the strength of the District Legal Services Authority, to prepare a detailed and comprehensive plan for the implementation of the project, to review the scheme quarterly and to exchange information on the progress of the programme and the difficulties faced by it.
The responsibilities of the Committee will be as follows: 1. To ensure that the day-to-day functioning of these programmes is carried out smoothly.
Suggest necessary amendments/ changes in the implementation, methodology and policy of these programmes.
To enter into necessary agreements with other departments of the Government as well as with external agencies, to ensure that the financial, legal and ethical values of the project implementers are met.
4. To support the implementation of good quality programmes in the State Prisons.
To select expert person/ institution to conduct research as per requirement to reduce the number of undertrial prisoners in the prison.
Determining the duties and responsibilities of social workers and project staff/ officers.
7. Judicial Prisoners, Police, Public Prosecutors and Ma. Permitting the conduct of training for judges with the permission of SLSA/ DLSA.
Implementation of the programme as per the MoU.
Azim Premji Philanthropics Initiative Private Limited (APPI) is responsible for providing financial support to the programme.
The programme will be implemented at Mumbai Central Jail (Arthur Road), Byculla District Jail, Taloja Central Jail, Thane Central Jail, Kalyan District Jail and Latur District Jail.
The programme will be implemented by Fair Trial Fellowship Programme (NLU Delhi) at Yerwada Central Jail, Pune and Nagpur Central Jail, Nagpur.
4. Preparation of Interview App on pilot basis in Yerawada Jail.
To develop a convenient computer system for providing legal advice in the prison and to make the inmates aware of the legal aid provided or to cooperate with the E-PRISMS.
6. Appointment of experts/ institutions in the field of law, social work, psychology, information technology and other disciplines on contract basis with the help of the Prison Department for implementation of this programme.
Appoint a senior officer to coordinate the programme with the State Government.
The employees of APPI and other affiliates will ensure that the information received by the program implementers remains confidential.
Responsibility of Prison Administration: (1) To ensure the success of the programme, an authorized person of the executing agency shall be given access to the prison, provided with working space and working materials.
