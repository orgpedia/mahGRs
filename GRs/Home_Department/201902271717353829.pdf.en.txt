The National Human Rights Commission had taken suo motu cognizance of a news item published in The Indian Express dated 15.06.2018 with the headline" Three Dalit children battered naked after swimming in village well." The National Human Rights Commission has directed the Chief Secretary, State of Maharashtra to issue guidelines to the concerned authorities to keep a close watch on such incidents, especially in such sensitive areas, to avoid recurrence of such incidents.
Based on the above observations, the following recommendations are issued. These instructions should be followed by all the concerned authorities with promptness.
All necessary action to maintain law and order and peace in areas dominated by Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes is taken by the concerned district administration. In order to prevent injustice to the people belonging to Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes, the District Administration shall identify sensitive areas where incidents of atrocity are likely to occur or where there is a threat of recurrence of such incidents leading to commission of offences under the said Act.
The concerned District Magistrate and the District Superintendent of Police/ Commissioner of Police or other officer shall visit such identified sensitive areas and review the law and order situation there.
3. Cancellation of arms licences issued to persons not belonging to Scheduled Castes/ Tribes, their close relatives, servants, family friends, etc. in the sensitive areas identified as per requirement and deposit the same in the Government armoury.

(7) A Vigilance and Monitoring Committee shall be constituted to suggest measures for effective implementation of the provisions of the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995.
8. Awareness centres should be set up to educate the people belonging to SC/ STs about their rights and protection under various laws, rules and schemes of the State and Central Governments and workshops should be organised in identified sensitive areas or other areas.
Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) should be encouraged by providing necessary financial and other assistance for setting up and arranging awareness centers, organizing workshops.
10. Special police forces to be deployed as per requirement in identified vulnerable areas.
The provisions of the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995 and the cases registered thereunder along with the proceedings conducted by the above-named Committees, Investigating Officers, Special Public Prosecutors and other Police Officers should be reviewed every quarter, including law and order.
12. The District Magistrate and the Superintendent of Police/ Commissioner of Police concerned shall visit the area where the atrocity incident took place, assess the loss of life and property there, and prepare a list of the victims/ their family members and dependents for payment of compensation.
The Superintendent of Police/ Commissioner of Police concerned shall ensure that the First Information Report is registered in the concerned police station and steps are taken to apprehend the culprits concerned.
After the incident of atrocity, the concerned Superintendent of Police/ Commissioner of Police shall immediately inspect the scene of the incident and take preventive measures as required. Also, adequate police force will be deployed to investigate the incident.
The concerned District Magistrate, Sub-Divisional Magistrate or any other Executive Magistrate shall take necessary administrative and other measures to provide relief in cash or kind or both to the victims of the incident of atrocity and their dependents within a period of seven days. This assistance is as mentioned in Annexure-I and II of the Schedule annexed to the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995.

should be made available. This includes food, water, clothing, shelter, medical care, and other necessities.
If the District Magistrate, the Sub Divisional Magistrate, the Executive Magistrate or any other police officer not below the rank of Deputy Superintendent of Police, on receipt of information, after inquiry as required by him, is satisfied that an offence under the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995 has been committed or threatened to be committed by a person belonging to the Scheduled Castes, the Scheduled Tribes or a group of persons residing in the local area within his jurisdiction or frequently visiting such place and is of the opinion that there is sufficient ground for proceeding further, such area shall be declared as sensitive to the subject of atrocity and necessary preventive action shall be taken for maintenance of peace and good behaviour, law and order therein.
The District Collectors as well as the Sub-Divisional Magistrates shall hold regular meetings with the Vigilance Committees, Control Committees at the district level as well as at the sub-division level in order to provide justice to the victims of atrocity incidents. Special public prosecutors will also be appointed as per requirement.
The Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance and the Department of Tribal Development shall provide financial assistance to the Police Department from its budgetary provision for conducting training camps and capacity building camps for the police officers working in the area for effective implementation of the provisions of the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995.
All the concerned training institutions shall conduct training sessions for police officers/ trainee police officers/ staff to make them aware of the provisions of the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995.
The Divisional Commissioner shall conduct training for the officers/ staff under him in respect of implementation of the provisions of the Schedule to the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995.
