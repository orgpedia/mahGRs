With a view to strictly enforce the provisions of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, the Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989 and the Bombay Motor Vehicle Tax Act, 1958, and to curb illegal traffic, vehicles have been provided to the Transport Commissioner's Office, various Regional/ Sub-Regional Transport Offices, Border Check Posts and Officers in the State. The inspection teams of every office are assigned the target of collecting maximum revenue.
The implementation of this target is being pursued by the Transport Commissioner's Office and the government.
Since the government vehicles are continuously used for the purpose of revenue collection, the vehicles become old and worn out after a few years, and in the case of some vehicles, the repair cost is unreasonable, such old vehicles have been registered by the Transport Commissioner's Office and Regional/ Sub-Regional Transport Offices as per the provisions of Section 19A and Rule 69 of Part 1, Sub-Division 2 of the Financial Powers Rulebook, 1978.
