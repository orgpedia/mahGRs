The Government Circular dated 29th January, 2019 is hereby cancelled. The revised circular in this regard. With the approval of the High Court, the following is being issued:
The proposal: The High Court in its Writ Petition 12/2019 dated 15th April, 2019, filed at Bombay High Court, referred to the draft circular submitted by the Government regarding the grant of police protection/ assistance in accordance with the ODAS issued by the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate or District Magistrate under Section 14 of the SARFAESI Act, 2002. The court has granted it.
Accordingly, the Government of Maharashtra has decided to issue a circular to the police officers at the divisional and zonal level to ensure proper implementation of the orders issued by the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate or District Magistrate under Section 14 of the Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 (SARFAESI Act) for recovery of loans given by banks and financial institutions, including urban cooperative banks, to their borrowers for getting physical possession of the secured assets. Under Section 14 of the SARFAESI Act, 2002, the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate or District Magistrate shall take action in accordance with the following instructions to provide police assistance during the execution of the orders made.
Circular:
All Commissioners of Police and Superintendents of Police in the State shall provide adequate police force on demand for smooth implementation of the orders passed by the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate or District Magistrate in respect of recovery of loans given by banks and financial institutions under Section 14 of the SARFAESI Act, 2002.

(2) The Inspector-in-charge of the concerned police station shall render necessary assistance to the bank within 10 days of receipt of the bank's application in accordance with the order of action received from the Advocate Court Commissioner appointed by the Manager or Chief Metropolitan Magistrate under section 14 of the SARFAESI Act, 2002 or the Tahsildar or Circle Officer appointed by the District Magistrate. The inspector in charge of the concerned police station should take immediate action considering the availability of necessary police personnel. The Inspector-in-charge of the concerned police station shall communicate his decision in this regard to the concerned authority as soon as possible before the date of the proposed action.
(3) The police officer shall not ask for or verify the loan and security documents from the concerned bank or financial institution while providing police assistance on demand. The police officers concerned should not record statements of bank officials, borrowers, mortgagors or persons occupying secured properties.
(4) The police officers shall assist the authority concerned in taking possession of the property for execution of the orders passed by the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate or District Magistrate. Force should be used when necessary to enforce such orders. The officials of the bank or financial institution should record the proceedings on video and preserve it.
5) After the provision is provided, the concerned bank should charge the provisioning fee as per the rules. The amount collected should be duly deposited in the government treasury and a proper record should be maintained.
All the above directions regarding recovery of loans by banks and financial institutions under SARFAESI Act, 2002 shall be strictly followed.
