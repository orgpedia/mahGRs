The Proposal:
The State Level Dog Training Centre under the aegis of Additional Director General of Police, Crime Investigation Department, Maharashtra State, Pune is presently functioning at Pune City Police Headquarters, Shivajinagar, Pune. The training center was established in 1964. At the state level dog training centre, the dogs of the Commissioner of Police/ Superintendent of Police units of the Maharashtra State Police are trained for different types of tasks such as crime detector, bomb detector, drug detector and how to use the agility and agility of the dog in police investigation.
The present Dog Training Centre, Pune is functioning in a small area of 5 to 6 guntas of land and the construction there is dilapidated. This space is inconvenient for training dogs, dog handlers, dog trainers and there is insufficient space for training. Also, the dogs in the dog squad and BDDS team of the Maharashtra State Police Force have to be sent to the dog training center in the outside state as the space available at the Dog Training Center, Criminal Investigation Department, Pune is insufficient.
Therefore, in order to create an increased capacity of dog training in the state, 65 kennels, equipped with 50 dog training capacity, all facilities, state-of-the-art dog training centers should be created.
