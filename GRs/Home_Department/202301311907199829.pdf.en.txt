In accordance with the provisions of the Government Decision" All India Services (Assessment of Work) Rules, 2007" and the Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs, Circular No. 17011/3/2003-IPS.II, dated 21/11/2008, all officers of the Maharashtra cadre of the Indian Police Service, for the year 2022-23, should get the medical check-ups specified in Annexure-II, from the Revenue Department wise designated hospitals in Annexure-I, as per the following instructions:
(1) The medical examination shall be conducted before 31.03.2023 in the hospital specified in Annexure-I for the Revenue Department in which the I.P.S. officer is posted.
(2) The B.P.S. Officer posted in the Amravati Revenue Division should conduct medical check-ups at the hospital specified in Annexure-I for other nearby Revenue Divisions.
(3) If there is a provision for medical examination in the Government/ Municipal Hospital in the Revenue Department in which the I.P.S. officer is posted, the said medical examination can also be conducted there.
4) The I.P.S. officers should first bear the cost of the medical check-up and then get reimbursement for the said cost.
5) The officials who reimburse the expenses of the said medical examinations. Department, No. The reimbursement of expenses on medical tests admissible once in every two years under MAG-1098/ PR No. 220/ Health-3, dated 31.08.2006 shall not be payable.
6) The B.P.S. officers should contact the concerned hospital and fix the date of medical examination. Also, they should take necessary preparations/ precautions for medical check-ups in consultation with the hospital.
7) These medical examinations will be conducted at one place and on one day. However, in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, it should be done on the next day.
The concerned Government/ Municipal Hospital/ Private Hospital referred to in Annexure-II shall submit the report of the prescribed format of medical examination of the I.P.S. Officers to the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, within eight days after the medical examination.
Also, the hospitals should submit a copy of the medical examination report to the concerned B.P.S.
the officer as well.
9) The day of the medical examination (one day) will be considered a duty day. If the medical examination is conducted on a government holiday, no transfer leave or any other leave will be admissible.

11) Before going for a medical check-up, the BPS officers will be required to give advance instructions to their superiors.

(13) If an IAS officer wants to get his medical check-up done at a designated hospital in a revenue division other than the revenue division in which he is posted, he can get his medical check-up done at the authorized hospital in the desired revenue division at the permissible rate.
