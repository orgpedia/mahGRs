GOVERNMENT DECISION, GENERAL ADMINISTRATION DEPARTMENT, NO. BAPRASE-2019/ Q.No. 251/2019/9, dated 11.12.2019& D. 13.12.2019
The All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 have been made applicable to the officers of the Indian Police Service with effect from 2008-09. In accordance with Rule 3 and Rule 4 thereof, and in terms of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, vide letter No. 17011/ 2003-IPS-1, dated 21.11.2008, all Indian Police Service officers in the State are required to get their medical examinations done annually in the prescribed format and a summary of their medical examination report is to be attached with the annual performance evaluation report. Accordingly, it is proposed to conduct medical examination of Indian Police Service officers of Maharashtra cadre for the year 2019-20. The Government has decided to conduct Medical Examination of the Indian Police Service Officers of Maharashtra Cadre for the year 2019-20 as under:
Government's decision:
The All India Services (Assessment of Work) Rules, 2007. 3 and in accordance with Sample No. 4 thereof, vide letter No. 3 of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. 17011/3/2003-IPS-II, dated 21.11.2008, all the Indian Police Service officers in the State should get their medical check-up done in the prescribed format in the year 2019-20 from the private hospitals identified revenue division wise as per the accompanying Form A. At the time of the examination, the following procedures should be carried out:
All Indian Police Service officers working in the Maharashtra State Cadre as well as officers on central deputation shall get all the medical check-ups mentioned in Form B done during the year 2019-2020 from the private hospitals/ medical institutions mentioned in Form A in the Revenue Department in which they are working.
2.The BHOPOSA officers working in the Amravati Revenue Division should get the medical check-up done from the private hospitals designated for other revenue divisions near them.
Medical examination can also be conducted in the government hospitals of the concerned district or government/ municipal hospitals of the revenue division where the facility of conducting all the medical examinations prescribed in Form-B is available.

The officers of the Indian Police Service shall get all the medical tests indicated in Form B done from the hospitals designated for the respective revenue divisions and shall first pay the amount required for the medical tests to the hospital and get reimbursement from their office. The reimbursement of the cost of annual medical check-up under this Government decision shall not be paid to the officials who reimburse the cost of medical check-up once in every two years as per the Government Decision, Public Health Department, No.MAG-1098/ PR No. 220/ Health 3, dated 31st August, 2006.
Officers of the Indian Police Service shall, within the period from the date of this Government Order to 31st March, 2020, mutually contact the concerned hospital, fix the dates of examination and conduct all medical examinations in Form B mutually.
The hospitals in Form A approved by the Government for medical examination shall submit the consolidated report of the medical examination of the officers of the Indian Police Service, in the accompanying Form B, to the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai (Office- I) within eight days of the completion of the examination and a copy thereof to the concerned officer of the Indian Police Service.
All the medical check-ups mentioned in Form B will be done at one place and on the same day. But in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, it should be done on the next day. The date and time of examination should be mutually decided by the Indian Police Service officers and the concerned hospitals. Also, the necessary precautions for the tests should be taken in consultation with the hospital.
One day for medical examination will be considered as duty time. No leave/ transfer leave will be admissible if the examination is conducted on a public holiday.
Since the period of medical check-up is deemed to be the" duty" period, it shall be payable at the same rate as the travelling allowance/ daily allowance (if applicable) paid to an officer of the Indian Police Service while he is otherwise on tour. The rates of such Travel Allowance/ Daily Allowance shall be as per the orders issued by the Finance Department from time to time. This should be considered and provided by the concerned salary issuing office.
Before going for the medical check-up, the officers will be required to give prior intimation to their immediate superiors that they are going to the headquarters or to a hospital outside the headquarters for the said check-up.
The expenditure thereon shall be defrayed under the head of accounts applicable to the salary and allowances at the establishment of the officer concerned and shall be met from the grants sanctioned for that year.
12. If any BHOPO officer wants to get his medical check-up done in a hospital other than the revenue department where he is working, he is allowed to get his medical check-up done at the rate permissible in the hospital authorized by the desired revenue department.
