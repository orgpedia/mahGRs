As per Rule 10 (4) and Rule 62 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, in the public interest, the age of 50/55 is prescribed for officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity.
It is the policy of the Government to review their services and retire them prematurely from Government service in the fifth year or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. As per the Government decision dated 02.08.2019, a Departmental Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary (Transport).
The Departmental Review Committee under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary (Transport) in its meeting held on 17.08.2023 has proposed to review the services of the Deputy Regional Transport Officers, Group-A cadre officers in the Transport Department at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. As per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, the services of the said officers have been evaluated.

The Government was considering to grant recognition to the officers who have been declared eligible to continue in service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
Government's decision
As per the recommendation of the Divisional Review Committee in its meeting held on 17.08.2023, the Deputy Regional Transport Officer, Group-A Cadre Officers of the Transport Department, as mentioned in the attached Form-A, are in the age group of 50/55 years or 30 years.
Years of qualifying service are being allowed to continue whichever is earlier.
The service of the Deputy Regional Transport Officer mentioned in the accompanying Form-A should be recorded in his service book after 50/55 years of service or after completion of 30 years of qualifying service.
