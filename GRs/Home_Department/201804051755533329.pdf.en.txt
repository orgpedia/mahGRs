The Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs has launched the" Special Infrastructure Scheme (SIS), including construction of 250 Fortified Police Stations in LWE affected States" for three years from 2017-18 to 2019-20. The total cost of the project is Rs. 1,048 crore, of which 60 per cent is Rs. 628.80 crore is the Central share and 40% i.e. The State share is Rs. 419.20 crore. This scheme is a" State Sector Scheme" and is to be implemented by the State Governments. The scheme will be implemented on a reimbursement basis.
The Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs has prescribed guidelines for the said scheme. As per these instructions, a State Level Committee with Nodal Officer (LWE) as its member is to be constituted for the purpose of approving the Action Plan/ Work Plan to be submitted by all the States to the Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs and to monitor and evaluate the progress of the projects/ works sanctioned under the said scheme and ensure their timely completion. Accordingly, the matter of constituting such a committee for the state of Maharashtra was under the consideration of the government.
In this regard, the Government has taken the following decision after considering the proposal submitted by the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, vide letter No.DGP/ 21A/ 195/ PCC-Meeting/ 151/2017, dated 30.12.2017, regarding the constitution of the said Committee.
