Holi, Dhulivandan and Rangpanchami-2022 Guidelines.
The this year. Holi will be celebrated on March 17, Dhulivandan on March 18 and Rangpanchami on March 22, 2022. Due to Covid-19, for the last two years, all religious festivals, festivals and various programs have been celebrated by the people in large numbers without gathering, preferably staying at home. As the corona infection is still going on, it is necessary for the citizens to celebrate Holi, Dhulivandan and Rangpanchami festivals without gathering in large numbers or celebrating in crowds, taking care of their own and others' health. In this regard, the following guidelines are being issued: 1. Circular No.
DMU/ 2020/ CR.92/ DisM-1, d. Follow the guidelines as of March 1, 2022.
Holi/ Shimoga is celebrated with great enthusiasm all over Maharashtra. This year's. Holi will be celebrated on March 17, 2022. Due to the Covid infection, this festival should be celebrated by following the Covid Appropriate Behaviour rules without crowding as much as possible.
3. d. Dhulivandan will be celebrated on 18/3/2022 and Rangpanchami on 22/3/2022. On this festival, people smear each other with colours, throw water on each other. However, the festivals of Dhulivandan and Rangpanchami should be celebrated in a simple manner to prevent the spread of Covid-19.
4. Palkhi procession is taken out on the occasion of Holi/ Shimoga festival (especially in Konkan).
But this year too, arrangements will be made for darshan in the local temple instead of taking the palanquin from house to house. Also, due care should be taken to ensure that there is no crowding and Covid Appropriate Behaviour rules are strictly followed.
To prevent the spread of COVID-19, the rules prescribed by the Government Relief and Rehabilitation, Health, Environment, Medical Education Department as well as the concerned Municipal Corporation, Police Administration, Local Administration should be strictly followed. Also, if any other instructions are published after this circular and in the period between the start of the actual festival, they should also be complied with.
