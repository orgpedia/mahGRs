The Proposal: -
Article 48 of the Constitution of India provides that the Government shall endeavour to protect and improve the breeds of cows and calves and other milch animals, and to take steps or make laws for the prevention of the slaughter of useful animals (in particular cows and calves). Also, the state government has received representations from various organisations regarding appropriate action to be taken in the matter of cow smuggling, illegal transportation of bovine animals, unauthorised slaughterhouses, etc. As law and order issues arose, the conservation, protection, and preservation of the bovine were under the consideration of the government, in accordance with the provisions of the Indian Constitution and various laws and regulations of the government.
Government Circular: -
Notwithstanding the considerations mentioned in the above proposal, the following guidelines are being issued regarding prevention of illegal transportation of cattle, illegal slaughter, unauthorised slaughterhouses and effective implementation of relevant laws.
Survey field offices in the state should strictly enforce the Maharashtra Animal Preservation Act, 1976, the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960, as well as other ancillary rules.
2. All Municipal Corporations / Municipal Councils / Municipalities / Nagar Panchayats concerned as well as all Zila Parishads / Panchayat Samitis and Gram Panchayats shall identify unauthorised and unlicensed slaughterhouses under their jurisdiction and take appropriate action against them. Also, ensure that there are no unauthorized or unauthorized slaughterhouses in any area.

3. The concerned police agency shall investigate and take appropriate action under the Maharashtra Control of Organised Crime Act (MCOCA) against the persons, gangs or organisations involved in ARAN organised cow slaughter in violation of the prevailing laws and regulations.
The Transport / Transport Department of the State shall conduct regular checking of vehicles carrying illegal cattle as mentioned above and take appropriate action against the vehicles concerned under the Motor Vehicles Act and other relevant laws.
All Superintendent of Police offices, Police Commissionerates as well as Animal Husbandry Commissionerates and Transport Commissionerates in the State shall appoint independent nodal officers from their level. The contact numbers of the designated officers should be made available to all concerned departments and citizens.
Every District Collector in the border area of the State of Maharashtra shall establish a Joint Check Post at the point of entry into the State of Maharashtra from other State on the border area consisting of officers and staff of the Police Department, Transport Offices, Urban / Rural Local Bodies, Animal Husbandry Offices in their district.
All the concerned departments of the state should form joint raiding teams and take regular action against illegal cattle smuggling and illegal slaughterhouses.
After receiving complaints regarding cow smuggling, illegal transportation of cattle, unauthorized slaughterhouses on the helpline number Dial 112 for effective conservation and conservation of cows, the concerned police agency should take immediate action on the said complaint.
Under Section WoR (2) of the Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, the licensee shall be bound to maintain cleanliness of the slaughterhouse, manage food and medical waste, and strictly follow all rules relating to public health and safety after obtaining written permission from the Commissioner, Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation office to set up a temporary cattle shelter on religious grounds.
Strict implementation of the above instructions shall be the responsibility of the concerned Collector, except in the Police Commissionerate area. Also, the Commissionerate of Police will be under the jurisdiction of the Commissioner of Police concerned. The concerned Collector B Commissioner of Police should conduct regular reviews in accordance with the above instructions and strictly implement these instructions.
