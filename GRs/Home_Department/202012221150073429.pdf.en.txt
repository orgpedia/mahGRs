Government Circular No. Sankirna-1220/ PR No.119/ VISA-1A dated: 22.12.2020
Subject: The date is January 1, 2021. Guidelines for Jaystambh Salutation Day at Haveli, Pune.
circular
It was established in the year 1822 in the memory of the soldiers who were killed and injured in the battle of Koregaon Bhima on January 1, 1818. The Jaystambh has been erected near the banks of the Bhima river in Haveli, Pune. The Jaystambh Salutation receives a large number of followers from all over the country on January 1 every year.
The government has issued instructions from time to time regarding the celebration of various religious events held this year such as Dr. Babasaheb Jayanti on April 14, Ashadhi and Kartiki Wari, Ganesh Utsav, Navratri, Eid, December 6, Mahaparinirvan Din. On the same lines, the Government has issued guidelines in the wake of COVID-19 in connection with the felicitation program at Jaystambh Pernefata on January 1, 2021:
1) Corona patients are still being found in large numbers in the state and in big cities. In view of the outbreak of COVID this year, all the religious festivals/ celebrations that have taken place in the last seven to eight months have been celebrated in a very simple manner and without the gathering of people. Therefore, the felicitation program to be held on January 1, 2021 at Jaystambh Parane should be held in a symbolic manner in a very simple manner with full precautions in the wake of COVID.
2) As devotees from all over Maharashtra and other states come to Jaystambh Perne on Salutations Day, restrictions should be imposed by the local administration in the Perne area to restrict the crowd in view of the risk of Covid.
3) In view of the restrictions on the arrival at Jaystambh Parane and the crowd in other places of Pune district in the wake of the Covid virus, the Jaystambh Salutation program will be broadcast live on Doordarshan and other media, so all the followers should greet the Jaystambh at home without coming to Jaystambh Parane.
4) Restrictions should be imposed on the kind of meetings, food and book stalls etc. in and around the venue of the Jaystambh Salutation program.
5) To prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus, the rules prescribed by the Urban Development Department, Relief and Rehabilitation Department, Health Department, Environment Department, Medical Education Department as well as the concerned Municipal Corporation, Police, Local Administration from time to time will be mandatory. as well as this
