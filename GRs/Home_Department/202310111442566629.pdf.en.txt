Government's decision:
Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Shri. 8 As per the proposal submitted vide letter dated 08.09.2020, the proposal for the revised pattern of posts in the establishment of the office of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai Police Unit, Maharashtra Police, is in the nature of creation of new posts/ revamping of posts and review of posts etc. The proposal was submitted to a high-level committee headed by the chief secretary.
First meeting of the High-Level Committee of Secretaries in 2023. As per the notification dated 06/03/2023, a total of 559 posts are being approved in the Maharashtra Police Force, including a total of 519 regular posts on a unit-wise basis in the establishment of the offices of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai, and a total of 40 services of manpower to be taken by external agencies.
As per the revised format, the details of regular posts in the establishment of the office of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai, in the Maharashtra Police Force, as well as the manpower to be recruited by external agencies (designation, pay scale, sanctioned posts) have been attached as shown in Annexure-A.
The revised pattern of posts in the establishment of the office of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai Police Unit in the Maharashtra Police Force is being approved subject to the following terms and conditions.
(a) The limited number of services of manpower referred to in (b) of Annexure-A to this Government Decision are being sanctioned to be taken up by external agencies as per requirement. However, if there are posts currently functioning as dead cadre in that respective cadre, the services should be taken from the external system in proportion to the number of manpower remaining excluding those posts. As the posts in the dead cadre become vacant, the services should be taken by external agencies as per the requirement.
(b) In case of availing services by external agencies, the reference No. 7 It is to be ensured that the guidelines contained in the Government Resolution dated 27/04/2022 and issued from time to time in this regard are followed.
