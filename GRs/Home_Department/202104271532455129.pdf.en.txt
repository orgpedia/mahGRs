GOVERNMENT DECISION, DEPARTMENT OF GENERAL ADMINISTRATION, No. 2021/ Q. No. 16/ 2021/9, dated 09.04.2021
The All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 have been made applicable to the officers of the Indian Police Service with effect from 2008-09. In accordance with Rule 3 and Rule 4 thereof, and in terms of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, vide letter No. 17011/3/2003-IPS-II, dated 21.11.2008, all the Indian Police Service officers in the State are required to get their medical examinations done annually in the prescribed format and a summary of their medical examination report is required to be attached with the annual performance evaluation report. Accordingly, it is proposed to conduct medical examinations for the year 2020-21 for the Indian Police Service officers of Maharashtra cadre. The Government has decided to conduct Medical Examination of Indian Police Service Officers of Maharashtra Cadre for the year 2020-21 as under:
Government's decision:
The All India Services (Assessment of Work) Rules, 2007. 3 and in accordance with Sample No. 4 thereof, vide letter No. 3 of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. 17011/3/2003 IPS-II, dated 21.11.2008, all Indian Police Service officers in the State should get their medical check-up done in the prescribed format in the year 2020-21 from the private hospitals identified revenue division wise as per the accompanying Form A. At the time of the examination, the following procedures should be carried out:
All Indian Police Service officers working in Maharashtra State Cadre as well as officers on Central Deputation shall get all the medical check-ups mentioned in Form B done for the year 2020-2021 from the private hospitals/ medical institutions mentioned in Form A in the Revenue Department in which they are working.
2. The BHOPOSA officers working in the Amravati Revenue Division should get the medical check-up done from the private hospital designated for other revenue divisions near them.

Medical examination can also be conducted in the government hospitals of the concerned district or government/ municipal hospitals of the revenue division where the facility of conducting all the medical examinations prescribed in Form-B is available. "
(4) The officers of the Indian Police Service shall, after conducting all the medical tests specified in Form B from the hospitals designated for the respective revenue divisions or from the Government or Municipal Hospitals, pay to the said hospital the amount required for the medical tests and get reimbursement thereof from his office. In case of medical examination by the concerned officers from the hospitals whose rates are higher than the revenue department-wise minimum rates fixed in Form A, the expenditure incurred in excess of the revenue department-wise minimum rates shall be borne by the concerned officer at his own cost. It should be noted that the government will not reimburse the excess expenditure over the fixed minimum rate.
The reimbursement of expenses on medical check-up shall not be paid to the officers who reimburse the cost of annual medical check-up under this Government Decision, Public Health Department, No. MAG-1098/ PR No. 220/ Health-3, dated 31st August, 2006 once in every two years.
Officers of the Indian Police Service shall, within the period from the date of this Government Order to 30th June, 2021, establish the dates of examination and conduct all the medical examinations in Form B mutually in consultation with the concerned hospital.
The hospitals in Form A approved for medical examination by the Government, as well as the Government or Municipal Hospitals, shall submit the consolidated report of the medical examination of the officers of the Indian Police Service in their possession, in the accompanying Form B, to the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai (Office-I) within eight days after the completion of the examinations and shall also give a copy thereof to the officer of the Indian Police Service concerned.
All the medical check-ups mentioned in Form B will be done at one place and on one day. But in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, it should be done on the next day. The date and time of examination should be mutually decided by the Indian Police Service officers and the concerned hospitals. Indian Police Service officers should also take necessary precautions in consultation with the hospital.
One day for medical examination will be considered as duty time. No leave/ transfer leave will be admissible if the examination is conducted on a public holiday.
Since the period of medical check-up is deemed to be the" duty" period, it shall be paid at the same rate as the travelling allowance/ daily allowance (if applicable) paid to an officer of the Indian Police Service while he is otherwise on tour.
