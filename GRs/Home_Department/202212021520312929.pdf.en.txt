Preamble: Mr. Suresh Ajinath Yewale, Poshi/ 141943, Armed Police, Taddeo, Mumbai has been in Government service since 18/03/2015. Mr Yeole draws a salary in the S7 (21,700-69, 100) pay scale for the post of constable. Mr. Yewale was working in the government service. On 25/12/2016, while on duty, he suffered a brain injury due to a fall and suffered a bleeding in the head.
He was undergoing treatment at a private hospital in Maxcare, Ahmednagar, when he suffered a paralytic stroke and suffered 52 per cent disability. Ahmednagar District Hospital (Government of Maharashtra) has issued a certificate of 52 percent disability to Mr. Yeole. A proposal has been submitted to the government to create a supernumerary post for Mr. Yeole as there is no equivalent post in the office of the Commissioner of Police, Brihanmumbai. Accordingly, as per the provisions of Section 20 (4) of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, in case of disability while in service, the employee shall be given posting in a suitable post in accordance with his disability and shall be given salary protection of the post holding origin. In case the post is not available, the employee will be given posting on the post by posting a higher number of posts till the availability of another suitable post or till the date of his retirement as per the prescribed age, whichever is earlier. In view of this provision, the High Level Committee of Secretaries has approved the creation of one supernumerary post in the cadre of Police Constable for Mr. Suresh Ajinath Yewale, Police Constable for the period from 25/12/2016 till the date of his retirement as per the prescribed age or for the period the post falls vacant due to other reasons, whichever is earlier.
