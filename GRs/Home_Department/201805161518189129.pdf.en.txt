The Proposal:
As per Rule 52 (1) of the Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, before renewal of registration of vehicles of non-transport category, the certificate of mechanical fitness of such vehicles as per Section 56 (1) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, is required to be obtained from the authority authorized by the Government or from the Government approved testing centre. As per Section 65 (1) (d) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, the State Government has the power to issue, renew, renew, and issue secondary certificate of fitness. Accordingly, a proposal to authorize Western India Automobile Association, Mumbai for renewal of eligibility certificate for vehicles in the category of' Vintage' and' Classic' is under consideration. 3 has been submitted to the Government as per the letter here and the matter was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
As per Section 65 (1) (d) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, the following decisions are being taken in respect of vehicles belonging to non-transport cadre falling under vintage and classic cadre for issue of fitness certificate, renewal thereof, issue of secondary certificate, etc. :
The said procedure shall be permissible only for vehicles of non-transport category, which are of vintage and classic category and are accordingly certified by the Vintage and Classic Club of India.

The Western India Automobile Association, Mumbai is being authorized to conduct mechanical fitness test and certification as per Section 56 (1) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 before renewal of registration as per Rule 52 of the Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989.
The Western India Automobile Association, Mumbai will provide the service of issuing/ renewing/ second copy of fitness certificate as per Section 56 (1) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 to only those vehicles of non-transporting cadre which are certified by the Vintage and Classic Car Club of India subject to the following conditions.
i) The employee to be engaged by Western India Automobile Association, Mumbai for this purpose should be a Diploma holder in Automobile/ Mechanical Engineering of at least three years duration.
ii) The designated employee should have at least 5 years of experience in vehicle repair in the light vehicle cadre.
iii) The employee should have at least 5 years of driving experience.
(iv) Should be familiar with the provisions of the Motor Vehicles Act, particularly those relating to registration, construction and maintenance of vehicles.
v) The place where the vehicle inspection is proposed to be carried out should be owned/ rented by the Western India Automobile Association which should have a mechanical fitness inspection facility of the vehicle.
(vi) Vehicle air pollution, engine tuning, engine inspection, brake system, headlight system, wheel alignment inspection system, speedometer and other parts of the vehicle.
(vii) The Western India Automobile Association shall make adequate financial provision for the operation of the said facility and shall keep updated copies of the Motor Vehicles Act and the relevant rules available at the place.
(vii) The Institute has paid Rs. 1,00,000/- (Rupees One Lakh only) should be deposited with the Commissioner of Transport, State of Maharashtra in the form of Bank Guarantee and it should be renewed in time and kept in term.
ix) For issuing/ renewal/ secondary certificate of fitness of vehicles of vintage and classic cadre, the fee as per Rule 81 of Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989 shall be collected from the concerned and deposited with the Transport Department.
