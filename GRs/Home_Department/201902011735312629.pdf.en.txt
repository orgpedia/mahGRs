In order to effectively curb the incidents of violence against women in public places and to take permanent measures for their safety, the Ministry of Home Affairs, Ministry of Women and Child Development, Government of India has approved the implementation of the Women Safety Initiative Scheme under the Safe City Project by selecting eight cities of the country namely Delhi, Mumbai, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Lucknow, Chennai, Bengaluru and Ahmedabad. The scheme will be implemented over a period of three years from 2018-19 to 2020-21 with the Central/ State share being 60:40. The Central Government has allotted Rs. The plan includes setting up of hotspots for such crimes, surveillance, augmentation of existing CCTV capacity, training of investigating officers, prosecutors and judicial officers, community policing and quick response GIS mapping, prevention of slum sexual abuse, abuse, public awareness, social media crimes, capacity building of police authorities, etc. Considering this, the matter of granting administrative approval to implement the Women Safety Initiative Scheme under the Centrally Sponsored Safe City Project to prevent crimes against women in Mumbai is under consideration.
As per the proposal submitted by the Commissioner of Police, Mumbai, to prevent crimes against women in Mumbai as per the directions of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, administrative approval is being given to implement the Women Safety Initiative Scheme under the Safe City Project of the Central Government in the ratio of 60: 40 subject to the following conditions.

This administrative approval will be subject to the following conditions:
(1) The appropriation/ expenditure of the funds disbursed by the Central Government, Home Department should be completed as per the prevailing norms of the Central Government/ State Government in the manner prescribed and subject to the total financial limit prescribed in the head and by following the financial formalities in this regard.
2) The funds received under any circumstances should not be kept in the form of parking. The utilization certificate should be submitted in the appropriate statement within the stipulated period after the funds received have been utilized. The accounts in this regard should be kept updated for internal audit as per the requirement of the Central Government as the sanctioning authority of the said scheme.
Under this scheme, administrative approval is being given to follow the procedure in the service plan as approved by the central government.
3) Government of Industries, Energy and Labour Department Decision No. 3/ 2014/ 82 Part-III/ Industry 4d. to strictly follow the procedures prescribed under the other Government Orders issued from time to time on 30.10.2015.
4) The rules/ policies/ orders issued by the Government from time to time regarding the permissibility and use of the approved items should be strictly followed.
5) The scheme should be implemented by the Commissioner of Police, Mumbai.
