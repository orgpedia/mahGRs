The month of Ramadan 2021
Corona virus is spreading widely in India. Appropriate measures are being taken in this regard. Due to the Corona crisis, all religious festivals, festivals and all programs are being celebrated in a very simple manner this year. This year's. 13 or 14/4/2021
(depending on the moon). The holy month of Ramadan will be celebrated by Muslims until 13 or 14/5/2021. During the holy month of Ramadan, Muslims in large numbers visit mosques and offer prayers in public. During this period, Muslims gather for prayers, taraweeh and iftar. In view of the increasing Corona epidemic in the state, according to the order issued by the Government of Maharashtra dated April 4, 2021, and its issue No. The holy month of Ramadan is to be observed this year under the terms 1, 2 and 13. Follow that. The following guidelines are being issued in connection with the observance of the holy month of Ramadan by Muslims starting from 13th or 14/4/2021:
During the holy month of Ramadan, Muslims should celebrate all religious events in their homes without gathering in mosques or public places for regular prayers, taraweeh and iftar. Gathering for prayers should not be done in mosques and open spaces.
The holy month of Ramadan should be celebrated in a very simple manner by strictly following all the rules of social distancing and hygiene (masks, sanitizers, etc.) without gathering more than 5 people at a time in public places.
During this holy month, Muslims fast from dawn to dusk every day for 30 days and break their fast before Maghrib prayer in the evening. A large number of fruit and other food vendors are expected to throng the market during the festival. The local administration should take the necessary steps in this regard.
It is customary to recite Dua (Goodbye Jumma) on the last Friday of the holy month of Ramadan.
For this, Muslims in large numbers come to the mosque and recite the Dua. But this time, in view of the increasing outbreak of Covid-19, no one should gather in the mosque for the recitation of the link. Read the link in your own home.

Shab-e-Qadr is a holy night celebrated on the 26th day of the month of Ramadan. After the Taraweeh prayers, Muslims offer night-long Nafil prayers and recite the Quran at mosques in their areas. But this year, all Muslims should do this religious program at home.
During the holy month of Ramadan, do not crowd or gather in the market to buy goods. Also, if the local administration has set a time limit for the purchase of goods in this regard, it should be followed exactly.
7. As religious places are closed, Vaz programs should be organized in closed spaces as much as possible online by following the rules of the government.
Since Section 144 of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) is in force in the state in view of the COVID-19 virus, and due to the night curfew, hawkers should not set up stalls on the road and citizens should not roam on the road without any reason.
No processions, religious, social, cultural or political programs should be organized in this holy month of Ramadan.
10.As religious places are closed, awareness should be created through religious leaders, social workers, political leaders and NGOs of the Muslim community regarding the celebration of the holy month of Ramadan in a simple manner.
11.The rules of social distancing must be strictly followed during the holy month of Ramadan.
Use of masks and sanitisers is also necessary.
12.A. To prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus, it will be mandatory to comply with the rules laid down by the Government Relief and Rehabilitation, Health, Environment, Medical Education Department as well as the concerned Municipal Corporation, Police, Local Administration. Also, if any other instructions are published after this circular and in the period between the start of the actual Ramadan festival and the current period, they should also be complied with.
