The Proposal:
The Lok Sabha elections for 48 parliamentary constituencies in Maharashtra were held in four phases on April 11, 18, 23 and 29, 2019.
During the period from the notification of the Lok Sabha General Election to the completion of the election process, the police officers and staff deputed for the full-time physical election work had to carry out the sensitive, sensitive and stonewalling work of the election with utmost diligence and responsibility. Accordingly, the proposal for payment of remuneration/ honorarium to the police officers and staff engaged for full time physical election duty for the Lok Sabha Elections-2019 was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Considering the stress of election work, scope of work and days of work etc. on the police officers and staff deputed for full-time physical election work during the period from the notification of the Lok Sabha General Elections-2019 in the State of Maharashtra till the completion of the election process, the Government is approving the payment of remuneration/ honorarium equivalent to the basic pay admissible to the police officers and staff in Annexure A and Annexure B for the month of April, 2019 as per the 7th Pay Commission. However, the police officers and staff who have done election duty for less than 30 days i.e. more than one month, will be paid remuneration for the number of days they have worked. Police officers and employees who have worked for more than 30 days in an election will be paid a maximum of one month's salary (basic salary). Such amount shall not exceed one month's salary (basic pay) of any police officer/ employee and shall be paid to the officer/ employee for the Lok Sabha General Elections 2019.
