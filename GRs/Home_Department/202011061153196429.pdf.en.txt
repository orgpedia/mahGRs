the prelude
As per Rule 10 (4) and Rule 62 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, in the public interest, it is the policy of the Government to prematurely retire officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity from Government service after reviewing their service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of age, whichever is earlier. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 1 here. The procedures and general instructions regarding service review have been prescribed under the Government Decision dated 10.06.2019. Accordingly, for review of services of 1) Group-A (Gazetted) (Senior Pay Scale) Officers in the cadre of 7600 and above but less than 10000, 2) Group-A (Gazetted) (Junior Pay Scale) Officers in the cadre of 7600 and below and 3) Group-B (Gazetted) Officers in the cadre of Motor Vehicles, Reference No. As per the Government decision dated 02.08.2019, a Divisional Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Transport).
The Departmental Review Committee under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Transport) in its meeting held on 22.10.2020 has submitted a proposal to review the services of Regional Transport Officers of Group A cadre in the Motor Vehicles Department at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
As per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, which evaluated the service of the officer, the government was considering to approve the officers who have been found eligible to remain in service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
