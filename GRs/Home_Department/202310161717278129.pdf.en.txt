Preamble: In the existing Police Stations in the State, under reference No. Manpower is employed on the basis of criteria laid down by the Act dated January 23, 1960. Also, as per the decision of the government, proposals for the formation of police stations are submitted to the government through the office of the Director General of Police from various constituent offices of the state.
In the government decision of 1960 the manpower criteria for the railway police station were not decided separately. But in view of the increasing traffic of the new technology equipped Indian Railways, the increasing number of passengers, as well as the number of crimes, it is necessary to set separate manpower criteria for the railway police stations.
Accordingly, the office of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai vide reference No. 2 and reference No. As per letter No. 3, the Commissioner of Police, Railways and the Superintendent of Police, Railways have submitted a proposal to the Government for the creation of police stations under the revised manpower standard/ criteria after conducting a standard re-evaluation.
The expansion of Indian Railways is increasing day by day and as part of providing maximum facilities to the passengers, there has been a tremendous change in the number of local/ mail/ express as well as in the type of freight transportation. As the existing police manpower is insufficient in comparison to this, the matter of making revised manpower standards/ criteria for the Railway Police Stations was under the consideration of the Government by making changes in it in accordance with time. Government's decision:
Accordingly, the Government is approving to fix the revised norms/ standards of manpower for Police Stations under Commissioner of Police, Railway as per Annexure-A and revised norms/ standards for Police Stations under Superintendent of Police, Railway as per Annexure-B.
After studying the government decision of 1960 and taking into account the changing nature and increasing number of crimes in the jurisdiction of the Railway Police Station, as well as taking into account the feedback of the Commissionerate of Railway Police and the Superintendent of Police, Railway, the revised standards/ criteria of manpower for the creation of the new Railway Police Station have been prepared.
