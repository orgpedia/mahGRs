circular
Navratri 2020 guidelines
Navratri/ Durga Puja/ Dussehra this year considering the contagious situation arising out of COVID-19
It was decided to celebrate the day in a simple manner. Accordingly, the following guidelines have been issued:
For public Navratri festival, the boards will be required to take due prior permission of the municipal corporation/ local administration as per their policy.
2. Considering the infectious situation arising out of COVID-19. Mandaps of limited size should be set up in accordance with the orders issued by the court and the policy of the municipal corporation and the concerned local administration regarding the mandaps.
Since this year's Navratri festival is expected to be celebrated in a simple manner, the decoration of the idol of the domestic as well as public goddess should be done accordingly.
The height of the idol of the goddess should be within 4 feet for public congregations and 2 feet for domestic goddesses.
This year, preferably, instead of the traditional idol of the goddess, the metal/ marble etc. idol in the house should be worshipped. If the idol is eco-friendly, its immersion should be done at home. If immersion is not possible at home, coordination should be made with the local administration regarding immersion at the artificial immersion site.
Donations/ donations for Navratri festival should be accepted. It should be seen that the display of advertisements does not attract crowds. Also, advertisements with health and social messages should be preferred. Awareness should also be created about the campaign" My Family My Responsibility."
Garba, Dandiya and other cultural programs should not be organized. Instead of health related activities/ camps (e.g. Organizing blood donation) should be given priority and through this corona, malaria, dengue, etc. should be controlled.
Awareness should be created about diseases and their preventive measures as well as cleanliness.
8. While organizing aarti, bhajan, kirtan or other religious programs, care should be taken to avoid crowding and rules and regulations regarding noise pollution should be followed.
9.Maximum arrangements should be made to provide the facility of darshan of the goddess online, through cable network, website and Facebook etc.
10. Adequate arrangements for disinfection and thermal screening should be made in the mandaps of the Goddess. Special attention should be paid to the physical distance and the observance of hygiene rules (masks, sanitizers, etc.) for the devotees who want to come and visit physically.

11. Arrival and immersion processions should not be taken out. In the traditional method of immersion, the aarti done at the place of immersion should be done at home and stay at the place of immersion for the shortest possible time. Children and senior citizens should avoid going to the immersion site for safety. The procession for immersion of the idols of all household deities in the entire chawl/ building should not be taken out together.
12. Artificial ponds should be created with the help of Municipal Corporation, various boards, housing societies, public representatives, NGOs etc. Also, to avoid crowding of citizens, the local administration should arrange a ward committee-wise idol acceptance center and give maximum publicity about it.
Not more than 5 workers should be present in the mandap at a time. Also, no food or drinks will be allowed inside the premises.
On the date of immersion, if the premises of domestic as well as public Navratri festival pandals are in a restricted area, immersion of idols in public places will be prohibited.
15. The Ravana Dahan program on the day of Dussehra should be symbolic by following all the rules. Only a minimum number of people will be present at the venue for Ravan Dahan. The audience should not be invited. Arrangements should be made to watch them through social media such as Facebook.
To prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus, it will be mandatory to follow the rules prescribed by the Government Relief and Rehabilitation, Health, Environment, Medical Education Department as well as the concerned Municipal Corporation, Police, Local Administration. Also, if any other instructions are published after this circular and in the period between the start of the actual festival, they should also be complied with.
