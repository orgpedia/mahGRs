The Proposal:
The High Court of Bombay, vide Public Interest Litigation No. 28/2013 (Shrikant Karve v. Government of Maharashtra). As per the decision dated 18.02.2016, all transport offices are required to have a 250-metre-long brake test track for vehicle inspection. However, considering the number of vehicles in Mumbai city and suburbs, it is necessary to set up a Vehicle Inspection and Certification Centre in Mumbai.
Vice Chairman and Managing Director, Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation As per the letter dated 29.11.2018, the Corporation owns 6138 sq.m.
No-objection certificate has been given for providing space. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to grant administrative and financial approval for setting up of Vehicle Inspection and Certification Centre at the said site through Automotive Research Association of India, Pune, an organization recognized under Rule 126 of the Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989.

The Automotive Research Association of India (ARAI), Pune has decided to set up a Vehicle Inspection and Certification Centre at the Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation (MSRTC) premises at Kurla, Nehrunagar in Mumbai. Administrative and financial approval is being accorded for the budget of Rs. 14,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Fourteen Crores only). The details are as follows:

The above approval is granted subject to the following conditions:
The proposed Vehicle Inspection and Certification Centre should be set up on the land of Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation.
(ii) Vehicle Inspection and Certification Centre.

The Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation will be responsible for the maintenance of the said centre.
(iv) the share of the revenue received by the said Centre on such terms and conditions as may be agreeable to both the parties; and
