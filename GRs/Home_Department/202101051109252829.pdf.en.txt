The Proposal:
The Maharashtra State Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) Act, 2018, provides for reservation of seats in educational institutions and posts in public services under the control of the state. A petition was filed in the High Court. The High Court. Against the decision dated 27/06/2019. In the Supreme Court, Civil Appeal No. 3123/2020 and other petitions have been filed. On the petitions. The Supreme Court Interim stay has been granted vide order dated 09/09/2020.
In pursuance of the aforesaid interim stay order passed by the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India on SEBC reservation, the matter was under consideration for taking action in pursuance of the advertisement issued in the year 2019 for filling up of vacancies in the Police Constables Cadre. Accordingly, the government has issued the following orders:
Government's decision:
The Supreme Court Civil Appeal No. 09/09/2020 In pursuance of the interim stay order passed in Petition No. 3123/2020 and other petitions, the advertisement issued for filling up of vacancies in the Police Constable cadre for the year 2019 should be amended by way of a corrigendum to the relevant advertisement to carry out the recruitment process without reservation for the SEBC category. Thereafter, while determining the eligibility of the candidates who have submitted their applications from the Socially and Educationally Backward Classes, the applications of the candidates who are fulfilling the terms and conditions for the open category by applying the age limit of the open category should be considered from the open category.
Also, while taking such action, the candidates who have paid the examination fee prescribed for the SEBC category should be given a period of 15 days to pay the amount of examination fee for the open category, the amount of difference required/ the fee to be paid. Also, check how to pay the examination fee.
