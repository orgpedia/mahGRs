In the wake of the bomb blasts in Mumbai on and after March 12, 1993, a Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad was constituted for the Brihanmumbai Police Force by a decision of the Home Department, Government of India, dated November 22, 1995. Subsequently, through various Government decisions, Bomb Detection and Disposal Squads were constituted in several districts of the State. This is in accordance with the terms and conditions laid down in the Government Resolution dated 22nd November, 1995, Issue No. 8A Special Pay:" The police officers and personnel who are assigned to extremely hazardous tasks shall be paid 50 per cent of the gross salary plus special pay, excluding house rent and supplementary allowances, payable in their parent department. " It said. The work of officers and staff in bomb detection and disposal squads is extremely dangerous. Special pay is also applicable to the officers and employees of all bomb detection and disposal squads in the state. Accordingly, the Government decision dated 22nd November, 1995 on the principle of equal justice, in point no. 8 As per the provisions herein, the police officers and staff working in all bomb and detection squads in the State are required to get special pay (risk allowance).
Accordingly, Department of Home Affairs, vide Government Resolution No. SSA-1010/ PR No. 331/ VISA-4, dated 03.09.2011, sanctioned incentive (risk) allowance@ 50% of the balance of 6th Pay Commission Band Pay+ Grade Pay to Police Officers/ Personnel working in Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad and@ 30% of the balance of 6th Pay Commission Band Pay+ Grade Pay to Armed Police Constables (Motor Vehicle Drivers).
The recommendations of the 7th Pay Commission have been made applicable to the State Government employees with effect from 01.01.2016 vide Finance Department, Government Notification dated 30th January, 2019.

In the 6th Pay Commission, the incentive was given on the basis of Basic+ Grade Pay. However, the concept of Grade Pay has been abolished in the 7th Pay Commission. Accordingly, a proposal was under the consideration of the Government to sanction incentive (risk allowance) to the police officers/ personnel working in the Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad on the basic pay as per the pay scale of the Seventh Pay Commission. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
7th Pay Commission for Police Officers/ Personnel of Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad
25% of their basic pay and Armed Police Constables (Motor Vehicle Drivers) are being granted Incentive (Risk) Allowance@ 15% of their basic pay as per 7th Pay Commission with retrospective effect from 01.01.2016.
The following formula should be adopted regarding the incentive allowance of the police officers/ staff of the Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad.
The basic pay as per the Seventh Pay Commission is X 0.25= Incentive in the Seventh Pay Commission.
The following formula should be followed for the incentive of Armed Police Constables (Motor Vehicle Drivers) in the Bomb Detection and Disposal Squad.
The basic pay as per 7th Pay Commission is X 0.15= 7th Pay Commission Incentive.
