the Proposal.
Government Memorandum, Revenue and Forest Department, No. Land- 2516/769/ S.No.53/ J-2, dated 02.12.2016, vide land tax No. 765 (Bawla Compound) has been allotted to the Home Department (Commissioner of Police, Brihanmumbai) for the office of Anti-Terrorism Squad under the lien of the occupant class-II subject to certain conditions. If there is an encroachment on the said land, it will be necessary to evict it by the Home (Police) Department with the help of the District Collector, Mumbai City, and the Planning Authority, the memorandum stated.
Inderjit Singh Kulwitsinh Sahani, New Canara Lunch Home, Shop No. 133/ D, Female Orphanage Building, and Mrs. Leela Narayan Shetty, United Auto Spare Parts, Shop No. 1,133, Female Orphanage Building, has encroached.
The Commissioner of Police, Brihanmumbai, in the matter of forcible eviction from the said encroachments, has filed an eviction case No. 1 in the court of the competent authority, Brihanmumbai, under the Maharashtra Government Sites Removal Act (Bombay Act No. 2 of 1956). 23814/ 57/ 2008 and 23815/55/2008. The competent authority disposed of both the cases on 17.01.2008 by issuing eviction orders.
Aggrieved by the said order of the competent authority, the said two establishment holders appealed to the City Civil Court, Mumbai. The city civil court set aside the orders of the competent authority in both the cases, but said that the competent authority would be free to take fresh action under the provisions of the Act for the removal of the owners of the two establishments from the Maharashtra government premises. According to the case No. Both cases 23814/57/2008 and 23815/ 55/ 2008 are pending in the court of the competent authority, Brihanmumbai.
Earlier, Advocate D.J. Walavalkar, Public Prosecutor, Small Cause Court, Mumbai, was appointed as Counsel to represent the Home (Police) Department in the Court of the competent authority in both the said cases. Therefore, the office of the Commissioner of Police has submitted a proposal that Advocate D. J. Walavalkar should be appointed as counsel to represent the Home (Police) Department in the court of the competent authority in the said cases.
Advocate D.J. Walavalkar has given his written consent for appointment as Counsel for Home (Police) Department in the said cases.
Accordingly, the government has taken the following decision.
