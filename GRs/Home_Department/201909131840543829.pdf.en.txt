Introduction: The Commissioner of Police, Mumbai has requested to implement the Intelligent Traffic Management System (ITMS) project in the city of Mumbai in view of the number of vehicles, traffic congestion problems, more travel time spent on fuel, impact on efficiency due to longer travel time, traffic congestion due to vehicle queues and other necessary traffic management needs. According to the detailed project report submitted by the Commissioner of Police, Mumbai, the estimated cost of the project is Rs. 891.34 crore, as per which the matter of administrative approval of the project was under consideration of the Government. The following decision has been taken:
The government has decided to implement the Intelligent Traffic Management System (ITMS) project for Mumbai city. 891,34,03, 382/- (Rupees Eight Hundred and Ninety One Crore Thirty Four Lakh Three Thousand Three Hundred and Eighty Two) out of which a total of Rs. 713 crore (project cost Rs. 648.24 crore+ Contingency Expenditure for projects/ other expenditure required Rs. 64.80 crore) is being given administrative approval.
After completion of the tender process of the Intelligence Traffic Management System (ITMS) project for Mumbai city, the revised administrative approval, if required, for the final project cost will be Rs. 891.34 crore and the year-wise expenditure will be shown at that time.

The Commissioner of Police, Mumbai shall issue tenders for the Intelligence Traffic Management System (ITMS) project, hold pre-tendering meetings, receive tenders, make technical and financial evaluation of the tenders received and then submit a report to the High Powered Committee on the final selection of the supplier. The final supplier will be selected by a high-powered committee. After the selection of the supplier, the process of giving the letter of intent, making the contract and giving the work order regarding this project should be done by the Commissioner of Police, Mumbai.
The Commissioner of Police, Mumbai shall constitute a Project Implementation Committee under his chairmanship to review the implementation of the project from time to time and submit its report to the meeting of the High Powered Committee headed by the Additional Chief Secretary (Home).
