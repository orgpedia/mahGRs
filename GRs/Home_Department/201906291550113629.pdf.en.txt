Regarding constitution of Departmental Review Committee for Service Review of officers/ employees of State Excise Department after the age of 50/55 years.
The Government of Maharashtra
the Home Department
Government Resolution No:- PEN-1019/ PR No.76/ Raushu-1,
Ministry, Mumbai- 400 032.
Date: 29th of June 2019
Read:- Government Resolution, General Administration Department, No. LPL-2017/ No. 21/ K. 10.06.2019.
Government Decision- The General Administration Department has decided to review the service of officers/ employees at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. The instructions have been issued in pursuance of the Government Decision dated 10.06.2019. Accordingly, a Committee is being constituted in the State Excise Department to review the services of 1) Group-A (Gazetted) (Senior Pay Scale) Officers in the cadre of 7600 and above but less than 10000, 2) Group-A (Gazetted) (Junior Pay Scale) Officers in the cadre of 7600 and below and 3) Group-B (Gazetted) Officers in the cadre of 7600.

The Commissioner, State Excise, Maharashtra State, Mumbai shall submit a proposal to the Government from time to time for review of the services of the officers concerned in the department. The committee will submit its recommendations to the appropriate authorities for final decision. Further, as per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, the Group A and Group B officers who would be ineligible to continue in service, would be reviewed, subject to the approval of the Departmental Review Committee. It will be done by the" Special Review Committee" of the General Administration Department as mentioned in the Governance Decision dated 1.06.2019.
The Committee is being constituted to consider the grievances of the officers/ employees who have been issued notices for being ineligible to continue in service after reviewing the services of Group-A (Gazetted) (Junior Pay Grade) Grade Pay of less than 7600 and Group-B (Gazetted) Cadre Officers in the State Excise Department.
