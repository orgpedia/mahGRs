The Proposal:
As per the recommendations of the Fifth Pay Commission, the Government has decided to grant 8 casual leaves instead of 12 in a calendar year to the Government employees and accordingly, as a special case, 12 days casual leaves in a calendar year to the Police Officers/ employees have been made admissible under the Government Decision Home Department, No. LVE 0299/ PR. No.151/ Pol-5B, dated 23.03.2000. The physical and mental health and performance of the officers/ staff of the police force is affected considering the scope of work in the police force, the responsibility of performing duties by staying more than 08.30 hours every day, the arrangements made in connection with various festivals and festivals, as well as the unavoidable increase in the total duty period due to VIP and other arrangements. The government was considering increasing the casual leave of police constables to police inspectors from the point of view of efficiency, health and family health of the officers/ employees of the police force. According to With the approval of the Cabinet, the following decisions have been taken regarding the leave of the officers/ staff of the police force.
Government Decision: A maximum of 20 (twenty) days casual leave is being allowed as a special case to the officers/ employees of the State Police Force from the rank of Constable to Inspector of Police instead of the present 12 days casual leave payable in a calendar year.
