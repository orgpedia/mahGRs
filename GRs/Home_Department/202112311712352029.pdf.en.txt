Government circular
Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and Special Director General of Police, Women and Child Crime Prevention, Maharashtra State, Mumbai have issued instructions through circulars from time to time regarding the investigation of crimes against women and children. In Virar Police Station, vide G.R. No. 870/2021, Bh. The missing minor girl was found after her medical examination in the case registered under Sections 4, 8, 10 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 read with Sections 363, 376 (2) (n) of the IPC. The medical examination revealed that the girl was 23-weeks pregnant. However, the investigating officer in the said case was required to produce the victim before the concerned Women and Child Welfare Committee within 24 hours of completion of her medical examination as prescribed in sub-section (6) of section 19 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012. However, the investigating officer did not do so and instead produced her before the Women and Child Welfare Committee, Palghar, after 45 days. The victim was 30 weeks pregnant at the time and was unable to have an abortion. On this In the High Court of Bombay, Writ Petition No. 26873/2021 has been filed and the court has expressed displeasure in the matter.
In the wake of this incident, all the police unit chiefs and investigating officers of the state are being asked to take the following action regarding the investigation of crimes against women and children: 1. If the victim is a minor, her/ his complaint should be taken in her/ his language, in her/ his words, in the presence of her/ his relatives and at least an officer of the rank of woman sub-inspector of police. (Section 24 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012)

(3) The victim shall be produced before the Women and Child Welfare Committee within 24 hours as prescribed in sub-section (6) of section 19 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012.
Section 228A of the Indian Penal Code prohibits disclosure of identity of a victim of certain offences. Therefore, the provisions of Section 228-A of the Indian Penal Code should be strictly followed.
(5) There shall be a separate Child Welfare Police Officer in each police station as prescribed in Section 107 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015.
The Tapasi Officer should guide the Government of Maharashtra, Department of Women and Child Development in the matter of Government Decision No. 2017/ No. 255/ K-2, dated 30.12.2017, to provide the benefit of Manodhairya Scheme for financial assistance and rehabilitation to the victims of rape and child sexual abuse. Also, the proposals of the eligible victims to get financial assistance under the Manodhairya scheme should be submitted to the concerned offices within the stipulated time.
7. The Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai shall make necessary provisions in the curriculum for all police officers while undergoing basic training at Maharashtra Police Academy and for newly recruited police officials while undergoing training at Police Training School in respect of Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 and Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015.
8. All the Heads of the Units shall organize workshops for the officers/ officials of their units from time to time to sensitize them in this regard.
9. Seek assistance of the Department of Women and Child Development, concerned NGOs, expert organizations and others as required for organizing the workshop.
10. Special Inspector General of Police, Women and Children Crime Prevention Department issued the circular.
Review the compliance of the given instructions from time to time and guide the concerned units as required.
