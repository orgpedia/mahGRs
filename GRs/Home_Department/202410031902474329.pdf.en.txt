The Proposal: -
The state of Maharashtra has a 720 km long coastline with 2 major ports and 48 non-major ports. The development of the maritime sector is of paramount importance for the economic and industrial development of the state.
The need of the hour is to create port projects with modern infrastructure for import and export of goods through water transport. For the past two decades, the government has announced port development policies from time to time to promote and regulate the maritime sector of the state.
The Maharashtra Maritime Development Policy-2023 is currently operational from the point of view of port development in the state.
Considering the significant contribution of the maritime sector to the country's economy, the Central Government has formulated the Maritime India Vision-2030 (MIV-2030) and the Maritime Amrit Kal Vision-2047, which highlights the Blue Economy. It aims to increase the volume of cargo transported through ports through transshipment, increase investment in the port sector and create employment opportunities.
With a view to achieve the maritime sector development targets set by the Central Government through its policies MIV-2023 and MAKV-2047 as well as in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Maritime Development Policy-2023, the Maharashtra Maritime Board has constituted a Sub-Director No. As per the proposal received by the Government through letters 2 and 3, the coastal perennial and multi-purpose port project at Murbay in Palghar district is to be developed as a "mega project in the port sector" and the port developer for the said project.
