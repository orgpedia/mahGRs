The provisions of the" All India Services (Assessment of Work) Rules, 2007" and the Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs, Circular No. 17011/3/2003-IPS. 2 As per Order, dated 21.11.2008, all officers of the Maharashtra cadre of the Indian Police Service are required to get the medical examinations specified in Annexure-II from the hospitals specified in Annexure-I for the year 2021-22, as per the following instructions:
(1) Medical examination shall be conducted before 31.03.2022 in the hospital specified in Annexure-I for the Revenue Department in which the I.P.S. officer is posted.
(2) The B.P.S. Officer posted in the Amravati Revenue Division should conduct medical check-ups at the hospital specified in Annexure-I for other revenue divisions nearby.
(3) If there is a provision for the medical examination specified in Annexure-II in the Government/ Municipal Hospital in the revenue department where the I.P.S. officer is posted, the said medical examination can also be conducted there.
4) The I.P.S. officer should first bear the cost of the medical check-up and then get reimbursement for the said cost.
5) The reimbursement of the expenses of the said medical examinations shall be made to the officials concerned. Come.
Department, No.MAG-1098/ P.No.220/ Health-3, dated 31.08.2006, reimbursement of expenditure on medical check-ups once in every two years will not be payable.
6) The B.P.S. officer should contact the concerned hospital and fix the date of medical examination. Also, they should take necessary preparations/ precautions for medical check-ups in consultation with the hospital.
7) These medical examinations will be conducted at one place and on one day. But in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, then it should be done on the next day.

The concerned Government/ Municipal Hospital/ Private Hospital referred to in Annexure-II shall submit the report of the medical examination of the I.P.S. officer in the prescribed format to the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, within eight days after the medical examination. Also, the hospitals should provide a copy of the medical examination report to the concerned BPS officer.
9) The day of the medical examination (one day) will be considered a duty day. If the medical examination is conducted on a government holiday, no transfer leave or any other leave will be admissible.
Since the period of medical check-up is deemed to be the" duty" period, it shall be payable at the rate at which the travelling allowance/ daily allowance is paid to the Indian Police Service officers, when they are otherwise on tour (if applicable). The rates of such Travel Allowance/ Daily Allowance shall be as per the orders issued by the Government (Finance Department) from time to time. This should be considered and provided by the concerned salary issuing office.
11) Before going for a medical check-up, the officers will be required to give advance notice to their immediate superiors.
12) The cost of the said medical examinations (reimbursement) shall be met from the grants available under the head of accounts being used for the salary and allowances at the establishment of the officer concerned.
(13) If an IAS officer wants to get his medical check-up done at a designated hospital in a revenue division other than the revenue division in which he is posted, he can get his medical check-up done at the authorized hospital in the desired revenue division at the permissible rate.
