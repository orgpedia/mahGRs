Circular
A case of cheating of depositors is registered at the police station under the Maharashtra Protection of Interest of Depositors (in Financial Institutions) Act, 1999. Detailed instructions have been issued vide circular dated 25.02.2019 in this regard to facilitate the victims affected by the said offences with respect to the time bound action to be taken with a view to refunding their deposits as provided in the said Act. Accordingly, Section 5 (3) of the Act mandates that the competent authority submit an application to a special court within 30 days of the notification being published in the Gazette regarding the attachment of the property. However, it has been observed that in most of the cases, time-bound action is not being taken as per these instructions. The mutual sale of these assets to be seized in the interim creates a third party interest in them and complicates matters further. Also, due to the non-compliance of the said procedure, the concerned investors face psychological distress and their money and time are wasted in this. In most of the cases, it is observed that the competent authority does not apprise the courts of the proceedings being conducted in person in the respective courts. In some cases.
Senior officers of the Home Department through the courts. Instructions are given to appear in court. In this regard, Shri. A case was filed in the Bombay High Court. The court had expressed its strong disapproval and listed the investors for imposition of severe monetary penalty to compensate them by the competent authorities concerned. This is not the case.
