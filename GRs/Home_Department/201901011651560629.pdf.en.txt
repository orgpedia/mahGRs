the prelude
Cybercrimes have increased in recent times due to the use of the internet and the changing technology. These include SMS/ chat, cyber defamation, child pornography, identity theft, romance scams, trolling, extortion, financial crimes, data theft, hacking of government websites, etc. The difficulty in collecting evidence in Internet crimes makes it necessary to conduct investigations in such crimes together and with the help of sophisticated technology. In this regard, Ministry of Home Affairs has provided State-wise grants for setting up of a training and functional forensic laboratory for preventive measures against cybercrime of women and children. The Special Inspector General of Police, Cyber Crime has proposed the structure of the unit as per the direction of the Central Government regarding the establishment of the cyber unit in the state.
Investigation of cybercrime: All cybercrimes and offences reported through the National Online Cybercrime Website shall be investigated under the Cybercrime Forensic Laboratory/ Training Centre. (CCPWC)
Training Centre: Training of Police, Public Prosecutors and Judicial Officers at the Training Centre as per the objectives set by the Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs.
The 3-day training will cover 1162 police stations and any person in them, 1100 and 110 women, 600 public prosecutors and 600 judges, and 5 days training will include 630 SHOs and 20 women.

Considering this, administrative approval was sought for setting up of a Judicial Assistance Laboratory and Training Centre for taking preventive measures against cyber crimes against women and children in the State.
Government's decision:
In pursuance of the proposal submitted by the Special Inspector General of Police, Cyber Crimes, to take preventive measures against cybercrimes against women and children in the State as directed by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, administrative approval is being accorded for setting up of a training and functional forensic laboratory under the Central Government's Cyber Crime Prevention for Women and Children (CCPWC) Scheme, subject to the following conditions:
This administrative approval will be subject to the following conditions:
The funds disbursed by the Central Government, Home Department should be utilized/ spent as per the cost arrived at and subject to the total financial limit allocated in the head wise manner as prescribed by the Central/ State Government and after completing the financial formalities in this regard.
2) Under no circumstances should the received funds be kept in the form of parking. The utilization certificate should be submitted in the appropriate statement within the stipulated period after the funds received have been utilized. As the authority of the said scheme, the accounts in this regard should be kept updated for internal audit as per the requirement of the Central Government.
Under this scheme, administrative approval is being given to follow the procedure in the training and service for the lab of Cyber Forensic Consultants as approved by the Central Government.
3) Government Resolution No. Bhankhas-2014/ P.C. 82 Part-III/ Industry-4D of the Department of Industry, Energy and Labour. to strictly follow the procedures prescribed under the other Government Orders issued from time to time on 30.10.2015.
4) The rules/ policies/ orders issued by the Government from time to time regarding the permissibility and use of the approved items should be strictly followed.
5) The scheme should be implemented by the Special Inspector General of Police, Cyber.
6) Expenditure on the said scheme," Demand No. B-01, 2055 Police, 115 Modernization of Police Force, (01) Technology Development Project, (01) (02) Cyber Crime Prevention Scheme on Women and Children (20550121), 13 Office Expenditure.
For this purpose, Special Inspector General of Police, Cyber is being declared as Controlling Officer and Office Superintendent, Cyber is being declared as Seizure and Disbursement Officer to spend the funds received under this head.
