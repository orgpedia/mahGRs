Committee's mandate:
The committee will study the manpower available with the Anti-Corruption Bureau (Headquarters+ all departments).
The Committee will take stock of the cases pending and currently pending with the Anti-Corruption Bureau (HQ+ all departments).
The Committee will study the availability of manpower and scope of work in the Central and State agencies like the Anti-Corruption Bureau (CBI), Anti-Corruption Bureaux of other States. Other agencies and state anti-corruption departments Page 2 of 5

The committee meeting:
Committee meetings shall be held as required by the President.
Recommendations of the Committee:
The committee will submit its recommendations to the government.
(1) Whether the manpower available with the Anti-Corruption Bureau at present is sufficient to deal with the pending work.
(2) To suggest measures to bring about equilibrium between the pending cases and the ongoing cases in the Anti-Corruption Bureau.
(iii) Whether additional manpower is required to be provided to the Anti-Corruption Bureau in case there is a gap between the scope of cases and the available manpower.
(4) If additional manpower is to be provided to the Anti-Corruption Bureau, whether a new post is required to be created for it, or

Time limit for submission of report to the Committee:
The Committee shall submit its report to the Government within a period of 2 months from the date of its constitution.
If the committee needs more than 2 months to submit the report, it will need to take prior permission from the government along with the reason-memo.
