The Indian Police Service (Uniform) Rules, 1954 have been amended vide the aforesaid notification issued by the Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions, Government of India (Department of Personnel and Training). As per the said amendments, the earlier uniformed allowances to the officers of the Indian Police Service (e.g. The office memorandum issued earlier regarding clothing allowance, initial equipment allowance, kit maintenance allowance, robe allowance, robe maintenance allowance, shoe allowance, uniform allowance and washing allowance has been cancelled and all the above mentioned allowances have been integrated into the uniform allowance. Hence, the fee of Rs. 14,000/- instead of Rs.
20,000/- per annum. Accordingly, it was under the consideration of the Government to implement the above amendment to the Indian Police Service officers of the Maharashtra cadre.
The Government of India has now taken a decision. Approves the amendments proposed by the NAMUDA Office Memorandum No. 2 and No. 3 to the Indian Police Service Officers of Maharashtra Cadre with effect from 01.07.2017.
Various uniform related allowances earlier being given to the officers of the Indian Police Service in the Maharashtra cadre (e.g. Clothing allowance, Initial Equipment allowance etc.) have now been included in the uniform allowance and they will get a total of Rs. 20,000/- per annum.
The said uniform allowance shall be credited to the concerned officers once in the month of July every year in the salary payable to them.
