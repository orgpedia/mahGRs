Government's decision:
The Mumbai-Pune National Highway No. 48 A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) has been signed on 11.02.2019 between the Department of Home Affairs, Government of Maharashtra and SaveLIFE Foundation, New Delhi (a Non-Governmental Organization) for collecting and analysing scientific data of accidents and taking/ suggesting and implementing necessary measures to reduce the number of accidents on this route. The Government is approving the constitution of a Project Steering Committee comprising of representatives of all the concerned departments including SaveLIFE Foundation under the Chairmanship of A.M.S. (Home) for the control of the project as provided in Clause 3.1 of the MoU. The composition of the committee will be as follows:
Additional Chief Secretary (Home), Home Department Chairman 2) Principal Secretary (Special), Home Department Member 3) Additional Director General of Police (Traffic), Maharashtra State Member 4) Joint/ Deputy Secretary, Public Health Department Member 5) Joint/ Deputy Secretary, Medical Education Department Member 6) Joint/ Deputy Secretary, Transport Department Member 7) Managing Director, Maharashtra State Road Development Corporation Member 8) Superintendent of Police (HQ), Highway Police Member 9) Chief Engineer, Maharashtra State Road Development Corporation Member 10) Chief Operating Officer, BVG 108 Ambulance Service Member 11) Chief Advisor, Save Life Foundation, New Delhi Member 12) Chief Executive Officer, Save Life Foundation, New Delhi Member 13) Joint/ Deputy Secretary

The Project Sookanku Committee is responsible for the construction of Mumbai Pune National Highway No. 48 It will review the progress made under the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) between the Home Department and SaveLIFE Foundation to reduce accidents on this route and give appropriate directions as required. It will also discuss the recommendations submitted by the SaveLIFE Foundation to take necessary measures regarding the compliance of traffic rules on the route in order to avoid accidents on the expressway.
The Committee shall review the progress of the proceedings under the Agreement every three months and issue necessary directions accordingly.
The Committee shall continue to exist until the MoU comes into force.
