Preamble: - Approval has been granted under the Government Decision dated 11.09.1998 No. 1 with reference to "Change in uniform of women police officers / staff of Maharashtra State Police Force." As per the provision in the said Government Decision, women police personnel of Maharashtra State Police Force are allowed to wear saree while on duty after 16 weeks of pregnancy.
However, the period from the first month to 16 weeks of a woman's pregnancy is an important B test in terms of a woman's pregnancy. In these critical times, if female policemen are given a belt around the waist, unnecessary abdominal pressure can lead to miscarriage. For this, considering the feedback of all the Commissioners of Police and all the District Superintendents of Police as well as the Police Surgeons, Police Hospital, Nagpada, Mumbai, it was under the consideration of the Government to allow the use of Saree in the first month of pregnancy on the request of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai Office of the Maharashtra State Police Force to women Police Officers / Officers on duty.
Government Decision: - The Government is approving allowing the use of Saree by women officers / Amaldars of Maharashtra State Police Force while on duty from the first month of pregnancy.
However, for the same, the women police officers / Amaldars shall be transferred to the concerned Superintending Officer.
A registered medical practitioner's certificate of pregnancy will be required.
In addition, the other provisions of the Government Decision dated 11.09.1998 at No. 9 in reference shall continue.
