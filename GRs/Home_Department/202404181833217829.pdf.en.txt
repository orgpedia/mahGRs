Conclusion with respect to Rules, 1979 and other matters relating to the Prisons Department.
History of the meeting.
The Proposal: -
The state of Maharashtra has 9 central jails, 28 district jails, 1 special jail, 1 juvenile jail.
There are a total of 60 jails, namely Sudharalaya (Nashik), 1 women's jail, 19 open jails, and 1 open colony (Atpadi). Administrative and other functions of these jails while discharging their day-to-day functions.
There are problems. Discussion / deliberation to address these issues
A state-level monitoring committee is being appointed to take action.
The government's decision:
Solving the difficulties faced in the day-to-day functioning of prisons in the state
A State Level Monitoring Committee is being set up to discuss / deliberate upon the measures to be taken. The composition of the Committee shall be as follows:

The functions of the Committee shall be as follows: -
(i) The Committee shall, as may be necessary and preferably once a month, review the establishment, finances, confinement and other matters relating to prisons in the State.
ii) Discuss / deliberate on the issues that arise and suggest measures accordingly.
(iii) The Committee shall take such action as may be necessary in connection with the implementation of these measures.
