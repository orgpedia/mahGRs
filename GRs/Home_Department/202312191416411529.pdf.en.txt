Circular:
Supreme Court of India, New Delhi. 231/2010, Shakti Vahini v. Government of India& Ors. Order dated 27.03.2018 prescribes preventive, remedial and punitive measures against honour killings. These measures are necessary to prevent incidents of honour killings by providing protection to couples entering into inter-caste or inter-religious marriages from honour killings.
2. m. The Supreme Court, New Delhi, in Writ Petition No. 231/2010, Shakti Vahini v. Government of India& Ors. The Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai shall instruct all the Police units in the State to comply with the instructions issued under the order dated 27.03.2018 and the compliance shall be reviewed quarterly in order to ensure that the said Court order is strictly complied with.
The Special Cell for providing security to couples who enter into inter-caste or inter-religious marriages will be as follows:
1. Superintendent of Police/ Commissioner of Police Chairman 2. District Social Welfare Officer Member
District Women and Child Development Officer Member Secretary
The Special Cell will take immediate action on all complaints received regarding inter-caste/ inter-religious marriages. It will also conduct a quarterly review of cases filed and sensitive areas.

It will also submit a report to the government. The composition of the committee will be as follows:
(a) The District Collector and the District Magistrate; (b) The Municipal Commissioner of the concerned district.
(3) The Superintendent of Police/ Commissioner of Police Member 4) The District Social Welfare Officer Member 5) The Women and Child Development Officer Member Secretary The functions of the said Committee shall be as follows:
(a) Ensure that couples who enter into inter-caste/ inter-religious marriages are provided with safe houses as required. The police station should be in a safe place.
(b) The couples having inter-caste/ inter-religious marriages should be provided with a safe house initially for a nominal fee for one month. After that, considering the seriousness of the case, the safe house should be provided for a maximum of one year. The Social Justice and Special Assistance Department should make arrangements for the safe house and adequate police security should be provided.
