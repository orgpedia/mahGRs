The Proposal:
The General Administration Department gave the government decision. Roll No.3 dated 26.05.2004 provides that the posts which do not require computer training and persons who are blind or physically unable to handle computers may be exempted from the Computer Eligibility Test by the Secretary of the concerned Department. In this regard, the letter No. 3 of the Directorate of Civil Defense has requested to" exempt these posts from passing the computer qualification examination, taking into account the duties of the 12 posts of personnel and technical on their installation." As per this request, the matter of exempting the said posts from passing the computer qualification examination was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: Reference No. 2 here. In accordance with the provisions of the Government Order dated 26.05.2004 on the establishment of the Directorate of Civil Defence, the Government is pleased to grant exemption from the Computer Qualification Examination to the following mentioned total 12 Staff and Technical posts of Group-C cadre, considering their duties:
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| A.A. | the designation                | A.A. | the designation                |
| 1    | The Chief Firefighter/ Rescuer |      | auxiliary mechanical connector |
| 2    | Firefighter/ Rescuer           | 8    | Samjhauta                      |
| 3    | the driver of the machine      | 9    | the Minister of Power          |
| 4    | driver                         | 10   | Autonomy Minister              |
| 5    | first-aid                      | 11   | the Assistant Self Electrician |
| 6    | a mechanical connector         | 12   | pair                           |
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The list of the above exempted posts shall be reviewed by the Director, Directorate of Civil Defence every three years and changes, if any, shall be communicated to the Government accordingly.
