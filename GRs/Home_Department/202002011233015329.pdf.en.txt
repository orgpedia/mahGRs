Measures to be taken to prevent social evils under the influence of alcohol in order to maintain the sanctity of the forts/ forts in Maharashtra.
The Government of Maharashtra
the Home Department
Government Circular, No: MIS-0120/ No.27/ Raushu-3,
5th Floor (Extension), Mantralaya, Madame Cama Road,
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Mumbai- 400 032,
Date: February 1, 2020.
The forts of Maharashtra are witness to the golden history of Maharashtra. It is necessary to maintain the sanctity of these forts/ forts. However, some miscreants consume liquor in public places while visiting these forts/ forts. Also, it has come to the notice of the Government that under the influence of liquor, they are indulging in malpractices/ malpractices that disturb public peace, damaging the glorious antiquity of the fort/ fort, etc. Accordingly, in order to curb the miscreants who are thus violating the sanctity of the fort/ fort, the following instructions are being prescribed:
The provisions regarding punishment under Section 85 of the Maharashtra Prohibition Act, 1949 (25 of 1949) for misconduct in public places other than places where consumption of liquor is permitted are as follows:
""" Section 85 of the Maharashtra Prohibition of Liquor Act, 1949 (25 of 1949). Punishment for drunkenness and disorderly conduct: (1) Whoever, while being drunk, acts undisciplined in any street or public place or in any place where the public may enter or be allowed to enter, is convicted of any offence, shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to six months and with fine which may extend to ten thousand rupees.\"""""
Provided that for reasons not specifically and sufficiently apparent to the contrary in the judgment of the court, the sentence of such imprisonment shall not be less than three months and the fine shall not be less than five thousand rupees, and (b) for the latter offence, the punishment shall be rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to one year and shall also be liable to fine of ten thousand rupees.
Provided that such imprisonment shall not be less than six months and such fine shall not be less than seven thousand five hundred rupees unless there are special and sufficient grounds to the contrary to be mentioned in the judgment of the court.

(2) Until the contrary is proved in a trial for the commission of an offence under sub-section (1), it shall be presumed that the person charged with the commission of such offence has consumed liquor or any other intoxicating substance for intoxicating purposes and not for medicinal purposes.
2) In order to maintain the sanctity of the forts/ forts in the state, in order to discourage the miscreants from consuming liquor at the forts/ forts, as well as drunken misbehaviour, damaging the antiquities at the forts/ forts, if such misbehaviour/ misbehaviour is found to be taking place, legal action will be taken immediately against the concerned, with clear instructions.
3) If it is found that there is any malpractice such as drinking and misbehaving in the state's fort/ fort, the concerned social worker should be immediately taken into custody by the police or the state excise department and a case should be registered against them under section 85 of the Maharashtra Liquor Prohibition Act, 1949 (25 of 1949) and necessary legal action should be taken immediately.
4) All the heads of police units as well as superintendents of police and state excise should instruct the agencies under them to strictly implement the above instructions and ensure that they are strictly followed.
The circular is being issued in coordination with the Home Department (Police) and the Cultural Affairs Department.
