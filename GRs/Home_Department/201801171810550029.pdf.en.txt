Government Decision As per the decision of the Government in the context of the Home Department, various 1,331 temporary posts in the office of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai were extended till 31st December, 2017.
2. A proposal has been received by the Government through the Office of the Director General of Police for further extension of 1330 temporary posts out of the above posts. As per the Government decision dated 12.07.2004, MIS 0104/ PR No. 77/1/ Pol-8 of Home Department, two posts of Sub-Inspectors of Police were created with various posts on the establishment of Commissioner of Police, Nagpur. Out of which, one post of Sub-Inspector of Police has been transferred to the office of the Commissioner of Police, Nagpur for the post of Junior Engineer, Construction, vide Government Order, Home Department No. OPO-3616/ PR No. 88/ Pol-3, dated 9th January, 2017. Therefore, the office of the Director General of Police has requested to reduce the post of Sub-Inspector of Police from the total 444 temporary posts on the establishment of the Commissioner of Police, Nagpur City to 1330 temporary posts on the establishment of the subordinate offices of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, instead of the earlier total 1331 posts.

The Office of the Director General of Police has clarified that the situation that was created in the above posts still exists. Accordingly, the Government hereby approve the extension of tenure of 1,330 temporary posts as per the above table (excluding one post of Sub-Inspector of Police in the establishment of Commissioner of Police, Nagpur) in various offices under the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai from 1st January, 2018 to 28th February, 2018 subject to the following conditions:
The extension will not be applicable to temporary posts (excluding executive posts) lying vacant for more than six months.
2. The reasons for which the temporary posts have been sanctioned subject to the terms and conditions to be followed.
Government Resolution, Finance Division, No: ASANK-1001/ Q.No. 29/2001/ Financial Reforms-1, dated 10th September, 2001, No: ASANK-1004/ Q. No. 12/2004/ Financial Reforms-1, dated 19th August, 2004, No. No. 8/16/ Fiscal Reform-1, dated 21st September, 2016 and No. 8/16/ PC, dated 10.02.2017 and dated 03.10.2017, the process of final approval of the High Level Committee by the Commissioner of Police, Brihanmumbai to the revised pattern of the office should be obtained by the end of 28.02.2017 in any case.
The proposal should be sent to the government immediately.
If the process of review of posts is not completed by 28th February, 2018, the above temporary posts do not get an extension for that further period and if the question of salary and allowances of the officers/ employees arises, it will be the responsibility of the concerned office.
The expenditure for this should be met from the grant sanctioned for the year 2017-2018 under the head of Home Department, Demand No: B-1," 2055 Police- 109 District Police- 109 (00) (01) District Police Force" and this expenditure should be spent under the same head.
The orders are being issued in accordance with the powers conferred on the Administrative Department under the Government Resolution, Finance Division No. Designation-2016/ PR No. 8/16/ APC, dated 08.01.2018.
