The government-
Whereas, Mr. Kiran Bidkar, Regional Transport Officer, Nagpur (City) while serving as the Head of Office, Nagpur City, was travelling in School Van No. MH-31-EM-0036 and School Bus No. The crash occurred between MH-31-FC-1913. A driver and a student were killed in the crash. The school van No. The vehicle MH-31-EM-0036 has been found to be operating without a valid fitness certificate. The matter is in contravention of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules as prima facie it is found that Mr. Kiran Bidkar, Regional Transport Officer, Nagpur (City), in his capacity as Head of the Office, was negligent, negligent and negligent in discharge of his duties;
Whereas, in pursuance of the said irregularity, disciplinary action is planned against Mr. Kiran Bidkar, Regional Transport Officer under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979;
Accordingly, under Rule 4 (1) (a) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979, Shri.
Kiran Bidkar, Regional Transport Officer, Nagpur (City) is being suspended from Government service with effect from the date of this order i.e. 07/05/2026;
The Government hereby further directs that Shri Kiran Bidkar's headquarters shall be the Office of the Transport Commissioner, Mumbai for the period for which these orders are in force. Also, they cannot leave the headquarters without the prior permission of the Transport Commissioner.
The subsistence allowance and other allowances thereon shall be admissible to Mr. Kiran Bidkar, Regional Transport Officer under Rule-68 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Provisions as to period of assumption of office, superannuation, suspension, dismissal and removal from service) Rules, 1981 for the period these orders shall remain in force.

Further orders are being issued that Shri Kiran Bidkar, Regional Transport Officer shall not take up any private job or engage himself in any trade or business during the period of suspension. Also, they should not be granted subsistence allowance unless they have accordingly furnished the certificate required under Rule 69 Potenium (4) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of assumption, superannuation, suspension, dismissal and removal from service) Rules 9981. If they take up a private job or carry on any trade or business during the suspension period, they will be eligible for disciplinary action against them for violating Rule 16 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules 1979.
