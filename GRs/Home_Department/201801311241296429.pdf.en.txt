The All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 have been made applicable to the officers of the Indian Police Service with effect from 2008-09. In accordance with Rule 3 and Rule 4 thereof, and in terms of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, vide letter No. 17011/3/2003 IPS-II, dated 21.11.2008, all the Indian Police Service officers in the State are required to get their medical examinations done annually in the prescribed format and a summary of their medical examination report is required to be attached with the annual performance evaluation report. Accordingly, it is proposed to conduct medical examination of Indian Police Service officers of Maharashtra cadre for the year 2017-18. The Government has decided to conduct Medical Examination of Indian Police Service Officers of Maharashtra Cadre for the year 2017-18.
Government's decision:
The All India Services (Assessment of Work) Rules, 2007. 3 and in accordance with Sample No. 4 thereof, vide letter No. 3 of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. 17011/3/2003-IPS-II, dated 21.11.2008, all the Indian Police Service officers in the State should get their medical check-up done in the prescribed format in the year 2017-18 from the private hospitals identified revenue division wise as per the accompanying Form A. At the time of the examination, the following procedures should be carried out:
All Indian Police Service officers and officers on Central deputation working in Maharashtra State Cadre shall get all medical check-ups mentioned in Form B done during the year 2017-18 from the private hospitals/ medical institutions mentioned in Form A in the Revenue Department in which they are working.

2. The BHOPOSA officers working in the Amravati Revenue Division should get the medical check-up done from the private hospital designated for other revenue divisions near them. Further, Rs. Hospitals are being authorized to select for the prescribed medical tests with a ceiling of Rs.5,400/-. The BHOPOSA officers working in Nagpur Revenue Division should get the said medical examination done from the private hospitals identified by the Divisional Commissioner, Nagpur or the following point no. 12 The medical examination should be done from the authorized hospital of other revenue department as mentioned here.
Medical examination can also be conducted in the government hospitals of the concerned district or government/ municipal hospitals of the revenue division where the facility of conducting all the medical examinations prescribed in Form-B is available.
The officers of the Indian Police Service shall get all the medical tests indicated in Form B done from the hospitals designated for the respective revenue divisions and shall first pay the amount required for the medical tests to the hospital and get reimbursement from their office.
Officers of the Indian Police Service shall, within the period from the date of this Government Order to 31st March, 2017, establish the dates of examination and conduct all medical examinations in Form B mutually in consultation with the concerned hospital.
The hospitals in Form A approved by the Government for medical examination shall submit the consolidated report of the medical examination of the officers of the Indian Police Service, in the accompanying Form B, to the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai (Office- I) within eight days of the completion of the examination and a copy thereof to the concerned officer of the Indian Police Service.
All the medical check-ups mentioned in Form B will be done at one place and on the same day. But in exceptional cases, if for some reason it is not possible to do all the tests in one day, it should be done on the next day. The date and time of examination should be mutually decided by the Indian Police Service officers and the concerned hospitals. Also, the necessary precautions for the tests should be taken in consultation with the hospital.
One day for medical examination will be considered as duty time. No leave/ transfer leave will be admissible if the examination is conducted on a public holiday.
Since the period of medical check-up is deemed to be the" duty" period, it shall be payable at the same rate as the travelling allowance/ daily allowance (if applicable) paid to an officer of the Indian Police Service while he is otherwise on tour. The rates of such Travel Allowance/ Daily Allowance as may be issued by the Finance Department from time to time.

As the provision of the All India Services (Performance Appraisal Report) Rules, 2007 requires the Indian Police Service officers to conduct these medical examinations, the proposal does not require the approval of the Finance Department every year and henceforth the proposal should be approved at the level of the Home Department as per the Government Resolution issued by the General Administration Department for the Central Government officers.
