Introduction: The Central Government has enacted the Banning of Unregulated Deposit Schemes Act, 2019. The Act came into force with effect from February 21, 2019.
The bill has already been approved by the Parliament. The President has consented. The details of the Act, received on 31st July, 2019, have been published in the Gazette. The state government had constituted a committee under the chairmanship of the Additional Director General of Police (Economic Offences) to draft the rules.
The Banning of Unregulated Deposit Schemes Act, 2019, under Section 38 of the Act, confers the power to make regulations on the State Governments in consultation with the Central Government. These powers are in addition to the powers vested in the Central Government under Article 37.
A committee under the chairmanship of the Additional Director General of Police (Economic Offences) has been constituted to draft the rules under the authority of the state government as per the provisions of the said Act. The composition of the committee is as follows:

The committee's mandate is as follows:
(i) to formulate rules necessary for the effective implementation of the said Act.
2. Drafting of various templates as may be required on the basis of the provisions of the said Act as well as the draft rules.
3. To finalize the draft rules keeping in view the detailed procedure as provided in the said Act.
(4) The Committee shall have the power to call upon an experienced and knowledgeable officer as an invitee, as may be necessary, to finalise the said rules.
The committee will have two months" time to submit their report.
