Government circular
The circular dated 31.12.2021 has been issued to all police units to investigate crimes against women and children. This circular supersedes the above circular dated 31.12.2021 and directs to take action as mentioned below in respect of investigation of offences against women and children.
Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and Special Inspector General of Police, Women and Child Crime Prevention Department, Maharashtra State, Mumbai have issued circulars from time to time.
In Virar Police Station, vide G.R. No. 870/2021, Bh. The missing minor girl was found after her medical examination in the case registered under Sections 4, 8, 10 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 read with Sections 363, 376 (2) (n) of the IPC. The medical examination revealed that the girl was 23-weeks pregnant. However, the investigating officer in the said case was required to produce the victim before the concerned Women and Child Welfare Committee within 24 hours of completion of her medical examination as prescribed in sub-section (6) of section 19 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012. However, the investigating officer did not do so and instead produced her before the Women and Child Welfare Committee, Palghar, after 45 days. The victim was 30 weeks pregnant at the time and was unable to have an abortion.
On this In the High Court of Bombay, Writ Petition No. 26873/2021 has been registered. The court expressed its dissatisfaction.
In the wake of this incident, all the police unit chiefs and investigating officers of the state are being advised to take the following actions regarding the investigation of crimes against women and children:
If the victim is a minor, her/ his complaint should be taken in her/ his language, in her/ his words, in the presence of her/ his relatives and as far as possible by an officer not below the rank of woman sub-inspector of police. (Section 24 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012) 2. The medical examination of the minor victim shall be conducted in accordance with Section 164-A of the Code of Criminal Procedure, Section 27 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012.
It is mandatory to report to the Child Welfare Committee and the Special Court (if there is no such Court, the Sessions Court concerned) within 24 hours as mentioned in sub-section (6) of Section 19 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012.
Section 228A of the Indian Penal Code prohibits disclosure of identity of a victim of certain offences. Therefore, the provisions of Section 228-A of the Indian Penal Code should be strictly followed.
