Guidelines for the festival of Gudi Padwa 2021
This year's festival is celebrated as Eid-ul-Adha. It will be celebrated on 13th April 2021. The festival is celebrated as New Year's Eve in the country. However, in view of the current situation of the second wave of COVID-19 and the increasing number of Corona patients, the Department of Revenue and Forest, Ministry of Disaster Management, Relief and Rehabilitation, Mumbai, vide its order dated 4th April, 2021, vide its order No. It is necessary to celebrate the festival of Gudi Padwa in a simple way this year under the auspices of 1, 2 and 13. Accordingly, the following guidelines are being issued: 1) In view of the outbreak of COVID in the State last year, all the festivals/ celebrations held till date have been celebrated in a very simple manner and without gathering of people. Corona infection is currently on the rise. Therefore, this year, the festival of Gudi Padwa is expected to be celebrated in a very simple manner from 7.00 am to 8.00 pm in order to avoid the increasing outbreak of COVID-19.
In some parts of the state, Gudi Padwa is celebrated as the New Year by wearing traditional costumes and organizing public events. In order to prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus, this year's Palkhi, Dindi, Prabhat Pheri, Bike Rally and Procession should not be taken out on the occasion of Gudi Padwa and no public program should be organized. Instead, the festival should be celebrated in a very simple manner by setting up a home kit by strictly following all the rules of social distancing and hygiene (masks, sanitizers, etc.), without gathering more than 5 people at a time in public places.
