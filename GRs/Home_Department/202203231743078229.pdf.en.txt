Accordingly, the Government is granting extension of tenure of 22 temporary posts mentioned in the above table from 01.03.2022 to 31.08.2022 subject to the following conditions:
1) The remaining posts should be extended by excluding vacancies for more than six months.
Also, the terms and conditions under which the posts have been sanctioned should be followed.
Government Resolution, Finance Division No. Asank-1001/ Q. No. 29/2001/ Financial Reforms-1, dated 10th September, 2001 and No. A proposal should be submitted to the Government immediately to complete the process of review of the posts as per Assn.1004/ Q.No. 12/2004/ Financial Reforms-1, dated 19th August, 2004 and final approval of the revised pattern of the office by the High Level Committee of Secretaries.
3) While reviewing the sanctioned posts in the establishment, the increasing use of technology in the functioning of the government, changes in the government's manpower over time and the need for effective use of manpower should be considered.
4) In any case, during the said period, the Finance Department has decided to review the posts before the high-level committee and take approval. In view of the Government decision dated 22.2.2022, if the process of review of posts is not completed by the end of 31.08.2022, if the above temporary posts are not extended for the next period and the question of salary and allowances of the officers/ employees arises, the responsibility of the concerned office will remain.
A single proposal for extension should be submitted along with the information contained in the letter of the Home Department No. CTE-3115/ PR No. 2/ Pol-3, dated January 16, 2015.
