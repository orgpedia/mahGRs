5. Letter from Additional Director General of Police, Training and Special Squads, Maharashtra State, Mumbai No. 27/06/2022
Preamble: The General Administration Department, Government of India, under the purview of the Department of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions, has decided to fill up the vacant posts in Group-B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D cadres of the erstwhile Secondary Service Selection Boards (outside the purview of the Maharashtra Public Service Commission) in direct service. Consolidated guidelines have been issued under 04/05/2022. In view of the above instructions, discussions were held with the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, regarding the procedure to be followed for the recruitment of police constables in the state.
According to the Director General of Police, Under Section 5, a proposal was submitted with supporting reasons for taking the written examination system in the OMR method and the matter of determining the examination system accordingly was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: 1. In the year 2020, 7231 vacancies in the Police Constable Cadre will be filled under reference No. As a special case, the government has approved the written examination process through OMR method to fill the vacancies.
Action should be taken to fill the vacancies in the Police Constable Cadre in the year 2020 by following the instructions/ orders given by the Government regarding the implementation of the OMR based written examination process.

3. As per the instructions/ orders issued by the Government from time to time in accordance with the said examination system, the Director General of Police should issue a detailed circular regarding the recruitment of police constables and give instructions to the units.
The Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai will be fully responsible for the transparent implementation and control of this recruitment process. In case of objections/ disputes/ court cases/ matters relating to the conduct of examinations, the responsibility will be of the Director General of Police, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the respective unit/ block heads under him.
