The following guidelines have been issued for the celebration of Rangpanchami in a very simple manner:

Holi/ Shimoga is celebrated with great enthusiasm all over Maharashtra. This year's. Holi is on 28/3/2021. In view of the increasing outbreak of Covid-19, the festival should be celebrated in a simple manner by following the rules of social distancing without any crowd.
2. d. Dhulivandan will be celebrated on 29/3/2021 and Rangpanchami on 2/4/2021.
On this day, people smear each other with colours, sprinkle water and apply gulal on each other. However, to prevent the spread of Covid-19, the festivals of Dhulivandan and Rangpanchami should be celebrated in a simple manner. For this, awareness needs to be created among the public and the local administration.
3. On the occasion of Holi/ Shimga, a procession of Palkhi is taken out especially in Konkan. But this year, arrangements will be made for darshan in the temple itself instead of taking the palanquin from house to house, so the local administration should take appropriate measures. Also, proper care should be taken to ensure that there is no crowding and social distancing is strictly followed.
4) Large-scale religious and cultural events should not be organized at the place of Holi and Dhulivandan celebrations.
Stricter restrictions than the guidelines issued earlier by the local authorities shall continue to be in force or may be imposed even after this circular. However, the guidelines cannot be extended beyond that.
To prevent the spread of COVID-19 virus, the rules prescribed by the Government Relief, Rehabilitation, Health, Environment, Medical Education Department as well as the concerned Municipal Corporation, Police Administration, Local Administration should be strictly followed. Also, if any other instructions are published after this circular and in the period between the start of the actual festival, they should also be complied with.
