Introduction: The eligibility of Indian Police Service officers is determined by the marks obtained in the examination conducted by the Union Public Service Commission and the marks obtained in the National Police Academy. Therefore, passing the departmental examination has no effect on their service excellence. However, since the officers of the Maharashtra cadre are required to have knowledge of the prevailing laws and rules of the State of Maharashtra, their departmental examination is being conducted by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission.
The Maharashtra Public Service Commission had recommended necessary amendments in the examination rules and syllabus of the departmental examination of the said posts to conduct a single common examination for the departmental examination being conducted for the officers of the Indian Police Service. The Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) was also facing difficulties in conducting the zonal examination of Indian Police Service officers regularly. The Departmental Examination of Indian Police Service Officers of Maharashtra Cadre is hereby approved to be conducted by Additional Director General of Police, Training and Special Squads, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and Director, Maharashtra Police Academy, Nashik instead of Maharashtra Public Service Commission. So far, the pending departmental examination by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission will also be conducted by the Additional Director General of Police, Training and Special Squads, Maharashtra State, Mumbai at the Maharashtra Police Academy, Nashik. Terms and Conditions:
The Maharashtra Police Academy, Nashik should conduct the examination pending with the Maharashtra Public Service Commission within 6 months.
The examination will be conducted in the month of January and July every year by the Additional Director General of Police, Training and Special Squads, Maharashtra, Mumbai and the Director, Maharashtra Police Academy, Nashik.
(3) The said departmental examination must be passed by the concerned officer within two years from the date of appearance in the Maharashtra cadre. If the concerned officers do not pass the departmental examination within the prescribed period, their pay hike will be withheld until they pass the departmental examination.
(4) Department of the Interior. As per the Government decision dated 09.05.1955, the syllabus prescribed for the departmental examination is being revised and approved by the Government.
