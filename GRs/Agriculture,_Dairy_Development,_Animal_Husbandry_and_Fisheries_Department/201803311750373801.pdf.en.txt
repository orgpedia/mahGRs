Preamble: The Centrally Sponsored Integrated Cereals, Pulses, Oilseeds and Maize Development Programme was being implemented in the State till 2013-14. The Central Government has restructured all centrally sponsored schemes in the 12th Five Year Plan.
Accordingly, the National Mission on Oilseeds and Oil Palm (NMOOP) has been launched from 2014-15, replacing the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Integrated Pulses, Pulses, Oilseeds and Maize Development (ISOPOM). The Mission comprises of three mini-Abhiyan-1 (oilseeds), mini-Abhiyan-2 (oilseeds) and mini-Abhiyan-3 (tree-based oilseeds). The funding pattern of the Mission was 75:25 between the Centre and the States in 2014-15. Since 2015-16, the ratio between Centre and States has been revised to 60:40.
During the year 2017-18, under this Mission, the Central Government with the pending liabilities and incremental programme for the previous year has sanctioned a total programme outlay of Rs. 7939.70 lakh. Updated as of July 10, 2017
