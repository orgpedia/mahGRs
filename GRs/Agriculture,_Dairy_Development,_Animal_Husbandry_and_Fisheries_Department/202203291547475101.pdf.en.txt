The following terms and conditions should be followed while implementing the traditional agricultural development scheme under the National Sustainable Agriculture Mission.
The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines of the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government and administrative approval.
The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central and State Governments along with the progress report as soon as possible by keeping the accounts of expenditure in order. The physical and financial progress reports on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central and State Governments by the 5th of every month.
3. The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit expenditure relating to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
4. It should be ensured that under no circumstances more than the approved programme is implemented.
5. It should be ensured that the funds are spent for the same cadre for which the funds have been allocated.
4. Subject to all financial laws/ tender rules and regulations/ procedures/ limits of financial powers/ P.W.D. Manual as per prescribed procedures while spending the said funds. V. C.
As per the principle/ direction of the CAG/ prevailing government decisions/ rules/ circulars/ provisions, the expenditure should be processed by the implementing agencies as per the budget and treasury rules.
It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Regional Office to ensure that no Government rule/ authority is violated under any circumstances. They were given to them by the government
The modalities for disbursement of all funds to all districts through PFMS system will be mandatory.
While spending the funds disbursed under the said Government Decision, the Controlling Authorities shall ensure compliance of the Financial Rules contained in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook, Financial Powers Rulebook as well as the Government Circular No.Finance-2019/ P.S. 64/ Finance-3, dated 06.04.2021 and Government Decision No. The directions given in the Government Resolution dated 24.06.2021 in Fince No.48/ Fince-3 should be strictly followed.
