1.2 Silver Pomfret (Paplet) holds a special place in the coastal environment and fishing economy of Maharashtra. This species is an integral part of the livelihood and cultural heritage of the coastal community. In addition to making a significant contribution to the fishing industry of the state," Silver Pomfret" is famous for its delicious taste and exceptional nutritional value. As a result, it has become a favorite seafood among consumers in the country and abroad.
1.3 Changes in fishing practices, including changes in the paplet trade in the domestic and international markets, have adversely affected the silver pomfret stocks in the north-western part of India, as fishing of small paplet fishes is rampant, leading to a decline in silver pomfret production in Maharashtra.

1.5 As per the report of International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), the Silver Pomfret (Paplet) fish falls under the category of" Not Extinct," but the production of Silver Pomfret fish in the state of Maharashtra has seen a significant decline in the last few years.
1.6 In view of this, it is necessary to raise awareness about the critical need to protect the marine ecosystem, curb juvenile fishing and promote sustainable fishing practices, besides conserving the remarkable fish species, Silver Pomfret (Pampus argenteus), in the marine wetlands of Maharashtra. The Maharashtra government has decided to declare silver pomfret as the state fish. Accordingly, the following resolution was adopted:
Government's decision:
The Government is hereby declaring Silver Pomfret/ Paplet (Pampus argenteus J) as the State Fish of Maharashtra.
