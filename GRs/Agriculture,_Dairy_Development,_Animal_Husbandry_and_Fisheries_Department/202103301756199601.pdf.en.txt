Introduction: There are many problems/ problems in the unorganized and unregistered food processing industry sector in the country.
For example, the Prime Minister's Scheme for Formalization of Micro Food Processing Enterprises (PMFME) has been approved by the Government of India to address the issues of unorganized and unregistered food processing enterprises such as non-availability of external credit, high cost of borrowing from financial institutions, lack of modernization, lack of integrated food supply chain and lack of health and safety rating.
There are about 2.24 lakh unorganised and unregistered food processing industries in the state. The Prime Minister's Scheme for Formalization of Micro Food Enterprises (PMFME) was being implemented in the state on the lines of the Central Government. Accordingly, as per the approval of the Cabinet in its meeting held on 2 nd December, 2020, the Government has approved the implementation of Pradhan Mantri Scheme for Formalization of Micro Food Processing Enterprises (PMFME) in the State as per the decision of the Government No.
Also, the head of accounts has been approved to implement the scheme under the government decision at reference number 4.
The scheme will be implemented in the state with 100% expenditure for the year 2020-2021 by the Central Government and 40% of the state's share will be adjusted by the Central Government in the next 4 years. For the financial year 2020-21, the Central Government has released the first instalment of Rs. Administrative approval for category wise expenditure of Rs. 9,89,78, 000/- was under consideration.
