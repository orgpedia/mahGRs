At/ P.H.P.Yo/ A.Ma/ 36462/ 2021, Dy. 03/11/2021 Preamble: In order to ensure financial stability of farmers by providing insurance cover to their fruit crops against climatic hazards, the State is implementing the Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme 2020-21 on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide crop compensation to the farmers, a restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme for the year 2020-21 for 8 crops viz., Orange, Mango, Cashew, Pomegranate, Mango, Banana, Grapefruit and Strawberry (Ambia Bahar) on pilot basis has been launched in the district by the Board of Revenue through its constituent entities namely HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co., Ltd., Bajaj Allianz General Insurance Co., Ltd. Agriculture Insurance Company of India Ltd. The insurance companies are referred to as No. Decisions 1 and 2 were implemented.
The Commissionerate of Agriculture has sought release of State share subsidy and funds for administrative expenses as demanded by Agriculture Insurance Company of India for the year 2020-21 for Ambia Bahar under Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme. 5 and 6 are presented. The issue of disbursement of funds was under consideration.
