Introduction: Lumpy skin disease is a scheduled disease under the Central Government's Prevention and Control of Infectious and Contagious Diseases in Animals Act, 2009. in the state. On 4.08.2022, an outbreak of viral lumpy skin disease in bovine cattle was first reported in Jalgaon district. Thereafter. By the end of 14.09.2022, the disease has spread rapidly to 22 districts of the state. In view of the rapid spread of this contagious and viral disease, it is necessary to control this disease immediately. For this, as per the instructions of the Central Government, all cattle and buffaloes within a radius of 5 km from the epicentre of the disease must be vaccinated.

The government was considering the formation of a state-level task force at the state level to make recommendations to the government regarding the decision. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: The Government hereby approve the constitution of the following State Level Task Force at the State level to periodically review the viral and communicable lumpy skin disease in bovine animals in the State and to guide the field machinery on the measures being implemented/ to be taken to control the outbreak of the disease, to recommend to the Government on the decisions to be taken to control the outbreak of the disease, etc.
