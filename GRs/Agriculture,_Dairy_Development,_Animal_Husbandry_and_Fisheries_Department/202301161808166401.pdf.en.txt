Preamble: The distribution of Soil Health Cards (SHC) under National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) has been started by the Government of India since 2014-15. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16. Central government. As per the letter dated 24.3.2022, the soil health cards and soil health management schemes under soil health and fertility have been included under the National Agriculture Development Scheme. Soil health cards are distributed to farmers after analysing soil samples. Through soil health cards, farmers are informed about the chemical properties of their land, the level of major food elements and the deficiency and condition of micro elements.
As per the minutes of the State Level Project Sanctioning Committee (SLC), the Annual Action Plan (AAP) based Soil Health Card and Management Plan under National Agriculture Development Scheme (NADP) for the year 2022-23 has been approved. The approval has been accorded vide letter dated 12.7.2022. The central government.
As per the letter dated 06.12.2022, for the year 2022-23, an amount of Rs. 599.78 lakhs and 40% of the State share of Rs. 399.86 lakh. An annual action plan of Rs. 999.64 lakh has been approved. Under this, the central government has allocated 25 percent of the central share (60%). Accordingly, the government was considering administrative approval and disbursement of funds for the component-wise physical and financial targets for implementation of the Soil Health Card and Management Plan in 2022-23.
