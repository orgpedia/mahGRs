The Proposal:
Two World Bank assisted projects namely Maharashtra Competitive Agriculture Development Project and Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission have been successfully implemented in the State.
Through the Maharashtra Competitive Agriculture Development Project, the facility of electronic trading through computerized auction system has been successfully created in the Agricultural Produce Market Committees. In this method, Dynamic Cash Credit has been incorporated with the participation of commercial banks. This will bring all the agricultural markets of the state on a single electronic trading system. Through this project, the establishment of Farmer Producer Companies (FPOs), linking small and marginal farmers directly to the market, increasing the value of agricultural products, improving the management and accounting aspects of market committees, creating a system of advising farmers on crop practices through the study of agricultural markets, etc. have started getting a primary boost. Similarly, the Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission and Gram Samajik Parivartan Foundation have made meaningful efforts to improve the lives of the people in rural areas through these self-help groups and local self-government institutions.
In view of the success of these two projects, the Steering Committee of these projects was advised to submit separate projects of next phases for future implementation of these projects.
Accordingly, two separate projects were submitted to the World Bank. Instead of these two projects, a single project project will be presented jointly and under the project, the social of private companies

At present, the work of Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project (SMART) should be started on a temporary basis in the office of Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project in Mumbai.
The Department of Agriculture will be the nodal department for the planning and implementation of the project and other departments or agencies will work as a participatory mechanism under the project. At the State level, the Additional Chief Secretary (Agriculture) will coordinate with all the departments/ agencies and prepare the project report through the Project Management Cell.
Till separate budgetary allocation is made for the project, the cost of the project should be met from the provisions of Maharashtra Competitive Agriculture Development Project and manpower and other resources of the project should be used as per requirement.
Out of the total funds required for the implementation of the project, 70% will be funded through World Bank loan and the remaining 30% will be provided by the State Government. The process of opening separate accounts for budgeting this fund should be done with the approval of the Cabinet.
The project has an initial outlay of about Rs.2050 crore (1 USD= Rs. 68.2797 per unit), however, the final design of the project will be decided by the Project Management Cell and World Bank officials to be set up for the implementation of the project and the final design will be approved by the Cabinet.
