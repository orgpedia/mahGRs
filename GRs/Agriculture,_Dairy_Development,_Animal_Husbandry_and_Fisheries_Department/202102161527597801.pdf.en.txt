Introduction: The day-to-day functioning of the educational institution has been disrupted since March, 2020 due to the lockdown announced across the country due to the outbreak of COVID-19 virus. However, the number of Covid-19 cases in the state is still on the rise. Accordingly, the Department of Higher and Technical Education vide reference No. As per the circular, regular classes in all non-agriculture universities, deemed universities, self-financing universities and colleges affiliated to them have been allowed to start from February 15, 2021. Also, reference No. According to the circular, regular classes in all agricultural universities and their affiliated government and private unaided schools and colleges in the state will start from February 15, 2021. On the same lines, the Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences has issued Reference No. The letter has requested to issue a government circular/ government decision regarding the commencement of regular classes in person in various colleges under the Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences. Accordingly, the matter of starting regular classes in person in various colleges under the Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences in the state was under the consideration of the government.
Government Circular: Regular in-person classes in various colleges under the Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences are being approved to start from February 22, 2021, subject to the following terms and conditions.
Universities and colleges affiliated to them in the containment zones in the State, which come under the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Municipality or Collector and Chairman, District Disaster Management Authority, may be consulted by the concerned University.

Considering the local level spread of COVID-19 disease and the local situation and the need for health infrastructure, the University and the colleges concerned should take immediate action to take necessary measures to start regular classes by opening the University and the colleges concerned. In this case, the university should issue instructions to start colleges with the consent of the local authority.
As per the UGC guidelines dated 5th November, 2020, subject to availability of space, up to 50% of the students should be admitted to the classes on rotation basis or the decision should be taken by the University at its own level after reviewing the situation at the local level of the college and considering the available facilities, subject to the COVID-19 preventive protocols and keeping in mind the academic interest of the students.
The guidelines or Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for the phased opening of hostels should be prepared by the university.
4. Examinations for the students should be conducted by the universities in online/ offline mode as per the convenience of the students.
Students should not be forced to attend.
The University should discuss with the concerned District Collector about the need to test the teaching-non-teaching staff and students for COVID-19.
The universities should decide on how much to reduce the undergraduate/ postgraduate courses as per the guidelines of the University Grants Commission.
Since hostels/ colleges are closed for many days, they should be subjected to electric/ safety audit.
The guidelines of the University Grants Commission, dated November 5, 2020, should be strictly followed. Also, the guidelines issued by the central and state governments regarding Covid 19 should be followed from time to time.
