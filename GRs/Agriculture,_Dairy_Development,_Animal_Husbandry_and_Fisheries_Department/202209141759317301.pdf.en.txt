Introduction: Turmeric is an important crop in Ayurveda. It is of great religious, social, economic and cultural importance. Turmeric is widely used in daily diet, medicines, cosmetics, biological insecticides. Social work also plays an important role.
India is the world's largest producer and exporter of turmeric. About 6% of the total area under spices and spices in India is under turmeric.
Government to advise the Government to formulate a Turmeric Research and Processing Policy considering the difficulties faced by the farmers from cultivation to processing and sale of turmeric vide Government Decision No. The study committee was headed by Shri Hemant Patil, Member of Parliament, Hingoli Lok Sabha Constituency. According to the report submitted by the committee through the Commissioner (Agriculture), Agriculture Commissionerate, Pune, 81 percent of the world's turmeric area is in India. It is followed by China (7%), Myanmar (4%), Nigeria (3%), Bangladesh (3%) and Vietnam and Sri Lanka (1% each).
In India turmeric is known as the main cash crop of spice crops. Due to the geographical favorable environment for the cultivation of turmeric in India, turmeric can be grown everywhere in India.
In India wheat is grown in the states of Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Punjab, Rajasthan, Bihar, Haryana, Maharashtra and Gujarat. In the year 2019-20, there was an area of 2.18 lakh hectare under turmeric cultivation in the country. Of this, 54,885 hectares was in Maharashtra. As per the data of 2021-22, a total area of 1.02 lakh hectare is under turmeric crop in the state. Maharashtra is the largest producer of turmeric in the country. Out of the total area under turmeric in the state, Marathwada has a total area of 82,009 hectares (80%) under turmeric.

Part-A: Implementation of Turmeric Research and Processing Policy for the State.
(ii) Objectives of Turmeric Research and Processing Policy: To understand the problems of turmeric farmers and provide them with cost-effective, reliable and viable technology.
2. To double the income of turmeric farmers by reducing the cost of cultivation and provide financial stability to them.
3. Preparation of ideal methods of turmeric cultivation.
4. To make recommendations with regard to new mechanization available at present in the field of turmeric extraction, processing etc.
Developing market chains for turmeric packaging, marketing, branding, export.
6. Convergence of various schemes to benefit the farmers.
7. Resolving the problems faced by farmers, processors and exporters in relation to turmeric cultivation and marketing.
(iii) Need for Turmeric Research and Processing Strategy: Turmeric is known as an important cash crop. However, as the farmers are not getting that much economic benefit, there is a need for a turmeric policy for all the stakeholders to come forward for the overall development of turmeric and to consider all the problems faced by turmeric from cultivation to extraction, processing and marketing and to suggest solutions. At present, considering the negligible participation of farmer producer companies in turmeric marketing and export, there is a need for a turmeric policy to work on a large scale in turmeric marketing and export.
To suggest solutions to the problems faced in the cultivation, processing and sale of turmeric and to provide the benefit of various agricultural, marketing and spice board schemes to the turmeric farmers through convergence, with the joint participation of all the concerned agencies working in the field of turmeric production, processing, export etc.
There is a need for an all-encompassing turmeric policy to address the various problems faced by (i) farmers, (ii) turmeric entrepreneurs, (iii) consumers, (iv) agriculture department, (v) agricultural universities/ research institutes, (vi) Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Corporation and Spices Board and thereby meet their expectations.
