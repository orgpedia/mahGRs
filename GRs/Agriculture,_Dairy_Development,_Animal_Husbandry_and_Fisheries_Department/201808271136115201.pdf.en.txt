Introduction: The Central Government has started the distribution of soil health cards under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture since 2014-15. In this, all the villages of the state will be selected in a phased manner and land health cards will be distributed to all the farmers of the state. Through soil health cards, information is being given to the farmers about the chemical properties of their land, the level of major nutrients and the status of micro element deficiency.
As per the Union Budget 2018-19, vide Reference No. 5 Administrative approval has been given by the government decision here. The Central Government vide its letter dated 27th June, 2018 has released the first instalment of Central share of Rs. An amount of Rs. 1560.21 lakh (for general category) has been disbursed. Accordingly, for the implementation of Mrida Arogya Patra distribution programme in the State during 2018-19, the Centre has released the first installment of Rs. 1560.21 lakhs and 40% of the State share of Rs. 1040.14 lacs out of which a total of Rs. 2600.35 lakh was under consideration of the Government.
