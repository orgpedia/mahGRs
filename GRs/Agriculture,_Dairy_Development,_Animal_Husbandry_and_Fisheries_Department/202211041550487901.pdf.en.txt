Measures to facilitate transfer of cattle to Gaushalas in the State for rearing of animals caught by the Police while transporting animals illegally.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Agriculture, Animal Husbandry, Dairying and Fisheries Development
Government Resolution No: PAVIYA-1022/ PR No.188/ PADUM-3
Madam Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk,
Ministry, Mumbai- 400 032.
Date of release: November 4, 2022
The ban on cow slaughter has been imposed under the Maharashtra Animal Preservation (Amendment) Act, 1995. However, some cases of illegal transportation of cattle/ bovine animals for slaughter from the State in violation of the provisions of the said Act have been noticed. Cattle are transferred to the state's gaushalas to rear the animals caught by the police while transporting such animals illegally. Complaints are being received from some of the Gaushalas/ cages to which such animals are transferred that the animals received in the transfer are being sold for slaughter. The Maharashtra Animal Preservation (Amendment) Act, 1995 and the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 were under the consideration of the government to take concrete measures to curb such illegal acts.
Government's decision:
In order to ensure strict compliance of the provisions of the Maharashtra Animal Preservation (Amendment) Act, 1995 in the State, the following Government Orders are being issued in this regard: (a) Action to be taken by Police Officers and Veterinary Officers.
(1) The provisions of the Act regarding immediate prosecution of the concerned by the police in respect of the animals seized by the police/ other agencies while illegally transporting the animals shall be complied with as it is.
(2) The health certificate of the animal after conducting a medical examination by a veterinary officer of the animal seized after a case is registered in such a case.

According to the court order, the written consent of the Deputy Commissioner of District Animal Husbandry and Member Secretary of the District Animal Husbandry Prevention Committee of the concerned district should be obtained before transferring the animals to the Gaushala for protection/ rearing of the seized animals.
(4) The seized animals should be registered in the police station and sent to the Gaushala/ cage as per the order of the court, according to the prescribed procedure.
(5) The Deputy Commissioner of Animal Husbandry and the Member Secretary of the District Animal Husbandry Committee shall transfer the cattle to the Gaushala/ cage in which such animals have been recommended/ agreed to be kept, with the permission of the concerned Gaushala/ cage.
(6) It should be ensured that the office bearers of the Gaushala/ Panjarpol having infrastructure facilities while transferring such animals are not booked under the provisions of the Maharashtra Animal Preservation (Amendment) Act, 1995 and the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960.
(7) The transfer of such animals to the nearest Gaushala/ cage should be done only with their permission. If it is not possible to transfer the cattle to the nearest Gaushala/ cage, the other Gaushala/ cage in the taluk should be handed over to that Gaushala/ cage.
(8) The veterinary officers of the nearest veterinary dispensary of the cowshed/ cage should conduct health check-up of the cowshed/ cage animals from time to time.
(b) the action to be taken by the institutions of Gaushala/ Panjarpol.
(1) Ma. Tagging of animals transferred and recording of animals caught in illegal transportation as per court orders on INAF system. Also, it will be mandatory for the concerned gaushala/ cage house to register it in a separate register.
(2) The responsibility of rearing the seized animals shall be of the concerned Gaushala/ Cage farm administration.
(3) The seized animals shall not be transferred to any other person/ institution without the written permission of the Deputy Commissioner of Animal Husbandry and the Member Secretary of the District Animal Welfare Committee. Also, farmers will not be allowed to use/ sell the produce.

(4) Livestock in Gaushalas/ cages shall not be sold under any circumstances.
(5) After the death of an animal in a gaushala/ cage farm due to some reason, it shall be mandatory for the concerned gaushala/ cage farm to obtain the death certificate of the animal and keep a record of the same and inform the same to the Deputy Commissioner of Animal Husbandry.
(6) The seized animals have been handed over to some Gaushala/ Cages. Their current status and other related issues should be reviewed every month in the meeting of the District Animal Cattle Control Society (SPCA).
The concerned police officer/ veterinary officer/ gaushala/ cage administration should strictly implement the above order.
4. The District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner shall communicate the decision to the administration of all Gaushalas/ Panjarpols under his jurisdiction.
