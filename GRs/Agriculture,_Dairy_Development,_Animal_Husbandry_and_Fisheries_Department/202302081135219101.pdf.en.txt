The proposal: The 31 st State Level Project Sanctioning Committee under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary, State of Maharashtra has approved the implementation of' Low Cost Onion Project' under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana in the year 2022-23 and 2022-24. A total of Rs. 101.00 crore will be implemented and Rs. 51.00 crore will be provided for a period of two years. Accordingly, the State Level Project Sanctioning Committee has sanctioned Rs. 51.00 crore has been sanctioned. The matter was under consideration of the government for administrative sanction. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
Government Decision: 1) Under the National Agriculture Development Scheme in 2022-23 and 2023-24, Rs. Administrative approval is being accorded for the programme with a budget of Rs. 51.00 crore. The project will be implemented on a 50:50 basis. Accordingly, to provide subsidy to individual beneficiaries as per the parameters of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission, Rs. 51.00 crore is being provided under the National Agriculture Development Scheme.
The project is to be implemented over a period of two years with an outlay of Rs. 25.50 crore for the year 2023-24 and Rs. A sum of Rs. 25.50 crore has been sanctioned. However, the Government shall have the power to increase/ decrease the fixed allocation to be made available year-wise in proportion to the demand received from the farmers.
The project will be implemented in 20 districts of the state (Nashik, Dhule, Jalgaon, Nandurbar, Pune, Ahmednagar, Solapur, Aurangabad, Jalna, Beed, Latur, Nanded, Parbhani, Hingoli, Osmanabad, Amravati, Akola, Yavatmal, Buldana and Washim).

4) The project will be implemented in the state as per the guidelines of the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission.
The Central Government will provide 60 per cent of the funds and the State Government will provide 40 per cent.
6) The total financial target to be achieved by the State shall be equitably distributed to all the districts/ taluks of the State and the farmers shall be allowed to choose within the limit of this financial target.
7) Applications of the beneficiaries should be invited online through the MahaDBT portal of the department. The beneficiaries of the project should be selected.
8) The selected beneficiaries should be given prior consent by the competent authority. The work completed should be inspected by an official. Only after this, subsidy will be allowed for onion cultivation.
9) After the beneficiary has set up the onion chawls, the records should be done through geo-tagging.
10) The subsidy should not be paid without registering on the 7/12 line of the beneficiary.
11) Under this scheme, financial assistance should be provided to the Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, women beneficiaries, small and marginal farmers in the prescribed amount as per the guidelines.
12) The distribution of funds for the implementation of the project will be done by the National Agriculture Development Plan Cell, Department of Agriculture and Padum, Ministry of Agriculture, Mumbai.
The project should be implemented by the Agriculture Department under the control of the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Maharashtra State, Pune.
The Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune shall ensure that the financial and physical progress report of the scheme is submitted to the Central Government as per the guidelines of the Central Government within the stipulated time.
15) Under the National Agriculture Development Scheme, the funds made available for the onion chawal construction project should be directly deposited to the Aadhaar-linked bank account of the beneficiaries.
The implementing agencies should ensure that these funds are not misused.
16) The audited utilization certificate of the fund expenditure in the scheme should be submitted by the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune to the National Agriculture Development Plan Cell, Department of Agriculture and Padum, Mantralaya, Mumbai from time to time.
17) The Excise and Disbursing Officer should ensure that the decision of the Government is strictly implemented. The expenditure for the scheme is being approved subject to compliance of all financial rules, regulations, financial governance decisions, notifications, orders, orders issued by the Central Government from time to time, guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission and the Comptroller and Auditor General of India.
