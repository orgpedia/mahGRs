Introduction: The recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission have been accepted by the Central Government. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode from 2014-15 onwards.
Under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMOET), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, Seeds and Plantation Material Sub-Mission (SMSP) is being implemented.
This sub-mission is being implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government with a view to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and in a time-bound manner by augmenting and expanding the existing scheme in the form of a mission to include new technologies in the production of quality or certified seeds for improving seed testing laboratories, assisting seed production institutions to adopt new seed production technologies, etc. Under the ambit of this sub-mission, support will be provided to farmers for supply of certified seeds to create conducive infrastructure for development of seed sector from centrally produced seeds to natural seeds.

As per the letter dated 04.06.2018, the Central Government has sanctioned Rs. 708.72 lakh for the Centrally Sponsored Village Seed Production Programme (Central Share 60%, State Share 40%) for the general category and Rs. 71.27 lakh for the Certified Seed Production Programme (Central Share 60%, State Share 40%) under the Village Seed Production Programme. 779.99 lakh has been provided directly to Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited (Mahabeej), Akola. Accordingly, the State share for general category is Rs. 472.48 lakh for village seed production programme and Rs. 47.51 lakh for certified seed production programme, totalling Rs. 519.99 lakh. The funds have been disbursed out of the provisions available under heading 24019154 of the Accounts under the Government Decision dated 14.12.2018.
As per Reference No.5 dated 20.11.2018, the Central Government has provided Rs. 422.00 lakh to the general farmers for implementation of Centrally Sponsored" Gram Seed Production Programme" in Rabi season 2018-19 (Central share 60%).
The State share (40%) is Rs. 281.33 crore.
In this regard, the Government was contemplating to allocate State share of Rs. 281.33 lakh for the general category for implementation of" Gram Seed Production Programme" in the ratio of Central and State share of 60: 40 in the Rabi season 2018-19 under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Seed and Plantation Material Sub Mission under National Agriculture Extension and Technology Mission.
Government's decision:
Out of the provision available under section 2401A154 for the general category for implementation of" Gram Seed Production Programme" in the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Seeds and Planting Material Sub Mission under National Agriculture Extension and Technology Mission (NAAKTIMAN) in the Rabi Season 2018-19 as per the Central and State share of 60: 40, an amount of Rs. 281.33 lakh is being released to Maharashtra State Seed Corporation Limited (MAHABIJ), Akola for the State share of Gram Seed Production Programme. The expenditure for this purpose shall be met from the grants sanctioned for the financial year 2018-19 and shall be credited under the following head:
Demand No.D- 3-2401, Crop Protection, 103, B-Seeds, (00) (27) Krishi Unnati Yojana-Seed and Planting Material Sub-Mission, (Centrally Sponsored Scheme) (State Share 40%), (General) (2401A154) 33- Financial Assistance under the Scheme.
The following conditions must be met when implementing the plan:
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
