The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). This scheme is for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products.
The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
The Annual Action Plan amounting to Rs. 178,14,12, 000/- for the implementation of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State during 2021-22 has been accorded administrative approval as per the Government decision in Reference No. The Central Government has released Central Account Fund of Rs. 1,86,30, 000/- has been disbursed to the State Government. 1,24,20, 000/- amounting to Rs. 3,10,50, 000/- to the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board and Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
Government's decision:
Krishi Unnati Yojana- Centre for Scheduled Castes to implement National Horticulture Mission under Integrated Horticulture Development Mission (MIDH) in 2021-22.
