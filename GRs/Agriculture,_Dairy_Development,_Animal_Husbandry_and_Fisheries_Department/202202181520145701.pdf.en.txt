KRIYA/ MUSAN/ PUHAFPIVIO/ A.MA/ 43104/2021, Dy. 27.12.2021 Preamble: In order to ensure financial stability of farmers by providing insurance cover to their fruit crops against climatic hazards, a re-designed weather based Fruit Insurance Scheme is being implemented in the State on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production on a large scale. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide effective compensation to the farmers, a restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme will be implemented in the state for 8 crops of orange, pomegranate, guava, lemon, citrus and grapes (c) (Mrig Bahar) in the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 by the Revenue Board in 26 districts by taking into account the component of HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co. Ltd., Reliance General Insurance Co. Agriculture Insurance Company of India Ltd. The insurance companies are referred to as No. The decision is being implemented by the government.
The Commissionerate of Agriculture has sought the release of State share subsidy as demanded by the Agriculture Insurance Company of India for the year 2021 Mrig Bahar in the restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme. 4 is presented. Accordingly, on the request of the Commissioner of Agriculture, Rs. 17,77,39, 682/- was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: In view of the recommendation made by the Commissionerate of Agriculture under the restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme Mrig Bahar 2021, the State share insurance premium subsidy of Rs. 17,77,39, 682/- (Rupees Seventeen Crores Seventy Seven Lakhs Nineteen Thousand Six Hundred Eighty Eight only) is being approved by the Government to be made available to the Agriculture Insurance Company of India for payment to the insurance companies as per the guidelines of the Scheme.
