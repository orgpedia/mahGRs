Currently, 19 districts in Maharashtra are affected by the disease. Hence, vide No. 2 As per the Government Notification issued herein under the powers conferred by the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Contagious Diseases in Animals Act, 2009, the State of Maharashtra has been declared as a" Controlled Area" in respect of Lumpy Leather. A massive vaccination campaign and preventive measures need to be implemented to control the increasing incidence of this lumpy skin disease. In such a situation, the number of officers/ staff under the control of the Commissionerate of Animal Husbandry available with the State is less due to the vacant posts, which limits the work of effectively preventing the outbreak of lumpy skin disease. Commissioner Animal Husbandry has informed the Government that in the meeting of the Council of Ministers held on 12th September, 2022, the Department had presented the current status of the outbreak of Lumpy Skin Disease and the action being taken by the Department for the control of the disease and the immediate measures to be taken to provide relief to the farmers by preventing the outbreak of this disease. The directive also includes a directive to fill the vacancies in the livestock supervisory cadre temporarily through external sources. In pursuance of this direction, considering the vacancies for Livestock Supervisor (Group-C) (District Council Level) cadre, it is decided to provide manpower services through outsourcing as follows.
Government Decision: In the cadre of Livestock Supervisor (Group-C) at the level of Zila Parishad under the Rural Development Department, a total of 873 manpower services have been provided through Outsourcing. It is being approved to be made available under the government decisions from 2 to 5.
In order to make the process of filling up the services of 873 manpower in the cadre of Livestock Supervisor (Group-C) through outsourcing in a purely temporary manner, a selection committee is being constituted in each revenue division as follows:
Composition of the Divisional Selection Process Committee (1) Divisional Commissioner, concerned Revenue Division Chairman (2) Collector, concerned District Member (3) Chief Executive Officer, concerned Zilla Parishad Member (4) Officer of Social Welfare Department at the concerned Division Level Member (5) Officer of Tribal Development Department at the concerned Division Level Member (6) Deputy Chief Executive Officer (General Administration), concerned Zilla Parishad Member Secretary
2) For out sourcing the said selection committee, the details of the department-wise/ district-wise vacancies should be submitted by the Deputy Chief Executive Officer (General Administration) of the concerned Zilla Parishad to the concerned Divisional Commissioner, Revenue Department.
3) The age limit will be applicable as per the educational qualification prescribed in the service admission rules for the post of Livestock Supervisor Group-C (District Council Level) and as per the prevailing policy of the Government while providing manpower services from external agencies.
The candidates to be recruited through the outsourced system selected by the said Selection Committee shall be engaged by the concerned Deputy Chief Executive Officer (General Administration) concerned Zila Parishad and their day-to-day activities shall be controlled by the concerned District Animal Husbandry Officer, Zila Parishad.

5) Outsourced candidates will be paid Rs. 15000/- (Rs. Fifteen thousand only) lump sum amount will be admissible in the form of honorarium. No other allowances will be admissible.
The services provided through this portal are only for the period up to the receipt of regular candidates for the post of Livestock Supervisor or temporarily for a maximum period of 11 months whichever is earlier.
The appointment will be automatically terminated after 11 months.
7) Even after this, the posts will remain vacant due to the non-availability of regular candidates and if there is an urgent need for these posts for the functioning of the department, the Chief Executive Officer, the concerned Zila Parishad will inform the said selection committee at least three months in advance. After this, the process of making available manpower through external sources should be implemented by the said selection committee in accordance with the vacancies.
While revising the process, the work/ experience of the candidate concerned in the past should be selected on the basis of merit only without considering it in any way while making a fresh selection. However, under no circumstances will the extension of tenure be admissible to the outsourced system selected by the selection committee.
Since the process of providing manpower through external sources is temporary in nature for a period of 11 months or till a regular recommendatory candidate is found for the post of Livestock Supervisor, the person concerned cannot claim any authority for the permanent absorption or retention of persons in the government service who are in service through external sources.
While implementing the said procedure, the candidates appointed shall take an undertaking/ bond in writing before appointment that no claim for absorption or retention in the service will be made as the service will be terminated in any case after a period of 11 months, while ensuring that no court case will arise in future regarding their retention in the government service.
