For the year 2018-19, Solapur district received 39.2 per cent less rainfall than the average, Aurangabad and Beed 50 per cent, Jalna, Latur, Osmanabad, Parbhani, Nandurbar and Jalgaon 60 per cent and 172 of the 355 talukas in the state received less than the average rainfall. In order to provide fodder to the cattle in the drought-like situation in the state and to help them maintain their immunity and fertility, the government is issuing the following decision in connection with the implementation of the project for the distribution of seeds and fertilizers of fodder crops on 100 percent subsidy under the National Agriculture Development Scheme.
Government Decision: In view of the possible fodder shortage in the State during 2018-19, the Government is providing administrative approval to the project of distribution of fodder seeds and fertilizers at 100% subsidy under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana.
