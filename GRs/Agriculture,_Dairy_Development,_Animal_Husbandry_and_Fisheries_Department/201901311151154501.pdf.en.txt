Preamble: The World Bank funded Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project is being implemented in a total of 5142 villages, including 4210 villages in Vidarbha and Marathwada, which are highly vulnerable to climate change, and 932 villages in the mangrove belt of the Purna river basin in Vidarbha.
As per the agreement signed with the World Bank, the State Government will initially spend 70% of the project cost as external share from the World Bank. The Project Directors are required to submit quarterly reports to the World Bank on the expenditure incurred under the project from time to time.
Under the Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project, micro-planning plans are being prepared for the selected villages and works are being undertaken under the project in the respective villages. For this, the Project Management Cell has been allocated Rs. A sum of Rs. 16.05 crore was disbursed. The government was considering the distribution of the remaining funds required for the implementation of the project in 2018-19 to the Project Management Cell, following which the government is taking the following decisions.
