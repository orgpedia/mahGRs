Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to ensure financial stability of the farmers, the State is implementing the Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production on a large scale. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide effective compensation to the farmers, the Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme will be implemented in the State for 8 crops namely Orange, Pomegranate, Pomegranate, Chikoo, Guava, Lemon, Papaya and Grape (C) (Mrig Bahar) in the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 by the Revenue Boards in 26 districts by taking the component of HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co., Ltd., Reliance General Insurance Co., Ltd. Agriculture Insurance Company of India Ltd. The insurance companies are referred to as No. The decision is being implemented in accordance with the Government 1.

Government Decision: In view of the recommendation made by the Commissionerate of Agriculture under the restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme Mrig Bahar 2022, the State share insurance premium subsidy of Rs. 22,40,58, 878/- is being approved by the Government to be made available to the Agriculture Insurance Company of India for payment to the insurance companies as per the guidelines of the scheme. This amount is being disbursed for Mrug Bahar season 2022 and its utilization will not be allowed for other seasons.
The expenditure on the said item shall be met from the budgetary provision approved for the year 2022-23 under the following heads:

The fund should be spent as per the guidelines of Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme and Government Decisions/ Circulars issued by the Government from time to time.
