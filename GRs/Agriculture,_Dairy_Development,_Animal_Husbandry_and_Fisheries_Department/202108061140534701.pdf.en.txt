Industry: Maharashtra is one of the most industrialized states in India, attracting large amounts of economic investment from both domestic and foreign sectors.
Increasing urbanization and rising incomes are the key developers of the retail industry in Maharashtra.
A progressive state like Maharashtra, which is rapidly urbanizing, has a huge scope for development in the food processing sector. Maharashtra has a total of 9 agro-climatic zones and the abundant availability of processable raw materials has created favourable conditions for the food processing industry. Maharashtra has the highest number of registered food processing industries in the country. Similarly, a large amount of foreign investment has also been made in the food processing industry. A total of 3 Mega Food Parks and 7 Food Parks have been set up in the state. In addition, 9 Agro Processing Groups, 62 Cold Chain Plants, 12 Forward and Backward Linkage Plants have been set up. Mumbai Port Trust (MPT), Mumbai and Jawaharlal Nehru Port (JNPT) are the major ports in the state for export of processed food products.
The state has three international and five national airports. Maharashtra has a coastline of 720 km. Therefore, Maharashtra has become the most preferred state for investment.

The growing trend towards double income in living and family has a huge scope for the development of agriculture and food value chain and food processing industry in the state.
Under the centrally sponsored National Mission on Food Processing (NMFP), the food processing industry was being promoted in the state till 2014-15. After the closure of this campaign, the state has implemented the" Mukhyamantri Krishi and Food Processing Scheme" from 20th June 2017. Accordingly, the State Food Processing Policy-2017 has been implemented under the Government decision of Reference 2. Under the new policy to promote the food processing industry, two separate directorates have been approved in the state. Under this, it has been approved to establish a Directorate of Food Processing in the Agriculture Commissionerate to provide services to the food processing industries to provide benefits of various subsidy schemes of the Central and State Governments, to handle individual cases of the schemes and to provide new markets and technologies to the industries. There is no proposal to create posts. Reference No. The administrative structure of the said Directorate was proposed as proposed under section 3. Reference No. Under Section 4, it has been approved to set up" Directorate of Agriculture and Food Processing" subject to availability of manpower available without creation of any post and on deputation or on contract basis at the level of Commissioner (Agriculture) from the office expenses received by the Commissionerate of Agriculture or from the administrative funds received by other Central/ State Schemes. Accordingly, the matter of determining the administrative structure, procedures and ancillary matters of the said Directorate was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: In order to promote the agriculture and food processing industry in the State, it is being approved to establish a separate Directorate of Agriculture and Food Processing at the level of Agriculture Commissionerate. The headquarters of the Directorate of Agriculture and Food Processing will be at the Commissionerate of Agriculture, Pune.
Administrative Structure: For the Directorate of Agriculture and Food Processing to be set up separately in the Commissionerate of Agriculture, as mentioned in the State Food Processing Policy-2017. The Minister (Agriculture) will head the Regulatory Board for Control and Monitoring and the Commissioner (Agriculture), State of Maharashtra will head the Food Processing Project Approval/ Evaluation and Control Committee.
