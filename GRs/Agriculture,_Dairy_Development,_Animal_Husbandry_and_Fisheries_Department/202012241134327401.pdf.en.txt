Introduction Under the National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture, the Rain-fed Area Development (RAD) scheme is being implemented with a Central and State share of 60:40 subsidy. As per the Government decision at Reference No.6, the Central share for the year 2020-21 is Rs. 1191.09 lakhs and State share of Rs. 795.60 crore. The Annual Action Plan amounting to Rs. 2615.52 lakh has been accorded administrative approval on 23.10.2020.

It will be mandatory for the State Governments to implement the Dryland Area Development Programme under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) as per the action plan approved by the Union Ministry of Agriculture for the year 2020-21 and the guidelines issued by the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time. In this regard, the Director (Extension and Training) should control the campaign at the state level. Similarly, subordinate offices should be directed from time to time for effective implementation and control of the mission.
The scheme should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Depletion Prone Area component in the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture issued by the Central Government and the guidelines of the Government Decision dated 27th April, 2018 of the Advanced Agriculture Prosperous Farmers Mission at No. 2 under reference.
Every month, the physical and financial progress of the scheme should be updated in Annexure-III with the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture and the information should also be sent to the State Government.
The detailed quarterly progress report of the scheme should be sent to the Department of Agriculture and Cooperation, Ministry of Agriculture, Government of India as well as to the State Government within a period of 3 months after the end of the financial year.
For the purpose of the Dryland Area Development (RAD) Programme, the Director (Extension and Training), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune, shall issue detailed guidelines to the field officers in accordance with the guidelines of the said department available on the Central Government website as well as the above mentioned terms and conditions.
10. Funds meant for Scheduled Tribes should be spent for Scheduled Tribe beneficiaries.
