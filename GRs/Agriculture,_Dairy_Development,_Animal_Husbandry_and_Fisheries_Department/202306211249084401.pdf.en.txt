The Proposal:
For the implementation of the Fodder and Livestock Development Programme under the District Annual General Plan, Read No. 1 here. The administrative approval has been accorded vide Government Resolution dated 30.08.2010.
The scheme is being implemented in the state through the government decisions, government supplementary and government purification letters issued after that. In view of the importance of mineral mixture in the daily diet of livestock, the government was considering to include four sub-components under the District Annual Plan namely subsidy for procurement of bags of millets for making millets, subsidy for use of millets, subsidy for use of TMR and subsidy for supply of mineral mixture.
The scheme is currently being implemented by taking into consideration all the government decisions, guidelines/ suggestions in the government supplementary and government purse sheets published in the Wacha No. 1 to 4. In order to facilitate the implementation of the scheme, in accordance with the guidelines/ suggestions contained in all the said Government Decisions, Government Supplementary Paper and Government Sanadhi Patra, comprehensive Government Decisions have been issued. All the government decisions from 1 to 4, government supplementary and government cleansing paper are being issued by authorizing comprehensive government decisions as follows.

Government Decision: Under the District Annual General Plan, the Government is giving administrative approval to implement this scheme in the State from 2023-24 by including the following various 06 sub-components under the Fodder and Livestock Development Programme.
(1) Allocation of Incentive Fertilizer Seeds to Farmers for Fertilizer Production: In order to make up for the shortfall in the production of Fertilizers in the State to some extent.
In order to increase the productivity of the livestock with the animal husbandry and accordingly to provide sufficient green fodder to the livestock with the animal husbandry for maximum milk production, it is necessary to take the production of the fodder on the maximum area on a large scale. The programme is aimed at encouraging farmers to take up horticulture.
1.1. While supplying the fodder seed, the beneficiary should have his own farm land and irrigation facility for fodder production. Under this programme, seeds of sorghum, maize, millet, berseem, lucerne, nutrifid and other seeds and improved perennial grass species like Napier, Yashwant, Jaywant etc. should be distributed.
1.2 The beneficiaries who have at least 3 to 4 animals of their own should be given priority under this scheme.
1.3. Rs. Rs. 1,500/- (Rupees One Hundred and Fifty only) Fertilizer seeds/ bales should be supplied within the limit of 1000).
1.4. The purchase of seeds and husk of the fodder should be done from the National Seeds Corporation, State Seeds Corporation (Mahabiz), other government institutions, agricultural universities, Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences, areas under Maharashtra Livestock Development Board, areas under Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Maharashtra Sheep and Goat Development Corporation.
1.5. Technical guidance on cultivation of hay crops should be provided to the beneficiaries before distribution of hay seeds.
1.6. Seed crops should be selected according to the season while distributing the seeds.
1.7. Fertilizers, bacterial culture required for the fodder crops/ haystacks should be procured by the farmer at his own cost.
1.8 Under this program, farmers of any category will be able to benefit. Also, a beneficiary should be given the benefit only once in a season.

(ii) Financial assistance for rearing of hybrid/ indigenous bovine calves, improved/ indigenous buffaloes under Special Livestock Production Programme: 2.1 The main objective of this programme is to create hybrid cows and improved buffaloes which will give more yield by providing them nutritious feed and training the cattle rearers to scientifically rearing the hybrid/ indigenous calves and improved/ indigenous buffaloes. Under this programme, animal feed should be provided to small land holders, marginal land holders and landless agricultural labourers at 50 per cent subsidy and insurance of hybrid/ indigenous culled/ improved/ indigenous pigeons from the cattle rearers.

The following procedures should be adopted for obtaining the necessary funds from the District Planning Committee, approving the proposals under the schemes and disbursing the funds directly.
1. The application form for availing the benefit under this scheme should be circulated from the office of the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune. The application form should be available on the website of the Commissioner of Animal Husbandry, in the office of the Livestock Development Officer (Extension), Panchayat Samiti and District Animal Husbandry Officer, Zilla Parishad.
The concerned District Animal Husbandry Officer, Zilla Parishad should fix a specific time period for getting the benefit under this scheme and invite applications from the farmers at the level of Livestock Development Officer (Extension), Panchayat Samiti. The date-wise record of the applications received should be kept by the Livestock Development Officer (Extension), Panchayat Samiti and all the applications received should be sent to the concerned District Animal Husbandry Officer, Zilla Parishad along with the recommendations for further action.
