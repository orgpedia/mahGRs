A total of 5,220 villages in Akola, Amravati, Buldhana, Washim, Yavatmal and Wardha districts of Vidarbha and 4682 villages in Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar, Jalna, Beed, Latur, Dharashiv, Parbhani, Hingoli and Nanded districts of Marathwada (including 932 villages in the mangrove belt of Purna river basin in Vidarbha), 460 villages in Jalgaon district and all villages in Malegaon taluka of Nashik district, which are highly vulnerable to climate change, have been provided with World Bank assistance for a period of 6 years from May 2018. Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project is being implemented at an estimated cost of Rs 4000 crore.
As per the Government decision dated 17.01.2017, the World Bank funded Climate Adaptive Project (PoCRA) will henceforth be called as" Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project." Accordingly, instructions have been issued that the new name" Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project" should henceforth be used for all office activities related to this project under the Government of Maharashtra.
However, the meetings, meetings, speeches and official documents and other correspondence of the World Bank-funded Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project are addressed by the short name PoCRA, instead of the name Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project of the project.
4.
