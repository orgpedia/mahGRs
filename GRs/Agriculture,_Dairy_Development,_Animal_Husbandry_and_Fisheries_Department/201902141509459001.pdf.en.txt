Introduction: The State Government has launched the Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Garden Plantation Scheme for farmers who are not eligible to cultivate fruit orchards under the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme.
The scheme will be implemented during 2018-19. 1 Administrative approval has been granted by the Government Decision dated 06/07/2018. To implement the scheme, Rs. 450.00 lakh has been budgeted in the Legislative Assembly session of November, 2018 through Supplementary Demands. Accordingly, the Finance Department has approved the scheme. As per Circular dated 25/01/2019, 80% of the limit of Rs. 360.00 lakh has been provided by the Tribal Development Department to this department. The matter of making available this fund to the Commissioner (Agriculture) on the budgetary distribution system was under the consideration of the government. The government is taking the following steps:
Decision of the Government: 1. For the implementation of Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Plantation Scheme in 2018-19, Rs. 360.00 lakhs (Rs. Three crore sixty lakhs only) is being made available to the Commissioner Agriculture on the budgetary distribution system.
(2) The funds shall be spent out of the provisions budgeted for the current year under the following headings:
Demand No. T-5 2401- Crop Protection 796, Scheduled Tribes Area Sub-Plan, (01) (36) Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Garden Plantation Scheme (2401 A898) 33 Financial Assistance
