Introduction Under the National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture, the Rain-fed Area Development (RAD) scheme is being implemented with a Central and State share of 60:40 subsidy. As per the decision of the Government in Reference No.3, the Central Share for the year 2018-19 is Rs. 2496.50 lakhs and State share of Rs. 1664.33 lacs out of which Rs. 4160.84 lakh has been administratively approved and Rs. Central share of Rs. 117.50 lakh and State share of Rs. 78.33 crore. A sum of Rs. 195.83 lakh has been disbursed. As per Reference No. 5, the Central Government has released Rs. 117.50 lakh has been released. Following this, the government took decision.
Government's decision:

3. While implementing the scheme, action should be taken in accordance with the provisions of the government decision issued earlier.
The Director (Extension and Training) is being authorized to allocate the funds made available under this Government decision district-wise/ office-wise and he should distribute the funds to be allocated district-wise under the Mission to the districts through the Divisional Agriculture Joint Director through the BDS system and the District Superintending Agriculture Officer should withdraw the funds received and deposit them in the PFMS account and make the entire payment on PFMS.
5. Funds meant for Scheduled Tribes should be spent for Scheduled Tribe beneficiaries.
The report of expenditure incurred from the provision made, the utilization certificate of the fund, the material objectives achieved in the headings/ sub-headings, the form in which the benefit of the scheme was given to the beneficiaries of the Scheduled Tribes, etc. should be submitted to the Government for submission to the Tribal Development Department.
