However, the United Arab Emirates (UAE) has banned the export of live goats and sheep to the country. As per the protocol issued on 24.10.2018, it is mandatory for a registered veterinarian holding an M.V.Sc degree to submit a health certificate attested with a signature.
Considering the above, it is necessary to authorize the concerned authority to conduct pre-export health check-up while exporting live goats and sheep from the state and to issue such certificate, if appropriate, and to ensure the sample of the certificate of health check-up. Accordingly, the following recommendations are made:
The Registered Veterinarians in Government Service, as defined in the Transport of Animals Rules, 1978 under the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960, as Qualified Veterinary Surgeon, as authorised under clause (c) of section 2 of the Veterinary Council of India Act, 1984, is being provisionally authorised as the competent authority to conduct pre-export health check-up and issue such certificate, if appropriate, while exporting live goats and sheep from the State.
(2) A veterinary examination of sheep and goats as prescribed in Schedule J (Rule 65) of the Transport of Animals Rules, 1978, as amended by the Act, 1960, relating to the prevention of cruelty to animals, shall be carried out by the competent authority.
3. For veterinary examination of sheep and goats, refer to the section No. mentioned in 4. The service charge should be levied as per the Government decision dated 04.01.2001.
4. Officers holding the rank of Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry and holding the rank of M.V.Sc in the service of the State Government are hereby authorised as the competent authority to issue pre-transport health certificates of the animals in the airport premises of the State from where the live goats are exported to the United Arab Emirates (UAE).
(5) Care should be taken to ensure that the relevant laws mentioned in the advisory in Articles 2 and 3 are strictly followed when issuing transport certificates to live animals.
