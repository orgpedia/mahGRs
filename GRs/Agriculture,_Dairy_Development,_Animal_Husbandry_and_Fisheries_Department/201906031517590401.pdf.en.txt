The Proposal:
Under the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS), the State Government has launched the Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Garden Plantation Scheme for farmers who are not eligible to cultivate fruit orchards.
As informed in Circular No. 2, the matter of grant of administrative approval for implementation of the scheme in the year 2019-20 was under consideration. The government is taking the following steps:
Government's decision:
1. in the state. Administration approval is being given to implement the Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Garden Plantation Scheme in the year 2019-20 and a total budgetary provision of Rs. 4000.00 lakh for general category, Rs. 456.84 lakh for SC category and Rs. 700.00 lakh for ST category is being approved for this scheme in the current year.
The budgetary funds available for this scheme should be spent under the following head of accounts for the general category, scheduled castes and scheduled tribes as allocated by the Government.

The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the provisions of the Government Decision referred to in Reference No. 1.
The Director (Horticulture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune will be the implementing agency for the scheme during 2019-20.
Commissioner (Agriculture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune will be the State Level Controller. The Assistant Director (Accounts-1), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune is being declared as the Procurement and Disbursement Officer for this scheme.
The Director (Horticulture) shall review the implementation of the scheme from time to time and submit a report to the Government through the Commissioner (Agriculture) from time to time.
The programme should be implemented with 100% of the budgetary provision approved for the current year.
