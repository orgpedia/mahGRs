Read:- 1) Go to No. Kriya/ Mrisamrucha/ M.S./ 2658/2020, dated January 22, 2020
Preamble: There are 31 District Soil Survey Soil Testing Laboratories functioning at the district headquarters in the State. Out of these, 8 labs are in private rented premises and 23 are in government buildings. Soil Health Cards are distributed to the farmers after examining the soil of their farmers within the stipulated time, and soil health cards are distributed after analyzing the soil samples received from the farmers under the ambitious project of Soil Health Cards and other schemes of the Central Government.
During the year 2019-20, for the payment of rent and tax of District Soil Survey Soil Testing Laboratory in the State, under the head Rent and Tax (14), Rs. 22.52 lakh has been provided. Of this Rs. 13.51 lakh has been disbursed for the period April to December, 2019 and the entire amount disbursed at the end of December, 2019 has been spent. In addition to the Rs. 33.00 lakhs for the purpose of payment of rent and taxes for the laboratory building. A sum of Rs. 33.00 lakh was proposed by way of supplementary demand. Since the demand was accepted, the matter was under consideration for disbursement of the said provision.
Government Decision: Under the State Sponsored Scheme of District Soil Survey and Soil Investigation and Soil Analysis Laboratory (Mandatory), Rs. 33.00 lakh is being disbursed.
The expenditure for this purpose shall be met from the grants sanctioned for the current financial year 2019-20 and shall be spent under the following heads.
