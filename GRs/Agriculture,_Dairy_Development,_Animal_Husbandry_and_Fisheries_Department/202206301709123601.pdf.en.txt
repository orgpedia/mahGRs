The State of Maharashtra has a coastline of 720 km with 173 fishing harbours. In order to sustain sustainable fishing in the catchment areas of the State, provisions have been made under the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981 in the State to regulate fishing activities in the coastal waters of the State and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto. The Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021 has been enacted by amending this Act. The Act provides for the power to regulate, restrict or prohibit certain activities of fishing in specified areas. There is a provision that the orders imposing or prohibiting such restrictions may be notified after consultation with the State Advisory and Controlling Committee and the Advisory and Controlling Committee may take into consideration the recommendations of the District Committees, if any, before recommending the regulations to the State Government.
However, in order to comply with the said Act, it was under the consideration of the Government to constitute a" District Committee" for each of the coastal districts of Palghar, Thane, Raigad, Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg under the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021.
The Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021 has been enacted by amending the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981. Under Section 3 sub-section (3) (a) of the said Act, the Government is pleased to approve the constitution of a District Committee for each of the coastal districts of Palghar, Thane, Raigad, Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg.
