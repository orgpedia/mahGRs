PROPOSALS In order to manage the pest diseases in the major agricultural crops of the State, namely, Soybean, Cotton, Paddy, Tur and Gram, a Pest Disease Surveillance and Advisory Project (CropSAP) on crops is being implemented in the State under the National Agriculture Development Scheme during 2009-10 to 2012-13 and under the Regular State Plan from 2013-14.
At present, the US Army bug has been found infesting sugarcane and maize crops in the state. Cane and maize crops in 1,17,512 and 5,139 hectares respectively have been affected due to these pests. The new pest, the American military bug, is also infecting maize crops as well as sorghum and sugarcane crops.
Meetings have been held with agricultural universities, crop research institutes, sugar factories in view of the management of humming and American military weevil. However, the scientists pointed out that since both hummingbirds and American armyworms are multi-eater, there is a possibility of damage to other crops as well. Awareness is being created at the moment.
The Annual Action Plan for the ongoing CropSap and HortSap projects in the State for pest control has been approved with an outlay of Rs. 4147.08 lakh. Approved vide Government Resolution dated 19.05.2018, out of which Rs. 2747.08 lakh has been disbursed. The project worth Rs. 802.50 lakh approved for Humani and US Military Base Control Rs. 802.50 lakh and the other savings from the CropSap and HortSap projects for the control of Humani and the US Army.
