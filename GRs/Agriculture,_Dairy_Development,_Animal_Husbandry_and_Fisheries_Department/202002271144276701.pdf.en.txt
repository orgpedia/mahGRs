In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-rule (1) of rule (3) of the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (Establishment and Regulation of Cruelty to Animals) Rules, 2001, in order to effectively implement the provisions of the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 of the Central Government, the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Society has been constituted in each district of the State by notification dated 14th March, 2017.
Section 35 of the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 provides that the State Government may establish Animal Shelters/ Homes (Infirmaries and Animal Shelters) for the treatment and care of animals in respect of which offences have been committed against this Act. Similarly, Rule 4 of the said Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (Establishment and Regulation of Society for Prevention of Cruelty to Animals) Rules, 2001 also provides for providing medical treatment to injured animals received from District Society for Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (SPCA) and setting up of Infirmaries and Animal Shelters for their shelter and providing working space to District Society for Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (SPCA).
(3) The matter of giving necessary directions in accordance with the above provisions was under consideration of the Government.
GOVERNMENT Circular: 4. In pursuance of the provisions mentioned above, the Government directs as under: (i) Separate cabins for SPCA should be provided in the office of the Deputy Commissioner of Animal Husbandry in each district. A board with the name" Office of District Animal Cattle Control Society (SPCA)" should be put up outside the office.
(ii) The name board to be put up outside the office of the Deputy Commissioner for Animal Husbandry should contain the telephone number of the office of the Deputy Commissioner and the mobile number of at least 2 non-official members, the e-mail ID of the SPCA by creating a new e-mail.
(ii) The office shall maintain a record of all the representations, applications, complaints, correspondence etc. received from the SPCA.

(iv) The office of the Deputy Commissioner shall be responsible for the ancillary work such as recording of incoming and outgoing letters, handling of complaints, representations, applications etc. received on e-mail, preservation of minutes of meetings, etc.
(v) The responsibility of handling the affairs of the above-mentioned SPCA in the office of the Commissioner Animal Husbandry should be entrusted to the responsible officers in his office.
(vi) The Commissioner Animal Husbandry should create a webpage of SPCA of each district and link it to the website of the Department of Animal Husbandry www.ahd.maharashtra.gov.in.
So that the citizens of the state can easily access the information of SPCA.
(vii) The webpage of SPCA should be kept updated by the Office of the Commissioner Animal Husbandry.
5. Govardhan Govansh Seva Kendra All the Gaushalas which have been/ are being funded under this scheme are being declared as Animal Shelters/ Ambulances (Infirmaries and Animal Shelters) for the respective districts.
Also, all District Veterinary Hospitals and Taluk Mini Veterinary Hospitals and Veterinary Dispensaries should provide treatment to injured/ sick animals at all Animal Shelters/ In-Hospital (Infirmaries and Animal Shelters) within their jurisdiction by assigning fixed time of one day in a week and visiting them from time to time in case of emergency.
