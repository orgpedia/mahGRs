The Proposal:
Private veterinarians selected by the selection committee constituted by the Government under the provisions of section 1 and 2 of the Maharashtra Animal Preservation Act (Amendment), 1995 for conducting pre-slaughter inspection of scheduled animals in slaughter houses in the State authorised by APEDA or similar authority of the Central/ State Government are appointed by the respective slaughter houses. Also, a vigilance team has been formed as per the government decision at serial number 4 in Read.
(2) The slaughterhouses shall regulate the conduct of pre-slaughter inspection of animals under the Schedule to the said Act in a proper manner and in accordance with the rules and regulations and also the functioning of the designated private veterinarian to prevent any illegal activity in such places. The government was contemplating issuing necessary orders in this regard.
Government Decision: In view of the above, the following inspection team is being appointed to control the activities of the private veterinarians appointed for pre-slaughter inspection of scheduled animals in APEDA authorized slaughterhouses and to review the activities of the Vigilance team constituted under Government Decision dated 22nd January, 2022 at A. No. 4, Wacha.
(1) Composition of the Screening Team: (i) The Regional Joint Commissioner Animal Husbandry concerned- Chairman (ii) The Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry in the office of the Deputy Commissioner, District Animal Husbandry of the concerned district- Member (ii) The Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry in the office of the Divisional Animal Disease Investigation Laboratory in the concerned division- Member Secretary

(2) The inspection team shall visit the slaughter house in their jurisdiction at least once in a month and conduct proper inspection by the appointed veterinarians, as well as review the activities of the District Vigilance Team and submit a report to the Commissioner Animal Husbandry.
4. Adoption of INAF system in pre-slaughter inspection (i) Scheduled animals coming for pre-slaughter inspection shall not be subjected to pre-slaughter inspection unless they are registered under the Tagging and Inaf (INAPH) system.
(ii) If the pre-slaughter inspection finds that the scheduled animal meets all the criteria prescribed in the Act for slaughter, the tag should be noted on the pre-slaughter inspection certificate.
(ii) The tags affixed to the animals before slaughter should be handed over to the vigilance team headed by the Deputy Commissioner of District Animal Husbandry and it will be mandatory for the office of the Deputy Commissioner of District Animal Husbandry to take necessary action on the Inaf system.
(iv) It should be ensured that no pre-slaughter inspection takes place without registration in the tagging/ INAPH system. Otherwise, legal action will be taken against the owner of the slaughterhouse/ veterinarian appointed at the slaughterhouse.
