INTRODUCTION: The Central Government vide order dated 01.02.2020 has prohibited the installation or operation of destructive fishing methods, including bullock and pear-trolling, without generator or generator, underwater/ submerged or floating on the surface of the water, artificial lights/ LED lights, fish attractants or any other light emitting artificial devices/ devices on mechanized and mechanized fishing vessels using trawling, persin and nets in the Indian Exclusive Economic Zone outside the territorial waters of the State.
In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of section 4 of the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981 (MFRCA), and in consultation with the Advisory Committees constituted under clause (a), (b) and (c) of sub-section (2) of section 4 and in accordance with the provisions of section 3 of the said Act, the State Government hereby prohibits the fishing boats from using artificial lights/ LED lights, fish attractants or any other light emitting artificial devices/ devices floating on the surface of the water in the marine catchment area (12 nautical miles) of the State of Maharashtra.
However, it has been found that large scale fishing is still going on using floating artificial lights/ LED lights, lights that attract fish or any other light emitting artificial devices/ devices. As per the letter, the Collector and District Magistrate, Raigad-Alibag, have pointed out that there is a conflict between the local fishermen and those fishing with LED lights, which is creating a serious law and order problem.

The government circular issued the following instructions to take strict action against boats fishing with LED lights.
(1) The Assistant Commissioner of Fisheries, Fisheries Development Officer, Fisheries Training Officer and Assistant Fisheries Development Officer of the District Office under the Department of Fisheries may direct the fishing boats carrying the materials prohibited in this notification to land at the fishing harbour/ fish in their jurisdiction.
Venturing into sea from the centre/ bay area shall be strictly prohibited.
(2) However, if the fishing vessel is found to be fishing within the territorial waters of the State (12 nautical miles) in such a manner by LED lights, the Licensing Officer shall take action for cancellation of the fishing license issued under section 8 (1) b of the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981, of the vessel violating the provisions of this notification and shall immediately take action for suspension or cancellation of the certificate of registration issued under section 9, as the case may be, under section 9 (4).
