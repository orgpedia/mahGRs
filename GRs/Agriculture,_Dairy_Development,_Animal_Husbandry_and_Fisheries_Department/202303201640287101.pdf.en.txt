Preamble: The distribution of Soil Health Cards (SHC) under National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) has been started by the Government of India since 2014-15. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16. Central Government Reference No. The above 02. As per the letter dated 24.3.2022, the Soil Health Card and Soil Health Management Schemes under Soil Health and Fertility have been included under the National Agriculture Development Scheme. Soil health cards are distributed to farmers after analysing soil samples. Through soil health cards, farmers are informed about the chemical properties of their land, the level of major food elements and the deficiency and condition of micro elements.
As per the minutes of the State Level Project Sanctioning Committee (SLC), the Annual Action Plan (AAP) based Soil Health Card and Management Plan under National Agriculture Development Scheme (NADP) for the year 2022-23 has been notified vide Reference No. Above the 03. The approval has been accorded vide letter dated 12.7.2022.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. 3322/ PR. No. 147/1 4. The following terms and conditions shall be observed while implementing the scheme:
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central and State Governments along with the progress report as soon as possible.
The physical and financial progress reports on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central and State Governments by the 5th of every month.
3) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
4) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
5) All financial laws/ tenders to be made in accordance with the prescribed procedures while spending the said funds.
Rules and Regulations/ Procedure/ within the limits of financial powers/ P.W.D./ Manual
in accordance with the principles of CVC/ as per the directions of CAG/ extant Government decisions/ rules/ circulars
As per the provisions, the expenditure should be processed by the implementing agencies as per the budget and treasury rules. It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Regional Office to ensure that no Government rule/ authority is violated under any circumstances.
6) All funds disbursed under Government decision from time to time shall be mandatorily routed through SNA system in PFMS.
