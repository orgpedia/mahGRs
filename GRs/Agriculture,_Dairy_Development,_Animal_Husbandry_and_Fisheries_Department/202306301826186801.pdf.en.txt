7. Benefit to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and women farmers as prescribed by the Central Government under this mission.
The Union Ministry of Agriculture has issued a reference letter to the State Government for the year 2023-24 under the Dryland Area Development Scheme. As per the administrative approval given vide letter dated 14.04.2023 and as per the action plan approved for the Mission during the current year and as per the directions of the Union Ministry from time to time, it will be mandatory for the Director (Extension and Training) to control the Mission from the State level and to direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the Mission. Priority should be given to small and marginal farmers or groups of such farmers while selecting beneficiary farmers under various components under the dryland development scheme. Also, applications of farmers should be invited and beneficiaries should be selected in a transparent manner.
Under this campaign, for the purchase of various inputs (fertilizers, medicines, seeds, irrigation tools, machines/ tools, etc.), the subsidy should be deposited directly into the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through direct benefit transfer as per the prevailing policy of the government. Beneficiaries will have the freedom to buy these items from the open market. However, if the supply of the feedstock produced by the Government agencies/ Public Undertakings of the Government under this mission is made, the Government in question shall take a decision. Action to be taken as on 19/04/2017.
