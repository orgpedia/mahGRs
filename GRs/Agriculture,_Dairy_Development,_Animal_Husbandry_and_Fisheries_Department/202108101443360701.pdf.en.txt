The Election Commission of India has directed all States to set up Electoral Literacy Clubs in the colleges of the State to make the students of the colleges aware of the electoral process, enrolling them as voters, voting and to make them aware of democracy under the Systematic Voter Education and Electoral Participation (SVEEP) programme of the Election Commission. The Principal Secretary and Chief Electoral Officer, Maharashtra, Shri. As per the letter dated 28.07.2021, it has been requested to set up Electoral Literacy Clubs in all agricultural universities/ colleges in the state.
Accordingly, under this circular, all the agricultural universities of the state are being directed to establish an Electoral Literacy Club in their university campus, as well as to direct the agricultural colleges affiliated to this agricultural university to establish an Electoral Literacy Club immediately.
The procedure for the establishment of Electoral Literacy Boards in the Agricultural University campus as well as in the college, as well as the programs to be taken under it, are as follows:
1) Agricultural universities and colleges should establish an electoral literacy board in consultation with the students.
2) A professor from an agricultural college should be appointed as the nodal officer and guide for the Electoral Literacy Board.

3) Students of the College of Agriculture will be members of the Electoral Literacy Board. A committee of students and faculty members from each class will be elected by the students.
4) The elected student representatives will form the executive committee of the Electoral Literacy Board.
The elected students will elect the President and Vice President of the Board.
5) The students of the agricultural college should be included in the electoral literacy board through the student head. Further, in the Agriculture College, the Student Head will be the Convener of the Electoral Literacy Board and will assist the Nodal Officer in his work.
6) The Campus Ambassador will act as the convener of the Electoral Literacy Board in the Agricultural University campus. Also, the student of the university should be included in the Electoral Literacy Board through the area representative who will help the nodal officers in carrying out their work.

11) Information about webinars, seminars being organized by the office of the Chief Electoral Officer should be sent to the Electoral Literacy Board group of the College of Agriculture and encourage students to listen to them.
12) Students who have completed 18 years of age in the College of Agriculture should get the records of whether the voter is registered or not, and update them. For this, the agricultural college will have to take the following points into account.
Students who are not enrolled should be encouraged to register.
Students should be encouraged to register by asking them about voter registration while admitting them to the next class, giving the result certificate.
Students can register online by visiting https:// www. nvsp. nic. in/ website or through Voter Helpline App or by visiting the Voter Registration Office where they are residing.
The nodal officer of the Electoral Literacy Board should coordinate with the office of the Agricultural University/ College regarding the registration, review the total number of students in the college, the students registered as voters and the students who are not registered and include that data in the annual report of the Electoral Literacy Board.
13) The activities conducted under the Electoral Literacy Board of the College of Agriculture; participation of students in the activities organized by the Office of the Chief Electoral Officer; half-yearly and annual report on the total number of students in the College of Agriculture, registered voters, unregistered students should be sent to the Chief Electoral Officer, Maharashtra State on the mail ID ceo _ maharashtra@eci.gov.in as well as to your respective Deputy District Election Officer on the email ID in Annexure-1 attached. The report should mention the mobile phone of the nodal officers of the Electoral Literacy Board, so that further coordination with them is facilitated.
14. District Collectors and District Election Officers and Deputy District Election Officers shall be the Liaison/ Coordination Officers at the District level in respect of Electoral Literacy Boards. The District Collector, the District Election Officer and the Deputy District Election Officer should take guidance from time to time regarding the activities to be undertaken under the Electoral Literacy Board.
15) Necessary support of the National Service Scheme administration should be taken for these activities.
The following activities should be undertaken under the Electoral Literacy Board in 2021-2022:
Online competitions: Essay, speech, cartoon, parody, poetry, song, meme, short film making, slogan, slogan writing, poetry writing, quiz, etc.
