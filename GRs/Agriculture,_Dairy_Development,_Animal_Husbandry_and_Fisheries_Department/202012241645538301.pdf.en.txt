Introduction: The Ministry of Food Processing Industries was established in the year 1988 during the Seventh Five Year Plan. The Ministry was providing grants to food processing training institutes and food processing projects. During the 8th to 10th Five Year Plan period, the Ministry of Food Processing Industries emphasized on setting up of Food Processing Complexes besides providing subsidy to food processing projects. Under this, a total of 7 Food Processing Parks have been set up in the state. Subsequently, a total of 3 Mega Food Parks were sanctioned during the 11th and 12th Five Year Plan period. Under the 12th Five Year Plan, the Centrally Sponsored National Mission on Food Processing was launched with the active participation of the State Governments. However, after accepting the recommendations of the Fourteenth Finance Commission, the Government of India decided to discontinue the Central funding to the scheme from 2015-16 and the State Government was informed to consider whether or not to continue the scheme as a State level scheme. The Government has approved the implementation of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) for Formalization of Micro Food Enterprises (PMFME) in the State for a period of five years from 2020-21 to 2024-25.
(a) Implementation of Centrally Aided Pradhan Mantri Scheme for Formalization of Micro Food Enterprises (PMFME) in the State as per the guidelines of the Central Government is being approved.
(b) It is being approved to spend in the ratio of 60:40 as per the guidelines of the Central Government for this scheme, as well as to open the required head of accounts and provide financial provisions for this scheme.
(c) The State Project Management Unit (SPMU) is being set up as the State Project Management Cell for the implementation of the scheme.
(d) Since the provisions in the implementation of both the Chief Minister's Agriculture and Food Processing Scheme and the Prime Minister's Micro Food Processing Industry Scheme are different, it is approved to continue the Directorate of the Chief Minister's Agriculture and Food Processing Scheme and the Director (Agricultural Processing and Planning) established at the level of Commissioner.
Objective of the Scheme: 1.1 To expand the scope of existing individual micro food processing units as well as Farmer Producer Organizations, Self Help Groups and Producer Cooperatives.
1.2. Strengthen branding and marketing of products and link them to organised supply chains.
1.3 To support the formalization of two lakh existing industries.
1.4 To provide greater benefits to the micro enterprises by providing common services such as common processing facilities, laboratories, storage, packaging, marketing and comprehensive services for industry growth.
1.5. Strengthening of research and training institutions in the food processing sector.

How to apply: Projects that want to take advantage of this scheme will have to submit an application online on the FME Portal. The Resource Persons (RPs) appointed at the district level will then assist the entrepreneurs who have submitted applications to prepare project plans, help in getting bank loans, licenses, registration etc. The applications for public infrastructure and branding and marketing facilities of Farmer Producer Organizations/ Self Help Groups/ Cooperative Societies will be submitted to the State Nodal Agency (SNA) along with the Project Plan (DPR) and recommendation of the District Committee.
The State Nodal Agencies will evaluate the project for grant and submit it to the State Level Project Approval Committee (SLAC). Once the SLAC approval is received, the SNA will recommend to the concerned bank for grant of loan.

Mode of disbursement of subsidy: Individual beneficiary will submit the application on FME Portal. The beneficiary bank will be informed about the approval of the project by the State Level Project Approval Committee. The concerned bank will inform the nodal bank (designated by MoFPI at the country level) about the sanction of loan to the beneficiary. The beneficiary will get to know from the beneficiary bank/ nodal bank/ FME Portal about the loan sanctioned to the beneficiary. After this, 60% of the subsidy amount will be transferred from the central government and 40% from the state government to the nodal bank. The Nodal Bank will transfer the consolidated 100% subsidy to the beneficiary's bank account. The above Nodal Bank will be appointed by the Ministry of Food Processing Industries, Government of India, New Delhi.
Special outlays will be earmarked for beneficiaries belonging to Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes in proportion to their population.
