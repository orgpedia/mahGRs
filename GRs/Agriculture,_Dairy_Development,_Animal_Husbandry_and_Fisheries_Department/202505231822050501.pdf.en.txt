the Government Order
The exchange of transfers of Maharashtra Government employees and periodic transfers for administrative reasons of officers of Group-A cadre, Livestock Development Officer, Department of Animal Husbandry, with the approval of the competent authorities as per the provisions of 4 (9) 4 (2) and 4 (3) of the Prevention of Delay in discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005 are being done through counselling-2025. The details of periodic changes through counselling in 2025 are as follows:
a) Transferable officers in difficult areas in respect of tribal / Naxalite victims - Statement-A
b) Transferable Officer in Non-Difficult Area in respect of Tribal / Naxalite Victims - Statement-B
2. Column No. He is being transferred to the position indicated in section 4. The officers with less than 9 years remaining for retirement out of the periodical transferable officers in the year 2025 are being given an extension of tenure as per the provisions of the Transfer Act, 2005.
3. The transferred officers in the accompanying statement shall immediately appear at the place indicated by the transfer as soon as such transfer order is received. The Head of the Office concerned shall relieve the transferred officers forthwith (within 7 days from the date of issue of the order) as there is no need to issue separate orders at the field level for discharge. The officers concerned shall forthwith submit their joining report along with the transfer certificate to the Government through the Commissioner, Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State, Pune without any delay in the prescribed manner after appearing for transfer.
4. The head of the office concerned shall be solely responsible if the head of the office concerned is not relieved within a minimum period of 7 days for the officers concerned to join the post of transferor. The Commissioner, Animal Husbandry, Pune is informed to propose disciplinary action against the head of the office and submit a report to the government immediately.
The salary of the transferred officers for the next month (for the month of June) should be drawn from the transferred office only. The head of the office concerned will be liable for financial dishonesty if the salary for the month of June is drawn from the office before the transfer without relieving the transferred officer and will be liable for disciplinary action in this regard.
6. They were provided with an opportunity to choose the places of their choice while conducting the transfer proceedings by counselling regarding the proposed transfer. Hence, the officers who have been transferred have been given regular tenures.

It should be noted by the concerned that no statement regarding partial change in revised post / posting will be entertained till completion. Also, if Rajkoy tries to pressurise the administration through AGATA or other office bearers with regard to getting a post at the revised transfer location, these officers will be eligible for disciplinary action as per the provision of Maharashtra Civil GAT (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979.
7. The transferred officers shall take care to join the place of transfer after superannuation within the time prescribed under the rules of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Incorporation) Rules, 1981. Those concerned should note that otherwise, their period of absence will count as a "non-deceasing day."
Leave shall not be granted for any reason except with the prior permission of the Government of the officer concerned.
Also, as per Rule-30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, no leave applications of officers transferred by the original office shall be entertained. If such applications are received, they should be returned by post to the concerned authorities at the original address.
9. Transfer proceedings have been carried out by counsel with respect to the present periodic transfer. In view of this, it may be noted by the concerned that no statement will be sought from the officers concerned regarding the appointment of the revised post. Those concerned should note that in case they do not immediately join the post of posting as per the said transfer order or attempt to cancel the transfer under any kind of pressure, disciplinary action will be taken against them as the act violates Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979, treating it as misconduct.
10. The Commissioner, Animal Husbandry, Pune is informed to immediately submit a report to the Government proposing disciplinary action against the officers concerned if they fail to join the place of transfer within the stipulated period or if the head of the office does not take immediate action to relieve / depute them. Request to submit the action taken report to the mansing.patil@nic.in e-mail address.
