The Prevention and Control of Infectious and Contagious Diseases in Animals Act, 2009: 1.1. It has been found that there is an outbreak of lumpy skin disease in cattle of cow and buffalo class in Jalgaon, Ahmednagar, Akola, Pune and Dhule districts of the state. Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD) has been included as a scheduled disease in the list of diseases notified by the Central Government under the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Communicable Diseases in Animals Act, 2009.
1.2.Agriculture, Animal Husbandry, Dairying and Fisheries Department 2 According to the government notification here, Read No. 1 In exercise of the powers conferred on the State Government by section 36 of the Central Government Act, the powers of the State Government under the said Act to prevent, control and eradicate the disease shall be delegated to all the District Collectors with immediate effect.
2. Formation of District and Taluk level Committees for Lumpy Skin Disease Sanitation: 2.1. District and Taluk level Committees are being constituted for regular review of the disease, monitoring of preventive vaccination programme, isolation of infected animals from the herd, treatment of infected animals, proper coordination with various departments and local bodies and scientific disposal of dead animals.

3.1.Viruses causing lumpy skin disease fall under the category of Capripox in the Devi virus group.
3.2. Lumpy Skin Disease is a viral disease of cattle belonging to the cow and buffalo category and is transmitted to animals of all ages. Young animals are more susceptible to the disease.
4.1. Spread of lumpy skin disease The disease is spread by external insects (mosquitoes, mites, flies, etc.) as well as by secretions from the skin of sick animals, nasal secretions, milk, saliva, semen and other secretions.
5.1. Symptoms of lumpy skin disease in infected animals include fever in animals, hard lumps 10-15 mm in diameter all over the body, sores in the mouth, nose and eyes, difficulty in chewing fodder, weakness, loss of appetite, weight loss, reduced milk production, vision loss due to ulcers in the eyes, sometimes there is puffiness or mastitis, swelling in the legs, miscarriage in stray animals.
5.2. Animals infected with this disease recover in two to three weeks.
6.1. In order to prevent the outbreak of Lumpy Skin Disease, various steps should be taken by the Animal Husbandry Department and the concerned departments of the Government to prevent the outbreak of Lumpy Skin Disease.

6.2. If symptoms of this disease are found in animals, it is mandatory for every person/ non-government organization/ concerned local body, etc. to immediately inform the nearest veterinary institution in writing under section 4 (1) of the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Communicable Diseases in Animals Act, 2009. All concerned should note that any delay in this may result in the registration of a case under the provisions of Section 32 of the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Communicable Diseases in Animals Act, 2009.
6.3. Read No. 5 The district level task force constituted under the decision of the Government here should immediately visit the affected areas and ensure that necessary action is taken and necessary guidance is given to the concerned.
6.4 Rapid Response Teams (RRTs) should be deployed in the affected areas immediately.
6.5 Further, awareness regarding this disease among the livestock rearers/ farmers, necessary vaccination and medication should be carried out through the mobile veterinary teams set up in the district/ taluka under the Chief Minister's Animal Health Scheme.
6.6 Meetings should be held regularly with the milk unions, gaushalas and gram panchayats of the district/ taluka to ensure strict adherence to biosecurity regarding the disease.
6.7. Once the Veterinary Institute is aware of the infection, the concerned Veterinary Institute should inform the District/ Divisional/ State level laboratory of the Animal Husbandry Department and take immediate action to collect and send the samples for diagnosis.
6.8. The State Government has delegated powers to all the concerned District Collectors and District Magistrates to declare the suspected/ affected areas as containment zones and accordingly the concerned Collectors shall take action to issue notification without delay.
6.9 Farmers/ herders should refrain from purchasing livestock from outside the State till the outbreak of the disease is controlled.
6.10 As directed by the Central Government, if the boundaries of the districts in which the disease has spread in the state are with other states, the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune should immediately inform the concerned state's Animal Husbandry Department to take precautions and implement preventive measures.
6.11. Animal markets within 10 km radius of the affected area shall be closed.
7.1 Transportation of infected animals from one State to another State/ District to another State/ District should be checked at the District/ State border check posts as there is a high possibility of transmission of the disease from infected animals to healthy animals and to ensure that infected animals do not enter the State/ District.
8. Lumpy Skin Disease Preventive Vaccination: 8.1. As this disease is a viral infection, it is important to take preventive measures immediately to prevent the spread of the disease. According to the guidelines of the Central Government, the cow and buffalo cattle in the village falling within a radius of 5 km from the affected area should be immediately vaccinated using the Goat Pox Vaccine (Uttarkashi Strain).

9.1. Healthy animals should be kept separate from infected animals to prevent them from getting infected. Separate arrangements for drinking water and grazing should be made for healthy and infected animals.
As the disease is caused by mosquitoes, gochids, flies, etc. in the cattle shed and the surrounding area, various allopathic and ayurvedic medicines should be sprayed immediately as directed by the central government.
9.3. The cattle sheds and the adjoining areas should be sprayed immediately with the help of the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Nagar Panchayat/ Municipal Corporation. Also, in case of death of animals due to disease, the concerned Gram Panchayat, Nagar Panchayat and Municipal Corporation will be responsible for their disposal through scientific methods under the guidance of a veterinary officer.
9.4. Syringes, needles, etc., used for treating infected animals should be disposed of immediately without reuse.
10.1. Antibiotics should be given/ injected to infected animals on the advice of veterinarian for 5-7 days as they are prone to other bacterial infections due to low immunity of infected animals. Along with this, fever-reducing medicines, immunity-boosting medicines like liver tonic, vitamins, painkillers and anti-histaminic medicines should be given as per the protocol of the central government.
10.2. Various ointments, medicines and oils should be used for healing wounds on the skin.
11.1. The Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune shall take action to submit the daily report of the outbreak of the disease to the Central Government in the statement prescribed by the Central Government and send a copy to the State Government.
12.1 If any person, concerned animal husbandry, concerned government/ semi-government officials, local bodies delay or obstruct in taking action in connection with the outbreak of the said scheduled disease, penal action may be taken against them in accordance with the provisions of section 31, 32 and 33 of the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Communicable Diseases in Animals Act, 2009.
