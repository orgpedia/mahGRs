Preamble Under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, the re-designed weather based crop insurance scheme has been approved to be implemented from the year 2016 under letter No. 1. Accordingly, as per the guidelines issued by the Government of India, in the year 2018-19, insurance cover is required to be provided to the fruit crops in the State against adverse climatic conditions. Commissioner (Agriculture), Shri.
3 As per the letter, 4 clusters were proposed. The proposal was approved by the State Level Crop Insurance Coordination Committee. It was approved at the September 26, 2018 meeting. Accordingly, the scheme for the year 2018-19 for the cultivation of mango, orange, guava, mango, mango, pomegranate, banana and citrus fruits was under the consideration of the government to be implemented in selected districts of the state.
The State Government has decided to implement the Restructured Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme 2018-19 (Ambia Bahar and Haarpeet) in the State under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana for the crops of grapes, oranges, watermelons, pomegranate, banana, mango, cashew, guava and lemon.
The scheme is being implemented in the year 2018-19 in the Revenue Circle of the Taluk in the district mentioned in Annexure-1 as per the fruit crop wise standard prescribed in Annexure-2. The insurance companies implementing the scheme shall pay the compensation to the farmers through the respective insurance companies by inter-bank transfer of the weather data recorded at the Reference Weather Station established under the scheme MAHAVEDHA authorized by the Central Government in the Revenue Circle mentioned in Annexure-I and the Fruit Crop-wise Standards mentioned in Annexure-II. The government will not be responsible for any loss.
Objectives of the scheme: To provide insurance cover and financial assistance to the farmers against climatic hazards such as low/ high rainfall, high velocity winds, hailstorm and relative humidity during the stipulated period.
2. Maintaining financial stability of farmers in the most difficult situation of crop loss.
