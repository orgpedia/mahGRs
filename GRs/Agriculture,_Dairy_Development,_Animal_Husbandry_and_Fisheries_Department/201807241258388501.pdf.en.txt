The Proposal:
The Government of India has included sugarcane and cotton as commercial crops under the National Food Security Mission since 2014-15. The main objectives of the mission are to increase the production and productivity of the crops covered under the mission, increase the fertility and productivity of the farm land, create employment opportunities and increase the productivity of the farmers.
National Food Security Mission An action plan for an amount of Rs. 793.40 crore has been approved under the Government Resolution dated 23rd May, 2018. The State has received Rs. 248.87 lakh in the first instalment as per the letter dated 12th June, 2018. Out of this, the central share for commercial crops (Cotton, Sugarcane) under National Food Security Mission for SC category is Rs. 21.98 lakh and State share of Rs. 14.65 crore. 36.63 lakh has been decided as follows:
Government's decision:
For 2018-19, under the National Food Security Mission (NFSA), the Central and State share of SC/ ST for commercial crops (cotton, sugarcane) has been increased to Rs. 36.63 lakh (Rupees Thirty Six Lakhs Sixty Three Thousand only) to Commissioner (Agriculture) is being approved for disbursement on Budget Distribution System. The Union Ministry of Agriculture has referred to the Annual Action Plan for 2018-19 under the National Food Security Mission. As per the administrative approval given under the Government Resolution dated 23rd May, 2018 and as per the work plan approved for the Mission during the current year and as per the directions of the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time, it will be mandatory for the Director (Extension and Training) to control the Mission at the State level and also to direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the Mission.
The concerned District Superintending Agriculture Officers/ Taluk Agriculture Officers should select the beneficiaries for the campaign and compile the list of beneficiaries at the Taluk level in order to implement the programs covered under the campaign. In this list, the information of the farmer's name, village, survey/ group number, mobile/ telephone number, bank account number, Aadhaar number, etc. should be collected. Similarly, it will be the responsibility of the concerned District Superintending Agriculture Officer to make the list of beneficiaries selected under the campaign available on the website of the Agriculture Department within 15 days from the receipt of funds.
Priority should be given to small and marginal farmers or groups of such farmers while selecting beneficiary farmers under various components under the National Food Security Mission, as well as under all components of individual benefits under the mission, the process of direct benefit transfer should be done to the bank account of the beneficiaries linked to the' Aadhaar number'.
