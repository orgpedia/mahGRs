Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to help in maintaining the financial stability of the farmers, the state is implementing a climate-based crop insurance scheme on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent.
The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide effective compensation to the farmers, the Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme will be implemented in the State for 8 crops namely Orange, Pomegranate, Pomegranate, Chikoo, Guava, Lemon, Papaya and Grape (C) (Mrig Bahar) in the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 by the Revenue Boards in 26 districts by taking the component of HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co., Ltd., Reliance General Insurance Co., Ltd. Agriculture Insurance Company of India Ltd. The insurance companies are referred to as No. The decision is being implemented in accordance with the Government 1.

Government's decision:
Considering the recommendation of the Commissionerate of Agriculture under the restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme Mrig Bahar Saan 2021, the State share insurance premium subsidy of Rs. 86,21,199/- is being approved by the Government to be made available to the Agriculture Insurance Company of India for payment to the insurance companies as per the guidelines of the scheme.
