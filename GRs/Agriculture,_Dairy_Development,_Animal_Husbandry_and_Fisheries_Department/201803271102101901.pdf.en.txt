The Proposal:
The Central Government has accepted the recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode from 2014-15 onwards.
The scheme is being implemented under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMAET). Under this campaign, the Seeds and Plantation Material Sub-Mission (SMSP) is being implemented.
This sub-mission is being implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government with a view to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and in a time-bound manner by augmenting and expanding the existing scheme in the form of a mission to include new technologies in the production of quality or certified seeds for improving seed testing laboratories, assisting seed production institutions to adopt new seed production technologies, etc. within the scope of this mission

The following conditions must be met when implementing the plan:
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central Government along with the progress report as soon as possible.
3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
5) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
6) It should be ensured that the fund is spent only for the beneficiaries belonging to the Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhist sections.
Commissioner (Agriculture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune is being declared as Controlling Officer of the Scheme and Assistant Director, Accounts 1 as Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the said Scheme.
The conditions of the circular dated 18.4.2017 and 30.6.2017 of the Finance Department should be fulfilled.
