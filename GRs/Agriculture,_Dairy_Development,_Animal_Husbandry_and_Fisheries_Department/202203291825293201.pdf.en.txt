The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). This scheme is for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
The Annual Action Plan amounting to Rs. 178,14,12, 000/- for the implementation of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State during 2021-22 has been accorded administrative approval as per the Government decision in Reference No. As per the Government of India Reference No. 2, the Department of Tribal Development has sanctioned Rs. 1,67,00, 000/- and Rs. 1,11,78, 000/- amounting to Rs. 2,78,78, 000/- has been transferred to Agriculture Department. The funds were to be distributed to the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board and Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
Government's decision:
Under the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission, the Centre provided by the Tribal Development Department for the Scheduled Tribes category is Rs. 1,67,00, 000/- and corresponding State share of Rs. 1,11,78, 000/- amounting to Rs. 2,78,78, 000/- (Rupees Two Crores Seventy Eight Lakhs Seventy Eight Thousand only) to the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board and Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune.
