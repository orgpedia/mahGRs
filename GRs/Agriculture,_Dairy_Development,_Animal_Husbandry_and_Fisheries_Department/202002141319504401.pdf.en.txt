The Proposal:
The state government has approved the implementation of the World Bank-funded Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project. In-principle approval was given under the Government Resolution dated 19th January, 2019 and it was approved to set up a Project Management Cell with 37 temporary posts under the Commissioner (Agriculture) on the establishment of the Commissionerate of Agriculture for preparation of the project plan and a Project Steering Committee under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary to control the project from the State level. Refer Note 3. As per the Government decision dated 27th May, 2019, 37 temporary posts have been created in the Project Management Cell with the approval of the High Level Committee of Secretaries.
Under the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project, US$ 300 million (about Rs. 2,220 crore), of which US$ 210 million will be borrowed from the World Bank (IBRD). In order to develop efficient value chains of various crops in the State and to increase the participation of the organized farmers in it, the Government intends to create a market linkage mechanism for the farmers who have been organized earlier through Government schemes/ projects to process the farm produce and sell it directly to the private entrepreneurs. Reference numbers 4 and 5. As per the Government decisions dated 11th September, 2019, the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project has been approved and the Government has approved the establishment and implementation of the Institute under the Societies Registration Act, 1860.

Initially, the project was to be implemented from the government establishment of the Commissionerate of Agriculture, however, now the project will be implemented through the" Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Institute," an autonomous body under the government registered under the Societies Registration Act. Similarly, there are 11 Project Implementation Cells under the project, some of which are Boards/ Corporations/ Federations and are not covered by the Budget Distribution System. Therefore, in order to facilitate the implementation of the project, it was necessary to change the target headings under the main head of the project, accordingly, the Government is taking the following decisions.
Government's decision:
The target headings under the head Accounting provided under the Government Decision dated 27th May, 2019 for the Maharashtra State Agriculture and Rural Transformation Project are being abolished and the target headings are being made available for the project along with the new target headings.

The PFMS system should be adopted for the disbursement of funds under the project, and the details of the various government targets and top-level expenditure of the funds to be disbursed under the head 31 Subsidiary Grants for the project should be kept at the level of the Project Coordination and Management Cell and the Project Implementation Cell.
The grants received for the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project will be mandatory to be spent in the respective financial year and the funds disbursed without prior approval of the government will not be utilized in the next financial year. In cases where the government does not extend the deadline, the remaining amount in the bank account of the project should be handed over to the government.
Monthly/ Quarterly reconciliation of the transactions in the bank account of the project as well as audit is required.
Similarly, internal and external audit of the said bank account shall be carried out regularly as per the procedures prescribed in the project financial booklet.
8. The funds allocated for the project should be utilised as per the existing financial powers and the accounts should be maintained as per the prescribed government procedure. The target head is being provided as per the demand of the project and the project will be fully responsible for any possible problems arising in future for accounting and other aspects.
9. Salaries and allowances admissible to all Government officers and employees under the project as per Government rules shall continue to be applicable to them and as amended by the Government from time to time.
If other facilities and allowances are to be applied to government officials and employees in the project, it will be necessary to get prior approval from the government.
This Government decision is being issued with the concurrence of the Department of Finance and as per Informal Reference No. 310/2019/ Expenditure-1, dated 30.01.2020.
