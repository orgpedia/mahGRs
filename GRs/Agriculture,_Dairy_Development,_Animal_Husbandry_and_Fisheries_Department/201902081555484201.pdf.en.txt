Introduction Under the National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture, the Rain-fed Area Development (RAD) scheme is being implemented with a Central and State share of 60:40 subsidy.
As per the decision of the Government in Reference No.3, the Central Share for the year 2018-19 is Rs. 2496.50 lakhs and State share of Rs. 1664.33 lacs out of which Rs. 4160.84 lakh has been accorded administrative approval and Rs. 1641.67 lakh has been disbursed. The Central Government vide letter dated 21.06.2019 in Reference No.5 has released the second instalment of Rs. 985.00 lakhs to the State Government has been communicated to their Pay& Accounts Officer (Sector).
Accordingly, under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) Dryland Area Development (RAD) Programme for 2018-19, the Centre has released the second instalment of Rs. 985.00 lakhs and Rs. 656.67 crore. The matter of disbursement of funds to the tune of Rs. 1641.67 lakh was under consideration of the Government.

It will be mandatory for the State Governments to implement the Dryland Area Development Programme under National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) as per the Action Plan approved by the Union Ministry of Agriculture for the year 2018-19 and as per the guidelines issued by the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time. In this regard, the Director (Extension and Training) should control the mission from the state level, as well as direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the mission.
The scheme should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Depletion Prone Area component in the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture issued by the Central Government and as per the guidelines of the Government decision dated 13th April, 2017 of the Advanced Agriculture Prosperous Farmers Mission at No. 2 under reference.
Every month, information on the physical and financial progress of the scheme should be updated.
The guidelines of National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture should be appended in Annexure-3 and the same should also be sent to the State Governments.
The detailed quarterly progress report of the scheme should be sent to the Department of Agriculture and Cooperation, Ministry of Agriculture, Government of India as well as to the State Government within a period of 3 months after the end of the financial year.
For the purpose of the Dryland Area Development (RAD) Programme, the Director (Extension and Training), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune, shall issue detailed guidelines to the field officers in accordance with the guidelines of the said department available on the Central Government website as well as the above mentioned terms and conditions.
This Government decision is in accordance with the circular of the Department of Finance No. 69/ Finance-3, 2018/ No. 69/ Finance-3, No. April 02, 2018
It is being issued in exercise of the powers conferred on the department and in fulfilment of the conditions contained in the said circular.
