Introduction: The recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission have been accepted by the Central Government. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode from 2014-15 onwards. Seeds and planting materials are being implemented under the Agriculture Improvement Scheme.
2. The scope of the Mission includes the entire seed chain, from producing centrally sourced seeds to supplying certified seeds to farmers, to supporting creation of viable infrastructure for seed sector development, to depositing seeds in banks to meet the requirement of seeds during natural calamities, etc. The mission includes village sowing programme, strengthening of taluka sowing areas, support to certified seed production.
3. The Government of India has notified the Scheme for Village Production Programme under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Seeds and Plantation Material (SPM) (erstwhile SMSP Scheme) under Krishi Unnati Yojana vide Reference No. 5 In 2022-23, vide letter dated 10th October, 2022, an amount of Rs.
4586.00 Lakhs (Central Share of Rs. 2751.60 lakhs (60%) and State share of Rs.
1834.40 lakhs (40%) has been approved. The administrative approval has been accorded vide Government Resolution dated 03.11.2022.

The following terms and conditions should be followed while implementing the scheme.
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines of the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central and State Governments along with the progress report as soon as possible by keeping the expenditure accounts in order.

The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central and State Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the unpaid amount at the beginning of the year and the income received by way of interest. So that the amount can be considered in a transparent manner and there will be no goodwill.
5) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
6) It should be ensured that the funds received for the scheme are spent for the same cadre for which it has been sanctioned.
