The Proposal:
The coronavirus pandemic has affected the health of people across the world. The government of India is taking preventive measures to control the spread of Corona virus. The entire country was put under lockdown from 24.3.2020. The state of Maharashtra. Lockdown has been imposed in the State since 19.3.2020. Due to the lockdown, the demand for packaged milk in the market has decreased and hotels, restaurants and confectionery manufacturing centers have closed down to a large extent. As a result, the daily sale of milk has come down by 17 lakh litres.
The government has proposed to convert 10 lakh litres of milk per day from the state's daily surplus into milk feed as the demand for milk in the state has come down drastically, posing a problem of surplus milk for dairy farmers. It was presented before the Cabinet meeting on April 24, 2020. On receipt of the approval of the Cabinet, the Government vide its decision dated 27/4/2020, has sanctioned a total of 4 crore litres of milk for the period from 6th April, 2020 to 31st May, 2020 and an amount of Rs. An amount of Rs 127 crore has been sanctioned for the project. The Managing Director, Maharashtra State Cooperative Milk Federation was declared as the implementing agency for the scheme. However, the lockdown has not been imposed in the state yet. Businesses with a large consumption of milk, such as hotel businesses, are closed. Even the sweet shops are not functioning at full capacity. In such a situation, the proposal for further extension of the scheme implemented under Contextual Government Decision No. 1 and 2 was discussed in the Cabinet meeting held on 25/6/2020. After the discussion of the Cabinet, the plan of the department will be implemented by 30/6/2020

An extension has been agreed. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decision:
Government's decision:
The Government hereby approve the extension of the scheme announced under Reference No. 1 and 2 till 30th June, 2020. In view of the volume of milk accepted per day under the scheme, the limit of milk accepted for the period from 6th April, 2020 to 30th June, 2020 is being restricted to 4.40 crore litres, taking into account the date of extension of the scheme. The Finance Department had earlier sanctioned Rs. 4 crore for the scheme. 127 crore has been sanctioned for 40 lakh litres of milk to be accepted during the extension period. An amount of Rs. 12.08 crore is being approved to be provided through the Contingency Fund. The remaining terms and conditions for the scheme shall continue to be as mentioned in the Government Resolution No. 2 in this context.
