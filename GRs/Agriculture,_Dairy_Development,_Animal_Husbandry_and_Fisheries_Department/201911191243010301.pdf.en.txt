Introduction: The recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission have been accepted by the Central Government. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode from 2014-15 onwards. Under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMOET), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, Seeds and Plantation Material Sub-Mission (SMSP) is being implemented.
Improving seed testing laboratories, supporting seed production organizations to adopt new seed production technologies, etc.

Government Decision No.: Scheme 3319/ PR No. 108/1 A In order to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and in a time-bound manner, the existing scheme is being implemented as a mission mode by augmenting and expanding the existing scheme by incorporating new technologies in the productivity. The scope of this sub-mission covers the entire seed chain, from producing centrifugal seeds for supplying certified seeds to farmers, to supporting creation of infrastructure for seed sector development, to depositing seeds in banks to meet the requirement of seeds during natural calamities, etc. The sub-mission includes Seed Village Programme, strengthening of Taluka Seed Cultivation Areas, support to certified seed production.

The following terms and conditions must be met when implementing the plan.
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central Government along with the progress report as soon as possible.
3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
5) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
