The Proposal:
The Cabinet in its meeting held on 12.9.2022 has decided to spend Rs. 1.00 crore should be immediately made available to the District Collectors. The Cabinet has given. Therefore, in view of the above directions given by the Cabinet, it was under the consideration of the Government to take the following necessary measures to control the outbreak of lumpy skin disease in the State. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: The following orders are being issued to take measures to control the outbreak of lumpy skin disease in bovine cattle in the state:
(i) Priority vaccination of bovine animals in the area affected by lumpy skin disease within a radius of 5 km of the area as per the guidelines of the Central Government, from the funds received from the Central Government under the scheme.
(ii) Priority will be given to vaccination in the affected areas and bovine cattle outside the affected areas, in 2022-23, out of the available funds of the District Planning Committee, Rs. 1.00 crore for vaccination.
(iii) Procurement of the said vaccine from a company selected by the Government through e-tendering process, approved by the Government at a cost of Rs. 5.25 per dose.
iv) Before purchasing the vaccine from this fund, the Commissioner Animal Husbandry should be informed and further action should be taken as per his directions.
