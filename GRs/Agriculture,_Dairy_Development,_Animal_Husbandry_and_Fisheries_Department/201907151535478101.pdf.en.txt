The Proposal:
Considering the necessary aspects to increase the income of the Scheduled Castes and New Buddhist farmers of the state and to improve their living standards, as well as to make the Scheduled Castes and New Buddhist farmers self-sufficient, the reference was made. Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana is being implemented in the State by revising the Special Component Plan as per the decision of the Government on 5th January, 2017. Under this scheme, Rs. The subsidy is provided to SC and OBC farmers with annual income up to Rs 1.50 lakh for construction of new wells, repair of old wells, borewell boreings, power connection sizes, pump sets, plastic lining of farmlands, micro irrigation sets, etc.
The Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance has sanctioned Rs. 27362.37 lakh has been made available at the district level. The matter of granting administrative approval to the program under the scheme within the limit of this fund for the current year was under the consideration of the government, on which the government has taken the next decision.
Decision of the Government: In the year 2019-20, under the Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana for the Scheduled Castes and New Boudh farmers, Rs. Administrative Sanction is being accorded for implementation of the programme within the funding limit of Rs. 27362.37 lakhs (Rupees Two Hundred and Seventy Three Crores Sixty Two Lakh Thirty Seven Thousand only).
The funds for this scheme will be distributed to the respective district councils through the Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and financial approval for the implementation of the scheme should be given by the collector of the concerned district.

The area limit will be applicable and the minimum will be 0.20 for other aspects of the scheme, excluding the drilling of a new well. Area limits will continue to apply and the maximum is 6 for all items under the plan. Area limits will apply.
Under this scheme, subsidy should be paid to the beneficiaries as per the parameters approved under the National Food Security Mission for the installation of electricity pumps up to 10 horsepower capacity.
The beneficiaries of this scheme will be provided a maximum of 55% subsidy from the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) per drop more crop scheme and 35% subsidy from the Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana (maximum Rs. 50,000/-) as follows:
1. The total cost of installation of drip irrigation set of beneficiaries as per approved parameters is Rs. 1,58,730/- (Rupees One Lakh Fifty Eight Thousand Seven Hundred and Thirty only) or less, the beneficiaries will be paid 90% subsidy under two schemes namely Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana- Per Drop More Crop and Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana.
(ii) The total expenditure of the beneficiaries as per the approved parameters is Rs. 1,58,730/-, they will be given 55% subsidy as per the norms under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana- Per Drop More Crop Scheme and Rs. 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only) will be given as subsidy.
III. The total cost of installation of Tushar Irrigation Sets of beneficiaries as per approved parameters is Rs. 79,365/- (Rupees Eighty Nine Thousand Three Hundred and Fifty Five only) or less, the beneficiaries will be provided 90% subsidy under two schemes namely Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana- Per Drop More Crop and Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana.

4.Cost of Rs. 79,365/-, they will be given 55% subsidy as per norms under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana- Per Drop More Crop Scheme and Rs. 25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five Thousand only) will be given as subsidy.
V. Farmers who want to take advantage of micro irrigation under Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana are expected to take advantage of the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana- Per Drop More Crop scheme in the first place and subsidy will be given only for the top up.
7. It should be widely publicized at the district level that 90% subsidy is available for micro-irrigation under the scheme. For this, the District Agriculture Development Officer should publish an advertisement in the major newspapers of the district with detailed details of the subsidy to be made available to the farmers under this scheme and the subsidy to be received for it and inform the interested farmers to apply for the available items under the scheme.
The funds to be made available to the districts under this scheme in the current year should be distributed taluka-wise in an equitable manner. For this, according to the agricultural census, the economic target of each taluk should be determined in proportion to the number of farmers of the said category in the respective taluk and within the limit of this target, eligible beneficiaries should be selected from the interested farmers of the respective taluk.
Under the scheme, the facility of applying is being provided on the Maha-Online website. At least 1 month's notice is required to apply.
10. If more applications are received than the financial target of the concerned taluk, the District Selection Committee shall select the beneficiaries in a staggered manner. This process should be carried out at the district level in the presence of applicants and public representatives. In this way, the selection list of beneficiaries in each taluk through draw of lots as well as the waiting list should be published in order. Copies of the list of taluka-wise lists should be put up on the notice board of the panchayat samiti and the zilla parishad and also published on the website of the zilla parishad.
11.The beneficiaries selected for the new well and old well repair through the scheme shall be informed in writing about their selection within 7 days from the date of release. Thereafter, on-site inspection of the area of the beneficiaries should be carried out and after technical feasibility study, the location of the new well to be drilled by the beneficiaries as well as the proposals for repair of the old well of the beneficiaries, if found to be technically sound, the budget of the said matter should be approved and the process of giving prior approval to the proposals of the concerned beneficiaries should be done within 45 days from the date of draw of lots. Similarly, the selection of beneficiaries whose proposals are found to be technically unsuitable in the site inspection should be cancelled and the next eligible beneficiaries in the waiting list should be selected.

Under the scheme, for other than new well and old well repair, the selected beneficiaries should be given prior consent within 7 days after the release and the benefit of the scheme should be given to them in the current financial year.
The prior consent of the beneficiaries who do not implement the matters under the scheme within the prescribed period should be cancelled and thereafter the next eligible beneficiaries should be selected as per the rank in the waiting list. Thus, the scheme should be implemented by selecting the beneficiaries under the scheme in a transparent manner.
A maximum period of 2 years will be admissible to the beneficiaries who jointly implement all other aspects including the digging of a new well under the scheme.
15. The beneficiaries selected for activities other than drilling of new wells will be required to complete the implementation of the activities in the current financial year itself.
The beneficiaries should be given prior consent within the limit of the financial target to be achieved for the district/ taluk under the scheme.
To provide supplementary subsidy under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana per drop more crop scheme to all the beneficiaries selected for micro irrigation under the Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana, the Agriculture Development Officers should fill the applications of the concerned beneficiaries on the e-drip system and send the list of such beneficiaries to the District Superintending Agriculture Officer for approval.
18. District Superintending Agriculture Officers should preferably approve the proposals of the beneficiaries who have applied on e-drip system under Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavlamban Yojana and provide them with permissible supplementary subsidy under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana- Per Drop More Crop Scheme. Similarly, they should send the list of beneficiaries who have been transferred the supplementary subsidy to the Agriculture Development Officers so that they are able to pay the subsidy amount admissible under Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana to the said beneficiaries.
19. Allowable amount of items under the scheme to be transferred electronically to the Aadhaar seeded bank account of the beneficiary.
20. The scheme to be implemented as agency scheme by the District Council. For this, the Commissioner (Agriculture) should issue detailed instructions to the field agencies in order to implement it in coordination with the Agriculture Department of the Zilla Parishad and the Agriculture Department of the State Government, in which the time limit for the implementation of each item under the scheme should be prescribed.
