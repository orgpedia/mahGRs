Preamble As per the provisions of the Drought Management Code, 2016, the Government vide its resolution dated 31.10.2018 has declared the taluks of the State as drought hit during Kharif season 2018.
Further, the Cabinet Sub-Committee constituted to suggest immediate measures/ take control/ decision for drought period had earlier sanctioned Rs. 2 crore to the Gaushalas under the scheme Govardhan Govansh Seva Kendra in the severely drought-affected districts of Marathwada. In the Gaushalas which have been selected for grant of Rs. 1.00 crore or where grant has been sanctioned, approval has been given to start Cattle Relief and Fodder Camps for the livestock of the area on a pilot basis with immediate effect. Livestock shelter and fodder camps run by Osmanabad Gaushalas Read No. As per the decision of the Government dated 05.09.2019, it was approved to continue till 30.09.2019.
The Revenue and Forest Department has decided to continue the ongoing fodder camps in the drought-hit areas of the state till September 30, 2019. The Government has issued a decision dated 30.08.2019. In view of the delayed arrival of monsoon in 2019, and as fodder availability for livestock will take some time, it has been decided to extend the date of opening of fodder camps for providing fodder and water to livestock in drought affected areas in the State till 30th September, 2019. Now, the Department of Revenue and Forest vide letter dated 01.10.2019 has informed the Collector, Osmanabad that the Fodder Camps in Osmanabad District have been extended till 31.10.2019 and the earlier terms and conditions regarding the Fodder Camps will continue.
Further, the Collector, Osmanabad vide letter dated 30.08.2019 has submitted a report regarding extension of the said Livestock Stay and Fodder Camp for the period till sufficient fodder is available in Osmanabad District.
