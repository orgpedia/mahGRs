The Proposal:
The Union Cabinet chaired by the Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi has given its approval for the implementation of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Animal Disease Control Programme (NADCP) for control of Infectious Diseases like Mucormycosis and Infectious Diseases in Livestock from 2019-2020. Also read No. The Central Government has accorded administrative approval for implementation of National Animal Disease Control Programme (NADCP) at the country level during 2019-2020. The state will get 100 per cent funding from the central government for the programme. Under the salivary gland disease control programme, 100 per cent of cows, buffaloes, goats, sheep and buffaloes in the state are to be vaccinated twice a year at an interval of six months and 100 per cent of cow calves and buffalo calves aged 4 to 8 months are to be vaccinated only once.

In a letter dated 29.11.2019, the Central Government has directed the State Implementing Agencies/ State Livestock Development Boards to inform the Central Government about the release of Central Government funds through PFMS system for implementation of the said programme and to inform the Central Government about the details of the same by opening a separate account in a bank for the same.
Under this programme, saliva-crushing and infectious abortion vaccines, ear tags and applicators for animal identification will be supplied by the central government.
The amount has been deposited in a newly opened separate account in a nationalised bank in the office of the Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra Livestock Development Board, Akola.
The following Government Decision is being issued to provide administrative approval for the implementation of this Centrally Sponsored Scheme in the State.
Decision of the Government: Administrative approval is being accorded for implementation of Centrally Sponsored National Animal Disease Control Programme (NADCP) in the State for control of Foot& Mouth Disease (FOMS) and Brucellosis in livestock from 2019-2020.
The programme should be implemented in the state subject to the guidelines issued by the central government from time to time and the funds received for this.
Joint Commissioner Animal Husbandry Disease Investigation Department, Pune-67 is being appointed as the nodal officer of the state to coordinate with the central government for the implementation of this program.
Besides, various State, district and taluka level committees have been formed to implement the programme.
