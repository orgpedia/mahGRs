The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). This scheme is for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
The Central Government has approved the Annual Action Plan for implementation of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State for 2019-20 (Rs. 146.40 crore for new programme and Rs. 116.93 crore for spill over/ committed programme) with a total outlay of Rs. 263.3332 crore.
Accordingly, as per the decision of the Government No. 7 mentioned above, administrative approval has been given to implement the scheme in the year 2019-20. The Central Government has disbursed the first instalment of Central share of funds to the tune of Rs. 4.71 crore to the State Government for Scheduled Tribes as per letter No. 5 mentioned above, and the State Government's matching fund of Rs. 3.14 crore totalling Rs. 7.85 crore was under consideration for disbursement to the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board and Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:

Government's decision:
Under the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission, the Central Government has released the first installment of Rs. 4.71 crore to the Scheduled Tribes under the letter dated 06.09.2019 of the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare, Government of India and the corresponding 40% State share of Rs. 3.14 crore out of the total budgetary allocation of Rs. 7.85 crore.
The budgetary funds available for this scheme should be spent under the following head of accounts for the Scheduled Tribes as specified by the Central Government.

The Accountant, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune is being declared as the Procurement and Disbursement Officer and the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board and Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune as the Controlling Officer for the withdrawal of funds from the treasury.
The funds distributed under the scheme should be spent from the district level. Also, the allocation made available under the Tribal Component Programme should be spent only for tribal beneficiaries and under the tribal sub-plan area. Also, the allocation made outside the tribal sub-plan area should be spent only for the tribal beneficiaries of that area.
The Commissioner (Agriculture) shall submit to the Government the utilization certificate regarding the allocation of funds made available under the scheme from time to time.
The said order is being issued as per the concurrence of the Finance Department vide Circular No. 1 and 2 read above and the Tribal Development Department vide its Informal Reference No. 284 Office No. 6, dated 26.11.2019.
