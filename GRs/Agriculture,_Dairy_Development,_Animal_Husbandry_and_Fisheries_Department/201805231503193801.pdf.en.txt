The Proposal:
The National Food Security Mission is being implemented with 60:40 funding from the Central and State Governments respectively and includes crop systems based on commercial crops such as sugarcane and cotton.
The main objectives of the mission are to increase the production and productivity of the crops covered under the mission, increase the fertility and productivity of the farm land, create employment opportunities and increase the productivity of farmers.
As per the letters 1 and 2 of the Commissionerate of Agriculture, the annual action plan for 2018-19 was submitted to the Central Government with a total amount of Rs. 793.40 lakh within the limit of the fixed allocation approved to the State Government for commercial crops under this mission. 793.40 lakh has been approved vide letter dated 03.05.2018. Accordingly, the Government has decided to accord administrative approval to the National Food Security Mission- Annual Action Plan for Commercial Crops (Cotton, Sugarcane) as under:

3. Benefits under Krishi Unnati Yojana-National Food Security Mission as envisaged by the Central Government should be provided to the beneficiaries belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and women farmers.
It shall be mandatory to implement the Mission as per the administrative approval given by the Union Ministry of Agriculture to the State Governments under the National Food Security Mission for the year 2018-19 vide letter dated 3rd May, 2018, as well as the action plan approved for the Mission for the current year and the directions issued by the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time. In this regard, the Director (Extension and Training) should control the mission at the state level under the supervision of the Commissioner (Agriculture), as well as direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the mission.
