The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). The scheme is meant for the production of fruits, vegetables, pulses, oilseeds, pulses, edible oils, spices, flowers, medicinal plants, medicinal plants, etc. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
The Central Government has approved the Annual Action Plan for implementation of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission (IHDM) in the State for 2019-20 (Rs. 146.40 crore for new programme and Rs. 116.93 crore for spill over/ committed programme) with a total outlay of Rs. 263.3332 crore. Accordingly, as per the decision of the Government in Reference No.7, administrative approval has been given to implement the scheme in the year 2019-20. The Central Government has disbursed the first instalment of SC/ ST Central Account and Rs. 5.235 crore to the State Government under Reference No.5 and the State Government's share of Rs. 3.49 crore, totalling Rs. 8.725 crore, was under consideration of the Government on Budgetary Distribution System (BEAMS) to the Commissioner of Funds (Agriculture). The government is taking the following steps:
Government's decision:
Under the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission, the Central Government has released the first instalment of Rs. 5.235 crore to the Scheduled Castes category under the letter dated 06.09.2019 of the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare, Government of India and the corresponding 40% State share of Rs. 3.49 crore out of the sanctioned budgetary provision of Rs. 8.725 crore.
