Introduction Rain fed Area Development (RAD) programme is being implemented under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture with a Central and State share of 60:40 subsidy. As per the Government decision dated 23.10.2020, the Central Share for the year 2020-21 is Rs. 1191.09 lakhs and State share of Rs. 795.60 lakh. Administrative approval has been accorded for Annual Action Plan for an amount of Rs. 2615.52 lakhs.
The Central Government vide letter dated 11.9.2020 in Reference No.3 has sanctioned Rs. 374.30 lakh has been communicated to the Pay and Accounts Officer (Sectors) for disbursement to the State Government.
The first instalment of Rs. 623.83 Lakhs (Central Share Rs. 374.30 Lakhs+ State share of Rs. 249.53 lakh) on Financial Management System (BDS) to the Commissioner (Agriculture) was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) Dryland Area Development (RAD) Programme for 2020-21, the first instalment of Central share of Rs. 374.30 lakhs (Rupees Three Crores Seventy Four Lakhs Thirty Thousand only) and Rs. 249.53 Lakhs (Rupees Two Crores Fifty Nine Lakhs Fifty Three Thousand only) out of which a total of Rs. 623.83 lakhs (Rupees Six Crores Twenty Three Lakhs Eighty Three Thousand only) is being released to the Commissioner (Agriculture) through Budget Distribution System as under.

The scheme should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Depletion Prone Area component in the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture issued by the Central Government and as per the guidelines of the Government Decision dated 13th April, 2017 of the Mission for Enhanced Cultivation of Prosperous Farmers at No. 2 under reference.
Every month, the physical and financial progress of the scheme should be updated in Annexure-III along with the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture and this information should also be sent to the State Government.
The detailed quarterly progress report of the scheme should be sent to the Department of Agriculture and Cooperation, Ministry of Agriculture, Government of India as well as to the State Government within a period of 3 months after the end of the financial year.
For the purpose of the RAD programme, the Director (Extension and Training), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune, shall issue detailed guidelines to the field officers in accordance with the guidelines of the said department available on the Central Government Website, as well as the above-mentioned terms and conditions.
