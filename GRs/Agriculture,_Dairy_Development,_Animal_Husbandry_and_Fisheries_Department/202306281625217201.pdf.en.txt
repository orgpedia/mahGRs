District level committee to be formed to prevent milk adulteration
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Agriculture, Animal Husbandry, Dairy Development and Fisheries Development
Government Resolution No: MLK-2023/ PR No.62/ Padum-8
Madam Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk
Ministry Extension, Mumbai 400 032
Date: June 28, 2023
Preamble: State Milk Producers Association, Co-operative and Private Milk Unions, Animal Feeding Companies and Farmers Associations. on 22.6.2023. The meeting was held under the chairmanship of the Minister (Animal Husbandry and Dairying Development). In the meeting, representatives of milk producers' organizations pointed out that large-scale adulteration of milk is taking place in the state. Due to this adulteration, there is a gap between the total milk production and demand and an artificial milk bubble is created in the state. As a result, the farmers are not getting fair price for their produce. Also, the consumption of milk has an adverse effect on the health of people. In order to stop the milk from leaking. As per the directions of the Minister (Dairy Development), the matter of forming a district-level committee was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
There is a huge shortage of milk in the state. Due to this, farmers will not get the right price for their milk. Milk also has a negative impact on human health. In order to ensure the supply of clean and quality milk to the people of the state, the following district level committees are being formed to prevent adulteration of milk and milk products.
1.Additional District Magistrate of the concerned district
2.Additional Superintendent of Police of the concerned district
Assistant Commissioner (Food) of the concerned district, Member of Food and Drug Administration
4. District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner Member
5.Deputy Controller, Member of the Legal Metrology
District Dairy Development Officer Member Secretary

Committee's mandate:
1. To prevent adulteration of milk and milk products, the above committee should take up inspection drives.
Action should be taken by registering a First Information Report (FIR) against the person/ establishment involved in the adulteration of milk and milk products. In this, the person/ establishment accepting adulterated milk or milk products should also be made co-accused along with the person adulterating the milk.
The proceedings to be carried out by this committee shall be jointly conducted by the Commissioner of Control, Dairy Development and Commissioner, Food and Drug Administration.
The progress report of the action taken under this should be submitted to the government every 30 days.
