About 80% of the farmers in Maharashtra are small and marginal landholders. The farmers are not able to purchase the machines/ implements required for mechanization due to their financial condition, for which financial assistance is required to be provided to the farmers. Under the Centrally Sponsored Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization, the programme of Agricultural Mechanization is implemented in the State. Similarly, small scale mechanization programmes are also implemented under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana, National Food Security Mission, National Oilseeds and Oilseeds Mission and Integrated Horticulture Development Programme. However, considering the huge demand for agricultural machinery and implements from the farmers, the government took the decision. It has been decided to implement the State Sponsored Scheme of Agricultural Mechanization from 12th September, 2018. The government has taken decision No. 2 to Rs. The administrative and financial sanction for the programme has been accorded with a budget of Rs 90 crore. In the first phase of 2021-22, Rs. 75 crore has been allocated for the purpose.
The Commissioner of Agriculture, No. 4 is here. In pursuance of the proposal of demand for funds submitted vide letter dated 01.02.2022, the matter of disbursement of remaining funds to be disbursed for the year 2021-22 under State Sponsored Agriculture Mechanization Scheme was under consideration of the Government.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: CRYANYO-0521/ PR.No. 95/14-A
Government Decision: For the year 2021-22, State Sponsored Scheme of Agricultural Mechanization with an outlay of Rs. 15 crore (Rupees Fifteen Crores only) is being disbursed to the Commissioner (Agriculture) on Budget Distribution System (BDS) and the same is to be spent for the year 2021-22 from the provision made in the Budget under the following heads:
Request No: D-3
2401- Crop Protection,
Section 113- Agricultural Engineering
(18) State-sponsored agricultural mechanization scheme,
(2401A913) 33 Financial assistance.
The process of selection of beneficiaries and distribution of subsidy under this scheme should be done through Maha DBT portal.
Under this scheme, 50% of the cost for SC/ ST, women, small and marginal farmers or Rs. 1.25 lakh whichever is less and 40% of the cost for other farmers or Rs. 1 lakh should be given as subsidy.
(4) In other respects, the guidelines of the Centrally Sponsored Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SAMM) shall be followed while implementing the scheme. The terms and conditions of the Government Resolution dated 12th September, 2018 should be strictly adhered to.
The Commissioner (Agriculture) is being declared as the Controlling Officer and Assistant Director (Accounts-I), Commissionerate of Agriculture as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer for drawing the said disbursed funds from the treasury.
