The Proposal:
Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Agricultural University, Parbhani is an agricultural university established by Vasantrao Naik. It happened on May 18, 1972. The jurisdiction of Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Agricultural University covers eight districts. Beed district is located in the Marathwada region and comprises Beed, Shirur (Kasar), Parli Vaijnath, Ambajogai, Kej, Dharur, Gevrai, Majalgaon, Patoda, Ashti and Vadwani taluks.
Beed district's fertile soil and favorable climate make it suitable for the cultivation of various crops. Different crops are grown in the Kharif and Rabi seasons in Parli Vaijnath taluka. Cotton and soybean are important cash crops during the kharif and rabi seasons. Other crops include maize, wheat, barley, oats, rye, barley, potatoes, turnips, potatoes, turnips, turnips, and potatoes. Fruits like apple, grapes, banana and orange are being planted. The district is known for producing high quality sugarcane which is used for sugar production. Also, Beed district is leading in the production of oilseeds and vegetables. The market in Parli Vaijnath town is quite large and is considered to be a center of grain trade. Parli Vaijnath railway station is located on the broad gauge railway line.
Soybean is an important crop in the Marathwada region. Crops are of great importance from the chemical, physical and biological point of view. Soybeans are high in protein. Also, this crop is known as the indigo crop because of the oil present in it. Soybean is also considered a cash crop as it is the most industrially advanced. Soybeans contain 40 per cent protein and 18 per cent oil. Soybean being a legume crop stabilizes the nitrogen in the air (100 to 120 kg per hectare) and makes it available for crop growth. Soybeans are intercrops in sugarcane, cotton, tur, sorghum and other crops.

And it's an important crop. Soybean plant helps in improving the soil texture. Soybean flour is used as a nutritious food for milch animals and poultry.
In 2022-23, soybean has been sown in about 120.90 lakh ha area in India, 49.09 lakh ha area in Maharashtra and 24.87 lakh ha area in Marathwada.
Although soybean is growing in a big way in India, the average productivity of the country is around 10 to 12 quintals per hectare. Due to various reasons like rain at the time of harvesting, late arrival and early withdrawal of monsoon, decrease in precipitation as well as increase in temperature, there has been a continuous decline in production in the last few years. Also, lack of use of improved technology is one of the major reasons for declining productivity. Currently, the Soybean Research Centre, Parbhani, is an All India Coordinated Research Centre under the Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Agricultural University.
The area under soybean crop in Beed district of Marathwada is around 3 lakh hectares.
The Soybean Research, Training and Processing Sub-Centre at Parli Vaijnath will help the farmers of this region to increase the production, productivity and income of soybean by providing information about new research, latest technology and high-quality training.

The objectives of the Soybean Research, Training and Processing Sub-centre are to collect the various genetic material (germplasm) of the soybean crop and to produce high yielding straight varieties.
• Produce high-quality proteins and fats.
To produce varieties resistant to pests, diseases and low moisture.
• To develop soybean production technology based on the changing climate. Conduct experiments in the field of research and conduct demonstrations on the farm of the farmers.
Selecting soybean varieties for cultivation in other seasons and verifying their suitability.
Development of soybean based cropping system. Increase net income from low-cost, cost-effective farming in particular, and develop sustainable soybean farming models.
To develop soybean based food processing technology.
To impart training to farmers, extension workers, NGOs, government agencies and other organizations engaged in production of soybean crop with new cropping pattern.
