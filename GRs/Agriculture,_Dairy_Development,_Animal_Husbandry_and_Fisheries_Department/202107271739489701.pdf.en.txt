The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). Accordingly, the National Horticulture Mission being implemented in the state has been included in the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission. This mission has been included in the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Agriculture Promotion. Integrated Horticulture Mission is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between the Centre and the States.
The main objectives of the scheme are to increase the productivity of crops covered under the mission, increase the fertility and productivity of farm land, create new employment opportunities and increase the overall income of farmers. The Central Government has approved a new programme of Rs. 12422.43 lakh and a Spill Over/ Committed programme of Rs. 5391.69 lakh for implementation of National Horticulture Mission under Krishi Unnati Yojana- Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State during 2021-22, with an annual outlay of Rs. 17814.12 lakh. Therefore, the Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune has requested for administrative approval of the annual work plan of Rs. 17814.12 lakh of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission for the year 2021-22 vide letter no.
However, in view of the impact of COVID-19 on the economy in the financial year 2021-22, the Department will be provided with 60% of the budgeted allocation in 2021-22 for the schemes under the programme.

The above amount of Rs. 17814.12 lakh, which has been accorded administrative approval for implementation in 2021-22, includes a Spill Over/ Committed Programme of Rs. 5391.69 lakh in 2020-21.
Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune will be the implementing agency for the implementation of National Horticulture Mission under Krishi Unnati Yojana- Integrated Horticulture Development Mission. This campaign should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government and as per the directions given from time to time.
The State Government will disburse funds through BDS system to the Managing Director and Commissioner (Agriculture), Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune for implementation of the said scheme.
The funds should be disbursed to the District Superintending Agriculture Officers through PFMS system and they should deposit the subsidy through PFMS system in the Aadhaar linked bank accounts of the beneficiaries.
The approved Action Plan of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission for the year 2021-22 is annexed as" Annexure-A" to this Government Decision. The mission should be implemented according to the approved components of this work plan. Priority should be given to the previous responsibilities.
