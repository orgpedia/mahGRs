Introduction: The 100% state sponsored scheme of Mukhyamantri Krishi and Food Processing Scheme has been launched in the state for the next five years from the financial year 2017-18 with the objectives of promoting the food processing industry in the state, encouraging the establishment of projects based on modern technology through the participation of farmers to increase the value of agricultural products, to increase the quality of food produced, to promote the modernization of projects to save energy. Further, the State Food Processing Policy-2017 has been announced under the Government Resolution No. 2 and the Government Resolution No. 3 has prescribed the area limit for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises under the Food Processing Scheme in the State. As per the decision of the Government in Resolution No. 4, approval has been accorded for implementation of the scheme in the year 2020-21 within the limit of 33% of the budgetary allocation. Funding is now being allocated to implement the plan. The government is taking the following steps:
Government's decision:
Administrative approval has been given for implementation of Mukhyamantri Krishi and Food Processing Scheme in the State in 2020-21 within the limit of 33% of the budgetary provision and the Government is approving the disbursement of 20% of the budgetary provision i.e. Rs. 12.00 crore (Rupees Twelve Crore only) out of the budgeted amount of Rs. 60.00 crore for 2020-21.
