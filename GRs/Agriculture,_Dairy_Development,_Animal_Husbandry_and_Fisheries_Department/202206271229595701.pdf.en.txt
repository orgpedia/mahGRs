The proposal:
To prevent the outbreak of infectious and dangerous diseases like avian influenza to the egg laying birds and broilers as well as poultry birds in the pastures on all small and large commercial poultry farms in the state, the field owners and the Department of Animal Husbandry have taken various measures. 2 here. The directions have been issued vide Government Order dated 24.03.2022.
During the review meetings held at the field level from time to time, it has been observed that the officials of the Department of Animal Husbandry are denied access to their areas by the private poultry traders for bio-safety checks, collection of disease samples, disease outbreak control etc.
Strict implementation of biosecurity measures, disease control is mandatory on all types of areas including poultry. If biosecurity measures are not implemented in an area, the possibility of the occurrence of various diseases in such an area cannot be ruled out and such disease outbreaks are likely to spread to nearby and other areas, causing undue economic losses to the business persons.
Section 19 of the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Contagious Diseases in Animals Act, 2009. Section 2. Here it is provided that" Any veterinary officer or other competent officer may enter upon and inspect any land or building or place, vessel or vehicle, for the purpose of insuring compliance of the provisions of this Act or the rules or orders made thereunder, by the persons responsible for such compliance."

Considering the provisions of the said Act as well as the Central Government's Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 and the Maharashtra Animal Preservation Act, 1976, it was under the consideration of the Government to issue necessary Government Decisions not to prohibit the entry of officers and staff of the Animal Husbandry Department into all the Gaushalas, Private Poultry and Livestock Areas of the State for biosecurity and other tests, collection of disease samples.
The government's decision:
6. The following Government decisions are being issued after taking into consideration the facts as read below:
(i) all cowsheds, lands, buildings and vessels transporting animals and poultry in the State being implemented in compliance with the provisions of the Central Government's Prevention and Control of Infectious and Communicable Diseases in Animals Act, 2009;
No person shall prevent the entry of officers and staff of the Animal Husbandry Department for ensuring biosecurity measures, field inspection, collection of bird/ animal disease samples, disease outbreak control and other ancillary official work.
(ii) It shall be mandatory for the concerned agency to provide necessary equipment in accordance with bio-security for the officers and staff of the Animal Husbandry Department to enter the above places.
(ii) Any person who prohibits/ restricts the entry of officers and staff of the Animal Husbandry Department into the cowsheds, lands, buildings, ships and vehicles where animals and poultry are reared.
