Introduction: In order to make the administration of the state more dynamic and people-oriented, the use of information technology in the administrative work of the state to provide services to the citizens at affordable prices, quickly and transparently, under the State e-Governance Policy, No. 1 has been announced by the government. In this decision, No. 12.3 Here are the guidelines for the formation of Project Implementation Committee (PIC) by the concerned administrative departments.
General Administration Department No. 2 of the Government Resolution No. 6 The composition of the Project Implementation Committee has been prescribed. Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a Project Implementation Committee to implement computerization and e-governance of the Padum Division of the Department of Agriculture and Animal Husbandry, Dairy Development and Fisheries as well as the field offices under it.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Under this decision, the Government approves the constitution of Project Implementation Committee for the implementation of computerization and e-governance for the Padum Division of the Department of Agriculture and Animal Husbandry, Dairy Development and Fisheries and its subordinate field offices as follows:

The functions of the above Committee are as follows: (1) To decide on the ancillary matters required for making available the necessary hardware/ software for implementing the e-Governance Policy of the Department of Animal Husbandry, Dairying Development and Fisheries.
(2) To determine the scope of computerization projects to be implemented by the department.
(3) To guide the tender process by finalizing the RPF (Request for Proposal) to start the e-governance project according to the scope of the committee to be implemented by the department.
(4) To approve the project proposal in the prescribed manner by selecting the IT solution provider companies for various computerization projects to be implemented by the Department.
(5) To review the e-governance work of the Department.
(6) The Project Implementation Committee shall have powers as per the decisions taken by the General Administration Department (IT Department) from time to time and the powers conferred.
(7) Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. Approvals for e-Governance related works for an amount not exceeding Rs. 5 crore (or as per the pattern adopted by the Department of Information Technology (DIT) at that time).
(8) The powers to grant administrative approval to a project approved by the Project Implementation Committee shall be in accordance with the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part I, Sequel Number Four of Sub-Division III, Paragraph No. 27 (2) of the Annexure to the Finance Department, Government Decision No. 2013/ PR No. 30/2013/ Regulations, D. 17/4/2015. If there is no unanimity in the committee regarding the approval of the e-governance project, the project will be decided by the government of the Department of Information Technology. The recommendations should be submitted to the High Powered Committee constituted under 5/10/2001.

(9) After the approval of the projects by the committee constituted as above, computerization and related matters, i.e., purchase of necessary equipment such as computers, software, hardware, etc. and development of computer systems, should be done in accordance with the policy determined by the relevant government decision and as determined from time to time.
(10) The Under-Secretary/ Cell Officer of the Office concerned shall be the Member-Secretary of the said Committee for the purpose of considering the matter before the Committee, the Office concerned shall conduct the meeting of the Project Implementation Committee, prepare the minutes of the meeting and take further action accordingly.
