In order to provide various veterinary services to the farmers and cattle rearers at the village level in the state, District Veterinary Hospital, Taluka Small Veterinary Hospital, Veterinary Clinic Grade-1 and Veterinary Clinic Grade-2 are functioning under the Animal Husbandry Department, where there are 2 to 5 officers/ staffs including Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Livestock Development Officer, Assistant Livestock Development Officer, Livestock Supervisor and Attendant. Apart from the regular duties related to the health of animals, these officials have to spend a lot of time for other government work.
To fill this gap," Accredited Agents for Health and Extension of Livestock Production (A-HELP)," a group of community-based animal husbandry activists, will be set up and the first of its kind centrally sponsored scheme will be implemented in the states of Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Karnataka, Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttarakhand and Jammu& Kashmir.

The MoU was signed on September 1, 2021. In pursuance of this MoU, the Department of Animal Husbandry, Government of India has issued Joint Implementation Guidelines for A-HELP. Following decisions are being taken by the government regarding the implementation of the scheme in the state.
Administrative approval is being given to implement the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Accredited Agent for Health and Extension of Livestock Production (A-HELP) in the State.
1.1 The Livestock Resource Persons (LRPs) available under A-HELP will be working at village level or group level of villages and may be more in number depending upon the number of livestock/ poultry/ village, if required.
1.2 The selected person will be the linkman of the Animal Husbandry Department and will provide 24x7 service at the doorstep of the farmers as the last extension worker. A-HELP will be upskilled through continuous training as per requirement.
1.3 They are to be used in various day to day activities at the level of veterinary clinics. For example, preventive vaccination, sterilization, etc., if such persons are working as Artificial Fodder Service Providers, they will help in the work of artificial sanding of cows and buffaloes, coordination with mobile veterinary centers, organizing animal health camps at village level, providing first aid to animal patients, attending meetings of village level Animal Husbandry Committees, and implementing the policies of the State Animal Husbandry Department. A-HELP workers will be the first link for animal health-related services, especially for those ranchers who are having difficulty accessing veterinary health services.
1.4 A-HELP will maintain records of all livestock and poultry birds in its jurisdiction and make the same available to the Livestock Development Officer (Extension) for the success and coordination of activities at the block level.
1.5 A-HELP will encourage animal husbandry by creating awareness among livestock farmers about Inaf tagging. After tagging the livestock, it will ensure that it is registered on the Inaf system. Also, Inaf will help to fill in the information in the system.
1.6 A-HELP will create awareness among the livestock farmers about basic hygiene and hygienic management practices to keep the livestock and livestock in the area healthy. Also, the veterinarian will give other information about health services.

1.7 A-HELP will provide all relevant information to farmers, livestock rearers by counselling them to avail loan or other benefits from various departments/ agencies and help them fill/ upload application for loan or track the progress of application.
1.8 A-HELP will provide its mobile number to the veterinary doctor of the mobile veterinary clinic for coordination with the call center of the Department of Animal Husbandry.
1.9 The Animal Husbandry Department will help the cattle rearers to insure the cattle as per the guidelines of the Department.
1.10. To guide the livestock farmers on fodder production and balanced feed for livestock.
1.11 A-HELP can also act as an enumerator in case of Integrated Survey Scheme and other programmes of animal census.
2 Roles and responsibilities of Animal Husbandry Department, National Rural Livelihood Mission and National Dairy Development Board of the Central and State Governments: 2.1 The Department of Animal Husbandry, Government of India will issue guidelines to the State Animal Husbandry Department.
2.2 The State Animal Husbandry Department and Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission will work in coordination. The Animal Husbandry Department will provide necessary publicity and extension materials to the Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission.
2.3 A- The Department of Animal Husbandry through its subordinate machinery will monitor the various technical activities carried out by HELPs such as vaccination, sterilization, artificial insemination etc. It will also provide necessary guidance and resources.
2.4 The Department of Animal Husbandry will supply the training and materials to A-HELP and monitor the functioning of the Artificial Insemination Centre on the lines of MAITRI.
2.5 A-HELP will leverage the national, state, district and block level mechanisms available with the National Rural Livelihood Mission.
2.6 The National Dairy Development Board will be the nodal agency for skill development of A-HELPs with the help of the Department of Animal Husbandry and provide continuous training as per requirement.
2.7 The training of TOT will be conducted by National Dairy Development Board to the officers of State Animal Husbandry Department.
3.1 A-HELP should be selected from amongst the persons who are working with Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission and willing to work as animal husbandry.
3.2 Priority will be given to those who have previously earned the trust of farmers and cattle rearers at the village level by doing good work as animal husbandry.

3.3 A-HELP at District/ Taluka level will be selected in a phased manner, if required.
4.1.A-HELP Basis: A-HELP must be a woman member of the Self Help Group of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM).
4.2.A-HELP must possess communication skills, leadership qualities and must have passed at least Class VIII. If the A-HELP of this educational qualification is not available, the educational qualification will be allowed to reduce.
4.3. In case of non-availability of A-HELP of appropriate educational qualification for providing artificial insemination services in cows and buffaloes, the educational qualification may be relaxed in consultation with the field officer.
4.4. Necessary representation should be given to the disadvantaged sections of the society.
4.5. Priority should be given to women friends/ artificial insemination service providers/ animal lovers from amongst the members of the SHGs.
4.6. A-HELP should be digitally literate, if required, they should be trained.
A-HELP Training: National Dairy Development Board will organize 16 days training for A-HELPs in collaboration with Maharashtra State Rural Livelihood Mission and State Animal Husbandry Department.

Functions of A-HELP: 7.1. The work schedule of A-HELP will be flexible and will be limited to about two to three hours per day, four days a week, excluding the time for camps and training.
7.2. A-HELP shall carry out artificial insemination as per the guidelines of the Department of Animal Husbandry.
Similarly, she will follow the guidelines received from them for coordination with the veterinarians at the mobile veterinary dispensaries.
7.3 At Taluk level, A-HELP will organize Animal Health Camps and Awareness Camps in consultation with Cooperative Dairies and other NGOs with the help of Animal Husbandry Department.
7.4 A-HELP shall make available and stock necessary first aid medicines, insecticides, nutritional supplements/ mineral mixtures etc. for distribution to the livestock from the Veterinary Doctor concerned. Also, necessary help in terms of first aid/ advice/ application on sick animals from needy farmers, cattle rearers etc. will be provided as per the guidelines of the Department of Animal Husbandry.
7.5 A-HELP shall attend meetings of village level Agriculture/ Animal Husbandry Committees as per requirement.
Remuneration payable to A-HELPs: As per the guidelines of the scheme, A-HELPs will work on an honorary basis and no separate remuneration will be payable to them. However, the prevailing rates at which they are paid honorarium through the Maharashtra State Rural Livelihoods Mission will continue to be applicable as per the decisions taken by the Government from time to time. Also, the Central/ State Government will pay the honorarium fixed from time to time for the vaccination, billing, Inaf entries, artificial sanding, etc.
