The Proposal:
The National Agriculture Development Programme (NARP) was launched in 2007-08 and is now known as Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY). Under this scheme, flexibility and autonomy will be given to the states in the planning and implementation of schemes related to agriculture and allied sectors, according to the local needs and priorities and placing them in the agricultural planning of the states. The main objective of the scheme is to achieve 4% growth rate in agriculture and allied sectors.
Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) is a Central Sector Scheme with funding pattern of 60:40 by the Central and State Governments respectively. As per letter dated 22/02/2019, a total amount of Rs. 418.45 crore has been sanctioned as annual allocation to Maharashtra for the year 2019-20. Out of this annual allocation, a total allocation of Rs. 418.45 crore has been approved for Central share of Rs. 251.07 crore (General Category Rs. 186.73 crore, SCs Rs. 34.88 crore and STs Rs. 28.46 crore) and for State share of Rs. 167.38 crore (General Category Rs. 124.49 crore, SCs Rs. 23.92 crore and STs Rs. 18.97 crore). The projects are to be implemented within this year's budget.
