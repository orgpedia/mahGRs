5. Krishi Unnati Yojana- Benefit under the scheme should be provided to the beneficiaries of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and women farmers as envisaged under the National Food Security Mission by the Central Government.
The Union Ministry of Agriculture has issued a Reference letter to the State Governments under the National Food Security Mission for the year 2021-22. It will be mandatory to implement the Mission as per the administrative approval given vide letter dated 14/06/2021, as well as the action plan approved for the Mission in the current year and the guidelines issued by the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time. In this regard, the Director (Extension and Training) should control the mission at the state level and direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the mission.
Priority should be given to small and marginal farmers or groups of such farmers while selecting beneficiary farmers under various components of the National Food Security Mission.

The concerned District Superintending Agriculture Officer/ Taluk Agriculture Officer should identify the beneficiaries for the campaign and compile the list of beneficiaries at the Taluk level for implementing the programmes covered under the campaign. In this list, the information of the farmer's name, village, survey/ group number, mobile/ telephone number, bank account number, Aadhaar number, etc. should be collected. Similarly, the District Superintending Agriculture Officer concerned will be responsible for making the list of beneficiaries selected under the campaign available on the website of the Agriculture Department within 15 days from the receipt of funds.
10. As per the list of beneficiaries, the programme undertaken on the farm of all the beneficiary farmers should be physically inspected by the agricultural assistants of the respective villages and they should submit their report to the District Superintending Agriculture Officer through the Taluk Agriculture Officer. Similarly, as soon as the reports of the agricultural assistants are received, the Director (Extension and Training) shall prescribe in the guidelines the appropriate scale of supervisory inspection of the works implemented under the said programme.
