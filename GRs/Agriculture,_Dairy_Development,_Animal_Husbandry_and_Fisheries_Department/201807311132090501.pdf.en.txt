The Proposal:
The Government of India has accepted the recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode from 2014-15 onwards.
The scheme is being implemented under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMAET). Under this mission, the" Seeds and Planting Materials Sub-Mission" Sub Mission on Seeds and Planting Materials (SMSP) is being implemented.
In order to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and in a time bound manner, the existing scheme is being augmented and expanded in the form of a mission to improve seed testing laboratories, assist seed production institutions to adopt new seed production technologies, etc.

The scheme is being implemented as per the guidelines of the central government. The scope of this sub-mission covers the entire seed chain, from producing centrifugal seeds for supplying certified seeds to farmers, to supporting creation of infrastructure for seed sector development, to depositing seeds in banks to meet the requirement of seeds during natural calamities, etc. The sub-campaign includes Seed Village Programme, Strengthening of Taluka Seed Cultivation Areas, Support to Certified Seed Production.
As per the letter dated 04.06.2018 in Reference No.3, the Central Government has directly provided Rs. 708.72 lakh to the general farmers, Rs. 408.39 lakh to the SC farmers and Rs. 245.82 lakh to the ST farmers for the Centrally Sponsored" Seed Village Programme" (Central share 60%), totalling Rs. 1362.93 lakh to Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited (MAHABIS) Akola. Accordingly, the State share (40%) is Rs. 908.62 lakh, with cadre-wise break-up being Rs. 472.48 lakh for General Cadre, Rs. 272.26 lakh for SC Cadre and Rs. 163.88 lakh for ST Cadre.
Further, vide letter dated 04.06.2018, the Central Government has directly provided Rs. 71.27 lakh to general farmers, Rs. 41.60 lakh to SC farmers and Rs. 24.20 lakh to ST farmers for" Certified Seed Production" programme (Central share 60%) under Centrally Sponsored Seed Village Programme, totalling Rs. 137.07 lakh to Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited (MAHABIS) Akola.
Accordingly, the State share (40%) is Rs. 91.37 lakhs, while the cadre wise details are Rs. 47.51 lakhs for General Cadre, Rs. 27.73 lakhs for SC Cadre and Rs. 16.13 lakhs for ST Cadre.
Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Seeds and Plantation Material Sub Mission under National Mission on Agricultural Extension and Technology (NAMAS), the Government was considering to grant administrative approval for implementation of" Seed Village Programme" in the State in 2018-19 with Central and State share of Rs. 1362.93 lakh and Rs. 908.62 lakh as per Central and State share of 60: 40 respectively and" Certified Seed Production" Programme under the Seed Village Programme in 2018-19 with Central and State share of Rs. 137.07 lakh and Rs. 91.37 lakh as per Central and State share of 60:40 respectively.
Decision of the Government: Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Seeds and Planting Material Sub Mission under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NAMA), the" Seed Village Programme" is being implemented in the State in 2018-19 with Central and State share of Rs. 1362.93 lakh and State share of Rs. 908.62 lakh as per 60:40 ratio and" Certified Seed Production" programme under the Seed Village Programme is being implemented in the State in 2018-19 with Central and State share of Rs. 137.07 lakh and State share of Rs. 91.37 lakh as per 60:40 ratio.

The following conditions must be met when implementing the plan:
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central Government along with the progress report as soon as possible.
3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
5) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
6) The payment of personal benefits under the Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) policy of the Department of Agriculture will be binding.
Commissioner (Agriculture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune is being declared as Controlling Officer of the Scheme and Assistant Director, Accounts 1 as Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the said Scheme.
The conditions of the circular dated 02.04.2018 of the Finance Department should be fulfilled.
