The Proposal:
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) has been implemented in the State from Kharif season 2016 as per the State Government's decision No.
2 is being implemented. On the same lines, as per the letter of reference No. 3 of the Central Government, the matter of continuation of Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana for the notified crops in the notified areas of the state in the Kharif season 2018 was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
The government has decided to implement the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana in the state in the Kharif season 2018 by taking the area approach for the notified crops in the notified areas as per the following guidelines.
Objectives of the Scheme:
1.To provide insurance cover to farmers in case of crop loss due to natural calamities, pests and diseases.
Encourage farmers to use innovative and improved machinery and equipment.
3. Maintain financial stability of farmers even in the most difficult circumstances of crop loss.
4. Maintain continuity of credit supply to agriculture sector.
Key features of the scheme:
1. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana is mandatory for loanee farmers for notified crops in notified areas and optional for non-loanee farmers.
Apart from the account holders, the farmers who do farming by family or lease are eligible to participate in this scheme.
Under this scheme, insurance premium will be charged at a realistic rate and to reduce the burden of insurance premium on farmers, the insurance premium to be paid by farmers has been limited to 2 percent for Kharif season, 1.5 percent for Rabi season and 5 percent for cash crops.
Under this scheme, the risk level has been fixed at 70 percent for all crops.
The threshold yield of the notified crops in the notified area shall be determined by taking into account the risk level of the crop multiplied by the average yield of the last 7 years (excluding 2 years when a natural disaster is declared).
6. Risk Factors: The scope of risks under the scheme has been widened to include the following.

7. Insurance Coverage: Under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, farmers will get insurance cover for losses due to unavoidable reasons.
7.1. Standing Crops during the period from sowing to harvesting: (a) Natural fire, lightning. (b) Floods and cyclones.
(c) Floods, landslides, droughts, monsoonal episodes (d) Pests and diseases etc.
7.2. Pre-sowing/ pre-planting losses:
The insurance cover will be payable for the area where notified major crops are not sown/ transplanted extensively in the notified area (sown/ transplanted area should be more than 75% of the normal area) due to adverse conditions of insufficient rainfall, climatic conditions etc.
7.3.Payment of claims due to mid-seasons adversity:
In adverse weather conditions, for example. The insurance cover will be payable if the expected income of the farmer is expected to decrease by more than 50 percent of the threshold income due to floods, rain interruptions, drought, etc.
7.4.Damage after deduction:
In case of damage to the notified crop spread in the field for drying after harvesting/ harvesting due to cyclone, untimely rain, compensation will be determined by conducting panchnama at individual level.
7.5. Local Natural Calamities: Under this, the loss of notified crop due to damage caused by local natural calamities like inundation of insured area, landslide and hailstorm will be determined by conducting panchnama at individual level.
War and nuclear war will not provide insurance cover for intentional damage and other avoidable risk.

10. Method of determining compensation:
10.1: Determination of compensation based on average earnings at the end of the season.
The compensation under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana will be determined as per the provisions of the National Agriculture Insurance Scheme. The compensation to be determined will be based on the average figure of production available from the crop harvesting experiment in the insurance sector unit determined by the government. If the average yield per hectare of the insured crop in a specified area falls below the threshold yield for that year, it will be assumed that all insured farmers in that area have incurred a loss. The threshold yield or guaranteed yield in an insurance component shall be the average yield√ó risk level for all crops for the last 7 years (minimum yield of 5 years excluding 2 years of natural calamity/ drought etc.). Under this scheme, a uniform risk level of 70 per cent has been fixed for all crops. For determining the threshold yield, the average yield of the crop for at least 5 years excluding 2 years of natural calamities/ droughts etc. in the last 7 years will be taken into account.
As per the Government Decision No. 2014/ PROCESS 257/ M-7, dated 14.01.2015 and SCY-2015/ PROCESS 311/ M-7, dated 09.11.2015 and SCY-2016/ PROCESS 01/ M-7, dated 23.03.2016 of the Department of Revenue and Forest, the years 2014 and 2015 have been declared as drought years. All lead banks/ Nodal Banks shall ensure that all loans and borrowers taken for notified crops in their jurisdiction are insured under the Kisan Yojana and not a single borrower farmer is left out of insurance cover and the banks should issue instructions to all branches under them. If due to the error/ delay/ negligence of the concerned bank, some of the loanee farmers do not get the insurance cover and the compensation is sanctioned during the season, then the responsibility of payment of sanctioned compensation to all the farmers who are not covered by the insurance will be solely of that bank/ branch.
It is very essential that all the branches of all the Nodal Banks/ Commercial Banks and Gramin Banks should send crop-wise and notified area-wise crop insurance proposals in the prescribed format (separate for non-loanee farmers) along with the premium amount to the concerned insurance company within the stipulated time. If a bank does not deposit the premium amount to the insurance company within the stipulated time, it will be the responsibility of the concerned bank to pay the premium amount to the insurance company along with the interest (prevailing interest rate payable on savings account) on the deposit insurance premium amount for the late period.
All banks are required to credit the amount of compensation sanctioned by the concerned insurance company under exceptional circumstances under the scheme to the bank account of the beneficiary farmers within 7 days of receipt. If a bank does not deposit the compensation amount in the farmers' bank account within the stipulated time, the concerned bank will be obliged to deposit the compensation amount along with interest (prevailing interest rate payable on savings account) in the farmers' account.
Nodal Bank/ Administrative Offices: Full name of the farmer, valid bank account number, name of the village, category of small and marginal landholder, category of Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe, women farmer, insurance covered area, name of the crop, deposit insurance premium, government subsidy, etc.
The information will be taken in soft copy from the concerned Primary Agricultural Credit Supply Cooperative Society/ Bank branch and the information will be examined and sent to the concerned insurance company within 15 days after the expiry of the deadline.
After the insurance company receives the insurance proposal from the bank, it will check it and send it to the bank. The concerned bank will re-examine the said insurance proposal from the documents available with it and if any correction is found in it, it will be brought to the direction of the insurance company within 15 days, otherwise the insurance proposal sent by the insurance company will be considered as final and action will be taken accordingly and no changes in it will be accepted in future.
The bank concerned must publish the list of farmers who have received compensation on the notice board.
Also, the certificate that the compensation amount received has been deposited in the bank account of all the farmers should be sent to the insurance company and the list of farmers eligible for compensation should be published on the crop insurance portal with the help of the insurance company.

Duties and Responsibilities of the Loanee Farmers: 1. As the scheme is mandatory for the loanee farmers, it is mandatory for all the loanee farmers to take insurance cover for the crop and area mentioned in their loan application.
The insurance proposals of the loanee farmers will also be accepted before the last date of joining the scheme.
The loanee farmers should inform the concerned bank within a week after sowing if there is any change in the crop plan for any reason.
4. In case of crop loss due to local natural calamities and damage to crops kept for drying in the field after harvesting due to cyclone, thunderstorm and untimely rain, the same shall be reported to the concerned bank/ financial institution/ insurance company within 48 hours.
Duties and responsibilities of non-loanee farmers: 1. Non-loanee farmers desirous of joining the scheme should contact the nearest authorized banks/ Primary Agricultural Credit Supply Cooperative Societies/ offices of the concerned insurance company or insurance representatives and submit the insurance proposal in the prescribed form along with the due premium and necessary documents in due course.
Non-loanee farmers are required to open an account in their own name in the concerned bank.
3. The farmers should submit survey no. of their land in the insurance proposal. to be mentioned.
4. Farmers are required to submit self-declaration form along with 7/12 extract of land holding and crop sown in the field along with the insurance proposal.
It is mandatory for farmers to take insurance cover for a notified crop for one area, simultaneously and from the same insurance company. If he takes 2 or more insurance cover for the same area from the same or different insurance company, then his deposit insurance premium will be seized and he will be prosecuted.
6. In case of crop loss due to local natural calamities and damage to crops kept for drying in the field after harvesting due to cyclone, thunderstorm and untimely rain, the same should be reported to the concerned bank/ financial institution/ insurance company within 48 hours.
In order to avoid the rush of farmers in the bank and to make it easier for the farmers to fill the applications, the applications/ insurance premiums of the farmers in the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana are being accepted online through the Common Service Center (CSC) since the last Kharif season.
For this, the" Apni Sarkar Seva Kendra" (Digital Service Center) facility, operated by CSCE Governance Services India Limited in the state, has been made available to the farmers for filing applications under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana. Our government authorized service centers operating in the state will help the farmers in filling the crop insurance applications on behalf of the insurance company appointed by the Department of Agriculture and will accept the insurance premiums. Documents required for insurance application such as 7/12 extract, Aadhaar card, bank account details, etc. will be linked to the application form online. The farmers will be provided with insurance cover. Accordingly, it is mandatory for the respective insurance companies to enter into an agreement through the CSC. In this regard, the instructions issued by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India will remain binding.

In order to provide ease of access, transparency and accountability in the process of delivery of government services, facilities, benefits and payments to the beneficiaries through the use of Aadhaar, the Central Government vide its Gazette dated 08.02.2017 has made it mandatory for all the borrower and non-borrower farmers participating in the scheme to get their Aadhaar card authenticated from Kharif season 2017 onwards.
All farmers are required to submit a photocopy of their bank account book with photograph as well as a photocopy of Aadhaar card at the time of making insurance proposal for participation in Kharif 2018. In case the Aadhaar card is not available, one of the following photo IDs must be submitted along with the Aadhaar enrolment acknowledgement. This document has to be verified by the concerned bank.
1. Voter ID Card or 2. Kisan Credit Card or 3. NREGA Job Card or 4. Driving License.
To participate in the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, the loanee farmers should immediately contact the bank to link their Aadhaar number with their bank loan account. Similarly, for non-loanee farmers to join the crop insurance scheme, the bank account number linked to the Aadhaar number will have to be mentioned on the application. In this regard, farmers who do not have an Aadhaar card yet, should immediately contact the nearest Aadhaar enrollment center and complete the registration process.
The use of Aadhaar-linked bank account for crop insurance scheme will make the implementation of the scheme easier, faster and provide better service to the farmers. Also, it will be easier to transfer crop insurance compensation directly from the insurance company to the farmers' account in the future. By linking Aadhaar number, mobile number etc. with the bank account, the farmers will also get the facility of crop insurance linked to the bank account.

Insurance representatives will be paid a commission at a rate determined by the insurance company. However, this commission will be within the limits set by the insurance regulatory authority.
