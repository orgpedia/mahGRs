Introduction: The Central Government. In pursuance of the revised instructions issued vide Memorandum dated 23rd March, 2021 for the management of funds disbursement and utilization of Centrally Sponsored Schemes, the Department of Finance has issued the revised instructions for the management of the referred schemes. The guidelines have been issued vide circular dated 1st July 2021. Department of Agriculture. As per the Government decision dated 29.09.2017, the modalities for implementation of Public Financial Management System (PFMS) for Centrally Sponsored and Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been prescribed and are required to be modified as per the revised instructions issued by the Central Government.
The government was considering appointing nodal officers, single nodal agencies and implementing agencies for centrally sponsored and centrally aided schemes of the Agriculture Department to adopt the revised methodology as per the instructions of the Central Government.
Similarly, to make the funds of centrally sponsored and centrally aided schemes available at the field level through the Public Financial Management System, the government was also considering issuing guidelines for conversion of current accounts opened for each scheme into savings accounts in the State Bank of India at the level of Commissionerate level, Divisional Joint Director of Agriculture and District Superintendent of Agriculture. Coordinating Officers: The Coordinating Officers shall work in coordination with the Public Financial Management System (PFSMS) for implementation of Centrally Sponsored and Centrally Sponsored Schemes implemented by the functionaries under them.
Single Coordination Mechanism:- It will be responsible for the implementation of all centrally sponsored and centrally assisted schemes under the Department of Agriculture.
Implementation Mechanism:- The Commissioner, Agriculture should declare the scheme-wise and designation-wise implementation mechanism as per the requirement after taking into consideration the revised guidelines issued by the Central Government for implementation of Centrally Sponsored Schemes through Public Financial Management System and guidelines of Centrally Sponsored Schemes of the Department of Agriculture.
Current accounts in State Bank of India at the level of Commissioner of Agriculture, as may be approved from time to time under Reference 3, should be converted into savings accounts and used as a single nodal account for the scheme.
Similarly, the Commissioner of Agriculture should take action to convert the current accounts in State Bank of India at the level of Divisional Agriculture Joint Director and at the district level into zero balance subsidiary accounts by linking all such bank accounts to the single coordinating account at the Commissionerate level.
The Commissioner of Agriculture shall fix the defined drawing limits for drawing funds from the single coordinating account to the zero balance sub-accounts to the implementing agencies.
7. The Commissioner, Agriculture shall fix a time limit for spending the unutilized amount in the existing bank accounts at the level of Joint Director of Agriculture and District level before converting them into zero balance sub-accounts for the implementation of the schemes being implemented by the Agriculture Department. After this time limit, guidelines regarding depositing the unutilized amount in the single coordinating account should be issued by the Commissioner of Agriculture.
The Coordinating Officers, Single Coordination Agencies (SNAs) and Implementing Agencies (IAs) appointed under paragraph 1 above shall strictly comply with the directions received from the Central Government under reference 1 and the instructions given under reference 2 of the Department of Finance on the management of funds disbursement and utilization of Centrally Sponsored Schemes.
