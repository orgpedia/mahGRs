The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). Accordingly, the National Horticulture Mission being implemented in the state has been included in the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission. This mission has been included in the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Agriculture Promotion.
Integrated Horticulture Mission is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products.
The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
The main objectives of the scheme are to increase the productivity of crops covered under the mission, increase the fertility and productivity of farm land, create new employment opportunities and increase the overall income of farmers. The Central Government has approved an annual plan of Rs. 146.40 crore for implementation of National Horticulture Mission under Krishi Unnati Yojana-Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State during 2019-20 and Rs. 116.9332 crore for 2018-19, totalling Rs. 263.32 crore.
Accordingly, the scheme will be implemented in the year 2019-20 as per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 6.

Administrative approval has been given for the same and the first installment of general category funds amounting to Rs. 70.675 crore has been disbursed on both Central and State accounts.
Now, to implement the said scheme in the year 2019-20, the Central Government has disbursed funds to the tune of Rs. 30.00 crore for the second instalment for the general category under Reference No.7 and the State Government was considering to disburse a total amount of Rs. 50.00 crore equivalent to 40% of the total amount. Following this, the government took decision.
Government's decision:
Under the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission, the Government of India, vide letter dated 08.01.2020 from the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare, Government of India, has approved the second installment of Central Government funds to the tune of Rs. 30.00 crore for the general category and 40% of the corresponding State share amounting to Rs. 20.00 crore out of the total sanctioned budgetary provision of Rs. 50.00 crore.
The budgetary funds available for this scheme should be spent under the following head of accounts for the general category as allocated by the Central Government.
