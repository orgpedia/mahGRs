Letter from the Commissioner of Agriculture No. KRIYA-2020/ KOKSHEVI/ WAKRAV/ PR No.10/ R-33/ RASAK, dated 19.06.2020 Introduction The Rainfed Area Development (RAD) scheme is being implemented under the National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSC) with Central and State share in the ratio of 60:40.
A meeting of the Project Sanctioning Committee at the Central Government level was held on 9.06.2020 for implementation of Dryland Area Development (RAD) programmes under National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) during 2020-21. In the meeting, the Central Government vide its letter dated 12.05.2020 sanctioned Rs. 1900.00 lakh and the State share of Rs. 1266.67 lakh. The Annual Action Plan for the year 2020-21 has been approved considering the provision of Rs. 3166.67 lakh. However, in order to plan for the impact of COVID-19 on the economy in the financial year 2020-21, the Department will be required to plan under the formula that 33% of the budgeted allocation for 2020-21 will be available for the schemes under the programme. The decision was announced on May 4, 2020.
Accordingly, under the National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA), a total budgetary provision of Rs. 4561.55 lakh is available for the year 2020-21 under the Dryland Area Development (RAD) with 33% limit of Rs. 1505.31 lakh will be available.
