The Proposal:
Agriculture is the main occupation of the villagers. The entire economy of rural areas is related to agriculture. Due to climatic changes, erratic rainfall, pests and diseases, non-availability of improved seeds, sudden fall in the price of agricultural products, etc. it is not possible to ensure sustainable income from agriculture. There is a need for a village-level committee to discuss and guide at the local level. Considering all these aspects, the government was considering the formation of a committee at the gram panchayat level with the participation of people's representatives, employees and agriculturalists. Following this, the government took decision:
Government's decision:
As per Section 49 (4) of the Maharashtra Gram Panchayat Act, a Gram Krishi Vikas Samiti should be formed in every Gram Panchayat to ensure optimum utilization of natural resources in the village for the overall development of agriculture. The committee will comprise of at least 12 persons from the village, who are associated with agriculture and allied sectors, but not less than half of the members will be from the women category. Functions of the Rural Development Board:
1. The Gramsevak shall organize the meetings of the committee in coordination with the agricultural assistants and conduct the work.
The meeting of this committee will be held at least once in every month 3. To disseminate and publicize all the agricultural schemes of the government for effective implementation in the Gram Panchayat area, to review the schemes regularly and to make efforts to take the schemes to the people.
4. To plan and guide the farmers in cultivating different types of crops in the Gram Panchayat area keeping in view the local climate, rainfall, availability of water, soil texture etc.
The committee will work on how to produce quality crops in low rainfall areas keeping in view the limitations of water conservation in the area and guide the farmers.
6. To provide guidance on modern technology, seeds, fertilizers and other agricultural inputs, integrated pest control, integrated nutrition management, mechanization, protected agriculture, orchard cultivation.
7. Supplementary Professions in Agriculture The Committee shall, as special invitees, call upon the officers of the department concerned as the case may be, to guide them in the matter of supplementary professions in dairy, poultry, goat rearing, sericulture, etc.
8. Guidance on harvesting technologies and markets for sale.
9. To inform the farmers about the availability of credit for agriculture, banks, co-operative societies etc. and to coordinate for easy availability and repayment of loans. (For this, the officials of the concerned agencies should be present for guidance.) 10.To discuss and take action with the help of the Department of Agriculture to address the pertinent agricultural issues arising out of the current situation.

Taluk level Panchayat Samiti Extension Officer (Agriculture) and Agriculture Officer (Agriculture), Agriculture Supervisor, Mandal Agriculture Officer and all field officers under the jurisdiction of Taluk Agriculture Officer will attend at least three Agriculture Development Committee meetings every month. All the senior control officers should note that this matter will be mandatory for the approval of the daily of all these officers. Also, the available manpower in the agriculture department and other rural development sectors should be utilised for this work.
The term of the Gram Krishi Vikas Samiti will be the same as that of the Gram Panchayat and the Gram Krishi Vikas Samiti will be constituted in the respective Gram Panchayat within 45 days of the formation of the new Gram Panchayat. The members other than the nominated members shall be appointed with the approval of the Gram Sabha and the nominated technical members and invitees shall not be entitled to vote. The term of the Chairperson of the Committee shall be the same as the term of office of the Sarpanch and the term of office of the members of the Gram Samiti shall be the same as the term of office of the member. After the formation of the new Gram Panchayat, the persons who have served as members of the previous committee are eligible for election as members of the committee, they can be reappointed.
Under Section 49 (4) of the Maharashtra Gram Panchayat Act, the Gram Sabha shall take action to form a committee. The provisions relating to Village Development Committees shall also apply to this Committee.
