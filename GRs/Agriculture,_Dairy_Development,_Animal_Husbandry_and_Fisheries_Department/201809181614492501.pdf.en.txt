Under this scheme, Rs. 800.00 lakh has been provided directly to Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited (MAHABIZ), Akola by the Central Government for the Seed Village Programme for Scheduled Caste farmers (Central share 60%). The State share was Rs. 533.34 lakhs. Out of Rs. 800.00 lakh received from the Central Government for this component, an amount of Rs. 463.90 lakh has been spent. A sum of Rs. 336.10 lakh is currently lying unutilised.
Further, out of the State share (40%) of Rs. 533.34 lakhs, Rs. 308.49 lakhs out of Rs. 406.20 lakhs has been spent and the State share of Rs. 224.07 lakhs is required to be disbursed in proportion to the unutilized amount of the Central Government.
Accordingly, in view of the permission granted for the financial year 2017-18 to spend the unutilized funds of Central share under the programme for SCs in 2018-19, the Central share (unutilized) for SCs has been increased from Rs. 336.10 lakh to Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited (Mahabeej), Akola for the year 2018-19. Further, the State share of Rs. 224.07 lakhs and the State share sanctioned in 2018-19 was Rs. 110.00 lakhs.
Decision of the Government: Considering the permission granted for spending the Central share of the Central share for the Scheduled Castes category in 2018-19 under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Seeds and Planting Material Sub Mission under the National Mission on Agricultural Extension and Technology (NAMA) for the financial year 2017-18, the Central share for the Scheduled Castes category has been increased from Rs. 336.10 lakh is being sanctioned to Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited (MAHABIZ), Akola for 2018-19. Further, the State share of Rs. 224.07 lakhs and since the State share sanctioned in 2018-19 is Rs. 110.00 lakhs, the Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited (MAHABIJ), Akola is being approved to disburse and spend Rs. 77.00 lakhs from this year's budgetary provision to the extent of 70% of the said provision (Head of Accounts 2401& 332).
The following conditions must be met when implementing the plan:
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines of the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central Government along with the progress report as soon as possible, keeping the accounts of expenditure in order.
3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central Government by the 5th of every month.
