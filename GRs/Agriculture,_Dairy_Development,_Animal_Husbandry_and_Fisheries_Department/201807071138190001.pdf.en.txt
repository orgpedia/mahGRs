The Proposal:
The farmers of Marathwada and Vidarbha regions have been badly hit by the rains and floods. Farmers in the region are under stress due to the impact of climate change. The state government has decided to implement the World Bank-funded Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project (external share 70 per cent and state share 30 per cent) in 5142 villages in 15 districts, including 14 districts and Jalgaon district, affected by farmers' suicides in Vidarbha and Marathwada. Approval has been granted under 17/01/2018. The state government has signed an agreement with the World Bank for the project. The World Bank on 06/04/2018. The Loan Effectiveness for the project has been implemented since 18/05/2018. The project will be implemented in full swing over a period of 6 years from 2018-19. Rs 4000 crore. Through this project, climate smart agriculture technology will be spread in the villages vulnerable to climate change and the necessary infrastructure will be provided to the farmers. Similarly, under the project, 932 villages in the Kharpan belt of the Purna river basin will be spread among the farmers with appropriate farming practices and technologies in accordance with the problem of groundwater salinity. This will increase the profitability of agriculture in 5142 villages of the project area and the farmer will be financially stable.
