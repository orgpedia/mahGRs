8. It should be widely publicized at the district level that 90% subsidy is available for micro-irrigation under the scheme. For this, the District Agriculture Development Officer should publish an advertisement in the major newspapers of the district with detailed details of the subsidy to be made available to the farmers under this scheme and the subsidy to be received for it and inform the interested farmers to apply for the available items under the scheme.
9. The funds to be made available to the districts under this scheme in the current year should be distributed taluka-wise in a equitable manner. For this, according to the agricultural census, the economic target of each taluk should be determined in proportion to the number of farmers of the said category in the concerned taluka out of the total scheduled tribe category farmers in the district and within the limit of this target, eligible beneficiaries should be selected from the interested farmers of the concerned taluka.
Under the scheme, the facility of applying is being provided on the Maha-Online website, through which aspirants should be given at least 1 month's time to apply.
11. If more applications are received than the financial target of the concerned taluk, the District Selection Committee shall select the beneficiaries in a staggered manner. This process should be carried out at the district level in the presence of the applicants and public representatives. In this way, the selection list of beneficiaries in each taluk through draw of lots as well as the waiting list should be published in order. Copies of these taluka-wise instructions should be put up on the notice board of the panchayat samiti and the zilla parishad and also published on the website of the zilla parishad.
The beneficiaries selected for the new well and old well repair through draw of lots should be informed in writing within 7 days after the draw of lots. Thereafter, on-site inspection of the area of the beneficiaries should be done and after the technical feasibility study, the location of the new well to be drilled by the beneficiaries as well as the proposals of the old well to be repaired by the beneficiaries, if found to be technically sound, the budget of the matter should be approved and the process of prior approval of the proposals of the concerned beneficiaries should be done within 45 days from the date of release.
Similarly, the selection of beneficiaries whose proposals are found to be technically unsuitable in the site inspection should be cancelled and the next eligible beneficiaries in the waiting list should be selected.
Under the scheme, prior consent should be given to the selected beneficiaries within 7 days of release for other than new well and old well repair and they should be given the benefit of the scheme in the current financial year itself.
The prior consent of the beneficiaries who do not implement the matters under the scheme within the prescribed period should be cancelled and thereafter the next eligible beneficiaries as per the waiting list rank on page 3 of page 7.

District Superintending Agriculture Officers should preferably approve the proposals of the beneficiaries who have applied on e-drip system under Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana (within and outside the area) and provide them with permissible supplementary subsidy under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana Per Drop More Crop Scheme.
