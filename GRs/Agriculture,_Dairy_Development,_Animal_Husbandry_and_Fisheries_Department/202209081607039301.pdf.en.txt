P.V.O/ A.Ma/ 20663/2022, Dy. 09/06/2022 Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to help the farmers in maintaining their financial stability, the State is implementing the Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide crop compensation to the farmers, the restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme has been implemented in the state for the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 for 9 crops namely orange, mango, cashew, pomegranate, mango, banana, grape (A& B), strawberry and papaya (Ambia Bahar) on a pilot basis in 30 districts by the Revenue Board.
Re-designed Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme Ambia Bahar under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) to be implemented in 2021-22 The administrative approval has been given in accordance with the government decision in 1. Government Decision: Considering the recommendation of the Commissionerate of Agriculture under the restructured weather based crop insurance scheme Ambia Bahar (Hailstorm) 2021-22, the State share insurance premium subsidy of Rs. 9,34,99, 785/- as per the scheme guidelines.
