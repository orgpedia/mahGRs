Read:- Revised Action Plan for Prevention, Control and Containment of Avian Influenza Disease 2021 issued by the Department of Animal Husbandry, Government of India.
Government circular:
The state had witnessed outbreak of Avian Influenza in poultry birds in 2005-06, poultry and other birds in 2020-21 and 2021-22. The Department of Animal Husbandry, Government of India has issued revised action plans in 2005, 2006, 2012, 2015 and 2021 for prevention, control and containment of the disease and all the provisions have been implemented at the country level.
As per the revised Action Plan, all villages within 1 km radius of the area from where the samples tested positive for Avian Influenza, and 1 to 10 km from the area where samples tested positive for Avian Influenza, will be covered. I.
All the villages in the periphery should be notified and the area in the range of 0 to 1 km should be declared as an infected zone and the remaining 10 km should be declared as a containment zone. The district administration has been asked to declare the area as a containment zone.
In the revised action plan, it has been mentioned that the action to be taken by various departments in terms of prevention, control and containment of the outbreak of avian influenza has been mentioned in Annexure-5 E Land and Land Reforms Department:- Drawing of scale map for culling and surveillance zone and identification of khas and other lands in the culling zone for burial of cull birds and other related disposal materials.
The detailed guidelines in this regard should be issued immediately by the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune to all the concerned field officers
