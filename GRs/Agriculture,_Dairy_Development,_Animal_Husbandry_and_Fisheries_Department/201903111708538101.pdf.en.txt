It will be mandatory for the State Governments to implement the Dryland Area Development Programme under National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) as per the Action Plan approved by the Union Ministry of Agriculture for the year 2018-19 and as per the guidelines issued by the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time.
In this regard, the Director (Extension and Training) should control the mission from the state level, as well as direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the mission.
The scheme should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Depletion Prone Area component in the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture issued by the Central Government and the guidelines of the Government Decision dated 27th April, 2018 of the Advanced Agriculture Prosperous Farmers Mission at No. 2 under reference.
Every month, information on the physical and financial progress of the scheme should be updated.
The guidelines of National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture should be appended in Annexure-3 and the same should also be sent to the State Governments.
The detailed quarterly progress report of the scheme should be sent to the Department of Agriculture and Cooperation, Ministry of Agriculture, Government of India as well as to the State Government within a period of 3 months after the end of the financial year.
For the purpose of the Dryland Area Development (RAD) Programme, the Director (Extension and Training), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune, shall issue detailed guidelines to the field officers in accordance with the guidelines of the said department available on the Central Government website as well as the above mentioned terms and conditions.
10. Funds meant for Scheduled Castes should be spent for Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhist beneficiaries.
The details of the form in which the benefit of the scheme was given to the beneficiaries of the Scheduled Castes should be submitted to the government for submission to the Social Justice Department for the year 2017-18 and 2018-19.
