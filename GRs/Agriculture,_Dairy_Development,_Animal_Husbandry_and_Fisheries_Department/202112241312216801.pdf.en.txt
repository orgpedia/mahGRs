Introduction: In line with the Union Government's vision to double farmers' income by 2022, inputs like inputs, training, irrigation, mechanization, post-harvest technologies, agro processing and marketing etc. are critical to increase crop production. Considering the need for collective farming, modern methods of farming and strict planning for a substantial increase in the production of farmers due to the low land holding of farmers in the state, the scheme of" Promotion of Group Farming of Farmers for Promotion and Empowerment of Group Farming" has been implemented. The approval has been accorded vide Government Resolution dated 5.10.2018. The scheme will be implemented during 2017-18 and 2018-19.
As a result of increasing population and land fragmentation, the per capita area of cultivated land has declined. Therefore, to make farming economically viable for the farmers,' Driving Farmers' Group Farming' has been started to encourage and empower group farming. In view of this objective and the outcome of the scheme, the scheme titled" Fostering group farming among farmers for promoting and empowering group farming" has been introduced in the context. The extension of two years (upto 2020-21) was granted vide Government Resolution dated 09.09.2019.

As per the Government decision dated 04.05.2020, in view of the availability of funds in the backdrop of COVID-19 and the delay caused by the lockdown, these groups have been given one year's time to complete the works as per the project plan. The matter of extension from 01.04.2021 to 31.03.2022 was under consideration of the Government. The government will take a decision on it.
