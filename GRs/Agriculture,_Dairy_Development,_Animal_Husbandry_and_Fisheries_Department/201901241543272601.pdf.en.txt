Government letter-
The main objective of the Central Government's Nilkranti Policy is to increase fish production by utilizing the available natural resources in the land, marine and brackish water sectors of the state to the fullest potential, maintaining bio-security and ecological balance in a sustainable manner. To implement the Nilkranti scheme in the state, the cabinet has approved the proposal of 21 schemes, including 9 schemes with revised changes and new ones. The Cabinet of Ministers in its meeting held on 03/03/2017 approved and gave the Government Resolution. Administrative approval has been given for implementation of Nilkranti Scheme in the State under 06/03/2017.
A letter from the central government. Under the Nilkranti Scheme under 28/09/2017, the schemes in the landless sector implemented under the Nilkranti Scheme have been given administrative approval as per the guidelines laid down for the integrated development of fisheries through management and doubling of the state's fish production.
Under the Nilkranti scheme, the government provides 40% subsidy for general category and 60% subsidy for SC, ST, women and their co-operative societies. The Government has issued Supplementary Letter to Scheduled Castes, Women and their Co-operative Societies by partially modifying the head of accounts available for 6 out of 2 schemes.
A total of 24 have been made available as of December 14, 2018.
Now, for the above mentioned 9 schemes, a total of 18 heads of accounts are being made available for Central and State share for the general category as under:
