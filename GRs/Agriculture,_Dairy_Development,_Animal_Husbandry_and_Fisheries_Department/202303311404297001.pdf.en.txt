The Proposal:
To provide sustainable irrigation facilities to the farmers of the state, the Chief Minister has decided to implement the Sustainable Agriculture Irrigation Scheme in the drought-prone areas as well as in the suicide-affected and Naxal-affected districts. As per the government decision dated August 19, 2019, the scheme has been approved and the government has decided to implement it in the remaining talukas of the state. The decision was taken on November 18, 2021.
Under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, 55% subsidy is given to small and marginal farmers of the state and 45% to other farmers (within the limit of 5 hectares). Apart from this permissible subsidy, under the" Mukhyamantri Saswat Krishi Sinchai Yojana," a total subsidy of 80% and 75% is provided for micro irrigation respectively by giving 25% supplementary subsidy to small and marginal farmers and 30% to other farmers.
The implementation of the Chief Minister Sustainable Irrigation Scheme has started from the year 2019-20. However, due to the economic situation arising out of COVID-19, the Scheme was extended for the year 2019-20 and
