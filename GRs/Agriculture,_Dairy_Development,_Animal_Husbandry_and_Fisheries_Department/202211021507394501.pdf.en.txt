Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences, Nagpur was established in the year 2000. The University has three faculties, namely, Animal Science, Dairy Technology and Fisheries Science, which carry out teaching, research and extension activities through various constituent colleges. The posts of teachers are getting vacant day by day, which is creating a lot of problems in the functioning of the university. At present, there are 672 sanctioned teaching posts in colleges under the university, out of which 318 posts are vacant
At present, there is a restriction on the recruitment of vacancies in the office of which the figure of posts on the establishment has not been determined. Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences (MUFMS) is yet to announce the number of vacancies.

They cannot be paid on a regular basis. As a result, teaching, research and extension work in the colleges of the university are facing difficulties. The revised pattern for the posts in Maharashtra Animal and Fisheries Sciences University and internal colleges is in the final stage and the process of finalizing the pattern is underway. After the finalization of the revised pattern, it will be possible to recruit vacancies in the teaching cadre on a regular basis through the prescribed procedure. However, it will take some time to finalise the plan. The Sub-Committee meeting held on 22nd June, 2022 under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Services) has approved to fill up 58 vacancies in colleges under the University on hourly basis till the completion of regular recruitment process or for a period of 11 months. Accordingly, the government has taken the decision.
Government's decision:
Out of the vacant posts of teachers in the constituent colleges under the Maharashtra University of Animal and Fisheries Sciences, the government is approving the temporary appointment of a total of 58 posts on hourly basis, including 40 posts in Animal Science, 10 posts in Dairy Technology and 08 posts in Fisheries Science subject to the following terms and conditions:
(1) A temporary appointment on an hourly basis shall be purely temporary in nature for a maximum period of 11 months or until regular appointment by the University, whichever is earlier. The appointment will be automatically terminated after the above period.
(2) Candidates appointed on hourly basis shall not be entitled to claim for regular appointment in any manner.
(3) Before appointing the teachers on hourly basis, the Associate Dean of the concerned college as well as the Registrar, Maharashtra Animal and Fisheries Sciences University shall ensure that all the teachers of the concerned faculty of the college as well as the teachers working in administrative positions have been given academic charge as per the prescribed standard.
(4) If any irregularity is found in the actual available academic workload and payment of honorarium on hourly basis, the entire responsibility of the concerned college shall be

(10) No departmental inquiry shall be initiated or proposed against retired teachers to be appointed on an hourly basis, nor shall there be any punishment in respect of any inquiry.
