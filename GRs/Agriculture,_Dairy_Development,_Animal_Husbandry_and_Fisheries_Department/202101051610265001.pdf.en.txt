The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). This scheme is for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
The Central Government has approved the Annual Action Plan for implementation of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State for the year 2020-21 (Rs. 11176.14 lakh for new programme and Rs. 8481.96 lakh for Spill Over/ Committed Programme) with a total outlay of Rs. 6,6658.10 lakh.
Accordingly, the administrative approval has been given to implement the scheme in 2020-21 as per the decision of the Government in Reference No.5. The Central Government has disbursed the first instalment of Central share of funds to the tune of Rs. 301.50 lakh to the State Government for the Scheduled Tribes category as per reference No.4 and the balance amount of Rs. 201.00 lakh to the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
