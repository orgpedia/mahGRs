Preamble: The Department receives proposals from the office of the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune for the import of cows, calves, goats, sheep, horses, bovine frozen beetles, calves and horses from abroad.
The policy of cattle breeding in the state has been decided by the relevant government decision and according to this policy, it is necessary to breed cattle in the state. After importing cows, calves, goats, sheep, horses, packaged sand and beetles from abroad, the possibility of the state's livestock being affected by emerging and exotic diseases cannot be ruled out. Also, the possibility of infection in humans can not be ruled out.
In view of the facts mentioned in the above paragraph, the proposals received by the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune office for importing sand from abroad in frozen traditional and sex-determined stocks of cows, calves, goats, sheep, horses, bovine frozen beetles, calves and horses need to be thoroughly scrutinized. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decision:
Government's decision:
For import of sand from foreign countries in traditional and sex-determined stocks of cows, calves, goats, sheep, horses, bovine frozen beetles, calves and horses.

The scope of work of the Scrutiny Committee: The proposals received from the office of the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune for obtaining the No-Objection Certificate of the Central Government for the import of sand from abroad in the frozen traditional and gender-defined stocks of cows, calves, goats, sheep, horses, bovine species, frozen beetles, calves and horses should be thoroughly examined in accordance with the guidelines of the Central Government in this regard, the terms and conditions of the Directorate General Foreign Trade and the policy of the State Animal Husbandry and submit self-explanatory feedback in accordance with the proposal to the Government.
The Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune, after considering the opinion of the Screening Committee, shall submit to the Government a proposal entitled only to a No Objection Certificate from the Central Government, along with all the necessary documents, including a self-explanatory opinion on the terms and conditions under which the proposal is to be submitted to the Central Government. So that such proposals are submitted to the central government with a recommendation to get a no-objection certificate from the central government.
