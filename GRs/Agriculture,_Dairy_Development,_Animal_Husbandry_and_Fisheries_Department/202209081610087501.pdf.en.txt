Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to help in maintaining the financial stability of the farmers, the state is implementing a climate-based crop insurance scheme on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide effective compensation to the farmers, the restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme will be implemented in the State for 8 crops namely Orange, Pomegranate, Pomegranate, Chikoo, Guava, Lemon, Papaya and Grape (C) (Mrig Bahar) in the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 by the Revenue Boards in 26 districts by taking the component of HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co.
