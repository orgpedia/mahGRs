(a) To recommend the crops and minimum area to be covered under the Restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme, while proposing the inclusion of crops and climatic factors in the scheme for which scientific information is available on the quantifiable losses of available climatic factors on the productivity and quality of crops.
(b) To scientifically determine the weather perils for crops by reviewing the prevailing weather perils crop-wise for a restructured climate-based crop insurance scheme.
(c) To scientifically recommend a trigger value for crop-wise climate risks.
d) To scientifically analyze the effects on the productivity and quality of crops based on the trigger value of crop-wise climate risks and determine the formula for the limit of economic losses.
e) Drafting of term sheet for effective insurance by formulating technical criteria as per the policy.
For this, it is expected to make new recommendations after examining the scientific reason for determining the prevailing climate risks, the findings of research confirming them, the demonstrated correlation with historical weather and yield data, etc. If there is a need for change in climate risk or change in the trigger value, then the opinion of the experts, scientific evidence about it and as per the need, the experts of the agricultural university of the state, research institutes associated with the Indian Council of Agricultural Research, e.g. It is expected to make a scientific recommendation based on scientific information after examining all the necessary aspects in consultation with the Indian Institute of Horticulture Research, Bangalore.
The Committee shall submit its report to the Government through the Commissioner (Agriculture) within one and a half months from the receipt of the proposal.
The order will come into effect from the date of its promulgation.
