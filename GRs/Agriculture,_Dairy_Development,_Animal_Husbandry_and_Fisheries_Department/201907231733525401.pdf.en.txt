9. The funds to be made available to the districts under this scheme in the current year should be distributed taluka-wise in a equitable manner. For this, according to the agricultural census, the economic target of each taluk should be determined in proportion to the number of farmers of the said category in the concerned taluka out of the total scheduled tribe category farmers in the district and within the limit of this target, eligible beneficiaries should be selected from the interested farmers of the concerned taluka.
Under the scheme, the facility of applying is being provided on the Maha-Online website, through which aspirants should be given at least 1 month's time to apply.
11. If more applications are received than the financial target of the concerned taluk, the District Selection Committee shall select the beneficiaries in a staggered manner. The beneficiaries selected for the new well and old well repair through draw of lots should be informed in writing within 7 days after the draw of lots. Thereafter, on-site inspection of the area of the beneficiaries should be done and after the technical feasibility study, the location of the new well to be drilled by the beneficiaries as well as the proposals of the old well to be repaired by the beneficiaries, if found to be technically sound, the budget of the matter should be approved and the process of prior approval of the proposals of the concerned beneficiaries should be done within 45 days from the date of release. Similarly, the selection of beneficiaries whose proposals are found to be technically unsuitable in the site inspection should be cancelled.
The next eligible beneficiaries in the waiting list should be selected.
Under the scheme, prior consent should be given to the selected beneficiaries within 7 days of release for other than new well and old well repair and they should be given the benefit of the scheme in the current financial year itself.
The prior consent of the beneficiaries who do not implement the matters under the scheme within the prescribed period should be cancelled and thereafter the next eligible beneficiaries should be selected as per the rank in the waiting list. Thus, the scheme should be implemented by selecting the beneficiaries under the scheme in a transparent manner.
A maximum period of 2 years will be admissible to the beneficiaries who jointly implement all other aspects including the drilling of a new well under the scheme.
The beneficiaries selected for other purposes other than drilling of new wells will be required to complete the implementation of the relevant activities in the current financial year itself.
The beneficiaries should be given prior consent within the limit of financial targets to be achieved for the district/ taluk under the scheme.
