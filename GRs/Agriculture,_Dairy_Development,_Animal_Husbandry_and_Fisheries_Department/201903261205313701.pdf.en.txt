Introduction: The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Soil Health Management (SHM) under National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) is being implemented since 2014-15. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16. Under this scheme, components like setting up of mobile/ permanent soil testing laboratory, strengthening of government soil testing laboratories, strengthening of fertilizer control testing laboratories etc. are implemented.
The Union Government has approved the Annual Action Plan for implementation of the scheme in the State with a total outlay of Rs. 775.20 lakh for the financial year 2018-19. The Central share (60%) is Rs. 465.12 lakh and the State share (40%) is Rs. 310.08 lakh. Out of the sanctioned Central share of Rs. 465.12 lakh, the Central Government has released the first instalment of Rs. 116.28 lakh for the year 2018-19 under the Scheme vide Note No. The State share (40%) is Rs. 77.52 lakhs as per the Centre. Accordingly, the Central Sector Scheme of Soil Health Management (SHM) under National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) has been accorded administrative approval for implementation in the State during the financial year 2018-19, as per the Government Decision No.6 of 2018. Accordingly, the Central share (60%) of Rs. 116.28 lakh and State share (40%) of Rs. 77.52 lakh were under consideration of the Government for implementation of the scheme.
