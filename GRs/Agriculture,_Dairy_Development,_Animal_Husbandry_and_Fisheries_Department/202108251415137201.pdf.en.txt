Introduction: The Centrally Sponsored Micro Irrigation Scheme is being implemented to encourage farmers to take up irrigation through micro-irrigation. Since 2015-16, the scheme is being implemented under the" Per Drop-More Crop" component of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana. Under this scheme, farmers are provided with a subsidy of 55% of the total cost to small and marginal farmers and 45% to other farmers for installing micro irrigation sets up to a maximum area of 5 hectares.
The government has decided to implement the Chief Minister Sustainable Agriculture Irrigation Scheme for the farmers of the districts affected by the suicide of farmers in the drought-prone areas of the state and the Naxal-affected districts. It was approved on August 19, 2019. Under this scheme, a total of 80% subsidy is admissible by giving 25% supplementary subsidy to 55% subsidy to small and marginal landholding farmers under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana and a total of 75% subsidy by giving 30% supplementary subsidy to 45% subsidy to other farmers. A proposal to take a loan under the Dedicated Micro Irrigation Fund (DMIF) and enter into a tripartite agreement to raise funds for this supplementary grant was under consideration of the government. The Cabinet in its meeting held on July 04, 2021 has approved raising of funds by borrowing under Dedicated Micro Irrigation Fund (DMIF) for providing supplementary subsidy for micro irrigation.

5. The necessary proposal for taking loan from the Department of Agriculture should be submitted to the Finance Department along with the detailed project report and the proposal should be submitted to the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare of the Central Government from the Finance Department for approval.
