Introduction: The State Government has launched the Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Garden Plantation Scheme under the Government Resolution No. 1 read above for the farmers who are not eligible to cultivate fruit orchards under the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme. The scheme has been given administrative approval for implementation in the year 2019 under the Government Decision No. 5 above.
For the year 2019-20, a sum of Rs.700 lakh (Rs.7 crore only) has been budgeted for the Scheduled Tribes category by way of account grant for implementation of the scheme.
The Tribal Development Department has approved the release of funds to the extent of 60% of the budgeted funds i.e.₹ 420.00 lakhs (in letters only₹ 4 crore and 20 lakhs) as per the Government Decision No. 8 read above and the funds have been disbursed on the Budget Fund Distribution System (BEAMS) of the Department. The matter of disbursement of this fund to the Commissioner (Agriculture) on the Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) was under consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: FUMPHALA-2019/ PR No. 105/9-A
Government's decision:
For the year 2019-20, 60% of the budgeted amount of Rs. 700.00 lakh for STs for implementation of Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Plantation Scheme, i.e. Rs. 420.00 lakh (literal Rs.4.20 crore only) is being made available to the Commissioner (Agriculture) on Budgetary Distribution System (BEAMS).
(2) The funds shall be spent out of the provisions budgeted for the current year under the following headings:
Demand No.T-5,2401- Crop Protection, (00) 796 Scheduled Tribes Area Sub-Plan, (00) (36) Bhausaheb Phundkar Fruit Garden Plantation Scheme (2401A898) 33 Financial Assistance
3. While implementing the scheme, action should be taken as per the provisions and guidelines of the government decision issued earlier.
Efforts should be made to provide benefits to maximum number of tribal beneficiaries in remote areas under this scheme by providing special assistance to tribal beneficiaries to apply on online system through the field machinery of the department.
The Controlling Officer/ Head of the Department shall ensure that the funds are spent during the year as per the approved plan in accordance with the existing Government policy on expenditure. Also, instructions should be given to send the report of the expenditure made from the provision made available, account-wise/ sub-account-wise and material objective-wise information achieved, fund utilization certificate, instructions to be sent by the concerned field officer to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik, the concerned Additional Commissioner, Tribal Development Department and Department by the 10th of every month.
The Controlling Officer and the Head of the Department shall ensure that the expenditure incurred on various schemes in the tribal sub-plan area, in the additional tribal sub-plan area as well as in the areas outside the tribal sub-plan area shall be recorded separately. So that the government can keep an eye on the expenditure on schemes in the tribal sub-plan area and outside the sub-plan area.
7. The provision made under the Tribal Component Programme should be taken up for creation of infrastructure and individual benefit schemes in the tribal sub-plan areas and only individual benefit schemes should be taken up in the areas outside the tribal sub-plan areas. It should be ensured that only tribal beneficiaries are benefited from the funds made available through tribal applications.
8. While spending the funds, the guidelines and prevailing terms and conditions mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget Rule Book and Financial Powers Rule Book issued by the Planning and Finance Department should be strictly followed.
