Government's decision:
Reference No. (1) to the above mentioned Government Decision dated 18/12/2000 has laid down the revised Supplementary Grant Formulary for sanctioning grants to Agricultural Universities in the State from the financial year 2000-2001. Accordingly, the four agricultural universities of the state have been given 100% and 80% (excluding 70%) of the budgetary provision for 2018-19 under the head" 2415 Agricultural Research and Education" for the financial year 2018-2019, under the head" 01 Crop Conservation," 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-salary), 35 Subsidiary Grants (Grants) for Capital Grants and 36 Subsidiary Grants (Salary). The Government is approving the release of grant-in-aid amounting to Rs. 10,08,81, 000/- (Rupees Ten Crore Eight Lakh Eighty One Thousand only) subject to the following terms and conditions.

The statement regarding the funds allocated (University wise colleges/ Research Centre wise) and the grants to be disbursed within the limits of 100% and 80% (excluding 70%) is attached as Annexure A. (a) Terms and Conditions
(1) The funds released by the Agricultural Universities and the Council of Agriculture in respect of sub-headings/ groups/ schemes and subject-wise should be spent only for the salary and allowances and permissible matters of the posts/ matters in the respective scheme which have been approved by the Government to be continued as schemes under the schemes. In case of non-availability of subsidy under a scheme due to vacancies or other reasons, the subsidy shall be adjusted in the next month's disbursement.
(2) If any of the sub-account, group, scheme and subject-wise disbursed funds are lying unutilized/ unutilized for any reason, the unutilized/ unutilized funds shall not be diverted or spent for other groups/ schemes/ activities without prior approval of the Government.
(3) The grants/ revenue income distributed among each group shall be used to meet the expenditure on permissible items in that group and for carrying out development works. Subsidies and revenue income of one group should not be diverted for schemes/ activities of another group. If more grants are allocated for a group scheme, the amount of the excess grant should be taken into account in the subsequent demand for funds.
The grant will be made only for the salary and allowances of the posts sanctioned by the Government which have been filled as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Agricultural Universities Act, 1983 and the Maharashtra Agricultural Universities Statute, 1990 and the Government has approved the continuation of these posts at the cost of the scheme.
(5) Each sub-account/ group/ scheme and subject-wise separate accounts/ accounts shall be maintained.
(6) The schemes/ schemes under which the period has expired shall not be continued after the relevant period at the cost of the scheme/ schemes without obtaining the approval of the Government.
The Maharashtra Council of Agricultural Education and Research shall submit to the Government, within a period of three months from the end of the relevant financial year, an assessment report of each financial year as to whether the grant-in-aid sanctioned to the agricultural universities is correct as per the grant formula, and how it is being spent as per the prescribed order/ procedure. Also, the amount of unpaid funds of the previous financial year should be immediately reported to the government.
8) While approving some new schemes of the University, the Government has approved to make available/ fill up the posts under the said scheme in an adjusted manner. As a result of creation of new posts for the said new scheme, the funds released under this Order shall not be spent for payment of salary and allowances for the posts in the original schemes where the posts in the original schemes have been transferred/ made available to the new scheme by internal adjustments. Also, while seeking quarterly grant, the funds required for salary and allowances of such posts should not be demanded.
The grants sanctioned to the Universities are as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Agricultural Universities Act, 1983, the Maharashtra Agricultural Universities Statutes, 1990, and the Maharashtra Agricultural Universities Accounts Code, 1991, and as per the extant Government Orders and prescribed procedures, the administrative and financial powers of the competent authority.
