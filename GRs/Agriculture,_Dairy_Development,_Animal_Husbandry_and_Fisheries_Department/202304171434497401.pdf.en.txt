Government circular:
The Maharashtra Shree Anna (Millets) Abhiyan has been launched in the state on the occasion of the International Year of Nutritious Cereals 2023, which is being celebrated in the state at the same time at the international level. The following activities/ measures should be taken for successful implementation of the Mission during the period from 1st April, 2023 to 31st March, 2024:
2. In order to reach out to the people about the importance of nutritious millets in the diet and to increase the use of these millets in the diet, necessary publicity of the Maharashtra Shri Anna (Millets) Mission should be done through state level agricultural conferences, district level agricultural festivals, millet runs/ walk for millet, culinary art/ activity competitions, lectures by dieticians.
To increase the area under cultivation, production and productivity of nutri-cereals in the state, Rs 110 crore should be spent under the National Food Security and Nutrition Mission for publicity, crop demonstrations, distribution of improved and hybrid certified seeds, distribution of medicines for pest and disease management, supply of organic fertilizers and micro-elements, distribution of various machines and agricultural implements, organizing farm shops and farmers training, publicity and publicity for increasing the production and use of nutri-cereals through district level workshops.

4. Rs. 5 crore to be spent for providing seed minikits and field extension of cereals under ATMA scheme, field production of cereals, crop demonstrations for increasing productivity, farmers training, farmers study tours, publicity through brochures, posters, pamphlets, etc., organizing culinary/ action competitions, essay competitions and advocacy competitions etc. through schools, colleges, universities, NGOs, self-help groups, hotel associations etc.
5. In the districts covered under Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project, the districts where nutri-cereals like sorghum and millet are taken up, to increase the area and productivity of these crops and to reduce the cost of production, to help strengthen the value chain, considering the adverse impact of climate change and the vagaries of rain on agriculture, to guide farmers, provide technical assistance, special workshops for Gram Krishi Sanjeevani members, Krishi Shalas for dissemination of climate friendly technologies, training/ workshops/ study tours on planting technologies, post-harvest management, sales management etc. for nutri-cereal producers, taluka wise agro-climatic advisory services, production of seeds of climate friendly varieties, value addition of inputs to farmers.
Under the Pradhan Mantri Micro Food Processing Scheme (PMFME) and Mukhyamantri Krishi and Food Processing Scheme, a sum of Rs.50 crore should be spent from the sanctioned allocation for inclusion of nutritious cereals based micro food processing project for Solapur, Thane and Nandurbar districts on one district one product basis, training facilities to beneficiaries etc.
7. m. Under the Balasaheb Thackeray Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project (SMART), priority will be given to nutritious cereal projects of farmer producer companies. It is also being approved to set up a Centre of Excellence for Millets for conducting special extension programmes for value chain development of cereals, setting up of infrastructure, setting up of primary and secondary processing units, providing branding and marketing facilities, and for training and capacity building of farmers groups and farmer producer companies working in cereal value chain development projects at Solapur in collaboration with National Institute of Millets Research (IIMR), Hyderabad. A sum of Rs 30 crore will be spent for this purpose.
Similarly, guidance on nutritious cereals to the members of the Farmers Scientists Forum through the agricultural universities of the state, organization of demonstration on the cultivation of sorghum, millet and nachni, organizing crop competition of cereal growers, cultivation of cereal crops- publicity through All India Radio and newspapers, information on intensive cultivation through All India Radio, millet research plan- crop demonstration, planning and distribution of tenders, publishing articles and success stories on cereals from Krishi Bhati magazine, publicity through lectures and mula khatis through the media, organizing programs on the importance of nutritious cereals in the diet (training and health check-up) for women, organizing cereals for school and college students and women at rural and urban levels.
