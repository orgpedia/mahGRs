The Proposal:
The Central Government has accepted the recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode in 2014-15.
Under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMOET), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, Seed Planting and Material Sub-Mission (SMSP) is being implemented.
An existing scheme for improving seed testing laboratories, incorporating new technologies in the production of quality or certified seeds to assist seed production institutions to adopt new seed production technologies, etc.

This sub-mission is being implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government in order to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and in a time-bound manner. The scope of this sub-mission covers the entire seed chain, from producing centrifugal seeds for supplying certified seeds to farmers, to supporting creation of infrastructure for seed sector development, to depositing seeds in banks to meet the requirement of seeds during natural calamities, etc. The sub-mission includes Seed Village Programme, strengthening of Taluka Seed Cultivation Areas, support to certified seed production.

The following terms and conditions should be observed when implementing the scheme.
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central and State Governments along with the progress report as soon as possible by keeping the accounts of expenditure in order.
3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central and State Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
