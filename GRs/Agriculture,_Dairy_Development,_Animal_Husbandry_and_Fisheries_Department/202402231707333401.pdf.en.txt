The Proposal: -
The state is implementing a priority repurposed climate-based crop insurance scheme to help farmers maintain their financial stability if they are insured against climatic hazards. Various climatic hazards adversely affect the productivity of the fruit crop leading to a large-scale decline in production. Alternately, farmers face an economic crisis as they do not get the expected yield. Considering all these factors, as a measure to ensure that farmers get fruitful compensation, a restructured climate-based fruit insurance scheme has been launched in the state for 9 fruits - oranges, guava, cashew, pomegranate, mango, banana, grapes (A & B), strawberries and papaya (Ambia bahar) in three years with a revenue board in 30 districts under the aegis of HDFC Ago General Insurance Ltd., Reliance General Insurance.
