Introduction In 2015, the National Academy of Animal Sciences, New Delhi, submitted a report on Human Resource Needs in Veterinary and Animal Science to the Indian Council of Agricultural Research, New Delhi, stating that there is currently a shortage of 50 percent of veterinarians in the country and by 2035, there will be a need for 1,25,000 such veterinarians. It has also been recommended to set up new veterinary colleges in the country.
The veterinary faculty in the state of Maharashtra needs to be expanded substantially and the number of students in the veterinary faculty needs to be doubled. Considering the number of veterinary manpower generated from the existing Veterinary College and the number of livestock in the State, the manpower required to provide professional services for providing animal husbandry related services in the State is inadequate. Therefore, it is necessary to invest on proper assessment and resources for the expansion of the Veterinary College as per the standards of the Veterinary Council of India. Considering all these aspects, the Cabinet in its meeting held on 19/10/2023 has approved the establishment of a new Degree Veterinary College in Ahmednagar district. Accordingly, the government was considering the establishment of a new degree veterinary college in Ahmednagar district.
