As it is mandatory to pay compensation for loss matters under local natural calamities, the insurance companies are required to pay 100 per cent farmer share as per the rules.
Under the comprehensive crop insurance scheme, as per the guidelines of the Central Government, the farmer's share of the insurance premium is payable to the insurance companies as per the premium rate, out of which the farmers actually pay the insurance premium amount of Rs. 1/- per share of Rs. Rs 1.71 crore
The remaining farmers share of the insurance premium amount is to be paid to the insurance companies. Accordingly, Director (Extension& Training), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Pune vide Reference No. (5) Pursuant to the request made under the letter herein, the amount towards Farmers Share Insurance premium payable in Kharif season 2023 to the insurance companies implementing the Crop Insurance Scheme under the Comprehensive Crop Insurance Scheme has been increased from Rs. 628,43,43, 542/- was under consideration of the Government to be disbursed by the Indian Agricultural Insurance Companies to the respective insurance companies.
Government's decision:
Considering the demand made by the Commissionerate of Agriculture and the guidelines of the Central Government, the crop insurance companies implementing the comprehensive crop insurance scheme under Kharif season 2023 will be paid an amount of Rs. 628, 43, 43, 542/- is being approved by the Government.
The amount is being released for Kharif 2023 season and will not be allowed for other seasons earlier.
The expenditure on the above matters shall be met from the budgetary provision approved for the year 2023-24 under the following heads:
Demand No. D-3 2401- Crop Protection 110, Crop Insurance (00) (08) Financial assistance for insurance premium under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana State share (2401 A 664) Non-plan, 33- Financial assistance.
The Commissioner (Agriculture) will be responsible for spending the funds distributed under this government decision in accordance with the guidelines of the Prime Minister Crop Insurance Scheme and the government decision issued from time to time by the government.
For this purpose, Assistant Director (Accounts), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune is being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer and Commissioner (Agriculture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune is being declared as the Controlling Officer.
