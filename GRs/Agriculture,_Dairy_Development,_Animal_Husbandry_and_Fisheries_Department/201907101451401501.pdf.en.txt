Preamble- Agriculture Extension Sub Mission is being implemented under the Centrally Sponsored National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMAET). Under this sub-mission, Assistance for Reforms in State Agricultural Extension Programmes (ATMA) is implemented in the ratio of 60:40 funding from the Central and State Governments. The total cost of the project is Rs 2019-20 crore. 11759.73 lakh has been prepared under the State Action Plan (SEWP). 6795.52 lakh and State share of Rs. 4964.20 lakh and the same was submitted for approval of the Union Ministry of Agriculture with the approval of the State Project Clearance Committee (SLSC).
Accordingly, the Government of India has decided to implement the scheme for 2019-20. As per letter dated 20.6.2019, Rs. 1410.18 lakh for Scheduled Castes and Rs. 211.17 lakh and Rs. 168.22 lakh. 1789.57 lakh has been disbursed, accordingly Rs. 1193.04 lakhs, at present, a total of Rs. 2982.61 lakh is likely to be disbursed. Accordingly, under this Government decision, the matter of disbursement of general category funds for the implementation of the said scheme and issuing separate orders for disbursement of funds to the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes was under consideration of the Government. The government is taking the following steps:
Government's decision:
Administrative approval is being accorded for implementation of the Scheme of Assistance for Reforms in Agricultural Extension Programmes (ATMA) of the State under the Sub-Mission on Agricultural Extension of the Centrally Sponsored National Mission on Agricultural Extension and Technology (NMAET) during 2019-20.

4. The Commissioner (Agriculture) shall issue detailed instructions to the field officers for implementation of the target under the scheme, taking into consideration the guidelines laid down by the Central Government for the scheme, which shall include the following salient features.
Priority should be given to small and marginal land holders, women farmers and backward class farmers while selecting beneficiaries and separate records should be maintained regarding the benefits given to such beneficiaries.
The funds allocated for the AatmaNirbhar scheme for the year 2019-20 for the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes should be utilised for the same category only.
The program should be evaluated by a third party evaluation.
The list of beneficiaries who have been given individual benefits in the year 2019-20 should be displayed in the Taluk Offices/ Panchayat Samiti/ District Collector's Office and the list should also be displayed on the website of the Agriculture Department.
During 2019-20, the following officers are being declared as Controlling and Drawing and Disbursing Officers for the purpose of implementation and control of the scheme in the State.
