Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to help in maintaining the financial stability of the farmers, the state is implementing a climate-based crop insurance scheme on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide effective compensation to the farmers, a restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme will be implemented in the state for 9 crops namely orange, apricot, cashew, pomegranate, mango, banana, grape (A& B), strawberry and papaya (Ambia Bahar) on a pilot basis in 30 districts in three years- 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24.
