The National Livestock Mission is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme being implemented by the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying, Government of India since 2014-15. Keeping in view the current requirement of the National Livestock Mission Scheme, the National Livestock Mission has been revamped with effect from 2021-22. The revamped scheme of National Livestock Mission aims at employment generation, entrepreneurship development, increasing productivity per animal and thus increasing meat, goat milk, egg production under one umbrella under the development programme. Under this programme, the surplus production can be exported after meeting the domestic demand. The concept of the National Livestock Mission Scheme is to develop entrepreneurship in the unorganized sector by linking it with the organized sector for the availability of products and raw materials.
As per the revised guidelines of the Central Government, the matter of providing administrative approval for the implementation of the scheme in the state was under consideration of the Government. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:
The Central Government is providing administrative approval to implement the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Livestock Mission (NLM) in the State from 2021-22 as per the revised guidelines of the Central Government.
The schemes which have been approved by the Central Government till the year 2020-21 and which are still being implemented in the State. 2, 5, 6, 7 and 8 are to be implemented in accordance with the decisions of the government. However, the schemes approved by the Central Government from the year 2021-22 should be implemented as per the decision of the said Government.
The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued/ to be issued by the Central Government from time to time as well as the Central share of funds to be disbursed directly to the State. The State share of funds for these schemes should be budgeted from the annual budget of Animal Husbandry, Dairy Development and Fisheries Department. It should be ensured that there is no repetition of the same scheme in the various schemes and centrally sponsored schemes being implemented by the Animal Husbandry Department.

The objectives of the revised Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Livestock Mission and the scheme implementation mechanism will be as follows.
Objectives of the Mission: 1. To develop employment generation and entrepreneurship through development of Small Cattle, Poultry, Sheep Farming and Fodder Sector.
2. Increase the production capacity of livestock by improving the pedigree of livestock.
3.Increase production of meat, eggs, goat milk, wool and hay.
4. Strengthening the fodder seed supply chain and increasing the availability of certified fodder seeds to significantly reduce the demand by increasing the availability of fodder and hay.
To encourage setting up of Fertilizer Processing Units to bridge the gap between demand and supply.
To promote risk management measures including livestock insurance for farmers.
To promote practical research in the priority areas of poultry, sheep, goat, animal feed and fodder.
8. Capacity building of State Government employees and livestock owners by strengthening the extension mechanism to provide quality extension services to the livestock farmers.
To disseminate skill based training and technology to reduce the cost of production and improve the productivity of livestock sector.

Functions of State Implementation Mechanism for implementation of National Livestock Mission Scheme:
The Commissioner Animal Husbandry will invite applications from entrepreneurs/ eligible organizations through Expression of Interest mode.
After scrutinizing the applications submitted by the entrepreneurs/ eligible organizations at the level of the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, it will be recommended to the entrepreneurs/ eligible organizations that the project is technically capable of being financed by the scheduled banks or financial institutions like National Cooperative Development Corporation (NCDC) etc. in the form of loan in addition to government grant for the project.
Once the bank guarantees the loan, the project will be submitted to the State Level Executive Committee (SLEC) for recommendation to the Central Government.
After receiving the recommendation for approval of the projects by the State Level Executive Committee (SLEC), the project proposals through AYUK Pashu Samvardhan will be approved by the Central Government under the National Livestock Mission.

Eligibility Criteria for Entrepreneurial Organizations to avail benefits under National Livestock Mission: Entrepreneurial organizations will be considered eligible for availing benefits under Entrepreneurship Programme if they fulfil the following criteria.
The entrepreneurial entities should have received project related training or they should have trained experts or they should have experienced experts having adequate experience in the management of the relevant area and in the relevant area of management of the project.
The entrepreneurial entities are required to obtain loan approval/ loan guarantee/ bank guarantee of financial institutions for the approved project from the scheduled bank in which they have an account.
The entrepreneurial entities should have their own land or leased land required for the project.
Business entities should have all the relevant documents for KYC.
Control of the scheme: The monitoring of the scheme should be done on the basis of the project information through the MIS online system. The assets of the project should be monitored through GI tagging.
The regular quarterly review of the project should be conducted at the district/ regional level and the physical and financial progress report of the project should be submitted to the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune in the prescribed format.
