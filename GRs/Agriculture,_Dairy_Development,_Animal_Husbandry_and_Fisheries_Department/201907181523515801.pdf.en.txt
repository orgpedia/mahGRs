Introduction: The Disease Investigation Department, Pune is a state level laboratory for the diagnosis of animal diseases functioning at Pune since 1947. Animal health being the backbone of animal husbandry business, the main objectives of the Institute are to maintain the health of the animals, diagnose various diseases in the livestock, guide the field officials for treatment, control and preventive measures to control the outbreak of diseases.
The Central Government has accorded the status of Western Regional Reference Laboratory to the Institute of Disease Investigation, Aundh, Pune. The western region of the country comprises Maharashtra, Goa, Gujarat, Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh and the Union Territories of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu.
Since the entire work related to disease investigation in the state of Maharashtra is being done by the' Disease Investigation Department, Pune', the government was considering to rename the existing' Disease Investigation Department, Pune' as' Bharat Ratna Atal Bihari Vajpayee State Level Animal Disease Investigation Laboratory, Pune'. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
Government's decision:
The government has decided to rename the Disease Investigation Section, Pune as Bharat Ratna Atal Bihari Vajpayee State Level Animal Disease Investigation Laboratory, Pune.
