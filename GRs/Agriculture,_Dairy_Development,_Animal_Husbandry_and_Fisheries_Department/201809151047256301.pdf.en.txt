Introduction: Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) was launched in 2007-08 and has been restructured by the Government of India and is now being implemented under the name of Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana-Raftaar (RKVY-RAFTAAR). Under this scheme, flexibility and autonomy will be given to the states in the planning and implementation of schemes related to agriculture and allied sectors, according to the local needs and priorities and placing them in the agricultural planning of the states.
The main objective of the scheme is to achieve 4% growth rate in agriculture and allied sectors.
Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) is a Central Sector Scheme with funding pattern of 60:40 by the Central and State Governments respectively. As per letter dated 14/05/2018, a total amount of Rs. 404.26 crore has been sanctioned as annual allocation to Maharashtra for 2018-19. This annual allocation includes Central share of Rs. 242.56 crore (General Category Rs. 180.75 crore, Scheduled Castes Rs. 34.38 crore and Scheduled Tribes Rs. 27.43 crore) and State matching share.
