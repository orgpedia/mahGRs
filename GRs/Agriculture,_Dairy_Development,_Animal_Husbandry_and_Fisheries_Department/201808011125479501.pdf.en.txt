The Proposal:
The decisions taken unanimously in a meeting held on 19.07.2018 with the cooperative and private milk producers of the state and the concerned public representatives were announced by the government through a statement in both the houses of the legislature. The detailed proposal in pursuance of the representations made in the Legislative Assembly was placed in the Cabinet meeting dated 19.07.2018. As the proposal was approved by the Cabinet, the Government Resolution in Reference No. 1 was issued. A meeting of cooperative and private milk plant owners of the state was held on 24.7.2018 and 31.7.2018 in the office of the Commissioner, Dairy Development, to implement this decision. As per the discussion held after a long discussion in the meeting, it was necessary to amend the Government Resolution No. 1 in the context, so the matter of issuing the revised Government Resolution was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Decision (a) Under this decision, the Government has sanctioned Rs. 50/- per kg and Rs. Approves payment of incentive subsidy@ Rs.5/-per litre subject to the following conditions:

The institutions participating in the above scheme will be required to submit the details of their packaged milk distribution and the additional milk received by them at the end of the month of July, 2018 to the concerned District Dairy Development Officer.
8.It will be mandatory for the institutions participating in the above scheme to keep the records of milk purchase and sale up to date on a daily basis and to give it to the concerned District Dairy Development Officer.
9.Enterprises which handle at least 10,000 litres of milk per day can apply for participation in the scheme. Milk producers/ collectors having less than 10 thousand litres per day should supply milk to cooperative/ private institutions as per their convenience and take advantage of this scheme.
The farmers/ milk producers will be required to pay the milk price as per the table mentioned in paragraph (1). However, in case of milk business, the payment of milk bill is made by deducting the advance, credit card dues, animal feed, other material in the store, etc.
It will be mandatory for the participating institutions to submit a report to the District Dairy Development Officer every 10 days regarding the excess milk given for conversion. The District Dairy Development Officer will examine such reports and submit a consolidated report to the Regional Dairy Development Officer on the same day or the next day. The Regional Dairy Development Officer shall examine such reports and submit the consolidated payment demand to the Commissioner Dairying every 10 days. It will be necessary to ensure that the subsidy amounts are paid only after the approval of the Commissioner Dairy.
12. The Commissioner, Dairying, shall depute teams, as required, to regularly inspect the above scheme.
If any irregularity is found in this scheme by any institution, such institution will be disqualified from receiving the grant, and the grant distributed to them will be recovered from them with interest. Legal action will also be taken against the violators.
