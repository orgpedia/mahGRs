The concerned officer should immediately join the post of the transferor and the concerned controlling officer should immediately inform the government on the email website vinod. mendhe@nic. in about the date on which the officer was relieved to appear in place of the new appointment and the date on which he appeared in place of the new appointment.
The concerned officer shall not be granted leave without prior permission of the Government for any reason and no leave application of the officer transferred by the parent office shall be accepted under Rule 30 (c) of the M.N.S. (Leave) Rules, 1981. If such applications are received, they should be returned to the concerned officer at the original address. If the leave application of the transferred officer is sent to the parent office, then these applications will not be taken into consideration.
If the transfer order does not appear at the place of immediate appointment or if any kind of pressure is exerted to cancel the transfer, disciplinary action will be taken against them considering it as a misconduct as the action is in violation of Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979.
