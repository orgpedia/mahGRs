The Proposal:
The state government has approved the implementation of the World Bank-funded Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project. In principle approval was given under the Government Resolution dated 19th January, 2019 and it was approved to set up a Project Management Cell with 37 posts under the Commissioner (Agriculture) to plan the project, and a Project Steering Committee under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary to monitor the project from the State level.
Referred to As per the Government decision dated 27th May, 2019, 37 posts have been created in the Project Management Cell with the approval of the High Level Committee of Secretaries.
Under the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project, an amount of US$ 300 million (about Rs. 2100 crore), out of which a loan of US$ 210 million will be taken from the World Bank (IBRD). In order to develop efficient value chains of various crops in the State and to increase the participation of the organized farmers in it, the Government intends to create a market linkage mechanism for the farmers who have been organized earlier through Government schemes/ projects to process the farm produce and sell it directly to the private entrepreneurs.
The project has been approved by the Government in the Cabinet meeting held on 9th September, 2019. The decision was taken on September 11, 2019. Accordingly, the government has approved the implementation of the project by setting up an institution under the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project's Societies Registration Act 1860. Accordingly, the Government was considering to approve the establishment of" Maharashtra State Agriculture and Rural Transformation Project (SMART) Institute" at the State level and to approve the opening of a separate bank account for the said Institute.

Government's decision:
The Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project (SMART) will be implemented in all districts of the state over a period of seven years (from 2019-20 to 2026-27). The Government has approved the implementation of the project by setting up of" Maharashtra State Agriculture and Rural Transformation Project (SMART) Institute" at the State level under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 for implementation of the project.
The Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune should immediately register the said organization and establish a coordination and management cell of the project under the Maharashtra State Agriculture and Rural Transformation Project (SMART) Institute under this organization. The Institute shall be constituted under the Chairmanship of Secretary (Agriculture) and the Governing Body of the Institute shall consist of the officers mentioned in Annexure-A. The Project Director of the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project will be the Executive Director of the Institute and the Project Coordination and Management Cell of the Project will be under his control.
The rules and by-laws of the Maharashtra State Agriculture and Rural Transformation Project (SMART) Institute shall be determined with the approval of the Project Steering Committee headed by the Chief Secretary.
4. Government decision referred earlier for coordination and management cell of Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project. Approval is being given to transfer 37 posts (14 government posts and 23 contractual posts) created on the establishment of the Commissionerate of Agriculture temporarily to the Maharashtra State Agriculture and Rural Transformation Project (SMART) Institute till the project period as of May 27, 2019. Similarly, the design of the required posts for the coordination and management cell of the project should be decided with the approval of the Project Steering Committee headed by the Chief Secretary, and with the approval of the High-Level Secretary Committee, the necessary posts should be created at the establishment of the said institution on a temporary basis till the project period.
No separate posts will be sanctioned for the said Institute except the posts required under the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project. The posts will be created on a temporary basis for the project period and will be automatically abolished once the project period is over.
As per the relevant Government Decision dated 27th May, 2019, the Project Director, Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation, Pune has been declared as the Head of Department and Controller for the project and the Finance Specialist, Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project has been declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the project. The project will now be implemented by the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project Society.

Maharashtra State Institute of Agriculture and Rural Transformation Project is being approved to open a current account in a nationalized bank for" Project Coordination and Management Cell." This bank account should be linked to the PFMS system and the bank accounts of all the project implementing agencies should be linked to this bank account through the PFMS system and the disbursement of funds under the project should be done through this system. The State Account and External Account funds to be disbursed by the State Government for the project should be deposited in the said bank account.
The funds for project implementation will be disbursed directly to the Project Directors through the Budget Distribution System. Under this project, the state government is taking financial assistance from the World Bank in the form of loan and the funds distributed should not be withdrawn from the treasury once and deposited in the bank account without use. The Project Director should himself verify the urgency and propriety of the expenditure of the funds allocated for the project and draw the funds from the treasury as per the requirement of the project. The project director and the finance specialist will be responsible for ensuring that the funds allocated for the project do not remain in the bank account without use.
The bank account opened for the project will only be for the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation (SMART) project and the funds received for the project should be used for the project implementation.
After completion of the project, the bank account will also need to be closed after completing the audit process.
The grants received for the Maharashtra State Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Project will be required to be spent in the respective financial year and can be spent as per the extension period with the approval of the government. In cases where the extension has not been granted, the balance in the said bank account will have to be adjusted with the government.
Monthly/ quarterly reconciliation of transactions in the said bank account and audit shall be required.
Similarly, internal and external audit of the said bank account shall be carried out regularly as per the procedures prescribed in the project financial booklet.
12. In case of closure of the said bank account, the balance should be reconciled with the balance and the balance should be deposited immediately under the appropriate government account head.
