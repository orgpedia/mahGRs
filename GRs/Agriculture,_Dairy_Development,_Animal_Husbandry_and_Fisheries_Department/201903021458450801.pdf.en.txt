Introduction: The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Soil Health Management (SHM) under National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) is being implemented since 2014-15. Since 2015-16, the scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60: 40 between the Centre and the State. Under this scheme, components like setting up of mobile/ permanent soil testing laboratory, strengthening of government soil testing laboratories, strengthening of fertilizer control testing laboratories etc. are implemented.
In the year 2018-19, the Government of India has allocated Rs. The approval of Annual Action Plan for an amount of Rs. 775.20 lakh has been communicated vide letter No. 2. The Central share (60%) is Rs. 465.12 lakhs with State share (40%) being Rs. 310.08 lakh. The approved central share of Rs. 465.12 lakh, out of which the Central Government has sanctioned Rs. 116.28 lakh has been disbursed as first instalment. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to grant administrative approval for implementation of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Soil Health Management (SHM) under National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) in the State during the financial year 2018-19.

The following terms and conditions should be observed when implementing the scheme.
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central Government along with the progress report as soon as possible by keeping the accounts of expenditure in order.
3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and income earned at the beginning of the year, so that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
5) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
For this scheme, the Director of Agriculture (Production and Quality Control), Agriculture Commissionerate, Maharashtra State, Pune is being declared as the Controlling Officer of the scheme, Assistant Director, Accounts 1, Agriculture Commissionerate, Maharashtra State, Pune at the level of Agriculture Commissionerate and the District Superintending Agriculture Officer as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer at the concerned district level.
The conditions of circulars dated 02.04.2018, 27.07.2018 and 05.12.2018 of the Finance Department should be fulfilled.
