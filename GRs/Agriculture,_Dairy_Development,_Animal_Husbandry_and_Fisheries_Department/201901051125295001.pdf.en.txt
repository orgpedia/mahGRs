Government circular
Considering the drought situation in the state, the scheme for production of various fodder crops on the land of individual beneficiaries as well as on the land of benefit under water bodies/ ponds under the National Agriculture Development Scheme for providing fodder for livestock has been approved. 23.10.2018 and D. The administrative approval has been accorded vide Government Resolution dated 15.11.2018 and the programme is being implemented in two phases with a total outlay of Rs. A sum of Rs. 35.00 crore has been sanctioned.
Also, read this. As per the Government decision dated 15.11.2018, the area under the reservoir/ pond shall be leased out for the Rabi and Summer season 2018-19 on nominal rent for the production of fodder crops.
The programme will be implemented immediately. As per the Government decision dated 15.11.2018, a time-bound programme has been prepared and the District Collector of the concerned district has been empowered to make necessary changes in the time-bound programme as per the local situation.
Since more silt area will be available in the coming period, it is expected that by the next summer season, the programme will be able to produce large quantity of hay by selecting individual beneficiaries who have access to other irrigation facilities as well as hay for hay production on the land under the reservoir/ pond/ beneficiation area and supplying them with various hay seeds and fertilizers.
