Introduction: Poultry farming is emerging as an independent source of income in the rural areas of the state without being a supplementary occupation. To promote poultry farming in rural Parsa by providing technology linkages to traditional poultry farming practices, developing skills of beneficiaries, improving poultry bird management and other schemes related to poultry and animal husbandry in Parsa keeping in view the need for balanced diet and increasing the economic income of families by creating self-employment, the scheme of providing financial assistance for procurement and rearing of 100 birds of improved indigenous breeds of four weeks age at 75% subsidy for Dhangar and similar tribes of nomadic tribes (C) category in the remaining 34 districts of the state excluding two districts of Mumbai and Mumbai Suburban. The Cabinet gave. The approval was given in the Cabinet meeting held on 30th July, 2019. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:
Government Decision: In order to promote poultry farming in rural areas, the Government is providing administrative approval for implementation of the scheme for procurement and rearing of 100 birds of improved indigenous breeds of four weeks old at 75% subsidy for Dhangar and similar tribes of nomadic tribes (C) in 2019-20.
The scheme will be implemented in all the 34 districts of the state, except Mumbai and its suburbs.
Format of the scheme: Under this scheme, 1,000 beneficiaries of Dhangar and similar tribes of nomadic tribes (C) category in each district should be selected. Each selected beneficiary will be provided 100 hybrid poultry birds of improved indigenous breeds (CARI Approved Low Input Technology Birds) aged four weeks. A total of 34,000 beneficiaries will be covered under the scheme at the rate of Rs 1,000 per district. Selected beneficiaries will be given 200 kg of seedlings at the rate of 2.0 kg per bird. Poultry feed should be supplied within this limit. The government will provide 75 per cent subsidy to the beneficiaries. The remaining 25 per cent will be borne by the beneficiary. In case of increase in the per capita and symbolical prices of food for the actual scheme implementation, the expenditure beyond subsidy is to be borne by the beneficiaries themselves.

Criteria for the selection of beneficiaries:
The beneficiary should be a holder of a caste certificate belonging to the nomadic tribe (C), Dhangar and similar tribes and should be between the ages of 18 and 60 years.
Only one person from a family should be given the benefit of this scheme.
3. The selected beneficiary should have sufficient space for rearing poultry in the backyard.
Priority should be given to 30 per cent women and 3 per cent differently-abled beneficiaries of Dhangar and similar tribes belonging to nomadic tribes (C) category under this scheme.
5. Sample of application to be made by the beneficiary to get the benefit of the scheme by the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune
After ascertaining, it should be made available to all concerned Taluk Livestock Development Officers (Extension), Panchayat Samiti.

6. The Livestock Development Officer (Extension), Panchayat Samiti shall accept the applications within the stipulated time for providing benefits under this scheme and submit all such applications to the District Level Committee for approval of the Selection Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of the Deputy Commissioner of Animal Husbandry with the recommendation of the District Animal Husbandry Officer, Zilla Parishad.
Methods of implementation:
A District Level Monitoring Committee under the Chairmanship of the District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner of the concerned district is being constituted to implement the scheme.
District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner: Chairman District Animal Husbandry Officer, District Council: Member Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare (State Level): Member Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry (Central Egg Uplift Centre) or Livestock Development Officer, Intensive Poultry Development Project, if available.
Functions of District Level Monitoring Committee: The selection process of beneficiaries should be completed by determining the taluk-wise target in proportion to the population of Dhangar and similar tribes in the nomadic tribes (C) category in the district, excluding the eligible application received.
After the selection of beneficiaries under the scheme is completed, one-day poultry training classes should be organized at the taluk level. No financial support will be provided for the training.
The services of the Livestock Development Officer of the taluk should be utilized for one-day training of the beneficiary.
3. The beneficiary is required to set up a shelter for birds at his own cost after completion of training.
4. The District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner shall transfer the due subsidy through DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) into the bank account of the beneficiary only after the Chief of the local Veterinary Institute and the Livestock Development Officer (Extension) jointly confirm the purchase of shelter, poultry feed and live birds for the beneficiary and submit the report to the District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner.
If the number of beneficiaries is found to be less than the minimum number of beneficiaries in a particular taluk, the remaining target may be transferred to another taluk with the approval of the District Level Control Committee.

If the number of beneficiaries is found to be less than the minimum number of beneficiaries in a district, the remaining target may be transferred to other districts with the approval of the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune.
Implementation Mechanism: The District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner of the concerned district will be the Implementation Officer of the scheme as well as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer. At Divisional level, Regional Joint Commissioner Animal Husbandry and Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune for the State will be the Controlling Officer.
Planning and Evaluation:
The head of the veterinary institution in whose jurisdiction the scheme is to be implemented shall follow the scheme 100%. Also, guide the beneficiaries for prophylactic vaccination and other aspects. The Livestock Development Officer (Extension), Panchayat Samiti should verify at least 50 per cent of the groups. The District Animal Husbandry Officer, Zilla Parishad and District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner should verify at least 10 per cent of the groups each.
The Regional Joint Commissioner Animal Husbandry (All) shall submit an assessment report of the implementation of the scheme in his department to the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune within the next three months after the end of the financial year. The Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune should submit the consolidated assessment report of the state to the government with their self-explanatory opinion.
Financial Implications of the Scheme: Under the scheme of implementing special programmes for the development of Dhangar and similar tribes in the category of Nomadic Tribes (C) on the lines of the ongoing schemes for Scheduled Tribes by the Tribal Development Department, the Department of Welfare of Scheduled Castes, Nomadic Tribes and Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes has provided Rs. 3060.00 lakhs will be available.
New Account Title for the scheme: The Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune should immediately propose a new account title to the Government for the implementation of the scheme. The proposed department of accounting, planning, finance and The same shall be communicated after obtaining the approval of the Accountant General, Mumbai.
Guidelines for the implementation of the plan:
In order to benefit the Dhangar and similar beneficiaries of the nomadic tribes (C) category, the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State, Pune should immediately issue detailed guidelines to all the concerned field officers from his level to increase the production of eggs and meat through poultry farming in rural areas, to include eggs of the highest nutritional value in the human diet, to increase the production of poultry meat and to provide opportunities for economic development to the Dhangar and similar unemployed people of the nomadic tribes (C) category.
