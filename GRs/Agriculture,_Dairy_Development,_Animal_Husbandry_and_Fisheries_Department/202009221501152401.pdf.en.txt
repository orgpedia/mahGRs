Government's decision:
Under the Central Agricultural Development Scheme for 2020-21, the benefit of new wells to the farmers of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes has been provided. 58.77 crore (Rs. Administrative approval is being given for the expenditure of Rs.
2) Under this scheme, Rs. A sum of Rs. 58.77 crore has been approved for providing new wells to the farmers belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes.
3. To provide sustainable irrigation facilities to the farmers belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in the State, under this programme, Rs. The new wells will be provided to farmers belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes with an annual income of up to Rs 1.50 lakh. 2.50 lakh per beneficiary.
4. The eligibility criteria for the beneficiary for the programme and the mode of implementation for the new well component will be on the lines of Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana and Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana.
The programme will be implemented through the convergence of both the schemes of Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana and Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana. Since only the new well component is to be covered under the programme, the benefit of other components of the regular scheme will be allowed to the beneficiaries from the respective scheme, so that more beneficiaries can be benefitted. The duration of the programme is one year as approved by the State Level Project Sanctioning Committee for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes under the National Agriculture Development Scheme. In view of the expenditure of funds during this period, the beneficiaries who have demanded a new well unit under the respective schemes should be given the benefit of funds under the National Agriculture Development Scheme on first priority. After the funds of the scheme are fully spent, the remaining beneficiaries should be given the benefit of this component from the regular scheme.
6.The Commissioner (Agriculture) shall take action to determine the district-wise target in proportion to the total number of farmers belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes as per the agricultural census under the Sadar programme. Also, the funds sanctioned under this programme should be distributed by the Commissioner (Agriculture) at the district level in accordance with the target fixed district-wise.
7.Sadar The funds received under the program should be distributed by the Agriculture Commissioner's Office to the concerned District Superintending Agriculture Officer.

8. District Superintending Agriculture Officer at the district level shall implement the scheme in coordination with the Agriculture Development Officer within the limits of the provision approved in the current year.
9.According to the agricultural census under the Sadar program, the agricultural development officer of each taluk should determine the economic target in proportion to the total number of farmers belonging to the Scheduled Castes and Tribes category in the respective taluks of the district and within the limit of this target, the district level selection committee should select the beneficiaries for this program from the interested farmers who have qualified for the Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana and Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana 2020-21 in the respective taluks.
10.The selection list of beneficiaries in each taluka as well as the waiting list should be published in order. Copies of these lists should be uploaded on the notice boards of the panchayat samitis, zilla parishads and the office of the district superintendent of agriculture.
11.The beneficiaries selected for the new well through tender shall be informed in writing about their selection within 7 days from the date of draw of lots. Thereafter, on-site inspection of the area of the beneficiaries should be carried out and after technical feasibility study, if the location of the beneficiaries to dig a new well is found to be technically suitable, the process of approving the budget of the concerned beneficiaries and giving prior approval to the proposals should be done within 45 days from the date of release. Similarly, the selection of beneficiaries whose proposals are found to be technically unsuitable in the site inspection should be cancelled and the next eligible beneficiaries in the waiting list should be selected.
12.Sadar The prior consent of the beneficiaries who do not implement the matters under the program within the prescribed time should be cancelled and thereafter the next eligible beneficiaries should be selected as per the rank in the waiting list. Thus, the scheme should be implemented by selecting the beneficiaries under the scheme in a transparent manner.
13.Sadar A maximum period of 1 year shall be admissible to the beneficiaries for implementation of the programme.
14.Sadar Prior consent should be given to the beneficiaries within the limits of the financial targets to be achieved for the district/ taluk under the programme.
15.The selection of beneficiaries under the Sadar program should be done by the selection committee constituted under the Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Krishi Swavalamban Yojana and Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana.
