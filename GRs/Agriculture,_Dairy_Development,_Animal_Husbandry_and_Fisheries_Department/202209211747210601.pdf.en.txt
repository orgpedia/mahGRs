Preamble: The Chief Minister's Sustainable Agriculture Irrigation Scheme will be implemented in drought prone areas as well as in suicide affected and Naxal affected districts with the aim of providing sustainable irrigation facilities to the farmers of the state. As per the government decision dated August 19, 2019, the scheme has been approved and the government has decided to implement it in the remaining talukas of the state. The decision was taken on November 18, 2021.
Under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, 55% subsidy is given to small and marginal farmers of the state and 45% to other farmers (within the limit of 5 hectares). Apart from this permissible subsidy, under the" Chief Minister Sustainable Agriculture Irrigation" scheme, a total subsidy of 80% and 75% is provided for micro irrigation respectively by giving 25% supplementary subsidy to small and marginal land holding farmers and 30% to other farmers.
In the year 2022-23, under the Mukhyamantri Saswat Krishi Sinchai Yojana, Rs. 84 crore to the Commissioner (Agriculture) was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
In the financial year 2022-23, under the Chief Minister Sustainable Agriculture Irrigation Scheme, Rs. 84 crore (Rupees Eighty Four Crores only) is being disbursed to the Commissioner (Agriculture) on Budgetary Distribution System.
