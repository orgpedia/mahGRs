The Proposal:
Maharashtra has a 720 km long coastline with 173 fishing harbours. In order to sustain sustainable fishing in the catchment areas of the State, provisions have been made under the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981 in the State to regulate fishing activities in the coastal waters of the State and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto. The Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021 has been enacted by amending this Act. The Act provides for the power to regulate, restrict or prohibit certain activities of fishing in specified areas. Orders imposing or prohibiting such restrictions may be notified after consultation with the State Advisory and Control Committee and there is a provision that the Advisory and Control Committee may take into consideration the recommendations of the District Committees, if any, before recommending the regulations to the State Government.
As per the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021, it is mandatory to constitute a" State Advisory and Regulation Committee" and a" District Committee" for each coastal district, the government has decided for Palghar, Thane, Raigad, Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg districts. The District Committee has been constituted with effect from 29th June, 2022. The state government has decided to set up two districts- Mumbai city and Mumbai suburban.
Government's decision:
The Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021 has been enacted by amending the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981.
