The Proposal:
At the fish landing centres located in the coastal districts of Maharashtra, basic facilities are available at many places for the fishermen. The government has decided to provide basic facilities to the fishermen at the fish unloading centre from the loan received from NABARD under the Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) Series-24. As per the scheme, 95 per cent of the project cost will be borne by NABARD and the remaining 5 per cent by the state government.
For the year 2018-19, under NABARD funded Rural Infrastructure Development Fund Scheme, a sum of Rs. Four works amounting to Rs. 180.51 crore were submitted for approval to NABARD, Pune, out of which NABARD has sanctioned 2 works for Department of Fisheries at Rs. 7525.89 lakh and Rs. 396.10 lakh. A sum of Rs 7921.994 lakh has been sanctioned. Accordingly, the matter of giving administrative approval to these 2 works was under the consideration of the government.

Government's decision:
Under the Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) Scheme, NABARD has sanctioned 95% share of Rs. 7525.89 lakh and State share of Rs. 396.10 lakh. Administrative Sanction is being accorded for an amount of Rs. 7921.994 Lakhs.
95% of NABARD funds will be met from the head 4405 3292 and the remaining 5% will be met from the head 4405 3309 of the State Government.
The Commissioner, Fisheries, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai shall take further action regarding the tender process as per the prevailing rules of the said works and obtain necessary level of no-objection permission. Claims for reimbursement of expenditure incurred under the said works should be submitted to NABARD from time to time in the prescribed format. The government should also submit a report on the progress of the work every quarter.
This decision was taken by the Government with the concurrence of the Planning and Finance Department and in the informal reference of the Finance Department No. 499/2018/ Expenditure-2, Dy. Issued on 19.09.2018.
