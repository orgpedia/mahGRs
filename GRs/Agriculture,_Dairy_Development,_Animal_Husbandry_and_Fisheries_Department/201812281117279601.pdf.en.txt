INTRODUCTION: To study the impact of coastal pollution on fish production in the coastal areas of Mumbai, Mumbai Suburban, Thane, Raigad, Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg districts of the state, read the following. The Committee was constituted as per the Government Resolution dated 09.07.2013.
Thereafter, the environmental imbalance due to the release of chemicals into Dharamtar Bay, the pollution caused by it and the impact on the traditional fishermen's fish production there, as well as the socio-economic survey of the fisheries industry since the inception of Nippon Denno Ispat Company adjacent to Dharamtar Bay at Dolvi in Pen tehsil of Raigad district and PNP Company located at Shahbaz in Alibaug tehsil, and the increase in the number of affected fishermen in this area, according to the data given by the action committee, as well as to study the impact of coastal pollution on fish production in the state's marine areas of Mumbai, Mumbai Suburban, Thane, Palghar, Raigad, Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg districts. Now the government was considering to form a committee to study the impact of chemical-laced water released in Dharamtar creek on fish production. Accordingly, the Government have decided to constitute the following Committee:

The committee will have the following functions:
1) To study whether the livelihood of fishermen has been exhausted due to the jetty of 2 companies in Dharamtar Bay.
(ii) To examine and determine the extent to which the fisheries in the Bay of Bengal have been affected by the water transport in the Dharamtar Bay.
(iii) The number of fishermen families affected and the extent of damage 4) To examine the impact of the discharge of sewage into the sea and other creeks and the sea in the Dharmatar creek and the coastal areas of the State and to determine the extent of the damage caused to the fisheries.
5) To suggest permanent measures to be taken if the fisheries in the creek are affected by the water transport in the Dharamtar creek and the industrial effluents discharged into the creek.
6) The species of fish affected by pollution and the measures to be taken for their conservation.

The committee shall visit the concerned district to study the issues related to its functioning, during which the Assistant Commissioner, Fisheries of the respective district shall be present and the committee shall be instructed to attend the representatives of the necessary cooperative institutions.
The committee will have a six-month term. The Committee No. Reports 1,2,3 are required to be submitted by 31.3.2019 and reports 4, 5 and 6 are required to be submitted by 30.6.2019.
The Commissioner, Fisheries, shall provide necessary office equipment to the Committee during the aforesaid period. Under the innovative scheme for the activities of the committee, especially for environmental studies, funds should be obtained from the district annual plan.
Remuneration, daily allowance, travel allowance, etc. of non-official members of the Committee. Payments will be made as required.
