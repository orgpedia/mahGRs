The Proposal:
As per the decision of the Department of Agriculture and Padum dated 10th May, 2018, an incentive subsidy of Rs. 3/- per litre has been allowed for conversion of milk powder for a period of 30 days in the month of March, 2018 to convert the excess milk produced into milk powder due to increase in milk production during the month of October to March in the state. Accordingly, a total of 21 projects in the state have been eligible for the grant and a grant of Rs. 5424.60 lakh has been released to them. A sum of Rs. 19,46,88, 000/-has been sanctioned for the purpose of payment of the said ex-gratia amount in the Supplementary Demand Proposal presented in the July, 2018 Session of the Legislature. The government was planning to disburse the funds.
Government's decision:
As per the decision of the Government dated 10th May, 2018, the Government is approving the release of funds to the tune of Rs. 19,46,88, 000/- to the Government Milk Schemes as per the head of accounts sanctioned by the Supplementary Demand for Milk Subsidy under the Appropriation Act, 2018 in the July, 2018 session of the Legislature.
