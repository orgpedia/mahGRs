The Proposal:
The scheme is being implemented on a 60:40 sharing basis between the Centre and the State governments. The Central share of Rs. 39.37 crore was sanctioned to the National Agriculture Development Scheme for the year 2017-18 for the Scheduled Tribes category and out of this, a total of Rs. 36.86 crore was released to the State, out of which Rs. 29.86 crore was released to the Central share and Rs. 19.91 crore to the State share. 5, 8, 9 has been distributed to the Commissioner (Agriculture) as per the Government decision.
In 2017-18, out of the total central share of Rs. 36.86 crore, Rs.7 crore was released by the Central Government. It was deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the State on 31/03/2018

It was not distributed. As a result, a total of Rs. 11.67 crore was yet to be disbursed as against the Central share of Rs.7 crore and the State share of Rs. 4.67 crore. The reference As per the letter dated 2nd May, 2018, the Central Government has approved the re-validation of the said fund for the current year. Accordingly, a total of Rs. 11.67 crore from the Central and State share was under the consideration of the Government to be disbursed to the Commissioner (Agriculture) to be spent in the current year on the projects sanctioned under the scheme.
Government's decision:
For the implementation of the projects sanctioned under the National Agriculture Development Scheme in 2017-18, a sum of Rs. 11.67 crore (Rs. 11 crore and six lakh only) of the Central and State share for the Scheduled Tribes category is being provided to the Commissioner (Agriculture) through the Budget Distribution System by way of re-financing during 2018-19.
