3. All Inclusive Crop Insurance Scheme for Kharif and Rabi seasons will be implemented covering the following risk factors: 1. Prevented Sowing/ Planting/ Germination of Crops due to Adverse Climatic Conditions, 2. Mid Season Adversity of Crops due to Adverse Climatic Conditions during the cropping season, 3. Loss of Crop Yield due to Natural Fire, Lightning, Hailstorm, Storm, Cyclone, Flood, Flood, Landslide, Drought, Rainfall, Pests and Diseases etc., 4. Loss of Crops due to Local Natural Calamities. Under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY), the Central Government has limited the insurance premium to 2% for Kharif season, 1.5% for Rabi season and 5% for Kharif and Rabi season cash crops. Under the comprehensive crop insurance scheme, the insurance premium of the farmers' share will be paid by the state government without the burden of the farmer's share. Hence, from the year 2023-24, farmers will be able to register on the crop insurance portal by paying only Rs 1/- and the crop-wise insurance premium amount of the farmer's share determined under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana and the insurance premium amount to be paid by the farmers in reality will be Rs. 1/- minus the remaining difference will be paid by the State Government as State Share Grant.
In this scheme, farmers can participate through the Central Government's Crop Insurance Portal, Common Facility Center, Bank, etc. as per the guidelines of the Central Government.
The scheme will be implemented on a Profit& Loss model or Cup& Cap model (80:110) for the Kharif and Rabi seasons from 2023-24 to 2025-26. The State Level Crop Insurance Coordination Committee was constituted under the chairmanship of Chief Secretary. As per the decision taken in the meeting on 04.05.2023, the tender process will be carried out by including the items mentioned in paragraph 3 and based on the comparative information of the insurance premium rates received, the government decision will be issued separately to approve the implementation of the scheme with appropriate options under the Profit& Loss Sharing Model and Cup& Cap Model (80:110) with the approval of the State Level Crop Insurance Coordination Committee.
As per the guidelines of the Central Government for the determination of compensation under the All Inclusive Crop Insurance Scheme, at least 30 percent of the weightage of paddy, wheat, soybean and cotton crops will be given to technology-based production while determining the average loss of crops. Compensation for the remaining notified crops will be determined on the basis of regular crop harvesting experiments. In this regard, the instructions issued by the Central Government from time to time and the instructions given by the State Level Crop Insurance Coordination Committee will remain in force.
