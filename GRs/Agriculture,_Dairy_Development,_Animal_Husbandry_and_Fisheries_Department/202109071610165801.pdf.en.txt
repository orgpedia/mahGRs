Introduction: The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Soil Health Management (SHM) under National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) is being implemented since 2014-15. Since 2015-16, the scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60: 40 between the Centre and the State. Under this scheme, components like setting up of mobile/ permanent soil testing laboratory, strengthening of government soil testing laboratories, strengthening of fertilizer control testing laboratories etc. are implemented.
The Central Government vide letter dated 10.06.2020 has sanctioned a total amount of Rs. An annual action plan of Rs. 76.50 lakh was approved. The Central share (60%) is Rs. 45.90 lakhs and State share (40%) is Rs. A sum of Rs. 30.60 lakh had been sanctioned for the project. The Government vide letter dated 12.02.2021 has approved the setting up of a new Government Standing Laboratory at Nandurbar under the Soil Health Management Scheme. The Central Government vide its letter dated 04.08.2020 has allocated 75% of the funds for the scheme. A sum of Rs. 34.43 lakh had been sanctioned for the project. During the financial year 2020-21, the said funds were not disbursed as the period for establishment of the laboratory was over. The proposal was sent to the central government for the revival of the fund as it was not spent in the financial year 2020-21. The Central Government vide letter dated 29.04.2021 has agreed to spend the said fund by way of revival. In view of the above, the matter of administrative approval and disbursement of funds to the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Soil Health Management (SHM) under National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) for the year 2021-22 was under consideration of the Government.
