The Proposal:
Currently, 19 districts in Maharashtra are affected by the disease. Hence, vide No. 2 As per the Government Notification issued herein under the powers conferred by the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Contagious Diseases in Animals Act, 2009, the State of Maharashtra has been declared as a" Controlled Area" in respect of Lumpy Leather. A massive vaccination campaign and preventive measures need to be implemented to control the increasing incidence of this lumpy skin disease. In such a situation, the number of officers/ staff under the control of the Commissionerate of Animal Husbandry available with the State is less due to the vacant posts, which limits the work of effectively preventing the outbreak of lumpy skin disease. Commissioner Animal Husbandry has informed the Government that in the meeting of the Council of Ministers held on 12th September, 2022, the Department had presented the current status of the outbreak of Lumpy Skin Disease and the action being taken by the Department for the control of the disease and the immediate measures to be taken to provide relief to the farmers by preventing the outbreak of this disease. Accordingly, the Cabinet took immediate measures to prevent the outbreak of lumpy skin disease and provide relief to the farmers.

Instructions have been given. The directive also includes a directive to fill the vacancies in the livestock supervisory cadre temporarily through external sources. In pursuance of this direction, considering the vacancies for Livestock Supervisor (Group-C) (State Level) cadre, it is decided to provide manpower services through external sourcing as under:
Government Decision: In the cadre of Livestock Supervisor (Group-C) (State Level) under the Department of Animal Husbandry, a total of 286 manpower services have been provided through Outsourcing Reference No. It is being approved to be made available under the government decisions from 2 to 5.
In order to make the process of providing services of the said manpower transparent while filling up 286 posts of Livestock Supervisor (Group-C) in the cadre purely on temporary basis through Outsourcing, a Selection Committee is being constituted in each Revenue Division as follows:
(a) Composition of the selection committee
(i) Divisional Commissioner, concerned Revenue Division Chairman, (ii) Collector, concerned District Member, (iii) Chief Executive Officer, concerned Zilla Parishad Member, (iv) Officers of Social Welfare Department at the concerned Divisional Level, (v) Officers of Tribal Development Department at the concerned Divisional Level, (vi) Regional Animal Husbandry Joint Commissioner, Member Secretary of the concerned Regional Division
(2) The details of department-wise/ district-wise vacancies shall be submitted by the Commissioner Animal Husbandry to the concerned Divisional Commissioner, Revenue Department for taking action for providing the services of manpower through outsourcing to the said Selection Committee.
(3) The age limit will be applicable as per the educational qualification prescribed in the Service Admission Rules for the post of Livestock Supervisor Group-C and as per the prevailing policy of the Government while providing manpower services from external agencies.
The candidates to be recruited through the outsourcing system selected by the said Selection Committee shall be engaged by the concerned Regional Animal Husbandry Joint Commissioner and their day-to-day work shall be controlled by the concerned Regional Animal Husbandry Joint Commissioner and the District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner.
5) Outsourced candidates will be paid Rs. 15000/- (Rs. Fifteen thousand only) lump sum amount will be admissible in the form of honorarium. No other allowances will be admissible.
The services provided through this portal are only for the period up to the receipt of regular candidates for the post of Livestock Supervisor or temporarily for a maximum period of 11 months whichever is earlier. The appointment will be automatically terminated after 11 months.

(7) Even after this, the said posts will remain vacant due to non-availability of regular candidates and if there is any urgency for these posts for the functioning of the department, the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State, Pune shall inform the said selection committee at least three months in advance. While revising the process, the work/ experience of the candidate concerned in the past should be selected on the basis of merit only without considering it in any way while making a fresh selection. However, in any case, the extension of time will not be admissible to the outsourced system selected by the selection committee.
Since the process of providing manpower through external sources is temporary in nature for a period of 11 months or till a regular recommendatory candidate is found for the post of Livestock Supervisor, the person concerned cannot claim any authority for the permanent absorption or retention of persons in the government service who are in service through external sources.
While implementing the said procedure, the candidates appointed shall take an undertaking/ bond in writing before appointment that no claim for absorption or retention in the service will be made as the service will be terminated in any case after a period of 11 months, while ensuring that no court case will arise in future regarding their retention in the government service.
