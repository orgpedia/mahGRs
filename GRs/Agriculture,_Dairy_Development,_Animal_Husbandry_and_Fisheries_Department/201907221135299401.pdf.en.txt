Further, the Cabinet Sub-Committee constituted to suggest immediate measures/ take control/ decision for drought period had earlier sanctioned Rs. 2 crore to the Gaushalas under the scheme Govardhan Govansh Seva Kendra in the severely drought-affected districts of Marathwada. In the Gaushalas which have been selected for grant of Rs. 1.00 crore or where grant has been sanctioned, approval has been given to start Cattle Relief and Fodder Camps for the livestock of the area on a pilot basis with immediate effect. Read as follows. Orders dated 09.01.2019 and 06.02.2019 respectively have been issued.
The Revenue and Forest Department has decided to continue the ongoing fodder camps in the drought areas of the state beyond June 30, 2019. The Government has issued a decision dated 26.06.2019. Considering the delayed arrival of the monsoon in 2019, it will take some time to make fodder available to the cattle, as fodder and water are available to the cattle in the drought areas in the state.
