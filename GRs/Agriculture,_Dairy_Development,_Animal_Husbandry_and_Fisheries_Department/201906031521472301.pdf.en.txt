The Proposal:
With the objectives of promoting the food processing industry in the state, encouraging the establishment of projects based on modern technology through the participation of farmers to increase the value of agricultural products, encouraging the modernization of the project to increase the quality of the food produced, to save energy, the 100% state-sponsored scheme" Chief Minister Agriculture and Food Processing Scheme" has been launched in the state for the next five years from the financial year 2017-18 under the Government Decision No. 1 above. Further, under the Government Decision No. 2, the" Food Processing Policy-2017" of the State has been announced and under the Government Decision No. 3, the area has been earmarked for small, micro and medium enterprises under the Food Processing Scheme in the State.
As per the Government Resolution No. 4, the approved budgetary allocation for the year 2019-20 of the Department has been communicated and guidelines have been issued on how to take action in this regard. As per the decision of the Government, a provision of Rs. 4000.00 lakh (Rs. Forty Crore only) has been budgeted for the implementation of Mukhyamantri Krishi and Food Processing Scheme in the year 2019-20. Accordingly, the matter of granting administrative approval for the implementation of the scheme in 2019-20 was under consideration. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
Administrative approval is being given for the implementation of Mukhyamantri Krishi and Food Processing Scheme in the year 2019-20 with a total budgetary allocation of Rs. 4000.00 lakh (Rupees Forty Crore only) for the current year.
