Preamble: Under the Tribal Component Programme, with the objective of reducing malnutrition in the tribal areas of 14 districts of the State and to provide nutritious food to the tribal brothers and sisters, the scheme of" Fruit and Vegetable Cultivation in the Neighbourhood of Tribal Families" is being implemented in the State since 2003-04 to promote the cultivation of nutritious vegetables and fruits in the neighbourhood of tribal families. For the year 2022-23, Rs. A sum of Rs. 33.00 lakh has been allocated for the project. Accordingly, the scheme will be implemented in the state in 2022-23 at a cost of Rs. 33.00 lakh has been proposed by the Director (Horticulture) in Reference No. 3 submitted to the government. Finance Department Reference No. The Government has approved the release of funds for the schemes under the programme within the limit of 60% of the budgetary allocation for 2022-23. Accordingly, to implement the scheme in the State in 2022-23, within the budgeted funds in 2022-23, an amount of Rs. The matter of giving administrative approval to the program of 33.00 lakh rupees was under the consideration of the government, according to which the government is taking the following decisions.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: APYO-2022/ PR No. 99/9 O
Government Decision: 1. To implement the Fruit and Vegetable Cultivation Scheme in the vicinity of tribal families in the 14 districts of Thane, Palghar, Pune, Nashik, Dhule, Nandurbar, Jalgaon, Ahmednagar, Nanded, Yavatmal, Gadchiroli, Chandrapur, Amravati and Gondia in the state in 2022-23, Rs. Administrative approval is being accorded for the programme with an outlay of Rs. 33.00 lakh (Rupees Thirty Three Lakhs only).
The Director Horticulture is being declared as the Controlling Officer for drawing and disbursing the amount disbursed through this Government Resolution from the Treasury and the Assistant Director, Accounts-I is being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer for Commissionerate level expenditure. Similarly, the District Agriculture Officer at the level of Joint Director of Agriculture, District Superintending Agriculture Officer at the district level, Sub-Divisional Agriculture Officer at the sub-division level and Taluk Agriculture Officer at the taluka level are being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officers.
3. Director (Horticulture), Office of the Commissioner of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune to ensure that the programme is implemented within the limits of available funds.
Since the problem of malnutrition is widespread in tribal families, as a special matter, the report of expenditure from the provisions made available, the information on account head-wise/ sub-head-wise and material objectives achieved, the certificate of utilization of funds, the concerned field officer should send to the Commissioner, Tribal Development, Nashik, the concerned Additional Commissioner, Tribal Development, Tribal Development Department and Agriculture and Padum Department by the 10th of every month.
The Controlling Officer and Head of the Department shall ensure that the expenditure incurred on various schemes in the tribal sub-plan area, in the additional tribal sub-plan area as well as in the areas outside the tribal sub-plan area shall be recorded separately. So that the government can keep an eye on the expenditure on schemes in the tribal sub-plan area and outside the tribal sub-plan area.
Individual benefit schemes in tribal applications should be taken up from the provision made under the Tribal Component Programme and only individual benefit schemes should be taken up in areas outside the tribal sub-plan area.
6. The action taken report should be submitted along with the district-wise list of beneficiaries.
7. Other proceedings in the case read as above. 2 shall be made in accordance with the provisions of the Government Decision referred to in paragraph 2.
