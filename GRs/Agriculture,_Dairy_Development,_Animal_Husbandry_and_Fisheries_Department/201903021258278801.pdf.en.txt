Department of Agriculture and Padum Government Decision No. REVIEW-2018/ Q.No.112 (3)/ 18/2-O, Dy. 01/03/2019 Introduction:
The Government of India has included sugarcane and cotton as commercial crops under the National Food Security Mission since 2014-15. The main objectives of the mission are to increase the production and productivity of the crops covered under the mission, increase the fertility and productivity of the farm land, create employment opportunities and increase the productivity of the farmers.
Government of India for the year 2018-19 As per letter dated 03/05/2018, an Action Plan for Maharashtra under National Food Security Mission for commercial crops (Cotton, Sugarcane) with an outlay of Rs. 793.27 lakhs (Central share Rs. 475.96 lakhs+ State share Rs. 317.31 lakhs) has been approved and accorded administrative approval by the Government vide Government Resolution dated 23/05/2018 and 21/08/2018 respectively.
As per the Interim Utilization Certificate submitted by the State Government to the Central Government in respect of the Central share of funds received for the cotton crop in 2015-16, 2016-17 and 2017-18, a total of Rs. 73.20 lakh was spent. A total amount of Rs. 73.20 lakh has been approved for the year 2018-19, to be utilized in the three years from the letter dated 12th June, 2018. However, the state government has sent a proposal to the Centre.
