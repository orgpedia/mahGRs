Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to help in maintaining the financial stability of the farmers, the state is implementing a climate-based crop insurance scheme on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent.
The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide effective compensation to the farmers, a restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme will be implemented in the state for 8 crops of orange, pomegranate, guava, lemon, citrus and grapes (C) (Mrig Bahar) in the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 by taking into account the Revenue Board in 26 districts, HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co., Ltd., Reliance General Insurance. Agriculture Insurance Company of India Ltd. The insurance companies are referred to as No. The decision is being implemented in accordance with the Government 1.
