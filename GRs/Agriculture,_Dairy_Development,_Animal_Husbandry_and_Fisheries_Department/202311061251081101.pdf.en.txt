Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to help in maintaining the financial stability of the farmers, the state is implementing a climate-based crop insurance scheme on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent.
The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide crop compensation to the farmers, a restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme will be implemented in the state for 8 crops of orange, pomegranate, guava, lemon, citrus and grapes (C) (Mrig Bahar) in the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 by taking into account the revenue board in 26 districts, HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co., Ltd., Reliance General Insurance Co., Ltd.
