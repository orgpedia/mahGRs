Agriculture: The main occupation of the people of this district is agriculture. At present, there is no government college in the district. If a government agricultural college is established in Buldhana district, the students of rural areas who have completed the four-year agricultural degree course, which is currently in vogue, will be able to work as expert trainers on the campus.
Manpower will be available at the local level to set up self-employment based agro-industries.
It will also help farmers in rural areas to increase agricultural production by providing agricultural services and advice. Budget 2016-17 The Minister (Finance) has announced to set up an Agriculture College in Buldhana. Accordingly, a proposal for setting up an Agriculture College in Buldhana district has been received from the Maharashtra Council of Agricultural Education and Research, Pune.
As per the criteria of the 5th Board of Governors of Indian Council of Agricultural Research, New Delhi, a minimum of 30 hectares of land is required to establish a new agricultural college with an intake capacity of 60 students per annum.

Out of 130 hectares of land under the jurisdiction of Vidyalaya, Agricultural Research Centre and Krishi Vigyan Kendra, 30 hectares of land will be made available for the proposed college.
The Executive Council of Dr. Panjabrao Deshmukh Agriculture University, Akola has approved the proposal for establishment of a new Government aided constituent College of Agriculture at Buldhana as per the recommendation of the 5th Board of Governors of Indian Council of Agricultural Research. The 318th meeting held on 28/05/2018 has approved the Resolution CAP/ 50/2018. The proposal for establishment of Government Agriculture College at Buldhana under Punjabrao Deshmukh Agriculture University, Akola has been approved vide Resolution No. 06/96/2018 and the proposal has been submitted for Government approval.
Government's decision:
Government Agriculture College at Buldhana under Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Agriculture University, Akola with an intake capacity of 60 students per annum is being approved to be started from the academic year 2023-24.
2. Approval is being given to rename the said college as" Bhausaheb Phundkar Government Agriculture College, Buldhana."
The approval for creation of 45 posts of teaching and 43 posts of non-teaching staff for the said College is subject to the approval of the High Powered Committee headed by the Chief Secretary as per the norms of the 5th Board of Governors of Indian Council of Agricultural Research, New Delhi.
4) Rs. 4772.33 lakhs, recurring expenditure Rs. 112.92 lakh and non-recurring expenditure of Rs. 9769.00 lacs out of which a total of Rs. 14654 lakh is being approved.
The admission process of students should be carried out after the availability of necessary manpower and other infrastructure for the Government Agricultural College in Buldhana.
