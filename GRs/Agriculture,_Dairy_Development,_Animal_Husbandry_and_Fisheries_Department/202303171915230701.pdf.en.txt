Preamble: In order to provide insurance cover to the farmers against climatic hazards and to help the farmers in maintaining their financial stability, the State is implementing the Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme for the year 2020-21 on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production on a large scale. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide crop compensation to the farmers, a restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme for the year 2020-21 for 8 crops namely Orange, Cashew, Pomegranate, Mango, Banana, Grape and Strawberry (Ambia Bahar) on a pilot basis will be implemented in the district by the Revenue Board by taking into account the contribution of HDFC ERGO General Insurance Co. Ltd., Bajaj Allianz General Insurance. Agriculture Insurance Company of India Ltd. The insurance companies are referred to as No. Decisions 1 and 2 were implemented.
