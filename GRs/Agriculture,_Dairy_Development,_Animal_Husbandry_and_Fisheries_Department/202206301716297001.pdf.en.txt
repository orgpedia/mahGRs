The State of Maharashtra has a coastline of 720 km with 173 fishing harbours. In order to sustain sustainable fishing in the catchment areas of the State, provisions have been made under the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981 in the State to regulate fishing activities in the coastal waters of the State and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto. The Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021 has been enacted by amending this Act. The Act provides for the power to regulate, restrict or prohibit certain activities of fishing in specified areas. There is a provision that the order to impose or prohibit such restriction can be notified after consultation with the State Advisory and Control Committee. The government was considering the formation of a state advisory and control committee under the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021, in order to comply with the said Act for sustainable fishing.
The Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation (Amendment) Act, 2021 has been enacted by amending the Maharashtra Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1981. The Act provides for a State Advisory and Controlling Committee to exercise the powers conferred under Section 3 (1) and to perform the duties imposed.
The government is giving its approval to form a" State Advisory and Supervisory Committee" for compliance of the said Act.
