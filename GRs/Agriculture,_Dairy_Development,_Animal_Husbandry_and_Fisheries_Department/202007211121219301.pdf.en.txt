The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH) from 01.04.2014. Accordingly, the National Horticulture Mission being implemented in the state has been included in the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission. This mission has been included in the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Agriculture Promotion. Integrated Horticulture Mission is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between the Centre and the States.
The main objectives of the scheme are to increase the productivity of crops covered under the mission, increase the fertility and productivity of farm land, create new employment opportunities and increase the overall income of farmers. In 2020-21, a new programme of Rs. 11176.14 lakh and a Spill Over/ Committed programme of Rs. 8481.96 lakh for implementation of National Horticulture Mission under Krishi Unnati Yojana-Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State.

Therefore, the Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune has requested for administrative approval of the Annual Work Plan for the year 2020-21 for an amount of Rs. 19658.10 lakh.
However, in view of the impact of COVID-19 pandemic on the economy during the financial year 2020-21, the Department has been directed by the Department of Finance vide Government Resolution dated 04/05/2020 to plan the schemes under the programme subject to the condition that 33% of the budgeted allocation for 2020-21 will be available to the Department. Considering the directions of the Finance Department, the matter of granting administrative approval for implementation of the said scheme in 2020-21 was under consideration. The government is taking the following steps:
