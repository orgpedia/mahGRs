Benefits under the scheme should be provided to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and women farmer beneficiaries as prescribed by the Central Government under the Krishi Unnati Yojana National Food Security Mission.
The Union Ministry of Agriculture has issued a Reference letter to the State Government for the year 2019-20 under the National Food Security Mission. It will be mandatory to implement the Mission as per the administrative approval given vide letter dated 18.05.2020, as well as the action plan approved for the Mission this year and the guidelines issued by the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time. In this regard, the Director (Extension and Training) should control the mission from the state level, as well as direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the mission.
Priority should be given to small and marginal farmers or groups of farmers while selecting beneficiary farmers under various components of the National Food Security Mission.
Under this campaign, for the purchase of various inputs (fertilizers, medicines, seeds, plants, machines/ tools, etc.), direct benefit transfer should be done directly to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries as per the prevailing policy of the government. The beneficiaries will have the freedom to buy these tenders from the open market, however, if the tenders produced by the government agencies/ public enterprises of the government are being supplied under this campaign, the relevant government will take a decision. Action to be taken as on 19.04.2017.
The concerned District Superintending Agriculture Officer/ Taluk Agriculture Officer should select the beneficiaries for the campaign and compile the list of beneficiaries at the Taluk level in order to implement the programs covered under the campaign. In this list, the information of the farmer's name, village, survey/ group number, mobile/ telephone number etc. should be collected. Similarly, the District Superintending Agriculture Officer will be responsible for making the list of beneficiaries selected under the campaign available on the website of the Agriculture Department within 15 days from the date of receipt of funds.
9.According to the list of beneficiaries, the agricultural assistants of the respective villages should physically inspect the program undertaken on the farm of all the beneficiary farmers and they should submit their report to the District Superintending Agriculture Officer through the Taluk Agriculture Officer. Similarly, as soon as the reports of the agricultural assistants are received, the Director (Extension and Training) should lay down the appropriate standard of supervisory inspection of the works implemented under the said programme in the guidelines.
