Preamble: As per the decision of the Government No. 1, a committee has been appointed under the chairmanship of Secretary (Padum) to fix the procurement and sale price of milk for the Government Milk Scheme in the State. The Committee met on 13.8.2018. The minutes of the meeting have been issued under Reference No. 6. In the above meeting of the committee, it was discussed to revise the cow milk procurement price fixed under the government decision at Reference No. 2, to revise the sale price of" Aarey Bhushan" milk fixed under the government decision at Reference No. 3 and to revise the milk commission rate paid to the distributors under the government milk scheme decided under the government decision at Reference No. 4.
As per the decision taken in the meeting, the government was considering to revise the government milk procurement rate, the sale rate of' Aarey Bhushan' milk and the commission rate given to the milk distributors under the government milk scheme.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: MLK 2013/ PR No. 98/ Padum 8
Government's decision:
The Government has decided to reduce the procurement price of cow milk and buffalo milk by Rs.2/- per litre for Government milk scheme and accordingly, the revised procurement price is fixed at Rs.25/- per litre for cow milk of 3.5% fat and 8.5% SNF quality and Rs.34/- per litre for buffalo milk of 6.0% fat and 9.0% SNF quality.
2. It is also approved to revise the selling price fixed under the Government decision at Reference No.3 for fortified toned milk" Aarey Bhushan" as follows:
a) The selling price of Aarey Bhushan milk for Brihanmumbai is Rs. 37/- p.l. and Rs. 18.50 per litre.
b) For the rest of Maharashtra, the sale price of Aarey Bhushan milk is Rs. 36/- p.l. and Rs. 18 per litre.
3. Also, the Government has already released Rs. 2.50 to Rs. The commission rate of Rs. 3.50 per litre was fixed as per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 4. This rate is being revised and instead of the phase-wise commission rate, it is being approved to give a commission rate of Rs. 3/- per litre (including transport commission).
All the above decisions shall come into effect from 1st October, 2018.
