The Proposal:
A total of 5142 villages in 15 districts, including 14 districts of Vidarbha and Marathwada and Jalgaon districts, have been covered under the World Bank-funded project. 4000 crore Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project. Approval was granted pursuant to a Government Resolution dated 17th January, 2018. Similarly, under the Government decision dated 28th September, 2017, a total of 805 posts have been sanctioned for the project, out of which 132 posts are filled by appointment or deputation from Government officers/ employees.
The Government has approved payment of 25% of basic pay (Basic Pay+ Grade Pay) as Project Allowance to these Government Officers/ Employees being engaged for the project as per Government Decision dated 25th May, 2018. The State Government has implemented the 7th Pay Commission with retrospective effect from 01.01.2016 and vide letter No.5, Project Director, Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project, Mumbai has requested for sanction of 25% Project Allowance as per 7th Pay Commission.
The Government is taking the following decisions in the matter of sanctioning project allowance at 12.50% of basic pay as an incentive to the officers/ employees working full time in Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project as per the formula fixed under the 7th Pay Commission.

Government's decision:
The Government has decided to allow payment of Project Allowance@ 12.50% of the basic pay fixed as per the 7th Central Pay Commission to the Government officers and employees appointed/ deputed on full time basis under Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project with effect from 01.01.2019.
The officers/ employees of Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project have been paid project allowance as per the 7th Pay Commission pay scale of 25%. However, as per the decision of the present Government, since the Project Allowance has been sanctioned as 12.50% of the basic pay fixed as per the 7th Pay Commission with effect from 01.01.2019, the excess payment of the officers/ employees engaged in the project/ who have exited the project due to transfer/ promotion shall be recovered by the concerned office. It should be controlled by the Project Director's Office.
The provisions regarding the recovery and adjustment of the excess amount paid to the retired officers/ employees are contained in Rule 134-A of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, and Rule 142 (2) of the said Rules provides for the adjustment of the amount due from the government employee after his death, other than the official residence. Action should be taken for overcharging as per the provisions of the said rules.
4. In cases where it is not possible to get the refund of the income tax paid on the overpaid project allowance, the overpayment should be recovered from the concerned officer/ employee by deducting the income tax paid on the difference of allowance.
The amount of Project Allowance payable to Government officers and employees under the project shall be subtracted from the project cost and shall be reimbursable by the World Bank.
Out of 805 posts sanctioned under the project, 132 posts have been sanctioned with project allowance and the list of such posts is attached with this Government decision at Annexure-A.
The said Project Allowance is completely independent and cannot be considered for any other consideration or purpose (including Dearness Allowance, Daily Allowance, Travel Allowance, Increase in Salary, Fixation of Pay, Retirement Allowance, etc.).
As per the provisions of the Government Resolution dated 28th September, 2017, if the sanctioned posts of Government officers/ employees under the project have been downgraded and filled up from the junior officers/ employees, the project allowance shall also be admissible to the junior officers/ employees.
9. Project allowance shall also be admissible to the Government officers/ employees if they are to be appointed in exceptional circumstances to the posts to be filled on contract under the project.
Project allowance will not be admissible to the employees working on contractual basis under Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project.
