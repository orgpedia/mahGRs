The Proposal:
The centrally sponsored schemes being implemented by the Department of Animal Husbandry like Livestock Insurance Scheme under National Livestock Mission, Information under ESCAD, Education and Public Participation Melas, National Artificial Insemination Programme as well as State level Animal Husbandry Extension and Publicity/ Publicity programmes, schemes, etc. are being implemented through the existing modes of publicity and publicity like wall painting, cloth banners, pamphlets, posters, brochures, clocks, newspapers, All India Radio, Doordarshan etc.
The Government is issuing the following decisions in accordance with the measures to be taken to make the information about the various schemes, programs, etc. implemented by the Department of Animal Husbandry reach all over the state by effective utilization of the funds available in the above mentioned various centrally sponsored and state level schemes.
Government's decision:
In the various centrally sponsored and state level schemes being implemented by the Department of Animal Husbandry, maximum publicity and publicity should be done through flex, electronic media, All India Radio, Doordarshan, Short Message Service (SMS).
At present, multi-purpose flex is being used on a large scale and its effectiveness is being shown to be good, information about the schemes from which such flexes are to be done should be prominently displayed in the middle of the flex and around this information, information about the toll-free number 1962 made available for farmers and cattle rearers under the Mukhyamantri Pashu Swasthya Yojana being implemented by the Department of Animal Husbandry, the National Artificial Feeding Program, various preventive vaccination programs, livestock insurance schemes, etc. should also be displayed.
The multi-purpose flexes should be installed at bus stands, railway stations, district collector's office, zilla parishad, panchayat samiti office, tehsildar's office, sub-divisional officer's office, agriculture department's office, all areas under the Animal Husbandry Department, all veterinary hospitals/ institutions, gram panchayats, etc.
