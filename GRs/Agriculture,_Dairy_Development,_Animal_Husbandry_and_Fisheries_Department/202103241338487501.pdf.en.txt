A total of 2192 posts have been sanctioned in the cadre of Livestock Development Officer Group-A in the Department of Animal Husbandry, out of which 535 posts are vacant. It is likely to take some time for these vacancies to be received by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission on a regular basis. In view of the outbreak of H1Five (bird flu) virus in poultry and other birds in some districts of the state, as an immediate measure, the matter of filling up of essential vacancies in connection with providing animal health services to the livestock farmers of the state was under consideration. However, in view of the time taken for filling up of these posts on regular basis, the vacancies in the cadre of Livestock Development Officer (Group-A) vide Reference No. As per the provisions of the Government Circular/ Decision No. 2 to 6, the following decisions are being taken for immediate payment through Outsourcing.
Government's decision:
Out of 535 vacancies in the cadre of Livestock Development Officer (Group-A) in the Department of Animal Husbandry, 206 posts are available in Reference No. The Government is giving its approval to make available through Outsourcing as per the procedures prescribed in the Government Circulars/ Decisions 2 to 6. The candidates to be selected while filling up the posts through out-sourcing should be selected by the organization/ company providing services through external agencies without being appointed immediately under any circumstances and the candidates should be made available through external agencies by conducting the tender process in the manner prescribed by the government.

In order to ensure that the recruitment process is fair and transparent, a selection committee is being constituted in each revenue division as follows: 1) Divisional Commissioner, concerned Revenue Division Chairman, 2) District Collector, concerned District Member, 3) Chief Executive Officer, concerned District Council Member, 4) Officer of Social Justice Department at the concerned division level, 5) Officer of Tribal Development Department at the concerned division level, 6) Regional Animal Husbandry Joint Commissioner, concerned Regional Division Member Secretary, 2) To fill up the said selection committee through Outsourcing.
3) The candidates selected by the said selection committee will be given posting with the approval of the government and the recruitment of the candidates given posting and their day-to-day work will be controlled by the concerned Regional Animal Husbandry Joint Commissioner and the District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner.
4) The candidates will get a monthly stipend of Rs. 35000/- (Rs. 5,000 only) and Rs. 38500/- (Rs. Thirty-eight thousand five hundred only) lump sum amount will be admissible in the form of honorarium. No other allowances will be admissible.
The said recruitment process shall be for the period up to the receipt of candidates from the Maharashtra Public Service Commission on regular basis for the post of Livestock Development Officer only or temporarily for a maximum period of 11 months, whichever is earlier. The appointment will be automatically terminated after 11 months.
(6) Even after this, the said posts will remain vacant due to non-availability of regular candidates and if there is an urgent need for these posts for the functioning of the department, the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State, Pune shall inform the said selection committee at least three months in advance. After this, the new recruitment process should be carried out by the said selection committee in accordance with the vacant posts. While revising the recruitment process, the work/ experience of the candidate concerned in the past should be selected on the basis of merit only without considering it in any way while making a fresh selection. However, in any case, the extension of time will not be admissible to the outsourced system selected by the selection committee.
(7) The appointment shall be temporary in nature for a period of 11 months or until a suitable candidate is found for the post of Livestock Development Officer.

The person concerned shall not be entitled to claim from any authority for the permanent absorption or retention in the Government service of persons employed through the outsourced system.
8) While implementing the said procedure, the candidates appointed shall take written undertaking/ bond before appointment that the services of the candidates concerned will not be terminated after 11 months to ensure that there is no court case regarding their future continuance.
9) The candidates to be recruited to the post of Livestock Development Officer Group A through the Outer Source System will not be assigned the duties of administrative, financial or similar responsibilities.
Therefore, these candidates should be assigned only duties and responsibilities related to the veterinary field. The duties of financial or similar responsibilities within their jurisdiction should be entrusted to the nearest regular Livestock Development Officer.
10) The Regional Animal Husbandry Joint Commissioner and the concerned Regional Department will be fully responsible for ensuring that the prescribed conditions are not violated while taking services through the external source system.
The concerned Regional Joint Commissioner of Animal Husbandry shall ensure that no claim or violation of the conditions prescribed in respect of appointment through the outsourcing mechanism is made.
