Introduction: The recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission have been accepted by the Central Government. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode from 2014-15 onwards.
Under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMOET), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, Seeds and Plantation Material Sub-Mission (SMSP) is being implemented.
This sub-mission is being implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government with a view to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and in a time-bound manner by augmenting and expanding the existing scheme in the form of a mission to include new technologies in the production of quality or certified seeds for improving seed testing laboratories, assisting seed production institutions to adopt new seed production technologies, etc. The scope of this sub-mission covers the entire seed chain, from producing centrifugal seeds for supplying certified seeds to farmers, to supporting creation of infrastructure for seed sector development, to depositing seeds in banks to meet the requirement of seeds during natural calamities, etc. The sub-campaign includes Seed Village Programme, Strengthening of Taluka Seed Cultivation Areas, Support to Certified Seed Production.
