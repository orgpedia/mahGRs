State Cabinet In the meeting held on 8th January, 2019, regarding Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, Shri. As per the directions of the State Cabinet, a decision has been issued to pay the difference of Rs.1000/- to the State Government in case the amount paid by the insurance company for crop insurance and crop insurance claim is less than Rs.1000/-
has done. The matter of issuing the procedure and terms and conditions to be adopted for the implementation of the said scheme was under consideration of the Government. Following is the government order:
Government circular:
The government has taken a decision. The scheme for payment of insurance compensation to the farmers under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana and Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme with a minimum sum assured of Rs.1000/- under 31.5.2019 has been approved under the relevant Government decision and the following procedures should be followed for implementation of the scheme:
1) Under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana and the Restructured Weather Based Fruit Crop Insurance Scheme, as per the norms of the Central Government, the amount of crop insurance and fruit insurance claim is Rs. For farmers with income less than Rs. 1000/- with the difference in figures
The information/ consideration should be submitted by the concerned insurance company to the State Government through the Commissioner (Agriculture).
2) As the name and application ID of the farmers are received by the insurance companies through the central portal, the information such as Aadhaar number, mobile number, etc. of the farmers should be made available by the insurance company and then the action for calculating the compensation should be taken.

3) Considering the difference, a farmer should have a minimum income of Rs. 1000/- should be considered as compensation.
4) The company should take care that the insurance loss is not paid more than once, in such a way that a separate ID is created for more than one registration by the farmers.
5) The insurance company should submit its payment to the Commissioner (Agriculture) after final consideration of the difference in the amount to be paid by the State Government i.e. up to a minimum of 1000/-.
After receiving the payment from the insurance company, the Commissioner (Agriculture) should scrutinize the payment and submit a proposal to the government for the distribution of necessary funds.
7) The Commissioner (Agriculture) shall take action to disburse the said funds to the concerned insurance companies within 10 days after the release of funds by the Government based on the proposal received from the Commissionerate of Agriculture.
8) The insurance companies will have to pay Rs. 1000/-, the amount of the difference and the amount payable by the insurance company as per the norms of the insurance scheme, totalling to Rs.
1000/- to be paid directly to the beneficiary's bank account by the concerned insurance company within 10 days. If the money is not returned to the farmers' account for various reasons, the amount should be transferred to the ESCROW account of the Commissioner of Agriculture (9) and distributed to the farmers.
10) The Commissionerate of Agriculture should take necessary action to ensure that the funds are paid to the eligible beneficiaries through the insurance company within the stipulated time.
The Commissioner (Agriculture) shall submit a quarterly progress report to the Government for effective implementation of the scheme.
