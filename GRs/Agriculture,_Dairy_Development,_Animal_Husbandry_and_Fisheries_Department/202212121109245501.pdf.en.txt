The proposal: 12.09.2022 and D. The Cabinet in its meeting held on 27.09.2022 was apprised of the various measures being taken to control the viral and communicable lumpy skin disease in the bovine livestock in the State. To take urgent measures to control the outbreak of lumpy skin disease in the livestock in the state, the Cabinet has directed the Collectors of all the districts to allocate Rs. 100.00 lakhs and thereafter an additional Rs. 100.00 lakhs out of which Rs. 200.00 lakh was directed to be made available immediately, in accordance with the decision taken by the Government of this department in agreement with the Planning and Finance Department. 16.09.2022 and D. 12.10.2022 has been issued. The Minister (Animal Husbandry) had earlier provided Rs. 200.00 lakh per district in addition to Rs. 100.00 lakh has been provided. The government was contemplating to take a decision in this regard.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 2. In view of the above facts, the following Government Decisions are being issued: 2.1 In order to control the viral and contagious lumpy skin disease in the bovine animals in the State immediately, Rs. 200.00 lakh per district in addition to Rs. 100.00 lakh should be immediately made available by the concerned District Collector to the concerned District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner.

An additional Rs. 2.2. 100.00 lakh should be spent for the purchase of equipment/ materials required for providing veterinary services to the veterinary patients in all State level and local level veterinary institutions in the respective districts. Also, equipment/ materials which are not usable should be identified and new equipment/ materials should be purchased from this fund. The Rs. 100.00 lakh should be used only for purchase of equipment/ material.
2.3 The concerned District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner shall prepare a consolidated plan for the purchase of equipment/ materials of all the state level and local level veterinary institutions in the district and get it approved by the Collector.
3. The said Government Decision is based on the informal reference of the Planning Department No. 63/1481, Dt. 28.11.2022 as well as, in Informal Reference No. 432/2022/ Expenditure-8D. 02.12.2022 and is being issued with their concurrence.
