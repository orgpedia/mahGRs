The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). Accordingly, the National Horticulture Mission being implemented in the state has been included in the Integrated Horticulture Development Mission. This mission has been included in the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Agriculture Promotion. The Integrated Horticulture Mission will focus on fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo.
It is a centrally sponsored scheme for the development of smart products. The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between the Centre and the States.
The main objectives of the scheme are to increase the productivity of crops covered under the mission, increase the fertility and productivity of farm land, create new employment opportunities and increase the overall income of farmers. Government's decision:
Administrative approval
The following administrative approval is being accorded for implementation of National Horticulture Mission in the State under Krishi Unnati Yojana- Integrated Horticulture Development Mission (MIDH) for the year 2018-19.
In the year 2018-19, a new annual action plan of Rs. 26333 lakh has been prepared for the implementation of National Horticulture Mission under Krishi Unnati Yojana Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State. Administrative approval is being given to implement the action plan with a balance fund of Rs. 6941.56 lakh with a total outlay of Rs. 33274.56 lakh. Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune will be the implementing agency for the National Horticulture Mission under Krishi Unnati Yojana-Integrated Horticulture Development Mission.
This campaign should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government and as per the directions given from time to time.
Out of the total fixed allocation sanctioned for 2018-19 (Rs. 26333 lakhs), 16.6 per cent should be spent for SCs and 8.6 per cent for STs and 30 per cent should be spent for women as per norms. The remaining amount will be spent in 2017-18 for this purpose.
4) The State Government will disburse funds through BDS system to Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board, Pune for implementation of the said scheme.
