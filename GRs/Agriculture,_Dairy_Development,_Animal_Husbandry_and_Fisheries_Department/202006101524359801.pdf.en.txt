Introduction: The Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana proposed by the Central Government is being implemented since 2016. The scheme is implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government to provide more stability to the farmers by providing insurance cover to them against weather hazards such as rain, temperature, relative humidity, hailstorm, gusty winds etc.
The meeting of the Cabinet Sub-Committee constituted for effective implementation of Restructured Weather Based Fruit Insurance Scheme under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana in the State was held on 6/5/2020. The topic of the meeting was No. 3. It has been decided to appoint a Committee of Experts under the Chairmanship of Commissioner (Agriculture) for assessment of climate risks under the restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme. The Government's decision is as follows:
Government Decision: Under the Restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme, a committee is being constituted to determine the climate risks as follows.
Commissioner (Agriculture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, State of Maharashtra, Pune Chairman 2. Representative of Ministry of Agriculture, Government of India Member 3. Director, Research (All Agricultural Universities) Member 4. Director National Fruit Research Centre (ICAR affiliated) Member 5. Director General, India Meteorological Department, Pune Member 6. Representative of MahaVedha Project Member 7. Representative of Agriculture Insurance Company of India Member 8. Special Invitee/ Expert Member with the approval of the Chairman 9. Director (Horticulture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Pune. The Committee shall consider and make recommendations on the following matters:
(1) To determine the climatic zone wise crop friendly districts for the fruit crops to be notified in mrig and ambia bahar.

(2) It is necessary to determine the climatic zone-wise, district-wise practical climatic hazards and parameters (including scientific explanation and research reference) for the fruit crops to be notified under the scheme so that there is no discrepancy between the loss of crops due to that particular climatic hazard and the compensation to be paid for it.
(iii) Determination of agricultural extension strategies of Resilience Strategies with respect to climatic factors affecting crop productivity and quality in general climate.
The Committee will submit its report to the Government by 15/7/2020.
