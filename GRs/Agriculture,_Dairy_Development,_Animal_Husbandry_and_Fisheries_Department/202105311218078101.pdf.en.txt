The proposal was under the consideration of the government to give administrative approval to implement the scheme in the state in 2021-22.
Government's decision:
The Integrated Pest and Disease Prevention and Advisory Scheme (CropSAP) scheme for management of pests and diseases in major crops like soybean, cotton, paddy, tur, gram, maize, sorghum and sugarcane as well as fruits mango, pomegranate, banana, mango, orange, chikoo, cashew, ladyfinger and tomato is being approved from 2021-22. In 2021-22, Rs. Administrative approval is being accorded for the programme with a corpus of Rs. 25.00 crore (Rupees Twenty Five Crore only).
For the year 2018-19, the Government vide Reference No. The Commissioner (Agriculture) should issue detailed guidelines for the implementation of the Pest and Disease Survey and Consultation Scheme in the year 2021-22 in accordance with the instructions issued under Government Decisions 1, 2, 4 and 6.
The scheme will be implemented in convergence with the centrally sponsored schemes of National Food Security Mission (NFSM), Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) and Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani Project (POCRA). For this purpose, priority should be given to spend the eligible items from the funds available under various Centrally Sponsored Schemes National Food Security Mission (NFSM), Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) and Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjeevani (POCRA) projects and thereafter, if the funds are short, the expenditure should be met from the provisions available under the said State Schemes as per requirement.
The CropSap scheme for crops such as soybean, cotton, paddy, tur, green maize, sorghum and sugarcane should be implemented with the participation of field staff of the Agriculture Department and for this purpose, mobile applications and computer systems developed by the National Informatics Centre, Pune should be used. For mango, pomegranate, banana, guava, orange, chikoo, cashew, ladyfinger and tomato crops, the scheme will be implemented with the participation of field staff of the Agriculture Department and computer system developed by National Institute of Integrated Pest Management Research, New Delhi will be used.

The Agricultural Assistant and the Agricultural Supervisor will be responsible for conducting separate meetings, meetings and demonstrations for providing necessary advice to the farmers at the village and taluka level of the concerned crop area through mobile SMS immediately after receiving guidance and advice from the experts through the system in a strictly regular manner, before the pest and disease reaches the economic loss level at various critical stages of the development of the concerned crop or near the economic loss level (during the yellow phase). Accordingly, it will be the responsibility of the Mandal Agriculture Officer and Taluka Agriculture Officer to ensure that the proceedings are regulated at the village level. It will be mandatory to review the regulations at the sub-divisional level every fortnight by the Sub-Divisional Agriculture Officer.
7. The District Superintending Agriculture Officer and the Joint Director of Divisional Agriculture should review the action taken as above. Also, the agricultural universities at the field level should provide guidance and advice on crop pests and diseases to the concerned experts within two days on a regular basis. This will also need to be reviewed by the heads of departments at the university level.
It will be mandatory to make available a separate updated list of villages before reaching the Economic Loss Level (ETL) or near the Economic Loss Level (when in the yellow phase) and the economic loss level, taluk-wise, on the project website in the open for the information of all concerned.
9. Provision available for Integrated Pest Disease Management under Food Security Mission Programme in villages with economic losses due to pest infestation on various crops Subsidy should be provided to farmers for emergency inputs like chemical pesticides as per scheme guidelines.
The Director (Horticulture) shall supervise and control the survey and advice of the fruit crops at the level of the Commissioner of Agriculture. The Director (Extension and Training) will monitor and monitor the survey and advice of other crops. The concerned director should regularly follow up and control the pest control by coordinating the existing schemes under his control. Also, during the entire season, both the directors should review the pest and disease situation at the field level in the respective crops every week and take necessary action and submit a detailed report to the government.
