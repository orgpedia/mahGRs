Government's decision:
As per the above decision dated 18/12/2000, revised Supplementary Grant Formulary has been prescribed for sanction of non-plan grants to Agricultural Universities in the State from the financial year 2000-2001. Accordingly, the four agricultural universities of the state and Maharashtra Council of Agricultural Education and Research, Pune have been given the following grants for the financial year 2018-19 under the head" 2415 Agricultural Research and Education," for the year 2018-19, for the year 2018-19, under the head" 01 Crop Conservation,"" 03 Animal Husbandry" and" 05 Fisheries," for the year 2018-19, under the sub-heading" 01 Pensionary Expenditure," and 36 for the matter of Subsidiary Grants (Salaries), 6216 under the head" Loans for Housing," 55 under the head" Loans and Advances," 100% of the budgetary Provisions for the year 2018-2019 and 31 for the matter of Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary), with a ceiling of 90% (excluding 70%) 268,59,66, 000/- (Rupees Two Hundred and Sixty Seven Crores Ninety-nine Lakhs Sixty-six Thousand only), subject to the following terms and conditions.

Terms and Conditions
(1) The funds released by the Agricultural Universities and the Council of Agriculture in respect of sub-headings/ groups/ schemes and subject-wise expenditure should be spent only for the salary and allowances and permissible matters of the posts/ matters in the respective scheme which have been approved by the Government to be continued as non-plan schemes. In case of non-availability of subsidy under a scheme due to vacancies or other reasons, the subsidy shall be adjusted in the next month's disbursement.
(2) If any of the sub-account, group, scheme and subject-wise disbursed funds are lying unutilized/ unutilized for any reason, the unutilized/ unutilized funds shall not be diverted or spent for other groups/ schemes/ activities without prior approval of the Government.
3. The Agriculture Council/ University should thoroughly examine whether the sub-title wise sanctioned schemes and the posts sanctioned under them have been properly grouped as per the revised pattern. No change in this classification should be made without the approval of the government.
4) The administrative department, while distributing and spending the funds, has to follow the guidelines and prevailing norms mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook and Financial Powers Rulebook as well as those issued earlier by the Planning and Finance Department, the delegation of financial powers and the guidelines issued by the Finance Department. April 2, 2018, the. July 27, 2018. December 5, 2018 and d. The terms and conditions of the circular dated 25th January and the instructions given from time to time should be strictly followed.
Further, the instructions issued and forthcoming from the Finance Department from time to time regarding the proceedings regarding the proposal to be purchased by the end of this financial year should be strictly followed.
5) The grants and revenue distributed in each group should be used to meet the expenditure on permissible items in that group and to carry out development works. Subsidies and revenue income of one group should not be diverted for schemes/ activities of another group. If more grants are allocated for a group scheme, the amount of the excess grant should be taken into account in the subsequent demand for funds.
The grant-in-aid shall be payable only for the salary and allowances of the posts sanctioned by the Government which have been filled up as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Agricultural University Act, 1983 and the Maharashtra Agricultural University Statutes, 1990 and the Government has approved the continuation of such posts at non-plan expenditure.
7) Separate accounts/ accounts shall be maintained for each sub-account/ group/ scheme and subject-wise.
(8) The schemes/ schemes under which the period of non-plan schemes has expired shall not be continued after the relevant period at the cost of the non-plan/ schemes without obtaining the approval of the Government.
The Maharashtra Council of Agricultural Education and Research shall submit to the Government, within a period of three months from the end of the relevant financial year, an assessment report of each financial year as to whether the grants-in-aid sanctioned to the agricultural universities are correct as per the grant formula, and how the expenditure is being made in accordance with the prescribed orders/ procedures. Also, the amount of unpaid funds of the previous financial year should be immediately reported to the government.

10) While approving some new schemes of the University, the Government has approved to make available/ fill up the posts under the said scheme in an adjusted manner. As a result of creation of new posts for the said new scheme, the funds released under this Order shall not be spent for payment of salary and allowances for the posts in the original schemes where the posts in the original schemes have been transferred/ made available to the new scheme by internal adjustments. Also, while seeking quarterly grant, the funds required for salary and allowances of such posts should not be demanded.
11) The government has given approval to agricultural universities to use their revenue income. The University shall take appropriate action in accordance with the order and procedure laid down. Action should also be taken to increase the revenue.
The grants sanctioned to the Universities should be spent within the prescribed limit after obtaining administrative and financial approval of the competent authority as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Agricultural University Act, 1983, the Maharashtra Agricultural University Statutes, 1990 and the Maharashtra Agricultural University Accounts Code, 1991 and in accordance with the prevailing Government Orders and prescribed procedures. It should not be spent only because there is a budgetary provision or a subsidy is disbursed.

Further, while submitting the demand for subsidy, the balance of subsidy disbursed earlier should be clubbed with the proposed demand and the actual amount of subsidy should be sought.
