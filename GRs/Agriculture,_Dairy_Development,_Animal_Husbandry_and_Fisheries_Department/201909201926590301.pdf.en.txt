Introduction: The main objectives of the National Food Security Mission are to increase the productivity of the crops covered under it, to increase the fertility and productivity of the farm land, to create employment opportunities and to increase the productivity of the farmers.
For the year 2019-20, the Government of India has sanctioned an action plan of Rs. 22019.8155 lakh for foodgrains crops (Rice, Wheat, Pulses, Coarse Cereals- Maize and Nutri Cereals) under National Food Security Mission for Maharashtra. It was the Central government. Approved vide letter dated 08/04/2019. Accordingly, the said Action Plan amounting to Rs. 22019.8155 lakhs (Central share Rs. 13211.8893 lakhs+ State share Rs. 8807.9262 lakhs) has been accorded administrative approval vide Government Resolution dated 03/06/2019.
In line with the recommendations of the National Council for Agriculture Research (NCERT) for the Rabi season 2018-19, the Government of India has directed to implement the TRFA-Pulses in Paddy Field Programme (TRFA-Pulses) from 2019-20 in the States of Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat and Karnataka.
Accordingly, the proposal submitted by the Agriculture Commissionerate was approved by the Central Government. 12/09/2019

Accordingly, the government was considering to implement a programme to increase the production of pulses in the paddy field. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government Decision: Under the National Food Security Mission Foodgrains Crops under Krishi Unnati Yojana for the year 2019-20 (Central share Rs. 418.500+ State share Rs. 279.000 lakh), the programme for increasing the production of pulses in the rice belt area with an outlay of Rs. 697.500 lakh is being approved subject to the following conditions.
The programme should be implemented as per the guidelines of National Food Security Mission.
The varieties used under this programme should be within 10 years.
iii. Demonstrations to be taken up under this programme by ICAR or Agriculture
should be recommended by the university (SAU).
The demonstration will be 100% geo-tagged and the responsibility of this will be of the District Superintending Agriculture Officer.
The component/ sub-component wise distribution of approved financial and physical targets of the programme is set out in Annexure A. The scheme will be implemented with 60:40 sharing between the Centre and the state.
The funds for the implementation of the Mission will be made available to the Commissioner (Agriculture) in the ratio of 60:40 from time to time through the Budget Distribution System. The funds shall be spent from the budgetary provision made for the year 2019-20 under the following head of Central and State share.
