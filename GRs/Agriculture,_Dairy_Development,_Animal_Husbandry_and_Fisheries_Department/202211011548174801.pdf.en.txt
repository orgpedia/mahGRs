The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided to implement the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIH) from 1st April, 2014 by converging all the schemes of National Horticulture Mission (NHM), Horticulture Mission for North Eastern and Himalayan States (HMNEH), National Horticulture Board (NHB), Coconut Development Board (CDB) and Central Institute of Horticulture (CIH). This scheme is for the development of fruits, vegetables, tubers, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo products.
The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
The Annual Action Plan amounting to Rs 199,33,97, 000/- for implementation of Integrated Horticulture Development Mission in the State during 2022-23 has been accorded administrative approval as per the Government Resolution No. The Central Government vide letter No.33 under reference has released Central Account Fund of Rs. 2,10,00, 000/- has been disbursed to the State Government. 1,40,00, 000/- amounting to Rs. 3,50,00, 000/- to the Managing Director, Maharashtra State Horticulture and Medicinal Plants Board and Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
Government's decision:
Krishi Unnati Yojana- Centre for Scheduled Castes to be set up for implementation of National Horticulture Mission in 2022-23 under Integrated Horticulture Development Mission (MIDH).
