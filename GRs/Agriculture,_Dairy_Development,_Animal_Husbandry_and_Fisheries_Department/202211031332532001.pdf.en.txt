The Central Government has accepted the recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of these restructured schemes into a Mission mode from 2014-15 onwards. The National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMOT) is being implemented.
The sub-mission is being implemented in accordance with the guidelines of the Central Government to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and within a stipulated time by enhancing and expanding the existing scheme in the form of a mission by incorporating new technologies in the production of quality or certified seeds to improve seed testing laboratories, assist seed production institutions to adopt new seed production technologies, etc. The scope of the sub-mission covers the entire seed chain, from producing centrifugal seeds for supplying certified seeds to farmers, to supporting creation of infrastructure for seed sector development, to depositing seeds in banks to meet seed requirement during natural calamities, etc. The sub-mission includes Seed Village Programme, strengthening of Taluka Seed Cultivation Areas, support to certified seed production.

The following conditions must be met when implementing the plan:
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines of the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central and State Governments along with the progress report as soon as possible by keeping the expenditure accounts in order.
The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central and State Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the unpaid amount at the beginning of the year and the income received by way of interest. So that the amount can be considered in a transparent manner and there will be no goodwill.
5) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
6) It should be ensured that the funds received for this scheme are spent for the category for which it is sanctioned.
