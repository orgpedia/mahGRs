Introduction: The coronavirus outbreak has had a major adverse impact on human health across the world. The government of India is taking preventive measures to control the spread of Corona virus. The entire country was put under lockdown from 24.3.2020. The state of Maharashtra. Lockdown has been imposed in the State since 19.3.2020. Due to the lockdown, the demand for packaged milk in the market has decreased and hotels, restaurants and confectionery manufacturing centers have closed down to a large extent. As a result, the daily sale of milk has come down by 17 lakh litres.
The government has proposed to convert 10 lakh litres of milk per day from the state's daily surplus into milk feed as the demand for milk in the state has come down drastically, posing a problem of surplus milk for dairy farmers. It was presented before the Cabinet meeting on April 24, 2020. After the approval of the Cabinet, the Government vide its decision dated 27/4/2020, has sanctioned a total of 4 crore litres of milk for the period from 6th April, 2020 to 31st May, 2020 and an amount of Rs. An amount of Rs 127 crore has been sanctioned for the project.
However, since the lockdown has not been fully relaxed beyond 31st May, 2020, the scheme has been extended till 30th June, 2020 vide Contextual Government Resolution No.
In the meeting held on 8th July, 2020, a proposal was submitted by the Department to extend the scheme till 31st July, 2020 as the situation of milk business in the State is still not improved due to lockdown. After the Cabinet approved the proposal, the State government had come out with a decision.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: SAMRICH-2020/ PR No.1/ PADUM-8 GOVERNMENT DECISION:
The Government hereby approves extension of the scheme announced under Reference No. 1 and 2 up to 31st July, 2020. In view of the volume of milk accepted per day under the scheme, the permissible limit of milk accepted for the period from 1st July, 2020 to 31st July, 2020 is being restricted to 1.60 crore litres, taking into account the date of extension of the scheme.
Under this scheme, Rs. 4.40 crore has been allocated for procurement of milk. An amount of Rs. 139.08 crore has been sanctioned for 1.60 crore litres of milk to be procured during the extension period from 1st July, 2020 to 31st July, 2020. A sum of Rs. 51.22 crore is being sanctioned as contingency fund.
The remaining terms and conditions for the scheme shall continue to be as mentioned in the Government Resolution No. 2 in this context.
