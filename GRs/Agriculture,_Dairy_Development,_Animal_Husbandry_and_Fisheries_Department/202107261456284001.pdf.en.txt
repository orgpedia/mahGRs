2. Scope of the Scheme Under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, more crop area per drop should be implemented in all the 34 districts of the state.
3.The Beneficiaries:
Farmers who meet the following conditions will be eligible to take advantage of this scheme:
3.1. Clauses 7/12 and 8-A of the title deed should be in the name of farmers.
3.2. The farmer availing the micro irrigation component should have access to irrigation facilities and should be registered on the 7/12 line. 7/12 If there is no record of irrigation facility on the land, self-declaration should be taken from the farmer about the well or farm. If there is a provision of irrigation through other means (dams/ canals), the certificate of the officer of the concerned (water conservation/ water resources) department should be taken from the farmer.
3.3. If community irrigation facility is available, then there should be agreement of other stakeholders.
3.4. There should be a permanent electrical connection for the electric pump. The farmers should take a copy of the electricity bill of the past near future along with the proposal. If there is a solar pump, the letter of installation of the solar pump and the documents related to the solar pump should be taken along with the proposal.
3.5. The farmer must have an Aadhaar card.
3.6 A beneficiary is eligible to participate in the scheme but does not have an Aadhaar number, such beneficiary should be given the benefit of the scheme by submitting proof of Aadhaar enrolment receipt/ Voter ID card/ PAN card/ Passport/ Ration card/ Identity card if he is a government employee/ Bank or Post Office Passbook/ MGNREGA Card/ Kisan Photo till the receipt of Aadhaar number.
The subsidy amount should be directly credited to the Aadhaar linked bank account of the farmers through Maha-DBT portal.

3.8 Benefit should be provided to eligible farmers within the limit of 5 hectares. Once the area is covered, no further subsidy will be admissible for the same area for a period of seven years. A written statement should be taken from the farmer that he has not availed the benefit of subsidy on this area within a period of seven years. Similarly, it should be actually verified from the records in the office of the Taluk Agriculture Officer. The Agriculture Assistant/ Agriculture Supervisor/ Taluk Agriculture Officer will be responsible for ensuring this.
Subsidy due: As per the guidelines of the Central Government, the subsidy payable to the beneficiaries under this scheme will be as follows: Small and marginal farmers 55% Other farmers 45%
Implementation of the plan:
5.1. The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government on Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana- Per Drop More Crop Component.
5.2. Applications under PMKSY per Drop More Crop component should be received from farmers on Maha-DBT portal for the year 2021-22.
5.3 With a view to publicize the scheme, the scheme should be given wide publicity by receiving applications from the beneficiaries under the scheme through All India Radio/ Doordarshan and Lokrajya Anka, as well as through SMS, on the notice boards of Gram Panchayats, Agriculture Supervisor, Mandal Agriculture Officer, Sub-Divisional Agriculture Officer, Taluk Agriculture Officer, District Superintending Agriculture Officer, Panchayat Samiti, Tehsil and Zilla Parishad offices by putting up brochures on the facade. The information sheet should clearly mention the acceptance of the application form online on the Maha-DBT portal, the terms and conditions required to take advantage of the scheme, the documents to be submitted along with the application by the applicant.
5.4. The Commissionerate of Agriculture shall set targets for the districts within the limits of the approved programme of administrative approval.

5.5. The subsidy will not be admissible to a farmer who has set up a micro-irrigation unit without prior approval and has submitted a proposal for subsidy.
5.6 The district level implementation mechanism should ensure that the selection of beneficiaries and disbursement of grants is done in a transparent and expeditious manner and a uniform approach should be adopted at all places.
5.7. The Taluk Agriculture Officer/ District Superintending Agriculture Officer should make extensive efforts to ensure that the applications are filled online by the beneficiaries within the prescribed time frame, as per the financial/ physical targets fixed for the Taluk/ District.
6. Guidelines for implementation of the scheme The following instructions should be strictly followed while implementing the Centrally Sponsored Micro Irrigation Scheme under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana.
6.1 The information of the micro-irrigation companies from which micro-irrigation materials are to be installed by the farmers for availing the subsidy of micro-irrigation sets should be made public.
6.2. The implementation of the scheme should be in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana- Per Drop More Crop Component.
6.3. Site and crop-wise specific technical irrigation systems should be adopted so that farmers are not burdened. Cost Effective Micro Irrigation System (CAMIS) is an irrigation system that takes into account the type of crops and geographical location.
6.4 The subsidy amount should be paid only after verifying that the equipment is installed by the farmers as per the provisions and standards of the guidelines.
6.5 The detailed quarterly progress report of the scheme should be sent to the Department of Agriculture and Cooperation, Ministry of Agriculture, Government of India as well as to the State Government within a period of three months after the end of the financial year.
6.6. The approved Annual Action Plan under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) should be published on the website of Centrally Sponsored Micro Irrigation Scheme. Similarly, every month, the physical and financial progress of the scheme will be updated.
