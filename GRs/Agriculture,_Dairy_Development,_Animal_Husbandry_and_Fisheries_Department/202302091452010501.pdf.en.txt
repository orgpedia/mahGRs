The Proposal:
Agriculture and animal husbandry are an integral part of rural life and contribute significantly to the rural economy. The objective of the Department of Animal Husbandry is to generate employment in the rural economy through animal husbandry and to raise the economic status of the livestock farmers. The activities of the Animal Husbandry Department are to protect the livestock from diseases and to increase milk production, egg production, meat and wool production, to provide means of income to rural farmers and needy beneficiaries by distributing milch animals, goat groups, poultry birds, to increase productivity by effective implementation of animal husbandry policy, to provide information to the livestock farmers through publicity and publicity activities, to produce vaccines for livestock, to increase the availability of fodder and animal feed for livestock. In pursuance of the above objectives and functions, the Animal Husbandry Department has a total of 4,850 Veterinary Institutions in the service sector, out of which the following important institutions are functioning as per the specific mission
Department of Disease Investigation, Pune. (Bharat Ratna Atal Bihari Vajpayee State Level Institute) 2) Zonal Disease Investigation Laboratory 3) Veterinary Biomaterial Manufacturing Institute 4) Excessive Sand Laboratory 5) Animal Feed Analysis Laboratory 6) Quality Control Laboratory

The strength of this department is the trained manpower providing services through the above institution.
While providing services to the livestock farmers through trained manpower, their knowledge and experience should be leveraged by the department through the concerned veterinary institution.
In order to achieve the objectives for which the said institutions/ offices in the field machinery of the Department of Animal Husbandry are working, measures need to be taken to ensure that the objectives are met with maximum quality and full efficiency and in turn, the services and facilities of the Department are better available to the farmers and the cattle rearers/ beneficiaries and the image of the Department of Animal Husbandry is better among the people. In this context, No. According to the proposal submitted by the Commissioner Animal Husbandry under section 2 and 3, the matter of determining the policy for posting of officers in accordance with the specifications of the technical post in various institutions of the Department of Animal Husbandry was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: With a view to ensure quality and more efficient functioning of various technical posts in the Veterinary Institutions/ Laboratories functioning under the Department of Animal Husbandry at the State level and for fulfilling specific functions and to utilize the knowledge of the officer having expertise in various branches of Veterinary Science for the functioning of the concerned institution, the Government is approving the posting of officers in the cadre of Deputy Commissioner Animal Husbandry (Group-A), Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry (Group-A) and Livestock Development Officer (Group-A) sanctioned in the Veterinary Institutions mentioned in paragraph 2, on priority basis as per special educational qualification.
The said approval is being granted subject to the provisions of the Transfer of Posts Act and the prevailing Government Policy.
