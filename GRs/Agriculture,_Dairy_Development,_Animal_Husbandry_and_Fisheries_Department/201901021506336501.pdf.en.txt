Department of Agriculture, Animal Husbandry, Dairy Development and Fisheries The following paragraphs of the Government Resolution dated 28.12.2018 at para 4 are being omitted.
Detailed guidelines for successful implementation of the project should be issued by the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State, Pune at his level to all concerned immediately.
Further, under the Government Resolution No. 4 (b) on Animal Inducation, the following paragraphs are being inserted after the first paragraph.
" While giving benefit under this component, priority should be given to the beneficiary who has a milch animal. Also, 30 per cent of the beneficiaries should be women and 3 per cent of the beneficiaries with disabilities.
Only one person from a family will be given the benefit under this scheme. While purchasing milch indigenous cows and buffaloes, the breed of indigenous cow/ buffalo should be selected as per the consent of the cattle breeder, subject to the prevailing Breeding Policy of the Government.
The milch animals allocated under this scheme should be insured for three years from the Livestock Insurance Scheme under the Centrally Sponsored National Livestock Mission. Also, if the animal allotted under the scheme dies, then another milch animal should be purchased and provided to the beneficiary from the insurance money and with the consent of the Animal Husbandry Department. The purchased milch animals should be registered on the system of INAF by tagging them under the centrally sponsored National Animal Productivity Mission (NAM) Pashu Sanjeevani Yojana. The health and breeding facilities should be provided to the milch animals by the Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Veterinary Development Officer/ Assistant Livestock Development Officer/ Livestock Supervisor of the District Veterinary Hospital/ Taluk Small Veterinary Hospital in the jurisdiction of the Veterinary Clinic in which the milch animals are distributed. Also, 100 per cent verification of the milch animals allotted every quarter should be done by the concerned officers/ employees at the doorsteps of the beneficiaries/ cattle rearers and the report should be submitted to the Project Director, Vidarbha Marathwada Special Dairy Project, Nagpur through the Livestock Development Officer (Extension). The beneficiary of this scheme will be required to do this business for at least 3 years. If it is found that the beneficiaries have misappropriated the government subsidy given under the scheme, an amount along with the interest of the subsidy should be recovered from the beneficiary by revenue mechanism.
