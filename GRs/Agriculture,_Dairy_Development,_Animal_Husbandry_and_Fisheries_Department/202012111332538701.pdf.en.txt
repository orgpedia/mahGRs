Introduction: The recommendations of the B. K. Chaturvedi Committee appointed by the Central Planning Commission have been accepted by the Central Government. Accordingly, all Centrally Sponsored Schemes have been restructured into 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The Government of India has decided to integrate some of the restructured schemes of 2014-15 into a Mission mode.
Under the National Mission on Agriculture Extension and Technology (NMOET), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, Seed Planting and Material Sub-Mission (SMSP) is being implemented.
This sub-mission is being implemented as per the guidelines of the Central Government with a view to improve the availability of quality seeds to the farmers at reasonable prices and in a time-bound manner by augmenting and expanding the existing scheme in the form of a mission to include new technologies in the production of quality or certified seeds for improving seed testing laboratories, assisting seed production institutions to adopt new seed production technologies, etc. Seeds from centrally produced seeds to supply certified seeds to farmers under the ambit of this sub-mission.

The following terms and conditions should be observed when implementing the scheme.
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central and State Governments along with the progress report as soon as possible by keeping the accounts of expenditure in order.
The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central and State Government by the 5th of every month.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
5) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
6) It should be ensured that the funds received for this scheme are spent for the same category for which it has been sanctioned.
The Commissioner (Agriculture), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State, Pune is being declared as the Controlling Officer of the Scheme and Assistant Director, Accounts 1 in his office as Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
4. Subject to all financial laws/ tender rules and regulations/ procedure/ limits of financial powers/ P.W.D. Manual, in accordance with the procedure prescribed while spending the said funds. V. C.
As per the principle/ direction of the CAG/ prevailing government decisions/ rules/ circulars/ provisions, the expenditure should be processed by the implementing agency as per the budget and treasury rules. It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Regional Office to ensure that no Government rule/ authority is violated under any circumstances.
