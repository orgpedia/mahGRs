Preamble: In order to reduce the frustration of non-teaching staff in agricultural universities and affiliated colleges due to lack of promotion opportunities, a time-bound promotion scheme to provide the concerned post holders with a pay scale at the top of their promotion chain after 12 years of regular service has been approved by the Department of Agriculture and Padum, Government Decision, D. It came into force on September 21, 1999.
Department of Finance, Government Decisions. As of July 20, 2001, the time-bound promotion scheme was discontinued and replaced by the In-Service Assured Progress Scheme (a benefit scheme).
The State Pay Revision Committee, 2008, which was appointed to make recommendations regarding the revision of pay scales of State Government and other employees, in its report had also recommended the implementation of the Assured Progress Scheme under the revised service for State Government employees.
Considering those recommendations, the State Government employees were given the" Assured Progress Scheme under the improved services" by the Finance Department, Government Decision, D. It came into effect from April 1, 2010.

Government Decision: In order to mitigate the frustration of non-teaching staff in agricultural universities and affiliated colleges due to non-availability of promotion opportunities, the Department of Higher and Technical Education is being approved to implement a 2-benefit" Assured Progress under Improved Services Scheme" by revising the time-bound promotion scheme of providing pay scale to the concerned post holders at the top of their promotion chain after 12 years of regular service.
2. Implementation of" Assured Progress Scheme under Improved Services" for non-teaching staff of Agricultural Universities and affiliated colleges. On April 1, 2010, the July 5, 2010 and d. All the provisions of September 6, 2014 are being implemented with retrospective effect.
