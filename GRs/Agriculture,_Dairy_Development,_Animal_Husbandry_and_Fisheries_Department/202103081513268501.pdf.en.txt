Introduction: The Department of Agriculture implements various schemes related to farmers.
With a view to implement the schemes in a transparent manner and to bring uniformity in the implementation of all the schemes in the state, the Department of Agriculture has now developed an integrated computer system on Maha-DBT portal with the view to provide the benefit of all the schemes under the" Shetkari Yojana" to the farmers through a" single application" from application to actual benefit. The eligible farmers will be selected through computer based selection process under the scheme through which these things can be given. 13 major schemes of the Agriculture Department will be implemented through the Maha-DBT portal.
The General Administration Department (GAD) has been entrusted with the task of providing grants/ benefits to eligible beneficiaries. The modalities for disbursement of funds through Direct Benefit Transfer (MahaDBT) system have been detailed under the Government decision dated 12th October, 2018. It is mandatory for every scheme to open a separate bank account linked to the PFMS system to draw funds from the treasury through the Maha-DBT system. While implementing the schemes through Maha-DBT system, one scheme one DDO is adopted.

At present, the Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana (within and outside the area) is a district-level scheme for which funds from the Tribal Development Department are distributed to the respective Zila Parishads through the Collector and the Agriculture Department machinery under the Zila Parishad implements the scheme. There is a separate account for each district. At the district level, there is a separate Drawing and Disbursing Officer (DDO) and the district gets a separate payment. Accordingly, a proposal to open a separate bank account linked to the PFMS system for each district for the implementation of the Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana (within and outside the area) scheme was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: The Government has approved the opening of a separate bank account linked to PFMS system for each district for the Birsa Munda Krishi Kranti Yojana (In-Space and Out-Space) scheme being implemented through Maha-DBT system.
The amount of PFMS account to be opened in the bank for this scheme will be used only for the implementation of this scheme.
The account for this scheme should be opened in the nationalized bank approved by the Finance Department.
4. The grants received for the scheme should be spent as per the decision of the Government of the administrative approval of the scheme within the prescribed period and as per the extension with the approval of the Government. In cases where the extension has not been granted, the balance in the said bank account should be adjusted with the government.
Monthly/ quarterly reconciliation of bank account transactions and audit should be done.
6. In case of closure of the bank account, the balance should be reconciled with the balance and the balance should be deposited immediately under the appropriate government account head.
No financial transactions other than PFMS should be done from the bank account. If this happens, it will be considered a financial irregularity.
Detailed guidelines regarding the District Level Drawing and Disbursing Officer, Controller of Agriculture, Name/ Designation of the officer in whose name the bank account is to be opened, Maker and Checker on PFMS system etc. for the scheme should be issued separately by the Commissioner (Agriculture).
This decision has been taken by the Tribal Development Department and Finance Department vide their No. 32/ 21/ COPR-5, No. Issued pursuant to approval dated February 16, 2021.
