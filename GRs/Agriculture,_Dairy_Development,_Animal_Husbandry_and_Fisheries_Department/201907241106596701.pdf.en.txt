Introduction: The Central Government has started the distribution of soil health cards under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture since 2014-15. In this, all the villages of the state will be selected in a phased manner and land health cards will be distributed to all the farmers of the state. Through soil health cards, information is being given to the farmers about the chemical properties of their soil, the level of major food elements and the state of deficiency of micro elements.
The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16. The Central Government No. 4 is here. As per the letter dated 27th June, 2018, for the implementation of Mrida Arogya Patra distribution programme in the State during 2018-19, the Central share of Rs. 3120.42 lakhs and 40% of State share of Rs. 2080.03 Lakhs out of which Rs. A sum of Rs. 5200.45 lakh has been sanctioned for the programme. Accordingly, administrative approval has been accorded to implement the scheme vide Government Resolution dated 24th August, 2018. Central share of Rs. 3103.81 lakh is available as on 21.02.2019 from Government of India for implementation of the scheme during 2018-19

Government's decision:
Under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA), the central share of Rs. 2000.00 lakhs and State share of Rs. 1333.00 lakhs out of which Rs. 3333.00 lakhs is being disbursed.
The expenditure for this scheme shall be met from the grants sanctioned for the year 2019-20 and shall be spent under the following heads:
Demand Number: D-3 2402, Soil and Water Conservation (00) (101) Soil Survey and Testing (00) (16) Krishi Unnati Yojana- Soil Health Card (Centrally Sponsored Scheme) (Central Share 60%) (2402 O 041) (General) Under Scheme 33- Financial Assistance.

The following terms and conditions must be met when implementing the plan.
(1) The scheme shall be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions specified in the letter of disbursement of funds of the Central Government.
2) The audited report and utilization certificate of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central Government along with the progress report as soon as possible.
(3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central Government by the 5th of every month. The report will be submitted to the state government.
