MA/ 26386/2022, Dy. 28/07/2022 Preamble: In order to ensure financial stability of farmers by providing insurance cover to their fruit crops against climatic hazards, a re-designed weather based Fruit Insurance Scheme is being implemented in the State on priority. Due to various climatic hazards there is adverse effect on the crop productivity and there is reduction in the production to a large extent. The farmers are facing financial crisis as they are not getting proper price for their produce. Considering all these factors, as a measure to provide crop compensation to the farmers, the restructured weather-based crop insurance scheme has been implemented in the state for the three years 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24 for 9 crops namely orange, mango, cashew, pomegranate, mango, banana, grape (A& B), strawberry and papaya (Ambia Bahar) on a pilot basis in 30 districts by the Revenue Board.
Re-designed Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme Ambia Bahar under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) to be implemented in 2021-22 Administrative approval by a decision of the Government in 1

Government Decision: In view of the recommendation made by the Commissionerate of Agriculture under the restructured Climate Based Fruit Insurance Scheme Ambia Bahar 2021-22, the State share insurance premium subsidy of Rs. 16,87,78, 522/- is being approved by the Government to be made available to the Agriculture Insurance Company of India for payment to the insurance companies as per the guidelines of the scheme. This amount is being disbursed for the Aambia Bahar season 2021-22 and will not be allowed for other seasons.
