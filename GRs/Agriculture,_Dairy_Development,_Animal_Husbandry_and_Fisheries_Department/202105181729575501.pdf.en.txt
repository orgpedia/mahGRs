Preamble: Regarding the development of Animal Husbandry and Dairy Development Department on BOT basis. The Minister for Animal Husbandry and Dairying, Shri. The meeting was held on February 10, 2021.
The Dairy Development Department and the Animal Husbandry Department have dairy farms, cold storage centres, offices, clinics and other buildings in various parts of the state. Some of these lands are at strategic locations in the city and if these lands are developed on BOT basis, the offices of the department will be redeveloped and equipped offices and clinics will be available free of cost without any financial provision to the department. Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a committee to formulate a policy on how to use the land on the basis of Build, Use and Transfer (BOT) so that the land held by the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying Development could be optimally used and funds could be raised for various schemes.

Government's decision:
In order to ensure optimum utilization of land available with the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying Development and to mobilize funds for various schemes, it is being approved to constitute a committee as follows to formulate a policy on how land can be utilised on Build, Use and Transfer (BOT) basis.
The committee:
Madhav Kale, General Manager Planning& Chairman, Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation Shri Rajendra Javanjal, Deputy Secretary, Public Works Department, Ministry of Road Transport& Highways, Mumbai Member Secretary 3. Deputy Secretary (Dairy), Ministry of Road Transport& Highways, Mumbai Member 4. Divisional Co-ordinator of the Department concerned. Deputy Engineer, Member of the Commission
The committee will have to submit its report to the state government in a month's time.
Based on the recommendations of the committee, a proposal should be submitted to the department to start the project on a pilot basis in Beed, Ahmednagar and Nashik.
