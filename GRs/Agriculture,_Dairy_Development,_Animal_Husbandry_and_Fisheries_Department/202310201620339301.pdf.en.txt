The Proposal:
In the State, Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PADY) is being implemented in the State with Central share of 60% and State share of 40% under National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSC) since 2016-17. The scheme includes promotion of commercial organic farming and use of organic manure, organic certification, production of organic manure on farmers' field, making available chemical pesticides residue-free farm produce to the consumers, development of organic farming villages as well as training and demonstration based on agriculture, adopting low-cost technologies and increasing the economic income of farmers by reducing the cost of production. A total of 2128 groups have been formed under this scheme.
The scheme is being implemented by forming 850 new groups in 2023-24. The scheme is group-based and groups are being formed in a group of 20 hectares. Under the scheme, the benefit is given to the selected group/ beneficiary once for 3 years.
Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana is being implemented under the National Agriculture Development Scheme from 2022-23. Accordingly, the Government was considering to give administrative approval to the Annual Action Plan for implementation of Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) for 850 groups in the current financial year 2023-24.

4) The following conditions must be met when implementing the plan:
1) The scheme should be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions of the release of funds by the Central Government.
2) The audit report and utilization certificates of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central and State Governments along with the progress report as soon as possible.
Physical and financial progress reports on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central and State Governments by the 10th of every month.
3) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly state the amount of interest and interest earned at the beginning of the year. So that the amounts can be considered in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
4) It should be ensured that no programme beyond the approved programme is implemented under any circumstances.
(5) In the exercise of these funds, all financial laws/ tender rules and regulations/ procedures/ limits of financial powers/ P.W.D./ Manuals shall be followed as per the prescribed procedures. As per the V.C. Principle/ CAG's directions/ prevailing Government decisions/ rules/ circulars/ provisions, the expenditure should be processed by the implementing agencies as per the Budget and Treasury Rules. It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Regional Office to ensure that no Government rule/ authority is violated under any circumstances.
6) All funds disbursed under Government decision from time to time shall be mandatorily routed through SNA system in PFMS.
7) It should be ensured that the category-wise programme for the scheme is approved and the expenditure is made for the beneficiaries of the respective category. Also, it should be ensured that the state share is spent in accordance with the Centre's share.
