The Prevention and Control of Infectious and Contagious Diseases in Animals Act, 2009 Announcement made on June 4, 2013
INTRODUCTION: Infectious disease outbreaks occur annually in domestic animals as well as poultry birds and other livestock. The disease is most common during the rainy season. Pre-monsoon preventive vaccination is carried out every year by the Animal Husbandry Department in the state of Maharashtra. Detailed guidelines are also circulated at the level of Animal Husbandry Commissioner to the officers/ staff at the field level to take precautions and prevent the outbreak of epidemics.
The Prevention and Control of Infectious and Communicable Diseases in Animals Act, 2009 is being implemented in the state. In line with that. The notification has been published in the Government Gazette dated 4th June, 2013.
In view of the various epidemics occurring in the animal and poultry birds every year, the government was considering to streamline the epidemic control by forming state-level, division-level and district-level task forces to take action on the prevention of such epidemics, timely treatment of animal-poultry birds infected with this disease, keeping the infected animal-poultry birds away from the herd to prevent the spread of the disease from them, preventive vaccination among other animals, etc. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decision:
Government Decision: In order to minimize the financial loss of farmers, ranchers and poultry traders by managing the various epidemics found in animal-poultry birds in the state in a scientific way, the government is approving the formation of a task force as follows.

The main objectives of the task force are to control the epidemic of various diseases found in animal and poultry birds in the state through scientific methods, to suggest appropriate treatment for livestock and poultry birds infected with such diseases, to recommend for the publication of notification in the government gazette as needed regarding the outbreak of diseases, to comply with the Prevention and Control of Infectious and Communicable Diseases in Animals Act, 2009 in the state, to manage the vaccine and preventive vaccination during the epidemic outbreak, to ensure the availability of necessary medicines during the outbreak of the disease, to implement all the above mentioned matters with the help of the local administration and to submit reports to the government from time to time. Also, the task force will be responsible for taking appropriate measures by sending special teams to the areas where the disease has occurred/ is occurring.
The Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State, Pune shall circulate detailed guidelines for the functioning of the Task Force as required. Also, meetings should be organized and necessary instructions should be given to the task force according to the situation. Orders shall be issued in their name and designation to constitute a Task Force consisting of the Chairman and members as above. The mobile and e-mail IDs of the Chairman and Member Secretary of the Task Force should be mentioned without fail in this order. At the district and district levels,

They will work under the control of the State Level Task Force and the decisions taken by the State Level Task Force will be final.
The Member Secretary of the State Level Task Force will be responsible for ensuring that all the above Task Forces meet on a regular basis every month. Also, in case of an epidemic, meetings should be held every week to take appropriate measures and submit written reports to the government from time to time.
The Departmental and District Level Task Force should immediately contact the State Level Task Force if they have any difficulty in carrying out the assigned tasks or need guidance on any matter.
Duties of the Task Force at State, Division, District level: 1. To check epidemics in poultry birds. For this, necessary mechanism to receive monthly reports on the epidemic from the officials in their jurisdiction at the state/ department/ district level should be prepared and adopted (the report should be submitted every week in case of an epidemic).
2. Use of necessary mechanisms by the task force at all the three levels to get real time information about epidemics in animal-poultry birds.
The following offices will be responsible for providing both the above-mentioned real-time data and monthly/ weekly reports, analyzing the reports of epidemiological samples, as well as taking measures to collect disease samples from all districts.

The task force should ensure that the necessary surveillance is done by collecting information about the epidemic diseases and registering it on the National Animal Disease Reporting System (NADRS) and other necessary systems of the Central Government with the help of the Disease Investigation Department.
5.To identify the source of the disease by conducting scientific analysis of the information received on epidemics with the help of the Central Government, Veterinary Colleges and the Disease Investigation Department and to submit a report based on it.
6. Ensure dissemination or dissemination of necessary advisories in relation to control of epidemic disease and necessary advisories in the affected/ suspected area. Conducting awareness campaigns in this area and publishing press notes from time to time.
7. If a national/ state action plan for the disease is available in the outbreak area, take action accordingly and if not available, take action to contain the outbreak with the help of the disease investigation department.

9. In case of outbreak of animal-transmitted human disease, immediately inform the Public Health Department and take necessary action with them regarding the outbreak of disease.

The competent authority concerned shall be bound to take prompt action in respect of various aspects to be recommended by the Task Force, such as availability and implementation of required manpower, preventive vaccines and drugs. Ensuring availability of adequate stocks of essential medicines during the period of outbreak and procuring urgent medicines, if required.
To inform the private sectors of egg and poultry birds, private dairy farms, private goat and sheep farm operators and other private animal/ bird farms in the state to take all necessary precautions to prevent any kind of disease on their premises and to issue appropriate orders in this regard. The Commissioner Animal Husbandry, Pune should immediately issue necessary detailed instructions in this regard to fix responsibility and take necessary legal action if the disease spreads in an area due to lack of precautions.
