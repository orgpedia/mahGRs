The state government has decided to give directions to cooperative and private milk unions to fix minimum cow milk price.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Agriculture, Animal Husbandry, Dairy Development and Fisheries, Government Resolution No. 2023/ 63/ 8
Madam Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk
Ministry Extension, Mumbai 400 032
Date: July 14, 2023
Read:- Government Decision dated 27.6.2023.
Preamble: Discussion with the representatives of various milk producers' organizations and animal feed companies on the issue of milk prices in the state. on 22.6.2023. The meeting was held under the chairmanship of the Minister (Animal Husbandry and Dairy Development). During the meeting, representatives of milk producers' associations pointed out that there has been a huge fall in the price of milk and this has caused dissatisfaction among the milk producing farmers. Milk collection in the state is mainly done by private and cooperative milk societies. Due to less production of milk, farmers are getting good price for their produce. However, due to the increase in milk production during the milking season, the milk of farmers is accepted at a lower rate by various private and cooperative milk unions. As a result, farmers are suffering.
In view of all the above, a committee has been constituted under the decision of the government to fix the milk price in order to ensure fair price for the milk of the farmers after taking into account the operating cost of the private/ cooperative milk unions as well as the production cost of the milk producing farmers. The committee will have representatives from the private and cooperative sectors. The committee In a meeting held on 3.7.2023, the State Government has unanimously decided to fix the minimum procurement price for the 3.5/8.5 quality milk to be procured by the cooperative and private milk plants in the State. Considering the recommendations made by the committee to the government, it was under consideration to approve the minimum procurement price for cow milk (3.5/8.5) in the state.
Government Decision: As recommended by the above committee, for cow milk of the quality 3.5/8.5 to be procured by cooperative and private milk plants in the state, a minimum of Rs. 34 per litre is being approved by the government. Also, the price of milk will be paid to the farmers without any deduction.
These rates will be effective from 21.07.2023.

However, in case of exceptional circumstances, the Committee will be required to recommend the milk price to the Government even before 3 months as per the directions received from the Government.
4. These directions are being issued in public interest so that the procurement price of milk is reduced and the milk producing farmers do not suffer financial loss.
