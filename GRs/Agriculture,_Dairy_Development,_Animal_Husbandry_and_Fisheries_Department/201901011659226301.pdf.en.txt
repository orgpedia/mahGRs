The Proposal:
The main objectives of the National Food Security Mission are to increase the production and productivity of the crops covered under it, increase the fertility and productivity of the farm land, create employment opportunities and increase the productivity of the farmers.

For the year 2018-19, the Government of India has approved an Action Plan for Maharashtra under National Food Security Mission for food crops (Rice, Wheat, Pulses and Nutri Cereals) for an amount of Rs. 23989.685 lakh. It was the Central government. Approved vide letter dated 01/05/2018.
In the current year, the Central Government has sanctioned an additional programme of Rs. 1200.00 lakh for pulses crops under this Mission. Accordingly, Rs. 1200.00 Lakhs (Central Share Rs. 720.00 Lakhs+ State Share of Rs. 480.00 lakhs) has been accorded administrative approval vide Government Resolution dated 31/10/2018. Accordingly, the Central Government has released Rs. 73.44 lakh as Central share for pulses crops under the Mission for Scheduled Castes category out of the enhanced programme and the State Government has decided to release Rs. 48.96 lakh as State share, totalling Rs. 122.40 lakh in the wake of Rabi season.
Government Decision: Under the Krishi Unnati Yojana-National Food Security Mission for 2018-19, a total amount of Rs. 122.44 lakh (Rs.1 crore, 22 lakh forty four thousand only) is being provided to the Commissioner (Agriculture), Pune, under this Government decision, crop-wise and category-wise, through the Budget Distribution System.
