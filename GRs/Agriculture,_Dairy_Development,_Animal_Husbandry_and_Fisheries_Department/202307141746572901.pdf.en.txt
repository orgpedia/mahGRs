Introduction: The quality of animal feed is very important in the animal husbandry and milk production business.
The quantity and quality of milk produced, as well as the health and reproductive capacity of the animal, depend on the quality of the animal feed. The quality of animal feed is an important factor affecting the economy of animal husbandry and dairying. Food Safety and Standards Authority of India 17.12.2019 and D. As per the directions in the letter dated 27.01.2020, the production and sale of animal feed is mandated to be ISI Mark (as per BIS standard). Accordingly, the issue of issuing a government decision was under consideration. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decision:
Food Safety and Standards Authority of India 17.12.2019 and D. As per the directions in the letter dated 27.01.2020, it is mandatory to produce and sell ISI mark (as per BIS standard) cattle feed in the state.
Animal feed manufacturing units licensed by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) shall also produce animal feed as per the standards of BIS and the quantity of animal feed component (e.g. Crude protein, Crude fat, Crude fiber, Calcium, Phosphorus, Ash, Moisture, etcetera) are bound to be highlighted. Similarly, the following points should also be prominently mentioned on the packing bag along with the standard of the food item.
(a) Name and address of the manufacturing unit (b) Manufacturer's license number (e) Date and batch number of the animal feed product (e) Last date of use (best before use) (e) Net weight (o) Name and address of the marketing company
Animal feed manufacturers in the state are required to comply with the norms of the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) under the provisions of the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, the Indian Standards Act and the Consumer Protection Act, 1986. Animal feed manufacturers who are not registered under BIS.

The manufacturers shall take action for obtaining license from the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) within a period of one month from the date of issue of the said Government Order. Otherwise, such animal feed manufacturers will not be able to sell the animal feed produced by them in the market.
Every Animal Feed Producers Organisation shall conduct regular testing of all types of animal feed produced in the presence of the Assistant Commissioner Animal Husbandry, who is in charge of the Taluk in which the said production unit is functioning, by taking random samples of such animal feed. According to the inspection report, the reports of the producers who do not produce animal feed according to the standard should be submitted to the mechanism authorized by the Department of Animal Husbandry by law for further legal action.
