the prelude
Under the National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture, the Centre and the States share 60:40 subsidy for the implementation of the Rain-fed Area Development (RAD) scheme.
As per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 3, the Central Share for the year 2021-22 is Rs. 1594.07 Lakhs and State share of Rs. 1067.15 lakh. Administrative approval has been accorded to the Annual Action Plan for an amount of Rs. 2661.22 lakhs. According to the State Government's White Book, for the year 2021-22, Rs. An amount of Rs. 3942.00 lakh has been provided.
The Central Government vide letter dated 22.4.2022 in Reference No.4 has sanctioned Rs. 315.20 lakh to the State Government has been communicated to the Pay and Accounts Officer (Sector). Accordingly, under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) Dryland Area Development (RAD) Programme for 2021-22, the first instalment for implementation for the general category has been released to the Centre at a cost of Rs. 315.20 lakh and Rs. 210.13 lakh, a total of Rs. 525.33 lakh was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The first instalment of funds to the Commissioner (Agriculture) is being disbursed through the Budget Distribution System for implementation for the general category in the year 2021-2022 under the Dryland Area Development (RAD) Programme under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA).

The State Governments will be mandated to implement the dryland development programme under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSC) as per the action plan approved by the Union Ministry of Agriculture for the year 2020-21 and the guidelines issued by the Union Ministry of Agriculture from time to time. In this regard, the Director (Extension and Training) should control the mission from the state level, as well as direct the subordinate offices from time to time for effective implementation and control of the mission.
The scheme should be implemented as per the guidelines of the Depletion Prone Area component in the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture issued by the Central Government and the guidelines of the Government Decision dated 13th April, 2017 of the Advanced Agriculture Prosperous Farmers Mission at No. 2 under reference.
Every month, the physical and financial progress of the scheme should be updated in Annexure-III with the guidelines of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture and the information should also be sent to the State Government.
Detailed quarterly progress report of the scheme by the 10th of the first month of the next quarter and annual progress report within 3 months after the end of the financial year.
