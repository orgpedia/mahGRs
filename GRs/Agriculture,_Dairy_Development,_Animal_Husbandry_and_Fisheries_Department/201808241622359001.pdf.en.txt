4) Letter No. No. No. Mr. Samricha/ Kriya/ Mr.Aap/ Nivi/ 454/2018 D. 09 July, 2018 Preamble: Under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture, the Central Government has started the distribution of soil health cards from the year 2014-15. In this, all the villages of the state will be selected in a phased manner and land health cards will be distributed to all the farmers of the state. Through soil health cards, information is being given to the farmers about the chemical properties of their land, the level of major nutrients and the status of micro element deficiency.
The scheme is being implemented in the ratio of 60:40 between Centre and State since 2015-16.
Reference No. The scheme has been approved by the government as per the decision taken here. The Central Government No. 3 here. The following terms and conditions must be met when implementing the plan.
(1) The scheme shall be implemented in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the terms and conditions specified in the letter of disbursement of funds of the Central Government.
2) The audited report and utilization certificate of the said expenditure should be submitted to the Central Government along with the progress report as soon as possible. Accordingly, it will be the responsibility of the implementing agency to maintain the accounts of expenditure for SC/ ST/ Women beneficiaries as above.
(3) The physical and financial progress report on various aspects of the scheme should be submitted to the Central Government by the 5th of every month. The report will be submitted to the state government.
4) The balance sheet and the amount of the audit deposit related to the scheme should be reported by the implementing agency. It should clearly mention the unpaid amount and the interest earned at the beginning of the year so that the amount can be taken into account in a transparent manner and there is no ambiguity.
5) It should be ensured that the funds are spent for the beneficiary farmers of the general category as distributed by the Central Government.
6) The list of farmers who have distributed soil health cards should be posted in the Gram Panchayat/ Taluk Office/ Panchayat Samiti/ Collector Office. The list should also be uploaded on the website of the Agriculture Department.
7) The Director of Agriculture (Quality and Quality Control), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Pune, should take necessary precautions to ensure that no program beyond the approved program is implemented under any circumstances.
At the level of Commissioner of Agriculture, the Director of Agriculture (Regulation of inputs and quality), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Maharashtra State-Pune will be the Controlling Officer of the scheme and the Assistant Director (Accounts-1), Commissionerate of Agriculture, Pune will be the Drawing and Disbursing Officer. Also, all the District Superintendents of Agriculture Officers are Controlling Officers and District Superintendents of Agriculture Officers in the office.
