Introduction: Animal Husbandry Sector Hettikundi, G. Wardha was established in the year 1946 with the main objective of rearing indigenous purebred cattle, preserving bovine breeds and providing training to farmers in animal husbandry. The government has decided to restructure the Animal Husbandry Department. The decision to close the area was taken by the Government vide its order dated 25.05.2004. But at that time, The Chief Minister has taken the decision. Earlier, 327.80 hectares of land was available in this area. As per the modification dated 25.05.2004, 40.80 hectares of land in Survey No. As per 101/1, the area was allotted and the remaining 287 hectares of land was allotted to Survey No. As per 101/2, the grazing rights have been given to the Forest Department.
dated 23.04.2020. The Minister (Animal Husbandry), during his visit to the Pashupaidas Kshetra Hetikundi, G. Wardha, has directed to transfer the area to Maharashtra Livestock Development Board, Nagpur due to vacancies and lack of manpower in the area.
The Government is issuing the following decision regarding the transfer of this area to the Maharashtra Livestock Development Board for the development of this area, fodder production and the preservation and conservation of this variety.
Government's decision:
Government Animal Husbandry Area, Hettikundi, Ta. Approval of the Government is hereby accorded for transfer of Karanja Ghadge, which belongs to Wardha, to Maharashtra Livestock Development Board.
