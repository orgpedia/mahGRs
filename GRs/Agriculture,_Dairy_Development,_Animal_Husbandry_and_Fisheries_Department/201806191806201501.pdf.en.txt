Preamble: Till 2013-14, the Centrally Sponsored Micro Irrigation Scheme was being implemented as National Mission on Micro Irrigation. The ratio between the Centre and the state is 80:20. During 2014-15, the scheme was implemented under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture- Water Management on Farmlands as a sub-mission. The ratio between the Centre and the state was 80:20. Since 2015-16, the Centrally Sponsored Micro Irrigation Scheme, Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) has been implemented under the component of Per Drop More Crop (Micro Irrigation). The ratio between the Centre and the state is 60: 40.
Under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, till 2017-18, two sub-components namely Micro Irrigation and Other Components were being implemented separately under the Per Drop More Crop component. Separate funds were also provided for these two components. The Central Government has issued No. 6. The scope of the scheme:
Under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, the implementation of per drop more crop component should be done in the rest of the 34 districts of the state, excluding Mumbai and Mumbai suburban districts.
4.The Beneficiaries:
Farmers who meet the following conditions will be eligible to take advantage of this scheme:
4.1 The farmers should have 7/12 and 8-A of the ownership rights.
4.2 Under the sub-component of micro irrigation and water management, the farmer who is getting the benefit of pump set, diesel engine, solar engine etc. should have the facility of irrigation and it should be recorded on the 7/12 line. 7/12 If there is no record of irrigation facility on the land, self-declaration should be taken from the farmer about the well or farm. If there is a provision of irrigation through other means (dams/ canals), the certificate of the concerned (water conservation/ water resources) department official should be taken from the farmer.
4.3 If community irrigation facility is available, then there should be agreement of other stakeholders.
4.4 There should be a permanent electricity connection for the electric pump. The farmers should take a copy of the electricity bill of the past near future along with the proposal. If there is a provision for solar pump, the letter of installation of solar pump and documents related to solar pump should be taken along with the proposal.
4.5 farmers need to have an Aadhaar card.
4.6 A beneficiary is eligible to participate in the scheme but does not have an Aadhaar number, such beneficiaries should be given the benefit of the scheme by submitting proof of Aadhaar enrolment receipt/ Voter ID card/ PAN card/ Passport/ Ration card if they are a government employee/ Bank or Post Office Passbook/ MGNREGA Card/ Kisan Photo till the receipt of Aadhaar number.
4.7 The subsidy amount should be directly credited to the Aadhaar linked bank accounts of the farmers through PFMS system.
4.8 The eligible farmer should be given the benefit within the area of 5 hectares. However, the benefit will not be admissible to the areas which have availed micro irrigation during the last 10 years before 2017-18.
Such a" written statement" should be taken from the farmer. Similarly, it should be actually verified from the records in the office of the Taluk Agriculture Officer. The Agriculture Assistant/ Agriculture Supervisor/ Taluk Agriculture Officer will be responsible for ensuring this.
