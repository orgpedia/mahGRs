Preamble: Production of Phosphate Rich Organic Manure (PROM) under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana at Amravati in Morshi District by Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation at a cost of Rs. The project proposal of Rs. 662.56 lakh was submitted in the meeting dated 4.7.2019 for approval of the State Level Project Sanctioning Committee for 75% contribution. The Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation (MAIDC) has been directed by the chairman of the state-level project approval committee to submit a revised proposal in order to make quality produce available to farmers without making any profit from the project.
Accordingly, the Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation (MAIDC) has submitted a revised proposal vide letter No. Presented by 2. In the proposal, the corporation stated that the corporation will adopt the highest manufacturing process to maintain the quality of prom product planned by the corporation. The Corporation will set up its own separate and state-of-the-art laboratory to test the product at different levels and the product will be brought to the market only after testing there by the training staff. The Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation (MAIDC) will provide prom fertilizers to farmers at a reasonable price compared to the market price.
The State Level Project Sanctioning Committee, Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana has approved to provide 75% subsidy to the Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation (MAIDC) for the production of Phosphate Rich Organic Manure (PROM) under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY). Accordingly, the proposal has been sent to the government for approval.
Government Decision: The Government has decided to implement the Phosphate Rich Organic Manure (PROM) production project under the Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana at Morshi, District Amravati by Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation at a total cost of Rs. A project proposal of Rs 6.63 crore is being approved. Of this, 75 per cent of the grant is Rs. 497 crore is being sanctioned under National Agriculture Development Scheme. The remaining 25% is Rs. The Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation will invest Rs 1.66 crore. The funds will be made available to the Maharashtra Agro Industries Development Corporation through the Commissioner (Agriculture).
