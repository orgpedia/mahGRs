Under the new state-level scheme, Govardhan Govansh Seva Kendra, an organization in each district working for the rearing of stray cows and bovines in the remaining 34 districts of the state, except two districts of Mumbai and Mumbai Suburban, will be given a one-time grant of Rs. 1.00 crore has been launched as per the Government decision dated 26.04.2017.
The State Level Selection Committee had met on 19.03.2018 to select the institutions eligible for grant under the scheme. As per the decision taken in the meeting, it was decided to provide subsidy to gaushalas/ panjal pols in 21 districts of the state in the first phase.
Accordingly, the orders sanctioning grants to the 21 institutions selected in the first phase have been issued vide Order No.2 dated 31.03.2018.
Now, the State Level Selection Committee meeting was held on 17.04.2018 to select the institutions eligible for second phase grant under the said scheme. In the meeting, proposals received from 7 districts were examined and discussed in detail, taking into account the proposals from the remaining districts that meet all the criteria. In the meeting, the Selection Committee has selected the institutions in Form-A attached with this order for district-wise grant for the year 2017-18.
Considering the number of bovine animals (1,54,84, 207) in the State, it was decided in the meeting dated 17.04.2018 to provide subsidy to two Gaushalas in the districts where the average number of bovine animals is more than 4.55 lakh. Proposals received from 13 districts of the state were considered on this criterion. Out of these, 2 or more proposals were found from 9 districts. Out of these 9 districts, proposals from 6 districts which were complete proposals were examined and discussed in detail. As per the above criteria, the Selection Committee in its meeting has selected the institutions in Form-B attached with this Order as additional institutions for the year 2017-18 for grant-in-aid to the districts.

5. The institutions in Form A and B along with the State Level Selection Committee, as selected, will be given a one-time grant of Rs. The government is approving the grant of Rs. 1.00 crore in four phases. The amount of grant-in-aid amounting to Rs. 25.00 lakhs (Rupees Twenty Five Lakhs only) of the first phase out of the above four phases should be deposited immediately in the bank account of the Institute by the Commissioner, Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State, Pune (as per DBT principle). Accordingly, the Government is sanctioning a total outlay of Rs. 3.25 crore (Rupees Three Crores Twenty Five Lakhs only) to 13 beneficiary institutions at a cost of Rs. 25.00 lakh each.
The institutions which have been sanctioned grants under the scheme shall submit the necessary proposals along with the budget and plan of expenditure in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 7 (a) of the Government Resolution dated 26.04.2017 for approval.
7. In the proposal, the estimate of the cost of the work in the form of construction of new shed for livestock, necessary electrification in the shed, etc. (as the cost is appropriate) should be certified by the office of the Executive Engineer, Public Works Department, of the concerned district.
If the cost of equipment for supplying electricity to a new well and borewell is proposed, the budget of this cost should be certified by the office of the Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company of the concerned district.
9. Paragraph No. After receipt of the proposal as mentioned in Section 7, the proposal will be approved by the Administrative Department. After this approval, the organization will be able to carry out the proposed work as well as the direct expenditure of the approved grant.
10. The concerned District Deputy Commissioner Animal Husbandry shall ensure that the subsidy disbursed is utilised only on the basis of the items approved by the Government and accordingly obtain the Utilization Certificate from the concerned institution and submit it to the Commissioner Animal Husbandry.
After this, the Commissioner Animal Husbandry should disburse the second phase subsidy (25 percent). Thus, after receipt of Utilization Certificate of the cost of Phase-II subsidy, Phase-III subsidy (25%) and after receipt of Utilization Certificate of the cost of Phase-III subsidy, Phase-IV subsidy (25%) should be disbursed.
11. It is mandatory for the grantee institutions to carry out the following activities, mainly related to animal husbandry, in collaboration with the department.
(a) In order to conserve and increase the number of indigenous cow breeds in accordance with the prevailing policy of animal husbandry of the State, the semen of pure indigenous cow breeds should be used for artificial insemination in the indigenous and village cows in the institution.
(b) The male calves produced by artificial sand as above should be made available to the lab at nominal rates as per the demand of Maharashtra Livestock Development Board.
(a) The remaining male calves and calves produced by artificial sand as above should be sold to farmers on demand.
(d) Interbreeding of livestock in the institution may lead to adverse effects such as stunting of male calves/ calves, late arrival of calves, premature conception, miscarriage, etc. To prevent this, the organisation should be strengthened.

(e) The Institute shall maintain separate accounts of the expenditure of the grant received and submit the same along with the certificate of Chartered Accountant to the Commissioner Animal Husbandry, State of Maharashtra, Pune.

12. District Controlling Joint Committee is being constituted as follows to control the use of this subsidy and to take care of the health of the cattle in the concerned Gaushala.
(1) The District Deputy Commissioner of the concerned district, Animal Husbandry, (2) The District Animal Husbandry Officer of the concerned district, (3) The Livestock Development Officer (Extension) of the concerned district, Panchayat Samiti, (4) The office head of the Veterinary Institute under the jurisdiction of the concerned institution,
13. Functions of the aforesaid Joint Control Committee: (a) The livestock in the institution should be vaccinated against various communicable diseases like salivary glands, mumps, diphtheria, infectious abortions, etc., and camps for infertility prevention, sterilization, etc. should be organized regularly.
(b) In order to ensure the good health of the livestock in the institution, veterinary examination of the animals should be done at least four times in a year at an interval of three months.
(c) The livestock in the institution should be tagged and registered in the computer system of INAF.
(d) to make recommendations for the next stage of grant on the basis of documents like utilization certificate, photograph of the work etc. received from the Institute after physically inspecting the work done by the Institute as mentioned in the said paragraph 10.
14. The Commissioner Animal Husbandry shall review the progress of the activities of the Gaushala from the concerned District Animal Husbandry Deputy Commissioner of the grantee district in the meetings held from time to time to review the activities of the Animal Husbandry Department.
15. In view of all the above, the Commissioner shall enter into an agreement (bond) with the institution concerned covering such matters as may be deemed necessary by the Commissioner Animal Husbandry including the matters on which the above terms and conditions of the Government shall be complied with by the selected institution.
The institutions whose returns are attached with this Government Order should immediately contact the Commissioner, Animal Husbandry, Maharashtra State Pune and take further action.
