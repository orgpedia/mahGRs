Introduction: Maharashtra is known as a leading state in the field of agricultural production and agro-industry. Vasantrao Naik Krishi Bhushan (Organic Farming), Jijamata Krishi Bhushan, Vasantrao Naik Kheti Mitra, Vasantrao Naik Krishi Ratna, Padma Shri Dr. The award is presented by the Governor of Maharashtra.
In this,' Vasantrao Naik Agrarian Farmer' award is given by selecting the progressive farmers from the farmers who use modern techniques, improved seeds etc. since 1967. Also, to commemorate 100 years of establishment of Directorate of Agriculture in the year 1984, 10 selected individuals/ institutions who have done commendable work in the field of agriculture and allied sectors are honoured with" Vasantrao Naik Krishi Bhushan" award.
At the same time, keeping in view the increasing participation of women in the field of agriculture, 5 selected women farmers have been awarded with" Jijamata Krishi Bhushan" award since 1995 with a view to give due recognition to their work and to create awareness among other women affected by the work of such women. Since 1994, 3 individuals/ organizations who guide or encourage the farmers of the area to take advantage of their agricultural knowledge, other individuals/ organizations who do not do farming themselves or who do not have their own farming, but do valuable work in this field through journalism or other ways, are honored with the award of" Vasantrao Naik Krishi Mitra." In addition, since 2000, individuals/ organizations who have done remarkable work in the field of agriculture, such as expansion, processing, export, production, crop rotation, use of modern technology in production, etc. have been given the title of' Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Krishi Ratna Award is given since 2002 to encourage the farmers who have done remarkable work in the field of horticulture in the state. Since 2010, Krishi Bhushan (Organic Farming) Award is given to those farmers who encourage farmers to use organic inputs through organic farming.
One of the most outstanding officers and employees of all cadres in the Agriculture Department, who are doing important and valuable work in terms of increasing agricultural income in the state.

The employee is awarded" Padma Shri Dr. Vithalrao Vikhe Patil, Krishi Sevaratna" by the state government since 2014.
On the lines of the above various awards, if the young farmers who have made remarkable and innovative achievements in the field of agriculture in the state are honoured with the award, then their willpower, morale will be boosted and the latest technologies will be used in the agriculture sector with more enthusiasm. With this objective, the State Government's Department of Agriculture was considering conferring the award to eight young farmers, one each from each of the Agriculture Departments of the State, for their contribution in enhancing agricultural production and for those young farmers who provide the benefit of their agricultural knowledge to other farmers in their locality and guide or encourage farmers to increase agricultural production, through journalism, social media or any other means.
Agricultural scientists have a unique contribution in the development of high yielding varieties (HYV), pest and disease resistant varieties and technologies in agriculture. In order to honour her work, the government was considering awarding the Best Agricultural Researcher Award to an outstanding researcher from an agricultural university for research in the field of agriculture from the year 2020-21.
Various government decisions were issued from time to time for the above various awards. Accordingly, different procedures were prescribed for different awards and different levels of committees were mentioned for making recommendations. This has resulted in various difficulties faced while making recommendations for the awards every year and delays in making recommendations and distribution of awards, besides the changing nature of the contributions of individuals, farmers/ institution groups working in the field of agriculture in tune with the changes in agriculture practices and technology every year, is expected to bring in ease and uniformity in the procedure for selection and recommendation of awards.
There were different types of forms for different awards to be applied by the farmers for the Krishi Puraskar, these different forms were causing confusion among the farmers. This is expected to bring uniformity by making changes in the form. A timeline is being worked out for holding a level-wise meeting for recommending the Krishi Puraskar and giving a time-bound format to the programme. So that the recommendations for the awards for that year can be made in the current year and the awards can be distributed in the same year.

4. Change of Form: There were different forms for different awards to be applied by the farmers for the Krishi Puraskar. The newly proposed forms for all the awards have been co-signed for 1) Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Krishi Ratna Award 2) Vasantrao Naik Krishi Bhushan Award 3) Jijamata Krishi Bhushan Award and 4) Vasantrao Naik Kheti Mitra Award 5) Vasantrao Naik Krishi Vishisht Shetkari Award (Tribal/ General) 6) Krishi Bhushan (Organic Farming) Award and 7) Udyanpandit Award.
5. Procedure: The maximum number of proposals of the eligible awardees will be received from the Taluk level as per the schedule and criteria shown below by giving wide publicity through regional newspapers, All India Radio and Doordarshan etc. from the District/ Block level.
A person who has received an award in the field of agriculture cannot apply again for the same award or for an award with less reward.
If a husband or wife has received an agricultural award, they cannot apply for the same award again.
Preference will be given to the candidates having Diploma/ Degree in Agriculture or allied to Agriculture in respect of all Agriculture Awards.
The information of the recommended farmers should be filled carefully and accurately in the form of a signed form.
If the proposal submitted is incomplete, the proposal should be rejected, after its rejection, no appeal will be filed in connection with the said proposal.
The proposal submitted and the documents attached therewith should be signed by the Member Secretary.
According to the proposal received by the district level committee, the farm should be physically inspected.

If the number of farmers proposed is less, then the reasons should be given, otherwise the proposal will not be considered and all the responsibility will be of the district level committee.
There should be complete confidentiality in the matter of scoring proposal.
If more than one proposal is being submitted from the department in case of all other awards except Vasantrao Naik Krishi Vichit Shetkari Puraskar (General Group) Award, then the proposals should be submitted by giving priority while submitting the proposals at the senior level. Also, in the case of Vasantrao Naik Krishi Vigyan Kisan (General Group) Award, the first two proposals per district in order of merit should be submitted at the senior level.
While submitting the proposal to the Agriculture Commissionerate, the Joint Director of Divisional Agriculture should submit two passport size photographs of the concerned (without stapling the short name and mobile number of the farmer on the back of the photo) as well as the prescribed format of the concerned to the Agriculture Commissionerate along with the complete history of the meeting of the committee at the divisional level. Only the original copy of the proposal should be sent to the Agriculture Commissionerate and the remaining copies should be preserved at the district and division level.
The following schedule is being prescribed for all the awards.  To publish advertisements in newspapers at the level of Commissioner of Agriculture every year in April and urge to submit proposals for various agricultural awards by June 30.
The District Level Committee shall receive proposals from the Sub-Divisional Taluks by 31st July every year.
The proposals received by the District Level Committee should be examined, visited and processed beforehand and the proposals should be submitted to the Department Level Committee by August 31.
The proposals received by the department level committee should be examined, presented before the department and the complete and eligible proposals as per the criteria recommended along with self-explanatory feedback on the work mentioned should be submitted to the Commissionerate of Agriculture together with the minutes of the meeting of the department level committee by September 30.
The Commissionerate level committee of Agriculture will scrutinize the eligible proposals received from the department level committee with recommendation and submit the proposal to the government for final selection with recommendation by November 30.
The responsibility of submitting the proposal of eligible farmers/ individuals/ groups/ organizations from each district for all agricultural awards will be with the concerned District Superintendent Agriculture Officer and the responsibility of controlling will be with the Divisional Joint Director of Agriculture.
The provisions of the said Government Decision shall continue to be applicable for selection of various Agriculture Awardees from the financial year 2020-21.
