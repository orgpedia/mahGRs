Preface : Audit of accruals/expenditures of Public Works Department and its subordinate offices as per changes in the scope of work of Accountant General Mumbai and Nagpur Accountant General (Audit)-|| It is done by Maharashtra, Nagpur. Complying with the audit objections received from the Office of the Accountant General and making them available to the Audit Team within the prescribed time frame helps in prompt disposal of such objections and avoids further proceedings. If there is no response to the objections received from the Accountant General&#39;s office from the sectoral level and departments, such objections are converted into paragraphs and submitted to the Legislature through various reports through the Comptroller and Auditor General of India. The Secretary of the Department has to testify on such pending passages from the Public Accounts Committee of the Legislature.
At present, the number of such pending paragraphs is large in the matter of the Legislature
It is being pointed out from various reports. The Public Accounts Committee has expressed strong displeasure about this.
Therefore, with a view to taking immediate action on the objections received from the Accountant General&#39;s Office, the pending draft paragraphs and the pending paragraphs mentioned in the report of the Public Accounts Committee, the following instructions are being given to the Public Works Department and all the controlling officers and heads of offices under them.
Government Circular 1) Audit Intimation Letter (Audit Intimation Letter) is sent to the concerned office generally two to three weeks before the audit of any office by the Accountant General, according to which the head of the office should appoint the responsible officer of the office as &quot;nodal officer&quot; for the audit.
2) Head of Office for entry conference conducted by audit team before commencement of audit as required in specific type of audit.

And all Controlling Officers and Drawing and Disbursing Officers should be present and have a detailed discussion with the audit team about the audit of accounts.
3) All records required for audit like registers / payments / payments etc. should be made available to the audit team immediately. The Head of Office should personally ensure that no complaint is received from the audit team regarding non-availability of records for audit and if any complaint is received he should resolve the complaint.
4) Half Margin Memos are issued by the audit team of the Accountant General&#39;s office while checking the records of the office or to ask for some additional necessary information. The head of the office should personally verify the issue raised by the audit team in the half-margin letter and give the self-evident compliance with his signature at the same time, so that the accounting objection is settled at the preliminary level and the said objection is not included in the inspection report of the Accountant General.
5) On the last day of the audit, the draft inspection report is discussed by the audit team while reading the report. At that time, the head of the office along with all the concerned officers should discuss each paragraph of the accounting objection with the audit team point by point and if the objection is submitted along with the necessary documents, the objection can be excluded from the audit report of the Accountant General.
6) Efforts should be made to remove the irregularities, deficiencies in the audit objections during the period till the inspection report is received from the office of the Accountant General, thus helping to submit the compliance of the audit objections within the prescribed period. The Head of Office while submitting compliance audit objections should be factual and should ensure that the irregularities in the objections are not repeated.
7) In the current audit if the previous pending audit objections are duly complied with, those objections are also dropped from the audit trail (On The Spot).
Hence the pending paragraphs should be given while the compliance audit is in progress.
8) Thereafter, the inspection report from the Accountant General&#39;s office is sent to the concerned office / their controlling officer and the government and if the objections are submitted within one month and the objections are submitted along with supporting documents, then the said objections are also excluded from the Accountant General&#39;s office.

A register regarding paragraphs should be kept up-to-date with newly added / omitted paragraphs and verified by the Head of Office.
11) From the office of the Accountant General, selected and critical paragraphs in the inspection reports of the audited offices are converted into draft paragraphs (Draft Audit Para DAP) and the said draft paragraphs are brought to the attention of the government. Compliance with the said paragraphs is required to be sent to the Office of the Accountant General with the approval of the Secretary within six weeks from the receipt of the paragraph by the Department, otherwise the said draft paragraphs are converted into paragraphs and included in the various reports of the Comptroller and Auditor General of India. Heads of all offices should ensure that if detailed compliance of the draft paragraphs is submitted to the government by the concerned offices within the prescribed period, at least the paragraphs will be included in the report of the Comptroller and Auditor General of India.
12) In case no action is taken as per serial no. 11) above the said paragraphs shall be included in the following reports submitted annually to the Legislature by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India viz.i) Audit Report on State Finances
ii) General and Social Sector Report
iii) Financial and Revenue Sector Reports etc.
The secretary of the department has to testify before the Public Accounts Committee regarding the paragraphs in the said report. The said report was submitted to the Legislature regarding such paragraphs

13) Rule 138 and Rule 149 of the Regulation on Audit and Accounts (Amendment 2020) should be followed by all offices while replying to the points / paragraphs raised by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India in their various reports and acting on the explanatory memorandum.

15) Care should be taken to ensure that the prescribed time limit is not exceeded under any circumstances while submitting compliance objections at all stages. Some passages are indispensable
If for any reason the final compliance cannot be submitted within the prescribed time, an interim compliance of the said objection should be submitted. Extension of time should be taken as per requirement in exceptional case.
16) The concerned Chief Engineer, Regional Department of Public Works shall have initial responsibility for the said work. They should organize a meeting of their subordinate offices in the first week of every month to review the audit objections and

18) In the report submitted to the Legislature by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India - such accounts as misappropriation, loss, fraud etc. are objected. All Heads of Offices are required to attend to such conduct themselves and any kind of outstanding/due amounts, employees/officers/contractors/related persons/account holders etc.
If they have, it should be recovered 100%.
19) If the financial irregularity is mild in nature, subject to regulation, the approval of the competent authority should be sought to bring it under regulation. Serious irregularity, embezzlement, embezzlement etc.
In case of departmental enquiry, court / police case, necessary action should be decided on a case by case basis.
20) As the matter of settlement of pending paragraphs by the Accountant General has been included in the Key Result Area (KRA), the work evaluation of all officers/employees related to the said work should be done on the basis of the completion of the presented legislative work and the same should be recorded in the confidential report of the concerned.
21) All Heads of Offices should do their daily work in accordance with the prescribed Government Acts / Rules / Government Decisions / Circulars and should ensure that there are minimum accounting objections.
All Controlling Officers should ensure that these instructions are strictly followed by the offices under their administrative control. Disciplinary action should be taken against such officer/employee by the controlling officer if it is directed by the head of the office that there has been harm in taking the above action.
