C. The Central Government. The instructions issued vide Circular No. 26.06.2020 are only for blocking of approach roads on National Highways. It has also been observed from various judgements that the provisions of IRC- 12-2009 are only a guideline. Also, other states have made a separate policy for the construction of approach roads. On the same lines, the committee was formed to make separate criteria for granting permission from state highways and major district roads in Maharashtra.
The committee has submitted new guidelines for the state to the government as per the guidelines of the central government, the criteria set by various states, various judgements as well as suggestions made by the representatives of the oil companies. Accordingly, the government has taken the following decisions:
Government decision
1. The road side petrol pump design of the Public Works Department and the approach roads to be adopted earlier for the construction of service stations/ resorts/ hotels/ dhabas along with the petrol pumps through the State Highways/ Major District Highways etc. are being superseded under the decision of the present Government.
Public Works Department Department, Govt. Decision No. RBD-2020/ No. 32/ Roads-7, D. 05.02.2021, the recommendations submitted to the Government by the Standing Committee are being accepted. The procedure to be followed for access through different grades of roads as submitted by the Committee shall be as per the attached The Maharashtra PWD Guidelines for Issuing of NOC for Access Permission to Fuel Stations and Private Properties/ Other Establishments (other than Fuel Station) along State Roads, 2021 standards.
(3) The provisions of this Government Order shall come into force from the date of issue of this Government Order. Also, the oil companies should come up with the following action plan for the advertisements issued before the current government decision on petrol pumps.
(a) The guidelines in force on the date of advertisement shall be examined and acted upon accordingly. or
B. The guidelines issued under the said government decision should be examined and action should be taken accordingly.
Proposals that do not fit into options A or B above should be submitted to the government before the implementation of the decision. A decision will be taken at the government level.
The proposal should be submitted to the government by the regional officers with the following points.
(i) The proposal for exemption from the standard shall be examined by the Chief Engineer of the concerned Regional Public Works Department and submitted on his recommendation.

(ii) While submitting such proposal, necessary measures regarding road safety should be proposed so that accidents do not occur. A proposal should be submitted along with the proposal.
(iii) In case the road is upgraded or widened, the applicant will be required to obtain fresh permission as per the rules applicable at that time.
iv) The cost required for the safety measures to be taken should be deposited by the petrol pump owner with the concerned Executive Engineer. A proposal should be submitted to the government.
(v) At the Government level, proposals should be submitted only before the implementation of the said Government decision.
The date of advertisement given by the oil companies, the date of Letter of Intent (LOI) given to the applicant (along with relevant documents) should be mentioned in the proposal.
(vi) It shall be mandatory for the concerned Executive Engineer to ensure that all safety measures as mentioned in the warranty are adopted before the actual opening of such petrol pump and submit a report to the Government.
5. The representatives of all the major oil companies are hereby informed that while advertising the petrol pumps, the location of the petrol pumps will be decided as per the standards decided by the government and the petrol pumps which do not meet the standards/ criteria to be decided by the Public Works Department will be denied permission.
