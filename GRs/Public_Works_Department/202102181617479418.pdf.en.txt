Introduction:
Public Works Department under reference no.1, vide Government Decision No.Apang 2010/P.No.375/Seva-4, dated 11th January, 2011, posts in this department were ensured for persons with disabilities. But now, as per Section 33 of the Central Government&#39;s Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, it is necessary to ensure the posts under the jurisdiction of the State Government, as per the notification dated 4.1.2021 of the Central Government Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, New Delhi. is
As per the posts mentioned in the said list, the issue of ensuring the posts in Group-A, B, C and D in the Public Works Department itself as well as in the Regional Establishments was under the consideration of the Government.
Government Decision:
Taking into account the matters in the preamble, the above referred no. 1 dt. Government decision dated 11th January, 2011 is being ratified.
2. According to the instructions given under the Government Decision No. 7 dated February 2, 2021 under the reference of the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, under the reference No. 5 of the Central Government. In view of the notification dated 4th January, 2021, the posts mentioned in the attached Annexure in Group A, B, C and D of the Public Works Department itself as well as in the field establishments for direct service recruitment are being ensured by this order for various categories of differently abled persons. Any post in the lower category of the said posts is ensured for the disabled category, all the posts in the promotion chain of that post shall be deemed to be ensured for the concerned disabled category. not in the accompanying appendix

If a person with significant disability is already working in the post, then the said post shall be deemed to be reserved for that category of significant disability. If the duties and responsibilities of a post are as per the posts ensured in the annexure herewith, the said post shall be deemed to be ensured for the respective category of persons with disabilities, even if the designation of the said post is different.
3. While direct service recruitment of posts provided for persons with disabilities, the provisions of the “Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016” should be taken into consideration as well as the orders issued by the Government from time to time. Also, the following points should be considered while recruiting the said direct service:
3.1) As per the Government Decision dated 29th May, 2019 of the General Administration Department at Reference No.4, 4% of the vacant posts for all cadres with prescribed disabilities should be reserved for persons with disabilities in the direct service recruitment of the assured posts in the attached appendix. Also, in this regard, action should be taken as per the instructions issued by the General Administration Department from time to time.
3.2) On receipt of the recommendation of the concerned candidates, such persons with disabilities should be appointed to the posts provided for persons with disabilities in Group-A, B, C and D under the Public Works Department only after the prescribed medical board recommends such candidates regarding physical qualification. Before making direct service appointments to the posts provided for persons with disabilities, the appointing authority concerned should verify the authenticity of the disability certificate of the candidate and check whether or not the candidate concerned is competent to perform the duties of the said post.
3.3) It should be ensured that the candidate possesses the qualification required as per the provisions of the entry rules of the post concerned.
3.4) The Appointing Authority should not recruit the relevant post for any single category of PWDs but for all the categories ensured for PWDs.
3.5) Under reference no. 6 of the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. According to the instructions given under the Government Decision dated February 1, 2021, assistive technology and equipment should be made available to the blind / visually impaired / deaf and disabled employees. Also, in this regard, action should be taken as per the instructions issued by the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance from time to time.
