Introduction:
Subject to the provisions of the Maharashtra State Service (Medical Maintenance) Rules, 1961 to Government employees and their families, in each case of medical expenses for treatment in a private hospital for 27 accidental diseases and for 5 serious diseases, treatment in a private hospital approved by the Government for full cost reimbursement Rs. 3,00,000/- (Rupees three lakhs only) the authority to sanction reimbursement is vested in the Head of Department vide Public Health Department Govt Decision dated 16th March, 2016 vide A.No.1 read above. Proposals are submitted to the respective ministerial administrative departments as the Heads of Administrative Departments are empowered to reimburse medical expenses above this limit. In view of the pending cases of proposals for reimbursement of medical expenses, subject to the terms and conditions of the Maharashtra State Service (Medical Maintenance) Rules, 1961 and subsequent Government Decisions issued from time to time for medical expenses up to Rs.3,00,000/- (literal rupees three lakhs only). In the financial authority of the Head of Department in respect of reimbursement, read above Public Health Department No. 2 The Government Decision dated June 6, 2019 has been modified. Taking into account the approved designations in the current configuration of the Public Works Department as mentioned in paragraph 6 of this Government Decision, the issue of declaring Heads of Departments and Heads of Regional Departments in all zonal offices under the department for approval of reimbursement of net medical expenses was under consideration.
Government Decision: Government Decision of Public Health Department, dt. As mentioned in paragraph 66 of 06 June, 2019, for approval of reimbursement of net medical expenses of officers/employees in the field offices under the Public Works Department, the Heads of Departments and Heads of Regional Departments are hereby approved to declare as follows and grant reimbursement powers to them within the prescribed financial limits. .
