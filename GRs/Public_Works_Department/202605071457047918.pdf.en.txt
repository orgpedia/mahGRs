The Proposal: -
Given the priority of providing opportunities and facilities to the various sectors responsible for achieving the economic development goals, it is imperative that all these sectors are connected in a dynamic manner to the core economic, trade, industrial and market sectors. That is, there is an urgent need for such a network of roads from the primary to the terminal connection of the required capacity under the infrastructure.
Mumbai is not only the capital of Maharashtra but also the largest capital of the entire country. Therefore, the primary requirement is that all major domestic corridors be connected to Mumbai. Naturally, the primary network of road networks in Maharashtra also connects all the far-flung development areas to this main hub.
Traffic to Mumbai, which covers a large part of Marathwada and the Ahilyanagar region, mainly passes through Pune. This large volume of traffic directly affects the traffic congestion in the Pune Shirur road area. The road directly connecting Nashik and Pune APTANA Ahmednagar to Shirurmag Mumbai B JNPT to the west crossing Sahyadrighat from the east of Maharashtra was not only part of the Pune Shirur road traffic congestion solution, but also created a need for a separate, high-speed road connecting Marathwada and Ahilyanagar regions to Mumbai Greater Centre, which would promote development in the areas along the route.
Under the privatisation, the government was considering approval to take up the construction of Neral, Bandra, Wee, Shiroli, Pabal, Malathan, Shirur roads in Raigad and Pune districts through the Public Works Department, approval for the design and approval for land acquisition. In accordance with this. Completed on 119.03.2026. In pursuance of the decision taken in the Cabinet Infrastructure Committee meetings, the Government has taken the following decision.
