The Proposal: -
Hon'ble Supreme Court in Sou Moto Writ Petition 05/2025. The order dated 07.11.2025 directs all concerned agencies like Municipal Corporations / Municipalities, Transport Department and Public Works Department to take immediate measures in coordination to avoid accidents caused by stray / stray animals on the highway. The following directions have been mainly issued under this order regarding prevention of accidents due to stray / stray animals on the highway: -
Municipal authorities, Road Transport and Highways Department, Public Works Department as well as National Highways Authority of India shall remove stray / stray animals from State Highways, National Highways and National Highways falling under their jurisdiction.
Municipal authorities, Road Transport and Highways Department and National Highways Authority of India should take action to identify dangerous / vulnerable areas on the highway by conducting a coordinated special drive to remove stray / stray animals found in the area and relocate them to designated shelters.
Each authority should set up separate highway patrol teams or take immediate necessary action by conducting frequent inspections through the existing Road Safety Committee. These teams should be operationalized in coordination with the respective local police stations, Animal Husbandry Officers and Municipal / Panchayat Raj Authorities with a strength of 2457.
Highlighted helpline numbers should be provided at regular intervals on all national highways, state highways and national expressways so that passengers and drivers can be informed immediately of the presence of stray animals or accidents caused by them. The helpline numbers should also be linked to the control room of the local police, National Highways Authority of India as well as the district administration so that immediate grievance redressal and monitoring can be done.
The Chief Secretaries of all States / UTs as well as the Chairman, National Highways Authority of India shall ensure strict implementation of the directions through appropriate administrative orders and field monitoring.

The District Road Safety Committee has been amended by a notification dated 05.12.2022 of the Department of Transport pursuant to the direction dated 29.03.2022 of the Supreme Court Committee on Road Safety. The District Road Safety Committee constituted by the said notification consists of the District Collector, Commissioner of Police / Superintendent of Police, Commissioner Municipal Corporations and Municipalities, Civil Society, CEOs of notified authorities, Regional Transport Officer, Principal Secretary, Municipal Council, Project Director, National Highways Authority of India, Superintendent, Highway Safety Police and Superintendent Engineer / Executive Engineer of Public Works Department. The function of the District Road Safety Committee in respect of road accidents has been fixed by a notification dated 05.12.2022 of the Home Department.
In pursuance of the aforesaid orders of the Hon'ble Supreme Court, the following instructions are being issued.
The Government's Decisions:
According to the accidents on the highway caused by stray / stray animals, Ma. The directions of the Supreme Court dated 07/11/2025 in the Sou Moto Writ Petition 05/2025 should be followed strictly by the District Road Safety Committee.
Action should be taken to remove stray / stray animals found in the area and migrate them to designated shelters by conducting special drives by the District Road Safety Committee to identify dangerous / vulnerable areas on the highways.
2. Highway Patrol teams to be constituted through District Road Safety Committee and necessary action to be taken immediately for removal of stray / stray animals to designated shelters after frequent inspection by these patrol teams. These teams should be operationalized in coordination with the respective local police stations, Animal Husbandry Officers and Municipal / Panchayat Raj Authorities with a strength of 2457.
3. National Highways Authority of India Helpline Number 1033 and State Government Helpline Number 112 to be made public and public through District Road Safety Committee. Action to be taken to highlight this Helpline Number at regular intervals as required by the risk zone on all National Highways, State Highways and National Highways. These helpline numbers should be linked to the control room of the local police, National Highways Authority of India as well as the district administration so that immediate grievance redressal and monitoring can be done.
4. Ma. The report of the action taken in accordance with the order of the Supreme Court should be submitted to the Secretary, Animal Husbandry Department and the Nodal Officer every month.
