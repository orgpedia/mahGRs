As per the provisions of the Maharashtra Prevention of Delay in Performing Government Duties Act, 2005 and with the approval of the competent authority, the Deputy Engineer (Construction) mentioned in Annexure 'A' attached to the Public Works Department has been posted in Column No. 4 Here he is being replaced in the position indicated before his name.
It is communicated to the Controlling Authorities concerned that they shall immediately relieve the Deputy Engineer (Construction) mentioned in the accompanying Annexure A by making such local alternate arrangements as may be necessary to enable him to take charge at the place of the new appointment and to ask him to join the place of the new appointment forthwith. However, the officers who have been posted to the post of superannuation shall be relieved by making such local alternate arrangements as may be necessary so that the officer concerned may take charge at the place of the new appointment after his retirement.
3. The Deputy Engineer (Architecture) mentioned in the accompanying Annexure A shall assume charge of the new post forthwith and submit his report of assumption of office to the Government without delay. The engineers mentioned in Appendix 'A' shall ensure that the transferred engineers join the place of transfer within the time prescribed by the rules of the Maharashtra Civil Service (Period of assumption, non-acceptance of service and Provisions during periods of suspension, dismissal and removal from service) Rules, 1981, or else, if they do not join the post of transfer during this period, the period of absence shall be deemed to be non-deceasing day.
Their attention is also being drawn to Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules 1979. Taking into account the provisions of this rule, they will be liable for disciplinary action if they submit any form of application for transfer of office.
