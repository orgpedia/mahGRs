During the session of the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly, during the proceedings of both the Houses, Shri. Promises are made by the Minister. It is the statutory responsibility of the Ministries concerned to ensure that the promises made by the Minister in the House are fulfilled expeditiously. It is also the statutory responsibility of the Ministries to ensure that the Parliamentary arms presented by the representatives of the two Houses of the Legislature are completed within the stipulated time and reported to the Legislative Secretariat. In order to implement this effectively, as per the instructions issued by the Government Circular dated 10th October, 2025 in the context of Department of Parliamentary Affairs, the following Arms Supplies Committee is being constituted in the Department of Public Works:

2. The Arms Supplies Committee as well as all the relevant offices of the Department shall act in accordance with the following instructions to dispose of the Promises and Parliamentary Arms.
1) Two separate registers with separate list of pending promises as well as separate list of pending parliamentary arms shall be maintained in the Department by the Cell of the Coordination Branch. The Deputy Secretary (Legislative Affairs Coordination) should do a self-check once a month to ensure that the lists are up to date.
The Deputy Secretary (Coordination of Legislative Affairs) of the Department shall convene the meeting of the Committee twice a month, every 15 days. The Regional Officer as well as the Executive Officer concerned should be invited to these meetings as required.

(iii) At these meetings, a list of the pending promises of the first division and the parliamentary armaments should be submitted to the committee.
Also, a decision should be taken to take necessary measures by setting a time limit for disposal of these promises and parliamentary arms.
(iv) to take special measures to dispose of Parliamentary arms pending for more than two years at these meetings and to take action to dispose of all promises and Parliamentary arms with a time-bound manner.
v) The Government shall take action accordingly by taking a decision in accordance with the circular regarding the non-fulfilment of the promises received in respect of the transfer of the promises from other departments and return the same to the department concerned as decided in the said meetings. Also, the current status of transfers of assurances from the department to other departments was discussed in the meetings.
Follow-up action should be taken in the department. Action should be taken on the transfer of the long pending posts in other departments after taking feedback from the General Administration Department (Structure and Works).
vi) The meeting should verify that the Department has taken note of the fulfillment of the Promises B Parliamentary Arms on the website of the Legislative Secretariat as well as in the corresponding chamber.
vii) The pending promises of the department are reviewed by the Parliamentary Assurances Committee on Parliamentary Weapons and the Ad-hoc Committee. Action should be taken in accordance with the correspondence made by the said committee and the same should be reviewed in the said meetings.
