Introduction:
The concept of Western Freeway was conceived as it was realized that there is a need to have a fast freeway in the west of the city to make the traffic system in the western part of the city of Mumbai fast and easy. As part of the same, the Maharashtra State Road Development Corporation has been appointed by the Government Decision dated 18.08.2000 to implement the project “Bandra-Worli Sagari Marg” on “Build, Operate and Transfer” (BOT) basis for the improvement of traffic in the western suburbs of Mumbai city. It has been announced. Also, as per the government decision dated 24.08.2004, the estimated cost of the project was approved at Rs.1306/- crore as per revised scope of work. After that as per government decision dated 28.03.2008 Rs. 1634/- Crore has been approved for revised price.
By Government decision dated 04.12.2017, taking into consideration the expenditure incurred on the Bandra - Worli Sea Bridge Project and the claims of the entrepreneur, a revised price of Rs.1975/- Crore (Rs. Nineteen Hundred Seventy Five Crores only) has been approved for this project. As per Government decision dated 04.12.2017 the revised price is Rs.1975/- Crore for repayment of Viability Gap Fund (VGF) grace period for obtaining internal return at the rate of interest rate + 2% on the loan raised to the Corporation on 31.03. Extension till 2052 has been approved. During the extended concession period of the project, the Corporation has approved the appointment of an independent contractor for the two works of road toll collection and road maintenance and repair, and taking into consideration the increase in future traffic on the Bandra-Worli Sea Bridge in real time, rescheduling the concession period after 10 years.
The issue of Bandra-Worli sea route toll collection and maintenance and repair rights as well as approval of concession agreement for fund raising with Maharashtra State Road Development Corporation was under consideration of the government. Accordingly, the matter has been approved in the meeting of the Cabinet Infrastructure Committee held on 20.12.2018. According to this, the government has now taken the following decision.
