The Proposal: -
Pursuant to the decision taken in the meetings of the Hon'ble Cabinet dated 29th November, 2022, a new Ministerial Administrative Department namely "Department of Welfare of Persons with Disabilities" has been created by the Government Notification dated 15th December, 2022 of the Department of General Administration. The Persons with Disabilities (Protection of Right to Equal Opportunities and Full Participation) Act 1995 defined 7 types of disability. While 21 categories of persons with disabilities have been defined under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016. Accordingly, a significant increase in the number of persons with disabilities is expected in the state.
The Government is committed to empowering persons with disabilities and bringing them into the mainstream of society through various initiatives and welfare schemes for their holistic development (education, training, rehabilitation, financial assistance, social security, positive action plans). Disabled people need health, employment, self-employment, education, training, awareness campaigns in the community to understand the causes of disability, early detection and early diagnosis and treatment to identify and reduce disability at an early age. In addition, the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 requires persons with disabilities to be made aware of their rights and responsibilities in order to bring them into the mainstream of society and make them self-reliant and self-reliant, enabling them to lead a normal life.
The various media outlets in the state can be effectively used to make every person with disabilities aware of their rights and entitlements guaranteed by law.
Important policy decisions taken by the government for persons with disabilities, important welfare schemes, initiatives / campaigns implemented by the government for the benefit of persons with disabilities, etc., need to reach the disabled in an expeditious and effective manner. Also, news channels, various newspapers, hoardings, information boards of government offices, public transport etc. are available here to build trust in the society towards persons with disabilities.

Along with advertisements, radio spots and jingles, radio, camps, social media platforms like Facebook, FedEx, YouTube, WhatsApp are also required to be promoted and publicized.
In the current financial year, the Department of Disability Welfare has made a budgetary provision of Rs.22351 crores for publicity and publicity under the heading (26) Advertisements and Publications (2373). The Government Decision dated 13.08.2021 of the General Administration Department prescribes the procedure for implementing the Government Advertisement Distribution Policy. Accordingly, the Publicity Media Framework of the Department of Disability Welfare has been approved by the Media Planning and Implementation Committee of the Directorate General of Information and Public Relations in its meetings held on 1st August, 2025. Administrative approval of the plan was under the consideration of the government.
The Government's Decisions:
