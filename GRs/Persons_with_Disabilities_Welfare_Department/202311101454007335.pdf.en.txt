Government circular:
In the meeting held on 18th October, 2023 under the chairmanship of Hon'ble Chief Secretary, the implementation of 4% reservation as per Section 34 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 was discussed.
2 In the meeting, Secretary, Divyang Kalyan elaborated that" Divyangjan Welfare Department is running a campaign at the doorstep of Divyangjan." Many complaints are being received about the non-implementation of Divyang reservation during this campaign. In this meeting, The Chief Secretary said," The Department of Disabled Welfare should issue a circular regarding the implementation of reservation. Also, all the departments should be given definite feedback on whether the disabled reservation is applicable to contractual posts," the order said.
As per Section 33 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the appropriate Government shall identify suitable posts for persons with disabilities as follows:
(1) In respect of employment, the posts in establishments which can be filled up by persons with significant disabilities in the said category shall be earmarked in accordance with the provisions of section 34.
2 An expert committee with representation from persons with significant disabilities should be constituted to determine such posts and, 3 the posts to be determined should be reviewed periodically, for a period not exceeding three years.

As per Section 34 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the appropriate Government shall appoint persons with significant disabilities to fill at least 4% of the total number of posts in each category in every Government establishment. Since many of the posts of the appropriate Government mentioned in paragraph 4 are to be filled on contract basis and their salaries are to be paid by the concerned Government Department, the contractual designation is also required to be reserved for the disabled persons by the concerned Department by following the procedure mentioned in the above paragraph 3.
