Preamble: The Maharashtra State Industrial Development Programme (MSI-CDP) has been announced vide Government Resolution dated 25/02/2014 for the development of small clusters in industrially underdeveloped areas of the State, thereby promoting the holistic development of Micro and Small Enterprises. Sadar
Under the scheme, grants are provided by the State Government for the implementation of capacity building programmes for the overall development and growth of micro and small enterprises and for the construction of common facilities. Under this scheme, a Diagnostic Study Report (DSR) is prepared by properly diagnosing the flaws in the production process of the industrial group, including the latest technology, market expansion, visits to various successful industrial groups, updated training programs and study tours, etc. After the diagnostic study report is received on the recommendation of the District Level Committee, the State Level Industrial Cluster Control Committee approves the diagnostic study report proposals and determines the subsidy for the capacity building programme. High quality common facilities and other necessary licenses, no-objection certificates, technical and financial evaluation reports to enhance the productivity and competitiveness of micro and small enterprises.
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is being prepared. The procurement process is initiated after final approval of the proposal of Common Facility Centre by the State Level Steering Committee. 60% of the first phase subsidy is disbursed after receiving the physical verification report of the machinery, equipment, workstation as approved.
