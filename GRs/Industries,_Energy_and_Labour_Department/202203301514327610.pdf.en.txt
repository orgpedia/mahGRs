Preamble: The Maharashtra State Industrial Complex Development Programme (MSICDP) has been announced vide Government Resolution dated 25/02/2014 to promote the development of small clusters in the industrially underdeveloped areas of the State, thereby promoting the overall development of Micro and Small Enterprises. Under this scheme, the state government provides grants for the implementation of capacity building programs for the overall development and growth of micro and small enterprises, as well as for the construction of common facilities. Under this scheme, a Diagnostic Study Report (DSR) is prepared by properly diagnosing the flaws in the production process of the industrial group, including the latest technology, market expansion, visits to various successful industrial groups, updated training programs and study tours, etc. After the diagnostic study report is received on the recommendation of the district level committee, the State Level Industrial Cluster Control Committee approves the diagnostic study report proposal and determines the subsidy for the capacity building programme. A Detailed Project Report (DPR) containing details of common facilities and other necessary clearances, no-objection certificates, technical and financial evaluation reports for enhancing productivity and competitiveness of MSME units is also prepared. The procurement process is initiated after final approval of the proposal of Common Facility Centre by the State Level Steering Committee. As per the revised guidelines dated 24/07/2019 of the scheme, after the final approval of the proposal of Common Facility Centre by the State Level Steering Committee, the projects whose procurement process has been completed through the Zonal Committee will be given the first report along with the details of procurement
