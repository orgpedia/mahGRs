Preamble: - In order to protect the interests of workers in building and other construction professions, the Central Government has enacted the Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996. Accordingly, the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2007 have come into force. In accordance with these rules, the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board has been formed.
A construction worker who has completed 18 years of age up to 60 years of age and has worked for at least 90 days in the previous one year is registered with the Board and has to renew his registration every year. The concerned construction worker is given the benefits of various schemes of the Board during his active (living) registration period.
Section (22) (b) of the Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996 provides for payment of pension to construction workers who have completed two years of service. In this regard, the Central Government vide No. The guidelines for model welfare schemes are detailed under Section 1. Accordingly, in the 62nd meeting of the Board dated 26.03.2025, a resolution has been passed regarding the implementation of pension to construction workers registered under the Board.

The Hon'ble Minister (Labour) has submitted a statement regarding the matter to both the Houses of the Legislative Assembly and the Legislative Council in the Budget Session of the Maharashtra Legislature. Accordingly, the Board referred to him as No. The proposal has been submitted to the government under letter No. 03.
After attaining the age of 60 years, the construction worker is not registered with the Board and his previous registration is not renewed. As a result, the worker is deprived of various schemes from the board. At that time, the government was considering implementing pensions for construction workers in order to contribute to their ease of living in old age.
The Government's Decisions:
Under this government decision, construction workers registered under the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board, Mumbai, are allowed to apply their pension on completion of 60 AT of their balance.
Eligibility Criteria: - (a) All construction workers registered with Board B who have completed 60 years of age shall be eligible under the scheme. Also, if both husband and wife of his family are construction workers, they will be eligible separately.
(b) On the death of the spouse, the spouse of the construction worker concerned shall be eligible for the pension. However, if the spouse is receiving a pension under the scheme, the relative will not get the benefit of double pension.
Construction workers receiving benefits under the Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 and the Employees' Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 (the Employees' Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952) will not be eligible for pension, as per the guidelines of Model Welfare Schemes No. 9 of the Central Government.
Financial Criteria: - (a) Pension will be paid to eligible construction workers as per the period of their registration with the Board.
(b) the amount of pension in respect of the year in which it is registered with the Board shall be as follows: -
