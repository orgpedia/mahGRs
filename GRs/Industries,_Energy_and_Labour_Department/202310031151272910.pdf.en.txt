Introduction: Given the available infrastructure in the Directorate of Geology and Mining and the huge investment in the field of industrial development, geology and mining need to be given priority attention. Also, the Directorate of Geology and Mining has come into existence as a separate agency in the form of a Directorate for the development of the mining sector in the State since 1st May, 1960. Since then, the" Director" is the administrative head of the system.
However, in order to effectively monitor the opportunities in the mining sector and the mineral deposits available in the State and to enhance the mineral revenue to the State, the Directorate of Geology and Mining has been constituted at the field level under the aegis of the Department of Mines. The post of Director General in the rank of Secretary has been created vide Government Order dated 21.09.2023. A senior administrative officer/ expert in the field of mining is to be appointed to this post with the approval of the competent authority from the Department of Industry, Energy, Labour and Mining. Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a selection committee to select a suitable candidate for the post and a screening committee to scrutinize the applications received from the candidates.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In the context of the Government decision dated 21.09.2023, it is proposed to formulate the service entry rules for the post of Director General created under the Directorate of Geology and Mining, as it will take some time, but since the post needs to be filled up immediately, the Director General will issue an advertisement for filling up the post and recommend to the Government to select suitable candidates from the eligible candidates. A selection committee headed by the Chief Secretary has been set up. The composition of the committee will be as follows:
