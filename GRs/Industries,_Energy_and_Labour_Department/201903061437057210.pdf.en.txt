Government's decision:
The scheme approved by the Central Government as" Atal Solar Krishi Pump Scheme" for installing solar agricultural pumps is being implemented by MSEDCL. Accordingly, the Government has approved the transfer of 5% share of the State Government amounting to Rs. 9,65,36, 550/- (Rupees Nine Crores Sixty Five Lakhs Thirty Six Thousand Five Hundred and Fifty only) to the Director General, Maharashtra Power Development Agency, Pune for handing over the non-transmission solar farm set up in 5662 towns to the Chairman and Managing Director, Maharashtra State Power Distribution Company Limited, Prakashgarh, Bandra (East), Mumbai (MSEDCL) at a cost of Rs. 320.2731 crore.
Walunj, Joint Secretary (Power), Industries, Power and Labour Department, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai as Controlling Officer and Shri N. R. Dhane, Under Secretary, Industries, Power and Labour Department, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai as Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
The amount should be paid in the name of Director General, Maharashtra Energy Development Agency, Pune. He said that the funds should be made available to the Chairman and Managing Director, Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Limited, Prakashgarh, Bandra (East), Mumbai (MSEDCL) immediately.
Monthly and quarterly reports with information on the cost, objectives and actual achievement of various schemes under the solar power agri-plant installation program should be submitted to the government by the 10th of the next month. The General Authority should ensure that the amount of funds sanctioned under this Government decision will be spent only for the financial year 2018-19 for the implementation of various schemes under the Solar Energy Agro-plantation Programme.
The documents relating to the accounting of the said fund should be submitted by Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Limited, Prakashgarh, Bandra (East), Mumbai to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai, for inspection, as and when required.
