Delegation visit to IONQ Facility at Basel, Switzerland.

Industries, Energy, Labour & Mining Department,

Date : 13 November, 2025
PREAMBLE
Dr. P. Anbalagan, IAS, Secretary, (Industries), Government of Maharashtra
and Mr. Prakash Chauhan, Chief Engineer, MIDC have been invited by Scandian AB
to visit the IONQ facility in Basel, Switzerland, between 7th and 10th November
2025. The visit aims to further collaboration on Quantum Computing Technology as
part of a Memorandum of Understanding to establish advanced quantum infrastructure
in Maharashtra, India. The visit will allow direct engagement with the IONQ technical
Team and review Quantum Technology Systems to support Maharashtra's initiative in
this field.
2. The visit will provide an opportunity for the delegation to engage directly with
the IONQ technical leadership, review the advanced quantum computing systems, and
discuss the roadmap for implementing this transformative technology within
Maharashtra. We believe this interaction will further strengthen our collaborative
efforts and accelerate the realization of the project's objectives. However, This
invitation 1s focused on continuing the combined mission between Maharashtra and
Switzerland to establish Maharashtra as a global hub for quantum technology.
Hon'ble Chief Minister of Maharashtra has approved Dr. P. Anbalagan, IAS,
Secretary, (Industries) to visit the IONQ facility in Basel, Switzerland for the period
of 7 November to 10 November, 2025 (including travel time).
RESOLUTION:-
Government of Maharashtra is now pleased to accord ex-post facto approval to
the visit of Dr. P. Anbalagan, IAS, Secretary (Industries), Government of Maharashtra
to visit IONQ facility in Basel, Switzerland for the period of 7th to 10th November,
2025 (Uncluding travel time) .

2. The Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India, New Delhi vide their
letters dated 05.11.2025 have approved the said visit of delegation of Maharashtra to
IONQ facility in Basel, Switzerland.
3. The member of the official delegation will be entitled for travel expenses and
per diem allowance as per prescribed norms of Government of India.
4. State Government is further pleased to authorize the absence of Dr. P.
Anbalagan, I.A.S., Secretary (Industries), Industries, Energy, Labour and Mining
Department from 07.11.2025 to 10.11.2025 (Including travel time) be treated as on
duty until they resume their duties on return to India from their foreign tour.
5. State Government is pleased to approve the ex-post facto sanction processing
fees of visa for Germany, as per actual and medical facilities admissible, under the
revised AMA Scheme continued in the Ministry of External Affairs O.M.No.Q/
GA/653/1/74 dated 5/3/1979 to them. The Government is also pleased to approve the
ex-post facto sanction charges for medical insurance for them.
6. The entire expenditure on travel and accommodation etc. for the members of
delegation will be borned by the Maharashtra Industrial development Corporation.
7. The detailed report for the tour and outcomes of the said tour with suggestions
if any should be submitted to the government within one month from the date of
returning.
