Preamble: The National Human Rights Commission, New Delhi had received a complaint regarding the death of 3 persons while cleaning a sewerage tank at Anuradha Industrial Estate, Kashimira, Mauje Ghodbunder G. Thane in the limits of Mira-Bhayandar Municipal Corporation. In this regard, the Deputy Commissioner (Law), Shri. 2297/13/30/ 2013, dated 7/2/2018. It has been observed that after such an incident, the claims of the heirs of the workers concerned for compensation under the Employees Compensation Act, 1923 are not being filed with the Commissioner, Workers Compensation. Accordingly, instructions have been received to issue circulars from the government level to instruct the concerned field offices to take necessary action. Following instructions have been issued in this regard:
Circular: 1) Due to lack of knowledge and ignorance of the provisions made for getting compensation under the Employees Compensation Act, 1923, in most of the cases, the claims/ applications from the heirs of the workers concerned do not reach the Commissioner, Workers Compensation. Therefore, with a view to give wide publicity to the provisions of the said Act, the Commissioner of Labour, Mumbai/ Commissioner, Workers Compensation, Mumbai and Chairman, Industrial Court, Maharashtra, Mumbai should immediately publish the same on their website.
(2) Publicity of the provisions of the said Act should be done through television, All India Radio, newspapers and other effective media for creating awareness with a view to making the provisions of the said Act available to the public.
(3) When an incident is reported, the officers of the concerned regional office of the Labour Department should immediately visit the place of occurrence and report the incident.

The information should be obtained by conducting necessary inquiry and the report should be submitted to the office of the Commissioner, Workers Compensation immediately so that action can be taken as per the provisions of the said Act.
4) It has been observed that after the occurrence of such an incident, claims/ applications for compensation under the Employees Compensation Act, 1923, from the heirs of the workers concerned are not being filed with the Commissioner, Workers Compensation. Therefore, after such an incident is noticed, the Labour Commissioner, Mumbai and the zonal office under him should immediately contact the concerned and take action to receive the prescribed applications/ claims from them and also guide them from time to time.
(5) The Commissioner, Workers Compensation, Mumbai shall take necessary action as per the provisions of the said Act immediately after receiving the report from the Regional Office of the Labour Department.
The Urban Development Department and the Rural Development Department should take action at their divisional level to instruct the local authorities to take appropriate action with a view to giving wide publicity to the provisions of the Employees Compensation Act, 1923 as mentioned above and to provide necessary guidance to the concerned.
7) The Labour Commissioner, Mumbai should review the matter from time to time and submit a report to the government.
