GOVERNMENT ORDER: The Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC), Government Labour Officer (Group-B) cadre under the Commissionerate of Labour under the Department of Industries, Energy and Labour, has approved the creation of a total of 48 supernumerary posts in the pay scale S-15 (41800- 132300) in the revised pay matrix for a period of two years from 03.10.2023 to 29.09.2025 for a total of 48 candidates who have been selected on the basis of the final result of the State Service (Main) Examination 2021 and joined training at Vanamati, Nagpur.
The 48 supernumerary posts are being created on the establishment of Labour Commissioner, Mumbai under the Department of Industry, Energy and Labour.
Expenditure on the salary of probationary officers for a period of two years from 03.10.2023 to 29.09.2025 for the said 48 supernumerary posts in the cadre of Government Labour Officer (Group-B) in the establishment of the Labour Commissioner, Mumbai, under Demand No.K-8, Head Account Head 3451 003- Training (00) (01) Training to Government Employees (Programme) (34512891)- 20- Other administrative expenses shall be covered under this head and the same shall be covered by the grant sanctioned for that year.
Guidelines for Combined Peripheral Training Programme-9 (CPTP-9) have been issued under the order of General Administration Department at Reference No. 4. Accordingly, instructions have been issued to register these trainee officers in the service system, obtain the DCPS number, appoint Drawing and Disbursing Officers for their salary, provide regular salary of the concerned post to the probationers from the first month of training, prepare service books of the probationers, etc. in the first 4 weeks of training. Therefore, taking into consideration the directions of this circular, the necessary action should be taken by the Additional Labour Commissioner, Nagpur as the nodal officer under the guidance of the Labour Commissioner.
This Government Order is being issued by the Finance Department in accordance with the provisions of Government Resolution No. 2016/ No. 7/16/ Financial Reforms-1, dated 20.02.2016.
