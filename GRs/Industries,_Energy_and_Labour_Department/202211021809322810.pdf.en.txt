No. 170/ Energy-4 GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the guidelines issued by the Ministry of Power, Government of India regarding payment of compensation for land damage due to construction of High Pressure Transmission Channels and the request made by the Maharashtra State Electricity Transmission Company, public representatives, farmers, landholders, general public and various transmission licensees in the state to increase the compensation for land damage and delay in the construction of transmission lines to various transmission licensees, the following decisions have been taken: The Government hereby approve the revised policy for payment of compensation for the land occupied by the tower and under the belt of the High Pressure Transmission Channel for the construction of High Pressure Transmission Channels for the entire State of Maharashtra (all urban and rural areas) by superseding the Government decision dated 31.05.2017 as follows:
1) The area covered by the high-pressure tower, according to the calculation of the land, district level/
The sub-divisional level appraisal committee should be paid at the rate twice the rate fixed in the government's market rate sheet (Ready Reckoner) in the relevant area prevailing at that time or the average rate determined on the basis of the purchase/ sale transaction of land in the last 3 years, whichever is higher.
(2) The computed area of land under the Line Corridor of the High Pressure Transmission Line shall be compensated at the rate of 15% of the average rate determined on the basis of the Government's market rate of return in the relevant area in force or the transaction of purchase/ sale of land in the last 3 years.
In addition to the remuneration mentioned in the above 2 2, additional remuneration in the form of" Non-Utilization Allowance" shall be paid at the rate of 15% of the average rate, whichever is higher, of the land area under the Line Corridor of the High Pressure Transmission Channel, as determined on the basis of the Government's Market Rate Sheet (Ready Reckoner) in the relevant area prevailing at that time or the land transaction in the last 3 years.
In short, for areas falling under the Line Corridor of High Pressure Transmission, remuneration will be paid at the rate of 15% as above 2 and an additional 15% as above 3 at the rate of 30%.
5) The compensation for the land under the belt of the super high pressure transmission line and the area occupied by the tower as above shall be determined by the Sub-Divisional Level Evaluation Committee.
(6) The Sub-Divisional Evaluation Committee at the sub-divisional level shall have the power to determine the appropriate compensation for the affected site in consultation with the transmission licensee in exceptional/ exceptional circumstances.
7) If the compensation for land damage determined by the assessment committee is not acceptable to the affected farmer/ land owner or the holder of the transmission license, such person/ institution shall be liable to pay compensation to the affected farmer/ land owner.

However, if the compensation for the land damaged due to the High Pressure Towers or High Pressure Transmission Channels in the project under construction i.e., which is yet to be commissioned, has been finalized and paid, and if the compensation for the land affected due to such High Pressure Towers and High Pressure Transmission Channels is higher as per the revised policy, the difference will be payable to the concerned land owner. However, if the compensation for land loss is less as per the revised policy, the difference will not be recovered from the land owner.
17) This Government decision will not be applicable to High Pressure Transmission Channel Projects commissioned on the date of issue.
18) The above policy shall be applicable to Government/ semi-government and private transmission licensee companies/ institutions.

NO. 170/ ENERGY-4 2.2 The land occupied by the High Pressure Tower and under the Line Corridor of the High Pressure Transmission Channel constructed under the concerned Sub-Division should be counted by the Transmission Licensee through the Land Records Department and the compensation for the compensation of the land should be determined by the Reference Committee in the above 2.1 as per the new revised policy.
2.3 If the compensation for land compensation fixed by the above Committee is not acceptable to the affected farmer/ land owner or transmission licensee, such person/ institution may appeal to the Appellate Committee headed by the concerned Collector.
2.4 The Appellate Committee shall consist of the District Collector concerned, the concerned Sub-Divisional Officer (Provincial), the representative authorised by the State Transmission Undertaking, the representative of the concerned Transmission Company.
2.5 If the Appellate Committee headed by the Collector finds the appeal to be true, the above committee will re-evaluate or send the appeal to the Sub-Divisional Level Evaluation Committee for reconsideration or reject the appeal.
2.6 The detailed functions and procedures of the Sub-Divisional Level Evaluation Committee and the District Level Appellate Committee shall be determined separately by the Government.
