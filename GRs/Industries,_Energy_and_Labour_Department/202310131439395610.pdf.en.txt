Establishment of a Study Group to notify the service services of Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board in the Labour Ward of Industries, Energy, Labour and Mining Department under the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Industry, Energy, Labour and Mining
Government Resolution No. 2023/ P. No. 187/ Labourer-9
Madam Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk,
Ministry, Mumbai 400 032.
Date is October 12, 2023
For effective implementation of the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, a total of 41 services offered by the Regional Office of the Labour Department have been notified under the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act. A total of 31 types of services are provided online under the Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board. However, it has been observed that the service is not notified under the Right to Service Act. Therefore, the matter of setting up a study group on how the service can be notified was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decisions: Under the Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board, a total of 31 types of services are provided online on the website of the department. However, since these services are not notified under the Right to Public Services Act, a Study Group is being constituted as to how many of these 31 types of services can be notified under the Right to Public Services:
Shri Dadasaheb Khattal, Deputy Secretary (Labour), Department of Industries, Power, Labour and Mines, Ministry of Labour& Employment, Mumbai-Chairman.
Mr. Vivek Kumbhar, Secretary and Mr. Member, Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board, Bandra (East), Mumbai.
Dhanaji Shinde, Chief Finance and Accounts Officer, Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board, Bandra (East), Mumbai- Member Secretary.
The committee has to submit its report to the government within a month.
