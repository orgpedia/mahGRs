Government Circular: -
Our country has adopted a democratic system, with registered citizens over the age of 18 expected to vote in every election. In view of this, Section 135 (b) of the Representation of the People Act, 1951 provides for paid leave on the day of poll to enable voters to exercise their franchise in general or reasonable relaxation of working hours in certain places. However, in the last few elections, it has been observed that some organizations / establishments etc. do not provide paid leave or concessions to their workers. As a result, many voters are deprived of their right to vote, which is extremely dangerous for democracy.
2. As per the order issued by the State Election Commission vide the letter in reference. On 02/12/2025, general elections to Municipal Councils B Nagar Panchayats are to be held as mentioned in the accompanying Annexure-I. In order to enable all electors to exercise their right to vote properly in these elections, and to enable all electors to exercise their right to vote properly in the elections as mentioned in Annexure-9 in the bordering district of the State of Maharashtra, the following orders are issued: -
1) Workers / officers / employees who are voters in the polling area where the election is to be held, even if they are working outside the polling area for work, should be given paid leave to exercise their right to vote on the day of the election.
il) The holiday shall apply to all establishments, factories, shops, etc., falling under the Industries, Energy and Labour Department (e.g. Establishments in private companies, all shops and other establishments, residential, hotel, eateries, other houses, theatres, trade, industrial undertakings or other establishments as well as IT companies, shopping centres, malls, retailers, etc.)
I) The establishment owners concerned shall be required to take care that in exceptional circumstances, if it is not possible to give full day leave to the concerned officers, staff, workers, etc. in respect of dangerous or public service or establishments where the absence would cause great loss, then two to three hours of special leave shall be provided in lieu of leave to exercise the right to vote.

IV) All establishments, factories, shops, etc., falling under the Industries, Energy and Labour Department as mentioned above shall strictly ensure that the above instructions are duly complied with by the owners / management. It is not possible to vote because the voter does not receive the appropriate coupon or discount for voting.
If complaints are received, action will be taken against the establishments concerned.
