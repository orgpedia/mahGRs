Preamble: In order to attract large investments in the field of ready-made garment manufacturing, gems and jewellery, micro electronics and engineering, the Government was considering to determine the criteria for various parameters for setting up of industrial complexes/ parks and their registration procedure.
Government Decision: The modalities for setting up of industrial parks/ gardens in the areas of readymade garment manufacturing, gems and jewellery, micro electronics and engineering components have been detailed in the Annexure. The accompanying annexure shall form part of the said Government Decision. The government is giving approval to take action according to that procedure.

4.7 LETTER OF INTENT FOR PARK: The owner (promoters)/ developer will have to obtain a letter of intent for the proposed industrial park.
While approving the Industrial Park, the developer's net worth, experience, financial arrangements for the construction of the project, etc. should be scrutinized and approved on the basis of merit. The validity of the contract will be 3 years. It will be mandatory for the developer to complete the construction of the industrial park and register with the Directorate of Industries.
The developer will be responsible for providing infrastructure and ancillary services to the unit at the time of taking the letter of intent. If the infrastructure and other ancillary services and facilities in the industrial complex are not provided, the local authority will be liable for penal action as per the provisions of the Development Manual/ Maharashtra Real Estate Regulatory Authority (MahaRERA).
4.8 Extension of Intent of Park: If the Industrial Park cannot be established within the period of 3 years mentioned above, the promoter/ developer may proceed with the same with a valid reason e.g. Details of construction, photographs of construction, plan for completion of further construction, along with certificate of architect, supporting documents and supporting documents, subject to a maximum of two extensions for a period of one year each, on submission of application for extension of tenure, will be granted by the competent authority based on the merit of the case. At the time of taking the second extension, it will be mandatory to submit an architect's certificate on completion of at least 30 percent of the approved construction area. In certain circumstances, if 30% of the construction of the park is not completed, then a decision will be taken at the level of Secretary (Industries) to examine all the aspects for the second extension.
4.9 Project Intent Certificate for Parks Despite completing the construction within a period of 5 years from the date of issue and applying to the Planning Authority for obtaining the occupancy certificate, if there is a delay in obtaining the occupancy certificate from the Planning Authority due to technical reasons, the developer will be required to inform the concerned in writing. In such a case, the Industries Department as the administrative department will be the competent authority to take the decision of the government.
4.10 Registration of Industrial Parks:
(a) After the developer has completed the construction of the industrial park and obtained the Building Completion Certificate (BCC/ OC) from the Planning Authority, the developer will be required to apply for obtaining permanent registration within six months.
