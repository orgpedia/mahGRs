Objective:- The main objective is to utilize the financial provision available for the development of infrastructure under the Special Group Incentive Scheme for entrepreneurs belonging to Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar (SC/ ST) category to create infrastructure for industrial estates/ clusters to be set up at local places for SC/ ST entrepreneurs in the State and thereby achieve all-inclusive industrial and social development of the State.
Scheme Details: Under the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar (SC/ ST) Special Group Incentive Scheme for Entrepreneurs announced by the Government of Maharashtra for SC/ ST entrepreneurs in the State, 100% financial assistance will be provided by the State Government for essential infrastructure (roads, electricity, water) for industrial group projects approved with Central Government and State Government assistance as per the provisions in the Budget. More than 50% of the beneficiaries in this group scheme will be from the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes category. The funds for the infrastructure facilities in the said Group Industrial Development Blocks such as roads, electricity, water, Community Processing Centre and Testing Centre will be funded from the funds made through budgetary provision by the Department of Social Justice and Tribal Development.
Management of Funds: The Principal Secretary (Industries) will be the controlling authority to manage and control the funds made available by the Social Justice Department and Tribal Development Department as above. The Development Commissioner (Industries) will be responsible for coordination, scrutiny of proposals and review of progress as well as procurement and disbursement.
Project Duration: Project Sanction Orders will be issued by the Directorate of Industries after final approval of the proposals by the State Level Steering Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary (Industries). The project should be completed within two years from the date of approval. The Steering Committee shall have the power to grant extension of time on merit basis to the project concerned in case of need of extension in certain cases.
Eligibility Criteria: (i) Infrastructure projects in industrial estates which are directly related to industrial development or which have proven to be conducive to industrial development and are undertaken to attract investment in the industrial sector and in which at least 50% of the beneficiaries are from the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes category will be eligible under the scheme. The project promoter is a person belonging to the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes category or an institution/ industrial group established by them.

Functions of State Level Industrial Infrastructure Steering Committee:
The State Level Industrial Infrastructure Steering Committee will be the apex body for final approval of infrastructure proposals. The Development Commissioner (Industries) shall submit the list of eligible proposals to the State Government for final approval after ensuring that the proposals approved in principle by the State Level Industrial Infrastructure Coordination Committee have completed the necessary effective stages for final approval. The Committee will take a decision on the final approval of eligible proposals which have received in-principle approval from the State Level Industrial Infrastructure Coordination Committee.
The implementation of the project:
(a) The Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation will act as the project implementing agency for the development activities under the Thematic Industrial Infrastructure Development Programme with the grant-in-aid sanctioned by the State Government and in the manner prescribed. The Development Commissioner (Industries) will take necessary action regarding the disbursement of funds after the concerned promoter completes the necessary aspects of the subsidy amount for the projects that have been given final approval by the State Level Industrial Infrastructure Steering Committee. As the implementing agency, the Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC) will complete the technical work of preparing the project budget, determining the technical feasibility, construction plan and approval, and project execution, and transfer the project to the promoter for implementation after the project is technically completed. An internal committee of promoters and entrepreneurs will be set up for day-to-day operations of the project and for maintenance, repair and upkeep. The committee will have representatives from the District Industries Centre, Social Justice and Tribal Development Department and Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC). More than 50% of the total industrial area to be covered under the project will be reserved for SC/ ST category and the land will be made available to SC/ ST category entrepreneurs on long-term lease at affordable rates (at least 30% of the prevailing rate in the industrial area of MIDC in the periphery). The proportion of industrial plots available under reserved areas shall be more than 50% of the total number of plots. Procedure for submission of projects/ proposals:
(a) The applicant shall submit two copies of the proposal to the Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai, on the recommendation of the District Level Committee. All the proposals will be submitted to the State Level Industrial Infrastructure Coordination Committee after scrutiny by the District Level Committee.
The eligible projects will be given in-principle approval after a decision is taken by the State Level Committee headed by the Development Commissioner (Industries) to decide on the further approval of the proposal. For projects which have been approved in-principle, the concerned Project Promoters shall complete and process the necessary formalities for grant of final approval to the project within a period of six months.
b) Under this, a special vehicle purpose vehicle will be set up consisting of the project promoter and the beneficiary proposed entrepreneur. It will also ensure compliance of necessary clearances, documents, no-objection certificates, etc. for the project and assured availability of share capital in addition to grant. Only after this completion, the Development Commissioner (Industries) will submit the proposals to the State Level Industrial Infrastructure Steering Committee for final approval of the proposals.
The method of distribution of funds:
For this purpose, funds will be released by the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance and the Department of Tribal Development under the scheme code numbers (28520031) and (28521013) respectively, using the regular budgetary allocation made for the respective year. The State Level Steering Committee will take action on the construction of the project with the coordination of the promoter and with the help of the District Committee and MIDC to facilitate the construction and implementation of the projects under the final approval and to complete the project within the stipulated time. The funds will be provided with the approval and recommendation of the State Level Industrial Infrastructure Coordination Committee headed by the Development Commissioner (Industries). The Drawing and Disbursing Officer of the scheme will be the Development Commissioner (Industries).
