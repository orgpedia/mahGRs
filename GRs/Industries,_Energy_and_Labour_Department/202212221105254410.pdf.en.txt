Government's decision:
India has expressed its commitment to Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) on emissions and expects all states to act in accordance with those goals to meet the revised commitments made at the COP-26 global conference in Glasgow. As per the directions given in the meeting held on 09.02.2022 under the Chairmanship of Union Minister, Ministry of Power and Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India, the following State Level Steering Committee are being constituted in the State with the approval of the Government to promote" Energy Transition" measures:
Chief Secretary, Maharashtra State President 2) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Urban Development Department Member 3) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Industries Department Member 4) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Housing Department Member 5) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Transport Department Member 6) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Agriculture Department Member 7) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Rural Development Department Member 8) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Environment Department Member 9) Additional Chief Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Energy Department Member-Secretary
The following members will be invited to the Committee: 1. Chairman and Managing Director, MSEDCL, Mumbai.
Chairman and Managing Director, DGM, Mumbai.
3. Director General, Power, Pune.
Vishwas Pathak, Independent Director, MSEB Holding Company, Mumbai.
Managing Directors of other Private Power Distribution Companies.
The mandate of the Committee: (a) The Committee will work towards devising a strategy for" Energy Transition" measures in the State.
States and UTs will have to work together on various avenues for energy transition. Such as, 1) Adding renewable energy (renewable energy) to the power generation mix to meet the ever-increasing demand for electricity. 2) Promote energy efficiency. Increased use of biomass and green hydrogen. The committee will look into the matter.
Implementing e-measures, increasing the use of biomass in thermal power stations, replacing conventional street lights with LED street lights, replacing traditional diesel engines with non-conventional energy sources (PM)
