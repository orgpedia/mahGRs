The Proposal:
Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India is implementing Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM) for farmers across the country with a mission to electrify the agricultural pump power connections in the State through solar energy. Under this scheme, the central government. Considering the approval given on 13 th January, 2021 to install 1 lakh Nagas of solar agricultural pumps, the Government has announced a mission to electrify the agricultural pump power connections in the State through solar energy by the decision dated 12 th May, 2021. Under Component B, 5 lakh solar agri pumps will be installed in the next 5 years at the rate of 1 lakh nos per annum. It is being implemented by the government. Under Kusum Scheme Phase-I, 2491 pumps have been installed. In the meeting held on 10 th February, 2022, it was approved to install 50,000 nos of solar farm units under KUSUM Phase-II in FY 2022-23 and thereafter, the remaining 46500 nos of solar farm units have been approved on 25.01.2023. The Central Government has approved on 26.04.2023 to install these pumps by 31.12.2023. Accordingly, 54893 pumps (2491+46216+ 6186) have been installed by MahaUrja, which has now been increased to 55000 pumps. Under this scheme, 10 per cent subsidy will be given to the beneficiaries of the general category. 19.1802 crore was under the consideration of the Government.

Government's decision:
As part of the mission to electrify the agricultural pump connections in the state through solar energy, the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India, has sanctioned 1,00,000 non-transmission solar agricultural pumps to MahaUrja in the first phase under Component B of the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam UtthanMahabhiyan (KUSUM) nationwide campaign for farmers. Out of the total 1,00,000 non-transmission solar agriculture pumps sanctioned, the Government will provide 10% share of the non-transmission solar agriculture pumps to be installed at the farm water supply source of the beneficiary. The Government has approved to disburse Rs. 19.1802 crore (Rupees Nineteen Crore Eighty Eight Lakhs Two Thousand only) to Maha Urja through NEFT/ RTGS.
