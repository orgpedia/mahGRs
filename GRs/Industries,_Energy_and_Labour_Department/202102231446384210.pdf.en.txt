The Proposal:
In order to enable the farmers to irrigate during the day and to achieve the objective of saving the cost of connecting the traditional agricultural pumps and the subsidy provided by the state government, the" Mukhyamantri Saur Krishi Pump Yojana" has been approved by the government decision dated November 15, 2018 and September 11, 2019 to provide 1 lakh non-transmission solar agricultural pumps to the farmers of the state in a phased manner. " Mukhyamantri Solar Krishi Pump Yojana" is a fully state government scheme under which one lakh solar agricultural pumps are planned to be installed in a phased manner. In the first phase, 25,000 nos of solar agri pumps have been installed and in the second and third phase, a target of installing 75,000 nos of solar agri pumps has been set. The general category beneficiaries will be given 10 per cent of the state's budgetary subsidy, 10 per cent of the beneficiary share and the remaining 80 per cent will be paid from the mutual amount collected from the tax on sale of surplus power in the escrow account of MSEDCL.
As per the decision of the government, the general category beneficiaries of the 75,000 solar agricultural pumps in the second phase have been considered for 10 percent of the state government's subsidy of Rs. 118.36 crore. A sum of Rs. 3.37098 crore has been allocated for 2019-20 for this purpose. Now, considering the suggestions of the Department of Finance in the Government Resolution dated 04.05.2020, an order was issued on 19.06.2020 to disburse the provision of Rs. 25.00 crore in cash to MSEDCL
