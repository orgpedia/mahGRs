The Maharashtra State Industrial Cluster Development Programme (MSICDP) is a scheme to promote the development of small clusters in industrially underdeveloped areas of the state. This was announced by the Government on 25/02/2014. The revised guidelines have been issued on 24/07/2019. The State Government has decided to extend the benefit of this scheme to women entrepreneurs as well as entrepreneurs belonging to Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes to enhance their social and economic empowerment. 11/02/2016 and dt. As per the Government decision dated 14/12/2017, industrial cluster projects with 100% women entrepreneurship and 100% SC/ ST entrepreneurship members have been approved for availability of subsidy up to 90%. The scheme will benefit 100 per cent of the projects sanctioned under the Maharashtra State Industrial Cluster Development Scheme for women, scheduled castes and scheduled tribes. In the Government Resolution dated 25.02.2014, vide para No. Relaxation of clause (iv) of section 8 (ii) (c) was under consideration of the Government.

Government's decision:
In order to increase the social and economic potential of women entrepreneurs and entrepreneurs belonging to SC/ ST category by taking advantage of the Maharashtra State Industrial Cluster Development Programme (MSICDP) scheme of the state government, the government has provided subsidy up to 90% of the project cost (maximum subsidy limit Rs. 10 crore). To benefit from this provision, eligible projects in the above mentioned categories will be eligible for the scheme.
