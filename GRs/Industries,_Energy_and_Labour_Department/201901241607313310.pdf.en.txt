Introduction: The Government of Maharashtra is constantly striving to ensure that industrial workers get the benefit of better health, safety and welfare schemes. Factory management is also important. Keeping this in mind, factory management should also spontaneously follow safety work culture. For this, the government was considering to take some measures to encourage factory management. As a part of this, to honour the best performing factories in the field of industrial safety, health and welfare schemes, the modalities and contours of the award and all related aspects are being decided and decided as follows.
National Safety Day is celebrated on March 4. In recognition of this, the best performing factories in the field of industrial safety, health and welfare schemes will be awarded. The government has decided to give the award in the name of Narayan Meghaji Lokhande Industrial Safety and Health. The criteria for selection for the award, the modalities of the award, the selection committee for selecting the awardees, the advertisement process, etc. have been incorporated in the Government decision and are as follows: Terms and Conditions for participation in the award competition 1) The registered factories under the Factories Act of Maharashtra can participate in the competition.
2) The production process in the factory must have been started at least 1 calendar year before the year of award, and the factory must have received a permanent license.
3) Participating factories are required to submit the application in the prescribed format within the stipulated time.

4) The application fee must be paid in the following manner:
(a) Factories with up to 250 workers Rs.1500/ (b) Factories with 251 to 1000 workers Rs.3000/ (c) Factories with more than 1000 workers Rs.4500/
The said fee shall be credited under the head 0230, Labour& Employment, 800, Other Deposits, (01) Other Deposits, (01) Other Deposits (02300107). In each group, there will be three categories of factories with up to 250 workers, 251 factories with up to 1000 workers and more than 1000 factories. The total number of prizes will be 18.
The application period for the competition will be from January 1 to January 31 every year. At the end of February, the zonal evaluation committees constituted by the respective state level selection committees will evaluate the applications and report the scores to the state level selection committee. Accordingly, the final winners will be selected by the State Level Committee and awards will be distributed during the Safety Week.

The State Level Selection Committee is being constituted to select the best performing factories in the field of industrial safety, health and welfare schemes as under: Chairman Principal Secretary (Labour), Industries, Power and Labour Department, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai Member-Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai Member-Director, National Safety Council, Mumbai Member-Director (Safety), Directorate General of Factory Advice Services and Labour Institute, Vyavasayi Suraksha Aur Swasthya Bhavan, Sion, Mumbai
Member Secretary- Additional Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai 2) The State Level Selection Committee will constitute the Evaluation Committee for each Revenue Division.
(3) The Director of Industrial Safety and Health shall take action to accept the applications from the Director of Industrial Safety and Health and forward the applications received from the factories to the Evaluation Committee of the Department in which the factory is located.
4) For the preliminary screening and evaluation of the applications, the evaluation committee of the members of the route and other industrial safety and health office members under the chairmanship of the joint/ deputy director at the divisional level will visit the concerned factories and evaluate their performance.

The committee will make the assessment and submit its report to the state-level selection committee by the end of February.
The State Level Selection Committee will select five factories in each category in order of merit. These five factories will have to make presentations before the State Level Selection Committee regarding the safety, health and welfare schemes of the respective factories.
6) The state-level selection committee will give the factory a score out of 10. Also, the marks given by the evaluation committee will be scrutinized by the state-level selection committee.
In this way, the State Level Selection Committee will determine the names of the 3 factories eligible for the prize in each category by determining the final score, including the score given by the Evaluation Committee and the score of the State Level Selection Committee, and if necessary, will decide to give a certificate as a stimulus prize in each category.
7) The State Level Selection Committee will announce the names of the award winning factories during the Safety Week and accordingly the Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai will organize the prize distribution ceremony.
The entry form for the factory to participate in the award competition will be as per Form" A."
Also, the guidelines for the evaluation committee will be as per Form B.
4. The Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health shall meet the expenses for the award of best performing factories in the field of industrial safety, health and welfare schemes and for the purpose of conducting prize ceremonies, etc., from his office expenses.
