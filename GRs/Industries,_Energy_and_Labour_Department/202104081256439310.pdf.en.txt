The order:
The Public Health Department, vide reference no. 3, d. Dr. Ambadas Mahadev Devam, Medical Superintendent, Group-A, Public Health Department, has been approved for deputation as Deputy Director (Medical), Group-A, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai, by retaining the Revenue Department after promotion as on 12/02/2021. The Department of Public Health has given this objection subject to the condition that Dr. Devmane should complete at least three years of service in the Nagpur Revenue Division allotted to him excluding the period of deputation.
Accordingly, subject to the following terms and conditions, the appointment of Dr. Ambadas Mahadev Devmane, Medical Superintendent, Group-A as Deputy Director (Medical), Group-A, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai, on deputation for a period of one year or until regular appointment to the said post, whichever is earlier, is approved:
If the Government deems it necessary in the interest of public service to recall the services of Dr. Ambadas Mahadev Devamane, Medical Superintendent, the Government/ Competent Authority i.e. Department of Public Health shall have the power to recall him at any time before the expiry of the period of deputation.
If their services are not required by the non-employer, the Department of Industry, Energy and Labour shall have the right to send them back to the parent department. However, before such repatriation, the non-recipient employer shall give a notice of three months to the competent authority i.e. Department of Public Health; and
The Department of Industry, Energy and Labour shall be at liberty to return Dr. Devamane to the parent Department after giving at least three months notice to the Department of Industry, Energy and Labour that he intends to return to the parent Department.
Dr. Devmane has been appointed as Maharashtra Civil Service (Period of Incorporation, Non-Ministerial Service). (e) The provisions of Rules 39 and 40 (2) and (3) of the 1981 Rules shall continue to apply. Also, the provisions of the Government Decision dated 17/12/2016 in the context of the General Administration Department will also continue to apply.
Dr. Devmane's service matters will be handled by his parent department, the Department of Public Health.
