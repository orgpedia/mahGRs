PREAMBLE
Vide letter dated 11/12/2024 Shri. Borge Brende, President, World Economic Forum, 91-
93 route de la Capite, CH-1223 Cology /Genva, Switzerland invited Hon'ble Chief Minister,
Government of Maharashtra to participate in "World Economic Forum Annual Meeting 2025" at
Davos-Switzerland from 20 "to 24TM January, 2025. The Forum is an international organization for
public-private cooperation which wants to work with Government of Maharashtra on the New
Vision for Agriculture and other significant initiatives for the Maharashtra and India.
The World Economic Forum Annual Meeting is the foremost platform where the world's key
decision-makers come together at the beginning of the year to shape the political, economic, social
and environmental agendas. Over 100 governments, all major international organization, the 1000
most important global companies, relevant civil society and academic institutions, as well as the
global media will interact at the highest level. Davos Serve as a critical platform to build and
advance a collective vision for shared prosperity in response to a rapidly changing geopolitical,
economic and social context driven by the Fourth Industrial Revolution.
In this background the State Government has decided to send Twelve Member's State Level Delegation led by Hon'ble Chief Minister, Government of Maharashtra to attend "World Economic
Forum Annual Meeting 2025" to be held on 20" January to 24" January, 2025 at Davos-
Switzerland.
RESOLUTION:-
Government of Maharashtra is pleased to accord ex-post-facto approval to the visit of the
State level delegation led by Hon'ble Chief Minister, Government of Maharashtra to participate in
"World Economic Forum Annual Meeting 2025" at Davos-Switzerland for the period mentioned
in table below.

During the visit 12 members of delegation will attended the "World Economic Forum
Annual Meeting 2025" at Davos-Switzerland organized by World Economic Forum (WEF).
2. The Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India, New Delhi vide their letter dated
31/12/2024 have approved the visit of delegation of Maharashtra to Davos-, Switzerland.
3. All the members of the official delegation will be entitled for travel expenses and per diem
allowance as per prescribed norms of Government of India.
4. State Government is further pleased to authorize the absence of Shri Vikas Kharage, Additional Chief Secretary to Hon. Chief Minister from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Shri Ashwini Bhide, Principal Secretary to Hon. Chief Minister from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Dr. P. Anbalagan, Secretary (Industries) from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Shri Vijay Singhal, Managing Director, City Industrial Development Corporation, Mumbai from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Dr.
Sanjay Mukherjee, Metropolitan Commissioner, Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority, Mumbai from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Shri P. Velrasu, Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation, Mumbai from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Shri Deependra Singh Kushwah, Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Shri Vikas Suryawanshi, General Manager (Marketing), Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation, Mumbai from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Shri Kaustubh Dhavse, Officer on Special Duty to Hon. Chief Minister from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), Shri Prasad Ukarde, Officer on Special Duty to Hon. Minister of Industries from 20/01/2025 to 24/01/2025 (Excluding travel time), participating as part of delegation to attend "World Economic Forum Annual Meeting 2025" to be treated as on duty for the above mentioned period until they resume their duties on return to India from the foreign tour.
5. State Government is pleased to ex-post-facto sanction the processing fees for visa for
Davos-Switzerland, as per actual and medical facilities admissible, under the revised AMA Scheme
continued 11 the Ministry of External Affairs O.M.No.Q/GA/653/1/74 dated 5/3/1979 to them. The
Government is also pleased to post facto sanction the charges for medical insurance for them.
6. The entire expenditure on travel and accommodation etc. for the members of delegation to
be borne by the Maharashtra Industrial development Corporation.
7. The detailed report for the tour and outcomes of the said tour with suggestions if any should
be submitted to the government within one month from the date of returning.
