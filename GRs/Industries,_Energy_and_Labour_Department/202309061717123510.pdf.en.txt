the prelude
The policy of the government is to keep the expenditure on administration under control and to make work through external agencies wherever possible so that adequate funds are available for development work.
Therefore, with the approval of the Finance Department, the Government vide its decision dated 18.06.2014, has decided to extend the grant of Rs. Brisk Facilities Pvt Ltd and Crystal Integrated Services Pvt Ltd. The panel of two bidders was approved for a period of three years. Also, the services of the agencies on the panel constituted under the said government decision were allowed to be taken by other departments of the government.
Since the term of the said panel has expired on 17.06.2017, the said panel was extended by this Department vide letter dated 11.09.2017 for a period of three months or till the selection of a new body through tender process. In the meantime, due to the long period of extension, the extension granted to the said panel has been terminated as per the decision of the Government dated 18.01.2023 of this Department. Also, all administrative departments have been informed that this service provider panel cannot be used to provide manpower from external agencies.
In the meantime, a tender committee under the chairmanship of the Labour Commissioner was formed to appoint a new panel of service provider agencies to provide manpower through external agencies.

It has been made mandatory for the government departments/ semi-government departments/ local bodies/ corporations/ public sector undertakings/ other establishments etc. of the state government to take the services of the service providers on this panel.
After the issuance of this government decision, various organizations, administrative departments have asked this department about the panel created by this government decision as there is no clear mention in the government decision about how much remuneration will be given to the employees appointed by the external agencies appointed by the supplier agencies and how much service fee will be charged by the supplier agencies. It has also been brought to the notice of the department that the manpower rates fixed by the department are high. Therefore, the government was considering the matter of amending the said government decision as it was necessary to bring clarity in the above matters.
Government's decision:
The Government Order dated 14.03.2023 and various orders/ notifications and letters issued in pursuance of this Government Order are being superseded.
The panel of 9 (nine) suppliers named in the proposal for supply of manpower through external agencies such as Government Departments/ Para-Government Departments/ Local Bodies/ Corporations/ Public Sector Undertakings/ Other Establishments etc. of the State Government are being approved by the Government subject to the terms and conditions in paragraph 3 below.

10) If the Central Government has fixed a specific rate for the manpower required for the Central Government scheme, then it will be mandatory for the suppliers to supply the manpower only at the rate fixed by the Central Government for that scheme. The department has prepared a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for effective implementation of this government decision. This SOP shall be as per Annexure-C. The provisions of this SOP will be binding on the consumer departments and agencies.
12) It will be mandatory for the concerned service provider agency to provide their honorarium/ salary in the bank account provided by the empanelled agency on the lines of the Central Government's Gem Portal.
13) The amount of statutory recovery payable to the employee appointed by the external agency must be clearly mentioned in the salary note of the concerned employee and then deposited with the concerned authority. This should be clearly mentioned by the consumer department while making the commissioning order/ agreement with the concerned service provider agency.
It will be mandatory to keep an attendance cum wage register regarding the wages paid to the manpower supplied by the concerned agency and upload it on the HRMS portal of the Labour Department. Also, the Labour Department will have the right to verify this attendance and salary sheet.
15) If the category of manpower determined by this department does not include the manpower category of a department/ office, the concerned department shall inform the Labour Department accordingly. The Labour Department shall determine the nearest category of manpower and make available to the department concerned the rates payable under that category. However, if it is not possible to determine the category of manpower required for a department/ office, the rates of such manpower as per the provisions of Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) at Annexure-C, may be revised taking into account the existing rates being paid to such manpower at present by the respective departments and the rates being paid to such manpower.
