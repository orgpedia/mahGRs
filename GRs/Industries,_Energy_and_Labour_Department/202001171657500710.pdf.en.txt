In line with the Chief Minister's directive to inspect chemical and hazardous industries in all districts of the state, the government was considering the formation of district-wise committees on industrial safety of chemical and hazardous industries. A decision in this regard will be taken later.
Government Decisions In view of the incidents of fire and explosion in chemical industries, the hazardous chemicals handled in such factories, the irregularities in the handling of chemical processes by the administration of the concerned factory, and the negligence regarding the safety of these factories in the implementation of various laws/ rules, the technical and safety inspection of the chemical industries operating should be done immediately and after the defects detected in them, such industries should be allowed to continue.
District-wise committee is being constituted under the chairmanship of Joint Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health for safety inspection of chemical and hazardous industries operating in various districts of the state and in the industrial estates as follows.

The committee will continue to work. However, the members shall act as representatives of the concerned Collector as Secretary.
The functions of the committee are as follows:
1) To obtain a list of hazardous and chemical factories in the district from the Joint Director, Industrial Safety and Health.
2) To inspect hazardous and chemical factories in the district, the Joint Director, Industrial Safety and Health, Deputy Commissioner of Labour, Deputy Regional Officer, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Special Planning Authority, Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation and Fire Officer, Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation should prepare a checklist of all-round inspection as per the act and rules of the concerned department.
3) Teams of technical officers and staff and representatives of ICT/ IIT Bombay should be formed to inspect the hazardous and chemical factories in the district.
The committee should suggest concrete measures to check chemical/ hazardous industries which are not covered under the Factories Act or are operating without any permission and impose restrictions on such industries.
All committees at the district level should complete all inspections within six months and submit a report to the Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health.
