Preamble: The Scheme for Grant-in-Aid to Enterprise Cluster Projects for creating Common State Facility for contributing to the construction of projects under the Micro and Small Enterprises- Cluster Development Programme (MSE-CDP) of the Central Government has been announced vide Government Resolution dated 09/06/2010. Further, as per the Government decision dated 22/10/2010, the State Government's participation under the scheme has been fixed at 10% of the project cost (upper ceiling subsidy limit Rs. 1.50 crore).
Under the Scheme, the Central Government vide its order dated 16/11/2021 has accorded final approval for setting up of Common Facility Centre at Hi-Tech Engineering Cluster, Kagal G. Kolhapur, an industrial cluster project with State Government's participation grant of Rs. 1.50 crore.
As per the guidelines of the Micro, Small Enterprises-College Development Plan (MSE-CDP), the sanctioned grant of the Central Government is disbursed in the ratio of 40:30:30 to the projects sanctioned under the scheme. GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Central Government has accorded final approval for the construction of Common Facility Centre (CFC) at Hi-Tech Engineering Cluster, Kagal G. Kolhapur under Micro and Small Enterprises-Collective Development Scheme (MSE-CDP) under the order dated 16/11/2021 at a total approved project cost of Rs. 2114.21 lakh with Central Government grant of Rs. 1400.00 lakh and State Government participation grant of Rs. 150.00 lakh. Accordingly, the Government have decided to disburse State Participation Grant of Rs. 105.00 lakh (Rs. One Crore Five Lakh only) to Hi-Tech Engineering Cluster, Kagal G. Kolhapur as per the project sanction, out of the total amount of Rs. 150.00 lakh, under the first phase, as per the provisions of the said scheme.
The Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai shall arrange to transfer the amount of Rs. 105.00 lakh (Rupees One Crore Five Lakh only) from the Government treasury to the High-Tech Engineering Cluster, Kagal G. Kolhapur, to the Special Purpose Vehicle Institute for the project. The powers to draw and disburse the aforesaid amount of Rs. 105.00 lakhs (Rupees One Crore Five Lakh only) are being delegated to the Accounts Officer in the office of the Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai.
The Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai shall take necessary action to monitor the progress of this special purpose vehicle and the proper utilization and utilization of the Government share capital.
