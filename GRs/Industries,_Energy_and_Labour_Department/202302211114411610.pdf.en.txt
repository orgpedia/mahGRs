The Proposal:
Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India is implementing Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM) for farmers across the country with a mission to electrify the agricultural pump power connections in the State through solar energy. Under Component B of the Mission, a total of 1,00,000 non-transmission solar agricultural pumps have been sanctioned and are being implemented by MAHAYURGA.
In the first phase of Kusum Yojana, 2478 pumps have been installed. State level committee. In the meeting held on 10 th February, 2022, it has been approved to install 50,000 nos of solar farm equipment under KUSUM Phase-II in FY 2022-23. Accordingly, out of 39400 pumps installed under KUSUM Phase-II, 30534 pumps have been installed for general category beneficiaries, 5320 pumps for beneficiaries of Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and 3546 pumps for beneficiaries of Tribal Development Department. As per the government decision, the beneficiaries of the tribal component of the solar agricultural pump to be installed will have to provide 65 percent of the state government's subsidy.

Government's decision:
As part of the mission to electrify the agricultural pump connections in the state through solar energy, the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India, has approved the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM) for farmers under Component B of the nationwide campaign.
32.50 crore (Rs. Thirty-two crore fifty lakhs only) is being sanctioned by the Government to Maha Urja and this fund is being made available to Maha Urja.
Narayan Karad, Deputy Secretary (Power), Department of Industries, Power and Labour, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai has been posted as Controller, while Mr. N. R. Dhane, Under Secretary, Department of Industry, Power and Labour, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai has been posted as Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
The monthly and quarterly reports with information on the expenditure, objectives and actual achievement of various schemes under the solar power agri-power installation programme should be submitted to the government by the 10th of the next month. It is to be ensured by the Government that the amount sanctioned under this Government decision will be spent for the financial year 2022-23 only for the implementation of the programme of installing solar power agri-pumps for tribal beneficiaries under" KUSUM."
Documents relating to the accounting of the said fund should be submitted for inspection to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai, by the Maharashtra Energy Development Agency (MEDA), Aundh Road, opposite Spicer College, next to the Commissionerate of Animal Husbandry, Aundh, Pune, as and when required.
