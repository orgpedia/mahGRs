The proposal: Large Fire Works, Mt. The day Dahanu G. Palghar 9 workers were injured in the fire accident on 17.06.2021. According to a report published in Hindustan Times, The National Green Tribunal, Principal Bench, New Delhi vide Suo-Moto Original Application No. 134/2021 was filed. In this case, National Green Tribunal, New Delhi The orders have been passed in the hearing conducted through video conference on 25.06.2021. According to the order, The National Green Tribunal, Principal Bench, New Delhi has directed the Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai to conduct a review in coordination with Maharashtra Pollution Control Board and Central Pollution Control Board to take measures at the highest level to avoid such accidents in the state. Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a committee to study and suggest measures to prevent possible accidents, occupational and environmental hazards in the factories that process highly toxic, hazardous and chemical products in the state. A decision in this regard is being taken.
Government Decision: To study and suggest measures to prevent accidents like fire accidents/ explosions occurring in factories processing highly hazardous, hazardous and chemical products in the state, as well as the quantity of materials used in hazardous processes, the number of workers engaged in hazardous processes, safety measures and compliance and the need to study the possible accidents, occupational and environmental hazards due to such processes in the state. The National Green Tribunal, Principal Bench, New Delhi has highlighted. Accordingly, a committee is being constituted under the chairmanship of the Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai, to study and suggest measures to avoid accidents like fire accidents/ explosions in the highly hazardous, hazardous and chemical manufacturing factories in the state.

The mandate of the said Committee shall be as follows: 1) The Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai shall review, in coordination with the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board and the Central Pollution Control Board, to prevent fire accidents/ explosions in the highly hazardous, hazardous and chemical manufacturing plants in the State.
The study to be carried out by the committee should mainly include the number of factories allowed, the size of the factories, the quantity of materials used in the factory, the criteria of the factories, the fire fighting system in the factory, the health and safety of the workers.
3) The Committee may seek the assistance of such expert persons as it may deem necessary for the completion of the said study.
The committee headed by the Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai, shall study the above matters in depth and submit a report to the Government with its recommendations within 3 months.
