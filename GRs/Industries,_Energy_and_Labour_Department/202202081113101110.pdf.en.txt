Preamble: A High Powered Committee under the Chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary has been constituted vide Government Resolution dated 12/09/1984 to approve the budget for participation of State Government in India International Trade Fair and the expenditure to be incurred on it. Accordingly, the committee plans and organizes the program. The expenditure for the programme is proposed to be met from the funds available during the financial year. The Drawing and Disbursing Officer for the event is Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai. Thereafter, vide Government Resolution dated 27/08/1996, the said High Powered Committee has been reconstituted and constituted under the Chairmanship of Secretary (Trade and Commerce), which was earlier headed by Additional Chief Secretary.
India International Trade Fair was organized by India Trade Promotion Organisation, New Delhi from 14th to 27th November, 2021 at Pragati Maidan, New Delhi. General Manager (Handicrafts& Exhibitions), Maharashtra Small Industries Development Corporation Limited.
In pursuance of the proposal received from him, the 67th meeting of the High Powered Committee was held on 20/10/2021 regarding the organization of the Maharashtra Pavilion in the fair. The meeting approved an estimated expenditure of Rs. 1.6 crore for this purpose. Accordingly, the Government was considering to disburse funds to the tune of Rs. 1.284 crore available on the BDS system under the Scheme for Participation in Industrial Exhibitions and Trade Fairs (2851 0398) during the financial year 2021-22 for organizing Maharashtra Pavilion at India International Trade Fair-2021.
Government's decision:
The 67th meeting of the High Powered Committee has approved the estimated cost of Rs. 1.6 crore submitted by General Manager (Handicrafts& Exhibitions), Maharashtra Small Industries Development Corporation for organizing the Maharashtra Pavilion at India International Trade Fair-2021. Participation in Industrial Exhibitions and Trade Fairs during the Financial Year 2021-22. A sum of Rs. 1.284 crore is available for disbursement on BDS system for this scheme, which is 60% of the budgeted amount of Rs. 2.14 crore. A sum of Rs. 1,28,40, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Twenty Eight Lakh Forty Thousand only) as proposed by Maharashtra Small Industries Development Corporation Limited, Mumbai and as approved by the High Powered Committee, is being released to Maharashtra Small Industries Development Corporation Limited, Mumbai for this purpose.
