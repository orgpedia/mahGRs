Introduction: The Government of Maharashtra is constantly striving to ensure that industrial workers get the benefit of better health, safety and welfare schemes. Factory management is also important. Keeping this in mind, the factory management should also spontaneously follow the safety work culture. For this, the government was considering to take some measures to encourage factory management. As a part of this, the process and framework of the award and all related aspects have been decided to honour the best performing factories in the field of industrial safety, health and welfare schemes. The following decisions are being taken by superseding the Government Decision dated 24.01.2019.
National Safety Day is observed on March 4 every year. The government has decided to give awards to the best performing factories in the field of industrial safety, health and welfare schemes in the name of" Narayan Meghaji Lokhande Industrial Safety and Health." The criteria for selection for the award, the procedure for award, the selection committee for selecting the awardees, as enshrined in this Government Resolution are as follows: 1. Terms and Conditions for participation in the award competition 1. Factories registered under the Factories Act in Maharashtra can participate in the competition.
The manufacturing process in the factory must have been in operation at least three calendar years prior to the award year, and the factory must have obtained a permanent license.
3) Participating factories are required to submit the application in the prescribed format within the stipulated period.
4) The application fee must be paid in the following manner:

3. Committee constituted for selection of factories for award and its functions
A State Level Selection Committee is being constituted to select the best performing factories in the field of Industrial Safety, Health and Welfare Schemes.
Principal Secretary (Labour), Department of Industries, Energy and Labour, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai.
Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai.
Director, National Security Council, Mumbai.
Member Director (Safety), Directorate General of Factory Advice Services and Labour Institute, Occupational Safety and Health Building, Sion, Mumbai.
Additional Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai.
(2) The Director shall constitute an Evaluation Committee for each department of the Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health.
3) The Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health shall take action to accept the applications from the factories and forward the applications received from the factories to the Evaluation Committee of the Department in which the factory is located.
(4) For preliminary scrutiny and evaluation of applications, the Evaluation Committee at the Divisional level shall visit the concerned factories and evaluate them and send their reports to the State Level Selection Committee for decision as decided by the Director. The evaluation committee will give marks out of 100 as per the guidelines of Form 2.
The State Level Selection Committee will select five factories in each category in order of merit. These five factories will have to make presentations before the State Level Selection Committee regarding the safety, health and welfare schemes of the respective factories.
6) The state level selection committee will give a score out of 10 to the presentation of the factory. Also, the marks given by the evaluation committee will be scrutinized by the state-level selection committee.
In this way, the State Level Selection Committee will determine the names of the 3 factories eligible for the prize in each category by determining the final score after combining the score given by the Evaluation Committee and the score of the State Level Selection Committee, and if necessary, will decide to give a certificate as a stimulus prize in each category. The State Selection Committee shall have the power to increase or decrease the number of prizes based on the number of applications received.
The State Level Selection Committee will announce the names of the award winning factories and accordingly the Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Maharashtra State, Mumbai will organize the prize distribution ceremony.
4. The entry form for participation in the award competition for the factory will be as per Form" A." Also, the guidelines for the evaluation committee will be as per Form B.
