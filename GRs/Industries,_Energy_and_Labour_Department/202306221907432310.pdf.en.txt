(2) The order shall come into force immediately. The Director, Industrial Safety and Health, shall take necessary action for the immediate release of the said Joint Director, instruct him to appear at the place of his transfer and submit a report to the Government.
(3) After the issuance of this order, it shall be ensured that he joins the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Incumbency, Non-Ministerial Service and Provisions in the Period of Suspension, Dismissal and Removal from Service) Rules, 1981, within the period prescribed in the rules. Otherwise, if he does not join the post, the period of his absence shall be deemed to be" dies non." They should also not be unauthorisedly absent otherwise their absence will be treated as unauthorised absence and will be made without pay. Also, the concerned are being made aware that the possibility of discontinuation of their services cannot be ruled out.

It may be noted that non-compliance of the order passed with the approval of the competent authority under the Transfer Act is serious in nature and the officer/ employee who does not comply with this will be liable for suspension under Section 2.1 (2) (v) of Section-2 of the Departmental Inquiry Rules, 1991.
Since the above officers are being posted on request, the period of posting, travel allowance and daily allowance etc. will not be admissible.
6. The concerned officer should not be granted leave without prior permission of the government. Also, as per Rule-30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, the parent office should not accept any leave applications of the transferred officers. If such applications are received, they should be returned to the concerned authorities at the original address.
7. It may be noted that in case of failure to join the post immediately as per the said order or any attempt to cancel the transfer by exerting any kind of pressure, disciplinary action will be taken against them as the act is in violation of Rule 23 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979.
(8) In accordance with the provisions of section 4 (4) and section 4 (5) of the Maharashtra Government Employees (Regulation of Transfer and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties) Act, 2005, and after taking into consideration the proviso to section 6, clause 2 and section 7 of the said Act, in terms of reference No. 2 These orders are being issued in exercise of the powers conferred on the competent authority under the Government Order.
