Government's decision:
In order to prevent the loss of the grape growing farmers of the state, to encourage the farmers to cultivate grapes, to make dry fruits and to create economically more profitable alternative products from grapes, and to promote the wine industry based on grapes, the government has launched the Grape Processing Industry Policy-2001. 19/10/2001
# Page 2
Government Resolution No: VAPUMA-2020/ PR No.104/ Industries-2
The government has announced the decision. As part of the implementation of the policy, 20 per cent of the value added tax payable on the sale of wine produced and eventually sold in the state will be levied, of which 16 per cent will be levied.
The government has come up with a scheme to provide a wine incentive subsidy equivalent to the tax amount to the wine industry. Implemented by the Government Decision of 31/08/2009. Joint Commissioner of State Tax (VAT Management), Nashik Division, Nashik, Joint Commissioner of State Tax, Big Tax Cell-3, Mumbai, Joint Commissioner of State Tax, Pune Division, Pune,
As per the proposal received from the Commissioner (VAT Management) Solapur Division, Solapur and the report submitted by the Additional Director of Industries, Directorate of Industries, Mumbai, vide letter No. 05, 07, 08, 10, 11 and 14 of the aforesaid order, the Government is presently in possession of the proposal for refund of incentives during the financial year 2019-20 or earlier.
32 claims are pending and a sum of Rs. 52,85,73, 001/- is required to settle these claims.
2. The claims of the grape growers are pending with the respective wineries and need to be settled.
A sum of Rs 30 crore has been budgeted for the scheme in 2020-21, out of which Rs 12 crore has been made available for disbursement through the Finance Department on BDS system at present.
