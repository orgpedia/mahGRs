The Proposal:
The proposed amount of Maharashtra State Power Transmission Company to participate in the Green Energy Corridor Program of the Central Government is Rs. A total of 27 schemes worth Rs. 367.00 crore and 40% of the total estimated cost of the scheme will be provided as grant from the Central Government's National Clean Energy Fund (NCEF), 20% from the funds under the Metropolitan Transport Company and 40% from the German Development Bank (KfW) to the Metropolitan Transport Company as loan. 1 has been approved by the government. Accordingly, out of the loan amount received from the German Development Bank (KFW) so far, the central government has deposited Rs. 50.6992 crore in the consolidated fund of the state government, which has been provided to the Maharashtra State Power Transmission Company after budgeting from time to time. Of the remaining loan amount from the German Development Bank (KFW), Rs. 17.0029 crore has been budgeted and sufficient amount has been budgeted through Supplementary Demand in December, 2022 to be made available to MRT. The Supplementary Statement of Expenditure in the Winter Session of December, 2022 was presented to the Legislature on 19.12.2022. The bill has been approved by the Lok Sabha. The Supplementary Appropriation Bill has been amended to include the appropriations for the financial year ending 31.03.2023. The Governor has given his assent and the same has been published as the Maharashtra (Second Supplementary) Appropriation Act, 2022 (Maharashtra Act No. 10 of 2022) in the Government of Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary, Part, dated 23.12.2022. Accordingly, the government has allocated Rs. 17.0029 crore to the Maharashtra State Electricity Transmission Company.

Government's decision:
As per the request of Maharashtra State Power Transmission Company, out of the loan amount received from German Development Bank (KFW) under Green Energy Corridor Phase-I programme of the Central Government, the amount deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the State Government is Rs. 17,00,29, 000/- (Rupees Seventeen Crores Thirty Nine Thousand only) to Maharashtra State Power Transmission Company.
Karad, Deputy Secretary (Power), Industries, Power and Labour Department, Mantralaya, Mumbai as Controlling Officer and Shri.
The amount should be paid through RTGS/ NEFT in the name of Chairman and Managing Director, Maharashtra State Electricity Transmission Company Ltd., Mumbai.
Monthly and quarterly reports with information on expenditure, targets and actual achievement of various schemes under the Green Energy Corridor Programme should be submitted by the 10th of the corresponding month.
It may be ensured that the amount of funds sanctioned under this Government decision will be spent only for the financial year 2022-23 for implementation of various schemes under Green Energy Corridor Phase-I programme. Administrative and technical approval of the concerned projects should be obtained from the competent authority in accordance with the actual expenditure before spending the said funds. If the funds are lying with the corporation without being spent within the stipulated period, the responsibility should be fixed on the concerned. The Chairman and Managing Director, General Transport will be responsible for the expenditure within the permissible and permissible period, the responsibility of submitting the quarterly report without fail should be placed on the officers looking after the charge as Assistant Director (Accounts) dealing with the accounting matters.
The documents relating to the accounting of the said fund should be submitted to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai, for inspection, as and when required by the Maharashtra State Power Transmission Company.
