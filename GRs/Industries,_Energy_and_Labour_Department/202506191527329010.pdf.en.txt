Preamble: - Section (22) (b) of the Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996 provides for payment of pension to registered construction workers of the Board who have completed 60 years of age. In pursuance of the Model Welfare Schemes Guidelines elaborated by the Central Government in this regard, the Government Decision dated 28.03.2025 in this regard has approved the implementation of pension to the registered construction workers of the Board on completion of their B.O. years. Paragraph 4 (d) of the said Government Decision provided for the determination of detailed procedures as required with respect to the present scheme. Accordingly, it was under the consideration of the government to lay down a detailed SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) regarding the pension scheme implemented for construction workers registered under the Board.

The Government's Decisions:
Under this Government Decision, detailed procedure of pension scheme implemented by construction workers registered under Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board, Mumbai after they complete 60 years of age is being approved. Accordingly, the detailed procedure (507) of the pension scheme applied to construction workers registered under the Board is prescribed as follows.
Eligibility Criteria for Pension Scheme: -
(a) Survey construction workers who have attained the age of 60 years and have been registered with the Board for at least one consecutive year shall be eligible under the scheme. Also, if both the husband and wife of his family are construction workers, they will be eligible separately.
(b) On the death of the spouse, the spouse of the construction worker concerned shall be eligible for the pension. However, if the spouse is receiving a pension under the scheme, the relative will not get the benefit of double pension.
(a) As per the guidelines of the Model Welfare Schemes of the Central Government, construction workers receiving benefits under the Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 and the Employees' Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 (The Employees' Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952) shall not be eligible for pension.
Criteria and scale / rate of pension: - (a) Pension will be paid to eligible construction workers registered with the Board as per their period of registration with the Board.

Procedure for grant of pension benefits.-
(a) The construction worker registered with the Board who qualifies under the scheme should download the prescribed sample application form free of cost from the Board's official website https://mahabocw.in. (Sample of application as "Form-A")
(b) Complete the said application and submit it to the District Construction Workers Facilitation Centre (WFC) in-charge (Deputy Commissioner of Labour / Assistant Labour Commissioner / Government Labour Officer) of the district in which the HIS is based.
