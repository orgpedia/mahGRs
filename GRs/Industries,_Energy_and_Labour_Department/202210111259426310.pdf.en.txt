Government's decision:
In order to prevent the loss of the grape growing farmers of the state, to encourage the farmers to cultivate grapes, to make dry fruits and to create economically more profitable alternative products from grapes, and to promote the wine industry based on grapes, the government has launched the Grape Processing Industry Policy-2001. Decision of 19/10/2001. As part of the implementation of the policy, the government has decided to provide a wine incentive subsidy to the wine industry equivalent to 16 per cent of the value added tax payable on the sale of wine produced and finally sold in the state. Implemented by the Government Decision of 31/08/2009.
A budgetary provision of Rs.30 crore has been made under the scheme for the financial year 2022-23. Out of this, an amount of Rs. 5.44 crore is currently available for disbursement on BDS system. Under this scheme, at present, there are instructions from the Finance Department to settle the pending claims of the year 2019-20 or earlier. At present, there are 6 claims pending for the period 2019-20 or earlier and an amount of Rs. 73,10,434/- is required to settle these claims. It was contemplated to disburse the amount to settle the above 6 claims from the available provisions.
(3) Therefore, subject to the condition of ensuring strict implementation of the provisions, criteria, eligibility etc. of the Government Decisions/ Circulars/ Corrigendum mentioned in Reference No. 1 to 4, taking into account the funds available for the said Scheme, the claims received from the Directorate of Industries during the financial year 2019-20 or earlier, in respect of the claims pending in the following returns, may be considered as No. The wine industry component mentioned in section 2 is referred to as Rakana No. This Government resolution approves the release of funds to the tune of Rs. 73,10,434/- (Rupees Seventy Three Lakhs Ten Thousand Four Hundred and Thirty Four only) as mentioned in section 5.
