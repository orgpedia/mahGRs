Preamble: In order to enable the farmers to irrigate during the day and to achieve the objective of saving the state government on the cost of connecting the traditional agricultural pumps and the subsidy provided by the state government, the" Mukhyamantri Solar Krishi Pump Yojana" has been approved by the government decision dated November 15, 2018 and September 11, 2019 to provide 1 lakh solar agricultural pumps without transmission to the farmers of the state for agricultural use in a phased manner. " Mukhyamantri Solar Krishi Pump Yojana" is a fully state government scheme under which it is planned to install one lakh solar Krishi Pumps in a phased manner in the next 3 years. In the first phase, 25,000 nos of solar pumps will be installed and in the second and third phases, 75,000 nos of solar pumps will be installed. The general category beneficiaries will be given 10 per cent of the state's budgetary subsidy, 10 per cent of the beneficiary share and the remaining 80 per cent will be paid from the mutual amount collected from the tax on sale of surplus power in the escrow account of MSEDCL. As per the decision of the government, the general category beneficiaries of the 75,000 solar agricultural pumps in the second phase have been considered for 10 percent of the state government's subsidy of Rs. 118.36 crore. For this purpose, the revised estimates have been approved by the Finance Department within the limit of Rs. 56.00 crore in line with the budgetary allocation of Rs.100 crore to the Department. As per the decision of the Government dated 01.08.2019, the balance amount of Rs. 3.37098 crore of the outstanding amount of the scheme for the year 2018-19 has been released during the year, the Government was considering to release the remaining amount of Rs. 52.62902 crore available for disbursement.
