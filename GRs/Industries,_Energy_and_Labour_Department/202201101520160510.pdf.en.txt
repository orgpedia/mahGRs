Preamble: The Maharashtra State Industrial Development Programme (MSE-CDP) has been announced vide Government Resolution dated 25/02/2014 for the development of small clusters in industrially underdeveloped areas of the State, thereby promoting the holistic development of Micro and Small Enterprises. Under the scheme, the state government provides grant-in-aid for the implementation of capacity building programmes for the overall development and growth of micro and small enterprises as well as for the construction of common facilities. Under this scheme, a Diagnostic Study Report (DSR) is prepared by properly diagnosing the flaws in the production process of the industrial group, including the latest technology, market expansion, visits to various successful industrial groups, updated training programs and study tours, etc. After the diagnostic study report is received on the recommendation of the District Level Committee, the State Level Industrial Cluster Control Committee approves the diagnostic study report proposals and fixes the grant for the capacity building programme under it.
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) containing details of high quality common facilities and other necessary clearances, no-objection certificates, technical and financial evaluation reports are prepared to enhance the productivity and competitiveness of MSME units.
The procurement process is initiated after final approval of the proposal of Common Facility Centre by the State Level Steering Committee. 60% of the first phase subsidy is disbursed after receiving the physical verification report of the machinery, equipment, workstation as approved. The remaining 40% subsidy is provided after the successful completion of the Common Facility Centre (CFC) certificate from the Co-Director of Industries. Accordingly, Steel Furniture Cluster, Latur G. Latur Group Project has been approved for setting up of Common Facility Centre.
