Introduction: Only one to two percent of the total coconut husk available in the Konkan region of the state is used to produce various coir products. The rest is used as fuel. Since coconut husk is the main raw material for the coir industry, it is necessary to use more and more husk as raw material for the development of the coir industry in the state.
Department of Industry, Energy and Labour. The Government has approved the Coir Industry Policy-2018 vide Government Resolution dated 15th February, 2018. As per the policy, training is provided under the Technical Skills and Entrepreneurship Development Programme. The Maharashtra Small Industries Development Corporation will be given a grant of Rs. Rs 350 crore has been set aside for the project. Accordingly, the training of 250 trainees in Ratnagiri district within the limit of the budgeted funds sanctioned to Maharashtra Small Industries Development Corporation is Rs. A proposal to grant administrative approval for spending Rs 43 lakh was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
250 young entrepreneurs from Ratnagiri district will be given 10 days training in making various items from coir at Coir Board, Alleppey, Kerala. The administrative approval is being given under this government decision to spend 43 lakh rupees.
A total of 250 trainees from Ratnagiri district will undergo 10 days training at Coir Board, Alleppey, Kerala, comprising of 35 training programmes. The training fee is Rs. 1000/- (plus GST). The training facility is located at Coir Board Complex Kalavoor. The estimated cost of training programme for these 250 trainees will be as per Annexure-I.
