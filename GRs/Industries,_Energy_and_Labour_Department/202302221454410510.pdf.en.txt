Preamble: In order to promote the development of small clusters in the industrially underdeveloped areas of the State and thereby promote the overall development of micro and small units, the Maharashtra State Industrial Cluster Development Programme (MSICDP) has been announced vide Government Resolution dated 25/02/2014.
Under the scheme, the state government provides grant-in-aid for the implementation of capacity building programmes for the overall development and growth of micro and small enterprises as well as for the construction of common facilities.
Under the MSICDP scheme, a Diagnostic Study Report (DSR) is prepared by properly diagnosing the flaws in the production process of the industrial group, including the latest technology, market expansion, visits to various successful industrial groups, updated training programs and study tours, etc. After the diagnostic study report is received on the recommendation of the district level committee, the State Level Industrial Cluster Control Committee approves the diagnostic study report proposal and determines the subsidy for the capacity building programme. A Detailed Project Report (DPR) containing details of high quality common facilities and other necessary clearances, no-objection certificates, technical and financial evaluation reports are prepared to enhance the productivity and competitiveness of MSME units. The procurement process is initiated after the final approval of the State Level Steering Committee for the proposal of the Common Facility Centre.

As per the revised guidelines of MSICDP Scheme dated 24/07/2019, after final approval of the proposal of Common Facility Centre by the State Level Steering Committee, the first phase 20% subsidy is disbursed to the projects whose procurement process has been completed by the Zonal Committee after receiving the report along with the procurement details. Thereafter, after receiving the physical verification report from the General Manager, District Industries Centre regarding the installation of machinery and equipment at the work site as per the project approval and after receiving the utilization certificate of the first phase grant, 60% of the second phase grant is disbursed. The remaining 20% subsidy is disbursed after receipt of certificate from the concerned Industry cum Director on successful completion of the Common Facility Centre.
As per proposal dated 14/02/2023 of Directorate of Industries, State Level Steering Committee for setting up of Common Facility Centre in Resin Cluster Land G. Osmanabad Industrial Cluster Project has given final approval in its meeting dated 12/07/2019 and in its meeting dated 05/08/2022, it has been extended till 31/12/2022. The certificate of successful completion of the Common Facility Centre of the project has been submitted by the Directorate of Industries to the Joint Director of Industries, Divisional Office, Aurangabad. Accordingly, the final grant of Rs. 93,34,000/- from the State Government's share for the construction of Common Facility Centre of the project was under consideration.
Government Decision: The Directorate of Industries has issued a proposal for the release of the final grant of Rs. 93,34,000/- from the State Government for the construction of a common facility centre of Resin Cluster, Bhoomi G. Osmanabad Project sanctioned under the scheme" Maharashtra State Industrial Cluster Development Programme (MSICDP)" of the State Government. 8 is presented here. Accordingly, the Government is approving the final grant of Rs. 93,34,000/- (Rupees Ninety Three Lakhs Thirty Four Thousand only) as per the provisions of the said Scheme.
The Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai shall arrange to transfer the amount of Rs. 93,34,000/- from the Government treasury to the Resin Cluster, Bhoomi G. Osmanabad Group Project.
The powers to draw and disburse the above mentioned amount of Rs. 93,34,000/- are being delegated to the Accounts Officer in the office of the Development Commissioner (Industries), Directorate of Industries, Mumbai.
