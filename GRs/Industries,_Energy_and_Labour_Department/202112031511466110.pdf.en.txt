Preamble: In order to provide 24x7 uninterrupted, quality, reliable and affordable power supply to the electricity consumers of the country and to improve the efficiency of the power distribution companies within the prescribed time frame and in the prescribed manner, the Union Ministry of Power has launched the Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme- A Reforms Based and Results Linked Scheme. The same has been issued vide Office Memorandum dated 20.07.2021. The objectives of the programme are:
a) To improve the quality and availability of power supply to the consumers and to provide electricity at reasonable price.
Bring down Aggregate Techno-Commercial Loss (AT& C loss) to 12 to 15% by 2024-25.
Zeroing of ACS-ARR Gap by 2024-25. (Average Cost of Supply- Annual Revenue Realised) 2. The two parts of this scheme are as follows.
Component 1- Installation of prepaid/ smart meters to customers, as well as distribution channels and
Installation of AMI meters to Distribution Rohiths.
Component 2: Distribution infrastructure works. Channel Isolation, laying of Aerial Bunch Cable, installation of SCADA and DMS systems in cities, setting up and strengthening of distribution infrastructure etc.
Part B- Training, Capacity Building and Empowerment Power Finance Corporation has been appointed as the Nodal Agency for this scheme for Maharashtra.
Under this scheme, 60% of the project cost of the infrastructure will be provided by the Central Government in a phased manner after evaluating the annual report and the remaining 40% will be provided by the power distribution company.

Loans are to be taken from banks/ financial institutions. Also, 15% of the total cost of smart meters will be subsidized (maximum Rs. 900 per metre). Further, 7.5% of the total cost of smart meters to be installed by December, 2023 (subject to a maximum of Rs. 450 per meter) will be given as an additional subsidy on incentives. The implementation period of the scheme is five years (2021-22 to 2025-26). Works completed after 31.03.2026 will not be eligible for Central Government grants.
The Detailed Project Report (DPR) to be carried out under the Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme- A Reforms Based and Results Linked Scheme is to be approved by the Distribution Reforms Committee. Accordingly, the matter of forming a Distribution Reform Committee was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme- A Reforms Based and
The Government has approved the constitution of a' Distribution Reform Committee (DRC)" for implementation of the' Results Linked Scheme." The members of the Committee shall be as follows:
Chief Secretary
A.M.S./ Secretary (Finance) Member
A. M. S./ P. Secretary (Revenue) Member
A.M.S./ P.S./ Secretary (Forest) Member
Secretary (Environment)/ Secretary (Environment)
Chairman and Managing Director, Member
The Best Member, General Manager
Principal Secretary (Energy)
The Committee shall have the power to call upon other officers as invited members as may be required.
The duties and responsibilities of the Committee shall be as follows:
Recommending the Detailed Project Reports (DPRs) for approval by the Monitoring Committee of the Central Government.
(b) recommend to the State Cabinet the action plan of the scheme for approval.
Accepting changes in Standard Bidding Document (SBD) with the consent of Nodal Agency.
Monitoring the implementation of the action plan.
Monitoring the progress and quality of implementation of approved projects and resolving issues related thereto.
