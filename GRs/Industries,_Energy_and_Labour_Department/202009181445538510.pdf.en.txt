Introduction: As per the policy of the government, the Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Limited gives discounts on electricity rates to consumers of agricultural pumps, machinery, textile and industrial categories in the state and the government reimburses it to the MSEDCL. Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company has received an amount of Rs.200 crore under Demand Number K-6 Head Account Heading 28015572 in the financial year 2020-21 towards financial assistance to agricultural pump consumers. 5019.9699 crore has been provided.
In pursuance of the above approved provision, subject to the formula that only 33% of the budgeted funds will be available in 2020-2021 for the schemes under the overall programme under the relevant Government Decision 1, the Finance Department has allocated Rs. 960.00 crore has been approved to be disbursed to MSEDCL by adjustment. For the fourth quarter of 2018-19, the amount due to the Government on account of electricity duty and electricity sales tax is Rs. 1602.9549 crore is yet to be adjusted. (As of January 2019, Rs. 558.6021 crore for the month of February, 2019. 520.4155 crore and Rs.
523.9373 crore) for the fourth quarter of 2018-19. Out of Rs. 1602.9549 crore, now Rs. 642.9549 crore is left for adjustment of electricity charges. 960.00 crore to MSEDCL was under consideration of the Government.
The Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Limited (MSDDCL) has decided to provide subsidy to the consumers of agricultural pumps, machinery, textile and industrial categories for the year 2018-19. This Government resolution approves release of Rs. 960.00 crore (Rupees Nine Hundred and Sixty Crores only) by way of adjustment instead of cash.
The expenditure for the said purpose should be spent under the head" Demand No.K-6,2801 Electricity, 05 Transmission and Distribution, 104, Power Development System National Load, (01) Electricity Rate Concession, (01) (01) Electricity Rate Concession to Agricultural Pump Customers (General) (28015572), (Programme) 33 Financial Assistance" and it should be met from the provision approved for the financial year 2020-21.
