The Government of Maharashtra is making continuous efforts to ensure that the business workers get the benefits of better health, safety and welfare schemes. The involvement of the factory management is also valuable. Keeping this in mind, the factory management also needs to make efforts for the safety of the workers on a voluntary basis. For this, the government was considering some measures to encourage factory management. As part of this, on the occasion of National Safety Day on 4th March, the best performing factories in the field of occupational safety, health and worker welfare will be awarded with the title "Narayan Meghaji Lokhande Industrial Safety and Health." The government decision was issued on 08.03.2023.
There have been many changes in the industrial sector over time. The government has taken a decision. The criterion for the "Narayan Meghaji Lokhande Industrial Safety and Health Award" under the 08.03.2022 category is to bring more clarity to the definition in terms of the award in the category of hazardous factories, to include an accounting title for the cost of the award, to reconstitute the selection committee, and to revise the criteria for finding errors in the said Government decision. The Government decision here was required to be amended. For this reason, the Department has been asked to make necessary corrections in the Government Decision and submit a proposal for improvement in the criteria, selection and procedure of this award. The letter dated 13.11.2025 was addressed to the Director, Directorate General of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai.

1) The terms and conditions of participation in the Prize Competition: -
1) Factories registered in Maharashtra can participate in this competition.
2) The production process at the factory must have been started at least three calendar years before the award year, and the factory must have obtained a permanent license.
It should be.
3) Participating factories are required to submit the prescribed sample within the stipulated time.
4) The following entry fee will be required to be paid along with the application.
a) Factories with a strength of up to 250 workers - Rs.1500 / -b) Factories with a strength of 251 to 500 workers - Rs.2000 / -c) Factories with a strength of 501 to 1000 workers - Rs.3000 / -d) Factories with a strength of more than 1000 workers - Rs.4500 / -
This fee should be credited under the head of deposit 0230, Workers and Employment, Coo, Other Deposits, (0) Other Deposits, (02) (02) Other Deposits (02300107).
5) Factories will be grouped into "hazardous" and "non-hazardous" factories. Factories with up to 250 workers in each group, factories with 251 and Goo workers, factories with 501 to 1000 workers, and factories with more than 1000 workers will be classified into four categories.
6) "Hazardous" factories are those that carry out hazardous processes in accordance with Section 2 (za) of the Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020 (Section 2 (a) of the erstwhile Factories Act, 1948).
The Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, Mumbai shall take necessary action at its level in the matter of acceptance of competition applications, preliminary screening of applications, constitution of an evaluation committee for each division for competition, process of forwarding applications received from factories to the evaluation committee of the division in which the factory is located, evaluation by the divisional committees and forwarding their reports to the State Level Selection Committee for decision as may be decided by the Director.

The State Level Selection Committee will select five factories in each category in order of merit. These five factories will have to make a presentation to the State Level Selection Committee regarding the outstanding work done by the respective plant on safety, health and welfare schemes.
3) The State Level Selection Committee will give 10 Paiko marks to the presentation of the factory. Also, the scores given by the Evaluation Committee will be scrutinized by the State Level Selection Committee.
Thus, the State Level Selection Committee will decide the names of the 3 prize-winning factories in each category by determining the final score, based on the scores given by the Evaluation Committee and the State Level Selection Committee, and if deemed necessary, will decide on the award of a Certificate of Incentive in each category. The State Selection Committee shall have the power to reduce / increase the number of prizes taking into account the number of awards received.
4) The State Level Selection Committee will announce the names of the award winning factories during the Safety Week and accordingly, the Director, Directorate of Industrial Safety & Health, Mumbai will conduct the prize distribution ceremony.
4. The entry for the factory to participate in the award competition will be as under Form "A." Also, the guidelines for the Evaluation Committee will be as in Form "B."
