Government circular:
In the wake of the Covid-19 pandemic, the government and private hospitals across the state have been asked to provide round-the-clock medical care to the needy. As a result, the infrastructure of all government and private hospitals in the state has been severely strained. These include maintenance of uninterrupted power supply in the hospital, provision of internal power supply through generator sets and inverters, maintenance of air-conditioning system in operation theatre/ ICU, regular maintenance and repair of effluents in use, maintenance and upkeep of internal mainpanels, sub-panels, wiring and electrical switch gears, safe handling of electrical appliances and medical equipment and availability of fire extinguishers. The Chief Inspector of Electricity, Mumbai, has conducted an inspection/ inspection to ascertain whether the power supply to the hospitals in the State is maintained as per the provisions of the Central Electricity Authority (Measures relating to Power Supply and Safety) Regulations, 2010. The exact cause of the fire in the hospital has been ascertained through these observations/ investigations. In order to prevent short circuit and other electrical faults in government and private hospitals in the state and to prevent loss of life and property due to fire, the power supply in government and private hospitals and public buildings across the state should be maintained safely.
