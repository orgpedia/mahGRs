Preamble: As per the definition of employer and original employer in the Maharashtra Mathadi, Hamal and Other Working Labourers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969 and the Maharashtra Private Security Guards (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1981, Mathadi Mandals and Security Guards Mandals do not fall within the definition of employer or original employer. As per the Government Decisions dated 28/09/2018 and 05/03/2019, the Government have issued a decision not to deduct the instalment of loan taken by Credit Unions/ Co-operative Banks/ National Bank from the salary of Mathadi workers and Security Guards respectively.

The above government. Orders have also been issued to implement the Government Decision dated 28/09/2018. Accordingly, action is being taken by Mathadi/ Security Guard Board in respect of deduction of applicant credit institutions. It is in this context that the question of deciding the deduction of non-applicant credit institutions in addition to the above petition No. 5427/2019 and 1257/2019 and other petitions was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The High Court of Bombay, No. 5427/2019 and 1257/2019 and other petitions. The High Court It is incumbent upon the Government to take action in accordance with the orders dated 03/05/2019 and 11/07/2019. Accordingly, the recovery proceedings are expected to continue. The High Court
Approval of the Government is being accorded to take action in respect of recovery pursuant to the orders dated 03/05/2019 and 11/07/2019. However, this permission is being given on the following terms and conditions.
The Mathadi/ Security Guard Boards shall deduct the instalment of the loan given to the Mathadi workers/ security guards by the credit institutions or co-operative banks/ nationalized banks etc.
The statement of demand and collection in respect of deduction shall be submitted to the Board every financial year with the signature of the Chairman and the Accountant/ Secretary of the respective credit banks/ credit societies/ cooperative banks and nationalized banks.
(3) It shall not be the responsibility of the Board or the Government to ensure that the rate of interest on the loan given and the individual limit of the loan and the period of repayment of the loan remain the responsibility of the credit institutions concerned.
The Government shall not be responsible in any way for the recovery or repayment of loans outstanding to banks/ credit institutions.
