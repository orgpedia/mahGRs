2. Under the ambitious Make in India programme of the Government of India, it has been decided to give priority to domestic products in the procurement policy of the Government of India and to give full priority to local manufacturers in the procurement policy. Further, under the National Procurement Policy of the Government of India vide orders dated 28.05.2018, 13.11.2018 and 16.09.2020, special provisions have been made for" Make in India" as well as for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises and Women Entrepreneurs.

The central government has announced a new policy for micro, small and medium enterprises. The Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India, New Delhi has implemented various schemes with a view to empowering Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) and women entrepreneurs with access to market opportunities. The detailed procedure in this regard has been laid down in the Central Government Order dated 28th May, 2018.
4. Reference No. 01.12.2016 of the State Govt. As per the Government decision dated 01.01.2020, a revised manual of procedures for procurement of offices by the State Government Departments has been issued and the revised procurement policy has been implemented accordingly. It has been made mandatory for all the departments/ offices under the government to carry out the procurement process in accordance with the provisions of the revised procurement policy.
The revised procurement policy of the State Government dated 01.12.2016 has been modified in accordance with the Government decision dated 23.07.2021 for Micro and Small Enterprises and others, incorporating changes in line with the policy of the Central Government. However, certain aspects of the National Procurement Policy of the Central Government have not yet been incorporated in the procurement policy of the State Governments. Accordingly, the Procurement Policy seeks to increase the proportion of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) to 25% from the present 20% of the total procurement and make provisions for reservation of 3% of the total procurement for MSMEs for women etc. In the 25th meeting of the Western Zonal Council of States, all the States have been informed about the implementation of the above amendments made by the Central Government by incorporating them in the State Procurement Policy.
