Government Resolution No: VAPUMA-2022/ PR No.38/ Industries-2
Government Decision: In order to prevent the loss of grape growing farmers in the state, to encourage the farmers to cultivate grapes, to make dry fruits and to create economically more profitable alternative products from grapes, as well as to promote the grape-based wine industry, the government has issued the Grape Processing Industry Policy-2001. Decision of 19/10/2001. As part of the implementation of the policy, the government has decided to provide a wine incentive subsidy to the wine industry equivalent to 16 per cent of the value added tax payable on the sale of wine produced and finally sold in the state. Implemented by the Government Decision of 31/08/2009.
The budgeted amount of Rs 30 crore has been spent 100 per cent in the financial year 2021-22 under this scheme. Since an amount of Rs. 24,08,06, 617/- is required to settle 34 pending claims for the financial year 2019-20 or earlier, additional funds have been made available through Supplementary Demands in the Budget Session 2021-22. A proposal has been submitted to the Finance Department for release of funds to the tune of Rs. 24,08,06, 617/-for settlement of pending claims out of the funds made available through Supplementary Demands.
Therefore, subject to the condition of ensuring strict implementation of the provisions, criteria, eligibility etc. of the Government Decisions/ Circulars/ Corrigendum mentioned in Reference No. 1 to 4, taking into account the funds available for the said Scheme, the claim received from the Directorate of Industries during the financial year 2019-2020 or earlier shall be considered as a claim in respect of the following returns: The wine industry component mentioned in section 2 is referred to as Rakana No. This Government resolution approves the release of funds to the tune of Rs. 24,08,06, 617/- (Rupees Twenty Four Crores Eight Lakh Six Thousand Six Hundred Seventeen only) as mentioned in clause (4).
