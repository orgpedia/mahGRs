Circular-
It has been observed that certain establishments are seeking clarification on the requirement
of registration under the Maharashtra Shops and Establishments (Regulation of Employment and
Conditions of Service) Act, 2017, subsequent to the enactment of the Occupational Safety, Health and
Working Conditions Code, 2020 (OSH Code 2020).
2. The OSH Code 2020, being a central legislation, consolidates and rationalizes provisions
relating to safety, health, and working conditions of workers employed in various establishments. It
provides a uniform framework for registration, licensing, and compliance across establishments,
thereby subsuming multiple state-level requirements.
3. In particular, Section 3 of the OSH Code 2020 lay down the requirement of registration of
establishments engaging 10 or more employees with the registering officer under the Code. Once
such registration is obtained after finalisation of the rules, the establishment is deemed to have
complied with the statutory requirement of registration for purposes of labour welfare, safety, and
working conditions.
4. Consequently, the requirement of separate registration under the Maharashtra Shops and
Establishments Act, 2017 is not mandated, as the OSH Code 2020 already covers the same subject
matter comprehensively. The principle of repugnancy under Article 254 of the Constitution of India
further establishes that in case of conflict between central and state legislation on concurrent subjects,
the central legislation shall prevail.
5. The establishments must continue to comply with other provisions of the Maharashtra Shops
and Establishments (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 2017 to the extent
they are not inconsistent with the OSH Code 2020.However,establishment employing less than ten
