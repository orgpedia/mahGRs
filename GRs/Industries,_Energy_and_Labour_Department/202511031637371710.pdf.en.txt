Introduction: - India's Global Capability Centres are playing a key role in shaping the strategic needs of the global industry, rapidly transforming the country's commercial and technological landscape significantly. The number of Global Capacity Centres in India has increased from approximately 1200 in 2021 to more than 1,900 in 2025, providing employment to about 1.9 million professionals cumulatively. India is at the forefront of expansion of Global Capacity Centres, with over 400 new Global Capacity Centres and approximately 1,100 additional units established during this period.
Global Capacity Centres are shaping India's technology ecosystem, as leading global industries are integrating their various technology-based operations in the country. Platforms, products, and technologies for future generations of high-tech based industries such as space, defense, and semiconductors
Using India's talent to develop. engineering research and development in particular.
(ER & D) The growth of global capacity centres in the sector has been 1.3 times faster than the establishment of total global capacity centres, which is a significant shift towards high manufacturing and innovation-based work in India.
It shows the signal.
India has a vast talent pool, accounting for about 28 per cent of the global STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) manpower and 23 per cent of the global software engineering capacity. The ecosystem of Global Capacity Centres in the country has become more robust over the last decade with these centres developing into high-end areas of product management and architecture.
It will also significantly expand India's global leadership role in the Global Capacity Building.
Global capacity centres in India are projected to grow from 6500 to over 30000 by 2030, this will be achieved with the help of extensive training programmes.
The growing adoption of AI and machine learning at the Global Capacity Centre as well as the establishment of AI Centres of Excellence are positioning India as a hub for cutting-edge innovation.
A strong middle-level management in the country is playing an important role in accelerating this growth.
Global capacity centres, previously seen as factors established only for cost control, have now become key enablers of digital transformation, business-oriented resilience, and innovation. The centres are Technology, Finance, Analytics, Engineers, Marketing and Marking (BRODIG) as well as R & D.

Providing high-value-based services to regions. India's skilled manpower, cost efficiency, and advanced strategic framework have made it a leading country in the global capacity building sector.
It aims to make India a developed nation with a $30 trillion economy by 2047. In line with the Prime Minister's vision of a developed India (62047), the Global Development Centres are going to play a key role in this transformation. Inclusive development of urban and rural areas
The Global Capacity Centres will focus on achieving this goal of fostering prosperity and increasing ease of governance at the same time. Accordingly, the Government was considering announcing the Maharashtra Global Capacity Building Policy-2025.
Government Decision: - Ma. In line with the Prime Minister's vision of a developed India (82047), attract new global capacity centres, promote global capacity centre-led research, enhance multinational collaborations and attract high-value-based knowledge-centric investments to achieve the goal of making India a $30 trillion economy by 2047. Developing world-class business districts and robust digital databases, financial and non-financial incentives for a global competency centre, ease of doing business, institutional framework, and convergence of other policies and programmes of the Union and state governments need special emphasis. Keeping all these aspects in mind, the new Maharashtra World Capacity Centre Policy-2025 has been formulated and approved in detail in the meetings of the State Cabinet held on 30.09.2025. The Government's approval on this background is being provided as follows.
1) Maharashtra Global Capacity HA (GCC) Policy-2025 is being approved (with Marathi policy "Annexure-9" and English policy "Annexure-2" attached). The policy will be valid for a period of 5 years or until a new policy is announced.
2) Financial and non-financial incentives are being granted to eligible GCC entities under the Maharashtra Global Capacity Centre (GCC) Policy-2025 as per the policy provision.
3) The estimated expenditure for the policy period (year 2025-30) for incentives for implementation of the policy is Rs. 2,960 crore, plus estimated expenditure for the next 10 years (2031-2040) of Rs. 8,472 crore. An expenditure of ₹11,702 crore is being approved.
Further, for the current financial year 2025-26, Rs. An additional budgetary provision of ₹12 crore is being approved. It is also being approved to open a separate head of accounts for the implementation of this policy.
2. Ma. Considering the approval given by the Cabinet, the Government decision / notification needs to be issued immediately separately from the various departments with respect to the relevant provisions of this Government decision. The list in this regard is mentioned in the "Form-A" accompanying this Government decision. Accordingly, action should be taken by the concerned department for immediate issuance of orders / guidelines / government decisions / amendments / notifications in the development control rules in this regard.
3. The Maharashtra Global Capacity Centre (GCC) Policy-2025 should consider provisions in the English language for interpreting the provisions in Marathi language. The Government shall have the power to interpret the provisions of this Policy and shall be deemed to be final.
4. Decision of the said Government. At the meetings of the Council of Ministers held on 30.09.2025, Shri.
