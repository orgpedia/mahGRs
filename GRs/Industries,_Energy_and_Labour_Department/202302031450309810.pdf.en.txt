Preamble: As per Rule 10 (4) and Rule 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, in public interest, the Government is of the view that officers/ employees of incompetent and doubtful integrity should continue in Government service beyond the age of 50/55 years/ after 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, after reviewing their service in order to test their eligibility (eligible/ ineligible) to continue in service. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 1 The procedures and general instructions regarding service review are prescribed under the Government Decision dated 10.06.2019. Accordingly, for the review of services of Group-A and Group-B officers of the Labour Commissionerate under this Department, Reference No. Under the decision of the government here, a departmental review committee has been formed under the chairmanship of the Principal Secretary (Labour). Accordingly, it was under the consideration of the Government to examine the eligibility of Mr. K. V. Dahiphalkar, Deputy Commissioner of Labour, Group-A to continue in service beyond the age of 50 years and take an appropriate decision.
Government's decision:
As per Rule 10 (4) and Rule 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, Mr. K. V. Dahiphalkar, Deputy Commissioner of Labour, Group-A, on the establishment of Labour Commissioner, Mumbai, is being made eligible to continue in service beyond the age of 50 years on the recommendation of the Review Committee and with the approval of the competent authority.
(2) A record shall be taken in the service book of Mr. Dahiphalkar that he has been approved to continue his service beyond the age of 50 years.
