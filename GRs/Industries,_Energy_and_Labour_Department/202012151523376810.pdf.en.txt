Background: The State Government has launched Mission Begin Again. Government Order issued on 14/10/2020. Permission has been granted to organize Business 2 Business (BIRBY) Exhibitions outside the Containment Zones from 15/10/2020. The department has also been directed to prepare a standard operating procedure in this regard on the lines of the standard operating procedure issued by the Union Ministry of Commerce and Trade while organizing the exhibition. Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India. vide letter dated 15/10/2020, the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) has been issued regarding the precautionary measures to be followed by Venue Providers, Exhibition Organizers, Service Providers, Exhibitors, Business Visitors/ Delegates while organizing Business2Business (BIRBY) Exhibitions in the wake of prevention of COVID-19 virus infection. On the lines of this, it was under the consideration of the government to prepare a standard operating procedure (SOP) to be followed while organizing B2B exhibitions in the state to prevent the spread of Covid-19 virus infection.
Circular: As per the directions in the Government Order dated 14/10/2020, Business Business (Birbi) Exhibitions are permitted to be held only outside the Containment Zones in the wake of COVID-19 virus infection, the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) regarding the health and other preventive safety measures to be followed while conducting such exhibitions is attached herewith in Marathi (Annexure-A) and in English (Annexure-B).
Attendees of the exhibition, while entering the exhibition site, should carry a medical report from a government-approved laboratory obtained 72 hours before that they themselves are corona negative in order to avoid corona virus infection. Only then should the concerned be allowed to enter the exhibition.

Government Circular No: SOP-2020/ PR No.114/ Industries-2
In view of the outbreak of COVID-19 virus infection, the organizers, service providers, buyers/ sellers/ exhibitors/ business visitors of Birbi Exhibition are required to strictly follow the Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) attached in the Annexure and the guidelines issued by the Central Government, State Government and local administration from time to time in this regard. Any person contravening this order shall be punishable under Section 51 to 60 of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, and Section 188 of the Indian Penal Code.
