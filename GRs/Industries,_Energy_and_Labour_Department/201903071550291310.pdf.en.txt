Department of Industry, Energy and Labour,

Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Madam Cama Marg,
Ministry of Home Affairs, Mumbai- 400032
Date: 7th of March, 2019
The Proposal:
With a view to provide social security to the unorganized workers in India through various welfare schemes, the Government of India has enacted the Unorganized Workers Social Security Act, 2008 on 31/12/2008 and in exercise of the powers conferred by this Act, the Government of Maharashtra has enacted the" Maharashtra Unorganized Workers Social Security Rules, 2013" vide Notification No.
The Maharashtra State Social Security Board was established on 3rd April 2018 under the Unorganized Workers Social Security Act, 2008. Under this Act, the government was considering to take a decision to form a study committee to ensure that the unorganized workers of newspaper vendors registered under the corporation get the benefits of the welfare schemes implemented by the state government and for the effective implementation of the schemes in this regard. In response to the suggestion. The Minister (Labour) had earlier assured that a study committee would be set up for the implementation of the newspaper vendor welfare scheme under the Maharashtra State Social Security Board set up by the government.
Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
Government's decision:
The Government hereby decides to constitute a Study Committee to study the benefits of the welfare schemes implemented by the State Government for the unorganized workers registered under the Maharashtra State Social Security Board constituted under Section 6 (1) of the Unorganized Workers Social Security Act, 2008 and how to implement them.
The composition of the Study Committee will be as follows:
Assistant Commissioner of Labour, (a) Member Secretary 3) Shri Anil Shivajirao Desai, Member Satara

The committee will study the welfare schemes to be implemented for the newspaper vendors and the necessary suggestions will be proposed to the government.
The committee will have to recommend to the government the funding and sources of the proposed schemes, matters under all labour laws applicable to these newspaper vendors, registration and other matters. No sitting allowance and other daily allowances shall be admissible to the non-official members of the Committee. The member secretary of the committee will be responsible for organizing the meeting of the committee, preparing the chronicle and sending the recommendations of the committee to the government.
The committee will submit its report to the state government in six working days.
