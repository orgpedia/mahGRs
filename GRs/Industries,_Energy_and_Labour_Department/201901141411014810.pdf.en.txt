Preamble: Maharashtra Construction Workers Housing Scheme under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana for construction workers in urban areas registered with Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board (ACWWB) has been implemented under the Government decision dated 03.02.2018 and the terms and conditions for selection of beneficiaries and the modalities for implementation of the scheme have been decided under the Government decision dated 29.10.2018.
In the meeting of Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board dated 30th October, 2018, it was decided that the registered (active) construction workers in rural areas will be allowed to construct new houses on their own land or convert the existing kutcha houses into pucca houses through the Board.
A resolution was passed regarding the implementation of the scheme and to provide financial assistance to the construction worker for the construction of a house under the scheme. Accordingly, the government was considering to implement the Atal Construction Workers (Rural) Housing Scheme for registered (active) construction workers in rural areas.
Government Decision: Atal Construction Workers Housing for the houses of the (active) construction workers in rural areas out of the (active) construction workers registered with the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board
The scheme (Gramin) is being approved and funded by the Government. The government is also giving approval to implement it through the Director, State Management Cell, Rural Housing Office, Belapur, Navi Mumbai under the Rural Development Department.

Design of house: The structure of house in Atal Nirman Karmachari Awas Yojana (Gramin) for construction workers registered in rural areas out of (active) construction workers registered with Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board is as follows.
A) The entire construction should be of bricks, sand and cement. Accommodation includes a lounge and kitchen. Bathrooms and showers are a must.
(b) The height of the house from the plinth shall be at least 10 feet.

How to apply, how to apply and how to apply:
(a) The registered (active) construction workers shall first apply to the District Executive Officer/ Deputy District Executive Officer, Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board in the form prescribed by the Board.
(b) After scrutinizing the application received from the construction worker, the eligibility of the applicant as well as the documents submitted along with the application will be examined by the District Beneficiary Selection Committee and the eligible construction worker will be selected as a beneficiary in the Atal Construction Workers Housing Scheme (Rural).
e) The Deputy District Executive Officer, Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board and Member Secretary Sanitary Committee will prepare a taluka-wise list of construction workers who have been qualified and approved by the Sanitary Committee. The list will be submitted by the Secretary and CEO to the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board and a copy of the same will be made available to the CEO, Zilla Parishad and Chairperson, District Rural Development Agency and Project Director, District Rural Development Agency.
(e) The Secretary and Chief Executive Officer, Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board will transfer the funds for construction of houses of eligible construction workers to the Director, State Management Cell, Rural Housing Department, Navi Mumbai under the Rural Development Department.
The subsidy will be transferred to the account of the construction workers through the Public Financial Management System (PFMS) system after ensuring the completion of the construction as determined by the Director, State Management Cell in stages according to the progress of the construction of the house.
f) 4% of the subsidy provided under the scheme will be paid to the State Management Cell-Rural Housing Office for administrative expenses.
