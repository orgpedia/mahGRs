Preamble: The Maharashtra State Industrial Complex Development Programme (MSICDP) has been announced vide Government Resolution dated 25/02/2014 to promote the development of small clusters in industrially underdeveloped areas of the State and thereby promote the overall development of micro and small units. Under the scheme, the state government provides grant-in-aid for the implementation of capacity building programmes for the overall development and growth of micro and small enterprises as well as for the construction of common facilities. Under this scheme, a Diagnostic Study Report (DSR) is prepared by properly diagnosing the flaws in the production process of the industrial group, including the latest technology, market expansion, visits to various successful industrial groups, updated training programs and study tours, etc. After the diagnostic study report is received on the recommendation of the district level committee, the State Level Industrial Cluster Control Committee approves the diagnostic study report proposal and determines the grant for the capacity building program under it. Also, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) comprising of high quality common facilities and other necessary licenses, no-objection certificates, technical and financial evaluation reports are prepared to enhance the productivity and competitiveness of MSME units. The procurement process is initiated after final approval of the proposal of Common Facility Centre by the State Level Steering Committee. As per the revised guidelines dated 24/07/2019 of the scheme, after the final approval of the proposal of Common Facility Centre by the State Level Steering Committee, 20% subsidy in the first phase is disbursed to the projects whose procurement process is completed by the Zonal Committee after receiving the report along with the procurement details.
