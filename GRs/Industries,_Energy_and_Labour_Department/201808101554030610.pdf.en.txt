In order to effectively implement the Electricity Act, 2003 and the rules and regulations made thereunder, the Electricity Inspectorate functioning under the administrative control of the Public Works Department, may be brought under the administrative control of the Department of Energy. 1 here. It has been approved by the Government Decision dated 26/02/2015.
All the posts in the Electricity Inspectorate are on the establishment of the Department of Power and as per the above decision, before the Electricity Inspectorate Branch came under the administrative control of the Department of Power, the administrative affairs of the Electricity Inspectorate and all the offices under it were handled by the Chief Engineer (Electricity), Public Works Department. However, the functions of the Electricity Inspectorate are under the control of the Department of Energy. After being brought in pursuant to the Governing Decision dated 26/02/2015, it is necessary to create a new post of" Chief Electrical Inspector" as per Section 161 (1) of the Electricity Act, 2003. 2 here. The Superintending Engineer, Regional Electrical Inspection Board, Mumbai has been authorised as" In-charge Chief Electrical Inspector" vide Government Order dated 24/04/2015.
Section 162 (1) of the Electricity Act, 2003 provides for the appointment of" Chief Electrical Inspector" for the purpose of compliance and enforcement of the provisions of the Electricity Act, 2003 and the Electricity Rules, 2005 and the Central Electricity Authority (Measures relating to Electricity Supply and Safety) Act, 2010 made thereunder. As per the said provision, the State Government may, by notification, appoint a person of reasonable qualification to the post of" Chief Electrical Inspector." In order to ensure compliance and effective implementation of the provisions of the said Act and Rules, it is necessary to appoint a" Chief Electrical Inspector" as per Section 162 (1) of the Electricity Act, 2003.

The duties and responsibilities of the post of" Chief Electrical Inspector" are briefly as follows:
(1) To collect important revenue such as electricity duty and electricity sales tax.
(2) To enforce the provisions of the Electricity Act, 2003 and the rules and regulations thereunder in the State.
(3) The Chief Inspector of Electricity shall be the Chairman of the Board of Licensors constituted for the purpose of granting licences to electrical contractors and for giving certificates to candidates who have passed the examination of the Supervisor of Electricity and the Director of Electronics.
" (4) The"" Chief Electrical Inspector"" shall be the"" appropriate authority"" appointed by the Government of Maharashtra to issue certificates under the Quality Control Orders, 1981, 1988, 1993 and 2003."
(5) To approve the maps of the power supply with an electrical pressure of more than 220 kV, to make the first inspection, to allow to energize, as well as to make the annual inspection.
(6) To approve, first inspect and permit energization of power grids having HVDC electrical pressure of 500 KV and above.
(7) To approve, first inspect and authorize the energization of all the power transmission maps of the new power generating station.
(8) To enforce the provisions of the Maharashtra Evacuation, Movement of Vehicles and Movement of Roads Act, 2018.
(9) The" Chief Electrical Inspector" is the Chairman of the Elevator and Escalator Committee ET-25 of the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), New Delhi, Government of India, to suggest amendments in the standard and to publish it by the Indian Standards Institute.
(10) To exercise administrative control over the entire functioning of the Electricity Inspectorate.
There are a total of 1046 posts of Superintending Engineer, Electrical Inspector, Assistant Electrical Inspector, Assistant Engineer Grade-2, Branch Engineer and Junior Engineer, Technical and Deputy Director (Accounts), Electrical Duty Inspector and other non-technical in the Electricity Inspectorate Branch. For effective control of the said establishment and for revenue collection to be more effective, the post of" Chief Electrical Inspector" needs to be created.
As mentioned above, the post of Chief Electrical Inspector is very important and the creation of this statutory post is due to the post of Head of Administrative Department for Divisional Offices.
