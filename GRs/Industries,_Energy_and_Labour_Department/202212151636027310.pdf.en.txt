The Proposal:
In order to prevent the loss of the grape growing farmers of the state, to encourage the farmers to cultivate grapes, to make dry fruits and to create economically more profitable alternative products from grapes, as well as to promote the grape-based wine industry, the government has launched the Grape Processing Industry Policy-2001. Decision of 19/10/2001. As part of the implementation of the policy, the government has decided to provide a wine incentive subsidy to the wine industry equivalent to 16 per cent of the value added tax payable on the sale of wine produced and finally sold in the state. Implemented by the Government Decision of 31/08/2009.
A budgetary provision of Rs.30 crore has been made under the scheme for the financial year 2022-23. Out of this, an amount of Rs. 10.50 crore is currently available for disbursement on BDS system. Under this scheme, the Finance Department is currently directed to clear the pending claims for the year 2019-20 or earlier. At present, there are 17 claims pending for the period 2019-20 or earlier and an amount of Rs. 44,53,161/- is required to settle these claims. It was contemplated to disburse the amount to settle 17 pending claims from the available provision.
