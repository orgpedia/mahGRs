The proposal: The Supreme Court As per the Judgment dated 21/03/2002, the recommendations of the Justice Shetty Commission have been concretely accepted and accordingly the Judicial Officers of the Subordinate Courts in the State have been recommended for higher pay scales and dearness allowance as per the Central Government rates. The Maharashtra government. In accordance with the judgment of the Supreme Court, the Judicial Officers in the Industrial and Labour Court and the Office of the Commissioner of Workers' Compensation have been paid salaries and allowances/ facilities as recommended by the Justice Shetty Commission. These are implemented as per the Government Order dated 19/3/2005. It was recommended to grant 3 advance increments to judicial officers who had taken L.M. Accordingly, the Department of Law and Justice has taken a decision at the Cabinet level to implement the one-time 3 advance increment to the Judicial Officers who have completed their Masters in Law (LL.M.) in the Secondary Courts under their administrative jurisdiction and has submitted the Government Resolution in accordance with the above.
Accordingly, a proposal was under the consideration of the Government to grant one-time 3 advance increments to judicial officers who have completed their Masters in Law (LL.M.) in the Industrial and Labour Court and the Office of the Commissioner of Workers' Compensation under this Department.
Government Decision: The Judicial Officers of the Industrial and Labour Court and the Office of the Commissioner of Workers' Compensation in the State who have completed their Master's Degree in Law (LL.M.) before joining or after joining the service are being approved to sanction the benefit of 3 advance increments.
