The Proposal: -
The Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996 has been enacted by the Central Government to regulate the conditions of employment of workers working in the building and other construction sectors as well as to provide for safety, health and welfare measures for construction workers. Accordingly, the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2007 have been framed to give effect to the Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996. The Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board has been set up in the state under Section 18 of the said Act.
Workers working in the building and other construction sectors in the state are registered by the board. Active in registration by the Board from the funds of the sub-tax accruing to the Board.
There are 29 different welfare schemes implemented for (living) construction workers. These schemes are classified into educational assistance schemes, health subject schemes, financial assistance schemes, social security, etc. Of these, 22 schemes are implemented under the DBT in Money mode. This involves transferring money directly into the bank accounts of construction workers.

Similarly, security kits, essential kits, and household kits are distributed through DBT in Kind. The open e-tendering system is implemented by following the procurement policy of the state government and the guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) of the central government while purchasing these items. Accordingly, various tender processes and ancillary purchases carried out by the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board have been notified under Government Reference No. The Purchase Committee was constituted by letter 2 here.
