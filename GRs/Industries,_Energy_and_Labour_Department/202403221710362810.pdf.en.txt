Government Circular Our country has adopted a democratic system and all registered citizens over the age of 18 are expected to vote in every election. In view of this, Section 135 (b) of the Representation of the People Act, 1951 provides for paid leave on the day of poll to enable voters to exercise their franchise in general or reasonable relaxation of working hours in certain places. However, in the last few elections, it has been observed that institutions / establishments etc. do not provide paid holidays or discounts. As a result, many voters are deprived of their votes, which is extremely dangerous for democracy.
Election Commission of India. A press release dated 16 March 2024 has announced the schedule for the Lok Sabha General Elections-2024. The election was held in the state. on April 19, 2024. April 26, 2024. May 7, 2024, d. May 13, 2023 and D. It will be held in five phases on 20 May 2024.
General elections to the Lok Sabha are to be held in all the districts of the state as per Annexure-I as per the order issued by the Election Commission of India. In order to enable all electors to exercise their right to vote properly in this election, the following orders are being issued: - (i) Workers / Officers / Employees who are electors in the polling area where the election is being held, even if they are working outside the polling area for work, shall be given paid leave to exercise their right to vote on the day of the election.
() \\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\ The holiday shall be applicable to all industry groups, corporations, companies and institutions, industrial undertakings or other establishments, etc., falling under the Industries Division.
() In exceptional circumstances, workers, officers, employees, etc., may be granted at least two hours in lieu of leave to exercise their right to vote, unless they are required to take a full day off. However, prior permission of the concerned District Collector and District Election Officer will be required for the same. In any case, the institutions concerned will be required to ensure that voters get at least two hours' relaxation to cast their votes.
(iv) All corporations, industry groups, companies and institutions, industrial undertakings, etc., falling under the Industries Division as mentioned above shall strictly ensure that the above instructions are duly complied with. Appropriate action will be taken against voters if they complain about not being able to cast their votes due to non-availability of proper leave or exemption for voting.
