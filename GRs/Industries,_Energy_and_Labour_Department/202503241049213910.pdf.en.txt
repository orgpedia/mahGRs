The government has decided to provide incentives to the cashew processing industry in the state. The decision has been taken by the government of 15/07/2010. As well as d. The Government decision of 12/03/2013 has approved continuation of the scheme by fixing a revised mechanism for allocation of incentive subsidy to the cashew processing industry. Under the scheme, the amount of the tax was being provided to the concerned entities as incentive subsidy after payment of Value Added Tax (VAT) of 5 per cent of the value of cashews finally sold in the state out of the cashews produced and processed in the state. However, d. A new system of levying Goods and Services Tax (GST) instead of Value Added Tax started from 01/07/2017. In the light of this tax structure. Under the government decision of 02/12/2020, the system of distribution of incentive grants under the scheme has been reformed. The state levies 5% Goods and Services Tax (SGST) and 2% CGST (CGST) on all cashews produced and processed and finally sold in the state. After GST is paid by the agro-industry units, 100% of them pay Gross State Goods and Services Tax (SGST). The cashew processing industry has been permitted as an incentive subsidy from the 01/04/2020. It was under the consideration of the Government to disburse the provisions available in the financial year 2024-25 to the concerned cashew processing units for settling the pending claims under the scheme.
