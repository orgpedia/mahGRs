The Proposal:
Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India is implementing Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM) for farmers across the country with a mission to electrify the agricultural pump power connections in the State through solar energy. Under Component B of the Mission, a total of 1,00,000 non-transmission solar agricultural pumps have been sanctioned and are being implemented by MAHAYURGA.
In the first phase of Kusum Yojana, 2478 pumps have been installed. State level committee. In the meeting held on 10 th February, 2022, it has been approved to install 50,000 nos of solar farm equipment under KUSUM Phase-II in FY 2022-23. Accordingly, under KUSUM Phase 2, 30527 beneficiaries have paid beneficiary share and out of 10065 pumps installed, 8918 pumps have been installed for general category beneficiaries, 696 pumps for beneficiaries of Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and 451 pumps for beneficiaries of Tribal Development Department. A total of 706 pumps, including 255 pumps installed for tribal beneficiaries in the first phase and 451 pumps installed in the second phase, require funds. As per the government decision, the beneficiaries of the tribal component of the solar agricultural pump to be installed will have to provide 65 percent of the state government's subsidy.
Demand No. 2022-23 T-5, Main Account Head- 2810- Solar Krishi Pump Scheme Rs. A sum of Rs. 50.00 crore has been allocated for the project. Now, the Government will provide finance for providing 65% share in the non-transmission solar farm pumps to be installed under Component B of the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM) Mission

As per the instructions of the Department, within the limit of 21% of the budgeted fund, the Tribal Development Department has available Rs. 10.50 crore has been provided to the Department of Energy. The matter of providing this fund to the Maha Urja was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
As part of the mission to electrify the agricultural pump connections in the state through solar energy, the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India, has approved the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM) for farmers under Component B of the nationwide campaign.
10.50 crore (Rs. Ten crore fifty lakh only) is being sanctioned by the government and this fund is being made available to Maha Urja.
Narayan Karad, Deputy Secretary (Power), Department of Industries, Power and Labour, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai has been posted as Controller, while Mr. N. R. Dhane, Under Secretary, Department of Industry, Power and Labour, Ministry of Labour, Mumbai has been posted as Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
The monthly and quarterly reports with information on the expenditure, objectives and actual achievement of various schemes under the solar power agri-power installation programme should be submitted to the government by the 10th of the next month. It is to be ensured by the Government that the amount sanctioned under this Government decision will be spent for the financial year 2022-23 only for the implementation of the solar power agri-plantation programme for tribal beneficiaries under" KUSUM."
Documents relating to the accounting of the said fund should be submitted for inspection to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai, by the Maharashtra Energy Development Agency (MEDA), Aundh Road, opposite Spicer College, next to the Commissionerate of Animal Husbandry, Aundh, Pune, as and when required.
The expenditure in this regard should be met from the provision approved for the financial year 2022-23 under the head" Demand No-T-5, 2810, New and Renewable Energy, 796, Tribal Area Sub-Plan, (01) Tribal Area Sub-Plan, (01) Mukhyamantri Solar Krishi Pump Yojana, (Programme) 31 Subsidiary Grants (28100974)."
Documents relating to the accounting of the said funds for the financial year 2022-23 under the Solar Energy Agri-Pump Installation Programme, while disbursing the funds to the Maharashtra Energy Development Agency (MEDA), Aundh Road, opposite Spicer College, next to the Commissionerate of Animal Husbandry, Aundh, Pune, the Finance Department is certifying the fulfillment of the inspection condition in the Annexure to the Government Decision No. This decision is being issued under the authority of the administrative department with the approval of the Finance Department.
