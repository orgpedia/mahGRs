As per the provisions of the Maharashtra Industrial Policy 2013, the scheme" Maharashtra State Industrial Cluster Development Plan (MSICDP)" for the development of Micro and Small Clusters in the industrially underdeveloped areas of the State has been announced under the aforesaid Reference No. 2. The main objective of the MSICDP scheme is to establish a common facility centre for easy access to state-of-the-art technology to enhance the productivity and competitiveness of micro and small enterprises in the state. As per the provisions of the scheme, industrial clusters operating in D, D+, non-industry districts (as provided in the Community Incentive Scheme-2013) and Naxal affected areas of the state are eligible under the scheme. Directorate of Industries, Mumbai is the implementing agency for the scheme" Maharashtra State Industrial Complex Development Plan (MSICDP)."
The MSICDP scheme is being effectively implemented in the state by the Directorate of Industries and the scheme is getting a good response in the state. Maharashtra is one of the leading states in the country in terms of industrial development. Since the concept of industrial cluster development is based on the concept of collective development, for the first time in the state, integrated development of micro and small units is being done under this scheme. The State Government has decided to extend the benefit of the scheme to women entrepreneurs as well as entrepreneurs belonging to the Scheduled Castes/ Scheduled Tribes category to enhance their social and economic empowerment. 11/02/2016 and dt. Industries with 100% SC/ ST and 100% women entrepreneurs as members by Government Decision issued on 14/12/2017
