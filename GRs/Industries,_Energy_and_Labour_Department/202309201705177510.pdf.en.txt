Introduction: Since 1971, 8 temporary posts have been created in the office of the Collector, Mumbai City, for the collection of labor dues and labor compensation in the Mumbai City District. These eight posts have been extended from time to time by the government. These positions are listed in Table 1. Decision of the Government of 21 March 2023. 01/03/2023 to Dy. It was allowed to continue till 31/08/2023. The government was considering to extend the tenure of these posts. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
The following eight temporary posts under the office of the Collector, Mumbai City and mentioned below for the purpose of recovery of dues under the provisions of the Labour Act, 1952 and other Acts, shall be exempted from the following conditions mentioned in the circular dated 20/12/1997, dated 20/12/1997, issued by the Finance Department vide their number DBB 1097/ PR No. 26/ Budget-19 and the posts lying vacant for a period of six months or more: 01/09/2023 to D. The above post holders should be given the special allowance, vehicle allowance and other allowances sanctioned by the government from time to time to the employees doing similar recovery work in the office of the Collector, Mumbai City District.
The expenditure on account of salary and allowances for these posts shall be covered under the head Main Accounts Head 2230-Labour and Employment (01) Labour 111 Social Security for Workers (00) (02) Expenditure in respect of Employees Provident Fund and other Acts (2230 0612) Demand No. K-4 and shall be met from the grant sanctioned for the financial year 2023-24.
The said Government Resolution is being issued in accordance with the powers conferred on the Administrative Departments under the Government Resolution No.1004/ PR 12/ (Part-II)/ 2004/ Financial Reforms-1 dated 4th October, 2004 of the Finance Department.
However, the above posts are not to be used by the District Collector for any work other than the work related to the Labour Department, such as the work to be done to eliminate child labour and other labour law enforcement activities, besides the work of recovery of pending amounts due to the workers under the Labour Department. The extension is being granted only on the condition that the process of formation of the said posts will be completed in time as per the powers conferred to the administrative departments by the Government Corrigendum dated September 6, 2023.
