3. Administrative Reforms in the functioning of Mathadi/ Security Guard Board, e. The Government has decided to conduct an inquiry by a one-man Inquiry Committee of the Development Commissioner under Section 23 of the Maharashtra Mathadi Hamal and Other Labourers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969 and Section 24 of the Maharashtra Private Security Guard (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1981. Accordingly, the Development Commissioner has been informed under the Government Letter No. 5 in the context, according to which the necessary decision will be taken in this regard after submitting the inquiry report of this committee to the Government. To take decisions regarding administrative office expenses of the Board, fixing of rate/ rate of levy, bank balance etc. The matter of change in the procedure mentioned in (a) fixed under 07/12/2017 was under consideration of the Government. Accordingly, the following orders have been issued:

(a) A Committee shall be constituted under the Chairmanship of the Labour Commissioner consisting of the Chairman of all Mathadis/ Security Guards. The committee will have to meet every 15 days and take administrative approval for administrative, office expenses, computer repair, maintenance, etc. of the said board. In this regard, it will be mandatory to issue an administrative order clearly mentioning the provisions of the existing office expenditure rules, act/ scheme, etc. All such administrative orders issued by the Board should be submitted to the Government as information from time to time.
The instructions contained in the Government Orders (b), (c) and (d) dated 07/11/2017 shall continue to apply.
The said orders are being issued in exercise of the powers vested in the Government under Section 7 (4) of the Maharashtra Mathadi Hamal and Other Labourers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969 and Section 8 (4) of the Maharashtra Private Security Guards (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1981.
The Labour Commissioner and all concerned should ensure that the said order is strictly followed.
(6) This order shall come into force with immediate effect from the date of issue.
