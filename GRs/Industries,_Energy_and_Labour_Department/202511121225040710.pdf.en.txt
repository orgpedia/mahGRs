Introduction: - The gems and jewellery industry is the backbone of India's economy, contributing 7 per cent to the country's GDP. It is an important sector for international trade, accounting for 15.71% of India's total merchandise exports, and also ranks third in commodity shares. In 2024, India was the eighth-largest exporter of gems and jewellery globally, accounting for 3.2% of the world's total exports. In the year 2022-23, Western India has contributed 74.66% to the export of gems and jewellery and has emerged as the primary centre for exports. The gems and jewellery sector in Maharashtra has seen a marked growth, with its contribution to India's Gross Value Added (GVA) estimated at $4 billion in 2022 to $6 billion by the year WRC. The Gross Value Added (GVA) of the state's gems and jewellery sector is expected to grow to $2 billion in the future, representing an annual growth rate of 15 per cent. It is expected that $1 billion of these will be raised from exports. The state currently accounts for 18 per cent of India's total gross mineral production in the region, further strengthening Maharashtra's position as a major centre for gems and jewellery in the country.
Mumbai is a centre of excellence in the gems and jewellery sector. Spread over an area of 20 acres in Mumbai, Bharat Diamond Bourse (BDB) is the world's largest diamond trading centre. The Zaveri Bazar area in Mumbai is occupied by traditional crafts, with over 10,000 units engaged in the production of handmade jewellery. At the same time, according to the modern consumer preference, there is a plant specializing in the production of machine-made jewelry on 191 acres of land at Santa Cruz Electronics Export Processing Zone (SEEPZ), Mumbai.
Mumbai also serves as a global trade platform for the industry by hosting prestigious events such as the India International Jewellery Show (ILS) and the "ILS Signature." The event attracts over 10,000 industry professionals every year, which helps strengthen Mumbai city's status as a global hub for the gems and jewellery trade. Accordingly, the Government was contemplating to announce the Maharashtra Gem and Jewellery Policy-2025.
Government Decisions: - The vision is to develop the image of the state of Maharashtra as a major centre of the gems and jewellery industry globally by promoting innovation, sustainability and inclusiveness, by SAAT of advanced technology including traditional artisans through the Maharashtra Gems and Jewellery Policy-2025. The key objectives of the policy are to promote sustainable manufacturing practices, strengthen skill development, empower artisans as well as create a future-ready ecosystem by attracting investments by enhancing industry infrastructure in Maharashtra as a hub for jewellery innovation, manufacturing, and global trade. Creation of new jobs by promoting entrepreneurship and providing targeted training programmes for new entrants as well as experienced artisans, doubling the sector's exports to make Maharashtra India's leading gems and jewellery export hub, regulatory support,

Converting SAMAR businesses into recognised industries by providing financial incentives and formal market access and strengthening the gems and jewellery sector requires special emphasis on fiscal and non-fiscal incentives, ease of doing business, institutional framework, and convergence of other policies and programmes of the Union and State Governments for the sector. Keeping all these aspects in mind, the Maharashtra Gem and Jewellery Policy-2025 has been formulated and approved by the State Cabinet in its meeting held on 07.10.20275. Against this background, the following Government approval is being granted.
1) Maharashtra Gems and Jewellery Policy-2025 is being approved (with Marathi policy "Annexure-I" and English policy "Annexure-II" attached). The policy will be valid for a period of 5 years or until a new policy is announced.
2) The estimated outlay for incentives for implementation of Maharashtra Gems and Jewellery Policy-2025 during the policy period (AD 2025-30) is Rs. 1,651 crore, plus estimated expenditure for the subsequent YoY (2031-2050) of Rs. 12,184 crore. A budget of ₹13,835 crore is expected and the expenditure is being approved.
3) It is being approved to provide separate mandatory headings of accounts for the implementation of this policy. For current excess AF 2025-26 under this heading, Rs. A budgetary provision of up to ₹100 crore is being approved.
4) Financial and non-financial incentives permitted to entities under the policy are being approved.
