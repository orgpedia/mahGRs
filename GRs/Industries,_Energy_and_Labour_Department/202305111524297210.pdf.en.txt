(2) The order shall come into force immediately. The Director-in-charge, Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health, shall take necessary action for the immediate release of the said Deputy Director, instruct him to appear at the place of his transfer and submit a report to the Government.
(3) The officers concerned shall ensure that they join the place of posting within 30 days from the date of the order within the prescribed period as provided under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Incumbency, Non-Ministerial Services and Provisions in respect of Suspension, Dismissal and Removal) Rules, 1981. Otherwise, if he does not join the post, the period of his absence shall be deemed to be" dies non." This should be noted.
It may be noted that non-compliance of the order passed with the approval of the competent authority under the Transfer Act is serious in nature and the officer/ employee who does not comply with this will be liable for suspension under Section 2.1 (2) (v) of Section-2 of the Departmental Inquiry Rules, 1991.
5.The above officers will be paid the period of appointment, travel allowance and daily allowance as permissible as per rules.
6. The concerned officer should not be granted leave without prior permission of the government. Also, as per Rule-30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, the parent office should not accept any leave applications of the transferred officers. If such applications are received, they should be returned to the concerned authorities at the original address.
