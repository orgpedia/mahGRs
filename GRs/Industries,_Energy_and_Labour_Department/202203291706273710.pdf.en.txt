Introduction: As per the policy of the government, the electricity distribution company is given a discount on the electricity tariff and the government reimburses it to the distribution company. Distribution Companies have been provided Rs.28015591/- budgeted for 2021-22 for power subsidy to textile consumers. 300.00 crore out of Rs. 300.00 crore has been disbursed in cash and the amount sanctioned for adjustment in the July, 2021 session through Supplementary Demands is Rs. An amount of Rs. 500.00 crore has been disbursed by adjustment under Government Decision dated 26.11.2021. Now, for this purpose, in the March 2022 session, Rs. Supplementary demand amounting to Rs. 387.09 crore has been sanctioned. Supplementary Statement of Expenditure was presented to the Legislative Council in the Budget Session of March, 2022.
The Supplementary Appropriation Bill has been approved by the Cabinet for inclusion in the Appropriation for the financial year ending March 31, 2022. The Governor has given his assent and the same has been published in the Government of Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part-IV, dated 15th March, 2022, as the Maharashtra (Second Supplementary) Appropriation Act, 2022 (Maharashtra Act No. 23 of 2022). Accordingly, the Government sanctioned Rs. 387.00 crore was under consideration of the Government to be made available to Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company in cash.
Government Decision: The amount of financial assistance payable to Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Limited for the discount given on electricity tariff to textile consumers is Rs. 387.00 crore (Rs. Three hundred and eighty seven crore) is being allowed to be disbursed in cash only by this Government decision.
The expenditure for the said purpose should be spent under the head" Demand No. K-6,2801 Electricity, 05 Transmission& Distribution, 104, Power Development System National Load, (01) Electricity Rate Concession, (01) (02) Electricity Rate Concession to Textile Consumers (General) (28015599), (Programme) 33 Financial Assistance" and it should be met from the provision approved for the financial year 2021-22.
