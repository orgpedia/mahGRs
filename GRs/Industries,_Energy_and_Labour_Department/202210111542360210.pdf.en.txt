Government Decision: The government has decided to give subsidy to the cashew processing industry in the state. The decision has been taken as per the Government Decision dated 15/07/2010. Also the. As per the Government decision dated 12/03/2013, it has been approved to continue the scheme by fixing the revised methodology of allocation of incentive subsidy to cashew processing industry. As per the scheme, the tax amount was being provided to the concerned units as incentive subsidy after payment of 5% value added tax payable on the sale of cashew nuts finally sold in the State out of the cashew nuts produced and processed in the State. The, however. The new regime of levy of Goods and Services Tax (GST) in place of Value Added Tax has been introduced with effect from 01/07/2017. Against the background of this tax. As per the Government decision dated 02/12/2020, the modalities of allocation of Incentive Grants under the scheme have been revised. The state levies 5 per cent Goods and Services Tax (SGST) and 2.5 per cent CGST on the sale of cashew nuts. After the input tax credit (ITC) is paid, 100 per cent of the gross state goods and services tax (SGST) is paid. Cashew processing industry has been allowed as incentive subsidy with effect from 01/04/2020. Also the. Pursuant to the Government Decision dated 09/06/2021. The government has allowed the submission of claims from 01/04/2020. The deadline has been extended to 30/06/2021.
A budgetary provision of Rs.5 crore has been made for the financial year 2022-23 for this scheme. Of this, Rs. 1.05 crore is currently available for disbursement on BDS system. 1,05,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Five Hundred and Fifty only), subject to verification of the provisions, criteria, eligibility etc., of the Government Decision/ Circular mentioned in Reference No. 4 and 5, subject to ensuring strict implementation of the condition.
