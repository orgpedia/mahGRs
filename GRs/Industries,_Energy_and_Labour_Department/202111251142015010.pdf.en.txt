Preamble: As per the policy of the government, the Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Limited (MSEDCL) provides concession in electricity rates to various categories of consumers in the state and the government reimburses the same to MSEDCL. MSEDCL has been allocated Rs. 28015581 for the year 2021 for electricity subsidy to the consumers. 600.00 crore out of Rs. 235.00 crore has been disbursed in cash as per Government Decision dated 29.06.2021 and Rs. Supplementary Demand of Rs. 350.00 crore has been approved for Samayona in the July, 2021 session. The Supplementary Statement of Expenditure was presented to the Legislative Council in the Monsoon Session of July, 2021. The Supplementary Appropriation Bill has been approved by the Cabinet for inclusion in the Appropriation for the financial year ending March 31, 2022. The Governor has given his assent and the same has been published in the Maharashtra Government Gazette Extraordinary Part-IV, dated 15th July, 2021 as the Maharashtra (Second Supplementary) Appropriation Act, 2021 (Maharashtra Act No. 08 of 2021). Accordingly, the Government sanctioned Rs. 350.00 crore was under the consideration of the Government for making available the same to the Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company (MSDDC) in an adjusted manner.
Government Decision: Out of the subsidy amount payable to Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Limited for the rebate given to the power tariff to the mill consumers for the financial year 2020-21, Rs. 350.00 crore (Rupees Three Hundred and Fifty Crores only) is being allowed to be released by this Government as adjustment instead of cash.
The expenditure for the said purpose should be spent under the head" Demand No.K-6,2801 Electricity, 05 Transmission and Distribution, 104, Power Development System National Load, (01) Discount in Electricity Rates, (01) (02) Electricity Rate Discount to Machinery Consumers (General) (28015581), (Programme) 33 Financial Assistance" and it should be met from the provision approved for the financial year 2021-22.
