The Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC) project is being implemented by the Ministry of Commerce and Industry in collaboration with the Government of Japan. The 1482-km-long freight corridor will connect six states- Uttar Pradesh, Delhi, Haryana, Rajasthan, Gujarat and Madhya Pradesh- with Maharashtra. The industrial belt will start from Dadri near Delhi and culminate at Jawaharlal Nehru Port Trust (JNPT) in Maharashtra. The project has been approved by the Union Cabinet in its meeting dated 15/9/2011. The Delhi Mumbai Industrial Corridor Development Corporation (DMICDC) has been set up by the Union Ministry of Commerce and Industry, New Delhi, for the construction of the Delhi Mumbai Industrial Corridor Project. The Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation has been appointed as the nodal agency by the state government. This is an ambitious project. The Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC) has been appointed as the nodal agency for the project. A DMIC Trust has been set up through DMICDC for the execution of the project.
A Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) has been formed for the implementation of this project in Dighi, Shendra and other places in a joint venture between DMIC Trust and Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation. For this purpose, a draft State Support Agreement (SSA) has been prepared between DMIC Trust and Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC) in respect of investment in the company for the state and central government, as well as for the facilities, concessions and assistance to be provided by the state government to the said Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV).

This was approved by the Union Cabinet in its meeting held on 29/1/2014. The Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC) project covers 10 districts of Mumbai, Mumbai Suburban, Thane, Raigad, Nashik, Pune, Ahmednagar, Dhule, Nandurbar and Aurangabad in Maharashtra.
The projects approved are Shendra (Aurangabad), Multi Modal Logistics Park at Karla under Dighi Port Industrial Area, Exhibition and Convention Centre at Aurangabad Industrial Area and Transport and Telecommunication Corridor along Pune-Nashik and Pune-Aurangabad Highway.
In this backdrop, the government is taking the following decisions with the approval of the State Cabinet.
Government Decision: Under the Delhi Mumbai Industrial Corridor Project of the Central Government, the Dighi Industrial Area has been notified as 12140.842. The Government is approving the development of 50% of the R area through Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation instead of Delhi Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC), after examining all legal aspects and subject to the approval of the Department of Industries and subject to the intimation or consent of the Central Government and the concerned department as per requirement.
