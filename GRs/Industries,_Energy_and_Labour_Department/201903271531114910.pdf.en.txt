The Proposal:
In order to enable the farmers to irrigate during the day and to achieve the objective of saving the cost of connecting the traditional agricultural pumps and the subsidy provided by the State Government, the Chief Minister's Solar Agriculture Pump Scheme has been approved by the Government through a decision dated 15th November, 2018 to provide 1 lakh non-transmission solar agricultural pumps to the farmers of the State in a phased manner. " Mukhyamantri Solar Krishi Pump Yojana" is a fully state government scheme under which it is planned to install one lakh solar Krishi Pumps in a phased manner in the next 3 years. In the first phase, 25,000 solar pumps will be installed, in the second phase, 50,000 solar pumps will be installed and in the third phase, 25,000 solar pumps will be installed. The general category beneficiaries will be given 10 per cent of the state's budgetary subsidy, 10 per cent of the beneficiary share and the remaining 80 per cent will be paid from the mutual amount collected from the tax on sale of surplus power in the escrow account of MSEDCL.
According to the government decision, the 10 percent share of the state government's subsidy for the general category beneficiaries of the 25,000 Nagasolar Krishi Pumps in the first phase has been calculated at Rs. 67.71 crore. 35.86 crore. However, the department now has a provision of Rs. 32.48902 crore for this purpose and accordingly, the Government was considering to disburse Rs. 32.48902 crore as 10% share of the State Government's subsidy in the" Mukhyamantri Solar Krishi Pump Scheme" within the limit of the available provision.
