The Proposal: -
The Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996 has been enacted by the Central Government to regulate the employment and conditions of service of workers working in the building and other construction sectors and to provide for the safety, health and welfare measures for construction workers. Accordingly, the Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2007 have been framed to give effect to the Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996. The Maharashtra Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Board has been set up in the state under Section 18 of the Act.
Workers working in the building and other construction sectors in the state are registered and renewed by the Board. The Board implements 29 different welfare schemes for active (living) construction workers registered with it out of the supplementary tax funds accruing to the Board. These schemes are classified into educational assistance schemes, health subject schemes, financial assistance schemes, social security and others.
