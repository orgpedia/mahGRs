As per Rule 10 (4) and Rule 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, it is the policy of the Government to retire officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity in public interest at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier. Accordingly, the General Administration Department vide Reference No. 1 here. The Government Decision dated 10/06/2019 prescribes the procedure and general instructions regarding service review. Accordingly, the Grade Pay of Group-A (Gazetted) (ex-officio Senior Grade) in the Electricity Inspectorate has been increased from Rs. (2) Group-A (Gazetted) (Senior Pay Grade) Officers in the cadre of 7600 and above but less than 10000 (3) Group-A (Gazetted) (Junior Pay Grade) Officers in the cadre of 7600 and above and (4) Group-B (Gazetted) Officers in the cadre of 7600 and below. 2 here. As per the Government decision dated 24/02/2020, a Departmental Review Committee has been constituted under the Chairmanship of Secretary (Power)/ Principal Secretary (Power).
The proposal for reviewing the services of Assistant Electrical Inspector, Group-A (Gazetted) Cadre Officers in the Electricity Inspectorate at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier, was submitted to the Departmental Review Committee headed by Principal Secretary (Power) on a rotational basis. As per the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee after evaluating the service of the said officers, it was under the consideration of the Government to approve the officers who have been found eligible to remain in service at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.

GOVERNMENT DECISION: In accordance with the recommendation of the Departmental Review Committee, the services of the Assistant Electrical Inspector, Group-A (Gazetted) cadre of the Electricity Inspectorate are being approved to continue in the service of the officers mentioned in the attached form at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
The officers mentioned in the attached form should be recorded in their service book as having been approved to continue their service after attaining the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service.
