Read:- Letter from the Labour Commissioner, Maharashtra State, Mumbai No. KA/ GRAVI/ KIWA/ 2018/ Work- 10/1136, dated 6th August, 2018
Preamble: The Government vide Notification dated 5th July, 2013 has re-fixed the minimum wages of workers in scheduled industries under the Minimum Wages Act, 1948, for work in any residential hotel, restaurant or eatery in the State or work incidental thereto or work in any such industry (as defined in the Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948). The Indian Hotel and Restaurant Association (IHRA) and the Hotel Owners Association of Nagpur (HOA) have opposed the move. The High Court of Bombay at No. 5542/2013 has been filed. The plea has sought stay of the notification dated July 5, 2013, and also an order directing deduction from their wages for food (tea, snacks, meals) provided to them by the employer and other matters. Following that. The High Court, Bombay on 12th April, 2018 has ordered to fix the cash value of subsidized food items provided to the workers by the employer. Accordingly, a tripartite committee comprising government representatives, employers" representatives and workers" representatives under the chairmanship of the Labour Commissioner was being considered by the government.
Government Decision:" Work in any residential hotel, restaurant or eatery in the State or work incidental thereto or work in any such industry (as defined in the Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948)" to be provided at concessional rates to workers in scheduled industries under the Minimum Wages Act, 1948.

Travel allowance and dearness allowance will be admissible to the non-official members appointed in the said committee as per the prevailing rules.
The expenditure should be met from the Main Account Head Demand No. K-4, 2230 Labour and Service Scheme (22300414) Operation and Administration during the financial year 2018-19.
The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
Section 11 (2) of the Minimum Wages Act, 1948 provides that" in cases where it is the practice of the appropriate Government to pay wages in whole or in part, as the case may be, it may deem necessary, the appropriate Government may, by notification in the official Gazette, authorise the payment of minimum wages in whole or in part." To examine whether this provision shall apply to work in a residential hotel, restaurant or eatery or to work incidental to such process or to work in any such industry.
2) To recommend to the Government, if the provision continues to be in force, the issue by notification of the cash price in the said industry.
