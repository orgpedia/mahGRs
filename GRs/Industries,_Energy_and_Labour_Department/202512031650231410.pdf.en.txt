Government Circular: -
Promises are made by the Minister during the session of the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly during the proceedings of both the Houses. It is the statutory responsibility of the Ministries concerned to ensure that the promises made by the Minister in the House are fulfilled expeditiously. It is also the statutory responsibility of the Ministries to ensure that the Parliamentary arms presented by the representatives of the two Houses of the Legislature are completed within the stipulated time and reported to the Legislative Secretariat. In order to implement this effectively, the following Committees are being constituted in the Department of Labour in accordance with the instructions given by the Government Circular dated October, 2025 in the context of the Department of Parliamentary Affairs: -

Joint / Deputy Secretary (Coordination of Legislative Affairs)
2. The Assurances and Parliamentary Arms Supplies Committee as well as all the relevant offices of the Department shall act in accordance with the following instructions for the settlement of promises and Parliamentary Arms.
1) Two separate registers with separate list of pending promises and separate list of pending parliamentary arms shall be maintained in the department by the Coordinating Branch. The Deputy Secretary (Legislative Affairs Coordination) of the department should conduct a self-check once a month to ensure that the lists are up to date.
The Joint / Deputy Secretary (Coordination of Legislative Affairs) of the Department shall convene a meeting of the Committee twice a month, every fifteen days. The Regional Officer as well as the Executive Officer concerned should be invited to these meetings as required.

3) At these meetings, a list of the pending promises of the first division and the parliamentary armaments should be submitted to the committee. A decision should also be taken to take necessary measures to address these promises and parliamentary arms by setting a time limit.
(4) The meetings shall take special measures to dispose of promises pending for more than two years, b) Parliamentary arms, and work out a time-frame for disposal of all promises, b) Parliamentary arms.
5) The government should take a decision according to the circular on the exclusion of promises that cannot be fulfilled and act accordingly. The decision regarding the transfer of assurance from other departments should be taken in the said meetings and the same should be returned to the concerned department accordingly, the current status regarding the transfer of assurance from the department to the other department should be presented in the meetings and action should be taken regarding the follow-up in other departments accordingly, the long pending transfer of assurance in other departments should be taken after taking the feedback of the General Administration Department (Structure and Works).
6) After the department has fulfilled the promises and parliamentary instruments, it should be verified at the meetings that the same has been taken up on the website of the Legislative Secretariat as well as in the respective cells.
7) The pending promises and parliamentary arms of the department are reviewed by the Assurances Committee and Ad-hoc Committee of the Legislature. Action should be taken according to the correspondence made by the said committee and a review should be done in this meeting.
The department should strictly follow the above instructions for the fulfillment of the promises pending with the department for more than the prescribed period, ensuring that the circular is consciously brought to the notice of all the officers and employees of the department.
8) This Government Circular is available on the Government of Maharashtra website www.maharashtra.gov.in.
