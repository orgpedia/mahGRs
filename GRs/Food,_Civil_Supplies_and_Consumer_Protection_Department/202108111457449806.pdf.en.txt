Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food and Public Distribution The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 has been enacted vide Notification dated 15.07.2020. It has come into effect from 20.07.2020.
Before the enactment of the said Act, the salaries, allowances and honorarium were paid to the Chairperson and Members of the State Commission and District Commission as per the provisions of the Consumer Protection (Appointment, Salaries, Allowances and Conditions of Service of Chairperson and Members of the State Commission and District Commission) Rules, 2019.
Section 102 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 empowers the State Government to make rules by notification. However, the proviso to Article 102 (1) provides that the Central Government may make model rules in respect of all or any of the provisions of which the State Government is empowered to make rules and these rules shall continue to be in force until the rules of the State Government come into force.
Under Section 102 of the Act, the State Government has the power to make rules regarding the salaries, allowances and other terms and conditions of service of the Chairman and Members of the State Consumer Redressal Commission and the District Consumer Redressal Commission. However, under the proviso to Section 102 (1) of the Central Government's Consumer Protection (Salary, Allowances and Conditions of Service of President and Members of the State Commission and District Commission) Model Rules, 2020, the Central Government has not made any provision for the benefit of the Commission. It has come into effect from 20/07/2020. Salaries and allowances to the Chairman and Members of the State Commission and District Commission
