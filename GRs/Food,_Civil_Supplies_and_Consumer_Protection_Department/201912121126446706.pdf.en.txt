The proposal-
The Minimum Support Price (MSP) scheme is a central government scheme that helps farmers sell their produce at a lower price than the guaranteed price. For this purpose, the Maharashtra State Co-op (in the non-tribal sector) will be formed through the agent agencies appointed by the state government. Marketing Federation, Mumbai and in tribal areas, Maharashtra State Cooperative Tribal Development Corporation, Nashik) procures paddy and millets of FAQ (Fair Average Quality) in the state. The CMR obtained by millers through the agency agencies for the paddy procured under the above scheme.
Custom milled rice was being deposited with the Food Corporation of India. Considering the recommendation of the Central Government regarding the implementation of the" Decentralized Procurement Scheme" (DCPS) for paddy in the State, the State Government has decided to implement the Decentralized Procurement Scheme in the entire State from the 2016-17 season.
The Central Government has allotted Rs. 10/- per quintal has been announced. The farmers were demanding that the state government should announce a higher price for paddy as the millers in the state could not afford to pay the same price. Keeping this in view, the Government was considering to announce a higher price for 2018-19 season in addition to the price announced by the Central Government.
Government's decision:
The Government has decided to sanction additional procurement price by the State Governments over and above the procurement price approved by the Central Government for paddy procured by the Agents under the Decentralized Procurement Scheme under the Minimum Support Price Purchase Scheme during 2018-19 as under:
