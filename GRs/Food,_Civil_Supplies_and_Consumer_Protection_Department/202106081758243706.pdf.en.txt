Introduction: Minimum Support Price Purchase Scheme is a Central Government scheme, which is implemented so that farmers do not have to sell food grains at a lower price than the guaranteed price.
For this purpose, the Maharashtra State Co-op will be formed through the agent agencies appointed by the state government (in non-tribal areas. Marketing Federation, Mumbai and in the tribal areas, Maharashtra State Cooperative Tribal Development Corporation, Nashik) procures paddy and millets of FAQ (Fair Average Quality) in the state.
The instructions for procurement of paddy/ coarse grains in the State are issued as per the guidelines received from the Central Government for procurement of paddy/ coarse grains in each season.
Notice regarding procurement of Paddy/ Coarse Cereals (Kharif) for the season 2020-21 vide Government Resolution No. Purchase 1020/ PR No.104/ NAPU-29, Dy. issued on 29.09.2020.
A large amount of millets is grown in the state during the Rabi season. Coarse Cereals (Jowar/ Maize) have been procured under MSP for Kharif season 2018-19 (Rabi) and Kharif season 2019-20 (Rabi). On the same lines, the Government is contemplating to issue instructions for procurement of coarse cereals (Jowar/ Maize) for the Kharif season 2020-21 (Rabi). Government Resolution No. Purchase 1020/ P.S. No. 146 (Part III)/ No.P. 29, D. Instructions have been issued for procurement of Jowar as on 02.06.2021. Now the government is giving the following orders regarding the purchase of maize.
Government Decision: In the Kharif season 2020-21 (Rabi), the Central Government has released the detailed specifications and MSP of coarse cereals (Maize) under the Minimum Support Price Procurement Scheme. Besides, the Centre has suggested opening of control rooms at the state level to ensure that farmers get the benefit of the MSP and do not have to sell their produce below the assured price.

Under the Minimum Support Price (MSP) procurement scheme, the procurement of coarse cereals should be done as per the specifications prescribed by the Central Government. Accordingly, the agent agencies should purchase millets only after inspection by trained graders. The agent agencies will be fully responsible for the procurement of low quality millets and in case of any financial loss or any other problem arising out of it.
The quality check of the procured paddy will be done by the government (quality control trained staff in the office of the District Supply Officer). The agent agency will be fully responsible if any defect is found in the inspection at the time of procurement or storage of quality. Also, the corrective action in the matter has to be taken by the agent agency and the agent agency will be fully responsible for the loss caused by such action.
Humidity: The Central Government has prescribed a maximum moisture content of 14% for procurement of Maize in the harvesting season 2020-21 (Rabi). Coarse grains should not be purchased if moisture content is more than that. (Annexure-I) Under no circumstances should you buy coarse grains which are too wet or have fungi. Agents should ensure that millets are clean and dry and are marketable. The agent agencies should take punitive action against the concerned on time if they buy coarse grains with more moisture content than the prescribed quantity.

Storage of procured Coarse Cereals (Maize): (a) Coarse Cereals procured as per the specifications by the agent agencies should be taken over by the representative of the State Government, preferably on the same day in the godown near the centre, after making full weight and ensuring the quality, as decided by the Collector and the same should be recorded in the godown accounts regularly.
