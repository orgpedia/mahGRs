Introduction: Minimum Support Price Purchase Scheme is a Central Government scheme, which is implemented so that farmers do not have to sell food grains at a lower price than the guaranteed price. For this purpose, the Maharashtra State Co-op will be formed through the agent agencies appointed by the state government (in non-tribal areas. Marketing Federation, Mumbai and in tribal areas, Maharashtra State Cooperative Tribal Development Corporation, Nashik) procures paddy and millets of FAQ (Fair Average Quality) in the state. The CMR obtained by millers through the agency agencies for the paddy procured under the above scheme.
Custom milled rice was being deposited with the Food Corporation of India. Considering the recommendation of the Central Government regarding the implementation of the" Decentralized Procurement Scheme" (DCPS) for paddy in the State, the State Government has decided to implement the Decentralized Procurement Scheme in the entire State from the 2016-17 season.
Under this scheme, paddy is transported from the Paddy Procurement Center to the Paddy Mill and from the Paddy Mill to the CMR Warehouse (Mill to Base Depot). For this, the transportation rates were being fixed according to the Provisional Cost Sheet fixed for the harvest season. Now, from the Kharif season 2019-20 onwards, the Central Government has decided to fix the rates for transportation of paddy/ CMR. The guidelines have been received vide letter dated 06.05.2019. The matter was under consideration of the government.

Government's decision:
In accordance with the guidelines received from the Central Government vide letter No. 192 (14)/ 2018-FCA/ CS, dated 6th May, 2019 for determination of rates for transportation of Paddy/ CMR, the following State Level Committee is being constituted under this Government for determination of Rates of Transportation (SoR) for transportation of Paddy procured under MSP from Kharif Marketing Season 2019-20 and CMR derived therefrom:
Principal Secretary, Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department Chairman General Manager, Food Corporation of India Member District Collector, Gondia Member 4 Collector, Gadchiroli Member 5 Joint/ Deputy Secretary Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department Member (Minimum Support Price Procurement Scheme)
6) Home (Member of Joint Transport Commissioner of Transport Department 7) Financial Advisor and Deputy Secretary, Member Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department.
Under Secretary, Department of Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Member Secretary (Minimum Support Price Purchase Scheme)
The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
. To finalize the list of transport rates based on the market survey for the state. These rates will be applicable for a maximum period of 2 years.
" For all the districts of the state, there will be stages of uniform distances as follows:- 0 to 8 km, 8 km to 20 km, 20 km to 40 km, 40 km to 80 km and above 80 km. It will also include transportation of paddy procured from the Tribal Development Corporation to the designated godowns.
(iii) For this, while fixing the traffic rates at the district level, they should be determined in a competitive manner through open e-tendering.
(iv) The State Level Committee shall determine the draft tenders to be invited by all the districts for the said transport rates.
(v) The State Level Committee on Transport Rates fixed at the district level shall take a decision on whether such rates should be adopted or not. If there is a large difference between the rates fixed in the districts and the rates fixed by the State Level Committee (which will be for 2 years), the rates which the State Level Committee may approve/ disapprove should be mentioned along with the minutes of the meeting of the State Level Committee, along with the reasons for their acceptance/ rejection.
(vi) In case of any difference of opinion between the representatives of the Food Corporation of India and the representatives of the State Government in the State Level Committee, the matter may be referred to the Chief Managing Director.
