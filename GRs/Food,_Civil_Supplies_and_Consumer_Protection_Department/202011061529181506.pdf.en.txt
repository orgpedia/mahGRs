The Proposal:
The Consumer Protection Act, 2019. The Consumer Protection Act, 1986 has lapsed with effect from 20.07.2020 under Section 107 (1) of the Act. As per Section 101 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, the Central Government is empowered to make rules by notification. Under these powers and under the provisions of Article 29 and Article 43, the Central Government may The Consumer Protection (Qualification for Appointment, Method of Recruitment, Procedure of Appointment, Term of Office, Resignation and Removal of the President and Members of the State Commission and District Commission) Rules, 2020 have been framed vide notification dated 15.07.2020. It has been implemented with effect from 20.07.2020. the State Consumer Redressal Commission and the District Consumer Redressal Commission under Rule 6 of this rule.
The appointment of the Chairperson and Members of the Commission shall be made by the State Government on the recommendation of the Selection Committee. Considering the provision of the selection committee in the said rule, it was under the consideration of the Government to constitute a selection committee to recommend to the State Government for appointment of Chairman and Members of the State Commission and District Commission.
Government's decision:
The appointment of Chairman and Members of the State Consumer Redressal Commission and District Consumer Redressal Commission in accordance with the provisions of Rule 6 of the Central Government Consumer Protection (Qualification for appointment, method of recruitment, procedure of appointment, term of office, resignation and removal of the President and Members of the State Commission and District Commission) Rules, 2020
