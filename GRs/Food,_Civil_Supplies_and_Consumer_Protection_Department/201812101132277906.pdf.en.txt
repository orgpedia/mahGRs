Preamble: In order to curb the irregularities related to petroleum products in the state, instructions regarding regular inspection/ raids of petroleum products have been issued vide Government Resolution No. 1 and 2 have been issued to all the regional offices. Further, to prevent pilferage/ misuse of essential commodities in the Public Distribution System in the State, instructions regarding regular inspections and surprise inspections/ raids of fair price shops and kerosene licensees in the State have been issued vide Government Circular No. 3 and 4 have been issued to all the regional offices. However, some incidents of illegal use of domestic gas in the state have come to the notice of the government. Therefore, like kerosene and other petroleum products, regular/ surprise inspections of gas agencies in the State are required to check the illegal use of domestic gas and make it available to the eligible beneficiaries.
Government Circular: In order to prevent pilferage/ misuse of essential commodities in the public distribution system in the state, instructions regarding regular inspections and surprise checks/ raids of fair price shops and kerosene licensees in the state have been issued under the relevant Government Circular No. 3 and 4 have been issued to all the regional offices. Accordingly, every fair price shop and kerosene license holder in the district/ rationing area will be inspected at least once in six months. Accordingly, while conducting inspections of kerosene and fair price shop licenses, an inspection drive should be carried out in every gas agency in the district/ rationing area at least once in six months.

Surprise inspections/ raids should also be conducted. The roster of the Regional Officers for Gas Agency Inspection should be prepared as per the standards prescribed and issued by each Regional Officer.
Gas agency inspections should be completed as required. Every field officer should set a monthly inspection target. The examination will be conducted at the district, taluk and district levels.
The Deputy Commissioner (Supplies) and the Collector shall make arrangements so that each gas agency is inspected at least once in every half-year and at least twice in a year while fixing the monthly frequency of regular inspections for all field officers. Apart from regular checks, surprise checks and raids should also be conducted in respect of complaints received regarding illegal use of domestic gas. These surprise checks and raids will be in addition to the regular checks.
The search/ inspection shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Essential Commodities Act, 1955 and the Liquefied Petroleum Gas (Restriction on Use and Fixation of Selling Price) Order, 2000. Action should be taken against the guilty as per the law. Also, it should be ensured that domestic gas is not used for commercial purposes.
The report on these inspections should be submitted to the government by the 5th of every month.
5. Disciplinary action as per Maharashtra Civil Services Rules to be taken by the concerned District Collector and Deputy Commissioner (Supplies) against the officers/ employees of domestic gas agencies and those who do not check the illegal use of gas as per the prescribed standards.
