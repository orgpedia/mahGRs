Read: Government Decision Revenue and Forest Department, No.CLS-2018/ Pr. 225/ M-3, Dy. 8th March, 2019 Preamble: The State of Maharashtra will have a special session in July, 2021. On July 22 and 23, 2021, many families have been affected due to heavy rains in Raigad, Ratnagiri, Sindhudurg, Kolhapur, Sangli and Satara districts. The state government was considering a proposal to provide foodgrains (wheat and rice), kerosene and tur dal to such affected families as a means of livelihood at least in emergency situations. The following decisions have been taken by the government:
(a) The districts of Raigad, Ratnagiri, Sindhudurg, Kolhapur, Sangli and Satara are likely to be affected by floods in the month of July 2021. The families affected by the heavy rains on July 22 and 23, 2021, should be provided 10 kg of wheat and 10 kg of rice per family (total 20 kg of food grains by increasing the quantity of rice if demanded by the affected family) and 5 litres of kerosene and 5 kg of tur dal free of cost.
(b) The number/ list of affected families should be made available by the concerned Collector from the concerned District Rehabilitation Officer of the district. The Collector should immediately start distribution of foodgrains from the balance of other schemes in the government godowns at the district level.
After the total quantity of foodgrains is intimated to the Government, the demand for foodgrains equivalent to this quantity shall be made to the Central Government at the MSP/ MSP determined rate and the funds required for the same shall be made available to the District Collectors as and when made available by the Finance Department.
(c) The Government should inform the oil companies BPCL, IOC and HPCL about the availability of kerosene required for the affected families to the District Collector immediately free of cost.
Necessary funds for this will be made available to the District Collector after it is made available by the Finance Department.

(d) The District Collector concerned should immediately start the distribution of tur dal required for the affected families in the quantity of 5 kg per family by purchasing it from the wholesale market in their district. The Collector should provide the required funds from his Disaster Relief Fund. After the Collector informs the government about the quantity of tur dal purchased, the funds equivalent to the amount spent for this tur dal will be made available to the Collector after it is available from the Finance Department.
(e) The Finance Department should immediately make available the necessary funds for the above matters after receiving the data from all the Collectors as to the quantity of foodgrains, pulses and kerosene distributed and the expenditure incurred on them.
