Introduction: Minimum Support Price Purchase Scheme is a Central Government scheme, which is implemented so that farmers do not have to sell food grains at a lower price than the guaranteed price. For this purpose, the Maharashtra State Co-op will be formed through the agent agencies appointed by the state government (in non-tribal areas. Marketing Federation, Mumbai and in tribal areas, Maharashtra State Cooperative Tribal Development Corporation, Nashik) procures paddy and millets of FAQ (Fair Average Quality) in the state.
The instructions for procurement of paddy/ coarse grains in the State are issued as per the guidelines received from the Central Government for procurement of paddy/ coarse grains in each season. Accordingly, comprehensive instructions regarding procurement of Paddy/ Coarse Cereals for Kharif Season 2021-22 have been issued vide Government Decision No. Purchase- 2621/ No.138/ No.29, Dy. 30.09.2021 (Refer No. 1) has been removed.
However, in the meantime, pending approval of the anticipated Coarse Cereals Procurement Plan for the Kharif Marketing Season 2021-22, the Government has decided to issue separate dates for procurement of Coarse Cereals for the said season. Central Government Reference No. Under Letter No. 4, the expected Coarse Cereals Procurement Plan for the said season has been approved. Accordingly, the government was considering to issue a decision regarding the time frame for the procurement of coarse grains under the Minimum Support Price Procurement Scheme in the Kharif Marketing Season 2021-22 in the state and to approve the procurement process by opening procurement centers. The government has issued an order in this regard.
Government Decision: In the Kharif Marketing Season 2021-22, the Central Government has announced the specifications and MSP for coarse cereals (Maize, Jowar, Bajra, Ragi) under the Minimum Support Price Procurement Scheme. Accordingly, the government is giving approval to set up procurement centers in the state and purchase coarse grains (maize, sorghum, millet, ragi) there so that the farmers of the state can get the benefit of minimum support price.
