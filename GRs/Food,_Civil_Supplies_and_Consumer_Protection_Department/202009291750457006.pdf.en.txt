Introduction: Minimum Support Price Purchase Scheme is a Central Government scheme, which is implemented so that farmers do not have to sell food grains at a lower price than the guaranteed price.
For this purpose, the Maharashtra State Co-op will be formed through the agent agencies appointed by the state government (in non-tribal areas. Marketing Federation, Mumbai and in tribal areas, Maharashtra State Cooperative Tribal Development Corporation, Nashik) procures paddy and millets of FAQ (Fair Average Quality) in the state. Earlier, till 2016-17, the procurement of paddy procured under the above scheme was being done by millers through procurement agents.
Custom Milled Rice (Raw Rice) was being deposited with the Food Corporation of India. Considering the recommendation of the Central Government regarding the implementation of the Decentralized Procurement Scheme (DCPS) in the state for the purchase of paddy, the State Government has decided to implement the Decentralized Procurement Scheme in the entire state from the 2016-17 season. Therefore, the CMR generated under this scheme is being deposited in the godowns specified by the state government. Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to issue instructions for procurement of paddy/ coarse grains during 2020-21 season.
Government's decision:
In Kharif Marketing Season 2020-21, the Central Government has announced Uniform Specifications and Minimum Support Price (MSP) for Paddy and Millets (Jowar, Bajra, Maize and Ragi) under the Minimum Support Price (MSP) procurement Scheme. Besides, the Centre has suggested opening of control rooms at the state level to ensure that farmers get the benefit of the MSP and do not have to sell their produce below the assured price.
