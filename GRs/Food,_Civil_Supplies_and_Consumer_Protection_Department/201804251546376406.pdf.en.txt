Preamble: A proposal to reconstitute the existing State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee to advise the Government on the measures to be taken to promote and accelerate the consumer movement in the State was under consideration.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Government after giving a comprehensive consideration to the matter, have decided to reconstitute the State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee as follows.

The term of the said Committee shall be for a period of three years from the date of issue of this Order or until further orders of the Government, whichever is earlier.
The term of office of the members to be appointed on the State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee shall be for a period of three years from the date of their appointment or until further orders, whichever is earlier.
Meeting of State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee: (a) The State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee shall meet once in two months.
(b) To hold meetings of the State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee on a regular basis even if all or some of its members have not been appointed.
Functions of the State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee: The functions of the State Consumer Welfare Advisory Committee are: 1) To advise the Government on the measures to be taken for the overall development of the consumer movement in the State by promoting it.
(ii) To organize and implement programmes related to consumer education, consumer awareness and related activities within the State.
3) To publish articles/ literature on consumer movement, consumer education and consumer awareness.
4) To effectively conduct World Consumer Day and National Consumer Day programmes in the State.
(v) To advise the Government from time to time on the policies and procedures of the various departments/ undertakings of the Government in the interest of the consumers.
6) Suggest topics for the State-level Consumer Protection Council meeting, follow up on the decisions taken in the meeting from time to time.
7) To see how the meetings of the District Consumer Protection Councils set up at the district level are held every month with a view to resolving consumer issues through consumer interaction, as well as to follow up on the decisions taken at the meetings.
8) To plan and effectively implement programmes related to consumer awareness.
7. Travel allowance and other allowances to the non-official members of the Committee shall be admissible as per the prevailing policy of the Government.
