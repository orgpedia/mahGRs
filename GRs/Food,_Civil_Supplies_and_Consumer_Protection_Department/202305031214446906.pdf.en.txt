The Proposal:
The Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection has a validated metrology system in place which implements the Legal Metrology Act, 2009 and various rules framed thereunder. Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Affairs Department. Under 22/04/1992, Controller, Valid Metrology has been declared as the Head of the Department. The government has also made a decision. Under 30/01/1990," Co-Controller" of Valid Metrology has been declared as Regional Head of the Department. The provisions of the extant Rules/ Act are required to be complied with while exercising powers in respect of leave of officers, additional charge and salary for the period, transfers, disciplinary proceedings, licences granted by the valid Metrology System and powers to dispose of cases. Accordingly, it was under the consideration of the Government to bring to the notice of all concerned the provisions of the existing rules/ act regarding the powers of the Regional Head of Department, Head of Department and Administrative Department.
Government circular:
Grant of leave.-Under Rule 29 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981 read with Annexure-I Sl. No. 6 of the said Rules, the Government servants in the State of Rajasthan are empowered to grant leave other than study leave and special disability leave.
(a) Head of Department:- 180 days in case of Group A and Group B officers.
The competent authority shall exercise its powers while granting leave to Group A and Group B officers of the valid Metrology System in accordance with the above provisions.

(ii) Additional Charge:- Rule 56 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pay) Rules, 1981 provides for entrustment of additional charge of one or more posts to a Government servant in addition to his post. Finance Department, Govt. The Head of the Department/ Regional Head of the Department concerned is empowered to make arrangements for additional workload and sanction special pay for the period up to one year from the date of first vacancy as on December 27, 2011. After this, the concerned administrative department has the power to arrange for additional workload for a period of up to 1 year and sanction special pay for it.
As per the above provision, additional charge up to 1 year shall be provided by the Regional Head of Department/ Head of Department. Also, the guidelines of the Government Decision Finance Department dated 05/09/2018 should be strictly followed while arranging the additional workload. After that, since the administrative department has the power to arrange for additional workload for a period up to the next 1 year, proposals for giving additional workload to the post lying vacant for more than 1 year should be submitted to the administrative department.
Transfer of Group-C and Group-D employees:- As per Section 6 (c) of the Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Employees and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005, the Head of the Department has the power to transfer Group-C employees. These laws should be implemented strictly.
Disciplinary Action:- As per Rule 2 (a) and Rule 6 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979, the Head of the Department is the disciplinary authority in respect of Group-A and Group-B employees. The Regional Head of Department will exercise the powers regarding minor punishments in respect of Group B employees.
As per Rule 7, the disciplinary authority has the power to initiate disciplinary proceedings against any government employee under its administrative control. Accordingly, disciplinary action (departmental inquiry) should be taken against the officer who violated the rules of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules 1979 by exercising these powers. The departmental inquiry must result in severe punishment to the employee concerned or a case is serious enough to warrant severe punishment.

Only such disciplinary proposals should be submitted to the government if it appears prima facie.
Licensing and Litigation Redressal: As per Section 50 of the Valid Metrology Act, 2009, the Controller, Valid Metrology is the Appellate Authority. Government Notification No.LMO/ 2010/410/ PR No.175/ (Part-II)/ GRASM-4D As on 18th June, 2011, the Controller, Valid Metrology, HQ, Mumbai has been empowered to grant State Level License to the manufacturers, distributors and distributors of Weights and Measures. Further, vide order dated 29.07.2011, the Controller of Synchronous, Valid Metrology has delegated certain powers to the Co-Controller, Headquarters, Mumbai and Regional Co-Controller, Valid Metrology for enforcement of the Valid Metrology Act, 2009 and the Valid Metrology (Enforcement) Rules, 2011. As per the said authority, the cases relating to implementation of Valid Metrology Act, 2009 should be disposed of at appropriate level.
The above powers regarding leave of Group A and Group B employees in valid Metrology System, additional charge of vacant posts in Group A and Group B cadres, transfers of Group C and Group D employees, disciplinary action, licences issued through valid Metrology System and disposal of cases should be strictly followed.
