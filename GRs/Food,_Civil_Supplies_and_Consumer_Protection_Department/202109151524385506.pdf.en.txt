Introduction: The Government of India has enacted the National Food Security Act, 2013 with effect from 5th July, 2013. As per the Act, a total of 700.16 lakh beneficiaries have been identified for the State comprising 76.32 per cent rural (469.71 lakh) and 45.34 per cent urban (230.45 lakh) beneficiaries. The Act has been implemented in the State with effect from 01.02.2014.
For selection of eligible beneficiaries under the National Food Security Act, 2013, firstly, the district-wise beneficiaries have been allocated for rural and urban areas as per the Government decision dated 17.12.2013 and thereafter revised from time to time as per the Government decision.
Instructions have been given to include ration cards up to 30.4.2018 as per the Government decision dated 21.5.2018. After that. 22.2.2019 and D. In order to meet the district-wise target of beneficiaries under National Food Security Act, 2013 as per Government decision dated 6.7.2019, the following schemes have been implemented: 15.2.2019 and D. Instructions have been given to include eligible ration card holders up to 30.6.2019 in the National Food Security Scheme.

(2) All ration cardholders holding ration cards of existing Antyodaya Anna Yojana and Priority Household beneficiary families, who are in possession of ration cards at present but are not included as eligible beneficiaries under the National Food Security Act, 2013, shall be required to submit a sample guarantee form along with this Government Order regarding annual family income to the competent authority.
(3) All citizens desirous of being included as eligible beneficiaries under the National Food Security Act, 2013, by way of a fresh ration card, shall also be required to submit to the competent authority a copy of the Guarantee Form along with this Government Order regarding annual family income.
4) The ration card holder should be included as eligible beneficiary under the National Food Security Act, 2013 as per the existing norms applicable from among the ration card holders available from time to time within the limit of the final allocation to the field office. Considering the technical period of computerization of ration card, action should be taken to provide the benefit of subsidized food grains to the ration card holder.
(5) All other instructions for selection of eligible beneficiaries under the relevant Government decisions shall remain in force.
