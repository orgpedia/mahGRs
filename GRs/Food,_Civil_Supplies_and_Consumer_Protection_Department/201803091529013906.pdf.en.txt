Preamble: The Personal Deposit Accounts (PLA) under the Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection are opened under Head 4408 (Capital Accounts). A total of 38 personal deposit accounts are functioning for each district in the state, excluding Mumbai and Mumbai Suburban districts, 1 (total 34) for each district collector as administrator, 1 (total 1) for each financial advisor and deputy secretary for each urban area of Mumbai and Thane, and 1 (total 3) for each district collector as administrator for each urban area of Pune, Solapur and Nagpur. In this, the amount paid by the fair price shop for lifting the grain, to be deposited as a fine, extortion or punishment, etc. is collected.
Reference No. As per the Government decision mentioned in section 3, a Government decision has been issued to deposit the amount collected under head 4408 through the grass system. Except for Mumbai and Thane cities, the amount is being collected through the grass system through the District Supply Officers/ Food Distribution Officers in the state. At present, the technical difficulties in depositing money through the grass system for Mumbai and Thane cities have been removed and the procedure for accepting the deposit in the personal loan account (PLA) through this grass system was under the consideration of the government.

Government Decision The revenue collected by the Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection under the head 4408 is to be deposited in the Personal Deposit Account through the Grass System for the cities of Mumbai and Thane. It will come into effect from 01/04/2018. The procedure for accounting, reconciliation and accounting of the accumulation after adopting this method is explained as follows.
Action to be taken by Fair Price Shop and other Adductors: 1. The Adductors shall deposit the amount in their own provident account under head 4408 by using the GRAS system. To deposit these amounts, the actors are expected to register and deposit on the GRASS system. However, the amount can be deposited even without registration.
The amounts to be deposited under the head 4408 should be deposited under 44080166-APL, Welfare, Sugar and Tur dal, 44080344-National Food Security Scheme and 44080406-Basic Price Scheme.
If the tenant is registered, he will not need to enter the information in the system again and again. After the payment of the amount on the grass system, the player gets to see the information of the invoice and the transaction made through the system and this information is securely stored in the state government server.
4) The payment can be made through Net Banking, Debit Card/ Credit Card.
Payments Across Bank Counter:- If the operator does not have the facility of Net Banking, then it will be possible to provide them through the grass system, so that the facility of payment across bank counter is provided. In this method, the details of the currency should be filled using Gras and its print should be taken out and the money should be paid in cash by going to the bank. This facility will be available only in banks available on the GRAS website.
After payment of the amount through GRASS system, the payee should provide the Government Reference Number (GRN) of the challan to the Circle Office in person, by telephone, SMS, e-mail or other means.
2. Procedure to be followed by the Circle Offices: 1. The Circle Offices shall have login facility in GRAS. After receiving the GRN from the administrator, the grass system should be logged in. The e-challan should be verified from the system based on GRN.

2. The perimeter wise PLA Receipt information on the grass system should be logged in to the grass and made available on a daily basis.
The Controlling Officer (Financial Adviser and Deputy Secretary) has published the Maharashtra Budget Rulebook Chapter No. The e-receipts and all other deposits and expenses shall be reconciled with the Pay and Accounts Office in the prescribed manner as before, in accordance with the procedure laid down in Rule 157 of Rule 14 and Paragraph 3 of Annexure III.

6. Revocation of Defacement/ Refund Entry: 1. There is a mistake or wrong entry in the return or defacement of currency from the office for which it is provided by the GRAS system. The facility of cancellation of such wrong entry or getting a new correct entry on e-challan has been made available in the Revoke option in the GRAS system. The office which has wrongly registered the defacement on the e-challan should submit the request proposal of Revoke to the virtual treasury office through the office of the financial advisor and deputy secretary. Also, in case of misreporting of returns, the request for revoke should be submitted to the Virtual Treasury Office through the Proposal Controller. Also, the request for revocation will have to be submitted by the office sending the original proposal through the Revoke option in the online mode through the grass system.
Revoke a return or a deformation is not a procedure but a facility provided to correct a wrong entry. The concerned office should take proper care while tampering with the invoice or taking notes of the return and ensure that such mistakes are not repeated.
7) Correction of Misclassification of Scheme Code: 1. Correction of the amount paid by the employer under the wrong scheme code has been made available in the Grass System under the option of Correction Memo/ Transfer Entry. The office for which the amendment has been provided for by the GRASS system must submit their request immediately to the Office of the Financial Adviser and Deputy Secretary by e-mail and by letter and online on the GRASS system as well as by e-mail to the Virtual Treasury Office.
The proposal for taking transfer entry through the office of Financial Adviser and Deputy Secretary will be submitted to the Accounts and Accounts Office, Mumbai. Accounts and Accounts Office, Mumbai

e-Report: e-Report facility has been made available to the concerned Heads of Departments/ Offices in the grass root system for downloading the report through Total Receipt option.
The following reports on e-accounting in the GRAS system have been made available in the login of the respective offices under the option of Reports Accounting Report.
i. Cash Account- Statement of deposits in respect of the head of account ii. Covering Schedule- Statement of indicia-wise balance of the scheme under the head Main Accounts iii. Daily Cash Book- Statement of daily accumulations as per scheme indicators iv. Total Receipt- Statement of various periods/ headings/ scheme indicators/ office-wise receipts.
The concerned offices should download the above report and take note of the e-deposit on the basis of it.
The above action is to be taken by the Board as well as the Office of the Financial Adviser and Deputy Secretary.
Verification of invoices provided through Payment Across Bank Counter option: Payment across bank counter facility has been provided to enable payment through grass system to those operators who do not have a net banking account. After receiving the cash, the bank staff will authenticate the challan by entering the details of the challan in the bank's system. In this regard, the issuer shall ensure that the GRN, Bank CIN, Bank Reference, Bank Scroll Number/ Date for the relevant e-challan is received through the system. After providing this option, the drawee submits the receipt provided by the bank to the concerned department/ office.
Once such an invoice is submitted by the assessee, the concerned department/ office is required to verify through the GRAS system whether the amount has been credited to the government accounts. However, on e-challans provided through Payment Across Bank Counter option, the GRN, Bank CIN, Bank Reference, Bank Scroll Number/ Date should be checked by the grass system and the service should not be provided to the issuer as it may result in loss of revenue to the government.
10) Heads of Departments/ Controlling Authorities concerned are required to ensure that the revenue and non-revenue amounts deposited with the Government through the grass system are accounted, reconciled, refunded, defacement of e-challans, etc. from time to time.
