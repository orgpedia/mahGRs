GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. 2022/ PR. No.46/ NAPU-22 It is hereby approved to implement the scheme of Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) of Rs. 150/- per beneficiary per month in lieu of foodgrains from the month of January, 2023 to the farmers beneficiaries holding APL (Kesari) ration cards who are not covered under the Food Security Act, 2013. Further, as per the provisions of the Cash Transfer of Food Subsidy Rules, 2015 notified by the Central Government based on the increase in the Minimum Support Price every year, the revised enhanced cash transfer per beneficiary per month (in the next decade) is being approved directly.
The Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for implementation of the scheme is as follows:
1). The bank account details required for DBT shall be filled up offline/ online in the attached format by the eligible ration card holders registered on the Ration Card Management System (RCMS) for the benefit of farmers as per the norms prescribed vide Government Order dated 24.7.2015. Applicants must provide the appropriate supporting documentation with their application.
2) The information from the applications received will be included in the computer manual by the data entry operator at the taluk level. After scrutinizing this information, the concerned Tahsildar will prepare a fair price shop-wise list of eligible beneficiaries.
The list will be sent to the district collector concerned. The concerned District Supply Officer will prepare a payment file in PFMS based on the information of the beneficiaries on RCMS for transfer of DBT benefit to their bank accounts and the payment will be made through PFMS system. For this, bank accounts should be opened at the district level.
After the payment file is prepared, the amount will be credited to the eligible beneficiaries' account from the bank account at the district level by the District Supply Officer. This process will be repeated every month.
8) The Tahsildar concerned shall submit the revised list of beneficiaries to the District Supply Officer after taking action on the inclusion of the names of beneficiaries as per the new applications received every month, as well as the exclusion of the names of ineligible or deceased beneficiaries.
The cash to be disbursed under this scheme will be deposited in the Aadhaar linked bank account of the woman head of the family. If the woman head of the family does not have a bank account, she should be encouraged to open a bank account. exceptional

Under the circumstances, the amount will be deposited in the Aadhaar-linked bank account of other family members with the approval of the concerned tehsildar.
6) To avail the benefit of this scheme, the Aadhaar number of all the members registered on the ration card must be linked to the Ration Card Management System (RCMS). This means that the benefit of DBT scheme will be available only to those beneficiaries whose Aadhaar number is linked to the RCMS system. If a member does not have an Aadhaar number, he will be guided by the concerned Tahsildar office regarding the process of preparing an Aadhaar card.
The benefit of DBT scheme will be available to the ration card holders who are currently registered on RCMS from January 2023.
4. d. The new ration card holders who are eligible for the scheme as per the criteria fixed under the Government decision dated 24.7.2015 will be given Rs. Fill in the self-declared certificate with the government circular dated 05.08.2015. The ration card holders should be registered on the RCMS system and the bank account details required for DBT should be filled in the attached form. The benefit of the DBT scheme will be allowed to the new eligible ration card holders in the next month after the completion of the bank account registration.
The preparatory expenditure for the implementation of the scheme is being approved to be met from the approved budgetary provision for the current financial year under the Kisan Yojana and to open a separate bank account at the district level as per the requirement for transfer of funds under the scheme.
6. Approval is being given to take decisions at the ministerial department level for the effective implementation of this farmers' scheme.
An expenditure of Rs. 59.96 crore per month is being approved for the implementation of the Cash Transfer (DBT) Scheme.
For the purpose of this scheme, the Financial Adviser and the Deputy Secretary are the Controlling Officers for the distribution of subsidy, the Collector as the Controlling Officer for this purpose and the District Supply Officer as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
