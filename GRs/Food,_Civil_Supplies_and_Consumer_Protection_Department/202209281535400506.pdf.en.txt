Minimum Support Price (MSP) Purchase Scheme is a Central Government scheme for the benefit of farmers. Under this scheme, the Central Government announces the minimum support price for various crops and the paddy (FAQ paddy and millets) is purchased by the state government so that the farmers do not have to sell the grain at a lower price than the guaranteed price (distress sale) to benefit from the support price.
The Food Corporation of India (FCI) is the nodal agency for the central government in the state of Maharashtra.
2. The Government had, from time to time, as per the prevailing situation, announced an incentive for the paddy produced during the kharif season and procured under the Minimum Support Price procurement scheme.
From time to time, complaints are received that traders and paddy growers of other states are getting the benefit of the Minimum Support Price procurement scheme as well as the incentive amount for paddy. The farmers of Maharashtra are expected to benefit directly from this scheme. There is a need to regularize the financial income of paddy farmers in the state and help them improve their living standards. It is also necessary to increase rice productivity and diversify crops and increase quality production rapidly. Accordingly, it is necessary to make a policy decision on how much, to whom, how and when to distribute the incentive amount, there should be a consistency in the incentive amount given by the state government.
In this regard, to take a decision regarding the payment of incentive for paddy. Cabinet sub-committee The directions have been given by the Cabinet in its meeting dated 12 th September, 2022.
