The Proposal:
The above reference No. 1 here. The format of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department was approved vide Government Resolution dated 26.06.2003. Finance Department Reference No. 2 As per the decision of the government here, instructions have been given to review the sanctioned posts in the administrative departments and offices under them and fix the revised figure. Also, the Finance Department No. 3 Guidelines have been issued to fix the revised figure through a government circular here. Accordingly, the proposal for the revised structure of the department was submitted to the Deputy Secretary Committee and the High-Level Secretary Committee. Review of posts and revised pattern in Subordinate Offices of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department. The approval has been given by the High Level Committee of Secretaries headed by the Chief Secretary in its 9th meeting dated 26.11.2021. Accordingly, the matter of approving the revised pattern of posts in the offices under the Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
1. m. As per the approval of the High Level Committee of Secretaries chaired by the Chief Secretary in its meeting held on 26.11.2021, a total of 5668 regular posts and a total of 1752 manpower to be recruited through external agencies in 9 zonal offices under the Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection, a total of 7420 posts have been revised in the revised figure of Reference No. 4 is granted subject to the following conditions: The details of the same shall be as per the Statements 1 to 9 annexed to this Government Resolution.

(i) Both the offices of the Director, Supply Commissioner's Office, Mumbai and Directorate of Civil Supplies (Go/ Wa), Mumbai shall be closed after adjustment of officers/ staff of both the offices to the vacancies in the Supply Branch after the Common Service Entry Rules of the Supply Branch are framed.
ii) A total of 1657 sanctioned posts of 1590 posts of watchmen, peons, sweepers and 67 posts of drivers in the field establishment of the department will continue till the retirement of the concerned employees. Hence, these posts should be filled. The posts will become vacant after the retirement/ resignation/ death of the person holding the post. These services will then be made available through external sources as per the requirement.
iii) The entire Group D cadre including the posts of drivers and some posts and constables in Group C cadre have been declared dead and the number of posts sanctioned is as many as the number of employees working in the cadre declared dead. The said posts shall stand vacated on their retirement/ resignation/ death and other consequential reasons. After that, the services will be taken over by external agencies as per the requirement.
