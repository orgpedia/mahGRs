(3) After the said order is issued, the transferred officer/ employee shall ensure that he joins the place of transfer within the period of his joining as provided under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Period of Incorporation, Non-Acceptance of Service and Provisions for Suspension, Dismissal and Removal from Service) Rules, 1981.
If he does not join the post of a replacement during this period, the period of absence shall be deemed to be non-dies. Therefore, the above Manager, District Consumer Redressal Commission should take charge of the new post immediately. They shall not be unauthorised absence otherwise their absence shall be treated as unauthorised absence and shall be made without pay. Also, the concerned are being made aware that the possibility of discontinuation of their service cannot be ruled out.
The period of appointment, travel allowance and other incidental allowances will be admissible to the concerned officers for joining the post of new posting.
The State Consumer Redressal Commission, Mumbai is informed that the date of demitting office and assuming charge by the concerned Manager, District Consumer Redressal Commission should be informed to the Government immediately. If the concerned officer does not report to the place of transfer within the prescribed time, the same should be informed to the government immediately, so that disciplinary action can be taken against the concerned immediately. As per Rule 30 (c) of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, leave applications of transferred officers should not be accepted/ approved.
