in the state. The National Food Security Act, 2013 has come into force with effect from 01.02.2014. As per the Act, 62.3 per cent of the state's population of about 7 crore is eligible for subsidised foodgrains, including 76.3 per cent (about 4.70 crore) rural and 45.3 per cent (about 2.30 crore) urban population. Under the Act, beneficiaries are categorised into two categories- Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) and Priority Household Beneficiaries (PHH). A pilot facility has been created in the Ration Card Management System (RCMS) to make online the application for new ration card, name correction, address change and name increase/ decrease, and the process will be made available to the public after inspection.
Through the Ration Card Management System (RCMS), the facility of online availability and download of QR code based e-Ration Cards will be made available to the beneficiaries of National Food Security Scheme. The ration card will mention Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY), Priority Household Beneficiary (PHH) and APL Farmer under the state scheme, in addition to the National Food Security Scheme (NPH). Accordingly, as per the proposal received from the Controller Rationing and Director Civil Supplies, Mumbai, the proposal to levy service-wise charges for the e-Ration Card facility was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Service-wise service charges for e-Ration Card facility through online service under Public Distribution System will be as follows.
