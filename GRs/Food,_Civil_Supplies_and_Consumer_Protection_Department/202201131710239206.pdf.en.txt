(2) Specifications and procedures for procurement of palm leaves:
2.1 Procurement of ISI marked palm leaves of 400 GSM quality standard of ISI 7903/2017 (Type-V) specification should be done through e-tendering process on Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal.
2.2 The Government e-Marketplace (GeM) Portal shall have ISI marked tarpaulins of the above standards.
Suppliers who have an average turnover of at least Rs.50 lakh in the preceding three financial years i.e. 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-2021 will be eligible to participate in the tender process
On receipt of e-bid from the bidders, the participating eligible bidders shall submit a letter of authorization for appointment of authorized distributor by the manufacturer, if they are authorized supplier distributors, a valid copy of ISO certificate, if they are ISO certified, a valid copy of ISI standard, if they are manufacturer tenderers, along with a certified audit report showing the financial turnover for the last 3 years i.e. 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21.

(4) Security Deposit: 4.1 The supplier is required to pay a security deposit to the Department of Industry, Energy and Labour. In the rule book accompanying the Government Resolution dated 1st December, 2016, at para. As per the provisions of Section 4.6, the reserve amount of 3% of the purchase price has to be deposited in the name of the Financial Adviser and Deputy Secretary as per the provisions of the Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal by Dhanakarsha along with the agreement.
4.2 No interest shall be payable on the deposit. Also, if the supplier violates any of the terms of the contract, the reserve amount will be forfeited and his name will be blacklisted.
Supply Period: The successful supplier is required to supply these palm leaves to the respective District Supply Offices within a period of 60 days at their own cost.
(6) Responsibilities of Suppliers and Buyers: 6.1 If the supplier is an authorised distributor of the manufacturing company, he has to submit an authorisation letter in this regard.
6.2 If the supplier is an authorized distributor of the manufacturing company, it will have to submit a valid copy of the ISO-900 certificate stating that the manufacturing company is an ISO certificate holder.
6.3 It is mandatory to supply ISI marked palm leaves as per the standard prescribed in paragraph (2) above.
6.4 If the tarpaulin is found to be defective, the supplier will be responsible for it. If the damaged tarpaulin is not replaced, the amount will be deducted from the payment due.
6.5 After delivery by the Supplier, the delivery challan shall bear the signature of the Warehouseman/ Warehouseman in possession of the Tarpaulin and the delivery challan shall be signed by the District Supply Officer/ Assistant District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer.
The concerned Warehouseman/ Warehouseman shall ensure that the tarpaulin is of ISI mark quality with the manufacturer's marking, date of manufacture, size and I. SI Mark No. 7903/2017 and submit the certificate as prescribed in Annexure-B.
6.6 The Supplier shall supply ISI Mark No. 400 GSM quality tarpaulin of type 7903/2017 (Type-V) specification. The sample size should be submitted to the office of the Secretary (N.P. 16A), Ministry of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection.
6.7 The warranty period of the palm leaves shall be one year from the date of supply by the supplier.

6.8 After taking action as per 6.5 above, the District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer shall register the receipt of goods on the Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal.
After that, the accounts officer, financial adviser and deputy secretary's office should make the payment, taking into account the deadline on the Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal for payment.
6.9 As per the procedure on the Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal, the online contract along with the terms and conditions are sent to the successful bidder after the tender is finalized on the GeM portal, there is no need to enter into a separate agreement with the successful supplier.
(7) Action to be taken in case of delay in supply: 7.1 The cost of goods as specified in Annexure-IV (A) of the Government Order dated 1st December, 2016 regarding levy of late fee is Rs. There is a provision to levy a late fee of 1⁄ 2 per cent per instalment and a late fee within a limit of 10 per cent of the ceiling, if it does not exceed Rs 2 lakh. Also, the product is priced at Rs. If the amount exceeds Rs 2 lakh, a late fee of 1⁄ 2 per cent per instalment and a late fee within a limit of 5 per cent of the maximum amount will be levied. In case of delay in supply, the late fee shall be recovered from the supplier's payment as per the said provision.
7.2 In case of non-supply by the Supplier due to non-supply within the stipulated time period, the difference in price shall be recovered from the Supplier's payment in case the palm leaves are to be procured locally.
(8) Disputes and Appeals regarding the provision in the online contract: The Supplier may approach the Government within 30 days from the date of giving the decision regarding the terms and conditions of the online contract as well as the penalty, recovery, etc. The decision taken by the government will be final and binding on both the parties. The Supplier may request the provision of the Online Contract within a maximum period of 6 months after the conclusion of the online contract.
