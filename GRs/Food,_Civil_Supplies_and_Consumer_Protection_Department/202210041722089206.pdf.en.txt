Circular:
Under the Public Distribution System of the State, it has been decided to provide a ration kit consisting of 4 ration kits of 1 kg Rava, 1 kg Gram, 1 kg Sugar and 1 litre Palm Oil to the beneficiaries of Antyodaya Anna Yojana, Priority Households and APL (Keshari) Farmer Ration Card holders of all the districts of Aurangabad and Amravati divisions and 14 farmer suicide affected districts of Wardha division of Nagpur division for the upcoming Diwali festival.
Accordingly, the following instructions are being issued regarding distribution of ration kits to eligible ration card holders:
Under the Public Distribution System in the state, 1 ration kit consisting of 4 ration kits of 1 kg semolina, 1 kg gram, 1 kg sugar and 1 litre palm oil should be distributed at the rate of₹ 100/- per kit through the e-PoS system to the beneficiaries of Antyodaya Anna Yojana, priority families as well as APL (Keshari) farmer ration card holders in all the districts of Aurangabad and Amravati division and 14 farmer suicide-affected districts of Wardha division in Nagpur division.
2. All District Supply Officers/ Food Distribution Officers/ Deputy Controllers shall place requisition for ration kits required for their district with the contractors by 06.10.2022. (Maharashtra State Co-operative Consumers Federation Limited, Mumbai, E-mail: consumerfed@rediffmail.com) Contact No. 022 23480520/5940/4967; Mob: No. 9834843166/ 9421302011/ 9930058641/8850997391)

Contractors should deliver the ration kits to the respective taluk godowns within the limit of the number.
The ration kits should be accepted only after obtaining NABL certified laboratory certificate from the contractor that the ration card included in the kit meets the FSSAI standard. It should be ensured that poor quality ration/ ration kits are not accepted under any circumstances.
4. On receipt of ration kits from the contractor in the Taluka godown, the District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer/ Deputy Controller himself or the Tahsildar.
A random sample collection of the ration kit should be done in front of the contractor's representative by appointing an officer not inferior to him. The samples will be sent to NABL laboratory for testing. If the sample is found to be not meeting the prescribed criteria, the supplier should be informed immediately and action should be taken to replace the concerned ration set.
5. Each ration kit provided includes 1 kg of semolina, 1 kg of gram, 1 kg of sugar and 1 litre of palm oil and it is mandatory to distribute 1 kit per ration card holder.
The concerned should ensure that ration kits are not distributed under any circumstances.
The ration kits are to be distributed at the rate of₹ 100/- per set. The fair price shop owners should remit the amount to the District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer/ Deputy Controller as per the prevailing practice.
The reference date for sale of these ration kits is 4th September, 2017
Fair price margin will be paid to the shopkeepers as per the provisions of the government decision.
The District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer/ Deputy Controller shall make available to the Government from time to time the details of ration kits received and distributed for their district and submit the utility certificate to the Government.
The Financial Adviser and the Deputy Secretary shall take action regarding the agreement with the Maharashtra State Co-operative Consumers Federation Limited, Mumbai for the purchase of the said ration kit.
