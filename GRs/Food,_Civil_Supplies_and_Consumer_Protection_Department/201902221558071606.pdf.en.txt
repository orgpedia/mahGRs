Introduction: The Government of India has enacted the National Food Security Act, 2013 with effect from 5th July, 2013. As per the Act, a total of 700.16 lakh beneficiaries have been identified for the State comprising 76.32 per cent rural (469.71 lakh) and 45.34 per cent urban (230.45 lakh) beneficiaries. The Act has been implemented in the State with effect from 01.02.2014.
For selection of eligible beneficiaries under the National Food Security Act, 2013, firstly, the district-wise beneficiaries have been allocated for rural and urban areas as per the Government decision dated 17.12.2013 and thereafter revised from time to time as per the Government decision.
Instructions have been given to include ration cards up to 30.4.2018 as per the Government decision dated 21.5.2018. However, in order to include more and more beneficiaries in the National Food Security Scheme. As per the decision of the Government dated 16.11.2018, the revised district-wise targets have not been met. The matter of inclusion of eligible ration card holders as on 15.2.2019 was under consideration of the Government.
The following decisions are taken by the Government: The decision taken to consider ration cards for selection of new eligible beneficiaries under the Government decision dated 21.5.2018 is being modified. instead of 30.4.2018.
