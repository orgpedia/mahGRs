Procedure for distribution of" Anandachya Ration" to eligible ration card holders under the Public Distribution System in the State on the occasion of upcoming festivals like Gudi Padwa, Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Jayanti, etc.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection
Government Circular No. Adhapu-2023/ P.No. 09/ NAPU-22.
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Madam Cama Marg,
Ministry, Mumbai- 400 032.
Date: 16th of March, 2023.
Read:- 1. Government Decision No. Adhapu-2023/ No. 09/ NAPU-22, D. 22.02.2023.
Government Resolution No.1017/ No.70/ NAPU-31 dated 04.09.2017.
Circular: In reference to Section No. 1 here. As per the decision of the Government on 22.2.2023, it has been decided to distribute" Anandachya Ration" consisting of 1 kg each of Rava, Gram, Sugar and 1 litre of Palm Oil on the occasion of upcoming festivals like Gudi Padwa, Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Jayanti, etc. to the Antyodaya Anna Yojana, Priority Households under the Public Distribution System in the State and to all the farmers of Aurangabad and Amravati divisions and Wardha Division of Nagpur Division, who are above the poverty line (APL) in 14 suicide-affected districts, at a discounted rate of₹ 100/- per unit through e-POS system for a period of 1 month from the Marathi New Year.
Accordingly, instructions are being issued regarding distribution of ration kits to eligible ration card holders as follows: 1. Under the Public Distribution System of the State, 1 ration kit consisting of 4 ration kits of 1 kg semolina, 1 kg gram, 1 kg sugar and 1 litre palm oil should be distributed at the rate of₹ 100/- per set through e-PoS system to the beneficiaries of Antyodaya Anna Yojana, Priority Households as well as 14 farmer suicide-affected APL (Keshari) farmer ration card holders of all the districts of Aurangabad and Amravati division and Wardha division of Nagpur division.
2. District-wise details of number of ration kits to be supplied are attached. Within this limit, the Central Warehouse, Mumbai should deliver the ration kits to the respective Taluka Warehouses.
3. It is necessary to keep a record of the ration coming to the warehouse and the ration being sent to the fair price shop in online mode. Accordingly, care should be taken to take proper records on the AePDS portal.
4. Ration kits should be accepted only after NABL authorized laboratory certifies that the Ration kit meets FSSAI standards. It should be ensured that poor quality ration/ ration kits are not accepted under any circumstances.

On receipt of ration kits from the contractor in the Taluka godown, the District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer/ Deputy Controller shall randomly collect samples of ration kits in front of the representative of the contractor by appointing an officer not below the rank of Tehsildar or himself. The samples will be sent to NABL laboratory for testing. If the sample is found to be not meeting the prescribed standards, the supplier should be informed of the time and take action to replace the stock of the ration kit concerned.
The ration kits are to be distributed at the rate of₹ 100/- per set. The fair price shop owners should deduct the margin and deposit the remaining amount with the District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer/ Deputy Controller immediately after the distribution of ration kits. For the sale of these ration kits, under reference No. Margin will be payable to fair price shopkeepers as per the provisions of the Government Order dated 4th September, 2017.
The District Supply Officer/ Food Distribution Officer/ Deputy Controller shall make available to the Government from time to time the details of ration kits received and distributed for their district and submit the utility certificate to the Government.
8. The Financial Adviser and the Deputy Secretary shall take action for entering into an agreement with Kendriya Bhandar, Mumbai for the purchase of the said ration kits.
The Kendriya Bhandar, Mumbai shall submit to the Government daily information on the following points on the e-mail napu22.mhpds@mah. gov. in as well as to the Financial Adviser and the Deputy Secretary: a) Ration kits delivered to the places specified by the Government (including the names of the places).
(b) Ration kits remaining with the tenderer/ Kendriya Bhandar, Mumbai.
10. All concerned shall take action in accordance with the above procedure. All concerned should note that if there is any lapse in this process, the concerned will be held responsible.
