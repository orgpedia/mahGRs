The Proposal:
The National Food Security Act, 2013 has been implemented in the State of Maharashtra with effect from 1st February, 2014. A total number of 700.16 lakh beneficiaries have been identified for the State of Maharashtra under this Act, which is 76.32 per cent rural (469.71 lakh) and 45.34 per cent urban (230.45 lakh).
In order to identify the eligible beneficiaries under the National Food Security Act, 2013, firstly, the district wise beneficiary index for rural and urban areas was provided as per the Government decision dated 17.12.2013. Thereafter, as per the decision of the Government dated 24.03.2015, separate allocation was made for Antyodaya Anna Yojana for rural and urban areas and for beneficiaries belonging to priority households. As the ration cards have been computerized while implementing the campaign of computerization of ration cards in the state, the beneficiaries will be provided with the same.
