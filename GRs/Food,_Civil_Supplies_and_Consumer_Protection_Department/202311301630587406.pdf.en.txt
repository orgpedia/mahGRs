The Proposal:
The Minimum Support Price (MSP) scheme is a central government scheme that helps farmers sell their produce at a lower price than the guaranteed price.
For this, the Maharashtra State Co-op will be formed through the agent agencies appointed by the state government (in non-tribal areas). Marketing Federation, Mumbai and in tribal areas, Maharashtra State Cooperative Tribal Development Corporation, Nashik) procures paddy and millets of FAQ (Fair Average Quality) in the state. Under the above scheme, the paddy procured from the millers through the agent agencies and the CMR (Custom Milled Rice) received from the millers was being deposited with the Food Corporation of India. Under this scheme, paddy is transported from the Paddy Procurement Center to the Paddy Mill and from the Paddy Mill to the CMR Warehouse (Mill to Base Depot). For this, the transportation rates were fixed according to the Provisional Cost Sheet determined for the respective marketing season. Government's decision:
As per the guidelines received from the Central Government vide letter No. 192 (14)/ 2018-FCA/ CS, dated 6th May, 2019 for determination of rates for transportation of Paddy/ CMR, a State Level Committee is being constituted under this Government to fix the following rates for transportation of Paddy procured under the Minimum Support Price Procurement Scheme (MSPP) and CMR derived therefrom:
Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department Chairman 2) Executive Director, Food Corporation of India Member 3) General Manager, Food Corporation of India Member 4) Collector, Gondia Member 5) Collector, Gadchiroli Member 6) Joint/ Deputy Secretary, Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department 1 Member (Minimum Support Price Procurement Scheme) 7) Executive Director, Maharashtra State Marketing Federation, Mumbai Invited Member 8) Labour Commissioner, Maharashtra State Invited Member 9) Home (Joint Transport Commissioner, Transport Department Member 10) Financial Advisor and Deputy Secretary, Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department.
11. Under Secretary, Department of Food Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Member Secretary (Minimum Support Price Procurement Scheme) 2. The functions of the said Committee shall be as follows:
(i) To finalize the list of transport rates based on the market survey for the State. These rates will be applicable for a maximum period of 2 years.
For all the districts of the state, there will be stages of uniform distances as follows:- 0 to 8 km, 8 km to 20 km, 20 km to 40 km, 40 km to 80 km and above 80 km.
It will also include transportation of paddy procured from the Tribal Development Corporation to the designated godowns.
For this, while fixing the transportation rates at the district level, they should be determined in a competitive manner through open e-tendering.
(iv) The State Level Committee shall determine the draft tenders to be called for the said transport rates by all the districts.
V. The State Level Committee on Transport Rates fixed at the district level shall take a decision on whether such rates should be adopted or not. If there is a large difference between the rates fixed in the districts and the rates fixed by the State Level Committee (which will be for 2 years), the rates which the State Level Committee may approve/ disapprove should be mentioned along with the minutes of the meeting of the State Level Committee, along with the reasons for their acceptance/ rejection.
