GOVERNMENT DECISION: SOCIAL JUSTICE AND SPECIAL ASSISTANCE, NO. VISIO-2021/ P. NO. 42/ VISIO, dated 22nd July, 2021, Reference No. 3 The government decision here is being overridden by this government decision.
In the financial year 2021-22, Rs. A budgetary provision of Rs. 531,01,80, 000/- (Rupees Five Hundred Thirty One Crores One Lakh Eighty Thousand Only) has been approved. Out of the budgetary allocation of Rs. 330,00,00, 000/- has been disbursed by the Finance Department. Accordingly, the amount sanctioned for the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for general beneficiaries for the period April, 2021 to March, 2022 is Rs. 330,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Hundred and Thirty Crores only). 2 It has been distributed to all the district collectors as per the decision of the government. Out of this, Rs. 115,40,38, 949/- taken back to the Government and disbursed by the Finance Department. A total amount of Rs. 316,42,18, 949/- (Rupees Three Hundred and Sixteen Crores Forty Two Lakhs Eighteen Thousand Nine Hundred and Nineteen Fifty Five only) is being disbursed to all the Collectorates as per the accompanying statement under this Government decision.
Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries. The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.

The expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No.N-3, Main Account Head 2235 Social Security and Welfare, 02-Social Welfare, 104-Welfare of the Aged, Disabled and Destitute Persons (08) (09) Sanjay Gandhi Niradhi Grant Scheme (Programme) (2235 O 294) 50-Other Expenditure."
4. All the District Collectors are hereby informed to ensure that these payments are deposited in the treasury. Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.
5. All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 840/2021/ Expenditure 14 dated 23/09/2021.
