Decision of the Government: Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Paramparagat Krishi Vikas under the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA), the demand number for the year 2022-23 for the Scheduled Caste category has been increased to Rs. N-3, 2401 Crop Protection, (00) (789)- Scheduled Castes Sub-Plan, (01) Scheme under Scheduled Castes Component Programme (01) (13) Krishi Unnati Yojana-Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana Central Share 60% (2401B203) Under this head Rs. 131.00 lakhs, and (01) (14) Krishi Unnati Yojana-Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana State Share 40% (2401B212) under the head Rs. 81.00 lakh has been sanctioned.
Finance Department No. The Circular dated 01.02.2020 has laid down the modalities for release of funds for the financial year 2022-23. In this regard, the Department of Agriculture, No. In accordance with the consensus reached by the Finance Department on the proposal of the Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, the Central share of 60% (2401B203) of the budgetary provision for 2022-23 under (01) (13) Krishi Unnati Yojana-Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana has been increased to Rs. 33.725 lakh and (01) (14) Krishi Unnati Yojana-Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana State Share 40% (2401B212) under the head Rs. 22.484 lakh. The Government is approving the disbursement of funds to the tune of Rs. 56.209 lakh to the Secretary, Agriculture and Padum Department as Head of Administrative Department on Budgetary Fund Distribution System.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. While spending the fund, the controlling officer should take precautionary measures in the prescribed manner. It should be ensured that the funds will be spent this year as per the approved plan in accordance with the current government policy. Also, the report of the expenditure made from the provision made, account-wise/ sub-account-wise and material objective-wise information achieved, the utilization certificate of the fund, the concerned field officer should ensure that it is sent to the Commissioner, Social Welfare, the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare, Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and Agriculture and Padum Department by the 10th of every month.
The Head of the Department/ Controlling Officer should ensure that the funds meant for SCs are spent on the same components so that the Government can have control over the expenditure on these schemes.
