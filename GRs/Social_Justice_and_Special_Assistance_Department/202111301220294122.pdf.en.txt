Reference: 1) Government Resolution, Department of Health, Family Welfare No. 2018/ PR No. 118/ Education, dated 11/10/2018 2) Government Charter, Department of Health, Family Welfare, No. SHIVARITI-2018/ PR.No.118/ Education, dated 12/11/2018 Preamble: To provide an opportunity to the students belonging to Visva-Bhaj, Emav and VIMAPRA categories to pursue higher education in reputed educational institutions abroad, the Foreign Scholarship Scheme has been introduced from the year 2018-19. The date mentioned herein has been brought into force by the Government Decision dated 11th October, 2018.
Under the scheme, a total of 10 students from QS and THE world ranking institutions within an average of 200 are selected and granted foreign scholarships for postgraduate degree and PhD courses for students belonging to the Vijabhaj, Emav and Vimapra categories in Maharashtra. Accordingly, the government was considering the matter of selecting eligible students and sanctioning foreign scholarships to them after examining the applications received by the department under the Foreign Scholarship Scheme for the year 2021-22.
Government Decision: Under the Foreign Scholarship Scheme, Reference No. A total of 10 students selected by the State Level Selection Committee constituted under the Chairmanship of Secretary (Other Backward Bahujan Welfare) vide Government Resolution dated 11th October, 2018 are being sanctioned scholarships for higher studies abroad. The details of the selected students are mentioned in the accompanying Form-A.
2. The Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare Department concerned is being authorized to grant admissible scholarship to the student selected for foreign scholarship.
The Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare Department concerned should take action to disburse the permissible scholarship to the student by taking the guarantee letters issued under the decision of the government in question and examining the original documents of the students.
The students who have been approved foreign scholarship by the government, after completing their education, are required to serve the country and benefit from their knowledge.
In this regard, it will be mandatory to write a letter of guarantee (BOND) from the concerned student before approving the scholarship.

The student selected for the foreign scholarship must complete the Master's degree or PhD course abroad within the prescribed period. Under no circumstances will the student be given an extension or increased cost.
The amount of the scholarship will be recovered in full from the student selected for the foreign scholarship if the student changes the place or course of the educational institution, university or course approved for higher education.
The Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare Department concerned shall, after obtaining authenticated information from the concerned university, pay the tuition fee to the concerned university and the amount of the maintenance allowance and other incidental expenses to the bank account of the students who have been sanctioned the scholarship out of the total cost of the course for which the scholarship is sanctioned.
The expenditure in this regard shall be met from the provision approved in the current financial year by making expenditure under the head" Demand No.ZG-03, Principal Head 2225, Welfare of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Minorities, 03, Welfare of Backward Classes, 277 Education, 02 Scholarship Vidyavadhan, (02) (18) Scholarship for meritorious children belonging to Vimukta Jati, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes for higher education in Paradesa" (2225F218). For this, the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare Department is being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer.
The Regional Deputy Commissioner concerned, Social Welfare Department, while sanctioning the foreign scholarship, issued the relevant Government Resolution No. Sewriti-2018/ PR No. 118/ Education, dated 11th October, 2018 and Government Corrigendum No. 118/ Education, dated 12th November, 2018 to be strictly adhered to.
