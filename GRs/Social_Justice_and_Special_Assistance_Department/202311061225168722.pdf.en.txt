At present, there are 443 government hostels for the education of backward class students under the Social Justice Department and a total of 93 government residential schools for the education of scheduled caste and neo-Buddhist boys and girls. The e-tendering process has been fixed as per the Government Decision, dated 24 th June, 2022 for supply of good quality food and tetra-packaged unadulterated milk to the admitted students of these Government Hostels and Government Residential Schools. As per the provisions prescribed under this Government decision, an e-tendering process has been implemented at the level of Commissioner, Social Welfare Commissionerate, Pune for the supply of good quality food and tetra-packed unadulterated milk to the admitted students of Government Hostels and Government Residential Schools. Accordingly, the proposal recommended by the purchase committee at the level of Commissioner, Social Welfare Commissionerate, Pune, to supply good quality food to the students admitted in government hostels and government residential schools under the department was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
In order to provide good quality food to the students admitted in government hostels and government residential schools under the Social Justice Department, the Commissioner, Social Welfare Commissionerate, Pune, has conducted an e-tendering process for the eligible low rate e-tender holders.

Orders at the level of Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare will be considered valid.
VIII. Referred No. The diet chart prescribed under the Government Decision No. 2 should be applied in all government hostels and government residential schools.
IX.To check the quality of food served to the admitted students in Government Hostels and Government Residential Schools, a dietician should be appointed from outside and the quality of food should be checked by them regularly. Also, each hostel-wise meal inspection management committee should be constituted.
X. If the food supplier violates the terms and conditions of the tender, he will be liable to penalty, for which the Commissioner, Social Welfare, Pune, should fix the criteria of penalty and get it approved by the government.
XI.If the food supplier stops his service in the meantime, action will be taken against the supplier as per the rules and the said supplier will be liable for penalty.
If the supplier organizations are found not complying with the terms and conditions of the tender, the concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare shall submit a report to the Government through the Commissioner, Social Welfare, Pune. The government will have the right to cancel the supply of food if the supplier is found guilty.
A food registry (taste register) should be maintained in every government hostel and residential school and the feedback of the students should be recorded in it on a daily basis.
The supply period of the tender shall be for a period of 3 years. However, after receiving the report from the Commissioner, Social Welfare Commissionerate, Pune, regarding the performance of the food suppliers every year, a decision will be taken at the government level to continue the supply of food next year.
