Introduction The State level scheme" Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Samajik Vikas Yojana" is being implemented from the year 2018-19 as per the Government decision dated 09.03.2018, after approving the works suggested by the people's representatives (only sitting MPs and MLAs) at the government level and determining the works to be taken under this scheme, the implementation mechanism and other criteria.
This scheme is a state level scheme to provide direct funding at the government level on the lines of the Rural Development, Minority and Urban Development Department as demanded by the people's representatives.
The work to be done in this scheme has been mentioned in the government decision. However, the existing MPs and MLAs are required to develop sites and places of cultural and historical importance associated with eminent persons working for the rights of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhists, erect their memorials and bring about cultural development of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhists (e.g. Many proposals are being received in the form of library, study center, Dhamma center, Vipassana center, etc. Since the objectives of the Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Development Scheme need to be enhanced, the Government was considering amending the relevant Government Decision to complete the projects/ schemes of the above mentioned works under the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Development Scheme.
Government's stand:
The Government have decided to enhance the works to be taken up under the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Development Scheme as mentioned in the Government Decision dated 9th March, 2018, including the development of sites related to eminent persons working for the Scheduled Castes and the neo-Buddhist communities, as well as places of cultural and historical importance, construction of their memorials and their scheduled places.

Under the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Development Scheme, proposals costing Rs.1 crore or more but less than Rs.15 crore should be submitted to the Government through the Collector.
The local self-government institutions shall, at the time of submitting the project to the Government, give an undertaking to take full responsibility for the maintenance and repair of the project after its completion.
Similarly, it will be mandatory for the local bodies to give an assurance that the maximum use of the buildings constructed from this fund of the Scheduled Castes Appropriation will be to the Scheduled Castes and the neo-Buddhist elements. The local bodies adjacent to the project will have to take responsibility for the maintenance and repair of the project built on government land. The project will not be sanctioned unless the local body takes full guarantee of maintenance and repair of such project on government land.
5. The Government shall have all the powers to select the works suggested by the people's representatives. The district collectors of the districts where the proposals are to be sent to the government, along with the construction plan and the budget with technical approval, should submit them to the government through the Commissioner, Social Welfare. The Commissioner, Social Welfare shall examine the project in its entirety and ensure that the project is suitable to the objectives of the scheme and submit it to the Government. The proposal will be approved by the government.
