Disbursement of the provision as indicated is being approved subject to the following terms and conditions:
(1) The expenditure to be incurred by the Central Government under the Special Central Assistance Scheme shall be as per the guidelines issued by the Central Government together at the end of May, 2019.
(2) At least 10% of the total budget of the scheme shall be spent on skill development training.
(3) Specific schemes implemented by the Corporation shall be approved in detail by the Board of Directors of the Corporation, if required.
(4) Special Central Assistance Utilization Certificate (filled in the prescribed statement of Central Assistance) and progress report of each quarter should be submitted to the Government before the 10th of the following month after the end of the quarterly period.
(5) Seed capital finance provided under SC deployment should be clubbed with special central assistance with the financial assistance available from lead banks/ nationalised/ cooperative banks.
(6) The cost of subsidy given to the beneficiaries under the 20-point programme should be reported to the government at the end of each quarter. Similarly, the utilization certificate should be submitted in the prescribed form to be submitted to the Central Government at the end of the quarter.
(7) While submitting the utilization certificate, it should be ensured that the figures of opening balance, sanctioned funds, expenditure from the total available funds and the balance at the end of the quarter are consistent.
The Joint Secretary (Budget), Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Mumbai shall be the Controlling Officer in respect of the said grants. The powers to draw and disburse funds to the above Corporations are being delegated to the Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare Department, Mumbai. They should look into reconciling the expenditure of the subsidy amount with the accounts of the Accountant General.
