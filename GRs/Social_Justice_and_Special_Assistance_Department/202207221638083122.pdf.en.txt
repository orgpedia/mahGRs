Introduction In the post-independence era, the majority backward class community in the rural areas of Maharashtra is moving towards self-sufficiency through various welfare schemes of the government. Under the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Samajik Vikas Yojana, the government is implementing various schemes for self-sufficiency as well as self-sufficiency for Scheduled Castes and New Buddhists in the state.
Government's decision
Under the government's Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Development Scheme, students of all residential schools, hostels, aided hostels and all central ashramshalas need an abundant supply of magazines/ books required for general knowledge, competitive examinations, books on the characters of great personalities, Hindi, Marathi, English newspapers and other examinations. However, 90 Government Residential Schools, 441 Government Hostels and various Kendriya Ashramshalas for SC students are functioning under the aegis of Social Justice Department. The total number of students admitted in the said school/ hostel is approximately 1,50,000 and a State Level Committee is being appointed as per the structure mentioned below to take further action for supply of magazines/ books to these students under the above mentioned scheme.
