Government Decision In the financial year 2022-23, for the implementation of Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for the beneficiaries of Scheduled Tribes, Rs. A budgetary provision of Rs. 120,00,00, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred and Twenty Crores only) has been approved. For the Rs. 90,00,00, 000/- as reference No. 1, 2 and 3 have been distributed to all the district collectors as per the government decision. Now, the Department of Tribal Development has approved the release of an amount of Rs. 30,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Thirty Crores only) for the period October, 2022 to March, 2023 to the beneficiaries of Scheduled Tribes in the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme. Accordingly, the Government hereby approve the release of Rs. 30,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Thirty Crores only) to the beneficiaries of Scheduled Tribes in the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for the period from October, 2022 to March, 2023 as per the accompanying statement under this Government decision. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries.
The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.

The expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No. T-5, Main Account Head 2235-Social Security and Welfare, 02-Social Welfare, 796-Tribal Area Sub Plan, (02) Out of Tribal Area Sub Plan (OTSP), (02) (03) Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme (Programme), 50-Other Expenditure, (2235 C 161)."
4. All the District Collectors are informed to ensure that these payments are deposited in the treasury. Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.
All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this department and this office by matching the expenditure made from the grant handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office.
If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 1120/22/ Expenditure-14 dated 29/12/2022 and Tribal Development Department vide Informal Reference No.14/ Work-6 dated 18/01/2023.
