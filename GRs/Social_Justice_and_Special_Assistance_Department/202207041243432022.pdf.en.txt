Preamble: To provide basic facilities to the Scheduled Castes and the neo-Buddhist communities for the development of their habitations/ villages in urban and rural areas of the State. The Government has decided to implement the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Development Scheme from the year 2018-19 to give recognition to the works suggested by the people's representatives at the government level.
Thereafter, as per the Government Resolution, dated 08.10.2021, the term' public representative' has been extended to include MPs, MLAs, all members and office bearers of local bodies as public representatives. Further, the works to be taken up under the scheme will include development of places of cultural and historical importance, development of monuments and cultural development of Scheduled Castes and New Buddhists (e.g.
Library, Study Centre, Dhamma Kendra, Vipassana Kendra, Training Centre) have been included in the Government Decision dated July 28, 2021. According to The matter of sanctioning works in the habitations of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhist communities in urban and rural areas suggested by the people's representatives was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
In order to provide basic facilities in the habitations of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhist communities in urban and rural areas of the State, the budget provision for the year 2021-22 under the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Samajik Vikas Yojana is providing administrative sanction for the estimated amount of works mentioned in Annexure A along with this Government decision.

These works should be carried out through the working mechanism suggested by the people's representatives, for example, Public Works Department, District Council Construction Department, Municipal Corporation, Municipality, Nagar Panchayat, Gram Panchayat or other departments of the government.

The executing agency shall prepare detailed maps/ plans and budgets mentioning the date and length of the starting point and the end point of the work in respect of the works at the estimated cost approved in principle and submit them to the Collector of the concerned district for administrative approval.
Administrative approval for these works should be given by the District Collector concerned.
The working system should implement the tender process of the works as per the prevailing rules of the government.
It should be ensured that these works are completed within the approved budgetary limit. If administrative approval is given for works costing more than the approved budgetary cost, no additional funds will be provided in any form.
(5) The Government shall have the power to cancel or modify the works if the people's representatives who have suggested such works make a request in writing, confirming that they are to be changed or that they cannot be done due to some unavoidable reasons.
Before starting the works, the Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district, Social Welfare, must ensure that the works are in the habitations of the Scheduled Castes and the neo-Buddhist communities. The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district will be held responsible for disbursing funds for works which are outside the habitation of Scheduled Castes and neo-Buddhist communities and disciplinary action will be taken against them.
It should be ensured that the terms and conditions of the Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Development Scheme, dated 09 March 2018, 28 July 2021 and 08 October 2021, are strictly adhered to.
(8) No code of conduct for general elections/ bye-elections is in force in the area of works sanctioned before the commencement of the said works. The district collector should ensure this.
