Government Decision: In the financial year 2022-23, for the implementation of Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for Scheduled Caste beneficiaries, Rs. 150,00,00, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred and Fifty Crores only) and sanctioned by way of Supplementary Demands in the Monsoon Session 2022 amounting to Rs. 90,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Ninety Crores only) aggregating to Rs. 240,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Two Hundred and Forty Crores only) has been sanctioned. For the Rs. 150,00,00, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred and Fifty Crores only) has been distributed to all the District Collectors as per the Government decision in Reference No. 2.
Now, the Finance Department has sanctioned Rs. 45,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Forty Five Crores only) has been approved. Accordingly, for the period April, 2022 to March, 2023, the amount sanctioned for the Sanjay Gandhi Destitute Subsidy Pension Scheme for Scheduled Caste beneficiaries is Rs. 45,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Forty Five Crores only) is hereby approved by the Government to be distributed as per the accompanying prospectus. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries. The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.

All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
