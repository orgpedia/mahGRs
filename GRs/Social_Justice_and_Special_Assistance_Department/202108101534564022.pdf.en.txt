Government Decision: For the financial year 2021-22, a budgetary provision of Rs. 102,77,62, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred and Two Crores Seventy Seven Lakhs Sixty Two Thousand only) has been approved for the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhi Subsidy Scheme for District Establishments and Rs. 39,01,36, 000/- (Rupees Nineteen Crores One Lakh Thirty Six Thousand only) for the establishments on the scheme of financial assistance to the old landless agricultural labourers. Of these, reference No. 10% of the budgetary provision approved under the Government decision here has been distributed to all the Divisional Commissioner's Offices for the purpose of 01-Salary. Now, the Department of Finance has allocated 5% of the approved budgetary provision for the purpose of 01- Salary and 10% for 06-Telephone, Electricity and Water and 13 Office Expenditure. Accordingly, the Government hereby approve the release of funds to the tune of Rs. 5,21,63, 050/- (Rupees Five Crores Twenty One Lakh Sixty Three Thousand Fifty only) for District Establishments and Rs. 1,95,70, 850/- (Rupees One Crore Ninety Five Lakh Seventy Eight Hundred and Fifty only) for the establishments on the scheme for the period of May and June, 2021 to all the Divisional Commissioner's Offices for the purpose of 01-Salary, 06-Telephone, Electricity and Water and 13-Office Expenditure as per the statement accompanying this Government decision.
All the Divisional Commissioners are hereby informed that they should allocate the grants distributed as per the attached returns A and B to the districts under their jurisdiction as per their requirement and the District Collectors should allocate the grants to the taluks of their district as per their requirement.

4. All District Collectors are hereby informed that they shall ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the grant handed over to them under any circumstances. They should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the audit office of this department and to this office by matching the expenditure made from the grant handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the office of the Accountant General.
Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government decision is based on the Government Circular of the Department of Finance, No. 48/ 2021/ 2021/ 2021/ 2021. It is being issued as per the administrative authority given to the departments as on April 06, 2021.
