The Proposal: -
Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar, through the Indian Constitution, has tried to unite the country into Indian society through the principles of freedom, equality and fraternity.
Article 46 of the Constitution of India lays down the responsibility of the state towards the weaker sections of the society and the downtrodden with utmost foresight and seriousness. The Constitution of India HOT 46: Promotion of educational and economic interests of castes, scheduled tribes and other weaker sections: "The State shall promote with special care the educational and economic interests of the weaker sections of the people, and in particular of the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes, and shall protect them from social injustice and all forms of exploitation." "It said. In line with these principles enshrined in the Constitution of India, the State Government is constantly striving to provide social justice, greater social protection to the Scheduled Castes, the Scheduled Tribes and other weaker sections as well as to all kinds of victims and oppressed. Accordingly, various welfare schemes are being implemented by the state government to achieve the goal of holistic development of persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and weaker sections of the underprivileged. Every year, it is decided to make all these welfare schemes known to the general public and create awareness among the public. The government was considering celebrating "Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Equality Week" in the state from 8 April to 14 April.
Government Decisions: - To achieve the goal of holistic development of persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and weaker sections of the underprivileged, the State Government undertakes various welfare schemes every year to make the general public aware and create awareness among the public. Under this Government decision, all the offices under all the Administrative Departments of the State, B. Divisional Commissioners and Collectors, all the offices under them, are directed to organize programmes to celebrate the "Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Equality Week" in the State from 8th April to 14th April as mentioned in the following table: -

3. The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall be solely responsible for the successful conduct of the "Bharat Ratna Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Social Equality Week."
