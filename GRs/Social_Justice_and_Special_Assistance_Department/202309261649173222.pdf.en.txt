Articles 39A and 41 of the Directive Principles of State Policy of the Constitution of India provide for senior citizens to be in good health. Considering the position of senior citizens in the society, they should spend their old age in a better way, their life should be comfortable in the society, their physical/ mental health should be in good condition, their economic ability in old age, right to work, right to education and public assistance should be provided. Seniority 2016/ Q. No.71/ SAMASU, dated 09.07.2008 has been issued. The state government has decided to celebrate October 1 as the International Day of Older Persons.
Accordingly, 1st October, 2023 should be celebrated as World Senior Citizen's Day at all places in the State and on this occasion, advertisements in newspapers, public walks, morning rounds, meetings, felicitation of elders who have done special work and NGOs working for senior citizens, felicitation, health camps, seminars/ symposiums etc. should be organized. Also, information on laws and schemes for senior citizens, rights of the elderly, facilities provided to senior citizens, helpline for assistance to senior citizens, etc. should be provided through this program.
