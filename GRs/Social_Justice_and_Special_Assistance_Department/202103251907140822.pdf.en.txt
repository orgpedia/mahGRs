Government's decision:
A budgetary provision of Rs. 5,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Five Crores only) has been approved for implementation of Centrally Sponsored Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme for the financial year 2020-21. Out of the budgetary allocation of Rs. 3,75,00, 000/- in reference No. It has been distributed to all the district collector offices as per the government decision of 4. The Government hereby approves disbursement of Rs. 1,25,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Twenty Five Lakh only) towards expenditure for the period October, 2020 to March, 2021 for the implementation of Indira Gandhi National Pension for Persons with Disabilities Scheme as per the accompanying Government Resolution. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code.
The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries. The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.
Expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No. N-3, Main Account Head 2235 Social Security and Welfare, 104 Welfare of Elderly, Disabled and Destitute Persons, (08) (12) Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme (Centrally Sponsored Scheme) (Programme), 50 Other Expenditure, (2235B049)."

All the District Collectors are informed that they have issued Reference No. 3 Care should be taken to submit the payments to the treasury by fulfilling the terms and conditions of the circular of the Finance Department here. All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 94/2021/ Expenditure 14 dated 23/03/2021.
