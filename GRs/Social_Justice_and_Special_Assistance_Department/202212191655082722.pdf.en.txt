Introduction: The Central Government has enacted the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 and the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995 to prevent atrocities against the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes. According to Rule No. As per section 9 (1), Dr. Surendra Bagade, General Manager, Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport Undertaking, Mumbai was appointed as the Nodal Officer at the State level to coordinate the work of the officers implementing the said Acts. However, due to the transfer of Shri Dr. Surendra Bagade, General Manager, Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport Undertaking, Mumbai, to the post of Additional Secretary, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, Government of India, under the order of the General Administration Department, the matter of appointing another Nodal Officer in his place was under consideration of the Government.

Government Resolution No: UTA-2020/ P. No.183/ SAMASU
GOVERNMENT DECISION: As per Rule 9 (1) of the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995, in reference No. Shri Dr. Surendra Bagade, General Manager, Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport Undertaking, Mumbai was appointed as the Coordinating Officer under the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 and Rules 1995 thereunder. Harshdeep Kamble, Principal Secretary (Industries), Ministry of Industries, Mumbai, is being appointed as the Nodal Officer under the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 and Rules 1995 thereunder.
The Nodal Officer will review the following after every three months.
Reports received by the State Government under sub-rules (2) and (4) of rule 4, rule 6 and rule 8 (XI) of this rule.
(2) The present status of the cases filed under this Act.
3) Law and order situation in the violence-prone areas.
4. Measures to be taken to expedite the disbursement of financial assistance to victims of atrocities.
Provision of ration, clothing, shelter, legal advice, travel expenses, daily allowance and vehicles to the victims or their dependents.
Review of the functioning of the Non-Governmental Organisations, Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Protection Cell, various Committees and all officers implementing other provisions under the Act.
Review of the implementation of the right of the victim and the witness mentioned in Chapter IV-A of the amended Acts.
The Nodal Officer shall submit his report to the Secretary/ Principal Secretary/ Additional Chief Secretary Social Justice and Special Assistance Department every three months.
