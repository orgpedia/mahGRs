GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: BGT 2021/ PR. No.96/ Budget (BGYO) 2. During the financial year 2021-2022, the aforesaid funds/ grants made available by the Finance Department on the BEAMS system are being released subject to the following terms and conditions: 1. No expenditure shall be incurred for any purpose other than the purpose for which the funds/ grants are sanctioned. Also, you can't switch to other titles.
Progress report of the expenditure should be submitted to the Government by the Controlling Officer/ Drawing and Disbursing Officer by 10th of every month as per the monthly fund statement.
3) The rules mentioned in the Maharashtra Budget rule book should be followed while disbursing the subsidy.
Further, the Finance Department shall pay the subsidy after obtaining the approval of the competent authority in accordance with the terms and conditions of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-Division-I as provided under Government Decision No. V.P.-2013/ PR. 30/2013/ Regulations, Part-II, dated 17th April, 2015.
4) When providing grants to private NGOs, the grant should be paid with the approval of the competent authority in accordance with the Financial Powers Rule Book 1978, Part-1, Sub-Division I, serial number 27, rule 149.
(5) In the case of the distribution of provisions for which an administrative sanction order is required, the grant shall be distributed after the order is issued with the approval of the competent authority as provided under Part-I, Sub-section-III, Schedule-IV, Rule 27 (2).
6) The post-salary grants to the Zilla Parishads should not be disbursed without their grants being determined.
7) The approval of the Finance Department is required in respect of the disbursement of funds for the schemes provided under the new head in accordance with the new expenditure.
8) Before sanctioning the grant-in-aid to various Government/ Administrative Institutions and Subsidiary Institutions, the Department should obtain the utilization certificates of the expenditure of the grant-in-aid disbursed earlier. Further grants should not be disbursed unless the utilization of the earlier grant is completed. The treasury officers should not pass the payment unless a copy of the bank account statement for the previous month is attached with the payment submitted for drawing funds from the subsidiary grant.
9) The salary subsidy given by the government will have to be transferred to the bank account of the beneficiaries through ECS. Under no circumstances should the salary subsidy amount be disbursed in lump sum and offline mode.
10) Before disbursing the grants to the local self-government institutions and corporations, the amount coming from these institutions to the state government should be reviewed and the remaining grants should be disbursed only after recovering those amounts. Also, detailed information should be collected about the utilization of the grants distributed by the government to these institutions and where the unutilized amount is kept. If the unaccounted money is kept in the bank, then its bank account statement should be submitted to the government. If the funds made available for expenditure on a programme are unutilized, the funds should not be released for the same purpose in the current year without the approval of the Finance Department.
11. While drawing the payments of beneficiaries under the individual beneficiary programme, the grants of individual beneficiaries shall not be drawn unless the list of beneficiaries is fixed and linked with the payment with proper details. The programmes of individual beneficiaries should be linked with Aadhaar number and the subsidy should be disbursed through Electronic Clearance System (ECS).

However, if the funds of the Central Government for the previous year are to be spent in the current year, it will be mandatory to obtain the consent of the Planning, Tribal Development Department and Social Justice and Special Assistance Department as well as the Finance Department, as the case may be.

16) Funds are often spent by departments through short payments. However, non-submission of its detailed payment may lead to accounting objections and possible default. However, henceforth, the departments should not draw funds under the head of accounts without the consent of the Finance Department unless they submit the head under which the old detailed payments are pending.
17) The departments should note that it is a kind of irregularity that the funds that have remained unutilized for various reasons are technically shown as spent by the departments by transferring them to their subordinate corporation.
Provisions shall not be drawn or disbursed unless the previous unutilized funds under them (kept outside the purview of their own provident fund account/ corporation account or otherwise showing expenditure) have been fully spent. It is also pointed out that under the self-registration accounts, the departments are found to be having huge funds lying unutilized. If the funds are not spent within the stipulated time, they should be returned to the government. If the unaccounted money is kept in the bank, its bank account statement should be submitted to the government.
