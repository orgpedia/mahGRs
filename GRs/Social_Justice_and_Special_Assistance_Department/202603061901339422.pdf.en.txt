Preamble: - Under the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, the backward class boys and girls should be promoted educationally, socially and economically and should lead a life at par with other sections of the society. Also, Government Hostels under the Department is implemented for the education of backward class boys and girls with the objective of providing all kinds of education and higher education opportunities. At present, 449 government hostels are functioning for the education of backward class students under the department and 92 government residential schools for the education of scheduled castes and newly educated boys and girls. These government hostels have a total intake capacity of 46,672 students and government residential schools have a total intake of 14,600 students. Out of these 449 government hostels, 301 hostels are in MAHA building and 148 hostels are functioning in rented building. While 92 government residential schools are functioning in these government buildings. Government hostels and Government Residential Schools are doing a commendable job to ensure the economic, social and educational progress of the students enrolled in Government Hostels and Government Residential Schools under the Department. The students admitted in Government Hostels and Government Residential Schools under the Department are provided with facilities such as free accommodation, food, health facilities, sports facilities, educational materials, boarding and lodging, living allowance, etc. as per the provision prescribed under the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, Government Decision, dated 26th July, 2011.
In hostels, it is more advantageous to use commercial washer extractors instead of domestic ATT machines as they are designed for heavy-duty use. They have a larger capacity than household machines and can wash and dry more clothes in less time, saving students time. These devices have high durability and are less prone to frequent breakdowns due to their industrial-grade spare parts. Also, commercial UTSs use water and electricity more efficiently, which reduces management costs in the long run and makes the system more reliable in places where the machine is in continuous use, such as hostels.
