Government circular:
Articles 39A and 41 of the Directive Principles of State Policy of the Constitution of India provide that senior citizens should be in good health. Considering the social position of the senior citizens, the comprehensive policy of the State is to ensure that they spend their old age in a good manner, their life in the society is comfortable, their physical/ mental health is good, their economic potential in old age, right to work, right to education and public assistance. The decision was taken on July 9, 2018. The state government has decided to celebrate International Day of Older Persons on October 1.
Accordingly, 1 st October, 2022, should be celebrated as World Senior Citizen's Day at all places in the State and on this occasion, advertisements in newspapers, public walks, morning walks, meetings, felicitation, felicitation, health camps, seminars/ symposiums etc. should be organized for the elderly who have done special work and non-governmental organizations working for senior citizens. Also, information on laws and schemes for senior citizens, rights of the elderly, facilities provided to senior citizens, helpline for assistance to senior citizens, etc. should be provided through this program.
World Senior Citizen's Day should be organized by all the district collectors, all the local self-government institutions, all the government/ semi-government offices, municipal corporations and municipalities/ municipal councils in the state. On this day, various activities for senior citizens are planned.
