Accordingly, in reference to Finance Department, No. 1, The Circular dated 16.4.2020 has approved the release of funds in the range of 15 to 25% of the total annual budgetary allocation of essential and programme expenditure for the financial year 2020-21, considering the pandemic situation in the State. However, in the wake of Covid-19, the Chief Minister's Rajgar Nirman Programme has been made available for the distribution of funds for this scheme by the Finance Department, which is currently 10 percent of the total budget provision on the budgetary fund distribution system. Accordingly, under the Scheduled Castes Component Programme, the Department of Industries, Energy and Labour, Read No. 2 As proposed herein at the table, out of the budgetary allocation for the financial year 2020-21 for the Mukhya Mantri Rajgar Nirman Karyakram, (28516692), Rs. 200.00 lakh is being sanctioned to the Principal Secretary, Industries, Power and Labour Department as Head of Administrative Department.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. Since the economic condition of the state is likely to be very serious for the next 3 to 4 months due to the lockdown, the controlling officer should take precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds. It should be ensured that the funds will be spent this year as per the approved plan in accordance with the current government policy. Also, the report of the expenditure incurred from the provision made, account-wise/ sub-account-wise and material objective-wise information achieved, the utilization certificate of the fund, the concerned field officer should ensure that it is sent to the Commissioner, Social Welfare, the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare, Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and the Industries, Energy and Labour Department by the 10th of every month.
The Head of the Department/ Controlling Officer should ensure that the funds meant for SCs are spent on the same components so that the Government can have control over the expenditure on these schemes.
