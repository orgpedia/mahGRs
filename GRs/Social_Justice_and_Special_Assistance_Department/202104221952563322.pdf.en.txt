GOVERNMENT DECISIONS, SOCIAL JUSTICE AND SPECIAL ASSISTANCE, NO. VISSIO-2018/ P. NO. 62/ VISSIO, D. August 20, 2019.
Government Decision: In the financial year 2021-22, Rs. A budgetary provision of Rs. 100,00,00, 000/- (Rupees One Hundred Crores only) has been approved. Out of the budgetary allocation of Rs. 45,00,00, 000/- has been disbursed by the Finance Department. Accordingly, the Government hereby approves disbursement of the amount sanctioned for the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for the beneficiaries of Scheduled Tribes for the period April, 2021 to March, 2022 as per the accompanying statement under the Government decision for an amount of Rs. 45,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Forty Five Crore only). Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries.
The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.
The expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No. T-5, Main Account Head 2235-Social Security and Welfare, 02-Social Welfare, 796-Tribal Area Sub Plan, (02) Out of Tribal Area Sub Plan (OTSP), (02) (03) Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme (Programme), 50-Other Expenditure, (2235 C 161)."
4. All the District Collectors are informed to ensure that these payments are deposited in the treasury. Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.

All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 432/2021/ Expenditure 14 dated 22/04/2021.
