Government's decision
In the financial year 2023-24, for the implementation of Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme for Scheduled Caste beneficiaries, Rs. A budgetary provision of Rs. 350,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Hundred and Fifty Crores only) has been approved. Out of the budgetary allocation of Rs. 35,00,00, 000/- has been released by the Finance Department.
Accordingly, the amount sanctioned for the Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme for Scheduled Caste beneficiaries for the period April, 2023 to March, 2024 is Rs. 35,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Thirty Five Crores only) is hereby approved by the Government as per the accompanying prospectus under this Government Order. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries.
The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.
The expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No.N-3, Main Account Head 2235-Social Security and Welfare, 02-Social Welfare, 789 Scheduled Castes Sub-Plan, (01) Grants to the Elderly, Weaker and Destitute, (01) (02) Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme (AjaUYO) (Programme), 50-Other Expenditure, (2235 C 143)."
4. All the District Collectors are hereby informed to ensure that these payments are deposited in the treasury. Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.

All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
