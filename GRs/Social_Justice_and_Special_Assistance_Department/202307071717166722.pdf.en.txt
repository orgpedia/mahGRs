The Proposal:
The Government of India has been implementing the Post Matric Scholarship Scheme for Scheduled Castes (including neo-Buddhists) students since 1959-60 with the objective of ensuring that the boys and girls belonging to Scheduled Castes get higher education, improve their quality and get higher education following the ideals of Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar. Under this scheme, students belonging to the Scheduled Castes (including neo-Buddhists) are given a subsistence allowance and the respective educational institutions are paid tuition fees and examination fees. Under this scheme, the income limit of parents has been increased from Rs. 2.00 lakh to Rs. 2.50 lakh as per the Government decision dated 18.09.2018.
As per the provisions of the revised guidelines issued by the Central Government in the month of March, 2021, the revised fund distribution procedure is to be adopted across the state from the academic year 2021-22. Further, in case of non-adoption of the revised distribution mechanism in the State as notified vide Central Government letter dated 01.11.2021, the Central subsidy (Central share 60%) admissible to the State Government under the said scholarship scheme shall not be paid. It has also been indicated that if the central grants received in that financial year remain unutilized, they cannot be reallocated for spending in the next financial year. In view of this, the revised guidelines for the year 2020-21 to 2025-26 issued in the month of March, 2021 of the Government of India Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme of the Central Government, with regard to the disbursement of funds, vide issue No. 10.1, 11.1 and 11.2 respectively issued by the Department on 17.3.2022, the Centre-State sharing formula under the Government of India Post Matric Scholarship Scheme has been adopted and the Government of India has decided to provide Rs.
