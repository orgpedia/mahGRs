""" Introduction:\"" It has been decided to subsume the\"" National Pension System\"" (NPS) of the Centre for the employees of the State Government, Zilla Parishads, recognized and aided educational institutions, agricultural/ non-agricultural universities and affiliated recognized and aided non-government colleges, who have been/ will be appointed on or after 1st November, 2005, in the service of the State Government.""" The decision was taken on August 27, 2014. Further, the National Pension System (NPS) has been made effective from 1st April, 2015 for the State Government employees joining Government service on or after 1st November, 2015 vide Government Notification dated 30th September, 2022. The defined contribution pension scheme being implemented in the state is being called the" National Pension System" (NPS).
In this regard, the Department of Finance has entered into an agreement with Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA), established by the Central Government, and NSDL e-Governance as Central Record Keeping Agency (CRA) on behalf of the Government on 10th October, 2014. Accordingly, the Department of Finance has prescribed the procedure for implementation of Level-1 of National Pension System for Government employees vide Government Resolution dated 6th April, 2015 at Reference No.6.
Similarly, the teachers and non-teaching staff appointed on or after 1st November, 2005 in 100% aided posts in Social Work Colleges covered by the State Defined Contribution Pension Scheme (DCPS) under the control of the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance, as a State Government employee, will be included in the National Pension System (NPS) of the Centre.

Government's decision:
The National Pension System (NPS) will be applicable to the employees who are appointed/ going to be appointed in teaching and non-teaching posts in 100% aided posts in aided social work colleges where the State Defined Contribution Pension Scheme (DCPS) is in force on or after 1st November, 2005.

It shall be the responsibility of the Drawing and Disbursing Officer to ensure that every employee appointed to the service is appointed in the prescribed manner, in a regular post in the regular pay scale, with the approval of the competent authority, and that such teaching and non-teaching staff are in regular posts on 100% grant.
To implement the scheme, the following steps are to be followed:
Central Records Maintenance Agency (CRA):- M.N.S.D.L.E-Governance Infrastructure Ltd.
The National Security Depositories Limited (NSDL) will act as the central monitoring agency. The Central Record Keeping Agency will be responsible for issuing Permanent Pension Account Numbers to all teaching and non-teaching staff under the National Pension System, maintaining and maintaining records in respect of the amounts credited and all other works prescribed in this regard by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority in relation to the National Pension Scheme. Accordingly, the State defined Pension Scheme (DCPS) under the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance has been extended to 100% Aided Posts in State Aided Social Work Colleges (DCPS). The letter of consent for joining the National Pension System (NPS) should be obtained from the teachers and non-teaching staff appointed on or after November 1, 2005. The Commissioner, Social Welfare, State of Maharashtra, Pune will be responsible as the single point contact with the Central Maintenance Agency. The Commissioner, Social Welfare, Maharashtra State, Pune will implement the scheme through the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare Department, and the concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare.
