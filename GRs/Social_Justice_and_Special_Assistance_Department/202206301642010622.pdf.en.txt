Introduction: It is seen that many types of misunderstandings rooted in some evil, Aghori, inhuman culture rooted in Indian society are causing great harm to the society. In order to save the lives of millions of people, to stop their exploitation and harassment and to improve the mental health of the society, the Government has issued the Maharashtra Prevention and Eradication of Human Sacrifice and Other Inhuman, Evil and Aghori Practices and Witchcraft Act-2013 on December 20, 2013.
The anti-magic law will save the lives of millions of people from harassment, exploitation and exploitation.

Duties of District Level Anti-Witchcraft Law Enforcement Promotion Committee (Dist PIMC): (i) To plan/ organize the promotion of Anti-Witchcraft Law at the district level/ village level. Venue determination, organizing public meetings, publicity, publicity after the completion of the program and other matters.
(ii) To implement the programmes directed by the State level PIMCs and the Zonal PIMCs in the districts.
(iii) To co-operate and co-ordinate with the Government authorities in the district; (iv) To plan and execute the activities of organizing lectures/ sending speakers/ conducting programmes in schools and colleges.
The non-official members of the said committee shall be appointed with the approval of the State-level PIMC Chairman and Joint Chairman and with the concurrence of the Commissioner, Social Welfare, Pune.
Applications are invited for the post of District Coordinator. The final selection should be done with the approval of the PIMC Chairman and Co-Chairman and with the concurrence of the Commissioner, Social Welfare, Pune.
The Committee shall meet at least twice a year. The allowances fixed by the Finance Department for the non-official members of the Committee shall be admissible as per rules.
