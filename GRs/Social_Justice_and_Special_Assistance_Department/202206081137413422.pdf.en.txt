The following four corporations are functioning under the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance under the Government of Maharashtra. (1) The Mahatma Phule Backward Classes Development Corporation Limited. (2) Sahityaratna Janadhikar Annabhau Sathe Development Corporation Limited. (3) Sant Rohidas Tanneries and Tanners Development Corporation Limited. (4) The Maharashtra State Handicapped Finance and Development Corporation Limited. The above Corporation provides financial assistance for the overall upliftment of the persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, Backward Classes and Persons with Disabilities through self-employment. These corporations are provided with managerial grants and equity from the government. The schemes of the State Government are being implemented with the funds from the official share capital provided to the Corporation. As the authorised shareholding of the corporation exceeded the limit, funds could not be provided by the government for the schemes, as a result of which the corporations were facing difficulties in implementing their schemes, so the matter of increasing the authorised shareholding of the corporations was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
The shareholding limit of Mahatma Phule Backward Classes Development Corporation under Social Justice and Special Assistance Department is Rs. 500 crore to Rs. 1000 crore, the shareholding limit of Sahityaratna Loktantrik Annabhau Sathe Development Corporation is Rs. 300 crore to Rs. 1000 crore, Sant Rohidas Tannery and Tannery Development Corporation with a shareholding limit of Rs. 73.21 crore to Rs. 1000 crore and the authorised shareholding of Maharashtra State Disabled Finance and Development Corporation limited to Rs. 50 crore to Rs. 500 crore is being approved under the following conditions under this Government decision.
1) The additional equity will be made available to the Corporation in a phased manner.
(2) There shall be a regular review at the level of the Chairman of the Corporation of the schemes and the shareholding to be distributed by the Corporation. The details should be submitted to the Planning and Finance Department.
(3) The additional share received shall be utilised only for the schemes to be implemented by the Corporation. It shall be the sole responsibility of the Company to ensure that it is not misused for any other purpose.
