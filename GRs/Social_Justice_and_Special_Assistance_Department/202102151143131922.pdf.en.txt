Government Decision: For the financial year 2020-21, a budgetary provision of Rs. 40,00,00, 000/- has been approved for administrative expenses for implementation of Sanjay Gandhi Niradhi Grant Scheme and Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme and Rs. 14,00,00, 000/- for administrative expenses for schemes under National Social Assistance Programme. The Government hereby approve the release of Rs. 1,50,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Fifty Lakh only) towards administrative expenses for the implementation of Sanjay Gandhi Niradhi Sanadhi Yojana and Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme and Rs. 1,00,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore only) towards administrative expenses for the schemes under National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP) out of the sanctioned outlay for the financial year 2020-21. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are hereby informed that the grants distributed as per the statement A and B attached herewith should be distributed to the taluks under their jurisdiction as per the requirement of the grants.
Grants disbursed under the head' Demand No.N-3, Head of Accounts 2235, (08) (08) Administrative Expenditure (Programme) 50 Other Expenditure for the implementation of Sanjay Gandhi Niradhi Grants Scheme and Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme' (2235 3121) for the administrative expenses for the implementation of the schemes under National Social Assistance Programme and Grants disbursed under the head' Demand No.N-3, Head of Accounts 2235, (08) (06) for the administrative expenses for the implementation of the schemes under the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhi Grants Scheme and Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme'.

The administrative expenses for the implementation of Old Age Pension Scheme and National Family Benefit Scheme (NCFBY) (Programme) 50, other expenses (2235 3014) under the head' Expenditure on account of expenditure on account of implementation of the scheme.
All the District Collectors are informed that they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the grant handed over to them under any circumstances. They should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the audit office of this department and to this office, matching the expenditure made from the grant handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the office of the Accountant General. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office.
If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
