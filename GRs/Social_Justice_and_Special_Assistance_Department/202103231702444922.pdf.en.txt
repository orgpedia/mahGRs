Government's decision
An amount of Rs. 50,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Fifty Crores only) was sanctioned through Supplementary Demand in the Winter Session for implementation of Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for Scheduled Caste beneficiaries during the Financial Year 2020-21. Accordingly, the Finance Department has approved the release of funds amounting to Rs. 50,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Fifty Crores only) which was sanctioned in the Monsoon Session. Accordingly, the Government hereby approve the release of Rs. 50,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Fifty Crores only) towards the cost of implementation of Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for the period January, 2021 to March, 2021 as per the accompanying statement under the Government Resolution. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries. The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.
The expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No. N-3, Main Account Head 2235-Social Security and Welfare, 02-Social Welfare, 789 Scheduled Castes Sub-Plan, (01) Grants to the Aged, Weaker and Destitute, (01) (01) Sanjay Gandhi Destitute Grant Scheme (AJAUYO) (Programme), 50-Other Expenditure, (2235 C 134)."

All the District Collectors are informed that they have issued Reference No. 3 Care should be taken to submit the payments to the treasury by fulfilling the terms and conditions of the circular of the Finance Department here. Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.
5. All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 267/2021/ Expenditure 14 dated 18/03/2021.
