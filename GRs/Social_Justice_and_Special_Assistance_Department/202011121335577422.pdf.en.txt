Presently, the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 has been implemented throughout the country with effect from 17.04.2017 and the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 1995 has been repealed.
Under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, 21 categories of persons with disabilities have been included. They're as follows:
1) Blindness, 2) Hearing impairment, 3) Physical disability, 4) Mental illness, 5) Intellectual disability, 6) Multiple disability, 7) Autism, 9) Cerebral palsy, 10) Muscular dystrophy, 11) Chronic neurological condition, 12) Learning disability, 13) Multiple skill
Article 34 of the Constitution provides for 4% reservation in government jobs for persons with disabilities. According to Public Interest Litigation filed in the High Court of Bombay, No. 139/2006 by the Hon'ble High Court vide order dated 10.12.2019

The above Committee will function under the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. The mandate of the committee will be to examine the proposals received from the concerned government departments, determine which disabled persons are capable of working in these posts and take a final decision on them.
After the decision of the committee, the decision will be issued by the concerned department. The Chairman of the Committee may invite any person, institution or officer of the department concerned for the meeting as per requirement.
(3) All the administrative departments of the Ministry shall send a consolidated proposal of all the posts in their subordinate offices for the identification of suitable posts for the disabled persons in the form of the accompanying prospectus in Form A to the concerned department for the approval of the said Expert Committee.
