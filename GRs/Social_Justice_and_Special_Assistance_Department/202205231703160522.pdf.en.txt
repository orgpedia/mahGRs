Introduction: Snehsadan Matimand Boys and Girls Special Non-Residential School, Shitalwadi, Ta. The proposal for grant of recognition to Ramtek, G.Nagpur as a special case was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: Snehsadan Matimand Special Non-Residential School for Boys and Girls, Shitalwadi, Ta. The Commissioner, Divyang Kalyan, Pune, has issued registration certificate No.52 under the Persons with Disabilities Act, 1995 on non-subsidized basis to a Special School for the mentally retarded category, Ramtek, G.Nagpur, having 30 residents and 40 non-residents. A-0805, Dy. Approval has been granted on 24.1.2003. Also, it is not possible to keep socially and physically weaker sections like mentally retarded children deprived for a long time in the absence of a comprehensive plan and policy. Ramtek, G. Nagpur. The Chief Minister has approved the grant in principle. Accordingly, the said school is being recognized under this government decision subject to the following terms and conditions.
The terms and conditions are as follows: 1) The School shall be governed by such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Government from time to time.
2) The School is being recognized on grant-in-aid basis subject to the approval of the Finance Department and the High Level Committee of Secretaries for Posts and Posts.

3) The School is affiliated to the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance. The terms and conditions as per paragraphs 1 to 27 of the Government Order issued under 8.4.2015 shall continue to apply.
4) The government decided to inspect this special school. As per Form-B dated 8.4.2015, it should be done every year and as per Form-C, the Divyang school should be evaluated and scored and accordingly the concerned special school should be made eligible for the grant.
(5) If it is found that a special school for the disabled is receiving regular grants from the Central Government, local bodies or other institutions, the institution will be ineligible to receive grants from the State Government.
(6) If it is found that the terms and conditions and procedures prescribed by the Government from time to time have not been complied with, the recognition of the institution shall be cancelled.
7) The special school is being recognized on a grant basis with a total sanctioned strength of 70 students, which includes 30 residential and 40 non-residential students. The proposal regarding the staff required as per the figure 18.8.2004 should be submitted through the Finance Department for the approval of the High Level Committee of Secretaries. Without this recognition, these posts should not be given recognition.
8) Ma. Petition No. 690/2013 Dr. Babasaheb Paranjape Pratishthan Ambejogai Vs. Government of Maharashtra& Ors. The Aurangabad bench of the Bombay High Court. As on 3.9.2013, the Government has issued an order to frame a policy regarding grant of special schools/ workshops for persons with disabilities. The school is being recognized on grant basis as per the norms of the said policy.
9) Snehsadan Matimand Boys Girls Special Non-Residential School, Shitalwadi, Tt. Ramtek, Nagpur. The Chief Minister has acknowledged it as a" special case." However, this will no longer be applicable for recognition of schools/ workshops for other disabled persons and cannot be taken as a precedent.
2) The decision of the government. It is being issued with the approval of the Chief Minister.
