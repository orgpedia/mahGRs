GOVERNMENT DECISION: For the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Gramin) for Scheduled Caste (SC) Category in FY 2022-23, Demand No. N-3, 2505-Rural Employment, 60, Other Programmes, 789 Special Component Schemes for Scheduled Castes, (01) Schemes under Scheduled Castes Component Programme, (01) (04) Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Gramin) (Central Share 60%) (Programme) 31-Subsidised Grants (25059102) Under the head Rs. 35188.50 Lakhs. (2505A111) under this head Rs. 23459.00 lakh has been sanctioned.
According to the Finance Department. In the circular dated 04.04.2022, the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2022-23 have been decided keeping in view the pandemic situation in the State. As per the approval of the Finance Department for the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Gramin) (2505A102), 60% Central Share of Rs. 18539.280 lakhs and (25059111) 40% State Share under the head Rs. 12359.520 lakh is being sanctioned to the Additional Chief Secretary, Rural Development Department as Head of Administrative Department.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. Since the financial position of the State is likely to be very critical, the Controlling Authority should take precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds in this distribution. It should be ensured that the funds will be spent this year as per the approved plan in accordance with the current government policy. Also, the report of the expenditure made from the provision made, account-wise/ sub-account-wise and material objective-wise information achieved, the utilization certificate of the fund, the concerned field officer should ensure that it is sent to the Commissioner, Social Welfare, the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare, Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and the Rural Development Department by the 10th of every month.
The Head of the Department/ Controlling Officer should ensure that the funds meant for SCs are spent on the same components so that the Government can have control over the expenditure on these schemes.
