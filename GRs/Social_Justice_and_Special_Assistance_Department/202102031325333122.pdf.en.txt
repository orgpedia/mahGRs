Government circular:
The process of transferring the educational benefits of four schemes, namely, Scholarship, Freeship, Vidyavetan to the Aadhaar linked bank accounts of the students belonging to the Scheduled Castes category has been started from the year 2018-19. Earlier, in the instant case, it was approved to pay 60% of the amount ad-hoc to the concerned educational institutions/ colleges for payment of pending scholarships, tuition fees, examination fees, etc. for the academic year 2010-11 to 2016-17 (excluding the delinquent institutions/ colleges and permissible courses for which action was recommended by the SIT). The decision was only in respect of pending scholarship, tuition fee and examination fee for the academic year 2010-11 to 2016-17. So in this case. In pursuance of the directions given in the meeting held on 28.8.2020 under the chairmanship of the Minister of Social Justice and Special Assistance, all concerned shall take action in accordance with the following instructions.
(1) After the period of inspection/ enquiry conducted by the Special Investigation Team on the 70 colleges mentioned in the Special Investigation Team's report, the eligible students belonging to the Scheduled Castes category of all the courses of study in the 70 colleges concerned admitted since 2017-18 are meeting the terms and conditions of the scheme, the Assistant Commissioner concerned, Social Welfare should immediately take action to pay the reimbursement of scholarship/ tuition fee, examination fee due to such students so that such students are not deprived of the educational benefits.
(2) Accordingly, during the year 2017-18, the pending applications for scholarship, tuition fee and examination fee are required to be processed offline under the Government Order dated 8th December, 2017 as per the prevailing situation and for the same, the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-Division-I, under the Bombay Financial Rules, 1959 read with the relevant Government Order dated 17.4.2015, No. 6, Rule-40.

Complete proposals with the fulfillment of the terms and conditions laid down in accordance with the powers granted to the following administrative departments should be submitted to the government and get the investigation certificate from the government.
(3) Since the scholarship/ friship schemes are being implemented on MahaDBT system since 2018-19, the Department of Information and Technology should provide the facility of application correction on MahaDBT system to dispose of the registered but unapproved applications of the admitted students of the respective 70 colleges during 2018-19 and 2019-20. Accordingly, the said application should be specifically computerized and registered in the system and disposed of.
(4) The applications of the students belonging to the Scheduled Castes category in the 70 colleges concerned during the period 2018-19 and 2019-20, whose applications have not been registered on the MahaDBT system, should be specifically computerized and registered in the system and the process of disposal should be carried out in accordance with the procedure given in A. No. (2) above. However, after such applications are disposed of, a report should be submitted to the Department of Information and Technology for taking note of them/ uploading the information on the website.
(5) The Department of Information and Technology should create a facility to save the applications disposed of offline for the period 2018-19 and 2019-20 in a specially computerized system and save them academic year-wise and scheme-wise under a separate tab on the homepage of the MahaDBT system.
(6) For the purpose of the audit, the responsibility of preserving the records/ documents regarding the pending application disposed of in accordance with the above procedure shall be of the concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare.
