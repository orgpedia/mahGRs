Government Decision: For the financial year 2019-20, a budget provision of Rs. 753,68,49, 000/- (Rs. 400,00,00, 000/ (Rupees Four Hundred Crores) has been sanctioned through Supplementary Demand. As per the Government Circular in Reference No. 2 of the Finance Department, 60% of the budgetary provision and the provision sanctioned through supplementary demand for the expenditure of the period April to December, 2019 for the Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme has been distributed to all the Collectors under the Government Decision in Reference No. 4, 5 and 6. Now the total balance amount of Rs. 461,47,39, 600/- for general beneficiaries of Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme (remaining budgetary allocation Rs. 301,47,39, 600/-+ remaining Supplementary Demand Provision of Rs. 160,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Four Hundred and Sixty-One Crore Forty-Seven Lakhs Forty-Nine Thousand Six Hundred Only) per month for the period January, 2020 to March, 2020 as per the accompanying statement under this Government Order. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.

2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries.
The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector. As per the circular at reference no. 1, it is allowed to draw the authorization letter in the minus manner.
The expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No. N-3, Main Account Head 2235 Social Security and Welfare, 02-Social Welfare, 104-Welfare of Elderly, Disabled and Destitute Persons (08) (07) Shravanbal Seva State Pension Scheme (Programme) (22353112), 50 Other Expenditure."
4. All the District Collectors are informed that they have issued Reference No. 2 Care should be taken to submit the payments to the treasury by fulfilling the terms and conditions of the circular of the Finance Department here.
Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.
5. All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 720/2019/ Expenditure 14 dated 07.12.2019.
