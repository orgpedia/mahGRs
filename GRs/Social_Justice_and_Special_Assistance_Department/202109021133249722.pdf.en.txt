Considering the pandemic situation due to Corona virus, the modalities for disbursement of funds for the financial year 2021-22 have been worked out and planning suggestions have been made for spending within the limit of 60 per cent of the schemes under the programme. The Department of Finance has made available 50% of the total budgetary allocation for the scheme on the existing budgetary allocation system for the rebate of electricity bill to the agricultural pump consumers. Accordingly, under the Scheduled Castes Component Programme, the Department of Industries, Energy and Labour, Read No. 3 As proposed hereinabove, for the scheme of rebate in electricity bills to the agricultural pump consumers, out of the budgetary allocation for the financial year 2021-22 (28015661), Rs. 96.43 crore to Principal Secretary (Power), Industries, Power and Labour Department as Head of Administrative Department.
The funds have been distributed on the budget distribution system. As the financial position of the State is likely to be very critical due to COVID-19, the Controlling Authority should take precautionary measures in the prescribed manner while spending the funds. It should be ensured that the funds will be spent this year as per the approved plan in accordance with the current government policy. Also, the report of the expenditure incurred from the provision made, account-wise/ sub-account-wise and material objective-wise information achieved, the utilization certificate of the fund, the concerned field officer should ensure that it is sent to the Commissioner, Social Welfare, the concerned Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare, Social Justice and Special Assistance Department and the Industries, Energy and Labour Department by the 10th of every month.
The Head of the Department/ Controlling Officer should ensure that the funds meant for SCs are spent on the same components so that the Government can have control over the expenditure on these schemes.
