Government Decision: In the financial year 2021-22, for the implementation of the centrally sponsored Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme, Rs. A budgetary provision of Rs. 6,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Six Crores only) has been approved. Out of the budgetary allocation of Rs. 1,50,00, 000/- has been disbursed by the Finance Department. Accordingly, for the Centrally Sponsored Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme, the amount sanctioned for expenditure for the period April, 2021 to March, 2022 is Rs. 1,50,00, 000/- (Rupees One Crore Fifty Lakh only) as per Reference No. 2 It has been distributed to all the district collectors as per the decision of the government. Out of which, the amount remaining with the Collector Office Mumbai City by disbursing the financial assistance to the beneficiaries till June, 2021 is Rs. 32,55,350/- has been withdrawn to the Government and the amount is being disbursed to all the Collectorates as per the accompanying returns under this Government decision. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code.
The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries. The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.
The expenditure out of the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No. N-3, Main Account Head 2235 Social Security and Welfare, 104 Welfare of Elderly, Disabled and Destitute Persons, (08) (12) Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme (Centrally Sponsored Scheme) (Programme), 50 Other Expenditure, (2235B049)."

4. All the District Collectors are hereby informed to ensure that these payments are deposited in the treasury. Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.
5. All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 432/2021/ Expenditure 14 dated 22/04/2021.
