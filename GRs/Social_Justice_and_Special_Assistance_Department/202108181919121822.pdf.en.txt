Government Decision: In the financial year 2021-22, Rs. A budgetary provision of Rs. 315,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Three Hundred and Fifteen Crores only) has been approved. Out of the budgetary allocation of Rs. 60,00,00, 000/- has been disbursed by the Finance Department. Accordingly, the amount sanctioned for the Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Subsidy Scheme for Scheduled Caste beneficiaries for the period April, 2021 to March, 2022 is Rs. 60,00,00, 000/- (Rupees Sixty Crores only) vide Reference No. 2 It has been distributed to all the district collectors as per the decision of the government. Out of which, the amount remaining with the Collector Office Mumbai City by disbursing the financial assistance to the beneficiaries till June, 2021 is Rs. 17,85,14, 165/- taken back to the Government and the amount is being disbursed to all the Collectorates as per the accompanying statement under this Government decision. Also, facility to draw forward/ reverse funds as per monthly statement has been provided on Budget Estimate Distribution and Control System.
2. All the Collectors are informed that in the accompanying statement, Column No. 3 Funds have been distributed to the respective districts on the new CO Code. The grants distributed by them should be distributed to the taluks of their districts in proportion to the beneficiaries. The subsidy has been distributed as per the number of beneficiaries informed by the District Collector.
Expenditure from the said fund should be spent under the head" Demand No. N-3, Main Accounts Head 2235 Social Security and Welfare, 02-Social Welfare, 789 Scheduled Castes Sub-Plan, (01) Grants to the Aged, Weaker and Destitute, (01) (01) Sanjay Gandhi Niradhi Grants Scheme (AJAUYO) (Programme), 50-Other Expenditure, (2235 C 134)."

4. All the District Collectors are hereby informed to ensure that these payments are deposited in the treasury. Also, they should ensure that the expenditure does not exceed the subsidy given to them under any circumstances.
5. All the District Collectors are also informed that they should send a copy of the reconciliation statement to the Audit Office of this Department and to this Office by matching the expenditure made from the grants handed over to them with the expenditure recorded in the Accountant General's Office. Utilization certificate should be sent to the Accountant General's office immediately and its copy should be sent to the planning office of this department and this office. If the expenditure reconciliation work is not done properly and the utilization certificate is not submitted to the Accountant General's office within the prescribed time, the entire responsibility will be on the concerned district collector.
This Government Resolution is being issued as per the concurrence given by the Finance Department vide Informal Reference No. 432/2021/ Expenditure 14 dated 22/04/2021.
