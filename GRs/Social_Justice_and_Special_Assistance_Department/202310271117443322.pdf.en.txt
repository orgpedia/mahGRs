Government Notification, Finance Department, No: RANIPR 2022/ PR No.32/ SEVA-4, dated 30th September, 2022 Preamble: It has been decided by the Government of India vide Govemment dated 27th August, 2014 to include in the National Pension System (NPS) of the Central Government the employees of the State who are/ will be appointed on or after 1st November, 2005 in the service of the State Government, District Councils, recognized and aided educational institutions, agricultural/ non-agricultural universities and affiliated recognized and aided non-government colleges. Further, the National Pension System (NPS) has been implemented with effect from 1st April, 2015 for State Government employees joining Government service on or after 1st November, 2015 vide Government Notification dated 30th September, 2022. The defined contribution pension scheme being implemented in the state is being called" National Pension System" (NPS).
The Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) and the Central Record Keeping Agency (CRA) have been established by the Central Government in this regard.
NSDL e-Governance has been signed by the Department of Finance on behalf of the Government on 10th October, 2014.

Accordingly, the Department of Finance has prescribed the procedure for implementation of Level 1 of National Pension System for Government employees vide Government Resolution dated 6th April, 2015 at Reference No.6.
On the same lines, the Government was considering to take a decision to include the teaching and non-teaching staff appointed on or after 1st November, 2005 in the Central National Pension System (NPS) on 100% subsidized posts in the aided Ashramshalas opened for the children of Scheduled Castes, who are covered under the State Defined Contribution Pension Scheme (DCPS) under the control of the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance.
Government Decision: The National Pension System (NPS) will be applicable to the employees who are appointed/ going to be appointed to the posts of teachers and non-teaching staff in the 100 percent subsidized posts in the aided ashramshalas for the children of Scheduled Castes (SC) who are covered under the State Defined Contribution Pension Scheme (DCPS) on or after 1st November, 2005.

It shall be the responsibility of the Drawing and Disbursing Officer to ensure that every employee appointed to the service is appointed in the prescribed manner, in a regular post in the regular pay scale, with the approval of the competent authority, and that the teaching and non-teaching staff are in a regular post on 100% grant.
