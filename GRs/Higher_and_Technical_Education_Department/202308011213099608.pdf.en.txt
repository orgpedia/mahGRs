Terms and Conditions:
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
2) As prescribed by the Council of Architecture in the present case. The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's intake capacity for this course will be as mentioned above.

3) The institution will have to sign an agreement on the acceptance of all terms and conditions prescribed by the government and prescribed by the Council of Architecture. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Post Graduate Degree Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the relevant course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
7) The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the norms. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) Books, periodicals required by the library in the institution must be purchased in accordance with the standards of the Council of Architecture. Also, in order to make full use of the library for the students, it is necessary to provide courses to the students in the library and all educational facilities to the students as per the standards of the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
9) Regarding the ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
10) Apart from this, other terms and conditions set by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the concerned university will remain applicable.
