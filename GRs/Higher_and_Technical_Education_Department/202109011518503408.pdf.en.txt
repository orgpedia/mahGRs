The proposal:
The Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi has approved the introduction of new unaided diploma courses in unaided institutions of the existing Bachelor of Pharmacy course in the State from the academic year 2021-22. Accordingly, the Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai vide the aforesaid Notification No. Proposals 1 and 2 have been submitted to the Government.
Government Decision: As per the approval given by Pharmacy Council of India, the institutions mentioned in attached Form-A and Form-B are being given approval by the Government to start new unaided diploma courses in the unaided institution of the existing degree course of Pharmacy and to start new unaided diploma courses in the unaided institution of the existing degree course of Pharmacy respectively from the academic year 2021-22 subject to the following conditions.
1) The grant will be on a grant-in-aid basis.
In this case, the institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983.
The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be limited as mentioned against their names in the prospectus.

4. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi.
The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
The concerned institution must be affiliated to Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for these diploma courses.
The Principals of the newly approved Diploma in Pharmacy course will be required to obtain the approval of the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi before admitting the students. In the absence of this approval, the concerned institutions will be fully responsible in case some court cases arise in the future. The undertaking has to be submitted by the head of the institution concerned to the Director, Directorate of Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai. Only after this, the directorate has to give consent to these institutions to start the course.
7. The institution concerned shall complete other legal formalities by depositing the requisite processing fee and accumulated deposits in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai.
Admission to the said Professional Diploma Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective courses approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned exceed the tuition fees prescribed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
10.The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per Government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
11.The library in the institution is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
12.Sadar Institute will be under the control of Director, Technical Education and the examinations of the relevant courses will be conducted by Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Bandra, Mumbai.
13.R. In respect of Ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
14.Yet other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai and the University concerned shall continue to apply.
