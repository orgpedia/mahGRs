The proposal:
The Director, College of Engineering, Pune vide letter dated 11/7/2019 had requested to allow admission of candidates to the above mentioned courses of Planning/ Architecture/ Civil Engineering/ Geography/ Economics/ Social Science by considering the candidates who have scored non-zero positive marks in the GATE entrance examination in the relevant subject as in the last academic year, if the seats of admission remain vacant despite the candidates having the Common Entrance Examination for Design (CEED) qualification prescribed as per the admission rules for the admission process to the Master's Degree course of M. Planning. Accordingly, the Director Technical Education had submitted a proposal to the Government vide letter dated 29/7/2019. In pursuance of this proposal, considering the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), the Government was contemplating to consider holding of GATE entrance examination for admission to Master of Planning course in the academic year 2019-20.
The government's decision:
In view of the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education vide letter dated 18/7/2019, the admission procedure prescribed for Master of Planning in the academic year 2019-20 after completion of the prescribed admission process as per the original educational qualification" Bachelor's Degree in Planning or Vastu Shastra or Architectural Engineering or Geography or Economics or Social Sciences or its equivalent."

Candidates who have passed the examination with at least 50% marks (45% marks for candidates belonging to Backward Classes and Persons with Disabilities category) and have secured non-zero positive marks in the Common Entrance Examination in Design (CEED) conducted by the Indian Institute of Technology (IITs)," in the seats falling vacant due to lack of adequate number of candidates," have been approved by the government to qualify for admission.
The admission rules for M.Planning course will be revised as per the norms.
