The proposal:
To make India a knowledge superpower, to provide necessary skills and knowledge to the students and to overcome the shortage of manpower in the field of science, technology, education and industry, to provide facilities of quality education, innovative education and research to the population of India, the Government of India has drafted a new National Education Policy. The state government has been asked to submit a proposal in this regard.
The Ministry of Education has sought comments from the Director of Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune on the draft of the new National Education Policy. Accordingly, the Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune has submitted his views to the Government. Suggestions/ comments on the draft have been received from Swami Ramanand Teerth Marathwada University, Nanded and Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA), Mumbai. The suggestions/ comments received are forwarded to the Ministry of Human Resource Development, New Delhi vide Reference No. (3) are presented here.
In line with the new National Education Policy, the government has constituted a task force of education experts to analyze the suggestions submitted by the workshops at the taluka and district levels on the subject related to them and submit the same to the workshops of all the concerned units at the state level. Appointment of non-official members of the Task Force Group vide Reference No. (4) Here. The Government's decision of January 24, 2020 has been revoked.
The Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD), Government of India has recently launched the National Education Policy 2020. Therefore, a Task Force Group has been constituted as per the Government Resolution dated 18th September, 2020 in Reference No. 5 to study and submit a report to the State Government regarding the National Education Policy-2020. Vasudha Kamat has been appointed as Chairperson and Bhalchandra Mungekar as Member of the Task Force. However, Ms. Vasudha Kamat has informed the Government that she is unable to work in the Task Force Group due to ill health and Mr. Bhalchandra Mungekar due to personal reasons.
In view of the above, a fresh Task Force Group is required to be constituted to study and submit a report to the State Government regarding the new National Education Policy-2020.
However, reference No. 5 The matter of forming a new task force group by superseding the Government decision dated 18th September, 2020 was under consideration of the Government. Accordingly, it is being decided to create a new workforce group comprising of the following experts in the field of education:
The government's decision:
In pursuance of the Task Force Group constituted to study the new National Education Policy-2020, Reference No. 5 The Government hereby supersedes the decision dated 18th September, 2020 and constitutes a new Task Force comprising of experts from the field of education to study and submit a report to the State Government on the new National Education Policy-2020.

The committee will study every aspect of the new National Education Policy-2020 and submit its report to the government within 3 months. However, if it is required to submit a report on some urgent issues to the government on priority, the committee should meet from time to time and submit an interim report to the government.
The Universities where the meetings of the said Committee shall be held shall mutually pay the honorarium and travelling allowance of the non-official members of the Committee.
