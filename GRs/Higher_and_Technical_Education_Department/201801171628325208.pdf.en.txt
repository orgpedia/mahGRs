Preamble: With the success of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan and Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA), the Central Government has decided to implement Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) as an umbrella scheme in the 12th and 13th Five Year Plan to empower the quality and foundation of higher education in the State. The National Mission on Higher Education (NMHE), a flagship programme of the Government of India, aims to ensure quality, equity and opportunity in higher education in Maharashtra. The decision has been taken in the Cabinet meeting held on 21/04/2015. In pursuance of the decision taken by the State Cabinet, a State Project Directorate has been set up for effective implementation of the scheme in the State. The Maharashtra State Council of Higher Education (MSSCHE) has received the funds from the central government.
Decision of the Government: The Government has approved the transfer of funds amounting to Rs. 18.00 crore to the State Project Director, Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA), as provided by the Ministry of Human Resource Development, New Delhi under the Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) on Central share for infrastructure development of 3 Universities.
The expenditure incurred in this regard is being sanctioned subject to the condition that it is certified by the competent authority. All the documents and accounts of the above grants are to be examined by the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1 or the officer authorised for the purpose by the Accountant General, Nagpur-2, as the case may be. The State Project Directorate is funded by the Central Government
