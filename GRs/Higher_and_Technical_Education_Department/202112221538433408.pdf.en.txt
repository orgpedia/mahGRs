Extension of the period of Letter of Intent for setting up of Self Financing Aided University in the backdrop of COVID-19 pandemic.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Higher and Technical Education
Government Resolution No: Parikh-2021/ P.No.76/ Vishy-4
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Madam Cama Marg
Ministry, Mumbai: 400032.
Date is December 22, 2021.
Preamble: The revised guidelines have been issued vide Government Resolution dated 19th January, 2019 to provide for the establishment of self-financing universities in the State, to regulate their functioning and functioning and to make provisions for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto. Under the provisions of the said guidelines, Letter of Intent is issued to the Sponsoring Boards for setting up of self-financing universities.
The lockdown was imposed from March 25, 2020, due to the outbreak of COVID-19 across the country. Most of the lives were lost due to this disaster. Also, a large number of labourers have returned home during this period, which has adversely affected the construction business. In the aforesaid emergency situation, the Sponsoring Boards desirous of setting up a self-financing University have been granted extension (first or second) of the Letter of Intent or Letter of Intent as they have not been able to fulfil the terms and conditions of the Letter of Intent. It is under consideration of the government to extend the period of letter of intent given to such sponsoring bodies to set up the university.
Government Decision: As per the decision taken by the Cabinet in its meeting held on 08.12.2021, the Sponsoring Board whose Intent was operational during the period from March, 2020 to next one year due to non-fulfillment of the terms and conditions of the Intent Letter issued for setting up of the Self-Financed University due to the outbreak of COVID-19 pandemic or the Intent was operational during the period by giving first or second extension of the Intent Letter by paying the prescribed fee, is being compensated for a period of nine months.
If the Sponsor Board does not meet the terms and conditions of the Letter of Intent and submit a compliance report to the Government within the extended nine-month compensation period, the Letter of Intent of the Sponsor Board shall be deemed to be automatically cancelled forever without further extension.
