Introduction: National Service Scheme (NSS) is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme under which 20% of the total number of students enrolled in self-financing institutions are sanctioned by the Central Government every year for regular activities and special camps. Proposals of Universities for fresh affiliation and recognition of student strength in 2022-23 have been received and it was under consideration of the Government to allocate the total 71700 self-employed students of Maharashtra University/ Directorate/ Institution wise by sanctioning them the student strength from the sanctioned number of other Universities but less than the number of students physically enrolled in the previous year.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As mentioned in the above Preamble, in the year 2022-23, the number of 71700 self-employed students has been approved for National Service Scheme regular activities and the number of students has been allocated as shown in the following table, category wise i.e. 11.8 percent for SCs, 9.4 percent for STs and 78.8 percent for others, 8460 for SCs, 6740 for STs and 56500 for others, total 71700 for regular activities and 35850 for special camps (50% of the sanctioned number of students for regular activities) University/ Directorate/ Institute wise:

The universities are to allocate grants at their level from the fees collected by the universities for this number of students. Every University/ Directorate/ Institution shall send a copy of the number of students allotted to them, category wise to all the colleges affiliated/ under them.
Universities should report the number of students actually enrolled by 30 th September, 2022.
