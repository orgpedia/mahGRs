Preamble: After the 3 years and 5 years Law courses in the Non-Government Law Colleges in the State were declared as Professional Courses vide Government Decision dated 11.03.2016, it was decided to conduct admission to these courses from 2016-17 through merit-based Law Centric Admission Process.
While registering for the Centralized Admission Process, it has been observed that in some of the colleges, Government approval orders are not available for starting an additional batch of Law course, but the said additional batch is approved by the concerned University from time to time and the Bar Council of India, New Delhi.
The Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune vide letter dated 17th January, 2019 has recommended regularization of the additional batches started in the colleges as mentioned below.
In view of the affiliation granted by the concerned University from time to time to the additional batches started on permanent non-grant basis in the Law College, the approval given by the Bar Council of India, New Delhi, the approval given by the Government from time to time to include these additional batches in the Centralized Admission Process and keeping in view the academic interest of the admitted students and their academic interest, and in accordance with the recommendation made by the Director, Higher Education, State of Maharashtra, Pune, under reference dated 17.01.2019, the Government was considering to grant regular approval on permanent non-grant basis to the additional batches started on permanent non-grant basis in such Law College as per the authority conferred by the Government under Section 109 (4) (d) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.
Government Decision: Affiliation of the additional batches started in the Law Colleges by the concerned University and approval of the Bar Council of India, New Delhi, approval of the Government from time to time to include these additional batches in the Centralized Admission Process and admission accordingly.

The College of Law in which the additional batches have been granted regular affiliation shall give an undertaking to the effect that since the said batches have been started on permanent non-grant basis, they will not demand grant in future under any circumstances. 100/- on non-judicial stamp paper to be submitted to their Divisional Joint Director.
The colleges are required to appoint faculty/ staff as per the norms prescribed by the concerned university and educational qualification prescribed by the University Grants Commission and all other necessary infrastructure should be provided and the same shall be ensured by the Joint Director of the department.
The college shall not, under any circumstances, grant admissions in excess of the sanctioned intake capacity.
4. The concerned Universities/ Colleges should follow up for conducting case-wise NAAC assessment/ re-assessment.
(3) The Divisional Joint Director shall, with the knowledge of the Government and the University, issue orders after periodically reviewing the additional batches sanctioned by the respective Universities and the Divisional Joint Director, if the additional batches show less than 50% of the sanctioned strength of students, the recognition of such additional batches shall be deemed to be cancelled.
