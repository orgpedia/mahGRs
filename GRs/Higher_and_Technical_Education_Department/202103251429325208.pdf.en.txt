At present, there are 9 government and 4 government aided engineering colleges in the state.
Available data shows that the intake capacity of undergraduate courses in these institutions is very low and a large number of students are willing to take admission for graduate courses in these institutions.
Admission in government engineering colleges in the state is low compared to government technical colleges. Considering the demand of the students for undergraduate courses in Government Engineering Colleges, in the given situation, the government technical colleges under the department are available.
Government Technical Colleges which have adequate facilities and space required for engineering colleges as per All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) standards and where there is a demand for engineering degree courses, at a minimum cost considering the resources, space, buildings and manpower.
The decision to convert Ratnagiri, Government Technological College Yavatmal, Government Technological College Dhule, Government Technological College Jalna, Government Technological College Latur (co-education of children) and Government Technological College Solapur into engineering colleges has been taken under the Government Decision, Higher and Technical Education Department No. Approval-2015/ (143/2015)/ Tanshi-5, dated 13/10/2016.
However, keeping in view the geographical features of Ratnagiri district and the various industries operating in the area, trained manpower with high level of technical skills should be available, for which Government Technological Institute, Ratnagiri should be established at Ratnagiri without upgradation.
