The Government has accorded approval for starting new institutions of non-aided pharmacy course in the State on permanent non-aided basis from the academic year 2022-23 as per the approval of Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi.

Read: Director, Technical Education 2/ NGC/ PCI/ Accreditation/ 2022/ 1161, dated 06.12.2022.
The proposal:
In accordance with the approval given by Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi and Director, Technical Education. As proposed under the letter dated 06.12.2022, the Government was considering grant of approval on permanent non-aided basis for starting new institutions of Bachelor and Diploma courses in Pharmacy as mentioned in the accompanying Annexure from the academic year 2022-23.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, the Government is granting approval for starting new institutions of Bachelor and Diploma courses in Pharmacy mentioned in the Annexure from the academic year 2022-23 on permanent non-aided basis subject to the following terms and conditions:
Terms and Conditions
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's intake capacity for this course will be as mentioned above.
(3) The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.

4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Diploma Courses shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(7) The Institute shall appoint a full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
9) Regarding the ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
Other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the University concerned shall also apply.
3. The institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, shall recover the same from the institutions before the final approval of the Director and the Director, Technical Education shall issue necessary orders within the next 10 days for the inclusion of the course as per the approval of this Government in the Centralized Admission Process.
