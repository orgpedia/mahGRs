Introduction: The day-to-day functioning of educational institutions has been disrupted since March, 2020 due to the lockdown announced across the country due to the outbreak of COVID-19 virus. However, the number of Covid-19 cases in the state is still on the rise. Therefore, it will be in the interest of the students to start the academic year of the students offline by taking general measures of Covid prevention in all the universities and affiliated colleges of the state. Accordingly, the matter of starting regular classes in all universities and affiliated colleges in the state was under the consideration of the government.
Circular: Regular classes in all non-agricultural universities, deemed universities, self-financing universities and colleges affiliated to them in the state are being approved to start from February 15, 2021.
The universities and affiliated colleges in the containment zones of the state, which come under the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Municipality or the District Collector and the Chairman, District Disaster Management Authority, in consultation with the concerned university, should take immediate action to take necessary measures to start regular classes by opening the universities and colleges, taking into account the local level prevalence of COVID-19 disease and the local situation and the availability of health infrastructure. In this case, the concerned universities should issue instructions to start colleges with the consent of the local authorities.
