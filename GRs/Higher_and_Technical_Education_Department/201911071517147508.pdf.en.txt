Introduction: It is not affiliated to the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) but is affiliated to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education (MSBTE). Gopalrao Gawande Multipurpose, Vidi, Ta. Vidarbha Institute of Technology, Wardha, Katol, Dist. 0816 (NON AICTE) has been granted Government approval vide Government Resolution No. 2006/ (162/06)/ TANSI-6, dated 6th October, 2006 to start various courses. However, in pursuance of the request made by the said Institute to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai, the Board has proposed to the Government to close the said Institute and deposit the fixed deposits for the course in the accounts of the Institute as per the above letter No. 1 and 2.
Government Decision: The Government is giving its approval to close the courses of the following listed institutions which are not under the purview of All India Council for Technical Education but are under the purview of Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education subject to the following terms and conditions.

1) The institute will be required to hand over all the documents related to the courses to be closed in this institute, such as marksheets, answer sheets, result sheets, addresses of students, fee receipts, etc. to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education.
(2) The Head of the Institution shall be responsible for all matters which are sub judice in respect of service and other matters of the faculty/ staff appointed for the concerned course.
The Company will be responsible for any legal issues arising during the period.
(3) The head of the institution shall be bound to pay to the Government immediately the dues of the Government from the institution, if any, are still outstanding or if any dues arise to the Government from the institution in future.
(4) It shall be the sole responsibility of the Institute to repay the loans taken by the Institute from the Government or any financial institution in respect of the immovable property required for the conduct of the said course in the Institute.
5) After the closure of the relevant courses in the institution, it will be mandatory for the institutions to make alternative arrangements for the admitted students and former students of the institution if they want to appear for the examination.
(6) The institution shall be bound to pay the salary, remuneration and other financial payments due to the faculty and staff of the institution appointed for the course concerned.
7) The next course of action for transferring the fixed deposits deposited by the respective institutions with the government for starting the above courses should be done by the Board.
