Introduction: Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan, a centrally sponsored scheme, has been launched in 2013 with the objective of providing policy funding to higher education institutions in the state. The state government The RUSA scheme was implemented in the state as per the government decision of 20 May 2015. The first phase of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) has been implemented in 2013 and the second phase in 2018. Now, the next phase of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) has been launched as Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (PM USHA) on the lines of National Education Policy (NEP). Guidelines for Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan Scheme. It was released on 24 June 2023. It has a 60:40 ratio of the Centre B state's contribution to the fund. The Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (PM-USHA) scheme comprises a total of 5 components.
The Project Sanctioning Board of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan / Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan, in its first meetings held on 17th and 18th January, 2024, approved the implementation of Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan component no. 1 Multi-Disciplinary Education and Research University (MERU) comprising 4 universities in the state of Maharashtra with an investment of G.200, 00 crores each and Component No. 2- Grants to Strengthen Universities (Accredited & Unaccredited Universities) to 7 Universities in the State of Maharashtra at a cost of Rs. 20.00 crore has been sanctioned.
