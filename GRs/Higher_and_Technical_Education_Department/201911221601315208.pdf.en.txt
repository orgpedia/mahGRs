Circular:
The Government vide its Order dated 18th November, 2019 issued vide Order No.2 above of the Revenue and Forest Department, has decided to provide special rate of relief and concessions to the affected farmers in 325 taluks of 34 districts of the State due to untimely rains in the month of October-November, 2019 due to cyclone Kyarr and Maha. Through this decision, the government has announced various concessions to the affected farmers, one of which is" waiver of school and college examination fees for the children of farmers who have suffered losses." In this regard, the concerned administrative departments have been directed to issue detailed orders as per the need and allocate necessary funds for the same. Accordingly, the following orders are being issued to all the universities as well as the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education to provide benefits as per the decision of the Government of Revenue and Forest Department:
As per the decision of the Government of the Department of Revenue and Forest dated November 18, 2019, the examination fee should be waived for the current academic year for the students whose parents have enrolled for courses under the Directorate of Technical Education in the academic year 2019-20, who have suffered crop and perennial crop damage in 325 taluks of 34 districts of the state.
2) The examination fee should be refunded by the concerned institution to the students from whom the examination fee has been charged till the date of this government circular.

3) The benefit of this concession will be limited to students belonging to the economically weaker sections. Since 50% of the examination fee is given as scholarship to the students eligible for scholarship under Rajarshi Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Tuition Fee Scholarship Scheme, only 50% of the examination fee should be waived off under this scheme. The remaining students with an annual family income up to Rs. 8.00 lakhs should be given 100% waiver of examination fee.
4) It shall be the responsibility of the institution to receive the application from the student for waiver of examination fee on this scheme, to qualify the student for this waiver and accordingly demand the examination fee from the University/ Board.
5) The cost for this should be borne by all the concerned universities as well as the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education from its own pool of funds.
6) These orders will also be applicable to autonomous bodies.
