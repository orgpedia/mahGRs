The proposal:
Yashwantrao Chavan Maharashtra Open University, Nashik University was established by the Maharashtra Legislative Council Act No. 20 respectively. 01.07.1989. The University is recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) and has jurisdiction over the entire state of Maharashtra. The university has 8 regional centres located at Amravati, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Mumbai, Nashik, Pune, Kolhapur and Nanded. 1600 study centers are controlled by 8 departmental centers of the university.
About 6 million students study at the university. The University offers various courses, programmes and programmes.
In view of the efforts of the University to provide higher education to all the deprived sections of the society, it is proposed to start a new Sub-Centre of the University in future.
In order to control the study centres of the university and to provide study materials in the form of books to the students taking admission in various courses in the university, it was proposed to establish the sub-centres of the university for two districts of Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg under the Kolhapur division with the aim of providing study materials to the study centres under the sub-centres through the sub-centres in a quick and timely manner.
