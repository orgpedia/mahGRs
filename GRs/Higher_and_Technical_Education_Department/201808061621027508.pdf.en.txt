Introduction: Council of Architecture, New Delhi has referred to Reference No. In accordance with the approval given vide letter dated 01-03, the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai vide Reference No. As per the letter dated 04.04.2014, it is proposed to approve the increase in the intake capacity of the course in 03 Vastu Shastra Institutions.
As per the approval given by Council of Architecture, New Delhi, the matter of approval of increase in intake capacity of the course in the Institute of Architecture as indicated in Annexure A attached was under consideration of the Government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval given by the Council of Architecture, New Delhi, the Government is granting approval to increase the intake capacity of the course in the Institute of Architecture as indicated in Annexure A attached herewith subject to the following terms and conditions from the academic year 2018-19.
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the Council of Architecture in this regard as well as prescribed under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The eligibility criteria for the course will be as mentioned in the admission prospectus.

(3) The Institution shall enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the Council of Architecture, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4) For the affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra State University Act, 1994.
(5) The institution concerned shall meet the other legal requirements by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
6) Admission to the said Degree Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the institutions charge more than the tuition fee prescribed by the Tuition Fee Committee, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
8) The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teachers and other staff.
9) The books, periodicals required by the library in the institution must be purchased in accordance with the standards of the Council of Architecture. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also students are provided with all educational facilities according to the standards of the Council of Architecture (e.g.
laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
10) In the context of ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
11) If the institute wants to close the course, it can be closed in a phased manner with the approval of the government.
12) Apart from this, other terms and conditions set by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the concerned university will remain applicable.
13) The institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, shall recover the same before final approval by the Director.
