Introduction: Classes 5 to 12 in rural areas and classes 8 to 12 in urban areas of the state. It was launched on October 4, 2021. College students are over the age of 18 and most students are likely vaccinated. Meanwhile, the number of Covid-19 cases in the state is also on the rise. Therefore, all universities and affiliated colleges in the state should take general measures for Covid prevention for students.
It will be in the interest of the students to start regular classes in the offline mode. Therefore, the matter of starting regular classes in all universities and affiliated colleges in the state was under the consideration of the government.
Government Circular: 1. Regular classes in all non-agricultural universities, deemed universities, self-financing universities and colleges affiliated to them in the state are being approved from October 20, 2021. However, the decision to start classes of universities/ colleges with 50 per cent or more capacity shall be taken by the universities at their level in consultation with the local authorities.
Students above 18 years of age who have taken both doses of the COVID-19 vaccine will be able to attend universities and colleges physically.
3. Universities and affiliated colleges falling in containment zones, which come under the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation/ Municipality or Collector and Chairman, District Disaster Management Authority, to contain the spread of COVID-19.
Considering the local level outbreak and local conditions, planning of containment zones and availability of health infrastructure, the decision to start universities and colleges should be considered and taken accordingly.

Online facilities should be provided to the students who are not able to attend the college physically.
Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune and Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai to review and take necessary action regarding the phased reopening of hostels.
For students who have not taken the COVID-19 vaccine, the university should complete the vaccination on priority by conducting a special campaign for vaccination in coordination with the local district administration with the help of the heads of the institutions/ principals of the colleges concerned. Teachers and non-teaching staff of the university/ college should also be vaccinated on priority.
