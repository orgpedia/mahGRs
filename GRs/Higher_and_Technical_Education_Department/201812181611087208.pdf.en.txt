Section 79 of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016 provides for the constitution of a Grievance Redressal Committee in every university, other than an institution managed and run by the State Government, the Central Government or a local authority, to deal with all kinds of grievances of the faculty and other staff of the university, affiliated and autonomous colleges and recognized institutions, other than grievances against the State Government and its officials, and which do not fall within the jurisdiction of the University and College Tribunal. However, in the absence of such Grievance Redressal Committees to deal with the grievances of the teaching and non-teaching staff in the offices/ colleges/ institutions under the Directorate of Higher Education, the number of court cases is increasing. While giving the decision in the petition no. 11613/2014 and 2527/2017 filed in the High Court, Mumbai, Shri. The court has directed to set up a grievance redressal mechanism for redressal of grievances of teachers and other staff. In view of this, a Grievance Redressal Committee is being constituted to deal with the grievances of the non-teaching staff in Government offices and Government colleges/ institutions under the Directorate of Higher Education as follows:
Government Decision: Grievance Redressal Committees are being constituted as follows to deal with the grievances of the teaching and non-teaching staff in the Government offices and Government Colleges/ Institutions under the Directorate of Higher Education.

(2) The Chairman of the Committee shall have the power to summon other officers to attend the hearing as invited members as may be required in addition to the above members.
After giving reasonable opportunity to both the parties to be heard, the Grievance Redressal Committee shall, as far as practicable, hear and dispose of the complaint in accordance with law within a period of 3 months from the date of filing of the complaint. In giving such a judgment, the policy of the government, the relevant act/ notification, the government decision/ circular/ order, judicial decisions related to the subject matter should be taken into consideration.
Separate instructions should be issued by the Director of Higher Education, Pune regarding the functioning of the Grievance Redressal Committee.
The Class-3 and Class-4 employees aggrieved by the decision of the Grievance Redressal Committee may file an appeal to the Director, Higher Education, Pune and the Class-1 and Class-2 officers/ teachers to the Secretary, Department of Higher and Technical Education, Ministry of Education, Mumbai within thirty days from the date of receipt of the decision of the Grievance Redressal Committee.
