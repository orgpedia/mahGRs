Preamble: The Director, Technical Education, has submitted a proposal to the Government under a letter of reference for grant of recognition to the Unaided Engineering, HMCT, MBA/ MMS, MCA and PGDM courses in the State, which were earlier recognized as second-term and part-time courses for the academic year 2019-20 and earlier, as regular courses from the academic year 2020-21, based on the decision of the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi. Approval was under consideration.
Government Decision: The Government is pleased to approve the implementation of the Unaided Engineering, HMCT, MBA/ MMS, MCA and PGDM Degree, Post Graduate Degree and Post Graduate Diploma Courses in the State recognized as Second Term and Part Time Courses from the academic year 2019-20 and earlier as per the approval of All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, as a regular course from the academic year 2020-21 as mentioned in Annexure-A and Annexure-B, subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983 of the Department. The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be as specified in Annexure-A and Annexure-B.

(3) The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. as well as related
Completion of documents and formalities will be done satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Post Graduate Degree/ Diploma Courses shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(7) The Institute shall appoint a full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules.
Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, all the students are trained as per the standards of the All India Council for Technical Education.

9) Regarding the ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, will have to be strictly implemented.
