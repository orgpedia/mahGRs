GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, with effect from April, 2016, the Central shareholding in the National Service Scheme (NSS) has become 100% and the scheme has become centrally administered. A separate savings account has been opened in the bank in which funds have been received from the Centre through ECS. According to the above letter of the Central Government, the funds have been deposited in the separate savings account of the National Service Scheme without directly going to the state. As per the decision of the government, this fund is distributed to the universities for the programs of the National Service Scheme. The release of funds for disbursement of grants-in-aid (conditional) to the respective Universities for the year 2016-17 (final instalment for all categories) and 2017-18 (first instalment for all categories) from the ICICI Bank savings account of National Service Scheme as mentioned in Annexure A and B is being approved under this Government decision.
Due to non-receipt of Utilization Certificates for the year 2016-17 from 7 Universities (Names included in Annexure B) out of 26 Universities/ Directorates, the remaining 19 Universities/ Directorates excluding the said Universities have been provided with funds for regular programme in the form of final instalment for the year 2016-17 (all categories) and for the year 2017-18 (1st instalment for all categories 77% Central share) amounting to Rs. 5,54,71, 308/- and Rs. 5, 03, 18, 630/- aggregating to Rs. 10,57,89, 938/- (Rupees Ten Crore Fifty Seven Lakh Ninety Nine Thousand Nine Hundred and Thirty Eight only) is being approved for disbursement of this grant. Universities having balance of funds for 2016-17 are being allowed to disburse funds by adjusting the same from the amount due for 2017-18 (with details of the amount due to the Universities).
