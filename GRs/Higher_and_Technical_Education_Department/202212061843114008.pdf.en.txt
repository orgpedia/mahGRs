the prelude
To make India a knowledge superpower, to provide necessary skills and knowledge to the students and to overcome the shortage of manpower in the field of science, technology, education and industry, to provide facilities of quality education, innovative education and research to the population of India, the Government of India has implemented the New National Education Policy-2020. The Task Force Group was constituted under the Chairmanship of Dr. Raghunath Mashelkar, Former Director General, Council of Scientific and Industrial Research, New Delhi vide Government Resolution dated 16.10.2020 to study the National Education Policy-2020 in the State and submit a report to the State Government. The group gave the task. The recommendations in the report submitted to the Government on 30th June, 2021 have been categorized into three stages considering their urgency, impact, ease of implementation, investment and funding requirement, priority in action, and are as follows:
(iii) Programmes with medium/ long-term funding requirements; (ii) Programmes with medium/ long-term funding requirements; (iii) Programmes with long-term funding requirements.
As per the approval of the Cabinet in its meeting held on 27 th January, 2022, as recommended by Dr. Mashelkar Committee, (i) to plan and prepare a roadmap for shifting from the existing 3 year curriculum to 4 year curriculum, (ii) to prepare a uniform academic curriculum, (iii) to use constructive pedagogy instead of generic pedagogy, (iv) to prepare a personalized learning roadmap for the holistic development of students. Pursuant to the Government Decision dated 26.04.2022

The committee was headed by Dr Ravindra Kulkarni, former Pro-Vice-Chancellor, University of Mumbai. The committee has submitted its report to the government. Further, it has been communicated to all the concerned Universities vide letter dated 20th October, 2022 and 4th November, 2022 that the Universities may take a decision on immediate implementation of this recommendation after obtaining approval from the prescribed authorities of the University. In view of this, the Government was contemplating to issue directions to all non-agricultural universities, deemed universities, self-financing universities and group universities and all educational institutions for effective and uniform implementation of National Education Policy-2020 from 2023-24.
The government's decision:
In pursuance of the recommendations of the report submitted by the Committee headed by Dr. Ravindra Kulkarni, Former Pro-Vice Chancellor, University of Mumbai, Mumbai constituted for the above mentioned matters under the National Education Policy, 2020, instructions are being issued in the Annexure attached and the same shall be implemented for all courses from the year 2023-24.
These directions are being issued in accordance with the provisions of Section 5 (81) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.
