The proposal:
The Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai has submitted a proposal to the Government under its reference letter, pursuant to the approval of the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, to reduce the intake capacity of the existing undergraduate pharmacy course in the unaided institution in the State from the academic year 2020-21. The proposal had been approved by the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, the Government hereby grants approval to reduce the intake capacity of the existing Diploma Courses in the existing Degree Pharmacy Courses in the permanent unaided institution from the academic year 2020-21 subject to the following conditions as mentioned in Annexure A.
1) The grant will be on a grant-in-aid basis.
In this case, the institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be limited as mentioned against their name in the prospectus.
3. The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4. The concerned institution shall complete other legal formalities by depositing the requisite processing fee and accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai.
