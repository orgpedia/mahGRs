The filmmakers have been demanding that the premises of the historic buildings like the Arts College, Sir J. G. College of Applied Arts and the Dean's Bungalow be made available on lease for shooting of films/ short films. The rates, terms and conditions of the said filming have been prescribed vide Government Resolution dated 19th July, 2011 at Reference No. 1. About 10 years have passed since the date of this painting.
The matter of revising the rates, terms and conditions of filming was under the consideration of the government, on which the following decision has been taken.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Approval is being given to levy revised rates for shooting of films/ short films in the premises of Sir J. G. Arts College, Mumbai subject to the following terms and conditions.

Terms and Conditions: 1) Only open area will be available for rent for shooting. Under no circumstances will filming be allowed in the interior of the building or the use of equipment inside the building.

3) The Principal/ Dean of the concerned college shall be required to enter into a written agreement with the film/ short film producer jointly with all the terms and conditions.
4) The amount equal to the total rent charged for the shooting must be deposited as a reserve amount with the office of the Director, Directorate of Arts, 5 days before the shooting.
5) In case of extension of time, Rs. 25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five Thousand only).
6) The time period for which the shooting is approved cannot be changed. However, if there is a demand from the filmmaker regarding the change in the duration of the shooting, a decision in this regard will be taken by the Director, Directorate of Arts, considering the availability. To change this period, Rs. An additional amount of Rs. 3,00,000/- will be charged.
7) 10% of the reserve amount will be deducted if the shooting is completely canceled.
The producer of the film/ short film should ensure that the students, teachers or staff of the college do not face any kind of problem while shooting.
9) No criticism of the government's goal policies can be made while filming.
10) The name of the college/ institution/ place/ Vastu cannot be shown in the photograph.
11) It will be mandatory to shoot in the area planned for filming.
No heavy stands, racks or strong lights and any other material which may cause damage to the structure shall be placed adjacent to any structure/ building for the purpose of filming.
The film/ short film maker shall ensure that the use of electric lights, heavy stands, racks and other materials used during the shooting does not cause damage/ damage to the architecture/ building, artwork, park, other movable and immovable objects.
13) The Principal/ Dean, Head of the Office of the concerned colleges should monitor and monitor that no other place will be used for filming.

14) If the duration of the shooting is up to 7 days, it will be approved by the level of Director, Directorate of Arts. Also, if the duration of the shooting is more than 7 days, it will be mandatory to take prior approval from the government.
In case of damage or destruction of any property, Vastu, garden and other movable and immovable property in the premises during the shooting, an amount equal to 20% of the amount deposited by the film/ short film maker or the actual loss, whichever is higher, shall be deducted.
16) 60% of the amount collected in connection with the shooting will be deposited in the government account. 10% of the amount will be deposited in the personal provident fund account of the art college in which the shooting took place. The remaining 30% of the amount and the entire reserve amount will be deposited in the self-financing account of the Directorate of Arts.
17) In case of extreme exceptional circumstances, it will be mandatory to obtain prior permission from the government if filming is to be allowed in the interior areas of the building, taking into account the requirements. Under this, the rent and reserve amount will be charged at double the rate for filming.
This is being issued as per the concurrence received under the Informal Reference No. 435/2022/ Expenditure 5, dated 30.03.2022 of the Department of Government Finance.
