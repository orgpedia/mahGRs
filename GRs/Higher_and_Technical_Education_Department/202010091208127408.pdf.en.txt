The proposal:
Under the authority conferred under Section 23 of the Act at Reference No. 1, the" Maharashtra Unaided Private Professional Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admission to Full-time Professional Law Undergraduate Courses) Rules, 2017" under Notification at Reference No. 2 was published in the Gazette. Rule 16 of the said rule has made provisions regarding change of course or institution after the first year.
As per letter No. 3 under reference, Director Higher Education, Pune has proposed to guide the candidates of professional educational institutions outside the purview of the Act regarding the change in the University of Mumbai-run Law College under the purview of the Act for the second year in the academic year 2020-21. Accordingly, the government was contemplating to take action in the matter.
The government's decision:
In accordance with the provisions of Rule 16 of the Maharashtra Unaided Private Professional Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admission to Full-time Professional Law Undergraduate Courses) Rules, 2017, the following steps are being taken for transfer of institution to the University of Mumbai Law Academy, Mumbai for the second year in the academic year 2020-21:
(a) of the applications received for the transfer of the said college, the candidates who qualify as per the provisions of Rule 16 of the Maharashtra Unaided Private Professional Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admission to Full-time Professional Law Undergraduate Courses) Rules, 2017, shall be first transferred to the college,
(b) if the seat still remains vacant, the applications received from the candidates of the institution of professional education outside the purview of the Act in accordance with the provisions of Rule 16 (4) shall be dealt with as follows, for admission to the vacant seats as the said college is unaided:
(1) The candidate concerned should have appeared in the Common Entrance Test (CET) of the Law Course conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell.

(2) The candidate concerned shall not have less than the marks obtained in the Common Entrance Test by the last of the candidates admitted to the said college in the relevant academic year (in the category to which the candidate belongs).
(3) All the necessary documents/ permissions for the transfer of the college should have been fulfilled.
(4) The NAAC assessment of the college from which the candidate is to come shall not be less than the NAAC assessment of the said college. If the college has not been assessed, the NAAC assessment of the university should be taken into consideration.
