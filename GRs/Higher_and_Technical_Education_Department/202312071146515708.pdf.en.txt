The Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019
The Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019 provides for appropriate welfare measures to be taken by the Government to protect the rights and interests of transgenders and to facilitate their access to welfare schemes framed by the Government. There is such a provision. Section 2 (k) of the Act prescribes the definition of a transgender person and Section 3 prescribes the provisions regarding authentication of identity of a transgender person.
National Education Policy, 2020 The review meeting was held on Tuesday, December 05, 2023 under the chairmanship of Minister, Higher and Technical Education. Vice-Chancellors of the universities were also present at the meeting. In the meeting, the issue of educational reform for transgenders was discussed with the Vice-Chancellor of Akashi University. All non-agricultural universities were urged to take the lead in providing free higher education to transgender students in order to bring them into the mainstream of education, taking into account the difficulties, financial problems faced by them in pursuing higher education. In response to this appeal, all the Vice Chancellors have unanimously accepted this decision. Besides, all the affiliated government, non-government, aided and unaided technical institutions under the Higher and Technical Education Department will provide free technical education to the students of the third gender. Minister of Higher and Technical Education. However, in line with the decisions taken in the said meeting and the implementation of the National Education Policy-2020 on inclusive education system and equity, the matter of providing educational fee concession to transgender students for higher education courses under this department is under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: In order to provide equal education to transgender students by providing them opportunities for higher education and bringing them into the stream of higher education, in order to provide free higher education to all types of transgender students under the Department of Higher and Technical Education, the universities are directed to pay the educational fees of transgender students studying in the university as well as in the colleges affiliated to them from the university's own fund, according to the provisions of Section 8 (7) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.
Also, the educational fees of the transgender students studying in all the affiliated government, non-government aided and unaided technical institutions under the Higher and Technical Education Department should be paid from the self-fund of Maharashtra State Technical Education Board, Mumbai.
