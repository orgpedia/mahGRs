Introduction: Department of Higher and Technical Education 25.03.2011, Dy. 17.06.2015 and dt. As per the Government decisions dated 17.03.2021, non-agriculture universities and affiliated non-government aided colleges/ colleges of Technology, Engineering, Pharmacy, Vastu Shastra/ Technicians, Non-government aided institutions under Directorate of Technical Education/ Directorate of Arts, Deemed to be Government aided universities will be allowed to open. Defined Contribution Pension Scheme/ National Pension System has been introduced for employees appointed on or after 01.11.2005. The benefits of Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, Maharashtra Civil Services (Depreciation of Pension) Rules, 1984 and General Provident Fund are not admissible to the employees who are covered under Defined Contribution Pension Scheme/ National Pension System.

Government Resolution No. Parichay-2304/ PR No. 87/ Special-1 2. Pursuant to the Government Decision dated 31.03.2023. As per the notification dated 20.04.2023, the defined contribution pension scheme/ National Pension System in the service of the State Government has been made admissible to the family of the employee who dies during the service, family pension and death gratuity and sickness pension and retirement gratuity to the retired employee and retirement gratuity to the employees retiring from service. " The provisions of the above Government decision shall continue to apply to the employees of Zilla Parishads, recognised and aided non-government primary and secondary schools, non-agriculture universities and affiliated recognised and aided non-government colleges and agricultural universities and similar aided institutions, with appropriate modifications. However, separate orders in this regard shall be issued by the concerned Ministries/ Administrative Departments at their level," it said.
In view of the aforesaid directions of the Department of Higher and Technical Education, orders regarding the applicability of the defined contribution pension scheme/ National Pension System to the family of the employee, family pension and death stipend in case of death during the service period of the employee appointed to the post of grantee in non-agricultural universities under the Department of Higher and Technical Education and affiliated non-government aided colleges/ colleges/ colleges of Technology, Engineering, Pharmacy, Vastu Shastra/ Technological, Directorate of Technical Education/ Non-government aided institutions under the Directorate of Arts and Deemed to be Government Universities.
Government decision: An employee who is appointed to a Government aided post in non-agriculture universities and affiliated non-Government aided colleges/ colleges of Technology, Engineering, Pharmacy, Vastu Shastra/ Technicians, Non-Government aided institutions under the Directorate of Technical Education/ Directorate of Arts, Government aided deemed universities, and is covered under the defined contribution pension scheme/ National Pension System, on or after 1.11.2005, (a) in case of death in service, family pension and death gratuity to his family, (b) sickness pension and retirement gratuity to the sick retired employee. Accordingly, the family pensioner and sickness pensioner will be entitled to family pension/ sickness pension as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982.
(c) Retirement Gratuity is also being applied to the employees retiring from service.
The provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982 shall continue to be applicable for the purpose of retirement gratuity and death gratuity to be provided in accordance with the above decision.

3. d. The families of the employees who died between 01.11.2005 and the date of this decision will have to submit the option in Form-3 along with the said Government decision to the Head of the Office to get the death gratuity, family pension arrears and regular family pension.
Accordingly, the benefit of family pension will be admissible to the family pensioner on the condition that the amount of ex-gratia grant received by him and the amount of government contribution under the defined pension scheme/ National Pension System is adjusted with interest/ dividend.
After this amount is adjusted, the family pension will be admissible to the family pensioner on a regular basis.
In the event of death or sickness of the employee in service, the family pension or sickness pension as per the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, or the accumulated amount deposited in his Permanent Pension Account Number (PRAN) under the National Pension System, as per the orders issued by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) from time to time, should be submitted to the Head of Office in Form-2 along with the family or his receipt, respectively.
The employees who submit the option of making family pension or sickness pension admissible as per the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, are required to submit the details of the family as per Sample-1.
All employees covered by the National Pension System shall receive the benefit of the above-mentioned pension scheme within one month from the date of the decision of the Government. 4 options are required.
Further, the personnel to be appointed in the service will be required to submit the options as above within 8 days of being appointed in the service.
Detailed instructions on the procedure in this regard shall be issued separately.
