The proposal:
The Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016 has come into force with effect from 01.03.2017. Considering the increasing pass percentage in higher secondary schools and the inclination of students towards a particular course, no student should be deprived of the desired higher education, Section 109 (8) of the Act provides for expeditious recognition of new batches in addition to specified faculties in exceptional circumstances. In the academic year 2021-22, the non-agricultural universities of the state had submitted proposals to the government for additional new batches of specific faculties. Reference No. 1 The inspection was carried out in accordance with the norms laid down by the government here. After this examination, the specific universities that have qualified among the proposals recommended to the government
Proposals for additional new batches of faculty were under consideration of the government for expeditious approval. The government has taken the following decisions:
The government's decision:
In the academic year 2021-22, new batches of faculty in addition to the specific faculty who have qualified out of the proposals recommended to the Government by the non-agricultural Universities in the State are being sanctioned on permanent non-grant basis to the existing colleges as per the powers vested in the Government under Section 109 (8) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016, subject to the following terms and conditions as mentioned in the statement:

The additional batch approved in this way will continue with the natural growth. However, if such a batch does not receive regular government recognition in the next academic year, the student will not be admitted to the first year in this batch. For such regular additional batch for the next academic year, it will be necessary to submit a fresh application and get government approval as per the criteria and procedures prescribed by the government.
7. Accelerated additional batch If a student is not admitted in the batch for the academic year for which it is sanctioned and the batch is not started due to some other reasons, the recognition of such batch shall be deemed to be automatically cancelled.
It should be ensured that 50% of the sanctioned strength of students is admitted in the fast track additional batch which has been sanctioned.
The students will be admitted to such fast-track additional batch only through the procedure prescribed by the government for admitting students to the concerned faculty.
Also, as per the prevailing policy of the Government, the sanction of additional fast track platoon will always be on non-subsidised basis.
The colleges which have provisionally made arrangements for the additional batch approved by the fast track, an undertaking is attached with the application that they will meet the criteria prescribed by the Government for the regular batch within the next 6 months, after fulfilling these criteria within the next 6 months, they will be required to submit the certificate of completion to the Government through the University.
The Universities shall not initiate the process of granting affiliation without first going through the Government approval orders and obtaining the certificate in Condition No. 2 of the Divisional Joint Director before taking further action as may be required for grant of affiliation. The universities should send the compliance report regarding the affiliation to the Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune and the concerned Divisional Joint Director, Higher Education.
The Divisional Joint Director shall, with the knowledge of the Government and the University, take orders to consider the recognition of such additional batches as cancelled if 50% or less of the sanctioned strength of students are found in such additional batches after periodically reviewing the additional batches approved by the respective Universities and Divisional Joint Directors expeditiously.
