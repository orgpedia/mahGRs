The following factors have been taken into consideration for setting up the above Centres of Excellence: 1) NBA rating of the disciplines in which the Centre of Excellence is to be set up.
2) The Principal Investigator of the subject in which the Centre of Excellence is to be established and other faculty of the subject in the institution.
3) Available space for setting up of Centre of Excellence.
MoU between Industry/ Research Institute on the subject in which the Centre of Excellence is to be established.
Mentor Industry/ Mentor Research Institute for the proposed institution in the subject in which the Centre of Excellence is to be established.
6) Revised specifications and prices of equipment required for setting up the Centre of Excellence.
Financial involvement of external Industry/ Research Institute in the Centre of Excellence.
8) Advisory Committee for the Centre of Excellence at the Institute level, including industry experts and faculty from reputed institutions.
Procurement Plan/ Process for the Centre of Excellence.
The modalities for establishment and implementation of the Centre of Excellence in the above mentioned 10 institutions for 4 years from FY 2023-24 to 2026-27 are as follows: 1) Procurement: a) In the institutions where the same type of machinery is to be procured, such procurement process shall be carried out at the Directorate of Technical Education level in a coordinated manner.
(b) The procurement process of the institutions in which different types of machinery are to be procured shall be carried out at the respective institution level.
(e) in respect of the financial powers of the Finance Department in respect of procurement, in the course of carrying out the procurement process at both the levels, subject to the provisions of Sec. With reference to the Financial Powers and Procurement Policy of the Department of Industry, Energy and Labor in the Government Decision of 17/04/2015, 01/12/2016 and the e-Governance Policy of the General Administration Department. The procurement process will be carried out through GeM portal as per the procedure laid down in the order dated 23/09/2011.
Planning and implementation of the project: Two committees are being constituted for planning, implementation and monitoring of the project.

Funds will be provided by the State Government to run the Centre of Excellence for the first four years from its inception. However, after a period of 4 years, the institution will be required to run the Centre of Excellence on a self-financed basis.
Financial implication: Rs. 13.81 crore for recurring expenditure and Rs. 39.85 crore. The government will bear a financial burden of Rs 53.66 crore.
The recurring expenditure will be towards training of students, staff and technicians associated with the industry, research fellowships, honorarium of students/ experts/ faculty and expenses for national/ international travel, conducting workshops/ conferences and registration fees for patents, copyrights and trademarks, etc.
The non-recurring expenditure will be for the purpose of computer software and hardware, software renovation, purchase of machinery, etc. The recurring and non-recurring expenditure will be phased over 4 years. 53.66 crore in the financial year 2023-24. 20.00 crore.
The recurring/ non-recurring expenditure for setting up of these 10 Centres of Excellence is being funded in a phased manner subject to the following conditions: 1. More than one Centre of Excellence shall not be set up in the same institution/ college.
2. Procurement for this centre of excellence should be done in the prescribed manner.
(3) No demand for more expenditure than that mentioned in the said Government Resolution shall be made.
The proposed Centres of Excellence, if established, should be reviewed every four years for achievement of the objectives.
No regular posts will be created for setting up of these Centres of Excellence, the manpower required for these Centres should be met from the existing teaching and equivalent posts.
