Preamble: In pursuance of the above decisions dated 10th June, 2019 and 26th June, 2019, the Government have approved the allotment of new seats in the academic year 2019-2020 to the existing seats in the educational institutions of OCI, PIO (Foreign Nationals/ Persons of Indian Origin)/ Children of Indian Workers in the Gulf Countries/ NRIs.
As per the above mentioned letter, the Director, Technical Education has submitted a proposal for recognition of seats of OCI, PIO (Foreign Nationals/ Persons of Indian Origin)/ Children of Indian Workers in the Gulf Countries/ NRI (Non-Resident Indians) in certain institutions recognized by the Pharmacy Council of India for the academic year 2019-2020. It was pending approval of the government.
Government Decision: In the academic year 2019-2020, seats in educational institutions in the Annexure with OCI, PIO (Foreign Nationals/ Persons of Indian Origin)/ Children of Indian Workers in the Gulf Countries/ NRI (Non Resident Indians) are being approved by the Government for the academic year 2019-2020 subject to the following terms and conditions as indicated against their names:

1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's eligibility for this course will remain as mentioned above.
(3) The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Post Graduate Degree Courses will be done as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(7) The Institute shall appoint a full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules.
Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
9) Regarding the ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
10) Apart from this, other terms and conditions set by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the concerned university will remain applicable.
