3) Maratha Reservation. In pursuance of the decision taken by the Cabinet Sub-Committee chaired by the Minister of Revenue, Relief and Rehabilitation, Public Works (Excluding Public Undertakings) in its meeting held on 31/10/2017 and 07/11/2017, the Government was considering to implement a scheme for extending hostel maintenance allowance to students belonging to economically weaker sections (excluding marginal farmers/ registered labourers) with an annual income up to Rs. 6.00 lakh.
Government Decision: The Government is approving the introduction of Dr. Panjabrao Deshmukh Hostel Allowance Extension Scheme from the academic year 2018-19 for the students belonging to economically weaker sections whose family (both parents together) has an annual income up to Rs. 6.00 lakh who take admission through centralized admission process for professional courses prescribed by the Government in recognized Government, Government aided, Government unaided and permanent unaided colleges/ technical institutions in the State. Under this scheme, eligible students are approved to pay a subsistence allowance of Rs. 10.000/- per annum for urban areas (all cities in Mumbai and Pune Metropolitan Region, Aurangabad, Nagpur etc.) and Rs. 8000/- for other cities/ rural areas. If an institution registered under the Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950, and the Societies Registration Act, 1860, is willing to run a hostel, the same rate of maintenance allowance should be paid by the students.
