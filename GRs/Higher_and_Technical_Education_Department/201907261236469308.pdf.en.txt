The proposal:
Government approval has been granted for the academic year 2019-2020 as per the list of approvals received for the academic year 2019-2020 for introduction of new courses in PGDM and PGDM institutions, enhancement of existing intake capacity, introduction of B.Pharmacy courses in Diploma Institutes of Pharmacy, restoration of earlier reduced intake capacity, merger of existing courses with other existing courses, etc.
The government's decision:
Siddhivinayak College of Pharmacy, Warora, Chandrapur, at Annexure B, Roll No.13 to the above mentioned Government Decision dated 10/6/2019, is being approved by the Government to start a new Bachelor of Pharmacy course with an intake of 60 for the academic year 2019-2020, subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's eligibility for this course will remain as mentioned above.
(3) The institution shall be required to sign an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi.

The formalities and documents will be completed satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Post Graduate Degree Courses will be done as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(7) The Institute shall appoint a full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students.
9) All educational facilities as per the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) standards. laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
10) In the context of ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, will have to be strictly implemented.
In the academic year 2019-20, since the Bachelor of Pharmacy course in this institution is not recognized by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, the institution should seek approval from the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi within 6 months.
12) In case of any court case arising in future without the approval of Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, the institution shall give an undertaking to the Directorate of Technical Education on a stamp paper of Rs.
If the institution does not get the approval of the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi within the stipulated time, the recognition of the state government will be automatically cancelled and admission will not be given to the institution for the academic year 2020-21. The Directorate of Technical Education will be responsible for this.
