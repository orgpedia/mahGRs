The proposal:
Rajarshi Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Tuition Fee Scholarship Scheme is implemented by the Department of Higher and Technical Education for eligible students from economically weaker sections. Under the Government Decision, Higher and Technical Education dated 7/10/2017, detailed orders have been issued regarding the expansion of the scope of benefits for eligible students of this scheme and the inclusion of new courses. The scheme is applicable to students belonging to Open Category and Economically Weaker Section (EWS) who take admission in professional/ non-professional courses through centralized admission process and whose family income is Rs. 8.00 lakh per annum and 50% of tuition fee and 50% of examination fee is paid as scholarship to eligible students.
Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Hostel Living Allowance Scheme has been implemented initially from the academic year 2016-17 and the scope of this scheme has been revised under the Government Decisions dated 7/10/2017 and 22/2/2018 of this Department. The scheme is applicable to students from the economically weaker section (EWS) category. Under this scheme, the children of marginal farmers and registered labourers are given subsistence allowance at the rate of Rs. 30,000/- per annum for Mumbai Metropolitan Region, Pune Metropolitan Region and Nagpur and Aurangabad Municipal Corporation areas and Rs. 20,000/- per annum for other urban/ rural areas if they are admitted to professional courses through centralized admission process. Apart from this, other eligible students are given subsistence allowance at the rate of Rs 10,000/- and Rs 8,000/- respectively.
The Maharashtra Act No. 62 of 2018, which came into force with effect from 30/11/2018, re-declared the socially and educationally backward classes to include the Maratha community. There is 12 per cent reservation in educational institutions. The Government has approved the implementation of Rajarshi Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Tuition Fee Scholarship Scheme for the students of Socially and Educationally Backward Classes from the academic year 2019-2020 under the Government Decision, Vimukt Jati, Nomadic Tribes and Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes, Welfare Department, No. CBC-2019/ PR. 108/ Mavak, dated 18/7/2019.
The Hon'ble Supreme Court in Special Leave Petition No. 15737/2019 and other petitions dated 9/9/2020 has granted interim stay on reservation for Socially and Educationally Backward Classes. Therefore, the General Administration Department has issued a Government Decision dated 9/10/2020 to provide various concessions to the socially and educationally backward classes.
An order in this regard has been sent to all the departments concerned.
