The decision to close down the private professional engineering and pharmacy diploma courses in the state from the academic year 2018-19, reducing the intake capacity of the courses, etc. is in line with the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Higher and Technical Education,
GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. APPROVAL-2018/ (P.S. 138/18) TANSHI-5
Mantralaya Extension Building, Madame Cama Road,
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Mumbai 400032.
The date: June 8, 2018.
Reference: Letter No.10/ NGP/ Approval/ 2018/577 dated 10.05.2018 of the Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai.
Preamble: The Director, Directorate of Technical Education has submitted a proposal to the Government through a reference letter in pursuance of the list received by the Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai vide e-mail dated 07.05.2018 and another list dated 14.05.2018 regarding approval of reduction in intake capacity and closure of courses in the institution of private professional engineering and pharmacy diploma courses in the State from the academic year 2018-19. The matter of reducing the intake capacity and giving approval to the institutions mentioned in Annexure A from A.No. 1 to 85 was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, the Government is granting approval for reduction in intake capacity and closure of courses as indicated against their names to the Private Professional Engineering and Pharmacy Diploma Courses Institutions listed in Annexure A attached as Annexure 1 to 85 from the academic year 2018-19 subject to the following terms and conditions.
The institution will be required to submit all the documents related to the courses to be closed in this institution, such as marksheets, answer sheets, results sheets, addresses of students, fee receipts, etc.
The Head of the Institution shall be responsible for all matters which are sub judice in respect of service and other matters of the faculty/ staff appointed for the concerned course.
In case of any court case in the future, the institution will be responsible for it.
3. It shall be incumbent upon the head of the institution to pay the arrears, if any, due to the Government from the institution.

4. The Institute shall be fully responsible for repayment of any loan taken by it from the Government or any financial institution in respect of immovable property required for the conduct of the said course in the Institute.
After the closure of the relevant courses in the institution, it will be mandatory for the institution to make alternative arrangements for the admitted students and former students of the institution if they want to sit for the examination.
The institution shall be bound to pay the salary, remuneration and other financial payments due to the faculty and staff of the institution appointed for the course concerned.
