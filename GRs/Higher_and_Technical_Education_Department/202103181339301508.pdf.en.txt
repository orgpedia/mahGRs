As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, with effect from April, 2016, the Central shareholding in National Service Scheme (NSS) has become 100% and the scheme has become centrally administered.

GOVERNMENT DECISION: As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, with effect from April, 2016, the Central shareholding in National Service Scheme (NSS) has become 100% and the scheme has become Centrally Sponsored. A separate savings account has been opened in the bank in which funds have been received from the Centre through ECS. According to the above letter of the central government, the funds have been deposited in the separate savings account of the National Service Scheme without directly going to the state. According to the government decision, this fund is distributed to the universities for the programs of the National Service Scheme. For the year 2020-2021 (1st instalment 75% Central Share for all categories), the funds for disbursement of Subsidiary Grants (conditional) to the respective Universities from ICICI Bank Savings Account of National Service Scheme as mentioned in Annexure A and B are being sanctioned as per the decision of the Government.
Out of 27 Universities/ Directorates, 14 Universities have been approved by the Department of Higher and Technical Education, Government Resolution No. Orders for disbursement of Subsidiary Grants Fund have been issued under 02/02/2021.
Submitted for approval to the remaining 09 Universities/ Directorates, excluding the above 13 Universities, to disburse the remaining funds by adjusting their balance at the end of the year 2019-20 from the amount due for the year 2020-21, (The details of the amount due to the Universities is given in Annexure A. ) is attached with Appendix A. 09 Universities/ Directorates are hereby approved to disburse a total amount of Rs. 1,28,75, 404/- (Rupees One Crore Twenty Eight Lakh Seventy Five Thousand Four Hundred and Forty Four only) as first instalment of 75% of the grant-in-aid for the year 2020-21 to all the students for regular programme amounting to Rs. 67,53,343/- (Rupees Sixty-Six Lakh Fifty-Three Thousand Three Hundred and Forty-Three only) and special camp programme amounting to Rs. 61,22,061/- (Rupees Sixty-One Lakh Twenty-two Thousand Sixty-One only).

The amount of grant-in-aid will be disbursed by ECS through Public Fund Distribution System (PFMS) to the Registrar/ Director/ Controller of the concerned University by the State National Service Scheme Officer (State Liaison Officer/ Officer on Special Duty) of the National Service Scheme with the approval of the Principal Secretary, Department of Higher and Technical Education. The Universities shall disburse the said grant amount to the colleges under their control through Public Fund Distribution System (PFMS) category wise and submit the same to the Government immediately with the signature of the Chartered Accountant along with the Assistant Grant Allocation Statement and Utilization Certificate letter. The University shall also submit to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai and to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-2, Nagpur and to this Department or its representative all accounts of grants under the National Service Scheme for inspection.
