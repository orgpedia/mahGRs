To meet the expenditure incurred in implementing the activities of the National Service Scheme, a regular programme at revised rates has been introduced with effect from 2010-11 with an outlay of Rs. 250/- and Special Camp Programme Rs. The scholarship amount is Rs.450/- per student per year. As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, the Central shareholding in the National Service Scheme has become 100% with effect from April, 2016 and the scheme has become centrally administered. For this purpose, a separate bank account has been opened in which funds were received from the Centre through ECS on 02.07.2018 and as per the decision of the Government, a proposal was under consideration to disburse these funds to the Universities for National Service Scheme programmes.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, with effect from April, 2016, the Central shareholding in the National Service Scheme (NSS) has become 100% and the scheme has become centrally administered. A separate savings account has been opened in the bank in which funds have been received from the Centre through ECS. According to the above letter of the Central Government, the funds have been deposited in the separate savings account of the National Service Scheme without directly going to the state. As per the decision of the government, this fund is distributed to the universities for the programs of the National Service Scheme. The release of funds for disbursement of grants-in-aid (conditional) to the respective Universities for the year 2017-18 (final instalment for all categories) and 2018-19 (first instalment for all categories) from the ICICI Bank Savings Account of National Service Scheme as mentioned in Annexure A and B is being approved under this Government decision.
Due to non-receipt of Utilization Certificates for the year 2017-18 from 2 Universities/ Directorates, the remaining 14 Universities/ Directorates excluding the said Universities have been allotted funds for the year 2017-18 (Final installment for all categories) and 2018-19 (First installment for all categories 78% Central share) amounting to Rs. 3,98,37, 267/- (Rupees Three Crore Eighty-nine Lakhs Seventy-two Thousand Two Hundred Sixty-six only) for regular programme and Rs. 3,82,66, 796/- (Rupees Three Crore Eighty-two Lakhs Sixty-seven Thousand Seven Hundred only) for special camp programme. Universities having balance of funds for the year 2017-18 are being allowed to disburse funds by adjusting the same from the dues of 2018-19 (Details of dues to Universities are given in Annexure A and B, Annexure Page 2 of Page 3).
