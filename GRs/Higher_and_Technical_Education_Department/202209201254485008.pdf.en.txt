Introduction: The Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India has implemented the National Education Policy-2020. Therefore, in order to implement the National Education Policy-2020 in the state, the policy will be studied and a report will be submitted to the state government. As per the Government decision dated 16th October, 2020, a Task Force Group was constituted under the Chairmanship of Dr. Raghunath Mashelkar, former Director General, Council of Scientific and Industrial Research, New Delhi.
The committee sent its report to the government. It was released on June 30, 2021. In order to facilitate the implementation of the policy in the State, the recommendations of the report have been categorized into three phases, taking into consideration factors such as their urgency, impact, ease of implementation, investment and funding requirement, priority in action, as follows: (i) Programme to be taken up immediately/ requiring minimum resources; (ii) Programme to be taken up in the medium term/ requiring medium resources; (iii) Programme to be taken up in the long term/ requiring large scale funding.
As per the recommendations of the Task Force Committee on National Education Policy, 2020, Discussed in the Cabinet meeting held on 27th January, 2022. A committee has been formed under the chairmanship of the Chief Minister.
In pursuance of the above decision, the National Education Policy, 2020 has been implemented. The government had constituted a committee under the chairmanship of the Chief Minister.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: To truly enable autonomy and excellence in the education sector in Maharashtra, an Empowered Group of Ministers can be set up to focus on all academic aspects from schools to skill development as well as higher and technical education. This is necessary to enable all educational institutions and departments at the state level to match the investments and resources, as envisaged in the National Education Policy, 2020, to succeed from pre-primary to higher education institutions. Accordingly, a Working Group on the implementation of the National Education Policy-2020 is being constituted as follows:

The mandate of the above Task Force will be as follows: (1) To act as an enabling financial mechanism for implementation of National Education Policy, 2020 in the State.
(2) Review from time to time the working of the committees to be constituted on various subjects as per the recommendations of the Dr. Mashelkar Committee and discuss the new changes to be made in accordance with the policy and give appropriate directions and guidance.
(3) The action taken/ to be taken by the Department in accordance with the recommendations in the reports of the above subject committees shall be reviewed from time to time.
(4) Policy discussions on changes to be made in higher education under the new education policy.
(5) The meeting of the said Working Group shall be held once in three months or as the case may be.
