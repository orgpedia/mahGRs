The proposal:
The procedure for starting short term vocational diploma courses under the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education from the academic year 2021-22 has been laid down under the Government Decision dated 03/05/2021 of the Department of Higher and Technical Education. Accordingly, the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education had issued a notification dated 25/05/2021 and dated 07/09/2021, inviting applications from interested institutions through online system.
The applications and the original documents along with the application received by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education as per the said notification were examined by the Document Verification Committee and the physical verification of the infrastructure in the eligible institution was done by the Expert Committee. Thereafter, the State Level Committee after verifying the reports of the Zonal Level Committee has submitted a proposal to the Government through the Board for starting new institutions, introducing new courses in the affiliated educational institutions, increasing/ decreasing the intake capacity of the courses and introducing new courses in place of the old courses. The matter was under consideration of the government.
The government's decision:
Starting from the academic year 2021-22, the institutions of short-term vocational diploma courses under the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education will be allowed to start new institutions as mentioned in Annexure-A, B and C.

1) The approval to be given to the government approved short-term courses under the board is the decision of the government. As per the Government Decision dated 21.5.1983, the courses are on permanent non-grant basis and no grant from the Government will be payable for these courses.
(2) The duration, eligibility, semester etc. of the said courses shall be as mentioned in the Annexure accompanying the Government decision.
(3) The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses and pay salaries and allowances to the staff as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
4) The institute must be affiliated to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for this short term course. Also, if the courses approved by this Government are decided to be implemented by the All India Council for Technical Education, it will be mandatory for the institution to get the approval of the All India Council for Technical Education.
5) Although the Government has a fee reimbursement policy for professional courses, it does not have a fee reimbursement scheme applicable to any short-term courses. Hence, the fee reimbursement scheme for the above courses will not be applicable to any category of students.
The fee reimbursement/ concession scheme will not be applicable to economically backward students as well.
6) The institutions will be required to submit an undertaking to the Board on a stamp paper of Rs.500 in the prescribed format that all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education are acceptable.

7) The concerned institution shall complete the other legal matters by paying the required term deposit in the joint account with the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai.
8) The Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai should take an affidavit regarding the appointment of regular teaching and non-teaching staff, providing machinery and up-to-date laboratories and library facilities to the students as per the standards. The responsibility of fulfilling these requirements lies with the concerned Deputy Secretary, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Divisional Office.
The books, journals required by the library in the institute are required to be purchased as per the standards of Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Bandra, Mumbai. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students.
10) The Institute shall be under the control of the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education and the examinations for the respective courses shall be conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Bandra, Mumbai.
11) Admission to these short-term courses should be done under the control of the Board as per the government policy on quality, reservation etc.
The course approved under the said Government Decisions will be required to be started by the institution from the academic year 2021-22. If the institution does not start the course in the prescribed manner, the recognition granted to the institution for the said course will be automatically cancelled.
No institution shall admit students without obtaining the approval of the Government and affiliation of the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education. In case of admission of students without recognition/ affiliation, the institution will be solely responsible.
If the institution charges more than the tuition fee prescribed by the government, action will be taken against the institution as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
15) Regarding the ragging. The Supreme Court's directions and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, should be strictly implemented.
16) Apart from this, other terms and conditions laid down by the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education will be applicable to the institute.
17) The Government shall have the power to modify the aforesaid terms and conditions or to insert new terms and conditions to enhance the quality of education.
