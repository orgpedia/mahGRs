Introduction: Refer No. As per the Government decision dated 17th May, 1984, the scheme of providing financial assistance for the further education of the students who have secured higher ranks in the merit list in the Secondary School Certificate and Higher Secondary School Certificate examinations in the State has been approved since the academic year 1984-85 and 11 and 15 sets have been sanctioned for each Divisional Board respectively. The Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education conducts the SSC examinations in Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Mumbai, Kolhapur, Amravati, Nashik and Latur divisions. A total of 88 sets of 11 each in Class 10 and Higher Secondary School Certificate (HSC) examinations. A total of 120 sets of merit students, 15 sets each in Class 12, are sanctioned financial assistance in the form of scholarships for further education.
Reference No. 2. As per the decision of the Government, the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Divisional Education, Kolhapur has approved the formation of Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Divisional Education (Konkan Divisional Board) for the districts of Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg in the Divisional Board. Therefore, the government was considering to include the Konkan Divisional Board in the scheme of providing financial assistance for the further education of the students who secured top ranks in the merit list in the Secondary School Certificate and Higher Secondary School Certificate examinations in the Konkan Divisional Board.

The government's decision:
As per the decision taken by the government in the context No. 1, the government has approved the inclusion of the Maharashtra State Secondary and Higher Secondary Divisional Board (Konkan Divisional Board), which has been formed for the districts of Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg, in the scheme of providing financial assistance for the further education of students who rank above in the merit list in the Secondary School Certificate and Higher Secondary School Certificate examinations in the state, in addition to the eight divisions of the Maharashtra State Secondary and Higher Secondary Divisional Education Board (MSBSD), namely Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Mumbai, Kolhapur, Amravati, Nashik and Latur.
The number of meritorious students to be selected under the scheme, the procedure and conditions for selection of meritorious students, the rate of financial assistance, as well as the conditions for continuation of this concession, shall be as mentioned in this Government Resolution, Department of Education and Employment, No: FED-1083/ 181635/ (2317)/ Sashi-5, dated 17th May, 1984 and 04.08.1985 and as amended from time to time.
