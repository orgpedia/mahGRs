Preamble: In pursuance of the aforesaid letter, the Director, Technical Education has submitted a proposal for grant of Government approval for new courses in Aditya Institute of Pharmaceuticals from the academic year 2019 to 2020 as per the list of approval received for the academic year 2019-2020 for starting new courses of Bachelor and Diploma in Pharmacy in the State from All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi. It was pending approval of the government.
The government's decision:
Approval of the Government is being accorded for starting of new Degree and Diploma courses in Pharmacy at Aditya Institute of Pharmaceuticals as indicated in the Annexure for the academic year 2019-20 subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education as well as the Pharmacy Council of India in this regard and as prescribed under the Government Order dated 21st May, 1983. The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be as specified in the Annexure.
(3) The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. as well as related

Completion of documents and formalities will be done satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Diploma Courses shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(7) The Institute shall appoint a full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules.
Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
9) Regarding the ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, will have to be strictly implemented.
10) Apart from this, other terms and conditions set by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the concerned university will remain applicable.
