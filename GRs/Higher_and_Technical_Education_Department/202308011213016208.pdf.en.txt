GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi and as proposed by the Director, Technical Education, the Government is granting approval for the increased intake capacity of B. Pharmacy course in the existing College of Pharmacy course of Maratha Vidya Prasarak Samaj, College of Pharmacy, Nashik from the academic year 2023-24 on permanent non-subsidized basis subject to the prevailing terms and conditions as shown in the table below:

Terms and Conditions 1) This Agreement shall remain in force on a non-recourse basis.
(2) In this case, as prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983.

(3) The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4) For the affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra State University Act, 2016.
(5) The institution concerned shall meet other legal requirements by depositing the requisite consolidated deposit with the Director, Technical Education in a joint account.
6) Admission to the said Degree Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
8) The Institute shall appoint full time faculty and other technical staff to run this permanent unaided course as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
9) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
10) In the context of ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
Other terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai, Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai and the University concerned shall continue to apply.
The Director shall recover the processing fee from the institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State and the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai, before giving final approval to the Director and for the inclusion of the course in the centralized admission process as per the approval of this Government.
