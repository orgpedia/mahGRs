The proposal:
The Government of India has drafted a new National Education Policy to provide quality education, innovative teaching and research facilities to the Indian population to enable them to acquire the necessary skills and knowledge to make India a knowledge superpower and to address the shortage of manpower in science, technology, education and industry. The state government has been asked to submit a proposal in this regard.
The Ministry of Education has sought comments from the Director of Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune on the draft of the new National Education Policy. Accordingly, the Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune has submitted his views to the Government. Suggestions/ comments on the draft have been received from Swami Ramanand Teerth Marathwada University, Nanded and Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA), Mumbai. The suggestions/ comments received are forwarded to the Ministry of Human Resource Development, New Delhi vide Reference No. (3) are presented here.
In line with the new National Education Policy, a task force group of education experts will analyze the suggestions submitted by the workshops at the taluka and district levels on the subject related to them and submit the same to the workshops of all the concerned units at the state level.

The Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD), Government of India has recently launched the National Education Policy 2020. Therefore, the government was considering the formation of a new task force to study the National Education Policy-2020 and submit a report to the state government. Accordingly, the following decisions are being taken to create a new workforce group of the following experts in the field of education.
The government's decision:
A new task force group of experts in the following education sector is being constituted to study and submit a report to the State Government regarding the new National Education Policy-2020.

The committee will study every aspect of the new National Education Policy-2020 and submit its report to the government within 3 months. However, if it is required to submit a report on some urgent issues to the government on priority, the committee should meet from time to time and submit an interim report to the government.
The Universities where the meetings of the said Committee shall be held shall mutually pay the honorarium and travelling allowance of the non-official members of the Committee.
