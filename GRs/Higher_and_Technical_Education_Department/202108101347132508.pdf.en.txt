The proposal:
Pursuant to the approval of the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, the Director, Technical Education vide letter dated 28/7/2021 mentioned above has proposed to the Government to approve the merger of the Institute under one management from the academic year 2021-2022.
The Government was considering to approve the merger of the institutions under the same management in the adjoining Annexure' A" from the academic year 2021-22 as proposed by the Director, Technical Education.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: As proposed by the Director, Technical Education, pursuant to the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, the merger of the Institute under a single management as indicated in the accompanying Annexure' A" from the academic year 2021-2022 is being approved by the Government subject to the following terms and conditions: 1. The approval shall be on a perpetual non-grant basis.
The said Post-Validation intake capacity shall be as approved by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi for the academic year 2021-22.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as prescribed under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's eligibility for this course will remain as mentioned above.
4. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.

5. Admission to the said Degree and Diploma Courses shall be made as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned exceed the tuition fees prescribed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the norms. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
The books, journals required by the library in the institution must be purchased as per the standards of the All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be fully equipped to accommodate the students.
Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
9. Regarding Ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
Other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the University concerned shall also apply.
The institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai, shall recover the same from the institutions before the final approval of the Director and the Director, Technical Education shall issue necessary orders within the next 15 days for the inclusion of the course as per the approval of this Government in the Centralized Admission Process.
