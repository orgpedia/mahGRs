Preamble: As per the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, a new Master's Degree (M.Pharm) will be offered in the existing Institute of Pharmacy in the State from the academic year 2023-24. The Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai has submitted a proposal to the Government through a letter in this regard.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval accorded by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi and as proposed by the Director, Technical Education, the institution mentioned in Annexure-A is being granted Government approval on permanent non-grant basis from the academic year 2023-2024 subject to the prevailing terms and conditions as indicated in the table below:

Terms and Conditions: 1) The award shall be made on a non-contributory basis.
(2) In this case, as prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983.
(3) The institution's eligibility for admission to the said course shall be as mentioned in Annexure A.
4) The Institute shall enter into an agreement with the Director, Technical Education, regarding acceptance of all terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. Also, the documents and formalities to be completed by the concerned institution will be done satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.

(5) The institution concerned shall meet other legal requirements by depositing the requisite consolidated deposit with the Director, Technical Education in a joint account.
6) Admission to the said Post Graduate Degree Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the institutions concerned charge more than the tuition fee prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
8) The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Also, strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
9) The library in the institution is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of Pharmacy Council of India. Also, the library should provide students with study material so that the students can make full use of the library. Also, the students are provided with all educational facilities as per the standards of Pharmacy Council of India (e.g. laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
10) In the context of ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
Further, other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the concerned University shall continue to apply to the Institute.
3. The institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai, shall recover the same before final approval by the Director and take further necessary action immediately.
