GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, with effect from April, 2016, the Central shareholding in the National Service Scheme (NSS) has become 100% and the scheme has become centrally administered. A separate savings account has been opened in the bank in which funds have been received from the Centre through ECS. According to the above letter of the Central Government, the funds have been deposited in the separate savings account of the National Service Scheme without directly going to the state. As per the decision of the government, this fund is distributed to the universities for the programs of the National Service Scheme. The funds for disbursement of grants-in-aid (conditional) to the respective Universities for the year 2018-19 (final instalment for all categories) and 2019-20 (first instalment for all categories) from the ICICI Bank savings account of National Service Scheme as mentioned in Annexure A and B are being sanctioned under this Government decision.
Due to non-receipt of Utilization Certificates for the year 2018-19 from 11 Universities/ Directorates, the remaining 15 Universities/ Directorates excluding the said Universities have been sanctioned a total amount of Rs. 3,61,97, 301/- (Rupees Three Hundred and Fifty Seven only) for the year 2018-19 (Final Instalment for all categories) and Rs. 51,93,683/- for the Special Camp Programme and Rs. 3,18,39, 529/- for the year 2019-20 (First Instalment for all categories 75% Central Share) for the year 2018-19 and Rs. 2,86,84, 243/- for the Special Camp Programme. Universities having balance of funds for 2018-19 are being allowed to disburse the funds by adjusting the same from the dues of 2019-20. (The details of dues to the Universities are given in Annexure A and B and are annexed to Annexure A and B).

The amount of grant-in-aid will be disbursed by ECS through Public Fund Distribution System (PFMS) to the Registrar/ Director/ Controller of the concerned University by the State National Service Scheme Officer (State Liaison Officer/ Officer on Special Duty) of the National Service Scheme with the approval of Secretary, Department of Higher and Technical Education. The Universities shall disburse the said grant amount to the colleges under their control through Public Fund Distribution System (PFMS) category wise and submit the same to the Government immediately with the signature of the Chartered Accountant along with the Assistant Grant Allocation Statement and Utilization Certificate. The University shall also submit to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai and to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-2, Nagpur and to this Department or its representative all accounts of grants under the National Service Scheme for inspection.
