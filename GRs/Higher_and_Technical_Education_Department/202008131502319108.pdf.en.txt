Preamble: The Director, Technical Education has proposed to grant Government approval for starting new courses, increase in existing intake capacity, restoration of earlier reduced intake capacity, completion of batches, etc. in the existing permanent unaided Engineering, HMCT, Management, MCA courses of the above mentioned date 10/7/2020.
As proposed by the Director, Technical Education, it was under consideration of the Government to grant approval for starting new courses in the existing institution as indicated against the names of the institutions in Annexure' A' from the academic year 2020-21, increase in the existing intake capacity, restoration of previously reduced intake capacity, completion of batch, etc.
The government's decision:
As proposed by the Director, Technical Education, pursuant to the approval granted by All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, starting from the academic year 2020-2021, the following new courses in the existing permanent unaided/ Government Engineering, HMCT, Management Science, MCA courses, undergraduate and postgraduate courses as indicated against the names of the institutions in Annexure' A' are being approved by the Government subject to the following conditions:

Government Resolution No: TEM-2020/ PR No.102/ TANSI-4
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983.
The institution's eligibility for this course will remain as mentioned above.
(3) The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Post Graduate Degree Courses will be done as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(7) The Institute shall appoint a full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE).
laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
9) Regarding the ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
Other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the University concerned shall also apply.
3. The institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, shall recover the same before final approval by the Director.
