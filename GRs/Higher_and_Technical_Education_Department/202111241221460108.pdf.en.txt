The proposal:
The procedure for starting short term vocational diploma courses under the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education from the academic year 2021-22 has been laid down under the Government Decision dated 03/05/2021 of the Department of Higher and Technical Education. Accordingly, the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education had issued a notification dated 25/05/2021 and dated 07/09/2021, inviting applications from interested institutions through online system.
The applications and the original documents along with the application received by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education as per the said notification were examined by the Document Verification Committee and the physical verification of the infrastructure in the eligible institution was done by the Expert Committee. Thereafter, the State Level Committee after verifying the reports of the Zonal Level Committee has submitted a proposal to the Government through the Board regarding starting of new institutions, introduction of new courses in the affiliated educational institutions, increase/ decrease in the intake capacity of the courses, closure of courses, change in the name of the institution, change in the address of the institution and closure of the institution. The matter was under consideration of the government.
The government's decision:
Starting from the academic year 2021-22, the institutions of short-term vocational diploma courses under the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education will be allowed to start new institutions as mentioned in Annexure-A, B, C, D, E and F.

5) The institute must be affiliated to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for this short term course. Also, if the courses approved by this Government are decided to be implemented by the All India Council for Technical Education, it will be mandatory for the institution to get the approval of the All India Council for Technical Education.
6) Although the Government has a fee reimbursement policy for professional courses, it does not have a fee reimbursement scheme applicable to any short-term courses. Hence, the fee reimbursement scheme for the above courses will not be applicable to any category of students.
The fee reimbursement/ concession scheme will not be applicable to economically backward students as well.
7) It will be mandatory for the institutions to submit an undertaking to the Board on a stamp paper of Rs.500 in the prescribed format that all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education are acceptable.
