Introduction: National Service Scheme is a 100% Central share scheme and every year the Central Government grants the State of Maharashtra two activities under the scheme, namely, regular activities and special camps, with the student strength divided into category wise SCs- 11.8 per cent, STs- 9.4 per cent and others- 78.8 per cent.
As per the reference letter, the Central Government has sanctioned the total number of students for the year 2023-24 to the State of Maharashtra for the National Service Scheme, by categorizing the number of students for regular activities as Scheduled Castes-42,104, Scheduled Tribes-33,535 and for other categories as 2,81,161, with a total of 3,56,800 for regular activities and 1,78,400 for special camps (50% of the sanctioned number of students for regular activities). Accordingly, the matter of category-wise allocation of total sanctioned strength of students to the Universities/ Directorates in the State for regular activities and special camps was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: As informed by the Government of India, for the year 2023-24, for the National Service Scheme, a total number of 3,56,800 students have been sanctioned for regular activities and 1,78,400 for special camps and the same are being sanctioned University/ Directorate wise as shown in the table below.
