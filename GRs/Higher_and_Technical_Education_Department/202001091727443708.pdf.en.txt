Preamble: It has been decided that persons who have entered government service on the basis of reservation for backward castes and whose caste claims have been invalidated thereafter will not be entitled to protection in government service. The Supreme Court in Civil Appeal No. 8928/2015 dated 6th July, 2017.
Also, Ma. The High Court, Bombay, Division Bench, Nagpur vide order dated 28.09.2018 in Writ Petition No. 3140/2008 has directed the Government of Maharashtra to take necessary steps to provide relief to the affected people. As per the Supreme Court order. Orders have been issued to take action by 31.12.2019.
02. Ma. The procedure for implementation of the orders of the courts has been prescribed under the Government Order dated 21st December, 2019 of the General Administration Department. Accordingly, the cadre-wise number of Scheduled Tribe officers whose caste certificate has been invalidated is to be determined and transferred to the superannuation post by December 31, 2019. Also, a time frame has been set for conducting a special recruitment drive to fill these posts.
The proposal for creation of more number of posts for transfer of services of Shri P.P.Raut, Senior Clerk on the establishment of Directorate of Arts as the certificate of Scheduled Tribe has been invalidated was submitted under the aforesaid letter dated 24th December, 2019 of the Directorate of Arts.
The following decisions are taken:
Government Decision: The Scheduled Tribe certificate of Shri P. P. Raut, Senior Clerk on the establishment of the Directorate of Arts has been invalid. Therefore, to transfer him to the post of Superintendent from the date of his service, 31st December, 2019, on the establishment of the Directorate of Arts, temporarily for a period of 11 months or till the date on which he would have retired if he had remained in service, whichever is earlier.
