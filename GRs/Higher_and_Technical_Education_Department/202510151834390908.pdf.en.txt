The Proposal:
In order to develop the Sanskrit language in the state, the government has enacted the Kavi Kulguru Kalidasa Sanskrit University Act, 1997. Section 62 of the Act provides for a time-bound provision to allow new colleges / faculties / courses / subjects / additional batches. In pursuance of the said provisions, from the academic year 2018-19, Kavi Vice-Chancellor Kalidasa Sanskrit Vishwavidyalaya, Ramtek University has notified the procedure and criteria for sanctioning new colleges / faculties, courses, subjects and incremental batches under No. 2 The government decision here was prescribed under the date 12.10.2017. In this Government Decision, Paragraph No. In 1.7, the university has mentioned about accepting applications through online system.
Accordingly, Kavi Vice-Chancellor Kalidasa Sanskrit Vishwavidyalaya, Ramtek University has developed an online system for accepting applications for recognition of new colleges / faculties / courses / subjects / additional batches. However, at the government level, a "New College Permission System" (NCPS) has been developed to submit approval proposals to the government for new colleges / courses / additional batches from this academic year. The government's decision:
The procedure for sanctioning new colleges / courses / additional batches from the academic year 1997 under the Kavi Vice-Chancellor Kalidasa Sanskrit Vishwavidyalaya Act, 1997 shall be as follows: -
The University shall prepare a Perspective Plan every five years in accordance with the provisions of Section 62 of the "Kavi Vice-Chancellor Kalidasa Sanskrit Vishwavidyalaya Act, 1997" for equitable distribution of facilities of higher education throughout its jurisdiction.
1.2 The said scheme shall be approved by the Maharashtra State Higher Education and Development Commission.
1.2 An annual plan should be prepared for starting new colleges / courses / additional units in accordance with the appropriate plan.
2. After determining the points for the places included in the Annual Plan, new colleges, courses, and additional blocks should be advertised in the newspaper along with the places in the appropriate plan. Advertisements should clearly state that they will not be accepted for positions outside the appropriate scheme and so applications should not be accepted.
Institutions interested in starting new colleges / courses / additional batches should submit applications through the online system "New College Accreditation System" before the last day of October of the year preceding the year in which permission is sought from the University as per the provision of Section 62 (3) of the Kavi Vice-Chancellor Kalidasa Sanskrit Vishwavidyalaya, Act, 1997. Along with this, the documents mentioned in Annexure "A" should be submitted through the system.
1.6 The University shall accept applications in the prescribed format only through the online system of "New College Accreditation System."
1.7 The Vice-Chancellors of the Universities shall appoint such Scrutiny Committees as may be necessary to scrutinize the applications received with the University.
The Scrutiny Committee shall submit a report to the Vice-Chancellor along with appropriate recommendations through the Planning Development Board in accordance with the list of required documents mentioned in Annexure A attached with the verification of the applications received.
If an error is found in the proposal or documents of the institution, the institution / applicant will be notified of the error.
Institutions that have been notified of an error will be required to report the error to the university.
19 Documents relating to error resolution will be scrutinized by the Scrutiny Committee The report on eligible proposals after scrutiny will be submitted to the Planning Development Board with appropriate recommendations.
The Vice-Chancellor shall constitute such expert committees at the university level as may be necessary to conduct site inspection and necessary inspection in accordance with the proposals received. The Expert Committee shall not include members of the Scrutiny Committee. The Expert Committee met in person and examined the preparedness of the institution concerned in accordance with the list prescribed in Annexure 'A' attached to it and the documents mentioned in the system and submitted its report along with video footage.
