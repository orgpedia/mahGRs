In this regard, the University Grants Commission vide Reference No. Under Section 7, guidelines have been issued regarding the implementation of Apprenticeship Embedded Degree Programme (AEDP) in higher education institutions. These guidelines address the need to bridge the gap between learning and hands-on training, provide employment opportunities to students by creating interactions between academia and industry, etc. A skill-based curriculum needs to be created to create the skilled manpower required for the industry sector. Apprenticeship can be an effective means of bridging the gap between education and employment as it involves hands-on work-based education and theoretical knowledge of the relevant subject.
The Sub-Working Group constituted under the State Level Steering Committee on Implementation of Apprenticeship Embedded Degree Programme (AEDP) in AYUSH Universities and Affiliated Colleges of the State, representatives of all non-agricultural universities, representatives of Zonal Skill Councils, discussions with various committees and discussions were held. The University Grants Commission (UGC) has approved the implementation of Apprenticeship Embedded Degree Programme (AEDP) in higher education institutions in view of the discussions in the workshop on 16th April, 2025. The implementation of the guidelines dated 24.03.2025 in the state was under the consideration of the government.
The Government's Decision-
The University Grants Commission (UGC) has approved the implementation of the Apprenticeship Embedded Degree Programme (AEDP) in higher education institutions. The government is approving the acceptance of ATER notifications dated 24.03.2025. The guidelines should be implemented in all non-agricultural universities and affiliated colleges in the state from the academic year 2025-26 onwards. University Grants Commission.
