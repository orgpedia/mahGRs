Introduction: National Service Scheme (NSS) is a Central Sector Scheme under which number of students are sanctioned by the Central Government every year to the Universities/ Institutions for two activities, namely, regular activities and special camps.
As per Reference 1, the Central Government has approved 3000 Government funded RASEO units (3,00,000 students) in the country for the year 2022-23, taking the total number of RASEO students to 39 lakh in 2022-23 from 36 lakh in the previous year and increasing the number of RASEO students sanctioned under the National Service Scheme for the State of Maharashtra by 29100 to 356800 for the year 2022-23.
Reference No. As per the letter, the Central Government has informed that the increased number of grant-in-aid students sanctioned in the year 2022-23 will be distributed to four aspirational districts (1) Nandurbar 2) Washim 3) Gadchiroli 4) Osmanabad in Maharashtra on priority.
As per Reference 1 for the year 2022-23, the Central Government has sanctioned University wise category wise allocation for National Service Scheme i. e. 11.8 for SCs, 9.4 for STs and 78.8 for Others, with a total of 42,104 for SCs, 33,535 for STs and 2,81,161 for Others, totaling to 3,56,800 for regular activities and 1,78,400 for special camps (50% of the sanctioned strength for regular activities) for the State of Maharashtra. In 2022-23, proposals for sanction of increased number of students in Government aided colleges have been received from some Universities and they have been sanctioned the increased number of students for the year 2022-23 by the Central Government. Accordingly, the total sanctioned strength of students for Maharashtra and its university/ directorate wise allocation was under consideration of the Government.

The universities/ directorates under which the colleges in the four aspirational districts (1) Nandurbar 2) Washim 3) Gadchiroli 4) Osmanabad of Maharashtra come under, should distribute the increased number of students sanctioned in the year 2022-23 to these four aspirational districts on priority and submit a report to the government.
The University/ Directorate shall not be allowed to admit more students than the sanctioned strength of the University/ Category wise as indicated against their names. Further, the category-wise allocation of the number of students allocated as per the category-wise student strength for the year 2022-23 should be made by the University/ Directorate to all the colleges affiliated/ affiliated to it and a copy of the same should be sent to the Government. The number of students actually enrolled by the universities should be intimated to the Government by 30 th September, 2022.
