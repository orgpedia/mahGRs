Introduction: As the students of the state's deemed university are not getting the benefit of the government's existing tuition fee scholarship scheme, the government has decided to allow this benefit. Public Interest Litigation in the High Court, Bombay Bench No. 69/2011 was filed. In this petition, The court issued the order on March 20, 2015. For the implementation of this order, there should be clarity in the number of course-wise/ category-wise students admitted to deemed universities in the state, the number of scholarship holders who qualify as per the prescribed norms, the annual expected expenditure incurred for it and the financial liability to be borne by the government towards the cost of the scholarship. Considering this. As per the order passed by the court on March 20, 2015, the government was considering to form an expert committee to recommend to the government a uniform formula for determining the course-wise amount of scholarship in order to implement the scholarship scheme for the students of deemed universities in the state.
The government's decision:
The benefit of any scholarship scheme implemented by the concerned department of the government, such schemes, for students admitted to professional courses in deemed universities of the state established under section 3 of the University Grants Commission Act.
An Expert Committee is being set up to determine the specific formula for determining the course-wise amount of scholarship per student and per annum to be applied as follows:
