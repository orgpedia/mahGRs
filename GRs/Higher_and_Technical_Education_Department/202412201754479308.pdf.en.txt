Read: Letter from Director, Technical Education No. 2 / PCI-approved / 2024-25/997, d. 13.12.2024.
Preamble: The Director, Technical Education has submitted a proposal to the Government in a letter read here regarding the Government approval to increase the intake capacity of postgraduate degree courses in the existing Institute of Pharmacy from the academic year 2024-25 as approved by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. Accordingly, the issue of government approval was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi and as proposed by the Director, Technical Education, Government approval is being accorded to increase the intake capacity of the existing Post-Graduate Degree course in the Institute of Pharmacy indicated in the accompanying Form-A from the year 2024-25 subject to the following academic conditions:
Terms and Conditions: - 1) The approval shall be on a non-grant basis for the time being.
2) Pharmacy as prescribed by HIT of India, New Delhi in the present case as well as D. The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed under the Government Decision dated 21st May, 1983.
3) The Institute shall enter into an agreement with the Director, Technical Education regarding acceptance of all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. Also, the required documents and formalities will be complied with satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra.
4) The institution concerned should meet other legal requirements by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admissions to the said course shall be made as per the rules / procedures for admission to the concerned course as approved by the Government.
6) If the fee charged by the concerned institutions is more than the tuition fee prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, those institutions will be debarred as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation) Act, 1987.
