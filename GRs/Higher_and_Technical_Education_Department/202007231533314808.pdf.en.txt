Preamble: The Director, Technical Education vide his letter dated 10/7/2020 has proposed to the Government to approve closure/ reduction in the intake capacity of the courses in the existing Degree/ Post Graduate Institute of Pharmacy.
As proposed by the Director, Technical Education, the matter of approval of reduction/ discontinuation of existing intake/ courses as indicated against the names of the institutions in Annexure" A" with effect from the academic year 2020-21 was under consideration of the Government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, the Government has accorded approval for phasing out/ reduction in the intake of courses in the existing Bachelor of Pharmacy/ Master of Pharmacy institutions as indicated against the names of institutions in Annexure' A' from the academic year 2020-2021, as proposed by the Director, Technical Education, subject to the following conditions:
1) The institution will be required to hand over all the documents related to the courses to be closed in this institution such as marksheets, answer sheets, results sheets, addresses of students, fee receipts, etc. to the concerned university.
(2) The Head of the Institution shall be responsible for all matters which are sub judice in respect of service and other matters of the faculty/ staff appointed for the concerned course.
In case of any court case in the future, the institution will be responsible for it.
3) If there are any pending dues of the government from the institution, it will be mandatory for the head of the institution to pay it.

(4) It shall be the sole responsibility of the Institute to repay the loans taken by it from the Government or any other financial institution in respect of the immovable property required for running the course in the Institute.
5) While closing the courses in the institution in a phased manner, it will be mandatory for the institutions to provide educational facilities to the admitted students and alumni of the institution till the completion of the course and make arrangements for the examination. If the institution does not provide these educational facilities, action will be taken against the institution as per the rules and the accumulated deposit with the government will be deposited by the government.
(6) It shall be obligatory on the part of the institution to pay the salary, salary compensation and other financial allowances payable as per rules to the teachers and staff of the institution appointed for the course concerned.
