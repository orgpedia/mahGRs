As per the letter dated 30th April, 2019, a new course on Applied Art at the Padmabhushan Vasantdada Patil Pratishthan's College of Engineering, Sion-Chunabhatti, Mumbai, a private unaided institution, has been approved to be started from the academic year 2019-20 on a non-subsidised basis. Directorate of Arts has examined the facilities required to start the course of Applied Art in the institute and the proposal along with the recommendations is given in the Reference No. The letter was dated June 4, 2019. Accordingly, the matter of granting approval to start a degree course in Applied Art at the Padmabhushan Vasantdada Patil Pratishthan's College of Engineering, Sion-Chunabhatti, Mumbai, was under the consideration of the government. The following decision has been taken:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi vide letter dated 30/4/2019 in the aforesaid Reference No.

The Under Graduate course in Applied Art offered by Padmabhushan Vasantdada Patil Pratishthan's College of Engineering, Sion-Chunabhatti, Mumbai with an intake capacity of maximum 60 students is being approved to start from the academic year 2019-20 subject to the following terms and conditions. :
1) The grant will be on a grant-in-aid basis.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education as well as prescribed under the Government Decision dated 21st May, 1983.
The maximum intake capacity for this course will be 60 students.
4. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the formalities and documentation will be done satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Directorate of Arts, Maharashtra State.
For the affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.
6. Required registration fee to be remitted by the institution concerned. Also, other legal matters should be settled by depositing the accumulated deposit in a joint account.
The admission to this degree course will be done as per the admission rules/ procedure approved by the government.
Any institution found charging more than the prescribed tuition fee by the Fee Regulatory Authority will be liable for action under the Maharashtra Education Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the rules of the government. Salaries and allowances should be paid to them. Strict action will be taken on the salary and allowances of teachers and other employees, as well as if complaints are received from students to the government.
The books, journals required for the library in the institution must be purchased as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students will get all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). Library, classrooms, etc. 2) need to be available.
11. Regarding Ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
