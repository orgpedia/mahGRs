The proposal:
Rashtrasant Tukadoji Regional Cancer Hospital, Nagpur (Institute No. 0592) was granted approval to start a short term course in RS-Advance Diploma in X-ray Radiography and Ultra Sonography Technician, which is not under the purview of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) but under the purview of Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, vide Government Resolution dated 01.10.2010. The institutions approved under condition number 14 of this government decision were required to start the course for two academic years from the date of the government decision. However, the institution has not started the course approved during this two-year period. Now the institute has requested to relax the condition number 14 of the said government decision and approve the said course from the academic year 2018-19. Accordingly, the Deputy Secretary, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Divisional Office, Nagpur has recommended to the Board to start the course RS-Advance Diploma in X-ray Radiography and Ultra Sonography Technician from the academic year 2018-19. 2 dated 21.06.2018.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: RECOGNITION-2018/ (163/18)/ TANSHI-5
Accordingly, the matter of approval to start this course from the year 2018-19 was under the consideration of the Government.
The Government has granted approval to Rashtrasant Tukadoji Regional Cancer Hospital, Nagpur (Institute No. 0592) to start the short term course RS Advance Diploma in X-ray Radiography and Ultra Sonography Technician from the academic year 2018-19 under Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education subject to the following terms and conditions.
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The course will be of 1.5 years duration. Admission to the course is limited to 60 students.
(3) The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses and pay salaries and allowances to the staff as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
4) The institute must be affiliated to Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for this short term course. Also, if the courses approved by this Government are decided to be implemented by the All India Council for Technical Education, it will be mandatory for the institution to get the approval of the Council for such courses.
5) Although the Government has a fee reimbursement policy for professional courses, it does not have a fee reimbursement scheme applicable to any non-AICTE courses.
Hence, the fee reimbursement scheme for the above courses will not be applicable to any category of students. The fee reimbursement/ concession scheme will not be applicable to economically backward students as well.
6) The institution will have to sign an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education.
7) The Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai should take an affidavit regarding the appointment of regular teaching and non-teaching staff, providing machines and updated laboratories and library facilities to the students as per the standards. The Deputy Secretary, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Divisional Office, Nagpur will be responsible for the fulfillment of these requirements.
