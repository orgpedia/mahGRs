In pursuance of the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi to start new institutions of Diploma courses in Pharmacy in the State from the academic year 2019-20, the Government has decided to grant approval to the new institutions of Diploma courses in Pharmacy in the State.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Higher and Technical Education,
GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. APPROVAL-2019/ (P.S. 109/19)/ TANSHI-5
Mantralaya Extension Building, Madame Cama Road,
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Mumbai 400032.
The date: June 25, 2019.
Reference: 1) Letter No.10/ NGP/ New/ Approval/ 2019/647 dated 14.06.2019 of the Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai.
Preamble: The Director, Directorate of Technical Education has submitted a proposal to the Government through a Reference letter in accordance with the list received by the Director, Technical Education through the link provided by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi vide email dated 15.05.2019 regarding recognition of new institutions for Diploma courses in Professional Pharmacy in the State from the academic year 2019-20. A total of 09 institutions mentioned in the attached Form-A and Form-B were under the consideration of the Government for grant of approval to start new Diploma Courses.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval given by All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, Government approval is being accorded to start new Diploma in Pharmacy courses from the academic year 2019-20 subject to the following terms and conditions.
1) The grant will be on a grant-in-aid basis.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as prescribed under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983.
The eligibility of the institution for admission to the said course shall be as mentioned in Form-A and Form-B.
4. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
For affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.

The institution concerned will be required to take affiliation of Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for the Diploma courses in Pharmacy.
7. The institution concerned shall complete other legal formalities by depositing the requisite consolidated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai.
8. Admission to the Diploma Course in Professional Pharmacy approved by the Government.
Admission to the courses will be done as per the norms.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned exceed the tuition fees prescribed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
10.The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per Government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
11.The library in the institution is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students.
Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
12.R. In respect of Ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
13.Even otherwise, the other terms and conditions laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the concerned University shall continue to apply.
14.The institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, shall recover the same from them before final approval by the Director.
