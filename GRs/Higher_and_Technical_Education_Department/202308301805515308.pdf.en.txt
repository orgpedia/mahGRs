The Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016 has come into force with effect from 01.03.2017. Considering the increasing pass percentage in higher secondary schools and the inclination of the students towards a particular course, no student should be deprived of the desired higher education, Section 109 (8) of the Act provides for expeditious recognition of new batches in addition to the specified faculties in exceptional circumstances. In accordance with this provision, in the academic year 2023-24, the non-agricultural universities of the state had submitted proposals to the government for additional new batches of specific faculties. Reference No. 2 Decisions made by the government
The examination was conducted as per the norms. After this inspection, new colleges will be set up in addition to the specific faculties that have qualified from the proposals recommended by the universities to the government.
The proposal was under consideration of the government for expeditious approval.
The government has taken the following decisions:
The government's decision:
In the academic year 2023-24, new batches of faculty in addition to the specific faculty who have qualified from the proposals recommended by the non-agricultural universities in the State to the Government are being sanctioned on permanent non-grant basis to the existing colleges as per the powers vested in the Government under Section 109 (8) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016, subject to the following terms and conditions as mentioned in the statement:

Terms and Conditions: 1. The existing colleges in which additional batches have been sanctioned in such expeditious manner, shall give an undertaking to their Divisional Joint Directors that they will not demand any grant in future. 100/- on non-judicial stamp paper.
2. Certificate of the Joint Director of the Department without the submission of guarantee by the college or
The university concerned should not start the process of affiliation without receiving it.
The colleges are required to appoint faculty/ staff as per the norms prescribed by the concerned university and educational qualification prescribed by the University Grants Commission and provide all other necessary infrastructure and the same shall be ensured by the Joint Director of the Department and only after that the aforesaid condition no. 2 certificate from the university.
Under no circumstances should the college admit more than the sanctioned intake capacity.

The University shall not proceed to grant affiliation to the colleges concerned unless the criteria prescribed for granting affiliation to the colleges in the University Grants Commission (Affiliation of Colleges by Universities) Regulations, 2009 are fulfilled.
The additional batch approved in this way will continue with the natural growth. However, if such a batch does not receive regular government recognition in the next academic year, the student will not be admitted to the first year in this batch. For such regular additional batch for the next academic year, it will be necessary to submit a fresh application and get government approval as per the criteria and procedures prescribed by the government.
7. Accelerated additional batch If a student is not admitted in the batch for the academic year for which it is sanctioned and the batch is not started due to some other reasons, the recognition of such batch shall be deemed to be automatically cancelled.
It should be ensured that 50% of the sanctioned strength of students is admitted in the fast track additional batch which has been sanctioned.
The students will be admitted to such fast-track additional batch only through the procedure prescribed by the government for admitting students to the concerned faculty. Also, as per the prevailing policy of the Government, the sanction of additional fast track platoon will always be on non-subsidised basis.
The colleges which have provisionally made arrangements for the additional batch approved by the fast track, an undertaking is attached with the application that they will meet the criteria prescribed by the Government for the regular batch within the next 6 months, after fulfilling these criteria within the next 6 months, they will be required to submit the certificate of completion to the Government through the University.
The Universities shall not initiate the process of granting affiliation without first going through the Government approval orders and obtaining the certificate in Condition No. 2 of the Divisional Joint Director before taking further action as may be required for grant of affiliation. The universities should send the compliance report regarding the affiliation to the Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune and the concerned Divisional Joint Director, Higher Education.
The Joint Director of the concerned university and the Joint Director of the concerned department shall take necessary action in the light of the matter raised in the petitions No. 2446/2013 and No. 2448/2013 filed in the Nagpur Bench of the High Court, Nagpur.
