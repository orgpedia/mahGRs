Terms and Conditions: 1. The letter of intent to start a new college as per the prevailing policy of the Government shall continue to be valid for starting a college on a non-subsidized basis.
Since the said Letter of Intent has been issued by the Institute with a view to fulfil the prescribed terms and conditions before obtaining final approval for the new college, the said Letter of Intent shall not enable the Institute to start the college without obtaining final approval of the Government for starting the new college.
The letter of intent issued by the government to start a new college will be valid until January 31 of the nearest next year. During this period, it will be mandatory to meet the terms and conditions on the basis of which the letter of intent has been issued and submit a compliance report to the university along with the necessary documents for final approval.
In view of the provisions of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, the Government has decided to... The procedure for final approval as on 15th September, 2017 has been clearly spelt out. As per the provisions of the Act and in accordance with the said procedure, the institutions receiving the Letter of Intent fulfill the terms and conditions of the Letter of Intent, they should apply to the University for final approval with all the documents and the universities should submit the application of all eligible applicant institutions and the compliance report with it to the Government along with the recommendation for approval in Annexure' C' and Form' B' and the accompanying documents.
The desirous institution seeking final approval to start a new college should submit a compliance report to the concerned university stating that the terms and conditions of the letter of intent have been fulfilled. Along with this, the documents mentioned in Annexure' C' should be attached.

The college should not admit students on the basis of a letter of intent under any circumstances.
If such an admission is found to have been made, action will be taken in accordance with the provisions of the law in this regard and the letter of intent will be cancelled.
It shall be the responsibility of the concerned University to verify the rectitude of the colleges which have been given the letter of intent in the authority obtained under the proviso to Section 109 (3) (d) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.
