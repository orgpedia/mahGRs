The main objectives of the Committee are:
To increase the quality, utility and number of innovation and intellectual property creation, strengthening the patent process in educational institutions.
3. Scope of the Committee: The Committee will suggest measures on the following issues.
Capacity Building and Training -
Implementing training programmes for e-Innovation Cell coordinators, faculty and researchers.
# Page 3
The training should cover topics such as novelty, inventive step, industrial applicability, prior art searches, drafting skills, filing procedures, etc.
Also, the training should not be theory-based only but should be provided by subject matter experts with hands-on experience.
3.2 State-level IP support system -
Setting up of IP Facilitation Centres at the state level.
Thereby providing services for drafting, filing and prosecution of pre-patented art searches at reasonable rates.
To include experienced subject matter experts with knowledge of academic research.
Institutional ? Strengthening of management -
Creating guidelines for IP cells.
Mandatory IP portfolio management and tracking for every organization.
Patent Evaluation and Grading System -
To compile a Patent TH by collating information on patents of all institutions and make it mandatory to publish the database on the website of the Directorate of Technical Education.
Evaluation and rating of patents by an expert committee.
Commercialization and Industry Cooperation -
To enhance cooperation with MSME sector and make this patent bank available to MSMEs in the state.
Formulating policies to promote industries that accept academic patents.
A government of state-level intellectual property. ? creating a market.
Standardization of Educational Patents -
To frame state level guidelines for recognition of patents in the field of education as contributions by institutions or faculties.
To lay down the criteria for consideration of patent matters in CAS, NAAC and Institutional Evaluation.
Classifying patents as filed, published, granted, and commercialized.
9 Removal of the procedural act -
Removing errors related to non-response to First Examination Report (FER), poor quality drafting, lack of further action, etc.
Monitoring / Monitoring / Supervision and Assigning Responsibilities at the Institutional Level.
# Page 4
3.8 Control of abuse -
Preventing the tendency to apply for patents only for academic scores.
To develop a quality inspection and screening system before patent application.
3.8 Expected results:
There will be an increase in the quality and valuation of patents.
There will be a reduction in procedural errors.
A trained and competent IP ecosystem will be created.
Industry-academia cooperation will increase.
There will be an increase in the commercialization of innovations in the education sector.
8. The aforesaid Committee shall hold physical / online meetings as and when required.
The expenditure should be met from the self-finance of the Institute of ICT, Mumbai. The committee has submitted its report.
It should be submitted to the government through the Directorate of Technical Education in 3 months.
