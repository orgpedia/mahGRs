Swami Ramanand Teerth Marathwada University was established on 17th September 1994.
The university campus has 14 campuses, sub-campuses, 4 campuses at Latur and sub-campus at Parbhani and there are 357 colleges affiliated to the university. Due to lack of adequate facilities for research in the campus, sub-campuses and affiliated colleges of the university, research scholars and faculty members face difficulties. To provide impetus to interdisciplinary research, Central Instrumentation Centre for Multidisciplinary Research and Innovation is being set up at Swami Ramanand Teerth Marathwada University. The proposal was submitted through a letter dated 1. As per the proposal, No. 4 here. As per the Government decision dated 19.09.2022, administrative approval has been accorded for procurement of various equipment for the Centre. The funds were budgeted through Supplementary Demands for Grants for this Centre. The Finance Department was requested to make the budgeted funds available on the BEAMS system. The Finance Department has approved the release of funds to the Centre. In pursuance of the said approval, the Government was contemplating to allocate funds for procurement of various equipment for the Central Instrumentation Centre for Multidisciplinary Research and Innovation.

Terms and Conditions: 1) The requirement/ demand for the said equipment and materials should be ascertained. Thereafter, the tender process will be initiated. This procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Government Decision, Industry, Energy and Labor Department, No. Bhankhas-2014/ No. 82/ Part-III/ Industry-4, dated December 1, 2016.
2) Ensure that the specifications of the equipment and materials are as per requirement and up to date.
The technical specifications of the items to be procured should be as per the specifications agreed by the concerned experts (Technical Committee/ Officials). Also, it should be ensured that the technical specification is not designed in such a way that it will benefit any particular company. After that, you have to buy them.
(3) The prescribed procurement procedure should be carried out in accordance with the provisions contained in the Revised Manual of Procedure for Office Purchases to be made by Government Departments published under Government Decision, Industries, Energy and Labour Department, No. 2014/ PR No. 82/ Part-II/ Industry-4, dated 1st December, 2016.
4) The rates of the proposed procurement are estimated at the time of granting administrative approval. Therefore, the price should be fixed at the end of the procurement process by verifying the GEM Portal and the rates of other states. It should be ensured that the price is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the supplier has supplied the item to other places.
5) Pre-supply/ post-supply sample inspection of the items to be purchased should be done to ensure that only the items with proper and correct specifications are purchased.
6) Storage and distribution of the items to be purchased after supply, supply chain management and inventory management, maintenance should be done in a proper manner to ensure that these items do not remain unused and are used within the stipulated time.
7) Reconciliation Certificate should be submitted by the Director, Higher Education, Pune.
8) Also, it should be ensured that there is no duplication in the purchase of these items.
The Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) guidelines should be strictly followed while carrying out the entire procurement process.
