The Government has approved the proposal of the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi to restore the admission capacity of the existing Diploma in Engineering courses in the state from the academic year 2019-20.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Higher and Technical Education
GOVERNMENT DECISION NO. APPROVAL-2019/ (P.S. 108/19) TANSHI-5
Mantralaya Extension Building, Madame Cama Road,
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Mumbai 400 032.
Date: 25th of June, 2019
Reference: 1) Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai vide letter No.10/ NGP/ Enhancement of Accessibility/ Accreditation/ 2019/634, dated 12.06.2019.
Preamble: In pursuance of the e-mail dated 15.05.2019 from the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, regarding approval of the institutions to restore and reduce the intake capacity of the existing Engineering Diploma Courses in the State from the academic year 2019-20, the Director, Directorate of Technical Education has submitted a proposal to the Government under the Reference Letter. The matter of granting permission to the government to restore the admission capacity of the Diploma in Engineering course as shown in the attached Form-A and Form-B to the nominated institutions before their names was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval given by All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, the institutions mentioned in Form A and Form B attached herewith are being granted Government approval to restore the intake capacity, reduce the intake capacity of Diploma in Engineering course from the academic year 2019-20 subject to the following terms and conditions.
1) The grant will be on a grant-in-aid basis.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as prescribed under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be limited as mentioned against their name in the prospectus.
3. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
For affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.

The institutions concerned for these diploma courses must take affiliation of Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education.
The concerned institution shall complete the other legal matters by paying the requisite processing fee and the accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai.
7. Admission to the said Professional Diploma Courses shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective courses as approved by the Government.
The rules and orders issued from time to time in accordance with the Maharashtra Act No. 28 of 2015 for regulating admission and fees in unaided private professional educational institutions in the State of Maharashtra and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto shall be applicable to the institutions concerned.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned exceed the tuition fees prescribed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
10.The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per Government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
11.The library in the institution is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
The Institute shall be under the control of the Director, Technical Education and the examinations for the respective courses shall be conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Bandra, Mumbai.
13.R. In respect of Ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
14.The institutions concerned will be responsible for any eventuality arising out of the change in the course/ discontinuation of the course. Such a guarantee has to be given by the concerned agencies.
15.Yet other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the concerned University shall continue to apply.
Institutions which have not paid the processing fee to the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, shall recover the same before final approval by the Director.
