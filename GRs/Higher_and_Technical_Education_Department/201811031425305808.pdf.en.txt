GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, with effect from April, 2016, the Central shareholding in the National Service Scheme (NSS) has become 100% and the scheme has become centrally administered. A separate savings account has been opened in the bank in which funds have been received from the Centre through ECS. According to the above letter of the Central Government, the funds have been deposited in the separate savings account of the National Service Scheme without directly going to the state. As per the decision of the government, this fund is distributed to the universities for the programs of the National Service Scheme. The release of funds for disbursement of grants-in-aid (conditional) to the respective Universities for the year 2017-18 (final instalment for all categories) and 2018-19 (first instalment for all categories) from the ICICI Bank Savings Account of National Service Scheme as mentioned in Annexure A and B is being approved under this Government decision.
Due to non-receipt of Utilization Certificates for the year 2017-18 from 16 Universities (Names included in Annexure B), the remaining 10 Universities/ Directorates excluding the said Universities have been sanctioned funds for regular programme for the year 2017-18 (Final installment for all categories) and 2018-19 (First installment for all categories 78% Central share) amounting to Rs. 3,41,44, 916/- and Rs. 2,99,36, 857/- amounting to Rs. 6,40,81, 773/- (Rupees Six Crores Forty Lakhs One Thousand Seven Hundred and Seventy Three only). Universities having balance of funds for the year 2017-18 are being allowed to disburse the funds by adjusting the same from the dues of 2018-19 (details of dues to Universities are given in Annexure A and B, annexed with Annexure A and B).
