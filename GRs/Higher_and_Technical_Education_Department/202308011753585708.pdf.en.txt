The proposal:
The Council of Architecture, New Delhi, the apex authority for architecture course, has provided a list of institutions in the state which have been approved to start Diploma Architecture courses from the academic year 2023-24 as well as a letter of recognition to the Directorate of Technical Education. 21.07.2023 has been provided.
As of the academic year 2021-22, Vastu Shastra courses were being approved by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi and the approval of the Council of Architecture was voluntary. The Council of Architecture, New Delhi has made it mandatory to obtain recognition of Diploma/ Degree and Post Graduate Degree in Architecture from the academic year 2021-22. The Council of Architecture vide its letter dated 17.6.2022 has published the guidelines for Diploma in Architecture on its website.
The curriculum includes standards and norms for duration of the course, mode of qualifying examination, staff, equipment, curriculum etc.
The Director, Directorate of Technical Education has submitted a proposal to the Government through a letter in this regard for grant of Government approval for starting Diploma in Architecture course on non-subsidized basis in the existing unaided course institution in the State from the academic year 2023-24 as per the approval of the Council of Architecture, New Delhi.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval given by the Council of Architecture, New Delhi, the apex authority for architecture course and as proposed by the Directorate of Technical Education, from the academic year 2023-24, the existing unaided architecture course in the State shall be offered on non-aided basis in the Institute.

The 21 institutions listed in Annexure A are being given Government approval for starting new Diploma Architecture courses as indicated against their names subject to the following terms and conditions.
Terms and Conditions- 1) The approval shall be on a perpetual basis without subsidy.
(2) In this case, the institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the Council of Architecture, New Delhi and the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983.
3) The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be limited as mentioned against their name in the prospectus.
(iv) The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the Council of Architecture, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
5) The institution concerned must be affiliated to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for these diploma courses.
In this case, it shall be obligatory for the institution concerned to fulfill all the conditions prescribed by the Council of Architecture, New Delhi from time to time and the guidelines prescribed by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai from time to time.
7) The fees for the courses will be fixed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, Maharashtra State, Mumbai/ Authority as directed by the Government from time to time.
8) The institution shall take action as per the rules for obtaining approval of the Council of Architecture, New Delhi for the extension of these courses.
The Principals of the respective institutions shall submit an undertaking to the Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai that the institution will be fully responsible for providing all the facilities as per the standards of Council of Architecture, New Delhi. Only after this, the directorate has to give consent to these institutions to start the course.
10) The concerned institution shall complete the other legal matters by paying the required processing fee and accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai.
11) Admission to the said Professional Diploma Courses shall be made as per the rules/ procedures for admission to the respective courses approved by the Government.
12) If the fees charged by the institutions concerned exceed the tuition fees prescribed by the Fee Regulatory Authority/ Authority specified by the Government from time to time, action will be taken against those institutions as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
