The proposal:
Yashwantrao Chavan Maharashtra Open University, Nashik University was established by the Maharashtra Legislative Council Act No. 20 respectively. 01.07.1989. The University is recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) and has jurisdiction over the entire state of Maharashtra. The university has 8 regional centres located at Amravati, Nagpur, Aurangabad, Mumbai, Nashik, Pune, Kolhapur and Nanded. 1600 study centers are controlled by 8 departmental centers of the university. About 6 million students study at the university. There are a total of 267 study centres in Pune, Satara and Solapur under the Pune Divisional Centre, with 160, 36 and 71 respectively, with a total intake of up to 1 lakh students. Considering the accessibility of the students and to decentralize the work, there is a need for a sub-center to provide facilities to the students, so it was proposed to construct a separate sub-center and library building at Baramati for the existing Satara and Solapur districts under the Pune Divisional Center.
