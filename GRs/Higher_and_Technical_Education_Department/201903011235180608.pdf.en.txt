GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: As per the Government of India, Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, vide letter dated 16.8.2016 and 9.9.2016, with effect from April, 2016, the Central shareholding in the National Service Scheme (NSS) has become 100% and the scheme has become centrally administered. A separate savings account has been opened in the bank in which funds have been received from the Centre through ECS. According to the above letter of the Central Government, the funds have been deposited in the separate savings account of the National Service Scheme without directly going to the state. As per the decision of the government, this fund is distributed to the universities for the programs of the National Service Scheme. The funds for disbursement of grants-in-aid (conditional) for the year 2017-18 (final instalment for all categories) and 2018-19 (2nd instalment Central Share for all categories) to the respective Universities are being sanctioned from the ICICI Bank Savings Account of National Service Scheme as mentioned in Annexure A.

The University shall also submit to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai and to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-2, Nagpur and to this Department or its representative all accounts of grants under the National Service Scheme for inspection.
