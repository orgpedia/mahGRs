The National Education Policy, 2020 envisages (i) planning and preparing a roadmap for migration from the existing 3 year curriculum to 4 year curriculum, (ii) creating a uniform academic curriculum, (iii) use of constructive pedagogy instead of generic pedagogy, (iv) holistic development of students.

Under the National Education Policy, 2020, the following members have been included in the committee constituted under the decision of the Government in the above context: (i) to plan and prepare a roadmap for the transition from the existing 3 year curriculum to 4 year curriculum; (ii) to prepare a uniform academic curriculum; (iii) to use constructive pedagogy instead of general pedagogy; and (iv) to prepare a personalized education roadmap for the holistic development of students.

Government Supplementary Paper No.: NEP-2022/ PR No.105/ VISHI-3,
(1) The Committee shall make necessary common rules/ amendments in the existing rules for providing dual degrees and creating participatory mode of education.
(2) The Committee shall devise a standard/ specific mechanism for developing a methodology for converting Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) into average scores for all the Universities. This approach will be based on a well-designed and accepted approach across the world.
(3) In line with the implementation of the Choice Based Credit System by all the universities in Maharashtra, the committee will formulate guidelines for the use of uniform terms by all the universities in the state.
(4) The Committee shall develop a mechanism for awarding dual degrees, linking the courses with vocational skill-based academic degree courses, transfer of credit points.
