Preamble: As per the letter dated 21.06.2023 from the Member Secretary, All India Council for Technical Education vide Reference No.3 dated 21.06.2023, Aditya College of Architecture, Borivali (Pt. A new Bachelor of Applied Arts (BFA) course in a private unaided institution has been approved to be started from the academic year 2023-24 on a non-subsidized basis with an intake capacity of 30 students. The facilities required to start this new course have been examined by a two-member committee at the level of the Directorate of Arts and the proposal for the same has been received under the letter of reference (4) along with the recommendations. Aditya College of Architecture, Borivali. The government was considering to start a new degree course in applied arts (BFA Applied Arts) in a private unaided institution in Mumbai. The following decision has been taken:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Pursuant to the approval accorded vide letter dated 21st June, 2023 from the Member Secretary, All India Council for Technical Education vide Reference No.3, Aditya College of Architecture, Borivali (P. BFA (Applied Arts) is a private unaided institute in Mumbai.

The courses are being approved on a non-subsidized basis with an intake capacity of 30 students, commencing from the academic year 2023-24, subject to the following terms and conditions: 1) The approval shall be on a perpetual basis without subsidy.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education as well as prescribed under the Government Decision dated 21st May, 1983.
The maximum intake capacity for this course will be 30 students.
4. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the formalities and documentation will be done satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Directorate of Arts, Maharashtra State.
For affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.
6. Required registration fee to be remitted by the institution concerned. Also, other legal matters should be settled by depositing the accumulated deposit in a joint account.
The admission to this degree course will be done as per the admission rules/ procedure approved by the government.
Any institution found charging more than the prescribed tuition fee by the Fee Regulatory Authority will be liable for action under the Maharashtra Education Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the rules of the government. Salaries and allowances should be paid to them. Strict action will be taken on the salary and allowances of teachers and other employees, as well as if complaints are received from students to the government.
The books, journals required for the library in the institution must be purchased as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students will get all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). Library, classrooms, etc. 2) need to be available.
11.R. In respect of Ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
