Introduction: The Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 has been published in the Gazette on 28th December, 2016. Section 32 of the Act provides: All Government institutions of higher education and other higher education institutions receiving aid from the Government shall reserve not less than five per cent seats for persons with benchmark disabilities.
2) The person with benchmark disabilities shall be given an upper age relaxation of five years for admission in institutions of higher education.
As per the provisions of the said Act of the Central Government, 5% seats are required to be reserved for persons/ students with significant disabilities for admission to Government and non-Government aided institutions running courses in Pedagogy, Physical Education and Law under the Directorate of Higher Education.

The government's decision:
As per the provisions of Section 32 (1) of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the Government has decided to reserve 5% seats for persons with significant disabilities in Government and non-Government aided institutions running Pedagogy, Physical Education and Law courses under the Directorate of Higher Education. The decision should be implemented at the higher education level.
