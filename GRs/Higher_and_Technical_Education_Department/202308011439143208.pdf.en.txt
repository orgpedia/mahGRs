The proposal:
The Director, Directorate of Technical Education, New Delhi has submitted a proposal to the Government through a letter in this regard for grant of Government approval for starting of new institutions of Diploma courses in Pharmacy in the State on non-subsidized basis, starting of new courses on non-subsidized basis in existing unaided and non-government aided institutions, change of address of existing institutions, etc. from the academic year 2023-24, as per the approval given by Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi.

Terms and Conditions- 1. The approval shall be on a perpetual basis without subsidy.
In this case, the institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983.
The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be limited as mentioned against their names in the prospectus.
4. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
The concerned institution must be affiliated to Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for these diploma courses.
The Principals of the respective institutions shall submit an undertaking to the Director, Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai that the institution will be fully responsible for providing all the facilities as per the standards of Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. Only after this, the directorate has to give consent to these institutions to start the course.
7. The institution concerned shall complete other legal formalities by depositing the requisite processing fee and accumulated deposits in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai.
Admission to the said Professional Diploma Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective courses approved by the Government.
The fees for the courses will be fixed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, Maharashtra State, Mumbai/ Authority as may be specified by the Government from time to time.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the prescribed tuition fees by the Fee Regulatory Authority/ Authority as directed by the Government from time to time, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
The books, journals required by the library in the institution must be purchased as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE).
