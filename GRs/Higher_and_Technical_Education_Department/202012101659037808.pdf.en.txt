Read:- Government Resolution No: PRI-5420/ No. 126/ Mashi-2, Dy. 14th September, 2020.
As per the decision of the government, a committee has been formed to improve the training centers at the State Administrative Vocational Education Institute, Mumbai and the Indian Administrative Service Pre-Training Center, Nagpur, Amravati, Nashik, Aurangabad and Kolhapur.
The government was considering the issue of reconstituting the committee.
The government's decision:
The state government has decided to reconstitute the committee formed for the improvement of the State Administrative Vocational Education Institute, Mumbai and the Indian Administrative Service Pre-Training Center, Nagpur, Amravati, Nashik, Aurangabad and Kolhapur.
Accordingly, the composition of the Committee constituted under the relevant Government Decision dated 14.09.2020 is being amended as follows:

Government Resolution No: PRI-5420/ PR No. 126/ Mashi-2,
(1) To undertake a SWOT analysis of the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and challenges of the training centres and to suggest improvements in teaching-learning and evaluation processes and upgradation of physical facilities, library resources and technical facilities.
(2) To make suggestions for the implementation of the recommendations/ findings of the Bongirwar Committee and for the establishment of new training centres in the State.
(3) The Secretary, Department of Higher and Technical Education shall preside. To make suggestions for implementation of the decisions taken in the meeting dated 26.09.2019.
(4) To make suggestions regarding the remuneration of the visiting faculty of the said training centres.
(5) To give information about the admission process of such training centers.
(6) To suggest the constitution of an Advisory Committee in the said Training Centre.
(7) To make recommendations for the evaluation of such training centres.
(8) To suggest for signing of MoU with private training institute to improve the quality of training in these training centres.
(9) Suggestions for strengthening the pattern interview training.
(10) To make suggestions for the effective implementation of the merit-based special scholarship scheme.
(11) To create awareness and interest among the students about the Civil Services Examination of the Union Public Service Commission and to make suggestions to increase the inclination of the students towards the said examination.
(12) To recommend introduction of the subject of Civil Services Examination of the Central Public Service Commission at all non-agricultural, agricultural and deemed universities and affiliated colleges (traditional and professional courses) level.
The committee shall submit its report to the Department of Higher and Technical Education within three months.
The meeting allowance, travel allowance and daily allowance shall be admissible to the non-official members of the said committee as per the provisions of the Government Resolution No. 2012/ No. 1/ Service-5, dated 24th May, 2012. The expenditure on this allowance should be met from the grant sanctioned for the financial year 2020-21 for the training centre.
