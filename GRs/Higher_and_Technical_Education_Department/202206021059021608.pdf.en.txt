Introduction: The Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India has implemented the National Education Policy-2020. Therefore, with a view to implement the National Education Policy-2020 in the State, a study of this policy and submission of report to the State Government is subject to reference.
As per the Government decision of October, 2020, a Task Force Group was constituted under the Chairmanship of Dr. Raghunath Mashelkar, former Director General, Council of Scientific and Industrial Research, New Delhi. The committee sent its report to the government. It was released on June 30, 2021. In order to facilitate the implementation of the policy in the State, the recommendations of the report have been categorized into three phases based on the factors such as their urgency, impact, ease of implementation, investment and funding requirement, priority in the course of action, i.e. (i) programme requiring immediate/ minimum resources; (ii) programme requiring medium/ medium resources; and (iii) programme requiring long-term/ large-scale funding.

The functions of the said Committee shall be as follows: (1) The Committee shall prepare a report on the financial position of at least two Universities after making a critical analysis of the financial strength of the Universities, aided and unaided colleges. The report takes into account the number of students, vacancies, income from fees and other sources, expenditure on infrastructure, and measures to increase financial resources in order to reduce unproductive expenditure and increase economic efficiency.

(2) Explain the overall position regarding the financial position/ financial resources of the universities/ educational institutions. It will also formulate measures/ action plan to meet the shortfall in fund requirement.
(3) The University shall suggest sources of financial income with a view to enable funding.
(4) To study the needs of higher education through public-private partnerships and suggest measures to make universities financially viable while providing new educational opportunities to students.
(5) Prepares a report on financial viability of higher education by conducting a study on the needs of higher education through public-private partnership.
(6) The Committee shall review the educational concessions available for education of socially and economically disadvantaged groups, with a view to enhancing their effectiveness, and to provide and reserve funds for providing financial incentives to these groups.
It will also formulate guidelines on how to ensure equal access to quality education.
(7) Analyse the admission rate of backward class students and propose measures or policies for how to increase and maintain the GER of this component.
(8) The Committee shall also formulate a policy or roadmap for an efficient mechanism for optimum allocation and utilization of funds reserved for socially and economically disadvantaged groups.
(9) The Committee will also suggest measures on how to make the students deprived of higher education aware of the opportunities available in the field of higher education, the cost of pursuing higher education, financial difficulties etc.
