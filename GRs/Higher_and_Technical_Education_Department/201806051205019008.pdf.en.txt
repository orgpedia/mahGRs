The proposal:
The event was held on 7th February 2018 at Raj Bhavan, Mumbai. The Governor and the Chief Minister. The meeting was attended by the Chief Minister, Minister (Higher and Technical Education), Additional Chief Secretary (Higher and Technical Education), Principal Secretary (Information and Technology), Vice-Chancellor, Mumbai University. Students and parents were unnecessarily upset as the teachers tagged on the On Screen Marking (OSM) system did not participate in the discussion and the evaluation of the answer sheets could not be done on time. They also faced difficulties in getting higher education. Considering the historical position and importance of the University of Mumbai in the context of higher education, it was felt necessary to conduct an academic audit of the colleges/ institutions affiliated to the University of Mumbai and their courses, faculties, courses, subjects, batches, etc.
The Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016 provides for the teaching, learning, efficient and sensitive administration, scientific and technological management of higher education within the jurisdiction of the university. Further, considering the feasibility of the management and the standards of academic performance of the colleges, faculties and subjects as determined from time to time, the University is also responsible for determining the conditions of affiliation and accreditation of the colleges and institutions and to assure itself by periodically or otherwise assessing that those conditions have been met. In this backdrop, the government was considering to take a decision to audit about 778 colleges/ institutes affiliated to the University of Mumbai and their courses, faculties, subjects, units, etc. through a tri-state system.
Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
The government's decision:
As per the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016, the following Committees/ Task Force are being constituted to audit about 778 colleges/ institutes affiliated to the University of Mumbai and their courses, faculties, subjects, units, etc. through a tripartite arrangement.

The Task Force Group will examine all the colleges/ institutions affiliated to the University and their courses, subjects, faculties, units, etc. on the following points and submit a detailed report to the State Level Committee based on the findings.
(a) To check the availability of physical facilities and amenities in the affiliated colleges and institutions as per the norms prescribed by the competent authority such as UGC/ AICTE/ Bar Council of India/ Council of Architecture/ NCTE etc.
(b) To examine whether there is any course-wise availability of qualified teachers in the affiliated colleges as per the criteria prescribed by the competent authority.
Affiliation and renewal of colleges/ institutions and their courses, subjects, faculties etc. are being done as per the provisions prescribed and the requisite fee is being paid/ collected accordingly or how.
To recommend the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for colleges/ institutions and their courses, subjects, faculties, units, etc. by analysing the modalities for affiliation and renewal.
To examine whether or not Government, Aided, Unaided and Perpetually Unaided Colleges etc. have obtained accreditation through NAAC/ NBA assessment.
