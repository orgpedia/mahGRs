Preamble: The National Council for Teacher Education (Regulation Norms and Procedure) Regulations, 2014 have come into force vide notification dated 28/11/2014 of the National Council for Teacher Education. As per the provisions of the notification, all the teaching colleges have been asked to start composite courses in future.
The reference No. As per the decision of the government, a committee has been formed to recommend the introduction of composite courses in government teachers' colleges in the state as per the revised norms of NCTE. On the same lines, the government was considering to set up a revised committee by amending the said committee to recommend the introduction of composite courses in non-government aided teaching colleges as per the revised norms of NCTE.
Government Decision: It is being approved to constitute the following committee to recommend the introduction of composite courses in government and non-government aided teachers colleges in the state as per the revised norms of NCTE.

Committee's mandate: (1) The above Committee may consider the revised norms of NCTE and the facilities available in Government/ Non-Government Teacher Colleges, teaching/ non-teaching staff, teaching staff required for starting new courses, instead of starting new courses, it may be possible to run composite courses with the help of traditional degree colleges in the area.
Whether or not, the prescribed norms of NCTE can be met in this regard or not, whether there is a demand for composite courses among the students at present, which composite courses can provide more job opportunities in the future, which model has been used in other states, all these aspects have been studied.
To submit detailed recommendations to the Government regarding the introduction of composite courses in Government/ Non-Government Teachers Colleges.
Due to poor response from students for admission to Ed/ M.Eng courses, a large number of seats are lying vacant. Therefore, the committee should submit a report on how to restructure the existing courses in these schools, how to close the colleges where a large number of seats remain vacant due to continuous admission of students.
(3) To submit a report as to whether or how the infrastructure and teaching/ non-teaching staff, which are not being used due to lack of student intake, can be utilised for other training, such as training of pre-primary teachers, other training classes, etc.
(4) To give suggestions on the problems of Government/ Non-Government Aided Teachers Colleges and the measures to be taken at the Government level accordingly.
