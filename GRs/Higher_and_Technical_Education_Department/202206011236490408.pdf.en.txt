Introduction: The Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India has implemented the National Education Policy-2020. Therefore, a Task Force Group was constituted under the Chairmanship of Dr. Raghunath Mashelkar, former Director General, Council of Scientific and Industrial Research, New Delhi, vide Government Resolution dated 16.10.2020, to study the National Education Policy-2020 in the State and submit a report to the State Government.
The committee sent its report to the government. It was released on June 30, 2021. In order to facilitate the implementation of the policy in the State, the recommendations of the report have been categorized into three stages, considering their urgency, impact, ease of implementation, investment and funding requirement, priority in action, as follows:
(i) A programme requiring immediate/ minimum resources; (ii) a programme requiring medium/ medium resources; (iii) a programme requiring long-term/ large-scale funding.
As per the recommendations of the Task Force Committee on National Education Policy, 2020, Discussed in the Cabinet meeting held on 27th January, 2022. It has been decided to constitute a working group under the chairmanship of the Chief Minister and to constitute various committees to prepare a definite roadmap for the implementation of this policy.
Accordingly, the Government decided to constitute the following Committee.
Government Decision: As per the recommendations of Dr. Mashelkar Committee, the action proposed by the Department, as per the approval given in the Cabinet meeting, under the National Education Policy 2020, the Department of Teacher Education has decided to implement the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

The functions of the Committee shall be as follows:
(1) The Committee shall prepare guidelines on the annual performance appraisal of teachers after studying the notifications issued from time to time by apex bodies like University Grants Commission, All India Council for Technical Education, etc.
(2) To improve the quality of education, the Commission shall make recommendations as to what should be included in the performance appraisal.
(3) The Cell will review the role and functioning of the Internal Quality Assurance Cell in line with the New Education Policy and suggest measures to further strengthen it.
(4) The University Grants Commission shall consider the guidelines issued from time to time in this regard and make recommendations to the Government.
(5) The Committee shall also consider the recommendations relating to the above mentioned points in the report of Dr. Mashelkar Committee and fix the guidelines.
The committee has to submit its report to the government within three months.
