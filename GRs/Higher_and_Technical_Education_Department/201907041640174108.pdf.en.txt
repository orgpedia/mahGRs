Preamble: The APH 2019-20 published by All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi provides for merger of institutions in a locality run by the same management as per 2.10. (page 47). The Government has not issued no-objection certificate to Shivajirao S. Jondhale College of Pharmacy, Asangaon of Vighnaharta Trust for merging the institutions of the same area running under the same management as proposed by the Director. The All India Council for Technical Education has approved the merger of the two institutions vide letter dated 30/4/2019. Accordingly, the Director, Technical Education vide letter dated 24/6/2019 has requested the Government to approve the merger of these two institutions.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: As approved by the All India Council for Technical Education, the merger of the following institutions is being approved by the Government subject to the following terms and conditions:

1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's eligibility for this course will remain as mentioned above.
(3) The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Post Graduate Degree Courses will be done as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
7) The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the norms. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
9) Regarding the ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, will have to be strictly implemented.
10) Apart from this, other terms and conditions set by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai and the concerned university will remain applicable.
