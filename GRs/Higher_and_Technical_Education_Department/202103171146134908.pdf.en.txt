Procedure to be followed in respect of deductible excess/ less contribution: 36) The Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall be responsible for recovering the prescribed 10% (Basic Pay+ Dearness Allowance thereon) contribution amount monthly from the salary dues of the employee and depositing the employer's 10% or equivalent contribution monthly as amended by the Finance Department from time to time. The Drawing and Disbursing Officer will be responsible for adjusting the excess amount of contribution in the employee's account in the subsequent month. The Drawing and Disbursing Officer will be responsible for depositing the required amount in the next month.
It will be the responsibility of the employee to ensure that the contributions being credited to the National Pension Scheme account are correct every month. If any error is found in it, the employee must give a written statement to the Drawing and Disbursing Officer. The employee who does not take such action will not be able to file any complaint (legal action) in the future.
It shall be the responsibility of the Drawing and Disbursing Officer to ensure that in case of unauthorised absence, extraordinary leave of an employee, the payment of salary of the employee is not withdrawn from the servicing system. It shall be the responsibility of the Drawing and Disbursing Officer to ensure strict compliance with the provisions of Rules 264 and 265 of the Maharashtra Treasury Rules, 1968. In case of non-compliance of all these rules, the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officer and the concerned Joint Director will be eligible for administrative action.
The Annual Return of Permanent Pension Account Number (PRAN) of the employee will be sent to the concerned employee by the Central Record Keeping Agency every year. Also, the Central Record Keeping Agency will make available the details of the amount deposited in the account of the concerned employee to the registered employees on its website at any time.
