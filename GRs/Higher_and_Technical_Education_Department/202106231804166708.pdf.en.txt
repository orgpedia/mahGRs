Introduction: Regarding the agricultural admission process and the upcoming agricultural education policy in the state, Ma. The meeting was held on March 17, 2021, under the chairmanship of the Deputy Chief Minister. In the meeting, it was directed to consider the agricultural admission process in the state and the upcoming agricultural education policy as well as the scores obtained in the Common Entrance Test (CET) and the marks obtained in the Class 12 of the Higher Secondary Education Board (HSCB) without considering only the marks of the Common Entrance Test (CET) in the admission to professional courses under the Higher and Technical Education Department and the Agriculture Department of the state.
Accordingly, the Government was considering the formation of a committee to consider and make recommendations to the Government regarding the marks obtained in the Common Entrance Test (CET) and the marks obtained in the Class 12 of the Higher Secondary Education Board (HSC Board) in admissions to professional courses under the Department of Higher and Technical Education and the Department of Agriculture in the State.

Government circular:
A committee is being constituted to consider the marks obtained in the Common Entrance Test (CET) for admission to professional courses under the Department of Higher and Technical Education and the Department of Agriculture in the State and to determine the ratio of the marks obtained in the Class 12 of the Higher Secondary Education Board (HSC Board) and to make recommendations to the Government after making a comparative study of the examination methods in other States.

(2) The said Committee shall determine the ratio of the marks obtained through the Common Entrance Test for admission to professional courses in the State and the marks obtained within the scope of class 12 of the Board of Higher Secondary Education and other Boards, and on that basis, determine the minimum marks for admission and make recommendations to the Government. While making these recommendations, the government should make a comparative study of the methodology and marks used for admission to professional courses in other states of the country. The committee has to submit its recommendations to the government before July 31, 2021.
The above Committee shall hold its meetings in compliance with the directions issued by the State Government from time to time in view of COVID-19.
