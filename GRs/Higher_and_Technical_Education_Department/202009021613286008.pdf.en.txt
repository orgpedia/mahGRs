GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi vide its letter dated 15th June, 2020 in the aforesaid Reference No. The existing intake of students for the Bachelor of Arts (F.A. Applied Art) course is being increased from 60 to 30 from the academic year 2020-21 subject to the following terms and conditions. :
1) The grant will be on a grant-in-aid basis.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education as well as prescribed under the Government Decision dated 21st May, 1983.
The maximum intake capacity for this course will be 90 students.
4. The institution shall have to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the formalities and documents concerned should be completed satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Directorate of Arts, Maharashtra State.
For affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016.
6. Required registration fee to be remitted by the institution concerned. Also, other legal matters should be settled by depositing the accumulated deposit in a joint account.
The admission to this degree course will be done as per the admission rules/ procedure approved by the government.
Any institution found charging more than the prescribed tuition fee by the Fee Regulatory Authority will be liable for action under the Maharashtra Education Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the rules of the government. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the teachers and other staff members if any complaint is received from the students.
The books, journals required for the library in the institution must be purchased as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students will get all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). Library, classrooms, etc. 2) need to be available.
