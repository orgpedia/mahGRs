Introduction: The All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, under reference No. 1 here. As per the letter dated 30th April, 2019, a total of 30 intake capacity for Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D.) course at Dattakala College of Pharmacy, Pune has been approved for the academic year 2019-20. However, the institute is not recognised by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. However, despite not having the approval of the government, the institute had admitted 15 students in the academic year 2019-2020 for the Pharm.D. course. It is approved by Pharmacy Council of India.
Writ Petition No. 92515/2020 was filed before the Hon'ble High Court of Bombay. In the petition

Government decision: The High Court of Bombay vide its interim order dated 21/9/2020 in Writ Petition No. 92515/2020, vide its interim order dated 21/9/2020, subject to the approval of the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, hereby grants approval to the Government for starting the first class with a capacity of 15 students for the academic year 2019-20 and the second class with a capacity of 15 students for the academic year 2020-21 only for the course Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D.) in the Dattakala College of Pharmacy, Pune, subject to the following terms and conditions:
These terms and conditions are as follows: 1) The approval shall be on a perpetual non-recourse basis.
(2) It shall be binding on the institution concerned to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India from time to time in the matter.
(3) The institution concerned shall meet other legal requirements by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
If the institution charges more than the prescribed tuition fee as per the provisions of the relevant Act, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(5) The Institute shall appoint full time faculty and other technical staff as per the norms prescribed for the conduct of the course and they shall be paid salary and allowances as per the rules of the Government. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
6) The books, journals required by the library in the institution must be purchased as per the prevailing standards of the University Grants Commission. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students.
Also, all educational facilities are provided to the students as per the standard. laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
