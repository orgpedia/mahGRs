Introduction: The University Grants Commission (UGC) has created a new post of' Professor of Practice' to engage experts from industry and various professional fields in the stream of education as recommended in the New Education Policy. Accordingly, under Reference No. 1 dated 30.09.2022," Guidelines for engaging professors of practice in universities and colleges" have been given in line with the recommendations in the New Education Policy 2020.
According to the new education policy, emphasis on skill-based learning is necessary to bring transformational changes in education. The post of' Professor of Practice' will be useful in identifying the needs of the industrial sector in higher education and incorporating business education in regular education, for which collaboration with industrial/ academic institutions will be required. Accordingly, the Director, Higher Education, Pune has submitted a proposal to the Government under Reference No. 2, Professor of Practices in University and Colleges, to formulate a policy of Professor of Practices in Government Colleges/ Institutions to engage experts from industry and various professional fields in the stream of education.
Accordingly, as per the guidelines of the University Grants Commission, the matter of approving the appointment of" Professor of Practice" in the ratio of 10% of the sanctioned posts of faculty in government institutions under the Directorate of Higher Education was under the consideration of the Government.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: As per the" Guidelines for Engaging Professors of Practice in Universities and Colleges" given by the University Grants Commission vide letter dated 30.09.2022, in pursuance of the recommendation in the New Education Policy 2020, the Government has decided to approve the appointment of" Professors of Practice" in the ratio of 10% of the sanctioned posts of Professors in Government Institutions under the Directorate of Higher Education.
Accordingly, the following guidelines are prescribed for appointment of Professor of Practice in Government Institutions under the Directorate of Higher Education (a) Qualification for appointment of Professor of Practice- 1. Engineering, Science, Technology, Industry, Media, Sociology, Literature, Architectural Services, Social Development, Panchayati Raj, Rural Development, Water Conservation, Organic Farming, Judicial

Specialists who have made distinguished contributions in various fields such as business, town planning, gender budgeting/ planning, tribal development and public administration, with at least 15 years of experience (preferably senior level service/ experience) in a particular profession, will be eligible for the post of Professor of Practice.
2. The qualifications prescribed for the post of Professor shall not be necessary for the post of Professor of Practice. However, certain skills must be acquired to perform the duties and responsibilities of the position.
The number of Professors of Practice in Higher Educational Institutions should not exceed 10% of the sanctioned posts.
Duties and Responsibilities: (b) Duties and Responsibilities: The faculty appointed under the scheme is required to teach at least one subject. These professors will be required to teach the third and final year students and take the academic load as prescribed for regular faculty. Students will also be required to complete projects, assessments, and semester work.
Participate in course modifications and new course design as required.
To create new courses and teach students as per the policy of the institution.
To encourage students to take up research and self-employment.
To enhance interaction between academic institutions and industry.
To carry out research projects jointly with faculty of the Institute and to provide consultancy services to the industry.
To conduct activities like workshops, seminars, special lectures jointly with the faculty of the institution.
General Terms and Conditions: 1. Appointments to the post of Professor of Practice under this scheme shall be for a fixed period.
(2) The appointments will not result in any change in the pattern of the original sanctioned posts in the institutions. Also, these posts will not have any effect on the appointments of regular teachers.
The appointments cannot be made from among the teachers who are in regular teaching service and have retired.
(d) Types of Appointments: As prescribed by the University Grants Commission, appointments to the post of Professor of Practice can be made in any of the following ways: 1. Funded by Industries 2. Own Resources of Higher Educational Institutions 3. On Honorary Basis

The expenditure for the post of Professor of Practice will be met from the funds available at the industry sponsored/ institution level. The government is not providing any aid in this regard.
Mode of selection for the post of Professor of Practice: The Vice-Chancellor of the University/ Director or Principal of the Institute shall invite nominations for the posts of Professor of Practice from eminent experts by issuing an advertisement in the newspaper.
Other experts who are interested and eligible may also be nominated or the expert may submit the nomination form to the Vice-Chancellor of the University/ Director or Principal of the institution along with their personal information and information about their substantial contribution to the institution of higher education.
Selection Committee for appointment: The selection committee shall consist of two senior faculty members of the Institute (in the concerned subject) and one eminent expert member from outside the Institute.
On the basis of the recommendations of the said Committee, the Academic Council and the Executive Council or the Statutory Bodies in the Institute shall take a decision regarding the election.
(c) Tenure: 1. The tenure of the appointed Professor of Practice shall initially be for one year.
The concerned institution will evaluate and decide on the completion of the first term of the working faculty or on the extension of the term after that.
3. The concerned institution will decide the evaluation process separately for grant of extension of tenure as per the demand and contribution of the experts appointed as Professor of Practice.
The period of extension shall be for a maximum period of 3 years. In exceptional circumstances, an extension of one year may be granted. However, in no case the total service will not exceed 4 years.
