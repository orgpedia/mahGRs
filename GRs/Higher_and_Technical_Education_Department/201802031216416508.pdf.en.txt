The proposal:
Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan launched to strengthen primary education in the State
With the success of Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA), the Government of India has decided to implement Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) in the 12th and 13th Five Year Plans as an umbrella scheme to empower the quality and foundation of higher education in the form of a mission mode. The National Mission on Higher Education (NMHE), launched by the Government of India, aims to provide quality higher education in Maharashtra. The decision was taken at the Cabinet meeting held on 21.04.2015. In pursuance of the decision taken by the State Cabinet, a State Project Directorate has been set up for effective implementation of the scheme in the State. Under the scheme, the Centre and the state have decided to share 60:40 subsidy for non-agriculture universities and government institutions. Central Government for State Project Directorate. As on 31/03/2017, 60% of the Central share for Preparatory Grants has been sanctioned and issued vide Reference No. 2 In respect of funds distributed under 40% State share of Rs. 1,83,36, 667/- to the State Project Director, Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan.
