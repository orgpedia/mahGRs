Considering the above background, a Tender Evaluation Committee was constituted under the Chairmanship of the Vice-Chancellor, Savitribai Phule Pune University, Pune, to select a professional body to advise and serve the universities in raising the rating of public non-agricultural universities in the state. The tender evaluation committee was under the consideration of the government to appoint a recommended institution for the work of raising the rating of public non-agriculture universities in the state.
The government has decided to appoint the Indian Center for Academic Ranking and Excellence, recommended by the Tender Evaluation Committee, to raise the rating of 13 non-agricultural universities and 100 colleges in the state. The scheme is being approved by the Government at a cost of Rs. 30.00 lakh. The Indian Centre for Academic Ranking and Excellence will work to improve the standards of 13 universities and 100 affiliated colleges in the state for a period of 5 years.
As per the terms and conditions specified in the tender, the Indian Centre for Academic Rankings and Excellence (iCare) will submit a statement of scope of work/ framework, stages of work and activities to be carried out under the project and the fee to be allowed accordingly as per Annexure A.
The list of 13 Universities under the Scheme is at Annexure B. The colleges under the university will be selected by the respective universities.
The draft Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to be entered into by all public universities with the Indian Centre for Academic Rankings and Excellence (iCare) for the above purpose shall be as per Annexure-C.
