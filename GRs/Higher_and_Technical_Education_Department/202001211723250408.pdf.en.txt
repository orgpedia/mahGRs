The untimely rains caused by the' Kyarr" and' Maha" cyclones in October-November 2019 have damaged agricultural crops and perennial crops in 325 talukas of 34 districts of the state. The government has taken a decision to provide special assistance to the affected people at special rates. It was released on November 18, 2019. Under this decision, the government has given exemption in school and college examination fees to the children of farmers who have suffered losses to their farmers. The concerned administrative department should issue detailed orders in this regard as per the need and the necessary funds should be allocated for it and it should be waived. As per the above decision of the Department of Revenue and Forest, the following orders are being issued to all the Universities as well as to the Directorate of Arts to provide the benefit of" Examination Fee Waiver" concession to the eligible students in the Arts Colleges under the control of the Directorate of Arts:
As per the decision of the Government of the Department of Revenue and Forest dated November 18, 2019, the examination fees of the current academic year should be waived for the students whose crops and perennial crops have been damaged in 325 taluks of 34 districts of the state who have enrolled for courses under the Directorate of Arts in the academic year 2019-20.
2. The examination fee should be refunded by the concerned institution to the students from whom the examination fee has been charged till the date of this Government Circular.
The benefit of this concession will be limited only to students belonging to the economically backward category. Students who are eligible for scholarship under Rajarshi Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Tuition Fee Scholarship Scheme are given 50% of the examination fee in the form of scholarship, so only 50% of the examination fee should be waived under this scheme. 100% exam fee waiver should be given to the remaining students whose family income is up to Rs. 8.00 lakh per annum.
