The government's decision:
As per the procedure prescribed under Section 109 (4) (d) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016 and the Government Decision, dated 13.09.2017, the non-agricultural universities in the state have submitted proposals for new courses / subjects / special level subjects, additional batches, new faculties for the academic year 2025-26 to the Government for approval. The proposals were scrutinized as per the norms laid down by the Government decision in the context. A total of 11 new courses / special level subjects / subjects mentioned in the accompanying prospectus-A *, a total of 1 faculty mentioned in prospectus-B, a total of 2 additional batches mentioned in prospectus-C * are being sanctioned on a perpetual non-grant basis from the academic year 2025-26 onwards, subject to the following terms and conditions, as per the provisions of Section 109 (4) (d) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016, to the existing college as per the authority of the Government.

Terms and Conditions:
Aided colleges which have been sanctioned AGM courses / subjects / special level subjects, additional batches, new faculties are always on a non-grant basis, and in pursuance of this recognition, such colleges cannot demand teaching and non-teaching staff on a grant basis.
The colleges which have been sanctioned new courses / subjects / special level subjects, additional batches, new faculties, have issued a letter of assurance to the respective Divisional Joint Directors that they will not demand any grant in future under any circumstances. Submit on a non-judicial stop paper of Rs.500 / -.
In accordance with the approved batches, faculties and courses, colleges are required to appoint faculty and non-teaching staff as per the norms prescribed by the respective university and the educational qualification prescribed by the University Grants Commission, and all other necessary infrastructure is to be provided and ensured by the Divisional Joint Director and ACHAT as per the aforesaid Condition No. The certificate in 2 should be certified and given to the university.
The universities concerned shall not proceed with the grant of affiliation unless the college / institution furnishes a certificate of affiliation or has so obtained the certificate of the Divisional Co-Director and meets the criteria prescribed by the University Grants Commission for grant of affiliation in the AHA of HIATT by Universities Regulation 2009.
The college shall not, under any circumstances, grant admissions in excess of the sanctioned intake capacity.
The University concerned shall be responsible for ensuring the correctness of new courses / subjects / special level subjects, additional batches, NACs and other errors in the proposal of new faculties as approved in the authority conferred under the proviso to Section 109 (4) (d) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016. Similarly, if the error is not corrected within 6 months, the approval will be automatically cancelled. The universities concerned should take necessary action in this regard. In case of cancellation of accreditation for this reason, the student's adjustment should be processed by the respective Joint Director, Higher Education.
Universities should not initiate the process of granting affiliation without reviewing the said Government decision. Universities should send a compliance report on affiliation to the Director, Higher Education, Maharashtra State, Pune and the respective Divisional Joint Director, Higher Education.
