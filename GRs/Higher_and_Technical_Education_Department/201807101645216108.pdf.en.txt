The proposal:
The All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi has issued the above Reference No. 1 In accordance with the approval given by the Director, Technical Education vide Reference No. 2. It has been proposed to the Government to grant approval for starting a new Degree course in Information Technology with intake capacity of 60 in Dr. D. Y. In pursuance of the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, the Government was considering to start a new Degree course in Information Technology with an intake capacity of 60 seats in Dr. D. Y. Patil Institute of Technology, Pimpri, Pune from the academic year 2018-19 as proposed by the Director, Technical Education.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval given by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi, the Government is pleased to grant approval to start a new Degree course in Information Technology with intake capacity of 60 seats in Dr. D.Y. Patil Institute of Technology, Pimpri, Pune from the academic year 2018-19 as proposed by the Director, Technical Education subject to the following conditions:

6. Admission to the said Degree and Post-Graduate Degree Courses shall be made as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned exceed the tuition fees prescribed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the rules of the government. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
The books, journals required by the library in the institution should be purchased as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students.
