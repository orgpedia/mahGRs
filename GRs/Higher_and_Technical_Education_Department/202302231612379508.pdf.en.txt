There are four Government Arts Colleges, namely, College of Art, Mumbai, Sir J. G. College of Applied Arts, Mumbai, Government College of Art and Design, Nagpur and Government College of Art and Design, Aurangabad. The Artist in Residence initiative will bring together all the students of the art institute to exchange their ideas, provide a platform and encouragement to their art. This initiative will also help in promoting the latent talent of the students of the state and provide a platform to their talent. With a view to making the students of the art colleges under the control of the Directorate of Arts aware of the art in the professional field, it was under the consideration of the government to organize a 5-day Artist in Residence initiative in the government colleges for talented artists working in the professional field. Accordingly, the following decision was taken.
The government's decision:
College of Art, Mumbai, Sir J.G.
Artist in Residence programme is being organised in four government arts colleges- Government College of Applied Arts, Mumbai, Government College of Art and Design, Nagpur and Government College of Art and Design, Aurangabad. Students of Government Arts Colleges and Government Aided/ Non-Aided/ Permanent Art Institutions can participate in the programme.
The following roles will be expected from the eminent artists participating in the" Artist in Residence" initiative.
1) In the workshop organized under this initiative, the students should be guided with demonstrations on new approaches and trends related to various topics in the field of art.
2) In the workshop organized under this initiative, the students should be guided about the different styles of art.

3) The workshop organized under this initiative should provide guidance on the latest technologies and their use at the national/ international level in the field of visual arts.
4) The workshop organized under this initiative should provide guidance on how to showcase your art in art galleries at national/ international level.
5) The workshop organized under this initiative should provide guidance on how to market the works of art at the national/ international level.
6) In the workshop organized under this initiative, guidance should be given on what aspects of visual art need to be researched in the future in comparison to the current state of the visual art field.
The" Artist in Residence" initiative should be organized as follows:
1) Artist in Residence should be organized at the beginning of the academic year keeping in mind the annual academic plan of the concerned Government Arts College.
This activity should be carried out 3 times in a school year.
The concerned art institutions should submit a proposal to the Directorate of Arts with the names of the artists to organize the Artist in Residence initiative and the approval of the Directorate of Arts should be taken before the month of July every academic year.
4) The responsibility of selecting the necessary place for the production of art will be of the concerned art college.
5) The concerned art institution will be responsible for providing the artist with canvas/ paper, colour, framing, easels, dunkies and other relevant materials required for the performance.
6) The proceeds from the sale of the exhibited works of the students should be distributed to 50% of the students and 50% to the art institution.
The following conditions will be applicable for the selection of artists for Artist in Residence activities.
While selecting a professional artist for the Artist in Residence initiative, the selection committee should consider the artist's social and educational contributions to the visual arts.
The artist should have at least 15 years of experience in the field of visual arts.
The following selection committee is being constituted to select the artists for the" Artist in Residence" initiative.
