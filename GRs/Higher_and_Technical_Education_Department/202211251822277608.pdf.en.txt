Terms and Conditions: 1) This Agreement shall remain in force.
(2) It shall be binding on the institution concerned to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi in the matter.
3) The institution's intake capacity for the said course shall be as mentioned above.
4) Admission to the said Degree and Post Graduate Degree Courses will be done as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
(5) The following Management Committee should be constituted for the financial and administrative management of this permanent self-financing postgraduate degree course run by the Institute.
Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai Chairman- 2. Joint Director, Technical Education, Divisional Office Member 3. An expert person in the field of finance and accounting- Member 4. Principal, concerned Government College of Pharmacy- Member Secretary.
(6) The teachers and staff appointed for the permanent self-financing post-graduate courses in these government institutions shall not be government employees. Salaries/ allowances and other service related liabilities of these employees will be borne by the respective organization. Also, the organization should not hire permanent employees.
7) The above committee will take action to select the teachers and non-teaching staff to be appointed in the institution as per the prescribed procedure. The provident fund, general provident fund and retirement scheme will not be applicable to the teachers and employees appointed for these self-financed courses. Since these employees are not government employees, the CPF scheme should be implemented for them.
8) The separate bank account of the department of the post-graduate course should be operated jointly by the Principal of the institution and the member secretary of the management committee and one of the remaining members.
9) The management committee should plan the organization properly and fulfill the financial responsibilities. The Government will not provide any financial assistance for the implementation of these courses nor will it be responsible for any liability arising in this regard.
10) Separate facilities should be created for this permanent unaided postgraduate degree course by utilising the development funds available in the institution and the funds received through tuition fees or other means.
11) In the context of ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
Other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the University concerned shall also apply.
