The government's decision:
As proposed by the Director, Technical Education, pursuant to the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, the approval of the Government is being granted for starting new courses, increase in the existing intake capacity, etc. in the existing permanent unaided undergraduate and postgraduate degree institutions of Pharmacy as indicated against the names of the institutions in Annexure' A' from the academic year 2021-2022, subject to the following terms and conditions:
1) The grant will be non-recurring.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education in this regard as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's intake capacity for this course will be as mentioned above.
(3) The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education, New Delhi/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.
4) The institution concerned shall meet the other legalities by depositing the required accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education.
5) Admission to the said Degree and Post Graduate Degree Courses will be done as per the rules/ procedure of admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned are more than the tuition fees prescribed by the fee regulatory authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
(7) The Institute shall appoint a full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. Salaries and allowances should be paid to the employees as per the rules.
Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
8) The library in the institute is required to purchase the required books, journals as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
