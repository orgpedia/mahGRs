The Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016 came into force with effect from March 1, 2017. Keeping in view the increasing pass percentage in higher secondary schools and the inclination of students towards a particular course, so that no student is deprived of the desired higher education, Section 109 (8) of the Act provides for expeditious recognition of additional fresh batches of specified faculties in exceptional circumstances. In the academic year 2022-23, the non-agricultural universities of the state had submitted proposals for additional new batches of specific faculties to the government. The Government Resolution No. The inspection was carried out as per the norms prescribed under NGC-2017/ (128/18)/ Mashi-4, dated 04.07.2017. After the inspection, the government was considering to approve the proposals of new batches in addition to the specific faculties that qualified from the proposals recommended by the universities to the government in an expeditious manner. The government has taken the following decisions:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In the academic year 2022-23, the non-agricultural universities of the state will be able to recruit new batches of eligible faculty from among the proposals recommended to the government under Section 109 (8) of the Maharashtra Public Universities Act, 2016. The existing college is being sanctioned on permanent non-grant basis subject to the following terms and conditions as mentioned in the following statement subject to the terms and conditions laid down under the Government Decision No. 2:
