The proposal:
The Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi has approved to reduce the intake capacity of the ongoing diploma course in the unaided institution of the existing undergraduate pharmacy course in the state to 60 from the academic year 2020-21. Approval for reduction/ reduction of intake capacity of Diploma courses to 60 as indicated in Annexure A attached to the said institutions was under consideration of the Government.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval given by the Pharmacy Council of India, the institutions mentioned in Annexure A annexed herewith as indicated against their names are being granted approval by the Government to reduce/ reduce the intake capacity of the existing Diploma Courses in the unaided institution of Degree Pharmacy Course to 60 from the academic year 2020-21 subject to the following terms and conditions.
1) The grant will be on a grant-in-aid basis.
In this case, the institution concerned shall be bound to fulfill the conditions prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi as well as under the Government Resolution dated 21st May, 1983. The intake capacity of the institution for the said course shall be limited as mentioned against their name in the prospectus.

3. The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government and prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education/ Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. as well as related documents and documents.
The compliance shall be done satisfactorily with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra.
4. The concerned institution shall complete other legal formalities by depositing the requisite processing fee and accumulated deposit in the joint account with the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai.
5. Admission to the said Professional Diploma Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective courses as approved by the Government.
If the fees charged by the institutions concerned exceed the tuition fees prescribed by the Fee Regulatory Authority, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses as per the Government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
The books, journals required by the library in the institution must be purchased as per the standards of the All India Council for Technical Education. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. Also, the students are provided with all the educational facilities as per the standards of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
The Institute shall be under the control of the Director, Technical Education and the examinations for the respective courses shall be conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Bandra, Mumbai.
10.R. In respect of Ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999 need to be strictly implemented.
Further, the other terms and conditions laid down by the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State, Mumbai, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai and the concerned University shall continue to apply.
12.The institutions which have not paid the processing fee, registration fee to the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, shall recover the same before final approval by the Director.
