Maharshi Dhondo Keshav Karve founded the Shrimati Nathibai Damodar Thackersey Women's University (the first women's university in the country) in 1916 to bring women into the mainstream of education in Maharashtra. Through this university, different faculties were started for women and started providing higher education to women. Today, the university's affiliated colleges are spread across seven other states, including Maharashtra. With 48,000 undergraduate and postgraduate students, a total of 200 courses and 9 faculties, the scope of women's education can be seen. Nathibai Damodar Thackersey Women's University It completed 100 years on 5th July 2016. Since 1916, this university has been working to educate and educate millions of women.
