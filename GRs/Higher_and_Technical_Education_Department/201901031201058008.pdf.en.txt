Circular:
The provisions of Section 20 (1) of the Maharashtra Unaided Private Professional Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admission and Fee) Act, 2015 are as follows:
(1) If the Regulatory Authority is of the opinion that any person has contravened any of the provisions of this Act or the rules made thereunder, it may, (a) direct such person to pay, for the first time contravention, a penalty of not less than one lakh rupees but which may extend to five lakh rupees or twice the amount charged in excess of the fee prescribed under this Act, whichever is higher; (b) for the second or subsequent contravention, a penalty of not less than two lakh rupees but which may extend to ten lakh rupees or double the amount charged in excess of the fee prescribed under this Act, whichever is higher.

In exercise of the powers conferred by section 23 of the Maharashtra Unaided Private Professional Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admission and Fee) Act, 2015, two separate rules have been notified on 24th April, 2017 to regulate the admission to the first year and direct second year full-time professional postgraduate technical courses in Engineering and Technology, Pharmacy, Vastu Shastra, Hotel Management and Catering Technology, Management Science, Computer Application and full-time professional undergraduate technical courses in Engineering and Technology, Pharmacy, Vastu Shastra, Hotel Management and Catering Technology. These rules have been amended by two separate notifications dated 5th June, 2018. Rule 15 has been included in the main rules of both these rules. The provisions of Rule No.15 (c) of both the Amendment Rules for admission to Post Graduate and Under Graduate courses are as follows:
""" (c)"""" No institution shall in any manner object to the return of certificates, diplomas, degrees and other documents deposited by the candidates for admission to such institution, if the candidates are not willing to continue the course or avail the facilities in such institution."""
He will not be forced to pay any other fees. "
(3) In view of the provisions of the Act as aforesaid and the Rules framed thereunder, no institution shall for any reason take away the certificates, diplomas/ degrees and other documents deposited by a student with it.
In any case, if the documents are demanded by the student, the institution will have to return them to the student. Instructions to strictly follow this provision of the rules are issued by the Joint Director of Technical Education of all the departments under the Directorate of Technical Education.
