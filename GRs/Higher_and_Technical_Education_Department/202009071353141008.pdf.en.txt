Preamble: In pursuance of the approval of Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, the Director has submitted a proposal to the Government through a letter of reference for grant of Government approval for starting the admission to the extended B.Pharmacy course at Bombay College of Pharmacy, Mumbai on non-subsidized basis for the academic year 2019-2020. It was pending approval of the government.
Government Decision: In pursuance of the approval of Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi, the Government is granting approval to start admission to the extended B.Pharmacy course at Bombay College of Pharmacy, Mumbai on non-subsidized basis for the academic year 2019-2020 subject to the following terms and conditions as mentioned in the table below.

Terms and Conditions: 1) The grant will be on a non-recourse basis.
The institution concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi in the said case as well as prescribed under the Government Order dated 21st May, 1983. The institution's eligibility for this course will be as follows.
(3) The institution shall be required to enter into an agreement to accept all the terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as prescribed by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. The formalities and documents will be completed with the consent of the Director, Technical Education, Maharashtra State.

4) For the affiliation of the said course, the concerned institution will be required to obtain the approval/ affiliation of the concerned university as per the provisions of the Maharashtra State University Act, 1994.
(5) The institution concerned shall meet other legal requirements by depositing the requisite consolidated deposit with the Director, Technical Education in a joint account.
6) Admission to the said Degree Course shall be made as per the rules/ procedure for admission to the respective course as approved by the Government.
If the institutions charge more than the tuition fee prescribed by the Tuition Fee Committee, action will be taken against them as per the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
8) The Institute shall appoint a separate full-time faculty and other technical staff to run this permanent unaided course as per the Government rules. They should be given salary and allowances as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
9) The library in the institute is required to procure the required books, journals as per the standards of Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students. The students are also provided with all educational facilities as per the standards of Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi. laboratories, libraries, classrooms, etc.
10) In the context of ragging. The directions of the Supreme Court and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, need to be strictly implemented.
Other terms and conditions as laid down by the Director, Technical Education, State of Maharashtra, Mumbai and the University concerned shall also apply.
