Preamble: The procedure for grant of recognition to short-term vocational diploma courses under the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education is laid down under the Government Decision dated 03.05.2021 of the Department of Higher and Technical Education. Accordingly, the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education had issued a notification dated 14.10.2022 inviting applications from interested institutions in the state through online system.
The application received from the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education and the original documents along with the application were examined by the Document Verification Committee and the physical verification of the infrastructure in the eligible institution was done by the Expert Committee. Thereafter, the State Level Committee after verifying the reports of the Department Level Committee, may consider the proposal of the institutions which are providing educational and infrastructural facilities and fulfilling the prescribed criteria and the necessary documents in support thereof, subject to the provisions of Reference Letter No. 3 recommendations were made to the Government. Accordingly, from the academic year 2023-24, the vocationally oriented short term courses under the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education will be affiliated to the Board of Technical Education, Govt. 4 The above decision has been approved by the government.
The state-level committee did not recommend to the government the institutions that have not completed the required documents from the proposals received from the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education. As some of these institutions have completed the required documents and errors, the proposals of these institutions have been submitted by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education in the form of a feedback letter under reference No. 5 and 6 have been submitted to the government. Accordingly, the proposal was approved by the government.

1) The approval to be given to the government approved short-term courses under the board is the decision of the government. As per the Government Decision dated 21.5.1983, the courses are on permanent non-grant basis and no grant from the Government will be payable for these courses.
(2) The duration, eligibility, semester etc. of the said courses shall be as mentioned in the Annexure accompanying the Government decision.
(3) The Institute shall appoint full-time faculty and other technical staff to run the courses and pay salaries and allowances to the staff as per rules. Strict action will be taken against the institution if complaints are received from the students to the government regarding the salary and allowances of the teaching staff and other employees.
4) The institutions are required to take affiliation of Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education for this short term course. Also, in case the courses approved by this Government are decided to be implemented by the All India Council for Technical Education, it will be mandatory for the institutions to get the approval of the All India Council for Technical Education.
5) Although the Government has a fee reimbursement policy for professional courses, it does not have a fee reimbursement scheme applicable to any short-term courses. Hence, the fee reimbursement scheme for the above courses will not be applicable to any category of students. The fee reimbursement/ concession scheme will not be applicable to economically backward students as well.
6) It will be mandatory for the institutions to submit a guarantee letter on a stamp paper of Rs 500 in the prescribed format to the Board regarding the acceptance of all terms and conditions prescribed by the Government as well as the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education.
7) The concerned institution shall complete the other legal matters by paying the required term deposit in the joint account with the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai.
8) The Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Mumbai should take an affidavit regarding the appointment of regular teaching and non-teaching staff, providing machinery and up-to-date laboratories and library facilities to the students as per the standards. The responsibility of fulfilling these requirements lies with the concerned Deputy Secretary, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Divisional Office.

The books, journals required by the library in the institute are required to be purchased as per the standards of Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Bandra, Mumbai. Also, the library should be made fully accessible to the students.
10) The Institute will be under the control of the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education and the examinations for the respective courses will be conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education, Bandra, Mumbai.
11) Admission to these short-term courses should be done under the control of the Board as per the government policy on quality, reservation etc.
The courses approved under the said Government Decisions will be required to be started by the institution from the academic year 2023-24. If the institution does not start the course in the prescribed manner, the recognition granted to the institution for the said course will be automatically cancelled.
No institution shall admit students without obtaining the approval of the Government and affiliation of the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education. The institution will be solely responsible for admitting students without approval/ affiliation.
If the institutions charge more than the tuition fee prescribed by the government, action will be taken against them under the Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987.
15) Regarding the ragging. The Supreme Court's directions and the Maharashtra Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1999, should be strictly implemented.
16) Apart from this, other terms and conditions laid down by the Director, Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education will be applicable to the institutions.
17) The Government shall have the power to modify the aforesaid terms and conditions or to insert new terms and conditions to enhance the quality of education.
