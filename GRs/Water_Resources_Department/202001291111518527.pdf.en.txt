Preamble: For the purpose of providing information to the citizens under the said Act, the Government Information Officer under Section 5 (1) of the Central Government Act," Assistant Government Information Officer" under Section 5 (2) and" Appellate Authority" under Sections 2 (1) and 2 (3) of the said Government Order, have been declared as per Rule 7.
Applications received under the Right to Information Act, 2005 are received by every office. The jurisdiction of the office bearer includes the office bearers of the respective office and the relevant documents. It is the responsibility of the office bearer to maintain and preserve them and accordingly the information received at the office may be immediately available to the applicant at the initial stage of application. The Deputy Commissioner acts as the nodal officer in the department.
Further, the Under Secretaries control the work done by the Administrative Cell Officers and the Assistant Cell Officers. Considering all these aspects, a proposal was under consideration of the government to appoint Assistant Cell Officer as Assistant Public Information Officer, Office Officer as Public Information Officer and Under Secretary as First Appellate Officer in the department to facilitate administrative work. A decision in this regard is being taken.

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: MAMA-2019/ PR No. 33/2019/ A (Ravka)
Government Decision: In the above background, as per the accompanying form, the" Assistant Public Information Officer,"" Public Information Officer" and" Appellate Authority" in the Water Resources Department (itself) are being declared. In case of change in the supervisory charge of the offices assigned to the Assistant Public Information Officer, the Public Information Officer and the Appellate Authority, the newly appointed officers will function as the Assistant Public Information Officer, the Public Information Officer and the Appellate Authority of the respective offices.
The applications of the citizens regarding the Right to Information of the Central Government will be accepted in the first registration branch. The registration branch will record it in a separate register and send the application to the concerned government information officer for further action on the same day. In the absence of the concerned Public Information Officer, the concerned Assistant Information Officer will accept the application and take necessary action.
The said Assistant Public Information Officer, Public Information Officer and Appellate Authority shall strictly comply with the provisions of the Central Right to Information Act, 2005.
These orders will come into effect immediately.
The information applications received before the order comes into force as well as the appeal applications should be handled by the Assistant Public Information Officer, Public Information Officer and the First Appellate Authority as declared earlier.
