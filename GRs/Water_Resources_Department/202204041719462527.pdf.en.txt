Constitution of an" Internal Grievance Redressal Committee" for redressal of service grievances of employees belonging to Scheduled Tribes.
The Government of Maharashtra
The Department of Water Resources
Government Decision No. Parikh-2021/ PR No. 248/2021/ A (Class-II)
Hutatma Rajguru Chowk, Madam Cama Marg,
Ministry, Mumbai 400 032.
Date:- 04/04/2022
Preamble: To examine and monitor all matters relating to the safeguards provided to the Scheduled Tribes under Article 338A of the Constitution of India. The National Commission for Scheduled Tribes has been constituted on 19/04/2004. The committee has been receiving complaints regarding the service matters of government employees belonging to the Scheduled Tribes.
Accordingly, the National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST) has suggested the setting up of an" Internal Grievance Redressal Committee" for redressal of employment/ service related grievances of ST employees. Accordingly, the Government was contemplating to constitute an" Internal Grievance Redressal Committee" to redress the grievances of the Scheduled Tribe officers/ employees in the field offices under the Water Resources Department.
Government Decision: An" Internal Grievance Redressal Committee" is being constituted to redress the grievances of the Scheduled Tribe officers/ employees in the Water Resources Department as well as in the offices under the direct administrative control of the Government.

(2) The Grievance Redressal Committees shall take immediate action to redress the grievances received in respect of the service matters of the Scheduled Tribe officers/ employees in respect of the above mentioned two Grievances Redressal Committees, such as non-filling of posts in the Point Nominations category by caste, promotion, seniority discrimination, termination/ dismissal of service, transfer/ posting discrimination, deprivation of pensionary benefits, etc.
The Committee shall submit to the Government every three months the action taken on the complaints received in respect of the above service matters.
3. Further, based on the information received from the Committee at the field level, the concerned office in the Water Resources Department shall take action and submit its report to the" Internal Grievance Redressal Committee" at the Ministry level.
