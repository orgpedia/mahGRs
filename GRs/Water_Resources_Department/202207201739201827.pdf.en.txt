Introduction: Mula Irrigation Project has two major canals namely Mula Right Bank Canal and Mula Left Bank Canal. The original right bank canal is 52 km long and was completed in 1972-73. The projected water carrying capacity from the origin is 1650 cusecs. The water management of the entire benefit area of the Mula Dam project has been transferred to the Water Utilization Agency since 2006. All water use agencies are functional and water is being metered as per demand at government rates.
The construction of the canal has been completed for 50 years and the canal irrigates the area in all three seasons- kharif, rabi and summer. As per the field inspection conducted by the field officers, there has been a lot of damage to the alignment at various places in the right canal from Km 15 to 26, leakage and erosion from the canal, the canal cutting is less than the planned cutting and hence the planned discharge is not being achieved and the filling of the canal is in a dangerous condition at some places and the local people's representatives have also demanded for the repair of the canal. The proposal to give administrative approval under special amendment to the budget for the strengthening of the monitoring and inspection team and seva path was under the consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Godavari Marathwada Irrigation Development Corporation, Aurangabad has decided to construct a new canal from 15/500 km to 26/00 km. The budget for strengthening of lining, repairing, pinching and inspection path and sevapath in the district has been allocated Rs. 4,03,76, 172/- (Rs.
