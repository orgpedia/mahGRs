Introduction: Mula Irrigation Project has two major canals namely Mula Right Bank Canal and Mula Left Bank Canal. The original right bank canal is 52 km long and was completed in 1972-73. The projected water carrying capacity from the origin is 1650 cusecs. The water management of the entire benefit area of the Mula Dam project has been transferred to the Water Utilization Agency since 2006. All water use agencies are functional and water is being metered as per demand at government rates.
The construction of the canal has been completed for 50 years and the canal irrigates the area in all three seasons- kharif, rabi and summer. According to the field inspection carried out by the field officers, there has been a lot of damage to the lining at various places in the Mula Right Canal Sonai Vitrika Km 0 to 13, leakage and erosion from the canal, the canal cut has been reduced to less than the planned cut and so the planned discharge has not been achieved, and the filling of the canal in some places has been found to be in a dangerous condition and the local people's representative has also demanded to repair the canal. No. 00/00 to 13/200 Km. A proposal to give administrative approval to the canal repair budget in the state under a special amendment was under consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:
