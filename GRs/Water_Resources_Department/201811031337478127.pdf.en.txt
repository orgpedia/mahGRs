Introduction: Moje Het under Aruna Madhyam Project, Ta. A dam with a capacity of 93.378 LMT is proposed to be constructed across Aruna river at Vaibhavwadi, Sindhudurg. The project is being implemented by the Konkan Irrigation Development Corporation (KIADC). Under the project, an area of 4475 hectares in Vaibhavwadi taluka of Sindhudurg district and 835 hectares in Rajapur taluka of Ratnagiri district is planned to be brought under irrigation. Thus, a total area of 5310 hectares will be benefited by the project.
The Aruna Medium Project. As per Reference No. 1 of 1993-94, the original administrative sanction for the project was Rs. 53.94 crore based on tariff. However, as the said administrative approval lapsed, the amount of Rs. 178.41 crore was re-administered based on the tariff of 2003-04 as per Reference No. 2. Thereafter, as per Reference No. 3, the first revised Administrative Sanction was granted for the rate based amount of Rs. 301.04 crore for 2005-06 and as per Reference No. 4, the second revised Administrative Sanction was granted for the rate based amount of Rs. 669.80 crore for 2009-10.
The Executive Director, Konkan Irrigation Development Corporation, Thane has submitted the third revised administrative sanction proposal of Rs. 1689.50 crore based on the rate schedule for the year 2016-17 to the Government of Maharashtra under reference No.5. The proposal for the third revised administrative approval was submitted for obtaining the concurrence of the Planning and Finance Department for submission to the Expenditure Priority Committee for approval. The departments concerned have been informed to submit their recommendations to the cabinet. The project has a cost benefit ratio of 1.51.

The proposal was placed before the Cabinet of Ministers at its meeting dated/ 10/2018.
The Cabinet has taken a conscious decision to accord administrative approval to the Third Revised Project Report of Aruna Medium Project at a cost of Rs. 1689.50 crore based on the 2016-17 schedule, after taking into consideration the current status of construction, reasons for cost escalation, etc.
Government's decision:
With this decision of the Government, administrative approval is being accorded to the Third Revised Project Report of Aruna Medium Project at a cost of Rs. 1689.50 crore based on the 2016-17 tariff list. Out of this, an amount of Rs. 1550.88 crore is provided for working capital and Rs. 138.62 crore is provided for collateral. Sub-heading wise provisions are being added to Goshwara.
The third revised administrative approval granted to the scheme has been recorded in the Medium Project Operations Register at serial number 4/2018.
The remaining work of the project should be completed within the stipulated time and within the limits of the revised administrative approval.

The cost of the project shall be covered under the head of the main account head 4701 Capital Expenditure on Major and Medium Irrigation 03, Medium Irrigation Projects (Commercial) Aruna Medium Irrigation Projects (Under Schemes) and shall be met from the grant sanctioned under the head of the project.
The Third Amended Administrative Sanction is granted subject to the following conditions:
5.1) The State Level Technical Advisory Committee Report, Issue No. Observations 26 and 27 should be fulfilled.
5.2. New elements that create financial liabilities should not be included in the project without government approval.
5.3 The project should be completed as per the plan and within the revised administrative approval cost.
5.4. Work on tenders in progress should be completed on priority. The priority of the remaining works should be decided and works/ tenders should be processed accordingly so that the water storage is maximized for irrigation.
5.5. Transfer the management of irrigation to the Water Utilization Agency by establishing a Water Utilization Agency in the entire beneficiary area.
5.6. Revised administrative approval shall not be construed as concurrence to various decisions taken by the field officers at various stages of project implementation and irregularities, if any, committed by the field officers.
