The Inter-State River Water Disputes (ISRW) Act, 1956 was enacted by the Central Government to settle disputes between the States of Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh and Maharashtra. The first water sharing tribunal was set up in 1969 under the chairmanship of Bachhawat. After hearing the petitions filed under section 5 (2) and section 5 (3) of the Act, the final decision was announced and notified in 1976 and the decision became binding on all the three states.
The Krishna Water Disputes Tribunal-I reliably allocated 75% of the total 2130 Aghfu waters in the basin with certain restrictions. Maharashtra received 585 mm of water, Karnataka 734 mm and Andhra Pradesh 811 mm.
Also, in 2004, a second water dispute arbitration was held to resolve disputes over the use of Krishna river water. The meeting was presided over by Mr Brijesh Kumar. Section 5 (2) of the Act provides for hearing the claims, evidence and arguments of the three States.

The mandate of the Committee is to: (i) finalize the actual utilization of water for the completed and under construction major, medium and minor irrigation projects and sub-irrigation schemes in the Krishna basin in accordance with Forms 1 to 9 of the prescribed format. Review meetings to be held every 6 months (15th October and 15th June and as per requirement).
2) Krishna Water Disputes Tribunal No. To review the actual implementation of the decisions/ orders and directions given by the Government.
3) To coordinate the exchange of information between the Krishna Water Disputes Tribunal and the Krishna Basin Development Corporation.
4) The annual water supply. To submit with recommendation to the Executive Director, Krishna Valley Development Corporation.
Determination of the quantum of water released annually from Krishna River to Karnataka State based on the data collected by the Central Water Commission and the Sarita Measurement Centre under the State of Maharashtra.
