Brahmagavhan Upasana Irrigation Scheme is located in the Urdhva Godavari sub-basin of the Godavari basin, under the Godavari Marathwada Irrigation Development Corporation, Aurangabad. The project is proposed in the catchment area of the Jayakwadi project built on the Godavari river and is located in the general area of the Godavari Marathwada Irrigation Development Corporation. The project will irrigate 20265 hectares of agricultural land in about 65 villages of Paithan taluka of Aurangabad district. Part 1 and Part 2 of the project totaled 18515. There is so much irrigation potential, out of which 3205 is through Part-1 Phase-1. The project has been completed and irrigation is in progress.
Further, the savings arising out of the proposed supply of water through closed tube distribution system instead of conventional distribution system from the Brahmagavhan lift irrigation scheme will be increased to Rs.1750 crore beyond the sanctioned benefit area of the project. In-principle approval has been accorded to Kherda Pachod Extended Irrigation Scheme for irrigation of the area. The government has also approved the inclusion of this extended scheme in the fourth Suprama of the Brahmagavhan Upasana Irrigation Scheme.
The State Technical Advisory Committee, Nashik has approved the fourth revised administrative approval proposal of the Brahmagavhan Upsurge Irrigation Scheme. The 159th meeting held on 11.6.2021 has taken the decision with the recommendation. Similarly, the first phase of the project. The proposal has been approved in the meeting held on 22.10.2021.
