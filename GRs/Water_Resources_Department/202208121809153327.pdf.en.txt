Proposal: Wardha Barrage (Hadgaon) Upasana Irrigation Scheme, Taluka Babhulgaon District Yavatmal, is proposed to be constructed on Wardha River with a capacity of 7.88 LMT. The project covers 5663 villages in 16 taluks of Babhulgaon taluka in Yavatmal district. The area is planned to be brought under irrigation.
The original administrative approval for Wardha Barrage (Hadgaon) Upsurge Irrigation Scheme, Taluka Babhulgaon District Yavatmal was accorded under Reference No. 1 for the year 2008-09 based on the schedule at an amount of Rs. 190.00 crore (Rupees One Hundred and Ninety Crores only).
In the meanwhile, due to the increase in the project cost due to change in tariff, increase in land acquisition cost, increase in tender acceptance of excess rate, increase in concept changes, increase in cost due to other reasons, increase in collateral cost, etc., the Executive Director, Vidarbha Irrigation Development Corporation, Nagpur has submitted a proposal to the Government for the first revised administrative sanction of Rs. 565.87 crore based on the 2017-18 tariff.
The original administrative sanction of the project is Rs. 190.00 crore, which is Rs. 375.87 crore higher than the first revised administrative sanction of Rs. 565.87 crore.

The profitability ratio of the project is 1.04. The project does not fit into the parameters (parameters greater than 1.5). The project's economic return rate (ERR) is 16.06.
The total water storage capacity of the project is 7.88 LMT and total water consumption is 23.64 LMT. 5663 in 16 villages of Babhulgaon taluka of Yavatmal district. The area will benefit from irrigation.
The first was the proposal for revised administrative approval. Submitted to Secretary level Sub-Committee dated 17/09/2019. The proposal was made by a sub-committee at the level of secretary. It was agreed to place the proposal before the Cabinet of Ministers for further consideration at the meeting of the Governing Council dated 28/01/2020. The Governing Council passed Resolution No. Proposition 77/5. The Cabinet of Ministers approved the proposal. As on 03/08/2022. The Cabinet, after taking into consideration the current status of construction, reasons for cost escalation, etc., has decided to accord administrative approval to the First Revised Project Report of Wardha Barrage (Hadgaon) Utilization Irrigation Scheme based on the 2017-18 schedule at a cost of Rs. 565.87 crore.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In pursuance of this decision of the Government, the first revised project report of Wardha Barrage (Hadgaon) lift irrigation scheme for the year 2017-18 is being accorded administrative approval at a cost of Rs. 565.87 crore (Rupees Five Hundred Sixty Five Crore Only) based on the schedule.
Out of this, an amount of Rs. 525.25 crore (Rupees Five Hundred and Twenty Five Crores Twenty Five Lakhs Only) is provided for works, establishment and incidental expenses amounting to Rs. 40.62 crore (Rupees Forty Sixty Two Crores Sixty Two Lakhs Only). Sub-heading provisions are being added to the Goshawara.
The first revised administrative approval granted to the scheme has been recorded in the Medium Project Operations Register at serial number 04/2022.
The project should be completed within the stipulated time and within the revised administrative approval.
The cost of the project shall be included in the head of the main account i. 5,4701 Capital expenditure on major and medium irrigation projects, under the head 190-Public Sector and Other Undertakings Investments (02) Schemes, (02) (01) Vidarbha Irrigation Development Corporation, Nagpur Shareholding Contribution (4701-H729) and shall be met from the following sanctioned grants.
The First Amended Administrative Sanction is being granted subject to the following conditions:
5.1 The observations made in the State Level Technical Advisory Committee Report from point number 1 to 27 should be corrected.
5.2 Work shall not be commenced until all statutory approvals have been obtained.
5.3 The concept of tube distribution system should be examined by the Central Resolution Drawing Committee and then the cost-effective option of tube distribution or open canal should be certified and selected by the Executive Director.

5.4 New elements that create financial liabilities should not be included in the project without government approval. Also, make sure that the cost of the project does not increase.
5.5 The project works should be completed by June 2023 and within the revised administrative approval cost limit.
5.6 Priority of works should be decided and works/ tenders should be processed accordingly so as to maximize the benefit of water storage for irrigation.
5.7. Transfer the management of irrigation to the Water Utilization Agency by establishing a Water Utilization Agency in the entire beneficiary area.
5.8 No additional expenditure or disbursement of funds shall be made for any purpose beyond the limit of administrative sanction granted for the purpose. In this regard, the existing government decisions, guidelines of the CVC, limits of financial powers, prescribed tender procedures should be strictly implemented.
5.9 The said revised administrative approval shall not be construed as concurrence to various decisions taken by the field officers at various stages of project implementation and such irregularities, if any, have taken place in the field.
5.10 Proceedings of the ongoing or proposed departmental inquiry into the scheme should be continued. The decision to grant the revised administrative sanction will not affect the ongoing or future proceedings of the departmental inquiry.
5.11 Action should be taken to fix responsibility on those who are responsible for the high expenditure incurred on this scheme.
