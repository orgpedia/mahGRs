Introduction: The Panzara River originates from Shendvad Ta Sakri and flows from west to east to Mudavad, Ta. Shindkheda, which joins Tapi river at Dhule. The length of the Panjra River is 138 km.
There are 30 fad-style dams at various places and they were built 200 to 500 years ago to facilitate irrigation. Beneficial area of Akkalpada Medium Project includes the irrigation of the dam of Phad system under it.
The Mudi Phad Dam is one of the above 16 Phad system dams, located 25 km from Dhule town, downstream of Nyhalod village. At present, the condition of the embankment is good and the river side walls of the canal are in a broken state at some places and the construction and remodelling of the culverts on the canal is in progress. Mudi Phad Dam- It has an irrigation area of 167 hectares and the main canal is 9.40 km long. It is 1.25 km.
The length of the extension is 1.80 km, with the aim of recharging the Bhalia Nala from the Panjra River and restoring the irrigation area.
The works include construction of river escape, construction of canal side wall, construction of river escape, construction of road crossing, construction of main canal and distributary no. 1, the construction of the main canal and the distributary No. 2 Construction of the redoubt of the Main Canal, Construction of the Iron Cross Regulator of the Main Canal and the Redoubt of the Minor, Construction of the Main Canal Road Crossing, etc.
work is included. Therefore, the dam should be repaired. A proposal to give administrative approval to the budget of Shindkheda, G. Dhule under special amendment was under consideration of the government. Following this, the government decided:
