The Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 (Act No. 18 of 2005) has been notified in the Gazette to provide for the establishment of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto for regulating the water resources in the State of Maharashtra and for determining the rates of water to be used for agricultural, industrial, drinking and other purposes for facilitating and ensuring efficient, equitable and sustainable management, allocation and utilization of water resources. Section 4 (1) (f) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority (Amendment) Act, 2016 (Maharashtra Act No. 8 of 2017) provides as follows:
" To assist the Authority, there shall be five Special Invitees, as may be prescribed, one from each River Basin Authority area, who shall have adequate knowledge, experience or demonstrated competence in dealing with issues relating to water resources, engineering, agriculture, ecology and environment, drinking water, industry, law, economics, commerce, finance or management: Provided that at least one Special Invitee shall be a woman: Provided further that no two Special Invitees shall be from the same area or group of areas. "

As per Section 2 (p) of Maharashtra Act No. 18 of 2005," River Basin Agency" means any of the five Irrigation Development Corporations functioning in the river basins. Accordingly, one Special Invitee from each of the 5 River Basin Authority areas in Maharashtra has been appointed as per the above Government Resolution No. 1 to 5 were previously used.
