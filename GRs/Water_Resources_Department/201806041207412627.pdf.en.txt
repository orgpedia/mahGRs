Executive Director, Godavari Irrigation Development Corporation, Aurangabad vide letter No.Gompavim/ Tan- 5/7088, dated 7.9.2017 Preamble: The Chief Engineer (South), National Water Development Agency, Hyderabad has stated in the pre-feasibility report that the Damanganga-Vaitarna-Godavari Uplift Scheme for diverting the surplus water of the Western Vahini rivers of the two river basins of Damanganga and Vaitarna to the Godavari basin in the east is successful as per the pre-feasibility report.
As per the pre-feasibility report prepared by the National Water Development Agency (NWDA), the scheme has been given two options with the benefit ratio being 1.656 for Option 1 and 1.647 for Option 2.
This is around 28,320. The area can benefit from irrigation (direct and indirect). Also, 137.01 Dalhousie water can be made available for non-irrigation purposes. Under the scheme, 5 dams are proposed to be constructed, which will be connected to each other through tunnels/ canals and 202 Dalhousie water is proposed to be lifted and diverted into River Dev in Godavari basin.
The National Water Development Agency (NWDA) had sent a detailed project report (DPR) for the project.
The letter No. 1 was submitted to the government for administrative approval. Accordingly, a proposal was under consideration of the government to give administrative approval to the budget of the expenditure to prepare the detailed project report.
Government's decision:
The Damanganga-Vaitarna-Godavari (Kadawadev) Upasa river link scheme will be developed at a cost of Rs. Administrative Sanction is being accorded to the Budget Estimates of Rs. 23,14,54, 680/- (Rupees Twenty Three Crores Fourteen Lakhs Fifty Four Thousand Six Hundred and Eighty only).
