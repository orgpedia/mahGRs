Introduction: K. P. Bandhara Shiswad in the tribal area of Akole taluka is the first number one Ko on the Mula river respectively. There is a barrier. The construction of the dam was completed in 1997 in the form of a masonry structure. The total number of sluices in the dam is 35 with 34 pillars. The length of the dam is 112 m and the height of the river bed is 7.60 m. The dam has a storage capacity of 17.08 LHG and irrigated area of 188 hectares.
The river has been receiving heavy rainfall since last year. As a result, the dam has suffered extensive damage. Water also seeps through the foundation. Therefore, the special repair of this Co. P. Dam is required immediately and there is a demand from the beneficiaries in this regard.
With the repair of the Shiswad KoP dam, the construction of the dam will be strengthened and the age of the dam will increase. This will help in increasing the revenue of the government by stopping the water leakage. In this background, K.P. Akole, G.
