Introduction: A 150-year-old British-era maintenance barrage has been constructed at Karad in Khodashi taluka on the Krishna river in Satara district, and the Krishna canal starts from the left bank of the river. Through this canal, water is supplied to the households of Karad taluka of Satara district and Walwa, Palus and Tasgaon taluks of Sangli district. There are 31 villages in the benefit area of the Krishna canal. The total length of the canal is 86 km and it joins the Vasagada Dam built across the Yerla River. The project was completed in 1868 and the canal was first opened for irrigation in the Rabi season of 1868-69. The total irrigated area of the project is 13366 hectares.
The Krishna canal dates back to the British era, about 150 years ago. The length of the canal from 80/010 to 80/550 km. The section in m is 100% black soil and runs through a deep soft trench. It further meets the 86 km Vasagadda dam built across the Yerla river. The Vasagadda dam is part of the Krishna canal, through which water is released into the Yerla river for irrigation. This water is used to irrigate 624 hectares of land in four villages of Mauje Vasgade, Nandre, Shirgaon and Bendri in the dam's benefit area. Due to heavy rains for the last three years, the water level in the canal has completely collapsed and the water has stopped flowing. as well as the canal

Against this background, the canal was constructed from 80/010 CE to ca. No. 80/550 K. It has become necessary to repair the completely collapsed section of the 540-meter long canal and build the Gabion wall as a protective measure to retain the flood on the inner side of the canal. After the completion of the proposal, the project irrigated area of 13366 hectares will be restored.
Accordingly, the Krishna Canal was constructed from 80/010 CE to ca. No. 80/550 K.
