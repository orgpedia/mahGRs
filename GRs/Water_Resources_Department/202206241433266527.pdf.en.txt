6. Government Circular No. Prama-2013/ (785/13)/ Simvya (Works) D. 1/1/2015 Preamble: Khodashi on Krishna river in Satara district. Karad has a 149-year-old British-era diversion weir, and the Krishna canal starts from the left bank of the river. Through this canal, water is supplied to the beneficiary farmers of Karad taluka of Satara district and Walwa, Palus and Tasgaon taluks of Sangli district for irrigation.
A total of 31 villages fall under the benefit area of the Krishna canal. The total length of the canal is 86.00 km.
The project was completed in 1968 and first opened for irrigation in the 1868-69 rabi season.
The proposal includes construction of a 510 m long main canal from 49/240 to 49/750 CE. Geomembrane geotextile method in length has been proposed. Further, strengthening of Punadi (Dudhondi) Vitarika No. 48/730 and construction of 270 mtrs. of bridge at selected places is in progress. Concrete lining is proposed to be done in length. And Ghorpade Vitarika of 64/460 C.E. 1920 m.
It is proposed to carry out strengthening, service track repair and paving in the length. The Special Repair Budget under Extension and Improvement of Main Canal and Distributaries of Krishna Canal No. 48/610 to 64/500 has been approved. A proposal to give administrative approval to the budget of Palus G. Sangli under special amendment was under consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:
