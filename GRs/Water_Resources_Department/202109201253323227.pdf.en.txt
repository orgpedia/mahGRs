The Proposal:
The Rampur Minor Irrigation Project is located near Rampur village in Akkalkuwa taluka of Nandurbar district on a local drain in the Tapi valley under Tapi Irrigation Development Corporation, Jalgaon. The total storage capacity of the project is 3.285 LMT. Out of this 2.996 Dalghami is a useful water body. A total of 382 villages in 7 taluks of Akkalkuwa Taluk of Nandurbar district have been covered under this project. The area will benefit from irrigation.
Rampur Minor Irrigation Scheme has been notified vide Government Resolution No. MIW 1180/1121/268/ 80 LAPA 4D. 11.12.1980 to Rs. 0.393 crore has been accorded original administrative approval as per Tariff Schedule 1979-80. Subsequently, the Government of India issued Decision No. Lapayo/ 1595/39/95/ Kharbhumi d. 29.10.1996 to Rs. Administrative Sanction was granted again as per Tariff Schedule 1995-96 at a cost of Rs. 3.51 crore. In the interim period, the cost of the project has been increasing due to price variation in the cost of the scheme, increase in land acquisition, increase in new works proposed, increase in cost due to other reasons, establishment and collateral costs. Considering this, the Water Resources Department based on the tariff list 2018-2019 has allocated Rs. 39.73 crore. The decision to grant the first revised administrative approval as per the approval on July 12, 2021, has been taken as follows.
Government's decision:
The Rampur Plan. Akkalkuwa G. (5) The decision to grant the first revised administrative approval shall not affect the proceedings of the ongoing or future departmental inquiries into the project, which shall continue to be conducted.
(6) Revised administrative approval shall not take into account technical aspects of the report or the approval of the decision at the field level on the tender or the field irregularities that have occurred. Also, the revised administrative sanctioning authority will not be responsible for the sectoral decisions and irregularities that have occurred, the responsibility will be of the decision-making sectoral officers.
7) A beneficiary water use agency should be set up on the project.
