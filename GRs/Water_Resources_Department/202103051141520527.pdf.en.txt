In the monsoon season of 2019, the Krishna Valley experienced unprecedented flooding due to excessive rainfall in the months of July and August. Kolhapur and Sangli districts are the worst affected. There was a huge loss of life and property. There are different opinions about the causes of flooding. The situation in 2019 is even worse. The work of Almatti Dam in Karnataka has also been completed up to 519.60 m. Other water resources projects have also been completed in both the states. There are many technical issues. There has been a lot of progress in the remote sensing information system through satellites. It must also be used. To conduct a thorough technical investigation of all these aspects, to find out the causes of the flood situation, to find solutions to prevent such unfortunate incidents from recurring in the future, an expert study committee like (MRSAC), India Meteorological Department (IMD), Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority (MWRRA), Water Sector Analysts has been constituted. The committee was formed in accordance with the Government Decision No. 2. Also, reference No. The decision regarding the remuneration of the non-official members of the committee has been taken as per the Government Resolution No. 3. Reference No. A committee has been formed under Section 4.

Government Resolution No: Purani-2019/ P.S. 291/2019/ CC (M).
The Committee has submitted its study report on Krishna sub-basin of Bhima and Krishna basins to the Government on 27th May, 2020. However, as the committee did not get sufficient time to study and submit the report on Bhima-Kho due to the widespread infectious disease of COVID-19, the committee was asked to study and submit the report on Bhima-Kho as requested by the chairman of the committee. The matter of extending the deadline till 30.06.2021 was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The above Committee has so far met and studied the dimensions of rainfall and heavy rainfall warning, integrated reservoir operation, emergency action plan and mechanism, use of new technology and guidelines for flood control and forecasting, etc. and visited the flood affected areas of Sangli and Kolhapur districts on 23rd and 24th September 2019 and submitted the study report of Krishna Sub-Division to the Government on 27th May 2020. However, as the committee did not get sufficient time to study and submit the report on Bhima-Kho due to the widespread infectious disease of COVID-19, the said committee is being given an extension of deadline till 30/06/2021 to study and submit the complete and final report on Bhima-Kho. After this period, the committee will not be able to extend the term.
The order was issued by the Planning Department vide informal reference No. 32/ Expenditure-12 dated 2/2/2021 and Finance Department Informal Reference No. 32/2021 Expenditure- 12D. Being issued as agreed on 03/02/2021.
