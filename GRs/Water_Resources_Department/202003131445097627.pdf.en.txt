Preamble: To provide for regular technical inspection and construction test for the construction of hydroelectric and power works under the Water Resources Department, in order to ensure quality, speed and transparency of the projects by removing the system defects from time to time. The decision was taken by the government. In this regard, the Chief Engineer (Electrical) has issued reference No. 2 Under the letter here, the draft of procedures and supplementary forms have been prepared and submitted to the government for the technical audit of the electrical works of the hydroelectric and sub-irrigation schemes under the Water Resources Department. The government was contemplating to issue these orders. In this regard, the following guidelines have been issued:
Guidelines: For the technical audit of the electrical works of the hydroelectric and sub-irrigation schemes under the Water Resources Department, the prescribed format of returns (return no. 1 to 18) are included. Accordingly, the following instructions should be followed while conducting the technical examination: 1. While commencing the technical examination in the first instance, the examination shall be conducted from the water year i.e. 1st June, 2019 to 31st May, 2020, assuming commencement from June, 2019 to May, 2020.
2.The technical inspection of the board office should be done every year. The technical examination of each departmental office should be done once in two years. The first phase of the technical examination will be held from June 1, 2019 to May 31, 2020.

The general procedure of technical examination is as follows: 1. The technical examination of every Regional Office and Circle Office of the Water Resources Department should be conducted every year. The technical examination of each departmental office should be conducted once in two years. However, at the time of inspection, all decisions from the date of the previous inspection should be examined.
2. In this examination, the decisions taken by the field officers regarding various levels of technical approval, tender approval, additional item tariff, increase in quantity of tender, concept approval, tariff list, external approved tariff, etc. are to be verified as per the guidelines, circulars, government decisions, IS code prescribed by the government. If there are any deviations in this regard, such things should be pointed out.
The technical inspection report should be submitted to the government in the form of half margin within eight days after the date of inspection and copies should be sent to all the concerned senior offices.
After receiving the technical inspection report, the Chief Engineer (Vigilance& Projects) and Joint Secretary will visit the Regional Office in the last week of every quarter and read the technical inspection reports made during that quarter and bring the important issues to the notice of the Government.
