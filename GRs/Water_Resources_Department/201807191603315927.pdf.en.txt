Introduction: The Shirapur Upsa Irrigation Scheme, located in a drought prone area under the Maharashtra Krishna Basin Development Corporation, Pune, will be taken up in two phases from the existing KoP Dam on the Seena River near Shirapur village in Mohol taluka of Solapur district. The project is located in the Krishna basin. The project will cover 10,000 villages in 19 drought-prone villages of 17 villages in North Solapur and 2 villages in Tuljapur taluka of Osmanabad district. The area will benefit from irrigation.
Based on the 1995-96 tariff, the project was sanctioned at a cost of Rs. 90.57 crore was given administrative approval.
The State Technical Advisory Committee (SLTAC-II) in its 12th meeting approved the implementation of the Shirapur Upsa Irrigation Scheme G. Solapur Project worth Rs. The first revised project report of Rs. 474.51 crore has been finalised.
The increase in project cost is mainly due to increase in tariff after administrative approval, increase in land acquisition cost, change in concept and inadequate provisions in the original project report.
Therefore, the Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation has submitted the first revised project report to the government for approval. The project report has been recommended by the State Level Technical Advisory Committee-II in its 12th meeting. Also the project report. Discussions have been held in the meeting of the Expenditure Priority Committee held on 23/4/2018.
The Expenditure Priority Committee has decided to give administrative approval to the first revised project report based on the 2013-14 schedule at a cost of Rs. 474.51 crore, based on the reason for the increase, the report of the State Technical Advisory Committee, the observations of the Expenditure Priority Committee and the completion of the Shirapur Upasana Irrigation Scheme, which is a boon to Mohol Taluka of Solapur district.
