The Proposal:
The Dam Restoration and Improvement Project Phase-II is a Centrally Sponsored Project with World Bank funding. The main objective of this project is to improve the safety and operational performance of selected dams in the country by strengthening the relevant agencies to manage them in a planned manner and maintain continuity in operation and maintenance of dams. Maharashtra is a part of this project. About 70 per cent of the project cost will be met through a loan from the World Bank.
Minister of State (Jal Shakti), Ministry of Water Resources, River Development and Ganga Rejuvenation, Government of India
New Delhi. As per the letter dated 02.09.2020, prior to the signing of the necessary agreements/ documents after the loan negotiations between Department of Economic Affairs, Ministry of Finance, Government of India and World Bank, some amount of tendering under Dam Restoration and Improvement Project Phase-II is required to be done as part of the preparatory activities required for the actual implementation of the project.
Accordingly, it was under consideration of the Government to approve the finalization of tender loan agreement documents before the signing of tender for completion of preparatory stage in respect of restoration and improvement works of 12 dams covered under Dam Restoration and Improvement Project Phase-II. Following this, the government decided:

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: DREAP-2021/ P.No. 178/20 (Part-II) (a)/ SINVYA (Works)
Government's decision:
Approval is being given to finalize the tender, loan agreement documents before the signing of tender and commissioning orders of works worth Rs 114 crore out of the administrative approval of the restoration and improvement works of 12 dams covered under Dam Restoration and Improvement Project Phase-II.
2. Even after finalization of tender for the said work before the commencement of the tender process, Rs. 114 crore shall be obtained from the Project Director, Dam Restoration and Improvement Project (DRIP) and Superintending Engineer, Dam Safety Board, Nashik.
The Project Director, Dam Restoration and Improvement Project (DRIP) and Superintending Engineer, Dam Safety Board, Nashik will be responsible for ensuring that the tender process of works costing above the above limit is not started.
After the loan agreement documents are signed, the tender process of the remaining works should be carried out in the prevailing manner.
The government decision The Cabinet gave. is being issued pursuant to the approval provided in the Cabinet Meeting held on 13/01/2021.
