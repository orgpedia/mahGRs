Introduction: The Shelgaon Barrage is a mega project. A dam with a capacity of 127.529 LMT is proposed to be constructed across Tapi river in Jalgaon district. The project is being implemented by the Tapi Irrigation Development Corporation under the Khandesh region. The project is being taken up under the' Baliraja Jal Sanjeevi Yojana." Under the project, 9128 villages of Yawal taluka of Jalgaon district will be covered. The area is planned to be brought under irrigation.
The original administrative sanction for the project was Rs. 198.06 crore (Rupees One Hundred and Ninety Eight Crores Six Lakh only) based on the 1997-98 tariff list as per Reference No. 1 for the project, namely, Shelgaon Barrage Medium Project Taluka Jalgaon District Jalgaon. Thereafter, as per Reference No. 2, the first revised administrative approval was granted for 2011-12 based on the tariff of Rs. 699.48 crore (Rupees Six hundred ninety nine crore forty eight lakhs only).
In the meantime, due to increase in the project cost due to increase in tariff list, increase in land acquisition cost, change in concept, new provisions etc., the Executive Director, Tapi Irrigation Development Corporation, Jalgaon, has submitted the second revised administrative approval proposal of 2016-17 based on the tariff at a cost of Rs. 968.97 crore to the State Government under reference number 3. The proposal for the second revised administrative approval has been submitted for approval of the Expenditure Priority Committee to obtain the concurrence of the Planning and Finance Department. The project has a benefit-cost ratio of 1.78.
The proposed First Revised Administrative Sanction for the project is Rs. 699.48 crore higher than the Second Revised Administrative Sanction of Rs. 968.97 crore by Rs. 269.49 crore. The main reasons for the price increase are:

The total live storage available in the project is 116.366 Dalgham and total live storage is 127.529 Dalgham. In Jalgaon district, 9128 hectares area of Yawal taluka will be benefitted by irrigation. The project is being implemented under the Jal Jeevan Mission of the central government.
The proposal was placed for consideration in the meeting of the Expenditure Priority Committee dated 06.09.2019. The proposal was approved by the expenditure committee. It was proposed for consideration of the Cabinet. As on 09/09/2019. The Cabinet has taken a conscious decision to accord administrative approval to the Second Revised Project Report of the Shelgaon Barrage Medium Project at a cost of Rs. 968.97 crore based on the 2016-17 schedule, after taking into consideration the current status of construction, reasons for cost escalation, etc.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: With this decision of the Government, the Second Revised Project Report of the Shelgaon Barrage Medium Project, based on the 2016-17 schedule, is being accorded administrative approval at a cost of Rs. 968.97 crore (Rupees Nine Hundred Sixty Six Crores, Ninety Seven Lakh only). Out of this, Rs. 881.42 crore (Rs.88.84 crore forty two lakhs only) is for working capital and Rs. 87.55 crore (Rs.7.85 crore fifty five lakhs only) is for establishment and ancillary expenditure. Sub-heading wise provisions are being added to Goshwara.
The Second Revised Administrative Approval granted to the Scheme has been entered in the Register of Medium Project Operations at serial number 6/ 2019.
The remaining work of the project should be completed within the stipulated time and within the limits of the revised administrative approval.

The capital contribution (4701-H729) should be included under this head and should be subtracted from the grants sanctioned under it.
The Second Revised Administrative Sanction is being granted subject to the following conditions:
5.1) The observations in point number 1 to 27 in the report of the State Level Technical Advisory Committee should be complied with.
5.2. New elements that create financial liabilities should not be included in the project without government approval. Also, make sure that the cost of the project does not increase.
5.3 The remaining works of the project should be completed by June 2021 and within the revised administrative approval cost limit.
5.4. In-progress tenders should be completed on priority. The priority of the remaining works should be decided and works/ tenders should be processed accordingly so that the water storage is maximized for irrigation.
5.5. Transfer the management of irrigation to the Water Utilization Agency by establishing a Water Utilization Agency in the entire beneficiary area.
5.6 No additional expenditure or disbursement of funds shall be made beyond the limit of administrative sanction granted for any purpose under this scheme. In this regard, the existing government decisions, guidelines of CVC, limits of financial powers, prescribed tender procedures should be strictly implemented.
5.7 The said revised administrative approval shall not be deemed to be an endorsement of various decisions taken by the field officers at various stages of project implementation and such irregularities, if any, have taken place in the field.
5.8. Proceedings of the ongoing or proposed departmental inquiry into the scheme should be continued. The decision to grant the revised administrative sanction will not affect the ongoing or future departmental enquiry proceedings.
