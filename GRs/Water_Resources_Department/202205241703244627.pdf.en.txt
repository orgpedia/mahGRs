Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005 Introduction- Under the policy of participatory irrigation, the state of Maharashtra has implemented the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005 in the year 2005. Before that, action was being taken through cooperative irrigation water use agencies. However, since 2005, under the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005, small, distribution, canal and project level water use agencies are being established and the irrigation system is transferred to the water use agencies. These agencies have been entrusted with major responsibilities like maintenance, repair, equitable distribution of water, collection of water table etc.
As of January 2022, 5658 water use agencies have been registered and out of which 3213 agencies have been transferred 12.84 lakh ha area for management. The Senior Engineer Conference was held in Pune on 17th and 18th February 2022. In the Report of the Senior Engineer Council, para. After discussions with all the secretaries and all the chief engineer level officers of the department, it has been recommended to the government that a comprehensive change in the law is necessary to overcome the difficulties in the implementation of the Maharashtra Irrigation System Management by Farmers Act 2005. Accordingly, the government was considering the formation of a study group to amend the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken at the government level.
The government has decided to amend the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005. The composition of the committee is as follows:

The course of the workshop will be as follows:
(1) The Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005 and the Council of Senior Engineers under the Water Resources Department. To study the report submitted in accordance with the suggestions of the meeting held on 17th and 18th February 2022 and suggest changes in the clauses as per the requirement.
(ii) To study the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Rules, 2006 and suggest changes in the provisions as per the requirement thereof.
3) To study the model of water use agencies of other states while suggesting these changes and give clear feedback on choosing the right model.
4) To suggest the inclusion of clauses as per requirement under the Commercial Sustainability among the provisions under the Co-operation Act of the State.
The committee will have to submit its report within three months.
The member secretary of this study group, in coordination with other members and if necessary, with the help of other officials working in this matter, should study the Maharashtra Irrigation System from the Farmer's Management Rule 2006 and suggest changes in its provisions as per the need, while suggesting these changes, study the model of the water use agency of other states and give clear feedback on choosing the right model. It is suggested to include clauses as per requirement from the provisions under the state co-operation act. The Study Group submitted its report to the Additional Chief Secretary (Water Resources) and Secretary (Lakshavi) on 8 th August 2022
