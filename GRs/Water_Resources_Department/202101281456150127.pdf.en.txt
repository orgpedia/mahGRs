The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 has come into force with effect from 08/06/2005. Section 12 (6) (d) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 provides as follows:
" The existing private irrigation systems will be continued for five years on the condition of efficient use of water. The provisions of section 14 (4) shall come into force on such date as the Government may specify. However, different dates may be notified for different areas depending upon the geographical situation. Section 14 (4) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 provides as follows:
" From the date the perennial crops in the area to be determined shall not be irrigated from the canal unless drip irrigation, frost irrigation or other water saving techniques approved by the Authority are used. From this saving, the remaining water will be distributed on equitable basis in the beneficiary areas and adjoining areas to meet the future demand for drinking water. " Many private irrigation projects in the state have been given water permission before the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 came into force. Therefore, as per Section 12 (6) (d) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005, the period of operation of the private lift irrigation scheme has expired as on 7/6/2010, subject to the approval of the concerned No. 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5

Except these 8 schemes, the proposal to specify the date for implementing the provision of section 14 (4) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 for the private irrigation schemes in the project benefit areas and reservoirs in the State, or to extend the date for the private irrigation schemes beyond 31.05.2020, was under consideration of the Government.
As per the provisions of Section 12 (6) (d) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005, in the interest of farmers, the Government has decided to extend the validity of all irrigation projects, except 1) Tembhu Upsurge Irrigation Scheme (Sangli) 2) Bhima (Ujani) 3) Mula (Ahmednagar) 4) Lower Manar (Nanded) 5) Hatnur 6) Utar Pus (Yavatmal) 7) Kanholi Nala (Nagpur) 8) Amboli (Sindhudurg) Private Upsurge Irrigation Schemes in the beneficiary areas and reservoirs in the State till 31st May, 2021, subject to the following conditions:
The Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority (MWRRA) is mandated to regulate the water supply in the benefit areas and reservoirs of all the projects except the existing private irrigation projects in the above mentioned 8 projects on the condition of efficient utilization of water, as mentioned in Section 13 (6) (d) of the MANIPRA Act, 2005. The extension will be applicable to all private lift irrigation schemes existing as on 31/5/2020.
Perennial crops under the license of existing private irrigation schemes shall not be irrigated without installation of equipment and techniques that will be mandatory for drip irrigation, drip irrigation or other water saving techniques approved by the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority.
