The Urdhwa Wardha Project is constructed on the Wardha River near Simbhora village in Morshi taluka of Amravati district under the Vidarbha Irrigation Development Corporation, Nagpur. The project falls under the Wardha sub-basin of Godavari basin.
A total of 70,169 areas in 279 villages of four taluks of Amravati district and two taluks of Wardha district are being irrigated through the right and left main canal of this project. The work of Gurukunj Upasa Irrigation Scheme (7109 hectares) and Pathargaon Upasa Irrigation Scheme (2163 hectares) on the right main canal is in progress, both of which will benefit 22 villages of Tivsa Taluka and 5 villages of Chandur Railway Taluka.
The project was completed in 1993 and the works, including the installation of an irrigation and distribution system, were completed in 2006. Under this project, direct irrigation has been started since 1997.
Beneficiary Water Utilization Societies have been set up under the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005. Section No. 1 of the said Act provides for transfer of benefit area including distribution system to water utility. 22 According to the regional authorities and water use agencies

A proposal has been prepared to make necessary amendments in the distribution system by conducting a joint field inspection with the chairman.
In this regard, the special repair of Urdhwa Wardha Project Taluka Morshi, District Amravati Chandrabhaga Branch Canal Straight Redeemer, Straight Shortcut and Satefal Distributorship (Straight Redeemer and Short Canals from 0 to 13 km on the Chandrabhaga Branch Canal of the Right Main Canal) and the transfer of area to the Water Utilization Agency and administrative approval for the works of the Water Utilization Agency was under the consideration of the Government. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
Government's decision:
1) Under Vidarbha Irrigation Development Corporation, Nagpur, special repair of Urdhwa Wardha Project Taluka Morshi, District Amravati Chandrabhaga Branch Canal Straight Redeemer, Straight Shortcut and Satefal Distributorship (Straight Redeemer and Short Canals from 0 to 13 km on the Chandrabhaga Branch Canal of the Right Main Canal) and administrative approval is being provided to the Water Utilization Agency for the works of area transfer and empowerment of Water Utilization Agency. Administrative sanction is being accorded for the said work at a total cost of Rs. 3,99,42, 000/- (Rupees Three Crore Ninety Nine Lakh Forty Two Thousand only) and other incidental expenditure of Rs. 99,14,000/- (Rupees Ninety Nine Lakh Four Thousand only) aggregating to Rs. 4,98,56, 000/- (Rupees Four Crore Ninety Eight Lakh Ninety Five Thousand only) subject to the following conditions.
Terms and Conditions: In view of the above, it is proposed to approve the proposal for this special repair work subject to the following conditions.
(i) Government Circular No. 2013/ (785/13)/ Simvya (Works), D. The Corporation is bound to follow the guidelines dated 01/01/2015, maintain records and take action.
(ii) The Corporation shall be solely responsible for making detailed survey and concept based provision of the construction before the technical approval.
(iii) The technical approval of the budget should be provided at the competent level by examining the price list, outlays and carrying gaps in the design/ concept envisaged in the budget.
(iv) To make necessary provisions for special amendment of the distribution system by taking into confidence the water utility members of the beneficiary areas jointly, after inspection by the concerned officers of the water utility organization and the field officers, in the implementation of the Management of Irrigation System by Farmers (MMISF) as per the Maharashtra Irrigation Act 2005. Timely planning for transfer of entire benefit area to the project water utility should be done before commencing the work and only then the tender process should be initiated.
The Superintending Engineer will be fully responsible for transferring the entire benefit area to the Water Utilization Agency within the stipulated time frame after completion of the special repair works of the proposed distribution system.
