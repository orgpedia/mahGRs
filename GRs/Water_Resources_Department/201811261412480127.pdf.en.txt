A total of 5.85-acre stone dam has been constructed on Tarli river in Satara. 6,507 of Tarali valley in Satara district by implementing 8 sub-irrigation schemes on Tarali river downstream of the dam. This area will be irrigated. Apart from this, the main canal of the Urmodi Upsa Irrigation Scheme has been removed by removing the branch canals, thereby taking the total number of Khatav and Man taluks in Satara district to 8,876. So many drought prone areas will get the benefit of irrigation. It is planned to use the water from the Arfal canal for this area through the Urmodi Upsa Irrigation Scheme and the same amount of water will be recharged in the Arfal canal through the canal reaching the corner of this project.
The Government has sanctioned Project No. The original administrative approval was granted at a cost of Rs. 194.32 crore based on the 1995-96 tariff. Thereafter, the Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation (MKVDC) has approved the project as reference No. The first Revised Administrative Sanction was granted at a cost of Rs. 685.93 crore based on the 2000-01 schedule.
Subsequently, the Government issued the project No. The Second Administrative Sanction was granted at a price of Rs. 954.01 crore based on the 2007-08 tariff schedule. The government has also approved the project No. The Third Administrative Sanction was granted at a cost of Rs. 1057.63 crore based on the 2008-2009 tariff list.
In the intervening period, the project cost has increased due to increase in tariff rates, increase in cost of land acquisition (especially under the new Land Acquisition Act), increase in cost due to change in concept, increase in cost due to inadequate provisions and other factors as well as establishment and ancillary costs.
Therefore, the Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation has submitted the fourth revised administrative approval proposal to the government.

The State Level Technical Advisory Committee (SLTC) has recommended the fourth revised project report of the Tarali project at a cost of Rs. 1610.32 crore.
The fourth revised administrative sanction proposal of Tarali Project for an amount of Rs. 1610.32 crore (Tariff 2013-14) has been approved by the Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA). The Planning and Finance Department had directed that it should be presented before the Cabinet.
The Cabinet has taken a conscious decision to give administrative approval to the 4th revised project report of the Water Resources Department based on the 2013-14 tariff list at a cost of Rs. 1610.32 crore with the objective of completing the project in order to provide irrigation benefits to the taluks of Patan, Karad and Satara in the Tarali valley of Satara district as well as the erosion prone areas of Khatav and Man taluks.
Government Decision- Tarali Major Irrigation Project, Ta. 4th Revised Administrative Sanction is being accorded to the project at a cost of Rs. 1610.32 crore (Rs. 1482.28 crore for direct works and Rs. 128.04 crore for collateral) based on the 2013-14 tariff list of Water Resources Department, Patan, G. Satara, subject to the following conditions: The details of sub-heading provisions of the project are annexed as Annexure A.
1) State Level Technical Advisory Committee Report Issue No. 26 and 27 must be fulfilled.
2) New elements that create financial liabilities should not be included in the project without government approval.
3) The project should be completed as per the plan and within the revised administrative approval cost.
4) The work of tenders in progress should be completed on priority. The priority of the remaining works should be decided and works/ tenders should be processed accordingly so that the water storage is maximized for irrigation.
5) Irrigation management should be transferred to the water use agency by establishing a water use agency in the entire benefit area.
(vi) Revised administrative approval shall not be deemed to be an endorsement of various decisions taken by the field officers at various stages of project implementation and such irregularities, if any, have occurred in the field.
