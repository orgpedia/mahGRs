The Maharashtra Water Resources Regulation Authority Act, 2005 has been published in the Maharashtra Government Gazette on 4th May, 2005 to provide for the establishment of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulation Authority and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto for regulating the water resources of the State of Maharashtra and for determining the rates of water to be used for agricultural, industrial, drinking and other purposes for facilitating and ensuring efficient, equitable and sustainable management, allocation and utilization of water resources. The Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority has been constituted by the said Act with effect from 12th August, 2005.
Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority In the meeting held on 24th October, 2017, the proposal to declare the Chairman, Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority as the Head of the Department has been unanimously approved. Accordingly, the matter of declaring the Chairman, Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority as the Head of the Department was under the consideration of the Government. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
Government's decision:
The decision was taken by the Finance Ministry. The Chairman, Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority is being declared as the Head of the Department subject to the conditions contained in the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, dated 17th April, 2015, Part-I, Sub-Division-I, Roll No. 1, Rule No. 2, Pillar 6. These powers will be limited to financial matters of the head of the department.
