The water for the project is being supplied from the Bhima (Ujani) project through the Karamba pump house at the end of the Mohol-Karamba branch canal under the left canal of the Bhima (Ujani) project. The water is drawn in two stages from the Ekrukh pond through a spring and then through the Durganahalli canal to 7200 villages in 21 villages in South Solapur taluka. It is planned to provide irrigation benefits to the area and provide 1.275 billion cubic feet of water for the Bori Medium Project through the Darsanal Canal to the Harana River. The project includes pumping stations, overhead ducts, canals and distribution systems.

The Expenditure Priority Committee has decided to give administrative approval to the first revised project report based on the 2013-14 schedule at a cost of Rs. 412.80 crore, based on the reason for the increase, the report of the State Technical Advisory Committee, the observations of the Expenditure Priority Committee and the work done in the project, with the aim of completing the work of the Ekrukh Upasa Irrigation Scheme, which is a boon to the South Solapur Taluka of Solapur district.

Government Decision: First Revised Administrative Sanction is being accorded to the project of Ekrukh Upasa Irrigation Scheme, Solapur at a cost of Rs. 412.80 crore (Rs. The subheading of the project is attached with the provision of Goshwara.
The irrigation potential of 10,000 hectares of Ekrukh Upas Irrigation Scheme should be created by June 2019.
1. Funds should be utilised on priority for land acquisition, distribution system works and for optimum irrigation generation.
2. As per the prevailing Government policy, the use of closed tube system should be done wherever technically feasible so as to save cost on land acquisition.

4. Establish water use agency in the entire beneficiary area and transfer the irrigation management to the water use agency.
