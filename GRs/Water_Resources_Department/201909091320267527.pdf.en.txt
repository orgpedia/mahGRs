Introduction: Many development works are undertaken with a view to creating infrastructure in the state. The people's representatives are constantly insisting on taking up irrigation projects considering the importance of irrigation in the state. The Government has taken up several irrigation projects to clear the backlog, utilise the water allocated to the State as per the arbitration and provide irrigation facilities to the drought prone areas. As a result, a large number of irrigation projects are under construction in the state. At present, 334 projects are under construction in the state. The State Government will allocate Rs. 8000 to Rs. 9000 crore is provided to the Water Resources Department. However, for works such as Benefit Area Development Programme, Mangroves, Hydel Power Projects, Flood Control, etc. and for works required for Common Schemes (Hydrology Projects, Quality Control Organizations, etc.), the amount required for Irrigation Projects is Rs. 7500 crore. This financial provision can compensate for the increase in cost due to the increase in the price of under-construction projects. However, the project could not be completed. Hence, it has become necessary to create additional sources of funds to complete the remaining under-construction projects.
