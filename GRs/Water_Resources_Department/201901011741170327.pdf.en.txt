The proposal: The Minister, Ministry of Water Resources, River Development and Ganga Rejuvenation, Government of India chaired a review meeting on the projects under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana on 03/05/2016 at Sahyadri Guest House, Mumbai. In the meeting, it was directed that the state government should submit a proposal to the central government to provide funds in the form of a special package for the completion of projects in 14 suicide-prone districts of Vidarbha and Marathwada and in the drought-prone districts of the rest of Maharashtra.
Accordingly, according to the discussion in various meetings held in accordance with the proposal submitted to the Central Government. As per the letter dated 05/12/2017, a special package was sought from the Central Government to complete 91 irrigation projects in the state. Accordingly, a special package of 91 projects has been proposed. It was approved by the Union Cabinet on 18/07/2018. According to the Central Water Commission. The office memorandum was issued on July 30, 2018. Accordingly, the scheme of the special package given by the central government to complete 91 irrigation projects in the state was under the consideration of the government to be named Baliraja Jal Sanjeevi Yojana.
The government has approved a special package scheme to be called Baliraja Jal Sanjeevi Yojana to complete 91 irrigation/ irrigation projects under construction in suicide-prone districts of Vidarbha and Marathwada as well as in the drought-prone areas of the rest of Maharashtra.
Baliraja Jal Sanjeevi Yojana covers 91 projects including 83 minor irrigation projects, 3 major and medium projects in suicide prone districts of Vidarbha and Marathwada and 5 major and medium projects in drought prone districts. This is listed in Appendix 1. The salient features of the scheme are:

91 irrigation projects. Remaining price as on 01/04/2017 is Rs. 15, 325.65 crore. In 2017-18, the State Government has allocated Rs. Rs 1, 674.04 crore.
The cost of these projects will be divided on 25: 75 (Centre: State) basis. Accordingly, a total amount of Rs. 3, 831.41 crore. The total expenditure for the year 2017-18 (Rs. 1674.04 crore) of 25% Central share (Rs. 418.51 crore).
The projects will be completed between 2018-19 and 2022-23.
The Central Government has allowed the States to borrow the remaining amount (75%) within the limits of Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management (FRBM).
After completion of the project, an irrigation potential of 3,76,915 hectares will be created.
