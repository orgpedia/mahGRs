Preamble: Dahigaon Upsa Irrigation Scheme, Ta. Karmala District Solapur, the origin of this scheme is from the reservoir of the Bhima (Ujni) project near the village of Dahegaon in Karmala taluka of Solapur district. Under this scheme, 1.81 Aghfu (51.32 Dalghami) water was lifted from Ujani reservoir to 10500 Ha in Karmala taluka. Irrigation benefits are proposed to be provided to the catchment areas. The project is being implemented by the Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation.
Dahigaon Upsa Irrigation Scheme, Ta. Karmala, Dist. Solapur, As per Reference No.1 of 1995, the project was originally sanctioned for an amount of Rs. 57.66 crore based on 96 schedule. Thereafter, reference No. The first revised administrative approval was granted for the year 2007-08 for a total amount of Rs. 178.99 crore based on the Rating List.
The SUPRAMA proposal received from the Executive Director, Maharashtra Krishna Basin Development Corporation, Pune vide letter dated 09/09/2020 is based on the 2018-19 tariff schedule at a cost of Rs. 342.30 crore.
As per the proposal for Second Revised Administrative Sanction, the profitability ratio of the project is 1.54 and the financial return is 10.23. The government was considering granting administrative approval to the project. Accordingly, the following decisions are taken:
Government Decision: Dahigaon Upsa Irrigation Scheme, Ta. Second Revised Project Report for 2018-19 based on Rating of Rs. 342.30 crore for Karmala, Dist. Solapur is being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions. Out of this, Rs. 308.72 crore is for working capital and Rs. 33.58 crore is for collateral.
The project work should be completed as per the plan and within the revised administrative approval cost limit.
2. Transfer the management of irrigation to the Water Utilization Agency by establishing a Water Utilization Agency in the entire beneficiary area.
No additional expenditure or disbursement of funds beyond the limit of revised administrative approval granted for any purpose for the said scheme.
The Corporation shall be fully responsible for following the prevailing Government decisions, rules, CVC principles, financial limits and prescribed tender procedures while implementing the scheme.
