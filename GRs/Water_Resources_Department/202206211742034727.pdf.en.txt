The Proposal:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| Under this scheme, Urmodi big project will be set up at Mauje Parli in Satara district.           |
| The earthen dam has been constructed on Urmodi, a tributary of river Krishna.                     |
| About five km south of the dam, on the Karhira Nala near Mauje Amble (Satara)                     |
| It is proposed to construct an earthen dam' Ambale' (supplementary reservoir).                    |
| The project is being implemented by the Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation, Pune. |
| The project is spread over 8300 hectares in Satara district.                                      |
| A total of 27,750 hectares (9725 ha) in each of the two drought prone taluks.                     |
| Irrigation will be beneficial.                                                                    |
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
As the project is located in Satara district, it has been included in the central government's Baliraja Jal Sanjeevi Yojana.
A great big project. The original administrative sanction for the project was Rs. 10.86 crore as per Reference No. 1. Thereafter, reference No. 212.07 crore based on the 1990-91 tariff list was accorded administrative approval and subsequently, the reference No. The first administrative approval was granted for an amount of Rs. 867.77 crore based on the 2000-01 tariff list. The profitability ratio is 0.77 and the financial return is 10.07. The government was considering granting administrative approval to the project. Accordingly, the following decisions are being taken: The Third Revised Project Report for 2018-19 based on the Tariff of Rs. 2499.62 crore is being accorded revised administrative approval subject to the following conditions: Out of this, Rs. 2374.29 crore is for work purposes and Rs. 125.33 crore is for incidental expenditure.
The project work should be completed as per the plan and within the limit of revised administrative approval cost.
2. Irrigation management should be transferred to Water Utilization Agency by establishing Water Utilization Agency in the entire beneficiary area.
(3) No further disbursement of funds or any additional expenditure beyond the limit of revised administrative approval granted for any purpose under this scheme.
4. Approval of forest land should be obtained before commencing work/ taking up work. The cutting of the link canal and tunnel to Amble Urmodi reservoir should be approved at the competent level on priority.
The Chief Engineer's approval should be obtained for the pipe distribution plan and concept after conducting a comparative study of the traditional canal and the closed distribution system as per the Government Decision dated 13/01/2017 regarding the closed tube distribution system in the left and right canals of Urmodi Dam and the benefit area on the left and right canal of Amble Dam.
6. Rehabilitation works should be completed on priority and the project works should be completed in such a way that irrigation benefits are achieved without creating inappropriate irrigation potential.
The Corporation shall be fully responsible for following the prevailing Government decisions, rules, CVC principles, financial limits and prescribed tender procedures while implementing the scheme.
(8) No agreement shall be deemed to have been reached in respect of any project in respect of which any financial and procedural irregularities have occurred in the previous period, any rule-breaking proceedings have been carried out or any matter which is likely to cause financial misappropriation. It shall be the responsibility of the Corporation to conduct such inspection in accordance with the prevailing Government decision of the time.
9. The issues in the report of the State Level Technical Advisory Committee should be addressed.
New elements that create financial liabilities should not be included in the project without government approval.
