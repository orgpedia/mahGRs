the prelude
The 44.80-km long Pravara Right Canal, which starts from Bhandardara Dam on the Pravara River, and continues for 76.80-km, is the longest canal in the country. The Pravara Left Canal was completed during the British rule from 1926 to 1930. Through Pravara Right and Left Canal a total of 23077 hectare area of Sangamner, Shrirampur, Nevasa and Rahuri taluks of Ahmednagar district gets the benefit of irrigation.
As per the directions of the Hon'ble High Court dated 23/09/2016, in order to make the water distribution system suitable and efficient, as per the proposal received, the Bhandardara Mega Project has been completed. The right canal chain of Pravara under Akola G. Ahmednagar No.

Terms and Conditions:
(i) Government Circular No. The Corporation is bound to follow the guidelines of the Act, 2013/ (785/13)/ CC/ V (Works), 01/01/2015, maintain records and take action.
(ii) The Corporation shall be solely responsible for making detailed survey and concept based provisions of the construction before the technical approval.
(iii) Technical approval of the budget should be provided at the competent level by examining the outlines of the rates and the carrying gaps in the plan/ concept envisaged in the budget.
(iv) To make necessary provisions for special repairs of the distribution system by conducting joint inspection by the concerned officers and field officers of the established water utility organizations and by taking the water utility members of the beneficiary areas into confidence regarding the implementation of the Management of Irrigation System by Farmers (MMISF) as per the Maharashtra Irrigation Act, 2005. Timely planning of transfer of entire benefit area to the water utility on the project should be done before starting the work. After that, the tender process will begin.
(v) The Superintending Engineer shall be solely responsible for transferring the entire benefit area to the Water Utilization Agency within the prescribed time frame after completion of the special repair works of the proposed distribution system.
(vi) Removal of soil and grass plants/ shrubs should be done by the available machinery of the Mechanical Department of the Water Resources Department.
(vii) The proposed works shall result in saving by making full use of available construction material by way of excavation/ demolition.
