Introduction: The Wang Medium Project is proposed to construct a 1200-metre long earthen dam and 55.50-metre long ogi-style sand dam on the Wang River near the village of Mauje Marathwadi, Taluka Patan, District Satara under the Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation. There are no canals under the project.
It is proposed to irrigate an area of 6200 hectares through lift irrigation. The project is under the Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation. By constructing 10 Kolhapur-style dams on the Wang River, the government plans to benefit 6200 hectares of irrigated area by drawing water from the self-financing irrigation scheme of the beneficiary farmers. There are no plans for the project.

Government's decision:
With this decision, the Wang Medium Project, Ta. Administrative approval is being accorded to the Fifth Revised Project Report of Patan, G. Satara at a cost of Rs. 594.25 crore (Rupees Five Hundred Four Nine Crore Twenty Five Lakh only) based on the 2017-18 tariff list. Out of this, an amount of Rs. 537.69 crore is provided for work-related expenditure and Rs. 56.56 crore for incidental expenditure. Sub-heading wise provisions are being added to Goshwara.
The Fifth Revised Administrative Sanction granted to the Scheme has been entered in the Register of Medium Project Operations at serial number 11/2019.
The remaining work of the project should be completed within the stipulated time and within the limits of the revised administrative approval.
The expenditure incurred on the operation of the scheme under the head Demand No. I-5, 4701 Capital expenditure on Major and Medium Irrigation, 80 General 190 Investment in Public Sector and other Enterprises (02) Under the Scheme, (02) (01) Share capital contribution to Maharashtra Krishna Valley Development Corporation (4701H629) and the subsidy sanctioned under it.
The Fifth Amendment Administrative Sanction is being granted subject to the following conditions:
5.1. The said revised administrative approval does not justify regularisation of irregularities committed at various stages of project implementation.
5.2) Proceedings of the ongoing or proposed departmental inquiry into the project should be continued. The decision to grant revised administrative sanction will not affect the proceedings of the departmental inquiry.
5.3. New elements that create financial liabilities should not be included in the project without government approval.
5.4. Work on tenders in progress should be completed on priority. The remaining works should be prioritized and works/ tenders should be processed accordingly so that the water storage is maximized for irrigation.
5.5 No additional expenditure or disbursement of funds shall be made for the said project beyond the limit of administrative sanction granted for any purpose. In this regard, the existing government decisions, guidelines of CVC, limits of financial powers, prescribed tender procedures should be strictly implemented.
5.6) If the expenditure beyond the approved administrative sanction price is made without the approval of the Government, action should be taken to regulate the expenditure by fixing the responsibility first.
5.7) Approval of the Central Water Commission should be taken as per requirement.
