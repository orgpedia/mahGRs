The Proposal:
The Dam Restoration and Improvement Project Phase-II is a Centrally Sponsored Project with World Bank funding. The main objective of this project is to improve the safety and operational performance of selected dams in the country by strengthening the relevant agencies to manage them in a planned manner and maintain continuity in operation and maintenance of dams. Maharashtra is a part of this project. About 70 per cent of the project cost will be met through a loan from the World Bank. The provisions in the tender document for such projects are mandatory as per the Standard Bid Document prescribed by the World Bank. The arbitration provisions in the World Bank's Standard Bid Document have been approved by the Department of Law and Justice, Government of Maharashtra. The institutional arbitration provisions are different from those implemented by the Government Decision dated 13/10/2016. The government was considering to approve the inclusion of arbitration provisions in the World Bank's Standard Bid Document in the tender under the Dam Restoration and Improvement Project Phase 2 instead of the government's existing arbitration provisions. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:

Government Decision: The Department of Law and Justice, Government of Maharashtra, has issued a notification regarding Dam Restoration and Improvement works in the State covered under the World Bank funded Dam Restoration and Improvement Project Phase-II. Approval is being given to incorporate arbitration provisions in the tenders as per the Standard Bid Document prescribed by the World Bank instead of the institutional arbitration provisions as applicable under the Governing Decision dated 13/10/2016.
This is the government's decision. The Cabinet gave. is being issued pursuant to the approval provided in the Cabinet Meeting held on 13/01/2021.
