The proposal-
1.1 The Government has taken an important decision to implement the concept of" Green Maharashtra" to reduce the intensity and severity of natural disasters such as climate change, rising temperatures, drought and water scarcity. Under the programme, a target of planting 50 crore trees has been set for the period 2017-2019. In addition, in 2016. A unique and ambitious programme of planting 2 crore trees on a single day was launched on 1st July, 2016.
The target is to plant 13 crore and 33 crore trees in 2018 and 2019 respectively. It is very important that the goal of tree plantation is fully achieved and at the same time the trees planted so far are protected, maintained and nurtured well.
Presentation of 4 crore tree plantation programme during 01st July to 07th July, 2017 In front of the council. As per the discussion held on 13th June, 2017, regarding the action to be taken in this regard, As agreed by the Council of Ministers, the matter of communicating the suggestions to the administrative departments was under the consideration of the government. Accordingly, the circular is being issued.

Circular:
In all the Irrigation Development Corporations under the Department of Water Resources as well as in the Taluk, District and State level meetings at the field level, the topics related to tree plantation and rearing should be included as a permanent topic in the agenda at the time of each meeting. In such meetings, the review of tree plantation, the proportion of live trees planted earlier, the planting of new trees in place of dead trees, the preparation of pits in the prescribed time frame as per the target for tree plantation in the next year, the availability of funds, etc. should be reviewed.
The decisions taken in such meetings regarding the tree plantation programme should be recorded in the minutes of the meeting. Further, the implementation of the decisions mentioned in the history and the difficulties encountered should be discussed in the next meeting and measures should be taken to solve the problems and difficulties faced by the field officers.
3) The officers at the taluk, district and state level who will visit the field work, a program should be organized to visit the places of tree plantation by that department. During such field visits, senior officers should inspect whether the trees planted under the concerned administrative departments are properly protected, whether new trees have been planted in place of dead trees, water arrangements and manpower for protection, etc. Also, there should be a solution to the problem.
4. Also inspect the sites selected for proposed tree plantation, preparation of pits of required size within the stipulated time and arrangements for water and protection for the trees to be planted at such sites, arrangement of funds for tree plantation, etc. and give necessary guidance to the field officers.
