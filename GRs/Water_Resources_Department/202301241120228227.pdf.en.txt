Government circular
The Government has decided to constitute Vigilance Committees, one in each of the six Revenue Divisions of the States, to investigate complaints of corruption, bribery, malpractices etc. in the Water Resources Department and to prevent such malpractices. Established on 20/08/1985. Accordingly, the water resources department is taking action on the monitoring and quality control works.
Department of Water Conservation, Government Decisions. As on 31/05/2017, the proposal for establishment of Commissionerate of Soil and Water Conservation for the State has been approved by the Council of Ministers and it has been decided to rename the Water Conservation Department as Soil and Water Conservation Department. In the revised and restructured structure of Soil and Water Conservation Department, 1 office of Chief Vigilance and Quality Control Officer and 6 Regional Vigilance and Quality Control Offices under him have been sanctioned for the entire State under Water Conservation Department.
The Chief Vigilance and Quality Control Officer shall inspect at least 5% of the work by himself or through his officers by a regional team. Further, the Department of Soil and Water Conservation has been empowered to review the works within the limits of funds made available to various departments for water conservation works, to take appropriate administrative action against the concerned officers/ employees in respect of irregularities/ malpractices in the same, as per the prevailing provisions of the M.N.S. (Discipline and Appeal) Rules.
4. Inquiry at the Government level as per the decision of the Department of Soil and Water Conservation, on the ongoing/ inquiry works in the State in matters of disciplinary matters, quality control.
