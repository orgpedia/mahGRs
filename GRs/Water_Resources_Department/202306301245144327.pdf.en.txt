The proposal-
The Jaikwadi project is a very ambitious project for the state of Maharashtra and is expected to make the state self-sufficient in food grains.
The construction of Paithan dam was started in 1965 and completed in 1974. The irrigation facility started from 1975-76 and is now fully developed. A total area of 18322 hectare is irrigated with 41642 hectare on Paithan left canal and 41682 hectare on Paithan right canal.
The project will benefit the irrigation of lands in Aurangabad, Jalna, Parbhani, Beed and Ahmednagar districts.
Public Interest Litigation (PIL) No. 1 regarding the implementation of the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers (MMISF) under the Maharashtra Irrigation Act, 2005. Hon'ble High Court on 173/2013. As per the directive dated 23.09.2016, it is mandatory to establish a Water Utilization Agency on the project cultivable (CCA) area by determining the benefit area of the project from Urdhva Godavari Project to Majalgaon Project.
The Godavari Water Utilization Agency has been constituted under the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005 in the area from 2000 m to 3980 m of the chain distance at distributary number 24 on Paithan Main Left Canal and has been transferred to the Water Utilization Agency by deleting section 22.
Accordingly, under the Jayakwadi project, the government was considering granting administrative approval under special repair work to the proposed works of Water Utilization Agency, Priority No. 1 and 2 in the benefit area from 2000 m to 3980 m on the Paithan Main Left Canal. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 1) Under the Godavari Marathwada Irrigation Development Corporation, Aurangabad, the works of priority number 1 and 2 of the Water Utilization Agency in the area of the chain distance from 2000 m to 3980 m on the Paithan Main Left Canal are being provided administrative approval under the special repair work.

Administrative Sanction is being accorded for the said work at a total estimated cost of Rs. 31174895/- (Rupees Three Crores Eleven Lakhs Seventy Four Thousand Eight Hundred and Ninety Five only) and other incidental expenditure of Rs. 8994727/- (Nineteen Ninety Four Lakhs Ninety Four Thousand Seven Hundred and Twenty Seven only) totalling to Rs. 4,01,69, 622/- (Rupees Four Crores One Lakh Seventy One Thousand Six Hundred and Twenty Two only) subject to the following conditions and conditions.
Terms and Conditions: In view of the above, it is proposed to approve the proposal for this special repair work subject to the following conditions.
(i) Government Circular No. 2013/ (785/13)/ Simvya (Works), D. The Corporation is bound to follow the guidelines dated 01/01/2015, maintain records and take action.
(ii) The Corporation shall be solely responsible for making detailed survey and concept based provision of the construction before the technical approval.
The technical approval of the budget should be provided at the competent level by examining the price list, outlays and carrying gaps in the plan/ concept envisaged in the budget.
(iv) To make necessary provisions for special amendment of the distribution system by taking into confidence the water utility members of the beneficiary areas jointly, after inspection by the concerned officers of the water utility organization and the field officers, in the implementation of the Management of Irrigation System by Farmers (MMISF) as per the Maharashtra Irrigation Act 2005. Timely planning for transfer of entire benefit area to the project water utility should be done before commencing the work and only then the tender process should be initiated.
(v) The Superintending Engineer shall be solely responsible for transferring the entire benefit area to the Water Utilization Agency within the prescribed time frame after completion of the special repair works of the proposed distribution system.
(vi) Removal of soil and grass trees/ shrubs should be done by available machinery from the Mechanical Department of the Water Resources Department.
vii) Save by making full use of the construction material available due to the proposed works (excavation/ demolition).
(viii) The cost of the work should be met from the available grants within the limit of administrative sanction and within the stipulated time.
ix) Quarterly progress report to be submitted to the Government till the completion of the said works. After the completion of the work, a report will be submitted to the government. The Regional Chief Engineer and the Superintending Engineer will be responsible for all the provisions, rates, carrying distance, concept and other technical parameters in the subject administrative approval budget.
(x) The funds shall not be disbursed or spent in excess of the limit of administrative sanction granted for any purpose for the purpose of the said administrative sanction budget. In this regard, the existing government decisions, CWC guidelines, financial limits, prescribed tender procedures should be strictly implemented.
(xi) The approval of the Board of Directors in this regard shall be taken.
