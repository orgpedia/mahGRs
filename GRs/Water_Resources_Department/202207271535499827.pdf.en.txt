Introduction: Mula Irrigation Project has two major canals namely Mula Right Bank Canal and Mula Left Bank Canal. The original right bank canal is 52 km long and was completed in 1972-73. The projected water carrying capacity from the origin is 1650 cusecs. The entire benefit of the Mula Dam project has been transferred to the Water Utilities since 2006. All water use agencies are functional and water is being metered as per demand at government rates.
The construction of the canal has been completed for 50 years and the canal irrigates the area in all three seasons- kharif, rabi and summer. As per the field inspection by the field officers, the Mula Right Canal/ Pathardi Branch Canal, Final Distributor and Distributor No. 3, there has been a lot of damage to the alignment at various places, leakage and erosion from the canal, the canal cut has been less than the planned cut and therefore the planned discharge has not been achieved, many structures on the canal are falling into disrepair and leaking from it, the filling of the canal has been found to be in a dangerous condition in some places and the local people's representatives have also demanded for the repair of the canal. A proposal to grant administrative approval to the above canal repair budget under special amendment was under consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:
