The proposal-
The Jaikwadi project is a very ambitious project for the state of Maharashtra and is expected to make the state self-sufficient in food grains. The construction of Paithan dam was started in 1965 and completed in 1975. The irrigation facility started from 1975-76 and is now fully developed. A total area of 18322 hectares is irrigated on Paithan left canal, 141640 hectares and 41682 hectares on Paithan right canal.
The project will benefit the irrigation of lands in Aurangabad, Jalna, Parbhani, Beed and Ahmednagar districts.
Public Interest Litigation (PIL) No. 1 regarding the implementation of the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers (MMISF) under the Maharashtra Irrigation Act, 2005. Hon'ble High Court on 173/2013. As per the directive dated 23.09.2016, it is mandatory to establish a Water Utilization Agency on the project cultivable (CCA) area by determining the benefit area of the project from Urdhva Godavari Project to Majalgaon Project.
At present, only 19 water utility units have been set up on the Paithan Left Canal.
The department has very less number of officers/ staff of irrigation management. 70% of the posts are vacant. So, there is no availability of manpower to set up water use organization and as a result the progress is very less. For this purpose, the notification regarding the establishment of Water Utilization Agencies on contract basis through external authorization No. 3 It is necessary to take action to publish and hold elections.
In this regard, the government was considering to provide administrative approval to the proposed works of establishing a total of 93 water use agencies on 44469 hectares in the area of Paithan Dawa Canal 0 to 122 km and Vitarika No. 1 to 46 and transferring the area by holding elections. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:

GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 1. Administrative approval is being accorded to the proposed works under Godavari Marathwada Irrigation Development Corporation, Aurangabad for setting up of 93 Water Utilization Agencies in 44469 hectares in the area of Paithan left canal 0 to 122 km and Vitarika No. 1 to 46 and transfer the area by conducting elections. The total cost of the project is Rs. 1,37,02, 695/- (Rupees One Crore Thirty Seven Lakhs Two Thousand Six Hundred and Ninety Five only) and other incidental expenses is Rs. 30,14,593/- (Thirty Lakh Four Thousand Five Hundred and Ninety Three only).
Terms and Conditions: In view of the above, it is proposed to approve the proposal for this special repair work subject to the following conditions.
(i) Government Circular No. 2013/ (785/13)/ Simvya (Works), D. The Corporation is bound to follow the guidelines dated 01/01/2015, maintain records and take action.
(ii) The Corporation shall be solely responsible for making detailed survey and concept based provision of the construction before the technical approval.
(iii) The technical approval of the budget should be provided at the competent level by examining the price list, outlays and carrying gaps in the design/ concept envisaged in the budget.
(iv) To make necessary provisions for special amendment of the distribution system by taking into confidence the water utility members of the beneficiary areas jointly, after inspection by the concerned officers of the water utility organization and the field officers, in the implementation of the Management of Irrigation System by Farmers (MMISF) as per the Maharashtra Irrigation Act 2005. Timely planning for transfer of entire benefit area to the project water utility should be done before commencing the work and only then the tender process should be initiated.
(v) The Superintending Engineer shall be solely responsible for transferring the entire benefit area to the Water Utilization Agency within the prescribed time limit after completion of special repair works of the proposed distribution system.
(vi) The cost of the work should be met from the available grants within the limit of administrative sanction and within the scheduled time.
vii) Quarterly progress report to be submitted to the Government till the completion of the said works. After the completion of the work, a report will be submitted to the government. The Regional Chief Engineer and the Superintending Engineer will be responsible for all the provisions, rates, carrying distance, concept and other technical parameters in the subject administrative approval budget.
(viii) The administrative approval shall not entail any additional expenditure or disbursement of funds beyond the limit of administrative approval granted for any purpose for the purpose of the budgetary allocation. In this regard, the existing government decisions, CWC guidelines, financial limits, prescribed tender procedures should be strictly implemented.
ix) Approval of the Board of Directors in this regard shall be taken.
