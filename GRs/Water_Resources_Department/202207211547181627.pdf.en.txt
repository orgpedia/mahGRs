Introduction: Mula Irrigation Project has two major canals namely Mula Right Bank Canal and Mula Left Bank Canal. The length of the original right bank canal is 52 km and the construction of the canal was completed in the year 1972-73 and the water is being released for the benefit of the area. The projected water carrying capacity from the origin is 1650 cusecs. The water management of the entire benefit area of the Mula Dam project has been transferred to the Water Utilization Agency since 2006. All water use agencies are functional and water is being metered as per demand at government rates.
The construction of the canal has been completed for 50 years and the canal irrigates the area in all three seasons- kharif, rabi and summer. According to the field inspection conducted by the field officials, the right canal km 36 to 52 has suffered a lot of damage to the lining at various places, the leakage and erosion from the canal, the canal cut has been less than the planned cut and the planned discharge has not been achieved, many structures on the canal are falling into disrepair, the filling of the canal has been found to be in a dangerous condition in some places, and the local people's representatives have also demanded the repair of the canal. No. 36/00 to 52/00 Km. A proposal to give administrative approval to the canal repair budget in the state under special amendment was under the consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:
