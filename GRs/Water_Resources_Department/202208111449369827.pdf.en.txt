Preamble: Padmalaya-II lift irrigation scheme G. Jalgaon is proposed to be constructed with a capacity of 70.36 LMT. The project is being implemented by the Tapi Irrigation Development Corporation under the Khandesh region. Under the project, 9000 houses will be constructed in Erandol and Dharangaon taluks of Jalgaon district. The area is planned to be brought under irrigation.
Padmalaya-II Lift Irrigation Scheme G. Jalgaon Project was sanctioned at a cost of Rs. 95.44 crore (Rupees Fifty Five Crore Forty Four Lakh only) was the original administrative sanction.
The Executive Director, Tapi Irrigation Development Corporation, Jalgaon has submitted the first revised administrative sanction proposal of Rs. 370.94 crore based on the price list of 2016-17 to the Government of Maharashtra under reference no. Profitability ratio of the project is 1.95.
The Administrative Sanction for the project is Rs. 95.44 crore higher by Rs. 275.50 crore than the First Revised Administrative Sanction of Rs. 370.94 crore.

The First Amended Administrative Sanction is granted subject to the following conditions:
5.1 The approval of the Central Water Commission for the project should be taken on priority.
5.2 The concept of the captive tube distribution system of the project should be examined by the Central Concept Picture Organization, Nashik and the approval of the Chief Engineer should be obtained for the concept and design of the captive distribution system.
5.3 The total number of projects is 9000. The area will be irrigated, of which 7000 will be. The original project of dredging (stabilizing) the area through the existing mill canal is proposed in the report. It is only 2000. As the area is newly irrigated, the project does not require environmental clearance under Gazette of India dated 14.01.2018. Therefore, the corporation should first verify the irrigation area of the project and the environmental clearance at the competent level.
5.4 The Corporation shall take action to obtain all the technical approvals required for the project at the competent level.
5.5 The Corporation shall ensure that the issues in the Scrutiny Report of the State Advisory Committee are addressed.
5.6 The Corporation shall be solely responsible for not disburse funds or incur any additional expenditure beyond the limit of administrative sanction granted for any purpose for the said project. In this regard, the existing government decisions, rules, guidelines of CVC, financial powers, prescribed tender procedures must be strictly implemented and the responsibility for this will be entirely of the corporation.
