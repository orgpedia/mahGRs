5.5 The Corporation shall take steps to obtain all necessary statutory and technical approvals from the competent authority.
5.6 Action should be taken regarding the irregularities in the tender process of the said project as mentioned by the Secretary level sub-committee.
5.7 The Corporation shall be solely responsible for not disburse funds or incur any additional expenditure beyond the limit of the revised administrative sanction granted for any purpose for the said project. In this regard, the Water Resources Department must strictly implement the prevailing government decisions, rules, guidelines of CVC, limits of financial powers, prescribed tender procedures and the responsibility for this will be entirely of the corporation.
5.8 The Government shall not be deemed to have given its consent to any financial and procedural irregularities, any extra-judicial action or any other matter which is likely to cause corruption in the earlier period in respect of the said project and no such consent/ approval has been given by the Government in respect of the said project.
5.9 The said administrative approval shall not be deemed to be an approval of the technical aspects of the report or the decision at the field level regarding the tender or the field irregularities that have occurred.
5.10 Proceedings of the ongoing or proposed departmental inquiry into the project will continue. Further, the decision to grant revised administrative sanction will not hamper the proceedings of the departmental inquiry.
5.11 New elements that create financial liabilities will not be included in the project without government approval.
5.12 In order to ensure that the works in progress are completed on priority so that the water storage can be maximized for irrigation, the remaining works will be prioritized and works/ tenders will be processed accordingly.
5.13 The project work should be completed as per the plan.
