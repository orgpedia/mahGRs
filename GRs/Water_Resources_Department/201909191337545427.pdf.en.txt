The Proposal:
The Paladag Medium Project is located in the Purna sub-basin of the Tapi basin. The project is built on the Vishwaganga river east of Paladag village in Motala taluka of Buldhana district. The project is located at a distance of 18 km from Motala village, which is 20 km away on the Buldhana-Malkapur state highway. The length of the earthen dam is 865 m. The maximum height is 24.06 m. That's so much. The length of the sewer is 124.50 m. The maximum flood flow carrying capacity is 1095 km per second. The total water storage available in the dam is 7.51 DLH and the length of the canal is 14.97 km. The carrying capacity of the canal is 1.54 g/ s. The total area covered under this project is 4317 hectares covering Paladag, Tarapur, Taroda Shirva, Kharbadi, Kothli, Motala and Varud in Motala Taluka of Buldhana district. The total area is 3032. The project area is 1932. The project has an irrigation potential of 2415 MW and is under the Vidarbha Irrigation Development Corporation (VIDC), which was transferred to the irrigation management in 1981.
The canal system of the Paladag medium project is 38 years old.
The main canal of the project is 14.97 km on the left bank of the dam wall. Also, the main canal runs along the lower side of the mountain. Since most of the canal passes through the Murmad area, rainwater during the rainy season and during irrigation is released from the canal.
