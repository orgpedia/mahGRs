Introduction: In the 168 km stretch of the Girna river, 60 to 70 villages face drinking water shortage. They have to depend on the rotation of the dam. As a result, most of the villages in the district are deprived of potable water. To solve this serious problem, the people's representatives of the area had demanded from the Maharashtra government and the central government to stop the rain water from the area below the Girna Dam.
The Executive Director, Tapi Irrigation Development Corporation, Jalgaon has stated in the pre-feasibility report that the project is successful as per the pre-feasibility report of seven pneumatically operated gated weirs. The preliminary investigation report (PIR) of the proposed seven dams was submitted to the The Government has given in principle approval on 29/06/2015. While approving the project, it has been directed that in order to reduce the cost of the dam, various options such as Weir on Permeable Foundation, Diaphragm Wall on the top/ bottom of the dam, Ferro Cement Technology, etc. should be considered in the concept.
As per the pre-feasibility report prepared by the Executive Director, Tapi Irrigation Development Corporation, Jalgaon, the benefit ratio of this scheme comes to 1.71%. This scheme will provide 21.49 Dalhousie water for irrigation. There are 7 projects under this scheme.
The project was approved by the State Technical Advisory Committee No. 1 has been approved. The project has been included in the Tapi Integrated Water Plan to meet the issues raised by the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority and is 100% funded.
