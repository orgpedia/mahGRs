The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 has come into force with effect from 8/6/2005.
Section 12 (6) (d) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 provides as follows:
" The existing private irrigation systems will be continued for five years on the condition of efficient use of water. The provisions of section 14 (4) shall come into force on such date as the Government may specify. However, different dates may be notified for different areas depending upon the geographical situation. Section 14 (4) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 provides as follows:
Perennial crops in the areas to be determined shall not be irrigated from the canal from the date to be determined, unless drip irrigation, frost irrigation or other water saving techniques approved by the Authority are used. From this saving, the remaining water will be distributed on equitable basis in the beneficiary areas and adjoining areas to meet the future demand for drinking water. " Many private irrigation projects in the state have been given water permission before the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005 came into force. Therefore, as per Section 12 (6) (d) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005, the period of operation of the private lift irrigation scheme has expired as on 7/6/2015, subject to the approval of the concerned No. The date was extended till 07/06/2018 under the Government Decision 1& 2. Although the extension has expired, it has been observed that a large number of private lift irrigation schemes are in operation in the state. Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority. Except these 8 schemes, the Government was considering a proposal to specify the date for implementing the provision of Section 14 (4) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005, for private irrigation schemes in the project benefit areas and reservoirs in the State, or to extend the date for private irrigation schemes beyond 07/06/2018.
Government's decision
The government has taken the following decisions:
In accordance with the provisions of Section 12 (6) (d) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority Act, 2005, in the interest of farmers, all irrigation projects, except 1) Tembhu Upasana Irrigation Scheme (Sangli) 2) Bhima (Ujani) 3) Mula (Ahmednagar) 4) Lower Manar (Nanded) 5) Hatnur 6) Utar Pus (Yavatmal) 7) Kanholi Nala (Nagpur) 8) Amboli (Sindhudurg) Private Upasana Irrigation Schemes in the beneficiary areas and reservoirs of the State are being extended till 7th June, 2019.
The extension will be applicable to all existing and newly proposed private lift irrigation schemes as on 07/06/2018 except the existing private schemes in the benefit area and reservoir of the above 8 projects on the condition of efficient utilization of water as stipulated in Section 12 (6) (d) of the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority (MANIPRA) Act, 2005. The proposals of the new private irrigation schemes are being approved subject to the condition that the perennial crops will have to use drip irrigation, drip irrigation or other water saving techniques approved by the Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority.
