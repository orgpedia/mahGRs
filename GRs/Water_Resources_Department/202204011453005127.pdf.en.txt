Introduction: The small project. Sakri, G. Dhule The project has been completed on the Kan river in the year 2000. The catchment area of the scheme is 113.20 sq km and the length of the dam is 580m. That's it. The maximum height is 21.92 m. The total live water storage available is 3.17 Dalghami and total live water storage is 3.96 Dalghami. The length of the drain is 172 m. The irrigated area is 255. That's it. With the completion of the dam, the dam and its reservoir have been transferred to the Irrigation Management Department since 2001, while the canal and its distribution system have not been transferred to the Construction Department.
The project is located 80 metres below the main dam. The soil near the nearby Vimochaka gets eroded every year in the rainy season. It has also been reported that due to the time frame of the project and other reasons, the construction has become hollow and leaking due to the uprooting of the stone pointing and the exit of the mortar. In this regard, the government receives budget from the Tapi Irrigation Development Board under special amendment to repair the fills and repair the sandy seeps.
This work was done under the Water Resources Department Government Resolution No. 2013/ (785/13)/ Simvya (Works), No. As per 1/8/2019, the work is proposed to be taken up as per Priority-2 of Type-1.
The proposal to give administrative approval to the repair work of Sakri, G. Dhule was under the consideration of the government under special amendment to the budget. The government has taken the following decisions:
