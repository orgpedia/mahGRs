Preamble: Under Section 3 (2) of the Maharashtra Regulation of Transfer of Government Employees and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Government Duties Act, 2005, there shall be no general term of office for Group D employees. They shall not be transferred outside the place where they are working, unless there is a vacant post in the place where they have requested for transfer or the transfer has been sought by mutual consent or there is a general grievance of serious nature against them.

With the approval of the government, Group D employees are also transferred from one circle to another.
As per the Government Resolution No. 4, General Administration Department, dated 3rd June, 2011, the terms and conditions have been prescribed for transfer of Group B (Non-Gazetted) and Group C employees in the Government service on request/ out of cadre basis. The seniority of the post will be determined on the date of joining after the transfer of the concerned employee at serial number 8. Also, the condition has been prescribed that the benefit of previous service will be admissible for salary fixation, leave, pension as per the provisions of the relevant rules of the Finance Department.
However, no clear orders have been issued as to how seniority of Group D employees should be determined in the new circle after their transfers/ transfers as per their request/ out of cadre. Therefore, it is possible to determine the seniority of different services by applying different criteria in each circle. In view of this, the matter of giving self-explanatory instructions to make it possible to take decisions in the direction of equal justice in the same case was under the consideration of the government.
The general principles of seniority have been prescribed under Rule No. 4 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1982.
""" 2 (a) provides that"""" the competent authority shall determine the seniority of the transferred Government servant in comparison with other Government servants in the rank, cadre or service to which he has been transferred, the class and pay scale of the post, cadre or service to which he has been transferred, his period of service therein and the circumstances leading to his transfer, taking into account the appropriate circumstances."""
As per Rule 3 (a) of the said Rules, the competent authority is defined as" the authority competent to appoint to any post, cadre or service."
That is, the competent authority to take such action is the appointing authority i.e. the Superintending Engineer of the concerned Board.
In case of transfer of Group D employees outside the circle on request/ in exceptional circumstances/ as a special case under the aforesaid provisions, the following procedure shall be followed for determining their seniority in service.
