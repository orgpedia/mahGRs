Introduction: Under the Adla Medium Project, the Adla Dam is built on the Adla River. Akole, G. Ahmednagar was built in 1977. The total capacity of the project is 1060 Dalghfu and the total benefit area of the project is 3913 hectares. The left canal crosses the right bank of the dam. The carrying capacity of this canal is 65 m.c. assuming 42 cubic feet per second. It is 249 m. In the drainage area, a cement pipe of 1100 m diameter is laid and crossed through a closed pipe. This work was done many years ago and at present, the carrying capacity of the canal is 62 cubic feet and due to silt accumulation in the closed pipeline, the sewage is not discharged at full capacity. Therefore, irrigation is not possible. It has been 43 years since the construction of this pipeline has been completed. Also, during the floods of 2019 and 2021, the airwell at No. 185 was broken due to which it was not possible to divert the floodwater to the benefit area. Accordingly, the left canal of 63 m. It is 249 meters. A proposal to give administrative approval to the special repair work to renovate the pipeline in the state was under consideration. The government will take a decision accordingly.
