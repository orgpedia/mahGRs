The Proposal:
Under the Haranbari Medium Project, the Jaikheda Barrage Canal has been taken out from the British era barrage near Sompur village on the Mausam River and the total length of the canal is 6 km with a carrying capacity of 20.00 g/ s. The canal irrigates 140 hectares of the Jaikheda suburb. Jaikheda Dam was damaged by floods in 2005. Its work was completed in 2010, after which regular water is being supplied for irrigation from the canal.
During the repair work of the canal near Sompur village, 780 meters of canal No. It is 1200 meters.
A large quantity of silt was found in the canal. In the length of this canal, the height of Sompur village on the right side of the canal is from Garhi (5 to 30 m). During the rainy season, the sewage/ sludge from the village gets collected in the canal. As a result, the canal is filled with silt. Since this problem will always be there, in order to make the canal safe and regular, it is necessary to construct a bridge from 791 to 1200 m. In the meantime, the construction of a closed pipeline has been proposed. The local people's representatives, the water utility and the farmers of the area had demanded this work from time to time. In this regard, Refer No. 2 In exercise of the powers conferred on the Department by the Government decision herein, a proposal for grant of administrative approval under a special amendment for the said work was under consideration of the Government.
