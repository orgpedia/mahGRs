the prelude
The 44.80-km long Pravara Right Canal, which starts from Bhandardara Dam on the Pravara River, and continues for 76.80-km, is the longest canal in the country. The length of the left canal is
It was under British rule from 1926 to 1930. Through Pravara Right and Left Canal a total of 23077 hectare area of Sangamner, Shrirampur, Nevasa and Rahuri taluks of Ahmednagar district gets the benefit of irrigation.
As per the directions of the Hon'ble High Court dated 23/09/2016, in order to make the water distribution system suitable and efficient, as per the proposal received, the Bhandardara Mega Project has been completed. There is an urgent need for restoration and renovation of right canal water course 1 to 14 under Akole, G. Ahmednagar. After this, it will be possible to set up and transfer water use agencies for irrigation management to be done by farmers.
Keeping in view the water supply to important towns and Gram Panchayats, water supply to sugar mills and industrial areas, etc., it is necessary to increase the carrying capacity of Pravara canals by reducing the period of rotation of the canal and strengthening/ repairing the old structures as they have started leaking due to about 90 years. Terms and Conditions:
(i) Circular No. The Corporation is obliged to follow the guidelines in the Proforma-2013/ (785/13)/ CENVIA (Works), 01/01/2015, keep records and take action.
(ii) The Corporation shall be solely responsible for making provision for detailed survey and concept based provision of construction before technical approval.
(iii) The technical approval of the budget should be provided at the competent level by examining the price list, outlays and carrying gaps in the design/ concept envisaged in the budget.
(iv) To make necessary provisions for special amendment of the distribution system by conducting joint inspection by the concerned officers of the water utility and field officers of the water utility established in accordance with the Maharashtra Irrigation Act, 2005 regarding the implementation of the Management of Irrigation System by Farmers (MMISF) and taking them and the water utility members of the beneficiary areas into confidence. Timely planning of transfer of entire benefit area to the water utility on the project should be done before starting the work. After that, the tender process will begin.
(v) The Superintending Engineer shall be solely responsible for transferring the entire benefit area to the Water Utilization Agency within the prescribed time frame after completion of special repair works of the proposed distribution system.
(vi) The removal of soil and grass plants/ shrubs should be done by the available machinery of the Mechanical Department of the Water Resources Department.
(vii) The proposed works shall result in saving by making full use of available construction material by way of excavation/ demolition.
