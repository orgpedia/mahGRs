Date: 27.10.2021
The Maharashtra State Non-Conventional Energy Policy-2020 has been issued as per the guidelines of the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Government of India. Under this, priority has been given to develop hybrid projects by combining solar or other power projects with other conventional/ non-conventional energy sources. Accordingly, hybrid projects are proposed by combining solar or other power projects with hydroelectric projects. The new Government Policy is expected to be issued by incorporating all the suggestions/ directions issued from time to time since the policy was announced on 15.09.2005, circulars/ government decisions/ power policy issued by the Department of Power, orders issued by the Maharashtra Electricity Regulatory Commission/ Central Electricity Regulatory Commission regarding the power tariff of the hydroelectric project and feedback received from the field offices/ promoters. A proposal to amend the Government Policy dated 15.09.2005 is under consideration. At the same time, it is necessary to determine the government's policy on the renewal and modernization of hydropower projects that have been set up by the government and transferred to the Mahajana Corporation for power generation. For this, the government was considering the formation of a study committee with members such as experts and analysts in this field.
Government's decision:
In line with the above proposal, small hydroelectric projects are to be developed under privatization. The Committee is being constituted to suggest amendments in the Government Policy dated 15.09.2005, submit a draft revised policy and make recommendations on the proposed policy for renewal and modernization of the hydroelectric projects constructed and commissioned by the Government under privatization.

Functions of the Committee: 1. To study and suggest a revised draft policy including all the suggestions/ directions issued through circulars/ clarifications/ circulars issued from time to time since the policy was announced on 15.09.2005, circulars/ government decisions/ power policy issued by the Department of Power, orders issued by the Maharashtra Electricity Regulatory Commission/ Central Electricity Regulatory Commission regarding the power tariff of hydel power projects, feedback received from the field offices/ promoters, policies on hydel power project development of various States and various hybrid power projects including hydel power projects.
(ii) Preparation of documents for renewal and modernization of hydroelectric projects constructed and commissioned by the Government under privatization in such a way that the draft of the BOT tender, lease agreement and other documents are in conformity with the plan.
3) Benefits to the Corporation- The committee should recommend how the revenue received from the developer for the hydroelectric project should be divided according to the corporation.
Duration of the Committee: (1) The Committee shall complete the proceedings in respect of the aforesaid (1) and (2) cases within two months.
(2) The Committee may, with the approval of the Chairman, call upon such officers of the Water Resources Department as may be deemed fit, to be special invitees as may be required.
(3) The Chief Engineer, Hydroelectric Project and Quality Control, Pune and the Chief Engineer (Power), Hydroelectric Project, Mumbai shall provide all the necessary documents for the functioning of the Committee.
4) The Chief Engineer, Hydel Power Project and Quality Control, Pune is being appointed as the Coordinating Officer for the entire functioning of the Committee and other facilities, organization of meetings and correspondence, records of work, preservation of information, providing vehicles for meetings, etc. He and his subordinates should make all arrangements for the smooth functioning of the committee. The committee has to submit a report to the government.
