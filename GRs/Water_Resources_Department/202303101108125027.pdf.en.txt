(1) The Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005 (2 of 2005) Government Resolution, No. 24th May, 2022
Introduction: Under the Participatory Irrigation Policy, the State of Maharashtra has enacted the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005 in the year 2005. In order to overcome the difficulties in the implementation of this Act, a comprehensive change in this Act has been proposed in Reference No. 2 here. As per the Government decision dated 24 th May, 2022, a Study Group was constituted under the Chairmanship of Secretary (LAKSHVI). The government was contemplating to reconstitute the committee.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: To overcome the difficulties in the implementation of the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005, the previous Government has passed a resolution to suggest changes in this Act. Instead of the Study Group constituted as on 24 th May, 2022, the Study Group Committee as indicated in the prospectus below is being reconstituted.

Government Resolution No: CDA-2022/ PR No.81/ Grants (Works)
The work of the study group will be as follows:
(1) The Maharashtra Management of Irrigation System by Farmers Act, 2005 and the Council of Senior Engineers in the Department of Water Resources. To study the report submitted in accordance with the suggestions of the meeting held on 17th and 18th February, 2022 and suggest changes in the clauses as required.
To study the Maharashtra Management of Irrigation by Farmers Rules, 2006 and suggest changes in the provisions as per the requirement.
(3) To study the model of other States' Water Utilization Agencies while suggesting changes as above and give clear feedback on the selection of suitable model.
4) To suggest the inclusion of clauses as per requirement in line with Commercial Sustainability among the provisions under the Co-operation Act of the State.
The Committee shall have the power to invite Government/ Non-Government members as may be required.
Duration of the Study Group: The Committee will be required to submit its report to the Government within four months.
