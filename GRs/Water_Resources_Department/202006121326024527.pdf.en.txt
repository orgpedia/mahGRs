Regarding the appointment of an inquiry committee in connection with the problems caused by the acquisition of Maharashtra's land due to the barrage in the inter-state project in Medigadda.
The Government of Maharashtra
The Department of Water Resources
Government Resolution No.2013/ PR No. 87/2013/ JASNI
Madam Cama Marg, Hutatma Rajguru Chowk,
Mantralaya, Mumbai- 400 032
Date:- 12/06/2020
The Medigadda Barrage project on the border of Maharashtra and Telangana has disrupted the region. This caused much hardship to the local people. It came up for discussion in the meeting dated 19/02/2020. As per the directive, the government was considering setting up an inquiry committee to review the problems arising out of the submergence and acquisition of Maharashtra's land due to the barrage in the inter-state project at Medigadda and suggest measures.
Government Decision: The following committee is being constituted to inquire into the problems arising out of the submergence and acquisition of Maharashtra's land due to the barrage in the inter-state project in Medigadda.

(b) Term of Reference: 1. Whether there is any violation of the provisions of the Inter-State Agreement between the States of Maharashtra and Telangana?
2. Medigadda Barrage Area falling under submergence as per height prescribed under Inter-State Agreement in terms of Group No. Also, after increasing the height, the area under submergence as per Group No.
Preparation of survey maps and comparative position of these areas with the areas decided under the inter-state agreement.
3. To demarcate the area adjacent to the main river, tributaries and nullahs falling under the submergence of Medigadda Barrage.
Also to determine the details of the canals, bridges and constructions that are sinking there and to suggest measures.
4. Information regarding land acquisition in Maharashtra by the State of Telangana registered in the Revenue Land Records of the State. What is the procedure followed in respect of other inter-state projects like Lendi, Babli etc? Procedure for rectification of land in Maharashtra acquired by Telangana State and registered in their name on Revenue Land Records (7/12 sample).
6.Farmers in Sironcha and other areas have been affected by the flood. To examine the agreement entered into with the Government of Telangana for the compensation of those losses and to take action and correspondence.
7. The Ministry was supposed to issue a No Objection Certificate that no forest would be submerged after the local Forest Range Office examined the extent of forest that would be submerged in the project and sent a detailed status report to the Ministry, but this does not seem to have happened. But inquire about it.
8. It is possible that the construction was carried out in a different manner than what Telangana had stated while entering into the inter-state agreement. It is also necessary to check how much the forest area will be submerged. Also, it needs to be investigated whether the certificate issued by the forest department was issued as per government norms or under whose pressure.
The local deputy conservator of forest had objected to some things while the construction was going on, but he was advised by the then district collector not to take action.
(a) The term of the Committee:
The committee will have to submit its report to the government within 1 month.
