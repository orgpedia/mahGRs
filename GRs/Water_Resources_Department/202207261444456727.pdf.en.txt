The Proposal:
The Mula Irrigation Project consists of two major canals, the Mula Right Bank Canal and the Mula Left Bank Canal. The original right bank canal is 52 km long and was completed in 1972-73. The projected water carrying capacity from the origin is 1650 cusecs. The water management of the entire benefit area of the Mula Dam project has been transferred to the Water Utilization Agency since 2006. All water use agencies are functional and water is being metered as per demand at government rates.
The construction of the canal has been completed for 50 years and the canal irrigates the area in all three seasons- kharif, rabi and summer. As per the physical inspection by the field officers, the main right canal/ branch canal No. 2, there has been a lot of damage to the lining at various places, leakage and erosion from the canal, the canal cut has been less than the planned cut and therefore the planned discharge has not been achieved, many structures on the canal are being damaged and leaking from it, the filling of the canal has been found to be in a dangerous condition in some places and the local people's representatives have also demanded for the repair of the canal. A proposal to grant administrative approval to the above canal repair budget under special amendment was under consideration of the government. The government has taken the following decisions:
