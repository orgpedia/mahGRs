The Proposal:
Flow Turning Scheme Ambad, Ta. The project is proposed to be constructed on the local western canal near Ambad village in Dindori taluka. The project is located in the Nar Par sub-basin of the Western Vahini river basin.
It is proposed to divert the water flowing westwards to the catchment area of Karanjavan Dam in the East Godavari basin. In the original approved plan of the scheme, the catchment area is 0.67 sq km. The total water available for domestic use is 1.043 DLH.
Water supply is planned. Also, 51 per cent of the water from Karanjawan dam and local use will be utilised through this scheme. area will be restored.
State Technical Advisory Committee, Nashik 168 dated 03/02/2022 has approved Rs. The original administrative sanction of Rs. 10.33 crore has been approved.
The project fits into the parameters of profitability ratio, there is no cost criterion for water storage for diversion schemes, however, the project falls in the hilly region and the parameters of the Lapa schemes are taken into account. The project was approved by the Planning and Finance Department. It was submitted to the Cabinet for approval. According to The Cabinet has approved the project in its meeting held on 16/09/2023. As per this approval, the proposal to issue the original administrative approval was for the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
The Cabinet discussed the proposal submitted by the department on the subject and approved the Flow Turning Scheme Ambad, Ta. Dindori, District Nashik, based on the table 2021-22 for this scheme, Rs. 10.33 crore (Rs. 9.39 crore and Rs. 0.94 crore) has been decided for grant of original administrative approval subject to the following conditions: A summary of the sub-heading provisions of the project is annexed as Annexure-A.
1) The proposal should be approved subject to the condition that the issues raised by the Planning Department and the Finance Department are met and compliance is submitted.
(2) The Corporation shall be responsible for carrying out the entire project within the prescribed cost limit.
3) The cost of the project should be included in the original project proposal.
(4) No additional expenditure or disbursement of funds shall be made for any purpose beyond the limit of administrative sanction granted for the purpose. 5) The process of establishing a beneficiary water use agency on the project should be completed in a timely manner.
