The Proposal:
Maharashtra is a progressive state and rural areas are being developed through several welfare schemes of the government. It is working to bring the rural population into the mainstream of development. As a part of the same, the Government of Maharashtra has issued the Rural Development Department Government Decision No. GRASP-2017, Pr 56 / Yo-3D. As on 1st March, 2017, approval was accorded for implementation of Village Social Transformation Mission in the State as well as for providing State Government share capital in the company set up under the Department.
The aim was to empower 1000 villages in the state through the convergence of government schemes and programmes, corporate social responsibility (CSR) funds, and participation of all sections of the society.
2. A detailed assessment report of the work done during this mission 2017-2021 should be submitted to the Mission Director. It has been approved by the Cabinet. Considering the experience of this mission, there is a huge scope for more and more effective programmes and funds to be made available in the state through Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) funds in Maharashtra. Also, Gram Samajik Parivartan Foundation is an official and important platform for CSR companies to work with the Government of Maharashtra in the social sector. In the budget speech of the Hon'ble Deputy Chief Minister (Finance), it has been announced that "Mission Mahagram" will be implemented in the state and the mission will be implemented in the state during the year 2022-2025. It has been approved by the Cabinet.
Given the response to the FR Mahagram, the government was considering extending the duration of the programme.
The government's decision:
With the objective of social transformation in rural Maharashtra, it is being approved to extend the deadline for implementation of Village Social Transformation Mission i.e. Mission Air in the state from 2022 to 2029 through Corporate Social Responsibility Funds to achieve the sustainable goal of development with focus on key aspects of human development in the state such as food, clothing, shelter, health, education, water management and livelihood during 2022 to 2025.
