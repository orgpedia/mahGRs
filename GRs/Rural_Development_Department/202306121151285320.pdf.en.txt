Preamble: The 73rd Constitutional Amendment to the Constitution of India has inserted the provisions of" Panchayats" in Part IX and extended these provisions to the Scheduled Areas under the" Provisions relating to Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996" (PESA Act). The main objective of the Act is to empower the Panchayats to function as institutions of self-government in the Scheduled Areas.

The PESA Act is intended to provide powers to the Panchayats in the Scheduled Areas by complete decentralization of such powers and authority by the State. Presently, there are 13 districts, 59 taluks, 2931 Gram Panchayats and about 8000 villages in the Scheduled Areas of Maharashtra. Further, the provisions of the Maharashtra Gram Panchayat (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Rules, 2014 have been made applicable to the implementation of the PESA Act in the State.
According to the 5th Schedule of the Indian Constitution. In discharge of the constitutional duties of the Governor, the State of Maharashtra, by virtue of the Government Resolution No. 1. A" Tribal Cell" has been set up in the office of the Governor, State of Maharashtra. Based on the suggestions made by this cell, a" pilot project" has been launched by the Tribal Department. A state-level committee has been formed to oversee the implementation of the project. In the 6th meeting of this committee, it was directed to set up a" State Level Pesa Cell" under the Rural Development Department. Accordingly, as per the decision of the Government of Rural Development Department in Reference No. 2, a State PESA Cell has been constituted to implement the PESA Act and also to control and supervise the" 5% Allotment Fund Scheme to PESA Gram Panchayats" at the State level.
As per the provisions of the Fifth Schedule to the Constitution of India. In view of the need and scope of supporting the Governor, the State of Maharashtra in discharging his constitutional responsibilities, as well as the implementation of the PESA Act and PESA related schemes, control, evaluation, the State Level PESA Cell has the need for a competent system, dedicated and subjective manpower from the state level to the village level. Accordingly, the Director, State (PESA) had submitted a detailed proposal to the Tribal Development Department under Reference No. 3 for strengthening and expansion of the PESA Cell.
The Tribal Development Department has agreed to this proposal and as per the revised proposal of the Rural Development Department, in the first phase, a budget provision of Rs.500 lakh has been approved annually from the year 2022-23 for 81 manpower to be paid through the external system, salary, allowances and other office recurring expenses of the said manpower.
