The Proposal:
As per the recommendations of the Fifteenth Central Finance Commission, the first instalment of the Basic Grant (Untied) for the year 2021-22 for disbursement to the Rural Local Bodies in the State of Maharashtra is Rs. 861.40 crore has been released. 8 here. 8.5.2021. Accordingly, the proposal for devolution of funds received by the State Government from the 15th Central Finance Commission to the Rural Local Bodies in the State was under consideration. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Central Government has released the first instalment of the 15th Central Finance Commission Untied Fund for the financial year 2021-22 amounting to Rs. 861.40 crore

This amount is being made available for disbursement to the Rural Local Bodies (Zila Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat) in the State of Maharashtra under the head of accounts (25152628/ 25152646/ 25152664 respectively) as shown in the accompanying prospectus (Form-A). Funds are being disbursed through Budget Distribution System (BEAMS) to all the Chief Executive Officers of the Zila Parishads as per the prospectus. The funds are being distributed to all the Panchayati Raj Institutions in the ratio of 10:10:80 in Zilla Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat respectively.
The entire grant received under the Fifteenth Central Finance Commission as per the guidelines of the Central Government is to be distributed to the Zilla Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat. National Electronic Fund Transfer (NEFT) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) is an instant money transfer system. The entire fund should be distributed to all the Panchayat Samitis and Gram Panchayats as per the prescribed norms and the Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad should submit the same to the government immediately as per the' Form-B" attached with the certificate. Also, the details of the distribution of funds received under the 15th Central Finance Commission at the level of Zila Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat should be made available on the website of the concerned Zila Parishad.
The rural local bodies should deposit the 15th Finance Commission funds in the separate savings bank account of the nationalized bank opened for the transaction of funds received under the 15th Finance Commission as per the instructions given earlier.
The Deputy Executive Officer (Gram Panchayat) will be responsible for planning, controlling and coordinating the distribution of funds at the Gram Panchayat level as per the guidelines given by the Government.
