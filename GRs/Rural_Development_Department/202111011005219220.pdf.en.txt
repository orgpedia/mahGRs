The proposal-
The 73rd Constitutional Amendment to the Constitution of India has laid down the need for decentralization of power, devolution of powers and responsibilities to make the Panchayati Raj Institutions more efficient. Effective decentralization must involve devolution of powers to local bodies, transparency and participatory approach. For the sake of society
Various stakeholders such as farmers, women, youth, SC/ STs, etc. need to be involved in the entire process.

The constitutional amendment gave constitutional status to the Panchayati Raj institutions and also provided for a uniform administrative structure. Panchayati Raj institutions have strengthened over time and are making significant contributions to village governance and development. They are becoming the identity of rural India, especially the Gram Panchayats are playing an important role in the development of their jurisdiction. The Gram Panchayat Development Plan (GPDP) for the development of their area is being prepared since 2015.
The Ministry of Panchayati Raj has prepared a comprehensive Gram Panchayat Development Plan.
The guidelines were issued in 2018. The Ministry of Panchayati Raj and Rural Development, Government of India has launched the People's Plan Campaign (PPC) under the name of" Sarvachan Ki Yojana, Sarvachan Vikas" to prepare comprehensive Gram Panchayat Development Plans (GPDPs) from the year 2018. Such campaigns have significantly improved the quality of village development plans.
As per the recommendations of the 15th Finance Commission, the Government of India decided to allocate the 15th Finance Commission funds to Gram Panchayats, Panchayat Samitis and Zila Parishads. The proposal was to prepare a Block Panchayat Development Plan (BPDP) and a District Panchayat Development Plan (DPDP).
Constitutional Provisions regarding Planning at Taluka and District Level Article 243G of the Constitution of India provides for the powers and responsibilities of Panchayats.
Subject to the provisions of the Constitution, the Legislature of a State may, by law, confer on the Panchayats such powers and authority as may be necessary to enable them to carry on their functions as self-governing bodies and to make schemes for economic development and social justice.

The provisions are" to implement the schemes relating to economic development and social justice which shall be entrusted to it, including the schemes relating to the matters mentioned in this section."
These provisions have been made to empower all the three tiers of Panchayati Raj Institutions. Accordingly, the State Government may delegate its powers and authority to the Panchayati Raj Institutions.
This will facilitate them to plan and implement schemes of economic development, social justice as per the subjects mentioned in the Eleventh Schedule of the Constitution of India. These provisions also include giving powers to Panchayat Raj Institutions to levy taxes and raise funds through them.
Article 243G of the Constitution of India provides for the formulation of Panchayat Samiti Development Plan (BPDP) and District Council Development Plan (DPDP) for economic development and social justice by integrating and harmonizing all the programmes/ schemes being implemented in the area of Central, State and Panchayats. Apart from this, it is also the responsibility of the local administration and panchayat committees to provide basic services to the local citizens and to look after the safety of the poor and marginalized sections of their area. This can be achieved only through the implementation of a comprehensive plan by using the available resources efficiently and responsibly and avoiding duplication of work at different levels.
Article 243G of the Constitution of India provides for the formulation and implementation of the District Council Development Plan for economic development and social justice by integrating all such programme plans of the Panchayat, State and Centre in the geographical area of the district. In addition, the District Council as the local government assumes the responsibility of delivering basic services to the local citizens and ensuring the safety of the poor and marginalized sections of the population.
This can only be achieved through the implementation of need-centered plans through efficient and responsible use of available resources and expanding resources to meet their priorities. It is also necessary to take care that the works undertaken by the Gram Panchayat, Panchayat Samiti, Zilla Parishad and other departments of the state government should not be doubled.
The Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti Development Plan is to be prepared on a long-term basis (for five years) and the work to be implemented in one year is to be prioritized. District Councils and Panchayat Samitis

Appropriate actions for implementation have to be taken considering the manpower, infrastructure and institutional capacity. Priority is to be given to works that will benefit two or more Gram Panchayats.
With the 15th Finance Commission Grants for Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti Development Plan, active involvement of Government of India and State Government, it is time to prepare Zila Parishad Development Plans in a comprehensive manner and enhance their quality with equity and inclusiveness. Moreover, most of the institutions are implementing various schemes and programmes of the Central and State Governments at the district level. Therefore, there is a huge scope for convergence in planning at the district level.
The process of preparing the development plan of the Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti is to be completed within the stipulated time frame. It is a part of the process of chain planning which is completed step by step. After the Gram Panchayats complete the Gram Panchayat Development Plan (GPDP) in their area, the Panchayat Samiti is required to prepare the Panchayat Samiti Development Plan and thereafter the Zila Parishad Development Plan.
In this regard, the Government has already issued Government Resolution No: GRV Vs BPDP-2021/ P.No.21/ Para-6D. The guidelines were issued on June 14, 2021.
Government Decision- Government Decision No: GR.V. Vs. BPDP-2021/ P.No.21/ Para-6, Dy. It will be updated on June 14, 2021. New guidelines are being issued for the preparation of Panchayat Samiti Development Plan and District Council Development Plan as follows.
The development plans of the Panchayat Samiti will be useful as a basic component for the Zila Parishad Planning Committee in preparing the Zila Parishad Development Plan.
The development plan of the Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti should be in harmony with the needs, basic services and aspirations of the people on priority basis as per the available resources.
The development plans of the Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti should be of five-yearly nature and should be implemented on an annual basis with priority according to the subject decided in the Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti meetings respectively.

As the 15th Finance Commission is the main source of funds for the preparation of Panchayat Samiti Development Plan and Zila Parishad Development Plan, a comprehensive plan for the period 2021-22 to 2025-26 and an annual development plan for each year is to be prepared. The four-year master plan for 2022-23 to 2025-26 and the annual development plan for 2022-23 have already been prepared.
The Panchayat Samiti and the Zila Parishad, while preparing the development plan, shall consider the amount of funds required to be doubled (200%) every year for the four-year master plan. The Annual Plan should be prepared taking into account 1.20 times (120%) of the expected budget of the Finance Commission for the year.
