Preamble: Due to COVID-19, global pandemic and lockdown, the government had imposed restrictions on office attendance and transportation. All government and private offices, except those dealing with essential services, remained closed. Therefore, the Maharashtra Public Service Commission, District Selection Committees and other selection mechanisms should complete the prescribed process. The candidates who were selected/ recommended before 09.09.2020 could not be given appointments/ inducted in the service after verifying the required documents before giving the appointment. As there was no fault of the candidates in this, they were deprived of appointment.
Considering the above situation. The Supreme Court 09.09.2020 before granting interim stay on the Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) Reservation Act, 2018. The Maharashtra Appointment of Selected Candidates Act, 2022 (Maharashtra Act No. 1 of 2022). 45) has been adopted.
The original selection lists were revised by the respective selection authorities after the Supreme Court's judgment. Due to revision of the original selection lists, the candidates belonging to the said categories which were at the bottom of the original selection lists could not make it to the revised selection lists and therefore, they have been deprived of appointments in Government services. In order to create more number of posts for the candidates who were deprived of such appointments, the Maharashtra Legislature has decided to appoint them. The" Maharashtra Creation of Overwhelming Number of Posts and Appointment of Selected Candidates Act, 2022" has been passed. The Act covers 1064 posts created under the earlier" Maharashtra Creation of Overwhelming Number of Posts and Appointment of Selected Candidates Act 2022." Accordingly, the existing 1064 posts and the new posts under this Act
