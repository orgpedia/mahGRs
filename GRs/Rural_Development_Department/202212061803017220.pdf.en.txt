The Proposal:
It has been decided under the Government Decision dated 3.12.1988 of the Rural Development Department to grant 15 days of earned leave every year to the headmasters of secondary schools in the Zilla Parishad as compensation for the work to be done during the long leave period.
As per the Government decision dated 6.12.1996 of the Finance Department, it has been provided that the teachers, principals, headmasters, librarians, laboratory assistants and paniwalas working in the long leave department of the Zilla Parishad schools will be entitled to 10 days of earned leave in every calendar year from 01.01.1997 instead of the total 20 days of half-pay leave admissible at present.
For the purpose of reimbursing the work to be done by the headmasters of private schools during the period of major holidays, 15 days of earned leave for each full year has been made admissible as per the Government Circular dated 15.5.1999 of the Department of School Education and Sports.

As per the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, the provision for accumulation of existing earned leave and encashment of earned leave at the time of retirement has been prescribed by the Government Decision dated 15.1.2001 of the Finance Department.
5. The Tribal Development Department vide Government Circular dated 24.7.2020 has approved payment of accumulated earned leave encashment to the teaching and non-teaching staff of Government Ashramshalas under the Tribal Development Department.
In the above background, the Maharashtra State Federation of Upgraded Headmasters, as well as the Hon'ble Member of the Legislative Council, had been consistently demanding that the promoted headmasters of Zilla Parishad Primary Schools be granted 15 days of earned leave every year for the work to be done during the long vacation period. Based on the policies laid down by the School Education and Finance Department in this regard, the matter of allowing 15 days of earned leave to the promoted headmasters of Zilla Parishad Primary Schools for the work to be done during the long vacation period and cashing the accumulated earned leave was under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
As per the decision of the Government dated 6.12.1996 of the Finance Department, the post of promoted Headmaster of Primary School in Zilla Parishad has been declared as a post for duty even during long holidays. Therefore, every calendar year, the promoted headmasters of Zilla Parishad Primary Schools are required to submit a report to the Principal in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, Rule No. As per the provision of section 54, 15 days earned leave shall be admissible on the following terms and conditions:
Principals should not be forced to attend school during the period of leave without work or pay should not be deducted.
2) The principal will be required to sign the attendance sheet on the day on which the business of the school office will be carried out during the holidays.
3) Special earned leave of 15 days is admissible for the purpose of work done by the principal during the vacation period. It is expected to be in the office for at least 30 days. However, full-time attendance is not required on school days. Attendance will be required depending on the overall scope of work.
(4) The Principal shall not be entitled to paid earned leave if he is absent during the period of leave.
Further, the Group Teacher/ Education Officer can take disciplinary action against the headmaster by holding him/ her guilty of not carrying out the urgent and important work of the school on time.
