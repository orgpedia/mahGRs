Preamble:" Housing for All- 2024" is an important policy of the Central Government and the State Government has also adopted this policy. In this regard, the State Management Cell-Rural Housing Office in the State is implementing the Centrally Sponsored Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana Gramin and State Sponsored Ramai Awas Yojana, Shabari Awas Yojana, Pardhi Awas Yojana, Adim Awas Yojana, Atal Nirman Yojana.

Government Resolution No: PRIO-2022/ PRI No.149/ Scheme-10, D. 16 th November, 2022 Various rural housing schemes such as Kamgar Awas Yojana, Yashwantrao Chavan Mukt Vasahat Yojana are being implemented, supplemented by Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Housing Scheme, Land Acquisition Scheme, Encroachment Regulation Scheme, etc.
According to the UN Sustainable Development Goal 11 (SDG 11), work in the affordable housing sector will have a positive impact on at least 14 of the 17 SDGs. For this, the government aims to ensure that the progress of the housing works is qualitative and not just numerical, to provide all the facilities to the beneficiaries by implementing innovative ideas, and to take the initiative to build houses that can face natural disasters effectively.
The Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana Gramin has been implemented in the country since November 20, 2016, and November 20 is celebrated as' National Housing Day' every year. This year, we are celebrating Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav as per the instructions of the Government of India. In line with this, a proposal was under consideration of the government to implement a campaign for quality improvement and to make all rural housing schemes more dynamic in the state from November 20, 2022.
Government Decision: To mark the Amrit Mahotsav of Indian Independence, the State of Maharashtra has been approved to implement the AMRUT MAHA AWAS Abhiyaan 2022-23 from the National Housing Day on 20 th November, 2022 to 31 st March, 2023. The objective of the Mission is to accelerate and bring quality in the effective implementation of all centrally sponsored rural housing schemes such as Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana- Gramin and State sponsored Ramai Awas Yojana, Shabari Awas Yojana, Pardhi Awas Yojana, Adim Awas Yojana, Atal Nirman Kamgar Awas Yojana, Yashwantrao Chavan Mukt Vasahat Yojana by taking various initiatives during the campaign period under the Government's Housing for All 2024 strategy.

3. Initiatives to be undertaken during" AMRUT Maha Awaas Abhiyan 2022-23": i. Providing land to landless beneficiaries:- Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Housing Scheme for providing land to the needy, eligible but not available beneficiaries under rural housing schemes, scheme for providing government land free of cost, scheme for regularization of encroachments in rural areas and other ways. Effective implementation of the prize, lease, etc. Also, at least one multi-storeyed building, housing complexes and housing apartments in each Zilla Parishad block in the state to accommodate more beneficiaries in less space considering the shortage of space and the cost of space.
Similarly, to create a' Land Bank' of land available in various ways at the Gram Panchayat level to provide land for construction of houses to the landless beneficiaries of all rural housing schemes.
100 per cent sanction of houses as per targets:- 100 per cent sanction of house site targets distributed to the districts under Centrally Sponsored and State Sponsored Rural Housing Schemes and immediate distribution of house site sanction letter to the beneficiaries for active participation of the beneficiaries.
Timely disbursement of all instalments to sanctioned households:- To ensure timely disbursement of first instalment to sanctioned households under Centrally Sponsored and State Sponsored Rural Housing Schemes.
The next instalments will be disbursed as per the physical progress of the households without any delay. While doing this, to organize installment distribution program at the local level for active participation of the beneficiaries. Also to help the interested beneficiaries in getting home loans from financial institutions as per the need.
Physical completion of all sanctioned houses:- Physical completion of all sanctioned houses under Centrally Sponsored and State Sponsored Rural Housing Scheme. This includes holding meetings/ gatherings of beneficiaries for their active participation, timely visits to the households by Sarpanch, Gram Sevak/ Village Development Officer, Rural Housing Engineer and completion of all sanctioned houses in the shortest possible time. For this, at least one Gharkul Mart will be started in every Zila Parishad Block by Women Self Help Groups/ Village Unions/ Ward Unions/ Various Executive Service Cooperatives/ Farmer Producer Company, etc. community based organizations in the state and construction of Gharkul will be done through them.

(v) Completion of Delayed Houses:- Completion of delayed houses, which are more than 12 months old but are still incomplete, under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Gramin). For this, according to the list of pending houses, the reasons for the pending of the houses should be identified and analyzed, and measures should be taken to complete the houses, as per the need, with the help of the Taluk Legal Aid and Advisory Committee, to motivate the beneficiaries to complete the houses through' Lok Adalat' or other means. Also, to refund the pending houses that cannot be completed as per the guidelines of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana Gramin and to ensure that no new houses are added to the list of pending houses.
Completion of Rural Masons Training:- To improve the quality of households, the objective of the rural masons training program under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana- Gramin is to complete their assessment in coordination with the training provider and to ensure that the information is updated on Awas Soft. Also, to make a panel of trained rural masons and other available masons in the Gram Panchayat area by preparing a list of them gram panchayat-wise and to involve them in the construction of houses in rural housing schemes.
vii. Completion of all remaining houses under Indira Awas Yojana and use of Area Officer App:- Financial completion of houses under Indira Awas Yojana by disbursing immediate funds as per single page entry to the beneficiaries of houses physically completed during 2014-15 and earlier. Further, 100% physical and financial completion of the houses as per the list of sanctioned houses in the year 2015-16 on priority basis by identifying the reasons for the pendency of the houses and taking remedial measures.
The Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India has developed an Area Officer App for effective implementation, control and evaluation of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana Gramin. Make maximum use of this app while monitoring the work of rural housing schemes.

Timely conduct of social audit:- Conducting social audit of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana Rural and State sponsored rural housing schemes. Adoption of audit guidelines while conducting social audit. Convergence with Government Schemes:- To have convergence with various schemes of the Government to improve the quality of life of the beneficiaries of all rural housing schemes. These schemes are: Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS) for providing employment to beneficiaries of rural housing schemes, Swachh Bharat Mission Grameen for toilets, Jal Jeevan Mission for providing piped drinking water supply, Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana for gas connection, Saubhagya Yojana for electricity connection, National Rural Livelihood Mission for providing means of livelihood, Maharashtra Urja Vikas Nigam (MAHAVURJA) for saving energy, etc.
To provide basic civic amenities like approach roads, internal roads, drainage system, street lights, etc. to the households, multi-storied buildings, housing complexes, apartments of the beneficiaries.
Innovations/ Best Practices: To bring in dynamism and quality in the implementation of rural housing schemes.
(a) Establishment of co-operative societies for multi-storeyed buildings, housing complexes and housing apartments of rural housing beneficiaries.
b) Use of Crush Sand, Cement Block, Fly Ash Bricks, Inter Locking Bricks, Hollow Bricks, etc. as an alternative to sand.
c) Use of local building materials, innovative construction technologies, cost-effective construction technologies, natural disaster resilient technologies, etc. for at least 10% of the house site construction.
d) At least 10% of the house construction work should be done using flooring/ flooring, colouring, kitchen garden/ furniture, rain water harvesting, solar energy devices and net billing, etc. Colouring of houses as per Government letter No. No. No. Ravya-Gr/ Bandh/ 140/2021 dated 18th May, 2021.
