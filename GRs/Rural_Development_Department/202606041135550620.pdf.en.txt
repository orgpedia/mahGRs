The Proposal:
In order to encourage and enhance the efficiency of Gram Panchayats, which are predominantly functioning as local self-government institutions in rural areas, by encouraging them to perform, Reference No.1 for implementation of "Mukhyamantri Samriddhi Panchayati Raj Abhiyan" in the State from this financial year for Gram Panchayats, Panchayat Samitis and Zila Parishads doing excellent work. Approval has been granted by a Government Decision of 1. Also, Reference No. Government Circulars 2 and 3 prescribe assessment periods at all levels under the Mission and have issued instructions regarding assessment.
At present, the process of taluk-level assessment under the mission has been completed and the process of district-level assessment is underway. The mission requires the quality of the scoring process to be verified to ensure uniform criteria and fair assessment of the state. For this, the government was considering setting up Evaluation Quality Verification Committees at the district level under the Mukhyamantri Samriddhi Panchayat Raj Abhiyan.
The government's decision:
With a view to ensure uniform standards and fair assessment across the state under the Mukhyamantri Samriddhi Panchayati Raj Abhiyan, the Government is approving the setting up of approximately 30 to 34 Assessment Quality Verification Committees to verify the assessment at the Taluk level.

The composition of the Evaluation Quality Verification Committee shall be Chairman + 2 members. The committee will be constituted under the aegis of the Department of Rural Development, Maharashtra Development Service, Professor / Assistant Professor, Gram Panchayat Training Centre, Executive Officer, State Institute of Rural Development / Yarda attached to the Department of Rural Development or Officer attached to the Department of Rural Development, Divisional Commissioner's Office. The committee will have 1 Block Development Officer and 1 Assistant Block Development Officer as its members.
The role / functions of the Committee: -
It will be mandatory to inspect 10 per cent of the Gram Panchayats randomly in the taluk or at least 3 Gram Panchayats, whichever is higher. Also, all Gram Panchayats that score more than 85 per cent in the taluk-level assessment will be subject to scrutiny.
2. The information submitted by the Committee to the Gram Panchayats in person shall be verified by the Taluk Level Evaluation Committee. Errors, discrepancies found during verification should be noted separately. Gram Panchayats which have also been inspected
The rating report should be submitted to the government.
3. The concerned Zilla Parishad shall reserve a government rest house and a single vehicle for the verification committee. The verification committee shall make the journey to the destination of the district to be visited by public transport and submit the payment of travel expenses to the concerned District Council.
4. Prepare a brief analytical report of Gram Panchayats in the district scoring less than yo%. The report should AAT the reasons for the low score, frequent errors, poor accuracy, and corrective measures.
In order to make verification more effective and transparent, action should be taken as follows: - 9. The information submitted should be true, accurate and up-to-date.
0. There should be consistency in the figures and photographs presented.
Objective and in-depth verification of geotagged photographs and other relevant evidence should be done.
9. The verification process should be videographed and recorded.
The names of the officers in the Evaluation Quality Verification Committees and the inspection schedule will be communicated separately.
