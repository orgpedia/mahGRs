Reference No. 1 here. Detailed guidelines for effective implementation of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme have been issued vide Government Decision dated 03.03.2014. The scheme has been restructured for the period 2018-2019 as Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan (RGSA). 2 here. This is being implemented as per the Government decision dated 20.02.2019. As the duration of this scheme is till 2021-2022, the Central Government has made some amendments in this scheme. Under the Revamped Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan, a scheme has been announced. Implementation of the scheme 01.04.2022 to dt. It is to be done within the period of 31.03.2026. The scheme is 60% funded by the central government and 40% by state governments. For effective implementation of this Centrally Sponsored Scheme of" Revamped Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan (RGSA)" in the State, detailed guidelines for implementation of various activities are given in Reference No. The letter has been sent to all the chief executive officers. However, reference No. The government was considering issuing a comprehensive instruction on the" Revamped Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan" scheme by bringing uniformity in the matter of human resource generation in the government decisions here 1 and 2.

Objectives of the Mission: 1) To develop good governance capacities in the Panchayati Raj Institutions to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals.
2) Capacity building for developing leadership qualities among the third and lowest level representatives in the Panchayati Raj Institutions, Gram Panchayats.
3) Capacity building of Panchayats for adequate utilization of available resources, convergence of various schemes and inclusive local governance to address critical national issues.
(iv) Capacity building of Panchayati Raj Institutions to augment their own sources of income.
Strengthening of Gram Sabhas to bring transparency and accountability in the Panchayati Raj system and to increase public participation.
Transfer of powers and responsibilities to Panchayati Raj Institutions taking into account the provisions and objectives of the Constitution of India and the PESA Act, 1996.
Creation of a network of institutions of excellence for training, empowerment and mentoring of Panchayati Raj Institutions.
Strengthening of institutions for capacity building at various levels in Panchayati Raj Institutions and empowering them to achieve quality standards in terms of infrastructure, availability of human resources and effective training.
9) To take e-governance and other technology-based measures for responsible and transparent governance, administrative efficiency and improving the quality of service in the Panchayati Raj Institutions.
10) To award incentives to the Panchayati Raj Institutions for achieving the Sustainable Development Goals and other achievements.
Capacity building of all stakeholders in various sectors through promotion, dissemination and publicity.
Exchange of information, ideas and activities through national and international organizations.
Implementation of the Mission: The" Revamped Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan" will be implemented across the state to build the capacity of the local bodies, availability of skilled manpower, creation of infrastructure to achieve the above objectives.
Special emphasis will be given on localization of sustainable development goals, capacity building for preparing Gram Panchayat Development Plans, Panchayat Samiti and Zilla Parishad Development Plans, as well as capacity building of people's representatives in the four districts of Nandurbar, Osmanabad, Washim and Gadchiroli in the country's 115 Aspirational Districts selected by NITI Aayog and the clusters selected under Mission Antyodaya, special assistance to Panchayat Learning Centers, specific structures for the profession, promotion and publicity, promotion of innovative activities, meeting the shortage of funds for small enterprises for economic development and income growth. For this, the following actions are taken:

Training and Capacity Building:
1.1 The State Rural Development Agency (SIRD) and the State Panchayat Resource Centre (SPRC) shall prepare an annual training and capacity building plan taking into account the number of Panchayat Raj Institutions in the State, the number of public representatives and officers/ employees working in them, various subjects transferred to and to be taken up by the Local Self-Government Institutions, management of institutions, financial aspects, localization of Sustainable Development Goals, convergence of various schemes and capacity building of Panchayats for inclusive local good governance, social mobilization, etc. Below are the main things to consider.
The training and capacity building activities should be based on the National Capability Building Framework (NCBF) developed by the Ministry of Panchayati Raj, Government of India.
The Ministry of Panchayati Raj, Government of India should undertake training and capacity building activities on nine themes for localization of Sustainable Development Goals- Poverty Free and Livelihoods Enhancing Village, Healthy Village, Child Friendly Village, Water Rich Village, Clean and Green Village, Self-Sufficient Infrastructure Village, Socially Secure Village, Good Governance Village, Gender Equitable Village/ Women Village.
Capacity building and training activities of newly elected representatives and officials/ staff in Aspirational Districts (Nandurbar, Osmanabad, Washim and Gadchiroli) and Mission Antyodaya Gram Panchayats.
The training and capacity building programmes should cover topics of national importance such as primary health and immunization, food, education, sanitation, water conservation, leadership development, local planning, office management, creation of self-generating resources, implementation and monitoring of various schemes, women empowerment, etc.
5) Orientation training of newly elected representatives should be done in 6 months after the election and their refresher training should be done in 2 years after the election.
6) Special attention should be paid to the training of women representatives and representatives of disadvantaged groups.
7) Under the" Our Village, Our Development," training should be conducted for the people's representatives, officers/ employees and other stake holders of Panchayat Raj Institutions for the preparation, implementation and other aspects of Gram Panchayat Development Plans, Panchayat Samiti Development Plans and Zilla Parishad Development Plans.
8) Study tours should be organized within and outside the state for the public representatives, officers/ employees of the Panchayati Raj Institutions.
