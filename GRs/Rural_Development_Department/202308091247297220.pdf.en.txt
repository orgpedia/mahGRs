Government circular:
Every year, the Election Commission of India undertakes a programme to verify and rectify the errors in the existing electoral rolls under the Special Summary Revision Programme and update the electoral rolls by enrolling new voters as on the qualifying date i.e. 1st January. The Election Commission of India vide its letter dated 29th May, 2023 has announced the Special Summary Revision Programme, 2024.
Under the Special Summary Revision Programme, the draft electoral rolls of the Assembly Constituencies will be published on October 17, 2023. Claims and objections will be accepted from October 17, 2023 to November 30, 2023.
Under the Special Summary Revision Programme, 2024, the citizens have been requested to conduct a special Gram Sabha in every village from 17th October, 2023 to 30th November, 2023 so that the initiative of checking their enrolment in the electoral roll and enrolling new voters and linking Aadhaar number with the enrolment in the electoral roll can be easily reached to every citizen of the village.

Accordingly, all the Gram Panchayats of the State are being directed to organize a special Gram Sabha from 17.10.2023 to 30.10.2023 for special summary revision of electoral rolls in all the Gram Panchayats of the State.
The following activities should be undertaken in this special Gram Sabha in pursuance of the special summary revision of electoral rolls.
The existing electoral roll of the Gram Panchayat should be made available to all the citizens of the village in the Gram Sabha for viewing/ checking. This list should also be read in the Gram Sabha.
(ii) All the citizens of the village should be asked to check the entries in the electoral rolls.
The Aadhaar number of the registered voters in the village should be collected through Voter Helpline App and BLO App for authentication of their registration. If citizens have any objection regarding the entry in the voter list/ they want to make corrections in the entry or eligible citizens who do not have a name want to register their name again, they should make the application form available in the Gram Sabha there itself. For this, the Gram Panchayat Office should already provide samples of the required application form from the office of the concerned Voter Registration Officer/ Tehsil Office. Under this, the names of married women, married women, and permanent migrant voters will be excluded. The emphasis should be on marking of PWD voters and taking advance applications for registration of new voters who are completing 18 years of age as on 01/01/2024.
The Gram Sevak or the Polling Station Level Officer (BLO) of the concerned village should guide the citizens in filling up the application. Farmers and labourers should cooperate.
The Gram Panchayat Office should collect the objections/ objections/ corrections or name registration applications received from the citizens and send them to the office of the concerned Electoral Registration Officer.
If possible, the polling station level officer (BLO) should inform the citizens about how to register their names online in this Gram Sabha from the NVSP Portal/ Voter Helpline App.
VIII. The Polling Station Level Officer (BLO) should inform the citizens about the functioning of the Electoral Roll from the Office of the Electoral Registration Officer.
Citizens should provide the name, contact number of the concerned Electoral Registration Officer, the concerned Polling Station Level Officer (BLO).
X. The concerned Polling Station Level Officer (BLO) shall guide the citizens on how and from where they can get information about the status of the application for registration of objections/ corrections made by the citizens of the village in the Gram Sabha.

As above, all the Gram Panchayats of the State should organize a special Gram Sabha for the special summary revision of electoral rolls in all the Gram Panchayats of the entire State from 17.10.2023 to 30.10.2023 and update the electoral rolls by 30.11.2023 and send a report to the Collector and the District Election Officer. The Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad should ensure that these special Gram Sabhas are held in each Gram Panchayat and they should give similar instructions to the Gram Panchayats at their level.
5. The Panchayat Committees concerned should also ensure that due publicity in this regard is given at the village level. Content for publicity in this regard should be made available to the District Election Officer/ Electoral Registration Officer/ Assistant Electoral Registration Officer. It should be published 10 days before the actual Gram Sabha. This will increase the participation of villagers.
