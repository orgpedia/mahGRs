The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) has invited online applications from eligible candidates for filling vacancies to the post of District Selection Committees (DSCs) through direct recruitment to various posts in the State Public Service Commission (MSCSC). Many candidates in the said category were given appointments on the basis of their merit in the competitive examinations. After the judgment of 27th June, 2019. The Supreme Court Appointments made to SEBC candidates in public services till the stay order dated 9.9.2020 shall be treated as deemed appointments. The protection was granted by a Supreme Court order. However, following due process, the concerned Selection Authorities have decided to conduct the examination. However, the selected candidates could not be given appointment letters due to the COVID-19 pandemic and lockdown in the state.

The Maharashtra Legislature, in order to provide for the creation of more number of posts for the candidates deprived of such appointment, has issued Reference No. The Maharashtra Appointment of Selected Candidates and Creation of Superintendents of Posts Act, 2022 has been passed. Section 3 of the said Act provides for the creation of supernumerary posts for the above candidates, and for the appointment to the department-wise and cadre-wise posts mentioned in the Annexure to the Act, by the competent authority concerned.

The candidates who could not be given appointments in government services before 9.9.2020 are in the same position as the candidates who have been given appointments before that date and they are victims of circumstances, therefore, the selection process of the candidates who have not been given appointments in government services before that date will be held as per schedule. 9.9.2020 and recommended by the Maharashtra Public Service Commission, District Selection Committees and other Selection Authorities after due process of selection. Accordingly, the matter of approving the creation of a maximum number of posts for the candidates selected but not appointed by the District Council under the Rural Development Department included in the said Act was under the consideration of the Government.
