4. Before giving technical sanction to the said work and before commencing the work, the following should be done.
(i) Before granting technical approval, the scope of the road work, Pavement Design and Hydraulic Calculations should be strictly checked and the concept should be determined accordingly.

(ii) The Technical Sanctioning Authority shall ensure that the work is not likely to go under the reservoir of any irrigation/ hydroelectric water supply project or that the work is not affected by such projects.
iv) The budget should have a provision of a board showing information about the work in Marathi. Also, as per the revised terms and conditions of the Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) scheme of NABARD, the ongoing projects under RIDF should be replaced with boards in the prescribed format showing the details of the project.
(v) The description of each rate/ rate/ rate differential/ amount/ assumed gap in rate differential and other related matters in the Administrative Sanction Budget are mandatory to be examined at their level by the competent authorities for grant of technical approval. The government has no role in this.
vi) The scope of work proposed by the competent authority granting technical approval is not included in any other scheme/ programme as well as in the Public Works
It should be ensured that the department, zilla parishad, municipal corporation, Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation, etc. do not have approval from similar bodies.
(vii) Technical approval should be accorded to these works as per the prevailing tariff schedule.
(viii) These works should be recorded in the computer system prepared for the Mukhyamantri Gram Sadak Yojana by the concerned Executive Engineer immediately.
ix) The works falling within the Dongri/ Tribal/ Naxalite affected area or within a radius of 15 km from the sugar factory as per the prevailing decision of the Government shall be inspected by the competent authority giving technical approval and shall ensure that the provision is made accordingly in each section.
The entire responsibility of this will be of the competent authority/ engineer who will provide the relevant technical approval.
