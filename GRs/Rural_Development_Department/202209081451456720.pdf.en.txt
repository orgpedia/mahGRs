No.J- 11011/60/2022- Media, dated 25th August, 2022 Preamble The United Nations has announced the" Global Transformation Programme for Sustainable Development 2030" with 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and 169 targets/ targets under SDGs. It has been implemented in India from 1st January 2016.
The action plan has been prepared to achieve people-oriented sustainable development by eliminating social and economic inequalities within the country and within the country. " Leave no one behind" is the core of this global programme.
Achieving these Sustainable Development Goals will be possible only through coordination with various institutions, organizations, private sectors, youth, communities and stakeholders.
The Expert Group constituted by the Department of Panchayati Raj, Government of India has suggested a conceptual approach for the Panchayati Raj Institutions to achieve these goals and has identified nine themes/ themes for the same (i) Poverty free and livelihood (employment) growth (ii) Healthy Village (iii) Child friendly Village (iv) Water rich Village (v) Clean and green Village (vi) Self sufficient Infrastructure Village (vii) Socially safe Village (viii) Good Governance Village (viii) Gender Equitable Village (viii) Through this theme, the Central and State Governments are moving towards achieving the Sustainable Development Goals in the Panchayati Raj system.

With a view to give a fillip to this campaign, the Department of Panchayat Raj, Government of India has announced the National Panchayat Awards in line with the nine themes of Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
Giving awards and incentives to the best performing local bodies like Zila Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat is an integral part of creating a healthy and competitive ecosystem for holistic implementation of development schemes. During this period, various initiatives/ reforms have been taken under these Awards by making the entire process of filing of nominations by Panchayats online, introduction of new award categories/ themes to focus on specific areas and socially important issues, etc.
Government's decision
The National Panchayat Awards will be a multi-level pyramid competition of Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs) at Panchayat Samiti, Zila Parishad, State and National level on 9 themes (identified by Expert Committee of Ministry of Panchayati Raj). The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) have 9 Localized Themes (LSDG): (i) Poverty free and Livelihood (Employment) Enhanced Villages (ii) Health Care Villages (iii) Child Friendly Villages (iv) Water Rich Villages (v) Clean and Green Villages (vi) Self Sufficient Infrastructure Villages (viii) Socially Secure Villages (viii) Good Governance Villages (viii) Gender Equitable Village Panchayats will be incentivized for their performance in achieving SDGs under this theme.
All the Panchayats (Zila Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat) will compulsorily participate under the Thematic Award Competition. The respective level evaluation committees (Panchayat Samiti, Zila Parishad, State and National) will score all the Panchayats under each theme and accordingly select the top three Panchayats at each level. Panchayat Samitis (BPs) and Zila Parishads (DPs) will be incentivized for the performance of their respective Gram Panchayats.
The National Panchayat Awards to Panchayats will be given for their achievements in various categories as follows:

Special Category of Awards: The following special categories of awards will be given under the National Panchayat Awards:
Gram Urja Swaraj Special Panchayat Award: The award will be given to 3 Gram Panchayats for their achievements in the adoption and use of renewable sources of energy. The award may be sponsored by the concerned Ministry/ Department
Carbon Neutral Special Panchayat Award: The award will be given to 3 Gram Panchayats for their exemplary work towards achieving net zero carbon emission. Like Gram Urja Swaraj Award, this award can also be sponsored by the concerned Ministry/ Department of Industries.
|| Nanaji Deshmukh Sarvottam Panchayat Sustained Development Award: A Gram Panchayat which has qualified and been selected for the National Panchayat Awards in the previous years will also be given this special award instead of an individual thematic award.

The Panchayat Kshetra Nirman Sarvottam Sanstha Puraskar will be given to 3 such institutions across the country who have provided institutional support to Gram Panchayats in achieving LSDGs.

Also, Zila Parishads where at least 90% of the Gram Panchayats participate in the competition will be awarded certificates for their performance

8) Minimum Qualifications
Minimum qualifying marks for individual themes and overall performance on all themes for Gram Panchayats will be 50%.
For thematic or overall performance at the block/ district level, the minimum qualifying marks will be 70%.
The above are the criteria for participation in the national level awards.
The period of evaluation: 2021-2022
Nominations with incomplete information or submitted after the prescribed time limit will be rejected outright at all levels.
Grievances/ Disputes: No disputes/ complaints/ requests regarding the awards will be entertained by the Central Panchayati Raj Department after the announcement of the National Panchayat Awards.
