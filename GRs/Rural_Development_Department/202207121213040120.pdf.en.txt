The Proposal:
As per the recommendations of the 15th Central Finance Commission, an amount of Rs. 1092.92 crore has been released. In the 7. dated 29.06.2022. Accordingly, the proposal for devolution of funds received by the State Government from the 15th Central Finance Commission to the Rural Local Bodies in the State was under consideration.
The government has taken the following steps in this regard:

GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: PAMVYA-2021/ PROCESS-50/ FINANCE-4 DATE: 12 JULY, 2022 GOVERNMENT DECISION: The second instalment of Tied Grants (TID) of the Fifteenth Central Finance Commission for the financial year 2021-22 has been released by the Government of India amounting to Rs. 1092.92 crore is being made available to Rural Local Bodies (Zila Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat) in the State of Maharashtra for disbursement in the ratio of 10:10:80 under the headings 25152637, 25152655, 25152673 respectively as shown in the accompanying prospectus (Form-A).
Under the 15th Central Finance Commission, the funds received by the state of Maharashtra are allocated in the ratio of 10 percent to the Zilla Parishad, 10 percent to the Panchayat Samiti and 80 percent to the Gram Panchayat. Central Government letter No.F.15 (4) FC-XV/ FCD/ 2020-25, dt. As on 29.6.2022, the second installment of the Bonded Fund for the year 2021-22 is Rs. 1092.92 crore through integration with e-Gramswaraj PFMS-Treasury Net, the possibility of delay in crediting of funds to the accounts of Rural Local Bodies cannot be ruled out. Therefore, it has been decided to disburse the second installment of the tied fund through PRIASOFT-PFMS Interface (PPI).
As per the guidelines issued by the Central Government and the State Governments from time to time, it will be mandatory to open a 15th Finance Commission Joint Savings Account only in ICICI Bank.
It will also be mandatory to distribute funds to the contractors and other beneficiaries of various projects/ schemes under the 15th Central Finance Commission at the level of Zila Parishad, Panchayat Samiti and Gram Panchayat through online mode i.e. PFMS system.
The amount of the second installment of the tied grant (TID) of the 15th Central Finance Commission for the financial year 2021-22 to 27860 gram panchayats in the state will be deposited in the ICICI Bank account of the gram panchayats through the Zila Parishad. The amount will be deposited in the ICICI Bank account of the Zilla Parishads as per Form A attached.
The total amount of funds allocated to the Gram Panchayats by the Zila Parishads in their accounts and the ratio of the population of the Gram Panchayats in the entire district (as per 2011 Census) multiplied by the population of the Gram Panchayats. Accordingly, the funds should be distributed to the Gram Panchayats.
The Deputy Executive Officer (Gram Panchayat) will be responsible for planning, controlling and coordinating the distribution of funds at the Gram Panchayat level as per the guidelines given by the government.

In the above process, the Gram Sabha and Gram Panchayat have the power to decide the works after analyzing the current situation in the village and determining the needs of the village.
As per the recommendations of the Fifteenth Central Finance Commission, the tied grant is to be used for the following infrastructure services: 1. Maintenance and repair of sanitation and open defecation free local bodies.
2. Drinking water supply, water recharge/ rainwater harvesting, water recycling.
The tied grant is to be utilized in the ratio of 50% each for the above two items. If one of the above two aspects is fully implemented, the funds should be spent on the other aspect. The complete implementation of the scheme has to be certified by the concerned Gram Panchayat/ Gram Sabha.
Also, this certification should be duly confirmed by the supervising authority of the Panchayat Raj. For this, the Superintending Authority for Gram Panchayat levels, Block Development Officer (PDC), Deputy Chief Executive Officer (CEO) for Panchayat Samiti levels, and the District Development Commissioner (DDC) will be responsible. The Chief Executive Officers (CEOs) for the Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti level will be the Chief Executive Officers (CEOs).
