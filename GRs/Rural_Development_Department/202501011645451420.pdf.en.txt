Preamble: "Housing for All" is an important policy of the Union Government and has been adopted by the State Governments as well. In line with this, the state government through the State Management Cell-Rural Housing Office has launched Centrally Sponsored Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin, Pradhan Mantri Janjatiya Adivasi Nyaya MahaAbhiyaan as well as State Sponsored Ramai Awas Yojana, Shabari Awas Yojana, Pardhi Awas Yojana, Adim Awas Yojana, Atal Nirman Karmikkar Awas Yojana, Yashwantrao Chavan Mukt Habitat Yojana, Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Holkar GharKul Yojana, Modi Awas GharKul Yojana.

Various schemes such as Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya Housing Purchase Assistance Scheme and Encroachment Regulation in Rural Areas are being implemented along with the housing scheme.
According to Goal 11 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, work in the affordable housing sector will have a positive impact on at least 14 of the 17 SDGs. For this, the government aims to ensure that the progress of housing works is qualitative rather than quantitative, innovative ideas are implemented to provide all-inclusive housing to the beneficiaries, and initiatives are taken by all to build natural disaster-resistant housing.
Maha Awaas Abhiyan is implemented in the state every year on 20 November to further accelerate and improve the quality of all rural housing schemes in the state, coinciding with National Housing Day. A proposal to implement the 'Maha Awas Abhiyan' in this financial year too was under the consideration of the government.

Initiatives to be undertaken during "Maha Awas Abhiyan 2024-25": - Provision of space to landless beneficiaries: - Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya Housing Purchase Assistance Scheme to provide space to needy, eligible but non-available housing beneficiaries under all rural housing schemes, scheme for free availability of government space, scheme for regularisation of encroachments in rural areas and other avenues e.g. To make available space to all the remaining landless beneficiaries by effectively implementing bounty, consent, lease, etc.
Approval of houses as per targets: - 100% approval as per the housing proposals distributed to the districts under the Centrally Sponsored Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana - Gramin, Pradhan Mantri Janjatiya Adivasi Nyaya Maha Abhiyan and State Sponsored Rural Housing Schemes and prompt delivery of house site clearance letters to the beneficiaries by organizing beneficiary meets for active participation of the beneficiaries, technical guidance on house site construction and commencement of construction of houses by conducting Bhoomi Pujan programme.
Delivery of installments to sanctioned houses on time: - Delivery of first instalment to all approved houses under Centrally Sponsored Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana - Gramin, Pradhan Mantri Janjatiya Adivasi Nyaya Maha Abhiyan and State Sponsored Housing Schemes within 7 days and subsequent instalments as per physical progress of houses without any delay. While doing this, organize an installment distribution program at the local level for the active participation of the beneficiaries.
Physical completion of sanctioned houses: - Physical completion of sanctioned houses under the Centrally Sponsored Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana - Gramin, Pradhan Mantri Janjatiya Adivasi Nyay Maha Abhiyan and all State Sponsored Rural Housing Schemes. This includes taking meetings / gatherings of beneficiaries for their active participation, timely visits to the quarters by Sarpanchs, Gram Panchayat Officers (Gram Sevaks / Village Development Officers), Gram Panchayat employees, Women Self Help Groups, Anganwadi workers, ASHA workers, Rural Housing Engineers, etc., and completion of all sanctioned houses at a fast pace and in a quality manner.
YT of Delayed Houses: - Under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin, priority completion of Delayed Houses, which are more than 12 months old but not yet completed, from the date of payment of first instalment to the households. For this, according to the list of pending houses, the reasons behind the pending houses should be found out, the reasons should be analyzed and the measures should be taken to complete these houses. For this purpose, with the help of the Taluk Legal Aid and Advisory Committee, as required, "to motivate the beneficiaries to complete the household through Lok Aad or other means." Also, refund and remand pending houses that cannot be completed as per Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana Gramin guidelines and ensure that no more new houses are added to the list of pending houses.

Completion of rural mason training: - The objective of the rural mason training program under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin for Gian construction of houses is to complete it expeditiously with assessment in coordination with the training provider. Also, Gram Panchayat-wise paneling of trained rural masons and other available masons in the Gram Panchayat area and participation of masons in construction of houses under rural housing schemes.
Construction of multi-storeyed buildings, housing colonies and housing apartments:: - By guiding the beneficiaries to accommodate more beneficiaries in less space considering the shortage of space and the cost of space, purchasing space from their consolidated fund and motivating them to fulfil their dream of housing through multi-storeyed buildings, housing complexes and apartments. Setting up of multi-storeyed buildings (7-1-7-4), STORM colonies and housing apartments at least one in each Zilla Parishad block in the State of such beneficiaries.
Effective use of Demo Houses: - Demo Houses have been created at Taluka and District level under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin to improve the quality of houses and guide the beneficiaries. Organizing physical visits of beneficiaries to these demo houses and showing the Gram Panchayat level demo house model to the beneficiary from time to time so that their house works can be effectively utilized for completion. In addition to this, setting up of information rooms in every demo house or co-op shop for sale of products of beneficiaries / women self-help groups in association with women self-help groups / village unions / ward unions / various executive service cooperatives / farmer producer companies, etc.
Convergence with Government Schemes: - To provide benefits of various schemes of the Government to enhance the standard of living of the beneficiaries of all rural housing schemes.
For example, Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme to provide employment to beneficiaries, Swachh Bharat Mission for toilets - Gramin, Jal Jeevan Mission to provide piped drinking water supply, Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana for gas connection, Saubhagya Yojana for electricity connection, National Rural Livelihood Mission to provide means of livelihood,
To provide basic civic amenities like TST, internal roads, drainage system, street lights, etc., to beneficiary houses, multi-storeyed buildings, housing colonies, housing apartments, etc., through schemes like Dalit Habitat Improvement Scheme, Thakkar Bappa Adivasi Habitat Improvement Scheme, Maha Urja Zilla Parishad Cess, Panchayat Samiti Cess, Gram Panchayat Cess, CSR Fund, etc.
Implementing Innovations / Best Practices: Implementing innovative initiatives to bring about dynamism and quality in the implementation of rural housing schemes as follows.
