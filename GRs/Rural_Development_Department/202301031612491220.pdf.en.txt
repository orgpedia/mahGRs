The contribution of Panchayati Raj Institutions in the development of rural Maharashtra is immense. All the development works in the state are being implemented by the Panchayati Raj Institutions. Then it is necessary to increase the work culture of the Panchayati Raj Institutions by encouraging them according to their performance. For this purpose, the award scheme" Yashwant Panchayat Raj Abhiyan" has been started at both the department and state level from the financial year 2005-2006 for the district councils and panchayat samitis who have done excellent work in administrative management and development work from the panchayat raj institutions in the state. The Department of Panchayati Raj, Government of India has resolved to implement 9 themes in line with the global goal of achieving 17 goals by 2030 under the Sustainable Development Goals. The selected villages are (i) Poverty free and livelihood (employment)-enhancing villages (ii) Arogya Dayi villages (iii) Child friendly villages (iv) Water rich villages (v) Clean and green villages (vi) Self sufficient infrastructure villages (vii) Socially secure villages (viii) Good Governance villages (viii) Gender Equitable villages.
The Department of Panchayati Raj, Government of India has announced National Panchayat Awards-2023 for the year 2021-22 for outstanding performance of Gram Panchayats, Panchayat Samitis and Zila Parishads through these 9 themes. The central government will give cash prizes to the gram panchayats, panchayat samitis and zila parishads selected at the national level. Under the National Panchayat Awards, the selection process is currently underway at the Taluka, District, State and National level. The awards will be presented on the occasion of National Panchayat Day on April 24, 2023.
