The Proposal:
Start-up Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP) was launched by the Government of India in 2015 to promote entrepreneurship among rural youth.
The decision to implement it as part of the campaign has been taken as per the decision of the government number one.
The State Government has decided to implement the National Rural Livelihood Mission in the State of Maharashtra as per the Government decision dated 18th July, 2011. The Start-up Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP) will be funded by the Government of India.
As the accounts of Maharashtra State Rural Livelihoods Mission are being received, the head of accounts for central share has not been opened. However, Mahe is to be received from the Central Government from December, 2020
Since the funds are credited to the Consolidated Fund of the State, the matter of opening a new separate head of account for disbursement of the Central share was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The Government has approved the opening of a new separate head of account for the distribution of the central share received for the Start-up Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP) as follows:
