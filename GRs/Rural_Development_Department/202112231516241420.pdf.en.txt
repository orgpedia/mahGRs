Public money is the money collected by the government from the taxpayers. In order to ensure that taxpayers' money is not wasted in any way, it is necessary to curb the amount of overpayment that cannot be recovered in the future. Considering this. In accordance with the above order, the High Court The Supreme Court in its judgment in Jagdev Singh has directed the Government to take an undertaking in writing that before providing any amount to the beneficiaries, if it is found in future that any excess payment has been made to him by the Government, the beneficiary shall be bound to return the amount to the Government.

Government Circular No: GPA-2021/ PR No.221/ Aastha-4
It has come to the notice of the Government that the State Government officers and employees belonging to Group-A, Group-B, Group-B (Non-Gazetted), Group-C and Group-D are being paid exorbitant amounts of Government money on various occasions during their service, as per rules or as per their entitlements and entitlements. In some cases, where the overpayment is found to be in future, an undertaking that the employee will return the amount to the government is required to be taken from the concerned government employee. As a result, it had come to the notice of the government that the employees were opposing the recovery of such overpaid amount. Instructions have been given to all the departments as per the government circular No. 4. The government had issued directions to all the district magistrates in this regard.
Accordingly, the following circulars are being issued.
Circular-
All the Zila Parishads are hereby directed to take an undertaking from all concerned to return the excess amount to the Zila Parishad in case the Zila Parishad/ Government comes across any instance of excess payment to the employees and officers of the Zila Parishad in terms of salary fixation/ increment/ or any other financial benefit/ payment. (Attached is a copy. (2) The employees who are presently in the service of the Zilla Parishad should take this undertaking within 3 months from the date of this circular. However, the employees who are retiring should be taken immediately before their retirement. The undertaking should be taken within 1 month of the appointment in respect of the employees who are newly inducted/ appointed in the government service.
It is mandatory for the concerned officer/ employee to fill this undertaking to the District Council within the stipulated time. If the undertaking is not given by the concerned within the stipulated period, the Drawing and Disbursing Officers shall take action for withholding their wages beyond the stipulated period till such time they pay the bond.
It may be noted that this undertaking shall remain in force for the entire period of service of the employee/ officer concerned. Also, the receipt of this undertaking should be recorded in the service book.
Also, the letter of intent should be included in the service book.
The primary responsibility of following this procedure shall be that of the Drawing and Disbursing Officer. In case of non-compliance of this procedure, the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officer shall be liable for appropriate action. (A copy of the letter of credit/ guarantee is attached.) )
