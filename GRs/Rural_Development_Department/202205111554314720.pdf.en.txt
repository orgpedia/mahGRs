Minutes of the discussion held on 19.03.2021 on the pending demands of Maharashtra Rajya Gramsevak Union DNE-136 under the chairmanship of Additional Chief Secretary (Rural Development)
The Maharashtra Rajya Gramsevak Union has demanded the abolition of the posts of Gram Sevak and Gram Vikas Adhikari and the creation of a single post of Panchayat Development Officer. In response to this request, As per the discussion in the meeting held on 15th June, 2021 under the chairmanship of Minister of State (Rural Development), Shri. Additional Chief Secretary (Rural Development) In pursuance of the directions given in the minutes of the meeting dated March 19, 2021, it was under the consideration of the Government to constitute an expert committee to examine and study the demand and submit a report.
Government Decision In line with the above proposal, the demand of the organization to abolish both the posts of Gram Sevak and Rural Development Officer and to create a single post of" Panchayat Development Officer," an expert committee is being formed to investigate and study and submit a report as follows.

The Chairperson of the Committee may invite the officers of the Regional Office at the Divisional/ District level to the meeting as per requirement.
2 The functions of the Expert Committee are as follows:
(1) Explain the need and rationale for creating a single Panchayat Development Officer post by abolishing both the posts of Gram Sevak and Village Development Officer.
2) Separate pay scale is applicable to both the posts of Gram Sevak and Village Development Officer.
To recommend the scale of pay to be admissible if one designation is made by cancelling both the posts.
3) To recommend changes in time bound promotions/ benefits of Assured Progress Scheme, other financial matters etc. in case of abolition of both the posts of Gram Sevak and Village Development Officer and creation of a single post of Panchayat Development Officer.
4) To recommend changes or necessary amendments in the total number of posts, service matters, reservation etc. if both the posts of Gram Sevak and Village Development Officer are abolished and a single Panchayat Development Officer post is created.
5) To make recommendations after making a comparative study of the posts, promotions, pay scales and time-bound promotion benefits etc. of the employees working at the village level in other departments of the State Government.
(6) To suggest the issues and solutions arising out of the abolition of both the posts of Gram Sevak and Village Development Officer and the creation of one post of Panchayat Development Officer.
7) Clarify the fiscal considerations and the financial burden on the government in accordance with the above points.
