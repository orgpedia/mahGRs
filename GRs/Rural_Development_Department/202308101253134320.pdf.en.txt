the prelude
The Government has issued guidelines from time to time for preparation of development plans for Panchayati Raj Institutions at all the three levels for the year 2024-25. In order to prepare these roadmaps, various level training activities for capacity building and training of public representatives and officers/ staff have been sanctioned under Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan. The district-wise details of all these activities are given in the Government letter No. All the districts have been informed by RUGRASWA 2023/ PR No. 12/ Aastha-15, Dec. 24, 2023. The funds required for this will be provided by the State Project Management Cell, Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan. All districts have been approved by July 18, 2023. The following key suggestions given earlier regarding the process of preparing Gram Panchayat, Panchayat Samiti and Zila Parishad Development Plans for the year 2024-25 and implementation of training activities are being taken up again. The government was contemplating issuing guidelines in this regard.
Government circular
(a) Thematic GPDP.
GPDP-2022/ Q. No.235/ Para-6, dated 29th November 2022 and Letter No. GPDP-2022/ Q.No.236/ Para-6, Dy. The procedure laid down under the letter dated 11th January, 2023 is to be followed exactly.
Each Gram Panchayat shall hold two Gram Sabhas to prepare the Gram Panchayat Development Plan as envisaged in the Government letter dated 29th November, 2022 and 11th January, 2023. It is mandatory to fill the schedule of both these Gram Sabhas on the Vibrant Gram Sabha portal. The schedule of Gram Sabhas should be publicized at the local level as much as possible.
Before the first gram sabha, the gram panchayat has to hold four meetings- women's meeting, ward meeting, children's meeting and meeting of deprived sections. The following topics will be discussed at these meetings:

3.1 PLAN FOR YEAR 2023-24 Physical and Economic Progress Report 3.2 List of themes selected by Gram Sabhas for the year 2024-25 and the activities mentioned on the e-GramSwaraj Portal under those themes.
3.3. Total funds available for Plan Schemes for 2024-25. (Finance Commission, SVANidhi, MGNREGA, Jal Jeevan Mission, Swachh Bharat Mission, 5% Gram Kosh Fund, Jan Vargani, etc.) 3.4. Initiatives to be taken from the 15th Finance Commission's 60% tied fund.
3.5. Provision of 50% (20% of the total fund) of the 15th Finance Commission's 40% of the funds under the selected theme for 50% of the activities mentioned in the e-Gram Swaraj portal.
3.6.c Information on flagship schemes and programmes of the State and Central Governments in the jurisdiction of Gram Panchayats.
3.7. Village Poverty Reduction Plan 3.8. Mission Antyodaya Survey Report.
4. First Gram Sabha After all the four meetings as mentioned above, the first Gram Sabha is to be held to prepare the Gram Panchayat Development Plan. In this meeting, the following items should be placed along with the above mentioned items 3.1 to 3.8.
3.9.Resolutions and recommendations of the meeting of women, wards, children and disadvantaged groups.
3.10. School Development Plan (to be read by the Headmaster of the Primary School) 3.11. Anganwadi Report (to be read by the Anganwadi Worker) 3.12. Approval of Draft Gram Panchayat Development Plan.
The Chief Executive Officer and Block Development Officer should fix the subject sheet in this way and give instructions to all the Gram Panchayats. Similarly, the Gram Panchayat should record the same in the Gram Sabha minutes as in this subject sheet.
5. Taluka Technical Scrutiny Committee- Block Development Officer should receive the draft Gram Panchayat Development Plan and Project Report of all Gram Panchayats in the Taluka within the deadline. The Taluk Technical Scrutiny Committee should scrutinize the draft plan within three days and send it to the concerned Gram Panchayat with their consent or mentioning the errors.
The Taluk Technical Scrutiny Committee should examine the planning of the 15th Finance Commission's bonded and non-bonded funds, the concepts chosen by the Gram Sabha and the provisions made thereon, the provisions made from the Self-Fund and other funds, and the information about the flagship schemes of the Central and State Governments.
The draft plan received after scrutiny by the Taluk Technical Scrutiny Committee should be given final approval in the second Gram Sabha.

Development Plan for Panchayat Samitis and Zila Parishads: The development plan for the year 2024-25 is to be prepared by every Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samitis in the state. The detailed procedure for preparation of Panchayat Samiti and Zila Parishad Development Plan is mentioned in the Government Decision No. BPDP-2021/ PR No. 21/ Para-6, dated- 1st November, 2021. Accordingly, Panchayat Samiti and Zila Parishad development plans should be prepared within the stipulated time and uploaded on the e-GramSwaraj portal.
Every Zila Parishad and Panchayat Samiti shall constitute Zila Parishad Planning Committee and Panchayat Samiti Planning Committee and Regional Working Groups respectively and those who have not yet constituted these committees/ groups shall do so forthwith. Wherever there are administrators, planning committees and field working groups should be formed by involving officials and experts from different fields.
(2) Wherever there is an Administrator, changes may be made after the election, if necessary, with the consent of the new members and with the approval of the Divisional Commissioner in the District Council Plan as mentioned in the Government Decision and with the approval of the Chief Executive Officer in the Panchayat Committee Plan.
While preparing Panchayat Samiti and Zila Parishad Development Plans, the 15th Finance Commission norms of 60% tied and 40% tied funds and in case of other funds, the criteria and guidelines of that source of funds will be applicable.
The approved Panchayat Samiti and Zila Parishad development plans are to be uploaded on the e-GramSwaraj portal, according to the activities shown on the portal.
Capacity Building and Training: District-wise objectives of capacity building and training on Gram Panchayat, Panchayat Samiti and Zilla Parishad Development Plan. Ragraswa 2023/ Q.No.12/ Astha-15 dated 24th May, 2023 has been notified.

1. Guidelines for Training Programme. Ragraswa 2022/ Q. No.49/ Astha-15, Dy. 13th July, 2022 and Government letter No. Ragraswa 2023/ Q.No.12/ Astha-15, dated 24th May, 2023. These recommendations should be implemented to ensure quality of training.
GPDP/ BPDP/ DPDP training programmes are to be conducted by the District Council. For this, all the necessary facilities, arrangements, suitable environment should be selected for training.
3.Every training activity should be conducted by a trained professional.
All training activities are to be completed as per the training module, presentation as prescribed by the State Rural Development Agency, Yashada, Pune and the reading material provided by Yashada should be printed and given to all the trainees in the form of booklet.
A training class should have 40 to 50 trainees. If there are more trainees then the training should be conducted in more than one class.
Training programmes should be held at more than one place in the district/ taluk simultaneously to ensure completion of training programmes within the stipulated time.
The schedule of training programmes should be prepared and filled on the Training Management Portal (TMP) and submitted to the State Project Management Cell. Also, all the processes before and after the training program on the TMP portal should be completed in time.
