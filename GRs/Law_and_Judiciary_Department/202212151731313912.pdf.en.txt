The Proposal- M. The High Court organizes various conferences, workshops, training, etc. at the High Court Main Bench as well as at Nagpur, Aurangabad and Panaji Benches. The proposal for it
It has been sent to the government for approval. However, considering that the process is time consuming and at times it is difficult to spend administrative sanction in time for the meetings that are held urgently, the High Court administration has decided to take up the above matters.
A proposal has been submitted to the Government under the Reference letter for grant of special powers in Part II of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978 to meet the expenditure (excluding regular sittings of the High Court) and to deduct the amount of such expenditure on summary payment.
It was for the government to approve it. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:
Government Decisions In accordance with the approval of the Financial Empowerment Committee, the Government has decided to provide grant-in-aid for expenditure on various conferences, workshops, training, etc. (excluding regular meetings in the High Court) to be organized by the High Court of Bombay. The Chief Justice, High Court of Bombay, on each occasion, is paid Rs. Grant of financial powers subject to the conditions set out in the Annexure attached, to grant financial authorization to the extent of Rs. 30.00 lakh and to recover the said expenditure on short payment.
The financial powers conferred by this resolution shall be exercised subject to compliance with the terms and conditions contained in the rules and regulations contained in the various financial statements and the executive orders issued by the Government from time to time.
