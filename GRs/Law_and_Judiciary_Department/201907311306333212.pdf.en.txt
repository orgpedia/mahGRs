The Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 dated 28th December, 2016
the prelude
Section 4, Section 23 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 contains the following provisions:
(1) For the purposes of section 19 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, every Government establishment shall appoint a Grievance Redressal Officer and inform the Chief Commissioner or State Commissioner, as the case may be, of the appointment of such officer.
(2) Any person aggrieved by non-compliance with the provisions of Section 20 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 may lodge a complaint with the Grievance Redressal Officer, who shall investigate the same and take up the matter with the establishment for corrective action.
(3) The Grievance Redressal Officer shall register the complaint in the register in the manner prescribed by the Central Government and every complaint shall be investigated within two weeks of its registration.
(4) If the aggrieved person is not satisfied with the action taken on his or her complaint, he or she may approach the District Level Disability Committee.
As per the provisions of the said section 23, the matter of appointing a Grievance Redressal Officer in the Department of Law and Justice (itself) and its subordinate offices was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision
As per the provisions of Section 23 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the following officers are being appointed as Grievance Redressal Officers at the level of Law and Justice Department (itself) (including Nagpur and Aurangabad) and offices under this Department.
