Preamble: - Under the High Court Judges Rules, 1956 Rule 2E, v. Judges of the High Court, whether residing in government accommodation or private, are allowed to be reimbursed for the cost of utilisation of 10,000 seed units per annum. In this provision, There has been no improvement since 29/06/1994 .The use of various sophisticated electronic items at the residence of the Hon'ble Justice has led to an increase in daily electricity consumption. At present. The annual limit of 10,000 seed units per judge is becoming inadequate. The cost of the power unit beyond this limit is Rs. The judges have to suffer and there is no provision for their compensation.
A proposal was received from the High Court Administration to amend Rule 2E of the High Court Judges Rules, 1956 to make this limit ASAT 20,000 per annum. Since the matter is under the jurisdiction of the Central Government, it has been submitted to the Central Government as an appropriate course of action.
Accordingly, the proposal will be considered by the Central Government while amending / amending the High Court Judges Rules. Until the said proposal is approved by the Central Government, the High Court Judges Rules, 1956, as per Rule 2 of the High Court Judges Rules, 1956, have been amended. The state government has been given the go-ahead to temporarily reimburse the cost of seed units beyond the permissible annual limit of 10,000 seed units per annum for judges. Accordingly, the issue of issuing a government decision is under consideration.
Government Decision: - The High Court Judges Rules, 1956, as per Rule 2 of the High Court Judges Rules, 1956 amends Rule 2E to extend the limit to 20,000 per annum until the proposal of the High Court Administration is approved by the Central Government.
