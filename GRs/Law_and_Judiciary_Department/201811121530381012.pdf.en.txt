Public trusts and charitable institutions in the state are governed by the provisions of the Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950. At present, the private charitable institutions are facing many problems related to the administrative functioning of the Charity Commissioner's office. Therefore, to improve the administration of the Charity Commissioner's office and the relationship between the charity and the public trust, to run the administrative affairs in a smooth and transparent manner, to provide maximum support to the public trusts and to have reasonable control over the charitable institutions, to ensure accountability, to prescribe responsive procedures, to expedite the work of the Charity Commissioner's office with the help of technology and to dispose of the pending cases in the office as soon as possible, to digitize/ online the records of the office, to make provisions for providing services under the Service Guarantee Act to the citizens, to create an atmosphere of trust among the people regarding the Charity Commissioner's office, to amend the Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950, as per the need, to increase the manpower
Government's decision
In the above background, a High Level Study Committee is being constituted under the Chairmanship of Shri Ratnakar Gaikwad, former Chief Secretary, Government of Maharashtra, to suggest administrative, technical and legal measures to improve the administrative functioning of the Office of the Charity Commissioner and accordingly to make recommendations for amending the provisions of the" Bombay Public Trusts Arrangement Act, 1950" as required:

The committee has to submit its report to the government within three months. The committee will have the following functions:
Administrative Matters: (1) To conduct a thorough review of the present functioning of the office of the Charity Commissioner. Define the core responsibilities of the office and suggest changes accordingly, decentralize funds, duties, human resources and decision-making powers. Suggesting a revised managerial structure with regard to necessary changes in the duties and responsibilities of human resources.
(2) To study in depth the various cases related to administrative matters, disposal of pending cases, appointment of Public Trust Trustees, improvement of infrastructure, digital technology, misuse of Right to Information, human service rules, regular transfers of officers and employees, complaints received against the Charity Commissioner's office, etc. and recommend high-quality procedures.
(3) Suggest ways to ensure easy registration of public trusts and public trusts to the public, support good institutions and public trusts and provide legal aid and advisory services to the public, suggest measures to strengthen the role of the Educational Enlightenment of the Charity Commissioner's Office, create standardized and accessible samples by eliminating errors in all types of documents,

(4) To determine the procedures to ensure that administrative delays are avoided by the Office of the Charity Commissioner, to determine the duties and responsibilities of the Office of the Charity Commissioner in relation to its various activities, to determine the mechanism and procedures for internal investigations, and to suggest remedial measures accordingly.
(5) Eliminate objections to the fulfillment of unnecessary matters, eliminate the procedure for issuing a no-objection certificate by imposing unnecessary conditions, and instead determine a simplified procedure.
(6) To lay down the procedure for the establishment of public trusts by Government servants.
(b) Technical Aspects: To work out the modalities for undertaking the following: (i) Assume automatic acceptance of change report if it is not disposed of within 30 days, (ii) prescribe the procedure for disposal of backlog and to ensure that the backlog is not created in future, (iii) develop a digital operating system to track the losses to avoid any loss due to improper procedures, (iv) develop a digital operating system to ensure that all records are updated automatically, (v) ensure confidentiality of digitized/ online information (like PAN card, property, audit report etc.).
(a) Legal matters:
(1) To determine the various services of the Commissioner of Charities to be included in the Right to Public Service Act.
(2) To determine the appropriate rules for the various areas of the State, taking into account the geographical division of the different areas into urban, suburban, rural, tribal and left-wing-influenced zones.
(3) To make a list of ambiguous and unnecessary legal provisions and tax matters and cancel them.
(4) To lay down the legal provisions and procedures to prevent misuse of the Right to Information Act without prejudice to its main object and object.
3. Ma. Yash Kirkire and Mr. Saurabh Kanade will assist and co-ordinate the committee. The committee will organise the workshop. It can hear the public, seek appropriate suggestions from the public, and seek the help of expert counsel.
