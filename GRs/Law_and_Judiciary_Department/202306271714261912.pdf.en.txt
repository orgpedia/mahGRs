As per Rule 10 (4) and Rule 65 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, it was the policy of the Government to review the service of officers/ employees of inefficient and doubtful integrity in the public interest at the age of 50/55 years or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
Accordingly, Reference No. The departmental review committee was formed under the government decision of 2.
General Administration Department Reference No. Provisions have been made regarding the review procedure by superseding all the previous orders under the government decision mentioned in section 1. The government's decision is as follows:
Group-A and Group-B Gazetted Officers: (a) Officers/ Employees who have come into Government service before the age of 35 As per the revised policy of the General Administration Department, the Gazetted Officers of Group-A and Group-B who have come into Government service before the age of 35 years are to be reviewed once on the completion of their 50 years of age or 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
(b) Officers/ Employees who have joined the Government service after 35 years of age: Gazetted officers who have joined the Government service after 35 years of age are to be reviewed at the age of 55 years.
(ii) Group B (Non-Gazetted), Group C and Group D employees: In case of Group B (Non-Gazetted), Group C and Group D employees, they are to be re-employed at the age of 55 years or on completion of 30 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier.
(ii) To review the eligibility of the Government officers/ employees to continue in Government service on the basis of the criteria prescribed for their eligibility to continue in Government service, it is in the public interest to review the eligibility of the eligible and efficient officers/ employees and to retire the inefficient and doubtful officers/ employees prematurely.

(iv) In order to check the eligibility on 1st August of every calendar year, the officers and staff are required to be enlisted on the completion of 49/54 years of age or 30/35 years of qualifying service, whichever is earlier and the proceedings are to be completed by the end of the calendar year (i.e. by 31st December) by the Divisional Review Committee, Special Review Committee and the Committee on Appeals.
Reference No. The above Government decision was for the officers/ employees of the Department of Law and Justice (itself). However, in view of the request made by the K-1 regarding the formation of a single committee for the review of officers/ employees of the Department of Law and Justice (itself) and officers of the offices of the Charity Commissioner, General Administrator and Government Trustee under the administrative control of the Department, as well as the Registrar, Partnership Agencies at the age of 50/55 years, and the change in their designation due to the promotion of the members of the Departmental Review Committee, it is necessary to make changes in the existing committee of the Department. Hence, reference No. 2 the date of the decision. The matter of reconstituting the Departmental Review Committee and Departmental Representation Committee by superseding it on 9th January 2020 is under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
Reference No. The above Government decision is hereby superseded.
Two separate committees are being constituted for the officers/ staff of Group' A,"' B,"' C" and' D" on the establishment of the Department of Law and Justice (itself) and the officers of Group' A" and' B" (gazetted) working in the offices of the Charity Commissioner, General Manager and Government Trustee under the administrative control of the Department as well as the Registrar, Partnership Institutions.
