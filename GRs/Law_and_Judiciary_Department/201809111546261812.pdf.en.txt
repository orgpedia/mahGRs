The Proposal:
Heavy rains and floods have created a havoc in Kerala. Lakhs of families have been affected due to floods. As the flood situation in Kerala has become very grim, a large number of people from within the country and abroad are providing assistance to control the flood situation and rehabilitate the flood victims. The government had earmarked Rs. A grant of Rs. 20 crore has been announced. The Kerala government has also been asked to provide immediate assistance to deal with the flood situation. The Maharashtra government has set up the Chief Minister's Kerala Flood Relief Fund to help the flood victims in Kerala.
The Executive Officer, Shri Vitthal Rukmini Temples Committee, Pandharpur, through his letter of reference, has sanctioned Rs. The temple committee has been asked to sanction Rs 25 lakh as financial assistance.
In response to this request, the Shri Vitthal Rukmini Mandir Samiti, Pandharpur, was under the consideration of the Government to provide funds for the Chief Minister's Kerala Flood Relief Fund set up by the Government of Maharashtra for the rehabilitation of flood victims in Kerala.
Government's decision:
The Chief Minister's Relief Fund for Kerala has been set up. 25 lakh from Shri Vitthal Rukmini Mandir Samiti, Pandharpur is being approved under this Government decision as" a special case."
