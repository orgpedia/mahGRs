The proposal: The Supreme Court has dismissed the petition No. Contempt Petition No. 523-524/2002 425-426/2015 to enable the facility of reimbursement of medical expenses uniformly by all States to retired Chief Justices and other Judges of all High Courts. The court gave. Issued on 24/10/2018.
In it. The Supreme Court has ordered that while passing the order in respect of Arunachal Pradesh, the Amicus Curiae has observed that in order to bring uniformity in the medical reimbursement facilities provided to retired Judges of High Courts in all States, the following facilities need to be provided by all States. Accordingly, all other States shall make medical reimbursement facility permissible to retired Judges.
The Supreme Court has adjudicated on the issue.
(i) To provide medical reimbursement facilities to retired Judges which are available to the working Judges.
2) Allowing reimbursement of medical treatment taken in private hospitals without prior approval of the State Government.
(3) to confer on the General Manager of the High Court the authority to approve reimbursement payments.
4) reimbursement of medical treatment taken in other states.
Cashless Medical Treatment Facility Out of the above medical reimbursement facilities at serial numbers 2, 3 and 4 are available to retired Judges of Bombay High Court vide Reference No. 2, 3 and 4 are permitted under the Government Notifications.
The proposal to allow facilities at the remaining serial numbers 1 and 5 was under the consideration of the government.
Government decision: The Supreme Court has dismissed the petition No. Contempt Petition No. 523-524/2002 425-426/2015. In pursuance of the order passed by the Supreme Court, the Government Decision, Public Health Department, No. Vaikhapra 2017/ Q.No. 10/17/ Rakavi 2, Dy. 27/01/2017 and Government Corrigendum, Public Health Department, No. Vaikhapra 2017/ Q. No. 10/17/ Rakavi-2, Dy. This Government Order provides for the grant of medical reimbursement facilities to the retired Chief Justice and other Judges of the Bombay High Court, which were made available to the sitting Judges of the Bombay High Court under 15/11/2017.
