The Proposal:
The e-Committee of Hon'ble Supreme Court has decided to use e-Payment for sanction of penalty and other amounts. The amount to be deposited in the civil and criminal courts of the state of Maharashtra is deposited in the government's consolidated fund or PLA (personal ledger account). Judicial deposits cannot be paid through the government's Government Receipt Accounting System (GRAS). A proposal was submitted to the government to allow the District Sessions Court, Pune to open a new targeted bank account in State Bank of India on a pilot basis to allow the High Court, Appellate Division, Mumbai to accept the fine and other amounts to be deposited in the court in accordance with the" Ease of Doing Business." According to the proposal, the government was considering allowing the office of the District Sessions Court, Pune, to open a new targeted bank account in the State Bank of India to accept the amount of fine and other amounts to be deposited in the court.
Government's decision:
The Office of the District Sessions Court, Pune is being permitted to open a new Targeted Bank Account in State Bank of India on a pilot basis for accepting the amount of fine and other amounts to be deposited in the Court using e-payment subject to the following terms and conditions as agreed by the Director, Accounts and Treasuries, Department of Finance, Ministry of Finance and the Accountant General, Mumbai:
1) The bank scroll should be submitted to the treasury, both account head wise and account payable.

2) The court will have to first reconcile the list of amounts deposited with it and the amount actually deposited in SBI with the list and then reconcile the same with the treasury. While taking final entries in the PLA of the courts, the amounts included in the treasury notes should be included. The amount of money deposited with SBI in case of unsuccessful transactions will not be included in the treasury account.
3) The terms and conditions of Rule No. 512 of the Maharashtra Treasury Rules 1968 should be followed.
4) There will be no change in the method of monthly account report submitted by the Treasury Office to the Accountant General's Office.
5) It should be ensured that the amount collected by the government is deposited immediately in the Consolidated Fund/ Personal Principal Account.
This Government Resolution is being issued in accordance with the opinion and concurrence given by the Accountant General's Office vide letter No.TM/ CH 2/2017-2018/ 71 dated 17.04.2018 and vide informal reference No. 232/18/ COPR-5 dated 17.04.2018 of the Finance Department, Department of Exchequer.
