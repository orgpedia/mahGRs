Preamble: As a measure of urgency for public service, the Department of Finance has decided to reimburse the expenses incurred on tea and lunch from the Ministerial Restaurant only to the non-gazetted State Government employees in the Ministry Department (including Class IV employees) and to the officers of the rank of Cell Officers and equivalent post. 1 The decision has been taken by the government. Also Reference No. 2 The rate of reimbursement of this expenditure has been increased as per the decision of the government here. On the same lines, a request for reimbursement of expenses on tea and lunch to the employees/ officers who have to stay late for office work in the Bombay High Court has been made vide Reference No. 3 letters have been sent to the government by the High Court administration.
A proposal to allow reimbursement of expenses on tea and lunch to the staff/ officials who have to stay late for office work in the Bombay High Court was under consideration at the government level. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
Tea and lunch for the staff/ officers who have to stay late for office work in the Bombay High Court as per the approval of the Financial Powers Committee.
The Government hereby grant fresh financial powers to the Manager, Original Branch, High Court, Bombay and the Manager, Appellate Branch, High Court, Bombay in respect of reimbursement of expenses as indicated in the Annexure annexed to this Government Order and subject to the prescribed conditions and prescribed financial limits.

(3) The orders granting approval in exercise of these financial powers shall clearly state the sequence of relevant financial powers in the Financial Powers Rules, 1978 Part II as well as the number, date and the financial powers in the Annexure to the said Government Order.
(4) This Act shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.
(5) The fiscal powers in the Annexure annexed to this Government Resolution shall be construed as part of the fiscal powers conferred by the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part II. These powers should be included in Part II when it is published.
(6) If the expenditure is expected to be more than the aforesaid financial limit and the conditions prescribed or any of them are not fulfilled, the proposal should be approved by the Government.
