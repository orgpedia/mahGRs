Introduction: Structural Audit of the building of the Charity Commissioner's Office regarding the dilapidated and dangerous building of the Charity Commissioner's Office was conducted by VJTI (Veermata Jijabai Technological Institute), Matunga, Mumbai. 01.09.2018. According to the report, the current condition of the building of the Charity Commissioner's House is dangerous and it is necessary to redevelop the building and relocate the Charity Commissioner's House office to another suitable place within the next six months. When the Charity Commissioner's Office requested the General Administration Department to provide 25,000 square feet of government vacancies, the Government Building in the Mumbai area did not have enough space at present. Space cannot be provided. However, the administrative department has been given full powers regarding the approval of renting and providing rent for the office as per the Financial Powers Rules. vide letter dated 25.03.2019. Also, Mr. Khushru Ratanshah Zaiwala has filed a complaint against the Maharashtra Government and others regarding the dilapidated and dangerous building of the Charity Commissioner's Office. Public Interest Litigation in the High Court of Bombay (No. 49/2019 (St. ) has been submitted. Accordingly, the matter of renting a place for the office of the Charity Commissioner was under the consideration of the government.
GOVERNOR'S DECISION- The following decision has been taken regarding the renting of space for the office of the Charity Commissioner. For a period of five years from the date of taking over the 32,148 sq.ft. built-up area of Sasmira, Sasmira Marg, Worli, Mumbai-400 030 for the office of the Charity Commissioner
