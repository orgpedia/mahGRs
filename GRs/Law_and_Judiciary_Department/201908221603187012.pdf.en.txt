The Proposal:
Government No. 1, d. By a letter dated 05.05.2006, the guidelines for providing medical assistance to the poor and needy patients through the Institute were approved.
In accordance with this approval, the Institute No. 2, d. In the meeting held on 24.07.2011, separate Rules for grant of medical financial assistance were approved. Since this rule needs to be amended with the passage of time, the decision to amend this rule was taken by the Shri Saibaba Devasthan Management Committee. The meeting was held on 17.09.2018.
Accordingly, the Chief Executive Officer, Shri Saibaba Sansthan Trustee System, Shirdi vide his letter No. 4 and 5 respectively. 03.10.2018 and D. The request has been made to the Government vide letter dated 06.05.2019. Based on this request, the government was considering amending the rules for providing medical grants to the poor and needy patients through the Shri Saibaba Sansthan.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: In exercise of the provisions of Section 17 (1) and Section 17 (2) (n) of the Shri Saibaba Sansthan Trustee System (Shirdi) Act, 2004, the following amended Rules regarding grant of medical assistance to poor and needy patients by Shri Saibaba Sansthan are hereby approved: (1) The financial assistance for medical treatment of the following surgeries/ treatments shall be provided.
a) Cancer treatment, surgery and post treatment b) Blood related diseases c) GBS (Guillain Barre Syndrome) Cochlear Implant e) All types of transplant surgery and post treatment

(2) The total annual income of the Yellow Ration Card holder patients and their family members by all means is Rs. The total annual income of patients and their family members with a ration card of 1 lakh 60 thousand or less is Rs. If the applicant's application is 85 thousand or less, the applicant's application will be considered acceptable for the grant. The income certificate should be as follows: (a) If the patient/ parent has a business/ occupation/ occupation/ trade, the patient or/ and his/ her parents should give an affidavit regarding their income from all sources.
(b) Income certificate issued by the Tahsildar of the area in respect of the total income of the patient or his/ her parents.
b) If the patient is above 60 years of age, the subsidy will be given considering his personal income only.
(3) The grant shall be provided only towards the cost of treatment/ surgery in a Government, Zilla Parishad or Municipal Hospital in India, a hospital run by a public charitable trust, a hospital run by a Public Limited Company or a hospital run by a Co-operative Society or a Government Medical College. But such a hospital must be registered in the grant list of the institution.
(4) No subsidy shall be provided by the Institute to the patients who are undergoing surgery/ treatment in the hospital of the Institute.
(5) The application for grant-in-aid should be accompanied by a certificate on the letterhead of the hospital with the signature, degree, registration number of the concerned medical officer indicating the cost of treatment/ surgery and the tentative date of surgery.
(6) In view of the increasing cost and expenses of drug treatment, the subsidy will be 25% of the total cost of surgical treatment or Rs. 50,000/- whichever is less will be paid. However, the cost of the surgery was Rs. Rs.15,000/- or less will not be given.
(7) The amount of subsidy shall be remitted through R.T.G.S. in the name of the respective hospital.
DD/ Cheque will not be issued in the personal name of the patient.
(9) The grant may be received after a maximum period of one month, if not earlier than the date on which the patient undergoes the surgery. Applications received thereafter will not be considered. However, if the application is incomplete, a letter will be sent to the patient to complete the documents, then a period of one month will be given to the patient to complete all the necessary documents.
Applications received thereafter will not be considered.
(10) If the applicant submits the complete application of the grant demand usually one month before the surgery, the grant amount will reach the hospital before the surgery, and in urgent cases, after the surgery but before the patient is discharged from the hospital, the applicant should submit the application by completing all the documents.

(11) The grant is given only once per patient for serious illness. The grant will be given every year for diseases like cancer etc.
(12) The demand for grant must be accompanied by a certified xerox copy of the ration card bearing the name of the patient and the patient's Aadhaar card.
(13) No subsidy shall be provided by the Institute to the patients availing the benefits under the Jeevanadayi/ Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jeevanadayi Health Scheme, similar schemes of the Central Government or the State Government.
(14) All documents to be attached with the application should be self-attested.
(15) The medical financial grant sent by the hospitals receiving the grant should be used for the same patient, if the patient is discharged, the stamp receipt and utility certificate of the amount given to the patient should be immediately submitted to the institution. If the amount of medical financial grant given is not used for the patient, then the amount must be deposited immediately in the accounts of the institution.
(16) If the hospital is to be included in the rules for giving medical financial assistance of Shri Saibaba Sansthan, the hospital must submit the following documents.
a) Xerox copy of the certificate of registration of the trust registered in the office of the Charity Commissioner.
(b) Trust Byelaws (c) Xerox copy of Income Tax 80G, 12A certificate, audit report for the last three years.
(d) Information about the type of surgery/ treatment being carried out in the hospital (including ratesheets).
(e) A letter of assurance that the hospital is a charitable institution. (with registration number on the letterhead of the hospital) f) Whether the hospital is recognized by the Government under Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Janarogya Yojana or how? Certificate from the head of the hospital.
h) Disclosure that the hospital is owned by a charitable institution or is taken to be run on contract or on lease.
Other relevant information about the hospital. All these documents will be taken on the hospital panel with the approval of the management committee after examining the grant department and finance department or accounting branch or auditor of Shri Saibaba Sansthan.
This rule should be followed strictly.
A budget must be provided each year for the implementation of the plan. Care should be taken not to spend more than the sanctioned provision.
