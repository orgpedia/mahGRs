The proposal: The Supreme Court in Suo Moto Writ (Criminal) Petition No. In the year 2/ 2020. The Supreme Court Reference No. 19.05.2022. Under the Pilot Study, the Government of India has decided to set up Special Courts on a pilot basis for a period of one year in five districts of Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Gujarat and Delhi having high pendency of cases under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. Appointment of retired judicial officers and retired support staff in these courts. Supreme Court has ruled.
In pursuance of the aforesaid order of the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India, it is proposed to set up a total of 5 Special Courts, one each at Nagpur, Nashik, Pune, Thane and the Court of the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate, Mumbai in the State for speedy disposal of cases filed under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. The High Court vide Reference No. 2 and 3 have been submitted to the Government.
In compliance with the Supreme Court's order, The proposal was approved by the state government in the High Court.
GOVERNMENT DECISION: Suo Moto Writ (Criminal) Petition No. 2/2020. The Supreme Court Pursuant to the order dated 19.05.2022, it is hereby approved to set up a total of 5 Special Courts, one each at Nagpur, Nashik, Pune, Thane and the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate, Mumbai in the State for speedy disposal of cases filed under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881.
