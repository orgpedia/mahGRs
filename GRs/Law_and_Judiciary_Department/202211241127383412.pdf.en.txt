2. now. The Gauhati High Court in Writ Petition No. 4224/2016 (Virendra Datt Gyani Vs. Union of India& Ors.) dated 15.3.2018 has held that the practice of giving additional pension to retired judicial officers after attaining the age of 80 years is wrong and quashed the same and passed a judgment that retired Judges/ Family Pensioners should be given 20% additional pension from the first day of their attaining the age of 80 years i.e. immediately after attaining the age of 79 years. Accordingly, it was contemplated to provide 20%, 30%, 40%, 50%, 100% additional pension to the retired Judicial Officers/ Family Pensioners immediately after the 1st day of commencement of 80/85/90/ 95/100 years of age (i.e. after completion of 79/84/89/ 94/99 years of age) instead of 80/85/90/ 95/100 years of age.

Government's decision:
The benefit of additional quantum of pension@ 20%, 30%, 40%, 50%, 100% is being allowed to the retired Judges/ Family Pensioners from the first day of commencement of 80/85/90/ 95/100 years of age i.e. immediately after completion of 79/84/89/ 94/99 years of age as follows:
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| Pensioners/ Family Pensioners                                 | an additional pension.                            |
| From the first day of 80 years to the completion of 84 years. | 20% of the revised basic pension/ family pension  |
| From the first day of 85 years to the completion of 89 years. | 30% of the revised basic pension/ family pension  |
| From the first day of 90 years to the completion of 94 years. | 40% of the revised basic pension/ family pension  |
| From the first day of 95 years to the completion of 99 years. | 50% of the revised basic pension/ family pension  |
| more than 100 years since the first                           | 100% of the revised basic pension/ family pension |
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
