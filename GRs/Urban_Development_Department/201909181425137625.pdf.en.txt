Government Decision: The Government of India has launched the" Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban)" with the objective of making all the cities of the country completely clean, healthy and livable and to provide clean and good health and clean environment to all the citizens and to provide sanitation and household toilet facilities to the citizens of all the constituent cities of the country. On the lines of this campaign, the" Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban)" is being implemented in the state as per the decision of the Government No. 1 in the context.
2. Under Swachh Bharat Mission, the State Government has been implementing the scheme of Solid Waste Management. With the approval of the High Powered Committee headed by the Chief Secretary, proposals worth Rs. 67,13,00, 000/- (Rupees Sixty Six Crores Thirteen Lakhs only) of 4 cities were submitted to the Central Government for funding.
The Central Government vide Reference No. 4 As per the order issued herein, out of the Central Government's permissible share of Rs. 23,58,00, 000/- (Rupees Twenty Three Crore Fifty Eight Lakh only), a sum of Rs. 11,79,00, 000/- (Rupees Eleven Crore Eighty Nine Lakh only) has been released to the State as first instalment.
The Government of India has approved the release of the first instalment of funds released to the State for the Urban Solid Waste Management Projects of 4 ULBs under Swachh Bharat Mission amounting to Rs. 11,79,00, 000/- (Rupees Eleven Crore Eighty Nine Lakh only) and State Government's share of Rs. 7,86,04, 000/- (Rupees Seven Crore Eighty Six Lakh Four Thousand only) to the Urban Local Bodies of the State amounting to Rs. 19,65,04, 000/- (Rupees Nineteen Crore Sixty Five Lakh Four Thousand only) on Central and State accounts. The expenditure shall be met from the provision made under the relevant head of accounts for the financial year 2019-20 as shown in the table below.
