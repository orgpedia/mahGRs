The Government has given in-principle approval for development of Ramtek Pilgrim Centre at a cost of Rs.150 crore for development of the historical monuments and precincts of Ramtek in Nagpur district. Accordingly, administrative approval has been accorded vide Government Resolution dated 21/5/2018 for works to the tune of Rs. 49.28 crore to be taken up in the first phase of the project.
For this purpose, a Supplementary Demand of Rs.14 crore has been approved in the Winter Session of December, 2020 and the amount is to be disbursed as per the Government Circular dated 22/12/2020 of the Finance Department at Reference No.5. Out of the Rs 14 crore, the Finance Department had approved the release of Rs 7 crore (subject to a ceiling of 50%). Accordingly, the amount has been disbursed as per the Government Decision dated 3 rd February, 2021. The remaining amount of Rs.7 crore is under consideration and the Finance Department is now required to release the remaining amount of Rs.7 crore.

Government Resolution No: MUN-2019/ P.S. No.178/ NV-18
After approval, the government is approving to hand over this amount to the Collector, Nagpur:
GOVERNMENT DECISION: As per the Government Decision dated 21/5/2018, the responsibility of coordinating and controlling the work of the Ramtek Pilgrim Development Plan and disbursing the grant-in-aid made available for the said plan to the concerned implementing agencies has been entrusted to the Collector, Nagpur. The government has given administrative approval for the first phase of the Ramtek pilgrimage development plan at a total cost of Rs. 49.58 crore. Keeping in view the pending payments and urgency of the works under Ramtek Pilgrimage Development Plan, the Collector, Nagpur had submitted a proposal to the Government seeking funds vide letter dated 17/03/2021. In view of the above, the balance amount of Rs.7 crore (Rs.7 crore only) is being disbursed to the Collector, Nagpur within the limit of 50% of the Rs.14 crore sanctioned through Supplementary Demand in the Winter Session, 2020. The Collector, Nagpur vide his letter No. Cell-12/ MUN-3/ KAVI- 620/2020, dated 18/11/2020, has proposed to the Government to disburse funds to the tune of Rs.7 crore to the concerned agencies for clearing the pending dues.
The expenditure is to be made under the head Main Account Head Demand No.F-2, 2217 Urban Development, 05-Other Urban Development Schemes, 192-Assistance to Municipal Councils, (00) (01) Development of Pilgrimage Area under Ramtek Pilgrimage Development Scheme (Programme) (2217 A225) 31-Assistant Grants (Non-Payment).
As per the terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated 21/5/2018, the District Collector, Nagpur shall ensure that the detailed budgets of the works to be carried out from Rs.7 crore in the first instance have been obtained the technical approval of the competent authorities and the tender procedures for the same are carried out as per the rules and properly.
4. The Collector, Nagpur shall immediately submit the Utilization Certificate to the Government after the said funds are spent.
The other terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated 21/05/2018 shall remain in force.
The Collector, Nagpur shall ensure that the conditions prescribed in the Government Circular Finance Division No.Finance-2018/ PR No. 69/ Finance-3, dated 2/4/2018 are fulfilled.
7. Collector, Nagpur is being declared as the" Controlling Officer" for collection and distribution of the said funds.
The said Government Resolution is being issued with the concurrence of the Department of Planning and in accordance with their Informal Reference No. 349/ Q. 1444, dated 15/1/2021 as well as their Informal Reference No. 124/ Expenditure-3, dated 23/3/2021 with the concurrence of the Department of Finance.
