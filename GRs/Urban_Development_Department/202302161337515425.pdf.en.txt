Government's decision:

In accordance with the Government Resolution No. 02, the said project of Nagpur Municipal Corporation is being approved subject to the following conditions:
4.1 The Commissioner, Nagpur Municipal Corporation has certified that the flyover is in the jurisdiction of Nagpur Municipal Corporation.
4.2 In respect of the work of flyovers, ROBs in the urban areas, it is the primary responsibility of the local bodies/ Municipal Corporations to acquire the land which is not the statutory responsibility of the NHAI/ Central Government. Accordingly, the Commissioner, Nagpur Municipal Corporation shall certify that the responsibility of acquiring the proposed site lies with the Nagpur Municipal Corporation.
4.3 As per the provisions of the relevant Government Decision, the Municipal Corporation is required to acquire the land by paying TDS for such work and also take action to make changes in the Development Plan where necessary. It will be necessary to certify by the concerned Collector, Nagpur that it is not possible to edit the place despite taking action like this. The approval is being given subject to the action being taken.

4.4 The Commissioner, Nagpur Municipal Corporation shall verify and certify that the work of the Centrally Sponsored Project of the flyover and road widening has been started by the Central Government after obtaining technical and administrative approval and that the Central Government has made budgetary provision for the completion of the project.
4.5 The condition that the amount of collateral investment for land acquisition by the Municipal Corporation should not exceed 25% of the total cost in proportion to the investment made by the Central Government or the Central Government in such projects is being relaxed.
4.6 Such land acquisition should be done in accordance with the land acquisition policy of the State Government. As per the proposal, the Collector, Nagpur, shall ensure that while considering the compensation to be paid for land acquisition, it is done as per the rates prescribed in the land acquisition policy of the State Government and certify the same.
4.7 As per the provisions of the Government Resolution No. 2 referred to above, the District Collector of the concerned district shall, before making available the funds for the purpose, make necessary scrutiny regarding eligibility of funds required as per the Land Acquisition Policy of the State for land acquisition of the projects and shall certify the demand of the Urban Local Body regarding the funds required for the purpose. Accordingly, the amount included in the proposal should be considered and certified by the Collector, Nagpur and a certificate should be submitted accordingly. In accordance with this certification, the price of the project shall be revised within the price limits set out in paragraph 2.
4.8 It shall be the responsibility of the Commissioner, Nagpur Municipal Corporation, to make and submit a resolution as the Municipal Corporation may decide to acquire the site for the purpose.
4.9 The funds to be disbursed for this purpose will be distributed mainly in three phases. The first phase of funds will be released by the state government after receiving the fund demand proposal from the municipal corporation after administrative approval. The funds should be made available to the concerned land holder.
4.10 The funds of the first installment and the share of the Nagpur Municipal Corporation in that amount as per the financial figure shall be made available to the concerned landholder by the Urban Local Body from the Consolidated Fund and after submitting its Utilization Certificate and providing the details of the possession of the land to the Government.
After this, the next installment of funds will be made available to the concerned urban local body.

Procedure for the distribution of funds:
6.1 After the administrative approval for the projects in the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urban Mission, the concerned civic body should submit the financial closure report of the project to the Urban Development Department. After that, the first installment of the project will be released. The necessary certificates referred to in paragraph 3 shall be presented.
6.2 After disbursing the last installment of funds for the projects sanctioned under the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urban Mission, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks prescribed by the government as per the prevailing policy of the Government.
The Nagpur Municipal Corporation should be formed by the Municipal Corporation from the 14th and 15th Finance Commission Grants in Goods and Services Tax or from its own funds.
6.3 The second instalment of the State Government will be disbursed after submitting the utilization certificate of 70% of the total amount of the first instalment of subsidy and the equivalent self-assurance fund of the Municipal Corporation to the Government through the concerned Collector.
6.4 The Urban Local Bodies should submit a proposal to the Government through the concerned Collector to demand the second installment. The Directorate of Municipal Administration should scrutinize the proposals and submit them to the government with appropriate feedback.
Non-Direction of Funds:- The funds sanctioned by the State Government for the sanctioned projects under the Maharashtra Suvarna Jayanti Nagarotthan Mahabhiyana can only be used for that project and it cannot be diverted or used for any other work. Any diversion of funds in any other form or in any temporary manner will be considered as serious financial irregularity for which action will be taken by the concerned local bodies/ concerned authorities.
Third party audit of projects: It will be mandatory for the Nagpur Municipal Corporation to conduct third party audit of the projects sanctioned under the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urban Mission at various stages of implementation.
