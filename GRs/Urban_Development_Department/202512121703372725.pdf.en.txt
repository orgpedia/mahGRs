The Proposal: -
TEAM-8 (Techno-Economic Approach for Africa-India Movement) was established in 2000 to enhance African AIA and industry through the inclusion of Indian technology, knowledge and skills.
In 2007, the initiative was transformed into the Africa India Economic Foundation (AIEF) to further strengthen India-Pan Africa relations and increase participation across the African continent. AIEF's sustained efforts and constructive dialogue with African leadership were established at the 2020 African Union Summit, where all member states unanimously decided to set up the Africa India Business Centre (AIBC) in Mumbai to strengthen private sector economic cooperation between Africa and India.
This commitment was further strengthened by the signing of a Memorandum of Understanding between the African Union and the AEF in 2023.
Approved in 2020 at the African Union Summit and signed in 2023, the MoU envisages the use of Indian technology, expertise, experience, and innovations available in this initiative so that African countries can leverage their own natural resources to become manufacturing hubs. This state-of-the-art business centre will have business community, trade representatives and Chamber of Commerce participants who will promote cooperation, tourism and entrepreneurship. The Government's Decisions:
1) The establishment of a Special Purpose Vehicle-SPV (AIEF 74 per cent equity and CIDCO 26 per cent equity) for the development of the Africa India Business Centre (AIBC) proposed by the Africa India Economic Foundation (AIEF) is being approved.
2) For the development of Africa India Business Centre (AIBC), Cidco Corporation is being approved to design SPV, site of plot and price of plot with the prior consent of the State Government.
