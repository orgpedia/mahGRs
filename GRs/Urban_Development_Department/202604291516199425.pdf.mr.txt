Introduction:
Mumbai Metro Rail Corporation Limited (MMRCL), having its registered office at
Transit Office Building, E-Block, Bandra-Kurla Complex (BKC), Bandra (East), Mumbai —
400051, is a Joint Venture Company of the Government of India and the Government of
Maharashtra, each holding 50% equity, incorporated under the Companies Act, 1956;
MMRCL 195 successfully completed the design, construction, testing and
commissioning of the Aqua Line (Mumbai Metro Line-3), a 33.5 km fully underground metro
rail project in Mumbai, at a total project cost of INR 372.76 billion (approximately USD 4.5
billion), comprising 26 underground stations, one at-grade station and one car depot;
MMRCL has further been entrusted with the implementation of another underground
metro corridor of approximately 17.50 km length (Line-11) in Mumbai at an estimated cost of
approximately INR 235.00 billion (USD 2.8 billion), and is also executing various construction
projects including high-rise buildings for rehabilitation of project-affected persons, and shall also be undertaking construction of long underground public vestibules totalling approximately
43.0 km;
The Government considers it expedient to leverage the technical expertise, institutional
capability and experience of MMRCL in the field of complex infrastructure development;
Hence, the Government of Maharashtra hereby resolves as follows:
02. Government Resolution:
1. Mumbai Metro Rail Corporation Limited (MMRCL) 15 hereby authorised to undertake
and execute, either directly or through appropriate contractual arrangements:
a) Heavy civil and infrastructure works, including but not limited to construction of
tunnels, caverns, underground structures, stations, depots, bridges, viaducts and other major infrastructure works;
b) Consultancy assignments, including but not limited to metro rail, urban transport and infrastructure projects;
c) Turnkey/EPC/D&B projects and other allied and complementary works in the fields
of infrastructure development and urban transport.
