Reference- Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015, dated 28.04.2015
The Maharashtra Right to Public Service Ordinance, 2015, was promulgated with a view to provide transparent, efficient and timely public service to the eligible persons of the state and to provide prompt and quality service to the people in view of their growing expectations from the local administration. It has been made applicable to the State of Maharashtra on 28.04.2015. As mentioned in Section 3 of the Ordinance, every public authority shall, within a period of three months from the date of promulgation of the Ordinance and from time to time thereafter, notify the public servants, designated officers, first and second Appellate Authorities and the time limits prescribed by it under the Ordinance. Accordingly, the matter of notifying the time limit, designated officers, first and second appellate authorities for the services of Pune Metropolitan Region Development Authority was under the consideration of the government. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
Government's decision:
The Government is pleased to notify the services under Schedule-A of the Pune Metropolitan Region Development Authority as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015. The details of the duration of public service, office of service notifying, designated officers, first appellate officers, and second appellate officers for the services provided by the Pune Metropolitan Region Development Authority under the Urban Development Department are mentioned in Schedule-A. Accordingly, the Pune Metropolitan Region Development Authority should take action to notify this service in the government gazette.
