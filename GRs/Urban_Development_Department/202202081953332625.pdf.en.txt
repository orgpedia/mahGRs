3.2 As per the instructions given at the time of disbursement of funds for the said project, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks allocated in accordance with the prevailing policy of the Government.
3.3 The funds allocated for the said project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, the matter will be considered as serious financial irregularity.
3.4 After the project is approved by the State Government for the Urban Local Body, the entire responsibility of any increase in the original project cost for any reason will be of the concerned Urban Local Body/ Implementing Agency. No additional funds will be provided by the State Government.
3.5 For Solid Waste Management projects sanctioned under Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban), in addition to the grants admissible by the Central and State Governments, the remaining funds (as per Para 5 of the above table) shall be required to be raised by the respective Urban Local Self-Government Institutions and spent in proportion to the Central and State accounts.
3.6 It shall be mandatory for the concerned Urban Local Self-Government Society to collect self-help funds in proportion to the funds distributed by the Central and State Governments as per the financial figures of the Swachh Maharashtra Mission and submit the utilization certificate of the combined expenditure in the form prescribed by the Central Government.
3.7 The second instalment of funds will be disbursed after submission of utilization certificate in the prescribed format of the first instalment of funds provided for the project to the State Government.
Terms and Conditions: 4.1 The Chandwad Municipal Council shall be the implementing agency for the said project.
4.2 It shall be binding on the implementing agency to implement the project within the prescribed time frame as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the Government Resolution No. 1 under the State Government and the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016.
4.3 The success of the project report is based on the on-site segregation of solid waste.
Therefore, it will be necessary to segregate 100% of the solid waste generated in the city at the construction site and collect it separately.
4.4 It will be mandatory to start the process on this segregated wet waste.
4.5 The Implementing Agency shall be responsible for implementing the aspects contained in the Detailed Project Report on transportation of segregated waste and the scientific procedures to be followed.
4.6 It will be mandatory to purchase the equipment/ machines/ machines etc. mentioned in the Detailed Project Report of the Solid Waste Management Project from the Government eMarketplace (GeM) portal only. Further, if the purchase is made by adopting other methods, the government will take further appropriate action by understanding the financial irregularities.
4.7 While preparing the Detailed Project Report, the requirements for the next 10 years have been taken into consideration. While starting the implementation of this project report, the necessary purchases and constructions should be done taking into account the current needs.
4.8 After the commencement of the project, it shall be the responsibility of the concerned local body/ executing agency to make payment of the work to the concerned contractor within 15 days within the limit of available funds after the competent authority certifies the quality of work and payment.
4.9 Solid Waste Management The local body concerned shall develop a mechanism for meeting the maintenance and repair cost of the project.

While implementing the Detailed Project Report (DPR) being administratively approved for solid waste management of Chandwad Municipal Council under Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban), it will be mandatory for the implementing agency to meet the target set as follows: 100% segregation of solid waste generated in the city at source.
Transportation of 100% segregated waste in segregated manner.
Preparation of compost manure from wet waste or processing it through biomethanisation.
If the compost is being produced, the organic manure produced from wet waste should be tested as per FCO standards by a laboratory authorized by the State Government and it should get the brand" Harit Mahasiti Compost." Also to sell this organic manure in the name of" Green City Compost."
Secondary segregation of dry waste should be done at the material recovery facility. The recyclable waste should be recycled or sold if possible.
After all the above processes, the remaining waste will be compulsorily disposed of in the landfill.
