Government Circular: Mr. S. Rajasekaran filed a writ petition No. In 295/2012. Following guidelines regarding pedestrian safety are being issued to all Municipal Corporations in the State in accordance with the directions given by the Supreme Court from time to time. These instructions should be strictly followed by all Municipal Corporations. As well as Ma. It should be ensured that the order of the Supreme Court of Delhi is not violated.
1. Budgetary provision for Road Safety and Awareness All other Municipal Corporations in the State including Mumbai Municipal Corporation, Pune Municipal Corporation, Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar Municipal Corporation, Nashik Municipal Corporation, Nagpur Municipal Corporation shall reserve at least 1% of their annual budget for road safety, traffic discipline and awareness activities.
For this, the municipal corporations should open a separate head of accounts and spend from it.
2. Footpath Audit and Improvement All Municipal Corporations shall conduct comprehensive audit of footpaths in their area within six months by audit agencies registered with the Public Works Department. Preference should be given to areas with high traffic, such as markets, bus / railway stations, schools, colleges, tourist spots, and crowded places.
All Municipal Corporations should submit a time-bound plan of amendment along with the audit report to the Urban Development Department.

4. Elimination of encroachments and protection of pedestrian areas.
Municipal Corporations structured to remove encroachments on footpaths and pedestrians in their areas.
An action plan should be prepared and the encroachment should be eliminated accordingly as per the provisions of the Act.
5. Pedestrian bridges and underground track safety.
Lighting, cleaning and CCTV of all foot overbridges and underground tracks in the Municipal Corporation area.
Regular checking of controls, LED lights should be done. Preferably within those three months if an error is found.
It needs to be corrected. Include safety and accessibility factors in the annual maintenance agreement with the contractor.
It should be mandatory.
6. Management of meridians and embankments.
Physical barriers, railings, and dividers to prevent unauthorized pedestrian crossing of roads
should be installed. This structure contains the traffic calming rules mentioned in IRC: 35-2015.
to be adopted.
7. Accessibility and installation of walk-in rooms.
Establishment of Accessibility & Pedestrian Cell in every Municipal Corporation.
To ensure planning, implementation, monitoring and grievance redressal.
8. Determination of accident prone areas.
More accident-prone routes, school areas, etc., using police department accident data.
Arrangements for high crossings, speed limit devices, signs, etc. should be made and security guards appointed. At the same time
Security guards should be deployed in accident prone areas with the help of the police department or
Municipal corporations should appoint private security agencies at their respective levels as required.

10. Evaluation of the need for new pedestrian crossings.
Municipal Corporations require pedestrian crossings by phasing out the main and subways.
Assess at least 20% of the total length of roads in the municipal area every year.
A new crossing should be made and designed where necessary.
11. Traffic discipline and enforcement.
Installation of automatic cameras, signage and rumble strips in collaboration with Traffic Police Department.

12. Compliance with road design and construction standards All Municipal Corporations shall comply with the design, construction and maintenance standards of urban roads to be notified under Section 210-D of the Vehicles Act.
13. Non-Motorized Ransport (NMT) Regulation Ensure safe coexistence of cycle, rickshaw, and pedestrian traffic by enforcing rules under Section 138 (1A) of the Motor Vehicles Act.
14. Grievance Redressal Mechanism Every Municipal Corporation should develop an online grievance redressal platform on which complaints regarding footpaths, encroachments or pedestrian amenities can be registered. Complaints must be resolved within 15 days and should be automatically forwarded to senior officials in case of delay.
