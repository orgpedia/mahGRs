the prelude
The Swachh Bharat Mission is being implemented in the state on the lines of the Swachh Bharat Mission of the Central Government. Under this campaign, two major aspects are to make the cities" open defecation free" by providing toilet facilities (individual or community) to the families in the cities who do not have toilet facilities, and to make the city" clean" by managing the solid waste of the city.
Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan is being implemented in every city of the state. Under this campaign, the entire urban area of the state will be connected with toilets by providing toilet facilities to the families who do not have access to toilets. The programme held in the presence of the President has been declared open defecation free.
As per the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, it is the responsibility of the concerned waste generators to segregate waste at the point of generation. Also, those who generate bulk waste (100 kg or more per day) will be responsible for processing the waste generated.
Accordingly, under Solid Waste Management, from 1st May, 2017, all Urban Local Bodies have been directed to segregate (wet and dry) waste at the place of generation under Garbage Lakh Mola in every city of the State and to process this segregated waste in a focused or decentralized manner.

Instructions have been given to the institutions and its implementation has started in all the urban local bodies.
In accordance with the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, the urban local bodies of the state are trying to provide services to the citizens of the city. In this way, while providing services from the urban local bodies, it is also expected to get support from the citizens. However, for those citizens who do not cooperate, as per Section 15 (z1) of the Solid Waste Management Rules 2016, the Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils are required to formulate bye laws and impose spot fines on the persons/ institutions who do not comply with the provisions. However, even after two years of the implementation of this rule, most of the municipal corporations in the state have not yet taken action by preparing bylaws.
6. In order to bring uniformity in the imposition of spot fine in all the Municipal Corporations of the State, the Government has decided to give power to the Municipal Corporations to take punitive action against the persons/ institutions who do not comply with the provisions of the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, as per the provisions of Section 450 (A) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, 1949.
3 It was directed to submit a written representation to the Government within fifteen days from the date of this order as to why the following directions should not be issued under the Government Order.
" The Government is fixing the rates for penalizing the persons/ institutions for non-compliance of the provisions of Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016 as mentioned in Annexure-I to this Government Order and all the concerned Urban Local Bodies are being empowered to collect the penalty for such violations. This order should be immediately implemented. "
No written representations have been received by the Government in pursuance of the aforesaid orders. The matter was under consideration of the government to finalise the order.
Government's decision
In view of the matters mentioned in the Preamble, the rates fixed by the Government under the provisions of Section 450 (A) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, for imposition of penalty as mentioned in Annexure-I to this Government Decision on persons/ institutions not complying with the provisions of the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, are final.
