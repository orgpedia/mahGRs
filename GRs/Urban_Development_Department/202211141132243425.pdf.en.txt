Preamble: To provide transparent, efficient and timely public service to the eligible persons in the State.
The Maharashtra Right to Public Services Ordinance, 2015, was promulgated with a view to meet the growing expectations of the people from the local administration and provide prompt and quality services to them. It has been made applicable to the State of Maharashtra on 28.04.2015. As mentioned in Section 3 of the Ordinance, every public authority shall, within a period of three months from the commencement of the Ordinance and from time to time thereafter, notify the public servants, designated officers, first and second appellate authorities and the time limits prescribed thereunder. Accordingly, the matter of notifying the time limit, designated officers, first and second appellate authorities for the services of the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority was under consideration of the government. The government has taken the following steps in this regard:
Government's decision:
The services under Schedule-A of the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority are being notified under the provisions of Section 3 of the Maharashtra Right to Public Services Act, 2015. The details of the period of public service for the services provided by the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority under the Urban Development Department, the office of the service notifier as well as the Designated Officer, First Appellate Officer, and Second Appellate Officer are mentioned in Schedule-A.
(2) The Designated Officer, First and Second Appellate Authorities responsible for providing services mentioned in Schedule-A shall take action to provide services to the citizens within the stipulated time.
