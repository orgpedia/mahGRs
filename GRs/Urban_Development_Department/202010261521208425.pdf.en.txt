The proposal: The National Green Tribunal (NGT) vide its original application No. 593/2017 (Committee on Environmental Protection v. Union of India and all States). As per the directions of the Tribunal dated 28.08.2019, it has been made mandatory for all the Urban Local Bodies to ensure 100% treatment of sewage generated in the urban areas. Accordingly, a total of 391 ULBs in the State are required to treat a total of approximately 9758 MLD of wastewater and out of this, a capacity to treat approximately 7747 MLD wastewater has been created/ is under construction from various schemes/ funds of the Government. The remaining 2011 MLD of sewage treatment capacity was under the consideration of the government to be set up by the concerned urban local body.
Government's decision:
The National Green Tribunal (NGT) has filed an application No. 593/2017 (Committee on Environmental Protection v. Union of India and all States). As per the directions of the Tribunal dated 28.08.2019, it has been made mandatory for all the Urban Local Bodies to ensure 100% treatment of sewage generated in the urban areas. Accordingly, the action plan and policy for treatment of sewage generated in urban areas of the State is prescribed as follows.

Necessary funds for creation of Decentralized Sewage Management System in the Nagar Panchayats should be made available from the centrally sponsored Swachh Bharat Abhiyan. The expenditure required for this should be met from the available budget of the Urban Development Department. The Finance Department should provide additional outlay for this purpose, if required.
The Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation (BMC) should set up the necessary funds for the construction of sewage treatment system in the Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation area.
