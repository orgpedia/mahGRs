The Proposal:
The Central Government has repealed the Urban Land (Maximum Tenancy and Regulation) Act, 1976 under the Nazakdha Repeal Act, 1999. Accepted on 29.11.2007. Section 20 of the Repeal Act protects the following exemption orders for various purposes, the proceedings under it and the proceedings on the lands acquired under section 10 (3) and 10 (5).
In this regard, the Collector and the competent authority have taken action under section 10 (3) and 10 (5) of the Act in respect of the additional declared lands without verifying that the buildings have not been constructed on such lands. In this regard, the Government has taken a decision regarding the registration of income-sheet and other rights of 7/12 by various organizations/ individuals. Considering the representations received from the two-member committee constituted under 16.06.2017, it is recommended to reduce such entries. Recommended. This has been approved in the Cabinet Meeting held on 18.11.2018. The recommendations of the committee were accepted by the Cabinet. the Supreme Court Civil Application
No. 558/2017. Considering the directions given on 02.07.2019, it was under consideration of the Government to take a decision in this regard.
Government's decision:
Under the Nazakdha Act, when the process of taking possession of the excess land under section 10 (3) and 10 (5) is underway, without verifying that there are no buildings on such land, such lands are taken over and registered in the land rights record in the name of the Government of Maharashtra.
