Government's decision:
The Government of India had launched the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban) with the objective of making all the cities of the country completely clean, healthy and livable and to provide clean and good health and clean environment to all the citizens and to provide sanitation and household toilet facilities to the citizens of all the constituent cities of the country.
Swachh Bharat Mission Urban 2.0 has been launched on 2 nd October, 2021 by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, Government of India, keeping in view the need to sustain the Swachh Bharat Mission and to make the entire urban India garbage free by ensuring continuity in investments for individual as well as community/ public toilets, treatment, disposal and recycling of used water and sewage, management of all types of solid waste and financial assistance for disposal and management of legacy waste, plastic waste, construction/ demolition waste etc. The Mission will be implemented till 01st October, 2026 and the main objective of the Mission is" Garbage Free Cities."
The guidelines for implementation of Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban) 2.0 as a Mission in the country have been recently released by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA), Government of India. The campaign is to be implemented in all the statutory cities of the country. Swachh Bharat Mission-20 will be implemented with a vision to achieve" Garbage Free City" status for all cities.
Under Swachh Bharat Mission 2.0, a sum of Rs. 8,04,00, 000/- (Rupees Eight Crore Four Lakh only) has been released to the State for Urban Local Bodies as mobilization advance for Solid Waste Management Projects and Used Water Management Projects.
