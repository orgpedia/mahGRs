The Maharashtra Suvarna Jayanti Nagarotthan Abhiyan is being implemented under the provisions of the Government Resolution No. 1 in the context of providing basic infrastructure in the urban areas of the state and raising their standards.
Sewerage (Phase 1) Project of Sangola Municipal Council under Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urbanization Mission is being implemented by Directorate of Municipal Administration under reference No. The letter of 9 was submitted to the State Level Accreditation Committee for approval. Government's decision:
The Sewerage Project (Phase-I) of Sangola Municipal Council submitted under Maharashtra Suvarna Jayanti Nagarothana Mahaabhiyan is being approved by this Government Resolution as mentioned in the following statement subject to the terms and conditions of the Government Resolution and the technical approval given by the Maharashtra Life Authority vide letter dated 7th August, 2023 at No. 10 in the context.
The State Government has decided to implement the sewerage project in two phases, considering the limit of funds for the implementation of the sewerage project and to focus on the creation of sewage treatment capacity on priority. 5 dated 30th November, 2017 as well as vide reference No. The decision was taken on December 31, 2018. According to this policy, in the first phase, the existing wastewater should be treated on priority by creating a sewage treatment system according to the future needs. The STP capacity required to treat the entire waste water should be sanctioned in the first phase.
These include water pumps, sewage treatment plants, sewage treatment plants, and sewage treatment plants. Along with this, arrangements should also be made to collect waste water in some of the different wards of the city. For the rest of the ward, the waste water from the Trunk Main should be collected in a drain and such a drain should be blocked near the Sewerage Treatment Plant (STP) and such waste water should be taken to the Sewerage Treatment Plant at that place and it should be treated. In the second phase, a sewerage system should be constructed and connected to the sewage treatment system created in the first phase. The state government has adopted a policy that this phase will include second-level sewers.
