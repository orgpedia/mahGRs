Introduction: In the general meeting of Akola Municipal Corporation held on 02.07.2020, as soon as the discussion on item number 6 on the subject list started, there was a commotion and in this commotion, a resolution was passed without any discussion on item number 7 to 10 and the other 12 items were taken as temporary items and passed without any discussion.
Similarly, the standing committee of Akola Municipal Corporation in its meeting dated 02.09.2020 had demanded a vote but it was not accepted and the matter number 4 to 7 was passed in a hurry.
The government had sought a report from the Divisional Commissioner, Amravati, on the complaint.
The Divisional Commissioner submitted his report to the Government on 1st November, 2020.
After considering the report of the Divisional Commissioner and the accompanying documents, it has come to the notice of the Government that in the general body meeting of Akola Municipal Corporation held on 02.07.2020, a total of 17 resolutions, namely, Resolution No. 6 to 10 on the subject list and Resolution No. 11 to 22 on the subject matter, were passed without any discussion in violation of the provisions of Schedule D of the Municipal Corporation Act. Similar violation of rules has also been observed in the matter of Resolution No. 5 to 7 passed in the Standing Committee meeting dated 02.09.2020.
Therefore, the General Body passed Resolution No. 02.07.2020. Resolution No. 5 to 7 dated 02.09.2020 of the Standing Committee was under consideration at the Government level for dissolution under Section 451 of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporations Act.
The government has taken the following decisions:

GOVERNOR'S DECISION: The Akola Municipal Corporation passed Resolution No. Resolution No.5 to 7 dated 02/07/2020 dated 06-22 and Standing Committee dated 02.09.2020 are deemed to be provisionally suspended under Section 451 (1) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act.
However, as per Section 451 (2) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, within one month from the date of the order, the Municipal Corporation as well as the Commissioner, Akola Municipal Corporation as well as the Standing Committee are being given an opportunity to make representations. If the complaint is not received within the stipulated time, further action will be taken as per Section 451 (3) of the Act.
