GOVERNMENT DECISION: The Government decision dated 18th September, 2019 regarding the release of Central and State Government funds for solid waste management projects to Urban Local Bodies in the State under Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban) is hereby rescinded.
The Government of India has launched the" Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban)" with the objective of making all the cities of the country completely clean, healthy and livable and to provide clean and good health and clean environment to all the citizens and to provide sanitation and household toilet facilities to the citizens of all the constituent cities of the country. On the lines of this campaign, the" Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban)" is being implemented in the state as per the decision of the Government No. 1 in the context.
3. Under Swachh Bharat Mission, the State Government has been implementing the scheme of Solid Waste Management. With the approval of the High Powered Committee headed by the Chief Secretary, proposals worth Rs. 67,13,00, 000/- (Rupees Sixty Six Crores Thirteen Lakhs only) of 4 cities were submitted to the Central Government for funding.
The Central Government vide Reference No. 5 As per the orders issued herein, out of the Central Government's permissible share of Rs. 23,58,00, 000/- (Rupees Twenty Three Crore Fifty Eight Lakh only), a sum of Rs. 11,79,00, 000/- (Rupees Eleven Crore Eighty Nine Lakh only) has been released to the State as first instalment.
Under Swachh Bharat Mission, the Central Government has released the first installment of Rs. 11,79,00, 000/- (Rupees Eleven Crore Eighty Nine Lakh only) to the State Government for the Urban Solid Waste Management Projects of the four Urban Local Bodies in the State and the State Government has released Rs. 7,86,04, 000/- (Rupees Seven Crore Eighty Six Lakh Four Thousand only) as Central and State share respectively.
