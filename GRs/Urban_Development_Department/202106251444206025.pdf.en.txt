The Proposal:
The Goods and Services Tax (GST) has been implemented in the state from 01.07.2017 through a notification of the Government of Maharashtra, Finance Department. The Department of Finance has issued Reference No. Clarifications have been issued by Public Works Department vide Circulars No. 1, 2 and 5 and Public Works Department vide Circulars No. 3, 4 and 8. The clarifications have been published under Circulars 6 and 7. On the same lines, the Department of Water Supply and Sanitation vide Reference No. Guidelines for implementation of Goods and Services Tax in respect of contracts for rural water supply schemes have been issued as per the decision of the Government. These instructions are in line with the feedback received from Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran regarding the implementation of the contract for the schemes of Amrit and Suvarnajayanti Nagarotthan Mahabhiyan of the Urban Development Department.

Government Decision No. 247/ N.V.-33 of 2019 was under consideration of the Government to apply for the contracts of water supply/ sewerage and other approved schemes under the Amrit and Suvarnajayanti Nagarothana Mahabhiyan.
Government's decision:
The implementation of Goods and Services Tax in the state has started from July 1, 2017. Following instructions are being issued in respect of tenders and contracts for implementation of water supply/ sewerage and other sanctioned schemes under AMRUT and Suvarna Jayanti Nagarothana Mahaabhiyan under Urban Development Department due to implementation of Goods and Services Tax.
Procedure to be followed in case of new works contracts after 01.07.2017:
1) The rates of various items in the tariff list should be excluding the Goods and Services Tax. While preparing the budget, they should be included in the Recapulation Sheet to refund the prevailing Goods and Services Tax.
(2) The tenders for the works in the Urban Water Supply/ Sewerage Scheme shall be invited at the budget price excluding Goods and Services Tax.
(3) While submitting the tender, clear instructions should be given to the contractor to submit the tender considering the Goods and Services Tax applicable after 01.07.2017. An article should be included.
After the contractor submits complete and authenticated documents in support of payment of GST, such proposals and documents should be scrutinized by the Accountant/ Chartered Accountant/ Accounts Officer and the refund of GST should be paid within 30 days from the date of receipt of the proposal.
Procedure to be followed in case of contracts awarded before 01.07.2017:
As GST has now been made applicable to all existing contracts, as on 01.07.2017, for existing contracts, as per the contract terms, sales tax/ value added tax/ excise duty/ LBT/ duty etc. are included in the tender price, the contractor shall take action for refund or recovery after duly considering the submission of official documents/ documents regarding payment of GST to the Government by deducting the same. Such proposals and documents should be scrutinized by the Accountant/ Chartered Accountant/ Accounts Officer/ Project Management Consultant and approval of the competent authority should be obtained.
Procedure to be followed in case of Contracts for which tender was approved before 01.07.2017 and commissioning order was issued after 01.07.2017:
In cases where tenders have been accepted before 01.07.2017 and work orders have been issued for the contract after 01.07.2017, the contract should not be cancelled and the contractor should be directed to start work on the contract.

In respect of deduction of Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) on payment of dues to all suppliers/ contractors for taxable goods/ services or both:
As per the instructions in the circular at Reference No. 5 of the Finance Department, TDS at the rate of 2% shall be deducted from the payment of dues of all suppliers/ contractors for taxable goods/ services or both.
