In view of the traffic problems arising out of the increasing population and number of vehicles in Nagpur city, the government approved the implementation of Nagpur Metro Rail Phase-I project in January, 2014. The 40.02-km-long project, with 32 stations, is being constructed by the Maharashtra Metro Rail Corporation Ltd. This SPV has been done by the company and the entire corridor of Phase-1 project has been dedicated on 11 th December, 2022.

Contextual Government Decision dated 14.01.2019 had approved implementation of 11239 crore elevated metro line through MahaMetro. The project proposal was sent to the central government for approval as the financial plan of the project was proposed on the basis of" equity sharing model." In view of the technical and financial issues raised by the Central Government from time to time, Phase-II of Line-5: Vasudev Nagar to Dattawadi has been dropped from the project and the project cost has also been revised. The Union Cabinet in its meeting held on 07.12.2022 approved the project at a cost of Rs. 6708 crore has been approved.
Accordingly, the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has accorded approval for implementation of the project vide order dated 29.12.2022.
The project needs to be approved by the central government.

The second phase of the Nagpur Metro Rail Project is being declared as an' Aspirational Urban Transport Project'.
The approval is being given for implementation under the Metro Railways Act, 1978, the Metro Railways Act, 2002, the Metro Railways (Amendment) Act, 2009 and the Railways Act, 1989.
9) Maharashtra Metro Rail Corporation Limited (MMRCL) will develop the extension of Maha-Metro Phase-II, including stations, parking and property development. The transferred area is being recognized as SPA.
The extension of Transit Oriented Development (TOD) corridor of Nagpur Metro Rail Phase-II is being approved under the Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966.
The Government is approving the scope of the Committee by constituting a High Power Committee as mentioned in Annexure III to take policy decisions with a view to implementing the said project.
12) It is being approved to set up a" Dedicated Urban Transport Fund" as mentioned in Annexure-IV and to utilise the funds available from other sources by depositing the same in the" Dedicated Urban Transport Fund" for repayment of loan taken for the said project, operation and maintenance (O& M), implementation of future infrastructure projects, etc.
13) Approval is being given to implement the" Mumbai Urban Transport Project Rehabilitation and Resettlement Policy (MUTP-R& R Policy)" implemented under the Housing and Special Assistance Department, Government Decision No. 1700/ CR 31/ Slum-2, dated 12.12.2000 for the rehabilitation and resettlement of Project Affected Persons (PAPs) under the Nagpur and Pune Metro Rail Projects.
In case of increase in the approved project completion cost due to cost escalation, change in the exchange rate, change in the scope of the project, inclusion of non-redundant items in the DPR, avoidable delay in or before the stipulated date of construction of the project, such additional cost is approved to be borne by the local authority, government/ semi-government institutions by providing their land (other than the land indicated in the DPR) and developing such land through TOD, FSI or other ancillary financial sources.
At present, 1% surcharge is levied in Nagpur Municipal Corporation limits under Section 149-B of the Municipal Corporation Act, 1966. As per the provisions of the Maharashtra Zilla Parishad and Panchayat Samiti Act, 1961, the project will be implemented in a phased manner.
The Maharashtra Metro Rail Corporation (MahaMetro) is being authorised to make minor changes in the corridors and stations of the project, if required, subject to obtaining prior approval of the Government in case of increase in the scope of the project or cost of the project.
17) MahaMetro should ensure that the cost of the project does not increase due to delays in land acquisition, rehabilitation and resettlement.
The construction of the metro rail project should be done in the next 5 years by ensuring that there is no unreasonable increase in the construction cost of the project.
