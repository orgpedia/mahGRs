The Centrally Sponsored AMRUT 2.0 Mission is being implemented in the State as per the decision of the Government of India in 2002. Guidelines have been issued under Reference 3 for implementation of projects under Centrally Sponsored AMRUT 2.0 Mission. Further, instructions regarding Model Tender Document for tendering of projects under the Mission have been issued under Reference 4.
In order to expedite the tendering process of the projects sanctioned under the Mission and to regulate the projects at the state level for completion within the prescribed time frame, the circular in reference No. 3 is being amended as under in line with the guidelines issued by the Central Government on AMRUT 2.0 Mission.
Paragraphs 7 and 8 of the Guidelines issued by the Government Circular dated 16 th September, 2022 regarding implementation of projects under Centrally Sponsored AMRUT 2.0 Mission should be omitted and the following paragraphs are being included in the said Circular.
For the tender process of the projects under the Centrally Sponsored AMRUT 2.0 Mission, as per the Model Tender Document made available by the Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran as per the Government Circular dated 19 th October, 2022 of this Department, the concerned Urban Local Body shall prepare the RFP and if any different provision is to be made in it than the provision in the said Model Tender Booklet, then the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan

Government Decision No. AMRUT-2023/ PR No. 331/ NAVI-33 should be approved by the Authority and before publishing the tender, it should be approved by the State Level Technical Committee. After the committee's approval, the tender will be published.
(iii) Once the tender is received, the concerned Urban Local Body after evaluating the same, with the approval of the competent authority, shall submit the tender for final approval of the State Level Technical Committee and after the approval of the State Level Technical Committee (SLTC), the project shall be commissioned.
The tender process of the projects approved under this campaign should be carried out as per the guidelines of the Central Vigilance Commission as well as the instructions given by the government from time to time in this regard.
The progress of all projects under this Mission will be reviewed by the Directorate of Maharashtra Urban Development Mission and at the government level as per the requirement in order to ensure completion of all projects within the stipulated time.
The Government Circular in Reference No. 3 is being amended along with the above. Apart from this, other guidelines issued by the government are also being followed.
