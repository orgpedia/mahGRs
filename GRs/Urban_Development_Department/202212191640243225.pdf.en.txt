Government circular:
The Central Government has enacted the Prohibition of Employment as Manual Scavengers and their Rehabilitation Act, 2013 with effect from 6th December, 2013. As per this Act and the guidelines of Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban) 2.0, it is mandatory for sanitation workers to prevent and rehabilitate manual scavengers in any manner. Accordingly, the Urban Local Bodies will have to clean septic tanks, sewerage lines, public/ community toilets etc. in their jurisdiction with mechanical devices. Also, the government was considering issuing guidelines to all the urban local bodies in the state regarding the complete mechanized cleaning of septic tanks and sewerage lines in order to ensure that the sanitation workers working in this regard are provided with all kinds of safety equipment and are not in any way connected with the handling of sewage.
All the Urban Local Bodies of the State should achieve the status of Safai Mitra Safe City by implementing the following instructions within the next 3 months regarding the complete mechanized cleaning of septic tanks and sewage lines in their jurisdiction. After this period of 3 months, septic tanks and sewerage lines will be cleaned in all urban local bodies only by mechanical means.
1.To completely prevent hazardous entry of sanitation workers into sewerage lines and septic tanks by cleaning them with fully mechanized machinery and to end manual scavenging.

5. Scientific training should be imparted to sanitation workers in respect of cleaning of septic tanks, sewerage lines, toilets etc. For this, funds from Swachh Bharat Abhiyan or other funds mentioned above should be used.
Thereafter, regular capacity building (training) should be provided to sanitation workers entering septic tanks and sewer lines.
7. Sanitation workers should be given the benefit of public welfare schemes of the Central and State Governments for economic upliftment.
The National Safai Karmachari Finance and Development Corporation (NSKFDC) will provide loans to the workers.
8. Training of personnel for proper use of manual scavengers, robotic cleaning equipment and other mechanical devices.
A minimum of 5 years contract should be signed with the concerned agencies for proper maintenance, repair and proper use of manual scavenging vehicles, robotic cleaning equipment and other mechanical devices.
Effective public awareness campaigns should be carried out so that citizens cooperate or take initiative to mechanize the cleaning of septic tanks and sewerage lines as per the prescribed schedule. Also, the helpline number 14420 set up by the central government should be launched and given wide publicity. The expenditure will be met from the funds available under the Information, Education and Communication (IEC) component of Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (NAA) 2.0.
