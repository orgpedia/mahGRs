A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) has been signed with the Geutsche Gesellschaft f√ºr Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ), an agency nominated by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, Government of India, for providing technical support for solid waste management under the Mission.
As per the agreement with GIZ, the wet waste will be scientifically processed to produce compost (green city compost), and from this, the quality of construction fertilizer will be improved, capacity building will be done, and all local bodies will be registered on the green mobile app developed jointly by GIZ and NABARD and through it, the sale and marketing of fertilizer will be supported.
After making urban areas of the state open defecation free under the Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban), the state is now moving towards 100 percent solid waste management and now the emphasis is on scientific treatment of wet waste.
As per the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed with the Geutsche Gesellschaft fur Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GLZ), the government was considering the formation of a State Executive Group Committee for the implementation and control of the program of scientific processing of wet waste into compost.
As stated in the Government Resolution, the composition of the State Executive Group Committee to be constituted for the implementation and control of the programme will be as follows.
