The Proposal:
Section 149A of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporations Act (59 of 1949) has been amended to provide that the stamp duty to be levied under the Maharashtra Stamp Act (60 of 1958) on the sale, gift and encumbrance of immovable property, respectively, shall be surcharged at the rate of one per cent of the value of the property so situated, in the case of sale or encumbrance of any such document in relation to immovable property situated in the city, and in the case of encumbrance of the same, on the amount secured by the encumbrance of that document. Accordingly, 1% stamp duty surcharge payable to 26 Municipal Corporations in the State will result in an accumulation of Rs. 79,28,01, 191/- to the Municipal Corporations is under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The total amount released to the Municipal Corporations within the limit of subsidy available on Budget Distribution System for disbursement of 3rd installment of 1% Stamp Duty for the year 2020-21 is Rs. 79,28,01, 191/- (Rupees Eighty-nine Crore Eighty-eight Lakhs One Thousand One Hundred and Ninety-Nine only) is hereby approved by the Government as under:

The District Collector of the concerned district is being declared as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer as well as the Controlling Officer for the disbursement of this grant to the aforesaid Municipal Corporations. The Collector shall draw the said grant without any deduction from the amount due to the Municipalities (without intimation to the Government) and disburse the same to the respective Municipal Corporations within 10 days.
The amount credited towards the 1% surcharge and the expenditure on this subsidy to be disbursed in lieu thereof shall be maintained separately by the concerned District Collector and the accounts and all related documents shall be made available to the Accountant General, Maharashtra-1, Mumbai and the Accountant General, Maharashtra-2, Nagpur, as required.
