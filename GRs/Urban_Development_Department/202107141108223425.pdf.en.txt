The Proposal:
The 15th Finance Commission came into effect from 2020-21. As per the recommendations of the 15th Finance Commission, separate funds have been sanctioned to Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) for Million Plus Cities and Non-Million Plus Cities. As per the Government's decision at Table No. 5, the division of cities included under the Urban Agglomeration in the state has been disseminated as per the instructions of the 15th Finance Commission. Now, it was under the consideration of the Government to work out the modalities for the distribution and allocation of grants for Air Quality Improvement for Urban Local Bodies in Million Plus Cities.

As informed under the letter dated 19.04.2021 and the letter dated 21.06.2021 and 24.06.2021 of the Member Secretary, Maharashtra Pollution Control Board, Mumbai, for Urban Local Bodies in the group of Million Plus Cities under the 15th Finance Commission
The modalities of disbursement and utilization of grants released during 2020-21 for the purpose of Air Quality Improvement are being prescribed as under:
(a) Under the 15th Finance Commission Grants for Air Quality Improvement for the year 2020-21 have already been disbursed to the Urban Agglomerations.
(b) In this regard, the 15th Finance Commission Grants for the year 2020-21 to the respective million cities for the purpose of Air Quality Improvement will be allowed to be used for implementation of the City Action Plan/ Micro Plans prepared by the respective cities for air quality improvement and approved by the Central Pollution Control Board.
(e) The funds distributed to the major cities of the urban agglomeration may be used by the various cities of the urban agglomeration in proportion to their population.
