The Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan is being implemented in the state on the lines of the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan of the Central Government. Under this campaign, two major aspects are to make the cities" open defecation free" by providing toilet facilities (individual or community) to the families in the cities who do not have toilet facilities, and to make the city" clean" by managing the solid waste of the city.
Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan is being implemented in every city of the state. Under this campaign, the entire urban area of the state will be connected with toilets by providing toilet facilities to the families who do not have access to toilets. The programme held in the presence of the President has been declared open defecation free.
As per the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, it is the responsibility of the concerned waste generators to segregate waste at the point of generation. Also, those who generate bulk waste (100 kg or more per day) will be responsible for processing the waste generated.
Accordingly, under Solid Waste Management, from 1st May, 2017, all the Urban Local Bodies have been instructed to segregate (wet and dry) the waste at the place of generation under" Garbage Lakh Molaacha" in every city of the State and process this segregated waste in a centralized or decentralized manner and the implementation has started in all the Urban Local Bodies.

In accordance with the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, the urban local bodies of the state are trying to provide services to the citizens of the city. In this way, while providing services from the urban local bodies, it is also expected to get support from the citizens. However, for those citizens who do not cooperate, as per Section 15 (zi) of the Solid Waste Management Rules 2016, the Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils are required to formulate bye laws and impose spot fines on the persons/ institutions who do not comply with the provisions. However, even after two years of the implementation of this rule, most of the Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats in the State are yet to take action by framing bye-laws.
6. In order to bring uniformity in the imposition of spot fine on individuals/ institutions in all the Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats of the State in the above background, the Government, in accordance with the provisions of Section 312 (a) of the Maharashtra Municipal Councils and Nagar Panchayats and Industrial Townships Act, 1965, has decided to empower the Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats to take punitive action against the persons/ institutions not complying with the provisions of the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016. 4 The Government had issued a Government Order dated 30th December, 2017 in the context of Reference No. 4 to submit a written representation to the Government within fifteen days from the date of this Order as to why the following directions should not be issued under the Government Order.
" The Government is fixing the rates for penalizing the persons/ institutions for non-compliance of the provisions of Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016 as mentioned in Annexure-I to this Government Order and all the concerned Urban Local Bodies are being empowered to collect the penalty for such violations.
This order should be immediately implemented. " No written representation in terms of the above orders has been received by the Government. The matter was under the consideration of the government.
In view of the matters mentioned in the Preamble of the Government Resolution, the Government is finalizing the rates for imposing penalties as mentioned in Annexure-I to this Government Resolution, on the persons/ institutions who do not comply with the provisions of the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, as per the provisions of Section 312 (A) of the Maharashtra Municipal Councils and Nagar Panchayats and Industrial Townships Ordinance, 1965.
The powers to collect fine mentioned in Annexure-I to this Government Order are being delegated to all the concerned Urban Local Bodies and the Commissioner/ Principal of the concerned Urban Local Bodies.
