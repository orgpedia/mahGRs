The Proposal:
Baburao Bhausaheb Shinde, a constable in the tax department of Sangli Miraj and Kupwad City Municipal Corporation, was found to have registered on the attendance sheet in advance of the next day while on duty. On the basis of the findings of the investigation, the Commissioner, Sangli Municipal Corporation, sentenced Mr. Shinde to withhold one annual increment for one year.
After Shinde and Shipai filed an appeal with the standing committee of the Municipal Corporation, the standing committee passed resolution No. 294 dated April 5, 2018, thereby approving the revocation of the punishment given to Mr. Shinde. However, the Commissioner, Sangli Miraj and Kupwad City Municipal Corporation have requested the Government to cancel the said resolution passed by the Standing Committee as it was passed without taking the opinion of the administration as per the provisions of Section 56 (4) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 and is not in the interest of administrative discipline.
3. The report of the Inquiry Officer submitted to the Government by the Commissioner, Sangli Miraj and Kupwad City Municipal Corporation states that the actions of Mr. Shinde are in violation of office discipline, his conduct is negligent and irresponsible and the charges against him are partially proved. In this regard, the Standing Committee of the Sangli Miraj Kupwad City Municipal Corporation passed a resolution dated April 5, 2018. 294 was not found to be in the interest of administrative discipline and hence the matter was under the consideration of the Government to dissolve the resolution.

Government Resolution No: MUP-2019/ No.47/ NV-19
Government's decision:
Standing Committee of Sangli Miraj Kupwad Municipal Corporation passed Resolution No. 294, dated 5th April, 2018 is being suspended prima facie under the provisions of Section 451 (1) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, as it is not in the best interest of the administrative discipline of the Municipal Corporation.
As per Section 451 (2) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, the concerned can make a representation to the state government within one month from the date of the said decision.
If the complaint is not received within the stipulated time, further appropriate action will be taken as per Section 451 (3) of the Act.
