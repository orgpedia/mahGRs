The State Cabinet in its meeting held on 27th September, 2016 has approved the Mumbai Metro Rail Line-4 (Wadala-Ghatkopar-Mulund-Thane-Kasarvadli) project undertaken by Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) to decongest traffic in Mumbai. Accordingly, the Urban Development Department, vide Government Resolution No. MRD-3316/ PR No. 41/ NV-7 dated 25/10/2016, has issued the said approval. The cost of the Mumbai Metro Line-4 project is Rs. 14549 crore. The financial participation statement of the project includes 80 per cent of the cost of the system components at Rs. The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) has been approved to take over the external debt of Rs 3916 crore. In-principle approval has been given to provide a guarantee from the state government, if the state government is required to provide a guarantee for external borrowing by MMRDA for this project. The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) had proposed to borrow a total of$ 500 million from the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB) Bank for the Mumbai Metro Rail Line-4. Accordingly, a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was signed between the Metropolitan Region Development Authority and the Principal Secretary (Finance) and the Principal Secretary (Urban Development) on behalf of the Government on June 15, 2017, for obtaining the guarantee of the State Government for this loan. Thereafter, vide letter No. BSP-2017/ PR No. 49/ Finance, dated 07.07.2017, the Finance Department of the Government had given a counter guarantee to the loan assistance to be taken by MMRDA from Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank for the Mumbai Metro-4 project. This action The Cabinet has accorded ex-post facto approval on 23.07.2017 and issued the Government Resolution dated 18.12.2017 in this regard. However, the proposal was put on hold as the terms and conditions of the loan from AIIB Bank and the interest rates were found to be comparatively high and it would not be financially viable to take a loan from Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank.
With KIW Bank agreeing to provide low-cost loans, MMRDA has proposed to borrow a total of EUR 545 million (about Rs. 4179 crore) has been approved by the Government in the month of August, 2020 and the corresponding agreements have been signed. Therefore, the government was considering to cancel the counter guarantee given by the government to the loan assistance to be taken by MMRDA from Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank for the Mumbai Metro-4 project.
