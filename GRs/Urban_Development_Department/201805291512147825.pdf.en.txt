Public Accounts Committee (2015-2016) (Thirteenth Maharashtra Legislative Assembly) Eighth Report
Introduction: The Public Accounts Committee in its 8th Report of the 13th Maharashtra Legislative Assembly for 2015-16 has recommended as follows:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| Recommendation No. | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| 5.25(2)            | A clear guideline should be set from the government level to maintain uniformity in functioning while taking action as per the provisions of the Municipal Corporation Act while giving space on lease to the developer.                                              |
| 5.37(2)            | While developing the sites in all the Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils of the State, the Department should take a universal review and set clear guidelines on the methodology of development work and the instructions should be issued by the Department. |
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(2) The matter of issuing guidelines in accordance with the above recommendations was under consideration of the Government.
Government circular: In view of the detailed observations made by the Public Accounts Committee in the said Report, the Committee has accordingly accepted the aforesaid recommendation No. In view of clauses 5.25 (2) and 5.37 (2), the Urban Local Bodies shall, accordingly, follow the following procedures and strictly follow the guidelines while leasing out the land belonging to them/ acquiring the land in question and developing the land in question:
(1) The transfer of immovable property owned by Municipal Corporations and Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats shall be strictly in accordance with the provisions of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporations Act and the Maharashtra Municipal Councils, Nagar Panchayats and Industrial Towns Act, 1965.
Maharashtra Municipal Councils, Nagar Panchayats and Industrial Towns in respect of Municipal Councils and Nagar Panchayats
Necessary action should be taken as per the Maharashtra Municipal Councils (Transfer of Immovable Property) Rules, 1983 prescribed under the Act, 1965.

(3) In cases where the approval of the Government is required, due prior approval of the Government shall be obtained.
(4) The e-tendering process shall be followed while granting/ renting out the immovable property on lease basis/ permanent basis and developing the same.
(5) To grant/ perpetuate land on lease as per the provisions of the Act.
The amount of remuneration to be charged should not be less than the market value in any case.
(6) While leasing/ developing the immovable property, the land shall be allotted for the purpose for which the land in question is reserved.
(7) While leasing/ developing immovable property as aforesaid.
It should be ensured that the interests of the urban local body are fully protected while signing the agreement in this regard.
(8) The civic bodies concerned shall regularly review the use of immovable property for the purpose for which it is allotted.
(9) Before transferring the immovable property in respect of Municipal Councils and Nagar Panchayats, the draft agreement should be approved by the Commissioner and Director, Directorate of Municipal Administration.
(10) The agreement should contain a condition in the form that in case of violation of the prescribed terms and conditions, the property concerned shall be taken over by the municipality/ municipal corporation.
(11) If there is/ is a breach of the terms and conditions on which the immovable property is given, further action should be taken immediately in accordance with the terms and conditions of the agreement.
Any violation of the above guidelines will be the responsibility of the concerned Commissioner, Municipal Corporation and Chief Officer, Municipal Council.
