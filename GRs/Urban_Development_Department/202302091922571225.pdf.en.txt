The Opex price of the said water supply project of Aurangabad Municipal Corporation is required to be set up by Aurangabad Municipal Corporation. The project will take 36 months to complete.
Administrative approval is being given to the water supply project of Aurangabad Municipal Corporation subject to the following conditions: 5.1 The implementation for the said project should be done on full deposit basis by Maharashtra Life Authority.
5.2 The share of the local body for the project should be paid by the Aurangabad Municipal Corporation.
5.3 The implementing agency shall be bound to implement the project as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the decisions of the Government in the context of the State Government.
5.4 The State Government vide No. It shall be incumbent upon the concerned executing agency to complete the tendering process and execution of the project within the prescribed time frame as per the procedures prescribed under Sections 2 and 3. D.I. for this project.
All types of pipes, including pipes, should also be included in the tender.
5.5 It will be mandatory to publish the model tender booklet issued by the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority for water supply and sewerage projects under the Centrally Sponsored AMRUT 2.0 Mission.
5.6 The implementing agency shall ensure that the work order for the said project is issued within 45 days from the date of order of administrative sanction.
5.7 The Mission City will be mandated to complete the reforms prescribed by the Central Government under the Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission within the prescribed time frame.
5.8. Mode of disbursement: As per the guidelines of Centrally Sponsored AMRUT 2.0 Mission, the Central share of funds will be disbursed in three tranches (20%+ 40%+ 40%). Accordingly, the State share of funds will be shared with the Central share in proportion to the funds released by the Central Government.
5.9 While raising self-financing funds for projects sanctioned under AMRUT Mission, Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) will be required to converge with the Finance Commission funds received by them. For this, they should mark the funds received from the Finance Commission.

5.10 As per the guidelines of the Mission, the funds allocated for the project will be based on the outcome of functional outcomes. Also, the project will not be funded if the said functional achievements are not met. The Commissioner, Aurangabad Municipal Corporation will be responsible for this.
5.11 The first installment of funds for the project from the Central and State Government will be distributed to the Aurangabad Municipal Corporation after issuing the work order.
Accordingly, after submission of Utilization Certificate to the Central Government as prescribed by the Central Government and completion of functional achievements, the next instalment of Central share and State share will be disbursed accordingly.
5.12 It shall be mandatory for the concerned Urban Local Self-Government Institution to raise funds on the basis of the funds distributed by the Central and State Governments as per the financial figures of the Mission and submit the utilization certificate to the Government in the form prescribed by the Central Government.
5.13 As per the instructions issued while allocating funds for the project, Aurangabad will be allocated funds in proportion to the funds released by the Central and State Governments for the project.
It will be mandatory for the municipal corporation to open a separate bank account and keep funds in it.
The funds allocated for the project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, it will be considered as serious financial irregularity.
5.14 After the approval of the project for the urban local body by the State Government, the original project price shall be as per the Government Decision No.
In case of extension of Grapadho-2021/ PR No. 122/ PAPU-06, dated 29th June, 2022, other than the stated reason, the entire responsibility will be of the concerned implementing agency. No additional funds will be provided by the State Government for this purpose.
5.15 The overall responsibility of completing the project within the stipulated time will be shared by the concerned implementing agency, Project Management Consultant and Project Management Development and Management Consultant.
5.16 Once the project is commissioned, the concerned executing agency shall be responsible for making payment of the work to the concerned contractor within 15 days within the limit of funds available after the project manager consultant certifies the quality of work and payments.
