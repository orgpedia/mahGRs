The Proposal:
The Government of India has decided on 03.09.2013 to convert the Swarna Jayanti Shahari Rozgar Yojana being implemented during the 12th Five Year Plan into" Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission" by restructuring and incorporating some new components.
1. Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana- National Urban Livelihoods Mission focuses on urban poor, urban homeless and urban street vendors. As per the guidelines of the Central Government, orders were issued under the Government Decision, Urban Development Department, dated 28.08.2014 regarding the implementation of the National Urban Livelihood Mission (NULM) in cities with a population of more than one lakh as per the 2011 Census and less than one lakh but having district headquarters. Similarly, since the above mission will be implemented in 53 cities of the state, under this decision, orders were issued to implement the State Government sponsored State Urban Subsidy Mission on the lines of National Urban Subsidy Mission in the remaining 206 cities of the state. Reference No. As per the Government decision dated 12.05.2017, Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) has been implemented in 259 cities in the State.
Under the said relevant Government Decisions dated 28.08.2014 and 12.05.2017, the implementation of the scheme in respect of various sub-sectors has been detailed.

Orders have also been issued to create a total of 988 posts on contract basis, 10 for State Mission Management Cell and 2 at Ministry level to implement the scheme.
3) Under the Central Government. Under the Mission vide letter dated 19.02.2016, the State Government has been allowed to include Statutory Towns, as may be notified by the State Government, in addition to the Towns covered under the earlier National Urban Livelihood Mission.
Under the new scheme, the financial ratio between the Centre and the states has been fixed at 60: 40.
The Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) covers 259 Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats in the State. Since then, a total of 146 new Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats (125 Nagar Panchayats and 21 Nagar Parishads) have been created in the States. At present, the newly established Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats are under the Urban Development Department and not under the Rural Development Department. Since there is no scheme for the urban poor in these cities, the government is considering to expand the scope of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission in the state and implement it in the newly created urban local bodies.
Government's decision:
In the above background, as per the decision taken in the Cabinet meeting held on 28.06.2023, the Government is approving the implementation of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission launched by the Central Government in the State as under:
(1) The decision of the Government. The Centrally Sponsored Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) is currently being implemented in 259 cities as per the provisions notified in 12.05.20217. Further, as per the policy and instructions of the Central Government, approval is being given to implement Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) in 153 newly established Urban Local Bodies.
As per the policy and instructions of the Government of India, the newly established 153 Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) comprising of components/ components under Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM), Social Mobilization and Institution Development (SM& ID), Self Employment Programme (SEP), Employment Generation through Skill Training (ES& TP), Support to Street Vendors (SUSV), Shelter to Homeless (SUH) are being implemented in the new 153 Nagar Panchayats/ Municipal Councils.
