The Proposal:
The Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban) is an initiative of the Government of India to provide toilets in all cities of the country to ensure clean environment and good health to the citizens. " Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban)" is being implemented in the State as per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 1 on the lines of" Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban)" of the Central Government. Under this mission, the two major components include making cities" Open Defecation Free" and" Clean" under solid waste management.
The detailed project report of the Daryapur Municipal Council's urban solid waste management project under the Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban) has been given technical approval by the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority and the proposal has been approved by the government. Accordingly, under the Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban), Rs. The High Powered Committee chaired by the Chief Secretary in its meeting held on 30th August, 2019 has approved the Urban Solid Waste Management Project of Daryapur Municipal Council at a cost of Rs. 2.44 crore. Accordingly, the matter of granting administrative approval to the Daryapur Municipal Council's urban solid waste management project was under consideration of the government.
Government Decision: Under Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban), the Government is granting administrative approval to the Urban Solid Waste Management Project, worth a total of Rs. 2.44 crore, mentioned in the statement accompanying this Government Decision of Daryapur Municipal Council, subject to the terms and conditions mentioned in the following paragraph 4.

Terms and Conditions: 4.1 The Daryapur Municipal Council shall be the implementing agency for the said project.
4.2 It shall be mandatory for the implementing agency to implement the project within the prescribed time frame as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the Government Resolution No. 1 under the State Government and Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016.
4.3 Detailed Project Reports of Solid Waste Management Projects have been prepared as per the guidelines prescribed under the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016. The success of this project report is based on the on-site segregation of solid waste
Therefore, it will be necessary to segregate 100% of the solid waste generated in the city at the construction site and collect it separately.
Accordingly, the Daryapur Municipal Council will be mandated to collect at least 90% of the solid waste generated daily in the city by December, 2019 by segregating it at the point of generation.
4.4 It will be mandatory to start the process on this segregated wet waste in a concentrated or decentralized manner.
4.5 The Implementing Agency shall be responsible for implementing the detailed project report on the transportation of segregated waste and the scientific procedures to be followed.
4.6 It will be mandatory to purchase the equipment/ machines/ machines etc. mentioned in the Detailed Project Report of the Solid Waste Management Project from the Government eMarketplace (GeM) portal only. Further, if the purchase is made by adopting other methods, the government will take further appropriate action by understanding the financial irregularities.
4.7 While preparing the Detailed Project Report, the requirements for the next 10 years have been taken into consideration. While starting the implementation of this project report, the necessary purchases and constructions should be done taking into account the current needs.
4.8 After the commencement of the project, it shall be the responsibility of the concerned local body/ executing agency to make payment of the work to the concerned contractor within 15 days within the limit of available funds after the competent authority certifies the quality of work and payment.
4.9 Solid Waste Management The local body concerned shall develop a mechanism for meeting the maintenance and repair cost of the project.

5. While implementing the Detailed Project Report (DPR) being administratively approved for Solid Waste Management of Daryapur Municipal Council under Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban), the following is being done:
The implementation mechanism will be bound to meet the target set. 100% segregation of solid waste generated in the city at source.
Transportation of 100% segregated waste in segregated manner.
Preparation of compost manure from wet waste or processing it through biomethanisation.
If the compost is being produced, the organic manure produced from wet waste should be tested as per FCO standards by a laboratory authorized by the State Government and it should get the brand" Harit Mahasiti Compost." Also to sell this organic manure in the name of" Green City Compost."
Secondary segregation of dry waste should be done at the material recovery facility. The recyclable waste should be recycled or sold if possible.
After all the above procedures, the remaining waste should be disposed of in the landfill.
