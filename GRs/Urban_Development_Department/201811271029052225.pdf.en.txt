Government's decision:
The Government decision at Reference No. 2 mandates that 50% of the 14th Finance Commission funds available to Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) be spent on Swachh Bharat Mission. The works to be undertaken from this fund earmarked for Swachh Bharat Abhiyan have also been decided.
2. As per the Government decision at Reference No.1, Swachh Bharat Mission is being implemented in the State and in the first phase, urban areas of the State have become" Open Defecation Free." Facal Sludge Management (FSM) is required to maintain the status of open defecation free cities. Under the scheme, the safety tanks of individual and public toilets in cities are required to be cleaned after regular intervals. Some cities do not have the required De Sludging Vacuum Emptier machines. Therefore, these cities have demanded permission for the purchase of Vacuum Emptier machines.
Further, in the second phase of Swachh Bharat Mission, action is being taken to clean the cities under solid waste management. For this, detailed project reports are being prepared under solid waste management of all the cities of the state. Some of the details of this project
Cities have been sanctioned Backhoe Loaders. However, it has not been included in the detailed project report of some cities. Detailed project report of the city

In view of the above, the Government is approving the purchase of Vacuum Emptier and Backhoe Loader from the 14th Finance Commission funds allocated to the Urban Local Bodies in the State, out of the 50% funds earmarked for Swachh Bharat Mission, subject to the following conditions:
These vehicles should be purchased from the e-GeM portal only.
(2) Cities that have Vacuum Emptier vehicles available will not be able to buy these vehicles again under this approval.
(3) The cities where the Backhoe Loader has been approved in the Detailed Project Report or the cities where the Backhoe Loader is available will not be able to buy Backhoe Loader again under this approval.
(8) The purchase of the above vehicles should be made taking into account the needs and requirements of the city.
5. Other provisions of the Government Resolution at Reference No. 2 shall continue.
