The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran had raised a total loan of Rs. 542.78 crore from the Life Insurance Corporation on behalf of the Municipal Corporations/ Municipalities from 1978 to 2003 for urban water supply schemes. It was the responsibility of the concerned Municipal Corporation/ Municipality to repay the loan. As on 2003, out of the issue of Rs. 542.78 crore, an amount of Rs. 204.38 crore had been paid. However, thereafter, the loan dues increased as the Municipal Corporation/ Municipality did not deposit the loan repayment amount with the Maharashtra Jeevan Authority within the stipulated time. Later in 2011, Rs. The amount of Rs. 350.12 crore (principal- Rs. 131.66 crore, interest- Rs. 205.68 crore, compound interest- Rs. 12.78 crore) was paid by the Government of Maharashtra to the Life Insurance Corporation through the Authority.
After this OTS in 2011, the principal balance was Rs. 206.74 crore. However, due to non-availability of the full amount of loan repayment by the Municipal Corporation/ Municipality, the direct interest, penal interest and compound interest on the loan increased. A proposal has been submitted by the Water Supply and Sanitation Department to the Life Insurance Corporation to waive off the entire amount of compound interest and 50% of penal interest charged in the case and make one-time repayment of the remaining total amount of Rs. 357.00 crore (balance Rs. 206.74 crore of the total principal plus Rs. 150.26 crore of the total interest) before 31.03.2022. As per the approval received from the Life Insurance Corporation, the one-time repayment of the loan of Rs. 357.00 crore has been made by the Department of Water Supply and Sanitation as per the decision of the Government in this regard.
The responsibility of repaying the loan raised from the Life Insurance Corporation shall be of the concerned Municipal Corporation/ Municipality, the entire amount of Rs. 357.00 crore to be made available for repayment of the loan shall be treated as a non-interest bearing loan given by the Government to the concerned Municipal Corporation/ Municipality and the recovery of the entire amount shall be made within a period of 10 years from the year 2022-23

Government Decision No: 2023/ S.No.61/ N.V.-04 has been approved under the relevant Government Decision subject to the condition that the Urban Development Department shall deposit the amount from the grants admissible to them by the concerned Municipal Corporation/ Municipality by book adjustment under the head of book adjustment.
The Urban Development Department provides various grants to the Urban Local Bodies. The water supply project worth Rs. 357.00 crore was under consideration of the Government to be recovered from the Government Grants due to the respective Urban Local Bodies and deposited in the respective head of accounts.
Government's decision:
The balance of the loan raised by the Maharashtra Jeevan Adhikaran for urban water supply schemes from the Life Insurance Corporation on behalf of the Municipal Corporation/ Municipality is Rs. 357.00 crore (balance Rs. 206.74 crore in whole principal plus balance Rs. 150.26 crore in whole interest) has been repaid through OTS mode to Department of Water Supply and Sanitation, Government of India vide Government Order dated 23rd March, 2022. The Rs. 357.00 crore is being approved to be credited to the head of accounts by diverting the following grants from the Urban Development Department to the respective Urban Local Bodies mentioned in the attached Returns-A, Returns-B and Returns-C.
(a) Subsidy to Urban Local Bodies under the head" Subsidiary Grants to Urban Local Bodies as per the recommendations of the Fifteenth Finance Commission (2217 A314)" from the Fundamental/ Untied Grants to Non-Million Plus Cities for the period from the financial year 2023-24 to 2025-26 (3 years) shall be deducted from the grants payable to the total 101 Urban Local Bodies as shown in the return.
Subsidy to Municipal Councils on account of abolition of VAT and Dearness Allowance (2217 8066) to be deducted from the grants payable to the total 13 Urban Local Bodies as shown in the return B for the period from the financial year 2023-24 to 2032-33 (10 years).
e) Provision for payment of 1% surcharge on stamp duty in the Municipal Corporation area on account of restoration of Local Body Tax in lieu of Tax (3604 0924) in the period from the financial year 2023-24 to 2032-33 (10 years) under the head of accounts in return-A.
The amount of subsidy to be paid to all the 3 Urban Local Bodies shown should be deducted and credited to the head of account.
(2) Further, while disbursing the amount of subsidy from the concerned scheme/ head of accounts as above, the concerned fund disbursement orders shall be made by referring to this Government decision and by diverting the funds to the head of accounts.
