Preamble: With a view to sustaining the" Swachh Bharat Abhiyan" and making the entire urban India garbage free, considering the need to maintain consistency in investment for individual, community and public toilets, treatment, disposal and recycling of used water and sewage, management of all types of solid waste and disposal and management of old accumulated waste, plastic waste, construction and demolition waste etc. 1 has been posted here. The main objective of this campaign is to have" Garbage Free Cities." Also, read about the city beautification campaign in the urban local bodies of the state. Guidelines have been issued through the government circular here.
2. For beautification of a city, along with beautification, cleanliness is also important. Without cleanliness, beautification of the city is impossible. Due to the above schemes, the face of the cities of the state is changing. Therefore, with the aim of making the cities look beautiful only when they are clean, the government was now considering to organize" City Beautification and Sanitation Competition-2022" in the urban local bodies of the state.
Government's decision:
The" City Beautification and Sanitation Competition-2022" at the city level and" Ward Beautification and Sanitation Competition-2022" at the ward level are being organized from 2 nd October, 2022 to 31 st December, 2022 in all the Urban Local Bodies of the State.
The following are the important aspects of the competition: 1. It is mandatory for all cities to participate in the competition.
The competition will be held from October 2, 2022 to December 31, 2022.
The criteria and marks of the Urban Beautification and Sanitation Competition shall be as mentioned in the accompanying Statement-1.
The criteria and marks of Ward Beautification and Sanitation Competition will be as mentioned in the accompanying Statement-2.
The competition for urban local bodies will be scrutinized by a third party agency in January.
Urban local bodies will conduct inspections under the ward-level competition in January.
The expenditure for the scheme should be met from self-funding, CSR funds and public participation.
