Government's decision
Following guidelines are being issued for implementation of Centrally Sponsored Scheme of PM Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi- Special Micro Credit Facility for Street Vendors in the State.
1. the objectives of the program
The Scheme is 100% Centrally Sponsored and is administered by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs with the following objectives:
(i) Facilitating the availability of working capital loans up to Rs.10,000/-.
(ii) Encourage regular repayment; and (ii) Encourage digital transactions.
Based on the above objectives, the scheme will help in formal financing of street vendors and provide new opportunities to this segment to monetize their finances.
Eligibility Criteria for the Beneficiary:
This is the plan. Applicable to all eligible Street Vendors who are selling in the city on and before 24th March 2020, Street Vendors will be eligible based on the following criteria.
(i) Street Vendors having Sale Certificate/ Identity Card provided by Municipal Corporation/ Nagar Parishad/ Nagar Panchayat; (ii) Those found in the survey conducted by Municipal Corporation/ Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat but have not been issued Sale Certificate/ Identity Card.
The Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats should issue temporary certificate of sale through IT based platform to such vendors and take action for issuing permanent certificate of sale and identity card to such vendors immediately and positively within a period of one month.
(iii) Street vendors who have been excluded in the survey conducted by the Urban Local Bodies or who have started selling after completion of the survey and have not been covered by the civic bodies; or

5. data in the public domain
The list and information of eligible street vendors at the level of urban local bodies should be made available on the website developed by the government and it should be updated from time to time.
Details of the Benefits:
Urban Street Vendors with a repayment tenure of 1 year Rs. The borrower will be eligible to take a working capital loan of up to Rs.10,000/- and repay it in instalments every month. The lending institution/ bank will not take any collateral for this loan.
Street vendors who repay on or before the stipulated period will be eligible for further working capital loan with enhanced limit. No penalty will be charged from the lending institution if the loan is repaid by the street vendors before the due date.
6.1.Interest Rate:
The interest rates of Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs), Regional Rural Banks (RRBs), Small Finance Banks (SFBs), Cooperative Banks and SHGs will be as per their prevailing interest rates.
In case of Non-Banking Finance Companies (NBFCs), Micro Finance Institutions (MFIs), etc., the interest rates will be as per the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) guidelines applicable to the category of lending institution concerned.
Under the scheme, interest rates for MFIs (non-NBFCs) and other lending institutions, which are not covered under RBI guidelines, will continue to be applicable as per existing RBI guidelines for NBFC-MFIs.
6.2.Interest Subsidy:
Under this scheme, street vendors will be eligible for 7% interest subsidy if they repay the loan within the stipulated time period. The interest subsidy amount will be credited to the applicant's account on a quarterly basis (at an interval of three months i.e. four times in a year). every one of them

6.3. Promotion of Digital Transactions by Vendors:
Under this scheme, cashback facility is being provided to the sellers who transact digitally to promote digital transactions. The credit score of the digital transaction by the street vendor will be generated, which will help the street vendors to generate credit for their future needs. The network of lending institutions and digital payment institutions such as NPCI (for BHIM), Paytm, GooglePay, BharatPay, AmazonPay, PhonePe etc. will be used to onboard street vendors for digital transactions. The On-board Street Vendor will get an incentive of Rs. 50 to Rs. 100 will be awarded to:
For first 50 eligible digital transactions in a month: Rs 50; II. For the next 50 eligible digital transactions in a month: Rs 25; (i.e., on 100 eligible digital transactions, the street vendor will receive Rs 75); and III. Further additional 100 or more eligible digital transactions: Rs 25; (i.e., on 200 eligible digital transactions, the street vendor will receive Rs 100).
To get cash-back, you need to make a digital transaction of at least Rs 25 at a time.

9. Town Street Vendors Committee
The Town Street Vendor Committee has an important role in the selection of beneficiaries. As per the Street Vendor Act, 2014, the maximum number of members in the Town Street Vendor Committee is 18.
Municipal Commissioner or Principal Chairperson (ii) 50% of the members are representatives of Local Authority (including Chairperson), Police, Street Vendor Associations, Traders Associations etc. (iii) 40% of the members are representatives of Street Vendors (iv) 10% of the members are representatives of nominated NGOs/ Community Based Organisations.
10.Establishment of groups/ clusters of vendors
As per the prevailing practice, an individual lending institution can form a Joint Liability Group (JLG) of eligible vendors. The Common Interest Groups (CIGs) of street vendors already created by the States can be converted into JLGs by lending institutions. In order to increase the maximum coverage of this scheme, it will be necessary to provide incentives to the street vendors to form CIGs by the urban local bodies.
The list of CIGs of street vendors already prepared by the Urban Local Bodies will be made available to the lending institutions and the lending institutions will be required to provide the list of eligible JLGs of street vendors to the respective Urban Local Bodies. The formation of such groups/ collectives should be encouraged. Even if there is an individual street vendor in this, he should be provided with a loan.
11.A capacity building and financial literacy
A comprehensive capacity building programme will be planned to enhance the capacity of all stakeholders such as lending institutions like BCs/ Banks/ NBFCs/ MFIs, SHGs/ Federations, Bank Agents, implementing ULBs/ TVCs and all persons working as digital payment aggregators to ensure effective delivery of the scheme. Financial literacy of street vendors will be developed by digital payment aggregators like NPCI to encourage on-boarding on digital platforms.

An Integrated IT Application (Mobile App) will be developed by the Central Government for effective implementation of the scheme. 13.A Implementation Mechanism An immediate meeting should be held at the level of Urban Local Bodies to provide inputs for effective implementation of the scheme. Members of TVCs, representatives of BCs/ Bank Agents/ Lending Institutions, Street Vendor Associations, Federations of Self Help Groups, etc. should be invited to the meeting. During the meeting, the information of street vendors as well as the concerned persons working in the field, and the lending institutions should be made available.
The Commissioner, all Municipal Corporations and Chief Officers, Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats will be responsible for the effective implementation of this scheme.
Coordination and control for all Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats shall be done by the Commissioner and Director, Directorate of Municipal Administration, Mumbai.
Detailed guidelines regarding the implementation of this scheme should be issued by the Commissioner and Director, Directorate of Municipal Administration, Mumbai and a report should be submitted to the Central and State Government after reviewing the action taken in accordance with this scheme every month.
