In view of the increasing urbanization and increasing scope of construction in most of the cities of the state, the government was considering the inclusion of construction and demolition waste in the same treatment plant (Co-Treat) in 79 cities near 16 of the 19 cities for which construction and demolition waste treatment plants have been approved.

The Government is granting administrative approval for the treatment of construction and demolition waste of the following 79 cities in the construction and demolition projects of the 16 approved cities, subject to the terms and conditions mentioned in paragraph 3 of this Government Decision.
Accordingly, the list of Urban Local Bodies to be included in the construction and demolition projects of the cities approved earlier and the amount of waste is mentioned in the Statement-A of the said Government Decision.
3. terms and conditions:
3.1 The implementing agency for the said project shall be the concerned Urban Local Body.
3.2 It shall be mandatory for the implementing agency to implement the project within the prescribed time as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the Construction and Demolition Waste Management Rules, 2016 (No. 3 of 2016).
3.3 Detailed Project Reports of Construction and Demolition Waste Management Projects have been prepared as per the guidelines prescribed under the Construction and Demolition Waste Management Rules, 2016. The success of this project report depends on the efficient functioning of the construction and demolition waste management system. Therefore, the construction and demolition waste management system in the city must be functional.
3.4 The Implementing Agency shall be responsible for implementing the matters contained in the Detailed Project Report on the transportation of waste and the scientific procedures to be followed.
3.5 It will be mandatory to send construction and demolition waste to the city and to accept it from the processing cities. Cities that accept waste should not impose any additional charges.
