The Proposal:
The Maharashtra Right to Public Service Ordinance, 2015. It has come into force on 28.04.2015.
As mentioned in Section-3 of the said Ordinance, the Government has decided to list 15 public services that are essential in the day-to-day life of the citizens by considering the various services provided by the Urban Local Bodies of the State under the administrative control of this Department as the urban focal point. as on 23.06.2015. In accordance with the Ease of Doing Business Policy of the Central Government, all Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats have been notified to include a total of 42 important services in the Right to Public Services Act, 2015 under various components such as property tax, water supply, no-objection certificate, business license, advertisement license and cinema shooting etc.

Government Resolution No.: Parichay- 0922/ No. 32/ No.V-32
The notifications have been issued vide Government Decisions dated 26.08.2019, 29.01.2021 and 30.09.2022.
The Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015 has been enacted with the objective of providing transparent, efficient and timely public service to the eligible persons in the State of Maharashtra. This notification was issued on 21.08.2015. In line with the Ease of Doing Business policy of the Central Government, a proposal was under consideration of the Government to include the following four services under the three components of Road Digging License, No-Objection Certificate and Water Supply and to notify the documents, fees, deadlines, designating officers and first and second appellate authorities etc. for these services. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
1 service related to road digging license, 2 service under Naharkat and 1 service under water supply, as per the statement" A,"" B" and" C" attached by this Government decision, all the Municipal Corporations/ Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats of the state should notify as per section 3 of the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015.
The relevant public authorities (Municipal Corporations, Municipal Councils and Nagar Panchayats) shall not be able to change the services, necessary documents and the due date mentioned in the prospectus' A,"' B" and' C" accompanying this Government decision.
The concerned public authorities will be able to fix the fee to be charged for providing services under the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015 and the concerned public authorities can make amendments in the posts of Designated Officer, First Appellate Officer and Second Appellate Officer etc. taking into account the structure of the concerned urban local body.
All the Commissioners, Municipal Corporations and Chief Officers, Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats of the State shall take action for the notification and implementation of the said service as per Section 3 of the Maharashtra Right to Public Service Act, 2015.
