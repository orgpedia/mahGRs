The Proposal:
As per Section 2 (i) of the Maharashtra Employment Guarantee Act, 1977, the definition of rural area includes C category Municipal Council area. Also, considering the situation in the state. The Cabinet in its meeting held on 09.04.2013 has approved the proposal for setting up of a C category Municipal Council in the areas where there is demand for work. The Government has decided to implement the Employment Guarantee Scheme as an ad-hoc arrangement by 31st July, 2013. It was released on April 16, 2013.
Also Reference No. Under Section 3, a separate procedure has been prescribed for the implementation of the scheme in the category' C' Municipal Council area in the entire State. In Section No. Maintaining the ratio of 60:40 of the cost of unskilled-skilled work under the Employment Guarantee Scheme in Class C Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats as per Section 10, Paragraph No. As per Section 12, 25% of the cost of the skilled area is to be met from the funds of the Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat. However, the government has not implemented the rules. The matter of giving instructions on the implementation of the provisions prescribed in the government decision was under consideration.
Government circular:
As per the Maharashtra Employment Guarantee Act, 1977, the State Employment Guarantee Scheme is being implemented in the C category Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats in the State as per the Government Decision dated 03.03.2014. The following steps should be taken to ensure the effective implementation of the scheme:

Out of the 40 per cent expenditure to be incurred on skilled work in all C category Municipal Councils and Nagar Panchayats in the State, the concerned C category Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats should provide 25 per cent of the amount from their own funds.
The Commissioner and the Director of Municipal Administration should control the proceedings through their system.
