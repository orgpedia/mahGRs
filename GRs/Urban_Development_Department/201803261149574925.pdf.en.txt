Preamble: The Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission is being implemented in the State since 2015-16 as per the decision of the Government in this regard. Under this campaign, the construction of water supply, sewerage, storm water drains, urban transport and green area development will be done in the cities. Accordingly, during the financial year 2016-17, Rs. The Centre has approved a Rs 2489.91-crore project for the construction of Amravati City Sewerage Project.
Under AMRUT Mission, the sewerage projects included in the plan for the financial year 2016-17 were given administrative approval as per the decision of the Government dated 08.02.2017. Subsequently, in view of the change in the scope of the project, the government was considering revising the administrative approval of the Amravati City Sewerage Project.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: The administrative decisions issued under the Central Government sponsored AMRUT Mission in respect of the Sewerage Project of Amravati City included in the State Annual Action Plan approved by the Central Government in the financial year 2016-17.

As per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 1, administrative approval is being given to the Amaravati City Sewerage Project subject to the following conditions: 4.1 The implementing agency for the project will be the Amaravati Municipal Corporation.
4.2 The share of the local body for the project should be paid by the Amravati Municipal Corporation.
4.3 The implementing agency shall be bound to implement the said project as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the decision of the Government in the context of the State Government.
4.4 It will be mandatory for the concerned executing agency to complete the tender process and implementation of the project within the stipulated time as per the procedures prescribed by the State Government vide Circular No. AMRUT-2016/ PR No. 163/ NV-33, dated 16th May, 2016 and 12th July, 2016.
Also, all types of pipes, including DI pipes, should be included in the tender for this project.
4.5 It shall be mandatory for the executing agency to tender the said project as per the model tender form issued by Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran for sewerage projects under AMRUT Mission.
4.6 The implementing agency shall ensure that the work orders for the said project are issued within 45 days from the date of order of administrative sanction.
4.7 The Mission Cities and the implementing agencies concerned shall be bound to fulfil the conditions mentioned in the minutes of the State Level High Powered Committee meetings.
4.8 Under the Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission, it will be mandatory for the concerned Mission City to complete the reforms prescribed by the Central Government within the prescribed time frame.
4.9 Methodology of Fund Distribution: As per the guidelines of Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission, the Central share of funds will be distributed in three tranches (20%+ 40%+ 40%).
Accordingly, the State share of funds will be shared with the Central share in proportion to the funds released by the Central Government.
4.10 While setting up the Self-Help Fund for the projects sanctioned under AMRUT Mission, the Urban Local Bodies will be required to converge with the 14th Finance Commission funds received by them. For this, they should mark the funds received from the 14th Finance Commission.
4.11 The first instalment of funds from the Central and State Governments will be disbursed only after the work order for the project is issued. Accordingly, after submission of Utilization Certificate to the Central Government as prescribed by the Central Government, the next instalment of Central share and State share will be disbursed accordingly.
4.12 Utilization Certificate of the expenditure by raising funds on the basis of funds released by the Central and State Governments as per the financial figures of the Mission.

It will be mandatory for the concerned implementing agency to submit to the government in the form prescribed by the central government.
4.13 As per the instructions issued while disbursing the funds for the said project, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks allocated as per the prevailing policy of the Government. The funds allocated for the project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, it will be considered as serious financial irregularity.
4.14 After the project is approved by the State Government for the Urban Local Bodies, the entire responsibility of any increase in the original project cost for any reason shall be with the concerned executing agency. No additional funds will be provided by the State Government for this purpose.
The concerned implementing agency will be fully responsible for completing the project within the stipulated time.
4.16 Once the project work is started, the concerned executing agency will be responsible for making payment of the work to the concerned contractor within 15 days within the limit of available funds after the project manager consultant certifies the quality of work and payments.
4.17 As per the guidelines of AMRUT Mission, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran will act as Project Manager Consultant for the project. Accordingly, the implementing agency will pay the fee of 3 per cent to the Maharashtra Life Authority as per the procedure prescribed by the government.
4.18 Action should be taken to make necessary minor modification in the site of the Sewage Treatment Plant and to obtain the No Objection Certificate for crossing the railway line before giving work order to the project.
4.19 In order to ensure sustainability of the project, necessary action should be taken with the approval of the General Assembly regarding the levy of Sewerage Tax as mentioned in the Detailed Project Report of the project.
