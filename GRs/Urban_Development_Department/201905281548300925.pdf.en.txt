Preamble: The Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) prepare a project report for providing various infrastructure facilities to the citizens and based on it, the project proposals are submitted for approval from the ULB Fund or Government Fund. However, during the testimony of the Public Accounts Committee in this regard, it was noticed by the government that unnecessary detailed project reports (DPRs) were being prepared by some urban local self-government institutions. Government Circular The following instructions are being issued regarding the Detailed Project Report (DPR) to be prepared by the Urban Local Bodies for the creation of necessary infrastructure in their areas.
While preparing Detailed Project Reports (DPRs) of various infrastructure projects through Urban Local Bodies (ULBs), it is necessary to prepare Detailed Project Reports (DPRs) of such projects on priority for the cities.
The Commissioner/ Principal concerned should take necessary precautions that the DPR of unnecessary projects will not be prepared while preparing the DPR.
While preparing the DPR of the concerned projects, it should be verified first whether the concerned Urban Local Self-Government Institution can raise the required funds for the project, whether the necessary space is occupied by the concerned Urban Local Self-Government Institution, whether it is feasible to build the project, whether the necessary provision has been made in the budget of the Urban Local Self-Government Institution, and other technical and financial aspects. After verifying all these aspects, the concerned Commissioner/ Principal should prepare the DPR of the concerned project only if it is necessary for the project city.
