The Proposal:
The lockdown in the wake of COVID-19 has had a major impact on the street vendors and street vendors in the cities of the state and to provide them with the capital to resume business, the Central Government announced the Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi on 1st June 2020. Detailed guidelines in this regard have been issued by the above-referenced Government Decision.
He said that the scheme should not be seen as a loan scheme only for street vendors, but should focus on their all-inclusive economic and social development. The Prime Minister has. With this approach, the Central Government has planned to survey the families of the beneficiaries of the Street Vendor Scheme, check their eligibility and provide them the benefits of other schemes of the government under' Svanidhi Se Samriddhi'. SVANidhi Se Samriddhi Yojana will be a part of Prime Minister Street Vendor's AtmaNirbhar Nidhi Yojana. Accordingly, the following guidelines have been issued by the Government for the implementation of the scheme. Accordingly, the following recommendations have been made for the implementation of the scheme.
Government's decision:
The following guidelines are being issued for the implementation of the" Svanidhi Se Samriddhi" programme in the State under the Centrally Sponsored Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor Atmanirbhar Nidhi Yojana.

The collection of information:
The survey will cover 1) Accommodation/ Housing and other assets, 2) Health and Disability/ Disability status, 3) Education, Skills and Employment, 4) Women and Child Welfare, 5) Banking and Insurance, 6) Migration, 7) Occupational status of households, 8) Ambition and 9) Other related issues. The following points are expected to be achieved by collecting this information.
(a) To check the eligibility of the beneficiaries and their families in terms of 08 Central Government Schemes (Appendix-I).
(b) To prepare baseline data of all the beneficiaries of PM Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi.

Survey and Data Collection: Street Vendors are expected to fill up the data of this survey on the website created by the Central Government after conducting the survey in person. After checking the eligibility under various schemes on this website, the information should be filled. This survey and data collection is expected to be done by the local bodies. The information related to each city of the beneficiaries of PM SVANidhi has been sent on the website created by the Central Government. The cost of the survey will be provided to the local bodies by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, Government of India.
The concerned officers/ staff should conduct a survey of street vendors by setting up camps at the local level. The local body will be responsible for conducting a survey and collecting data of street vendors in the city. The information collected from this survey will also be provided to the concerned departments for the implementation of other schemes.
Promotion and dissemination of the scheme: Promotion of the scheme by engaging various stakeholders in an innovative manner through local social media platforms is being encouraged. Also, the necessary IEC material will be provided by the central government.
Control Mechanism: The concerned departments are expected to appoint nodal officers at the State level and at the city level for the matters mentioned in Annexure-I to control the implementation in the cities covered under the above mentioned Phases 1, 2 and 3. The nodal officers at the department/ city level will coordinate with the district administration/ municipal corporation and take action for inclusion of eligible persons in various schemes. Also, the Central Government has given instructions to form State Level and District Level Controlling Committees under the scheme.
