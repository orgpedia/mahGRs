Letter from the Commissioner, Sangli Miraj Kupwad City Municipal Corporation vide No. Amrit/ Tansha/ Papuvimi/ 337/2019 dated 28/02/2019.
Preamble: The Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission is being implemented in the State since 2015-16. Under this campaign, infrastructure facilities like water supply, sewerage, storm water drains, urban transport and greenfield development will be created in the cities. The detailed project report of Miraj Water Supply Project, which is included in the plan for the financial year 2016-17 under AMRUT Mission, was prepared by Sangli Miraj Kupwad City Municipal Corporation. As per the instructions of the Central Government, the project report has been approved by the State Level Technical Committee constituted under AMRUT Mission and subsequently by the State Level High Powered Committee.
The Government has accorded administrative approval to Miraj Water Supply Project on 13/12/2016 as recommended by the State Level High Powered Committee.
General Assembly Resolution No. 2 regarding the implementation of the Miraj Water Supply Project. 101, dated 24/12/2018, has been passed. While passing the resolution, some of the decisions taken in it are inconsistent with the provisions prescribed by the Central Government and the Government of Maharashtra.
Therefore, as the Government is of the opinion that the said resolution is against the authority of the Government and against the larger public interest, the General Assembly passed Resolution No. 101 dated 24/12/2018, the matter of suspension was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
In view of the fact that some of the decisions in the said resolution passed by the General Assembly of Sangli Miraj Kupwad City Municipal Corporation are against or beyond the scope of the said power and in the larger public interest for the implementation of the said scheme within the stipulated period, some of the decisions are being suspended in the first instance under the provisions of Section 451 (1) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 as follows:

General Assembly Resolution No. 101, dated 24/12/2018, the following mentioned decisions: a) Decision No. 1 (in part) of this:" There is evidence of irregularities in the sanction of the scheme. Therefore, a special audit of the tender process and all the matters related to it should be done. " This is it.
(a) Decision No. 2 (in full):
" Standing Committee Resolution No. 342, p. 31/05/2018, the House is of the view that the said resolution is bogus and illegal. The report of the retired judge should be presented before the House before the next meeting after conducting an inquiry in this regard through a retired judge. 3 (in full):
" The report of the aforesaid special audit and the inquiry conducted by the retired judge should be presented in the next General Assembly. Till then, the meeting is ordering that the MACTDAR should not be allowed to make payments of the approved tender rates at rates higher than the budgeted rates.
As per Section 451 (2) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, the concerned can make a representation in this regard within one month from the date of the order. If the complaint is not received within the stipulated time, further action will be taken under Section 451 (3) of the Act.
