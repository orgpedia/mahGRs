As per the decision of the Government No. 1 in the context, administrative approval is being given to the Kalyan-Dombivli City Water Supply Project subject to the following conditions: 4.1 The implementing agency for the project will be the Kalyan-Dombivli Municipal Corporation.
The local body's share for the project should be paid by the Kalyan-Dombivli Municipal Corporation.
4.3 The implementing agency shall be bound to implement the said project as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the decision of the Government in the context of the State Government.
4.4 It will be mandatory for the concerned executing agency to complete the tender process and implementation of the project within the stipulated time as per the procedures prescribed by the State Government vide Circular No. AMRUT-2016/ PR No. 163/ NV-33, dated 16th May, 2016 and 12th July, 2016. Also, all types of pipes, including DI pipes, should be included in the tender for this project.
4.5 It shall be mandatory for the executing agency to conduct the tender of the said project as per the model tender form issued by Maharashtra Jeevan Authority for water supply projects under AMRUT Mission. For this, the draft tender form should be made available by the Maharashtra Life Authority to the Municipal Corporation immediately.
4.6 The implementing agency shall ensure that the work orders for the said project are issued within 45 days from the date of order of administrative sanction.
4.7 Under the Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission, it will be mandatory for the mission city to complete the reforms prescribed by the Central Government within the prescribed time frame.
4.8 Mode of disbursement:- As per the guidelines of Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission, the Central share of funds will be disbursed in three tranches (20%+ 40%+ 40%).

4.9 While setting up self-financing funds for projects sanctioned under AMRUT Mission, Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) will be required to converge with the 1% stamp duty and Goods and Services Tax (GST) subsidy received from the State Governments. For this, they should identify the funds received from them.
4.10 The first instalment of funds from the Central and State Governments will be disbursed only after the work order for the project is issued. Accordingly, after submission of Utilization Certificate to the Central Government as prescribed by the Central Government, the next instalment of Central share and State share will be disbursed accordingly.
4.11 As per the financial framework of the Mission, it will be mandatory for the concerned implementing agency to raise funds on the basis of the funds distributed by the Central and State Governments and submit the utilization certificate of the said expenditure to the Government in the form prescribed by the Central Government.
4.12 As per the instructions issued at the time of disbursement of funds for the said project, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks allocated in accordance with the prevailing policy of the Government. The funds allocated for the project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, it will be considered as serious financial irregularity.
4.13 After the project is approved for the Urban Local Bodies by the State Government, the entire responsibility of any increase in the original project cost for any reason will be of the concerned executing agency. No additional funds will be provided by the State Government for this purpose.
4.14 It shall be the responsibility of the concerned implementing agency to complete the project within the stipulated time.
4.15 Once the project work is started, the concerned executing agency will be responsible to make the payment of the work to the concerned contractor within 15 days within the limit of available funds after the project manager consultant certifies the quality of work and payments.
4.16 As per the guidelines of AMRUT Mission, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran will act as Project Manager Consultant for the project. Accordingly, the implementing agency will pay the fee of 3 per cent to the Maharashtra Life Authority as per the procedure prescribed by the government.
