Development of Metro Rail based public rapid transit system through implementing agencies
is being done. Metro Rail Project Implementing Agencies prepare Detailed Project Reports (DPRs) of metro rail projects and accordingly, the technical and financial aspects of the project are worked out.
The projects are approved by the government.
The Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) provides 50 per cent of the total cost of preparation of Detailed Project Report (DPR) of the metro rail projects planned under the Urban Transport Planning and Capacity Building Scheme (UTSBY) to the implementing agencies of the metro rail projects as grant-in-aid on certain terms and conditions. Similarly, the remaining 50 per cent of the project cost is to be borne by the State Government as" Subsidiary Grants" to the Project Implementing Agency.
A proposal to open a new head of account to disburse funds as grant-in-aid under the programme expenditure for the said purpose was under consideration of the Government. The government has taken the following decisions:
