Government Decision: To make all the cities of the country completely clean, healthy and livable and to provide clean and good health and clean environment to all the citizens.
The Government of India has launched the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban) with the objective of providing household toilet facilities to the citizens of the cities. On the lines of this campaign, the" Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban)" is being implemented in the state as per the decision of the Government No. 1 in the context.
2. Under Swachh Bharat Mission, the State Government has been implementing the scheme of Solid Waste Management. With the approval of the High Powered Committee headed by the Chief Secretary, proposals worth Rs. 75,97,93, 434/- (Rupees Seventy Five Crore Ninety Nine Lakh Ninety Three Thousand Four Hundred Thirty Four only) for 116 cities were submitted to the Central Government. The Central Government vide Reference No. 5 As per the order dated 29.09.2021, the Central Government's permissible share of Rs. 75,97,93, 434/- (Rupees Seventy Five Crores Ninety Nine Lakhs Ninety Three Thousand Four Hundred Thirty Four only) has been released to the State.
The Government of India has approved the release of the second installment of funds to the State under Swachh Bharat Mission for the management of Urban Solid Waste in 116 Urban Local Bodies amounting to Rs. 75,97,93, 434/- (Rupees Seventy Five Crores Ninety Nine Lakh Ninety Three Thousand Four Hundred Thirty Four only) and Rs. 51,01,02, 149/- (Rupees One Hundred Eighty Eight Lakh only) to the Urban Local Bodies of the State, aggregating to Rs. 126,98,95, 583/- (Rupees One Hundred Twenty Six Crores Ninety Eight Lakh Ninety Five Thousand Five Hundred and Thirty Three only). The expenditure shall be met from the provision made during the financial year 2021-22 under the respective head of accounts as shown in the table below.
