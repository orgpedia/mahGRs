The Proposal:
The Mumbai Urban Transport Project is a joint initiative of the Government of India and the Government of Maharashtra to improve transport and transportation in the Mumbai Metropolitan Region. This project includes the railway project as well as the rehabilitation of the people affected by this project. The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) has been appointed as the nodal agency for the Mumbai Urban Transport Project. 5,300 crore. The work under the Mumbai Urban Transport Project-IIA has been taken up with financial assistance from the World Bank. The World Bank has allocated Rs. 1,910 crore.
The Mumbai Railway Development Corporation (MRDC), the implementing agency for the project, pays 50% of the cost to the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) on behalf of the Government of Maharashtra. Expenditure reimbursement by Mumbai Railway Development Corporation
