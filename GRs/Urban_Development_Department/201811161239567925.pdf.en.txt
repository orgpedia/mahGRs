The Proposal:
The Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban) is an initiative of the Government of India to provide toilets in all cities of the country to ensure clean environment and good health to the citizens. " Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban)" is being implemented in the State as per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 1 on the lines of" Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban)" of the Central Government. Under this mission, the two major components include making cities" Open Defecation Free" and" Clean" under solid waste management.
The detailed project report of the urban solid waste management project of 32 cities in the state under the Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban) has been appraised by the Maharashtra Life Authority (MJA), giving technical approval to the projects. Accordingly, under the Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban), Rs. A meeting of the high-powered committee chaired by the Chief Secretary has approved urban solid waste management projects worth Rs. 174.95 crore in these 32 cities. This includes Balapur Municipal Council's Urban Solid Waste Management Project worth Rs. 2.8638 crore. Accordingly, the matter of granting administrative approval to the urban solid waste management project of Balapur Nagar Panchayat was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: The Government is granting administrative approval to the Urban Solid Waste Management Project under Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban) at a total cost of Rs. 2.8638 crore mentioned in the statement accompanying this Government Decision of Balapur Municipal Council subject to the terms and conditions mentioned in the following paragraph 4.

The process of distribution of funds:
3.1 As per the guidelines of the Centrally Sponsored Swachh Bharat Mission, the admissible share of funds of the Central and State Governments will be distributed in two tranches (50%+ 50%).
3.2 As per the instructions given at the time of disbursement of funds for the said project, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks allocated in accordance with the prevailing policy of the Government.
3.3 The funds allocated for the said project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, the matter will be considered as serious financial irregularity.
3.4 After the project is approved by the State Government for the Urban Local Bodies, any increase in the original project cost for any reason will be the sole responsibility of the concerned Urban Local Bodies/ Implementing Agencies. by the state government
No additional funds will be provided for this.
3.5 For Solid Waste Management projects sanctioned under Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban), in addition to the grants admissible by the Central and State Governments, the remaining funds (as per Para 5 of the above table) shall be required to be raised by the respective Urban Local Self-Government Institutions and spent in proportion to the Central and State accounts.
3.6 The Urban Local Self-Government Institutions shall set up a self-accounting fund in proportion to the funds distributed by the Central and State Governments as per the financial figures of the Swachh Maharashtra Mission.
It will be mandatory for the concerned urban local self-government institution to submit the utilization certificate of the combined expenditure in the form prescribed by the central government.

Implementation of the Detailed Project Report for the Solid Waste Management of Balapur City Council under the Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban).
The implementation mechanism will be bound to meet the target set as follows. :
100% segregation of solid waste generated in the city.
Transportation of 100% segregated waste in segregated manner.
Preparation of compost manure from wet waste or processing it through biomethanisation.
If the compost is being produced, the organic manure produced from wet waste should be tested as per FCO standards by a laboratory authorized by the State Government and it should get the brand" Harit Mahasiti Compost." Also to sell this organic manure in the name of" Green City Compost."
Secondary segregation of dry waste should be done at the material recovery facility. The recyclable waste should be recycled or sold if possible.
After all the above procedures, the remaining waste should be disposed of in the landfill.
80 to 90% of the land will need to be reclaimed after processing the old waste stored at the city dumping sites.
