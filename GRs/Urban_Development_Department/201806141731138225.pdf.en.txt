In accordance with the Government Resolution No. 1, the administrative approval for the Mira-Bhayander City Water Supply Project is being granted subject to the following conditions:
4.1 Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) will be the implementing agency for the project.
4.2 The share of the local body for the project should be paid by the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority.
4.3 The implementing agency shall be bound to implement the said project as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the decisions of the Government in the context of the State Government.

4.4 Mission cities and implementing agencies shall be bound to fulfil the conditions mentioned in the minutes of the State Level High Powered Committee meetings.
4.5 Under the Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission, it will be mandatory for the mission city to complete the reforms prescribed by the Central Government within the prescribed time frame.
4.6 Methodology of Fund Distribution: As per the guidelines of Centrally Sponsored AMRUT Mission, the Central share of funds will be distributed in three tranches (20%+ 40%+ 40%). Accordingly, the State share of funds will be shared with the Central share in proportion to the funds released by the Central Government.
4.7 The first instalment of funds from the Central and State Governments will be disbursed only after the work order for the project is issued. Accordingly, after submission of Utilization Certificate to the Central Government as prescribed by the Central Government, the next instalment of Central share and State share will be disbursed accordingly.
4.8 It shall be mandatory for the concerned implementing agency to raise funds on the basis of the funds distributed by the Central and State Governments as per the financial figures of the Mission and submit the utilization certificate of the said expenditure to the Government in the form prescribed by the Central Government.
4.9 According to the instructions given at the time of disbursement of funds for this project, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks allocated in accordance with the prevailing policy of the Government. The funds allocated for the project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, it will be considered as serious financial irregularity.
4.10 Any increase in the original project cost for any reason shall be the sole responsibility of the concerned executing agency. No additional funds will be provided by the State Government for this purpose.
4.11 The concerned implementing agency shall be fully responsible for completing the project within the stipulated time.
4.12 Once the project is commissioned, the concerned executing agency will be responsible for making payment of the work to the concerned contractor within 15 days within the limit of available funds after the project management consultant certifies the quality of work and payment.
4.13 The implementing agency shall be responsible for obtaining No Objection Certificates for the crossing of railway/ road and other aspects of the project. Also the executing agency should ensure that all the required space for this project is physically occupied.
4.14 In order to make the project sustainable, the Mira-Bhayander Municipal Corporation should take necessary action after obtaining the approval of the General Assembly to levy water supply tax as mentioned in the Detailed Project Report of the project.
