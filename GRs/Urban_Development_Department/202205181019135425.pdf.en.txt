The Proposal:
The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) is implementing the Mumbai Trans-Harbour Link (MTHL) project. It is 22 km long (16.5 km at sea). 5.5 m on the ground. The total cost of the project is estimated at Rs. 17,843 crore. The government had earmarked Rs. The government has given a guarantee of Rs 15,100 crore. The authority has also proposed the MTHL extension project from Chirle to Mumbai Pune Expressway in view of the traffic planning on the Mumbai Trans Harbour Link Road. Funds are needed to implement these projects in a time bound manner.
Therefore, considering the fund requirement of the Authority for implementation of MTHL Road and MTHL Extension (Chirle to Mumbai Pune Expressway) projects and the decision taken by the State Level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC) on March 10, 2022, to grant loan for the said projects, in terms of Section 21 of the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority Act, 1974, the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority has sanctioned a total amount of Rs. 2649 crore was under the consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
To implement the MTHL Road and MTHL Extension (Chirle to Mumbai Pune Expressway) projects, the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) has sanctioned a total of Rs. 2649 crore is being sanctioned under Section 21 of the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority Act, 1974 subject to the following conditions:

1) Loans from the Mumbai Megacity Scheme Revolving Fund will be mandatorily utilised only for MTHL Road and MTHL Extension (Chirle to Mumbai Pune Expressway) projects.
2) The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority should take up the loan as per the requirement.
(3) The Government shall not guarantee the repayment of the loan.
4) The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority will be obliged to comply with all the terms and conditions regarding the loan from the Mumbai Megacity Scheme Revolving Fund.
5) The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority should review the sources of income and take necessary action to increase the income and make repayment of the loan fund and the loan fund raised for the project within the prescribed time.
6) No property owned by the government or requiring government approval can be mortgaged in connection with this loan.
