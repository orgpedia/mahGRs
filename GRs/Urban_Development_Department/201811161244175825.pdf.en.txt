The process of distribution of funds:
3.1 As per the guidelines of the Centrally Sponsored Swachh Bharat Mission, the admissible share of funds of the Central and State Governments will be distributed in two tranches (50%+ 50%).
3.2 As per the instructions given at the time of disbursement of funds for the said project, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks allocated in accordance with the prevailing policy of the Government.
3.3 The funds allocated for the said project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, the matter will be considered as serious financial irregularity.

4. terms and conditions:
4.1 The Kamthi Municipal Council will be the implementing agency for the project.
4.2 It shall be mandatory for the implementing agency to implement the project within the prescribed time frame as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the Government Resolution No. 1 under the State Government and Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016.
4.3 Detailed Project Reports of Solid Waste Management Projects have been prepared as per the guidelines prescribed under the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016. The success of this project report is based on on-site segregation of solid waste. Therefore, it will be necessary to segregate 100% of the solid waste generated in the city at the construction site and collect it separately.
Accordingly, it will be mandatory for the Kamathi Municipal Council to collect at least 90% of the solid waste generated daily in the city by December 2018.
4.4 It will be mandatory to start the process on this segregated wet waste in a concentrated or decentralized manner.
4.5 The Implementing Agency shall be responsible for implementing the detailed project report on the transportation of segregated waste and the scientific procedures to be followed.

4.8 The Detailed Project Report has been prepared keeping in view the requirements for the next 10 years. While starting the implementation of this project report, the necessary purchases and constructions should be done taking into account the current needs.

4.10 The local body concerned shall develop a mechanism for meeting the maintenance and repair cost of the urban solid waste management project and shall bear the maintenance and repair cost of the project.
While implementing the Detailed Project Report (DPR) being administratively approved for solid waste management of Kamathinagar Parishad under Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban), it will be mandatory for the implementing agency to meet the target set as follows: 100% segregation of solid waste generated in the city at source.
Transportation of 100% segregated waste in segregated manner.
Preparation of compost manure from wet waste or processing it through biomethanisation.
If the compost is being produced, the organic manure produced from wet waste should be tested as per FCO standards by a laboratory authorized by the State Government and it should get the brand" Harit Mahasiti Compost." Also to sell this organic manure in the name of" Green City Compost."
Secondary segregation of dry waste should be done at the material recovery facility. The recyclable waste should be recycled or sold if possible.
After all the above procedures, the remaining waste should be disposed of in the landfill.
80 to 90% of the land will need to be reclaimed after processing the old waste stored at the city dumping sites.
