Government's decision:
The sewerage project of Gadchiroli Municipal Council, which was presented under the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urbanization Campaign, has been given the status of No. The administrative approval was granted in accordance with the government decisions dated March 30, 2017 and May 26, 2017. The government is taking decisions.
The Chief Officer, Gadchiroli Municipal Council, in regard to the revised approval of the said project, issued Reference No. 4 The request was made to the government. The revised estimate has been approved by the Chief Engineer, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, Nagpur. The decision in this regard was taken at the meeting of the State Level Technical Committee held on December 21, 2018.
Accordingly, with the approval of the Government, the project is being accorded revised administrative approval through this Government Resolution as mentioned in the following statement. The details of the project and its cost are as follows:

As per the decision of the Government in Reference No. 1, revised administrative approval is being given to the sewerage project (Phase-I) of Gadchiroli Municipal Council subject to the following conditions:
(a) Implementation of the project:
The project should be implemented by Gadchiroli Municipal Council.
It will be mandatory for the project to appoint the Maharashtra Life Authority as the Project Management Consultant (PMC). A decision in this regard will be taken by the municipal council.
The Project Management Consultancy Fee (PMC Charges) equivalent to 3% of the project cost should be provided by the Municipal Council to the Maharashtra Life Authority from its own funds. (As prescribed under Government Resolution dated 4th June, 2018 and subject to the provisions of that Government Resolution) 4. The implementing agency shall be bound to fulfil all the conditions prescribed by the State Level Project Approval Committee while approving the project.
(b) Fulfillment of Reforms:- In the context of Maharashtra Suvarna Jayanti Nagarothana Mahabhivana No. As per the Government Resolution No. 1, the Urban Local Bodies will be required to complete the following binding and optional reforms. Of these, 80% of the optional conditions must be met by the second year of the project.
(i) Compulsory Reforms: It will be mandatory for the Municipal Councils to fully computerize their activities in the first year of project approval. This will include 100% implementation of e-governance, accounting, and birth and death registration reforms.
2.To collect at least 80% of the tax by applying appropriate consumer tax.
3. Provision of certain funds in the budget for the poor in urban areas.
The concerned local civic body will be required to complete the two-letter registration process within six months.

(a) The method of distribution of funds:
1. Reference No. The first installment of funds for the project has already been disbursed to the municipal council after the previous administrative approval.
After disbursing the first installment of funds to the projects approved under the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urban Mission, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks prescribed by the government as per the prevailing policy of the government. Accordingly, the municipal council should ensure that the said funds are kept in a separate bank account. The chief officer, Gadchiroli Municipal Council will be responsible for this.
The second installment of the State Government will be disbursed only after the utilization certificate of 70% of the total amount of the first installment of subsidy disbursed by the State Government and the first installment of self-assurance deposited by the Urban Local Bodies is submitted to the Government through the Directorate of Municipal Administration.

The urban local bodies should submit a proposal to the government through the Directorate of Municipal Administration to demand the second installment. The Directorate of Municipal Administration should scrutinize the proposals and submit them to the government with appropriate feedback.
After the State Government has sanctioned the share of the State Government to the concerned local body for the project approved under this mission, the local body concerned shall provide the amount of self-assurance to the local body at each stage of expenditure in accordance with the amount to be provided from the State Government's account in each payment. With this, there will be no time to pay the share of the local body in such a scheme at once.
The funds sanctioned by the State Government for the projects sanctioned under the Maharashtra Suvarna Jayanti Nagrothan Mahabhiyana can be used only for that project and cannot be diverted or used for any other work. Any such diversion of the principal amount or interest thereon, whether permanent or temporary, will be treated as a serious financial irregularity for which action will be taken by the concerned local bodies/ concerned authorities.
(d) Tendering Process, Work Order and Implementation: 1. It shall be mandatory to float tenders within 15 days of administrative approval of the project sanctioned under this Mission and to issue work orders within a period of three months and to commence work physically before the 91st day.
The implementing agency shall be bound to follow the instructions contained in the Government Resolution No. 3 in the context of the tender process of the project.
In case of failure to take action, the concerned Urban Local Body shall be required to return the funds sanctioned for such project to the Government along with interest thereon.
(4) It shall be mandatory to follow the procedure prescribed by the State Government while completing the sewerage project.
5. It shall be binding on the concerned implementing agency to complete the project within the time frame prescribed for the project.
After the work order of the project, the purchase of pipes, etc. included in the project should be done under the guidance of the project management consultant according to the requirements of the project.
7. Procurement of any kind of pipes for the project which are not required at the beginning and providing for the same from the project fund will be considered as serious financial irregularities and the concerned executing agency and project management consultancy will be held responsible.

e) Third Party Technical Audit of Projects: Under the Maharashtra Suvarna Jayanti Nagarotna Abhiyan, it will be mandatory for the concerned Urban Local Bodies to conduct" Third Party Technical Audit" of the approved projects at various stages of implementation by one of the mechanisms set up by the Government from time to time.
