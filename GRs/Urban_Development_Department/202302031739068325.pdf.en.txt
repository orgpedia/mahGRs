Government Decision: NACOF India Ltd. In view of the various initiatives taken by the Institute in various States across the country, the Institute is being permitted to set up generic drug stores on the pattern of" AMRUT Stores" in the premises of hospitals run by the local civic bodies under the Urban Development Department, subject to the following conditions.
(a) Administrative Terms and Conditions: (1) As per the provisions of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 and the Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, the concerned Municipal Corporation shall establish NACOF India Ltd. in the premises of the hospital owned by it. While allotting land to this institution, the concerned Municipal Commissioner shall constitute a committee to decide on the determination of the site, area of the site, lease of the site, duration of lease etc. and the committee shall take action regarding the distribution of the site as per the provisions of the said Act.
The terms and conditions in this regard may be decided by the concerned Municipal Corporation.
As per the provisions of the Maharashtra Municipal Councils, Nagar Panchayats and Industrial Townships Act, 1965 and the Maharashtra Municipalities (Transfer of Immovable Property) Rules, 1983, there is a provision for leasing out of land for public purposes. NACOF India Ltd., in the premises of their respective owners by the concerned Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat. While allotting land to the institution, the concerned Principal shall constitute a committee to decide on the determination of the site, area of the site, lease of the site, duration of lease etc. and the said committee shall take action regarding the distribution of the site in accordance with the provisions of the said Act. The terms and conditions in this regard may be decided by the Municipality/ Town Panchayat.
3) The local civic body concerned should sign an agreement with the concerned body as per the rules.
4) In case of non-availability of Jan Aushadhi, it will be mandatory to provide a discount of 5% on the face value of the branded medicine to the patients at a time when other branded or generic medicines have to be made available by the concerned institution.
(b) Technical Conditions: (i) A Committee shall be constituted at the concerned Hospital level under the Medical Superintendent consisting of the Head of Pharmacy, Head of Pharmacy, Administrative Officer and representative of the Department of Food and Drug Administration. The committee will review the functioning of the medicine shop run by the concerned organization every 6 months and will send a report to the government through the Commissioner and Director, Directorate of Municipal Administration, Navi Mumbai.
2) The place where the drug business is proposed to be carried out as per the rules of the Food and Drug Administration Department.
A license for the same place must be obtained.
Since these pharmacies are meant to be open for 24 hours, it will be mandatory by law to appoint at least three registered drug manufacturers for the purchase and sale of medicines in the pharmacy. According to the government policy, priority should be given to disabled persons and women.
4) It will be mandatory for the drug manufacturer appointed by the institute to be registered with the Pharmacy Council of the state and update the registration from time to time.

5) It will be mandatory to have enough coolers to store medicines and other items at a certain temperature as per the rules of the Food and Drug Administration Department and to fulfill other requirements as per Schedule-P.
6) The stocks of narcotics and psychotropic substances shall be sealed separately and their documents shall be strictly adhered to as per the rules of the Department of Food and Drug Administration.
7) According to the rules of the Food and Drug Administration Department, there should be adequate space and air-conditioned arrangement for storing medicines.
8) Medicines that are on government contract but need to be purchased from outside, including medicines that are not supplied on contract, will also be made available from this shop.
9) It will be mandatory to follow the price as per the rules of the National Pharmaceutical Pricing Authority while selling medicines.
10) Every medicine that comes from outside the pharmacy should be kept in a register.
The records and physical stocks in such registers shall be periodically verified by the Technical Committee.
The technical committee appointed will conduct a half-yearly inspection of all the above technical aspects and strict compliance of the rules and the report will be submitted to the government through the Commissioner and Director, Directorate of Municipal Administration, Navi Mumbai.
12) NACOF India Limited will be responsible for the procurement, sale, storage, necessary financial investment and setting up the necessary system for running the shop accordingly.
