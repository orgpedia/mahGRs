Introduction: The lockdown in the wake of COVID-19 has had a major impact on street vendors and street vendors in the cities of the state and to provide them with the playing capital to resume business, the Central Government announced the Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi on 1st June 2020. Detailed guidelines in this regard have been issued by the above-referenced Government Decision.
He suggested that the scheme should not be just a scheme to provide loans to street vendors, but should focus on their economic, social and all-inclusive development. The Prime Minister has given. With this approach, the Central Government has planned to survey the families of the beneficiaries of the Street Vendor Scheme, check their eligibility and provide them the benefits of other schemes of the Government under' SVANidhi Se Samriddhi'. SVANidhi Se Samriddhi Yojana will be a component of Prime Minister Street Vendor's Atmanirbhar Nidhi Yojana. Accordingly, the following guidelines were issued to implement the scheme. Accordingly, the following recommendations have been made for the implementation of the scheme.
Government Decision: The following guidelines are being issued for the implementation of the" Svanidhi Se Samriddhi" program in the state under the centrally sponsored Prime Minister Street Vendor Atmanirbhar Nidhi Yojana.
Objectives of the scheme:
To conduct a socio-economic survey of all the beneficiaries of PM-SVANidhi and check their and their family's eligibility for 8 Central Government Schemes (Annexure-I). Also, various socio-economic welfare schemes of the state can be included here wherever the state government deems fit.

Municipalities included in the first phase:
In the first phase, all the beneficiaries of PM Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi Yojana and their families will be included in the municipal corporation areas of four cities of the state- 1. Nagpur Municipal Corporation, 2. Nashik Municipal Corporation, 3.a Solapur Municipal Corporation, 4. Sangli-Miraj-Kupwad Municipal Corporation.
In regards to collecting information:
The survey to be conducted under" SVANidhi Se Samriddhi" will collect information as follows. Accommodation/ house and other assets, health and disability status, 3.
Education, Skills and Employment, Women and Child Welfare, Banking and Insurance, 6. Migration, 7.
Professional status of the family, 8. Ambition and 9. Other related questions.
The following points are expected to be achieved by collecting this information.
To check the eligibility of the beneficiaries and their families in terms of 8 Central Government Schemes (Appendix-I).
To prepare baseline data of all beneficiaries of PM Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi.

Data collection and analysis:
Street vendors are expected to fill the information of this survey on the website made by the Central Government by conducting a survey in person. After checking the eligibility under various schemes on this website, the information should be filled. This survey and data collection is expected to be done by the local bodies. The information related to each city of the beneficiaries of PM-SVANidhi has been sent on the website created by the Central Government. The expenditure for the survey will be provided to the local bodies by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, Government of India.
The concerned officers/ staff conducting the survey will be able to set up Pailin Camps for street vendors at the local level by following the Covid-19 rules. The local body will be responsible for conducting a survey and collecting data of street vendors in the city. The information collected from this survey will also be provided to the concerned departments for implementation of other schemes.
Promotion and Promotion of the Program:
Spread of the scheme is being encouraged through innovative ways on local social media, involving various stakeholders. Also, the necessary IEC material will be provided by the central government.
The control system:
The concerned departments are expected to appoint nodal officers at the state level and city level for the subjects mentioned in Annexure-I to monitor the implementation of the pilot in the 4 cities.
The nodal officers at the department/ city level will coordinate with the district administration/ municipal corporation and take action for inclusion of eligible persons in various schemes.
Also, the Central Government has given instructions to form State Level and District Level Controlling Committees under the scheme.
