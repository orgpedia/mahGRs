The process of distribution of funds:
3.1 As per the guidelines of the Centrally Sponsored Swachh Bharat Mission, the admissible share of funds of the Central and State Governments will be distributed in two tranches (50%+ 50%).
3.2 As per the instructions given at the time of disbursement of funds for the said project, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks allocated in accordance with the prevailing policy of the Government.
3.3 The funds allocated for the said project are bound to be used for the same project and if it is used for any other purpose, the matter will be considered as serious financial irregularity.

4. terms and conditions:
4.1 The Jaisinghpur Municipal Council will be the implementing agency for the project.
4.2 It shall be mandatory for the implementing agency to implement the project within the prescribed time frame as per the guidelines of the Central Government and the Government Resolution No. 1 under the State Government and Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016.
4.3 Detailed Project Reports of Solid Waste Management Projects have been prepared as per the guidelines prescribed under the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016. The success of this project report is based on the on-site segregation of solid waste
Therefore, it will be necessary to segregate 100% of the solid waste generated in the city at the construction site and collect it separately.
Accordingly, it will be mandatory for the Jaysingpur Municipal Council to collect at least 90% of the daily solid waste generated in the city by December 2018.
4.4 It will be mandatory to start the process on this segregated wet waste in a concentrated or decentralized manner.
4.5 The Implementing Agency shall be responsible for implementing the detailed project report on the transportation of segregated waste and the scientific procedures to be followed.
4.6 Bio-mining is an innovative process to scientifically process and dispose of the solid waste stored without processing in the cities of the state, so a committee was formed under the chairmanship of the Commissioner and Director, Municipal Administration under the Government Decision dated 27.10.2017 and dated 17.11.2017 to study the process and determine the procedure. As per the recommendations of this committee, bio-mining projects were allowed to be implemented in some selected cities on a pilot basis. Taking into account the experience gained in this pilot project, the Government is now permitting the Jaysingpur Municipal Council to carry out the biomining work in its Detailed Project Report subject to the following terms and conditions:
(1) The rates for biomining work are mentioned in the Detailed Project Report of Solid Waste Management of the respective cities. The rate has been revised
