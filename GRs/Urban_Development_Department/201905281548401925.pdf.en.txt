Government circular:
The report of the Comptroller and Auditor General of India points out that the final outcome and utility of the work being carried out through various schemes/ programmes of the Central Government and the State Governments are not being taken into account. Similarly, in the 14th Report of the Public Accounts Committee for 2015-16, Recommendation No. 2.28. It is recommended to take measures to obtain final utility/ achievement in respect of the work done under such scheme.
2.In this regard, the following actions are advised to be taken:
While preparing the project report of various infrastructure projects being carried out under various schemes/ programmes funded by the Central Government, State Governments and Urban Local Bodies, it should be ensured that the detailed project report is prepared in a comprehensive manner by incorporating all the elements related to the project.
Also, planning and measures should be taken to ensure that the investment made by the government in the scheme project after its completion does not remain unutilized or the investment made in it is used to its full potential.
(iii) Adequate measures should be taken to ensure full utilization of the treatment capacity created after completion of any sewerage/ water supply project.
(4) All necessary measures shall be taken to ensure that the assets created through such schemes/ programmes are not left unutilized or underutilized.
In short, all necessary measures should be taken to achieve the desired results from the work to be accomplished through such a scheme.
