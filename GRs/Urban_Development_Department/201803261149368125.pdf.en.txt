(b) The implementation of reforms:
In the context of the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Nagarothana Mahabhiyana No. As per the Government Resolution No. 1, the Urban Local Bodies will be required to complete the following binding and optional reforms. Of these, 80% of the optional conditions must be met by the second year of the project.
(i) Compulsory Reforms: It will be mandatory for the Municipal Councils to fully computerize their activities in the first year of project approval. This will include 100% implementation of e-governance, accounting, and birth and death registration reforms.
To collect at least 80% of the tax by applying appropriate consumer tax.
• Provision of certain funds in the budget for the poor in urban areas.
The respective local civic body will be required to complete the two-line registration process within six months.
If the property tax in their area has not been reassessed by the concerned urban local body, it will be mandatory to complete it within a period of one year from the project approval.
It shall be mandatory for the concerned Urban Local Bodies to ensure proper management of solid waste in their jurisdiction as per the provisions of the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016.
It will be mandatory for the concerned Urban Local Bodies to collect at least 80% of the property tax and water charges in their jurisdiction in the first year. In the following year, the balance 90% will be required to be recovered.
(ii) Alternate Improvement: Urban Local Bodies undertaking sewerage projects should include in their Detailed Project Report the components of re-treatment and re-use of sewage in urban areas.
The concerned urban local bodies should store rainwater on the building owned by them.
Regular water audits should be carried out by the respective urban local bodies. In respect of rectification of defects mentioned in the Water Records Inspection Report, the concerned Urban Local Body shall be required to complete the defects within the prescribed time frame by formulating necessary action programmes in this regard.

(a) The method of distribution of funds:
After the administrative approval for the projects in the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urban Mission, the concerned civic body should submit the financial closure report of the project to the Urban Development Department. After that, the first installment of the project will be released.
After disbursing the first installment of funds to the projects approved under the Maharashtra Golden Jubilee Urban Mission, it will be mandatory to open a separate account in the banks prescribed by the government as per the prevailing policy.
The second installment of the State Government will be disbursed only after the utilization certificate of 70 percent of the total amount of the first installment of subsidy disbursed by the State Government and the first installment of self-assurance deposited by the urban local body is submitted to the Government through the Directorate of Municipal Administration.
• Urban local bodies should submit the proposal to the government through the Directorate of Municipal Administration while demanding the second installment. The Directorate of Municipal Administration should scrutinize the proposals and submit them to the government with appropriate feedback.
Tendering process, work orders and implementation: It will be mandatory to issue tenders within one month after the release of the first installment of funds to the projects approved under this mission and to issue work orders within three months and to start the work physically before the 101st day.
The implementing agency shall be bound to follow the instructions contained in the Government Resolution No. 3 in the context while implementing the tender process of the said project.
In case of non-action, the concerned urban local body will be required to return the funds sanctioned for such project to the government along with interest thereon.
• While completing this water supply project, the State Government under reference No. 4 It will be mandatory to follow the procedures prescribed by the government circular here.
• It will be mandatory for the concerned implementing agency to complete the project within the prescribed time frame.
• After the work order of the project, the purchase of the pipes, etc. included in the project should be done according to the requirements of the project, under the guidance of the project management system.

e) Third Party Technical Audit of Projects: It will be mandatory for the concerned Urban Local Bodies to conduct third party technical audit of the sanctioned projects under the Maharashtra Suvarna Jayanti Nagarotna Abhiyan from time to time at various stages of implementation. After that, the second installment will be released.
Under the Maharashtra Suvarnajayanti Nagarotna Abhiyan, the concerned urban local bodies will be allowed to borrow from nationalised banks, HUDCO, Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA), Maharashtra Urban Infrastructure Development Company Limited (MUIDCL) and government approved financial institutions to raise their own funds for the sanctioned projects.
However, in case of borrowings from financial institutions, the permission of the government will be taken for borrowing subject to the prevailing rules after fulfilling the necessary requirements in this regard. Necessary action will be taken as per the government rules.
