The Proposal:
Mumbai Metro-4 (Wadala-Ghatkopar-Mulund-Thane) is being implemented by the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) in the Mumbai Metropolitan Region.
The loan has been taken from KfW, Germany for the projects (including multi-modal integration) of Kasarwadavli and Mumbai Metro Line 4A (Kasarwadavli to Gaimukh).
The external debt will be provided by the central government in two forms- ODA Plus Loan and Reduced Interest Loan. For this purpose, Loan Agreements between Government of India and KfW Bank and Project Agreements between Government of Maharashtra, MMRDA and KfW Bank have been signed in November, 2020.
The amount of the debt to be deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the State by the Central Government
The proposal to open a new accounting head for the disbursement of MMRDA through the budget and the credit of the loan issue and interest thereon from MMRDA to the Consolidated Fund of the State was under consideration of the Government. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
Mumbai Metro Line-4 (Wadala-Ghatkopar-Mulund-Thane-Kasarvadavli) and Mumbai Metro Line-4A (Kasarvadavli-Kasarvadavli) are being implemented by the Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA).
To disburse the ODA Plus Loan and Reduced Interest Loan to MMRDA from KfW, Germany through Central Government for these projects (including multi-modal integration).
The Government hereby approves opening of new head of accounts for the purpose of recovery of the loan amount and interest thereon from MMRDA as under:
