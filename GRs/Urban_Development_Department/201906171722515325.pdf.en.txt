Government circular:
In order to achieve the goal of providing clean environment and good health to all the citizens of the country, the Government of India is implementing the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban) throughout the country from 2nd October, 2014 till the 150th birth anniversary of the Father of the Nation, Mahatma Gandhi i. e. 2nd October, 2019. In line with the Central Government's" Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Urban)," the implementation of" Swachh Maharashtra Abhiyan (Urban)" has started in the State as per the Government Resolution dated 15th May, 2015 at Reference No.
2. Swachh Bharat Mission includes two major components of making cities" Open Defecation Free" (ODF) by providing individual or community toilets to ASHAs and" Clean" cities by managing solid waste in cities under Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016.
3. The urban areas of the State have been declared Open Defecation Free (ODF) on 1st October, 2017 by implementing the Swachh Bharat Mission and with a view to ensure continuity in the Open Defecation Free (ODF) status of these cities and to provide better toilet facilities to the urban households, all the cities of the State have now been given the target of achieving at least ODF+ status by 2nd October, 2019 as per the Government decision dated 24.5.2019 in Reference No.
It is the responsibility of all urban local bodies to fulfil the targets set by the State Government. The Government is issuing guidelines to enable the Urban Local Bodies to achieve this objective:

(1) Every city shall complete the construction of all 100% individual toilets sanctioned on the IHHL portal of the Central Government and upload the complete photos of those individual toilets on the IHHL portal. Accordingly, each city of the state should take action and prepare an action plan to complete the gap between the approved and completed toilets by June 30, 2019 and implement it according to this action plan.
The revised guidelines for ODF, ODF+ and ODF++ cities have been issued vide letter No. 15/54/2017/ SBM-1 dated 20th May, 2019 of the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, Government of India. (A copy of these guidelines is attached as Annexure-1 for easy reference). In accordance with the guidelines
(a) Necessary action shall be taken by all the Urban Local Bodies with a view to have all the public/ community toilets in the city as per the Mandatory Directions.
(b) To make the city ODF+ or ODF++, 10% or 25% of the total number of public/ community toilets available in the respective city, respectively, must be the best. For this, cities should preferably choose toilets on pay-and-use basis or for which a care taker has been appointed for public/ community toilets. In cities where there are no such toilets, the best public toilets in the city should be chosen. The best toilets selected in this way should provide the points mentioned under the essential, desirable and aspirational indicators as per the guidelines of the Central Government.
(a) In such a manner, all the public/ community toilets in the city shall be provided with the facilities as per the guidelines of the Central Government, after which it shall be ensured that all these public/ community toilets are in good condition and the responsibility of regular inspection of these toilets shall be entrusted to one or more staff as per the number of public/ community toilets in the city. These employees should visit all the public/ community toilets assigned to them in the city and inspect these toilets and submit their inspection report in the attached Annexure-2 to 5 to the concerned Commissioner, Municipal Corporation as well as the Principal, Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat. The report points out some flaws in the toilet.

If there are any errors, they should be corrected immediately. Thereafter, the concerned should be instructed to ensure that all these public/ community toilets have the required facilities as per the guidelines of ODF+/++ of the Central Government.
Thereafter, all the public/ community toilets in the Municipal Corporation area and Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat area shall be inspected by the concerned Deputy Commissioner of the concerned Municipal Corporation and the concerned Chief Officer in the Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat area and the certificate should be sent to the concerned Divisional Regional Deputy Director, Municipal Council Administration Branch and State Mission Directorate by 30th July, 2019 after ensuring that the necessary facilities are provided in all these community/ public toilets as per the guidelines of ODF+/++ of the Central Government.
(e) On receipt of the certificate from the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation as well as the Chief Officer, Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat, the Deputy Director/ Assistant Director of the concerned department, Divisional Technical Expert should visit the respective cities and inspect and submit a report to the State Mission Directorate.
(e) All cities in the State shall take action as above to ensure that their city is ready for ODF+ inspection by 30th July, 2019.
(3) For making the city ODF+ or ODF++, if the condition of the public/ community toilets in the city is not good and these public/ community toilets need to be repaired, the repair of such public/ community toilets and the expenditure for the same is being approved as a special case from the 50% funds earmarked for Swachh Bharat Mission in the 14th Finance Commission.
The Commissioner, Municipal Corporation as well as the Chief Officer, Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat will be responsible for obtaining the minimum ODF+ status for the concerned city. Such adverse remarks shall be recorded in the Annual Confidential Report of the Commissioner, Municipal Corporation as well as the Principal, Municipal Council/ Nagar Panchayat of the cities which have not achieved the minimum ODF+ status by 2nd October, 2019.
