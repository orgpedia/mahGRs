The state government is working on the decision. Under this campaign, the urban areas of the state were declared open defecation free in October 2017. Now cities in the state have started moving towards ODF+, ODF++, Faecal Sludge Treatment Plant (FSTP). The focus is on faecal sludge and septage management (FSSM).
The Bill& Melinda Gates Foundation (BMGF) has signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) on 23/7/2019 for the implementation of Faecal Sludge Treatment Plant (FSTP) programme. As per the provisions of the MoU, the Review and Control Committee Reference No. 2 was constituted for effective implementation of Swachh Maharashtra Mission. The government was contemplating to change the committee. Therefore, under this Government decision, the Government decision at Reference No. 2 is being revoked and a new Project Advisory Committee (PAC) is being constituted under the Chairmanship of Principal Secretary (Navi-2) for effective implementation of Faecal Sludge Treatment Plant (FSTP).
Government's decision
As mentioned in the Preamble, the composition of the Project Advisory Committee (PAC), as headed by the Principal Secretary (Navi-II), shall be as follows:
Principal Secretary (Urban Development Department, Ministry of Urban Development) Chairman (Navi-II) Commissioner and Director, Directorate of Municipal Administration Member (3) Representative Member of The Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation (BMGF) Member (4) Representative Member of C-WAS, CEPT University Member (5) Representative Member of NEERI Member (6) Deputy Secretary (Navi-34), Urban Development Department, Ministry, Mumbai Member (7) State Mission Director, State Mission Directorate, Mumbai Member Secretary

The main functions and responsibilities of the Committee shall be: 1. To review the implementation of the said programme and to provide necessary technical and managerial guidance for achieving the targets.
2. Review the obstacles and difficulties in the implementation of the said programme and suggest measures thereon.
3. Make appropriate recommendations to the Urban Development Department for successful implementation of the programme.
The Committee shall have the power to examine any records and summon the concerned if any lapses are found in the implementation of the Mission.
5. Suggest necessary improvements by making field visits as per agreed schedule.
The Principal Secretary (Navi-II) and the Chairman shall have the power to invite experts to the Committee as required.
Procedure of the Committee: i. The Committee shall meet once in every three months to review the implementation of the Mission.
ii.The meeting of the said Committee shall be convened by the State Mission Director, State Mission Directorate as Member Secretary.
(iii) The Principal Secretary (Urban Development Department) as the Chairman shall have the power to make any amendments in the said Committee.
