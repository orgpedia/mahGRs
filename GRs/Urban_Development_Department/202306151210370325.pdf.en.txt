The Proposal:
The lockdown in the wake of COVID-19 has had a major impact on the street vendors and street vendors in the cities of the state and to provide them with the capital to resume business, the Central Government announced the Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi on 1st June 2020. Detailed guidelines in this regard have been issued by the above-referenced Government Decision.
He said that the scheme should not be seen as a loan scheme only for street vendors, but should focus on their all-inclusive economic and social development. The Prime Minister has.
With this approach, the Central Government has planned to survey the families of the beneficiaries of the Street Vendor Scheme, check their eligibility and provide them the benefits of other schemes of the government under' Svanidhi se Samriddhi'. SVANidhi Se Samriddhi Yojana will be a part of Prime Minister Street Vendor's AtmaNirbhar Nidhi Yojana. Accordingly, the following guidelines have been issued by the Government for the implementation of the scheme. Accordingly, the government has taken the following steps:

Government Decision: In order to implement the" Svanidhi Se Samriddhi" program under the centrally sponsored Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi Yojana in the state, the government is taking a decision to include all the beneficiaries of the Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor AtmaNirbhar Nidhi Yojana and their families in 31 municipal councils/ nagar panchayats in the fourth phase. The guidelines for this is as follows.
The objective of the scheme is to conduct a socio-economic survey of all the beneficiaries of PM-SVANidhi and check their and their family's eligibility for 8 central government schemes. Also, various socio-economic welfare schemes of the state can be included here, wherever the state government deems fit.
As per the guidelines issued by the Central Government, the following 8 schemes have been fixed for street vendors to convergence. Necessary action should be taken at the local level.
