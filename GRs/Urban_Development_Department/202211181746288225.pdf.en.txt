The Proposal:
Since Sindhi Market is located at Rajwada Chowk in Sangli city and the contract of the shops/ stalls in the said market has expired on 31/03/2012, the 51 shop/ stall holders of the market have requested the Municipal Corporation on 04/07/2014 to get the said shops/ stalls on contract for 29 years. Accordingly, after fixing the rent by the Town Planning Department, the Municipal Administration presented a proposal before the General Assembly to pay the arrears and service tax at the end of 2014-15 by increasing the rent of the shops/ stalls in Sindhi Market by 20% every 3 years from 2008, to extend the period of 29 years from 01/04/2012, to charge the revised annual rent from 01/04/2014 according to the market rate of 2014 and to increase the rent by 20% every 3 years.
On the said proposal of the administration of the Municipal Corporation, the General Assembly of the Municipal Corporation passed a resolution to demolish the Sindhi Market Shop No. 746/ 1, owned by the Municipal Corporation. The annual rent for the students of classes 1 to 51 is Rs. 5000/- each for a period of 29 years with effect from 01/04/2012 by increasing the fare by 20% every 3 years and also by paying arrears and service tax for the year 2014-15. 132 AN, dated 17/01/2015 passed.
The Commissioner, Sangli Miraj Kupwad City Municipal Corporation has requested the Government to dissolve the said resolution passed by the General Assembly of the Municipal Corporation as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act as it has been passed without considering the financial interest of the Municipal Corporation. On the proposal of the Committee, Resolution No. 132 A.D. 17/01/2015 was under the consideration of the Government to suspend the resolution in the first place to dissolve it as it appeared to be against the financial interest of the Municipal Corporation.

Government Resolution No: MUP-2019/ P.S.No.149/ NV-19
Government's decision:
The General Assembly of the Sangli Miraj and Kupwad City Municipal Corporations passed the General Assembly Resolution No. 132 AN, dated 17/01/2015 is being suspended prima facie under the provision of Section 451 (1) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 as it is against the financial interest of the Municipal Corporation.
As per Section 451 (2) of the Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act, the concerned can make a representation in this regard within one month from the date of the order. If the complaint is not received within the stipulated time, further action will be taken under Section 451 (3) of the Act.
