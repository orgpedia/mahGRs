The Proposal: -
Subsidized grants are distributed to these municipal councils as a result of the abolition of excise duty and dearness allowance of the municipal councils in the state. For this purpose, ₹580 crore of the ₹1 crore budgeted for the financial year 2025-2026 has been made available on the Budget Estimates, Allocations and Control System.
As per the above, the grants received are to be disbursed to the Directorate of Municipal Administration through the BEAMS system.
Government Decision: - Subsidized grants are given to these municipal councils as a result of the abolition of excise duty and dearness allowance of the municipal councils in the state. For this purpose, out of the budgeted funds for the year 2025-2026, a grant-in-aid of ₹580 crore received from the Finance Department through the distribution system is being disbursed to the Directorate of Municipal Council Administration for distribution to the respective Municipal Councils / Nagar Panchayats / Municipal Corporations.
2. Subsidised grants received through Beams system for Municipal Councils. According to the government decision of the 16.06.2017, the Directorate of Municipal Administration should directly deposit it in the back account of the Municipal Councils / Nagar Panchayats.
3. The Deputy Commissioner, Directorate of Municipal Administration is being declared as the Controller Officer B. Assistant Commissioner (Accounts), Directorate of Municipal Administration as the Drawing and Disbursing Officer for direct deposit of the Auxiliary Grants to Municipal Councils into the back accounts of Municipal Councils / Nagar Panchayats by the Directorate of Municipal Administration.
4. Circular of the Government, Department of Finance, Govt. of India while disbursing the said funds. The funds should be disbursed to the respective Municipal Councils only after the conditions in the 02.04.2018 are met and 75% utilization certificate is submitted.
