Preamble: - The proposal was submitted to the High-Level Committee of Secretaries on 13th July, 2023 (4th meeting) after reviewing 227 posts in the Urban Development Department (itself). Out of these 227 posts, the High-Level Committee of Secretaries has approved the revised structure for a total of 236 posts with a total of 209 regular posts and 27 external manpower posts with 2 posts being abolished / cancelled and 11 posts being created afresh. The issue of issuing an approval order for this revised framework was under the consideration of the government.
Government Decision: - A total of 197 posts in the Department of Urban Development (itself) have been demarcated earlier by a Government Decision dated 10th November, 2003 after a review of the posts in the Department. Since then, the establishment of the Urban Development Department has seen an increase in the number of posts due to the transfer of some posts from the newly created / other departments, while the number of available posts has decreased due to the abolition of some posts. The details in this regard are given in the accompanying Statement-1.

The High-Level Committee of Secretaries (HLC) has declared 27 posts of Group-C (Drivers) cadre, 3 B Group-D (Multi-Purpose Personnel) cadre, among the regular posts at the Urban Development Department (itself) establishment as dead. According to the revised structure, the existing vacancies in the driver and group-D cadre should be filled through external mechanisms. Care should be taken to follow the guidelines in the Government Decision dated 27th April, 2022 of the Department of Finance while availing services through external agencies.
The posts decided by the deceased cadre will be automatically cancelled as soon as these posts become vacant on account of promotion / retirement / resignation / death etc. of the employees working in that cadre. Thereafter, no fresh recruitment can be made in that cadre. However, a separate table of posts has been shown so that the existing employees of this dying cadre are not inconvenienced with their salaries.
