The Proposal:
The Regional Rural Banks have been established under the Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976 for the economic development of rural areas. The objective of setting up Regional Rural Banks is to provide credit to the rural economy for development of rural areas with the objective of eliminating unemployment, poverty and socio-economic backwardness in rural areas. Currently, Maharashtra Gramin Bank and Vidarbha Konkan Gramin Bank are the two regional rural banks (RRBs) in the state of Maharashtra, in which the central government holds 50 per cent stake, sponsored banks 35 per cent and the state government 15 per cent. NABARD has recommended to provide additional equity to the Regional Rural Bank to bring down its Capital to Asset Risk Ratio (CRAR) to a minimum of 9%. Similarly, Ma.
As per the decision of the Supreme Court in SLP (C)- 39288/2012 dated 25th April, 2018 and the letter dated 25th November, 2019 of the Ministry of Finance, Government of India, the pension to the employees of Regional Rural Banks has been implemented at par with the employees of Nationalized Banks.
Additional shareholding is required to meet the above requirements and the Central Government and the concerned Sponsored Banks have disbursed their shareholding to the Regional Rural Banks. Further, vide letter No. 3/9/2020-RRB, D.R.B. of Additional Secretary, Department of Financial Services, Ministry of Finance, Government of India, the following information has been received: February 21, 2020: The Maharashtra government has been instructed to disburse 15 per cent of its share. Against this backdrop, the Government was contemplating to disburse Recapitalization Assistance to the concerned Regional Rural Banks in the financial year 2021-22 from the funds available under 15% share of Government.
