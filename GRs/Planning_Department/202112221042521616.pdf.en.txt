The main objective is to bring the economically weaker sections into the mainstream by raising the" Human Development Index (HDI)" of 125 of these talukas. In order to implement the schemes related to (1) health, (2) education and (3) income generation in a more efficient manner under the" Human Development Programme," the Government was considering to enter into" Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)" with" Bharat Rural Livelihood Foundation (BRLF)" in some of the districts on a priority basis.
Bharat Rural Livelihoods Foundation (BRLF) is an autonomous organisation under the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India. The main objective of this independent society is to work with the central and state governments on the issues of rural development, livelihood and poverty. BRLF (Bharat Rural Livelihoods Foundation) is an independent social organisation working in several states.
Under the" Human Development Programme" being implemented by the Planning Department, various schemes related to (i) health, (ii) education and (iii) income generation are being implemented on a large scale in 125 backward blocks of 23 selected districts. Most of these taluks fall in the areas notified for the" Tribal Sub Plan."

The Government of India has agreed to enter into a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Bharat Gramin Aajeevika Foundation (BRLF), an independent society under the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India, to implement a pilot project in a total of 36 blocks of 5 districts in 3 financial years from 2022-23 to 2024-25 on a pilot basis. A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) will be signed between the Commissioner, Human Development, Aurangabad and the Chief Executive Officer, Bharat Rural Livelihood Foundation (BRLF) with the approval of the Government.
The Bharat Rural Livelihoods Foundation (BRLF), an independent society under the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India, in collaboration with various Central and State Government schemes currently being implemented by (i) Tribal Development Department and (ii) Rural Development Department, will be able to make district/ taluka specific plans for the 5 districts of Gadchiroli, Gondia, Nandurbar, Amravati and Yavatmal more effective. The entire expenditure on trained manpower at the district level will be borne by Bharat Rural Livelihood Foundation (BRLF) for providing manpower in 36 talukas of these 5 districts with the participation of Civil Society Organizations. There is no financial burden on the state government.
