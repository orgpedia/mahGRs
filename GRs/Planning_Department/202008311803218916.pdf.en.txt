Government's decision:
In a circular dated 16th April, 2020 issued vide Reference No.2 of the Department of Finance, considering the pandemic situation in the State due to Corona Virus, it has been approved to disburse funds within the limit of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary allocation of mandatory and programme expenditure for the first quarter of the financial year 2020-21. Accordingly, under the District Annual Plan (General) for the year 2020-2021, a total of Rs. 9800.00 crore has been disbursed to all the District Collectors as per the Government decision dated 28.04.2020, out of which 10% of the sanctioned size of all the districts i.e. 981.35 crore (Rupees Nine Hundred One Crore Thirty Five Lakh only) has been released. Further, instructions have been issued vide Circular dated 29th May, 2020 regarding the precautionary measures to be adopted by the District Collectors and Controller Officers while spending the funds released under District Annual Plan (General) 2020-2021 on Budget Distribution System as the financial condition of the State is likely to be critical due to the emergency situation arising out of COVID-19 virus.
The Department of Finance No. 170/2020/ Expenditure- 8/2020 dated 20.08.2020 has been approved for additional 15% scheme wise devolution of funds under District Annual Plan, 2020-2021.
Accordingly, under the District Annual Plan (General) 2020-2021, Demand No. O-14 to O 85 (Demand No. O-63 Nagpur District (excluding Nagpur District) 15% of the budgeted funds are being disbursed on" Budget Distribution System" (BDS) subject to further instructions. Necessary action will be taken in this regard soon.
