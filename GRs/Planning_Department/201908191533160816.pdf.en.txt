Government's decision:
The Government has decided to expand the scope of this programme by implementing the concept of" Human Development" at the Taluk level instead of the District level as per the Government decision dated 19/07/2011. To ensure effective implementation of the Human Development Programme and to ensure that the selected blocks receive adequate funds, the programme is being implemented from the financial year 2011-12 in over 125 blocks in 23 districts (including newly created Palghar) of the State.
2. Under the Human Development Programme, for the year 2019-2020, under the head" Demand No. O-1, 2053 District Administration, 093 District Establishment, Five Year Plan Undertaking, 04 Commissioner, Aurangabad (04) (01) Increasing Human Development Index (2053 1231) and (20531311) Dattamat," 31 Subsidiary Grants (non-salaried), the budgetary allocation for the year 2019-2020 is Rs. 33800.00 lakh and Rs. 400.00 lakh for the year 2019-20 on Budgetary System for the first nine months (ending December, 2019) subject to a ceiling of 60% of the total annual outlay of Rs. 20280.00 lakh and Rs. 240.00 lakhs has been received. Out of which, a total amount of Rs. 11266.63 Lakhs has been disbursed to the concerned districts as per the Government decision dated 24/06/2019 under the above mentioned scheme of raising Human Development Index (2053 1231).
Out of the remaining funds on the Budget Distribution System, the following 31 Subsidiary Grants (non-salaried) under Demand No. O-1, (2053 1231) Rs. 6013.37 lakhs (Rupees Sixty Crores Thirteen Lakhs Thirty Seven Thousand only) as well as the following Rs. 240.00 lakhs (Rupees Two Crores Forty Lakhs only) is being distributed to the respective District Collectors mentioned in the return' A' along with the funds. Similarly, for the remaining purposes except the fund under Demand No. O-1, (2053 1231), 31 Subsidiary Grants (Non-Salary), funds are being disbursed to the" Commissioner, Human Development, Aurangabad" as mentioned in the return" B."
