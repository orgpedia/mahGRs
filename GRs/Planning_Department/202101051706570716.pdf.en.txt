Government's decision:
Pursuant to the Maharashtra Appropriation Act, 2020 Fund Distribution (Tentative Arrangement), the Finance Department, Government vide Circular No: Arthas-2020/ P.S. 64/ Arthas-3, dated 16/04/2020, vide Circular No. As per No.64/ Earth-3, dated 10.11.2020, under the Fiscal Measures for" Stimulating the Economic Cycle," 100% fund disbursement under" District Annual Plan (General)" has been approved as a far-reaching measure to boost employment generation and bring the economy back on track in the State.
After the Finance Department has approved the disbursement of 100% of the budgeted allocation under the District Annual Plan (General) 2020-2021, before this order, the demand under the District Annual Plan (General) 2020-2021 has been approved. Under O-14 to O-85, 20 districts have been provided 100% of the total budgeted funds and 16 districts have been provided 75% of the total budgeted funds on" Budget Distribution System" (BDS) subject to certain conditions. The entire functioning of the District Planning Committee has been mandated to be done through IPAS (Integrated Planning Office Operation System) computer system from 01.04.2020 and only 75% of the budgeted funds were disbursed under the District Annual Plan (General) 2020-21 to the 16 districts which were not using or underutilized IPAS (Integrated Planning Office Operation System) system under the District Annual Plan (General) 2020-21. The use of IPAS (Integrated Planning Office Operation System) in these districts was reviewed on 01.01.2021

However, out of these 16 districts, the progress of use of IPAS (Integrated Planning Office Operation System) in 9 districts namely Mumbai City, Mumbai Suburban, Thane, Jalna, Latur, Amravati, Yavatmal, Washim and Nagpur has been found to be satisfactory and the remaining 25% funds under District Annual Plan (General) 2020-21 are being disbursed to these 9 districts as per the instructions in the Government Resolution dated 08.12.2020 of the Planning Department. Apart from this, the remaining 7 districts of Sangli, Aurangabad, Beed, Nanded, Buldhana, Bhandara and Gondia are not satisfied with the use of IPAS (Integrated Planning Office Operation System). In the case of these 7 districts, time-bound action will be expected at the level of Collector and Member Secretary, District Planning Committee and District Planning Officer. The use of IPAS (Integrated Planning Office Operation System) in these seven districts will be reviewed after the next fortnight and a decision will be taken on the release of funds.
