Government circular:
It is the responsibility of the Central Government and the State Governments to fulfill the 17 Sustainable Development Goals and 169 targets set by the United Nations in a time-bound manner.
The Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI), Government of India has identified 306" National Indicators" for achievement of 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and 169 targets by 2030, which are being implemented by the concerned Ministries and Departments in a planned manner under the guidance of NITI Aayog.
In this context, the Planning Department has informed the administrative departments from time to time about the mapping of 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and 169 targets to be implemented by the administrative departments for effective implementation, evaluation and monitoring of the 17 SDGs and 169 targets by 2030 in the state.
The NITI Aayog has prepared the" SDG India Index" to review the action taken by various states on these 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and 169 targets, in which the competitive performance of the states is being considered and they are being rated. The" SDG India Index" is available on NITI Aayog's website http:// niti. gov. in.
