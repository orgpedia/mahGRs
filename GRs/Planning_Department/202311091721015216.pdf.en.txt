The Proposal:
Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Research Training and Human Development Institute (Sarathi) was established in 2018 by the Government of Maharashtra under the Companies Act, 2013 with the objective of social, educational and economic development of the target groups of Marathas, Kunbis, Maratha-Kunbis and Kunbis-Marathas. Instructions regarding opening of residential hostels at district level through Sarathi Sanstha for boys/ girls belonging to the target group having annual income upto Rs. 8.00 lakhs. Chairman, Cabinet Sub-Committee (Maratha Reservation and Amenities) given in the meeting of 10/10/2023. For this, it has been directed in the meeting to start hostels for the students of the target group through Sarathi Institute at the district level through private registered institutions and its rules should be made according to the policy of the Higher and Technical Education Department. Later, the Department of Social Justice and Special Assistance read the above No. 2 As on 30.10.2023, a comprehensive policy has been formulated to bring necessary uniformity in various schemes and programmes benefiting students from different communities.
Department of Higher and Technical Education, Government Decision No: Sankiran-2018/ PR No. 249/18/ Tanshi-4, dated 13th July, 2018, the scheme of leasing out government and other buildings for running hostels has not received adequate response and the construction of hostels of Sarathi Institute will take some time to be operational. Therefore, the matter of providing hostels of registered private institutions to the students of the target group of Sarathi was under the consideration of the government.

Government's decision
Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Research, Training and Human Development Institute (Sarathi), Pune, has been approved to run hostels of private registered institutions under the" Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Sarathi District Level Hostel Complex Scheme" and to implement this scheme from the academic year 2023-24 by linking it with the Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Hostel Maintenance Allowance Scheme implemented by the Department of Higher and Technical Education.
The procedure and rules for the private institutions registered to run the hostels under the" Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Sarathi District Level Hostels Complex Scheme" will be as follows: 1) The Collector should invite applications by advertising from the registered institutions willing to run the hostels under this scheme.
2) The subsequent selection process should be done at the level of the concerned Collector.
The requisitioning institution should be registered under the Bombay Trust Act, 1950 or the Societies Registration Act, 1860.
Such an institution must have completed at least 3 years of audit.
5) Priority will be given to private registered institutions having well-equipped hostels at the district headquarters.
6) If the registered private institutions do not have their own private building, they will have to submit an agreement that they have taken such a building on rent.
7) The Executive Engineer (Public Works Department) and the Principal/ Head of Department (Architecture) of the Government Technical College in that district should inspect the site and report to the Collector about the hostel that can be useful.
8) The Collector is being empowered to monitor the implementation of this initiative and select the institution on the basis of merit after scrutinizing the applications received. The District Hostel Control Committee-Sarathi will consist of the following members: (a) District Collector (b) Chief Executive Officers of Zilla Parishads (c) Superintendent of Police of the concerned district (d) Local Municipal Commissioner/ Municipal Chief Executive Officer (f) Senior Women Officers of any department in the district (g) Principal of Government Engineering College or Government Technological University (g) Executive Engineer, Public Works Department (g) Member

Joint/ Deputy Managing Director Sarathi- Member Secretary The Collector may include other members in the above committee as per requirement.
After the selection of the institution, a 3-year contract with that institution should be done on behalf of the Collector.
10) The selected institution will be responsible for all types of maintenance in the private building of the institution. No government subsidy will be given.
11) Eligible students of the Maratha, Kunbi, Kunbi-Maratha and Maratha-Kunbi communities should be given admission in the hostel on priority according to the Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh subsistence allowance scheme of the Department of Higher and Technical Education. The institution will be allowed to take students other than the target group of the SARATHI institution.
The selected institution will be responsible for providing necessary facilities to the students in the hostel such as staff for management, tables, chairs, cupboards, beds, electricity, water, cleaning arrangements, security arrangements (appointing security guards day and night), installation of CCTVs, etc.
The amount of subsistence allowance to be given to the students of the target group of eligible Sarathi under the Dr. Punjabrao Deshmukh Subsistence Allowance Scheme of the Department of Higher and Technical Education will be deposited in the bank account of the students twice in an academic year through the DBT portal by the Department of Higher and Technical Education. The amount will be given to the selected institution from the students staying in the hostel as decided by the district-level control committee. Apart from this, no subsidy will be given by the government.
14) The staff required to run the hostel shall be required to be appointed by the institution.
The government will not be responsible for this. This should be mentioned in the contract.
15) The Collector shall be entitled to include in the agreement such terms and conditions as he may deem necessary for the proper functioning of the hostel.
16) Sarathi, Co-/ Deputy Managing Director will be the Controlling Officer of the Scheme. The scheme will be implemented through a committee headed by the district collector.
17) The Collector should form a Grievance Redressal Committee for complaints regarding facilities.
18) The time-bound orders of the Department of Higher and Technical Education in this regard will remain in force during the implementation of this scheme.
These orders are being issued as per the approval given by the Department of Higher and Technical Education vide No. No. 20/2023, dated: 31.10.2023.
