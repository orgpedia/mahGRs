Government's decision:
The Maharashtra Legislative Council has passed the Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2020 and the same has been passed with the concurrence of the Governor of Maharashtra vide Maharashtra Act No. 15 Therefore, the Government of Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part IV, dated 18th March, 2020 has been published. Accordingly, vide circular dated 16.04.2020, dated 16.04.2020, in the context of COVID-19 pandemic situation in the State, the Department of Finance has approved the release of funds in the range of 15-25% of the total annual budgetary allocation of essential and programme expenditure for the first quarter of the financial year 2020-21.
Under the District Annual Plan (General) for the financial year 2020-2021, Rs. 9800.00 crore has been provided in the Budget. Out of this provision, 10% of the sanctioned amount of all districts is Rs. The Department of Finance has agreed to release Rs. 981.35 crore as first instalment for 2020-21. Accordingly, a sum of Rs. 981.35 (Rupees Nine Hundred One Crores Thirty Five Lakhs only) is being sanctioned under the head Revenue and Capital Accounts under Demand No. O-14 to O-85 under District Annual Plan (General) for the financial year 2020-21 and is being placed at the disposal of all the concerned District Collectors as indicated in the attached statement.
The funds for the District Annual Plan (General) for the year 2020-2021 have been disbursed on the Budget Distribution System. From this fund, the Planning Department has issued Government Resolution No.DAP-1020/ No. 63/ K. 1481 dated 20th March, 2020, the Collector and the Controlling Officer should take precautionary measures while spending the funds as the financial condition of the State is likely to be critical due to the emergency situation arising out of the COVID-19 virus.
The Collector and the Controlling Officer shall forthwith disburse the provisions made available to them (as indicated in the Annexure) to the Drawing and Disbursing Officers under them, and the Controlling Officers shall strictly follow the financial rules in the Maharashtra Budget Rule Book, Financial Powers Rule Book and the directions given in the relevant Government Decisions/ Circulars.

Government Resolution No: DAP-1020/ PR No.64/ K. 1481,
It has been decided at the Government level to allocate up to 25% of the funds available under the District Annual Plan (General) for the financial year 2020-21 for health purposes. Accordingly, all the District Planning Committees have been directed to utilise the funds as per requirement up to 25% of the funds made available under the District Annual Plan (General) 2020-21 in the first phase and thereafter, on priority basis, for taking measures against the outbreak of COVID-19 and for strengthening the district level health infrastructure of the Public Health Department and Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals Departments. The Maharashtra District Planning Committee (MDCC) has decided to provide funds under the' Other District Scheme" under the head' Revenue" and' Capital" during the financial year 2020-21 to all the health institutions of the State Government and local bodies, such as Medical College Affiliated Hospitals/ Women's Hospitals/ Sub-District Hospitals/ Rural Hospitals/ Primary Health Centres and Sub-Centres, etc. and the District General Hospitals/ Referral Service Hospitals, as per the requirement as a one-time" special case," and the hospitals run by Municipal Corporations and Municipalities, as per the requirement. In this way, the proposals to be approved as a one-time" special matter" under the" Other District Scheme" should be informed to the Guardian Minister of the concerned districts and the Chairman, District Planning Committee, the Collector and the Member Secretary, District Planning Committee should give" administrative approval" and post-facto approval should be taken in the next meeting of the District Planning Committee.
This Government Decision is being issued under the authority conferred on the Administrative Department by the Government Circular No. 2 dated 16/4/2020 in the context of the Finance Department.
