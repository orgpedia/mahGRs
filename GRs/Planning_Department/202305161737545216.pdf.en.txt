The Proposal:
The Regional Rural Banks have been established under the Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976 for the economic development of rural areas. Currently, Maharashtra Gramin Bank and Vidarbha Konkan Gramin Bank are the two regional rural banks in the state of Maharashtra, in which the central government has 50 per cent stake, sponsored banks 35 per cent and the state government 15 per cent. Letter No. 3/9/2020 of Additional Secretary, Department of Financial Services, Ministry of Finance, Government of India- RRB, D. Instructions have been received from the Government of Maharashtra to disburse their 15% share for the financial year 2022-23 by 29th March, 2023. Against this backdrop, the Government was considering to disburse the Recapitalization Assistance to the concerned Regional Rural Banks for the financial year 2022-23 from the funds available under 15% Government share.
Government Decision: For the financial year 2022-23," Recapitalization Assistance" from the funds available under the 15% share of the State Government is Rs. 13,60,96, 950/- to Maharashtra Gramin Bank and Rs. 23,22,03, 050/- to Vidarbha Konkan Gramin Bank. 36.83 crore to the two Regional Rural Banks.
