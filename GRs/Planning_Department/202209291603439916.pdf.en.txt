The Regional Rural Banks have been established under the Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976 for the economic development of rural areas. Currently, Maharashtra Gramin Bank and Vidarbha Konkan Gramin Bank are the two regional rural banks in the state of Maharashtra, in which the central government has 50 per cent stake, sponsored banks 35 per cent and the state government 15 per cent.
