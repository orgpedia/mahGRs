Introduction: The role of the State of Maharashtra is crucial in the realization of the Government of India's goal of" Developing India's Economy to$ 5 trillion by 2027." The government has set a target of making Maharashtra a$ 1 trillion economy. In order to achieve this objective, the State Government has constituted the" Maharashtra Economic Advisory Council" vide Government Resolution No. 1 dated 30/12/2022 to determine the parameters of key indicators in all sectors, to formulate strategies for growth in sectors relevant to this objective, to hold extensive and in-depth consultations with various stakeholders in the State and to suggest measures to increase the share of domestic production of all sectors. Three meetings of the Economic Advisory Council were held on 13/02/2023, 10/05/2023 and 12/07/2023. The report of the Economic Advisory Council was submitted to the Government in its third meeting dated 12/07/2023. The government was considering setting up of an early action committee for effective implementation of various recommendations made by the State Economic Advisory Council.
Government's decision:
The Maharashtra Economic Advisory Service has studied various sectors such as manufacturing, services, tourism, health, agriculture, energy, infrastructure, construction, transportation, ease of doing business, micro, small and medium enterprises, financial services and made some recommendations.
The report submitted by the Economic Advisory Council should be examined by the Maharashtra Institute for Transformation (MITRA) and an action plan should be prepared in collaboration with the concerned departments to take into account the sector-wise recommendations. The concerned departments should take action according to the action plan and submit the proposal to the cabinet for approval as per the requirement.
3. The following Quick Action Committee is being constituted to review the action taken by the concerned departments for effective implementation of various recommendations made by the Economic Advisory Council and to guide the departments on the difficulties being faced in implementation.
