The Proposal:
In pursuance of the Government of India's "One State One Regional Rural Bank" policy, the Department of Financial Services, vide its Act dated 5th April, 2025, amalgamated the two Regional Rural Banks of the State, namely, Maharashtra Gramin Bank and Vidarbha Konkan Gramin Bank, into a single Regional Rural Bank, Maharashtra Gramin Bank, with effect from 1st May, 2025. The Government was considering appointing two representatives of the State Government as Directors on the Board of Directors of this Regional Rural Bank under Section 9 (1) (e) of Section 3 of the Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976.
The government's decision:
The appointment of State Government representative on the Board of Directors of Regional Rural Banks is being superseded by the Government Decision of the Planning Department vide Government Decision No. RRB-2019 / Q. No. 111 / Q. 1417 dated 6th February, 2023.
The Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, Section No. Section 3 of No. Under Section 9 (1) (e), two officers of the State Government are being appointed as Directors on the Board of Directors of the Regional Rural Bank, Maharashtra Gramin Bank, as shown in the table below.

The appointment of the Directors of the State Government appointed as aforesaid shall be for a period of three years from the date of issue of the Government Decision. These directors will not be paid any separate allowance by the government for attending periodic meetings of the Board of Directors of Maharashtra Gramin Bank. However, the travel allowance and ancillary charges as per the prevailing rules of the Government shall remain admissible.
