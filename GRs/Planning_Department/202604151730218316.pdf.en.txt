Proposal
On the occasion of India's "Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav," the Government of India has resolved to make the country a "developed nation-India" (62047 (India (82047)). For India to achieve the status of a developed nation by 2047, all states must also achieve full development. To this end, Maharashtra has set its own economic goals. It aims to make the state's economy $2 trillion by 2047, up from $5 trillion by 2047. To achieve this goal, the focus of development efforts must be at the district level, leading to targeted policies that support inclusive and sustainable growth. Considering the challenges of socio-economic disparities, unequal natural resources, and increasing urbanization in the districts, the Planning Department issued a Government Decision on 20 April 2023 to prepare District Development Plans (DSPs) for each district.
The MahastraRIDE project has been approved by the Government Decision dated 14th March, 2024 of the Planning Department.
Under the Result Area 1 of the Proposed Appraisal Document (PAD), Heard will be assessed on the performance criteria set out in the District Development Plan and eligible districts will receive incentive funds. The Cabinet in its meeting held on 26.02.2026 has approved the mode of distribution of Incentive Funds to the districts under the Mahastride project and the Government decision in this regard will be issued separately.

The Government's Decision
Criteria B of distribution of incentive funds to districts under the Mahastride project fund distribution system has been approved. Reference No. 1 to provide incentive funds to districts that successfully implement Annual Action Plans (AAPs) under the District Development Plan if they meet the Annual Action Plan and Performance Conditions of the District Development Plan. 5 have been approved.
As detailed in the Operation Manual of the Mahastride project, incentive funds will be provided to the districts as per Performance (118) criteria. The performance of the districts will be assessed through an independent verification mechanism (1065810611 Verification Agency-IVA) designated for the Mahastride project.
9. Distribution of Incentive Funds
Districts with a composite score of at least 60 per cent in the 9.9 annual performance criteria will be eligible for the incentive fund. The incentive funds will be disbursed to the eligible districts declared after the independent verification SATAN (IVA) Annual Assessment verification.
The Mahastride Project Management Cell (PMU) in the Mitra office will scrutinise the reports received from the districts and submit the proposal to the Project Coordination Committee (PCC). Accordingly, the Project Coordination Committee will determine the incentive funds for the eligible districts.
9.2 The criteria for the Incentive Fund shall be as follows: -
16 crore for each district with a performance score of 7 80% or above, ₹12 crore for each district with a performance score of 7 70% but less than 80 per cent, ₹8 crore for each district with a performance score of 7 60% but less than 70 per cent.
The details of the scoring method shown above are shown in Annexure 1.
Districts that score at least 60 percent aggregate in the annual assessment to be carried out by the independent verification mechanism designated for the Mahastrind project will be eligible for final selection by the Project Coordination Committee under the performance conditions mentioned in Annexure 1.
The funds may be used for the activities / projects mentioned in paragraph 2.2.3.

2.3 Procedure for utilisation of funds
2.3.2 The Project Coordination Committee under the ASRS project will have the power to approve the incentive funds to the respective districts as per the criteria, if they meet the Annual Action Plan and Performance Conditions of the District Development Plan, and to disburse the funds from the Mahastride project.
2.3.2 Based on the evaluation of the proposal, the incentive funds payable for each district will be determined by the Project Coordination Committee headed by the CEO, MITRA.
2.3.3 The Collector shall demand funds for the works sanctioned by the District Executive Committee to the Project Management Cell. Funds will be distributed by the Project Management Cell accordingly.
2.4 Monitoring & Compliance 2.4.1 Districts are required to submit quarterly progress reports to the Project Management Cell, which will include the following information:
7 Utilization of funds 7 Progress compared to the targets approved in the Annual Action Plans 7 Challenges and Solutions Plan
2.4.2 The districts are required to submit an Annual Utilization Report at the beginning of each financial year and the districts will be responsible for conducting the audit.
2.4.3 The Mahastride Project Management Cell will be responsible for regulating the sanctioning and disbursement of incentive funds for the districts.
