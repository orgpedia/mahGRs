Since the autonomy enjoyed by the Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Research, Training and Human Development Institute (SARATHI) under the Companies Act, 2013, for day-to-day functioning, is required to be retained, the Government of the Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Department has issued a Government Resolution No. Sarathi-2020/ S.No.63/ Corporations Dated:- It will not be appropriate for the provisions of 28th February 2020 to be applicable to the company Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Research, Training and Human Development Institute (Sarathi). A decision in this regard was pending with the government.
GOVERNMENT DECISIONS: 1. The Government of Bahujan Welfare Department vide Decision No. Sarathi-2020/ S.No.63/ Corporations dated 28th February, 2020 shall not be applicable to Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Institute of Research, Training and Human Development (Sarathi).
The Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Research, Training and Human Development Institute (Sarathi) shall perform administrative duties and financial responsibilities under the supervisory control of the" Board of Directors" as per the Memorandum of Association and the Articles of Association. As per the provision of the Companies Act 2013, the company committees which are mandatory for a company registered under section 8, should be established at the level of Managing Director with the approval of the Board of Directors. The Managing Director should make arrangements for their meetings to be held regularly and also the Annual General Meeting (AGM) of the company Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj Research, Training and Human Development Institute (SARATHI) should be held regularly.
(i) Instructions and instructions regarding the business and policy and day-to-day affairs of the Company from the Registrar of Companies, Mumbai in respect of the Company Act, 2013 and Rules/ Notifications/ Circulars thereunder; (ii) Report on the financial management and audit of the Company by the Principal Accountant General (Auditor), Mumbai/ Nagpur, State of Maharashtra, as per the provisions of the Comptroller and Auditor General of India Act, 1971; and (iii) Instructions and directions from the competent authority regarding the implementation of the Act and the Rules of the concerned Administrative Department of the State Government.
The Managing Director will be responsible for submitting the Annual Report of the company to the Maharashtra Legislature.
