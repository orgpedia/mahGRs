The Proposal:
The Government have approved the following schemes for the provision of civic services and amenities in the jurisdiction of Urban Local Bodies, namely," Civic Services and Amenities in Municipal Corporations" and" Civic Services and Amenities in Municipalities/ Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats." As there is a short time left for the financial year 2021-22 to end, it was not possible to provide information about the works of civic services and facilities and the funds required for it by various urban local bodies. Therefore, in the Budget 2021-22, Rs. 800.00 crore has been approved to be deposited in the account of District Planning Officer, Pune under Collector, Pune in Canara Bank, Pune vide Government Resolution No. Read paragraph (1) here. The provisions of (2.4), 3, 4 and 7 were amended by the Government in pursuance of the decision at Annexure (3). The Government were considering the matter of repealing the amendments made under the Government decision at serial number (3) and reinstating the provisions of the Government decision at serial number (1).
Government Decision: Planning Department, Government Decision No. 2021/ No. 36/ No. 1411, Dy. The provisions of Article No. B of July 7, 2022 are being repealed and the Planning Department, Government Decision No. 1. SANKIRNA-2021/ Q.No.33/ K. 1411, Dy. The Government is pleased to re-impose the status quo as on 28th March, 2022.

The provision regarding opening up is being approved to be included subject to the following terms and conditions.
A separate bank account to be opened in the name of the concerned District Collector for each district should be credited only for the schemes" Civic Services and Amenities in Municipal Corporation Area (3604 1009) and Civic Services and Amenities in Municipalities/ Municipal Councils/ Nagar Panchayats Area (3604 1018)."
(b) The balance in the said bank account shall be required to be adjusted against the Government.
(c) Monthly/ quarterly reconciliation of transactions in the said bank account and audit shall be mandatory.
d) The funds should be withdrawn from the treasury at the same time as it is required for immediate utilization so that the funds do not remain unutilized for a long time in the said bank account.
e) It shall be the responsibility of the concerned Collector to coordinate the expenditure with the office of the Accountant General in respect of the funds to be disbursed and to submit the utilization certificate of the expenditure incurred under the said scheme.
e) Balance of the balance of the said bank account if it is closed
