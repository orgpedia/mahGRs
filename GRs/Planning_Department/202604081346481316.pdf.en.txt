The Proposal: -
The "Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme" is being implemented in the state during the period 2025-26 as per the relevant government decision. The Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme was implemented to enable the youth to gain hands-on experience of the functioning, coordination and decision-making process of the government machinery while understanding the stages of the development process in the state, thereby creating honest, goal-oriented, well-rounded citizens to serve the society with a GA attitude. Through this program, the youth of the state got the experience of working with the administration and at the same time helped to widen their knowledge and experience circles. The creativity and differentiated thinking ability of the youth has been harnessed by the administration, and the enthusiasm and the love of technology among the youth has benefited the administration. Also, under the SAS Government decision, a reputed educational institution in the country was roped in to add an educational program to the program to help the fellows in their future endeavours. Accordingly, the implementation of the Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme 2026-27 is under consideration of the Government.
The Government's Decisions:
1. Government Decisions are being issued to implement the Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme and accordingly to lay down the criteria, terms and conditions for selection of fellows and to outline the academic programme to be implemented by the reputed educational institutions.
The criteria for selection of fellows for the "Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme" 2026-27, the terms and conditions for appointment of fellows, and the design and implementation of the educational programme to be implemented by the educational institutions should be dealt with as follows.

(a) Implementation of the Programme:
The programme should be implemented through the Directorate of Economics and Statistics, Government of Maharashtra, under the Planning Department.
(b) The criteria for selection of Fellows: -
(9) The applicant must be a citizen of India.
Educational Qualification: Graduate in any discipline (minimum 60 per cent marks required).
(3) Experience: - At least one year of full-time work experience will be required. Also, one year of experience with full-time internship / apprenticeship / apprenticeship will be required for a professional course student as part of their course. Full-time self-employment, self-employment experience will also be considered. The applicant will have to submit a self-declaration form as such.
(4) Language and Computer Knowledge: - It will be necessary to be able to speak and write Marathi language. Adequate knowledge of Hindi and English will be required. Also, knowledge of computer handling and the Internet will be required.
(5) Age Limit: - The minimum age of the candidate should be 21 years and maximum 26 years as on the last date of submission of application form.
(6) How to apply: - The candidate has to apply through the online application system prescribed by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics.
(7) Fee to be charged for applications: - Rs.500 / -
(8) Number of Fellows: - The number of fellows in the programme has been fixed at 60, out of which the number of female fellows will be 1 / 3 of the total number of fellows. If 1 / 3 of the female fellows are not available, a male fellow will be selected instead.
(9) The rank of Fellows shall be equivalent to that of Group-A officers in Government service.
(Qo) Proceedings with respect to appointment: - The Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Government of Maharashtra, Mumbai, shall conduct the process of advertisement for selection of fellows, inviting applications, screening of applications, conducting examinations, selection of candidates, appointment of manpower on contract basis for implementation of the programme, creation and updating of websites / webpages as per requirement, appointment of necessary agencies for promotion and publication of the programme, etc.

(12) Duration of appointment for the Fellowship Programme: - The appointment of Fellows shall be for a period of 12 months. It will not be extended. Also, the Fellow's appointment will automatically terminate after 12 months from the date of his / her joining.
(13) Scholarship: - The fellows selected under the programme will be given a stipend of Rs.56,100 / - per month with a VATA cost of Rs.5,400 / - aggregating to Rs.61,500 / -.
(94) Academic Programme: - The academic programme shall be an integral part of the fellowship programme. For the selected fellows, a separate Post-Graduate Certificate Course in Public Policy will be conducted in collaboration with the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, Powai, Mumbai. Under this program, offline web online lectures will be organized on various topics that will be useful for Heal while working at the designated place.
Offline lectures will be held at the Indian Institute of Technology, Mumbai for two weeks at the beginning of the fellowship and one week each at the end of the fellowship after six months. In addition, online lectures will be held on Saturdays, Sundays, or public holidays depending on the need of the event throughout the year. HAT is mandatory to attend all lectures offline and online. The purpose of the program will be to help HAT use appropriate tools and scientific methods when working in the public interest and policy making, as well as to build the knowledge, skills, and capabilities needed to implement development programs.
Upon successful completion of this Master's certificate course, fellows will be awarded a separate certificate by the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay. HATS will be mandatory for successful completion of both the fold work and the academic program during the fellowship period with the respective office or authority appointed to obtain the certificate of successful completion of the fellowship program from the government.
(95) Other events: - Introduction sessions to academic programmes, visits to various social institutions, GARE with dignitaries, certificate distribution ceremony for completion of Chief Minister's Fellowship are some of the other events that will be organized during the year for this purpose.
(b) the terms and conditions being prescribed in connection with the appointment of Fellows: -

(2) After the appointment of Fellows a) Police verification shall be done by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics after the joining of Fellows. b) A certificate of competence of a registered medical practitioner shall be required to be produced at the time of joining. (c) Examination of documents (at the time of interview) regarding the authenticity of the fellows' educational qualification, experience, etc. shall be done by the Directorate of Earth Sciences Statistics, Mumbai.

(3) The candidate selected under this programme shall be appointed on a fellowship on contract for a period of 12 months. This 12-month period will include the duration of the academic program as well as other activities that are carried out under the fellowship program. No increase in remuneration will be admissible during the duration of the fellowship.
(4) On receipt of the letter of selection, the candidate shall appear at the prescribed place and time at his own expense. It will not be supported under any circumstances. The appointment of the candidate who fails to appear at the prescribed place within the stipulated time will be cancelled. The decision taken by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Government of Maharashtra in this regard shall be final.
(5) Even if the candidates are asked for preference in respect of the department / office / authority, the department / office / authority shall be appointed to the candidate taking into account the priorities of the Government as well as the suitability and usefulness of the candidate. The decision of the Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Government of Maharashtra will be final in this regard. The Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Government of Maharashtra shall have the power to disqualify from the selection process a candidate who attempts to exert any pressure on the authorities concerned in this regard and who does not make the appointment even after selection.
(6) The Department / Office / Authority in which the appointment will be made shall provide the concerned Fellow with computer facilities with internet connection during the tenure of the Fellowship. The office concerned will be responsible for taking the computer facility away from the fellow at the end of the fellow's term.
(7) Fellows will be provided with a provisional ID card and a provisional official government e-mail ID during the fellowship period. At the end of the term of the Fellow concerned, the identity card held by the Fellow will be required to be returned to the Director, Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Government of Maharashtra. Also, the facility of e-mail ID given to the fellow will be discontinued.
(8) Dearness Allowance, Interim or Pay Commission benefits shall not be admissible to fellows.
(9) SAHA reimbursement shall not be admissible to fellows during the fellowship period.
(10) Service during the fellowship period shall not be considered for pension, bonus, leave travel allowance, leave encashment or any other financial benefit and service-related benefits.
(11) The business tax shall not be collected from the fellows' remuneration. (Department of Finance, Circular No. PFT-1076 / Q. No. 29/76, dated 5/6/1976) (12) Insurance under the Hera Group Personal Accident Insurance Risk Scheme shall be governed by the rules of the Department of Finance.
(13) The duties performed by officers of the same cadre shall continue to be those of fellows. Accordingly, the fellows will perform their duties by attending the office as per the prescribed time by following all the rules in the office.
Also, fellows will not be able to pursue full-time courses, private businesses, and part / full-time jobs during this period. If duty WAS due for office work on a public holiday other than an office working day, the fellows will have to attend work. No additional remuneration of any kind shall be admissible for the same.
(14) During the 12-month period of the fellowship, fellows will be allowed 8 days of casual leave. If they enjoy any other type of leave, the honorarium will be deducted accordingly.

(15) Fellows are required to attend the headquarters and cannot leave the headquarters without prior permission of the authority concerned.
(16) The Hara period shall be of twelve months only. The selection of fellows will automatically terminate at the end of the twelve-month period. The selection of fellows will be only on a temporary basis. Fellows will not have the right to join government service.
(17) In the event of the death or disability of the fellow during the fellowship period, the amount of the scholarship / scholarship during the period of his / her duty shall be paid to his / her declared family. However, appointments will not be made on the basis of ex-gratia or compassionate grounds.
(18) If the Fellows wish to resign during the period of the Fellowship, an application signed at least one month in advance has to be submitted to the Directorate of Statistics, Government of Maharashtra through the appointed office. If the application is not submitted in this way, the scholarship for that month will not be paid.
(19) It shall be obligatory to provide a bond on completion of all HAT fellowship programs.
(20) In the event of non-completion of the fellowship programme in the bond, a certain amount shall be mentioned in the bond as compensation to the Government.
(21) The bond will not be paid one-third of the scholarship amount for the first three months of the fellowship program each month. This amount will only be paid to fellows upon completion of the fellowship program.
(22) If a Fellow's Fellowship is terminated by reason of resignation or otherwise, one-third of the amount of the scholarship for the first three months of the Fellowship Programme, unpaid each month, shall not be paid to the Fellow as Government Compensation under the Bond.
(23) Full confidentiality shall be maintained with respect to the work done by Fellows during the duration of the Fellowship as well as after the expiry of the Programme. No information may be given to them during the fellowship period or even after that to anyone other than the authority officer regarding the activities / research / information submitted / generated based on the information received during the fellowship period.
(24) The Fellow may not participate in any royal movement during the duration of the Fellowship. They should also ensure that no untoward incident takes place from them. It will be essential for fellows to maintain integrity, not to participate in elections, not to report to the press, not to accept donations of any kind, etc.
(25) The prior approval of the head of the office appointed shall be taken while visiting the projects / establishments in the HAM State. The head of the projects / establishments to be visited in this regard will be required to give a prior view.
(26) HAL shall be solely responsible for any criminal and unlawful acts committed by Fellows during and after the entire duration of the Fellowship.
(27) The Fellowship of Fellows shall be terminated as a Fellow (appointment) without any prior notice on account of unsatisfactory conduct or indiscipline during the Fellowship period.
(28) The District Collector / CEO concerned, Zilla Parishad shall designate HAT works at the beginning of every three months and prepare a quarterly plan. Also, list the expected contributions from fellows in the quarterly plan. The draft should be submitted to the Directorate of Statistics, Government of Maharashtra through HEAT email / web portal.
