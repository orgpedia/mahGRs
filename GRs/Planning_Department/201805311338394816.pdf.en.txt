(2) Any officer who directly or indirectly tries to pressurise the transfer order to be modified shall be liable for disciplinary action.
(3) The head of the office concerned shall immediately relieve the officers of the post of the concerned officers by entrusting the charge of the post to another officer so that the officers can be transferred. Also, if the above officers do not join the transferred post within the period of joining which is payable/ permissible to them, the concerned office should not withdraw their salary. Under no circumstances will the waiting period be regularized. In this regard, the Government of the Department of Finance vide Government Resolution No. Seniway- 1001/130/ SEWA-4, dated 2/6/2003, vide Annexure-1, Rule No. 9 (14) (f), Sec. According to section 2, the responsibility will be fixed on the concerned officer who will be responsible for creating the mandatory waiting period.
After the transferred officers join the office of the transferor, they should immediately contact the concerned Drawing and Disbursing Officer and take action to update the e-profile within seven days.
The salary payment of the concerned officers should not be passed by the treasury office unless the updated e-profile is submitted by the concerned office to the treasury office along with the salary payment of that month. Also, copies of the updated e-profile should be sent to the Director, Directorate of Finance and Statistics and the Government. It will be the responsibility of the concerned office head to update the e-profile.
It has been observed that some officers are not complying with the transfer orders, which is hampering the administrative work. Therefore, the above officers should join the place of transfer within the period of their appointment payable/ admissible to them. Disciplinary action will be taken if there is violation of the order.
(6) The transferred officers shall forward to this Department a copy of the order of their discharge and assumption of charge.
