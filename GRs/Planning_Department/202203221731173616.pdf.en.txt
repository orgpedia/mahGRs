The Proposal:
Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri districts of the state are blessed with unique geographical conditions, high rainfall, enchanting beaches and natural wealth. Considering the population of these districts from the social point of view, both these districts get very little funds from the tribal component or the scheduled caste component. Similarly, the Human Development Programme is implemented in selected talukas of various districts of the state, out of which only one taluk, Vaibhavwadi, in both Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri districts, has been included under the Human Development Programme. Therefore, both the districts are not getting adequate funds from this program. In this regard, Ma. Deputy Chief Minister (Finance and Planning) Shri. The Budget Speech dated 08.03.2021 has announced the following:
" It has been decided to implement a pilot scheme" Sindhuratna Samriddhi" for development of tourism, fisheries and micro industries based on natural resources in two districts of Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri. An amount of Rs. 100.00 crore per annum will be provided for the scheme over the next three years. " In line with this announcement, it is being decided to formulate and implement a model of development of the district by combining all the schemes/ programmes of the district such as agriculture, horticulture, livestock development, fisheries, tourism, rural development, development of minor ports, micro industries based on natural resources, skill development.
Government's decision:
In order to effectively plan and develop the natural resources of the two districts of Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri, the Government has approved the implementation of the" Sindhuratna Samriddhi" pilot scheme for the two districts of Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri for a period of 3 years from 2022-23 to 2024-25.

3. Duration of' Sindhuratna Samriddhi" Scheme

5. Structure of' Sindhuratna Executive Committee"
(5.1) A Sindhuratna Executive Committee is being constituted to prepare a separate micro plan for both Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri districts and implement it in a time bound manner.
Former Minister of State (Finance and Planning), Shri Deepak Kesarkar is being appointed as the Chairman of" Sindhuratna Executive Committee." The composition of the committee is as follows:
Deepak Kesarkar, former Minister of State (Finance and Planning) Chairman 2) Deputy Commissioner, Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri 3) Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri 4) Chief Executive Officer, Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri District Level Implementation Agencies Member 5) District Planning Officer, Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri- Member Secretary
(5.2) In addition to the above members, two special invitees will be appointed by the Planning Department on the" Sindhuratna Executive Committee."
(5.3) In addition to the above members of the Sindhuratna Samriddhi Yojana, officials at the divisional level with special experience in various administrative departments of the Konkan region in the mentioned development areas, eminent non-governmental organizations and experts in the private sector may be called as" Invited Members" as per the direction of the Chairman in the Executive Committee meeting.
6. Headquarters of' Sindhuratna Samriddhi" Scheme
The headquarters of the" Sindhuratna Executive Committee" appointed for the effective implementation of the" Sindhuratna Samriddhi" scheme will be at the Collectorate, Sindhudurg. For this, space should be made available at the Collector Office, Sindhudurg. The executive committee chairpersons can hold meetings at the office of the district collector, Ratnagiri, as per the requirement.
7. Project Management Cell for' Sindhuratna Samriddhi" Scheme
For the time-bound implementation of the Sindhuratna Samriddhi Yojana, a' Project Management Cell" under the District Planning Officer should be set up under the control of the Collectors of both Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri districts. For both Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri District Management Cell, there will be 5 posts including 01 Project Manager, 01 Assistant Project Manager, 01 Project Assistant and 02 Data Entry Operator. All the above five posts are proposed to be filled through" Outsourcing" and the educational qualification, salary etc. for these posts are proposed as follows.

9. Convergence of" Sindhuratna Samriddhi" Scheme and" District Annual Plan" and Special Plan.
(9.1) To achieve the objectives of rapid development of both Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri districts by convergence of the programmes/ schemes being implemented under the District Annual Plan (General) in the works/ schemes being implemented under the Sindhuratna Samriddhi Yojana. In this regard, the President, the Executive Committee will prepare a suitable proposal for implementation and take further necessary action after receiving approval from the Executive Committee.
(9.2) If the programs/ schemes and projects under some similar" Special Schemes" of the State Government implemented earlier in the" Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri" districts remain incomplete, the process of implementation with minor changes after review by the" Sindhuratna Executive Committee" should be completed in the financial year 2022-23. The pilot project will be implemented on the basis of" Guidelines" communicated through Government Decisions/ Circulars/ Orders issued earlier for implementation of special schemes from time to time by the concerned administrative departments in this regard. Up to 25% of the funds available for implementation of innovative schemes will be allowed for" innovative schemes" considering the local needs of both Sindhudurg and Ratnagiri districts.
10. Establishment Fund for Sindhuratna Samriddhi Yojana
2% of the budgeted funds for" Sindhuratna Samriddhi Yojana" will be allowed to handle establishment related matters. Out of this, 0.5 per cent can be spent on the salary of the manpower required for the" Project Management Cell" and the travel expenses to be incurred in pursuance of their project. The remaining 0.5 per cent of the funds will be used for organising meetings of the executive committee.
Funds will be admissible to the Chairpersons of the Committees for travel to other States for implementing" innovative schemes" as per requirement. Apart from this, 1% of the fund will be allowed for organizing workshops and training, publicity materials and publicity, publishing annual reports, etc. to be organized in accordance with the pilot scheme" Sindhuratna Samriddhi Yojana." The results of this study will be analyzed and evaluated. The impact assessment will be done after the completion of the project. For this' Yashda, Pune' or reputed external agencies can be contracted.
