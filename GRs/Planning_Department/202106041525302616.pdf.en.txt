Notification
The Central Government has decided to appoint Grievance Redressal Authority (designated officer to look into complaints against public authority) under Section 27 (1) of the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act at every district of the State vide its resolution No. J. 11011/21/2008-NREGA, D. Ordered on September 7, 2009. In order to clarify the contents of the said order, the Central Government vide order dated 14/01/2014 has amended the said order. Under this order, a Grievance Redressal Authority will be appointed. A committee has been formed under the chairmanship of the Chief Secretary.
Applications were invited from eligible and interested candidates by advertising in the local newspaper of the concerned district for the vacant posts of Grievance Redressal Authority in 6 districts of the state. After scrutinizing the applications received by the Collector, the interviews of the candidates who qualified for the interview were conducted by the Grievance Redressal Authority Selection Committee. Taken by VC on March 19, 2021. The Selection Committee has selected candidates for a total of 6 Grievance Redressal Authorities posts, one each for the following 6 districts, taking into account their experience in the legal, education and social sectors.

The Central Government has directed to complete the following process regarding the selection of Grievance Redressal Authority.
Before giving appointment to the Grievance Redressal Authority, their names should be published on the official website of the state government and the public's opinion and objections should be sought within 30 days. After the expiry of the period of 30 days, the committee shall examine the feedback and objections received and decide on the selection of suitable candidates within 30 days from the date of publication of the selection.
Comments and criticisms are not welcome.
