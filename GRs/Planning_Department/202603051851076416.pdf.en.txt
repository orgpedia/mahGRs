Providing SAS infrastructure is essential to achieve sustainable and sustained growth of the economy. This type of development requires a great deal of complexity, including efficient management of resource functions. However, these needs cannot be met by the public sector alone.
Against this backdrop, the concept of public-private partnership becomes essential for capital formation as well as resource augmentation and service delivery efficiency. Using private sector capital, managerial skills, operational capabilities, innovative approaches, and technological capabilities, public-private partnerships enable the creation and maintenance of high-quality infrastructure and public services, with effective risk sharing, long-term value assurance, for both the economy and the public. Public-private partnerships help in increasing the capacity of the government to undertake infrastructure projects. There was a need to formulate a comprehensive public-private partnership policy of the state for speedy and quality completion of public-private partnership projects.
The Maharashtra Public-Private Partnership Policy 2026 has been approved in the cabinet meetings dated February 2026 to ensure a multi-level committee, institutional and financial framework for the implementation of the policy. Accordingly, the issue of issuing a government decision on the Maharashtra Public-Private Partnership Policy was under consideration of the government.
The government's decision:
Under this government decision, the "Maharashtra Public-Private Partnership Policy 2026" is being formulated. An English-language copy of the policy is provided in Annexure A * to the Government Decision.
