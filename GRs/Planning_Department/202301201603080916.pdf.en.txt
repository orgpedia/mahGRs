Introduction: Refer No. The" Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme" was implemented in the state from 2015-16 to 2019-20. The Chief Minister's Fellowship program was implemented so that the youth could get the experience of the work of the government system, the coordination of its components and the decision process while knowing and understanding the development process and its stages in the state, and to prepare honest, goal-oriented, good citizens to serve the society with a dedicated attitude. Through this program, the youth of the state got the experience of working with the administration and at the same time helped in expanding the scope of their knowledge and experience. The creativity and the ability to think differently among the youth was used for governance and the enthusiasm and interest in technology among the youth accelerated the administrative processes.
Reference No. 2 The" Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme" was terminated as per the decision of the government here.
But in view of the insistent demand for the resumption of the" Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme," the government was considering the possibility of re-launching the programme and involving reputed educational institutions of the country to add an academic programme to the programme to make it useful for the fellows in their future journey.
Government's decision:
The government has decided to resume the" Chief Minister Fellowship Programme." The said Government Decision is being issued with a view to implement the said programme and accordingly to determine the criteria, terms and conditions for selection of Fellows and to outline the academic programme to be implemented by the nominated educational institutions.
2. The criteria for selection of fellows for the" Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme," the terms and conditions for appointment of fellows and the outline and implementation of the educational programme to be implemented by the educational institutions should be dealt with as follows.

Criteria for selection of Fellows: (1) The applicant should be a citizen of India.
Educational Qualification: Graduate in any discipline with at least 60% marks. However, preference will be given to the highest educational qualification.
Experience: At least 1 year of full-time work experience is required. Also, 1 year of experience with full time internship/ apprenticeship/ articleship will be required as part of their course for vocational course students. Full-time self-employment and self-employment experience will also be considered. Applicants must submit a self-attested.
(4) Knowledge of Marathi Language and Computers.- Must be able to read, write and speak Marathi language. A good knowledge of Hindi and English is a must. Computer skills and Internet access are also required.
Age Limit:- The age of the candidate should be minimum 21 years and maximum 26 years as on the last date of submission of application.
(6) Mode of Application:- The candidate has to apply through the online application system specified by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics.
Application Fee: Rs. 500/-
Number of Fellows: The number of Fellows in the programme has been fixed at 60 out of which women Fellows will be 1/3 rd of the total number of Fellows. If 1/3 rd of the female fellows are not available, male fellows will be selected instead.
(9) The rank of Fellows shall be equivalent to that of Group-A officers in Government service.
(10) Procedure in respect of appointment:- The proceedings in respect of advertisement for selection of fellows, calling for applications, scrutiny of applications, conduct of examinations, selection of candidates, appointment of manpower on contract basis for the implementation of the said programme, creation and updating of website/ webpage as per requirement, appointment of necessary agencies for the promotion and publicity of the programme, etc. shall be carried out by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai.
Selection Procedure: (i) The candidate applying for the fellowship will have to apply online and pay the examination fee online.
Aadhaar Card or any other identity proof is mandatory for applying online.
(i) The candidate who pays the examination fee will have to appear for an online objective test.
(iii) The procedure for conducting the online examination will be announced on the website of the Directorate at mahades.maharashtra.gov.in and the terms and conditions thereof shall be adhered to by the candidate.
(iv) The online examination will be conducted in all the major cities of the country and all the districts of the state. However, considering the number of applications received, the decision of the Directorate of Finance and Statistics regarding the examination centre to be made available to the candidate shall be final.

(v) Fellows who have previously worked in the Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme will not be eligible for selection as Fellows under this programme. Applicants must mention this in the application form.
(vi) The 210 candidates who secure the highest marks in the objective test will have to submit online essays on the given topics within a specified period.
(vii) The interview of 210 candidates who secure the highest marks in the Objective Test will be conducted at Mumbai.
(vii) The Committee for interview of these 210 candidates shall be constituted as follows: 1. Director, Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai:- Chairman 2. Officer on Special Duty, Deputy Chief Minister's Secretariat:- Member 3. An officer of the rank of Deputy Secretary or above nominated by the Deputy Chief Minister's Secretariat*: Member 4. An officer of the rank of Group A nominated by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics:- Member 5. One representative each from IIT, Mumbai and IIM, Nagpur:- Member 6. Chief Research Officer, Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai:- Member Secretary.
(ix) At the time of interview, along with educational qualification, the candidate's commitment to social and public work, strong character, positive attitude, team spirit, relevant work experience, his suitability for the said programme and other factors will be considered.
(x) In the final selection, the marks for objective test, essay and interview will be 15, 30 and 50 respectively. 5 marks will be based on the post-graduate degree or professional degree of the recognized institution.
(xi) If two candidates score equal marks in the final selection of candidates, the candidate who is a resident of Maharashtra will be given preference.
A total of 60 candidates will be selected.
Period of Appointment for the Fellowship Programme: The appointment of Fellows will be for a period of 12 months. It will not increase. The appointment will automatically be terminated after 12 months from the date of joining.
Scholarships: The selected fellows will be given a monthly honorarium of Rs.70,000/- and travel expenses of Rs.5,000/- aggregating to Rs.75,000/- as scholarship.
Educational Programmes: Educational programmes will be an integral part of the fellowship programme. Separate academic programmes will be conducted for the selected fellows in collaboration with IIT, Mumbai and IIM, Nagpur. A ten-day training programme will be conducted at the beginning of the fellowship on various topics that will be useful to the fellows while working at the designated place under this programme. The objective of the programme will be to assist the fellows to use appropriate tools and scientific methods while working in the public interest and in policy making and to enhance the knowledge, skills and competencies required to implement development programmes. The event will be held at IIT Bombay and IIM Nagpur.

In the second phase of academic programmes, online training will be provided in various subjects through IIT, Mumbai and IIM, Nagpur. On successful completion of the course, the students will be awarded a certificate from the respective institute. 50% fellows will be selected for each of IIT, Bombay and IIM, Nagpur. The Directorate of Finance and Statistics shall have full powers to allocate institutions for training of fellows. The Directorate of Finance and Statistics shall have the power to exclude from the selection process any candidate who attempts to exert any pressure on the concerned authorities for selection of institutions. It will be mandatory for the fellows to complete both the field work and the educational program with the concerned office or authority appointed to get a certificate of successful completion of the fellowship program from the government.
(15) Other Programmes: In addition to the academic programme, a number of other programmes will be organised during the year for the purpose of introduction sessions, visits to various social organizations, interaction with dignitaries, presentation of certificates.
(b) the terms and conditions of appointment of the members:
(1) The Directorate of Finance and Statistics shall have the power to remove from the selection process any candidate who at any stage of the selection process attempts to pressurize the concerned officers in any manner.
(2) After the appointment of the fellows, a) Police verification will be done by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics on joining. (b) A certificate of competency of a registered medical practitioner shall be required to be produced at the time of joining. (c) The verification of documents regarding educational qualification, etc. of the fellows (at the time of interview) etc. will be done by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai.
(3) The candidate selected under this programme will be appointed on a fellowship on a contract of 12 months. The period of 12 months shall include the duration of the academic programme as well as other activities undertaken under the fellowship programme. No increase in remuneration will be allowed during the period of the contract.
(4) On receipt of the letter of selection, the candidate shall appear at his own cost at the place and time specified. No concession will be given under any circumstances. The appointment of the candidate who fails to appear at the specified place and time will be cancelled. The decision taken by the Directorate of Finance and Statistics in this regard shall be final.
(5) Even if the candidates are asked about the order of preference in respect of the department/ office/ authority, the candidate will be assigned a department/ office/ authority taking into account the priorities of the government as well as the merit and utility of the candidate. The decision of the Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai will be final. The Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai shall have the power not to appoint and exclude from the selection process any candidate who attempts to exert any pressure on the concerned officers in this regard.
(6) The Department/ Office/ Authority in which the Fellows will be appointed shall provide computer facilities with internet connection to the respective Fellows during the tenure of the Fellowship. It shall be the responsibility of the concerned office to take away the said computer facility from the concerned fellow after the expiry of his tenure.
(7) The fellows will be provided with temporary identity card and temporary official e-mail ID during the period of fellowship. At the end of the tenure of the concerned Fellow, the Fellow will have the identity card of the Director, Finance and

(8) Dearness Allowance, Interim or Pay Commission benefits will not be admissible to the Fellows.
(9) Medical reimbursement shall not be admissible to the fellows during the contract period.
(10) Service during the contract period shall not be considered for pension, bonus, leave travel concession, leave encashment or any other pecuniary benefit and service related benefits.
(11) The business tax shall not be recovered from the emoluments of the fellows. (Department of Finance, No. PFT 1076/ Q. No. 29/76, dated 5/6/1976) (12) The insuring under the scheme of Fellows- Group Personal Accident Insurance Risk will be processed as per the rules of the Finance Department.
(13) The duties performed by officers of the same cadre shall be those of fellows. Accordingly, the fellows will perform their duties by attending the office as per the stipulated time by following all the rules of the office. Also, fellows will not be able to pursue full-time courses, private businesses and part/ full-time jobs during this period. Apart from the working day, the fellows will have to report to work on public holidays if they are required to attend office. No extra fee is levied.
(14) 8 days of casual leave shall be admissible to the Fellows during the 12 months period of the contract. If they avail any other kind of leave, the amount will be deducted accordingly.
(15) Fellows are required to be present at the headquarters and shall not leave the headquarters without prior permission of the concerned authorized officer.
(16) The term of the Fellows shall be for a period of twelve months and the election of the Fellows shall be automatically terminated on the expiry of the period of twelve months. The selection of the fellows will be temporary only. The fellows will not be entitled to join government service.
(17) In case of death or disability of a Fellow during the contract period, the amount of scholarship/ scholarship during the period of his duty shall be paid to his declared family. However, the appointment will not be made on the basis of grant-in-aid or compassionate grounds.
(18) If the fellows are to resign during the period of the contract, an application signed at least one month in advance shall be submitted to the Director, Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai through the appointed office.
(19) If the fellowship of a Fellow is terminated due to resignation or other reasons, the Fellow shall not be able to complete the academic programme.
(20) Complete confidentiality shall be maintained regarding the performance of the Fellows during the term of the contract as well as after the expiry of the programme. No information regarding their performance/ research/ data collected/ data generated during the period of contract shall be disclosed to any person other than the authorised officer during the period of contract or thereafter.
(21) During the period of fellowship, the fellow shall not take part in any political movement. They should also ensure that no untoward incident takes place. Fellows should take care to maintain integrity, not to participate in elections, not to give information to the press, not to accept any form of donation, etc.
(22) The fellows will have to take prior approval of the Head of the appointed office while visiting the projects/ establishments in the State. In this regard, the head of the projects/ establishments to be visited should be given prior information.
(23) The Fellows shall be solely responsible for any criminal and illegal acts committed by the Fellows during the entire period of the Fellowship and thereafter.

(24) The contract of appointment of Fellows as Fellows shall be terminated without any prior notice on the ground of unsatisfactory performance or non-discipline during the period of their contract.
(25) The Fellows will have to submit their performance report every month by email to the Head of the appointed office and the Director, Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai.
(26) The report is supposed to record the work done by the fellows during the month and what they have learnt from that work and their suggestions accordingly.
The performance of the Fellows will be evaluated by the Director, Directorate of Finance and Statistics, Mumbai every three months after the appointment of the Fellows.
(28) In the interest of the Government, these terms and conditions, if modified/ extended, shall be binding on the Fellows.
(29) The consent letter to the effect that the above terms/ conditions are acceptable shall be obtained from the fellows before giving the appointment.
The services of a coordinator on contract basis under the Officer on Special Duty in the Deputy Chief Minister's Office can be taken for the effective implementation of the Chief Minister's Fellowship Programme. The Directorate of Finance and Statistics can take services on contract basis with a maximum manpower limit of 5 as per requirement.
4. The expenditure on organizing educational programmes and other programmes and on the honorarium of expert mentors, if invited as per requirement, shall be met from the approved provision. In case accommodation and food arrangements are not made as per requirement for these programmes, an allowance at the rate of Rs.2,250/- (per day) will be provided to each of the Fellows.
