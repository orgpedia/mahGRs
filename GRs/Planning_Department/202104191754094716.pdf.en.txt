Government's decision
The Maharashtra Legislative Council has passed the Maharashtra Appropriation Bill, 2021 and the same has been published in the Government of Maharashtra Gazette Extraordinary Part IV, dated 15th March, 2021 as Maharashtra Act V with the concurrence of the Governor of Maharashtra.
2. As per the decision of the Government dated 12/04/2021 of the Planning Department, the District Annual Plan (General) for the financial year 2021-22 has been issued under Section 12 of the Maharashtra District Planning Committee (Composition and Works) Act, 1998 to spend on strengthening health facilities permissible under the regular district level plans of the Public Health Department and Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals Department.
The Planning Department has made a budgetary provision of Rs 11035 crore for the financial year 2021-22 under the District Annual Plan (General). Out of this provision, the Finance Department has agreed to release 10% of the sanctioned amount i. e. Rs. 1103.50 crore to all the districts as first instalment for 2021-22. Accordingly, under the District Annual Plan (General) for the financial year 2021-22, an amount of Rs. 1103.50 crore (Rupees One Thousand One Hundred Three Crore Fifty Lakh only) is being sanctioned under the head" Revenue" and" Capital" Accounts under Demand No. O-14 to O-85 and is being placed at the disposal of the concerned" Collector and Member Secretary, District Planning Committee" as indicated in the attached statement.
Under District Annual Plan (General), up to 30% of the sanctioned funds i.e. Rs. 3310.50 crore in the financial year 2021-22 is related to the outbreak of COVID-19 only.

Department of Health and Family Welfare, Medical Education and Family Welfare
A decision has been taken at the government level to allocate funds for strengthening the district level health infrastructure. Accordingly, all the District Planning Committees have been directed to implement the District Annual Plan (General) 2021-22 in the first phase.
10% of the funds released will be used for the State Governments response to COVID-19 on priority basis and for the District Level Health Services of Public Health Department and Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals Department.
It should be used to strengthen the district level health infrastructure.
The State Government, as per its powers under Section 12 of the Maharashtra District Planning Committee (Composition and Works) Act, 1998, is directed to provide funds for the permissible measures against the outbreak of COVID-19 to all health institutions of the State Government and local bodies, such as medical college affiliated hospitals, women's hospitals, sub-district hospitals, rural hospitals, primary health centres and sub-centres, etc. and as a one-time" special case," district general hospitals/ referral service hospitals, as well as hospitals run by the Municipal Corporations and Municipalities, as required, through re-allocation under the scheme of" Other District Scheme." In this way, the proposals to be approved as a one-time" special matter" under the Other District Scheme should be informed to the" Guardian Minister and Chairman, District Planning Committee" of the concerned districts and the" Collector and Member Secretary, District Planning Committee" should give administrative approval and the post-facto approval should be taken in the next meeting of the District Planning Committee.
10% of the first phase of the District Annual Plan (General) 2021-22 is being disbursed on the Budget Distribution System. However, considering the local emergency situation arising out of COVID-19, the District Collector and the members are spending the required funds on special measures prescribed by the Government.
Secretary, District Planning Committee should be vigilant as controlling officer and take appropriate action.
As the economic condition of the State is likely to be critical due to the emergency situation arising out of the COVID-19 virus, the Collector and the Controlling Officer should take precautionary measures while spending the funds in this distribution.
The Collector and the Controlling Officer shall forthwith disburse the provisions made available to them (as indicated in the Annexure) to the Drawing and Disbursing Officers under them, and the Controlling Officers shall strictly follow the financial rules in the Maharashtra Budget Rule Book, Financial Powers Rule Book and the directions given in the relevant Government Decision/ Circular.
