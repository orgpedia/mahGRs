Government Circular: As per the relevant Government Circular, after the period of investigation/ inquiry conducted by the Special Investigation Team on the 70 colleges named in the report of the Special Investigation Team, it has been decided to pay the scholarship/ tuition fee, examination fee reimbursement due to eligible students belonging to the Scheduled Castes category of all the courses in the concerned 70 colleges admitted since the year 2017-2018, if they meet the terms and conditions of the scheme, so that such students are not deprived of educational benefits. The scholarship has been sanctioned to the students admitted from the academic year 2017-18 in the Scheduled Castes as per the decision of the Government in this regard. However, the students belonging to Other Backward Classes, Vimukt Jati, Nomadic Tribes and Special Backward Classes are suffering educational and financial loss as the scholarship of the students admitted from the academic year 2017-18 has not been sanctioned. Therefore, the scholarship of the students admitted from the academic year 2017-18 to the students of Other Backward Classes, Vimukt Jati, Nomadic Tribes and Special Backward Classes on the basis of the relevant government circular should be approved as mentioned below.
The process of transferring the educational benefits of a total of four schemes, namely, Scholarship, Freeship, Vidyavetan to the Aadhaar linked bank accounts of the students of Vijabhaj, Emav and Bima categories has started from the year 2018-19. After the period of inspection/ inquiry conducted by the Special Investigation Team on the 70 colleges mentioned in the Special Investigation Team's report, the eligible students of the prescribed course, subject and insurance categories in the 70 colleges concerned admitted from the year 2017-18, if they meet the terms and conditions of the scheme, such students should not be deprived of the educational benefits, so that the proposal for reimbursement of scholarship/ tuition fee, examination fee due to them from the academic year 2017-18 should be examined by the concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare and submitted to the government immediately.
(2) Accordingly, the pending applications for scholarships, tuition fees, examination fees for the period 2017-18 are required to be processed offline as per the prevailing situation and for this purpose, the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, Part-I, Sub-

(6) For the purpose of the audit, the responsibility of preserving the records/ documents regarding the pending application disposed of in accordance with the above procedure shall be of the concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare.
Proposals for pending scholarships, tuition fees, examination fees for the academic year 2010-11 to 2017-18 of all colleges/ institutions other than the 70 colleges concerned from the year 2017-18 onwards should be submitted to the district-wise government.
