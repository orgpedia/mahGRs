The terms and conditions to be followed while giving admission to the nominated school are as follows: 1. The concerned institution/ school will be required to fulfill the terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated 4/9/2019.
2. The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare, Osmanabad shall inspect the concerned school in accordance with the Nominated Residential School Scheme. Also, all students enrolled in the school should be enrolled by verifying their Aadhaar card. After that, the government will take a decision after verifying the number of beneficiary students present in person and the number of direct resident students. The detailed report as per the terms and conditions of 04/09/2019 should be submitted to the Government through the Director, Directorate of Other Backward Bahujan Welfare, Pune for disbursement of funds with the approval of Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare.
3. The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare concerned shall ensure that the institution is ready to provide all the facilities mentioned in the proposal submitted to the Government through the concerned institution/ school regarding student approval under the scheme and shall enter into an agreement with the institution.
The institution/ school is not expected to start a separate batch/ class for the students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category, it will be mandatory to teach the students admitted under the scheme along with other students in the regular batch/ class running in the school. If it is found that a separate batch/ class has been started for the students, action will be taken to register a case against the said institution and the recognition of the school will be cancelled immediately.
It is mandatory to appoint a female superintendent and caretaker in the girls' hostel admitted in the institution. If the school fails to do so, its recognition will be cancelled.
The Assistant Commissioner of Social Welfare of the concerned district should take care to admit students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category only in the schools approved by the government under this scheme and within the limit of the number of students approved for the concerned school.
As per the original provision of the scheme, the benefit of this scheme should be given only to those children whose annual family income is within the maximum limit of Rs. 1.00 lakh.
8. The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall complete the process of admission of the students immediately by giving priority to the nearest school as well as the selected schools of the concerned project while admitting the students.

If the number of applications received for admission under the scheme is more than the number of schools selected in a project area and the number of students approved therein, the Assistant Commissioner of Social Welfare of the concerned district should announce the selection list by selecting the students through a draw of lots.
A list of selected students will also be published. Students will be admitted to the selected schools.
The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district, along with the Institute Director/ Headmaster of the nominated schools selected under the scheme, should hold a meeting in the first and second semester of the academic year to review the academic progress of the admitted students and the facilities provided to them.
After the selection of the school under the nominated scheme, the Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district, Social Welfare, should visit the nominated schools in person twice in the first semester and twice in the second semester and submit a report to the government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune, regarding the friendly atmosphere in the school, the facilities provided to the students, the medicine, the educational quality, nutrition, etc.
12.The Government shall strictly comply with the provisions prescribed in the Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Department, No.Dhaishap-2019/ PR.No.72/ Education, dated 4/9/2019. Schools that do not comply with these regulations will be disaffiliated.
Steps should be taken to admit new students in classes 1 to 5.
14.As per the decision of the Government, the students of the schools which have been derecognized under the nominated scheme, as well as the students of the government English medium schools and the students of the government English medium schools should be adjusted elsewhere, the Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district, Social Welfare should make such adjustments and submit a report to the Government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune.
(15) While admitting students to a nominated school approved under a nominated scheme, it shall be ensured that the number of students admitted to the school other than the nominated scheme in the class in which the students are to be admitted shall be within the ceiling of the equivalence ratio. (At least 50% of the total number of students enrolled in the program)
2. The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare concerned shall enter into an agreement with the nominated institution where a student of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category is to be admitted. The agreement should include the provision of education, accommodation, security, health, food and other facilities to the students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category from the class admitted under the nominated scheme to class 12 and not to hold separate classes. Further, all the nominated schools selected under the nominated scheme will be scored on the online system to be developed for the scheme before the start of the new academic year every year and if there is any deficiency found in the selected nominated schools or in the required prescribed educational and residential facilities.
