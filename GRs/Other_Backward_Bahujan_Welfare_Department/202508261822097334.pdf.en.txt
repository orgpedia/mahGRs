In 1871, the British government declared certain tribes as criminals under the Criminal Tribes Act. This kept the community within these castes away from the mainstream even after independence. Even after suffering inhumane atrocities in the freedom struggle, there was a delay in justice for the nomadic society after independence, which had a long-term social impact on this society. After independence, the Act was repealed on 31 August 1952.
These castes were declared as "Vimukt Jati" (that is, liberated). Nomadic tribes are groups of social units that traditionally make their living as nomads, rather than living in one place. The nomadic society has been contributing to the progress of the state of Maharashtra and the country. 31st August in his honour in recognition of the contribution of the nomadic society to freedom and nation building.
