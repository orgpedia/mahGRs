Reference No. 1 to the scheme "Admitting students from the" Special Backward Class "Goan community to reputed English medium residential schools in cities" on the lines of ongoing schemes for Scheduled Tribes by the Tribal Development Department. 2 hereby approved by the Government Decision dated 14.05.2025.
It was under the consideration of the government to include additional criteria in the school selection criteria as well as in the student selection criteria as determined under the said government decision. Also, a committee has been constituted under the chairmanship of the Collector to scrutinize the proposals received by the schools as per the said Government decision. However, in view of the additional workload of the Collector, as well as the exorbitant workload of the Collector being the head of several committees, it is also under the consideration of the Government to constitute a Scrutiny Committee under the Chairmanship of the Director, Directorate of Other Backward Classes Bahujan Welfare, Pune instead of a Scrutiny Committee under the Chairmanship of the Collector.

2. Reference No. 2 Additional criteria are being incorporated in the School Selection Criteria as well as in the Student Selection Criteria as determined by the Government Decision dated 14.05.2025 as follows: -
A. Additional standards other than those mentioned in paragraph 7 (a) (1) of the Government Decision, dated 14.05.2025:
1) The result of the school concerned for the last 3 years should be at least 80 percent, personal approval of the teacher from the education department.
2) The schools with the highest score should be selected according to the "School Accreditation Assessment Experiment" being conducted by the Department of School Education.
3) There must be a separate website with complete information about the school.
4) As per the provision of the RTE Act, 2009, it will be mandatory for every school to set up a School Management Committee and provide adequate representation to the parents of the students in the committee.
5) If a student is lagging behind in academic progress, it will be mandatory to appoint a full-time teacher.
6) It will be mandatory for the school to follow all hygiene norms.
7) It will be mandatory for the school to conduct various competitions every half-month to assess the academic progress of the students.
8) The school shall be bound to comply with the Government Decisions / Rules / Conditions as may be enforced by the School Education Department from time to time.
9) Out of the eligible schools that meet the criteria set by the government, schools that score above 90 should be allotted students in the first phase, taking into account their potential as well as other factors.
10) If there are still students left after the first round of student allocation, then the eligible schools should allocate students in the second round taking into account their potential as well as other factors to the schools that have received a score above 80 and the same procedure should be followed till the next round (up to 60 marks) till the completion of the batch of students.
B. Additional criteria other than those mentioned in paragraph 3 of the Government Decision, dated 14.05.2025:
If more than the approved number of applications are received, the following students should be admitted on priority:
1) Children of eligible beneficiaries who have applied under RTE but have not been selected.
