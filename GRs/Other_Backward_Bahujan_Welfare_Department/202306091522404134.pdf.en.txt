The Proposal:
The decision regarding imparting education to the students of Dhangar community in English medium reputed residential schools in the cities has been taken vide Reference No. 1 here. The decision was taken on 4/9/2019. The proposal to approve the admission of students from the Dhangar community in the school selected by the Global Gurukul School, Jalna, operated by the S Research and Education Institute, Jalna, G. Jalna, has been received by the government through reference letters along with the inspection report and scoring report (total score 73). The government was considering to approve the admission of 100 students of Dhangar community for the academic year 2023-24 by selecting Global Gurukul School, Jalna, G. Jalna, operated by S Research and Education Institute, Jalna, subject to the terms and conditions of the relevant government decision.

The following terms and conditions shall be followed while granting admission to a nominated school: (1) The institution/ school concerned shall be required to fulfill the terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated 4/9/2019.
(2) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare, Jalna shall inspect the school in accordance with the Nominated Residential School Scheme and all the students admitted in the school should be registered by verifying their Aadhaar card. After that, the government will take a decision after verifying the number of beneficiary students present in person and the number of direct resident students. 04/09/2019
The detailed report as per the terms and conditions of the same should be submitted to the government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune for the distribution of funds with the approval of the Regional Deputy Commissioner.
(3) The concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare shall ensure that the institution is ready to provide all the facilities mentioned in the proposal submitted to the Government through the concerned institution/ school regarding the student approval under the scheme and shall enter into an agreement with the institution.
(4) Separate for the students of Dhangar community belonging to nomadic tribe-C category through institution/ school
It is not expected to start batches/ classes, it is mandatory to teach the students admitted under the scheme along with other students in the regular batch/ class running in the school

If it is found that a separate batch/ class has been started for the students, action will be taken to register a case against the said institution and the recognition of the school will be cancelled immediately.
(5) It is mandatory to appoint a female superintendent and a caretaker in the girls' hostel admitted in the institution. If the school fails to do so, its recognition will be cancelled.
(6) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall take care to admit students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category only in the schools approved by the Government under this scheme and within the limit of the number of students approved for the school concerned.
(7) As per the original provision of the scheme, the benefit of the scheme should be given only to those children whose annual family income is within the maximum limit of Rs. 1.00 lakh.
(8) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall complete the process of admission of students immediately by giving priority to the nearest school as well as the selected schools of the concerned project while admitting the students.
(9) If the number of applications received for admission under the scheme is more than the number of schools selected in a project area and the number of students approved therein, the Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall announce the selection list by selecting the students through a draw of lots. A list of selected students will also be published.
Students will be admitted to the selected schools.
(10) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district along with the Institute Director/ Headmaster of the nominated schools selected under the scheme shall hold a meeting in the first and second semester of the academic year to review the academic progress of the admitted students and the facilities provided to them.
(11) After the selection of the school under the nominated scheme, the Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district, Social Welfare, should visit the nominated schools in person twice in the first semester and twice in the second semester and submit a report to the government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune, regarding the friendly atmosphere in the school, the facilities provided to the students, the medical treatment, educational quality, nutrition, etc.
(12) The Government decision should be strictly in accordance with the provisions prescribed in the Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Department, S.Dhaishapra-2019/ S.No.72/ Education, dated 4/9/2019. Schools that do not comply with these regulations will be disaffiliated.
(13) Steps should be taken to admit new students in classes 1 to 5.
(14) The students of the schools which have been derecognized under the nominated scheme as per the decision of the Government as well as the schools which have not been upgraded to Government English Medium.

The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare concerned shall enter into an agreement with the nominated institution where a student belonging to the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category is to be admitted. The agreement should include the provision of education, accommodation, security, health, food and other facilities to the students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category from the class admitted under the nominated scheme to class 12 and not to hold separate classes. Further, all the nominated schools selected under the nominated scheme will be rated on the online system to be developed for the scheme before the start of the new academic year every year and in case the selected nominated schools are found to be deficient in the required prescribed educational and residential facilities or if the school is found" ineligible" as per the prescribed rating, the students belonging to Dhangar community belonging to nomadic tribe-C category who have been de-recognized under the nominated scheme of the concerned school will be accommodated elsewhere. This should also be included in the contract.
The schools selected under the nominated scheme must comply with the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 and all rules/ provisions made and made thereunder as well as the provisions set by the Education Department regarding the minimum age of a child for admission to school in Class 1.
The scheme will be implemented by the Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district and the Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare will be the" Supervisory Officer" of the scheme and the Director, Directorate of Other Backward Bahujan Welfare, Pune will be the" Controlling Officer."
