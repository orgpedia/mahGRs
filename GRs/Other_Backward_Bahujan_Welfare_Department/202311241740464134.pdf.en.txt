The following terms and conditions shall be followed while granting admission to a nominated school: (1) The institution/ school concerned shall be required to fulfill the terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated 4/9/2019.
(2) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare, Nanded shall inspect the school in accordance with the Nominated Residential School Scheme and all the students admitted to the school shall be registered by verifying their Aadhaar card. After that, the government will take a decision after verifying the number of beneficiary students present in person and the number of direct resident students. The detailed report as per the terms and conditions of 04/09/2019 should be submitted to the Government through the Director, Directorate of Other Backward Bahujan Welfare, Pune for the disbursement of funds with the approval of the Regional Deputy Commissioner.
(3) The concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare shall ensure that the institution is ready to provide all the facilities mentioned in the proposal submitted to the Government through the concerned institution/ school regarding the student approval under the scheme and shall enter into an agreement with the institution.
(4) The institution/ school is not expected to start a separate batch/ class for the students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category, it will be mandatory to educate the students admitted under the scheme along with other students in the regular batch/ class running in the school. If it is found that a separate batch/ class has been started for the students, action will be taken to register a case against the said institution and the recognition of the school will be cancelled immediately.
(5) It is mandatory to appoint a female superintendent and a caretaker in the girls' hostel admitted in the institution. If the school fails to do so, its recognition will be cancelled.

(6) Under this scheme, students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic Tribe-C category will be admitted only in the schools approved by the government and within the limit of the number of students approved for the concerned school.
The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district should take care of admission.
(7) As per the original provision of the scheme, the benefit of the scheme should be given only to those children whose annual family income is within the maximum limit of Rs. 1.00 lakh.
(8) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall admit the students by giving preference to the nearest school as well as the selected schools of the concerned project.
The admission process should be completed immediately.
(9) If the number of applications received for admission under the scheme is more than the number of schools selected in a project area and the number of students approved therein, the Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall announce the selection list by selecting the students through a draw of lots.
A list of selected students will also be published. Students will be admitted to the selected schools.
(10) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district along with the Institute Director/ Headmaster of the nominated schools selected under the scheme shall hold a meeting in the first and second semester of the academic year to review the academic progress of the admitted students and the facilities provided to them.
(11) After the selection of the school under the nominated scheme, the Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district, Social Welfare, should visit the nominated schools in person twice in the first semester and twice in the second semester and submit a report to the government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune, regarding the friendly atmosphere in the school, the facilities provided to the students, the medical treatment, educational quality, nutrition, etc.
(12) The Government decision should be strictly in accordance with the provisions prescribed in the Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Department, S.Dhaishapra-2019/ S.No.72/ Education, dated 4/9/2019. Schools that do not comply with these regulations will be disaffiliated.
(13) Steps should be taken to admit new students in classes 1 to 5.
(14) The students of the schools which have been derecognized under the nominated scheme as per the decision of the Government, as well as the students of the schools which have not been upgraded to Government English Medium and the students of the schools which have not been upgraded to Government English Medium will be accommodated elsewhere.
Such adjustments should be made by the Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district, Social Welfare and the report should be submitted to the government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune.
(15) The class in which the students are to be admitted at the time of admission to the nominated school approved under the nominated scheme. In such a class, it should be ensured that the number of students admitted other than the nominated scheme in the concerned school remains within the limit of equivalence. (At least 50% of the total number of students enrolled in the program)
