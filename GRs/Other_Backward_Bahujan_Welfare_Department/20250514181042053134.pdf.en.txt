The Proposal: -
Under the Government Decision dated 15.10.2024 under the context of this Department, the implementation of special programmes for the development of the "Goan" community in the Special Backward Classes on the lines of ongoing schemes for the Scheduled Tribes by the Tribal Development Department has been approved subject to the concurrence of the Finance Department. Under this special programme, the guidelines for the implementation of the scheme regarding admission of "Goan" community students to reputed English medium residential schools in cities, eligibility criteria and conditions for the beneficiaries of the scheme have been prescribed. The decision has been taken in the cabinet meeting dated 29.04.20275. Accordingly, the implementation of the scheme was under the consideration of the Government.
The Government's Decisions:
About 6000 students of the "Gowari" community belonging to the Special Backward Classes are being approved by the government to be educated every year from Class I to Class XII in reputed English medium residential schools in nearby cities. For this, the Assistant Director, Other Backward Bahujan Kalyan should seek information through public notice of students who have / wish to take admission in classes I to XII in the villages under their jurisdiction. The student's parents should be informed about the objective and outcome of the proposed scheme. Also, at the level of each MAHA / aided school, the list of children who wish to take advantage of the present scheme, admission forms and consent letters of the parents should be taken by the Assistant Director, Other Backward Classes Bahujan Kalyan. Thereafter, each Assistant Director, Other Backward Bahujan Kalyan shall select a minimum of 100 students and carry out all necessary procedures to ensure that the selected students are admitted to an English medium residential school in the cities of Jabal from the current academic year 2025-26.

6) Students living in residential schools should be allowed to travel to Swagnami to meet their parents twice a year. The Assistant Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare, concerned will be responsible for paying the travel expenses.
