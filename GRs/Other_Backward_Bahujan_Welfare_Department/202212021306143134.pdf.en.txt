The terms and conditions to be followed while giving admission to a nominated school are as follows:
(1) The concerned institution/ school will be required to fulfill the terms and conditions of the Government Decision dated 4/9/2019.
(2) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare, Parbhani shall inspect the concerned school in accordance with the Designated Residential School Scheme. Also, all students enrolled in the school should be enrolled by verifying their Aadhaar card. After that, the government will take a decision after verifying the number of beneficiary students present in person and the number of direct resident students. The detailed report as per the terms and conditions of 04/09/2019 should be submitted to the Government through the Director, Directorate of Other Backward Bahujan Welfare, Pune for disbursement of funds with the approval of Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare.
(3) The concerned Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare shall ensure that the institution is ready to provide all the facilities mentioned in the proposal submitted to the Government through the concerned institution/ school regarding the student approval under the scheme and shall enter into an agreement with the institution.
(4) The institution/ school is not expected to start a separate batch/ class for the students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category, it will be mandatory to educate the students admitted under the scheme along with other students in the regular batch/ class running in the school. If it is found that a separate batch/ class has been started for the students, action will be taken to register a case against the said institution and the recognition of the school will be cancelled immediately.
(5) It is mandatory to appoint a female superintendent and a caretaker in the girls' hostel admitted in the institution. If the school fails to do so, its recognition will be cancelled.

(6) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall take care to admit students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category only in the schools approved by the Government under this scheme and within the limit of the number of students approved for the school concerned.
(7) As per the original provision of the scheme, the benefit of the scheme should be given only to those children whose annual family income is within the maximum limit of Rs. 1.00 lakh.
(8) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall complete the process of admission of students immediately by giving priority to the nearest school as well as the selected schools of the concerned project while admitting the students.
(9) If the number of applications received for admission under the scheme is more than the number of schools selected in a project area and the number of students approved therein, the Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district shall announce the selection list by selecting the students through a draw of lots. A list of selected students will also be published. Students will be admitted to the selected schools.
(10) The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare of the concerned district along with the Institute Director/ Headmaster of the nominated schools selected under the scheme shall hold a meeting in the first and second semester of the academic year to review the academic progress of the admitted students and the facilities provided to them.
(11) After the selection of the school under the nominated scheme, the Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district, Social Welfare, should visit the nominated schools in person twice in the first semester and twice in the second semester and submit a report to the government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune, regarding the friendly atmosphere in the school, the facilities provided to the students, the medical treatment, educational quality, nutrition, etc.
(12) The Government decision should be strictly in accordance with the provisions prescribed in the Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Department, S.Dhaishapra-2019/ S.No.72/ Education, dated 4/9/2019. Schools that do not comply with these regulations will be disaffiliated.
(13) Steps should be taken to admit new students in classes 1 to 5.
(14) The Assistant Commissioner of Social Welfare of the concerned district shall make such adjustments that the students of the schools which have been derecognized under the nominated scheme as per the decision of the Government, as well as the students of the schools which do not have the grade of Government English Medium and the students of the schools which do not have the grade of Government English Medium should be accommodated elsewhere and submit a report to the Government through the Director, Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Directorate, Pune.
(15) The class in which the students are to be admitted at the time of admission to the nominated school approved under the nominated scheme. In such a class, it should be ensured that the number of students admitted other than the nominated scheme in the concerned school remains within the limit of equivalence.
(At least 50% of the total number of students enrolled in the program)

The Assistant Commissioner, Social Welfare concerned shall enter into an agreement with the nominated institution where a student of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category is to be admitted. The agreement should include the provision of education, accommodation, security, health, food and other facilities to the students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribe-C category from the class admitted under the nominated scheme to class 12 and not to hold separate classes. Further, all the nominated schools selected under the nominated scheme will be rated on the online system to be developed for the scheme before the start of the new academic year every year and in case the selected nominated schools are found to be deficient in the required prescribed educational and residential facilities or if the school is found" ineligible" as per the prescribed rating, the students belonging to Dhangar community belonging to nomadic tribe-C category who have been de-recognized under the nominated scheme of the concerned school will be accommodated elsewhere. This should also be included in the contract.
Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 and all rules/ provisions made and made thereunder and the provisions prescribed by the Department of Education regarding minimum age of a child for admission to school in Class 1 must be complied with by the schools selected under the nominated scheme.
The Assistant Commissioner of the concerned district will implement the scheme and the Regional Deputy Commissioner, Social Welfare will be the" Supervisory Officer" of the scheme and the Director, Directorate of Other Backward Bahujan Welfare, Pune will be the" Controlling Officer."
