Introduction: Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Swayam of the Tribal Development Department and the Social Justice Department will be implemented to provide grants for the annual expenses of students who have not been admitted to the hostel.
The cabinet decided to start a scheme for students belonging to Other Backward Classes, Vimukt Jati, Nomadic Tribes and Special Backward Classes on the lines of Dr Babasaheb Ambedkar Swadhar Yojana. This has been approved in the Cabinet Meeting held on 19.10.2023.
Accordingly, the meeting decided to formulate a comprehensive policy to ensure uniformity in various types of schemes such as scholarships, scholarships, hostel and hostel allowance, Swadhar, Swayam, etc. offered by various departments and the schemes proposed in future. The decision was taken by the government.
The decision was taken by the state government under the chairmanship of Chief Minister (CM). As per the decision taken in the meeting held on 01.12.2023, the Other Backward Classes, Vimukt Jati and Nomadic Tribes (Other Backward Castes) Welfare Department is implementing the Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya Swayam Scheme of subsistence allowance for the students of the Dhangar community belonging to the nomadic tribes-C category, who are taking advantage of this scheme.

It was under the consideration of the Government to issue a decision regarding the implementation of the scheme for students of Dhangar community (excluding students belonging to nomadic tribe-C category) and special backward class students pursuing higher education.
Government decision:
On the lines of the Swayam Yojana of the Tribal Development Department and the Swadhar Yojana of the Social Justice and Special Assistance Department, the Other Backward Classes, Vimukt Jati and Nomadic Tribes (excluding the students of the Dhangar community of the Nomadic Tribes-C category who are taking advantage of the scheme/ taking advantage of the Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya Swayam Yojana, since the Other Backward Castes Welfare Department has implemented the scheme of subsistence allowance for the students of the Dhangar community of the Nomadic Tribes-C category) and the students of the Special Backward Classes who are pursuing higher education, are being approved to implement the" Gyanjyoti Savitribai Phule Aadhaar Yojana" for a total of 21,600 students per district.
The amount should be disbursed directly to the bank account of the students with Aadhaar number as specified below for providing food, accommodation and other educational facilities under the scheme.
