Revised Guidelines for Government of India Post Matric Scholarship Scheme issued by Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India, dated 05.05.2022.
The Proposal:
The Government of India is implementing Pre-Matric and Post-Matric Scholarship Schemes through the Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Department to provide opportunities for education to the children belonging to the Deprived Castes, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes, to reduce the dropout rate of students in schools and colleges, and to provide opportunities to meritorious children for higher education.
As on FY 2021-22, the Central and State shareholding for Government of India Pre-Matric Scholarship was 50:50 and for Post-Matric Scholarship was 100%.
The Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India has issued revised guidelines vide letter dated 05.05.2022 for the period 2021-22 to 2025-26 under the Government of India Post Matric Scholarship Scheme for students belonging to Vimukta Jati, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes. Accordingly, the distribution of benefits under the Government of India Pre-Matric and Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme for the students belonging to the Vimukta Jati and Nomadic Tribes, Special Backward Classes and Other Backward Classes in the state from the academic year 2022-23 will be 60% central share and 40% state share. Accordingly, the government is considering to implement the guidelines in the state.
Government's decision:
Issue No.1 of the revised guidelines issued by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India vide letter dated 05.05.2022 for the period 2021-22 to 2025-26 under the Government of India Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme for students belonging to Vimukt Jati, Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Classes.
