Government decision: An employee who has been appointed to the post of Subsidiary in the Primary/ Secondary/ Higher Secondary Ashramshala/ Vidyaniketan/ Ashramshala for the children of sugarcane workers under the Other Backward Bahujan Welfare Department on or after 01/11/2005 and is covered under the Defined Contribution Pension Scheme/ National Pension System, 1. Family Pension and Death Gratuity to his family in case of death in service, 2. Sickness Pension and Service Gratuity is being covered to the sick retired employee.
Accordingly, the family pension/ sickness pension will be applicable to the said family pensioner and sickness pensioner as per the provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982. 3. Service gratuity is also being applied to the employees retiring from Government service.
The provisions of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982 shall continue to apply for the purpose of service gratuity and death gratuity provided as per the above decision.
2. Reference No. 3 mentioned here. The ex-gratia scheme implemented under the Government decision dated 19/08/2019 is being discontinued with effect from the date of this Government decision. Applications received for grant-in-aid should not be considered and pending cases should be closed.
3. d. The families of the employees who died between 01/11/2005 and the date of this Government decision will have to submit the option in Form-3 along with the said Government decision to the Head of the Office to get the death gratuity, family pension arrears and regular family pension. Accordingly, the benefit of family pension will be admissible to the family pensioner on the condition that the amount of ex-gratia grant received by him and the amount of government contribution under the defined pension scheme/ National Pension System is adjusted with interest/ dividend. After this amount is adjusted, the family pension will be admissible to the family pensioner on a regular basis.
In the event of death in service or retirement due to illness, an employee presently working under the National Pension System (NPS) and subsequently appointed in Government service shall submit to the Head of Office the option in Form-2 regarding the family pension or sickness pension admissible under the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982 or the accumulated amount deposited in his Permanent Pension Account Number (PRAN) under the National Pension System to the family or his/ her spouse respectively, as per the orders issued by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) from time to time.
The employees who submit the option of making family pension or sickness pension admissible as per the Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, are required to submit the details of the family as per Sample-1.
All employees covered by the National Pension System shall receive the benefit of the above-mentioned pension scheme within one month from the date of the decision of the Government. 4 options are required. Also, the personnel to be appointed in the government service will be required to submit the options as above within 8 days of being appointed in the government service.
7. Detailed instructions on the procedure to be followed in pursuance of this decision shall be issued separately.
