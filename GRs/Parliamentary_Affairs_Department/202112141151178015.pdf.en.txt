The 3rd Winter Session of the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly, 2021 is being held at Vidhan Bhavan, Mumbai from 22nd December, 2021. Instructions regarding the conduct of the proceedings of the session on priority basis have been issued to all the departments from time to time in the circulars at serial number 2 and 3 in the above context, as well as earlier. Accordingly, the following recommendations have been made.
Starred Questions:- (1) The Legislature Secretariat has developed a computer system called QIS and as per their revised circulars at serial numbers 6 and 7 under reference, instructions have been given to send the copies of the answers to the starred questions online through the QIS system and to transfer the same to other departments online through the same system. These instructions should be strictly followed. If the answers to the questions are not sent in full and within the prescribed time, the relevant answers are not included in the printed list of questions and there is a strong disapproval from the honourable members of the House. Accordingly, in the first session of 2010, instructions were issued to seek clarifications from the Secretaries of the concerned departments. Therefore, all the Ministries/ Departments have taken this matter seriously and prescribed copies of the answers to the starred questions related to them.
Please take care to send it to the Legislature Secretariat within the deadline and send the information to this department in the accompanying return-1 on the next day after the expiry of the relevant due date.

Government Bills:- Government Bills to be discussed and passed in both the Houses of the Legislature. This is a very important issue and the members have raised this issue. The Minister/ Minister of State is expected to give satisfactory answers. Therefore, a self-explanatory note containing complete information regarding the amendments included in the bills to be introduced will be issued by the Secretary of the concerned department. The Minister/ Minister of State should prepare the same before the discussion in the House and send the copy to the Chief Minister's Secretariat and the Department of Parliamentary Affairs for information.
Issues of propriety, special mention, suggestions for deduction:- Hon. The members do not receive replies to the issues of propriety, deduction suggestions and special mentions raised by them within the stipulated time and objections are often raised by them. Therefore, the questions of propriety, deduction suggestions and special mentions are answered by Ma. Members should be sent within the stipulated time. Also, the answers to the most urgent questions should be sent to the Legislature Secretariat within the prescribed time. Accordingly, since the meeting of the Ad-hoc Committee on' Laying of Papers on the Table of the House" of the Legislative Assembly has taken a serious note, the proceedings in respect of the above matters should be taken up on priority and within the stipulated time frame. Also, the information received in the current session should be sent to the Department of Parliamentary Affairs immediately after the announcement of the end of the session in the accompanying statement-2. Since the information on deduction suggestions is being compiled by the Finance Department, it must be ensured that the information on deduction suggestions is sent to the Budget-3 Branch of the Finance Department and the same is recorded on the e-review system without fail.
6. Non-Governmental Business:-Department under the Department of Parliamentary Affairs. In the Government Resolution dated 2 December 1998, No. Procedure for passing of non-government Bills/ Resolutions has been detailed under Section 15. All departments should ensure that action is taken as per the instructions given in this procedure.
In this regard, it is mentioned that if the non-government business (Bills/ Resolutions) which did not take place on the scheduled day is shown on another day in the same session and if there is no change in the facts and details in the original proposal of the Bill/ Resolution in respect of which the Cabinet has taken a decision and no change is required to be made in the proposal and details taken earlier before the Cabinet, then the proposal regarding such Bill/ Resolution will not be required to be submitted again for the approval of the Cabinet.
Annual Report:- As per the instructions given under the Government Circular, Department of Parliamentary Affairs, No. Insurance-2008/ No. 59/ 5, dated 15th September 2008, the annual report of the business, boards, corporations, autonomous bodies, etc. adopted by the Government should be submitted to the Legislature in the prescribed manner and within the prescribed time. In this regard, due serious consideration is being taken in the meetings of the Ad-hoc Committee of the Legislative Assembly on" Laying of Documents on the Table of the House," all the concerned departments have been directed to ensure the compliance of the directions.

Proper action regarding the tabling of annual reports of boards, corporations, undertakings, autonomous bodies etc. related to them should be taken on priority and if there is a delay in the submission of the report, the reasons should be reported to the Legislature Secretariat.
Note on issues/ assurances/ directions in the House.- The issue of any department may be raised by the Hon'ble Members in the proceedings of both the Houses of the Legislature and the Council at any time and accordingly, the Hon'ble Chairman may refer the matter to the Chairman. Instructions are given by the presiding officers for action. Therefore, in order to expedite action as per the said direction, all the departments are being instructed to ensure that the officers of their departments are present in the galleries of both the Houses of the Legislative Assembly and the Legislative Council on a daily basis. The directions given by the presiding officers, Mr. The Minister shall direct the officers of the concerned offices of his department to take note of the assurances given in the House and report the same on the same day so that the concerned can take further action without delay.
All the officers may kindly ensure that as per the above instructions, all the Ministries/ Departments may kindly give serious and all-out attention to the legislative business and the replies to the issues raised by the Hon'ble Members through various instruments shall be coherent, complete and comprehensive and the same shall be forwarded to the Legislature Secretariat/ respective Hon'ble Members within the stipulated time.
