Government circular
The 2nd (Monsoon Session) of the Maharashtra Legislature 2021 has been convened at Vidhan Bhavan, Mumbai from Monday, 05.07.2021. The issue of any department in relation to any matter can be raised at any time in the proceedings of both the Houses of the Legislature. Therefore, the presence of the secretaries of the departments at the headquarters as well as in the gallery of the House of the Legislature, if necessary, is mandatory for immediate action on the relevant issues. During the legislature session, on the day when the House has business related to their departments, discussion and voting on supplementary demands and other important business, the secretary of the concerned department must be present in the secretary gallery of the House and the nearest senior officer in the other House. Also, information about the issues that will be discussed in the House. Care should be taken that the Minister/ Minister of State will be made available immediately.
In view of the above, all the Additional Chief Secretaries/ Principal Secretaries/ Secretaries are required to be present at Mumbai during the period of the session. But if, for some unavoidable reason, he is compelled to go on a tour outside Mumbai, he should take prior permission from me and his concerned ministers and appoint an officer responsible for the conduct of legislative business in his absence.
Starred/ Unstarred Questions, Calling Attention Notices, Notices under Rule 93 of the Maharashtra Legislative Council, Adjournment Motions, Discussion of propriety in the House of the Legislature and the Legislative Council.

The time limit for action by the Government in respect of matters raised through Parliamentary Articles such as issues, special mentions, etc. is prescribed by the Legislative Assembly and Legislative Council Rules. However, in this case, similarly, the House expresses disapproval of the Hon'ble Members for not acting on the assurances given by the Government within the stipulated time. Also, in this regard. Presiding officers, from time to time, in meetings or through letters, direct the government to take strict action against the concerned. In this background, Hon'ble Members should ensure that the matters raised by various Parliamentary arms are disposed of within the prescribed time frame and that no such matters remain pending.
In view of the facts mentioned in the aforesaid paragraph, all the Additional Chief Secretaries/ Principal Secretaries/ Secretaries shall personally ensure that the replies to the matters raised by the Hon'ble Members through various instruments are complete and comprehensive and that the same are forwarded to the Legislature Secretariat/ respective Hon'ble Members within the prescribed time and the Assurance Reports are submitted within the prescribed time.
Please note that if any matter raised through various Parliamentary instruments and the time limit set out above is pending beyond the said time limit, strict action will be taken against the Secretary of the concerned department.
Be sure to follow the instructions above.
