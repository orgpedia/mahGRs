The ensuing Winter Session of the Maharashtra Legislature 2022 is scheduled to commence from Monday, the 19 th December, 2022 at Vidhan Bhavan, Nagpur. Instructions regarding the conduct of the proceedings of the session on priority basis have been issued to all the departments from time to time through circulars at serial numbers 2 and 3 in the said context. Accordingly, the following recommendations have been made.
Starred Questions:- (1) The Legislature Secretariat has developed a computer system called QIS and has given instructions for sending the copies of the answers to the starred questions online through the QIS system and transferring the same to other departments online through the same system. These instructions should be strictly followed. If the answers to the questions are not submitted in full and within the prescribed time, the answers are not included in the printed list of questions and there is a strong disapproval from the honourable members of the House. The issue was also discussed in the parliamentary board meeting. Accordingly, in the first session of 2010, instructions were issued to seek clarifications from the Secretaries of the concerned departments. Therefore, all the Ministries/ Departments may kindly take this matter seriously and ensure that the copies of the answers to the starred questions relating to them are sent to the Legislature Secretariat within the stipulated time.

For carrying out the process of placing the accepted suggestions on the table of the House before the commencement of the session, the statements on the suggestions that have not been discussed in the House should be sent to the Legislature Secretariat one day before the end of the session, and the information regarding the same should be sent to this Department in the accompanying statement-2 within one week after the end of the session.

Government Bills:- It is very important that government bills are discussed and passed in both houses of the legislature. The Minister/ Minister of State is expected to give satisfactory answers. Therefore, a self-explanatory note containing complete information regarding the amendments included in the bills to be introduced will be issued by the Secretary of the concerned department. The Minister/ Minister of State should prepare the same before the discussion in the House and send the copy to the Chief Minister's Secretariat and the Department of Parliamentary Affairs for information.

6. Non-Governmental Business:-Department under the Department of Parliamentary Affairs. In the Government Resolution dated 2 December 1998, No. Procedure for passing of non-government Bills/ Resolutions has been detailed under Section 15. All departments should ensure that action is taken as per the instructions given in this procedure.
In this regard, it is mentioned that if the non-government business (Bills/ Resolutions) which did not take place on the scheduled day is shown on another day in the same session and if there is no change in the facts and details in the original proposal of the Bill/ Resolution in respect of which the Cabinet has taken a decision and no change is required to be made in the proposal and details taken earlier before the Cabinet, then the proposal regarding such Bill/ Resolution will not be required to be submitted again for the approval of the Cabinet.
Annual Report:- As per the instructions given under the Government Circular, Department of Parliamentary Affairs, No. Insurance-2008/ No. 59/ 5, dated 15th September 2008, the annual report of the business, boards, corporations, autonomous bodies, etc. adopted by the Government should be submitted to the Legislature in the prescribed manner and within the prescribed time. In this regard, as the meetings of the Ad-hoc Committee of the Legislative Assembly on" Placing Documents on the Table of the House" are being taken seriously, all the concerned departments should take appropriate action on the timely placing of the annual reports of their respective boards, corporations, enterprises, autonomous bodies, etc. on the table of the House on priority, and if there is a delay in the submission of the report, the reasons should be reported to the Legislature Secretariat.
Note on issues/ assurances/ directions in the House.- The issue of any department may be raised by the Hon'ble Members in the proceedings of both the Houses of the Legislature and the Council at any time and accordingly, the Hon'ble Chairman may refer the matter to the Chairman. Instructions are given by the presiding officers for action. Therefore, in order to expedite action as per the said direction, all the departments are being instructed to ensure that the officers of their departments are present in the galleries of both the Houses of the Legislative Assembly and the Legislative Council on a daily basis. the directions given by the presiding officers, as well.
The Hon'ble Minister may direct to take note of the assurances given in the House and to communicate the information regarding the same to the officers of the concerned offices of his department on the same day so that the concerned can take further action without delay.
During the session, the coordination officer of the department should receive the schedule of the Legislative Council/ Assembly House on the next day to be published by the Legislature Secretariat on the previous day and accordingly, keeping in mind the business of the House, the ministers of the department and the concerned officers of the department should be present in the gallery during the work of the House.
All the officers may kindly ensure that as per the above instructions, all the Ministries/ Departments should attend to the affairs of the Legislature in all earnestness and on all priority and the replies to the issues raised by the Hon'ble Members through various instruments shall be coherent, complete and comprehensive and the same shall be forwarded to the Secretariat of the Legislature/ to the Hon'ble Members concerned within the stipulated time.
Information about the above weapons is available from time to time. The Chief Secretary's Maharashtra e-Samiksha system should be updated without fail.
