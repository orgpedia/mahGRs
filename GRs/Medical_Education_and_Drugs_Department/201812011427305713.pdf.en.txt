The Proposal:
Ashtanga Ayurveda Mahavidyalaya, Pune is a private government-aided Ayurveda institution run by the Board of Education, Pune. The college offers BAMS (Ayurveda) degree course of five and a half years duration as prescribed by the Central Council of Indian Medicine, New Delhi.
During the financial year 2003-04, the Government has decided to sanction 100% wage subsidy to the private Government aided Ayurveda and Unani Institutions in the State with effect from January, 2004. Accordingly, Ashtanga Ayurveda Mahavidyalaya, Pune has been awarded Rs. 1,65,485/- is being paid as recurring subsidy. Accordingly, the matter of sanctioning grant to the institution was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The Government have decided to provide 100% grant-in-aid to the aided Ayurveda and Unani colleges and affiliated hospitals run by private institutions in the State and to provide subsidy for the salary expenditure of teachers and non-teaching staff as per the Government Decision, Medical Education and Medicines Department No.ADR-MUMS/ 2002/ PR.No. 116/02/ AYUSH-2, dated 05/12/2003 with effect from January, 2004. Accordingly, the final instalment of the grant-in-aid for the years 2007-08 to 2016-2017 of the private government-aided Ashtanga Ayurveda College, Pune has been completed and the final instalment of the grant-in-aid for the year 2016-2017 has been increased to Rs. An amount of Rs. 1,65,485/- (Rupees One Lakh Sixty Five Hundred and Forty Five only) is being sanctioned under this Government Resolution.
