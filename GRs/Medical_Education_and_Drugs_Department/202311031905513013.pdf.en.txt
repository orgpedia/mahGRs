circular
The Election Commission of India is mandated to verify and rectify the errors in the existing electoral rolls under the Special Summary Revision Programme every year. The programme of updating the electoral roll by registering new voters as per the eligibility date of January 1 is implemented. Election Commission of India. The Special Summary Revision Programme, 2024 has been announced vide letter dated 29/05/2023.
Special Summary Revision Programme to be launched in 2024. The programme is being organized to revise the electoral roll by enrolling new voters who will complete 18 years of age on January 1, 2024.
The citizens who will complete 18 years of age on or before the four qualifying dates of January 1, April 1, July 1 and October 1, 2024, will be able to register their names in the electoral roll in advance. Under this special brief review programme, The draft electoral rolls of all the Assembly Constituencies of the State will be published on 17/10/2023 and the 17/10/2023 to D. Claims and objections will be accepted from 30/11/2023.
The Additional Chief Secretary and Chief Electoral Officer, Maharashtra State have been requested to take up the initiative of enrolling the students of all the health science courses of Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Deemed Universities, AIIMS as voters in the electoral rolls under the Special Summary Revision Programme, 2024.
In pursuance of the special summary revision programme of electoral rolls, the following activities should be undertaken by Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Deemed Universities, Colleges of all Health Sciences courses at AIIMS:
The University and its affiliated college students should be informed about the Special Summary Revision Programme, 2024 by the Electoral Literacy Board.
