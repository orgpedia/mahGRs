The Cabinet in its meeting held on 25.04.2017 has approved the establishment of" Government Integrated Medical Education Hub" at Jalgaon in view of the shortage of doctors in the State as per WHO standards, Jalgaon district being comparatively backward in providing medical facilities in the State and to promote interdisciplinary research in modern and ancient Indian systems of medicine and to provide quality medical education and health facilities for Jalgaon and other districts of North Maharashtra. Approval has been granted under Government Resolution No. 1 for creation of posts, construction, equipment and maintenance, recurring expenditure and out-source expenditure as per the standards to start a Government Medical College with 100 student intake capacity and an attached 500-bed hospital. The Government Medical College, Government of India, is a Medical College affiliated to Medical Council of India. As per the decision of the Government of India, 109 posts have been approved for the first year. Director No. As proposed under letters 3 and 4, the Government was considering the proposal to approve the creation of 128 posts in the Government Medical College, Jalgaon, for the first year, which had not been approved by the Government, as well as year-wise and designation-wise for the second, third and fourth years.

Government's decision:
For Government Medical College at Jalgaon with an intake capacity of 100 students, approval is being given for creation of posts in total 3 phases as per prospectus A.
The remaining posts of the first year, second, third and fourth phases mentioned in the prospectus-A will be sanctioned in a phased manner and the next phase of posts will be created on April 1 every year.
2. The next stage of salary should be provided in the annual budget of the organization.
3. The posts in the following phases should be included in the proposal for extension of temporary posts every year.
