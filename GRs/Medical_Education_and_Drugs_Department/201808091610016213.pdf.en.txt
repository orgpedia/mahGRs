Preamble: The National Health Policy, 2017 envisages provision of quality primary health care services to the people through Health and Wellness Centres. In February 2018, the Government of India decided to convert 1.5 lakh primary health centres and sub-centres across the country into Arogyavardhini Kendras. Out of these, 12415 Arogyavardhini Kendras are to be set up in the state of Maharashtra.
Even after conversion of Primary Health Centres and Sub Centres into Arogyavardhini Kendras, the existing Primary Health Centres and Sub Centres will continue to provide services for family welfare, maternal and child health care, planning and treatment of communicable diseases, non-communicable diseases, ear, nose and throat diseases, vision related diseases, accidents etc. Middle Level Service Provider (Middle Level) to Ayurvedic degree holders in Arogyavardhini Kendras in the State of Maharashtra

A proposal was under the consideration of the government to approve the implementation of a course through the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences to train the Ayurveda graduates to provide all the services expected under the Arogyavardhini scheme. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government Decision: With a view to impart training to Ayurveda graduates to be appointed as mid-level service providers under Arogyavardhini Yojana in the State of Maharashtra, a six-month course titled" Modern Mid-Level Service Provider Course" is being approved by the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences with a view to imparting training on rendering all the services required under the scheme.
The following terms and conditions are prescribed for the implementation of this course: 1) The course should be started in Government Institutions under the Public Health Department with a minimum of 200 beds with an intake capacity of 50.
The syllabus of the said course should be prepared by the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences after considering the requirements of the office under the National Health Mission.
The duration of the training will be 6 months.
4) The fee for this course should be fixed by the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences.
Ayurveda practitioners who are authorized by the Commissioner (Health Services) and registered with the Maharashtra Bharatiya Chikitsa Parishad will be eligible for admission to this course.
The candidate who successfully passes this course should be awarded a certificate of completion of the Modern Middle Level Service Provider course by the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Nashik.
(7) The said candidates will be eligible for post-training services in the State of Maharashtra only and at Arogyavardhini Kendras.
