Regarding the delay in the post-mortem viscera report at Group Hospitals, Mumbai, under the heading" Delay in Viscera Report" (3000 reports pending in six years, police investigation stalled) (Situation at JJ Hospital), a report published in Dainik Maharashtra Times has been published. As per the news published on 21.01.2019, Shri. Maharashtra State Human Rights Commission in Suo-Moto Case No. MAS/ Suo-Motu- 20/13/16/ 2019 has been filed.
Regarding the above. The Maharashtra State Human Rights Commission has taken a suo motu case against the government. A High Level Committee under the Chairmanship of Director, Medical Education and Research, Mumbai was constituted on 28.11.2019. In this regard, the matter of examining the post-mortem viscera of Grant Government Medical College and Sir J. G. Group of Hospitals, Mumbai and the division of work was under the consideration of the Government to take action as per the recommendation of the said Committee. The government has taken the following decisions:
# Page 2
Government Resolution No: MAHA 2019/ P.No. 124/ P.S.-2, D. 22.12.2020
Government's decision:
As per the order of the Maharashtra State Human Rights Commission, in order to avoid the delay in submitting the report of the viscera examination in the state, the following decisions are being taken regarding the examination of post-mortem viscera and the division of work.
1) The examination of post-mortem viscera is being divided between the Government Medical College under the Medical Education and Drugs Department of Mumbai and the Medical College under the Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation according to the jurisdiction of that police station. (Annexure A).
2) To avoid delay in submission of viscera test report, police stations have been divided on the lines of district-wise medical colleges and viscera test should be conducted in government medical colleges in the district. (Annexure-B)
This Government decision is being issued in accordance with the approval given by the Home Department and the feedback given by the Urban Development Department under Informal Reference No. 2020/ No. 122/ NO.
