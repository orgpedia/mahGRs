The following terms and conditions should be followed while taking action to start generic medicine shops through NACOF in the above institutions.
Administrative Terms and Conditions: (1) The Commissioner of Medical Education shall be responsible for determining the area of land, the lease of land, the period of lease and the terms and conditions for making available space to NACOF India in the institutions under the Directorate of Medical Education and Research.
(2) The Dean, Government Medical College and Hospital shall enter into an appropriate agreement with the institution concerned as per rules.

3) In case of non-availability of generic drugs, it is mandatory to provide at least 5% discount on the face value of branded drugs in case other branded or other drugs are required to be made available by the concerned institution.
4) Generic medicine shops should be provided space as per the prevailing rules of the Food and Drug Administration Department.
(b) Technical terms and conditions: (1) A Technical Committee shall be constituted consisting of the Department of Pharmacology of the Government Medical College and Hospital concerned, the Head of the Drug Store, the Administrative Officer and a representative of the Department of Drug Administration. The committee will review the functioning of the generic medicine shops run by the concerned institution every 6 months and submit a report to the government through the Commissioner, Medical Education.
2) The place where the pharmaceutical business is proposed to be carried out as per the rules of the Food and Drug Administration Department. A license for the same place must be obtained.
Since these generic drug stores are meant to be open 24 hours, it will be mandatory to appoint at least three registered drug manufacturers for the purchase and sale of medicines in the drug store. According to the government policy, priority should be given to disabled persons and women.
4) It will be mandatory for the drug manufacturer appointed by the institute to be registered with the Pharmacy Council of the state and update the registration from time to time.
5) It will be mandatory to have enough coolers to store medicines and other items at a certain temperature as per the rules of the Food and Drug Administration Department and to fulfill other requirements as per Schedule-P.
6) As per the rules of the Food and Drug Administration, the stock of narcotics and psychotropic substances should be sealed separately and their documents should be strictly followed.
7) According to the rules of the Department of Food and Drug Administration, there must be an air-conditioning system to store enough space.
8) Medicines which are on government contract but need to be purchased from outside along with medicines which are not supplied on contract, such medicines should also be made available from this shop.
9) When selling medicines, it will be mandatory to follow the price as per the rules of the National Pharmaceutical Pricing Authority.
10) Every medicine that comes out of the drug store must be kept in a record. The records and physical stocks in such registers shall be periodically verified by the Technical Committee.
