Introduction: Frequent attacks on doctors in government medical colleges and affiliated hospitals and vandalism of hospitals by relatives of hospitals. Petition filed in Bombay High Court 77/2015 (Ashfaq Mandaviya v. State of Maharashtra) regarding measures to be taken for the safety of doctors. The order was upheld by the Bombay High Court. Accordingly, the Department had sanctioned the services of 1100 Security Guards from Maharashtra Security Corporation for 14 Government Medical Colleges and Hospitals as on 31.03.2017. A total of 1088 security personnel have been provided by the Maharashtra State Security Corporation in 14 government medical colleges and hospitals. The Director, Maharashtra State Security Corporation, Mumbai vide letter dated 29.12.2018 has decided that as per the decision taken in the meeting of the Corporation held on 15.11.2018, with effect from 01.04.2019, the salary of the security personnel of Maharashtra State Security Corporation will be Rs. 3000/- per month (for all ranks), it was informed that the revised rates would be applicable from 01.04.2019. Accordingly, the Directorate has proposed to increase the honorarium of the said security guards by Rs.3000/-. The above was submitted to the Government vide letter dated 15.02.2019. Accordingly, Rs.
3000/- was being considered by the Government.

Government's decision:
Medical Education Department No. 3 As per the Government decision dated 30.03.2017, a total of 1088 security guards made available by the Maharashtra State Security Corporation in 14 Government Medical Colleges and Hospitals have been provided with Rs. 3000/- is being approved for payment of honorarium payable as per the enhanced rates. It is also being approved to enter into an agreement with the Maharashtra State Security Corporation on the revised rates by the concerned agencies.
The said Government Decision is being issued in accordance with the financial powers vested in the Administrative Department under the Finance Department, Government Decision dated 17.04.2015, Fiscal Powers Rules Part I, Sub-Division-4, No. 11.
