A new Government Medical College at Sindhudurg with an intake capacity of 100 students and upgradation of the District General Hospital attached to it to 500-bed hospital.
The Government of Maharashtra
Department of Medical Education and Drugs
GOVERNMENT DECISION NO.: MED 2016/ PR No. 231/16/ Education 1
9th Floor, Go to Hospital Complex, Lokmanya Tilak Marg, New Mantralaya, Mumbai 400 001 Dated: 8th December, 2020.
Introduction: According to the World Health Organization standards, there should be 1 doctor for every 1000 population. At present, the ratio of doctors per 1000 population in the state is 0.64. Due to the shortage of doctors in the state, most of the posts of doctors in the medical education department and public health department are vacant. As a result, the people in the semi-urban, rural, remote and inaccessible areas of the state face difficulties in providing basic health facilities. Considering the increase in the population of the state and the awareness about medical education and health facilities, it is necessary to start a new government medical college.
Also, Sindhudurg district is comparatively backward in terms of medical facilities available in the state. Therefore, the government was considering the construction of a new government medical college in Sidhudurg. Accordingly, the proposal. It was submitted to the Cabinet for approval. It is now dated 24.11.2020. This was decided at the Cabinet meeting. The government has taken the following decisions:
The Government hereby approves the establishment of a new Government Medical College at Sindhudurg with an intake capacity of 100 students and a 500-bed hospital by upgrading the District General Hospital attached to it, subject to the approval of the Central Government and the National Medical Commission.
2. Creation of posts in various cadres for these colleges as shown in the statement A attached to this Government decision by making budgetary provision in the prescribed manner.

Government Decision No.: MED 2016/ Q. No. 231/16/ Education 1 is being allowed to be made and filled. This action does not require the approval of a high-level secretarial committee.
(3) The creation and filling up of posts in various cadres as indicated in the statement' B' attached to this Government Resolution for the affiliated hospitals attached to the said colleges is being allowed in the prescribed manner by making budgetary provision. This action also does not require the approval of the High-Level Secretarial Committee.
Necessary and additional construction and construction cost for these colleges and affiliated hospitals are being approved as shown in the statement" C."
The total estimated expenditure for creation of posts, construction, maintenance of equipment and equipment, recurring expenditure and out-of-turn expenditure as per National Medical Commission (NMC) norms at the site of the said college and affiliated hospitals is Rs. 966.08 crore (Non-recurring expenditure Rs. 692.00 crore and recurring expenditure of Rs. 274.08 crore) is being approved for making available and spending as shown in the Statement D. Similarly, it is being approved to provide funds through supplementary demand, if required. Director, Medical Education and Research, Mumbai to make necessary budgetary provisions for the financial year 2021-22 to 2024-25.
6. All the activities required for these colleges and affiliated hospitals, such as sanitation, food, safety, clothing and hygiene, are being allowed to be carried out through external sources.
These works should be done with the prior approval of the government by appointing contractors through a public tender process.
For the new Government Medical College and Hospital, it is being approved to transfer the District General Hospital under the Public Health Department along with movable and immovable property (with a minimum of 20 acres of land) to the Department of Medical Education and Medicines free of cost as per the standards of the National Medical Commission.
The Director, Directorate of Medical Education and Research, Mumbai shall take necessary action to start the said college from the academic year 2021-22.
This Government Decision is in accordance with the opinion of the Department of Finance in Informal Reference No.- 454/2020/ Expenditure-13, dated C- 23.11.2020. This is being issued with the approval of the Cabinet dated 24.11.2020.
