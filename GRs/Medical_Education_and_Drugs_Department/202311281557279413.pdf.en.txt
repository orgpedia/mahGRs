Preamble: Government Reference No. 1 As per the Government decision dated 03.01.2023, approval has been accorded for establishment of Government Medical College with intake capacity of 100 students and 500 bedded hospital at Baramati. The government was considering allowing private hospitals to open their own private accounts to deposit in-patient fees, out-patient fees, laboratory fees and other amounts collected in the hospitals.
Government Decision: The Government Medical College and Hospital, Baramati has decided to deduct inter-patient fees, out-patient fees, laboratory fees and other amounts collected from the institution for making allowable expenditure therefrom and in terms of Reference No. 2 The Government hereby approve the opening of a Personal Loan Account (PLA) in the name of the Dean, Government Medical College and Hospital, Baramati, as per the provisions of Rule 494 of the Maharashtra Treasury Rules, 1968, Volume-I, to meet the expenditure for the matters prescribed under the above Government Decision dated 04.03.2020.
2.It will be mandatory to follow the guidelines and financial rules set by the government from time to time while using the funds deposited in this account. It will also be necessary to comply with the provisions of the Maharashtra Treasury Rules.

The amount in the said account shall be spent in accordance with the financial powers conferred on it for various purposes in accordance with the Government Decision, Finance Division No. VYPR-2013/ P.S. 30/2013/ Regulations, Part-II, dated 17.04.2015 and amendments thereto from time to time.
The Drawing and Disbursing Officer of the concerned institution will be responsible for monthly/ quarterly reconciliation of the amount deposited and spent on this account with the Accountant General's Office. A detailed monthly statement will be submitted to the Commissioner, Directorate of Medical Education and Research through the institute. A quarterly report will be submitted to the government by the directorate after examining it.
The Dean, Government Medical College and Hospital, Baramati shall be responsible for ensuring that the transactions on this account are carried out in accordance with the government/ financial rules and for maintaining proper control over this account.
6. It is necessary to carry out regular transactions on the self-financing account. It shall be the responsibility of the Dean, Government Medical College and Hospital, Baramati, to ensure that the said account is not closed due to non-transaction.
7. The financial transactions on this account need to be regularly audited.
