The Proposal:
The Department of Finance, Government of India, vide its letter dated 12th April, 2023, has been directed to adopt the revised methodology as prescribed under the guidelines dated 02nd May, 2023 of the Government of India for effective management of funds of Centrally Sponsored Schemes and to bring more efficiency in the process of fund allocation. Accordingly, special assistance in the form of loan (non-interest loan) for capital investment expenditure to be implemented in the state through the Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals with the financial assistance of the Central Government, by mapping this scheme on the Public Financial Management System (PFMS), opening a separate Nodal Bank Account (Parent Account) in the authorized bank of the Single Nodal Agency (SNA) for the implementation of this scheme and opening a Child Account for the implementing agencies were under the consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
For the implementation of the ongoing/ future centrally sponsored schemes under the Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals, the Government of this Department has issued Decision No. SANKIRNA-2021/ PR.No. 72/2021/ ALEP, dated 03.08.2021, the State Scheme Manager (SSM) has been appointed as Nodal Officer along with Joint Secretary/ Deputy Secretary (Administration), Department of Medical Education and Drugs and Director, Directorate of Medical Education and Research, Mumbai as Single Nodal Agency (SNA).

Proposals were invited from various banks in the above context. The Bank of Maharashtra is being selected to open a Single Nodal Bank Account and Child Account for the scheme after examining the proposal received from the bank. In this regard, Bank of Maharashtra will provide full technical support from time to time through specialized officers for training related to Public Financial Management System (PFMS) related to this scheme, assistance in opening accounts, mapping and smooth handling of the process for transfer of funds to the central and state implementing agencies. Subject to this condition, it is hereby approved to open a separate Single Nodal Bank Account (Parent Account) for the concerned scheme of the Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals and Child Account for all the implementing agencies in Bank of Maharashtra, Mantralaya Branch, Mumbai as per their proposal.
Under the Central Government's Scheme for Special Assistance as Loan to State for Capital Investment, funds will be made available to the State Government for specific works under Part-1 (Capital Investment). The Finance Department has suggested that the fund distribution and utilization management of the fund should be done through the Central Public Financial Management System (PFMS). Accordingly, it will be appropriate to take a decision regarding the opening of a separate account head for the implementation of these schemes, as well as a separate Nodal Bank Account (Parent Account) in the authorized bank by mapping the scheme with the PFMS and a Child Account for the implementation mechanism. However, in accordance with the revised guidelines, the decision is being taken on the basis of the following terms and conditions.
These schemes will be mapped through Public Financial Management System (PFMS).
2) Under this decision, it is being approved to open a separate SNA Bank Account and Child Account of all the implementing agencies in Bank of Maharashtra, Mantralaya Branch, Mumbai.
3) The SNA account and zero balance sub-account should be linked to the PFMS system.
4) This account should be used only for the purpose of the said schemes.
As per the guidelines of the Central Government, the funds of the scheme should be deposited in the concerned State Scheme Manager (SSM) level SNA (Parent Account) in a centralized manner and the implementing agency should be given the drawing limits through the Public Financial Management System (PFMS) without transferring the necessary funds to the Child Account.
Necessary training and technical support for the implementation of the scheme will be provided by Bank of Maharashtra.
