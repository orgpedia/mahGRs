In view of the possible third wave of COVID-19, the outbreak of Mucormycosis epidemic in pediatric and general patients, Director, Medical Education, Mumbai, Dr. Purchase of drugs/ surgical items amounting to Rs. 98,92,96, 958/-as per the proposal submitted vide letter dated 22.07.2021. The matter of giving administrative approval to the proposal of expenditure to be made by Haffkine Corporation, Mumbai was under consideration of the government.
Government's decision:
Under the article 22103114 of the Director, Medical Education and Research for COVID-19 measures, (21) Material and Supply Objectives, in the Monsoon Session of July 2021, Rs. 18.00.00 crore has been sanctioned through Supplementary Demand. Accordingly, as proposed by the Director, Medical Education and Research, Mumbai, Rs. Administrative approval is being accorded for procurement of medicines/ surgical items mentioned in Annexure-A and Annexure-B by Haffkine Corporation for an amount of Rs. 98,92,96, 958/- (Rupees Eighty Nine Crore Ninety Two Lakh Six Thousand Nine Hundred and Fifty Eight only) subject to the following terms and conditions.
Terms and Conditions: 1) As per the Government decision dated 26.07.2017 of the Public Health Department, it has been made mandatory to purchase the medicinal and surgical materials, chemicals and equipment etc. required by the Government Medical Colleges and Hospitals and Institutions under the Department of Medical Education and Pharmaceuticals from Haffkine Jeeva Pharmaceuticals. It should be purchased.
(ii) The requirement/ demand for the said medicinal and surgical materials, chemicals and equipments etc. has been ascertained by the Directorate.
It has been ensured that the specifications of the said medicinal and surgical materials, chemicals and equipment etc. are comprehensive and comprehensive, so that three tenders can be received from at least three manufacturers/ suppliers who can supply the same.

4) In this regard, the Government Decision No. 2014/ Q. 82/ Part/ Industry-4, issued by the Department of Industry, Energy and Labor, titled" Manual of Procedures for Office Procurement to be made by the Government Department." The prescribed procedure as mentioned in the Government Order dated 01.12.2016 and the corresponding Government Decision should be followed.
5) Procurement of these items should be done through GeM portal/ Mahatender.
6) It should be ensured that the items proposed for purchase are not left unused and are used within the prescribed time.
The technical specifications of the said items are required to be certified by the Director, Medical Education and Research, Mumbai from the National Institute of Virology, Pune (NIV)/ National Institute of Reproductive Health Research, Mumbai (NIRRH).
8) It should be ensured that there is no duplication of purchase matters.
9) The Director of Medical Education should immediately report the demand for urgent matters to the Haffkine Corporation. Also, the demand for items required every month should be registered with the Haffkine Corporation at least 15 days in advance.
10) Haffkine Corporation shall issue supply orders on a monthly basis as per the demand received per month from the Director, Medical Education and Research, in respect of the above items, without placing supply orders at the same time to the supplier identified by GeM/ Mahatender for the purchase of the same.
11) Necessary further action should be taken immediately to obtain the said medicine and related matters from Haffkine Biopharmaceutical Corporation.
Under no circumstances should the expenditure be more than the limit of available funds.
13) The Director, Medical Education, Mumbai vide his letter dated 2210-3314 has sanctioned Rs. 98,92,96, 958/- should be immediately transferred to Haffkine Corporation.
14) Haffkine Corporation should publish the tender every time considering the stock of medicines and surgical items available and the expiry date.
15) The Director of Medical Education and Research, Mumbai shall make an advance requisition of the items to be supplied by the Public Health Department to the Directorate of Health Services.
