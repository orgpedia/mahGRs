The Proposal:
Aryagala Medical College, Satara is a private government-aided Ayurveda institution run by the Ayurveda Prasarak Mandali, Pune. The college offers BAMS (Ayurveda) degree course of five and a half years duration as prescribed by the Central Council of Indian Medicine, New Delhi with an intake capacity of 50 and a sanctioned hospital bed capacity of 120.
During the financial year 2003-04, the Government has decided to sanction 100% wage subsidy to the private Government aided Ayurveda and Unani Institutions in the State with effect from January, 2004. Accordingly, Aryagala Medical College, Satara has been awarded Rs. 1,99,207/- is being paid as recurring subsidy. Accordingly, the matter of sanctioning grant to the institution was under consideration of the Government.
Government's decision:
The Government have decided to provide 100% grant-in-aid to the aided Ayurveda and Unani colleges and affiliated hospitals run by private institutions in the State and to provide subsidy for the salary expenditure of teachers and non-teaching staff as per the Government Decision, Medical Education and Medicines Department No.ADR-MUMS/ 2002/ PR.No. 116/02/ AYUSH-2, dated 05/12/2003 with effect from January, 2004. Accordingly, the final instalment of grant-in-aid for the years 2007-08 to 2016-2017 of the private government-aided Aryagala Medical College, Satara, has been completed and the final instalment of grant-in-aid for the year 2016-2017 is Rs. 1,99,207/- (Rupees One Lakh Ninety Nine Thousand Two Hundred and Seven only) is being sanctioned under this Government decision.
The cost of the project is Rs. 1,99,207/- (Rupees One Lakh Ninety Nine Thousand Two Hundred and Seven only) to be met out of the approved provision for the financial year 2018-2019 and the said expenditure to be covered under the following head.
