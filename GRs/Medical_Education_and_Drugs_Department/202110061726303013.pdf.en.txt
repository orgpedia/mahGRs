Payment of token amount as credit to resident doctors for their contribution towards treatment during COVID-19 period.
The Government of Maharashtra
Government Resolution No. MED 1021/ Q.No. 281/21/ Education-2,
Department of Medical Education and Drugs,
Hospital premises, New Ministry, 9th Floor,
Lokmanya Tilak Marg, Mumbai-400001.
Date: 6th of October 2021
Preamble: The Central Government had imposed a nationwide lockdown on 24th March, 2020 amidst a surge in COVID-19 cases in the country. The lockdown was imposed in the state during this period. Meanwhile, Covid-19 cases in the state continued to rise. In such an adverse situation, the students of post-graduate courses (junior residents and senior residents) in government and municipal medical colleges in the state performed a very valuable job of providing treatment to Covid infected patients. The matter of paying a symbolic amount as a loan instruction for this contribution was under the consideration of the government.
The Government has approved a token amount of Rs. 1,21,000/- (Rupees One Lakh Twenty One Thousand only) each as a loan to the Post Graduate students of Government and Municipal Medical Colleges and Post Graduate students (Resident Doctors) of Government Ayurveda Colleges for their invaluable contribution to the patient care during the COVID-19 pandemic.
This amount will be admissible only to the students of postgraduate courses (Junior Resident- 1/2/3 and Senior Resident- 1/2/3) currently working in Government/ Municipal Medical Colleges and Government Ayurveda Colleges.
The amount will be paid in two installments (October 2021 and March 2022).
