The Proposal:
Candidates who have completed PG Diploma/ Degree are required to complete the bonded service of the prescribed period for the relevant course. The bonded service has been renamed as' Social Responsibility Service' under the Government Charter at' Vacha'. The medical services rendered by the Indian Defence Forces at any place or provided by the Government health institutions located in the State of Maharashtra are considered as social responsibility services. Various measures are being taken across the country to control the spread of Covid-19. COVID Care Centres (CCCs), Dedicated COVID Health Centres (DCHCs) and Dedicated COVID Hospitals (DCHs) have been set up in the State as per the guidelines issued by the Ministry of Health& Family Welfare, Government of India for treatment of COVID-19 patients depending upon the nature of symptoms. It was under the consideration of the government to declare the medical services provided in these facilities for the treatment of Covid-19 as a social responsibility service.
Government's decision:
In accordance with the guidelines issued by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India for the control of COVID-19 disease and with the approval of the competent authorities, the medical services provided for the treatment of COVID patients in the COVID Care Centre (CCC), Dedicated COVID Health Centre (DCHC) and Dedicated COVID Hospital (DCH) health facilities set up in the State are being recognized as Socially Responsible Services subject to the following terms and conditions:

2. terms and conditions:
1) The above health facilities must be started after obtaining due approval from the competent authorities. The competent authority includes the concerned District Collector, Deputy Director (Health Services)/ District Surgeon, Municipal Commissioner/ Municipal Chief Officer or Chief Health Officer of the Municipal Corporation/ Municipality and other government officials.
(2) A certificate shall be issued to the candidate by the competent authority concerned after taking into account the records relating to the appointment of the candidate in the said health facility, the actual period of service rendered by him (excluding the period of leave which is not admissible) and the contribution of the candidate in the treatment of COVID-19 patients. The Director, Directorate of Medical Education and Research is responsible for issuing the Social Responsibility Service Free Certificate.
(3) If the prescribed period of service of the candidate's social responsibility service is not completed even after the period of service rendered by him in the above health facilities, in such a case, he will be required to complete the remaining period of service in vain. After that, the candidate will be eligible for release from the Social Responsibility Service.
(4) If it is found that the certificate of the competent authority regarding service in the said health facility has been obtained by fraudulent means, the concerned candidate will be liable to legal action.
