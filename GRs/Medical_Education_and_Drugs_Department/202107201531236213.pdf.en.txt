Introduction: As part of the Health Policy of the Union Government, a separate Ministry of AYUSH (Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, Homoeopathy) has been created to promote and propagate Indian systems of medicine as well as other systems of medicine in addition to modern medicine. The Ministry of AYUSH is working to provide health services to the people in a very effective and efficient manner. The state medical education and drugs department is also moving in the same direction.
Under this, Ayurveda graduates are coming to the society by taking quality Ayurveda education through Ayurveda colleges and are providing services for the health of the society.
Accordingly, keeping in view the requirement of Ayurveda experts in the health care sector in the State to achieve the basic principle of Ayurveda" Swasthya Swasthya Rakshanam," the Government was contemplating to establish a new Government Ayurveda College with an intake capacity of 100 students and an Ayurveda Hospital with 100 attached hospitals at Baramati, Pune. Accordingly, the proposal. It was submitted to the Cabinet for approval. It is now dated 04.07.2021. This was decided at the Cabinet meeting. The government has taken the following decisions:
The Government hereby approve the establishment of a new Government Ayurveda College with an intake capacity of 100 students and an attached Ayurveda Hospital of 100 beds at Baramati, Pune subject to approval of the Central Government and Central Council of Indian Medicine (CCIM), New Delhi.
