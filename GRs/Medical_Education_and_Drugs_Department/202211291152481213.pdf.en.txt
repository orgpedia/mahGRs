The government has decided to construct a new medical college with a capacity of 100 students and a 430-bed hospital attached to it, as well as the Maharashtra Postgraduate Institute of Medical Education and Research building of the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Nashik. In accordance with the decision taken by the High Level Committee of Secretaries, the construction budget of Rs. 348,41,12, 255/- (Rupees Three Hundred and Forty Eight Crores, Forty One Lakh, Twelve Thousand, Two Hundred and Fifty Five only) is being accorded administrative approval subject to the following terms and conditions.

Terms and Conditions: 1) The construction is being allowed subject to availability of 14.31 hectare land out of 20.47 hectare land in possession of the Institute. Also, the ownership of the land should be confirmed before the construction begins.
2) If the work falls under the jurisdiction of the Archaeological Department, prior permission of the concerned department as well as the Municipal Corporation and similar authorities should be taken.
3) While giving technical approval, the Government Circular No. 2017/ PR No. 11/ Planning-3, dated 11.04.2017 should be taken for the settlement of non-state tariff matters.
4) The suggestions issued under the Government Decisions dated 01.12.2016 and 24.08.2017 of the Department of Industry, Energy and Labour should be taken into account by adopting the e-tendering system for the procurement related matters in the proposal.
5) The Department of Environment, Government Decision No. ENV-2013/ Q. No.177/ T. Action should be taken as per the guidelines mentioned in No. 1, dated January 10, 2014.
6) The electrification and other ancillary works should be properly planned and completed during the construction period itself.
7) Action should be taken in accordance with the instructions given by the government from time to time regarding the various facilities to be provided for the disabled in the building.
8) It should be ensured that the area of the proposed building is in accordance with the permissible standards of the staff.
9) In the present case. The directions given in the meeting held on 05/08/2022 of the High Powered Committee constituted under the chairmanship of the Chief Secretary should be complied with.
10) Thematic work should be done with special care so that it does not require revised administrative approval.
