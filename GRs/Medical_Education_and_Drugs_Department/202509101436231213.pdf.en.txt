3) The proposed purchase rates are approximate when given administrative approval. Hence, the prices should be fixed at the end of the procurement process. 4) The specifications of the equipment (80911008 | specification) BIH should be comprehensive and accordingly the equipment supply WHI should be of such type that three tenders can be received from at least three manufacturers / suppliers. Also, the procurement process should be done only after verifying that the technical specification is not designed to benefit any particular company.
5) The Commissioner (Medical Education) and the Dean concerned shall ensure that the proposed equipment is not included in any proposal elsewhere / is not allowed to be purchased and that the equipment is required by the institution concerned.
6) While deciding on the purchase of machinery in this regard, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the provision of grant-in-aid sanctioned under the head of accounting provided for administrative approval.
7) It will be necessary to show the prices of the main equipment and their accessories as different.
8) While deciding on the purchase of machinery in this regard, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the provision of grant-in-aid sanctioned under the head of accounts provided for administrative approval.
9) Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) guidelines should be strictly followed while executing the entire procurement process.
