1) This administrative recognition is being provided to Government Medical Colleges and Hospitals subject to the availability of funds to the institution concerned from the grants available under the State Schemes during the financial year 2025-26.
1) The Government issued a "Manual of Procedure for Office Procurement to be carried out by the Government Department" in this regard by the Department of Industries, Power and Labour.
The procedures mentioned in the government decisions should be followed.
2) The proposed purchase rates are approximate when given administrative approval. Hence, the prices should be fixed at the end of the procurement process. The Commissioner (Medical Education) as well as the Principal concerned shall ensure that the rate is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the concerned supplier has supplied these items elsewhere.
3) The specifications of the equipment (8011109 specification), Bh, are comprehensive and should be such that three bids can be received from at least three manufacturers / suppliers who can supply the equipment accordingly. Also, the procurement process should be done only after verifying that the technical specification is not designed to benefit any particular company.
4) The Commissioner (Medical Education) and the Dean concerned shall ensure that the proposed equipment is not included in any proposal elsewhere / is not allowed to be purchased and that the equipment is required by the institution concerned.
5) While deciding on the purchase of machinery in this regard, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the provision of grant-in-aid sanctioned under the head of accounting provided for administrative approval.
6) It will be necessary to show the prices of the main equipment and their accessories as different.
7 While deciding on the purchase of machinery in this regard, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the provision of grant-in-aid sanctioned under the head of accounts provided for administrative approval.
The Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) guidelines should be followed strictly while executing the entire procurement process.
