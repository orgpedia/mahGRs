It should also be re-verified that the specification includes all the equipment required for the ICU.
The proposed purchase rates are estimated at the time of administrative approval. Hence, the prices should be fixed at the end of the procurement process. The Commissioner (Medical Education) as well as the Principal concerned shall ensure that the rate is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the concerned supplier has supplied these items elsewhere.
The ATH (Technical Specification) of the equipment should be comprehensive and the AHIR should be of such a type that at least three tenders can be received from three manufacturers / suppliers. Also, the procurement process should be done only after verifying that the technical specification is not designed to benefit any particular company.
The Dean will be responsible for ensuring that the turnkey project is set up as specified.
The Commissioner (Medical Education) and the Principal concerned shall ensure that the proposed equipment is not included in any proposal elsewhere / is not allowed to be purchased and that the equipment is required by the institution concerned.
