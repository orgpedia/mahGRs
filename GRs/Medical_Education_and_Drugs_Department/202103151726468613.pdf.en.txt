Nanded district is a district of Maharashtra in central India. Nanded is a holy city of Sikhs and attracts a large number of pilgrims and tourists every year. Also, Nanded city is developing as a major center in Marathwada from educational and commercial point of view, as many people from the district and surrounding areas visit the city.
As a result, the health care system is strained. Therefore, the establishment of a nursing college attached to the government medical college would have put a strain on the health system.
It will help to reduce. At present, there are 116 nursing colleges in Maharashtra. Out of these, 4 are State Government Nursing Colleges, 2 are Central Government Nursing Colleges and 110 are Private Nursing Colleges.
Keeping in view the requirement of nurses in government medical colleges and health services in the state, it is a matter of establishing a Government Nursing College (B.Sc.) with an intake capacity of 50 students at Dr. Shankarrao Chavan Government Medical College and Hospital, Nanded.
It was under the consideration of the government. Accordingly, the proposal. The proposal was approved in the Cabinet meeting held on 28.02.2021. The government has taken the following decisions:
Government's decision:
Considering the need of nurses in government medical colleges and health services in the state, Dr. Shankarrao Chavan Government Medical College and Hospital, Nanded
