Introduction:- Matoshree Asrabai Darade Homoeopathy College of Homoeopathy (BHMS) Course. A" No Objection Certificate" was issued to Jagdamba Education Society, Nashik under reference letter No. 10 in connection with the opening of a new college at Babhulgaon, T. Yevlanashik. Also, Consent of Affiliation was also granted by Maharashtra University of Health Sciences to start the said college of BHMS course as per Reference No. 9. However, to start this new Homoeopathy College, the Ministry of AYUSH, Government of India vide Reference No. The above was denied vide letter dated 11/08/2018. In Writ Petition No. 9458/2018 filed by the Institute against the said decision of the Central Government. The request of the institution to admit the students was allowed by the interim order dated 16/10/2018 of the High Court, Bombay. Accordingly, the students have been admitted to the college of the institute through the State Common Entrance Test Cell for the academic year 2018-19. Also, the academic year 2019-20 has been extended. The High Court of Bombay in Writ Petition No. 9867/2019 also referred to the admission of the students. By the interim order dated 25/09/2019 of the Court, the request of the Institute has been accepted and the students have been admitted for the academic year 2019-20 through the State Common Entrance Test Cell. In the academic year 2018-19 and 2019-20, 60 and 58 students of the said college respectively have passed B.H.M.S.
courses have been introduced. Some of these admissions have been approved by the admission regulatory authority and some of the admissions have been rejected.
During the period. In pursuance of the order passed by the Court, the Central Government has re-examined the said college and denied recognition to the new Homoeopathy College of the Institute vide order dated 13/04/2021. The organisation has challenged the decision. Writ Petition (Stamp) No. 10932/2021 was filed in the High Court, Bombay. The petition The High Court dismissed the petition. Appeal filed in Supreme Court also {Special Leave to Appeal (C) No. 11901/2021}, has been set aside vide order dated 09/08/2021. Students who were admitted in the academic year 2018-19 and 2019-20 affected by this, have been asked to declare their results and transfer them to other colleges. Writ Petition No. 5535/2021 and Writ Petition (Stamp) No. 99750/2020 have been filed in the High Court, Bombay respectively. In view of this, the Directorate of AYUSH is of the view that it would be appropriate to shift the said admitted students to other private homoeopathy colleges.
Based on the interim orders of the High Court in Writ Petition No. 9458/2018 and Writ Petition No. 9867/2019, the Government was considering to take a decision regarding the transfer/ shifting of the students admitted to the BHMS course in the academic year 2018-19 and 2019-20 to other private homoeopathy colleges in the State in view of their academic future.

Government Decision:- In the academic year 2018-19 and 2019-20, Matoshree Asrabai Darade Homoeopathy College of Jagdamba Education Society, M.P.O. Babhulgaon, T. The government is also giving approval to transfer/ shift eligible students admitted in Homeopathy colleges of Yeola, Nashik to other recognized private unaided Homeopathy colleges of the state in accordance with the completion of their remaining course in the vacant seats.
The students who have been admitted through the prescribed procedure and whose admissions will be approved by the Admission Regulatory Authority, will be eligible for transfer to another college.
3. The approval for the said migration/ transfer is being given keeping in view the academic future of the students admitted in the said colleges. However, this approval is subject to orders in the relevant court cases as well as approval of the Central Government, as the case may be.
The following action will be taken in connection with the allotment of college for the said transfer/ shifting.
(9) The" Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Nashik" shall be the competent authority to allot the college to the students.
(2) The vacancies available after the declaration of the result of the examination of the respective year of the previous BHMS course of the University in connection with the transfer of students admitted in the academic year 2018-19 and 2019-20 to other private homeopathic colleges shall be taken into account.
(3) The preferences of the private homeopathy colleges in which seats of the respective year of the BHMS course are vacant in accordance with the change of college will be filled by the students. Students will be provided with details of college-wise vacancies available to them along with the details of educational fees before filling the preference list.
(4) Firstly, the merit lists of all eligible students of BHMS course in the said institution will be prepared and published on the website of the University. The total marks obtained in the common entrance test (NEET) will be considered as the basis for preparing the merit list of first-year students. The total marks obtained by them in the corresponding examination (Term) of the university will be taken into account for preparing the merit lists of other years and accordingly the process of college allotment will be done.
(4) Every notice/ notification/ information and such other necessary publicity related to the allotment of colleges shall be published on the website of the University.
(6) Since the said college allotment process is being carried out in exceptional circumstances, other rules applicable to the general admission process, such as reservation, shall not apply. However, the students will have to pay the tuition fee of the Government of India or the State Government before their transfer.

The benefit of the reimbursement/ scholarship scheme which was permissible will continue to be applicable even after the transfer of the college with appropriate modifications.
(7) The institution concerned shall not charge any additional fee from the students in excess of the fee fixed by the competent authority.
(8) In accordance with the above guidelines, the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences will take immediate action to allocate college to the students. Necessary guidance/ support will be provided to the University by the Directorate of AYUSH in this process.
