The Proposal:
Reference No. 1 provides for reservation for the Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) in the State for admission to educational institutions in the State and in appointments to public services under the control of the State. Accordingly, under Reference No. 2, the said reservation has been made applicable for admission to Health Science courses.
Under Article 15 (4) and Article 16 (4) of the Constitution of India, 10 per cent reservation of seats in educational institutions/ aided schools, colleges, all higher educational institutions, excluding educational institutions of minority status, has been made applicable to open category students in the state. There is 10 per cent reservation in educational institutions.
Due to the implementation of the above SEBC and EWS reservation, the admission of some students in the open category has been affected and they have been forced to take admission in private colleges instead of government colleges. In pursuance of the representation received from Save Merit Save Nation Foundation, a meeting was held on 14.07.2019 at Mumbai. The meeting was chaired by the Chief Minister. It was agreed in principle that the gap in accessibility resulting from SEBC and EWS reservation will be filled from next year onwards and the students of open category of medical and dental courses affected by SEBC and EWS reservation in the current academic year will be reimbursed by the government for the academic fees of their respective courses. The government had to take a decision in this regard.
The ruling:
Due to the implementation of SEBC and EWS reservation, the admission of some students in the open category in the academic year 2019-20 has been disrupted and they have been forced to take admission in private unaided colleges instead of government colleges. In-principle approval is being given to reimburse the tuition fees charged by the competent authority for such courses to the open category students who have taken admission in medical and dental undergraduate/ postgraduate courses in private unaided colleges in the academic year 2019-20.
(2) While reimbursing the tuition fee of the aggrieved student, the amount of the fee that he would have had to pay if he had been admitted to the Government Medical/ Dental College shall be deducted and the remaining fee paid shall be reimbursed.
Separate instructions will be issued regarding the eligibility criteria for the scheme and the detailed procedure for fee reimbursement.
It will be applicable only to open category students who are affected by the reservation of EWS categories and admitted to private unaided medical/ dental (undergraduate and postgraduate) colleges in the state. Four and a half years, B.D.S. four years, Post Graduate [-3]
