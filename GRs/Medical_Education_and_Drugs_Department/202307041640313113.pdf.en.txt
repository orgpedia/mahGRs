Subject:- To increase the number of MBBS seats in the country, to allocate funds for upgrading the existing government medical colleges.
With a view to increase the intake capacity of MBBS course by creating more facilities in medical colleges in line with the reservation provided to economically weaker sections, the Central Government has announced a scheme" Upgradation of existing State Government/ Central Government Medical Colleges to increase MBBS seats in the country" with Centre-State share of 60:40.
Under this scheme, 14 government medical colleges have been approved by the central government and each institution will get funds up to Rs 60 crore. The central share will be Rs 36 crore (60 per cent) and the state share will be Rs 24 crore (40 per cent). Thus, a total of Rs. 504.00 crore will be received from the Central Government for a total of 14 Government Medical Colleges in the State at the rate of Rs.36 crore per institution and a total of Rs.336.00 crore will be the liability for 14 Government Medical Colleges at the rate of Rs.24 crore per institution.
The Central Government has already released funds to the tune of Rs.36 crore each to Shri Vasantrao Naik Government Medical College and Hospital, Yavatmal and Indira Gandhi Government Medical College and Hospital, Nagpur under the above scheme vide its orders dated 09.01.2020 and dated 12.10.2020. Also, the central government has not yet released funds for Government Medical College and Hospital, Nagpur. At present, the remaining 11 government medical colleges and hospitals under the department have been given by the central government under this scheme. As of March 3, 2023, a total of Rs. 99.00 crore has been disbursed. 171 crore has been deposited in the Consolidated Fund of the State.
Accordingly, the State Government has released Rs. 114 crore.
